Centaurea kemulariae

Centaurea kemulariae, commonly known as the Kemularia thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
