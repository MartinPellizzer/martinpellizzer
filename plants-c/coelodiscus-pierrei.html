Coelodiscus pierrei

Coelodiscus pierrei, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
