Crocus kosaninii f. albidus

Crocus kosaninii f. albidus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a botanical variant of the species Crocus kosaninii, characterized by its pale to white flower color.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, particularly in Albania and Kosovo. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is appreciated by horticulturists and botanists alike.
