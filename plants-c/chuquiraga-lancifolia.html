Chuquiraga lancifolia

Chuquiraga lancifolia, commonly known as the Andean burr or chuchuhuasi, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and spiny, woody fruits that enclose its seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Chuquiraga lancifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for its purported health benefits.
