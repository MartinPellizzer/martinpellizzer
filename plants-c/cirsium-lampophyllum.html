Cirsium lampophyllum

Cirsium lampophyllum, commonly known as the red-centred thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's robust structure and defensive spines help it thrive in harsh environments.
