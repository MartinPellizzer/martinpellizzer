Collomia grandiflora subsp. diffusa

Collomia grandiflora subsp.

diffusa, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its star-shaped, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. It has hairy, glandular leaves and a short, upright stem.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native environment.
