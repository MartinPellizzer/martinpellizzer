Carex longipes var. sessilis

Carex longipes var. sessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, a common trait among sedges. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that contribute to soil stabilization.
