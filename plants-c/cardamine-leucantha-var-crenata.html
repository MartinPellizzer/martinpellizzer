Cardamine leucantha var. crenata

Cardamine leucantha var. crenata, commonly known as the serrated watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are often submerged or floating, adapting to aquatic conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
