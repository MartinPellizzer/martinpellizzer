Cissus assamica

Cissus assamica, commonly known as the Assam devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are typically glossy and can vary in color from light green to dark green.

Cissus assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
