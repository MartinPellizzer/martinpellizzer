Corydalis aurea var. speciosa

Corydalis aurea var. speciosa, commonly known as the golden corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, ascending stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its flowers are characterized by their unique structure, with two upper petals forming a hood and two lower petals that are spurred.

Corydalis aurea var. speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
