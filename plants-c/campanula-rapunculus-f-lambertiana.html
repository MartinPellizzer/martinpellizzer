Campanula rapunculus f. lambertiana

Campanula rapunculus f. lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
