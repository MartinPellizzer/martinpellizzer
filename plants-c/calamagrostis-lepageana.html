Calamagrostis lepageana

Calamagrostis lepageana, commonly known as the blue joint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Calamagrostis lepageana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
