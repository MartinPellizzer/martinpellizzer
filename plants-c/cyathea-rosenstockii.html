Cyathea rosenstockii

Cyathea rosenstockii, commonly known as the Rosenstock's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant features a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea rosenstockii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
