Centaurea cataonica subsp. syriaca

Centaurea cataonica subsp.

syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Syria and Turkey. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
