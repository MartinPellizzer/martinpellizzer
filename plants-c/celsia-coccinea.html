Celsia coccinea

Celsia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vivid red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Celsia coccinea is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
