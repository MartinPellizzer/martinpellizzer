Cassia holwayana

Cassia holwayana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is recognized for its bright yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring or early summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they have multiple pairs of small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance.

Cassia holwayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
