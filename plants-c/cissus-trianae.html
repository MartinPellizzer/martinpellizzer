Cissus trianae

Cissus trianae, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rapid growth habit makes it popular as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.

Cissus trianae is also notable for its ability to thrive in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to diverse ecological conditions.
