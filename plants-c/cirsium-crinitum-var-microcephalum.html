Cirsium crinitum var. microcephalum

Cirsium crinitum var. microcephalum, commonly known as the narrow-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact growth habit. Its small, rounded flower heads are surrounded by bracts that contribute to its thistle-like appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in poor soils.
