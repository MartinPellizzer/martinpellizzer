Calamagrostis nitidula var. elata

Calamagrostis nitidula var. elata, commonly known as the tall blue joint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its striking blue-green foliage that turns golden in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife and stabilizing soil in wet environments.
