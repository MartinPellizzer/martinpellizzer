Conyza chrysocoma

Conyza chrysocoma, commonly known as golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Conyza chrysocoma is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
