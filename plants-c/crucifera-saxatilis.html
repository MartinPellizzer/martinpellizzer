Crucifera saxatilis

Crucifera saxatilis, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

Crucifera saxatilis is known for its resilience to harsh conditions, making it a common sight in mountainous regions.
