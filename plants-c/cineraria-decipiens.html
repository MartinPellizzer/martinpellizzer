Cineraria decipiens

Cineraria decipiens, commonly known as the false cineraria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Cineraria decipiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
