Cacalia livingstoniana

Cacalia livingstoniana, commonly known as the Livingston's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Cacalia livingstoniana thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
