Cerinthe minor subsp. auriculata

Cerinthe minor subsp.

auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers with distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) lobes on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
