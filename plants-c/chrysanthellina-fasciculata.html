Chrysanthellina fasciculata

Chrysanthellina fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
