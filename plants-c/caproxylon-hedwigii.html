Caproxylon hedwigii

Caproxylon hedwigii, commonly known as the Hedwig's caper.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits that are typically harvested for their edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
