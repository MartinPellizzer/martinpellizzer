Cyperus kyllingia var. latifolius

Cyperus kyllingia var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
