Coronopus violaceus var. condensatus

Coronopus violaceus var. condensatus, commonly known as the dense crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and trifoliate leaves with purple-tinged veins. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It has deep taproots that help stabilize soil and fix nitrogen, making it useful in erosion control.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance contribute to its widespread use in landscaping and restoration projects.
