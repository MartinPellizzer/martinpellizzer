Cucumis umbellatus

Cucumis umbellatus, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, spiny fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a characteristic known as monoecious.
