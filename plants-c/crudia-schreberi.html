Crudia schreberi

Crudia schreberi, commonly known as the Schreber's bushveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Crudia schreberi is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
