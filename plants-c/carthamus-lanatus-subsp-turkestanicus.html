Carthamus lanatus subsp. turkestanicus

Carthamus lanatus subsp.

turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
