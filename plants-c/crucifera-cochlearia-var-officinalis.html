Crucifera cochlearia var. officinalis

Crucifera cochlearia var. officinalis, commonly known as the common horned poppy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped seed pods and bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

The species has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
