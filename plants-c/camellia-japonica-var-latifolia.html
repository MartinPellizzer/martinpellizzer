Camellia japonica var. latifolia

Camellia japonica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and seasonal beauty.
