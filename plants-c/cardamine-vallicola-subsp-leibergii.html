Cardamine vallicola subsp. leibergii

Cardamine vallicola subsp.

leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette, with slender, hairy stems. Its flowers are white or pale green, borne in clusters and resembling those of other members of the Ranunculaceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
