Clematis tubulosa var. davidiana

Clematis tubulosa var. davidiana, commonly known as the David's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white to pale blue, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
