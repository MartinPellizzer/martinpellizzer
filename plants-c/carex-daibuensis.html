Carex daibuensis

Carex daibuensis, commonly known as the Daibu sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Carex daibuensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
