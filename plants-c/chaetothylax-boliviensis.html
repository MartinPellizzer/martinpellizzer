Chaetothylax boliviensis

Chaetothylax boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian feathered grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly in Bolivia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
