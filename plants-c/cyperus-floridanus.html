Cyperus floridanus

Cyperus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color. It is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Cyperus floridanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
