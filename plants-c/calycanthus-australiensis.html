Calycanthus australiensis

Calycanthus australiensis, commonly known as the Australian sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Calycanthaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral scent and aesthetic appeal.
