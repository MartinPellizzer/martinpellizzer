Cotyledon adunca

Cotyledon adunca, commonly known as the wrinkled cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is known for its ability to store water in its leaves and stems.

Cotyledon adunca is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
