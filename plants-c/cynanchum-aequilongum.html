Cynanchum aequilongum

Cynanchum aequilongum, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cynanchum aequilongum is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
