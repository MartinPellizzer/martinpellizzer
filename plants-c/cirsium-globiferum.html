Cirsium globiferum

Cirsium globiferum, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue-purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiny, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium globiferum is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
