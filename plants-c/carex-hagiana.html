Carex hagiana

Carex hagiana, commonly known as the Hag Island sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. Carex hagiana has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
