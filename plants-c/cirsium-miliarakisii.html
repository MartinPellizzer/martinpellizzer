Cirsium miliarakisii

Cirsium miliarakisii, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Greece.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
