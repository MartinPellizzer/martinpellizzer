Cyperus manimae

Cyperus manimae, commonly known as the Manimae flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually less than 1 meter tall, and it produces small, clustered flowering spikes.

Cyperus manimae is characterized by its narrow leaves and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
