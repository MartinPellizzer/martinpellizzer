Ceratostylis backeri

Ceratostylis backeri, commonly known as the black mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It typically grows in dense, shaded understorey environments, often in rainforest or heathland habitats. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and delicate, usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

It is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
