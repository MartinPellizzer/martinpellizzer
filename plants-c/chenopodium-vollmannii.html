Chenopodium vollmannii

Chenopodium vollmannii, commonly known as New Mexico goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has alternate, ovate to rhombic leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in dry, three-lobed capsules, and the plant is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Chenopodium vollmannii is also sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
