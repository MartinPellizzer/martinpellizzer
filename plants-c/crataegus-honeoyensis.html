Crataegus honeoyensis

Crataegus honeoyensis, commonly known as the Honeoye serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer.

Its botanical features include a rugged appearance, making it well-suited for naturalized landscapes and erosion control.
