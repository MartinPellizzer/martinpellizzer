Cerinthe glabra subsp. longiflora

Cerinthe glabra subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the slender horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking, orange-red flowers with long, slender petals that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
