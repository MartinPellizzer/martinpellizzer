Chamaescilla corymbosa var. paradoxa

Chamaescilla corymbosa var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxa chamaescilla, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in corymbs, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
