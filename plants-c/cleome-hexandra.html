Cleome hexandra

Cleome hexandra, commonly known as sweet cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its branching structure and clusters of small, often yellow or white, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate and typically have a slightly serrated edge.

Cleome hexandra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and ease of growth.
