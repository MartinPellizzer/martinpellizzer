Chenopodium × binzianum var. obtusum

Chenopodium × binzianum var. obtusum, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prickly, spherical fruit heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough, textured surface, while the stems are erect and branching.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes considered a weed.
