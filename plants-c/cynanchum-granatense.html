Cynanchum granatense

Cynanchum granatense, commonly known as the granate milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
