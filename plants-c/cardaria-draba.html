Cardaria draba

Cardaria draba, commonly known as whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cardaria draba is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
