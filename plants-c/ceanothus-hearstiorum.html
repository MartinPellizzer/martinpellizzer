Ceanothus hearstiorum

Ceanothus hearstiorum, commonly known as Hearst's ceanothus, is a flowering shrub native to California.

It produces clusters of showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has fragrant, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Ceanothus hearstiorum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
