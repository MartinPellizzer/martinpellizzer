Caesalpinia rubicunda

Caesalpinia rubicunda, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several hard, angular seeds.

Caesalpinia rubicunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
