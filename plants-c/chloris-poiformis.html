Chloris poiformis

Chloris poiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris poiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
