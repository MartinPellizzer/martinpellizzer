Cyclanthera mathewsii var. subintegrifolia

Cyclanthera mathewsii var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive narrow, serrated margin. Its pale yellow flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is typically elongated and has a bitter taste, though it is occasionally cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
