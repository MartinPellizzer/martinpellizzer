Carlina acanthifolia subsp. acanthifolia

Carlina acanthifolia subsp.

acanthifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with rough, bristly hairs.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive serrated edge and a hairy surface. The plant produces showy, yellow-centered composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and wastelands.
