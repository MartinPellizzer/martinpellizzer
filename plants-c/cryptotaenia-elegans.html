Cryptotaenia elegans

Cryptotaenia elegans, commonly known as Japanese cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
