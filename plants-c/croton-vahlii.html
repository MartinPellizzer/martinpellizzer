Croton vahlii

Croton vahlii, commonly known as the Vahli's croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
