Clematis columbiana var. columbiana

Clematis columbiana var. columbiana, commonly known as the Pacific clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to western North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can reach lengths of up to 15 meters, making it a vigorous climber.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in forested areas along streambanks.
