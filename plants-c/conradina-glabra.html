Conradina glabra

Conradina glabra, commonly known as smooth coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or lavender in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in a fine, smooth texture.

Conradina glabra is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in coastal and woodland habitats.
