Celastrus geminiflorus

Celastrus geminiflorus, commonly known as the twin-flower staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its distinctive twin-flowered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in pairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, elliptical seeds.
