Caladenia fluvialis

Caladenia fluvialis, commonly known as the river orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as riverbanks and swampy areas. The plant has a single, erect stem that bears one or two flowers, which are usually pale green to yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers have a distinctive, spurred lip that is often a different color from the rest of the flower.

Caladenia fluvialis is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate and elegant appearance.
