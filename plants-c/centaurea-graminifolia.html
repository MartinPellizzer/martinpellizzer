Centaurea graminifolia

Centaurea graminifolia, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
