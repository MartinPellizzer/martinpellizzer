Coronilla minima var. extensa

Coronilla minima var. extensa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crownvetch, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
