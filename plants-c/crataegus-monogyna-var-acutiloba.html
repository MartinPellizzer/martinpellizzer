Crataegus monogyna var. acutiloba

Crataegus monogyna var. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharplobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
