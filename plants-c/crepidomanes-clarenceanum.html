Crepidomanes clarenceanum

Crepidomanes clarenceanum, commonly known as the Clarencean cliffmoss, is a rare and specialized liverwort that thrives in rocky, coastal environments.

It is characterized by its small, closely packed leaves that form dense mats on vertical surfaces. The plant exhibits a unique thalloid structure, with a flattened, ribbon-like body that is often covered in a layer of fine, moss-like growth. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its harsh habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in certain regions.
