Cryptanthus vexatus

Cryptanthus vexatus, commonly known as the "Flame Violet," is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance.

Cryptanthus vexatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and seasonal blooms.
