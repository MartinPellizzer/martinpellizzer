Cistanche tubulosa var. flava

Cistanche tubulosa var. flava, commonly known as the yellow sand whisker, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia, including China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients, typically parasitizing species of the genus Haloxylon. Its structure consists of a yellowish, branched stem with small, scale-like leaves.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported tonic and rejuvenating properties.
