Carlina sicula subsp. sicula

Carlina sicula subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, rough-textured leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, yellow-centered composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Carlina sicula subsp.

sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
