Chusquea hatschbachii

Chusquea hatschbachii, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically reaches heights of up to 10 meters and has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
