Camellia yunnanensis var. yunnanensis

Camellia yunnanensis var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan camellia, is a species native to China, particularly in Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.
