Corybas grumulus

Corybas grumulus, commonly known as the woolly hooded orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that resembles a small, fuzzy cup. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually dark red or purple, with a distinctive, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Corybas grumulus is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
