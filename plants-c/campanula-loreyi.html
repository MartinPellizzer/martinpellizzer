Campanula loreyi

Campanula loreyi, commonly known as the Lorey's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula loreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
