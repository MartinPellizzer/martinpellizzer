Cadaba linearifolia

Cadaba linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for coastal and arid landscapes.
