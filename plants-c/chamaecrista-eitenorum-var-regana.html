Chamaecrista eitenorum var. regana

Chamaecrista eitenorum var. regana, commonly known as the Eiten's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and distinctive, cylindrical, spiny pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
