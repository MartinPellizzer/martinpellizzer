Cordia latiloba

Cordia latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits that are often red when ripe. Cordia latiloba is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
