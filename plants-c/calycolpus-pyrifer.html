Calycolpus pyrifer

Calycolpus pyrifer, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Calycolpus pyrifer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
