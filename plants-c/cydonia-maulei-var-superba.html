Cydonia maulei var. superba

Cydonia maulei var. superba, commonly known as the Chilean quince, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and oval, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its fruit, which is often used in traditional cooking, has a strong, aromatic flavor and is rich in nutrients.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
