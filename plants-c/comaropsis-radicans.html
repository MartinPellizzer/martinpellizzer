Comaropsis radicans

Comaropsis radicans, commonly known as the creeping cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a sprawling habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields.

Comaropsis radicans is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
