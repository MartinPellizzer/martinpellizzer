Crossotropis arenaria

Crossotropis arenaria, commonly known as the sand crossotropis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in sandy soils and can withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crossotropis arenaria is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
