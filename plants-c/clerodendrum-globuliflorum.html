Clerodendrum globuliflorum

Clerodendrum globuliflorum, commonly known as the globe-flowered cleverweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Clerodendrum globuliflorum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
