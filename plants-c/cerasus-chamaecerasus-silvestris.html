Cerasus chamaecerasus silvestris

Cerasus chamaecerasus silvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry or wild cherry tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are typically dark red to black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and gray in younger trees.

This species is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
