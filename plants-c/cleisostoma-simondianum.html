Cleisostoma simondianum

Cleisostoma simondianum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

The genus Cleisostoma is known for its intricate floral structures and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
