Cereus campylacanthus

Cereus campylacanthus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces striking, white or pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its stems are often segmented and have a unique, swollen appearance.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
