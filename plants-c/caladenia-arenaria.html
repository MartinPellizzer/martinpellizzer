Caladenia arenaria

Caladenia arenaria, commonly known as the sandy spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. The plant features a single, slender stem that bears a single, strikingly shaped flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with intricate patterns that mimic the texture of spider silk.

Caladenia arenaria is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
