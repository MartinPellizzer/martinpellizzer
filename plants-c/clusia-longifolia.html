Clusia longifolia

Clusia longifolia, commonly known as the evergreen shrub or wax plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and thick, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Clusia longifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
