Campanula rotundifolia

Campanula rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, velvety leaves and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Campanula rotundifolia is also a food source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
