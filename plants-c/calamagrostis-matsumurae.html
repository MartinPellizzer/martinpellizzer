Calamagrostis matsumurae

Calamagrostis matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsumura's reed, is a grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are often pinkish or purplish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Calamagrostis matsumurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
