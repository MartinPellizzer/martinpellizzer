Croton myriadenus var. parvifolius

Croton myriadenus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage with leaves that are typically small and ovate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
