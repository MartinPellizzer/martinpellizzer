Carex muricata var. cephalantha

Carex muricata var. cephalantha, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly rough to the touch. Its flowering spikes are tall and cylindrical, bearing small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
