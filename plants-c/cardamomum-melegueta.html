Cardamomum melegueta

Cardamomum melegueta, commonly known as African cardamom, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped berries. The plant produces bright red, three-lobed fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, spicy scent.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its valuable spice.
