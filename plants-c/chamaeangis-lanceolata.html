Chamaeangis lanceolata

Chamaeangis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf chamaeangis, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale green, and appear in late spring.

Chamaeangis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
