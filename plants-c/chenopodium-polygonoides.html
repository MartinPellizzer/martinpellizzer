Chenopodium polygonoides

Chenopodium polygonoides, commonly known as sand spinach, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, green to reddish leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
