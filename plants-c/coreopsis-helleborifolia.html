Coreopsis helleborifolia

Coreopsis helleborifolia, commonly known as the whitecoreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like flowers and composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its white or pale yellow blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators, contributing to ecological biodiversity in its habitat.
