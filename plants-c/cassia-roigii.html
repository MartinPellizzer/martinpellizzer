Cassia roigii

Cassia roigii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

Cassia roigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
