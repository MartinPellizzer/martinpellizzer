Cuphea parsonsia

Cuphea parsonsia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows as an upright shrub. Cuphea parsonsia thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
