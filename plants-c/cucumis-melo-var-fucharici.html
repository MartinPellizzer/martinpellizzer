Cucumis melo var. fucharici

Cucumis melo var. fucharici, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon characterized by its elongated, thin, and crisp fruit.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant typically grows on a trailing or climbing vine, producing small, pale yellow flowers that give way to the fruit. Its skin is thin and pale green, often with a slight waxy coating, and the flesh is juicy and sweet.

This variety is valued for its refreshing taste and is commonly used in salads and fresh consumption.
