Campanula glandulosa

Campanula glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered in small glands, which give it its scientific name.

Campanula glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
