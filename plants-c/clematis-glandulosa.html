Clematis glandulosa

Clematis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in small, sticky glands. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can grow up to 20 feet long, making it a vigorous climber.

Clematis glandulosa thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
