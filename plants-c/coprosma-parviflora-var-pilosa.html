Coprosma parviflora var. pilosa

Coprosma parviflora var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a rough texture due to the fine hairs covering them.
