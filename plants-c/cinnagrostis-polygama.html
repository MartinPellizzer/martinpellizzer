Cinnagrostis polygama

Cinnagrostis polygama, commonly known as the common ragwort, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive bitter taste, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Cinnagrostis polygama is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many areas.
