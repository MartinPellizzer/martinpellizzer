Carex verna f. chlorantha

Carex verna f. chlorantha, commonly known as the spring sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
