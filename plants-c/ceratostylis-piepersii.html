Ceratostylis piepersii

Ceratostylis piepersii, commonly known as the New Zealand violet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip, which is a distinguishing feature of many orchid species.

Ceratostylis piepersii is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with mosses and lichens in its natural habitat.
