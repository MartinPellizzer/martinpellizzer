Clematis chengguensis

Clematis chengguensis, commonly known as the Chenggu clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Clematis chengguensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability.
