Capsicum annuum var. bicolor

Capsicum annuum var. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor chili pepper, is a variety of the common chili plant characterized by its distinctive two-colored fruits.

The fruits typically display a mix of white, yellow, and red hues, depending on the stage of maturity. This plant is native to the Americas and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. It produces small to medium-sized peppers that are often used in culinary applications for their heat and flavor.

The plant is an annual herb with compound leaves and erect growth habit.
