Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. exile

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

exile, commonly known as slender everlasting, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to golden-yellow flower heads that bloom in spring.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender stems and smaller flowers compared to other subspecies of the species.
