Cotula sessilis

Cotula sessilis, commonly known as the sessile cotula, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cotula sessilis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
