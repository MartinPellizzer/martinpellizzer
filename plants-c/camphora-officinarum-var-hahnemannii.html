Camphora officinarum var. hahnemannii

Camphora officinarum var. hahnemannii, commonly known as the Hahnemann's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant, resinous wood. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark, spherical berries. Its essential oils, particularly camphor, are extracted from the bark and wood and have been used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in the production of various commercial products.
