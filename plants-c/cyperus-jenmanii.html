Cyperus jenmanii

Cyperus jenmanii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus jenmanii thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.
