Ceropegia × meyeri-arthuri

Ceropegia × meyeri-arthuri, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or pink markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which are pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
