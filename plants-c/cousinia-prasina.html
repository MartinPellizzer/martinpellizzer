Cousinia prasina

Cousinia prasina, commonly known as the green huskflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Cousinia prasina is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
