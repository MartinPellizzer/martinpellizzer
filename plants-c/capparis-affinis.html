Capparis affinis

Capparis affinis, commonly known as the small caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and aromatic, often thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are pinnate, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and the fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Capparis affinis is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
