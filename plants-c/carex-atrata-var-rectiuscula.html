Carex atrata var. rectiuscula

Carex atrata var. rectiuscula, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its dark green foliage and produces small, clustered brown flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex atrata var. rectiuscula plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
