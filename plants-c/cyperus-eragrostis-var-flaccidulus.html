Cyperus eragrostis var. flaccidulus

Cyperus eragrostis var. flaccidulus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny flowers. This variant is typically found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, often saline soils. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of coastal and freshwater wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
