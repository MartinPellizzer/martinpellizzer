Chenopodium album f. ovalifolium

Chenopodium album f. ovalifolium, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a variant of the common lambsquarters.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and smooth, sometimes prickly surfaces. The leaves are typically alternate, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to accumulate heavy metals, making it a common weed in various environments.
