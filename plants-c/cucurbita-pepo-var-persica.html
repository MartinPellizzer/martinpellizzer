Cucurbita pepo var. persica

Cucurbita pepo var. persica, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the gourd family.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and usually ribbed fruit, which can range in color from pale green to yellow or orange. The plant is typically annual, producing separate male and female flowers on the same plant. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often cultivated for its edible, crisp texture and mild flavor.
