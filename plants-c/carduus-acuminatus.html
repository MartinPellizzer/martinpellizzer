Carduus acuminatus

Carduus acuminatus, commonly known as the thistleweed or prickly thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in stiff, prickly hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carduus acuminatus is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry meadows, where it thrives in full sun.
