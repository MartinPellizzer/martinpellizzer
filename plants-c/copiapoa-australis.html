Copiapoa australis

Copiapoa australis, commonly known as the Chilean rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a low-growing, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick stem and having a shallow root system.

Copiapoa australis is often found growing on rocky outcrops and is a popular specimen for cactus enthusiasts.
