Callista cretacea

Callista cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous Callista, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, reflecting its drought-resistant nature. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its historical significance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
