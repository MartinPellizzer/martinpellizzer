Cirsium × rohlenae

Cirsium × rohlenae, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 60–120 cm and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
