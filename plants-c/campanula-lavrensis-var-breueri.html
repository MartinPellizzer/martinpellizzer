Campanula lavrensis var. breueri

Campanula lavrensis var. breueri, commonly known as the Breuer's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
