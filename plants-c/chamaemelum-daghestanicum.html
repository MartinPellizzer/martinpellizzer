Chamaemelum daghestanicum

Chamaemelum daghestanicum, commonly known as the Daghestan chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly in the Daghestan area of Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting species within the genus Chamaemelum.
