Capparis pedunculosa var. longispina

Capparis pedunculosa var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, and its showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
