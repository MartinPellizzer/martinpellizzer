Cucurbita citrullus var. viridis

Cucurbita citrullus var. viridis, commonly known as the green leaf gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, green fruits with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible young fruits, which have a mild, slightly bitter flavor.
