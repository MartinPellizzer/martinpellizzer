Centaurea × johannae

Centaurea × johannae, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowering heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
