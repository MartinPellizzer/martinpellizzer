Chlorocyperus melanostachyus

Chlorocyperus melanostachyus, commonly known as black-stalked flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, pale flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.
