Crossolepis pusilla

Crossolepis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf crossolepis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water availability.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
