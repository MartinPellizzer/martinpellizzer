Carex pseudocyperus var. lechleri

Carex pseudocyperus var. lechleri, commonly known as the Lechleri sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes. Native to certain regions of South America, this sedge thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
