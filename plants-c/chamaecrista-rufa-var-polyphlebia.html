Chamaecrista rufa var. polyphlebia

Chamaecrista rufa var. polyphlebia, commonly known as the red-winged cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The seeds are encased in hairy pods, which help in seed dispersal through the environment.
