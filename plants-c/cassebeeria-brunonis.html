Cassebeeria brunonis

Cassebeeria brunonis, commonly known as the velvetleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Cassebeeria brunonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
