Carex polyrrhiza f. breviscapa

Carex polyrrhiza f. breviscapa, commonly known as the short-spatulate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This subspecies is typically found in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often dense and forms a carpet-like growth, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
