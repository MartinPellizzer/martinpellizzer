Coleus aromaticus

Coleus aromaticus, commonly known as Greek sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its aromatic leaves, it is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
