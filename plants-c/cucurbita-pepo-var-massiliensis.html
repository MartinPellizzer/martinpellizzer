Cucurbita pepo var. massiliensis

Cucurbita pepo var. massiliensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean gourd, is a variety of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit with a smooth, pale green skin. The plant is a vine that produces both male and female flowers, typical of many cucurbit species. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which can be used in various culinary applications.
