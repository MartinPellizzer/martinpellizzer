Clarkia speciosa subsp. immaculata

Clarkia speciosa subsp.

immaculata, commonly known as the white clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
