Cardamomum scottii

Cardamomum scottii, commonly known as the African cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to tropical Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces aromatic, dark brown pods containing small, flavorful seeds. These seeds are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive spice.

Its botanical features include rhizomatous growth and lance-shaped, hairy leaves.
