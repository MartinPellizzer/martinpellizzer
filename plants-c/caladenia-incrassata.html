Caladenia incrassata

Caladenia incrassata, commonly known as the broad-leafed spider-orchid, is an endemic species found in Western Australia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in sandy soils within heathlands and open woodlands. The plant features a single, erect stem with a single, showy, spider-like flower that is usually pink to purple in color. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Caladenia incrassata is known for its unique floral structure and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and conservation significance.
