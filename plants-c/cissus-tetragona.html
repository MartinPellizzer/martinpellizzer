Cissus tetragona

Cissus tetragona, commonly known as the diamondleaf or four-cornered fig, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves with four prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular ornamental plant.
