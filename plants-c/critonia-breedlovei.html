Critonia breedlovei

Critonia breedlovei, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy shrub or small tree suitable for landscaping in arid climates.
