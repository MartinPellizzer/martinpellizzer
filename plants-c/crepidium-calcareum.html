Crepidium calcareum

Crepidium calcareum, commonly known as the limestone orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in calcareous soils, thriving in open woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pale green or white in color. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
