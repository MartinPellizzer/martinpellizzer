Crataegus monogyna f. rotundicarpa

Crataegus monogyna f. rotundicarpa, commonly known as the rounded-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive, round, and often fleshy fruits that differ from the typical elongated berries of the species. The plant is deciduous, producing small white flowers in spring followed by its unique fruiting bodies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to wildlife.
