Cliffortia conifera

Cliffortia conifera, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and is well adapted to harsh, maritime climates. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Cliffortia conifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
