Campanula turbinata

Campanula turbinata, commonly known as the Turban bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Campanula turbinata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
