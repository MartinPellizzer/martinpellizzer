Cirsium chanroenicum var. lanceolata

Cirsium chanroenicum var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. Native to specific regions of Europe, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant is known for its robust growth and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
