Centaurea kartschiana subsp. curictana

Centaurea kartschiana subsp.

curictana, commonly known as the Curicta Centaurea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple capitula, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought resistance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
