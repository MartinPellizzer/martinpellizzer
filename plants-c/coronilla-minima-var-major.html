Coronilla minima var. major

Coronilla minima var. major, commonly known as the greater narrow-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
