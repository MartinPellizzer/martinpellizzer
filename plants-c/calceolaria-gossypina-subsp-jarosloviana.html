Calceolaria gossypina subsp. jarosloviana

Calceolaria gossypina subsp.

jarosloviana, commonly known as the Jaroslovian slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like petals that resemble small slippers, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, and it blooms during the cooler months.
