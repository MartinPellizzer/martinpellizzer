Calceolaria aconcaguina

Calceolaria aconcaguina, commonly known as the Aconcagua slipperflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, adapted to withstand the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Calceolaria aconcaguina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
