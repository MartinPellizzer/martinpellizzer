Convolvulus meisneri

Convolvulus meisneri, commonly known as the Meisner's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant has a climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers open in the morning and close by late afternoon.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
