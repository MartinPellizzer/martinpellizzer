Campomanesia racemosa

Campomanesia racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The berries are a food source for various wildlife species.
