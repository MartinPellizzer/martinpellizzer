Canariopsis altissima

Canariopsis altissima, commonly known as the tall canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
