Caltha alpestris

Caltha alpestris, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

Caltha alpestris is adapted to cold climates and is a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
