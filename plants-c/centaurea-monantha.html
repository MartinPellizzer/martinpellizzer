Centaurea monantha

Centaurea monantha, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with distinctive central purple or black discs, which attract various pollinators. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Centaurea monantha is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
