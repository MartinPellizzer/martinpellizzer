Campanula rotundifolia f. subracemosa

Campanula rotundifolia f. subracemosa, commonly known as the rounded-leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant features rounded, hairy leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
