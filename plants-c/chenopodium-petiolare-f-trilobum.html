Chenopodium petiolare f. trilobum

Chenopodium petiolare f. trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is notable for its adaptability and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
