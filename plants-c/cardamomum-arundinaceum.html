Cardamomum arundinaceum

Cardamomum arundinaceum, commonly known as bamboo cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces spindle-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Cardamomum arundinaceum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and culinary practices.
