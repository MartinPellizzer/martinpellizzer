Cineraria monticola

Cineraria monticola, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers range in color from white to purple.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
