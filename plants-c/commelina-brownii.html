Commelina brownii

Commelina brownii, commonly known as the brown bermuda grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers with three sepals and two petals. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can form dense mats in its native habitat.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential weed in some areas.
