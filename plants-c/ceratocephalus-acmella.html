Ceratocephalus acmella

Ceratocephalus acmella, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
