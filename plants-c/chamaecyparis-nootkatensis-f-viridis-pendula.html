Chamaecyparis nootkatensis f. viridis-pendula

Chamaecyparis nootkatensis f. viridis-pendula, commonly known as the green weeping cedar, is a unique form of the Alaska cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading, pendulous branches that create a lush, green canopy. The foliage is dense and needle-like, with a vibrant green color that remains throughout the growing season. This cultivar is often used in ornamental horticulture for its striking visual appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
