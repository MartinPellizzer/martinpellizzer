Chamaecrista elata

Chamaecrista elata, commonly known as the tall crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion, making it a useful species in ecological restoration. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
