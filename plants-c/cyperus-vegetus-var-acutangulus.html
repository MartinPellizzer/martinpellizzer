Cyperus vegetus var. acutangulus

Cyperus vegetus var. acutangulus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
