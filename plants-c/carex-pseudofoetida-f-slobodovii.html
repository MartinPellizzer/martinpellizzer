Carex pseudofoetida f. slobodovii

Carex pseudofoetida f. slobodovii, commonly known as the Slobodovii sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus, characterized by its distinctive morphological features.

It typically exhibits narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical structures. This plant is native to specific regions of Eastern Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

The botanical interest in this form lies in its unique variation and ecological adaptability.
