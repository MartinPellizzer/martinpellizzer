Campanula leucosiphon

Campanula leucosiphon, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender stems that often branch near the base, supporting clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
