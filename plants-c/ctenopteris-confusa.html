Ctenopteris confusa

Ctenopteris confusa, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are often divided into narrow, ribbon-like segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ctenopteris confusa is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
