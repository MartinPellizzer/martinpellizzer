Cochlospermum fraseri subsp. heteroneurum

Cochlospermum fraseri subsp.

heteroneurum, commonly known as the red-flowered glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with alternate arrangement, and its bark is often rough and fissured. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
