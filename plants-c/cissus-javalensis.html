Cissus javalensis

Cissus javalensis, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a waxy coating.

Cissus javalensis is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
