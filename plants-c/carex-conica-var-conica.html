Carex conica var. conica

Carex conica var. conica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats.

Carex conica var. conica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
