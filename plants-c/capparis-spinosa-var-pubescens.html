Capparis spinosa var. pubescens

Capparis spinosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible caper buds, which are used in various culinary applications.
