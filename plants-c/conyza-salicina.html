Conyza salicina

Conyza salicina, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Conyza salicina is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds carried by the wind.
