Cyanella odoratissima

Cyanella odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a distinctive sweet fragrance, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
