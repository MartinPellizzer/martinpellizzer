Coptis trifoliolata var. oligodonta

Coptis trifoliolata var. oligodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldthread, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by follicle fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
