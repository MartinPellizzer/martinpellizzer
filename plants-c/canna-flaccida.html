Canna flaccida

Canna flaccida, commonly known as the drooping cannas, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, showy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Canna flaccida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
