Crocus vernus var. maculosus

Crocus vernus var. maculosus, commonly known as the spotted crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, early spring blooms with purple petals adorned with white or yellow spots. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that emerge from the soil. Its flowers typically appear in shades of violet and white, often with a distinctive central yellow or white spot.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
