Crataegus calva

Crataegus calva, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Crataegus calva is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
