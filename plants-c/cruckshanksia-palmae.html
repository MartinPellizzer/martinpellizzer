Cruckshanksia palmae

Cruckshanksia palmae, commonly known as the palm-like fern, is a unique plant that exhibits characteristics of both ferns and palms.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a tall, slender stem with a crown of large, feathery fronds that resemble those of a palm tree. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pinnae that give it a lush, green appearance.

Cruckshanksia palmae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
