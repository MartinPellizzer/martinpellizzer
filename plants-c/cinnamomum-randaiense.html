Cinnamomum randaiense

Cinnamomum randaiense, commonly known as the Japanese cinnamon tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
