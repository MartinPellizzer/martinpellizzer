Centaurea nigrescens var. vochinensis

Centaurea nigrescens var. vochinensis, commonly known as the Vočin violetcentaurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
