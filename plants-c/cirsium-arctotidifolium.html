Cirsium arctotidifolium

Cirsium arctotidifolium, commonly known as the arctic thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, adapting to cold and harsh environmental conditions. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient environments.

Its ecological role includes providing food for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitats.
