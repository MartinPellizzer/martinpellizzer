Calea granitica

Calea granitica, commonly known as the granite calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive shape, while its stems are erect and branched.

Calea granitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
