Calea integrifolia

Calea integrifolia, commonly known as the sacred plant or angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calea integrifolia is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in spiritual rituals.
