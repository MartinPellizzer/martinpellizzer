Clinopodium alpinum subsp. sardoum

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

sardoum, commonly known as alpine woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually blue or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
