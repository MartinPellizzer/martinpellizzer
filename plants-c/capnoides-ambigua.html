Capnoides ambigua

Capnoides ambigua, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Capnoides ambigua is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.
