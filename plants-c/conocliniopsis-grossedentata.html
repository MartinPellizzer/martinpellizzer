Conocliniopsis grossedentata

Conocliniopsis grossedentata, commonly known as the large-toothed conchuela, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and large, pinnate leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
