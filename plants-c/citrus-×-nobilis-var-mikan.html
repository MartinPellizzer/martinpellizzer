Citrus × nobilis var. mikan

Citrus × nobilis var. mikan, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. The fruit has a thin, edible peel that is rich in vitamin C and contains essential oils. The flesh inside is sweet and juicy, often consumed fresh or used in cooking.

This plant thrives in warm climates and is well-suited for container gardening.
