Calamus oligophyllus

Calamus oligophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Calamus oligophyllus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
