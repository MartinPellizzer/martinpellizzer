Crotalaria bahiaensis

Crotalaria bahiaensis, commonly known as Bahia cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Crotalaria bahiaensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop.
