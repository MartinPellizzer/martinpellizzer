Crotalaria aridicola subsp. densifolia

Crotalaria aridicola subsp.

densifolia, commonly known as the desert crownflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, providing additional protection against desiccation.

This subspecies is well adapted to low-nutrient soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.
