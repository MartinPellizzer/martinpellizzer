Calamus amplijugus

Calamus amplijugus, commonly known as the broad-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach several feet in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus amplijugus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
