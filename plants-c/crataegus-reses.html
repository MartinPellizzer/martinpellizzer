Crataegus reses

Crataegus reses, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Crataegus reses is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
