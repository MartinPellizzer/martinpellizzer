Caragana arborescens var. pendula

Caragana arborescens var. pendula, commonly known as weeping shrub willow, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring, followed by narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is well adapted to cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking visual element.
