Cyphostemma leucotrichum

Cyphostemma leucotrichum, commonly known as the white-haired cyphostemma, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and its distinctive white or pale green hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Cyphostemma leucotrichum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
