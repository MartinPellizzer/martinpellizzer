Carex murrii

Carex murrii, commonly known as the Murri's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Carex murrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
