Centaurea abdelkaridi

Centaurea abdelkaridi, commonly known as the Abdelkaridi knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its striking purple or pink flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
