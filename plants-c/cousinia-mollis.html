Cousinia mollis

Cousinia mollis, commonly known as the silky cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Cousinia mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
