Chamaecrista jaegeri

Chamaecrista jaegeri, commonly known as Jaeger's burrlegume, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its unique, spiny seed pods that encase the seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Chamaecrista jaegeri plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
