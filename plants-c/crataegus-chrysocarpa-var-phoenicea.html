Crataegus chrysocarpa var. phoenicea

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. phoenicea, commonly known as the golden-hued hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish fruits and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks. Its botanical features include thorny branches, ovate leaves, and small, clustered flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
