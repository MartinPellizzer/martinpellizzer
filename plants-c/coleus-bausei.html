Coleus bausei

Coleus bausei, commonly known as the Bause's coleus, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, and they are usually pale purple or white.

Coleus bausei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
