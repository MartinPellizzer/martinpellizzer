Commiphora daro

Commiphora daro, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The tree produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.
