Calliandra houstoniana var. calothyrsus

Calliandra houstoniana var. calothyrsus, commonly known as the Houstonia calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that appear in clusters, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
