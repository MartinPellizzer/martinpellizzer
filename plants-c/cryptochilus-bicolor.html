Cryptochilus bicolor

Cryptochilus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive two-toned lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cryptochilus bicolor is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
