Cleistocactus tupizensis var. sucrensis

Cleistocactus tupizensis var. sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucrensis Christmas Cactus, is a unique and rare cactus species native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that typically bloom during the holiday season, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
