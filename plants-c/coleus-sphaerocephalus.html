Coleus sphaerocephalus

Coleus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble small, rounded leaves, which are often more colorful than the actual tiny flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, also known as the mint family, and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and as a border plant.
