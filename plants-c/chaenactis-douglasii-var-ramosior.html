Chaenactis douglasii var. ramosior

Chaenactis douglasii var. ramosior, commonly known as the Douglas's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It features a low-growing, mat-like structure with numerous branches and small, blue or purple flowers.

The species is adapted to harsh conditions, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.
