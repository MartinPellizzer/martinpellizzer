Coreopsis galericulata

Coreopsis galericulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with dark orange centers, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Coreopsis galericulata is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
