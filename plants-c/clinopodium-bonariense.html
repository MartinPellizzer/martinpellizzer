Clinopodium bonariense

Clinopodium bonariense, commonly known as Argentine mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, ovate leaves with a fragrant, mint-like scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clinopodium bonariense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
