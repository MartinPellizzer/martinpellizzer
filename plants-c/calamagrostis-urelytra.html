Calamagrostis urelytra

Calamagrostis urelytra, commonly known as the tall bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of blue-gray or silver-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Calamagrostis urelytra plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
