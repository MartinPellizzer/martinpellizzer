Campanula schimperi var. quartiniana

Campanula schimperi var. quartiniana, commonly known as the Ethiopian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
