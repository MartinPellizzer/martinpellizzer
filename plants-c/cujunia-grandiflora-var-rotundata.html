Cujunia grandiflora var. rotundata

Cujunia grandiflora var. rotundata, commonly known as the rounded-flowered cujunia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and often pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often hairy, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its vibrant floral display and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
