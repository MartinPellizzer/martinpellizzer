Cynosurus elegans subsp. aurasiacus

Cynosurus elegans subsp.

aurasiacus, commonly known as the Aurasiac bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing mats.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its delicate appearance.
