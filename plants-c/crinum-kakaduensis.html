Crinum kakaduensis

Crinum kakaduensis, commonly known as the Kakadu lily, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in wetland or swampy environments and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Crinum kakaduensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
