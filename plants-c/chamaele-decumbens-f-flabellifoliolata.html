Chamaele decumbens f. flabellifoliolata

Chamaele decumbens f. flabellifoliolata, commonly known as the fan-leafed prostrate chamaele, is a unique variant of the prostrate chamaele plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that give it a striking visual appearance. The plant exhibits a low-growing, sprawling habit, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded or moist environments. Its foliage is often a deep green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture and its ability to thrive in humid, partially shaded conditions.
