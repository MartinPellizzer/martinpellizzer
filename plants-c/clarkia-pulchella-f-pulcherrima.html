Clarkia pulchella f. pulcherrima

Clarkia pulchella f. pulcherrima, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red petals and is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae). The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
