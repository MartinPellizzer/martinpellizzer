Canna tenuiflora

Canna tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna tenuiflora is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
