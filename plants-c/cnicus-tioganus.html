Cnicus tioganus

Cnicus tioganus, commonly known as the Tioga thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a spiny appearance. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive blue-green color.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing purple to pinkish blooms that attract pollinators.
