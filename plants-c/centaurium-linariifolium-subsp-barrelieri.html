Centaurium linariifolium subsp. barrelieri

Centaurium linariifolium subsp.

barrelieri, commonly known as Barrelier's lesser centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bright green leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are typically pale blue to violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower lip. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and limestone soils.

This subspecies is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
