Crataegus deducta

Crataegus deducta, commonly known as the Florida hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The species is characterized by its rugged, often twisted growth form and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
