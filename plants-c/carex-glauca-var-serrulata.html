Carex glauca var. serrulata

Carex glauca var. serrulata, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
