Cussonia transvaalensis

Cussonia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal arrowroot, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
