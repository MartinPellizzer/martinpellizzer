Cornidia radiata

Cornidia radiata, commonly known as the starflower or moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cornidia radiata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
