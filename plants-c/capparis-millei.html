Capparis millei

Capparis millei, commonly known as the red caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its leaves are succulent and often have a slightly prickly texture.

The red caper is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
