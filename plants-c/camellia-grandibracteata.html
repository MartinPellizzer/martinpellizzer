Camellia grandibracteata

Camellia grandibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow center. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Camellia grandibracteata is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
