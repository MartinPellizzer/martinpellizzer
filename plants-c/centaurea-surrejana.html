Centaurea surrejana

Centaurea surrejana, commonly known as the Southern Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its striking blue to purple flowers are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets, making it visually distinctive.

Centaurea surrejana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
