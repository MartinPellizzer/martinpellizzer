Clusia dichrophylla

Clusia dichrophylla, commonly known as the wax plant or silverleaf waxplant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its foliage is evergreen, and it is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Clusia dichrophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
