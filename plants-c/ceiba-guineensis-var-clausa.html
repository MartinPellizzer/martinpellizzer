Ceiba guineensis var. clausa

Ceiba guineensis var. clausa, commonly known as the African ceiba, is a tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed trunk and broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
