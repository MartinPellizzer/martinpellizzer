Crataegus monogyna subsp. intermedia

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the middle European hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
