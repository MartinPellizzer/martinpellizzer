Crolocos colorata

Crolocos colorata, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a strikingly colorful flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, multi-hued petals that range from deep purples to bright yellows. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant worldwide.
