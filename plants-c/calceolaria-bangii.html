Calceolaria bangii

Calceolaria bangii, commonly known as the Bangi's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria bangii is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
