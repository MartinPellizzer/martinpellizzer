Cyathea oblonga

Cyathea oblonga, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in persistent, overlapping leaf bases. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are large, pinnately compound, and often have a distinctive, wavy appearance.

Cyathea oblonga plays an important ecological role in humid forests, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
