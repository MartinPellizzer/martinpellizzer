Citrus × dulcimedulla

Citrus × dulcimedulla, commonly known as the hybrid mandarin orange, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between mandarin and pomelo.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth rind and a high juice content. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
