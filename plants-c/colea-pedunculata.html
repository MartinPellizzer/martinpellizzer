Colea pedunculata

Colea pedunculata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

Colea pedunculata is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
