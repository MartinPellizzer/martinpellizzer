Coris monspeliensis subsp. hispanica

Coris monspeliensis subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rockrose, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its subspecific distinction highlights its adaptation to the specific ecological conditions of the Iberian Peninsula.
