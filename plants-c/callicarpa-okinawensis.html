Callicarpa okinawensis

Callicarpa okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, orange-red berries that appear in the fall, which are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are ovate and have a glossy surface, often turning red in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and seasonal color changes.
