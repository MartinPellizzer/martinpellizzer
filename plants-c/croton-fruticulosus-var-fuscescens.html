Croton fruticulosus var. fuscescens

Croton fruticulosus var. fuscescens, commonly known as the black croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
