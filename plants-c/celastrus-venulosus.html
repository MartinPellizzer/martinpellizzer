Celastrus venulosus

Celastrus venulosus, commonly known as the redroot creeper, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Celastraceae family and typically grows as a trailing vine or shrub. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine, though its toxicity requires caution.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
