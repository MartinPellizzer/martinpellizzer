Clerodendrum discolor var. pluriflorum

Clerodendrum discolor var. pluriflorum, commonly known as the clustered flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that produce vibrant red and orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
