Cordia fasciata

Cordia fasciata, commonly known as the spotted cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, oval-shaped leaves with a striking white to pale yellow margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cordia fasciata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit.
