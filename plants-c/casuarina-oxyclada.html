Casuarina oxyclada

Casuarina oxyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved she-oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and distinctive cylindrical seed cones. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in spiral patterns along the branches. Casuarina oxyclada is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in dunes and heathlands.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various native wildlife species.
