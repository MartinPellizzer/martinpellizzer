Croton tuberculatus

Croton tuberculatus, commonly known as the rough croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Croton tuberculatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive foliage.
