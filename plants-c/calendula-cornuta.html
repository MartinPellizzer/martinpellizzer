Calendula cornuta

Calendula cornuta, commonly known as the horned calendula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Calendula cornuta is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
