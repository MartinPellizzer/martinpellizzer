Conocalpis umbellata

Conocalpis umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence, which consists of multiple small white flowers clustered together. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
