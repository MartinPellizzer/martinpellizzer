Crataegus oxyacantha var. sutheriana

Crataegus oxyacantha var. sutheriana, commonly known as the Sutherland hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in hedgerows for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
