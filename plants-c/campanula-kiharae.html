Campanula kiharae

Campanula kiharae, commonly known as the Kihara bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, clustered inflorescences.

Campanula kiharae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
