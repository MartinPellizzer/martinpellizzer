Convolvulus cymosus

Convolvulus cymosus, commonly known as the clustered bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant has trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats such as fields and rocky areas.
