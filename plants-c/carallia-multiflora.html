Carallia multiflora

Carallia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered carallia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its blooms appear in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carallia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
