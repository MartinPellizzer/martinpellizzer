Cassia cochabambae

Cassia cochabambae, commonly known as the Cochabamba cassia, is a shrub native to the Andean region of Bolivia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia cochabambae is adapted to high-altitude environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
