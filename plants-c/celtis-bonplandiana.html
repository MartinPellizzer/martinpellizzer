Celtis bonplandiana

Celtis bonplandiana, commonly known as the white ash or the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by small, round fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

Celtis bonplandiana is often found in dry to moist forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
