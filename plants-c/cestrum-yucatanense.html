Cestrum yucatanense

Cestrum yucatanense, commonly known as the Yucatan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. Its fruits are small, berry-like, and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Cestrum yucatanense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
