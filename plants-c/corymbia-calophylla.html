Corymbia calophylla

Corymbia calophylla, commonly known as the Moreton Bay chestnut, is a species of flowering tree native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, spreading crown with lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces distinctive red or pink flowers that appear in clusters, followed by woody, edible fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal rainforests.

The Moreton Bay chestnut is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
