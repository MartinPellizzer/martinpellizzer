Calyptrion pubescens

Calyptrion pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Calyptrion pubescens is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils, making it an important species in riparian ecosystems.
