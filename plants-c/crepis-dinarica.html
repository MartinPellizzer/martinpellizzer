Crepis dinarica

Crepis dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and are arranged in a single head.

Crepis dinarica is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
