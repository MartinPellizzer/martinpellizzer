Carex monostachya

Carex monostachya, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces a single, slender spike of small, three-lobed flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological restoration projects and a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
