Carex truncatigluma subsp. rhynchachaenium

Carex truncatigluma subsp.

rhynchachaenium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a species of sedges characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has a fibrous root system and produces stolons that help it spread vegetatively. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curved, with a rough texture.

The flowering structure consists of two distinct spikelets, one terminal and one lateral, which are often subtended by bracts.
