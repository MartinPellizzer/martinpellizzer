Coumarouna polyphylla

Coumarouna polyphylla, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist forested areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
