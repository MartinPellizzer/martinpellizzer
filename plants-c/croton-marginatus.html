Croton marginatus

Croton marginatus, commonly known as the white croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Croton marginatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and vibrant fruit.
