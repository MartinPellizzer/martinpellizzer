Cyperus lanuginosulus

Cyperus lanuginosulus, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 60 cm, with slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne in clusters at the base of the stem. Its leaves are linear, narrow, and often have a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Cyperus lanuginosulus is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
