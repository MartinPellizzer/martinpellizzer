Chamaedendron xyridioides

Chamaedendron xyridioides, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange bract-like flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring full sun and well-drained soil.

Chamaedendron xyridioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
