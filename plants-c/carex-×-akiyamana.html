Carex × akiyamana

Carex × akiyamana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, native to Japan.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant produces dense clusters of grass-like leaves and spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland or boggy conditions.

Carex × akiyamana is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its tolerance for wet soils and ability to stabilize shoreline areas.
