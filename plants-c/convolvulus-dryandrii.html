Convolvulus dryandrii

Convolvulus dryandrii, commonly known as the dryandra bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing structures.

Convolvulus dryandrii is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.
