Carduus platyphyllus

Carduus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branching stem that produces purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
