Cirsium armenum

Cirsium armenum, commonly known as Armenian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are usually lobed or pinnately divided, and the upper leaves are smaller and often spine-tipped. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.
