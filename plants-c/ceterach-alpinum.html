Ceterach alpinum

Ceterach alpinum, commonly known as mountain sedge, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds.

Ceterach alpinum plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas and is often found in alpine meadows.
