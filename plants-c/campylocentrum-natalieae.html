Campylocentrum natalieae

Campylocentrum natalieae, commonly known as the Natalie's poinsettia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety bracts that resemble the leaves of the poinsettia plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
