Convolvulus argyracanthus

Convolvulus argyracanthus, commonly known as silver-armed bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant has a trailing habit and can spread rapidly, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive. Its flowers bloom in summer, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant's silver-green stems and foliage give it a distinctive appearance in gardens.
