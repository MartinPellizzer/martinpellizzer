Crinum caspium

Crinum caspium, commonly known as the Caspian crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Crinum caspium is also noted for its ability to produce multiple stems and flowers from a single plant, enhancing its ornamental value.
