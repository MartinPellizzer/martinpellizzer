Coleus spicatus var. rondinella

Coleus spicatus var. rondinella, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, contrasting foliage with deep green leaves featuring prominent red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and decorative purposes.
