Centaurea seridis var. auriculata

Centaurea seridis var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers with red-brown markings. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base. This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
