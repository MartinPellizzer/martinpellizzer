Cirsium zapalowiczii

Cirsium zapalowiczii, commonly known as the Zapalowicz thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
