Coresantha

Coresantha, commonly known as the red swamp crake, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible. Coresantha thrives in marshy areas and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
