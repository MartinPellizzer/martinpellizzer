Cypripedium venustum

Cypripedium venustum, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of white to pale pink flowers that resemble a truss of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the stem.

Cypripedium venustum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
