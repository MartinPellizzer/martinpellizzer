Comolia sessilis

Comolia sessilis, commonly known as the sessile comolia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
