Croton pannosus

Croton pannosus, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically bright yellow or green with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
