Carex turbinata

Carex turbinata, commonly known as the turban sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex turbinata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
