Cytisus candicans var. colmeiroi

Cytisus candicans var. colmeiroi, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
