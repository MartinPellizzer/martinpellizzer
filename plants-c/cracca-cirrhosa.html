Cracca cirrhosa

Cracca cirrhosa, commonly known as the slender cracca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Cracca cirrhosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
