Carphochaete pringlei var. simulans

Carphochaete pringlei var. simulans, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in the summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated than other feverfew varieties.
