Carex fraseri

Carex fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by cylindrical seed heads.

Carex fraseri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
