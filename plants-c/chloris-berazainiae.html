Chloris berazainiae

Chloris berazainiae, commonly known as the Berazaini's grass, is a species of flowering plant native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers, typically in shades of green or brown. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
