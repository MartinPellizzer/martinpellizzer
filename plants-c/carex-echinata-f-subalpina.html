Carex echinata f. subalpina

Carex echinata f. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers. This subspecies is adapted to grow in moist, rocky or sandy soils in alpine and subalpine environments. It typically thrives in open habitats such as meadows and stream banks at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
