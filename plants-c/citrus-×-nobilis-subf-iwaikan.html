Citrus × nobilis subf. iwaikan

Citrus × nobilis subf.

iwaikan, commonly known as the Iwaikan mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It is well adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.
