Cirsium tanegashimense

Cirsium tanegashimense, commonly known as the Tanegashima thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan, specifically the island of Tanegashima.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment. Its botanical features include a tall, erect stem with dense, bristly foliage and showy composite flower heads that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
