Cymbopogon ariani

Cymbopogon ariani, commonly known as the aromatic grass or ariani grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is known for its fragrant essential oils, which are often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Cymbopogon ariani is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
