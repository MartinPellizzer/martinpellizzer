Cattleya mossiae var. lawrenceana

Cattleya mossiae var. lawrenceana, commonly known as Lawrence's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking, fragrant flowers.

It originates from the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows as an epiphyte on trees. The plant features long, arching stems with clustered blooms that are often a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
