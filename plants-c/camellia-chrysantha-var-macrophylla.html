Camellia chrysantha var. macrophylla

Camellia chrysantha var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf camellia, is a species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is evergreen, with a compact to medium-sized shrub growth habit. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The flowers are typically borne in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the landscape.
