Cassia occidentalis var. entreriana

Cassia occidentalis var. entreriana, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
