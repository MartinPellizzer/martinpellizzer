Calandrinia splendens

Calandrinia splendens, commonly known as the splendour portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, bright-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, yellow, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Calandrinia splendens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
