Campanula balcanica subf. umbrosa

Campanula balcanica subf.

umbrosa, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and hairy, adapted to thrive in shaded, moist environments. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
