Convolvulus capituliferus var. foliaceus

Convolvulus capituliferus var. foliaceus, commonly known as the leafy bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
