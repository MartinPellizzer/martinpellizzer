Cinnamomum lampongum

Cinnamomum lampongum, commonly known as Java cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, cinnamon-like bark that is highly valued for its scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique fragrance and potential health benefits, it is often cultivated for both commercial and traditional uses.
