Carex japonica var. trichostyles

Carex japonica var. trichostyles, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that mature into dark brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
