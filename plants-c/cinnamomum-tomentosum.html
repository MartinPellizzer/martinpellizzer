Cinnamomum tomentosum

Cinnamomum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
