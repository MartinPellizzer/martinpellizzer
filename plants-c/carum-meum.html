Carum meum

Carum meum, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its aromatic seeds are often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle anise-like flavor to dishes.

Carum meum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
