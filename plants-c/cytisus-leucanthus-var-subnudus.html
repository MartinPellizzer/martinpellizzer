Cytisus leucanthus var. subnudus

Cytisus leucanthus var. subnudus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, yellowish hairs. The leaves are small, narrow, and usually lack stipules, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are bright yellow and form in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.
