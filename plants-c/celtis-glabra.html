Celtis glabra

Celtis glabra, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, spiny fruits that mature in the fall. Celtis glabra is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
