Colea longepetiolata

Colea longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled colea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features elongated petioles, which give it its common name, and its foliage consists of lance-shaped, glossy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Colea longepetiolata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
