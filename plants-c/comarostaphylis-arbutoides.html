Comarostaphylis arbutoides

Comarostaphylis arbutoides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows on rocky or coastal soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and have a fragrant aroma when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
