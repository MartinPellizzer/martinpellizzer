Cynanchum albidum

Cynanchum albidum, commonly known as white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features a slender, branched stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose racemes, while its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
