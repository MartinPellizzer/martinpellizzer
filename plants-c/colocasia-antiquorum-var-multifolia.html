Colocasia antiquorum var. multifolia

Colocasia antiquorum var. multifolia, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Its foliage is often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding visual interest to gardens.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
