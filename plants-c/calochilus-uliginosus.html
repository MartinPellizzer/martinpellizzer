Calochilus uliginosus

Calochilus uliginosus, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in wet, boggy environments, often in heathlands or swampy areas. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. Its roots are adapted to waterlogged soils, and it is often found in regions with high humidity.

Calochilus uliginosus is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
