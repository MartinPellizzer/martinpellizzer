Cyperus rigens var. capitatus

Cyperus rigens var. capitatus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically purple or reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
