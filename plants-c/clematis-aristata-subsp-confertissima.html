Clematis aristata subsp. confertissima

Clematis aristata subsp.

confertissima, commonly known as the dense-flowered clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for climbing structures and trellises in temperate climates.
