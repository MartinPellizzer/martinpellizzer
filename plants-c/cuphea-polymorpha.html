Cuphea polymorpha

Cuphea polymorpha, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-tolerant plants.

Cuphea polymorpha is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
