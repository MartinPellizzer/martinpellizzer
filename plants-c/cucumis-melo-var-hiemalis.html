Cucumis melo var. hiemalis

Cucumis melo var. hiemalis, commonly known as the winter melon, is a variant of the melon genus characterized by its large, elongated fruit.

The plant is an annual vine that produces a smooth, pale green to white fruit with a thick, edible rind and a sweet, mild interior. It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season to mature. The leaves are broad and lobed, while the flowers are yellow and typically unisexual.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
