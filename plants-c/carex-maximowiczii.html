Carex maximowiczii

Carex maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex maximowiczii prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
