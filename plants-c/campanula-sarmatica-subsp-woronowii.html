Campanula sarmatica subsp. woronowii

Campanula sarmatica subsp.

woronowii, commonly known as the Sarmatian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
