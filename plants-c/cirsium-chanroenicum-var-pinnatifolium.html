Cirsium chanroenicum var. pinnatifolium

Cirsium chanroenicum var. pinnatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive pinnate arrangement.

The species is often mistaken for other thistle varieties due to its similar appearance and ecological habitat.
