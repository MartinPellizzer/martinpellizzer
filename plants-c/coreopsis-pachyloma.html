Coreopsis pachyloma

Coreopsis pachyloma, commonly known as the Mexican coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
