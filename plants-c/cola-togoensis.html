Cola togoensis

Cola togoensis, commonly known as African cola or Togo cola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to western Africa, particularly found in countries such as Ghana, Togo, and Nigeria. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red berries containing seeds. The fruit is often harvested for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
