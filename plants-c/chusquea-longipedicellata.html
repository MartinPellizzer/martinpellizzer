Chusquea longipedicellata

Chusquea longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-bracted weeping bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that form dense, cascading clusters, giving it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with long, bract-like structures, which are a defining feature of the species. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Chusquea longipedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique visual characteristics.
