Carex grayi var. hispidula

Carex grayi var. hispidula, commonly known as gray's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats.

The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
