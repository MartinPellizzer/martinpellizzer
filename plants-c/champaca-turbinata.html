Champaca turbinata

Champaca turbinata, commonly known as the yellow champaca, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, spicy scent. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, oval fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume and incense.
