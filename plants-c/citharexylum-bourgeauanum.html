Citharexylum bourgeauanum

Citharexylum bourgeauanum, commonly known as the Bougainville beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of vibrant, papery bracts that enclose small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
