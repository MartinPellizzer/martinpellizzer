Claytonia parviflora

Claytonia parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf springbeauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
