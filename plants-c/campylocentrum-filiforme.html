Campylocentrum filiforme

Campylocentrum filiforme, commonly known as the slender flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that resemble small flames, typically in shades of red and orange. The plant has slender, wiry stems that allow it to climb and trail along structures. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
