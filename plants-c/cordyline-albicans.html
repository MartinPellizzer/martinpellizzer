Cordyline albicans

Cordyline albicans, commonly known as the white cordyline, is a striking plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.

Cordyline albicans is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
