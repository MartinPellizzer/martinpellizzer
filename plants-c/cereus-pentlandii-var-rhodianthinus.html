Cereus pentlandii var. rhodianthinus

Cereus pentlandii var. rhodianthinus, commonly known as the Rhodianthinus Cereus, is a striking cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often with a branched appearance. The plant produces large, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom at night, a feature typical of many desert plants. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid gardens and greenhouses.
