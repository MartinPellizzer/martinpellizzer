Caperonia spinosa

Caperonia spinosa, commonly known as the prickly caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Caperonia spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
