Cymbidium goeringii var. tortisepalum

Cymbidium goeringii var. tortisepalum, commonly known as the twisted-sepal orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a subtle fragrance.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and botanical interest.
