Caragana polourensis

Caragana polourensis, commonly known as the Mongolian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought and poor soil conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion.

Caragana polourensis is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
