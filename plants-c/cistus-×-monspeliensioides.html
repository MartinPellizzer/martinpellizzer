Cistus × monspeliensioides

Cistus × monspeliensioides, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus × monspeliensioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
