Cheirostylis thailandica

Cheirostylis thailandica, commonly known as the Thai orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
