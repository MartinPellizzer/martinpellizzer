Caruelia

Caruelia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a central disk of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, windy environments. Caruelia has hairy leaves that help reduce water loss and protect against harsh weather.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
