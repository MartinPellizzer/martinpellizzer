Cineraria uncinata

Cineraria uncinata, commonly known as the hooked cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Cineraria uncinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
