Cotoneaster brickellii

Cotoneaster brickellii, commonly known as Brickell's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Cotoneaster brickellii is also valued for its ability to attract birds and support local wildlife.
