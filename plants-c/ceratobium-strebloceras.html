Ceratobium strebloceras

Ceratobium strebloceras, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a species of grass found in various parts of the world.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.
