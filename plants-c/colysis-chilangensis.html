Colysis chilangensis

Colysis chilangensis, commonly known as the Chilango lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a robust, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in cooler climates.
