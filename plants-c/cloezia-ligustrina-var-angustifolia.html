Cloezia ligustrina var. angustifolia

Cloezia ligustrina var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cloezia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. It has a woody stem with a smooth, greyish bark and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
