Cuscuta palmeri

Cuscuta palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure covered in tiny, thread-like branches. The plant typically attaches itself to various host plants, including grasses and other flowering plants, using specialized suction cups. Its life cycle involves germination, attachment to a host, and the production of seeds through its flowers.

Cuscuta palmeri is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in dry, open environments.
