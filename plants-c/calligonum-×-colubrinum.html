Calligonum × colubrinum

Calligonum × colubrinum, commonly known as the hybrid saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is highly tolerant of saline soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing coastal and desert soils.
