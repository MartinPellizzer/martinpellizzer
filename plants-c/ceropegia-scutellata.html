Ceropegia scutellata

Ceropegia scutellata, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white. The leaves are glossy and often have a silvery or bluish sheen, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from hanging baskets or shelves.
