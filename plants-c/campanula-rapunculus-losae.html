Campanula rapunculus losae

Campanula rapunculus losae, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine down, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Campanula rapunculus losae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
