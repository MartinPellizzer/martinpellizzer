Clausena platyphylla

Clausena platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf klipper or flatleaf kewra, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are yellowish-green, followed by orange to red berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cultures.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
