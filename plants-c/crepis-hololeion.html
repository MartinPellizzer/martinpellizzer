Crepis hololeion

Crepis hololeion, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Crepis hololeion is often found in disturbed soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
