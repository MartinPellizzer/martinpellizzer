Carex malinvaldii

Carex malinvaldii, commonly known as the Malinvald sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers.

Carex malinvaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet areas.
