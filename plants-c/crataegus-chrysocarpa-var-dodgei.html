Crataegus chrysocarpa var. dodgei

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. dodgei, commonly known as the golden-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange fruits that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pink flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal display.
