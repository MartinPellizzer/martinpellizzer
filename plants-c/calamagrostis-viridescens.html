Calamagrostis viridescens

Calamagrostis viridescens, commonly known as green stemmed reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are typically green when young and turn reddish-brown with age. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Calamagrostis viridescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
