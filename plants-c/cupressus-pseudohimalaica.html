Cupressus pseudohimalaica

Cupressus pseudohimalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine environments.

Cupressus pseudohimalaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic features.
