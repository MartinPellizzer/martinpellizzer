Clematis hexapetala f. dissecta

Clematis hexapetala f. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected hexapetala clematis, is a variant of the hexapetala clematis, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like foliage.

This plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive, recurved form. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be grown as a climbing or shrub-like plant.

It prefers partial to full sun and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
