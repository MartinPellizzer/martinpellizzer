Campanula cochleariifolia subf. compacta

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

compacta, commonly known as the compact bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically on slender stems.

The plant has dark green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens seeking low-maintenance, attractive flora.
