Commelina communis f. leucantha

Commelina communis f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered spiderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and three sepals that resemble a spider's legs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, inconspicuous fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats.
