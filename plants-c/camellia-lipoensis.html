Camellia lipoensis

Camellia lipoensis, commonly known as the Lipoensis camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan and Guizhou provinces. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. Camellia lipoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
