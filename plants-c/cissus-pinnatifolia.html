Cissus pinnatifolia

Cissus pinnatifolia, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that develop into green, fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a versatile plant for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
