Crotalaria Burmanni

Crotalaria burmanni, scientifically known as Crotalaria burmanni, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Crotalaria burmanni is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a subject of interest for botanists and environmental scientists alike.
