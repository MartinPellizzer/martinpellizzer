Crocus lageniflorus var. aureus

Crocus lageniflorus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, bell-shaped corolla that resembles a small cup. The plant produces showy, saucer-like blooms in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that appear after flowering.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and early bloom.
