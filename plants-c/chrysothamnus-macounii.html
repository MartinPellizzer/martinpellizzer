Chrysothamnus macounii

Chrysothamnus macounii, commonly known as Macoun's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in composite inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
