Cheilanthes mysurensis

Cheilanthes mysurensis, commonly known as the Mysore rock fern, is a terrestrial fern native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and is often found growing on rocky outcrops and cliffs. The plant has dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance.

Cheilanthes mysurensis is a valuable species for ecological studies due to its unique morphology and habitat specificity.
