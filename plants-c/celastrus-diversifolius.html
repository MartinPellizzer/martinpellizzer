Celastrus diversifolius

Celastrus diversifolius, commonly known as the variable leaf staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that vary in shape and size, contributing to its common name. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its stems are twining and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Celastrus diversifolius is typically found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
