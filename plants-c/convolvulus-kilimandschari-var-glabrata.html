Convolvulus kilimandschari var. glabrata

Convolvulus kilimandschari var. glabrata, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky outcrops and along mountain trails, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
