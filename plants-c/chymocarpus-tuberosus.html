Chymocarpus tuberosus

Chymocarpus tuberosus, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial wetland plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a bulbous root system that stores nutrients and helps it survive in waterlogged soils. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
