Convolvulus atriplicifolius

Convolvulus atriplicifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive, narrow leaflet arrangement. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
