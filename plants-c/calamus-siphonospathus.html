Calamus siphonospathus

Calamus siphonospathus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often glossy in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus siphonospathus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
