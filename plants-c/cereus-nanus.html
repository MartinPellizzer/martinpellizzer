Cereus nanus

Cereus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf holiday cactus, is a small, compact cactus native to South America.

It features a short, branching stem covered in white, woolly spines. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing during the winter months. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect sunlight.

It is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
