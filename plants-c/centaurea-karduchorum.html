Centaurea karduchorum

Centaurea karduchorum, commonly known as the Karduchian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea karduchorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
