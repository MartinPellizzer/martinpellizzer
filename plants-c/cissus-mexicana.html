Cissus mexicana

Cissus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ease of care.

Cissus mexicana is also valued for its ability to purify air and its ornamental appeal.
