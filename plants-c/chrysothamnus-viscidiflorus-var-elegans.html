Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. elegans

Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically covered in a sticky resin, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
