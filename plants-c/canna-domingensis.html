Canna domingensis

Canna domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican canna, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a striking green or reddish hue. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically in clusters at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna domingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and blooms.
