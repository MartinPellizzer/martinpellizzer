Clematis missouriensis

Clematis missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the central United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural habitats. It is often found along stream banks and in moist woodlands, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its rapid growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular plant for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
