Cucumis melo var. nucinus

Cucumis melo var. nucinus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that typically measure between 15 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a vine-like growth habit, typical of many cucurbit species. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a matte green surface.

The fruit is generally harvested when still immature, offering a crisp, mildly sweet flavor.
