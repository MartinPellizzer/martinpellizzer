Cerasus vulgaris f. persiciflora

Cerasus vulgaris f. persiciflora, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus species known for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its clusters of delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and turn a vibrant red in the fall.

This cultivar is widely cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal display.
