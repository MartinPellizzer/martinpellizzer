Croton multicaulis

Croton multicaulis, commonly known as the stem-croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton multicaulis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
