Crataegus monogyna f. liocarpa

Crataegus monogyna f. liocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contrast with its typical white or pink flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 8 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and greyish in texture.

This cultivar is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens for its seasonal interest.
