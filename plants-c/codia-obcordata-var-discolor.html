Codia obcordata var. discolor

Codia obcordata var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated codia, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that display a unique variegation of green, white, and sometimes pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.
