Ceanothus prostratus

Ceanothus prostratus, commonly known as the creeping ceanothus, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Ceanothus prostratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
