Calligonum × barsukiense f. pseudotetrapterum

Calligonum × barsukiense f. pseudotetrapterum, commonly known as the pseudotetrapterum form of the hybrid calligonum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green foliage, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its stems are typically woody and branched, with a distinctive appearance due to the arrangement of its branches.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and resilience, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry landscapes.
