Coscinium maingayi

Coscinium maingayi, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds. It is often found in moist forested areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
