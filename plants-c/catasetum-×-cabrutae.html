Catasetum × cabrutae

Catasetum × cabrutae, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with a specialized column for pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its native habitat. Its flowers are highly attractive to specific pollinators, such as bees and wasps, due to their intricate morphology.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
