Cissus rotundifolia

Cissus rotundifolia, commonly known as the snow vine, is a climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive waxy, white berries that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often used in ornamental gardening. It has a vigorous growth habit and can climb using its tendrils.

Cissus rotundifolia is also valued for its ability to purify air and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
