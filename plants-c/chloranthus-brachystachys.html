Chloranthus brachystachys

Chloranthus brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked angel's trumpets, is a member of the Chloranthaceae family.

It is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, resembling the shape of a small trumpet.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
