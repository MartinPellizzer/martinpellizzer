Carduus rufescens

Carduus rufescens, commonly known as the redstem thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are often lobed or deeply divided, with a generally prickly texture. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Carduus rufescens is adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
