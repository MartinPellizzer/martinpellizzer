Calamintha origanifolia

Calamintha origanifolia, commonly known as lemon balm mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are fragrant, with a citrusy scent reminiscent of lemon, and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Calamintha origanifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
