Cardamine calbucana

Cardamine calbucana, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, mountainous regions. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
