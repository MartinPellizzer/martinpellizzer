Celastrus confertus

Celastrus confertus, commonly known as the Chinese staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in horticulture and natural ecosystems.
