Cynanchum intricatum

Cynanchum intricatum, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing toxic seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
