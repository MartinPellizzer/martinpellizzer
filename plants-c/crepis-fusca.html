Crepis fusca

Crepis fusca, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Crepis fusca is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
