Canarium samoense

Canarium samoense, commonly known as the Samoan nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the islands of Samoa and nearby regions in the Pacific.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, aromatic shell and are used for various traditional and commercial purposes.
