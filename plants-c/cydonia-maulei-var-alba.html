Cydonia maulei var. alba

Cydonia maulei var. alba, commonly known as the white quince, is a variant of the quince species native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces yellow, fragrant flowers in the spring. The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is typically white or pale yellow and has a tough, inedible skin. The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
