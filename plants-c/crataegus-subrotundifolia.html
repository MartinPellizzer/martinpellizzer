Crataegus subrotundifolia

Crataegus subrotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ovate to round leaves that are often lobed and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade.

Crataegus subrotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for birds and wildlife.
