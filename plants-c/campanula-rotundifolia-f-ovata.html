Campanula rotundifolia f. ovata

Campanula rotundifolia f. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, ovate leaves and bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
