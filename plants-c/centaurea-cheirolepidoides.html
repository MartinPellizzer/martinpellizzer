Centaurea cheirolepidoides

Centaurea cheirolepidoides, commonly known as the rock centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing on rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea cheirolepidoides is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.
