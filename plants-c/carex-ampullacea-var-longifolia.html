Carex ampullacea var. longifolia

Carex ampullacea var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along streambanks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are long and narrow, with a distinctive blue-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
