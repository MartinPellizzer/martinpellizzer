Canna cearensis

Canna cearensis, commonly known as the Brazilian canna, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found near water bodies.

Canna cearensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and wetland landscapes.
