Copaifera glycycarpa

Copaifera glycycarpa, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. The plant belongs to the family Leguminosae and is known for its large, compound leaves and yellowish flowers. Its bark and resin are harvested for use in perfumes, pharmaceuticals, and traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, lowland tropical forests and plays a role in local ecosystems.
