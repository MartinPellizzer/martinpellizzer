Carex plocamostyla

Carex plocamostyla, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex plocamostyla plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
