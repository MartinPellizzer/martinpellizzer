Conyza chilensis var. carnea

Conyza chilensis var. carnea, commonly known as the pink weed or pink conyza, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its wind-dispersed seeds.
