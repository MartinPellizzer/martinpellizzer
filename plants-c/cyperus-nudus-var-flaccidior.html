Cyperus nudus var. flaccidior

Cyperus nudus var. flaccidior, commonly known as the soft sedgeland sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Its botanical structure is adapted for water retention, making it a common species in wetland ecosystems.
