Ceanothus cuneatus var. ramulosus

Ceanothus cuneatus var. ramulosus, commonly known as the California barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in its native range.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
