Cardamine antiscorbutica

Cardamine antiscorbutica, commonly known as the scurvy grass, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant features trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and white, typically clustered in a raceme, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

This plant is known for its high vitamin C content, historically used to prevent scurvy.
