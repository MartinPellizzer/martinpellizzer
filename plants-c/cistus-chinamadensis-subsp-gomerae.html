Cistus chinamadensis subsp. gomerae

Cistus chinamadensis subsp.

gomerae, commonly known as the Gomera rockrose, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the unique flora of the area.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
