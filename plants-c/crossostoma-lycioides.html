Crossostoma lycioides

Crossostoma lycioides, commonly known as the Texas crossostoma, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like, containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
