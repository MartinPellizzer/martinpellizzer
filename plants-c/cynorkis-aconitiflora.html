Cynorkis aconitiflora

Cynorkis aconitiflora, commonly known as the blue bell orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
