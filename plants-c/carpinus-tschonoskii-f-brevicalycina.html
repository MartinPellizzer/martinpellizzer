Carpinus tschonoskii f. brevicalycina

Carpinus tschonoskii f. brevicalycina, commonly known as the short calyx hornbeam, is a rare horticultural form of the hornbeam tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded leaves compared to the typical species. The plant exhibits distinctive floral characteristics, with reduced bracts that give it its specific epithet. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

This form is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with partial shade.
