Crataegus tennowana

Crataegus tennowana, commonly known as the Tennessee hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as pomes, which are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
