Cieca doratophylla

Cieca doratophylla, commonly known as the golden fan plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fan-like arrangement of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically a striking golden-green color. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
