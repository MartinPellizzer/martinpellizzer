Carex pallida f. angustifolia

Carex pallida f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its pale green to yellowish foliage and small, clustered flowers appear in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
