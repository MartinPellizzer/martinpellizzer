Camutia perfoliata

Camutia perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy perfoliate, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Camutia perfoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to gardens in various parts of the world.
