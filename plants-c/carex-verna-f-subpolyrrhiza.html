Carex verna f. subpolyrrhiza

Carex verna f. subpolyrrhiza, commonly known as the spring sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

The subspecies subpolyrrhiza is distinguished by its slightly longer and more slender leaves compared to the typical form of Carex verna.
