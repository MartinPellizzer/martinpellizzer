Chionanthus henryae

Chionanthus henryae, commonly known as Henry's fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique floral structure and delicate appearance make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
