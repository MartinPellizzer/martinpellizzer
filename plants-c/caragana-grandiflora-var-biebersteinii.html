Caragana grandiflora var. biebersteinii

Caragana grandiflora var. biebersteinii, commonly known as the Siberian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces legume pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for reclamation projects.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
