Croton martinianus

Croton martinianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and hue depending on the variety. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
