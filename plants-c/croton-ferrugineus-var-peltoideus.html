Croton ferrugineus var. peltoideus

Croton ferrugineus var. peltoideus, commonly known as the rusty croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, yellow, and red. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
