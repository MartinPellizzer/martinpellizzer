Chamaecrista catharticoides

Chamaecrista catharticoides, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woods or along roadsides.

Botanically, it is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and ecological importance.
