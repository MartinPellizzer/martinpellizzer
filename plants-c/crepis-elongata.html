Crepis elongata

Crepis elongata, commonly known as the slender hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are elongated with a hairy texture. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, achene fruits.

Crepis elongata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
