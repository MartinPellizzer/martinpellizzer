Carica omnilingua

Carica omnilingua, commonly known as the multilingual papaya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flesh. The plant produces a single, large fruit that ripens quickly, often within a few weeks of flowering. Its botanical features include a fibrous, aromatic stem and a distinctive, rough, hairy surface on its leaves.

The fruit is rich in enzymes like papain, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
