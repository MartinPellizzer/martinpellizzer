Carex dabieensis

Carex dabieensis, commonly known as Dabie sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Carex dabieensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
