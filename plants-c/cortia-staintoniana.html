Cortia staintoniana

Cortia staintoniana, commonly known as the Stainton's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Cortia staintoniana is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
