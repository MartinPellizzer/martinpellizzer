Cryptoceras verticillare

Cryptoceras verticillare, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
