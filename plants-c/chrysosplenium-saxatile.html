Chrysosplenium saxatile

Chrysosplenium saxatile, commonly known as the rock spleenwort, is a small evergreen perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky, shaded environments and is often found growing on limestone or granite substrates. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

Chrysosplenium saxatile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky habitats.
