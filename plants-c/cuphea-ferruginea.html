Cuphea ferruginea

Cuphea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea ferruginea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
