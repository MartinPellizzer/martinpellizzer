Carthamus tamamschjanae

Carthamus tamamschjanae, commonly known as the Afghan safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with serrated leaves and a robust stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
