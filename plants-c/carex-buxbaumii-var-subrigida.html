Carex buxbaumii var. subrigida

Carex buxbaumii var. subrigida, commonly known as the rigid sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering structures consist of male and female spikelets, which are often separated on the same plant.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
