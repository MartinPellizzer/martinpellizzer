Cienfuegosia ulmifolia

Cienfuegosia ulmifolia, commonly known as the Cuban elm, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, fleshy fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and along riverbanks in its native habitat.
