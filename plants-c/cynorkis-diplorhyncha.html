Cynorkis diplorhyncha

Cynorkis diplorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waterwheel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and filter water.

Cynorkis diplorhyncha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
