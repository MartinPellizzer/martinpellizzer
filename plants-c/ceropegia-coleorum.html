Ceropegia coleorum

Ceropegia coleorum, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting specific pollinators like flies. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia coleorum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
