Centaurea graeca

Centaurea graeca, commonly known as Greek centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, cone-like clusters.

Centaurea graeca is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
