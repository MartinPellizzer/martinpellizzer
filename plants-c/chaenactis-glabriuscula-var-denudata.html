Chaenactis glabriuscula var. denudata

Chaenactis glabriuscula var. denudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are usually reduced or absent, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.
