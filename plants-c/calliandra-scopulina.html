Calliandra scopulina

Calliandra scopulina, commonly known as the rock calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink or red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscapes.

Calliandra scopulina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility in ecological restoration projects.
