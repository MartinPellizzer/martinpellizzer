Chirita fulva

Chirita fulva, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Chirita fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
