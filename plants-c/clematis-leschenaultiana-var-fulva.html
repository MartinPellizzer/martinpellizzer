Clematis leschenaultiana var. fulva

Clematis leschenaultiana var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant in floral arrangements.
