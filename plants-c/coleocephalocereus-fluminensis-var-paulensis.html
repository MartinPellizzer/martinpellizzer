Coleocephalocereus fluminensis var. paulensis

Coleocephalocereus fluminensis var. paulensis, commonly known as the Paul's Christmas cactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its branching stems and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant typically grows in a shrub-like form, with thick, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

This plant is popular as a decorative ornamental due to its attractive appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
