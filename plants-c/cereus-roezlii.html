Cereus roezlii

Cereus roezlii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and can last for several days.

Cereus roezlii is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and unique ornamental features.
