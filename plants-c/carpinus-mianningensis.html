Carpinus mianningensis

Carpinus mianningensis, commonly known as the Mianning hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and smooth, grey bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, winged fruits. It is typically found in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
