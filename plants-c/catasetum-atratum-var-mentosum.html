Catasetum atratum var. mentosum

Catasetum atratum var. mentosum, commonly known as the Mentosum Catasetum, is a rare orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and striking, fragrant flowers with a unique structure that includes a specialized lip and a pollinia-bearing column. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, and it is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
