Coreopsis dichotoma

Coreopsis dichotoma, commonly known as the greenhead coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. The plant has a bushy growth habit with numerous branches that emerge from the base. Its leaves are typically linear to lance-shaped and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Coreopsis dichotoma is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.
