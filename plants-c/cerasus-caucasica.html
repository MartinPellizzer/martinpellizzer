Cerasus caucasica

Cerasus caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cerasus caucasica is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
