Cardamine hirsuta subsp. sylvatica

Cardamine hirsuta subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive lobed or divided structure, often with a waxy coating.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
