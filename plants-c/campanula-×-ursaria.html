Campanula × ursaria

Campanula × ursaria, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, this plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine down.

Campanula × ursaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
