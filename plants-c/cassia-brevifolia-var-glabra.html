Cassia brevifolia var. glabra

Cassia brevifolia var. glabra, commonly known as smooth cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically trifoliate or pinnately compound. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the legume family. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia brevifolia var. glabra thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
