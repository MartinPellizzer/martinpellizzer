Coleus argentifolius

Coleus argentifolius, commonly known as silverleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its vibrant, colorful flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, silvery hairs. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
