Chrysanthemum quercifolium

Chrysanthemum quercifolium, commonly known as the oakleaf chrysanthemum, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and vibrant foliage make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and horticultural collections.
