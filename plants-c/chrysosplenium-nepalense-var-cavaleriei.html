Chrysosplenium nepalense var. cavaleriei

Chrysosplenium nepalense var. cavaleriei, commonly known as the Nepal sedge, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forest understories.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
