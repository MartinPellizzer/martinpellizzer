Ceanothus cuneatus var. dubius

Ceanothus cuneatus var. dubius, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. It has adapted to drought conditions and is resistant to coastal salt spray.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
