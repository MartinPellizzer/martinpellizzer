Centaurea resupinata subsp. saguntina

Centaurea resupinata subsp.

saguntina, commonly known as the Saguntina Bladder Campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, sprawling stems that often lie close to the ground, covered in small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
