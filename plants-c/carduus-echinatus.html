Carduus echinatus

Carduus echinatus, commonly known as the thistleweed or spiny centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a spiny texture, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
