Cistus × clausonii nothosubsp. crespoi

Cistus × clausonii nothosubsp.

crespoi, commonly known as the Corsican rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, glandular leaves and produces showy, pink to reddish flowers in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental flowers and drought tolerance.

The subspecies is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various ecological conditions.
