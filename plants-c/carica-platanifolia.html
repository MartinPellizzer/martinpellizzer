Carica platanifolia

Carica platanifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian papaya, is a tropical plant belonging to the Caricaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in enzymes like papain. The plant is native to Central and South America but is now cultivated in various warm climates. Its botanical features include a rough, bark-like stem and distinctive, fragrant flowers.

The fruit is not only a food source but also has medicinal and industrial applications.
