Colubrina nelsonii

Colubrina nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in wetlands. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Colubrina nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
