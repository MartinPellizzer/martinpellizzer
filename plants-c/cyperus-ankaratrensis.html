Cyperus ankaratrensis

Cyperus ankaratrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus ankaratrensis is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
