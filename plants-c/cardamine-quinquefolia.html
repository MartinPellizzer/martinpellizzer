Cardamine quinquefolia

Cardamine quinquefolia, commonly known as five-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to North America and thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features a basal rosette of pinnately compound leaves, with each leaflet typically having five lobes. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are borne in terminal racemes and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is often used in wildflower gardens and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
