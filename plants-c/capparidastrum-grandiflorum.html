Capparidastrum grandiflorum

Capparidastrum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge. The fruits are capsule-like, containing several seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
