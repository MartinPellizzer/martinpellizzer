Crucifera stricta

Crucifera stricta, commonly known as the rigid crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
