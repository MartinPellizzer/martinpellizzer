Centaurea boissieri subsp. spachii

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

spachii, commonly known as the Spach's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the stems are erect and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
