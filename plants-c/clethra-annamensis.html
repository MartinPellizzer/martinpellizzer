Clethra annamensis

Clethra annamensis, commonly known as the Annam clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value.

Clethra annamensis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
