Cirsium lacinulatum

Cirsium lacinulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in a cluster at the top of the plant.

Cirsium lacinulatum is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun.
