Cavendishia striata

Cavendishia striata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with distinctive markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

Cavendishia striata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
