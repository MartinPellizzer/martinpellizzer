Cayaponia racemosa var. scaberrima

Cayaponia racemosa var. scaberrima, commonly known as the rough cayaponia, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, bean-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local use.
