Colchicum algeriense

Colchicum algeriense, commonly known as Algerian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves and has a corm-like structure for storage. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
