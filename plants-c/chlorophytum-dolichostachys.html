Chlorophytum dolichostachys

Chlorophytum dolichostachys, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white flowers, which are followed by blue or black berries. It is a hardy plant that thrives in low light conditions and requires minimal water, making it popular as a houseplant.

Its ability to purify indoor air has further contributed to its widespread cultivation.
