Cristaria multifida

Cristaria multifida, commonly known as the fringed leaf fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often divided into narrow, thread-like segments. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters where it can anchor itself to substrates. Its structure allows it to thrive in humid environments, providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.

Cristaria multifida is valued in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to improve water quality.
