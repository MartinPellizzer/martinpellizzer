Crotalaria thomasii

Crotalaria thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
