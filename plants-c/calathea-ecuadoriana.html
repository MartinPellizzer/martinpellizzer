Calathea ecuadoriana

Calathea ecuadoriana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian calathea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically broad and oval, often displaying shades of green, pink, and purple in a mesmerizing arrangement. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The plant's ornamental value is largely due to its unique leaf patterns and vibrant coloration.
