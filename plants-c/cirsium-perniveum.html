Cirsium perniveum

Cirsium perniveum, commonly known as the silver centaury, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the thistle family (Compositae) and is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, spiky flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
