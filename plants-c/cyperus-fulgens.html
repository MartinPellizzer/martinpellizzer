Cyperus fulgens

Cyperus fulgens, commonly known as the flame sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant orange to red inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Cyperus fulgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic environments.
