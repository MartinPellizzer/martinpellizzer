Calliandra juzepczukii

Calliandra juzepczukii, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance.

Calliandra juzepczukii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
