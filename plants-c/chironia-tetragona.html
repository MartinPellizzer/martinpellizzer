Chironia tetragona

Chironia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled chironia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

Chironia tetragona is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
