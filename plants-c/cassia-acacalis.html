Cassia acacalis

Cassia acacalis, commonly known as yellow cassia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy flower clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Cassia acacalis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
