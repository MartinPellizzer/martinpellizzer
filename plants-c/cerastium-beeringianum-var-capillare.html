Cerastium beeringianum var. capillare

Cerastium beeringianum var. capillare, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, aiding in water retention in cold climates. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its succulent-like characteristics and frost tolerance.
