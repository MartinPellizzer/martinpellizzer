Chamaesciadium subnudum

Chamaesciadium subnudum, commonly known as the naked-stemmed chamaesciadium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairless stems that ascend or lie prostrate. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are linear to oblanceolate, typically with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
