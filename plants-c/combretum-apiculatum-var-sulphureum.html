Combretum apiculatum var. sulphureum

Combretum apiculatum var. sulphureum, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that appear in dense clusters during the warmer months. The leaves are small, elliptical, and often have a slightly velvety texture. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
