Camissonia boothii

Camissonia boothii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring.

Camissonia boothii is adapted to dry environments and is often found in the foothills of mountain ranges.
