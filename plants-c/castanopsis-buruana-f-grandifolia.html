Castanopsis buruana f. grandifolia

Castanopsis buruana f. grandifolia, commonly known as the Buru tanoak, is a variant of the castanopsis genus native to the tropical forests of Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its thick, ridged bark and dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
