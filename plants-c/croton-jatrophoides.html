Croton jatrophoides

Croton jatrophoides, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from deep red to purple, often with contrasting green veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
