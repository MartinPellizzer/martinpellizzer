Clematis × divaricata

Clematis × divaricata, commonly known as the evergreen clematis, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species.

It is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 15 feet long, making it suitable for trellises or arbors.

Clematis × divaricata prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, thriving in humid climates.
