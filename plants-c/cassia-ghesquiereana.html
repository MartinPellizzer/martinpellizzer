Cassia ghesquiereana

Cassia ghesquiereana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, feather-like inflorescences that bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. Its flowers are not only showy but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Cassia ghesquiereana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
