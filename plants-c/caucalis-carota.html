Caucalis carota

Caucalis carota, commonly known as the lesser carrot or wild carrot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Caucalis carota is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
