Citrus × limonum var. calabrina

Citrus × limonum var. calabrina, commonly known as the Calabrian lemon, is a citrus hybrid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic peel. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is valued for its high vitamin C content and use in culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is also cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
