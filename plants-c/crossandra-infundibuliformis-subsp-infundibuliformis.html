Crossandra infundibuliformis subsp. infundibuliformis

Crossandra infundibuliformis subsp.

infundibuliformis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
