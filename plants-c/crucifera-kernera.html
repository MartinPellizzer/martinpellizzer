Crucifera kernera

Crucifera kernera, commonly known as the Kernera crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar edible plants like broccoli and cabbage.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the cruciferous group. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to parts of southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
