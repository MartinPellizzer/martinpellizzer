Crepis pannonica subsp. blavii

Crepis pannonica subsp.

blavii, commonly known as the Pannonian hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, often hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single flowering stem.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
