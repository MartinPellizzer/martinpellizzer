Carex nigromarginata var. muehlenbergii

Carex nigromarginata var. muehlenbergii, commonly known as Muehlenberg's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its dark brown margins on the leaf blades, giving it its scientific name.
