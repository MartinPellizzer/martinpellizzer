Cytisus austriacus var. thessalus

Cytisus austriacus var. thessalus, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to the Alps.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees. The plant has slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Fabaceae family.
