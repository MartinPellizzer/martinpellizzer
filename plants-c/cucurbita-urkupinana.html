Cucurbita urkupinana

Cucurbita urkupinana, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other squash and gourd plants. The plant produces large, round fruits with a thick, rugged rind and a sweet, nutty flavor. Its leaves are large and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

This variety is valued for both its culinary uses and ornamental appeal.
