Calandrinia affinis var. graminifolia

Calandrinia affinis var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy purslane, is a low-growing annual plant native to South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, due to its ability to spread rapidly.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in various ecological settings.
