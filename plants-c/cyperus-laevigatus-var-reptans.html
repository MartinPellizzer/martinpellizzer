Cyperus laevigatus var. reptans

Cyperus laevigatus var. reptans, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly folded, with a distinct, triangular stem. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color.

Cyperus laevigatus var. reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
