Crotalaria nigricans var. nigricans

Crotalaria nigricans var. nigricans, commonly known as blackhorse cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
