Cassia hornemannii

Cassia hornemannii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia hornemannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.
