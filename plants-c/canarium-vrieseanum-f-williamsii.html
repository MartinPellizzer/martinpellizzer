Canarium vrieseanum f. williamsii

Canarium vrieseanum f. williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' candlenut, is a variant of the candlenut tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. The seed is encased in a hard shell and is valued for its oil, which is used in traditional medicine and industry.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and economic importance.
