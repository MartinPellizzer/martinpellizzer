Cheiranthus collinus

Cheiranthus collinus, commonly known as the rock wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are borne in racemes.

Cheiranthus collinus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
