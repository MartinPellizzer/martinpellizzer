Colysis kiusiana

Colysis kiusiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
