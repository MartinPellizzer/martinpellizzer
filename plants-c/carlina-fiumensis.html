Carlina fiumensis

Carlina fiumensis, commonly known as the Italian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are usually pale blue or white.

Carlina fiumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
