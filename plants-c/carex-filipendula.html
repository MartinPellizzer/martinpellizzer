Carex filipendula

Carex filipendula, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, contributing to its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
