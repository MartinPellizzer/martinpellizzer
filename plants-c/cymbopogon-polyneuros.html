Cymbopogon polyneuros

Cymbopogon polyneuros, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lemon grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and strong aromatic scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of grasses. Its essential oils, particularly citral, are widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a ornamental and medicinal plant.
