Chrysanthemum japonense var. crassum

Chrysanthemum japonense var. crassum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the fall, producing dense clusters of ray florets that are often used in ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different soil types.
