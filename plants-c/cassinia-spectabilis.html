Cassinia spectabilis

Cassinia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow to orange flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts, creating a striking visual display. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Cassinia spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
