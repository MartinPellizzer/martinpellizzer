Corymbia eremaea

Corymbia eremaea, commonly known as the desert gum, is a species of flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates. The tree produces small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in spinifex plains and sandy deserts.

Due to its resilience, it plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
