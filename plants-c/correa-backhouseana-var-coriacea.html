Correa backhouseana var. coriacea

Correa backhouseana var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
