Crinum congolense

Crinum congolense, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant produces long, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bulbous roots are also utilized in various cultural and medicinal practices.

Crinum congolense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
