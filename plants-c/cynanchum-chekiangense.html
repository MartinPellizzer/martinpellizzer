Cynanchum chekiangense

Cynanchum chekiangense, commonly known as Chinese milkweed or Chinese snake root, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

Cynanchum chekiangense is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
