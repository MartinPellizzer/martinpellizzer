Cheirodendron samoense

Cheirodendron samoense, commonly known as the Samoan cheirodendron, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa and nearby regions.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and moist lowland forests. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some island communities.
