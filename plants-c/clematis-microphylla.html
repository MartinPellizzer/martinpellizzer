Clematis microphylla

Clematis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb to heights of up to 15 feet. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
