Ceradenia clavipila

Ceradenia clavipila, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The genus Ceradenia is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its diverse floral morphology.
