Convolvulus argyraeus

Convolvulus argyraeus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces spiral-shaped seed pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
