Cerasus hortensis var. parva

Cerasus hortensis var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet red berries. The plant has small, oval leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces a bushy growth habit. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

This variety is well-suited for container growing and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
