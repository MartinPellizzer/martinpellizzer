Chloris truncata

Chloris truncata, commonly known as cutgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Chloris truncata is an important plant in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
