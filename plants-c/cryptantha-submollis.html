Cryptantha submollis

Cryptantha submollis, commonly known as the softleaf cryptantha, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
