Centaurea scabiosa subsp. kotschyana

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

kotschyana, commonly known as Kotschy's coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia. It features striking purple to pink flower heads with a distinctive central disk, surrounded by spiny, bristly rays.

The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and serrated. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
