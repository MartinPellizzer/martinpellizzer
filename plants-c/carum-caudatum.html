Carum caudatum

Carum caudatum, commonly known as the woolly cumin or goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems covered in dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a mild, aromatic scent.

Carum caudatum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and delicate flowers.
