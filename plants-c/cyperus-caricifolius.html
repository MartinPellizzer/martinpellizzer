Cyperus caricifolius

Cyperus caricifolius, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus caricifolius is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
