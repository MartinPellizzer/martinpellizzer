Calycera pulvinata f. crenata

Calycera pulvinata f. crenata, commonly known as the crested calycera, is a unique variant of the calycera plant characterized by its distinctive, raised leaf bases.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
