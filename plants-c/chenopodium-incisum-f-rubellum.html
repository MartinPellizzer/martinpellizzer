Chenopodium incisum f. rubellum

Chenopodium incisum f. rubellum, commonly known as redleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and the undersides are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This plant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
