Cryptanthus dianae

Cryptanthus dianae, commonly known as the "Rainbow Waxlip" or "Crimson Waxlip," is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with contrasting dark veins. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, glossy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.

Its unique appearance and compact size make it a striking addition to rock gardens or as a container plant.
