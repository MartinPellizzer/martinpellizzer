Carya tomentosa f. ovoidea

Carya tomentosa f. ovoidea, commonly known as the oval-winged hickory, is a variant of the shagbark hickory.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ovoid-shaped nuts enclosed in thick, woody husks. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a serrated margin.

The tree produces edible nuts that are popular in both culinary and commercial applications.
