Coleus apoensis

Coleus apoensis, commonly known as the Philippine coleus, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often brightly colored, ranging from red, purple, and green to variegated patterns.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
