Cyanicula ixioides subsp. candida

Cyanicula ixioides subsp.

candida, commonly known as the white-flowered bluebell, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained, moist soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

The subspecies is distinguished by its lighter flower color compared to other members of the species.
