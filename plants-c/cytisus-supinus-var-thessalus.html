Cytisus supinus var. thessalus

Cytisus supinus var. thessalus, commonly known as the creeping yellow flowering shrub, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, rounded growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
