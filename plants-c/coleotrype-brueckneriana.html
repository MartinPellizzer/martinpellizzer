Coleotrype brueckneriana

Coleotrype brueckneriana, commonly known as the Brueckner's coral fungus, is a rare and striking member of the Clavariaceae family.

It is characterized by its branched, coral-like structure and vibrant orange to reddish coloration. This fungus typically grows on decaying wood in humid, shaded environments, often in temperate forests. Its spore-bearing structures, called apothecia, are embedded within the coral-like branches.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of particular interest to mycologists and conservationists.
