Cyperus sporobolus var. sexflorus

Cyperus sporobolus var. sexflorus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically purple or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation.
