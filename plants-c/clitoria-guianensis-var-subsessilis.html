Clitoria guianensis var. subsessilis

Clitoria guianensis var. subsessilis, commonly known as the Guianan winged bean, is a tropical legume characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped flowers.

It is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.
