Cousinia hypoleuca

Cousinia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant is characterized by its pale yellow to white composite flowers and hairy, silver-green leaves. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Cousinia hypoleuca is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows.
