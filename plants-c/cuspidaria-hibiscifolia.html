Cuspidaria hibiscifolia

Cuspidaria hibiscifolia, commonly known as the hibiscus-like cuspidaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of the hibiscus genus, with five petals and a central column of stamens. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cuspidaria hibiscifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
