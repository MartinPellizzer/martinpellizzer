Cassinia collina

Cassinia collina, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with narrow, leathery leaves. Its flowers are often arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it produces small, oval fruits.

Cassinia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
