Calamagrostis coahuilensis

Calamagrostis coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuila grass, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy or rocky soils in its native habitat.

Calamagrostis coahuilensis is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of desert washes.
