Chenopodium sooanum

Chenopodium sooanum, commonly known as the Chinese goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its seeds are small and dry, often dispersed by wind, and it is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and arid environments.

Chenopodium sooanum is sometimes cultivated as a leaf vegetable in traditional cuisines.
