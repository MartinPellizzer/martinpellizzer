Crataegus spinifera

Crataegus spinifera, commonly known as the Prickly Crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus spinifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
