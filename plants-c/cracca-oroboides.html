Cracca oroboides

Cracca oroboides, commonly known as the rock fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. It is often found in dry, coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Cracca oroboides is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
