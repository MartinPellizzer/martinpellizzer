Callitris columellaris f. glauca

Callitris columellaris f. glauca, commonly known as the blue cypress, is a variant of the white cypress, native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and is typically evergreen, retaining its foliage throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
