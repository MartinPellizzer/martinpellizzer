Crotalaria littoralis

Crotalaria littoralis, commonly known as the coastal crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along shorelines. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate with a heart-shaped base. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its habitat.
