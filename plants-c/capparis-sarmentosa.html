Capparis sarmentosa

Capparis sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

Capparis sarmentosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
