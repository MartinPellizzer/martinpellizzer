Ceanothus parvifolius

Ceanothus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved bluecurls, is a shrub native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus parvifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
