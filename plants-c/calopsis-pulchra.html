Calopsis pulchra

Calopsis pulchra, commonly known as the pretty calopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
