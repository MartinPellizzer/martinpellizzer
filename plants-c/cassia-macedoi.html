Cassia macedoi

Cassia macedoi, commonly known as Macedonian cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its feathery foliage and vibrant blooms.

Cassia macedoi is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
