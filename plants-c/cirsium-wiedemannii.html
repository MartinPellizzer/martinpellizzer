Cirsium wiedemannii

Cirsium wiedemannii, commonly known as the Wiedemann's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its prickly nature, it is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.
