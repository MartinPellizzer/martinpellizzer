Clinopodium grandiflorum

Clinopodium grandiflorum, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters. Clinopodium grandiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
