Cistus ladanifer var. genuinus

Cistus ladanifer var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that exude a sticky resin known as labdanum. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or limestone substrates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in perfumery and herbal medicine.
