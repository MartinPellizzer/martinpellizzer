Calamus neelagiricus

Calamus neelagiricus, commonly known as the Nilgiri calamus, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Western Ghats in India.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately arranged leaves. The plant is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and hill slopes. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Calamus neelagiricus plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
