Calamus depauperatus

Calamus depauperatus, commonly known as the poor calamus, is a member of the Aroid family, Araceae.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
