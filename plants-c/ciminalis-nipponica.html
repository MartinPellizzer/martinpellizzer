Ciminalis nipponica

Ciminalis nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ciminalis nipponica is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
