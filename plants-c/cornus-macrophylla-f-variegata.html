Cornus macrophylla f. variegata

Cornus macrophylla f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cornus or variegated dogwood, is a decorative shrub prized for its striking foliage.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a variegated pattern of white, yellow, and green, creating a vibrant visual display throughout the growing season. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique leaf coloration and seasonal interest make it a standout addition to any landscape.
