Collinsonia canadensis var. tuberosa

Collinsonia canadensis var. tuberosa, commonly known as Canada bluemink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its tuberous roots are an important feature, contributing to its ability to survive in shaded environments.
