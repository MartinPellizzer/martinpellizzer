Chamaedorea fibrosa

Chamaedorea fibrosa, commonly known as the Fibrous Bamboo Palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant that typically reaches a height of 2 to 3 meters, featuring slender, clustered stems covered in a fibrous sheath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue, edible fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with consistent moisture.

Chamaedorea fibrosa is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
