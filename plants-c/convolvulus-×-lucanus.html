Convolvulus × lucanus

Convolvulus × lucanus, commonly known as the hybrid sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This hybrid is often used in containers or as ground cover due to its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.
