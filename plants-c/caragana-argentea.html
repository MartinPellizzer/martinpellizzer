Caragana argentea

Caragana argentea, commonly known as silverleaf shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caragana argentea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and erosion control capabilities.
