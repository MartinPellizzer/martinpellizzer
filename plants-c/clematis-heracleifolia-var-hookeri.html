Clematis heracleifolia var. hookeri

Clematis heracleifolia var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
