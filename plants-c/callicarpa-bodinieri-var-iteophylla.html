Callicarpa bodinieri var. iteophylla

Callicarpa bodinieri var. iteophylla, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, which are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and a smooth texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its fragrant foliage and ability to attract wildlife.
