Cucurbita pepo var. phyllapion

Cucurbita pepo var. phyllapion, commonly known as the scallop pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scalloped-shaped leaves and vibrant orange to yellow fruits. The plant is an annual vine that produces both male and female flowers, typical of cucurbits. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, including pies and soups, due to its sweet and nutty flavor.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring moderate water and warm temperatures for optimal growth.
