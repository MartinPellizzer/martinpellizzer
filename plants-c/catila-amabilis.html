Catila amabilis

Catila amabilis, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or lavender-colored bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates and well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Catila amabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
