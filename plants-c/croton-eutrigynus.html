Croton eutrigynus

Croton eutrigynus, commonly known as the red croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from deep red to orange and yellow, often displaying a mosaic of hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically large, broad, and have a textured surface with prominent veins.

Croton eutrigynus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
