Chamaecyparis obtusa subsp. formosana

Chamaecyparis obtusa subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Formosan cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Taiwan. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree in horticulture.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and landscape design.
