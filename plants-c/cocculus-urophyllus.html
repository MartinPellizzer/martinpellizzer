Cocculus urophyllus

Cocculus urophyllus, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported antispasmodic and sedative properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological study.
