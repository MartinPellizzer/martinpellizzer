Croton venezuelensis

Croton venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuela croton, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with distinctive floral structures. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that vary in shape and size, often with prominent veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton venezuelensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
