Carex barrattii

Carex barrattii, commonly known as Barratt's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in swampy or pond edge habitats. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex barrattii is often found in wetland ecosystems and is an important component of these habitats.
