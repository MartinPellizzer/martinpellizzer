Chrysopsis godfreyi f. viridis

Chrysopsis godfreyi f. viridis, commonly known as the green chrysanthemum, is a variant of the chrysanthemum family native to the United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to its parent species. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
