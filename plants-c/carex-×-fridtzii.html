Carex × fridtzii

Carex × fridtzii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic environments.

Carex × fridtzii is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
