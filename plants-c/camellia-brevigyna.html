Camellia brevigyna

Camellia brevigyna, commonly known as the dwarf camellia, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Camellia brevigyna prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
