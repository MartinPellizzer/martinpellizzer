Caesalpinia rostrata

Caesalpinia rostrata, commonly known as the flame bean or red torch tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow with reddish or orange markings, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Caesalpinia rostrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
