Cephalocereus chrysomallus

Cephalocereus chrysomallus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and sparse, white to yellow spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a popular choice for succulent collectors.
