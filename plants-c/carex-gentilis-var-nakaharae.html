Carex gentilis var. nakaharae

Carex gentilis var. nakaharae, commonly known as the Nakahara's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-angled berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
