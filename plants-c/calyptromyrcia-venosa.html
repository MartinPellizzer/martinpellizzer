Calyptromyrcia venosa

Calyptromyrcia venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Calyptromyrcia venosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.
