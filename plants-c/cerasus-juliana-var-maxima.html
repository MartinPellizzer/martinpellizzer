Cerasus juliana var. maxima

Cerasus juliana var. maxima, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red or dark purple berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The species is well-suited for container gardening and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in ornamental landscapes.
