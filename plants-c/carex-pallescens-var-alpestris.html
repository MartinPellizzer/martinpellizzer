Carex pallescens var. alpestris

Carex pallescens var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. It has a rosette growth habit and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
