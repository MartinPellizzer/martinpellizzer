Cercis siliquastrum

Cercis siliquastrum, commonly known as the Persian or Middle Eastern Catalpa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
