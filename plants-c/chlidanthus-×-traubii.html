Chlidanthus × traubii

Chlidanthus × traubii, commonly known as the hybrid evergreen hibiscus, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Hibiscus coccineus and Hibiscus palustris, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.
