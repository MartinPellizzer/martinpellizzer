Cerastium catharinense

Cerastium catharinense, commonly known as the Carpathian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, white flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium catharinense is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
