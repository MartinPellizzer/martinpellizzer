Centaurea rhapontica f. bicknellii

Centaurea rhapontica f. bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's cornflower, is a botanical variant of the common centaurea.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy addition to wildflower displays.
