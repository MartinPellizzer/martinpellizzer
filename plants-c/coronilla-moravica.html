Coronilla moravica

Coronilla moravica, commonly known as Moravian crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and fixes nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in some regions.
