Cruckshanksia flava

Cruckshanksia flava, commonly known as the golden cruckshanks, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features striking yellow-orange petals that bloom in clusters during the late spring and early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
