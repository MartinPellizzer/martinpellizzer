Croton catatii var. tricholepis

Croton catatii var. tricholepis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance.
