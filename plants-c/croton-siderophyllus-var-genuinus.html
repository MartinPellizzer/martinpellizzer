Croton siderophyllus var. genuinus

Croton siderophyllus var. genuinus, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from deep red to orange and yellow hues. The plant typically has smooth, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
