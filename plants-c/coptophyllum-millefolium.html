Coptophyllum millefolium

Coptophyllum millefolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with numerous leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

The seeds of Coptophyllum millefolium are edible and can be used in various culinary applications.
