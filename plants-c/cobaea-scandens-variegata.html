Cobaea scandens variegata

Cobaea scandens variegata, commonly known as the variegated cup-and-saucer vine, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with striking variegation of white, green, and purple. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in summer. It is a vigorous climber that thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in gardens as a decorative vine.
