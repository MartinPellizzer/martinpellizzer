Calceolaria glabra

Calceolaria glabra, commonly known as the naked slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are often pale pink or white.

Calceolaria glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
