Campanula persicifolia var. hispida

Campanula persicifolia var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall rugged aesthetic.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
