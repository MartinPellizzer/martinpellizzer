Cystopus coerulescens

Cystopus coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are linear and aromatic, while its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cystopus coerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
