Cattleya gracilis

Cattleya gracilis, commonly known as the slender cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically has a single pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are borne on lateral spikes and are known for their elegance and longevity.

Cattleya gracilis is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and relatively easy cultivation requirements.
