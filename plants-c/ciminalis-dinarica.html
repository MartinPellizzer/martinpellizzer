Ciminalis dinarica

Ciminalis dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

Ciminalis dinarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
