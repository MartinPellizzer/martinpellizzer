Centaurea pichleri

Centaurea pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. Centaurea pichleri is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of mountainous ecosystems.
