Cephalaria tatarica

Cephalaria tatarica, commonly known as Tatarian hemp or Siberian hemp, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and temperate regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Cephalaria tatarica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
