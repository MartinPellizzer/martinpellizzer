Crepis prattii

Crepis prattii, commonly known as the Pratt's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Crepis prattii is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
