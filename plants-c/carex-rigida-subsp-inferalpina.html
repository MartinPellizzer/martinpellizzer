Carex rigida subsp. inferalpina

Carex rigida subsp.

inferalpina, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate frost, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical structure reflects its ecological niche, with specialized growth habits suited to high-altitude conditions.
