Carex bicolor

Carex bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with the lower portion of the inflorescence often appearing white and the upper part green, giving it its common name.

Carex bicolor plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
