Clematis villosa var. angolensis

Clematis villosa var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan rough clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody vines and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in warmer climates.
