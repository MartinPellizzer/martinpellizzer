Campanopsis dalmatica

Campanopsis dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Campanopsis dalmatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
