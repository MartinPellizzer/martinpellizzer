Cucumis melo var. reticulatus

Cucumis melo var. reticulatus, commonly known as the honeydew melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy skin that is typically pale green with a yellowish hue when ripe. The fruit has a sweet, mild flavor and is rich in water, making it a popular choice for fresh consumption. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and features a fibrous, seedy interior.

Its growth pattern involves a trailing vine that produces large, round fruits with a distinct netted pattern on the surface.
