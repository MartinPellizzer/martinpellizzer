Cobaea pringlei

Cobaea pringlei, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that resembles a small, elongated capsule. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cobaea pringlei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
