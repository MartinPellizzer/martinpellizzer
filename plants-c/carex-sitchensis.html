Carex sitchensis

Carex sitchensis, commonly known as Sitka sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex sitchensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
