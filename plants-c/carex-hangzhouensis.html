Carex hangzhouensis

Carex hangzhouensis, commonly known as the Hangzhou sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China, particularly the Hangzhou region, and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.

Carex hangzhouensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
