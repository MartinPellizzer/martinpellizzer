Centaurea × buekkensis

Centaurea × buekkensis, commonly known as the Buekkensis cornflower, is a hybrid species within the genus Centaurea, native to the Carpathian region of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and upright, branching stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea × buekkensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
