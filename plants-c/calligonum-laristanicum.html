Calligonum laristanicum

Calligonum laristanicum, commonly known as the Laristan spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in steppe and semi-desert habitats.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soils in arid landscapes.
