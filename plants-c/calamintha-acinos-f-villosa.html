Calamintha acinos f. villosa

Calamintha acinos f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy mountain germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
