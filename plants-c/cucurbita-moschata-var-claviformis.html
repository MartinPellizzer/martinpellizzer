Cucurbita moschata var. claviformis

Cucurbita moschata var. claviformis, commonly known as the squash with the club-shaped fruit, is a variant of the winter squash species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and club-like fruit shape, which can range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and typically grows as a trailing vine with large, lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the fruit develops from the female flowers.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
