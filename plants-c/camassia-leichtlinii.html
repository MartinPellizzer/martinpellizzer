Camassia leichtlinii

Camassia leichtlinii, commonly known as the Nuttall's camas, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while its stems are often leafless.

Camassia leichtlinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous peoples for both food and medicine.
