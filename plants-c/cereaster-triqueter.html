Cereaster triqueter

Cereaster triqueter, commonly known as the three-angled burrweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive triquetrous (three-angled) seeds, which are often covered in hooked bristles. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and bloom in early spring.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
