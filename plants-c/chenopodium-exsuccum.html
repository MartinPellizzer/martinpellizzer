Chenopodium exsuccum

Chenopodium exsuccum, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in arid or coastal regions where soil salinity is high. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

Chenopodium exsuccum plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
