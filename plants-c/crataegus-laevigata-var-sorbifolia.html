Crataegus laevigata var. sorbifolia

Crataegus laevigata var. sorbifolia, commonly known as the white thornapple or smooth crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
