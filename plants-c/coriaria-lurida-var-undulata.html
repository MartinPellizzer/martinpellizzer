Coriaria lurida var. undulata

Coriaria lurida var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy coriaria, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that are often covered in a soft, grayish pubescence. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
