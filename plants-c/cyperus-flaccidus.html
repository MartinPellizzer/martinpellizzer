Cyperus flaccidus

Cyperus flaccidus, commonly known as the drooping flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs.

Cyperus flaccidus is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in clusters at the base of the plant.
