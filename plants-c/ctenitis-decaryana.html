Ctenitis decaryana

Ctenitis decaryana, commonly known as the red-tipped fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lobed fronds that are often tinged with red at the tips. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Ctenitis decaryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
