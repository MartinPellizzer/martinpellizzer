Callitris robusta var. intratropica

Callitris robusta var. intratropica, commonly known as the coastal melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, flaky bark and narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, cylindrical cones that contain both male and female reproductive structures. It thrives in coastal and saline environments, demonstrating a high tolerance for salt and wind.

This variety is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and harsh coastal conditions.
