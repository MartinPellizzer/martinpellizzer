Cansenia angulosa

Cansenia angulosa, commonly known as the angular canesena, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, angular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
