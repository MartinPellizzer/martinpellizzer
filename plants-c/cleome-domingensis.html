Cleome domingensis

Cleome domingensis, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its seeds are small and dispersed by wind.

Cleome domingensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
