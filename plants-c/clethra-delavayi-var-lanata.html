Clethra delavayi var. lanata

Clethra delavayi var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly clethra, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwest China.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular-hairy foliage and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a thick layer of fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
