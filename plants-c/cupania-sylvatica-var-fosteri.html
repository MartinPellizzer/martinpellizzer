Cupania sylvatica var. fosteri

Cupania sylvatica var. fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's cupania, is a variant of the cupania sylvatica species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
