Cereus chichipe

Cereus chichipe, commonly known as the Chichipe cactus, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
