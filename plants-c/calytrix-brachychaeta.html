Calytrix brachychaeta

Calytrix brachychaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The flowers are predominantly pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.
