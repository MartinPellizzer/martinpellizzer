Crusea rosea

Crusea rosea, commonly known as the Rosa rugosa, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its robust growth, thorny stems, and large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and resilient shrub.

Crusea rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural ingredients in perfumes and medicines.
