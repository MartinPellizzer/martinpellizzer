Caladenia pendens

Caladenia pendens, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers that hang from the stem. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open woodland or heathland environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Caladenia pendens is a perennial herb that produces a single stem with several flowers, making it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in its natural habitat.
