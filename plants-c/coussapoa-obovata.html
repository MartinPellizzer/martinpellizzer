Coussapoa obovata

Coussapoa obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, ovate-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding structure that supports its unique ecosystem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

Coussapoa obovata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
