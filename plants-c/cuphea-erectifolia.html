Cuphea erectifolia

Cuphea erectifolia, commonly known as the upright flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it grows as an upright shrub or small tree. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
