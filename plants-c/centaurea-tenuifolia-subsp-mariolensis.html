Centaurea tenuifolia subsp. mariolensis

Centaurea tenuifolia subsp.

mariolensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions.
