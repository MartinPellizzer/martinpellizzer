Cyathea pelliculosa

Cyathea pelliculosa, commonly known as the silky tree fern, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive silvery sheen on its fronds, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Cyathea pelliculosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
