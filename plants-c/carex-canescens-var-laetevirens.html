Carex canescens var. laetevirens

Carex canescens var. laetevirens, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, silky-hairy leaves and produces erect, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
