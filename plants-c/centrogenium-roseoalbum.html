Centrogenium roseoalbum

Centrogenium roseoalbum, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and its striking white and green variegated foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with a subtle pink hue. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Centrogenium roseoalbum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
