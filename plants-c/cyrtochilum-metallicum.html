Cyrtochilum metallicum

Cyrtochilum metallicum, commonly known as the metallic orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive metallic sheen, which give it its common name. Cyrtochilum metallicum thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
