Cucurbita pepo var. leucotyrus

Cucurbita pepo var. leucotyrus, commonly known as the white-fruited pumpkin, is a cultivar of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white, elongated fruit that typically measures between 15 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant is a herbaceous annual with lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to North America.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its edible fruit, which has a mild, sweet flavor.
