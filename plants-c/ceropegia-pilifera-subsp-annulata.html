Ceropegia pilifera subsp. annulata

Ceropegia pilifera subsp.

annulata, commonly known as the ringed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers.

The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Asclepiadaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.
