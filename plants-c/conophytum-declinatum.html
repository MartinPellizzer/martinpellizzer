Conophytum declinatum

Conophytum declinatum, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Conophytum declinatum is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
