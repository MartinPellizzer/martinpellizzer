Clematoclethra variabilis var. multinervis

Clematoclethra variabilis var. multinervis, commonly known as the variable clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple veins, giving it the "multinervis" designation. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
