Clematis manipurensis var. manipurensis

Clematis manipurensis var. manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or arbors.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
