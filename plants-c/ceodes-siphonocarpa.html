Ceodes siphonocarpa

Ceodes siphonocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and distinctive scent. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

The species is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, where it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.
