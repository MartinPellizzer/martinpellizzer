Cynanchum paniculatum

Cynanchum paniculatum, commonly known as Chinese snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom in summer. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal use in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, containing various iridoid glycosides.
