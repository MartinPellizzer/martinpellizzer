Cordyline salicifolia

Cordyline salicifolia, commonly known as the silky cordyline, is a tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The leaves are typically bright green and can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
