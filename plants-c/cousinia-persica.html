Cousinia persica

Cousinia persica, commonly known as Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, yellow to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons.

Cousinia persica is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry garden settings.
