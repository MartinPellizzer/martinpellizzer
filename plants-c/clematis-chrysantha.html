Clematis chrysantha

Clematis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in cymes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
