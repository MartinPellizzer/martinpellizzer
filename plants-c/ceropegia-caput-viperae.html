Ceropegia caput-viperae

Ceropegia caput-viperae, commonly known as the "viper's head" or "snake's head" plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces delicate, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually rounded or oval in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia caput-viperae is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
