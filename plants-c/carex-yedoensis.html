Carex yedoensis

Carex yedoensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly textured, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex yedoensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
