Chloroleucon mangense var. vincentis

Chloroleucon mangense var. vincentis, commonly known as Vincent's mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its salt-tolerant physiology, allowing it to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant exhibits a unique combination of morphological traits, including a robust root system and waxy leaf surfaces. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinct bluish-green hue.

This variant plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
