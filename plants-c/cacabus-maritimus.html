Cacabus maritimus

Cacabus maritimus, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in late summer.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
