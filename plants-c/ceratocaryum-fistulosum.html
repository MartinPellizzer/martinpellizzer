Ceratocaryum fistulosum

Ceratocaryum fistulosum, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Ceratocaryum fistulosum is known for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
