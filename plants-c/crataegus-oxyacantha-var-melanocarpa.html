Crataegus oxyacantha var. melanocarpa

Crataegus oxyacantha var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, black berries in the autumn. These berries are used in traditional medicine and are also a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
