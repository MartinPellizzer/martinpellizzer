Canarium expansum

Canarium expansum, commonly known as the broad-leafed candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, oval fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are valued for their high oil content, which is used in cosmetics and as a source of biofuel.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
