Crotalaria kamatinii

Crotalaria kamatinii, commonly known as the Kamatinii cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow-orange flowers with prominent spurred petals, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. Crotalaria kamatinii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry to moderately wet conditions.

It is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
