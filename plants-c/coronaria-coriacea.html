Coronaria coriacea

Coronaria coriacea, commonly known as the rough coroner's hat, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and conical, spiky flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often growing in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

Coronaria coriacea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
