Cucumis chate

Cucumis chate, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to elongated, spiny fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the fruit is typically less than 10 centimeters in length.

While not commonly cultivated, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.
