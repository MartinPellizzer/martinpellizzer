Capparis indica

Capparis indica, commonly known as the Indian Caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparidaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in warm, arid climates. The plant features woody stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, acidic berries. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
