Centaurea seusana var. angustifolia

Centaurea seusana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
