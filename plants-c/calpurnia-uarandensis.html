Calpurnia uarandensis

Calpurnia uarandensis, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
