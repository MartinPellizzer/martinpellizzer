Cupia scandens

Cupia scandens, commonly known as the climbing cupia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vine that can grow up to several meters in length, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Cupia scandens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
