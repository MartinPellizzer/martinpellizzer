Carex pseudoarenaria

Carex pseudoarenaria, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Carex pseudoarenaria is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of sedges.
