Cucumis citrullus var. pasteca

Cucumis citrullus var. pasteca, commonly known as watermelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and thick, fleshy stems.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a large, round or oblong berry with a thick rind and juicy, sweet flesh that varies in color from red to orange. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season with ample sunlight and well-drained soil.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in water, vitamins, and minerals.
