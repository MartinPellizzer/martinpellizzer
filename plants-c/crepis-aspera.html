Crepis aspera

Crepis aspera, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Crepis aspera is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of conditions.
