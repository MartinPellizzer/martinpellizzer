Campylotropis capillipes

Campylotropis capillipes, commonly known as the slender cowpea or slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruit is a slender, curved pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Campylotropis capillipes is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
