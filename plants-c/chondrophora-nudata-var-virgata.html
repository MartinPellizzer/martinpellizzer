Chondrophora nudata var. virgata

Chondrophora nudata var. virgata, commonly known as the Virginia chondrophora, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are white to pale pink, clustered in umbels, and bloom in the spring.

It is often found in rich, sandy soils and is a characteristic species of the Appalachian region.
