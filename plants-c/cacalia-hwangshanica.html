Cacalia hwangshanica

Cacalia hwangshanica, commonly known as the Hwangshan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Cacalia hwangshanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
