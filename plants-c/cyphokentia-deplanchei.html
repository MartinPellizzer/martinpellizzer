Cyphokentia deplanchei

Cyphokentia deplanchei, commonly known as the Deplanche's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. Native to Madagascar, this cycad thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants, a feature common in cycads.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
