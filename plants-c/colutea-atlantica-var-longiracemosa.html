Colutea atlantica var. longiracemosa

Colutea atlantica var. longiracemosa, commonly known as the Atlantic broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, branching clusters of small flowers, which give it its specific epithet *longiracemosa*. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodland and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
