Chuquiraga aurea

Chuquiraga aurea, commonly known as the golden cholla, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing succulent features that aid in water retention. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing yellow to orange petals.

Chuquiraga aurea is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
