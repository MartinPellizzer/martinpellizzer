Ceropegia europaea

Ceropegia europaea, commonly known as the African violet or string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which are pollinated by specific moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is a popular houseplant.
