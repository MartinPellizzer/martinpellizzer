Cucurbita maxima var. triloba

Cucurbita maxima var. triloba, commonly known as the scallop pumpkin, is a variant of the giant pumpkin species native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed, and often scalloped-shaped fruits, which can vary in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces large, palmate leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
