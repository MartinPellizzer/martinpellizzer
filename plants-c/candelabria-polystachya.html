Candelabria polystachya

Candelabria polystachya, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a dramatic and eye-catching appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Candelabria polystachya is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a low-maintenance plant for warm climates.
