Conospermum longifolium var. lingulatum

Conospermum longifolium var. lingulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible, honey-scented nectar that attracts various pollinators, including birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
