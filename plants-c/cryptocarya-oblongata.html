Cryptocarya oblongata

Cryptocarya oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits.

Cryptocarya oblongata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in rainforest or coastal areas.
