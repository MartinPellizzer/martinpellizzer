Chamaecrista keyensis

Chamaecrista keyensis, commonly known as Key's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Chamaecrista keyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
