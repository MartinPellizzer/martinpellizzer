Campanula guinochetii var. rhatica

Campanula guinochetii var. rhatica, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and high-altitude conditions. This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for alpine gardens and rockeries.
