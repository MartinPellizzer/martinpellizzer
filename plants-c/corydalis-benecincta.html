Corydalis benecincta

Corydalis benecincta, commonly known as the Pacific fairybells, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Corydalis benecincta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
