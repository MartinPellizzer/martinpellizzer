Calhounia helianthifolia

Calhounia helianthifolia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red composite flowers, which resemble those of the sunflower genus. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Calhounia helianthifolia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
