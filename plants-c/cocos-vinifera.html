Cocos vinifera

Cocos vinifera, commonly known as the wine palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, edible fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that can grow up to 20 meters in height.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
