Cassia conferta

Cassia conferta, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in long, hanging clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically compound, with several pairs of lance-shaped leaflets.

Cassia conferta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
