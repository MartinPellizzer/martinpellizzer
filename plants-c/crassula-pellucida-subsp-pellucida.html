Crassula pellucida subsp. pellucida

Crassula pellucida subsp.

pellucida, commonly known as the glassy crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, translucent leaves that give it its common name.

The leaves are typically blue-green in color and often have a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
