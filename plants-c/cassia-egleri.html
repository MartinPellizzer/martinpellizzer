Cassia egleri

Cassia egleri, commonly known as golden flame or Chinese flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Cassia egleri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
