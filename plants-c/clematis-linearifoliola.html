Clematis linearifoliola

Clematis linearifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are elongated and have a linear shape, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
