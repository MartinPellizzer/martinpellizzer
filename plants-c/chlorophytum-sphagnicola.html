Chlorophytum sphagnicola

Chlorophytum sphagnicola, commonly known as the swamp spider plant, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense cluster. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are borne on slender, upright stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in swampy or boggy environments.

Due to its unique habitat and appearance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
