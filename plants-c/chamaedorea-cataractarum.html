Chamaedorea cataractarum

Chamaedorea cataractarum, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and moderate humidity. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Chamaedorea cataractarum is often used as a decorative houseplant because of its air-purifying properties and ease of care.
