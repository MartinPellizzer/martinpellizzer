Catasetum macrocarpum var. chrysanthum

Catasetum macrocarpum var. chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden-fruit orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
