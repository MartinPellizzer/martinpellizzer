Cissus bainesii

Cissus bainesii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Cissus bainesii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
