Cyclanthera tomentosa

Cyclanthera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a long, cylindrical pod that matures to a pale green or yellowish color. The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
