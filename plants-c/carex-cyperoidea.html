Carex cyperoidea

Carex cyperoidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown, and it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting wetland ecosystems.

Carex cyperoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
