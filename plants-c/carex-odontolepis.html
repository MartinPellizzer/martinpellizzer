Carex odontolepis

Carex odontolepis, commonly known as the toothed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers often found on separate spikes.

Carex odontolepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
