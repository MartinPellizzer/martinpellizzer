Celsia speciosa

Celsia speciosa, commonly known as the showy celsia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Celsia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
