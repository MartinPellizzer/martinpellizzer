Chenopodium rubrum f. nanum

Chenopodium rubrum f. nanum, commonly known as dwarf goosefoot, is a compact, low-growing plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas.
