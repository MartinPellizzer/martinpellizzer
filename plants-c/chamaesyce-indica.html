Chamaesyce indica

Chamaesyce indica, commonly known as the Indian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
