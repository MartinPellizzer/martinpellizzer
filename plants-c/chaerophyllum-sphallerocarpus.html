Chaerophyllum sphallerocarpus

Chaerophyllum sphallerocarpus, commonly known as the round-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant in the familyApiaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and small, white to greenish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in moist, calcareous soils near streams and wetlands. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a culinary herb in some regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
