Croton senescens

Croton senescens, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red leaves that turn from green to vibrant red as the plant matures. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic leaf coloration.
