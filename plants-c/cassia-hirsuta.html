Cassia hirsuta

Cassia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense panicles, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its small, elongated pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cassia hirsuta is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
