Campylospermum flavum

Campylospermum flavum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
