Crucifera argentea

Crucifera argentea, commonly known as silver crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are elongated and pinnately lobed, while its stems are often hairy.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
