Cynanchica nitida subsp. subcapitellata

Cynanchica nitida subsp.

subcapitellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
