Calligonum ozunraicum

Calligonum ozunraicum, commonly known as the Turkestan spurge, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and salt resistance.

Calligonum ozunraicum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
