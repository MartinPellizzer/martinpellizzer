Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. apiculatum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

apiculatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-gray underside that reflects sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
