Caulophyllum gracile

Caulophyllum gracile, commonly known as the slender blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Papaver and Fumaria. The plant is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
