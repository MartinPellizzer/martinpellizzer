Ceropegia nutans

Ceropegia nutans, commonly known as the hanging basket flower, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardens.
