Carex basilata

Carex basilata, commonly known as white-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Carex basilata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
