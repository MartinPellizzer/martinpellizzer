Chassalia chondrophylla

Chassalia chondrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and prominent central disc. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are silver-gray in color, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
