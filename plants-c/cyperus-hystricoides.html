Cyperus hystricoides

Cyperus hystricoides, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. Cyperus hystricoides thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
