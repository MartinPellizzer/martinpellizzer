Castanopsis cuspidata f. variegata

Castanopsis cuspidata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated tanoak, is a variant of the tanoak tree native to the temperate rainforests of western North America.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its broad, leathery leaves. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a dense, rounded crown. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
