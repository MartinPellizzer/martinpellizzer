Calamagrostis neglecta subsp. aculeolata

Calamagrostis neglecta subsp.

aculeolata, commonly known as the rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in short, stiff hairs.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
