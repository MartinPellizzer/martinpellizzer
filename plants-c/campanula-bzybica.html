Campanula bzybica

Campanula bzybica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of garden conditions.
