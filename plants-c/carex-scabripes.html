Carex scabripes

Carex scabripes, commonly known as the rough-skinned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark brown, three-sided seeds.

Carex scabripes plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
