Campanula cochleariifolia subsp. andorrana

Campanula cochleariifolia subsp.

andorrana, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Mediterranean. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
