Celastrus homaliifolius

Celastrus homaliifolius, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright purple berries in the fall, which are a striking feature and attract various wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Celastrus homaliifolius thrives in partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
