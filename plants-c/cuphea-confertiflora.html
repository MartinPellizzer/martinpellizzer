Cuphea confertiflora

Cuphea confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Cuphea confertiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
