Chordifex amblycoleus

Chordifex amblycoleus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
