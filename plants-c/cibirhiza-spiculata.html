Cibirhiza spiculata

Cibirhiza spiculata, commonly known as the burr vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and the presence of small, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Cibirhiza spiculata is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
