Cyathea fuscopaleata

Cyathea fuscopaleata, commonly known as the dark pale fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to two meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.
