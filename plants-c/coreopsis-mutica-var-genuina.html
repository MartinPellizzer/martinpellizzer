Coreopsis mutica var. genuina

Coreopsis mutica var. genuina, commonly known as the rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded to sunny conditions.
