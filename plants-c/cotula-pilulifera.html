Cotula pilulifera

Cotula pilulifera, commonly known as the buttonweed or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, round seed capsules that resemble tiny pills, giving it its common name. Cotula pilulifera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
