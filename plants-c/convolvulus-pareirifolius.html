Convolvulus pareirifolius

Convolvulus pareirifolius, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
