Cereus hermannianus

Cereus hermannianus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a flowering plant that produces striking white flowers that open at night. The plant has a tall, spiny stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
