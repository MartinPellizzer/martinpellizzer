Cuminia eriantha var. fernandezia

Cuminia eriantha var. fernandezia, commonly known as the fern-like cumin, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and herbal collections.
