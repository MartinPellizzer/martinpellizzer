Cleistocactus acanthurus subsp. canetensis

Cleistocactus acanthurus subsp.

canetensis, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus, is a species native to the Canary Islands. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically brown or reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
