Collomia glutinosa

Collomia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the tribe Lapeyrii, which includes other members of the genus Collomia.
