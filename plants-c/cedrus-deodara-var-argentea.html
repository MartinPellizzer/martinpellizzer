Cedrus deodara var. argentea

Cedrus deodara var. argentea, commonly known as the silver cedar, is a variant of the deodar cedar characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage.

This ornamental tree features scale-like leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a shimmering appearance. It is native to the western Himalayas and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Its cones are small and reddish-brown, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
