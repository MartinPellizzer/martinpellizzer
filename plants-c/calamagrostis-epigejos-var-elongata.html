Calamagrostis epigejos var. elongata

Calamagrostis epigejos var. elongata, commonly known as the tall bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This variety is known for its upright growth habit and ability to thrive in moist to wet soils. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Calamagrostis epigejos var. elongata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal.
