Cucurbita maxima var. corticosa

Cucurbita maxima var. corticosa, commonly known as the rough-skinned pumpkin, is a variant of the giant pumpkin species characterized by its thick, rugged rind and vibrant orange to yellow-orange flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and gourds. This variety is native to Central and South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for culinary use. Its unique texture and flavor make it a distinctive choice for both decoration and cooking.

The plant typically grows as a sprawling vine with large, lobed leaves and produces large, round fruits that can weigh several kilograms.
