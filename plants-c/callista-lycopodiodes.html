Callista lycopodiodes

Callista lycopodiodes, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of small, evergreen shrub native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that form a compact, mat-like growth. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
