Canavalia parviflora

Canavalia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bean or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Canavalia parviflora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
