Clinopodium mexicanum

Clinopodium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Clinopodium mexicanum thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
