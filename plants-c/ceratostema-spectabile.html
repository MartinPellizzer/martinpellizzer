Ceratostema spectabile

Ceratostema spectabile, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Ceratostema spectabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
