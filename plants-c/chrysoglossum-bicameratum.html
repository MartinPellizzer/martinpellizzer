Chrysoglossum bicameratum

Chrysoglossum bicameratum, commonly known as the two-faced chrysoglossum, is a rare and unusual plant species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its strikingly different leaf surfaces, with the upper side being dark green and the underside a lighter, almost silvery hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
