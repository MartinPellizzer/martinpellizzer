Carex vallis-rosetto

Carex vallis-rosetto, commonly known as the valley sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth pattern of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex vallis-rosetto plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
