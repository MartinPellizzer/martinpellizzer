Camellia semiserrata

Camellia semiserrata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, double flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant is typically evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for both ornamental and garden use. Camellia semiserrata thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring cooler climates.

Its blooming season usually occurs in late winter to early spring, adding early color to gardens.
