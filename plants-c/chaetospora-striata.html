Chaetospora striata

Chaetospora striata, commonly known as the striped carpetgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct striped pattern on the blade. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure with small, inconspicuous flowers.

Chaetospora striata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
