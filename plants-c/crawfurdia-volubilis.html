Crawfurdia volubilis

Crawfurdia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing corymbia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and long, flexible stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Crawfurdia volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
