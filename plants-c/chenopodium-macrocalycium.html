Chenopodium macrocalycium

Chenopodium macrocalycium, commonly known as the giant goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several feet tall, with a sprawling growth habit.

Chenopodium macrocalycium is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
