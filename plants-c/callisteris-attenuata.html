Callisteris attenuata

Callisteris attenuata, commonly known as the desert rat's tail, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with linear, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Callisteris attenuata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
