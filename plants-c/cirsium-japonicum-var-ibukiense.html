Cirsium japonicum var. ibukiense

Cirsium japonicum var. ibukiense, commonly known as the Ibuki thistle, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple-tinged flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
