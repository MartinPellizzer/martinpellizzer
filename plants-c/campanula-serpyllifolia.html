Campanula serpyllifolia

Campanula serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula serpyllifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
