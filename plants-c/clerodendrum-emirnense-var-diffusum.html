Clerodendrum emirnense var. diffusum

Clerodendrum emirnense var. diffusum, commonly known as the Persian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its bark is typically smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
