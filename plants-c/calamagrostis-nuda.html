Calamagrostis nuda

Calamagrostis nuda, commonly known as the naked reed, is a perennial grass native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, hollow stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

Calamagrostis nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize wetland soils.
