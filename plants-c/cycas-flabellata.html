Cycas flabellata

Cycas flabellata, commonly known as the fan cycad, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that resemble a fan, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, stout trunk with a crown of leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. It produces cones instead of flowers, with male and female cones on separate plants.

Cycas flabellata is native to southern China and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
