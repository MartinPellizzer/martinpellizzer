Cistus sampsucifolius

Cistus sampsucifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its woody stems and evergreen foliage make it a hardy species suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Cistus sampsucifolius is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
