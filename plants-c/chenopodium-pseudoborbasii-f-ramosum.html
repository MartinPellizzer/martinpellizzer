Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. ramosum

Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. ramosum, commonly known as the ramose goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in parts of Central Asia, and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its nutritional content.
