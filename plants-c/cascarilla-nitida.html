Cascarilla nitida

Cascarilla nitida, commonly known as the slender senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its yellow flowers and its use in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
