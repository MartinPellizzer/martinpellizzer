Coryanthes marcaliana

Coryanthes marcaliana, commonly known as the Marcaliana orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown or helmet. The plant produces large, colorful flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, porous media for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
