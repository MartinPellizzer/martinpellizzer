Crataegus mollicula

Crataegus mollicula, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus mollicula typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with temperate climates.
