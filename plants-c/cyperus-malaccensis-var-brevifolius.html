Cyperus malaccensis var. brevifolius

Cyperus malaccensis var. brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
