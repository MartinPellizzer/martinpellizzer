Cousinia greuteri

Cousinia greuteri, commonly known as the Greuter's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cousinia greuteri is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
