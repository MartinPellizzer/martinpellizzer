Chilita umbrina

Chilita umbrina, commonly known as the shadow fern, is a rare and elusive plant species native to the understory of dense, shaded forests.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that thrive in low-light conditions, making it a unique example of shade-tolerant flora. The plant's reproductive structures are typically hidden among its foliage, contributing to its difficulty in cultivation. Chilita umbrina is often found in humid, temperate regions with high organic soil content.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
