Clavimyrtus lineata var. procera

Clavimyrtus lineata var. procera, commonly known as the tall flame myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
