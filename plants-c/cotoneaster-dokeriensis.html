Cotoneaster dokeriensis

Cotoneaster dokeriensis, commonly known as the Doker's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
