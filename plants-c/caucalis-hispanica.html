Caucalis hispanica

Caucalis hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and scrublands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

Caucalis hispanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
