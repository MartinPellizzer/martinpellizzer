Cakile aegyptica

Cakile aegyptica, commonly known as Egyptian rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water. Cakile aegyptica is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate saline environments, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
