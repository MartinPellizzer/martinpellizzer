Clusia aemygdioi

Clusia aemygdioi, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Clusia aemygdioi is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
