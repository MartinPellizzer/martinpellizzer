Calamagrostis kamczatica

Calamagrostis kamczatica, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wetlands, riverbanks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. Its flowers are small and spike-like, arranged in panicles, and it blooms in late summer.

Calamagrostis kamczatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
