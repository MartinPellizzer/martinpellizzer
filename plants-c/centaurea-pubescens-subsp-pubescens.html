Centaurea pubescens subsp. pubescens

Centaurea pubescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the downy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, bracteate heads of florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its hairy leaves and stems provide a distinctive appearance, aiding in its identification.
