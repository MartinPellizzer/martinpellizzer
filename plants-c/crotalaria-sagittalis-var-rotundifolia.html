Crotalaria sagittalis var. rotundifolia

Crotalaria sagittalis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It has a bushy growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
