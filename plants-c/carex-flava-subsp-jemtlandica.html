Carex flava subsp. jemtlandica

Carex flava subsp.

jemtlandica, commonly known as the Jemtland yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, bright yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
