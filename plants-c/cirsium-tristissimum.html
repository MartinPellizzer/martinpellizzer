Cirsium tristissimum

Cirsium tristissimum, commonly known as the saddest thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its spiny, purple flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine, prickly hairs, contributing to its defensive adaptations.

Cirsium tristissimum is known for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
