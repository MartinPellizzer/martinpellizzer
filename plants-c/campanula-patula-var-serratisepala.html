Campanula patula var. serratisepala

Campanula patula var. serratisepala, commonly known as the serrated-sepal bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
