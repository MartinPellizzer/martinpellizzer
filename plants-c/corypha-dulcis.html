Corypha dulcis

Corypha dulcis, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces a sap that can be tapped and processed into a syrup similar to palm sugar. Its fruit, known as the "sugar apple," is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant plays an important role in both traditional medicine and local economies.
