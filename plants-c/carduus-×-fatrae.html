Carduus × fatrae

Carduus × fatrae, commonly known as the Fatra thistle.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
