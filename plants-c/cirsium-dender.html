Cirsium dender

Cirsium dender, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flower heads that resemble small cones. Native to Europe, it thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant's flowers are composed of many tiny florets, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium dender is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
