Cerastium alpinum var. carinthiacum

Cerastium alpinum var. carinthiacum, commonly known as the Carinthian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Alps.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.

Due to its delicate appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
