Coleus dewevrei

Coleus dewevrei, commonly known as the dwarf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. Its leaves are often variegated with shades of green, red, and yellow, adding visual interest to landscapes.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring warm and humid conditions.
