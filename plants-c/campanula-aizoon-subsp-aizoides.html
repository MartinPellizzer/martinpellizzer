Campanula aizoon subsp. aizoides

Campanula aizoon subsp.

aizoides, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in alpine gardens.
