Cyathea hodgeana

Cyathea hodgeana, commonly known as the Hodge's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its fronds are bi-directionally pinnate, with a distinctive pattern of leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Cyathea hodgeana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
