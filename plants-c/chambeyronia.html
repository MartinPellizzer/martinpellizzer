Chambeyronia

Chambeyronia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb trees or trellises, making it a popular choice for ornamental use. Its flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Chambeyronia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
