Cerasus avium var. aspleniifolia

Cerasus avium var. aspleniifolia, commonly known as the aspleniifoliate cherry, is a variant of the sweet cherry tree characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are finely serrated and have a lanceolate shape, resembling the foliage of the fern genus Asplenium. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to other cherry species, though it is less commonly cultivated for commercial purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
