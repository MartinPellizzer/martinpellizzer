Calamintha laxiflora

Calamintha laxiflora, commonly known as looseflower germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like scent, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Calamintha laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
