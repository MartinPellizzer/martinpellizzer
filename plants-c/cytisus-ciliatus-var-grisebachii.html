Cytisus ciliatus var. grisebachii

Cytisus ciliatus var. grisebachii, commonly known as the grey-fringed broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, with finely toothed edges, and the plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
