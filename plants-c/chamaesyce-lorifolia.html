Chamaesyce lorifolia

Chamaesyce lorifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf sandweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically thrives in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
