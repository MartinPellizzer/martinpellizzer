Cestrum poeppigii

Cestrum poeppigii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that open at night and emit a strong scent to attract pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cestrum poeppigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
