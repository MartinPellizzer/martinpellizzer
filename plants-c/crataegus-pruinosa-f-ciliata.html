Crataegus pruinosa f. ciliata

Crataegus pruinosa f. ciliata, commonly known as the silky hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. It is typically found in woodland edges and dry soils across parts of Europe and western Asia.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to wildlife.
