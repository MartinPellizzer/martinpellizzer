Clitoria falcata

Clitoria falcata, commonly known as the sickle bean or falcate butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

Clitoria falcata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
