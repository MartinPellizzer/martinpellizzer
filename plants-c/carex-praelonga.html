Carex praelonga

Carex praelonga, commonly known as the long-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like flower clusters that are typically green to brown in color. Carex praelonga is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
