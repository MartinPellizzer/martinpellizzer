Carex darwinii var. robustior

Carex darwinii var. robustior, commonly known as Darwin's sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it flowers in the spring or early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
