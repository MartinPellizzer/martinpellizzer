Cyperus pungens var. tenuis

Cyperus pungens var. tenuis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in dense spikes, and it often serves as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
