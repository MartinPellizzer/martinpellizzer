Chenopodium pratericola f. rubricaule

Chenopodium pratericola f. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed goosefoot, is a variant of the annual herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dry, three-angled fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
