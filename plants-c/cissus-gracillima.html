Cissus gracillima

Cissus gracillima, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Cissus gracillima is also known for its ability to climb using adventitious roots that form along its stems.
