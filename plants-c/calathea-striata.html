Calathea striata

Calathea striata, commonly known as the black velvet plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage with striking silver and green veins. The leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a unique, intricate pattern that changes with light conditions. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Calathea striata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
