Capparis loheri

Capparis loheri, commonly known as the Loher's caper, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes other caper species known for their edible flowers and fruits. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant often grows in coastal or saline environments.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, Capparis loheri is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
