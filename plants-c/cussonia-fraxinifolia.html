Cussonia fraxinifolia

Cussonia fraxinifolia, commonly known as the African mountain banana, is a tall, evergreen shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, finger-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
