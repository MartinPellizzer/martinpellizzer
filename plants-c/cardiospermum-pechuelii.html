Cardiospermum pechuelii

Cardiospermum pechuelii, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both drought and partial shade.
