Cassia tenuicaulis

Cassia tenuicaulis, commonly known as slender cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Cassia tenuicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
