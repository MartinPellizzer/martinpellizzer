Croton boutonianus

Croton boutonianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a garden plant.
