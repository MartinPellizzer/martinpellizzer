Cytisus capitatus var. virens

Cytisus capitatus var. virens, commonly known as the green-headed broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are small, oval, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
