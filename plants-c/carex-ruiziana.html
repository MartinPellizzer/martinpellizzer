Carex ruiziana

Carex ruiziana, commonly known as Ruiz's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ruiz's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
