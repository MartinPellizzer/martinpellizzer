Capparidastrum pachaca

Capparidastrum pachaca, commonly known as the Pachaca leaf or Pachaca plant, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various flowering plants characterized by their distinctive leaves and small, often yellow or white flowers. The plant is known for its pinnately compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine by local communities. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
