Carex speciosa subsp. varmae

Carex speciosa subsp.

varmae, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking, showy spikelets that are typically pink to reddish in color, making it a popular ornamental in wetland gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
