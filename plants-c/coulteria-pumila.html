Coulteria pumila

Coulteria pumila, commonly known as the dwarf coulteria, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Coulteria pumila is often found in shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
