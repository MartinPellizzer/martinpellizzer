Cyathea pilosula

Cyathea pilosula, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy layer. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea pilosula thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
