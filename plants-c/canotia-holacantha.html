Canotia holacantha

Canotia holacantha, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
