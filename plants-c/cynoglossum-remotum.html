Cynoglossum remotum

Cynoglossum remotum, commonly known as hoary bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of stems.

Cynoglossum remotum is a perennial herb that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
