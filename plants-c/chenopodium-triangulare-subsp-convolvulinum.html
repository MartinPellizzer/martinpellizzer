Chenopodium triangulare subsp. convolvulinum

Chenopodium triangulare subsp.

convolvulinum, commonly known as redroot morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and distinctive red stems, which become more prominent as the plant matures.

The leaves are ovate to triangular in shape, often with a waxy coating, and the flowers are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
