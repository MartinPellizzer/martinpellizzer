Cochlearia officinalis var. tridactylites

Cochlearia officinalis var. tridactylites, commonly known as the three-fingered saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early summer. It is typically found in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
