Calamagrostis quitensis

Calamagrostis quitensis, commonly known as the Andean feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Calamagrostis quitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
