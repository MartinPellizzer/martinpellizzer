Carum purpurascens

Carum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple caraway, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to purple flowers that bloom in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a mild, carroty scent when crushed.

Carum purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
