Chondrosum trifidum

Chondrosum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed cushion plant, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form composed of tightly packed, fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant has small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
