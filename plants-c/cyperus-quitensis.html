Cyperus quitensis

Cyperus quitensis, commonly known as the Andean flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. This perennial herb typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in humid and temperate climates. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered together, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
