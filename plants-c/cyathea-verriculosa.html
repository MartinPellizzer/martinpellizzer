Cyathea verriculosa

Cyathea verriculosa, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

The fronds are typically evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
