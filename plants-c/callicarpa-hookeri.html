Callicarpa hookeri

Callicarpa hookeri, commonly known as the red-berried beauty, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by showy clusters of red berries in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy green surface and turn reddish in the autumn.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
