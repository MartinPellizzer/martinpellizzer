Carex speciosa subsp. platyrhina

Carex speciosa subsp.

platyrhina, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, usually green or brownish flowers. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, often forming dense colonies.
