Campylotropis ichangensis

Campylotropis ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang brier, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
