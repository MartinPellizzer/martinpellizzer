Carex reuteriana

Carex reuteriana, commonly known as the Reuter's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex reuteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
