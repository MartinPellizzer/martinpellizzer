Corydalis × harzi

Corydalis × harzi, commonly known as the hybrid corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Corydalis × harzi thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for woodland gardens.

Its foliage is often heart-shaped or lanceolate, with a hairy texture that adds to its visual appeal.
