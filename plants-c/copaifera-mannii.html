Copaifera mannii

Copaifera mannii, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood and leaves. The plant produces yellowish to reddish sap, which is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties. Its bark and leaves contain compounds such as flavonoids and terpenoids, contributing to its medicinal value.

Copaifera mannii thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
