Cistus salviifolius proles apricus

Cistus salviifolius proles apricus, commonly known as the apricot-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, resembling the leaves of sage. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
