Clematis hookeri

Clematis hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's clematis, is a flowering vine native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are distinctive and often used for identification. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Clematis hookeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to native wildlife.
