Calamagrostis lanceolata var. subulivalvis

Calamagrostis lanceolata var. subulivalvis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often blue-green in color, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
