Cassia mimosoides var. nomame

Cassia mimosoides var. nomame, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental planting in warm climates.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
