Callicarpa longibracteata

Callicarpa longibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to violet berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and small mammals.
