Cyperus hallii

Cyperus hallii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus hallii is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
