Calluna vulgaris subf. pilulifera

Calluna vulgaris subf.

pilulifera, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round foliage.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and moorlands, where it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
