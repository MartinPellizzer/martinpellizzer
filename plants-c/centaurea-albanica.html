Centaurea albanica

Centaurea albanica, commonly known as the Albanian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched.

Centaurea albanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
