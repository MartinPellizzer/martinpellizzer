Calamus banggiensis

Calamus banggiensis, commonly known as the Banggi reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it relies on wind pollination. Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in local herbal remedies.

The species is adapted to wetland and swampy environments, making it an important part of the ecological balance in its native habitat.
