Cogswellia caruifolia var. vaseyi

Cogswellia caruifolia var. vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's Cogswellia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features elongated, fleshy stems that often branch out into a shrub-like form, with thick, waxy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a prized specimen among succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its limited natural range and susceptibility to environmental stress, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
