Crocus seisumsiana

Crocus seisumsiana, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated by gardeners and botanists alike.
