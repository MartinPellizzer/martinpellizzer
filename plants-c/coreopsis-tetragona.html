Coreopsis tetragona

Coreopsis tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and narrow, four-angled stems. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Coreopsis tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
