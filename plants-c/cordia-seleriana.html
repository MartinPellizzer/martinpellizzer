Cordia seleriana

Cordia seleriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are usually five to seven lobes and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries that contain several seeds. Cordia seleriana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
