Chasmanthe aethiopica

Chasmanthe aethiopica, commonly known as the African firecracker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care make it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
