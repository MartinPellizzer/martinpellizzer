Ceropegia stalagmifera

Ceropegia stalagmifera, commonly known as the hanging basket flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and ability to form dense clusters, making it popular as a houseplant. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia stalagmifera is also known for its ability to bloom profusely during the warmer months.
