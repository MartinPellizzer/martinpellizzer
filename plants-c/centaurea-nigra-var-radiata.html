Centaurea nigra var. radiata

Centaurea nigra var. radiata, commonly known as the radiate nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are typically arranged in a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
