Coreopsis tinctoria var. similis

Coreopsis tinctoria var. similis, commonly known as the scarlet coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is closely related to the common coreopsis, but it is distinguished by its more vibrant and showy blooms.
