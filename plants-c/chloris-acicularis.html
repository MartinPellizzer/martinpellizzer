Chloris acicularis

Chloris acicularis, commonly known as slender grass or needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris acicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
