Cymbachne ciliata var. pachyarthra

Cymbachne ciliata var. pachyarthra, commonly known as the thick-ribbed feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its robust root system makes it a valuable plant for soil stabilization and erosion control.
