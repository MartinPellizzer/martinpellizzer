Celastrus dubius

Celastrus dubius, commonly known as the slender staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
