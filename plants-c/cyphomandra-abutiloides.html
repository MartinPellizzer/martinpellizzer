Cyphomandra abutiloides

Cyphomandra abutiloides, commonly known as the purple husk tomato, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically purple or red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, temperate climates.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tangy or slightly bitter flavor.
