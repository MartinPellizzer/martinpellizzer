Catasetum trimerochilum

Catasetum trimerochilum, commonly known as the three-lobed cattleya, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure called a resupinate lip. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The orchid blooms in the summer and is prized for its vibrant colors and ornamental value.
