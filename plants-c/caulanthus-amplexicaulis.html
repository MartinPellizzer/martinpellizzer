Caulanthus amplexicaulis

Caulanthus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that clasp the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Caulanthus amplexicaulis is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
