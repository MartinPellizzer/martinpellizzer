Carex stachydesma

Carex stachydesma, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences known as spikes, which contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex stachydesma is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
