Cupressus duclouxiana subsp. likiangensis

Cupressus duclouxiana subsp.

likiangensis, commonly known as the Yunnan cypress, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China. It is characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit and scaly, reddish-brown bark that exudes a distinct aromatic resin.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species.
