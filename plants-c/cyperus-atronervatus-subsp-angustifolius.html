Cyperus atronervatus subsp. angustifolius

Cyperus atronervatus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct midrib, contributing to its botanical identification.

It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
