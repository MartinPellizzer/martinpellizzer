Carex graciliculmis

Carex graciliculmis, commonly known as slender-panicle sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex graciliculmis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
