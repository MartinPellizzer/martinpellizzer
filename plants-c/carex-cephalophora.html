Carex cephalophora

Carex cephalophora, commonly known as the helmet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
