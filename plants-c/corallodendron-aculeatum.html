Corallodendron aculeatum

Corallodendron aculeatum, commonly known as the spiny coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which are clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
