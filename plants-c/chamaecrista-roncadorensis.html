Chamaecrista roncadorensis

Chamaecrista roncadorensis, commonly known as the Roncador ticktree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
