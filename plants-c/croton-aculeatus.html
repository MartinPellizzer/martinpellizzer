Croton aculeatus

Croton aculeatus, commonly known as the spiny croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
