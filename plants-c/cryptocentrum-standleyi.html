Cryptocentrum standleyi

Cryptocentrum standleyi, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, with flowers that resemble small stars. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season.

Cryptocentrum standleyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
