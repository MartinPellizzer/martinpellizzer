Chenopodium × fallax f. mirabile

Chenopodium × fallax f. mirabile, commonly known as the red-rooted goosefoot, is a hybrid species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and leaves that often display a striking variegation of green and red. This plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Chenopodium × fallax f. mirabile is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
