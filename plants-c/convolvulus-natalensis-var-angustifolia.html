Convolvulus natalensis var. angustifolia

Convolvulus natalensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as Natal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a climbing herb with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports and structures. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are typically pale blue or violet with five petals. It blooms in the early morning, hence its common name, and is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
