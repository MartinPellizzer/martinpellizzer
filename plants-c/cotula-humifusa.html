Cotula humifusa

Cotula humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel or lesser groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Cotula humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
