Cyperus truncatus var. orthostachyus

Cyperus truncatus var. orthostachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
