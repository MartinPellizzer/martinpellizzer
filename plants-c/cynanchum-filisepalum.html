Cynanchum filisepalum

Cynanchum filisepalum, commonly known as the thread-sepal cynanchum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing elongated seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
