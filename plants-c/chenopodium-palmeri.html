Chenopodium palmeri

Chenopodium palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and are wind-pollinated.

Chenopodium palmeri is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
