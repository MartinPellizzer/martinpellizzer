Cavendishia axillaris

Cavendishia axillaris, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure with fleshy stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
