Crotalaria ericoides

Crotalaria ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf rattlebox, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with woody stems and pinnately compound leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated pod that splits open when mature.

Crotalaria ericoides is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
