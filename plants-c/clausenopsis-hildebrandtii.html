Clausenopsis hildebrandtii

Clausenopsis hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's candelabra, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, branched inflorescence that resembles a candlestick. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
