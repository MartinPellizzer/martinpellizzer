Cyperus strigosus f. capitatus

Cyperus strigosus f. capitatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
