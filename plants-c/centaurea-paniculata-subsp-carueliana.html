Centaurea paniculata subsp. carueliana

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

carueliana, commonly known as Caruel's knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. Its inflorescence is a paniculate arrangement, consisting of many small, composite flower heads.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in marginal soils.
