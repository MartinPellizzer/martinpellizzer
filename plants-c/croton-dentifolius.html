Croton dentifolius

Croton dentifolius, commonly known as the tooth-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, toothed leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in some regions.
