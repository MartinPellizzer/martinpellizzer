Calamagrostis nutans

Calamagrostis nutans, commonly known as the nodding sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale green florets that droop downward, giving it a graceful appearance. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it suitable for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.

Calamagrostis nutans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other wet habitats.
