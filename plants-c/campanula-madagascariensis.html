Campanula madagascariensis

Campanula madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
