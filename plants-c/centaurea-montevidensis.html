Centaurea montevidensis

Centaurea montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, purple flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
