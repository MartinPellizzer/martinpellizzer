Cissus sicyoides f. tamoides

Cissus sicyoides f. tamoides, commonly known as the silver-leafed cissus, is a variant of the species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention in its natural habitat. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
