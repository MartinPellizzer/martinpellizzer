Cassia jalapensis

Cassia jalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican cassia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring pinnately compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia jalapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal properties in folk remedies.
