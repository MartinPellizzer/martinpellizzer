Carponema aggregata

Carponema aggregata, commonly known as the clustered liverwort, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, leaf-like structures called thallus, which are typically green and grow in moist, shaded environments. Carponema aggregata often forms colonies on rocks or soil surfaces, contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for other organisms.

Its simple structure and reliance on water for reproduction make it a typical representative of the liverwort group.
