Catevala affinis

Catevala affinis, commonly known as the slender leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to absorb nutrients efficiently from the soil.

Catevala affinis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
