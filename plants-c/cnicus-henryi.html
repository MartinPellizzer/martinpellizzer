Cnicus henryi

Cnicus henryi, commonly known as Henry's thistle, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cnicus henryi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
