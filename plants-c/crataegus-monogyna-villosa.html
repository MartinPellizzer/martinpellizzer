Crataegus monogyna villosa

Crataegus monogyna villosa, commonly known as the woolly hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, giving it its common name.
