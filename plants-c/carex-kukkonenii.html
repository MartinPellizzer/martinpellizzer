Carex kukkonenii

Carex kukkonenii, commonly known as Kukkonen's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to northern regions of Europe and is typically found in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and heathlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Carex kukkonenii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
