Chytranthus calophyllus

Chytranthus calophyllus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Chytranthus calophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
