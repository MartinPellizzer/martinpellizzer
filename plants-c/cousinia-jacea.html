Cousinia jacea

Cousinia jacea, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are usually silver-gray with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia jacea is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
