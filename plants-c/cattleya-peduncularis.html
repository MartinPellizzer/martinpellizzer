Cattleya peduncularis

Cattleya peduncularis, commonly known as the orchid of the hills, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its leaves and flower spikes, which are often tall and adorned with vibrant, fragrant blooms. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with dark veins, and they bloom in the summer months.

Cattleya peduncularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
