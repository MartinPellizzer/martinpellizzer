Chamaesaracha grandiflora

Chamaesaracha grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered chamaesaracha, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
