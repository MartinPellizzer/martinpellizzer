Carex filicina subsp. pseudofilicina

Carex filicina subsp.

pseudofilicina, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
