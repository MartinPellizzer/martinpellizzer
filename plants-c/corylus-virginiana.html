Corylus virginiana

Corylus virginiana, commonly known as the American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its clustered, hazelnut-like fruits that mature in the fall. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow-green catkins in early spring. Its flowers are monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The shrub thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
