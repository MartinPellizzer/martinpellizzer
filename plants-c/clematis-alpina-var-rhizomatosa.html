Clematis alpina var. rhizomatosa

Clematis alpina var. rhizomatosa, commonly known as the alpine clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, woody stems that climb by means of twining stems and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
