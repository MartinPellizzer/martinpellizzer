Colubrina greggii

Colubrina greggii, commonly known as Gregg's colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and limestone areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Colubrina greggii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in drought conditions.
