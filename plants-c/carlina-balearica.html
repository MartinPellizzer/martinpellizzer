Carlina balearica

Carlina balearica, commonly known as the Balearic cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balearic Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or coastal habitats and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Carlina balearica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
