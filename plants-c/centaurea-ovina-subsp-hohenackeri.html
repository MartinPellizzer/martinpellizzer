Centaurea ovina subsp. hohenackeri

Centaurea ovina subsp.

hohenackeri, commonly known as Hohenacker's hoary centaury, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, and its small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and open habitats at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family, with a unique appearance that aids in its identification.
