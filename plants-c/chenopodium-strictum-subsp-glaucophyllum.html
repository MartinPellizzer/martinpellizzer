Chenopodium strictum subsp. glaucophyllum

Chenopodium strictum subsp.

glaucophyllum, commonly known as silver goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
