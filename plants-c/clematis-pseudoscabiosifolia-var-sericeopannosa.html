Clematis pseudoscabiosifolia var. sericeopannosa

Clematis pseudoscabiosifolia var. sericeopannosa, commonly known as the silky plectranthus clematis, is a flowering plant characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a climbing habit, with slender stems that can be trained along supports or trellises. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with a dense layer of silky hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of growing conditions.
