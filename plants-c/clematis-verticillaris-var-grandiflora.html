Clematis verticillaris var. grandiflora

Clematis verticillaris var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its showy, fragrant flowers are typically blue to purple in color. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis verticillaris var. grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
