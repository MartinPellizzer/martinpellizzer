Cymbopogon diplandrus

Cymbopogon diplandrus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that contain high concentrations of citral. The plant is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, cooking, and traditional medicine. Its rhizomes and roots are also utilized in various herbal remedies.

Lemon grass thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
