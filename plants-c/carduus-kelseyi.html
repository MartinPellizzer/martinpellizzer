Carduus kelseyi

Carduus kelseyi, commonly known as the Kelsey's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a robust taproot system. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Carduus kelseyi is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.
