Cephalanthera latifolia f. adenophora

Cephalanthera latifolia f. adenophora, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's tresses, is a rare and striking orchid species characterized by its dense, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous small, hooded flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it is noted for its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitats.
