Cucumis hirsutus var. dissectus

Cucumis hirsutus var. dissectus, commonly known as the hairy cucumber or dissected cucumber, is a variant of the hairy cucumber species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with deeply lobed, dissected margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green, cucurbit-like fruits that are typically harvested when immature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible, crisp fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental features as well as its culinary uses in certain regions.
