Cassia swartzioides

Cassia swartzioides, commonly known as the Swartzi's cassia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Cassia swartzioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
