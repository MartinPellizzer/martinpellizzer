Carex consocialis

Carex consocialis, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex consocialis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
