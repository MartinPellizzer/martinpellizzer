Cytisus monspessulanus var. colmeiroi

Cytisus monspessulanus var. colmeiroi, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding vibrant color to its habitat. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
