Cystium latum

Cystium latum, commonly known as the broadleaf holly or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Cystium latum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in saline environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small wildlife.
