Centaurea granatensis subsp. boetica

Centaurea granatensis subsp.

boetica, commonly known as the Granada knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking purple to reddish flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, composite flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and hardy nature.
