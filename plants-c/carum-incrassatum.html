Carum incrassatum

Carum incrassatum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are aromatic and used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both traditional medicine and herbal gardening.
