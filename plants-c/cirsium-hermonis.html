Cirsium hermonis

Cirsium hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Cirsium hermonis is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
