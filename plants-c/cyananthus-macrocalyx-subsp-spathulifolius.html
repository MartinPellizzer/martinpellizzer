Cyananthus macrocalyx subsp. spathulifolius

Cyananthus macrocalyx subsp.

spathulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lanceolate to spatulate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
