Carex petasata

Carex petasata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Carex petasata is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
