Cycnium tubatum

Cycnium tubatum, commonly known as the bladder gourd, is a herbaceous annual plant native to warm temperate regions.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated, bladder-like structure that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
