Carex scirrobasis

Carex scirrobasis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
