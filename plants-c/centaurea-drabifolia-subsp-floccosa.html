Centaurea drabifolia subsp. floccosa

Centaurea drabifolia subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the woolly centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its soft, floccose (fluffy) leaves, which give it a unique appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and scrublands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
