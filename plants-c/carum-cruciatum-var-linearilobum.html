Carum cruciatum var. linearilobum

Carum cruciatum var. linearilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, branched stems and alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves with a pungent odor. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in umbels, which are characteristic of the Apiaceae family. Its fruits are oval, dry, and contain a single seed, typical of the genus Carum.

This variety is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a spice.
