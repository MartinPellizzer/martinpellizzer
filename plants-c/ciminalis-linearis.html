Ciminalis linearis

Ciminalis linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its linear, slender leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in catkins, a common feature among willow species. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, often forming dense stands.

Ciminalis linearis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
