Coix lacryma-jobi var. oocarpa

Coix lacryma-jobi var. oocarpa, commonly known as Job's tears or Chinese pearl millet, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, teardrop-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces tall, slender stalks with narrow leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, including protein, fiber, and various minerals.

The plant is native to East Asia and has been cultivated for thousands of years for both its nutritional and medicinal properties.
