Calliandra peninsularis

Calliandra peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular calliandra, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional practices due to its medicinal properties.
