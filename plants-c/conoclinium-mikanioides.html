Conoclinium mikanioides

Conoclinium mikanioides, commonly known as Mexican honeysuckle, is a perennial vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers in shades of orange to red. The plant is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its rapid growth habit and climbing nature make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
