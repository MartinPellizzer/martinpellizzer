Cardamine nasturtium

Cardamine nasturtium, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

The entire plant is edible, with a peppery flavor, and has been used for both culinary and medicinal purposes throughout history.
