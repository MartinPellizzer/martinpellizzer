Cissus kakoma

Cissus kakoma, commonly known as the rock fig or Kakoma fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus kakoma thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth.
