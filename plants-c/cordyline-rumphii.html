Cordyline rumphii

Cordyline rumphii, commonly known as the red ginger or blood cane, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to purple, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom at the base of the leaf stalks. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
