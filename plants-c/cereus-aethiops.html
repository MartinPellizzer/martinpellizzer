Cereus aethiops

Cereus aethiops, commonly known as the Natal Thorn, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The plant's structure and flowering habits are adaptations to its native habitat, where it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.
