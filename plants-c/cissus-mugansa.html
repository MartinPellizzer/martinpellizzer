Cissus mugansa

Cissus mugansa, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus mugansa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
