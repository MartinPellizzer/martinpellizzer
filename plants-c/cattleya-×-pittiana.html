Cattleya × pittiana

Cattleya × pittiana, commonly known as the Pitta's cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with intricate patterns and a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in low-light conditions. It prefers well-draining soil and moderate to high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in hybridization to create new orchid varieties.
