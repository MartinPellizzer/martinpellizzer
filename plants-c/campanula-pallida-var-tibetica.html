Campanula pallida var. tibetica

Campanula pallida var. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of pale blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
