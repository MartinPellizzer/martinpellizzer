Cataputia major

Cataputia major, commonly known as the giant cataputia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This herbaceous perennial is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a vibrant mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
