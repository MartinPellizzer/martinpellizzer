Cytisanthus corsicus

Cytisanthus corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican cytisanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that helps it thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.

Cytisanthus corsicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
