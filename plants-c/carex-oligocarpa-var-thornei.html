Carex oligocarpa var. thornei

Carex oligocarpa var. thornei, commonly known as Thorne's sedges, is a variant of the sedges belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear small, three-ranked spikelets.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
