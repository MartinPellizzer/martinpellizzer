Carex pseudodispalata

Carex pseudodispalata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny brown flowers.

Carex pseudodispalata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
