Carex camposii

Carex camposii, commonly known as the Campo sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex camposii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
