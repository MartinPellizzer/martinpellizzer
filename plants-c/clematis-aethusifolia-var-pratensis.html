Clematis aethusifolia var. pratensis

Clematis aethusifolia var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow clematis, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it contributes to biodiversity and ecological stability.
