Cheirostylis griffithii

Cheirostylis griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces spike-like inflorescences with several pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
