Calamagrostis schugnanica

Calamagrostis schugnanica, commonly known as the Schugnan reed, is a perennial grass native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
