Cestrum graciliflorum

Cestrum graciliflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.
