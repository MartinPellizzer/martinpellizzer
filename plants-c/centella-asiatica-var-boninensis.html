Centella asiatica var. boninensis

Centella asiatica var. boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin centella, is a variant of the Indian pennywort plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its rounded leaves and stoloniferous growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal areas or humid forests. Its botanical features include small, white flowers that bloom in clusters and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture.

This variant is valued for its potential medicinal properties, similar to other members of the Centella genus.
