Chloracantha spinosa

Chloracantha spinosa, commonly known as the spiny chloracantha, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which provide a defensive mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Chloracantha spinosa is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it has adapted to thrive with minimal water.
