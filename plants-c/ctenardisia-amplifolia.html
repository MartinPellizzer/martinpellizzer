Ctenardisia amplifolia

Ctenardisia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ctenardisia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often prominently veined. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal panicles, and it produces capsule fruits.

Ctenardisia amplifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
