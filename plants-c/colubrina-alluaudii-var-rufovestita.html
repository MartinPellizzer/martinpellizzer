Colubrina alluaudii var. rufovestita

Colubrina alluaudii var. rufovestita, commonly known as the red-barked colubrina, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark, which becomes more pronounced with age. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in dry forest or scrub habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
