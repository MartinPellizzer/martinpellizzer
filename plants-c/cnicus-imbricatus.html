Cnicus imbricatus

Cnicus imbricatus, commonly known as the sandwort or yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, often blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Cnicus imbricatus is a hardy species that is well adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
