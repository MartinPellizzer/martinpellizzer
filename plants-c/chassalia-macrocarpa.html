Chassalia macrocarpa

Chassalia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited chassalia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellow to orange composite flowers. Its distinctive feature is the large, showy bracts that surround the flower heads, which are often mistaken for petals.

Chassalia macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
