Centaurea saguntina

Centaurea saguntina, commonly known as the Saguntine thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually spiny.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
