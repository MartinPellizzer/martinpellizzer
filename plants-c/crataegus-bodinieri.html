Crataegus bodinieri

Crataegus bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

Crataegus bodinieri is also noted for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
