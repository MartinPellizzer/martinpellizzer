Cyperus bulbipes var. pallescens

Cyperus bulbipes var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale bulbous flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in humid environments.

The pale bulbous flatsedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
