Corokia buddleioides

Corokia buddleioides, commonly known as the mountain gloryflower, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role.

Corokia buddleioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
