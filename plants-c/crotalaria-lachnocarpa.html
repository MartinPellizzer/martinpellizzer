Crotalaria lachnocarpa

Crotalaria lachnocarpa, commonly known as the weeping crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its unique seed pods that hang downward, resembling small weeping vessels, hence its common name. It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
