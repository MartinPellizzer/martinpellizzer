Caralluma shadhbana

Caralluma shadhbana, commonly known as the Indian devil's tongue, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments, storing water in its thick stems. It produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the monsoon season.

Caralluma shadhbana is also known for its traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine.
