Cestrum glomeratum

Cestrum glomeratum, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant is renowned for its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Cestrum glomeratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant evening fragrance.
