Colobanthus crassifolius

Colobanthus crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically thick and leathery. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of coastal and mountainous regions in its native habitat.
