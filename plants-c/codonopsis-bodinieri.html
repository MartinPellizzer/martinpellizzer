Codonopsis bodinieri

Codonopsis bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or the mountain ginseng, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The root system of Codonopsis bodinieri is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
