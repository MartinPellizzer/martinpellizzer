Cladium falcatum var. sumatrense

Cladium falcatum var. sumatrense, commonly known as the Sumatran sawgrass, is a variant of the sawgrass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland and marshy environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a distinctive saw-tooth edge along the margins. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.
