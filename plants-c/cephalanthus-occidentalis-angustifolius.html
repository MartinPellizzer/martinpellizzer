Cephalanthus occidentalis angustifolius

Cephalanthus occidentalis angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf buttonbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its round, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist soils near streams or wetlands, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
