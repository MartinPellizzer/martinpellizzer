Cananga monosperma

Cananga monosperma, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow, orange, or pink. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces a fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. Its roots are fibrous, and the tree can grow up to 10 meters tall.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
