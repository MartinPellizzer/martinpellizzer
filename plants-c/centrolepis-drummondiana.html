Centrolepis drummondiana

Centrolepis drummondiana, commonly known as the Drummond's grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Australia, characterized by its unique, compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stems.

Centrolepis drummondiana is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
