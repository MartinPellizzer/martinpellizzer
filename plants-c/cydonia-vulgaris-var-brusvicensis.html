Cydonia vulgaris var. brusvicensis

Cydonia vulgaris var. brusvicensis, commonly known as the Bruswick quince, is a lesser-known variant of the common quince.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring. The fruit is typically pear-shaped and has a rough, pale yellow skin that turns reddish when ripe. It is native to eastern Europe and is often grown for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
