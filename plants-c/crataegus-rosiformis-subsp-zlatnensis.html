Crataegus rosiformis subsp. zlatnensis

Crataegus rosiformis subsp.

zlatnensis, commonly known as the Zlatan rosehip, is a subspecies native to the Balkan region. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal potential make it a notable species in its native habitat.
