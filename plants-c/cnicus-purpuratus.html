Cnicus purpuratus

Cnicus purpuratus, commonly known as the purple sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish-gray color, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Cnicus purpuratus is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of soil conditions in its native habitat.
