Cyathea borbonica var. sevathiana

Cyathea borbonica var. sevathiana, commonly known as the Sevathian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to the plant's ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability through its extensive root system.
