Clematis integrifolia subsp. fremontii

Clematis integrifolia subsp.

fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, all with smooth, entire margins.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
