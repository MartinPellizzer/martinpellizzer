Calanthe peyrotii

Calanthe peyrotii, commonly known as the Peyrot's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Calanthe peyrotii is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
