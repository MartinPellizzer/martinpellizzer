Cyperus retusus

Cyperus retusus, commonly known as the sharpleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus retusus has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to moist environments.
