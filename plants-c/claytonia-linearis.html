Claytonia linearis

Claytonia linearis, commonly known as the slender springbeauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, linear leaves that are typically less than an inch wide, giving it a grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Claytonia linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
