Capparis lobbiana

Capparis lobbiana, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, tangy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Capparis lobbiana is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
