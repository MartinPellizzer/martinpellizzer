Cordia gracilipes

Cordia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a yellowish or greenish color, and it produces small, fleshy, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
