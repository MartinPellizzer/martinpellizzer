Cyperus brevispicatus

Cyperus brevispicatus, commonly known as the short-pinioned flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this species typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-lobed, dry fruits.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
