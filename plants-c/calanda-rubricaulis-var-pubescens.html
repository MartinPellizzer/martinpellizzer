Calanda rubricaulis var. pubescens

Calanda rubricaulis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calanda, is a variant of the calanda plant characterized by its dense, soft pubescence on its stems and leaves.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, adding ornamental value to its habitat. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
