Cousinia schiraziana

Cousinia schiraziana, commonly known as the Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid climates. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense involucres, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Cousinia schiraziana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
