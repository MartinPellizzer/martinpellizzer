Crepis lacera subsp. titani

Crepis lacera subsp.

titani, commonly known as the Titan's hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its slender stems and tufted inflorescences make it easily distinguishable from other members of the genus.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
