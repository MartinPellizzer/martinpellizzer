Cyananthus incanus var. bicolor

Cyananthus incanus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, often growing in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
