Chorizanthe rosulenta

Chorizanthe rosulenta, commonly known as the Rosulate Hackberry, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit, with dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
