Crinum imperiale

Crinum imperiale, commonly known as the imperial crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces clusters of trumpet-shaped blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are long and strap-like, providing a dramatic foliage display.

Crinum imperiale thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
