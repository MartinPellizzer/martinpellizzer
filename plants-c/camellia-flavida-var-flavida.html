Camellia flavida var. flavida

Camellia flavida var. flavida, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Japan.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

Camellia flavida var. flavida is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and cold hardiness.
