Celerina

Celerina, commonly known as celery, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that cluster in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Celerina thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its roots and stalks are widely used in culinary applications for their distinctive flavor and nutritional value.
