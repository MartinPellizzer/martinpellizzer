Convolvulus portoricensis

Convolvulus portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Convolvulus portoricensis is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
