Crataegus johnstonii

Crataegus johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
