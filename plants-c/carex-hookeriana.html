Carex hookeriana

Carex hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Carex hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
