Cladrastis lutea f. tomentosa

Cladrastis lutea f. tomentosa, commonly known as the white ash or tomentosa cladrastis, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
