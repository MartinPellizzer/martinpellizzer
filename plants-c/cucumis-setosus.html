Cucumis setosus

Cucumis setosus, commonly known as the hairy cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is often cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
