Calamus moseleyanus

Calamus moseleyanus, commonly known as the Moseley's canes.

It is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae) and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces striking inflorescences with a spathe and spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus moseleyanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
