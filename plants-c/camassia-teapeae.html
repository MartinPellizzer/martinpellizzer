Camassia teapeae

Camassia teapeae, commonly known as the Townsend's camas, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and slender.

Camassia teapeae is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
