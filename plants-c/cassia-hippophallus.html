Cassia hippophallus

Cassia hippophallus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bright orange to red inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods containing flat, brown seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
