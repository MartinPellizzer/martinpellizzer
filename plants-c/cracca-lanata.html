Cracca lanata

Cracca lanata, commonly known as the woolly cracca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cracca lanata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
