Cousinia commixta

Cousinia commixta, commonly known as the mixed cushion cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cousinia commixta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
