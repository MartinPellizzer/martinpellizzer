Caltha marginata

Caltha marginata, commonly known as the white marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive white margin, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky layer of hair.

Caltha marginata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
