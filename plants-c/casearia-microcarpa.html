Casearia microcarpa

Casearia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited casearia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its growth habit and morphology make it suitable for ornamental use in tropical gardens.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
