Cereus coccineus var. cylindricus

Cereus coccineus var. cylindricus, commonly known as the red torch cactus, is a columnar cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its spines are typically short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.
