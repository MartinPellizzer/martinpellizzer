Cicerbita benthamii

Cicerbita benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham's cicerbita, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
