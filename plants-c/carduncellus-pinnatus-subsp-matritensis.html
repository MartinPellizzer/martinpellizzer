Carduncellus pinnatus subsp. matritensis

Carduncellus pinnatus subsp.

matritensis, commonly known as the Matritensis thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean regions. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
