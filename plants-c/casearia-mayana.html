Casearia mayana

Casearia mayana, commonly known as the yellow casearia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting habit.
