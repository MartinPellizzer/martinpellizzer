Cryptocarya globularia

Cryptocarya globularia, commonly known as the globularia ash or smooth ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

Cryptocarya globularia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive form and foliage.
