Celtis appendiculata

Celtis appendiculata, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals.

Celtis appendiculata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
