Cyperus laetus

Cyperus laetus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wet or marshy environments. Cyperus laetus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, fountain-like growth habit.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and filter water in wetland ecosystems.
