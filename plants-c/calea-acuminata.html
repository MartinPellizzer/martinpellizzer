Calea acuminata

Calea acuminata, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calea acuminata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
