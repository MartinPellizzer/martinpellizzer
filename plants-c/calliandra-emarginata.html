Calliandra emarginata

Calliandra emarginata, commonly known as the winged bean, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, pinnate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.

Additionally, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
