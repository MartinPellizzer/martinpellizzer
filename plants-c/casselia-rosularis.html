Casselia rosularis

Casselia rosularis, commonly known as the rose-like casselia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Casselia rosularis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
