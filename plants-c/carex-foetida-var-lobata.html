Carex foetida var. lobata

Carex foetida var. lobata, commonly known as the stinking sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its distinctive odor and ecological role, it is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
