Coreopsis stellata

Coreopsis stellata, commonly known as the threadleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with ray florets. The plant has narrow, thread-like leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
