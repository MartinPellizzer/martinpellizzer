Chromolaena tyleri

Chromolaena tyleri, commonly known as the stinging nettle weed, is a tropical plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It is native to the Americas but has been introduced to various parts of the world.

Chromolaena tyleri is known for its stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
