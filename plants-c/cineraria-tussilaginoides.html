Cineraria tussilaginoides

Cineraria tussilaginoides, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to bloom in sunny conditions.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential respiratory benefits.
