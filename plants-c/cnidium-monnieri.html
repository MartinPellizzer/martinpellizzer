Cnidium monnieri

Cnidium monnieri, commonly known as Chinese snakegrass, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, containing compounds like cnidiosides.

Cnidium monnieri is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though it can be toxic if not used properly.
