Carphalea bernieri

Carphalea bernieri, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are dry capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Carphalea bernieri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
