Cerastium semidecandrum var. macilentum

Cerastium semidecandrum var. macilentum, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
