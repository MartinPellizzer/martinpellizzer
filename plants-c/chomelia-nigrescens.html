Chomelia nigrescens

Chomelia nigrescens, commonly known as black chomel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Chomelia nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
