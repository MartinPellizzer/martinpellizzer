Cestrum hediundinum

Cestrum hediundinum, commonly known as the sweet shrub or night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual appeal.
