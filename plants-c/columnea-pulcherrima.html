Columnea pulcherrima

Columnea pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower or red carnation, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with contrasting green or white throats. The plant typically grows as a shrub or trailing plant, with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Columnea pulcherrima is valued for its striking appearance and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
