Clematis tibetana var. lineariloba

Clematis tibetana var. lineariloba, commonly known as the Tibetan linearleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its stems are often twining or climbing, and it produces small, follicle-like fruits.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential ornamental value.
