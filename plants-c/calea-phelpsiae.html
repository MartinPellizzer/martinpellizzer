Calea phelpsiae

Calea phelpsiae, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom at night. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, white fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calea phelpsiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
