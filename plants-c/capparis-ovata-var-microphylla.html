Capparis ovata var. microphylla

Capparis ovata var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf caper, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its unique, edible, greenish-yellow fruit, which is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
