Calliandra haematostoma var. genuina

Calliandra haematostoma var. genuina, commonly known as the crimson wattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright red, feathery inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
