Corylus balcana

Corylus balcana, commonly known as the Balkan hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its nuts are encased in a spiny husk and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Corylus balcana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical uses in traditional medicine.
