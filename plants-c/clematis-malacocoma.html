Clematis malacocoma

Clematis malacocoma, commonly known as the Malacocoma Clematis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis malacocoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
