Caprifolium tschonoskii

Caprifolium tschonoskii, commonly known as the Tschonoskii honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible.

Caprifolium tschonoskii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
