Crocus sieberi var. thessalus

Crocus sieberi var. thessalus, commonly known as the Thessalian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
