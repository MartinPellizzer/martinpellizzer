Cyphomandra capsicoides

Cyphomandra capsicoides, commonly known as the yellow horned melon, is a tropical climbing vine native to South America.

It produces large, yellow, egg-shaped fruits with a thick, rough rind and a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and emits a strong, unpleasant odor when mature. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.

The species is related to other members of the Solanaceae family, including tomatoes and potatoes.
