Callistemon pungens subsp. synoriensis

Callistemon pungens subsp.

synoriensis, commonly known as the Northern River Bottlebrush, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red bottlebrush flowers that appear in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
