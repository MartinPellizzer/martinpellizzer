Curcuma graminifolia

Curcuma graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though they are less commonly used than other Curcuma species.
