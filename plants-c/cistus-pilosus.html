Cistus pilosus

Cistus pilosus, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable species in Mediterranean ecosystems.

Cistus pilosus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
