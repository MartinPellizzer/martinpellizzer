Carex acutatiformis

Carex acutatiformis, commonly known as the sharp-pointed sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
