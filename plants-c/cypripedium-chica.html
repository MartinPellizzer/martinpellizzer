Cypripedium chica

Cypripedium chica, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, large, slipper-shaped flower. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white with a prominent pouch-like structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Cypripedium chica is also known for its showy appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
