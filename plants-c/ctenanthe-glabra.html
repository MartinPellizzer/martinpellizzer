Ctenanthe glabra

Ctenanthe glabra, commonly known as the velvet leaf, is a tropical African plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are borne on a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Ctenanthe glabra is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
