Cyperus semiochraceus

Cyperus semiochraceus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
