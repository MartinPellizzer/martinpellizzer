Caesalpinia miranda

Caesalpinia miranda, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red and orange feathery flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia miranda is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
