Cereus triqueter

Cereus triqueter, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is often cultivated for its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a columnar growth habit and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
