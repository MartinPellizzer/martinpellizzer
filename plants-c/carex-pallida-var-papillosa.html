Carex pallida var. papillosa

Carex pallida var. papillosa, commonly known as the pale sedge with silky fringes.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually pale green to silvery in color. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The variety papillosa is distinguished by the presence of silky, hair-like structures on the bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.
