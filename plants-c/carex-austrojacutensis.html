Carex austrojacutensis

Carex austrojacutensis, commonly known as the southern narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetland or marshy habitats. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex austrojacutensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
