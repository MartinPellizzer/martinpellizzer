Cousinia litwinowiana

Cousinia litwinowiana, commonly known as the litwinowian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Cousinia litwinowiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
