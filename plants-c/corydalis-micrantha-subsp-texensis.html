Corydalis micrantha subsp. texensis

Corydalis micrantha subsp.

texensis, commonly known as the Texas dwarf corydalis, is a member of the Fumariaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, nodding flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is native to the central and southern United States, particularly Texas, and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
