Carex hirta var. subglabra

Carex hirta var. subglabra, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

The leaves are generally hairy, especially along the midrib, giving the plant its common name.
