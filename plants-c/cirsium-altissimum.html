Cirsium altissimum

Cirsium altissimum, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant features large, silvery-green leaves with coarse serrations and a distinctive purple-blue flower head. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Cirsium altissimum plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
