Campanula vicinituba

Campanula vicinituba, commonly known as the narrow-tubed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Campanula vicinituba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
