Clinopodium chinense f. setilobum

Clinopodium chinense f. setilobum, commonly known as the broad-leafed woodruff, is a variant of the woodruff plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically broader and more elongated compared to the typical form, with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
