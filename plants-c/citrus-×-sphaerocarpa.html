Citrus × sphaerocarpa

Citrus × sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the pomelo or shaddock, is a hybrid citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruit that can weigh up to several kilograms. The plant is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit contains a segmented, juicy pulp that can be sweet or sour depending on the variety.

The botanical features of Citrus × sphaerocarpa make it a valuable species in both horticulture and agriculture.
