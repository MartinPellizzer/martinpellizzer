Carex pelia

Carex pelia, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to regions of Asia, particularly China, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex pelia is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams and ponds.
