Cousinia linczewskii

Cousinia linczewskii, commonly known as the Armenian cushion flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Cousinia linczewskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
