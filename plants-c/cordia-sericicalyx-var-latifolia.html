Cordia sericicalyx var. latifolia

Cordia sericicalyx var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
