Cereus fragrans

Cereus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet scent cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is a flowering cactus that produces striking, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a tall, spiny stem with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. Its flowers emit a sweet, pleasant aroma, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
