Cyphostemma subciliatum

Cyphostemma subciliatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive ciliate margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and has a slow growth rate.

Cyphostemma subciliatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
