Cornuella

Cornuella, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Cornuella is a member of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other poppies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.
