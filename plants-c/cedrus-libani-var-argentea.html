Cedrus libani var. argentea

Cedrus libani var. argentea, commonly known as the silver cedar, is a variety of the Lebanon cedar.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This coniferous tree is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, particularly in Lebanon and Syria. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
