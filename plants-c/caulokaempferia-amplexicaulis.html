Caulokaempferia amplexicaulis

Caulokaempferia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the climbing ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
