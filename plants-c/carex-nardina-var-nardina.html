Carex nardina var. nardina

Carex nardina var. nardina, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

The flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
