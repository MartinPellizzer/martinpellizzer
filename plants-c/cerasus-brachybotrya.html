Cerasus brachybotrya

Cerasus brachybotrya, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamabuki, is a species of flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams and preserves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its early spring blossoms and is also valued in traditional Japanese culture.
