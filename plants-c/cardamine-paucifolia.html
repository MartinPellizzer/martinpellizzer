Cardamine paucifolia

Cardamine paucifolia, commonly known as slender water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive basal rosette structure. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in an umbel-like cluster.

Cardamine paucifolia is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
