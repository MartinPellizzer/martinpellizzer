Cereus lamprospermus

Cereus lamprospermus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and ability to bloom at night, producing large, showy flowers. The plant has a columnar growth habit and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and emit a strong fragrance.

Cereus lamprospermus is a popular choice for succulent gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
