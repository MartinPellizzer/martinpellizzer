Castalia gigantea

Castalia gigantea, commonly known as the giant castalia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in clusters. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, though it can become semi-woody in certain conditions.

Castalia gigantea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its unique appearance and foliage.
