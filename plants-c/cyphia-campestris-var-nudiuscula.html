Cyphia campestris var. nudiuscula

Cyphia campestris var. nudiuscula, commonly known as the naked-stemmed cyphia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced floral structures and is of interest to botanists studying plant morphology and adaptation.
