Canavalia multiflora

Canavalia multiflora, commonly known as the cowpea or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are widely used in cuisines around the world. Canavalia multiflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a versatile plant for both food and ecological purposes.
