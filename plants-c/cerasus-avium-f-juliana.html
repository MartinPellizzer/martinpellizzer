Cerasus avium f. juliana

Cerasus avium f. juliana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum or dwarf cherry plum, is a compact, ornamental form of the cherry plum.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. The plant is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in bonsai and small garden settings.
