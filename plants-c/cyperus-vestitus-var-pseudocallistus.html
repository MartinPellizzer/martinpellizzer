Cyperus vestitus var. pseudocallistus

Cyperus vestitus var. pseudocallistus, commonly known as the velvet flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
