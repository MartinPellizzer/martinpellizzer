Canarium maluense f. celebicum

Canarium maluense f. celebicum, commonly known as the Celebes candlenut.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia, characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, kidney-shaped nuts encased in a hard shell. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its oil-rich seeds, which have traditional and commercial uses.
