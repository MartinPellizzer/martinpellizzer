Crataegus placens

Crataegus placens, commonly known as the European hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are edible but often used in jams and wines.

Crataegus placens is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
