Castanea crenata var. femina

Castanea crenata var. femina, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its oval, chestnut-like fruits that mature in the fall. The plant produces small, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it has a dense, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and edible nuts.
