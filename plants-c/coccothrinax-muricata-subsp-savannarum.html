Coccothrinax muricata subsp. savannarum

Coccothrinax muricata subsp.

savannarum, commonly known as the Cuban palm or the savanna palm, is a species native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Isle of Youth. It is a small, slow-growing palm with a slender trunk and a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
