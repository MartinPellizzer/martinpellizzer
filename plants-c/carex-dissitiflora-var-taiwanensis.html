Carex dissitiflora var. taiwanensis

Carex dissitiflora var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Carex dissitiflora var. taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
