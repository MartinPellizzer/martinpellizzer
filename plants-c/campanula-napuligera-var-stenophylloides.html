Campanula napuligera var. stenophylloides

Campanula napuligera var. stenophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
