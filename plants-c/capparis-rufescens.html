Capparis rufescens

Capparis rufescens, commonly known as the red-fruited caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry habitats, characterized by its thorny stems and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features include a woody structure and the ability to thrive in saline and arid conditions.

Capparis rufescens is part of the Capparaceae family, which is known for its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
