Cocculus dichopetalus

Cocculus dichopetalus, commonly known as the devil's fig, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive purple or violet flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
