Corydalis canadensis

Corydalis canadensis, commonly known as Canadian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Corydalis canadensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
