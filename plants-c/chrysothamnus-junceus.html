Chrysothamnus junceus

Chrysothamnus junceus, commonly known as golden sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its striking color and drought tolerance.
