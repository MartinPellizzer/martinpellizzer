Cyclanthera rostrata

Cyclanthera rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant produces small, pale green, cucurbit-like fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Cyclanthera rostrata is characterized by its unique, elongated fruit shape and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
