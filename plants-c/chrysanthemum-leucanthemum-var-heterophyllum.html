Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. heterophyllum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the meadow chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
