Carpesium eximium

Carpesium eximium, commonly known as the yellow oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
