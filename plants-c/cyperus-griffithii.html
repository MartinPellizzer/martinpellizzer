Cyperus griffithii

Cyperus griffithii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it helps in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.

Cyperus griffithii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to moist conditions.
