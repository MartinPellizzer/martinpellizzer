Colletia cruciata

Colletia cruciata, commonly known as the crucifix cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often arranged in a cruciform pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
