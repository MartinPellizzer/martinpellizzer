Carduus heterolepis

Carduus heterolepis, commonly known as the western coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a cluster of bracts beneath the flower head, forming a conical shape. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a rough, textured surface.

Carduus heterolepis is often found in prairie habitats and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
