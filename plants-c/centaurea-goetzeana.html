Centaurea goetzeana

Centaurea goetzeana, commonly known as Goetze's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces striking blue to purple flowers with prominent yellow centers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
