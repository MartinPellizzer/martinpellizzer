Compsoa

Compsoa, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, white to pale pink ray flowers surrounding a central disk of yellow florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Compsoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
