Carex brizopyrum

Carex brizopyrum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex brizopyrum is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
