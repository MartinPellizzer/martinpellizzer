Cylindrolobus cyrtosepalus

Cylindrolobus cyrtosepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical pseudobulbs and narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
