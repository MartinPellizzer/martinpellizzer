Convolvulus pycnanthus

Convolvulus pycnanthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus pycnanthus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy, trumpet-shaped blooms.
