Carex nardina subsp. hepburnii

Carex nardina subsp.

hepburnii, commonly known as Hepburn's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
