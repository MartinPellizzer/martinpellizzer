Cornus ferulacea

Cornus ferulacea, commonly known as slender dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cornus ferulacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
