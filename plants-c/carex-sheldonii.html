Carex sheldonii

Carex sheldonii, commonly known as Sheldon's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

Sheldon's sedge plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
