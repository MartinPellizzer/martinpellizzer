Crocus leucostylosus

Crocus leucostylosus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky or well-drained soils, and it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
