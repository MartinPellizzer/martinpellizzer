Clematis vitalba subsp. grata

Clematis vitalba subsp.

grata, commonly known as the sweet clematis, is a climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy texture.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are wiry and covered in fine, hooked hairs that aid in climbing.

This subspecies is often found in woodlands and hedgerows, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
