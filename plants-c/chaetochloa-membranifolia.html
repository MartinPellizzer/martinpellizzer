Chaetochloa membranifolia

Chaetochloa membranifolia, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves that are often covered with short, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with slender stems that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and often concealed within the foliage.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
