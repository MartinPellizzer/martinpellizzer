Ceratoscyphus coeruleus

Ceratoscyphus coeruleus, commonly known as the blue holly, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage that turns a vibrant blue-green in the winter months. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that persist through the winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a distinctive evergreen plant.
