Cyrtandra suffruticosa

Cyrtandra suffruticosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Cyrtandra suffruticosa is part of the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in Hawaiian ecosystems.
