Cucumis flexuosus

Cucumis flexuosus, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its vines can climb or trail along the ground.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.
