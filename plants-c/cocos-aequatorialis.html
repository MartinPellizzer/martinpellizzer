Cocos aequatorialis

Cocos aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian coconut, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces coconuts that are typically smaller than those of the more widely known Cocos nucifera. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts in its native habitat.
