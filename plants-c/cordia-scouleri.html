Cordia scouleri

Cordia scouleri, commonly known as the red-flowered cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys.

Cordia scouleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
