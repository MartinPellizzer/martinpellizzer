Carduus picris

Carduus picris, commonly known as the greater hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. The plant features a robust, upright stem covered in coarse hairs, and its basal leaves are deeply lobed with spiny margins. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single, large composite head, surrounded by pale green bracts.

Carduus picris is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
