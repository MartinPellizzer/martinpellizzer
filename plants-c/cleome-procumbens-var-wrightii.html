Cleome procumbens var. wrightii

Cleome procumbens var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom throughout the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
