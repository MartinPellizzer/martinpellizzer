Carex × kohtsii

Carex × kohtsii, commonly known as the Kohtsii sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, native to parts of East Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in the summer.

Carex × kohtsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
