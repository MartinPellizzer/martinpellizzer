Codonopsis ussuriensis f. viridiflora

Codonopsis ussuriensis f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered codonopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which is a distinguishing feature of this form. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
