Combretum obtusifolium var. griseolepidotum

Combretum obtusifolium var. griseolepidotum, commonly known as the grey-leafed combretum, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, greyish pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
