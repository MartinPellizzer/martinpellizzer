Copernicia clarensis

Copernicia clarensis, commonly known as the Clarense palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible fruits. Copernicia clarensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora.
