Chenopodium polispermum

Chenopodium polispermum, commonly known as the many-seeded goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. Its succulent stems and leaves allow it to thrive in arid and disturbed environments.

Chenopodium polispermum is often found in dry soils along roadsides, fields, and disturbed areas.
