Cyperus bufonius

Cyperus bufonius, commonly known as the frog's eye sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. Cyperus bufonius is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
