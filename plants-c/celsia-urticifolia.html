Celsia urticifolia

Celsia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small greenish-white flowers. The plant contains irritant chemicals in its leaves and stems, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Celsia urticifolia plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
