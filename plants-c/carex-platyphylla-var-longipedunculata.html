Carex platyphylla var. longipedunculata

Carex platyphylla var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
