Calamagrostis hookeri

Calamagrostis hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Calamagrostis hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
