Corymbium africanum var. scabridum

Corymbium africanum var. scabridum, commonly known as the African corymbia, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in corymbs, which are typically followed by small, woody fruits.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
