Carduus cardunculus

Carduus cardunculus, commonly known as the cardoon, is a tall, robust plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and culinary uses. Its leaves are edible when young and tender, though they require careful preparation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and landscapes.
