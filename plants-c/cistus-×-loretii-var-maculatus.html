Cistus × loretii var. maculatus

Cistus × loretii var. maculatus, commonly known as the spotted rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.

The spotted varietal name refers to the distinctive markings on its leaves, which add to its visual appeal.
