Crataegus meridiana

Crataegus meridiana, commonly known as the Mediterranean hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and features thorned stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include simple, ovate leaves and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
