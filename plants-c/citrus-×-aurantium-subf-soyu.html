Citrus × aurantium subf. soyu

Citrus × aurantium subf.

soyu, commonly known as the sour orange, is a citrus hybrid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, peels and small, acidic fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its botanical features include a rough, segmented fruit peel and a high concentration of essential oils. This subsp.

is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in citrus breeding programs.
