Chlorophytum gramineum var. sciaphilum

Chlorophytum gramineum var. sciaphilum, commonly known as the shade-loving spider plant, is a variant of the spider plant that thrives in lower light conditions.

It features long, arching leaves that are typically a darker green compared to the standard variety. This plant is characterized by its ability to produce offsets, which can be propagated easily through division. It prefers well-draining soil and moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
