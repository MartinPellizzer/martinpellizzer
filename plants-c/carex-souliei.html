Carex souliei

Carex souliei, commonly known as the Souchong sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex souliei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
