Commelina karwinskiana

Commelina karwinskiana, commonly known as the Karwinski's spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.
