Carex winterbottomii

Carex winterbottomii, commonly known as the winterbottom sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Carex winterbottomii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
