Campomanesia ciliata

Campomanesia ciliata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with soft, hairy margins. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible, with a tart flavor, and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
