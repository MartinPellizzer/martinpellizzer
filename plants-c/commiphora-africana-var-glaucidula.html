Commiphora africana var. glaucidula

Commiphora africana var. glaucidula, commonly known as the African myrrh.

This plant is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa, characterized by its aromatic resinous exudates. It has small, elliptical leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow. The plant produces a gum resin, which is used in traditional medicine and incense.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna habitats.
