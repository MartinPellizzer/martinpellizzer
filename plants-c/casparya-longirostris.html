Casparya longirostris

Casparya longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked casparya, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender petals that resemble a beak, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Casparya longirostris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
