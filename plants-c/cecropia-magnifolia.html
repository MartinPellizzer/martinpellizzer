Cecropia magnifolia

Cecropia magnifolia, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and towering height, often reaching up to 40 meters. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically large and have a velvety texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in panicles, and it produces woody fruits containing seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
