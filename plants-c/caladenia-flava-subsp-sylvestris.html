Caladenia flava subsp. sylvestris

Caladenia flava subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in heathland and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant produces a single, slender stem bearing a single, strikingly colored flower with a yellow dorsal sepal and a distinctive, elongated labellum. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by thynnid wasps, which are attracted to its nectar.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
