Citrus × pumila var. myrtifolia

Citrus × pumila var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the dwarf mandarin or myrtle-leaved citron.

This plant is a compact citrus species characterized by its small, rounded leaves that resemble those of myrtle trees. It produces small, fragrant flowers and round, orange-colored fruits that are typically less than an inch in diameter. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and citrus scent.
