Conyza andryaloides

Conyza andryaloides, commonly known as the common fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 100 centimeters, with opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza andryaloides is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
