Colutea perennans

Colutea perennans, commonly known as the perennial cowpea or scarlet boston ivy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Colutea perennans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
