Corydalis montana

Corydalis montana, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Corydalis montana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
