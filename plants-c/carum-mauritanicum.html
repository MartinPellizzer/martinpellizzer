Carum mauritanicum

Carum mauritanicum, commonly known as wild cumin or Moroccan cumin, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, three-lobed fruits. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and cuisine for their pungent, spicy flavor.

Carum mauritanicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
