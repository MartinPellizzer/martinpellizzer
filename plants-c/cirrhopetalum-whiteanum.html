Cirrhopetalum whiteanum

Cirrhopetalum whiteanum, commonly known as the Whiteanum orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers with intricate petal structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
