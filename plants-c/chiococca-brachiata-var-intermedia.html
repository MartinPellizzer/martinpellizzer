Chiococca brachiata var. intermedia

Chiococca brachiata var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian climbing plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
