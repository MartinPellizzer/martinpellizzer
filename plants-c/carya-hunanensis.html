Carya hunanensis

Carya hunanensis, commonly known as the Hunan hickory, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by oval-shaped nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for carpentry and furniture-making.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
