Cola acuminata var. trichandra

Cola acuminata var. trichandra, commonly known as the African cola or kola nut tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant produces distinctive capsule fruits that contain the stimulant-rich seeds used in traditional and commercial contexts. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its economic importance, particularly for the production of kola nuts used in social and cultural practices.
