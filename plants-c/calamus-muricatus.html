Calamus muricatus

Calamus muricatus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its thick, ridged rhizomes that are often used as a starch source. The plant produces tall, slender stems with broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus muricatus is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in wet, tropical environments.
