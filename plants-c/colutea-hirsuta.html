Colutea hirsuta

Colutea hirsuta, commonly known as hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant has small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in woodland areas.
