Campanula glomerata subsp. congesta

Campanula glomerata subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that bear dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticulture.
