Cyphostemma cyphopetalum

Cyphostemma cyphopetalum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thin, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in semi-arid to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
