Convolvulus ensifolius

Convolvulus ensifolius, commonly known as the swordleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often appearing in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Convolvulus ensifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
