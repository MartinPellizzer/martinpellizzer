Cnidoscolus kunthianus

Cnidoscolus kunthianus, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
