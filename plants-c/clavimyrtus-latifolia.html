Clavimyrtus latifolia

Clavimyrtus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Clavimyrtus latifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Australian medicine.
