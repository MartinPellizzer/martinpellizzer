Calochortus gunnisonii var. immaculatus

Calochortus gunnisonii var. immaculatus, commonly known as the Gunnison's mariposa lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
