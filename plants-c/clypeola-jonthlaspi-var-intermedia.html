Clypeola jonthlaspi var. intermedia

Clypeola jonthlaspi var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. This variety is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in temperate regions. The plant is perennial and exhibits a compact growth habit, often thriving in well-drained environments.

Its morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in ecological studies of alpine and arctic flora.
