Conyza disticha

Conyza disticha, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It is commonly found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

Conyza disticha is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
