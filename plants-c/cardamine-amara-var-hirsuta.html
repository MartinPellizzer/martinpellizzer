Cardamine amara var. hirsuta

Cardamine amara var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
