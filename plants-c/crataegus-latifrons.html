Crataegus latifrons

Crataegus latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
