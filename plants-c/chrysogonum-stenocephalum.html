Chrysogonum stenocephalum

Chrysogonum stenocephalum, commonly known as narrow-headed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange composite flowers with a distinctive, narrow head. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert and rocky habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role in pollination and ecological interactions within its native range.
