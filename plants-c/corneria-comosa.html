Corneria comosa

Corneria comosa, commonly known as the hairy corneria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry environments.

Corneria comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
