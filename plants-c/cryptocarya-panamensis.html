Cryptocarya panamensis

Cryptocarya panamensis, commonly known as the Panama cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and has a dense canopy with elliptical, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a woody drupe, containing a single seed, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
