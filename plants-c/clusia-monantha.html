Clusia monantha

Clusia monantha, commonly known as the evergreen glorybower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Clusia monantha is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its tolerance to drought conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
