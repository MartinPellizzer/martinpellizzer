Chamaesyce myrtillifolia

Chamaesyce myrtillifolia, commonly known as the blueberry leaf or myrtillifolia purslane, is a low-growing succulent native to the southeastern United States.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open, sunny areas. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly tart flavor, making it a unique addition to some culinary uses.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
