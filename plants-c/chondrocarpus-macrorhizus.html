Chondrocarpus macrorhizus

Chondrocarpus macrorhizus, commonly known as the giant tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, fibrous roots that form a deep rhizome system. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and can grow up to several meters in height.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in native grasslands and wetlands.
