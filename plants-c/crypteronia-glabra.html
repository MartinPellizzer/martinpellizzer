Crypteronia glabra

Crypteronia glabra, commonly known as the smooth crypteronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Crypteronia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
