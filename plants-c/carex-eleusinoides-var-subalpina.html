Carex eleusinoides var. subalpina

Carex eleusinoides var. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate high altitudes and low temperatures.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
