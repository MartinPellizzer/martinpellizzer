Carex nubigena f. viridans

Carex nubigena f. viridans, commonly known as the green-fruited sedges, is a variant of the sedges native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, elongated, greenish-brown spikelets that distinguish it from other Carex species. This sedge typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as stream banks, marshes, and forest understories. Its leaves are generally narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
