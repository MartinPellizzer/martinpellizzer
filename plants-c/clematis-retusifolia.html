Clematis retusifolia

Clematis retusifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis retusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
