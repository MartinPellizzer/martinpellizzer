Crataegus tatnalliana

Crataegus tatnalliana, commonly known as the Tatnall hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. Its leaves are oval to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it typically grows in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
