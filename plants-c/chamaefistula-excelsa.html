Chamaefistula excelsa

Chamaefistula excelsa, commonly known as the noble ash, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a straight, trunk-like stem with a dense crown of compound leaves. The leaves are pinnately compound, with 10–20 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during spring, followed by small, winged fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
