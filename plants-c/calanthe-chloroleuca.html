Calanthe chloroleuca

Calanthe chloroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-leaved calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking yellow-green in color, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme, and are adorned with intricate patterns.

Calanthe chloroleuca thrives in shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
