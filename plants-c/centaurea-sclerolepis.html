Centaurea sclerolepis

Centaurea sclerolepis, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in dry, rocky or gravelly soils. It is often found in open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and wastelands.

Centaurea sclerolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and visual interest.
