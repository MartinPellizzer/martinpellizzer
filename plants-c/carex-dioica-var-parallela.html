Carex dioica var. parallela

Carex dioica var. parallela, commonly known as the parallel sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
