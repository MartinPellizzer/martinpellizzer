Cytisus atropurpureus

Cytisus atropurpureus, commonly known as purple flame acacia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
