Celtis liukiuensis

Celtis liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries that turn dark purple when mature. Celtis liukiuensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for traditional crafts and construction.
