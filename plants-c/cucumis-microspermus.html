Cucumis microspermus

Cucumis microspermus, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, hairy stems, and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to small, round fruits with a mild, cucumber-like flavor. Cucumis microspermus is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient member of the Cucurbitaceae family.
