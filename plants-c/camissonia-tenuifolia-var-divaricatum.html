Camissonia tenuifolia var. divaricatum

Camissonia tenuifolia var. divaricatum, commonly known as the slenderleaf camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a low-growing habit with stems that often spread horizontally, forming a mat-like structure.

The leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and a slight bluish tint.
