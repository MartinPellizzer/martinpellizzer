Cinnamomum wallichii

Cinnamomum wallichii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The bark, similar to cinnamon, contains essential oils and has been used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
