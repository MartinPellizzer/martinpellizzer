Chloris × subdolichostachya

Chloris × subdolichostachya, commonly known as the long-stemmed grass or hybrid chloris, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, with elongated branches bearing small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
