Carex lanuginosa var. kansana

Carex lanuginosa var. kansana, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its soft, hairy texture and pale green to silver-gray foliage. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.
