Calamus siamensis

Calamus siamensis, commonly known as Siam reed or Siamese calamus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among aroids. Calamus siamensis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
