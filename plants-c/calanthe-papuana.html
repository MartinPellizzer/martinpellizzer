Calanthe papuana

Calanthe papuana, commonly known as the Papuan calanthe, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Papua, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, high-altitude habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in photosynthesis.

Calanthe papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
