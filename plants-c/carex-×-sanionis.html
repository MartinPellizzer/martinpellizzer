Carex × sanionis

Carex × sanionis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Carex brevicollis and Carex sphaerocarpa, resulting in a plant with distinctive morphological features. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are grass-like, with a V-shaped cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets.

Carex × sanionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
