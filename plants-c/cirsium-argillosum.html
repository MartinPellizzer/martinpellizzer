Cirsium argillosum

Cirsium argillosum, commonly known as the clay thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Cirsium argillosum is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
