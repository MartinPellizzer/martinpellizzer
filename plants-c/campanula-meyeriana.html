Campanula meyeriana

Campanula meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and often have a bluish-gray hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula meyeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
