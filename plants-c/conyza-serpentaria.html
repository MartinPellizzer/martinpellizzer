Conyza serpentaria

Conyza serpentaria, commonly known as the slender weed or snake's head, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in moist environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
