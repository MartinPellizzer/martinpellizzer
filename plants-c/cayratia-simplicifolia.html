Cayratia simplicifolia

Cayratia simplicifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by clusters of red, berry-like fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine and is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Cayratia simplicifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
