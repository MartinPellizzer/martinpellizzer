Carduus nutans var. armenus

Carduus nutans var. armenus, commonly known as Armenian thistle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. The plant features a robust taproot and upright stems that are usually covered in coarse, prickly hairs. Its leaves are deeply lobed, with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers in a distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
