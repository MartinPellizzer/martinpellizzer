Crataegus isfajramensis

Crataegus isfajramensis, commonly known as the Isfajram hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it is distinguished by its simple, ovate leaves and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
