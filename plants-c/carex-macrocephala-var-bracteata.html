Carex macrocephala var. bracteata

Carex macrocephala var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with bracts. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinct midrib, while the flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
