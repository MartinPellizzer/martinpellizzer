Caesalpinia angolensis

Caesalpinia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange floral bracts that surround its small yellow flowers, creating a striking visual display. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Caesalpinia angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
