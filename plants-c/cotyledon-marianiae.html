Cotyledon marianiae

Cotyledon marianiae, commonly known as the "Mariana's windowleaf," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. The leaves are typically oval or rhomboid in shape and have a waxy texture that helps the plant retain moisture. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well-suited for dry environments and requires minimal watering to thrive.
