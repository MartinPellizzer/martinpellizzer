Costus phyllocephalus

Costus phyllocephalus, commonly known as the broad-leafed costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with prominent stamen that add to its ornamental appeal.

Costus phyllocephalus is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
