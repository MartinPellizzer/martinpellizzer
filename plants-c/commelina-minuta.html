Commelina minuta

Commelina minuta, commonly known as the dwarf arum or small commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple sheath at the base, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
