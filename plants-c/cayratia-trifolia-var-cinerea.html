Cayratia trifolia var. cinerea

Cayratia trifolia var. cinerea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
