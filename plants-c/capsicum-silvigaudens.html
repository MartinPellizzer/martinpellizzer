Capsicum silvigaudens

Capsicum silvigaudens, commonly known as the forest pepper.

It is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Capsicum silvigaudens is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
