Cerasus laurocerasus var. colchica

Cerasus laurocerasus var. colchica, commonly known as the Turkish cherry or Prunus colchica, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy flowers and berries.
