Commelina foliacea subsp. foliacea

Commelina foliacea subsp.

foliacea, commonly known as the broadleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features striking blue-violet flowers with white markings, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
