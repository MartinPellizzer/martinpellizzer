Cobananthus

Cobananthus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with lance-shaped leaves and a tendency to form dense clumps. Cobananthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular choices for tropical and subtropical regions.
