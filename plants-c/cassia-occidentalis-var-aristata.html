Cassia occidentalis var. aristata

Cassia occidentalis var. aristata, commonly known as the arrowleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into three to five lobes resembling arrowheads, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow-orange inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cassia occidentalis var. aristata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
