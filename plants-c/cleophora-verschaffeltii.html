Cleophora verschaffeltii

Cleophora verschaffeltii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact, herbaceous perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect light, making it a popular houseplant.

Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white, adding visual interest to indoor spaces.
