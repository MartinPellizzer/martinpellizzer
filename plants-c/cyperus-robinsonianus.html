Cyperus robinsonianus

Cyperus robinsonianus, commonly known as the dwarf flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
