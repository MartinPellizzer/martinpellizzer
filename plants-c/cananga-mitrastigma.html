Cananga mitrastigma

Cananga mitrastigma, commonly known as the white champaca, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, emitting a sweet, spicy scent. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties. Its flowers are often used in traditional perfumery and floral arrangements.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves.
