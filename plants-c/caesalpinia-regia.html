Caesalpinia regia

Caesalpinia regia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange feathery flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, elongated leaflets. Its bark is smooth and often features a reddish or brownish color.

Caesalpinia regia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and as a street tree in warm climates.
