Carex yakusimensis

Carex yakusimensis, commonly known as Yakutsk sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of eastern Siberia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ability to thrive in moist and often nutrient-poor environments. This species typically grows in wetlands, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and rivers, where it forms dense, grass-like clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green color, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often inconspicuous.

Carex yakusimensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native habitat.
