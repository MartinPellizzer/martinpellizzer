Cardamine rotundifolia

Cardamine rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf water-cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found growing in rocky or stream-side habitats.

The species is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
