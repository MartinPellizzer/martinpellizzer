Carex globulosa

Carex globulosa, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. The plant features cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is often found in shaded areas.

Carex globulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
