Carex rubrobrunnea

Carex rubrobrunnea, commonly known as the red-bronze sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Carex rubrobrunnea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
