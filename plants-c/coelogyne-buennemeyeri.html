Coelogyne buennemeyeri

Coelogyne buennemeyeri, commonly known as the Buehner's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with fleshy leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant blooms once every few years, typically in the summer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rain, making it well-suited for humid environments.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty and fragrance.
