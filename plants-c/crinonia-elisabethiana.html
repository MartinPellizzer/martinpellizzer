Crinonia elisabethiana

Crinonia elisabethiana, commonly known as the Elizabeth's crinum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
