Crataegus meyeri

Crataegus meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ornamental and ecological purposes.
