Corydalis caseana subsp. aquae-gelidae

Corydalis caseana subsp.

aquae-gelidae, commonly known as the alpine corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It features slender, hairy stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. Corydalis caseana subsp.

aquae-gelidae is well-suited to rocky and alpine soils, thriving in environments with full sun to partial shade.
