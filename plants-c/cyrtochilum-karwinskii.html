Cyrtochilum karwinskii

Cyrtochilum karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has a pseudobulbous structure with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, fragrant flowers during the rainy season. Cyrtochilum karwinskii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
