Coccoloba punctata var. microstachya

Coccoloba punctata var. microstachya, commonly known as the dwarf poke or coastal coccoloba, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, clustered yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and is often found in dune environments.

This species is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.
