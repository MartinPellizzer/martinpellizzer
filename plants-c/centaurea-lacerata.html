Centaurea lacerata

Centaurea lacerata, commonly known as the broken centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Centaurea lacerata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
