Chimonobambusa tumidissinoda

Chimonobambusa tumidissinoda, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a species native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps. The plant has a woody stem that is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a decorative plant. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
