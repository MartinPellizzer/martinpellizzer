Corylus avellana var. divergens

Corylus avellana var. divergens, commonly known as the European hazelnut or filbert, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its bushy growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces catkins and edible nuts that are encased in a cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

The variety divergens is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical European hazelnut.
