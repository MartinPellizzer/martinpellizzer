Crudia subsimplicifolia

Crudia subsimplicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
