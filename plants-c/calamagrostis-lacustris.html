Calamagrostis lacustris

Calamagrostis lacustris, commonly known as the lake reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-white underneath. This plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of lakes and ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish and appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis lacustris plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
