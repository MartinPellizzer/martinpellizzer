Commelina saxosa

Commelina saxosa, commonly known as the rock commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Commelina saxosa is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
