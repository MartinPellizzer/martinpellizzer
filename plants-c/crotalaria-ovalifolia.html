Crotalaria ovalifolia

Crotalaria ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, ovate-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, adding visual appeal to its appearance. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, a common feature among legumes, and the plant is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.

Crotalaria ovalifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
