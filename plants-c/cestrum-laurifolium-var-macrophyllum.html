Cestrum laurifolium var. macrophyllum

Cestrum laurifolium var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its night-blooming nature makes it a unique addition to gardens and landscapes.
