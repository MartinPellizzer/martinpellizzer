Campanula cervicarioides

Campanula cervicarioides, commonly known as the "hairy bellflower," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy glands, which give it its common name. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and also covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula cervicarioides is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
