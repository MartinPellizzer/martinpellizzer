Cucumis melo var. tarra

Cucumis melo var. tarra, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruit with a thin, edible skin and a mild, sweet flavor. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its refreshing fruit, which is used in salads and as a snack.
