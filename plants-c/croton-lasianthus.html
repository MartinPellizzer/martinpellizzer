Croton lasianthus

Croton lasianthus, commonly known as the silver croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
