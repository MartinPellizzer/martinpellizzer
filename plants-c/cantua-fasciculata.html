Cantua fasciculata

Cantua fasciculata, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces slender, hairy stems and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Cantua fasciculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
