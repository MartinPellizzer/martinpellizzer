Chamaecrista acosmifolia var. euryloba

Chamaecrista acosmifolia var. euryloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crown flower, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded environments.
