Carum lacuum

Carum lacuum, commonly known as water cumin, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in moist environments such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Carum lacuum is valued for its medicinal properties, including carminative and digestive benefits.
