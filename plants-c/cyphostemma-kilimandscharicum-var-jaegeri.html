Cyphostemma kilimandscharicum var. jaegeri

Cyphostemma kilimandscharicum var. jaegeri, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Africa.

It features distinctive, waxy, dark green leaves that are often lobed or deeply divided. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid regions.
