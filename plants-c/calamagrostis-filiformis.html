Calamagrostis filiformis

Calamagrostis filiformis, commonly known as slender threadgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairless and have a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Calamagrostis filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
