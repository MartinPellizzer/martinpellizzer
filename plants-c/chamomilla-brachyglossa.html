Chamomilla brachyglossa

Chamomilla brachyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed chamomile, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, including parts of California and Oregon.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
