Carex sinensis

Carex sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It typically grows in shaded, moist areas such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Carex sinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
