Cochlearia groenlandica subsp. islandica

Cochlearia groenlandica subsp.

islandica, commonly known as the Greenlandic sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy, basal leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single umbel.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is part of the Brassicaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
