Centella villosa

Centella villosa, commonly known as hairy centella, is a creeping perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Centella villosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential skin-healing benefits.
