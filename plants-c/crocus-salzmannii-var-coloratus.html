Crocus salzmannii var. coloratus

Crocus salzmannii var. coloratus, commonly known as the Salzmann's crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with a prominent central stigma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, early blooming flowers.
