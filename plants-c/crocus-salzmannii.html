Crocus salzmannii

Crocus salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its early-season blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in mild climates and well-drained conditions.
