Ceanothus riparius

Ceanothus riparius, commonly known as the riparian ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian zones. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Ceanothus riparius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate periodic flooding.
