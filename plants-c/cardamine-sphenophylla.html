Cardamine sphenophylla

Cardamine sphenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a distinctive lobed structure, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting aquatic and terrestrial invertebrates.
