Clerodendrum caulanthum

Clerodendrum caulanthum, commonly known as the climbing clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white in color. These flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
