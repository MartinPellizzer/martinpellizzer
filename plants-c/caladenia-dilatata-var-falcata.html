Caladenia dilatata var. falcata

Caladenia dilatata var. falcata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower spike that bears a single, vividly colored orchid. The flowers typically display a combination of pink, purple, and white hues, with a distinctive, narrow labellum that sets it apart from other orchid varieties. This variety thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
