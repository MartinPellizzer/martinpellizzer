Chrysochosma aliena

Chrysochosma aliena, commonly known as the desert goldpoppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

The species is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
