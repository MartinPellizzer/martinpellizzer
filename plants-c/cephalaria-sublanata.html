Cephalaria sublanata

Cephalaria sublanata, commonly known as the woolly cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, producing small, yellowish to pale green composite flowers.

Cephalaria sublanata is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
