Croton integer

Croton integer, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with white or yellow veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
