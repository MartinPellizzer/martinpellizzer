Chunia

Chunia, commonly known as the bluebell or wild bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Chunia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
