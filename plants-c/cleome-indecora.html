Cleome indecora

Cleome indecora, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in loose inflorescences, which are often surrounded by the colorful bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Cleome indecora is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
