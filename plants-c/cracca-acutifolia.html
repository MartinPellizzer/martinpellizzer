Cracca acutifolia

Cracca acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Cracca acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
