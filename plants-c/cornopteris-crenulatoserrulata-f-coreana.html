Cornopteris crenulatoserrulata f. coreana

Cornopteris crenulatoserrulata f. coreana, commonly known as the Korean rock fern, is a fern species native to eastern Asia, particularly Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its leaves are usually light green and have a distinctive, serrated edge that gives it a rugged appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
