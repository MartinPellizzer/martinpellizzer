Cryptocoryne caudata

Cryptocoryne caudata, commonly known as the long-leaved cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. It is often used in aquascaping due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Cryptocoryne caudata is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions.
