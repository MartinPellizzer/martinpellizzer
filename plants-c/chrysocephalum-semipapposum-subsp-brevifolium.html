Chrysocephalum semipapposum subsp. brevifolium

Chrysocephalum semipapposum subsp.

brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage.

The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
