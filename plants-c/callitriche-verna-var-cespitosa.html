Callitriche verna var. cespitosa

Callitriche verna var. cespitosa, commonly known as early water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, often in still or slow-moving streams, ponds, and ditches. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and appear in early spring.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
