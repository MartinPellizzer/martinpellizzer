Chiritopsis lingchuanensis

Chiritopsis lingchuanensis, commonly known as the Lingchuan dandelion, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
