Colbertia scabrella

Colbertia scabrella, commonly known as the roughleaf colbertia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its intricate and often showy floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a rough texture that gives it its common name.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.
