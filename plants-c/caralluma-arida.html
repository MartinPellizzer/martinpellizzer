Caralluma arida

Caralluma arida, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and peppers, and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red when ripe.

Caralluma arida is also known for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
