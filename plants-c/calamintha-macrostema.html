Calamintha macrostema

Calamintha macrostema, commonly known as large-flowered calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Calamintha macrostema is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
