Calathea concinna

Calathea concinna, commonly known as the "Princess Calathea," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, silver, and pink hues. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns that change subtly in response to light exposure.

Calathea concinna is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
