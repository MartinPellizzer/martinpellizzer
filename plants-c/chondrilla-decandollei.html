Chondrilla decandollei

Chondrilla decandollei, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves and stems. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Chondrilla decandollei plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
