Croton uruguayensis

Croton uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay croton, is a shrub native to South America, specifically Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic plants. The plant features thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive greenish-blue coloration, and its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
