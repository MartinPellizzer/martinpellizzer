Carex melinacra var. changningensis

Carex melinacra var. changningensis, commonly known as the Changning sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves arranged in dense, grass-like clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
