Copaifera confertiflora

Copaifera confertiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that is tapped for its aromatic and medicinal properties. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Copaifera confertiflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
