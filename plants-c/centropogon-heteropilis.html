Centropogon heteropilis

Centropogon heteropilis, commonly known as the variable flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Centropogon heteropilis is adapted to coastal and tropical environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
