Copaifera depilis

Copaifera depilis, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its aromatic bark and resinous sap. The plant produces yellowish flowers and pods that contain dark, elongated seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and pinnately compound, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
