Cerasus furum

Cerasus furum, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, turning vibrant colors in autumn.

Cerasus furum is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value and is a symbol of renewal and beauty in many cultures.
