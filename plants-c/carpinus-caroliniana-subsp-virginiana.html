Carpinus caroliniana subsp. virginiana

Carpinus caroliniana subsp.

virginiana, commonly known as the American hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, spiny fruits that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
