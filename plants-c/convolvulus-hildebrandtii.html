Convolvulus hildebrandtii

Convolvulus hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
