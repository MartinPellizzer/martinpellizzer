Centaurea wolgensis

Centaurea wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgen's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea wolgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
