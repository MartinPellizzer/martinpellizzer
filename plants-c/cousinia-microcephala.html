Cousinia microcephala

Cousinia microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf globular gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in alpine and arctic environments, where it adapts to cold and dry conditions.

Cousinia microcephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
