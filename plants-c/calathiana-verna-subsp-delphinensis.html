Calathiana verna subsp. delphinensis

Calathiana verna subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
