Ceropegia abyssinica var. songeensis

Ceropegia abyssinica var. songeensis, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a climbing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and thin, trailing stems with small, waxy leaves. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its delicate appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rockeries.
