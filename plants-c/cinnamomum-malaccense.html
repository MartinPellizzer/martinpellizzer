Cinnamomum malaccense

Cinnamomum malaccense, commonly known as Malacca cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is used as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its bark is harvested for its inner layer, which contains essential oils and has medicinal properties.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its economic and ornamental value.
