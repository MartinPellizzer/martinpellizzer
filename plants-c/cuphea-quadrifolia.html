Cuphea quadrifolia

Cuphea quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea quadrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
