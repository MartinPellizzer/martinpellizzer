Callista pachyphylla

Callista pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved callista, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Callista pachyphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
