Convolvulus helianthemifolius

Convolvulus helianthemifolius, commonly known as the sunflower leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in the morning. Its leaves are usually lobed and resemble those of the sunflower, giving it its common name.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
