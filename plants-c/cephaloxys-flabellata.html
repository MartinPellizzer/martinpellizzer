Cephaloxys flabellata

Cephaloxys flabellata, commonly known as the fan-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fan-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it suitable for borders or containers.

Cephaloxys flabellata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
