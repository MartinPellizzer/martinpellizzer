Chrysanthemum × aphrodite

Chrysanthemum × aphrodite, commonly known as the Aphrodite chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of different chrysanthemum varieties.

It is characterized by its vibrant flower colors, ranging from white to deep red, and its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing dense clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
