Capparis diversifolia

Capparis diversifolia, commonly known as the variable caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form, with thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis diversifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
