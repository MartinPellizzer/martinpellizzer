Calamagrostis arundinacea var. grandis

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. grandis, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, grass-like perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, with narrow, grassy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces plumes of feathery flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found near waterways, wetlands, and marshy areas.

Its robust growth and ability to stabilize soil make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
