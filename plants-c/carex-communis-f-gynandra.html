Carex communis f. gynandra

Carex communis f. gynandra, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
