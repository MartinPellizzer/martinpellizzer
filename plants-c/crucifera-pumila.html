Crucifera pumila

Crucifera pumila, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, and it produces small, round seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
