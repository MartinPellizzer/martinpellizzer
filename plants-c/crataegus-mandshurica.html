Crataegus mandshurica

Crataegus mandshurica, commonly known as Manchurian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
