Carex eurycarpa var. attenuata

Carex eurycarpa var. attenuata, commonly known as narrow-leaved marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous roots and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are slender and grass-like, with a distinctive tapering shape at the tips.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.
