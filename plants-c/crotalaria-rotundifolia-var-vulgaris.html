Crotalaria rotundifolia var. vulgaris

Crotalaria rotundifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the roundleaf crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its round or oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. It has a taproot system and can grow in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and can be toxic to livestock if ingested.
