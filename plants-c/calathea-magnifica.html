Calathea magnifica

Calathea magnifica, commonly known as the Amazonian cathedral window, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply veined, and intricately patterned leaves that display a vibrant array of colors, including green, silver, and red. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are not only visually appealing but also serve as a means of photosynthesis and moisture retention.

Calathea magnifica requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
