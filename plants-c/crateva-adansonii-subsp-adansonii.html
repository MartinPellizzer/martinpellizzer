Crateva adansonii subsp. adansonii

Crateva adansonii subsp.

adansonii, commonly known as the bladder nut or African shield tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. Its leaves are often used as a vegetable in local cuisines, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
