Cordylanthus palmatus

Cordylanthus palmatus, commonly known as the palmate spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cordylanthus palmatus is often found in California and Oregon, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
