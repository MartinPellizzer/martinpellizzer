Clematis kirilowii var. kirilowii

Clematis kirilowii var. kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilow's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in woodland environments and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
