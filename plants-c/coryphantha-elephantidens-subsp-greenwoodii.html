Coryphantha elephantidens subsp. greenwoodii

Coryphantha elephantidens subsp.

greenwoodii, commonly known as the Greenwood's ghost cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to parts of South America. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features a globular or cylindrical stem with a smooth, greenish-gray surface. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
