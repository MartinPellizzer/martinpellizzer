Caroxylon pulvinatum

Caroxylon pulvinatum, commonly known as the desert willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
