Curcuma gulinqingensis

Curcuma gulinqingensis, commonly known as the Guangxi turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric species. The plant produces striking orange-yellow flowers and has aromatic, rhizome-like roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its ornamental and therapeutic uses.
