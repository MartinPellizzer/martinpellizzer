Chlorocyperus badius

Chlorocyperus badius, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering stems are usually slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Chlorocyperus badius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
