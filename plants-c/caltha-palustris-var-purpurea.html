Caltha palustris var. purpurea

Caltha palustris var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in northern regions.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a purple tinge in the variety. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
