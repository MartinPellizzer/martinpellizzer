Carex slobodovii

Carex slobodovii, commonly known as the Slobodovii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Carex slobodovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
