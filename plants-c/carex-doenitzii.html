Carex doenitzii

Carex doenitzii, commonly known as the Doenitz sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, streambanks, and wet meadows.

Carex doenitzii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
