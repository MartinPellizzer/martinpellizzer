Chorisandra

Chorisandra, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Chorisandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
