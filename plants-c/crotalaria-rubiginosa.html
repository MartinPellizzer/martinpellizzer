Crotalaria rubiginosa

Crotalaria rubiginosa, commonly known as the stinkweed or red root, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive nature make it a significant weed in many ecosystems.
