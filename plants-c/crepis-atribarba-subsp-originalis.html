Crepis atribarba subsp. originalis

Crepis atribarba subsp.

originalis, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish composite flowers and hairy, basal leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold climates. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in open meadows and scree slopes.
