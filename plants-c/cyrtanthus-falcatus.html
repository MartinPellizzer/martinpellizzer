Cyrtanthus falcatus

Cyrtanthus falcatus, commonly known as the sickle lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, upward-facing flowers with recurved petals that resemble sickles, hence its common name. The plant typically blooms in spring, showcasing vivid colors ranging from white to deep red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and unique floral structure make it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
