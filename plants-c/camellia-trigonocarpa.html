Camellia trigonocarpa

Camellia trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China and Vietnam.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant produces elliptical, glossy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Camellia trigonocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
