Campanumoea lanceolata

Campanumoea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and bell-like, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands or along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Campanumoea lanceolata is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas.
