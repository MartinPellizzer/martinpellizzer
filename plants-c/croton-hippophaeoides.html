Croton hippophaeoides

Croton hippophaeoides, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a yellowish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton hippophaeoides is also known for its ability to tolerate dry conditions once established.
