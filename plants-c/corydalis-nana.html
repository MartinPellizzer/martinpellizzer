Corydalis nana

Corydalis nana, commonly known as dwarf corydalis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Corydalis nana is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
