Capsicum cardenasii

Capsicum cardenasii, commonly known as the Cardenas pepper, is a rare and endangered species native to the Sonoran Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous plant with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, yellowish pepper that ripens to red, and it is used in traditional local cuisines.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically threatened and requires conservation efforts to survive.
