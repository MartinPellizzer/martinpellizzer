Centaurea apiculata subsp. spinulosa

Centaurea apiculata subsp.

spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its spiny or bristly leaves and stems, which provide a unique texture. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful blooms.
