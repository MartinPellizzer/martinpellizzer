Corydalis gigantea subsp. multiflora

Corydalis gigantea subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the giant corydalis, is a member of the Fumariaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces numerous seed pods that split open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
