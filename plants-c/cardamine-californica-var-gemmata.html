Cardamine californica var. gemmata

Cardamine californica var. gemmata, commonly known as the gemmate lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique growth habit includes the formation of gemmae, which are vegetative propagules that aid in its spread.
