Cineraria pinnata

Cineraria pinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
