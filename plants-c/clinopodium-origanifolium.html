Clinopodium origanifolium

Clinopodium origanifolium, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stems.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
