Chamaecrista inaguensis

Chamaecrista inaguensis, commonly known as the Inagua burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Chamaecrista inaguensis is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.
