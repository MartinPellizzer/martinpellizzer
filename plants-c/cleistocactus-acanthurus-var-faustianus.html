Cleistocactus acanthurus var. faustianus

Cleistocactus acanthurus var. faustianus, commonly known as the Faustian cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at the top of the stem during the warm season. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in succulent collections.
