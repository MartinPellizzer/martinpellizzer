Clematis komaroviana

Clematis komaroviana, commonly known as the Komarov's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmately divided, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its colorful blooms. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Clematis komaroviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
