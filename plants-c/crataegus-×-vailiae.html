Crataegus × vailiae

Crataegus × vailiae, commonly known as the Vail's hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring. It produces small, red berries in the autumn, which are edible but often tart.

Crataegus × vailiae is native to the United Kingdom and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
