Cardamine leucantha

Cardamine leucantha, commonly known as white watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a waxy coating and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system consists of thin, fibrous roots that help it anchor in wet soil.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its peppery flavor in culinary applications.
