Chrysanthemum carolinianum

Chrysanthemum carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow to white petals and a central disc of yellow florets, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, with uses in traditional herbal remedies.
