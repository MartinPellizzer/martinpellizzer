Cydonia oblonga subsp. integerrima

Cydonia oblonga subsp.

integerrima, commonly known as the European white pear, is a subspecies native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pale green, rough bark and large, ovate leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, aromatic fruits. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, with a smooth, thin skin and a juicy, sweet flesh.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value in gardens.
