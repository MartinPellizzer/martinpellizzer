Carex podperae

Carex podperae, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as alpine meadows and mountain slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that contain its flowers.

Carex podperae is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with poor soil conditions.
