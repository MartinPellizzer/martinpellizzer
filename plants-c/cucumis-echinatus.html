Cucumis echinatus

Cucumis echinatus, commonly known as the spiny cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and borne on separate male and female plants.

The spiny cucumber is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.
