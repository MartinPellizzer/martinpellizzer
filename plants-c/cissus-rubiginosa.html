Cissus rubiginosa

Cissus rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Cissus rubiginosa is also valued for its ability to grow on rocky surfaces, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
