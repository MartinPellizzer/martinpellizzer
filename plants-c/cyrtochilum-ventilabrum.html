Cyrtochilum ventilabrum

Cyrtochilum ventilabrum, commonly known as the ventricle orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with distinctive labellum features. Cyrtochilum ventilabrum thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and specific ecological preferences make it a notable species within its native habitat.
