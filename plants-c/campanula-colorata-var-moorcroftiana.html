Campanula colorata var. moorcroftiana

Campanula colorata var. moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
