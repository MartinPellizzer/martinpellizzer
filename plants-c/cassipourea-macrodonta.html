Cassipourea macrodonta

Cassipourea macrodonta, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cassipourea macrodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
