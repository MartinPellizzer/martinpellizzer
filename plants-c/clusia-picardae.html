Clusia picardae

Clusia picardae, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Clusia picardae is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and air pollution.
