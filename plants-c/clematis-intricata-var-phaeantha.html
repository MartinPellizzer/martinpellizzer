Clematis intricata var. phaeantha

Clematis intricata var. phaeantha, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to yellow, often with a bluish tinge, and are borne in clusters along the stems. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped leaflet at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a climbing plant in gardens.
