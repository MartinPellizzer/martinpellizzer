Cerinthe aspera subsp. gymnandra

Cerinthe aspera subsp.

gymnandra, commonly known as the rough-flowered cerinthe, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, rough hairs.

The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a serrated margin. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, typically blue to purple in color, and are arranged in dense clusters.

This species thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
