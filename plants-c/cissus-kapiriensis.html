Cissus kapiriensis

Cissus kapiriensis, commonly known as the Kapiriensis grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are flexible and can root at the nodes, making it easy to propagate.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
