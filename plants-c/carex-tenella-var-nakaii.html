Carex tenella var. nakaii

Carex tenella var. nakaii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikes and are typically wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
