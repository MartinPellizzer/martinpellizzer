Carex rigida f. paradoxa

Carex rigida f. paradoxa, commonly known as the rigid sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
