Cyphia sylvatica

Cyphia sylvatica, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist climate.

Cyphia sylvatica is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
