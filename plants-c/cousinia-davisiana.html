Cousinia davisiana

Cousinia davisiana, commonly known as the Davis' fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia davisiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
