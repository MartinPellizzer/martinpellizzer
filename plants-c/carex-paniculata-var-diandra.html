Carex paniculata var. diandra

Carex paniculata var. diandra, commonly known as the pink-fruited sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces pinkish to reddish spikelets that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The botanical features of this sedge make it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
