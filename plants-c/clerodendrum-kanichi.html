Clerodendrum kanichi

Clerodendrum kanichi, commonly known as the Japanese flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
