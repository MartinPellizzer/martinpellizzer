Conyza blakei

Conyza blakei, commonly known as white cockleburs, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small white flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are typically hairy and alternate along the stem. Conyza blakei is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides.

It is also known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its wide distribution.
