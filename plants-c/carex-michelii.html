Carex michelii

Carex michelii, commonly known as Michel's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant has fibrous roots and forms dense clumps, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its flowers are usually green or reddish, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.
