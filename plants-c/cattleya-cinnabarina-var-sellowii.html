Cattleya cinnabarina var. sellowii

Cattleya cinnabarina var. sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and red flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it produces multiple inflorescences during the flowering season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses for its striking appearance.
