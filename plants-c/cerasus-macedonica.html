Cerasus macedonica

Cerasus macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red or black berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Cerasus macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in regional horticulture.
