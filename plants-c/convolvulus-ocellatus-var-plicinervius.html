Convolvulus ocellatus var. plicinervius

Convolvulus ocellatus var. plicinervius, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped stems that curl and twist as it grows. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
