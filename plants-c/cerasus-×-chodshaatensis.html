Cerasus × chodshaatensis

Cerasus × chodshaatensis, commonly known as the Korean cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe that ripens to a deep red or black color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
