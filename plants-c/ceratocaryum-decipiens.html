Ceratocaryum decipiens

Ceratocaryum decipiens, commonly known as the false acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
