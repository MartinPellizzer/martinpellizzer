Canangium monospermum

Canangium monospermum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Ghana.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit, which resembles a star, is edible and has a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild flavor. The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
