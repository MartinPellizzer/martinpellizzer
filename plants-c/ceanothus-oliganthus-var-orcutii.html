Ceanothus oliganthus var. orcutii

Ceanothus oliganthus var. orcutii, commonly known as the San Diego cypress, is a shrub native to southern California.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and inland chaparral habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
