Celosia arborescens

Celosia arborescens, commonly known as the flame flower or crested grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames or crests. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by the showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Celosia arborescens is also known for its resilience to drought and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
