Cestrum moritzii

Cestrum moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
