Coelogyne magnifica

Coelogyne magnifica, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, golden-yellow flowers with dark red veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a prized ornamental plant.

Coelogyne magnifica is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
