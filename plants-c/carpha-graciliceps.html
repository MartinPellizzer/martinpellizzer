Carpha graciliceps

Carpha graciliceps, commonly known as slender-headed panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is known for its delicate, airy appearance.

Carpha graciliceps is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
