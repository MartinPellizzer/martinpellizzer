Crucifera rugosa

Crucifera rugosa, commonly known as the rough cabbage, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive cruciform flowers.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has serrated, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, green pods.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
