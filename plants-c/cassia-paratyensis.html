Cassia paratyensis

Cassia paratyensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia paratyensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
