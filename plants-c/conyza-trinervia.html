Conyza trinervia

Conyza trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is an annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across the Americas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with three distinct veins, giving it its common name.

Conyza trinervia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
