Carduus lusitanicus subsp. lusitanicus

Carduus lusitanicus subsp.

lusitanicus, commonly known as the Iberian thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny leaves.

The plant produces purple to pinkish composite flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a resilient species within its native range.
