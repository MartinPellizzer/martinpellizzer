Cardiocrinum mirabile

Cardiocrinum mirabile, commonly known as the noble lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to several feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
