Carex smithii

Carex smithii, commonly known as Smith's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or brown in color.

Carex smithii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
