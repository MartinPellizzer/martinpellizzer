Carica mexicana

Carica mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit that is rich in vitamins and enzymes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
