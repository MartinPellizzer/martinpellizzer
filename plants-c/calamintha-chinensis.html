Calamintha chinensis

Calamintha chinensis, commonly known as Chinese germander, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or pink and bloom in clusters.

Calamintha chinensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive benefits.
