Coffea arabica var. monosperma

Coffea arabica var. monosperma, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee plant, is a variant of the Arabica coffee species.

It is characterized by its small, elongated berries that typically contain a single seed, distinguishing it from other varieties. This plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and consistent rainfall. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and it produces fragrant white flowers.

The beans from this variety are prized for their complex flavor profile and are often used in specialty coffee blends.
