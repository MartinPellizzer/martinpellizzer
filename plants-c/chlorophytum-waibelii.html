Chlorophytum waibelii

Chlorophytum waibelii, commonly known as the Tiger Tooth Plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often marked with striking white or yellow stripes, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, slender stems with clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various conditions make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
