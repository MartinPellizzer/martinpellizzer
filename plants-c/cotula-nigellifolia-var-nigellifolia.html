Cotula nigellifolia var. nigellifolia

Cotula nigellifolia var. nigellifolia, commonly known as the black cumin daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, dark seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent.
