Chloris digitata

Chloris digitata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish to brownish flowers.

Chloris digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
