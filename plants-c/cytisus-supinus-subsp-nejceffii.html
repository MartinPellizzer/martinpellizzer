Cytisus supinus subsp. nejceffii

Cytisus supinus subsp.

nejceffii, commonly known as the creeping yellow broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, stipitate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its persistent, feathery foliage throughout the growing season.
