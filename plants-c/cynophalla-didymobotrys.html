Cynophalla didymobotrys

Cynophalla didymobotrys, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The species is noted for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
