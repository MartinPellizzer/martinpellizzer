Cassinia diminuta

Cassinia diminuta, commonly known as the small cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It produces small, yellow to golden flower heads that are often surrounded by pale green bracts.

Cassinia diminuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
