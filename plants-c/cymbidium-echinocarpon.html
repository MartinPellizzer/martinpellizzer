Cymbidium echinocarpon

Cymbidium echinocarpon, commonly known as the spiny fruit orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and produces large, showy orchid flowers with a distinctive spiky structure on the lip. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing fragrant, pink to purple flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Cymbidium echinocarpon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
