Claytonia ambigua

Claytonia ambigua, commonly known as the common spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cool, temperate climates.
