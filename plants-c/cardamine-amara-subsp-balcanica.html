Cardamine amara subsp. balcanica

Cardamine amara subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan bittercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
