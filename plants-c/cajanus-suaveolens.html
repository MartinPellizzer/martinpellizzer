Cajanus suaveolens

Cajanus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented pigeon pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several oval, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.

Additionally, Cajanus suaveolens plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
