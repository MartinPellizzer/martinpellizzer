Cypripedium × siamense

Cypripedium × siamense, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive greenish-yellow lip and a prominent, recurved spur. The plant typically grows in forest understories, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a fan-like pattern, and it produces a single, upright stem each year.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
