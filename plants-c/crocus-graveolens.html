Crocus graveolens

Crocus graveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in rocky soils and sunny locations. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its pleasant, sweet fragrance.

Crocus graveolens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
