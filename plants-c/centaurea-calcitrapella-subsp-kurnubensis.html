Centaurea calcitrapella subsp. kurnubensis

Centaurea calcitrapella subsp.

kurnubensis, commonly known as the Kurnub centaury, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture that helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in dry gardens and rockeries.
