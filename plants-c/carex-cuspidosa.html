Carex cuspidosa

Carex cuspidosa, commonly known as beak sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream edges. It has narrow, grass-like blades that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Carex cuspidosa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
