Capsella bursa-pastoris f. prostrata

Capsella bursa-pastoris f. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate common chickweed, is a low-growing variant of the common chickweed.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, covered with small, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

This form is adapted to low-light conditions and is often used as a ground cover in horticultural settings.
