Campomanesia costata

Campomanesia costata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in jams and juices.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, while its ecological importance supports various wildlife species.
