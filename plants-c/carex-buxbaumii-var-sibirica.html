Carex buxbaumii var. sibirica

Carex buxbaumii var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as bogs, marshes, and along riverbanks. It produces small, clustered flowers in early summer, which are often surrounded by bracts.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
