Callitriche brutia subsp. naftolskyi

Callitriche brutia subsp.

naftolskyi, commonly known as the Naftolsky's water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows.

The plant has finely divided, floating leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is characterized by its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving water, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
