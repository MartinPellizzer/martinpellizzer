Colubrina cubensis var. floridana

Colubrina cubensis var. floridana, commonly known as Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is typically found in moist, open woodlands. It produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting birds and other wildlife.
