Collinsia sparsiflora var. typica

Collinsia sparsiflora var. typica, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
