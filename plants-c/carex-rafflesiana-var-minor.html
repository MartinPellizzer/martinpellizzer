Carex rafflesiana var. minor

Carex rafflesiana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf ribbon sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for water gardens and bog gardens.
