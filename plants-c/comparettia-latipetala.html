Comparettia latipetala

Comparettia latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petalled orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
