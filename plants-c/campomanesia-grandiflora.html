Campomanesia grandiflora

Campomanesia grandiflora, commonly known as the Natal plum or Brazilian plum, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Campomanesia grandiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
