Calesiam welwitschii

Calesiam welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's calea, is a striking perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calesiam welwitschii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
