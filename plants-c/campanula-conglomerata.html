Campanula conglomerata

Campanula conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Campanula conglomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and borders.

It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
