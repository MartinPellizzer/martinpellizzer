Clematis japonica f. obvallata

Clematis japonica f. obvallata, commonly known as the Japanese climbing vine, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that mature to a dark blue color. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
