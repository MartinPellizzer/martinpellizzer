Coronilla orientalis f. macrantha

Coronilla orientalis f. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered oriental coronilla, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
