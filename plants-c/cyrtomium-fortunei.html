Cyrtomium fortunei

Cyrtomium fortunei, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green to golden in color, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Cyrtomium fortunei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
