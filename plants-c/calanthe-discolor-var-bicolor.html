Calanthe discolor var. bicolor

Calanthe discolor var. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, two-toned flowers that range from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and elegance.
