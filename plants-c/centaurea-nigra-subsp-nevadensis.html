Centaurea nigra subsp. nevadensis

Centaurea nigra subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its striking blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
