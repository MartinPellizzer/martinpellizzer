Camassia leichtlinii subsp. typica

Camassia leichtlinii subsp.

typica, commonly known as the western camas, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are strap-like and grass-like, and it produces bulbous underground storage organs.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
