Calligonum murex

Calligonum murex, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Calligonum murex is also valued for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and prevent soil erosion.
