Chlorophytum anceps

Chlorophytum anceps, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a tropical perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a lighter midrib. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its tolerance to drought. It produces small, white flowers on long, slender stalks during the growing season.

Its air-purifying properties make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
