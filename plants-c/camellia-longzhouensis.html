Camellia longzhouensis

Camellia longzhouensis, commonly known as the Longzhou camellia, is a species native to China, specifically the Guangxi region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and striking, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a waxy texture and a strong fragrance.

Camellia longzhouensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
