Crinum obliquum

Crinum obliquum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Crinum obliquum is also noted for its strong fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and moths.
