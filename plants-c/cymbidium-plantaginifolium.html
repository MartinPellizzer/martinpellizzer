Cymbidium plantaginifolium

Cymbidium plantaginifolium, commonly known as the Japanese cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces tall, branched inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem. This orchid prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is a perennial plant that blooms in late winter to early spring.
