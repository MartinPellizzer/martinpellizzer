Calamagrostis uncinoides

Calamagrostis uncinoides, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, silver-blue blades with a soft, silky texture along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its attractive foliage and adaptability, it is a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
