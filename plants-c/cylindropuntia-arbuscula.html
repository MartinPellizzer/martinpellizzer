Cylindropuntia arbuscula

Cylindropuntia arbuscula, commonly known as the cholla cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless, cylindrical cactus that typically grows in shrubland and desert scrub habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its stems are segmented and can break off easily, allowing the plant to propagate through vegetative means.

Cylindropuntia arbuscula is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system and the ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
