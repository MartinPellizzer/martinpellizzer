Calliandra × cumbucana

Calliandra × cumbucana, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a hybrid leguminous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from red to pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calliandra × cumbucana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
