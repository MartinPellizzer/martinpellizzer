Cardiospermum

Cardiospermum, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Leguminosae.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped seeds, which are encased in a fleshy, inflated pod. The plant typically features climbing stems with opposite leaves and produces showy, pea-like flowers. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

Cardiospermum species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive foliage and unique seed pods.
