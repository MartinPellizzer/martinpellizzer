Cyathogyne bussei

Cyathogyne bussei, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive cyathium inflorescence, which consists of a cup-like structure that houses the flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

Cyathogyne bussei is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
