Cirsium platycephalum

Cirsium platycephalum, commonly known as the broad-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is known for its robust structure and is often found in grasslands and wastelands.
