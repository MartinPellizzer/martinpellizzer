Cnidium mongolicum

Cnidium mongolicum, commonly known as Chinese pepper or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the carrot family. Its rhizomes are the primary part used in traditional medicine, containing various bioactive compounds.

Cnidium mongolicum is valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
