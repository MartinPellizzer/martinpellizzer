Cacalia malabarica

Cacalia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Cacalia malabarica is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
