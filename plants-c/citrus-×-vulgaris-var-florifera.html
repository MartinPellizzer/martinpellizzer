Citrus × vulgaris var. florifera

Citrus × vulgaris var. florifera, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its smooth, yellow-orange peel and juicy, segmented fruit.

It is native to Southeast Asia and has been widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit development is influenced by climate, with warm temperatures and adequate sunlight promoting optimal growth and flavor.

This variety is also valued for its use in aromatherapy and as a source of essential oils.
