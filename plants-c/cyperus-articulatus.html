Cyperus articulatus

Cyperus articulatus, commonly known as the spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Cyperus articulatus is typically found in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
