Croton julopsidium

Croton julopsidium, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that range from ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
