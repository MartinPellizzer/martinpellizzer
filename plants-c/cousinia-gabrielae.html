Cousinia gabrielae

Cousinia gabrielae, commonly known as Gabriel's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy, blue-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cousinia gabrielae is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
