Calospermum mammosum var. ovoidea

Calospermum mammosum var. ovoidea, commonly known as the egg-shaped milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, egg-shaped seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
