Cassia arduinervis var. brevipila

Cassia arduinervis var. brevipila, commonly known as the narrow-veined cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with closely spaced, narrow veins. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a slender, elongated pod that contains several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
