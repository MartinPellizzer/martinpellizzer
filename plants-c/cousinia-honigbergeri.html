Cousinia honigbergeri

Cousinia honigbergeri, commonly known as the Honigberger's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is usually grayish-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Cousinia honigbergeri is often found in rocky soils and is a hardy species that thrives in dry, sunny locations.
