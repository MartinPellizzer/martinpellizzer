Cephalaria leucantha subsp. linearifolia

Cephalaria leucantha subsp.

linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces yellow to orange disc flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
