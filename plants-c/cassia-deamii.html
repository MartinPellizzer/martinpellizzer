Cassia deamii

Cassia deamii, commonly known as the Deam's cassia, is a shrub native to the southern United States, particularly Texas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Cassia deamii is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It has been used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
