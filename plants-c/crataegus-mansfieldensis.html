Crataegus mansfieldensis

Crataegus mansfieldensis, commonly known as Mansfield hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream valleys. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
