Cerasus laurocerasus var. rotundifolia

Cerasus laurocerasus var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the cherry laurel or Prunus laurocerasus, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically 4–8 inches long. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark red, edible drupes. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6–9 and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The plant is also noted for its strong scent and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
