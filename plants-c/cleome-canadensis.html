Cleome canadensis

Cleome canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian cleome, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleome canadensis is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
