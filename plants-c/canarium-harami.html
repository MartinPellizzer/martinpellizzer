Canarium harami

Canarium harami, commonly known as the Malayan canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

Canarium harami is also valued for its hard, durable wood in local crafts and construction.
