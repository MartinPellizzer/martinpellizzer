Corallodendron suberifera

Corallodendron suberifera, commonly known as the thick-leaved coral tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its bark is thick and cork-like, contributing to its hardy nature.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
