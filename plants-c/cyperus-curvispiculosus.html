Cyperus curvispiculosus

Cyperus curvispiculosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
