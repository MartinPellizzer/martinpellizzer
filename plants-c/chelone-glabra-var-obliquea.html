Chelone glabra var. obliquea

Chelone glabra var. obliquea, commonly known as the oblique hound's tongue, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often wavy or notched along the edges. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its unique leaf shape and ornamental flowers make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
