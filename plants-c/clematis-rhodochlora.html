Clematis rhodochlora

Clematis rhodochlora, commonly known as the red-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically a striking shade of red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis rhodochlora is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
