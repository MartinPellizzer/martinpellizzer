Calea oliveri

Calea oliveri, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a short, upright stem with hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Calea oliveri is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in indigenous communities.
