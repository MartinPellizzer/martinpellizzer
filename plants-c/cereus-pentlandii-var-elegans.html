Cereus pentlandii var. elegans

Cereus pentlandii var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure. The plant produces striking, white, night-blooming flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
