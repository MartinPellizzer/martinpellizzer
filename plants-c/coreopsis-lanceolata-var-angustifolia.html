Coreopsis lanceolata var. angustifolia

Coreopsis lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf tickseed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long and have a rough texture. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and ray florets that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for attracting bees, butterflies, and other beneficial insects.
