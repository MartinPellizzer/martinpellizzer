Cardamine dalmatica

Cardamine dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery flavor.

It produces small white or yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
