Cattleya × amanda

Cattleya × amanda, commonly known as the Amanda orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya × amanda thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
