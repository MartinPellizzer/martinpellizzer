Cleistochloa uncinulata

Cleistochloa uncinulata, commonly known as the hookleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, scarcely visible flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Cleistochloa uncinulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping due to its dense, upright growth.
