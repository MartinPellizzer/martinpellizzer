Cestrum diurnum var. marcianum

Cestrum diurnum var. marcianum, commonly known as the golden nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its fruit is a fleshy capsule containing several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
