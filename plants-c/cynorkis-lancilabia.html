Cynorkis lancilabia

Cynorkis lancilabia, commonly known as the long-labiated cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its leaves are long and narrow, often reaching several meters in length.

This cycad is native to specific regions in Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
