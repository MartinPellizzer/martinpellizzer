Cymbopogon pilosovaginatus

Cymbopogon pilosovaginatus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky oat grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats. It produces small, clustered inflorescences and is known for its aromatic properties.

Cymbopogon pilosovaginatus is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for its potential aromatic and medicinal benefits.
