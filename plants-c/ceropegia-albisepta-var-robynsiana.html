Ceropegia albisepta var. robynsiana

Ceropegia albisepta var. robynsiana, commonly known as the Robyn's moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique, intricate structure. The leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
