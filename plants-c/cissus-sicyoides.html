Cissus sicyoides

Cissus sicyoides, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
