Chromatopogon

Chromatopogon, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. Switchgrass is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates. It is widely used for biofuel production due to its high biomass yield and low input requirements.

Additionally, it plays an important role in ecological restoration and soil conservation.
