Camellia capitata

Camellia capitata, commonly known as the Sicilian camellia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and provide a dense canopy. Camellia capitata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its blooming period occurs in late winter to early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens during the colder months.
