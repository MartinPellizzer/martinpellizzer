Chrysothamnus bloomeri var. angustatus

Chrysothamnus bloomeri var. angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its drought tolerance.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
