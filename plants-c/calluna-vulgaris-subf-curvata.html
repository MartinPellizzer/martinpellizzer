Calluna vulgaris subf. curvata

Calluna vulgaris subf.

curvata, commonly known as the curled heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

This sub-species is often found in heathland and moorland habitats, where it plays a key role in maintaining ecological balance.
