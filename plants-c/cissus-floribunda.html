Cissus floribunda

Cissus floribunda, commonly known as the climbing fig or silver vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often used as a decorative houseplant. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

Cissus floribunda is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions and is relatively easy to care for.
