Campanula hispida

Campanula hispida, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Campanula hispida is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
