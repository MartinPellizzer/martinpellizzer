Coronilla fischeri

Coronilla fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil and drought.

Coronilla fischeri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
