Cirsium ruderale

Cirsium ruderale, commonly known as the field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, while its purple or pink flowers bloom in late summer.

Cirsium ruderale is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
