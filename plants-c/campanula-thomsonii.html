Campanula thomsonii

Campanula thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula thomsonii is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
