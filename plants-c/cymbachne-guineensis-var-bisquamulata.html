Cymbachne guineensis var. bisquamulata

Cymbachne guineensis var. bisquamulata, commonly known as the Guinea grass variant, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, two-ranked flowers.

This variety is characterized by its distinct leaf sheath and the presence of two distinct scales on the lemma, giving it its specific epithet.
