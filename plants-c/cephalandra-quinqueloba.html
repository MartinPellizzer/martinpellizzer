Cephalandra quinqueloba

Cephalandra quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its foliage is not only visually appealing but also has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
