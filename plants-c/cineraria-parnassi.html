Cineraria parnassi

Cineraria parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian cineraria, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of thick, hairy leaves that help retain moisture in arid conditions.

Cineraria parnassi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
