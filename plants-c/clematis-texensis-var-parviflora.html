Clematis texensis var. parviflora

Clematis texensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the Texas clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are woody and twining. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
