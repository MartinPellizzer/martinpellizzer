Carex mollicula var. naipiangensis

Carex mollicula var. naipiangensis, commonly known as the Naipiang sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
