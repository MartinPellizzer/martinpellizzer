Cyperus ranko

Cyperus ranko, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus ranko plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
