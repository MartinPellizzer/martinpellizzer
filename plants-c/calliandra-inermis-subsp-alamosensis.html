Calliandra inermis subsp. alamosensis

Calliandra inermis subsp.

alamosensis, commonly known as the Alamos calliandra, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compound, pinnately arranged leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it an important resource for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
