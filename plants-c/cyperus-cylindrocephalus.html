Cyperus cylindrocephalus

Cyperus cylindrocephalus, commonly known as the slender flathead sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its small, three-lobed flowers are usually clustered in dense, rounded spikes.

Cyperus cylindrocephalus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
