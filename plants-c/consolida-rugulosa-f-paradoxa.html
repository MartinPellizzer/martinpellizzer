Consolida rugulosa f. paradoxa

Consolida rugulosa f. paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical sweet peas, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking, bicolor flowers that typically feature a mix of white and violet hues.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage is finely divided, adding a delicate texture to its overall appearance.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique color patterns.
