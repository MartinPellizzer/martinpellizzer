Citharexylum amabile

Citharexylum amabile, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features fragrant, white or pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Citharexylum amabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual interest.
