Ceropegia sinuata

Ceropegia sinuata, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia sinuata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
