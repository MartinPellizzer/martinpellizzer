Cirsium fauriei

Cirsium fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. It has a rosette of basal leaves that form a dense cluster, with the flowering stems rising above them.

Cirsium fauriei is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and forest edges.
