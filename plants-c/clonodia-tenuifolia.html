Clonodia tenuifolia

Clonodia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clonodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in width, giving it its species name "tenuifolia.". The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
