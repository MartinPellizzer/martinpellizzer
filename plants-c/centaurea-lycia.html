Centaurea lycia

Centaurea lycia, commonly known as Lydian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bracteate flower heads.

Centaurea lycia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
