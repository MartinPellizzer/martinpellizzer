Correa cardinalis

Correa cardinalis, commonly known as the scarlet correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Correa cardinalis is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
