Cardamine heterophylla var. uniflora

Cardamine heterophylla var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered water crowfoot, is a herbaceous plant native to moist, calcareous habitats.

It typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive basal rosette and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are borne on a single, terminal stem, giving it its varietal name "uniflora.".

The species is characterized by its delicate foliage and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
