Croton sonorae

Croton sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran croton, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert region of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that vary in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Croton sonorae is often found in rocky areas and is an important component of the desert flora.
