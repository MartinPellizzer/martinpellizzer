Crataegus ambitiosa

Crataegus ambitiosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Crataegus ambitiosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for various species.
