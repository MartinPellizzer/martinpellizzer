Cuphea epilobiifolia var. costaricensis

Cuphea epilobiifolia var. costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
