Carex longebracteata f. angustifolia

Carex longebracteata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green to brown in color, typical of the Carex genus. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
