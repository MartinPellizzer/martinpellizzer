Calyptridium parryi var. hesseae

Calyptridium parryi var. hesseae, commonly known as the Hesse's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its resilience to arid conditions and is often found in desert and chaparral ecosystems.
