Calanthe flava

Calanthe flava, commonly known as the yellow calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring well-drained soil. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Calanthe flava is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
