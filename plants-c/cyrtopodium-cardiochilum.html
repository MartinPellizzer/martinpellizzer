Cyrtopodium cardiochilum

Cyrtopodium cardiochilum, commonly known as the heart-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive heart-shaped lip. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white with a contrasting purple or red lip.

Cyrtopodium cardiochilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
