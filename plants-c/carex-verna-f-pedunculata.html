Carex verna f. pedunculata

Carex verna f. pedunculata, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

The foliage is often bright green, with a slightly bluish tinge, adding aesthetic value to its natural setting.
