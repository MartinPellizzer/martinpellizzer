Chimonocalamus auriculatus

Chimonocalamus auriculatus, commonly known as the golden bamboo or auriculate bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a fine, golden-brown sheath. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Chimonocalamus auriculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
