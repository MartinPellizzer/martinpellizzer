Capsicum cerasiflorum

Capsicum cerasiflorum, commonly known as the purple bell pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and oblong, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a short, hairy stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which have a mild, sweet flavor.
