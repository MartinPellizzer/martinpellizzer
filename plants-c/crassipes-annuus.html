Crassipes annuus

Crassipes annuus, commonly known as the common water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has dark green, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern and produce showy purple flowers. The plant is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of water conditions, including stagnant and slow-moving waters. It is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats covering water surfaces.

This growth can significantly impact water quality and aquatic ecosystems.
