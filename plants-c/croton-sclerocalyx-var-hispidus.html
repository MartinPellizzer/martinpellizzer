Croton sclerocalyx var. hispidus

Croton sclerocalyx var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
