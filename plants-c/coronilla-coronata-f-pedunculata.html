Coronilla coronata f. pedunculata

Coronilla coronata f. pedunculata, commonly known as the pedunculate crown vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in soil enrichment. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its stems are often hairy, and the flowers are arranged in racemes, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is also used in erosion control due to its extensive root system and vigorous growth.
