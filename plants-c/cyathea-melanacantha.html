Cyathea melanacantha

Cyathea melanacantha, commonly known as the black-stem tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its dark, almost black stem is a distinguishing feature, while its fronds are typically a deep green.

Cyathea melanacantha is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
