Cissampelopsis corymbosa

Cissampelopsis corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered cissampelopsis, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
