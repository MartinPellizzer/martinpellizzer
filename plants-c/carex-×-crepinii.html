Carex × crepinii

Carex × crepinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Carex × crepinii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
