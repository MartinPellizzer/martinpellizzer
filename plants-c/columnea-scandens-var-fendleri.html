Columnea scandens var. fendleri

Columnea scandens var. fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a vigorous climber with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that are highly attractive to hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative vine in tropical gardens.
