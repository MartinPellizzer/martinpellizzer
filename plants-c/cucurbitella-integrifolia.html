Cucurbitella integrifolia

Cucurbitella integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It produces small, pale green fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with smooth margins, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowering pattern includes both male and female flowers on the same plant, typical of cucurbits.

The species is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is adapted to warm, sunny environments.
