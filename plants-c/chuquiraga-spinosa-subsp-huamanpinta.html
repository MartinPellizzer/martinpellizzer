Chuquiraga spinosa subsp. huamanpinta

Chuquiraga spinosa subsp.

huamanpinta, commonly known as the Huamanpinta burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, silver-green foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
