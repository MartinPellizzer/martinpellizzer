Carduus altissimus

Carduus altissimus, commonly known as the greater fennel or tall thistle, is a tall biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, silver-green, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by spiny, spherical seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.
