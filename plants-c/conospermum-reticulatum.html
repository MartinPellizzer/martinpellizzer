Conospermum reticulatum

Conospermum reticulatum, commonly known as the honeycomb bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, honeycomb-like bark that peels in papery layers, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Conospermum reticulatum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
