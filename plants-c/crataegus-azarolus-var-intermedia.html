Crataegus azarolus var. intermedia

Crataegus azarolus var. intermedia, commonly known as the Spanish whitethorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits called pomes, which are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean scrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
