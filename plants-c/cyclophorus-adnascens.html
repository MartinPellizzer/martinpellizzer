Cyclophorus adnascens

Cyclophorus adnascens, commonly known as the creeping cyclophorus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand periods of drought.

Cyclophorus adnascens is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it forms dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.
