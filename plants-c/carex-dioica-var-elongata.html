Carex dioica var. elongata

Carex dioica var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed sedges, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
