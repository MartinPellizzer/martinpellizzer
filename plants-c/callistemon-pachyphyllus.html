Callistemon pachyphyllus

Callistemon pachyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
