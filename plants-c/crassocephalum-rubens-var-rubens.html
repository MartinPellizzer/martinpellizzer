Crassocephalum rubens var. rubens

Crassocephalum rubens var. rubens, commonly known as the red-headed grass or red-headed panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, reddish bracts that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its success in a variety of environmental conditions.
