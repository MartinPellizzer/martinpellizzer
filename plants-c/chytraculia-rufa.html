Chytraculia rufa

Chytraculia rufa, commonly known as the red-spored chytrid, is a type of aquatic fungus that belongs to the phylum Chytridiomycota.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish spores, which are released into the water to propagate the species. This organism typically inhabits freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist, organic-rich substrates. Chytraculia rufa plays a role in the decomposition of organic matter, contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems.

Its simple structure and rapid reproduction make it an important component of microbial communities in water bodies.
