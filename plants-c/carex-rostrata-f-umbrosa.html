Carex rostrata f. umbrosa

Carex rostrata f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy sedges, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive rosette formation at the base.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
