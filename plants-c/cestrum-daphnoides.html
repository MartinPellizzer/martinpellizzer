Cestrum daphnoides

Cestrum daphnoides, commonly known as the white angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has smooth, aromatic leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
