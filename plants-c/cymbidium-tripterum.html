Cymbidium tripterum

Cymbidium tripterum, commonly known as the long-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cymbidium tripterum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
