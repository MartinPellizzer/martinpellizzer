Cotoneaster alaunicus f. pluriflorus

Cotoneaster alaunicus f. pluriflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered cotoneaster, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense growth habit and seasonal color changes.

Cotoneaster alaunicus f. pluriflorus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
