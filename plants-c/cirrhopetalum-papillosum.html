Cirrhopetalum papillosum

Cirrhopetalum papillosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with intricate, velvety petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in cultivation.
