Carex macrogyna var. brevipedunculata

Carex macrogyna var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stemmed marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This variety typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is often found in temperate regions of North America and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
