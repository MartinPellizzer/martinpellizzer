Capparis sessilis var. securidacea

Capparis sessilis var. securidacea, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small lantern. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers with five petals and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its fruit, which turns orange-red when ripe, is edible and has a tangy, sweet flavor.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary applications.
