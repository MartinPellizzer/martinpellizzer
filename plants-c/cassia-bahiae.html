Cassia bahiae

Cassia bahiae, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that resemble cascading showers, hence its common name. The plant produces small, flat, green pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia bahiae is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
