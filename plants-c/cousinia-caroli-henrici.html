Cousinia caroli-henrici

Cousinia caroli-henrici, commonly known as the Caroli-Henrici's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia caroli-henrici is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
