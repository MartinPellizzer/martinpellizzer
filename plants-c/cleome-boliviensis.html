Cleome boliviensis

Cleome boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescences that consist of small, white or yellow flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cleome boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
