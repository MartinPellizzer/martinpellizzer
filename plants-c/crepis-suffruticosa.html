Crepis suffruticosa

Crepis suffruticosa, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas.

Crepis suffruticosa is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
