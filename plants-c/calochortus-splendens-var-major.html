Calochortus splendens var. major

Calochortus splendens var. major, commonly known as the showy globemallow, is a striking flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring full sun and well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seed pods that are spherical and segmented.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, long-lasting blooms.
