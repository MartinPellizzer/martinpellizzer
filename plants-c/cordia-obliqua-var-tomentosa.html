Cordia obliqua var. tomentosa

Cordia obliqua var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical areas.
