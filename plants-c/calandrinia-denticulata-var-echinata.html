Calandrinia denticulata var. echinata

Calandrinia denticulata var. echinata, commonly known as the spiked purslane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

The species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
