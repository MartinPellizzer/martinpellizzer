Cirsium erisithales

Cirsium erisithales, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
