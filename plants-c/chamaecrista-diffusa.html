Chamaecrista diffusa

Chamaecrista diffusa, commonly known as the coastal goldenball, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy coastal plant. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Chamaecrista diffusa is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its erosion control capabilities and ability to stabilize dunes.
