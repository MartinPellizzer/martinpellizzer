Cavendishia alata

Cavendishia alata, commonly known as the feathered cactus, is a unique plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often adorned with long, feathery spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
