Cyrtochilum cordatum

Cyrtochilum cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique heart-shaped pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cyrtochilum cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
