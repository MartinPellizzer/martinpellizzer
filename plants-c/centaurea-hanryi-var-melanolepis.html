Centaurea hanryi var. melanolepis

Centaurea hanryi var. melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with the lower leaves often displaying a distinctive black or dark purple coloration. This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
