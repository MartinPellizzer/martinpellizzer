Cassia intermedia

Cassia intermedia, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warmer climates.

Cassia intermedia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
