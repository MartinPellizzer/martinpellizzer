Convolvulus carnosus

Convolvulus carnosus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and leaves, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, often appearing in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus carnosus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habit.
