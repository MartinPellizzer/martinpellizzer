Cyperus melanostachyus

Cyperus melanostachyus, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of dark purple to black spikelets, which are its distinguishing floral features. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus melanostachyus is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of thatching material in some cultures.
