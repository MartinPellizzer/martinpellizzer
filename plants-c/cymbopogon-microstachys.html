Cymbopogon microstachys

Cymbopogon microstachys, commonly known as lemon grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, aromatic leaves that emit a distinct lemony scent. The plant is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are extracted from the rhizomes and lower stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Lemon grass is also valued for its use in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
