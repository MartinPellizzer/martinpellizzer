Chiliotrichum kingii

Chiliotrichum kingii, commonly known as the King's hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant features fine, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Chiliotrichum kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
