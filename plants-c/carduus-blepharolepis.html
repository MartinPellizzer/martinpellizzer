Carduus blepharolepis

Carduus blepharolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, and it typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple to pinkish flower heads that are composed of numerous tiny florets. It is characterized by its narrow, often hairy leaves and a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The plant is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
