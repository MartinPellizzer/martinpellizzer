Cuspidaria cordata

Cuspidaria cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaved cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage in its native environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. Its structure is similar to other cacti, with a stem that may branch out and bear spines for protection.

Despite its cactus-like appearance, it is classified within the family Cactaceae, sharing traits with other desert plants.
