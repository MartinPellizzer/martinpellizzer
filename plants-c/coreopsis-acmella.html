Coreopsis acmella

Coreopsis acmella, commonly known as the tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis acmella is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
