Chorisanthera pumila

Chorisanthera pumila, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
