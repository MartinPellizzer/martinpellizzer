Crassula muscosa

Crassula muscosa, commonly known as the wooly crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, allowing the plant to thrive in arid conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental species due to its unique texture and low maintenance requirements.
