Castalia pubescens

Castalia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy castalia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Castalia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
