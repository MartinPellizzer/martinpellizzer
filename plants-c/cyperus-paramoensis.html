Cyperus paramoensis

Cyperus paramoensis, commonly known as the Andean flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude grasslands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus paramoensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
