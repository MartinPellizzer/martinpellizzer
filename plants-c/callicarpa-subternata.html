Callicarpa subternata

Callicarpa subternata, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
