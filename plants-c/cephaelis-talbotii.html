Cephaelis talbotii

Cephaelis talbotii, commonly known as the pink flame or talbot's cephaelis, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cephaelis talbotii is valued for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
