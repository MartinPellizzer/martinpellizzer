Chrysanthemum leucanthemum margaritae

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum margaritae, commonly known as the margaret daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow disk, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
