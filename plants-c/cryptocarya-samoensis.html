Cryptocarya samoensis

Cryptocarya samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially vulnerable species.
