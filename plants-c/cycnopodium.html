Cycnopodium

Cycnopodium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Cycnopodium species are often found in the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable ornamental plant.
