Convolvulus malvaceus

Convolvulus malvaceus, commonly known as the Malvaceae convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
