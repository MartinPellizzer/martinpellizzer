Calamagrostis lagurus

Calamagrostis lagurus, commonly known as the silvergrass or silver reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis lagurus is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
