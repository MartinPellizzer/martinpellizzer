Crepis biennis var. leptophylla

Crepis biennis var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in grassy and disturbed habitats. The plant has a short, stout stem and produces seeds in a capsule.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it plays a role in pollinator support.
