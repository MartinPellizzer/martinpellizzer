Croton gardneri var. cordatus

Croton gardneri var. cordatus, commonly known as the cordate croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
