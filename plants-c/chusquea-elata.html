Chusquea elata

Chusquea elata, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
