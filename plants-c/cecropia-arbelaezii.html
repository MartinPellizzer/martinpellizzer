Cecropia arbelaezii

Cecropia arbelaezii, commonly known as the Andean ceiba, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to the highlands of South America.

It is a large, deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its buttressed roots and smooth, greyish bark. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a cottony fiber, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays an important role in the ecosystems of the Andes.
