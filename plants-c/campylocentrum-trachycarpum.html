Campylocentrum trachycarpum

Campylocentrum trachycarpum, commonly known as the rough-seeded hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to red. The plant has rough, ridged leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Campylocentrum trachycarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
