Cirsium scleranthum

Cirsium scleranthum, commonly known as the hardstem thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny, rough leaves that are typically alternate and can be lanceolate or ovate in shape. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing purple to pinkish composite flowers.

Cirsium scleranthum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
