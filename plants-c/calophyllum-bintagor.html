Calophyllum bintagor

Calophyllum bintagor, commonly known as the Bintagor calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
