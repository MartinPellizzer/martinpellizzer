Capsicum villosum

Capsicum villosum, commonly known as the woolly capsicum, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically covered in fine hairs. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique morphology, it is sometimes used in horticultural studies for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
