Citrosma polyantha

Citrosma polyantha, commonly known as the Malayan citron tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, round, and somewhat similar in appearance to citrus fruits, though they are not typically consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
