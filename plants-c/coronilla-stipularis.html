Coronilla stipularis

Coronilla stipularis, commonly known as the stipular coronilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules, which are small, leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks. Its flowers are small and pea-like, usually appearing in clusters, and are typically pink or purple in color.

Coronilla stipularis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny conditions.
