Cleome kotschyana

Cleome kotschyana, commonly known as the "Mexican spider flower," is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking, spider-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy and branched. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance blooms.

Cleome kotschyana is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
