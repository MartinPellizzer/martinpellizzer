Ceropegia paricyma

Ceropegia paricyma, commonly known as the Cape carnation, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia paricyma is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ease of care.
