Cordylanthus rigidus subsp. rigidus

Cordylanthus rigidus subsp.

rigidus, commonly known as the rigid thornweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to California. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, showy flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is characterized by its rigid, upright growth habit and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range.
