Clarkia mosquinii subsp. xerophila

Clarkia mosquinii subsp.

xerophila, commonly known as the Mojave Clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its striking pink to red flowers and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system to access water and reduce water loss through its small, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky habitats or open plains.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
