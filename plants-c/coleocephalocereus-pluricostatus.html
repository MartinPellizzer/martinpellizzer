Coleocephalocereus pluricostatus

Coleocephalocereus pluricostatus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange spines and a globular, segmented stem. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
