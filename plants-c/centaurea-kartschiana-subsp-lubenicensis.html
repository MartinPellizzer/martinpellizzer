Centaurea kartschiana subsp. lubenicensis

Centaurea kartschiana subsp.

lubenicensis, commonly known as the Lubenician cornflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are typically surrounded by bright yellow ray florets.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
