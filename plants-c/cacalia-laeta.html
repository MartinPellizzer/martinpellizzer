Cacalia laeta

Cacalia laeta, commonly known as the cheerful bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cacalia laeta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
