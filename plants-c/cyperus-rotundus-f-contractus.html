Cyperus rotundus f. contractus

Cyperus rotundus f. contractus, commonly known as shattergrass or sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, triangular stems and small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense, spike-like structures, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its aggressive growth and adaptability.
