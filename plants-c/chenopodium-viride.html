Chenopodium viride

Chenopodium viride, commonly known as green goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium viride is also valued for its nutritional content, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.
