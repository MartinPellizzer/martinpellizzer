Carex × langeana

Carex × langeana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and triangular stems. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
