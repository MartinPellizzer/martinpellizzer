Croton griseus

Croton griseus, commonly known as the gray croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that range from gray-green to deep green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
