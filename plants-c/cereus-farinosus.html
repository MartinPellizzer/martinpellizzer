Cereus farinosus

Cereus farinosus, commonly known as the powdered cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem covered in white, powdery spines, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and bloom at night.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
