Clitoria cordiformis

Clitoria cordiformis, commonly known as the heartleaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Clitoria cordiformis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
