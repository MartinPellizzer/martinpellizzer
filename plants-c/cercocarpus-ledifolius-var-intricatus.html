Cercocarpus ledifolius var. intricatus

Cercocarpus ledifolius var. intricatus, commonly known as the Sierra Valley curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its twisted, curling leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
