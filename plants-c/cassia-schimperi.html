Cassia schimperi

Cassia schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian cinnamon, is a deciduous shrub native to Ethiopia and Eritrea.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Cassia schimperi is valued for its aromatic bark and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
