Cereus adelmarii

Cereus adelmarii, commonly known as the Adelmarii Cereus, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, solitary plant that typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and produces white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom at night. It has a shallow root system adapted to the arid environment of its native habitat.

The plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xerophytic landscapes due to its drought tolerance.
