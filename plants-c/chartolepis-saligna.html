Chartolepis saligna

Chartolepis saligna, commonly known as the silky greenbark, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features smooth, light green bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall.

Chartolepis saligna is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
