Caucalis marilandica

Caucalis marilandica, commonly known as Maryland spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 6 to 12 inches. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking feature is the clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
