Cardamine anemonoides f. major

Cardamine anemonoides f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic habitats, such as marshes, stream sides, and wet meadows. The plant is often found in shaded, damp environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
