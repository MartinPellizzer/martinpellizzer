Crowea angustifolia var. platyphylla

Crowea angustifolia var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved crowea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in terminal clusters, and attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
