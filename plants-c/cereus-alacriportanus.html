Cereus alacriportanus

Cereus alacriportanus, commonly known as the Brazilian night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The plant is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and is often grown as an ornamental species.
