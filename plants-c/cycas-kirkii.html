Cycas kirkii

Cycas kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's cycad, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces both male and female cones, with the female cones bearing a distinctive red, fleshy aril that encases the seed. Cycas kirkii is a slow-growing plant that can live for centuries, making it a valuable component of its native ecosystem.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
