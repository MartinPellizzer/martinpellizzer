Cassia inaguensis

Cassia inaguensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia inaguensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
