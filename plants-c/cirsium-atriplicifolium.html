Cirsium atriplicifolium

Cirsium atriplicifolium, commonly known as the blackleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive dark green to almost black coloration.

The flowers are usually arranged in a dense cluster and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
