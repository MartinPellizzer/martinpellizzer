Clematis zygophylla

Clematis zygophylla, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include small, greenish sepals and five petals that are often tinged with red or purple.

Clematis zygophylla thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
