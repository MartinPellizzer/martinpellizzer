Chamaesyce serpens var. montevidensis

Chamaesyce serpens var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidensian sandmat, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in dry climates.
