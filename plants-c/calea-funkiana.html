Calea funkiana

Calea funkiana, commonly known as the sacred flower or angel's trumpet, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional practices.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
