Cyperus nemacaulos

Cyperus nemacaulos, commonly known as narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it thrives in wetland or marshy environments.

Cyperus nemacaulos is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
