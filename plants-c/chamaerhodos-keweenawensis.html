Chamaerhodos keweenawensis

Chamaerhodos keweenawensis, commonly known as the Keweenaw bog rosemary, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in acidic, moist soils and is often found in bogs and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
