Croton domingensis

Croton domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican croton, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with distinctive latex-producing properties. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
