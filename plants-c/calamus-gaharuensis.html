Calamus gaharuensis

Calamus gaharuensis, commonly known as the Java sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental foliage. Calamus gaharuensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
