Cavanilla umbellata

Cavanilla umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella cavanilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking, umbel-like inflorescence composed of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy, often pink or red flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Cavanilla umbellata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
