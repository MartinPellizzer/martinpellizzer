Clematis wightiana

Clematis wightiana, commonly known as the Himalayan clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal flowering.

Clematis wightiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
