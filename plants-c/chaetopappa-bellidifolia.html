Chaetopappa bellidifolia

Chaetopappa bellidifolia, commonly known as the blueweed or bluegrass.

This flowering plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss.
