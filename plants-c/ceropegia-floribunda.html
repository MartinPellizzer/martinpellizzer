Ceropegia floribunda

Ceropegia floribunda, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It is known for its ability to thrive in semi-shade and well-drained soil conditions.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
