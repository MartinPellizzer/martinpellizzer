Copaiba arnoldiana

Copaiba arnoldiana, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by orange-yellow, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its sap, known as copaiba balsam, is widely used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.

The plant plays an important ecological role in the rainforest, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
