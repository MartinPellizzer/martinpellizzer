Croton mutabilis

Croton mutabilis, commonly known as the variable croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that changes with age, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and can vary in shape and color depending on environmental conditions.

Croton mutabilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
