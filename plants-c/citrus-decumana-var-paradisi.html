Citrus decumana var. paradisi

Citrus decumana var. paradisi, commonly known as the Sicilian lemon, is a citrus species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic peel. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is valued for its high acidity and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is closely related to the common lemon but has distinct genetic and morphological traits.
