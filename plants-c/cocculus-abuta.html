Cocculus abuta

Cocculus abuta, commonly known as Indian apple or wild apple, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces round, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
