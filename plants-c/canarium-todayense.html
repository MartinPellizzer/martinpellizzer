Canarium todayense

Canarium todayense, commonly known as the Philippine canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological importance, it is of interest for conservation and horticultural purposes.
