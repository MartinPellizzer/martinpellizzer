Carex paupercula

Carex paupercula, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in shaded, wet habitats.

Carex paupercula plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
