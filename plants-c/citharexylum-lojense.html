Citharexylum lojense

Citharexylum lojense, commonly known as the Andean mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on the branches of host trees, drawing nutrients and water from them. Its berries, which are red and fleshy, are an important food source for various bird species.
