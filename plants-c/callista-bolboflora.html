Callista bolboflora

Callista bolboflora, commonly known as the Bolboflora daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disc florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
