Cardamine glaucescens

Cardamine glaucescens, commonly known as blue bladder, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other similar species. The plant is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
