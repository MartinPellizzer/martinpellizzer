Cotula paradoxa

Cotula paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass or yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Cotula paradoxa is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in various environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
