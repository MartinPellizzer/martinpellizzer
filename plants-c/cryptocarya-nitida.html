Cryptocarya nitida

Cryptocarya nitida, commonly known as the velvetleaf sycamore, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight pubescent texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cryptocarya nitida is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
