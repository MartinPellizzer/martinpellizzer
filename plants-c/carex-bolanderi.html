Carex bolanderi

Carex bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander’s sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its grass-like leaves are usually dark green and arranged in three rows, characteristic of many sedges.

Bolander’s sedge produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by triangular seed capsules.
