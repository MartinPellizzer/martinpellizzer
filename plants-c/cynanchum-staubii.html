Cynanchum staubii

Cynanchum staubii, commonly known as the blue cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and showy, blue-violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and have a distinctive silvery underside.

Cynanchum staubii is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
