Campanula minuta

Campanula minuta, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula minuta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
