Carex acuta var. minor

Carex acuta var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
