Cyperus arenicola

Cyperus arenicola, commonly known as sand flats sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in coastal and sandy environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water or wet sand, often in salt marshes or estuaries. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus arenicola is well adapted to saline and drought-prone conditions, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
