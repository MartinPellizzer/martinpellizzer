Carex vesicaria var. colorata

Carex vesicaria var. colorata, commonly known as the colored burr sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps, with leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that mature into distinctive, inflated, seed-bearing structures called "burr-like" capsules. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value, especially when the seed heads turn a striking red or purple color in the fall.
