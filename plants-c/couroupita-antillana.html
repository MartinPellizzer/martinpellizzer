Couroupita antillana

Couroupita antillana, commonly known as the Caribbean trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
