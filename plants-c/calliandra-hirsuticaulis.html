Calliandra hirsuticaulis

Calliandra hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that consist of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, hairy leaflets, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The hairy calliandra is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
