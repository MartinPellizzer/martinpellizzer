Camellia hamyenensis

Camellia hamyenensis, commonly known as the Yunnan camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in subtropical climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Camellia hamyenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
