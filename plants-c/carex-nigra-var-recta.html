Carex nigra var. recta

Carex nigra var. recta, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like sedges. This variety is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat.
