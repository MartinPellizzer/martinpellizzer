Cephalanthus occidentalis var. obtusifolius

Cephalanthus occidentalis var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the smooth buttonbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, spherical flower heads that appear in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. The leaves are elliptical, smooth, and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value. This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies such as swamps and stream banks.

It is also notable for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
