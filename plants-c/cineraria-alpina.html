Cineraria alpina

Cineraria alpina, commonly known as the alpine cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Cineraria alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
