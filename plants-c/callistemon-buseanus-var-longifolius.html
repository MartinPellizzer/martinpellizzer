Callistemon buseanus var. longifolius

Callistemon buseanus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking crimson or red flowers that resemble bottlebrushes, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically longer than they are wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract native birds.
