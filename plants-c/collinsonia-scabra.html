Collinsonia scabra

Collinsonia scabra, commonly known as roughleaf bladdernut, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped, toothed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by inflated, bladder-like fruits. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Collinsonia scabra is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
