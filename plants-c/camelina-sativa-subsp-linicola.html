Camelina sativa subsp. linicola

Camelina sativa subsp.

linicola, commonly known as the linseed plant, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves.

The plant produces small, oval seeds that are rich in oil, making it valuable for both culinary and industrial uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its oilseed production.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender, linear leaves and is closely related to other Camelina species.
