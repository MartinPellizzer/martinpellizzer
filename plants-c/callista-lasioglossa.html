Callista lasioglossa

Callista lasioglossa, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky layer of hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
