Chionanthus cubensis

Chionanthus cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban snowflower, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and oval-shaped with a glossy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
