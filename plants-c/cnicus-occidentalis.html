Cnicus occidentalis

Cnicus occidentalis, commonly known as western prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. It has a taproot system and is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
