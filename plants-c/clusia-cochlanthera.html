Clusia cochlanthera

Clusia cochlanthera, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Clusia cochlanthera is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
