Ceropegia sabuliphila

Ceropegia sabuliphila, commonly known as the sandplain milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, waxy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, attracting specific pollinators such as moths and flies. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal dune ecosystems.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
