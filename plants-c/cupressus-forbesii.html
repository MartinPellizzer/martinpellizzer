Cupressus forbesii

Cupressus forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage and aromatic bark. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its compact form and drought tolerance.
