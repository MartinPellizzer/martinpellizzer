Carex affinis

Carex affinis, commonly known as slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. Native to moist environments such as wetlands and marshes, it thrives in areas with consistent water availability. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
