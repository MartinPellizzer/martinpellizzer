Cardamine virginica

Cardamine virginica, commonly known as Virginia waterleaf, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves that resemble a waterleaf. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
