Cereus speciosissimus var. maelenii

Cereus speciosissimus var. maelenii, commonly known as the Maelenii Christmas cactus, is a unique hybrid of the Schlumbergera truncata and Schlumbergera russelliana species.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its popularity as a decorative plant is due to its long blooming period and ease of care.
