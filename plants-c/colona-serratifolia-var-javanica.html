Colona serratifolia var. javanica

Colona serratifolia var. javanica, commonly known as the Javanese serrated colona, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and attract various pollinators.
