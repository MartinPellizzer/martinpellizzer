Cordyline schlechteri

Cordyline schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, strap-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The leaves are usually a deep green with a waxy texture and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. This plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic appearance in gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
