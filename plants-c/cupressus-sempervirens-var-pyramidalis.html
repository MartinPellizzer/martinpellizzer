Cupressus sempervirens var. pyramidalis

Cupressus sempervirens var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Italian cypress, is a variant of the Mediterranean cypress.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by reddish-brown cones.

This variety is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
