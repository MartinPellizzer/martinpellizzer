Costus lanceolatus

Costus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf costus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spadix, often surrounded by a colorful bract. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.

Costus lanceolatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
