Coelogyne kopfii

Coelogyne kopfii, commonly known as the Kopf's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and a subtle fragrance, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Coelogyne kopfii is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care, including well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.
