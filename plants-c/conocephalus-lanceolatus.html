Conocephalus lanceolatus

Conocephalus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers make it a distinctive addition to native gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
