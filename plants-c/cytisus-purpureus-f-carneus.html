Cytisus purpureus f. carneus

Cytisus purpureus f. carneus, commonly known as the pink flame acacia, is a variant of the purple broom plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are green with a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy shrub suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and long-lasting blooms.
