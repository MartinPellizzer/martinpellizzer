Capparis corymbifera

Capparis corymbifera, commonly known as the Indian caper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and tropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds.

The plant is adapted to saline and arid environments, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
