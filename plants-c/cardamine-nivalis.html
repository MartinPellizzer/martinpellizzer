Cardamine nivalis

Cardamine nivalis, commonly known as the snow buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It typically grows in rocky or moist, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant has a basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals. It is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Cardamine nivalis is adapted to cold climates and is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.
