Carex frigida var. densa

Carex frigida var. densa, commonly known as dense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in bogs, marshes, or along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly rough to the touch.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in tight, cylindrical spikes, which are typically green to brown in color.
