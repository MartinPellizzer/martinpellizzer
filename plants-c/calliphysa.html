Calliphysa

Calliphysa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern and central United States, where it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink and white. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.
