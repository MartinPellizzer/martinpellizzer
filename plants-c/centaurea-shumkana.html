Centaurea shumkana

Centaurea shumkana, commonly known as the Shumkian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it produces achene seeds for propagation.

Centaurea shumkana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
