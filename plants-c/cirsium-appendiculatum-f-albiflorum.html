Cirsium appendiculatum f. albiflorum

Cirsium appendiculatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

The whiteflower thistle is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
