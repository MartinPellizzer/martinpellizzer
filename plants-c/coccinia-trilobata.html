Coccinia trilobata

Coccinia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are often consumed in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Coccinia trilobata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
