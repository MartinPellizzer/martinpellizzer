Calamus nielsenii

Calamus nielsenii, commonly known as the Nielsen's canescent palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Calamus nielsenii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
