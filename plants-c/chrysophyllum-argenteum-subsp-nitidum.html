Chrysophyllum argenteum subsp. nitidum

Chrysophyllum argenteum subsp.

nitidum, commonly known as the silverleaf star-apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically silver-green in color.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced silver sheen on the foliage compared to other varieties.
