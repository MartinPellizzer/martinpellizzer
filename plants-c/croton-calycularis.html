Croton calycularis

Croton calycularis, commonly known as the tropical croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from green to red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton calycularis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
