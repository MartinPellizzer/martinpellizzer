Cirsium × morrisii

Cirsium × morrisii, commonly known as the Morris thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple to pink flower heads and robust, upright stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to the United Kingdom. It exhibits features of both its parent species, Cirsium arvense and Cirsium vulgare, combining their ornamental and ecological traits.

Due to its hardiness and visual appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
