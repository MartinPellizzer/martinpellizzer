Crinum polyphyllum

Crinum polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved lily, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to pink and even purple. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern. Crinum polyphyllum is a member of the Amaryllis family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
