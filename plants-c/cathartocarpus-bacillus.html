Cathartocarpus bacillus

Cathartocarpus bacillus, commonly known as the small-leafed catkin grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
