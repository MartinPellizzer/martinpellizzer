Caiophora absinthiifolia

Caiophora absinthiifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf caiophora, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
