Cineraria integrifolia var. alpina

Cineraria integrifolia var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that prefers cool, moist conditions and full sun to partial shade.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
