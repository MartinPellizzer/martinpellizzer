Cyathea adenochlamys

Cyathea adenochlamys, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor firmly in moist soil, while its fronds play a role in photosynthesis and water retention.

Cyathea adenochlamys is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
