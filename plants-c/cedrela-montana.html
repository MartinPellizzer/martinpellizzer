Cedrela montana

Cedrela montana, commonly known as the mountain cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its straight trunk, smooth bark, and dense canopy of elongated leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody fruits containing seeds.

Cedrela montana is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland and mountainous forest ecosystems.
