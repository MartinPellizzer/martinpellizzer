Clematis occidentalis var. grosseserrata

Clematis occidentalis var. grosseserrata, commonly known as the western clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
