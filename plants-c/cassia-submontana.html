Cassia submontana

Cassia submontana, commonly known as mountain cassia, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cassia submontana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
