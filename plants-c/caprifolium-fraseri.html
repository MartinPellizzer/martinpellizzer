Caprifolium fraseri

Caprifolium fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small berries that ripen to a dark red or black color, attracting various bird species.

Caprifolium fraseri is often found in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes.
