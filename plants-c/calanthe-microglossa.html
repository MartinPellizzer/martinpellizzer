Calanthe microglossa

Calanthe microglossa, commonly known as the small-lipped calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with a distinctive dark green lip, and it produces small, round, dark brown seeds.

Calanthe microglossa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
