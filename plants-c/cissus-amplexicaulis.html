Cissus amplexicaulis

Cissus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the ivy creeper or emerald ivy, is a vigorous trailing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can grow long, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained on trellises.

Cissus amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
