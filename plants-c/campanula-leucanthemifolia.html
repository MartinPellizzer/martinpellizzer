Campanula leucanthemifolia

Campanula leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white thlaspi, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula leucanthemifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
