Carlina pyrenaica

Carlina pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean cudweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Carlina pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
