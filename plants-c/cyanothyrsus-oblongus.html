Cyanothyrsus oblongus

Cyanothyrsus oblongus, commonly known as the blue thornapple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has woody stems covered in sharp thorns, which provide protection against herbivores. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cyanothyrsus oblongus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
