Camirium cordifolium

Camirium cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a flowering species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
