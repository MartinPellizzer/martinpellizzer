Cuspidaria populeaster

Cuspidaria populeaster, commonly known as the poplar spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cuspidaria populeaster is known for its latex-like sap, which can be toxic and cause skin irritation.
