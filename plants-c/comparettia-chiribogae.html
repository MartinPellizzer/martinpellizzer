Comparettia chiribogae

Comparettia chiribogae, commonly known as the Chiriboga orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
