Calophyllum comorense

Calophyllum comorense, commonly known as the Comorian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant produces woody, elliptical fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

The tree thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
