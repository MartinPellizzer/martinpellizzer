Cyperus concinnus

Cyperus concinnus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus concinnus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
