Crambe wolgensis

Crambe wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgensis crambe, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, with a rough texture.

Crambe wolgensis is often grown for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications.
