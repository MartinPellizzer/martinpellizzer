Carum creticum

Carum creticum, commonly known as Greek cumin or cretan cumin, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae, characterized by its fine, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are highly valued for their distinct flavor and aroma. Carum creticum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

It is widely used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine for its digestive and aromatic properties.
