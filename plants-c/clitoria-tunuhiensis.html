Clitoria tunuhiensis

Clitoria tunuhiensis, commonly known as the flame bean or tropical senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing legume that produces vibrant purple to red pea-like flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
