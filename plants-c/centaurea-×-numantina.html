Centaurea × numantina

Centaurea × numantina, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
