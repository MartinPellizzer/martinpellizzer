Caragana arborescens var. dubia

Caragana arborescens var. dubia, commonly known as the Siberian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought and poor soil conditions.

This species is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant due to its hardy nature.
