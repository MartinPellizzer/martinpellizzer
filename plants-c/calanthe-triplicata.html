Calanthe triplicata

Calanthe triplicata, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae, characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves and bears showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
