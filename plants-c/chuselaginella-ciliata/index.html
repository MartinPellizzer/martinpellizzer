Chuselaginella Ciliata

Chuselaginella ciliata is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is known for its unique, finely hairy leaves and small, clustered blooms that range in color from pale pink to soft white. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered by conservationists. Its beauty and fragility make it a subject of both scientific interest and botanical fascination.
