Cyperus cristatus subsp. ascolepidioides

Cyperus cristatus subsp.

ascolepidioides, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its growth habit makes it a common weed in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
