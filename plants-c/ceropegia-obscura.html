Ceropegia obscura

Ceropegia obscura, commonly known as the ghost flower or silver leaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces unique flowers that are often pale green or white, with a distinctive shape resembling a bird's beak. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia obscura is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
