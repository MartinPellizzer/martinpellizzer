Ceanothus greggii var. perplexans

Ceanothus greggii var. perplexans, commonly known as the Arizona bluebunch, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery-green foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
