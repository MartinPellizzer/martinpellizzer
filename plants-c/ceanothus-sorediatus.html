Ceanothus sorediatus

Ceanothus sorediatus, commonly known as the Sierra Ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds coated in a sticky substance, aiding in seed dispersal by ants. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Ceanothus sorediatus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
