Corypha minor

Corypha minor, commonly known as the lesser palm, is a small, evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by local communities.

Corypha minor is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological and economic significance in its native habitat.
