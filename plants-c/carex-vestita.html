Carex vestita

Carex vestita, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, pointed leaves that often have a silvery or pale green hue. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and pond edges. Carex vestita is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
