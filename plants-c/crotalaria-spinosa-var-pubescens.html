Crotalaria spinosa var. pubescens

Crotalaria spinosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its varietal name. Its yellow flowers are typically clustered in racemes, and it produces elongated, curved seed pods.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
