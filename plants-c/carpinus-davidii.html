Carpinus davidii

Carpinus davidii, commonly known as the David's hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, nut-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
