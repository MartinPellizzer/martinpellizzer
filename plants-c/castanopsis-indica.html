Castanopsis indica

Castanopsis indica, commonly known as the Indian chinkapin, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood. The tree produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Castanopsis indica is valued for its ecological importance and is also used in traditional medicine.
