Cocculus lacunosus

Cocculus lacunosus, commonly known as the lacunar cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and distinctive, spherical fruits that resemble small, rough balls. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit contains seeds enclosed in a spiky, fibrous husk, which aids in seed dispersal.

Cocculus lacunosus is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
