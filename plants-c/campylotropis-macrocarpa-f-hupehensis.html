Campylotropis macrocarpa f. hupehensis

Campylotropis macrocarpa f. hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh hairy vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
