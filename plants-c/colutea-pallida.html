Colutea pallida

Colutea pallida, commonly known as the pale colutea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Colutea pallida is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
