Cistus monspeliensis subsp. affinis

Cistus monspeliensis subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the Spanish rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local flora.

Its subspecies affinis is distinguished by slightly different leaf morphology and flowering patterns compared to the main species.
