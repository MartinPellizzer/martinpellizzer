Citharexylum vallense

Citharexylum vallense, commonly known as the velvetleaf citharexylum, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
