Campanula alpina subsp. orbelica

Campanula alpina subsp.

orbelica, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments. Campanula alpina subsp.

orbelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine habitats.
