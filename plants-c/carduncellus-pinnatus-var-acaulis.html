Carduncellus pinnatus var. acaulis

Carduncellus pinnatus var. acaulis, commonly known as the leafy cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, lobed, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry climates.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
