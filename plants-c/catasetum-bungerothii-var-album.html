Catasetum bungerothii var. album

Catasetum bungerothii var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking white blooms with a subtle greenish tinge. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are known for their unique structure, featuring specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific pollinators.

The plant prefers warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
