Correa backhouseana var. backhouseana

Correa backhouseana var. backhouseana, commonly known as the pink correa, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
