Cardamine africana proles borbonica

Cardamine africana proles borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of finely divided, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
