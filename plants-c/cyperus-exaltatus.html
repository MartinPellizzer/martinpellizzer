Cyperus exaltatus

Cyperus exaltatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. Cyperus exaltatus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by wind or insects.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
