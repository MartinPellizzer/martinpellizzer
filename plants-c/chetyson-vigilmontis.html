Chetyson vigilmontis

Chetyson vigilmontis, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and distinctive silvery-gray foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale yellow.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to cover trellises or walls.
