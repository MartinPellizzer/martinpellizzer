Causonis japonica var. japonica

Causonis japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese spurge, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
