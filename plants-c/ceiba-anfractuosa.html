Ceiba anfractuosa

Ceiba anfractuosa, commonly known as the "silk-cotton tree," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive crown of spreading branches. The tree produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a cotton-like capsule that contains numerous seeds surrounded by a fluffy, white fiber.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in many regions.
