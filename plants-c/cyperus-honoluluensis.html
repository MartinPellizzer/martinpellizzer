Cyperus honoluluensis

Cyperus honoluluensis, commonly known as the Honolulu flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Cyperus honoluluensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
