Chaenorhinum crassifolium subsp. cadevallii

Chaenorhinum crassifolium subsp.

cadevallii, commonly known as the Cadevall's horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
