Carex straminea var. moniliformis

Carex straminea var. moniliformis, commonly known as the threadleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in shaded areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
