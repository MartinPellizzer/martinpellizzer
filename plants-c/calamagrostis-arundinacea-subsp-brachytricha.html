Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp. brachytricha

Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp.

brachytricha, commonly known as narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This subspecies typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and ditches. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
