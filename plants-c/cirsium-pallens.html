Cirsium pallens

Cirsium pallens, commonly known as the pale thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cirsium pallens is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
