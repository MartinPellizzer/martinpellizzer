Corispermum leptopterum f. purpurascens

Corispermum leptopterum f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-fruited bladderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its simple, glabrous stems and alternate, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its fruits are distinctive, inflated, and often tinged with a purplish hue, which is a key feature of this form.

It is typically found in sandy or disturbed soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions.
