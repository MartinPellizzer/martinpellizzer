Crotalaria desaegeri

Crotalaria desaegeri, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Crotalaria desaegeri is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
