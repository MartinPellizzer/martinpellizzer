Crataegus brachyphylla

Crataegus brachyphylla, commonly known as the shortleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Crataegus brachyphylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
