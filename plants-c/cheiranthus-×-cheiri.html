Cheiranthus × cheiri

Cheiranthus × cheiri, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to red and orange. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia, often found growing in rocky or disturbed soils. It produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cheiranthus × cheiri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
