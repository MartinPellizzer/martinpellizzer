Chrysothamnus frigidus var. concolor

Chrysothamnus frigidus var. concolor, commonly known as the white rabbitbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and its yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
