Carlina corymbosa subsp. graeca

Carlina corymbosa subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and a tall, branched inflorescence that produces showy blue to purple composite flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for gardening purposes.
