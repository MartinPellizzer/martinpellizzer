Carthamus cancellatus

Carthamus cancellatus, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Carthamus cancellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
