Caladium camoensii

Caladium camoensii, commonly known as the Camoens' arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Caladium camoensii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, as it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.
