Capparis cynophallophora f. emarginata

Capparis cynophallophora f. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginated caper, is a unique variant of the caper plant native to southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, emarginated (notched) leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, textured surface, while the stems are often woody and branched. It produces small, round, greenish fruits that mature into a pale yellow color.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
