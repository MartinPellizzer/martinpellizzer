Chamaecytisus ciliatus var. grisebachii

Chamaecytisus ciliatus var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-haired broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has finely haired leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
