Calendula parviflora

Calendula parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange-yellow flowers, which are typically small and clustered in a single head. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lobed or serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Calendula parviflora is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in herbal remedies.
