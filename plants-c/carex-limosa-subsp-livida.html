Carex limosa subsp. livida

Carex limosa subsp.

livida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to pale blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated, a common trait among sedges. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
