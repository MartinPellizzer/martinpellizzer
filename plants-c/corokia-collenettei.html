Corokia collenettei

Corokia collenettei, commonly known as the Kauri daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 1 meter tall. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
