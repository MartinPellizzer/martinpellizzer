Ceropegia concanensis

Ceropegia concanensis, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, trailing stems and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are often pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Ceropegia concanensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
