Clematis mandshurica var. lancifolia

Clematis mandshurica var. lancifolia, commonly known as the Manchurian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that give it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
