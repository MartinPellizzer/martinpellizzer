Cirsium remotifolium var. odontolepis

Cirsium remotifolium var. odontolepis, commonly known as the toothed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, spiny leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive tooth-like structure along their margins.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience in challenging environments.
