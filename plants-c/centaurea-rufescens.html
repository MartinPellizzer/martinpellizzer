Centaurea rufescens

Centaurea rufescens, commonly known as the red centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea rufescens is often used in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
