Cynorkis papilio

Cynorkis papilio, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
