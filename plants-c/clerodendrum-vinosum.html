Clerodendrum vinosum

Clerodendrum vinosum, commonly known as the purple flame or wineberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
