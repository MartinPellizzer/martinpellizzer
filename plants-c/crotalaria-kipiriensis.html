Crotalaria kipiriensis

Crotalaria kipiriensis, commonly known as the Kipiri crownflower, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, often in regions with a tropical or subtropical climate. Its bright yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, making it visually striking and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
