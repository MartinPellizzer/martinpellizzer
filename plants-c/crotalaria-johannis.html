Crotalaria johannis

Crotalaria johannis, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are palmately compound with three leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria johannis is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
