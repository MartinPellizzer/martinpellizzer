Cynanchum macranthum

Cynanchum macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered milkweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in moist woodlands and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Cynanchum macranthum is also known for its latex-like sap, which can be toxic to livestock and humans.
