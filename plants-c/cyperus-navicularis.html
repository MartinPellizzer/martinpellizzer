Cyperus navicularis

Cyperus navicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus navicularis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
