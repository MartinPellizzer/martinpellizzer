Coelogyne dempoensis

Coelogyne dempoensis, commonly known as the Dempo orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water and supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments.

Coelogyne dempoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
