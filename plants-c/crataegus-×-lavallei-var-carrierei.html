Crataegus × lavallei var. carrierei

Crataegus × lavallei var. carrierei, commonly known as the Carrière hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
