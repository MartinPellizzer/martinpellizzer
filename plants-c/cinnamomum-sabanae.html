Cinnamomum sabanae

Cinnamomum sabanae, commonly known as the Sabana cinnamon or Caribbean cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and a strong cinnamon scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
