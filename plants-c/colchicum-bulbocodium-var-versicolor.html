Colchicum bulbocodium var. versicolor

Colchicum bulbocodium var. versicolor, commonly known as the Variable Colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, depending on the variety. The plant produces large, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are broad and grass-like.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
