Cereus phatnospermus

Cereus phatnospermus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange "skirt" of flowers that bloom at night, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and is typically green, with a unique coloration due to the presence of a symbiotic fungus. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
