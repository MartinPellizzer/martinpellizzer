Cistus × florentinus var. arrigentiformis

Cistus × florentinus var. arrigentiformis, commonly known as silverleaf rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, glandular-hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice in xeriscaping and Mediterranean gardens.
