Carpesium linearibracteatum

Carpesium linearibracteatum, commonly known as the narrow bract cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, bract-like leaves and produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a weed in agricultural settings.
