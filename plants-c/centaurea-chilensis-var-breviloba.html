Centaurea chilensis var. breviloba

Centaurea chilensis var. breviloba, commonly known as the Chilean knapweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
