Conyza lucens

Conyza lucens, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Conyza lucens is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new habitats quickly.
