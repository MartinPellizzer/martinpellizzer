Cereus melanacanthus

Cereus melanacanthus, commonly known as the black spined cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring dark green stems and spines that are black or very dark in color. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's unique morphology and nocturnal flowering habits make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
