Crucifera hornungia

Crucifera hornungia, commonly known as the hornung's crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its small, aromatic leaves. The plant's flowers are usually white or yellow, and it produces small, rounded seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
