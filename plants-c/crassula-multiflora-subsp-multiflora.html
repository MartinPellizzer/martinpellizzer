Crassula multiflora subsp. multiflora

Crassula multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the rock rose or forest crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, shrub-like growth with dense clusters of fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its drought tolerance and ease of care make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
