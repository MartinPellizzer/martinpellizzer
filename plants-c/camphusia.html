Camphusia

Camphusia, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rough bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Camphusia is valued for its ability to produce camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and as a natural preservative.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stability in its native regions.
