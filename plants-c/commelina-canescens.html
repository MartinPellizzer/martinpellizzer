Commelina canescens

Commelina canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue-violet flowers and hairy, silver-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
