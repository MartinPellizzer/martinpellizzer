Croton lobatus f. crispus

Croton lobatus f. crispus, commonly known as the crinkled croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored leaves that are deeply lobed and have a crinkled texture, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiky capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
