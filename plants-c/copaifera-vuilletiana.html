Copaifera vuilletiana

Copaifera vuilletiana, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has alternate, simple leaves. Its most notable feature is the resin it exudes, which has been used for medicinal purposes.

The botanical significance of Copaifera vuilletiana lies in its role as a valuable resource for both traditional medicine and ecological diversity.
