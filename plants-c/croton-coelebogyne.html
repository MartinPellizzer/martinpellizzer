Croton coelebogyne

Croton coelebogyne, commonly known as the sacred croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, palmate leaves that can range in color from green to variegated patterns. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, surrounded by bracts.

Due to its cultural significance in some African communities, it is also valued for its medicinal properties.
