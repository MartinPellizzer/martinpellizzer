Croton amphileucus

Croton amphileucus, commonly known as the red-stemmed croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that range from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it contains latex, which is a common feature in the Croton genus.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
