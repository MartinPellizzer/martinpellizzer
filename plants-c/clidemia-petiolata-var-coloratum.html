Clidemia petiolata var. coloratum

Clidemia petiolata var. coloratum, commonly known as the red-leaf false hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red to purple leaves, which contrast with its smaller, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are glossy and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
