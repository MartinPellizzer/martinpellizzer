Centipeda capensis

Centipeda capensis, commonly known as the Cape centipeda, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It typically grows in coastal areas and has a distinctive appearance with its hairy, upright stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Centipeda capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
