Cissus setosa

Cissus setosa, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light.

Cissus setosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
