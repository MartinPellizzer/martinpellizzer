Calamagrostis kashmiriana

Calamagrostis kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Kashmir and northern India.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tall, arching culms that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Calamagrostis kashmiriana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides habitat for various small wildlife species.
