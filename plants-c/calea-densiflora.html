Calea densiflora

Calea densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Calea densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
