Clematis buchananiana var. sericea

Clematis buchananiana var. sericea, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with finely divided, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in spring and early summer. Its stems are covered in a silvery, silky pubescence, which is characteristic of the varietal name "sericea.".

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
