Cyathea inquinans

Cyathea inquinans, commonly known as the black tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive dark, almost black, trunk that is covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. Cyathea inquinans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
