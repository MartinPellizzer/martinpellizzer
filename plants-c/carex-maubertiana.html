Carex maubertiana

Carex maubertiana, commonly known as Maubert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex maubertiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
