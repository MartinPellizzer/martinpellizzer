Centropogon cordifolius

Centropogon cordifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
