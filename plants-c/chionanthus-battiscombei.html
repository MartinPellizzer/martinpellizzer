Chionanthus battiscombei

Chionanthus battiscombei, commonly known as the whiteflower fringe tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet and has a rounded, spreading form. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that matures in the fall.
