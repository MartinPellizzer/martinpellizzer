Colchicum bulbocodium subsp. bulbocodium

Colchicum bulbocodium subsp.

bulbocodium, commonly known as the purple autumn crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has a short, erect stem with a single, showy flower that has three petals and three sepals. Its bulbous underground structure stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry periods.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
