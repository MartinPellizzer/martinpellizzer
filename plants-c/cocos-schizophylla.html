Cocos schizophylla

Cocos schizophylla, commonly known as the split-leaf coconut, is a unique species within the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive palm-like appearance, with fronds that split into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant typically grows in tropical regions, preferring well-drained sandy soils and full sunlight. Its reproductive structures include a compound inflorescence that produces both male and female flowers.

Despite its ornamental value, it is relatively rare and has limited cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
