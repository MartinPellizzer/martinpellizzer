Ceratolobus subangulatus

Ceratolobus subangulatus, commonly known as the narrow-angled acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and have a distinctive narrow angle between the leaflets. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
