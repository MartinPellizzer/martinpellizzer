Cephalanthus sarandi

Cephalanthus sarandi, commonly known as the Andean beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are visually striking and serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Cephalanthus sarandi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and showy fruit.
