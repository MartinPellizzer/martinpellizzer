Cyperus maculatus var. naumannianus

Cyperus maculatus var. naumannianus, commonly known as the spotted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikes of tiny, greenish or brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands, rice fields, and along waterways.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
