Campanula pamphylica

Campanula pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula pamphylica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
