Culcasia gracilis

Culcasia gracilis, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and swamps. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it is primarily propagated through rhizomes.

Culcasia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in water ecosystems.
