Centaurea fallacina

Centaurea fallacina, commonly known as the false sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, spiny seed heads that resemble burrs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Centaurea fallacina is an annual or biennial plant with a widespread distribution across Europe and parts of North Africa.
