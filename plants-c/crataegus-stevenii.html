Crataegus stevenii

Crataegus stevenii, commonly known as Steven's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Crataegus stevenii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
