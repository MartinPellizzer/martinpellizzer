Crotalaria bracteata

Crotalaria bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate cowpea or yellow crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and palmate leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Crotalaria bracteata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
