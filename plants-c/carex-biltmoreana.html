Carex biltmoreana

Carex biltmoreana, commonly known as Biltmore sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, dark green leaves that form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, clustered brownish flowers in late summer, which are adapted for wind pollination.
