Centaurea cretica

Centaurea cretica, commonly known as Cretan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Centaurea cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
