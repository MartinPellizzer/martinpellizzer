Centaurea benedicta

Centaurea benedicta, commonly known as blessed breadthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which are typical of the genus Centaurea. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea benedicta is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
