Carissa brownii

Carissa brownii, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Carissa brownii is also valued for its ability to provide habitat and food for various wildlife species.
