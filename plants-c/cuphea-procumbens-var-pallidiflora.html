Cuphea procumbens var. pallidiflora

Cuphea procumbens var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower cuphea, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It features delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, often with a contrasting red or purple throat. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
