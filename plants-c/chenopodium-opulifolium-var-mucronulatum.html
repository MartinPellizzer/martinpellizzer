Chenopodium opulifolium var. mucronulatum

Chenopodium opulifolium var. mucronulatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This variant is distinguished by its short, abrupt leaf tips, which give it its specific epithet *mucronulatum*.
