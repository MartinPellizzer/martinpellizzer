Centaurea riparia

Centaurea riparia, commonly known as the river centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as riverbanks and marshes. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in a dense, globular shape. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea riparia is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
