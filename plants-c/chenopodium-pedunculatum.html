Chenopodium pedunculatum

Chenopodium pedunculatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant has alternate, ovate to triangular leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits called utricles.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.
