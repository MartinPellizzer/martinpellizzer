Chenopodium latifolium subsp. rectum

Chenopodium latifolium subsp.

rectum, commonly known as broadleaf goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or used in traditional medicine.
