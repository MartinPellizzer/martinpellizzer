Crocus granatensis

Crocus granatensis, commonly known as the Granada crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its early blooming period, often appearing in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape. Its corms are small and are typically used in traditional medicine.

Crocus granatensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in botanical studies.
