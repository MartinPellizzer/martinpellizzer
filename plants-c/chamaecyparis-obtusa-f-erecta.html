Chamaecyparis obtusa f. erecta

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. erecta, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or upright Japanese cedar, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer.

It features a dense, pyramidal shape with dark green, needle-like foliage that is arranged in crossed pairs. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its neat, formal appearance.
