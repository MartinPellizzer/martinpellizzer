Citrus × limonum var. amalphitana

Citrus × limonum var. amalphitana, commonly known as the Amalfi lemon, is a citrus variety native to the Amalfi Coast of southern Italy.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a thick, aromatic peel and a high concentration of essential oils. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is prized for its tart flavor and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
