Cyclobalanopsis stewardiana var. longicaudata

Cyclobalanopsis stewardiana var. longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and durable wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, sweet-smelling fragrance.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.
