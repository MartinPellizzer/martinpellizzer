Cirsium angustissimum

Cirsium angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive narrow, pointed shape.

The plant is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
