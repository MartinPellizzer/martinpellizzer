Carduus lacerus

Carduus lacerus, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem covered in rough, bristly hairs, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a serrated edge. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Carduus lacerus is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including roadsides and meadows.
