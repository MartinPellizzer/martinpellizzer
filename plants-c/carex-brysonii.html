Carex brysonii

Carex brysonii, commonly known as the Bryson sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally bright green and can reach heights of up to 3 feet. Its flowering spikes are usually brown or reddish and appear in late spring to early summer.

Carex brysonii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
