Cucubalus aegyptiacus

Cucubalus aegyptiacus, commonly known as the Egyptian cucumber, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant produces long, slender fruits that are typically green and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
