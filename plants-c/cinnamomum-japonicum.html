Cinnamomum japonicum

Cinnamomum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or Japanese camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for its medicinal and aromatic properties. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
