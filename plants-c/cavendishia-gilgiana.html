Cavendishia gilgiana

Cavendishia gilgiana, commonly known as the Gilgian Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Cavendishia gilgiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
