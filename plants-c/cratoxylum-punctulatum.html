Cratoxylum punctulatum

Cratoxylum punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted tamanu, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with rough, fissured bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing elliptical seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its healing benefits.
