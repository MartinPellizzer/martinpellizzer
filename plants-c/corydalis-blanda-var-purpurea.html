Corydalis blanda var. purpurea

Corydalis blanda var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple fringed corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, hairless stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers with purple or pink petals and distinctive fringed edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.
