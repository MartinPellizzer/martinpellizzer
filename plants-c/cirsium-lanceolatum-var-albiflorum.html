Cirsium lanceolatum var. albiflorum

Cirsium lanceolatum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, showy white or pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, with the ray florets being particularly prominent.

The plant has a robust root system and is often found in areas with rich, organic soil.
