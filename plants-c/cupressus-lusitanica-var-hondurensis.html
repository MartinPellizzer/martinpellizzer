Cupressus lusitanica var. hondurensis

Cupressus lusitanica var. hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras cypress, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones, typical of the cypress family, and has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, though it is somewhat sensitive to extreme weather conditions.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
