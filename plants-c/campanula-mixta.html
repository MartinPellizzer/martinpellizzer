Campanula mixta

Campanula mixta, commonly known as the mixed bellflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
