Crepis tectorum var. gracilis

Crepis tectorum var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender roofweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is often found in temperate climates. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in a cluster, and the leaves are narrow and linear.

This variety is distinguished by its slender stems and delicate appearance, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
