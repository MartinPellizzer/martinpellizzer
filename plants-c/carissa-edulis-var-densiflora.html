Carissa edulis var. densiflora

Carissa edulis var. densiflora, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. The berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in warm climates.
