Clerodendrum volubile

Clerodendrum volubile, commonly known as the climbing clerodendrum, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is also known for its fragrant foliage, which can be used in traditional medicine and as a natural air freshener.
