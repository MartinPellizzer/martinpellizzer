Cryptorhiza

Cryptorhiza, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant's roots are often hidden beneath the soil, contributing to its elusive nature. Cryptorhiza is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within its ecosystem and its potential for medicinal use.
