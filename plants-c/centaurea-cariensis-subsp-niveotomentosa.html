Centaurea cariensis subsp. niveotomentosa

Centaurea cariensis subsp.

niveotomentosa, commonly known as the white-tomentose bluemink, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a similar tomentose layer, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
