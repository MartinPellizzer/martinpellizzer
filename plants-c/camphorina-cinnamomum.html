Camphorina cinnamomum

Camphorina cinnamomum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China, Vietnam, and other parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a strong cinnamon scent when cut. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, reddish fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant, spicy fragrance.

The tree is widely cultivated for its bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in cooking.
