Clerodendrum incisum

Clerodendrum incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaved rose of Sharon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
