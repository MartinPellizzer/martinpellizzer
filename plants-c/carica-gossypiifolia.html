Carica gossypiifolia

Carica gossypiifolia, commonly known as the cottonleaf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to the common papaya but are typically smaller and less sweet. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant's foliage and fruit contribute to its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
