Catonia elliptica

Catonia elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf catonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
