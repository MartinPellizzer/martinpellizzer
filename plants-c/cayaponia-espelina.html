Cayaponia espelina

Cayaponia espelina, commonly known as the Espelina tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential for agricultural use.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
