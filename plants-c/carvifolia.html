Carvifolia

Carvifolia, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by small, edible berries that are often consumed by both humans and animals.

Carvifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
