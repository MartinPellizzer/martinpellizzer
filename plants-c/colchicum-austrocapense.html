Colchicum austrocapense

Colchicum austrocapense, commonly known as the Southern Cape colchic, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a cluster of waxy, purple or violet flowers. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are typically broad and arrow-shaped.

Colchicum austrocapense is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
