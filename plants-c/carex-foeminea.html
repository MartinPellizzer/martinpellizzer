Carex foeminea

Carex foeminea, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex foeminea plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
