Cynanchum kingdonwardii

Cynanchum kingdonwardii, commonly known as the kingdonwardii milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive crown of hooked appendages, typical of the milkweed family. Its latex sap contains milky sap that can be toxic, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Cynanchum kingdonwardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
