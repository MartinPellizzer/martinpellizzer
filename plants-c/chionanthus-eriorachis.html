Chionanthus eriorachis

Chionanthus eriorachis, commonly known as the downy chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring, often in clusters along the branches. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
