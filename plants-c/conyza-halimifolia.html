Conyza halimifolia

Conyza halimifolia, commonly known as whiteflower false dandelion, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features a taproot system and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Conyza halimifolia is also known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
