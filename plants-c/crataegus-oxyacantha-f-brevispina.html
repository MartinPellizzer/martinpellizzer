Crataegus oxyacantha f. brevispina

Crataegus oxyacantha f. brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined hawthorn, is a cultivated form of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, spiny thorns compared to the typical species. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is suitable for gardens and landscapes.
