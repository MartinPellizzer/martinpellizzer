Caltha leptosepala var. howellii

Caltha leptosepala var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's marsh marigold, is a variant of the marsh marigold native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
