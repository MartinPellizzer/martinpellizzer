Clusia amazonica var. parvifolia

Clusia amazonica var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Clusia amazonica var. parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
