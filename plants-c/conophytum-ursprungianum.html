Conophytum ursprungianum

Conophytum ursprungianum, commonly known as the "Cape Karoo star" or "Olive star," is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often swollen stems that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, rounded structures, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Conophytum ursprungianum is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
