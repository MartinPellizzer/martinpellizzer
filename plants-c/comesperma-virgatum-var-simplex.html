Comesperma virgatum var. simplex

Comesperma virgatum var. simplex, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
