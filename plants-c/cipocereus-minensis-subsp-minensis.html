Cipocereus minensis subsp. minensis

Cipocereus minensis subsp.

minensis, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its distinctive bright red or orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant has a short, cylindrical stem covered in spines, and its roots are adapted to absorb water efficiently in arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
