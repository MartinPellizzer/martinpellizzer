Canarium copaliferum

Canarium copaliferum, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, red berries containing a single seed. Its most notable botanical feature is the production of copal, a resinous substance used in traditional medicine and incense.

Canarium copaliferum thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
