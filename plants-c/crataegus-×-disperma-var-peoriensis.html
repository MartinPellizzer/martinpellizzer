Crataegus × disperma var. peoriensis

Crataegus × disperma var. peoriensis, commonly known as the Peoria hawthorn, is a hybrid species native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy upper surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
