Cacalia plantaginodes

Cacalia plantaginodes, commonly known as the silverleaf cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are silver-gray in color, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.
