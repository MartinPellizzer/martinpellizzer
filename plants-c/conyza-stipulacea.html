Conyza stipulacea

Conyza stipulacea, commonly known as the slenderleaf fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as fields and roadsides, and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its small yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Conyza stipulacea is often considered a weed but has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
