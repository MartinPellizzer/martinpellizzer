Crataegus × grignonensis

Crataegus × grignonensis, commonly known as the French hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, characterized by its small, rose-like flowers and red or orange berries.

It is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. Its fruits are edible but have a tart flavor, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife, providing food for birds and insects.
