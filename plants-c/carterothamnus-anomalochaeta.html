Carterothamnus anomalochaeta

Carterothamnus anomalochaeta, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Carterothamnus anomalochaeta is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique texture and color.
