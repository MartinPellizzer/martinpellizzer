Crepidium wenzelii

Crepidium wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant features a compact, rhizomatous structure with a single, upright stem bearing a few lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often with a subtle fragrance, and are clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Crepidium wenzelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
