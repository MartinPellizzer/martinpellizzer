Conophyllum nanum

Conophyllum nanum, commonly known as the dwarf conophyllum, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Conophyllum nanum is often found in sandy or rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.
