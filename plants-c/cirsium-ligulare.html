Cirsium ligulare

Cirsium ligulare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and spiny, while its flowers are purple or pink, forming a dense, globe-like structure.

Cirsium ligulare is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
