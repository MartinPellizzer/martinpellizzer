Carex coulteri

Carex coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-white flowers.

Coulter's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
