Cotoneaster nima-yushijii

Cotoneaster nima-yushijii, commonly known as the Nima-Yushijii cotoneaster, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are pink and appear in clusters.

Cotoneaster nima-yushijii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
