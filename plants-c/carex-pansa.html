Carex pansa

Carex pansa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to wetland and riparian habitats, Carex pansa thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant typically grows in dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
