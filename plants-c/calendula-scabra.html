Calendula scabra

Calendula scabra, commonly known as rough calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Calendula scabra is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
