Combretum myrtillifolium

Combretum myrtillifolium, commonly known as the purpleleaf combretum, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
