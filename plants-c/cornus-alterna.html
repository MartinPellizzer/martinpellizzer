Cornus alterna

Cornus alterna, commonly known as the alternate cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
