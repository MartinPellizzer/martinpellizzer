Campanula lehmanniana var. capusii

Campanula lehmanniana var. capusii, commonly known as the Lehmann's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
