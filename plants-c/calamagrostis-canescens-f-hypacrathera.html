Calamagrostis canescens f. hypacrathera

Calamagrostis canescens f. hypacrathera, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a grass species characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and upright growth habit.

It is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive, grass-like appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brownish in color.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual interest and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
