Clematis heracleifolia var. decaisneana

Clematis heracleifolia var. decaisneana, commonly known as the Decaisne's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its climbing habit and floral display.
