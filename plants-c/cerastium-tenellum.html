Cerastium tenellum

Cerastium tenellum, commonly known as the common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, silky hairs. The leaves are small, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are white or pink, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it a common weed in gardens and natural habitats.
