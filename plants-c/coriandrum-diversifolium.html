Coriandrum diversifolium

Coriandrum diversifolium, commonly known as the mountain coriander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and carrots. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often mistaken for ferns due to their delicate appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its aromatic seeds and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb in various cultures.
