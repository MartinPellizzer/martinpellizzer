Cacoucia barteri

Cacoucia barteri, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and twining stems that help it climb support structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

The botanical features of Cacoucia barteri make it an important crop in traditional agriculture across its native range.
