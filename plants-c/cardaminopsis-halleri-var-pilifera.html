Cardaminopsis halleri var. pilifera

Cardaminopsis halleri var. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial herb native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in mountainous regions.
