Catasetum ivaneae

Catasetum ivaneae, commonly known as the Ibanez orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are notable for their unique structure, with specialized appendages that aid in pollination by specific wasp species.

Catasetum ivaneae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
