Cheirolophus canariensis

Cheirolophus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus.

Cheirolophus canariensis is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
