Carex ampullacea f. pendulina

Carex ampullacea f. pendulina, commonly known as the weeping sedge, is a variant of the broader Carex ampullacea species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America, particularly in wetland and marshy environments. The plant is characterized by its drooping, pendulous inflorescences, which give it its common name. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, green leaves that form dense clumps.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
