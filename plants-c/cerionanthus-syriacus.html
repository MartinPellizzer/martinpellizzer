Cerionanthus syriacus

Cerionanthus syriacus, commonly known as the Syrian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Syria and Turkey.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
