Chamaesyce biconvexa

Chamaesyce biconvexa, commonly known as the two-convex sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a sprawling habit, often forming dense mats on sandy or rocky soils. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a waxy surface and a distinctive biconvex shape, contributing to its common name.

Chamaesyce biconvexa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dune habitats.
