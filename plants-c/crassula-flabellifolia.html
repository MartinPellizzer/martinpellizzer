Crassula flabellifolia

Crassula flabellifolia, commonly known as the "Flamboyant Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect sunlight.

Crassula flabellifolia is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
