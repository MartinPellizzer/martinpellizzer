Crotalaria parviflora

Crotalaria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria parviflora is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
