Cynoglossum officinale microcarpum

Cynoglossum officinale microcarpum, commonly known as lesser bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to Europe and western Asia. It is often found in meadows, waste areas, and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
