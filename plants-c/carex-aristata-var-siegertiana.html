Carex aristata var. siegertiana

Carex aristata var. siegertiana, commonly known as the Siegert's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
