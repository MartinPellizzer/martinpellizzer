Centaurea cassia subsp. cassia

Centaurea cassia subsp.

cassia, commonly known as the yellow star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has spiny stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature in some areas, it is considered a noxious weed in parts of North America.
