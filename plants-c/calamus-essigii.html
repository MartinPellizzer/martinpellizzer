Calamus essigii

Calamus essigii, commonly known as the Essig's bamboo reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves.

Native to the wetlands and marshy areas of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. The plant's rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Calamus essigii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
