Casearia prunifolia

Casearia prunifolia, commonly known as the roseapple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a crisp, juicy pulp with a sweet and slightly acidic flavor, and it is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Casearia prunifolia thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
