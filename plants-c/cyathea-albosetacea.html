Cyathea albosetacea

Cyathea albosetacea, commonly known as the white-setose tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant exhibits a unique feature of having white, silky hairs on its young fronds, which give it its common name. Cyathea albosetacea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
