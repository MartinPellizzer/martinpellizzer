Curio pinguifolius

Curio pinguifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed curio, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and often fleshy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
