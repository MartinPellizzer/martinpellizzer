Caragana arborescens f. pendula

Caragana arborescens f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping shrub willow, is a unique cultivar of the caragana genus.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it an ornamental appearance. This plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, with fine, feathery leaves that are green on top and pale underneath. It produces small yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
