Cirsium neumanni

Cirsium neumanni, commonly known as the Neumann's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Cirsium neumanni is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
