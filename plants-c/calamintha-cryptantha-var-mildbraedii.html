Calamintha cryptantha var. mildbraedii

Calamintha cryptantha var. mildbraedii, commonly known as the Sierra calamintha, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet to blue in color, and it produces small, round, hairy fruits.

This variety is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical form of Calamintha cryptantha.
