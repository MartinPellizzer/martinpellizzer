Calamintha nepeta subsp. glandulosa

Calamintha nepeta subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as glandular mint or sticky mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive scent and texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in whorled clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is often used in herbal medicine and as a fragrant ornamental plant.
