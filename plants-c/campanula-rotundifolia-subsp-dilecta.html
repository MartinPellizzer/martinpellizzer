Campanula rotundifolia subsp. dilecta

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

dilecta, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
