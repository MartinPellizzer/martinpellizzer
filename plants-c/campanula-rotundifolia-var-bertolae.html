Campanula rotundifolia var. bertolae

Campanula rotundifolia var. bertolae, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rounded, toothed leaves that are typically hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense colonies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
