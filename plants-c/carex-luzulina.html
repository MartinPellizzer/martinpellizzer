Carex luzulina

Carex luzulina, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland margins.

Carex luzulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
