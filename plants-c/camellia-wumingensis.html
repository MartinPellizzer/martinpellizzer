Camellia wumingensis

Camellia wumingensis, commonly known as the Wuming camellia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a lush backdrop to its striking blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
