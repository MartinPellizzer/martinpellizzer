Craccina ervoides

Craccina ervoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved craccus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along streambanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The small, pale blue or white flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.
