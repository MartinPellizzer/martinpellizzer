Cistus breviorifolius

Cistus breviorifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and greyish, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Cistus breviorifolius is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
