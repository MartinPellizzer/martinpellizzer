Chrysocoma monantha

Chrysocoma monantha, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chrysocoma monantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
