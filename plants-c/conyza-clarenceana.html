Conyza clarenceana

Conyza clarenceana, commonly known as Clarence's weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
