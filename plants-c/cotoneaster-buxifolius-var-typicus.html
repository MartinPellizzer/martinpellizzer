Cotoneaster buxifolius var. typicus

Cotoneaster buxifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the boxleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves that resemble those of boxwood. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
