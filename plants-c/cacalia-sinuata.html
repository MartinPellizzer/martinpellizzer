Cacalia sinuata

Cacalia sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the eastern and central regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy or sinuous margin, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets that bloom in late summer.
