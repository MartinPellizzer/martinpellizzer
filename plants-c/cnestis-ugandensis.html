Cnestis ugandensis

Cnestis ugandensis, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and it thrives in sandy or well-drained soils.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
