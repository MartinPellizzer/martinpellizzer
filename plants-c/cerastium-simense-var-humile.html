Cerastium simense var. humile

Cerastium simense var. humile, commonly known as dwarf white stitchwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.
