Caraipa richardiana var. typica

Caraipa richardiana var. typica, commonly known as the Caraipa orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green or white with a subtle fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
