Cleistochloa rigida

Cleistochloa rigida, commonly known as rigid cleistochloa, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, stiff stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, often invisible flowers. Cleistochloa rigida is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
