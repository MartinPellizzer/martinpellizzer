Crataegus viridimontana

Crataegus viridimontana, commonly known as the green mountain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 centimeters long and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
