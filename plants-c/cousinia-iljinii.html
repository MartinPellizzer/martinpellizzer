Cousinia iljinii

Cousinia iljinii, commonly known as the Iljin's cushion plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing cushion-like structure formed by tightly packed foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils at high elevations.

Cousinia iljinii is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a hardy species found in alpine and steppe habitats.
