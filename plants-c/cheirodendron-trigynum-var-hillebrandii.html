Cheirodendron trigynum var. hillebrandii

Cheirodendron trigynum var. hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red inflorescences that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental species. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The flowers are not only showy but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
