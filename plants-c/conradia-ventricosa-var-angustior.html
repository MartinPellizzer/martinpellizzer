Conradia ventricosa var. angustior

Conradia ventricosa var. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf conradia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
