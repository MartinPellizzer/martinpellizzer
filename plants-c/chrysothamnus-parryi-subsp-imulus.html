Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. imulus

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

imulus, commonly known as the Parry's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and dense, branching structure.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
