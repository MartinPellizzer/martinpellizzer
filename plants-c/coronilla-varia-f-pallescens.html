Coronilla varia f. pallescens

Coronilla varia f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale coronilla, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale green to silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
