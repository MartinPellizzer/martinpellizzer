Cylindropuntia hualpaensis

Cylindropuntia hualpaensis, commonly known as the Hualpaensis prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to rocky and well-drained soils.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide food and shelter for local wildlife.
