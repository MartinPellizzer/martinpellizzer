Crataegus crus-galli var. bellica

Crataegus crus-galli var. bellica, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a thorny shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its red, thimble-shaped fruits that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
