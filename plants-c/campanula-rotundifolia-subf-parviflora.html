Campanula rotundifolia subf. parviflora

Campanula rotundifolia subf.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are rounded and often hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
