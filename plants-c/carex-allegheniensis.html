Carex allegheniensis

Carex allegheniensis, commonly known as Allegheny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are usually dark green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or reddish flowers, which are wind-pollinated and contribute to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
