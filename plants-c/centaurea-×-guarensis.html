Centaurea × guarensis

Centaurea × guarensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea × guarensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
