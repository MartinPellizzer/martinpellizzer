Carmona lycioides

Carmona lycioides, commonly known as the desert rose or Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and gray-green in color, often covered in fine hairs.

Carmona lycioides is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry climates.
