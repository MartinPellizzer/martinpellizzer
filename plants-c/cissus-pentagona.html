Cissus pentagona

Cissus pentagona, commonly known as the Five-angled Cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, grape-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-draining soil.

Cissus pentagona is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
