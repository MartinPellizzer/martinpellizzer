Carex forsteri var. insularis

Carex forsteri var. insularis, commonly known as the island sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain island regions in the Pacific.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often green to bluish-green in color.

The flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
