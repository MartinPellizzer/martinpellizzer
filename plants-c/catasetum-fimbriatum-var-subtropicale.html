Catasetum fimbriatum var. subtropicale

Catasetum fimbriatum var. subtropicale, commonly known as the subtropical catasetum, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and its unique, pendulous flowers that often display a vibrant mix of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant produces specialized pollinia that are transferred to the pollinators during its distinctive pollination process. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
