Cucurbita maxima var. zipinka

Cucurbita maxima var. zipinka, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flesh and elongated, oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary versatility and ornamental appeal in gardens.
