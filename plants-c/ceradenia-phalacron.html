Ceradenia phalacron

Ceradenia phalacron, commonly known as the red-flowered ceradenia, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a unique, elongated spur that extends from the base of the flower. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees and hummingbirds, showcasing a fascinating mutualistic relationship.

Due to habitat loss, Ceradenia phalacron is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
