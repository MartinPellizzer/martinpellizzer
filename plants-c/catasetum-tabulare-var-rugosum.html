Catasetum tabulare var. rugosum

Catasetum tabulare var. rugosum, commonly known as the rugose catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating water retention.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration.
