Centaurea rupestris subsp. finazzeri

Centaurea rupestris subsp.

finazzeri, commonly known as the Finazzeri's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and striking blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescences compared to other members of the species. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing on limestone or sandy soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
