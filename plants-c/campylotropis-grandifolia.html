Campylotropis grandifolia

Campylotropis grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered campylotropis, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped fruits called legumes, which contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Campylotropis grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
