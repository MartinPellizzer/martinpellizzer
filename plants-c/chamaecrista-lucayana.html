Chamaecrista lucayana

Chamaecrista lucayana, commonly known as the Lucayan burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that enclose its seeds. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers are usually small and yellow or reddish, arranged in dense clusters.

Chamaecrista lucayana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
