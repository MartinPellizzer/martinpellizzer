Cornus sanguinea subsp. cilicica

Cornus sanguinea subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Caucasian dogwood, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its reddish stems and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
