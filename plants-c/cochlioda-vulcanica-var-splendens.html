Cochlioda vulcanica var. splendens

Cochlioda vulcanica var. splendens, commonly known as the Flame Spike, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions and is characterized by its vibrant red and orange bracts that resemble flames. The plant features upright, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
