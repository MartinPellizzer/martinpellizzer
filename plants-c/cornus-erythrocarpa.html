Cornus erythrocarpa

Cornus erythrocarpa, commonly known as red-fruited dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often found in moist woodlands and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
