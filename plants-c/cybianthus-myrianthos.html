Cybianthus myrianthos

Cybianthus myrianthos, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Cybianthus myrianthos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
