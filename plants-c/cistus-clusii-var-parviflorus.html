Cistus clusii var. parviflorus

Cistus clusii var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, aromatic leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in arid climates.
