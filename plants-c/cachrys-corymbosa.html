Cachrys corymbosa

Cachrys corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Cachrys corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
