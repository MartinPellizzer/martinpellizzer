Carludovica jonghei

Carludovica jonghei, commonly known as the Jonghei cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently in its arid habitat. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its spines are relatively short and soft, providing minimal protection against herbivores.

Carludovica jonghei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
