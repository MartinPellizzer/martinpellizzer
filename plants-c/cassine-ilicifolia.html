Cassine ilicifolia

Cassine ilicifolia, commonly known as the holly-leaf cassine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and a waxy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are often white or pale yellow in color.

Cassine ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
