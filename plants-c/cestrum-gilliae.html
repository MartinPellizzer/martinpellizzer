Cestrum gilliae

Cestrum gilliae, commonly known as the Gill's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in the evening. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
