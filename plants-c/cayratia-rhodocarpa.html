Cayratia rhodocarpa

Cayratia rhodocarpa, commonly known as the red-flowered cayratia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
