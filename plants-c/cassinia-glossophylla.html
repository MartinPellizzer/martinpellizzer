Cassinia glossophylla

Cassinia glossophylla, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spherical seed heads. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Cassinia glossophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
