Carex spicata

Carex spicata, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a tight rosette at the base.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
