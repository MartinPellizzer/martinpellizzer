Carex sadoensis var. integra

Carex sadoensis var. integra, commonly known as the Sado sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and sword-shaped, with a smooth texture.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
