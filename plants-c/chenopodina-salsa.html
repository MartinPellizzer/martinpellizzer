Chenopodina salsa

Chenopodina salsa, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is well adapted to high salinity and drought conditions, making it a valuable species for reclaiming degraded soils. Its roots have a high capacity to absorb and tolerate salt, which aids in soil stabilization.

Chenopodina salsa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
