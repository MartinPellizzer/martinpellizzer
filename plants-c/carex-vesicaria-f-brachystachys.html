Carex vesicaria f. brachystachys

Carex vesicaria f. brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked bladder sedge, is a variant of the bladder sedge species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include small, three-lobed bracts and a distinctive, bladder-like structure at the base of the inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
