Carex sempervirens f. albicans

Carex sempervirens f. albicans, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and shaded woodland areas.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
