Chenopodium paniculatum var. reniforme

Chenopodium paniculatum var. reniforme, commonly known as New Mexico goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, kidney-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is adapted to drought conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
