Cakile lacustris

Cakile lacustris, commonly known as the lake sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils near water bodies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in coastal or wetland environments.

Cakile lacustris is adapted to saline conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
