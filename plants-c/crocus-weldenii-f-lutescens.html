Crocus weldenii f. lutescens

Crocus weldenii f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered crocus, is a botanical variety characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to parts of southern Europe and the Mediterranean region. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped blooms in early spring. Its flowers typically have six petals and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and early blooming habit.
