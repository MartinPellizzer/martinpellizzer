Crucifera diversifolia

Crucifera diversifolia, commonly known as the variable crucifer, is a species belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive four-petaled flowers.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments and features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its flowers are often white or pale yellow, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Due to its variability in leaf shape and flower color, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
