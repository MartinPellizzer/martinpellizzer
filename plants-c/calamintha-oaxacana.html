Calamintha oaxacana

Calamintha oaxacana, commonly known as Mexican catnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. Its foliage emits a mint-like fragrance when crushed, and it is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Calamintha oaxacana thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
