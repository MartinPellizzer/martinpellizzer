Cinna pendula

Cinna pendula, commonly known as the hanging cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces oval, greenish-yellow berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cinna pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
