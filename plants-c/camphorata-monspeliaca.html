Camphorata monspeliaca

Camphorata monspeliaca, commonly known as the Mediterranean camphor tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are glossy and leathery, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Camphorata monspeliaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
