Centaurea gentyi

Centaurea gentyi, commonly known as Genty's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles.

Centaurea gentyi is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
