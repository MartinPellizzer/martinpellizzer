Cereus spinibarbis

Cereus spinibarbis, commonly known as the spiny-barbed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, a feature common among many cacti. Its root system is adapted to absorb water efficiently in dry environments.

Overall, Cereus spinibarbis is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant well-suited to desert conditions.
