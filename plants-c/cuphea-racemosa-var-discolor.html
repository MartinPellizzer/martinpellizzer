Cuphea racemosa var. discolor

Cuphea racemosa var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated cigar plant, is a shrub native to Central America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is popular in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
