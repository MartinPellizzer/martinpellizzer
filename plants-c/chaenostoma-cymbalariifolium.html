Chaenostoma cymbalariifolium

Chaenostoma cymbalariifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved chaenostoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
