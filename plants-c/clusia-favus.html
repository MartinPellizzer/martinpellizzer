Clusia favus

Clusia favus, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Clusia favus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
