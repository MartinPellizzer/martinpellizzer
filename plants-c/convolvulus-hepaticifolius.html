Convolvulus hepaticifolius

Convolvulus hepaticifolius, commonly known as liverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing and trailing plants.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
