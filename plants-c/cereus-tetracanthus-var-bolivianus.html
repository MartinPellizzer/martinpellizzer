Cereus tetracanthus var. bolivianus

Cereus tetracanthus var. bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian torch cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and has a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in desert gardens.
