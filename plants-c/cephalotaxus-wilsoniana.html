Cephalotaxus wilsoniana

Cephalotaxus wilsoniana, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to China.

It features a distinctive, compact pyramidal shape with dense, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, reddish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Cephalotaxus wilsoniana is also noted for its longevity and resistance to pests and diseases.
