Commelina polyclada

Commelina polyclada, commonly known as the spreading dayflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar characteristics. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It spreads through rhizomes and stolons, making it an aggressive weed in agricultural and natural areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its success in various soil types and climates.
