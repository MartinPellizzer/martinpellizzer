Carissa cordata

Carissa cordata, commonly known as the Natal plum or sweet apple bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring thick, woody stems with thorns. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly hairy surface. Its fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by yellow, plum-like berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
