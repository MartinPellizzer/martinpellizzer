Cuphea spicata f. robusta

Cuphea spicata f. robusta, commonly known as the spike cuphea, is a compact, bushy shrub native to Mexico.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in spikes, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or containers due to its compact growth habit.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and unique floral structure.
