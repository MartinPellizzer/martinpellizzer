Chamaecrista setosa var. detonsa

Chamaecrista setosa var. detonsa, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often accompanied by distinctive, feathery stipules. Its seeds are encased in narrow, elongated pods, typical of the legume family, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Chamaecrista setosa var. detonsa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
