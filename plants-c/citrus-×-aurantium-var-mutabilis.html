Citrus × aurantium var. mutabilis

Citrus × aurantium var. mutabilis, commonly known as the sour orange.

This citrus variant is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant is a shrub or small tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, subtropical climates.

The fruit is known for its high acidity and is a key ingredient in traditional recipes and herbal remedies.
