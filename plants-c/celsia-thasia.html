Celsia thasia

Celsia thasia, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, rounded stem covered in dense, woolly hair that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Celsia thasia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
