Cousinia pseudocirsium

Cousinia pseudocirsium, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. It has a compact growth habit and often forms dense mats or low shrubs.

Cousinia pseudocirsium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
