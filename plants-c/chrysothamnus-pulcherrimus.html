Chrysothamnus pulcherrimus

Chrysothamnus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the beautiful goldenbush, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chrysothamnus pulcherrimus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
