Chrysolaena candelabrum

Chrysolaena candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick plant, is a striking shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching structure that resembles a candlestick, with clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
