Clutia pulchella f. microphylla

Clutia pulchella f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf clutia, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for use in ornamental gardening and as a ground cover.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
