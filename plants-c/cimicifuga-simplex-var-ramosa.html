Cimicifuga simplex var. ramosa

Cimicifuga simplex var. ramosa, commonly known as the American black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems with compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are followed by red berries. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its potential health benefits.
