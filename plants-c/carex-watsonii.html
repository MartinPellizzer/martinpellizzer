Carex watsonii

Carex watsonii, commonly known as Watson's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to North America, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, flat leaves and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

It is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
