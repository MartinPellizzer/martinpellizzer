Clematis stans var. urticifolia

Clematis stans var. urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its long, flexible stems and is often found in moist, shaded woodland areas. Its common name refers to the stinging hairs on the stems and leaves, which can cause skin irritation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
