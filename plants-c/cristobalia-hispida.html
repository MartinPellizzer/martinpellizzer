Cristobalia hispida

Cristobalia hispida, commonly known as the rough cristobal, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
