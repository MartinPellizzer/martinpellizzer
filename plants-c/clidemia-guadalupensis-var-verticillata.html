Clidemia guadalupensis var. verticillata, commonly known as the Guadalupine mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
