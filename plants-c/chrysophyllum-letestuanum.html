Chrysophyllum letestuanum

Chrysophyllum letestuanum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellow fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine. Its fruit has a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, aromatic flavor.
