Catasetum discolor var. roseoalbum

Catasetum discolor var. roseoalbum, commonly known as the pink-tinged orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern, featuring a pale pink or white corolla and a contrasting yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers emit a faint fragrance, which plays a role in attracting specific pollinators.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
