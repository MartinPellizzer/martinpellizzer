Cucurbita pepo var. ovalis

Cucurbita pepo var. ovalis, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits.

The plant produces large, ribbed, and often hairy stems, with heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the fruit develops from the female flowers. The skin of the fruit is thick and typically a deep green color, while the flesh is pale yellow and sweet.

This variety is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various culinary applications.
