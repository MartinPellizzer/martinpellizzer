Calyptranthes aromatica

Calyptranthes aromatica, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Calyptranthes aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual beauty.
