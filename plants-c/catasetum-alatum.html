Catasetum alatum

Catasetum alatum, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like sepals and petals that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a unique structure that includes a specialized pollination mechanism.

Catasetum alatum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
