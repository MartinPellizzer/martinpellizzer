Cerasus hortensis var. acidula

Cerasus hortensis var. acidula, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, tart red or black fruits that are widely used in cooking, baking, and making jams and syrups. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges. It blooms with white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the characteristic sour cherries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
