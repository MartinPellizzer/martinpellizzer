Carex ornithopoda f. elongata

Carex ornithopoda f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
