Carex acuta var. trinervis

Carex acuta var. trinervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
