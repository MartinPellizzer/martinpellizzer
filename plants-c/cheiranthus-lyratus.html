Cheiranthus lyratus

Cheiranthus lyratus, commonly known as the lyreleaf candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in spring. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
