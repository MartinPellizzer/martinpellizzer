Ceropegia kamerunensis

Ceropegia kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Cameroon and neighboring regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive corona. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
