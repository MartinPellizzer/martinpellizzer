Cousinia elwendensis

Cousinia elwendensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri cushion plant, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Cousinia elwendensis is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of alpine ecosystems.
