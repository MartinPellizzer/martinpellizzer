Callistemon comboynensis

Callistemon comboynensis, commonly known as the Comb-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical red or pink flower spikes, which are produced in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Callistemon comboynensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
