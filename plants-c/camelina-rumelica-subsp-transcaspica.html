Camelina rumelica subsp. transcaspica

Camelina rumelica subsp.

transcaspica, commonly known as the Transcaspian camelina, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important oilseed crops.

The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves, adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in steppe and desert regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a potential candidate for agricultural use in drought-prone areas.
