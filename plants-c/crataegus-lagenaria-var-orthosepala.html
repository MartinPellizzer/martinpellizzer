Crataegus lagenaria var. orthosepala

Crataegus lagenaria var. orthosepala, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
