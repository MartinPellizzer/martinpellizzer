Celastrus europaeus

Celastrus europaeus, commonly known as European bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. Its rapid growth and invasive nature make it a common weed in gardens and disturbed soils.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
