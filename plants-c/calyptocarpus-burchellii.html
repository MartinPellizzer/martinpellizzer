Calyptocarpus burchellii

Calyptocarpus burchellii, commonly known as the African cypress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in saline environments.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
