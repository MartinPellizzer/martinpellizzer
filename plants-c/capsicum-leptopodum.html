Capsicum leptopodum

Capsicum leptopodum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chili or the slender pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. This plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellow or orange fruits that mature to a fiery red. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and potential culinary use.
