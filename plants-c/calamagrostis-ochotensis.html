Calamagrostis ochotensis

Calamagrostis ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, and is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy flowers that appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis ochotensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife in its native habitat.
