Croton fluviatilis

Croton fluviatilis, commonly known as the river croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Croton fluviatilis thrives in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and in dense forests.
