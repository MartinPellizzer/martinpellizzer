Cachrys vaginata

Cachrys vaginata, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its inflated, pod-like seed cases that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Cachrys vaginata produces small white or pale yellow flowers and is often found in sandy or gravelly areas.
