Croton sparsiflorus

Croton sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant typically features alternate, simple leaves that vary in shape and coloration. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or yellow, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
