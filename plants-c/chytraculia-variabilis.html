Chytraculia variabilis

Chytraculia variabilis, commonly known as the variable chytrac, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-colored leaves that shift in hue depending on light exposure, a trait linked to its adaptive photosynthetic mechanisms. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is particularly complex, forming symbiotic relationships with specific fungi that enhance nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, Chytraculia variabilis is now classified as a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
