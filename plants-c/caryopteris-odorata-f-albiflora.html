Caryopteris odorata f. albiflora

Caryopteris odorata f. albiflora, commonly known as the fragrant bluemouth, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic when crushed, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both landscape design and herbal use.
