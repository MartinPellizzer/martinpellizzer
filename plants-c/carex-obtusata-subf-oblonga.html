Carex obtusata subf. oblonga

Carex obtusata subf.

oblonga, commonly known as broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers, and it produces distinctive, three-angled berries. Carex obtusata subf.

oblonga is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
