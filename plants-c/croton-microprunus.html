Croton microprunus

Croton microprunus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of red, yellow, and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
