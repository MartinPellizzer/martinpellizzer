Capparis sicula subsp. leucophylla

Capparis sicula subsp.

leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaved caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its pale green to white leaves, which are often covered in a fine, woolly layer.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, tangy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
