Combretum petitianum

Combretum petitianum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
