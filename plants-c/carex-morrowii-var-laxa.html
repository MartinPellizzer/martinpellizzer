Carex morrowii var. laxa

Carex morrowii var. laxa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like flower clusters that are often tinged with purple or brown, appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
