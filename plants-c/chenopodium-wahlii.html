Chenopodium wahlii

Chenopodium wahlii, commonly known as wild spinach, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central and South America and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often used as a food source. Chenopodium wahlii is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
