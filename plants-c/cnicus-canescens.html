Cnicus canescens

Cnicus canescens, commonly known as the Hoary cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It is a perennial species with a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
