Corallocarpus glomeratus

Corallocarpus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, coral-like clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets arranged in pairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Corallocarpus glomeratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
