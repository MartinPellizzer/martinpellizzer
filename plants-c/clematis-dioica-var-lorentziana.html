Clematis dioica var. lorentziana

Clematis dioica var. lorentziana, commonly known as the Lorentziana Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
