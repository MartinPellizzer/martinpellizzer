Clerodendrum incisum var. vinosum

Clerodendrum incisum var. vinosum, commonly known as the purple-veined cleerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple leaves with prominent veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
