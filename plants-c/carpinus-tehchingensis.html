Carpinus tehchingensis

Carpinus tehchingensis, commonly known as the Tehching hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow fissures, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
