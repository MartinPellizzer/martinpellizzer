Claytonia parvifolia var. depressa

Claytonia parvifolia var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf white groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, waxy, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has rosettes of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Montiaceae family.
