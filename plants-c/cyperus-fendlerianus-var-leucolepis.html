Cyperus fendlerianus var. leucolepis

Cyperus fendlerianus var. leucolepis, commonly known as the white-veined flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed spikelets that are often reddish in color.

This species is often considered an indicator plant for disturbed or wetland habitats due to its adaptability and prevalence in such environments.
