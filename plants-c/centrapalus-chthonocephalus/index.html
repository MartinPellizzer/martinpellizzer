Centrapalus Chthonocephalus

Ghost plant, scientifically known as Centrapalus chthonocephalus, is a rare and enigmatic species found in the deep, dark environments of tropical rainforests. It thrives in nutrient-poor soil and relies on specialized symbiotic relationships with fungi for survival. Despite its name, it is not a true plant but a type of liverwort, belonging to the group known as mosses. Its translucent, ghostly appearance and elusive nature have made it a subject of fascination for botanists. This unique organism highlights the adaptability of life in extreme ecological conditions.
