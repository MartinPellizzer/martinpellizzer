Centaurea kanitziana

Centaurea kanitziana, commonly known as the Kanitz's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a branched stem that grows up to 30 centimeters tall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Centaurea kanitziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
