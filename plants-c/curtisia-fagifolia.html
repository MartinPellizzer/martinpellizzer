Curtisia fagifolia

Curtisia fagifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed drupe that matures to a reddish color.

Curtisia fagifolia is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is often used in furniture making and as a source of tannins.
