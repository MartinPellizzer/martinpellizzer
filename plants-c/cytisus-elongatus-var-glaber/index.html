Cytisus Elongatus Var. Glaber

Cytisus elongatus var. glaber is a rare and intriguing species of broom plant native to parts of southern Europe. It is distinguished by its slender, glabrous stems and delicate yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. This variant is less commonly seen than its more widespread relatives, making it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts. Its unique characteristics make it valuable for ecological studies and conservation efforts. Understanding this plant helps highlight the diversity within the Cytisus genus.
