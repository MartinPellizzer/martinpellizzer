Camellia multipetala

Camellia multipetala, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia multipetala is also valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its winter blooming period.
