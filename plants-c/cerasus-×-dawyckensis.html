Cerasus × dawyckensis

Cerasus × dawyckensis, commonly known as the Dawyck cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red fruits that are edible but typically not cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The Dawyck cherry is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
