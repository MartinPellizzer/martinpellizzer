Carex kamikochiana

Carex kamikochiana, commonly known as the Kamikochan sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.

Carex kamikochiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
