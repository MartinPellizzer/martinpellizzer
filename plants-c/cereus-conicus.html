Cereus conicus

Cereus conicus, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of white, night-blooming flowers. The plant is a succulent, storing water in its fleshy stems to survive in arid environments. Its flowers open only during the night, releasing a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
