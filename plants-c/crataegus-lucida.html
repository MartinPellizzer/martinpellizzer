Crataegus lucida

Crataegus lucida, commonly known as the shining hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus lucida is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
