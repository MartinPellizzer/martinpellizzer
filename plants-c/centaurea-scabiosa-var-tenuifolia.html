Centaurea scabiosa var. tenuifolia

Centaurea scabiosa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its delicate, spindly stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while the plant produces a distinctive, spiky seed head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
