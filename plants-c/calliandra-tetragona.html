Calliandra tetragona

Calliandra tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled calliandra, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and showy, pink or red flower clusters. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for warm climates.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop.
