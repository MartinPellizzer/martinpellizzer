Camellia longicarpa

Camellia longicarpa, commonly known as the longpod camellia, is a species native to China and is part of the Theaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

Its fruits are woody capsules that contain seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
