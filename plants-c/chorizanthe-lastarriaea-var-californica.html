Chorizanthe lastarriaea var. californica

Chorizanthe lastarriaea var. californica, commonly known as the California tansy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It produces small, dry fruits called utricles, which contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.
