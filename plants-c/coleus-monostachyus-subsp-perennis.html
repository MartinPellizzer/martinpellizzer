Coleus monostachyus subsp. perennis

Coleus monostachyus subsp.

perennis, commonly known as the perennial coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
