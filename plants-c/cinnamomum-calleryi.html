Cinnamomum calleryi

Cinnamomum calleryi, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or fragrant cypress, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The wood of Cinnamomum calleryi is also valued for its durability and aromatic qualities.
