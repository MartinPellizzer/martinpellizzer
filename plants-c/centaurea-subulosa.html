Centaurea subulosa

Centaurea subulosa, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, and its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Centaurea subulosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
