Cyathea macarthurii

Cyathea macarthurii, commonly known as the MacArthur's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive trunk that is covered in remnants of old fronds, giving it a unique, ancient appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Cyathea macarthurii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
