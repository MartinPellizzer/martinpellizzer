Cymbopogon rufus var. major

Cymbopogon rufus var. major, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and tall, slender flowering stalks. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
