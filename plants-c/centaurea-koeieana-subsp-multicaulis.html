Centaurea koeieana subsp. multicaulis

Centaurea koeieana subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the multi-stemmed centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of bright red to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as achenes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
