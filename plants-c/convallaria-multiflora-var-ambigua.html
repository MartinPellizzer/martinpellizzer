Convallaria multiflora var. ambigua

Convallaria multiflora var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces red berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
