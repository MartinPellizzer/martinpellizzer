Cyperus cinnamomeus

Cyperus cinnamomeus, commonly known as cinnamon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its ornamental value comes from its striking reddish-brown coloration, which resembles cinnamon, hence its common name.

Cyperus cinnamomeus is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
