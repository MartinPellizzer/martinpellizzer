Cucumis melo var. gracilior

Cucumis melo var. gracilior, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a cultivated variety of melon characterized by its long, slender fruits.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant typically grows on a vigorous vine, producing pale yellow flowers that precede the fruit development. Its fruits are usually thin-skinned, elongated, and have a mild, sweet flavor.

This variety is often grown for both ornamental and edible purposes due to its unique appearance and taste.
