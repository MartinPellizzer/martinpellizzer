Cineraria integrifolia var. depauperata

Cineraria integrifolia var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
