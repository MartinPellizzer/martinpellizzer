Calamagrostis erecta

Calamagrostis erecta, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a tall, grass-like perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, with narrow, silver-gray blades that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds in the fall, which are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
