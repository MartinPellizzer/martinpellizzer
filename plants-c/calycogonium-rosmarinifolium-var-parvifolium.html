Calycogonium rosmarinifolium var. parvifolium

Calycogonium rosmarinifolium var. parvifolium, commonly known as the dwarf rosemary.

It is a compact shrub with aromatic, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its fragrant properties.
