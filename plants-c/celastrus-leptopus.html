Celastrus leptopus

Celastrus leptopus, commonly known as the slender staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental berries make it both ecologically and botanically significant in its native habitat.
