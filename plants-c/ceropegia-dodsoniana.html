Ceropegia dodsoniana

Ceropegia dodsoniana, commonly known as the Dodson's African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
