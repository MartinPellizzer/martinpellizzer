Cytisus × praecox albus

Cytisus × praecox albus, commonly known as the white broom, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural habitat.
