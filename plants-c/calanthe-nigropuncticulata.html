Calanthe nigropuncticulata

Calanthe nigropuncticulata, commonly known as the black-spotted calanthe, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Thailand. The plant features striking, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its inflorescence is typically a raceme of small, lance-shaped blooms.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens and greenhouses.
