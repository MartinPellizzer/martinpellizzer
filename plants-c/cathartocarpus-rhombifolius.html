Cathartocarpus rhombifolius

Cathartocarpus rhombifolius, commonly known as the rhombifoliate grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
