Carex displodensis

Carex displodensis, commonly known as the Displodens sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
