Caesalpinia vernalis

Caesalpinia vernalis, commonly known as the flame bean or spring glory, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual display. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, palmate leaves, and a distinctive floral structure with bright colors and prominent stamens.

Caesalpinia vernalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
