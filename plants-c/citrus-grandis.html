Citrus grandis

Citrus grandis, commonly known as the longan tree, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, round fruits with a smooth, thin skin that turns from green to reddish when mature. The fruit contains a single large seed and is known for its sweet, fragrant pulp. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
