Carex scopulorum var. subconcoloroides

Carex scopulorum var. subconcoloroides, commonly known as the subconcoloroides sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and stiff, with a narrow, cylindrical shape. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive leaf coloration and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological value.
