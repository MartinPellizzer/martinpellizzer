Cnesmocarpon discoloroides

Cnesmocarpon discoloroides, commonly known as the discolored cup fungus, is a small, cup-shaped fungus that belongs to the family Cortinariaceae.

It is characterized by its pale to reddish-brown cap and a smooth, often sticky surface. The species is typically found in forested areas, growing on decaying wood or leaf litter. Its microscopic features include spores that are ellipsoid and smooth, which aid in identification.

This fungus is not particularly notable for its medicinal properties but is of interest to mycologists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
