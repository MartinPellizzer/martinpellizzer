Capsicum annuum f. violaceum

Capsicum annuum f. violaceum, commonly known as the purple bell pepper, is a botanical variant of the common chili pepper.

It is characterized by its distinctive deep violet to purple coloration, which is most pronounced when the fruit is mature. The plant typically produces elongated, bell-shaped fruits that are often harvested before full maturity for optimal color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for healthy growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it popular in both culinary and garden settings.
