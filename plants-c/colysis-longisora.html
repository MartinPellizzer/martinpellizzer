Colysis longisora

Colysis longisora, commonly known as the long-stalked colysis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are usually pubescent, with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Colysis longisora is often found in shaded undergrowth and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
