Cyphostemma marlothii

Cyphostemma marlothii, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, horn-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
