Crocodilodes polyacantha

Crocodilodes polyacantha, commonly known as the many-thorned crocodilodes, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, pale green leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded environments.
