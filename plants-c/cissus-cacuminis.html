Cissus cacuminis

Cissus cacuminis, commonly known as the starleaf goosefoot, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves. The plant has thin, tendrils that allow it to climb and spread over surfaces. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Cissus cacuminis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
