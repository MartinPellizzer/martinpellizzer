Cyclosorus paucipaleatus

Cyclosorus paucipaleatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a small aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, fan-shaped fronds that are often divided into narrow, ribbon-like segments. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Cyclosorus paucipaleatus is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
