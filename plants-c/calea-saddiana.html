Calea saddiana

Calea saddiana, commonly known as the sacred candleplant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Calea saddiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional healing practices.
