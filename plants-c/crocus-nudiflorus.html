Crocus nudiflorus

Crocus nudiflorus, commonly known as the naked-flowered crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pale blue. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The bulbs of Crocus nudiflorus are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
