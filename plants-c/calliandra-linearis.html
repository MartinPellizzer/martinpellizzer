Calliandra linearis

Calliandra linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or pink tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological gardens.

Calliandra linearis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sunlight.
