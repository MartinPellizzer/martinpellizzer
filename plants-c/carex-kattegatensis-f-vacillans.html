Carex kattegatensis f. vacillans

Carex kattegatensis f. vacillans, commonly known as the Kattegat sedge variant, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. This plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it may exhibit slight variations in leaf texture compared to the typical form.

It is a relatively rare plant, often found in specific regions of northern Europe.
