Centaurea cheirolopha

Centaurea cheirolopha, commonly known as the pink centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers display a mix of pink and white hues.

Centaurea cheirolopha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and resilience.
