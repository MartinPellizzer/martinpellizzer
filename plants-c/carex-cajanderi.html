Carex cajanderi

Carex cajanderi, commonly known as the Cajander sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular stems and grass-like leaves, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Carex cajanderi plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
