Cryptomeria nana

Cryptomeria nana, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cedar, is a small evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features scale-like leaves that are densely packed on short shoots, giving it a compact, shrub-like appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
