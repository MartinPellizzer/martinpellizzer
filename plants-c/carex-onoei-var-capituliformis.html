Carex onoei var. capituliformis

Carex onoei var. capituliformis, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
