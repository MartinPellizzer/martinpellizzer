Costus glabratus var. niveopurpureus

Costus glabratus var. niveopurpureus, commonly known as the Purple-veined Costus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with prominent purple veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are usually yellow or orange with a red or purple throat, arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
