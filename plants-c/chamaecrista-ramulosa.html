Chamaecrista ramulosa

Chamaecrista ramulosa, commonly known as the slender ticktree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers in dense clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is part of the legume family, Fabaceae.

The plant is adapted to various soil types and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.
