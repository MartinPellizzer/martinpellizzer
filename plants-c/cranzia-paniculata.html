Cranzia paniculata

Cranzia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled cranza, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cranzia paniculata is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and floral structure.
