Cereus horribarbis

Cereus horribarbis, commonly known as the devil's claw, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are pollinated by moths, which are attracted to its strong scent.

The plant is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
