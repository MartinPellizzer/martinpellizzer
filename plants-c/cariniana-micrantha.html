Cariniana micrantha

Cariniana micrantha, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or piquiá, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically 30–50 cm in length. The tree produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its seeds are rich in oil and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
