Calea suffruticosa

Calea suffruticosa, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has hairy, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calea suffruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
