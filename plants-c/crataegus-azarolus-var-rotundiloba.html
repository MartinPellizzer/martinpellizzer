Crataegus azarolus var. rotundiloba

Crataegus azarolus var. rotundiloba, commonly known as the Persian white thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and autumn foliage.
