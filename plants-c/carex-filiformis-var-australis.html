Carex filiformis var. australis

Carex filiformis var. australis, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common trait among sedges. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as swampy areas or along waterways, and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
