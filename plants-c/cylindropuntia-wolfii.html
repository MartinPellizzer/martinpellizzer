Cylindropuntia wolfii

Cylindropuntia wolfii, commonly known as the wolf's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its adaptations to arid environments include a reduced leaf surface and the ability to store water in its stems.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
