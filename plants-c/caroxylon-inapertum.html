Caroxylon inapertum

Caroxylon inapertum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.
