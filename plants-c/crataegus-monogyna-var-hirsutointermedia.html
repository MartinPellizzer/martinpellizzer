Crataegus monogyna var. hirsutointermedia

Crataegus monogyna var. hirsutointermedia, commonly known as the hirsute-intermedia hawthorn, is a variety of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge and a slightly hairy texture. This plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges across Europe.
