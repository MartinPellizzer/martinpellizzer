Cissus dregeana

Cissus dregeana, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
