Carex serotina var. fennica

Carex serotina var. fennica, commonly known as the Finnish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
