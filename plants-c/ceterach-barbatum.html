Ceterach barbatum

Ceterach barbatum, commonly known as the hairy sea sparrow, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.
