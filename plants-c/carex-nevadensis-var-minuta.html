Carex nevadensis var. minuta

Carex nevadensis var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf tule, is a compact sedge species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, meadows, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually a deep green color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the local ecosystem, supporting various insects and wildlife.
