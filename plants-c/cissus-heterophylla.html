Cissus heterophylla

Cissus heterophylla, commonly known as the variegated cutleaf grape, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that vary in shape and size along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
