Calamus tigrinus

Calamus tigrinus, commonly known as tiger bamboo, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often display a striped or mottled pattern resembling a tiger's markings. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Calamus tigrinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and medicine in some cultures.
