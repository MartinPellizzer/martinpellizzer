Conyza ageratoides

Conyza ageratoides, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Conyza ageratoides is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
