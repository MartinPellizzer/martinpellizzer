Carapa hygrophila

Carapa hygrophila, commonly known as the water mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Carapa hygrophila is valued for its hard, heavy wood and has traditional uses in various indigenous communities.
