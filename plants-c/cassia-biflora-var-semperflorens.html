Cassia biflora var. semperflorens

Cassia biflora var. semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers throughout the year. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its continuous flowering and aromatic foliage.
