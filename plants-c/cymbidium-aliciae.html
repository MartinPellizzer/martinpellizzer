Cymbidium aliciae

Cymbidium aliciae, commonly known as the Alice's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the winter months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers.

Cymbidium aliciae is relatively easy to care for, requiring moderate light and consistent moisture to thrive.
