Cyperus rotundus var. pseudotuberosus

Cyperus rotundus var. pseudotuberosus, commonly known as nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, sedge-like flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant has tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in a variety of soil conditions. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, rice fields, and disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and spreading nature make it both a common weed and an important resource in some traditional medicinal practices.
