Carex gracilis subsp. eugracilis

Carex gracilis subsp.

eugracilis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream edges. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is often found in temperate regions of North America and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
