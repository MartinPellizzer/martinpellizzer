Clidemia simplicicaulis

Clidemia simplicicaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive silver underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions, though it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
