Capparis cynophallophora f. intermedia

Capparis cynophallophora f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate caper, is a botanical variant of the caper plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Capparaceae family. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting berries that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and biodiversity.
