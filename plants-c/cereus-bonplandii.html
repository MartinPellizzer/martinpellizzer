Cereus bonplandii

Cereus bonplandii, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce striking white flowers at night. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
