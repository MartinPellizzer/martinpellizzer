Cheirodendron trigynum var. confertiflorum

Cheirodendron trigynum var. confertiflorum, commonly known as the thick-flowered cheirodendron, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface. The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
