Commelina benghalensis subsp. hirsuta

Commelina benghalensis subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and are often covered in fine, hairy structures.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
