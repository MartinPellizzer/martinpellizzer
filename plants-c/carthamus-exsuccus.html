Carthamus exsuccus

Carthamus exsuccus, commonly known as the Egyptian saffron or Persian saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a unique yellow-orange dye from its dried stigmas, which has been historically used for coloring textiles and food. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a fibrous root system.

Carthamus exsuccus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.
