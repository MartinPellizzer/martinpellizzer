Calophyllum piluliferum

Calophyllum piluliferum, commonly known as the pilule tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that resemble tiny pills, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
