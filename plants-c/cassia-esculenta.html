Cassia esculenta

Cassia esculenta, commonly known as sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia esculenta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
