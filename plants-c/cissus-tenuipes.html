Cissus tenuipes

Cissus tenuipes, commonly known as the creeping grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and vertical gardens.
