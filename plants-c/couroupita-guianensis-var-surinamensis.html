Couroupita guianensis var. surinamensis

Couroupita guianensis var. surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Suriname and Guyana.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a distinctive fruit with a star-shaped calyx, which turns from green to yellow as it ripens. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary attributes.
