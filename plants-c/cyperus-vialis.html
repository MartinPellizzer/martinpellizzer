Cyperus vialis

Cyperus vialis, commonly known as the common flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color. Cyperus vialis is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
