Crepis autaretica

Crepis autaretica, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are hairy and form a rosette, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Crepis autaretica is often found in meadows and on slopes, contributing to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.
