Chenopodium eastwoodiae

Chenopodium eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in rocky areas and is considered a rare species due to its limited distribution.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
