Calystegia sepium subsp. roseata

Calystegia sepium subsp.

roseata, commonly known as the rose-flowered bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The flowers are typically pink to rose-colored, with five petals and a prominent central spur. This subspecies is known for its strong climbing ability and can become invasive in certain environments.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.
