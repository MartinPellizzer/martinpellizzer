Chaetogastra dichotoma

Chaetogastra dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous chaetogastra, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, with stems splitting into two at each node, creating a dichotomous structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically narrow and adapted to reduce water loss, making it well-suited to its harsh environment.

Chaetogastra dichotoma is also known for its ability to regenerate from its root system after periods of drought.
