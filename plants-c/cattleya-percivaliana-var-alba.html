Cattleya percivaliana var. alba

Cattleya percivaliana var. alba, commonly known as the white Cattleya percivaliana, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, white flowers that often have a slight pink or purple tinge at the base. The plant produces broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting mix.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
