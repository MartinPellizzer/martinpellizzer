Carica leptantha

Carica leptantha, commonly known as the slender papaya, is a species of flowering plant in the Caricaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and it has a short, fibrous stem.

The slender papaya is valued both for its edible fruit and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
