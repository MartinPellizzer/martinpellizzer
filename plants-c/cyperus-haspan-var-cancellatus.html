Cyperus haspan var. cancellatus

Cyperus haspan var. cancellatus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth habits. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the midrib, giving it its common name. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-ranked flowers characteristic of the sedge family.
