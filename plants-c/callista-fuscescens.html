Callista fuscescens

Callista fuscescens, commonly known as the dark red lily, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking dark red to almost black petals, which contrast with its pale green foliage. The plant typically grows in waterlogged soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
