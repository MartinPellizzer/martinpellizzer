Coussarea suaveolens

Coussarea suaveolens, commonly known as sweet-scented coussarea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and attractive foliage.
