Capparis biloba

Capparis biloba, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red capparis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, often thorny foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Capparis biloba is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
