Cadetia quinquenervia

Cadetia quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Cadetia quinquenervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
