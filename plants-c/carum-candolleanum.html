Carum candolleanum

Carum candolleanum, commonly known as the Indian cumin or wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.

Its botanical features make it both a culinary and medicinal plant with historical significance.
