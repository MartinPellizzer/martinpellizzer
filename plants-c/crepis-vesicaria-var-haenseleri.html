Crepis vesicaria var. haenseleri

Crepis vesicaria var. haenseleri, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and often lobed, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
