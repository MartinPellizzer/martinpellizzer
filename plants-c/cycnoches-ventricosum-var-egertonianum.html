Cycnoches ventricosum var. egertonianum

Cycnoches ventricosum var. egertonianum, commonly known as the Egertonian moonflower, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals and a distinctive, fragrant flower that opens at night. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip that serves as a nectar source for nocturnal pollinators.

The plant is prized in horticulture for its beauty and rarity, making it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
