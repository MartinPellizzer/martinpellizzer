Cryptanthus univittatus

Cryptanthus univittatus, commonly known as the red-tipped cryptanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tint.

Cryptanthus univittatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant colors and relatively easy care requirements.
