Crataegus monogyna f. filicifolia

Crataegus monogyna f. filicifolia, commonly known as the leafy hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is typically found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
