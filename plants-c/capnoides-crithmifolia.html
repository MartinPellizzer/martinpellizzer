Capnoides crithmifolia

Capnoides crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and silver-gray, aromatic leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates. Capnoides crithmifolia is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers arranged in clusters.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
