Clerodendrum cubense

Clerodendrum cubense, commonly known as the Cuban flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Clerodendrum cubense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
