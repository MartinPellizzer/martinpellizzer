Chirita polyneura var. amabilis

Chirita polyneura var. amabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf chireta, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
