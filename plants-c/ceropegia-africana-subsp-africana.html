Ceropegia africana subsp. africana

Ceropegia africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to spread rapidly.
