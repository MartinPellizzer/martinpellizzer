Crassula sinclairii

Crassula sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclaire's jade.

It is a succulent plant native to South Africa, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant forms dense clusters of rosettes, with leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or summer.

Crassula sinclairii is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
