Chamaecrista setosa

Chamaecrista setosa, commonly known as the hairy burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, hairy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a slender, twisted pod that contains several seeds.

Chamaecrista setosa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a medicinal plant.
