Cyathea tripinnatifida

Cyathea tripinnatifida, commonly known as the three-finger tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with fronds that are divided into three main sections, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value.
