Cyathula achyranthoides

Cyathula achyranthoides, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in cyathia, which are cup-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Cyathula achyranthoides is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
