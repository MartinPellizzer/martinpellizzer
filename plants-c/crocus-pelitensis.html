Crocus pelitensis

Crocus pelitensis, commonly known as the Sicilian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily, and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during late winter to early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry conditions, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Crocus pelitensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
