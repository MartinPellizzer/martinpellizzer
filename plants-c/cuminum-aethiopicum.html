Cuminum aethiopicum

Cuminum aethiopicum, commonly known as Ethiopian cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has feathery, aromatic leaves. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent aroma.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid regions.
