Ciconium lanuginosum

Ciconium lanuginosum, commonly known as the downy ciconium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
