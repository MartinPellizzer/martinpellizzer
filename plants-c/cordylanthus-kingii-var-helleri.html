Cordylanthus kingii var. helleri

Cordylanthus kingii var. helleri, commonly known as Heller's woollypod, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, often glandular foliage. Its flowers are small and usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
