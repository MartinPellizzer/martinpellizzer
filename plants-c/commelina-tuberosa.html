Commelina tuberosa

Commelina tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower clusters. The plant has tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in various soil conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
