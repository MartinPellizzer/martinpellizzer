Cystopteris mexicana

Cystopteris mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican water fern, is a species of fern native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Cystopteris mexicana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
