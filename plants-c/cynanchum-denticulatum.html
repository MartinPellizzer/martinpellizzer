Cynanchum denticulatum

Cynanchum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic.

Cynanchum denticulatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
