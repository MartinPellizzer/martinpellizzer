Cassia spinulosa

Cassia spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Cassia spinulosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
