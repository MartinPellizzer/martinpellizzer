Crataegus beckiana

Crataegus beckiana, commonly known as the Beck's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly in the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant often grows as a shrub or small tree.

Crataegus beckiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
