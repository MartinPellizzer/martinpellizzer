Cyrtopera longifolia var. amazonica

Cyrtopera longifolia var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian mistflower, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
