Castanopsis calathiformis

Castanopsis calathiformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed tanoak, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, chestnuts, and beeches. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its small, cup-shaped flowers are followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule.

This species is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
