Cerasus nakaii

Cerasus nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai cherry, is a species of shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan and China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically red or pink in color. The plant produces fragrant white or pale pink flowers in early spring, preceding the development of its fruit. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and is also valued for its fruit in some regions.
