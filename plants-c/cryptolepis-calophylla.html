Cryptolepis calophylla

Cryptolepis calophylla, commonly known as the African bitters or bitterleaf, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential medicinal studies.
