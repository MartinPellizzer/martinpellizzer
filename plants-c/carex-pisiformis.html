Carex pisiformis

Carex pisiformis, commonly known as the small-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

Carex pisiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
