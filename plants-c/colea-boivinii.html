Colea boivinii

Colea boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's cobra lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the spadix, which is often a deep red or orange.

Colea boivinii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
