Cornus alpina

Cornus alpina, commonly known as the alpine dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by red or orange berries that attract birds. Its leaves are elliptical and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the autumn.

Cornus alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
