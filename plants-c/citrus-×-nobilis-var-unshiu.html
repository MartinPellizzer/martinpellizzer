Citrus × nobilis var. unshiu

Citrus × nobilis var. unshiu, commonly known as the honey柚 or unshiu mandarin, is a citrus fruit native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and seedless fruit with a thin, loose rind. The plant is a hybrid of the mandarin and pomelo, belonging to the Rutaceae family. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, while the flowers are fragrant and white.
