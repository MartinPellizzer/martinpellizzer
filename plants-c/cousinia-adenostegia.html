Cousinia adenostegia

Cousinia adenostegia, commonly known as the Armenian carpet grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with silver-gray foliage that helps it withstand harsh conditions.

Cousinia adenostegia is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
