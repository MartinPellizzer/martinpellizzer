Cyperus virens

Cyperus virens, commonly known as the green flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, along waterways, and in disturbed sites. Its slender, grass-like stems are often mistaken for true grasses, but they have three-sided stems characteristic of the sedge family.

Cyperus virens produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
