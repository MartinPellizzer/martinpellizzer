Cyathea novoguineensis

Cyathea novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its distinctive and imposing appearance.

Cyathea novoguineensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
