Carex cinnamomea

Carex cinnamomea, commonly known as cinnamon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually a dark green color with a waxy coating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Cinnamon sedge is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
