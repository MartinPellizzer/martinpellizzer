Comarostaphylis spinulosa subsp. glandulifera

Comarostaphylis spinulosa subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have glandular dots along the veins.

This subspecies is distinguished by its glandular structures and is part of the Asteraceae family.
