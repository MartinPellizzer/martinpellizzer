Catasetum cornutum

Catasetum cornutum, commonly known as the horned catasetum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, including a specialized column that facilitates pollination by specific bee species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that often resembles a bee in appearance. Catasetum cornutum typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its intricate pollination mechanism and its role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
