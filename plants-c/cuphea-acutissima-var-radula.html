Cuphea acutissima var. radula

Cuphea acutissima var. radula, commonly known as the flame wheel plant, is a striking ornamental species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red bracts that surround clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, adding contrast to the showy bracts.

It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping due to its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
