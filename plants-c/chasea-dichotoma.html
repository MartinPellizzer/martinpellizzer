Chasea dichotoma

Chasea dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is common among grass species.

Chasea dichotoma plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
