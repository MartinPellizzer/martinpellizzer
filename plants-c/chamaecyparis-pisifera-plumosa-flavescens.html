Chamaecyparis pisifera plumosa-flavescens

Chamaecyparis pisifera plumosa-flavescens, commonly known as the golden thread cedar, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its fine, feathery foliage that turns a golden-yellow in the summer. The plant has a narrow, columnar growth habit, making it suitable for small spaces. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often used as an ornamental tree in gardens.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance.
