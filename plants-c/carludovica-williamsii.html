Carludovica williamsii

Carludovica williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green or blue-green in color, often covered with white spines. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
