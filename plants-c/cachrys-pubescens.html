Cachrys pubescens

Cachrys pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cypress, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pubescent foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a leathery texture.

Cachrys pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
