Chimonanthus praecox f. intermedius

Chimonanthus praecox f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate fragrant winter cherry, is a hybrid form of the fragrant winter cherry, characterized by its unique floral and foliar features.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, typically growing in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, particularly during late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking winter blooms.
