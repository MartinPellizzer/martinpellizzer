Chamaecyparis pisifera var. plumosa

Chamaecyparis pisifera var. plumosa, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar or lacey cedar, is a deciduous conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its feathery, soft foliage that gives it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat, spreading sprays, creating a unique, airy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various climates.
