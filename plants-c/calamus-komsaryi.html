Calamus komsaryi

Calamus komsaryi, commonly known as the Siberian calamus, is a perennial plant belonging to the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly found in parts of Russia and China. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Calamus komsaryi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
