Cytisus austriacus var. pallidus

Cytisus austriacus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale broom, is a low-growing shrub native to Central Europe.

It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white or pale yellow hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is adapted to sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
