Carex pedata

Carex pedata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Carex pedata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
