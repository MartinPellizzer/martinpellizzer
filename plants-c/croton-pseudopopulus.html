Croton pseudopopulus

Croton pseudopopulus, commonly known as the false poplar croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically green or yellow, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Croton pseudopopulus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
