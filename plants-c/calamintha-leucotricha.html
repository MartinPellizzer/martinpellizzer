Calamintha leucotricha

Calamintha leucotricha, commonly known as white-haired mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often aromatic and used in traditional herbal remedies.

Calamintha leucotricha is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental and practical uses.
