Cupania jackiana

Cupania jackiana, commonly known as the Jack's cup or Chinese cup, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Cupania jackiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
