Croton simiarum

Croton simiarum, commonly known as the monkey tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from red to yellow and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton simiarum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
