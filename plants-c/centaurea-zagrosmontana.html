Centaurea zagrosmontana

Centaurea zagrosmontana, commonly known as the Zagros mountain centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Zagros Mountains in Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its vibrant blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
