Calophyllum laxiflorum

Calophyllum laxiflorum, commonly known as the broadleaf calophyllum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

Calophyllum laxiflorum is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
