Crataegus laciniata var. decussata

Crataegus laciniata var. decussata, commonly known as the cutleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with finely serrated margins, giving it a distinctive, lacy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
