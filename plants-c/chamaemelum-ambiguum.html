Chamaemelum ambiguum

Chamaemelum ambiguum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Chamaemelum ambiguum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
