Calamus trispermus

Calamus trispermus, commonly known as the three-seeded rattan, is a climbing fern belonging to the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are typically large, arrow-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by the development of three-seeded berries.
