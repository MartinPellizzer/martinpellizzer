Corethrogyne lavandulacea

Corethrogyne lavandulacea, commonly known as the mountain lavender, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the cold, dry conditions of its habitat.

Corethrogyne lavandulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
