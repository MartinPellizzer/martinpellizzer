Centaurea phrygia subsp. salicifolia

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

salicifolia, commonly known as the Silversword Centaurea, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange-red composite flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
