Calamintha alpina var. parviflora

Calamintha alpina var. parviflora, commonly known as alpine calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and possesses narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
