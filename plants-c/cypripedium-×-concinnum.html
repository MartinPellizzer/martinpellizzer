Cypripedium × concinnum

Cypripedium × concinnum, commonly known as the concinnum lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, upright stem with broad, leathery leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
