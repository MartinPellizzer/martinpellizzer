Camomilla patens

Camomilla patens, commonly known as the Mexican tarragon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, yellowish-white flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a mild, herbaceous scent, often used in traditional medicine.

Camomilla patens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
