Claytonia dianthodes

Claytonia dianthodes, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to woodland areas.

Claytonia dianthodes is also known for its edible leaves, which have a mild, peppery flavor.
