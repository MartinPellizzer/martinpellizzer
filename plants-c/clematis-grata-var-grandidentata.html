Clematis grata var. grandidentata

Clematis grata var. grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with large, serrated leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures, such as trellises or fences, for optimal display.
