Carex suifunensis

Carex suifunensis, commonly known as the Sui Fun sedge, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous roots and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, compact growth habit that helps it thrive in wet environments.

The Sui Fun sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
