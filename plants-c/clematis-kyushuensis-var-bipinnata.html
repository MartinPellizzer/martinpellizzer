Clematis kyushuensis var. bipinnata

Clematis kyushuensis var. bipinnata, commonly known as the Kyushu Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive bipinnate structure, giving it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
