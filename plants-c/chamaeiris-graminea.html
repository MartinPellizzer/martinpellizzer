Chamaeiris graminea

Chamaeiris graminea, commonly known as the grassy chamaeiris, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. It is native to certain regions of Africa and thrives in dry, open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
