Crambe cordifolia var. microcarpa

Crambe cordifolia var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited heartleaf crambe, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, typically ranging from 5 to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small capsules containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
