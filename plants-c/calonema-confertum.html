Calonema confertum

Calonema confertum, commonly known as the silver shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calonema confertum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and indoor settings.
