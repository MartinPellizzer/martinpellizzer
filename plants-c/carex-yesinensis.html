Carex yesinensis

Carex yesinensis, commonly known as the Yesin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, typical of the genus Carex.

The leaves are grass-like, with a distinct midrib and often have a waxy coating.
