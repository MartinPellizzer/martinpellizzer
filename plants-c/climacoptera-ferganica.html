Climacoptera ferganica

Climacoptera ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana mountain holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.
