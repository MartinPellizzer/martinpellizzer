Casuarina leucodon

Casuarina leucodon, commonly known as the white ghost gum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves that are clustered around woody spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Casuarina leucodon is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and its ornamental appeal.
