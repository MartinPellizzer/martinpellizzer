Chenopodium album subsp. olukondae

Chenopodium album subsp.

olukondae, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is a cosmopolitan species, widely distributed across temperate regions of the world.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in various habitats.
