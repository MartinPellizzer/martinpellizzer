Cyperus radians var. griffithii

Cyperus radians var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. Its slender, cylindrical stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features compared to other Cyperus species.
