Commelina radiciflora

Commelina radiciflora, commonly known as the root-flowered commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect stem, broad heart-shaped leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces white or pale blue flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the stems. Its roots are fibrous and can become quite extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Commelina radiciflora is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
