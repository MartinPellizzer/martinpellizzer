Crataegus oxyacantha var. multiplex

Crataegus oxyacantha var. multiplex, commonly known as the many-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
