Conopodium subcarneum var. pubescens

Conopodium subcarneum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bladder nut, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its most distinctive feature is the inflated, bladder-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This variant is noted for its pubescent or hairy surface, which gives it its specific epithet.
