Calyptranthes tuberculata

Calyptranthes tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps or tubercles, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
