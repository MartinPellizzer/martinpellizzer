Carex montana f. marginata

Carex montana f. marginata, commonly known as the variegated marsh sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often variegated, featuring white or cream-colored margins that distinguish it from other Carex species.

It produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by triangular, three-sided seed capsules.
