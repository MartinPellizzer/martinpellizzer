Carpinus polyneura var. sunpanensis

Carpinus polyneura var. sunpanensis, commonly known as the Sunpan horse chestnut, is a variant of the horse chestnut tree native to China.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are palmately lobed, with five to seven lobes, and are often a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by spiny, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
