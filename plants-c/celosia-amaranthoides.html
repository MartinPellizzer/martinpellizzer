Celosia amaranthoides

Celosia amaranthoides, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense, colorful inflorescences.

Celosia amaranthoides is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.
