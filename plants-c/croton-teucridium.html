Croton teucridium

Croton teucridium, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Croton teucridium make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
