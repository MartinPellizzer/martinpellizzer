Cucurbita pepo var. hassiaca

Cucurbita pepo var. hassiaca, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that can range in color from green to yellow or orange. The plant is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

The fruit is often used in traditional cuisines for its edible, fibrous texture and mild, sweet flavor.
