Calliandra umbellifera

Calliandra umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

Calliandra umbellifera is also valued in some regions for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
