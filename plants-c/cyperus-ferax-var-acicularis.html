Cyperus ferax var. acicularis

Cyperus ferax var. acicularis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-sided flower clusters.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
