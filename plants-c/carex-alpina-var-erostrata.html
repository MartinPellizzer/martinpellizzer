Carex alpina var. erostrata

Carex alpina var. erostrata, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine environments. Its flowering spikes, which appear in early summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
