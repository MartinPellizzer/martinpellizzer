Calappa plumosa

Calappa plumosa, commonly known as the feathery calappa, is a striking flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and airy floral display.
