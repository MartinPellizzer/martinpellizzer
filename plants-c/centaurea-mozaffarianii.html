Centaurea mozaffarianii

Centaurea mozaffarianii, commonly known as the Persian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually bright red or purple, with a distinctive central yellow disk.

Centaurea mozaffarianii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
