Campanula rapunculus var. calycosa

Campanula rapunculus var. calycosa, commonly known as the bellflower or lesser bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
