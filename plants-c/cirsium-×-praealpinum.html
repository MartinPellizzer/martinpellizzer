Cirsium × praealpinum

Cirsium × praealpinum, commonly known as the Alpine thistle, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Cirsium arvense and Cirsium vulgare.

It is a perennial herb with spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers, typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. Its growth habit includes a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Cirsium × praealpinum plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas and provides food for various herbivores.
