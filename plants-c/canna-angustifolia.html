Canna angustifolia

Canna angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cannas, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Americas.

It features large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, providing a dramatic foliage display. The plant produces showy, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to orange to yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
