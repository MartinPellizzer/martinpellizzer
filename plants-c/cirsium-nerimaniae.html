Cirsium nerimaniae

Cirsium nerimaniae, commonly known as the Nerimania thistle, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish composite flowers. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
