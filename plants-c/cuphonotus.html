Cuphonotus

Cuphonotus, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and hairy, often succulent leaves. These plants are typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Cuphonotus species are often perennial and can form dense mats, which help in stabilizing soil in their native habitats.

Their flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
