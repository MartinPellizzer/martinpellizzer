Chamaedorea vistae

Chamaedorea vistae, commonly known as the Vista palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender trunk with a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It is often cultivated as an indoor plant due to its ability to adapt to lower light conditions and its air-purifying qualities.

The flowers, which are small and inconspicuous, are typically followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible for some bird species.
