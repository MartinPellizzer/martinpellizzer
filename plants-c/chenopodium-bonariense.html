Chenopodium bonariense

Chenopodium bonariense, commonly known as wild spinach or lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium bonariense is valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional cuisines.
