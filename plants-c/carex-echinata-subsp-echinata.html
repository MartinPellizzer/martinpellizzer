Carex echinata subsp. echinata

Carex echinata subsp.

echinata, commonly known as the spiny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, with the flowering structures often bearing bracts. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas. Carex echinata subsp.

echinata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
