Carex cheesemanniana

Carex cheesemanniana, commonly known as the Cheeseman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its natural habitat.
