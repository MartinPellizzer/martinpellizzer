Ceropegia baylissii

Ceropegia baylissii, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
