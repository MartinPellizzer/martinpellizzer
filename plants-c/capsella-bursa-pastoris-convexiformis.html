Capsella bursa-pastoris convexiformis

Capsella bursa-pastoris convexiformis, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

This variant of the common weed is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
