Claotrachelus rupestris

Claotrachelus rupestris, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Claotrachelus rupestris is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
