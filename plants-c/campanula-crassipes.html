Campanula crassipes

Campanula crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

Campanula crassipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
