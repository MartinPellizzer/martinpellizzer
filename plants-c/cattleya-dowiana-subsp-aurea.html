Cattleya dowiana subsp. aurea

Cattleya dowiana subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive red or purple lip.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, supporting its evergreen foliage.

This subspecies is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively manageable care requirements.
