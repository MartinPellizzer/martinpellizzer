Centaurea uniflora subsp. bugellensis

Centaurea uniflora subsp.

bugellensis, commonly known as the Bugell's singleflower centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces a single, showy flower per stem, typically in shades of red or purple.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
