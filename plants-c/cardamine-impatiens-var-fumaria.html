Cardamine impatiens var. fumaria

Cardamine impatiens var. fumaria, commonly known as the smoky chickweed, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
