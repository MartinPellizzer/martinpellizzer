Carex tenuispicula

Carex tenuispicula, commonly known as narrow-spiked sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a fibrous root system. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex tenuispicula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet or semi-aquatic conditions.
