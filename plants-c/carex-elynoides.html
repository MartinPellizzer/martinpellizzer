Carex elynoides

Carex elynoides, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has triangular, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture.

Carex elynoides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
