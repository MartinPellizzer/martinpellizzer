Conyza sagittalis

Conyza sagittalis, commonly known as the arrowleaf conyza, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Conyza sagittalis is often found in temperate regions and can be an invasive species in some areas outside its native range.
