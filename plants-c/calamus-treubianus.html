Calamus treubianus

Calamus treubianus, commonly known as the Treub's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The rhizomes of Calamus treubianus are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
