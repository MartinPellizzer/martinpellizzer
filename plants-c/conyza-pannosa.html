Conyza pannosa

Conyza pannosa, commonly known as the common weed or goatweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across the Americas. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem. Conyza pannosa is known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common invasive species in many areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
