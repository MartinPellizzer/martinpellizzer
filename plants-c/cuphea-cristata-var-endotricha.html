Cuphea cristata var. endotricha

Cuphea cristata var. endotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

The varietal name 'endotricha' refers to the dense, hairy covering on its leaves, which gives it a velvety texture.
