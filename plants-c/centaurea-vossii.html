Centaurea vossii

Centaurea vossii, commonly known as the Voss's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a distinctive fuzzy texture.

Centaurea vossii is often found in open meadows and along mountain slopes, where it thrives in full sun.
