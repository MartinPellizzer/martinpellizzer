Chamaecrista gracilior

Chamaecrista gracilior, commonly known as slender crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Chamaecrista gracilior is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
