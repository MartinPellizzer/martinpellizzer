Citrus × kotokan

Citrus × kotokan, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or kumquat, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically orange in color and have a thin, edible peel. The plant is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant white flowers and has glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The fruit is commonly used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its high vitamin C content and unique flavor profile.
