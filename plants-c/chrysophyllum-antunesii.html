Chrysophyllum antunesii

Chrysophyllum antunesii, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a yellow or orange berry with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit, which is popular in local cuisines.
