Carduus spachianus subsp. spachianus

Carduus spachianus subsp.

spachianus, commonly known as the Spach's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branching stem that produces purple to pink flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
