Cinnamomum wilsonii var. multiflorum

Cinnamomum wilsonii var. multiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Wilson's cinnamon, is a species native to China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by ellipsoid berries. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves and bark are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
