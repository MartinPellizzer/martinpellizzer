Cirsium chailletii

Cirsium chailletii, commonly known as the Chaillet's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, purple or pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces seeds in a composite flower head.

Cirsium chailletii is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
