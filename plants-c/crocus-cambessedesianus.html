Crocus cambessedesianus

Crocus cambessedesianus, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and the Caucasus.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, waxy appearance, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.

The species is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique genetic makeup and ecological adaptations.
