Cassinia rubra

Cassinia rubra, commonly known as the red cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, creating a striking visual contrast.

Cassinia rubra is often used in native landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
