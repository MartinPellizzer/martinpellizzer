Crepis pterosperma

Crepis pterosperma, commonly known as the slender hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Crepis pterosperma is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
