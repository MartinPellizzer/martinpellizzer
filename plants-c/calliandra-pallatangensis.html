Calliandra pallatangensis

Calliandra pallatangensis, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which can improve soil fertility.
