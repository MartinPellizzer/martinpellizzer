Celosia melanocarpos

Celosia melanocarpos, commonly known as the blackseed celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, spiky inflorescences that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, dark seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Celosia melanocarpos is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a companion plant in agricultural settings.
