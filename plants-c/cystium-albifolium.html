Cystium albifolium

Cystium albifolium, commonly known as the white-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and pale green, almost white leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer.

Cystium albifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
