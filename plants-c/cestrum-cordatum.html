Cestrum cordatum

Cestrum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
