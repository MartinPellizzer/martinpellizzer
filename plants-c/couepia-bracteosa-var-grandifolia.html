Couepia bracteosa var. grandifolia

Couepia bracteosa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved couepia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
