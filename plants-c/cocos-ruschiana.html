Cocos ruschiana

Cocos ruschiana, commonly known as the Ruschian coconut, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay. The plant features a slender trunk with a crown of pinnate leaves, and it produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible fruits. Its fruit is a drupe with a fibrous husk and a sweet, starchy pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
