Cotoneaster microphyllus f. lanatus

Cotoneaster microphyllus f. lanatus, commonly known as the woolly cotoneaster, is a compact shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The leaves are small, ovate, and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental gardening for its winter interest and hardy nature.
