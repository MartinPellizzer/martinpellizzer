Carex pisiformis var. pineticola

Carex pisiformis var. pineticola, commonly known as the northern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
