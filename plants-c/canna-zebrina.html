Canna zebrina

Canna zebrina, commonly known as the zebra plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, strap-like leaves that often feature distinctive white or yellow stripes. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by vibrant bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Canna zebrina thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a tropical plant in warmer climates.
