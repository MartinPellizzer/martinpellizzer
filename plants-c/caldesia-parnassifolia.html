Caldesia parnassifolia

Caldesia parnassifolia, commonly known as the Parnassian caldesia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, palm-like leaves and showy, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush, green appearance throughout the growing season.

Caldesia parnassifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
