Cistus × timbalii

Cistus × timbalii, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
