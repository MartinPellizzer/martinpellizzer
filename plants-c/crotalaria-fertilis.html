Crotalaria fertilis

Crotalaria fertilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers. It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Crotalaria fertilis is also recognized for its potential as a bioindicator of soil fertility due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
