Cistus clusii subsp. multiflorus

Cistus clusii subsp.

multiflorus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to full sun. It produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
