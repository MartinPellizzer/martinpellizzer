Cytisus albidus

Cytisus albidus, commonly known as the white broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, arching branches covered in small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus albidus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
