Chavica lanceolata

Chavica lanceolata, commonly known as the Indian chervil or wild chervil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.
