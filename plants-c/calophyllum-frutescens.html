Calophyllum frutescens

Calophyllum frutescens, commonly known as the yellow cedar or white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often aromatic bark and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky aril. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in humid, warm climates.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in various cultures for medicinal and ceremonial purposes.
