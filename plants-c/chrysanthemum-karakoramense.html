Chrysanthemum karakoramense

Chrysanthemum karakoramense, commonly known as the Karakoram chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Karakoram mountain range in Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
