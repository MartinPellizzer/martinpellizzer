Cuphea teleandra

Cuphea teleandra, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Cuphea teleandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
