Clematis albicoma

Clematis albicoma, commonly known as the white-flowered clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with thorns and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clematis albicoma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm climates.
