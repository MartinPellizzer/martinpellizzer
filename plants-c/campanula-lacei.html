Campanula lacei

Campanula lacei, commonly known as the lace bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Campanula lacei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
