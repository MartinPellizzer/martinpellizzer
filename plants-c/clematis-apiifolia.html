Clematis apiifolia

Clematis apiifolia, commonly known as the wild clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound, fern-like leaves that resemble those of the carrot plant, hence its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Clematis apiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to climb using its tendrils.
