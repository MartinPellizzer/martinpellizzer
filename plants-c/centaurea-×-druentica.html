Centaurea × druentica

Centaurea × druentica, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Centaurea × druentica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
