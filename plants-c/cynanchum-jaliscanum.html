Cynanchum jaliscanum

Cynanchum jaliscanum, commonly known as Mexican milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in umbels, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Cynanchum jaliscanum is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
