Carex hirtigluma var. arcuata

Carex hirtigluma var. arcuata, commonly known as the curved hairgrass, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, adding a subtle ornamental quality to its appearance. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland landscaping.
