Chamaesyce pekinensis var. glaberrima

Chamaesyce pekinensis var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in the spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
