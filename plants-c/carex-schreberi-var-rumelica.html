Carex schreberi var. rumelica

Carex schreberi var. rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The Rumelica sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
