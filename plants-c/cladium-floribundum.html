Cladium floribundum

Cladium floribundum, commonly known as the flowering spikerush, is a perennial wetland plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and erect, flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes and swamps. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are often green or reddish in color.

Cladium floribundum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
