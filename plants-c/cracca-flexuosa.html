Cracca flexuosa

Cracca flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible cracca, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems that are often contorted, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and have a distinct, spicy scent when crushed.

The small white to greenish flowers appear in umbrella-like clusters and are followed by small, dry fruits.
