Catha integer

Catha integer, commonly known as khat, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Catha integer is notable for its stimulant properties due to the presence of cathinone and cathine alkaloids.
