Cynanchum cardozoi

Cynanchum cardozoi, commonly known as the Kashmiri milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
