Cyrtidium frontinoense

Cyrtidium frontinoense, commonly known as the Frontinoense Pothos, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically bright green, with some varieties displaying variegation. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Cyrtidium frontinoense prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
