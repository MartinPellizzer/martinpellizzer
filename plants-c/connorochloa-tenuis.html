Connorochloa tenuis

Connorochloa tenuis, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Connorochloa tenuis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
