Conyza guineensis

Conyza guineensis, commonly known as Guinea ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Conyza guineensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
