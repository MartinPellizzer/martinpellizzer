Calophyllum piaroanum

Calophyllum piaroanum, commonly known as the Piaroa calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a fragrant, orange-red fruit that contains several seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

The wood of Calophyllum piaroanum is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of timber.
