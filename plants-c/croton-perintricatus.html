Croton perintricatus

Croton perintricatus, commonly known as the silky croton, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
