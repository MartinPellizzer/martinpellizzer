Cyrtandra ilicifolia

Cyrtandra ilicifolia, commonly known as the hibiscus-like plant, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including Hawaii, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Cyrtandra ilicifolia is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
