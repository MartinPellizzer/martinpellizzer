Chlorophytum elongatum

Chlorophytum elongatum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal houseplant. It produces small, white flowers on long, slender stems, which are followed by bright orange berries.

Chlorophytum elongatum is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to indoor air quality.
