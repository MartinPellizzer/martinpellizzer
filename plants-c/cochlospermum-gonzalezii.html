Cochlospermum gonzalezii

Cochlospermum gonzalezii, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a distinctive, fluted trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Cochlospermum gonzalezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
