Clianthus baueri

Clianthus baueri, commonly known as the New Zealand glory of the snow, is a striking flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its tall, spindly stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Clianthus baueri is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
