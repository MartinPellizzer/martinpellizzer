Celastrus rotundifolius

Celastrus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved bindweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous vine that produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along forest edges or in moist woodlands. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
