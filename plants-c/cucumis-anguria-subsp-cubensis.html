Cucumis anguria subsp. cubensis

Cucumis anguria subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as Cuban cucumber, is a wild climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. It produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green and slightly hairy.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects. The fruit is edible and has a mild, slightly bitter taste, often used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
