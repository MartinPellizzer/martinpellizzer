Canarium vulgare

Canarium vulgare, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single, oily seed used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its roots and leaves are also used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and economic importance in local communities.
