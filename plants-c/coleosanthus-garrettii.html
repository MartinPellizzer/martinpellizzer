Coleosanthus garrettii

Coleosanthus garrettii, commonly known as the New Zealand sea holly, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered an important species for ecological studies in New Zealand's marine ecosystems.
