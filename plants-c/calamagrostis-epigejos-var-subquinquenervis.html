Calamagrostis epigejos var. subquinquenervis

Calamagrostis epigejos var. subquinquenervis, commonly known as the silver grass or silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-green inflorescences that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a silvery sheen, and its flowers are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
