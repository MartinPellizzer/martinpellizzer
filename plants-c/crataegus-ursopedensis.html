Crataegus ursopedensis

Crataegus ursopedensis, commonly known as the Ursopedan hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Crataegus ursopedensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
