Celosia corymbifera

Celosia corymbifera, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is known for its upright growth habit and long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months under favorable conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branching.

Celosia corymbifera is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, sunny environments.
