Capparis ovata var. herbacea

Capparis ovata var. herbacea, commonly known as the common caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale green leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and succulent, edible young shoots and buds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
