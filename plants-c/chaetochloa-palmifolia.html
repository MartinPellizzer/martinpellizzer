Chaetochloa palmifolia

Chaetochloa palmifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia and Australia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by wind rather than insects. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
