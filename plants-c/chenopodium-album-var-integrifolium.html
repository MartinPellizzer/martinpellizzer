Chenopodium album var. integrifolium

Chenopodium album var. integrifolium, commonly known as lambsquarters, is a common weed found in various habitats across temperate regions.

It is an annual herb with a sprawling growth habit and a rough, hairy stem. The leaves are alternate, ovate to triangular in shape, and have a distinctive bluish-green color with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

It is known for its high nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
