Coleus sylvestris

Coleus sylvestris, commonly known as wild coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its striking foliage and ease of care.
