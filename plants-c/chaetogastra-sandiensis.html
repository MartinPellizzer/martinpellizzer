Chaetogastra sandiensis

Chaetogastra sandiensis, commonly known as the Sand River cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a short, cylindrical stem that is often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
