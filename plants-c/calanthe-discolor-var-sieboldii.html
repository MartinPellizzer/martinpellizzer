Calanthe discolor var. sieboldii

Calanthe discolor var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's calanthe, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white and green mottled leaves and large, showy flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
