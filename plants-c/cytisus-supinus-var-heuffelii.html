Cytisus supinus var. heuffelii

Cytisus supinus var. heuffelii, commonly known as the dwarf yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, thornless stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, drought-tolerant plant.
