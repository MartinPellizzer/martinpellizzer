Carex leptalea subsp. harperi

Carex leptalea subsp.

harperi, commonly known as the Harpers' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. It is often found in association with other wetland plants and plays a role in stabilizing soil in these environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
