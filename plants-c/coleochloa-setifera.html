Coleochloa setifera

Coleochloa setifera, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, spikelike inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Coleochloa setifera plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
