Caturus oblongatus

Caturus oblongatus, commonly known as the oblong leaf cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, fleshy stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce evaporation.

Caturus oblongatus is well adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
