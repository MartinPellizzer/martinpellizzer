Cosmos herzogii

Cosmos herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's cosmos, is a striking annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cosmos herzogii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and wildflower gardens.
