Crataegus segobricensis

Crataegus segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobric Mountain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
