Croton rhamnifolioides

Croton rhamnifolioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
