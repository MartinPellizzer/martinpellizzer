Carex dispalata var. takeuchii

Carex dispalata var. takeuchii, commonly known as the Takeuchi sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to greenish flowers, which are typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in Japanese gardens.
