Cupressus thujioides

Cupressus thujioides, commonly known as the Japanese cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and pyramid-shaped crown. The tree produces small, round cones that contain seeds, typical of gymnosperms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

Its historical use in traditional Japanese architecture and as a source of timber adds to its cultural significance.
