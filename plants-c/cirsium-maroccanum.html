Cirsium maroccanum

Cirsium maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand dry conditions. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role.

Cirsium maroccanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
