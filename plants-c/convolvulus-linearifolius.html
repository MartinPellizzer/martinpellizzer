Convolvulus linearifolius

Convolvulus linearifolius, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
