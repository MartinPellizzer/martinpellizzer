Cephalotaxus harringtonia var. koreana

Cephalotaxus harringtonia var. koreana, commonly known as Korean yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Korea.

It features a distinctive, compact growth habit with short, stiff branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green female cones and reddish-brown male cones, which are typically borne on different parts of the plant. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance and hardiness.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some parts containing compounds used in traditional medicine.
