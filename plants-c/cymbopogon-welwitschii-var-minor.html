Cymbopogon welwitschii var. minor

Cymbopogon welwitschii var. minor, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fine-textured leaves that emit a pleasant, sweet scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, cylindrical spikelets that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is also cultivated in gardens and for essential oil production.
