Camelina sativa subsp. microcarpa

Camelina sativa subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as dwarf camelina, is a small annual herb native to Central Asia. It produces small, yellow flowers and has narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other oilseed crops like mustard and rape.

Due to its resilience to harsh environments, it is of interest for agricultural and ecological studies.
