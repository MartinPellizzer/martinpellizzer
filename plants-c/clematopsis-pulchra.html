Clematopsis pulchra

Clematopsis pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful clematopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge, while the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Clematopsis pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
