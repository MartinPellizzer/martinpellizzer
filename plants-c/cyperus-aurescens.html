Cyperus aurescens

Cyperus aurescens, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often yellow or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
