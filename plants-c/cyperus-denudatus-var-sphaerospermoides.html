Cyperus denudatus var. sphaerospermoides

Cyperus denudatus var. sphaerospermoides, commonly known as the spherical sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Cyperus.

It is characterized by its unique, spherical or globular inflorescence, which sets it apart from other sedge species. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters.

This variant is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its specialized adaptations to aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
