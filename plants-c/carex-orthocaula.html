Carex orthocaula

Carex orthocaula, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish flower spikes that are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex orthocaula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
