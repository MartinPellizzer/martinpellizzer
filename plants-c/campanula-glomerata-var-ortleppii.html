Campanula glomerata var. ortleppii

Campanula glomerata var. ortleppii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features compact, mound-like growth with dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula glomerata var. ortleppii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
