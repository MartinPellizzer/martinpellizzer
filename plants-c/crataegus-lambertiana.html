Crataegus lambertiana

Crataegus lambertiana, commonly known as Lambert's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
