Cyperus Melanacme

Cyperus melanacme is a unique species of sedge native to wetland environments in southern Africa. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. This plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in maintaining wetland ecosystems. Its adaptability makes it a valuable subject for ecological studies.
