Carex setacea var. ambigua

Carex setacea var. ambigua, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a variant of the Carex setacea species, belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowering structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
