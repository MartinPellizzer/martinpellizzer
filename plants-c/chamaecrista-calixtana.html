Chamaecrista calixtana

Chamaecrista calixtana, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pairs of small, bean-like pods. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chamaecrista calixtana is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
