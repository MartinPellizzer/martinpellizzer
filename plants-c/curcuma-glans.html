Curcuma glans

Curcuma glans, commonly known as the golden root or Indian saffron, is a member of the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant produces striking orange to yellow rhizomes that are rich in curcumin, a compound known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically reaching up to 1 meter in length.

Curcuma glans is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
