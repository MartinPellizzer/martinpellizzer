Cassia chamaecrista f. pubicaulis

Cassia chamaecrista f. pubicaulis, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
