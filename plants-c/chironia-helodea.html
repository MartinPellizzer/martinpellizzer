Chironia helodea

Chironia helodea, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are heart-shaped and broad, providing a striking visual contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Chironia helodea is also known for its ability to purify water, making it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
