Calamus oblongus subsp. natunaensis

Calamus oblongus subsp.

natunaensis, commonly known as the Natuna calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
