Centaurea paniculata subsp. aplolepa

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

aplolepa, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, globular clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
