Crataegus tardipes

Crataegus tardipes, commonly known as the late-flowering hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
