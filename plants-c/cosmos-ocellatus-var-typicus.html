Cosmos ocellatus var. typicus

Cosmos ocellatus var. typicus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red blooms, which appear in late summer and early fall. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are gray-green in color, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for perennial gardens.

Cosmos ocellatus var. typicus is easy to grow from seed and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
