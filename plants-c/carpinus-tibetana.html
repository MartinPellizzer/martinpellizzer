Carpinus tibetana

Carpinus tibetana, commonly known as the Tibetan hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and adjacent areas of China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by small, edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

It is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soils.
