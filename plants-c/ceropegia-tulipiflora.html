Ceropegia tulipiflora

Ceropegia tulipiflora, commonly known as the Cape tulip or the yellow moon ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent vine characterized by its fleshy, tuberous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia tulipiflora is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
