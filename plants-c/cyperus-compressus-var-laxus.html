Cyperus compressus var. laxus

Cyperus compressus var. laxus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

Cyperus compressus var. laxus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and remediate contaminated soils.
