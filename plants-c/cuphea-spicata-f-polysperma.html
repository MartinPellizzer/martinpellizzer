Cuphea spicata f. polysperma

Cuphea spicata f. polysperma, commonly known as the many-seeded cuphea, is a cultivar of the cuphea genus native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces numerous small, clustered flowers along its stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The 'polysperma' form is noted for its increased seed production, making it a popular choice for seed propagation.
