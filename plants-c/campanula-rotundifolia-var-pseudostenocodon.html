Campanula rotundifolia var. pseudostenocodon

Campanula rotundifolia var. pseudostenocodon, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full to partial sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
