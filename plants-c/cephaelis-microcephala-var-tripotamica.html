Cephaelis microcephala var. tripotamica

Cephaelis microcephala var. tripotamica, commonly known as the small-headed cephaelis, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing vibrant pink to reddish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
