Carex × rubrovaginata

Carex × rubrovaginata, commonly known as the red-veined sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that often display striking red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
