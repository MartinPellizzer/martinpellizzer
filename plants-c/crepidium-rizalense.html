Crepidium rizalense

Crepidium rizalense, commonly known as the Rizal's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Its botanical characteristics include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact inflorescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
