Cytisus albus var. landozii

Cytisus albus var. landozii, commonly known as the white broom, is a variant of the common broom.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, white, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological succession in disturbed areas.
