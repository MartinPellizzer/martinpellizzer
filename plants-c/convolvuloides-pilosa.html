Convolvuloides pilosa

Convolvuloides pilosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Convolvuloides pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
