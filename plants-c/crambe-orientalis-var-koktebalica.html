Crambe orientalis var. koktebalica

Crambe orientalis var. koktebalica, commonly known as the Koktebel candleplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, candle-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and native plant collections.
