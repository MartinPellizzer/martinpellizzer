Caragana jubata var. jubata

Caragana jubata var. jubata, commonly known as the goldenpea or yellow pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
