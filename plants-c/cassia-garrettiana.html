Cassia garrettiana

Cassia garrettiana, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cassia garrettiana thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its popularity in landscaped areas.
