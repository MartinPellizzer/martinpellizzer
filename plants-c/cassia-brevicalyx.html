Cassia brevicalyx

Cassia brevicalyx, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate with 10 to 20 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Cassia brevicalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscape gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
