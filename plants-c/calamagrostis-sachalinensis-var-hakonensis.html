Calamagrostis sachalinensis var. hakonensis

Calamagrostis sachalinensis var. hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakone grass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and feathery, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces plume-like flower spikes in late summer, which are both attractive and wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or bog gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
