Cowania mexicana

Cowania mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican cowania, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
