Canarium perkinsae

Canarium perkinsae, commonly known as the Perkin's candlenut, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
