Crepis szechuanica

Crepis szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous regions and is often found in grasslands and meadows. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Crepis szechuanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
