Cytisus multiflorus

Cytisus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cytisus multiflorus is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility.
