Claytonia spathulata var. major

Claytonia spathulata var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaf springbeauty, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
