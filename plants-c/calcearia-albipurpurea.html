Calcearia albipurpurea

Calcearia albipurpurea, commonly known as the purple-white daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features a short, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically white with a pinkish-purple center, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Calcearia albipurpurea prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
