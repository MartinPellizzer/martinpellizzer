Coccoloba punctata var. jacquinii

Coccoloba punctata var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered purple to blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils near coastal areas.
