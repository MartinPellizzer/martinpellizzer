Croton litoralis

Croton litoralis, commonly known as the coastal croton, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
