Chuquiraga infundibularis

Chuquiraga infundibularis, commonly known as the Andean burro's tail, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and unique, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the stems are often covered in a thick layer of fine hairs.

Chuquiraga infundibularis is adapted to harsh alpine conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
