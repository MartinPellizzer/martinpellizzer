Clematis recta var. glaucescens

Clematis recta var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green clematis, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb to considerable heights, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, flowering vine.
