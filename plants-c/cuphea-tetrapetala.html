Cuphea tetrapetala

Cuphea tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
