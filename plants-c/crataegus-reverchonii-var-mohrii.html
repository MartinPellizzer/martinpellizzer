Crataegus reverchonii var. mohrii

Crataegus reverchonii var. mohrii, commonly known as Mohr's hawthorn, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
