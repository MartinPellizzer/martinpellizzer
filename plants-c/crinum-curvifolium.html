Crinum curvifolium

Crinum curvifolium, commonly known as the zigzag lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that curve and twist, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Crinum curvifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
