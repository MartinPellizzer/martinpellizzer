Chrystolia violacea

Chrystolia violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered chrystolia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features delicate, violet-pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and compact growth habit.
