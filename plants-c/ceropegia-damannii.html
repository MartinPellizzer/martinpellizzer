Ceropegia damannii

Ceropegia damannii, commonly known as the African violet or Madagascar ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and trailing stems that produce opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia damannii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
