Crocus cartwrightianus

Crocus cartwrightianus, commonly known as the Persian crown imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers that range from deep red to orange and yellow, with a distinctive crown-like shape. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a short stem that supports its vibrant blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late winter to early spring.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders in temperate climates.
