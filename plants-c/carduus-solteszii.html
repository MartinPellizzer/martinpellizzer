Carduus solteszii

Carduus solteszii, commonly known as the Soltesz's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The species is relatively rare and often found in specific microhabitats within its range.
