Cactus meonacanthus

Cactus meonacanthus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. These flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours.

The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.
