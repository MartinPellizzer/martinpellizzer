Codonopsis parviflora

Codonopsis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of China, Korea, and Japan.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Codonopsis parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
