Cyperus rotundus var. elongatus

Cyperus rotundus var. elongatus, commonly known as slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown. The plant is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems and is often found in wet or moist soils. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and grass-like plants.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in a variety of environments, including disturbed soils and agricultural fields.
