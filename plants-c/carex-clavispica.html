Carex clavispica

Carex clavispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate stems.
