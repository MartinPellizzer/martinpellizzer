Callitris juniperoides

Callitris juniperoides, commonly known as the white cypress-pine, is a coniferous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived component of its ecosystem.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture.
