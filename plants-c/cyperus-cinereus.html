Cyperus cinereus

Cyperus cinereus, commonly known as the grey flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its silver-gray foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, making it both ornamental and ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus cinereus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
