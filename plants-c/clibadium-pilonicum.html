Clibadium pilonicum

Clibadium pilonicum, commonly known as the giant sycamore, is a large tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its size and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in supporting local biodiversity.
