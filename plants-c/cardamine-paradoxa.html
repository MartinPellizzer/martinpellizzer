Cardamine paradoxa

Cardamine paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical watercress, is a rare and delicate member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of botanical interest.

Due to its rarity, it is often found in specialized habitats and is considered a conservation concern.
