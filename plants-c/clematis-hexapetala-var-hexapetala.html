Clematis hexapetala var. hexapetala

Clematis hexapetala var. hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers with six white petals and a prominent central cone of stamens. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, often climbing supports with its twining stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
