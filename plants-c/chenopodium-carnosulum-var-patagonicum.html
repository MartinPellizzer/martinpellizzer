Chenopodium carnosulum var. patagonicum

Chenopodium carnosulum var. patagonicum, commonly known as Patagonian goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that grow in clusters. It is often found in rocky soils and can tolerate harsh climatic conditions.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in local ecosystems for its resilience and potential medicinal properties.
