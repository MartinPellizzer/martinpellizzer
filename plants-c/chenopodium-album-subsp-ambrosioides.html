Chenopodium album subsp. ambrosioides

Chenopodium album subsp.

ambrosioides, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves and small greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its seeds are small and dry, dispersing easily by wind, which contributes to its widespread distribution.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
