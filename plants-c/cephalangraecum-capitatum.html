Cephalangraecum capitatum

Cephalangraecum capitatum, commonly known as the Cape mountain lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is a bulbous plant with a distinctive, helmet-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
