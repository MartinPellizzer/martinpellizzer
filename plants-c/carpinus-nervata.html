Carpinus nervata

Carpinus nervata, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hard wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
