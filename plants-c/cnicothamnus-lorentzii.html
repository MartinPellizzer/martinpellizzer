Cnicothamnus lorentzii

Cnicothamnus lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's thornbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features woody stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
