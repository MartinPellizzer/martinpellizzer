Cotoneaster horizontalis f. variegatus

Cotoneaster horizontalis f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated cotoneaster, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves with distinctive variegation, often displaying white or yellow margins. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries in the fall, which are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used as a hedge or container plant.
