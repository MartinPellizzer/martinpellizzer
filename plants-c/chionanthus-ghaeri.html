Chionanthus ghaeri

Chionanthus ghaeri, commonly known as the Persian snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western and central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often accompanied by a faint, sweet fragrance.

Chionanthus ghaeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
