Calyptranthes detecta

Calyptranthes detecta, commonly known as the Hawaiian whiteflower or white-flowered calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Calyptranthes detecta is notable for its ecological significance in supporting native insect pollinators in Hawaii.
