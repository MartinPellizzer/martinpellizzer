Crepis taraxifolia

Crepis taraxifolia, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Crepis taraxifolia is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
