Combretum punctatum

Combretum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted combretum, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often dotted with small, raised glands. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in dry woodlands and thicket habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
