Chrysopogon zizanioides var. nigritana

Chrysopogon zizanioides var. nigritana, commonly known as blackgrass or nigritana grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This grass is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its fine texture and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
