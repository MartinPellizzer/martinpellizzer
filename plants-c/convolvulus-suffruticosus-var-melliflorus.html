Convolvulus suffruticosus var. melliflorus

Convolvulus suffruticosus var. melliflorus, commonly known as sweet bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that allow it to climb or spread along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
