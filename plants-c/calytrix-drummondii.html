Calytrix drummondii

Calytrix drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Calytrix drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
