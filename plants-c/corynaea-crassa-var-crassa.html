Corynaea crassa var. crassa

Corynaea crassa var. crassa, commonly known as the roughleaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, rough-textured leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
