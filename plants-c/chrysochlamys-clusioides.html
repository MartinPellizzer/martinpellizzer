Chrysochlamys clusioides

Chrysochlamys clusioides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange-red leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is known for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
