Chenopodina bacciformis

Chenopodina bacciformis, commonly known as the red goosefoot, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often reddish in color, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in shades of green or yellow. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Chenopodina bacciformis is native to regions with dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
