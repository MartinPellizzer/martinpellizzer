Conyza carolinensis

Conyza carolinensis, commonly known as horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers and hairy, branching stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
