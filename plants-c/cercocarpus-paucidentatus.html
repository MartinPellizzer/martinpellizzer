Cercocarpus paucidentatus

Cercocarpus paucidentatus, commonly known as the desert curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are often curled at the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
