Commiphora stocksiana

Commiphora stocksiana, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties. The plant has a dense, rounded canopy and smooth, grayish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and the plant relies on insect pollination.
