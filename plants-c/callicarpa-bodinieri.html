Callicarpa bodinieri

Callicarpa bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland or moist areas.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
