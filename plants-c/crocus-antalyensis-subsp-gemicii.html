Crocus antalyensis subsp. gemicii

Crocus antalyensis subsp.

gemicii, commonly known as the Antalya crocus, is a rare and endemic species native to Turkey. It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
