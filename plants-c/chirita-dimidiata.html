Chirita dimidiata

Chirita dimidiata, commonly known as the half-leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often split into two distinct lobes. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
