Chaenomeles speciosa f. gaujardii

Chaenomeles speciosa f. gaujardii, commonly known as the Gaujardii flowering quince, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red or orange fruits in the fall, which are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
