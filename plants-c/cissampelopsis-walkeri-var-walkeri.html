Cissampelopsis walkeri var. walkeri

Cissampelopsis walkeri var. walkeri, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins and a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in tropical gardens for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and hanging baskets.
