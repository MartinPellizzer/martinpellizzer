Centaurea vankovii

Centaurea vankovii, commonly known as the Vankov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and meadows. It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its slender stems and narrow leaves are adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
