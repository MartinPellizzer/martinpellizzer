Cleistes calantha

Cleistes calantha, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, lantern-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal moths.

Cleistes calantha is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
