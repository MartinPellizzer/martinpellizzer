Carex caricina var. leptocarpa

Carex caricina var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and moist forest environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear three-stamened flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
