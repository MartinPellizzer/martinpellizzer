Cattleya fulva

Cattleya fulva, commonly known as the orange lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and red flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Cattleya fulva is prized for its showy blooms and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
