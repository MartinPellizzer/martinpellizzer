Celtis dysodoxylon

Celtis dysodoxylon, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, pale green fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species for urban landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for birds and insects.
