Cousinia dissecta

Cousinia dissecta, commonly known as the dissected globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Cousinia dissecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
