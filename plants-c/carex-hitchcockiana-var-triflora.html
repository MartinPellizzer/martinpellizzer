Carex hitchcockiana var. triflora

Carex hitchcockiana var. triflora, commonly known as the Hitchcock's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its clusters of three small, spicate inflorescences, giving it the name "triflora," meaning "three-flowered.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and riparian areas.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
