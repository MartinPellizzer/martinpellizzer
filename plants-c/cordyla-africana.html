Cordyla africana

Cordyla africana, commonly known as the African breadfruit tree, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by edible, starchy fruits that resemble small bread loaves. Its roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for both its nutritional value and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
