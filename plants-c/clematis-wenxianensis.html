Clematis wenxianensis

Clematis wenxianensis, commonly known as the Wenxian clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually white or pink flowers. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its stems are often armed with hooked prickles. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains a relatively rare species in horticulture.
