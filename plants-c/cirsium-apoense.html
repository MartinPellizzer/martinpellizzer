Cirsium apoense

Cirsium apoense, commonly known as the Apennine thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Italy and surrounding areas.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a rounded head, typical of the Cirsium genus.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in moderate climates.
