Crassula smutsii

Crassula smutsii, commonly known as the Smuts' crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and has a compact, bushy appearance. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in spring or early summer.

Crassula smutsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
