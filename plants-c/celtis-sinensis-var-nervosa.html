Celtis sinensis var. nervosa

Celtis sinensis var. nervosa, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with distinct venation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
