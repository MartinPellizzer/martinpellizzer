Cissus lemurica

Cissus lemurica, commonly known as the rock fig or African snowball, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which give it its common name. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Cissus lemurica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
