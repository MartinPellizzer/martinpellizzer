Caladenia radialis

Caladenia radialis, commonly known as the pink-streaked spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a single, erect stem with a single, showy pink or purple flower that has a distinctive spider-like appearance. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

The orchid is pollinated primarily by thynnid wasps, which are attracted to its nectar-rich flowers.
