Clematis chinensis f. vestita

Clematis chinensis f. vestita, commonly known as the white-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a climbing plant.
