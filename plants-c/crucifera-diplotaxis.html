Crucifera diplotaxis

Crucifera diplotaxis, commonly known as the desert mustang grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in desert regions. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert flora and a subject of ecological study.
