Carex fusca var. carpetana

Carex fusca var. carpetana, commonly known as the carpet sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variant is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, often forming a dense carpet-like growth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
