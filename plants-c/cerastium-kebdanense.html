Cerastium kebdanense

Cerastium kebdanense, commonly known as the Armenian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and often covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Cerastium kebdanense thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
