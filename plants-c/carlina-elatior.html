Carlina elatior

Carlina elatior, commonly known as the greater cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. Carlina elatior typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, often in temperate climates.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
