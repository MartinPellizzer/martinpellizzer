Cephalocereus alensis

Cephalocereus alensis, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It has a dense covering of spines and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience to drought.
