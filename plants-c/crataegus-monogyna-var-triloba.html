Crataegus monogyna var. triloba

Crataegus monogyna var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called pomes, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
