Carum wolffianum

Carum wolffianum, commonly known as Wolff's caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, carroty scent.

Carum wolffianum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and fragrance.
