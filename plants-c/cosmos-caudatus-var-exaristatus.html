Cosmos caudatus var. exaristatus

Cosmos caudatus var. exaristatus, commonly known as the fringed cosmos, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery petals that give it a delicate, almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include palmate leaves and a bushy growth habit, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
