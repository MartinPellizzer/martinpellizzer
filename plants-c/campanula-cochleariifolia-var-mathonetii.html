Campanula cochleariifolia var. mathonetii

Campanula cochleariifolia var. mathonetii, commonly known as the Mathonet's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
