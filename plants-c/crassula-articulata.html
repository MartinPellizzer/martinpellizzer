Crassula articulata

Crassula articulata, commonly known as the jointed crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are segmented and often branched, giving it a unique, branching appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Crassula articulata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
