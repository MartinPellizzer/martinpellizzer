Chrysothemis ovata

Chrysothemis ovata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange ray flowers and green bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
