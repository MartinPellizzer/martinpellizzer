Carex vulgaris subsp. juncella

Carex vulgaris subsp.

juncella, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of sedges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
