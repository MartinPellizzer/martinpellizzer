Calpidisca

Calpidisca, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that resemble small holly berries, hence its common name. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in coastal areas with well-drained soils. Calpidisca has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
