Chelone centranthifolia

Chelone centranthifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hound's tongue, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.

Chelone centranthifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
