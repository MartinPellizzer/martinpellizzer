Chrysurus

Chrysurus, commonly known as the golden-eyed orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vivid yellow and green foliage, with flowers that resemble miniature suns. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique floral structure includes a prominent golden stamen that serves as a visual lure for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, Chrysurus is considered endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
