Centaurea boissieri subsp. resupinata

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

resupinata, commonly known as the creeping centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial with woody stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to dry, sunny environments.
