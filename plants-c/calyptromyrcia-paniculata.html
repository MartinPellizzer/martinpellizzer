Calyptromyrcia paniculata

Calyptromyrcia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
