Cacalia pycnostachya

Cacalia pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Cacalia pycnostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
