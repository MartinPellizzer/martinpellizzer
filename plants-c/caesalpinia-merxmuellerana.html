Caesalpinia merxmuellerana

Caesalpinia merxmuellerana, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that resemble peacock feathers, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Caesalpinia merxmuellerana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
