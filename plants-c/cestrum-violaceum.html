Cestrum violaceum

Cestrum violaceum, commonly known as the purple cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming primarily during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Cestrum violaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
