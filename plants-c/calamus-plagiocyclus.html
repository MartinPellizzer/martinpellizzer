Calamus plagiocyclus

Calamus plagiocyclus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Aroid family.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.
