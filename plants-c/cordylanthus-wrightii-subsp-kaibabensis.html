Cordylanthus wrightii subsp. kaibabensis

Cordylanthus wrightii subsp.

kaibabensis, commonly known as the Kaibab silversword, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often found in desert regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and intense sunlight.
