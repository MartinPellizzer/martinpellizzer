Cynanchum velutinum

Cynanchum velutinum, commonly known as velvet bindweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cynanchum velutinum is also known for its aggressive growth habits, making it a challenging weed in agricultural settings.
