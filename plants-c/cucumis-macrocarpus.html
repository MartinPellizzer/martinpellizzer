Cucumis macrocarpus

Cucumis macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant cucumber, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces elongated, yellowish-green fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is typically harvested when immature, before it fully ripens, for culinary use.
