Chenopodium pallidicaule

Chenopodium pallidicaule, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb characterized by its succulent, branched stems and alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. Chenopodium pallidicaule is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
