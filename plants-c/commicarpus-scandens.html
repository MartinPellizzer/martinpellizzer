Commicarpus scandens

Commicarpus scandens, commonly known as the climbing evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that twine around supporting structures. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in panicles, and it produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and ornamental qualities in tropical and subtropical gardens.
