Centaurea erucifolia

Centaurea erucifolia, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, purple to pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

The seeds are small and dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.
