Cucumis cordifolius

Cucumis cordifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Cucumis cordifolius make it a unique and valuable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
