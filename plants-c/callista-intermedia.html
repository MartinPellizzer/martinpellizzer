Callista intermedia

Callista intermedia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Callista intermedia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
