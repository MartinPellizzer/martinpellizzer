Cerastium campanulatum f. ciliatum

Cerastium campanulatum f. ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "ciliatum.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its small, clustered flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
