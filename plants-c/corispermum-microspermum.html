Corispermum microspermum

Corispermum microspermum, commonly known as the small-seeded bladderwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its seeds are minute and dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across dry regions.

Corispermum microspermum is often found in saline soils and is adapted to thrive in environments with limited water availability.
