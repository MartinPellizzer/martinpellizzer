Cnicus chrismarii

Cnicus chrismarii, commonly known as the Christmas carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright red, pink, or white flowers that bloom in the winter months, typically from December to March. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and seasonal blooms, it is often cultivated in gardens during the holiday season.
