Cryptocarya zamboangensis

Cryptocarya zamboangensis, commonly known as the Zamboanga cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland to montane forests, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
