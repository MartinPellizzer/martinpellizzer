Crataegus oxyacantha var. incisa

Crataegus oxyacantha var. incisa, commonly known as the sharplobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The species is notable for its ecological value, providing food for birds and insects.
