Calyptrogyne intermedia

Calyptrogyne intermedia, commonly known as the salt marsh morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in saline or brackish wetlands. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Calyptrogyne intermedia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
