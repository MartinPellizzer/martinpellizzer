Centranthus angustifolius subsp. battandieri

Centranthus angustifolius subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry meadows. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
