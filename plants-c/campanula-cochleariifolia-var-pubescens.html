Campanula cochleariifolia var. pubescens

Campanula cochleariifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a similar pubescent coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
