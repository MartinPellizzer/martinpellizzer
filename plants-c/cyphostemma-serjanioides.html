Cyphostemma serjanioides

Cyphostemma serjanioides, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy, yellow fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cyphostemma serjanioides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
