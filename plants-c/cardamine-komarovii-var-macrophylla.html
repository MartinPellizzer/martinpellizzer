Cardamine komarovii var. macrophylla

Cardamine komarovii var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with elongated, lobed segments and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces numerous stolons that help in vegetative propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
