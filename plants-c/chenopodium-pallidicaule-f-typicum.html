Chenopodium pallidicaule f. typicum

Chenopodium pallidicaule f. typicum, commonly known as tumbleweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and production of dry, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is native to North America and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

This species is well-adapted to harsh conditions, making it a common pioneer plant in disturbed soils.
