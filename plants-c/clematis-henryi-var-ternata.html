Clematis henryi var. ternata

Clematis henryi var. ternata, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
