Callista bursigera

Callista bursigera, commonly known as the Bursigera wildflower, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to low rainfall environments.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its resilience and ornamental value.
