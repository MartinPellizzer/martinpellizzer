Croton coccymelophyllus

Croton coccymelophyllus, commonly known as the red-leaved croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage, which often features shades of red, green, and yellow. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

While it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value, it can also serve as a source of traditional medicine in some cultures.
