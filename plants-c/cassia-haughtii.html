Cassia haughtii

Cassia haughtii, commonly known as the Hawaiian cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cassia haughtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
