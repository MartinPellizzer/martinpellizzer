Carex sutchanensis

Carex sutchanensis, commonly known as Sutchan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex sutchanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
