Cassia acutisepala

Cassia acutisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Cassia acutisepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
