Cryptolepis laurentii

Cryptolepis laurentii, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its roots and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it a subject of interest in pharmacological research.
