Cuphea epilobiifolia var. canescens

Cuphea epilobiifolia var. canescens, commonly known as the gray cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and landscapes.
