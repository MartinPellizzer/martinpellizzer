Camellia flosculora

Camellia flosculora, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow or orange base. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. Camellia flosculora thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
