Coelogyne saigonensis

Coelogyne saigonensis, commonly known as the Saigon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Coelogyne saigonensis thrives in humid, shaded conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
