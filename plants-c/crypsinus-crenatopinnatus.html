Crypsinus crenatopinnatus

Crypsinus crenatopinnatus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinctive zigzag pattern along their edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Crypsinus crenatopinnatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
