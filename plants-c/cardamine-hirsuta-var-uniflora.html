Cardamine hirsuta var. uniflora

Cardamine hirsuta var. uniflora, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a single cluster at the tip of each stem.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, wet soils and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
