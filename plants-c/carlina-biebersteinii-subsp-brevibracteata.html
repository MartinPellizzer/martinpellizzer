Carlina biebersteinii subsp. brevibracteata

Carlina biebersteinii subsp.

brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bract carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features a robust stem with grayish-green, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

The plant produces showy, purple-blue composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its bracts are short and narrow, distinguishing it from other species in the genus.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
