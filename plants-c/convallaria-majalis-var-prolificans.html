Convallaria majalis var. prolificans

Convallaria majalis var. prolificans, commonly known as the fragrant lily-of-the-valley, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma. The plant produces showy, pendulous blooms in early spring, often preceding the emergence of its dark green, strap-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its prolific flowering and is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
