Carex forficula

Carex forficula, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Carex forficula is valued for its ecological role in wetland habitats and its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
