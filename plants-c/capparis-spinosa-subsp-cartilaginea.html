Capparis spinosa subsp. cartilaginea

Capparis spinosa subsp.

cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, round, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and covered in a waxy coating.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
