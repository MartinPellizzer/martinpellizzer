Caladium asperulum

Caladium asperulum, commonly known as the roughleaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Caladium asperulum is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage in gardens and as a houseplant.
