Celsia megrica

Celsia megrica, commonly known as the giant celsia, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and violet, which bloom during the cooler months. Its unique morphology allows it to thrive in harsh, mountainous environments with limited nutrients.

Celsia megrica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
