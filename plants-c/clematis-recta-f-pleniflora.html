Clematis recta f. pleniflora

Clematis recta f. pleniflora, commonly known as the full-flowered climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its attractive blooms and vigorous climbing habit.
