Corymborkis angustifolia

Corymborkis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed coral tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often arranged in corymbs, giving it the genus name "Corymborkis.".
