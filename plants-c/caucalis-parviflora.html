Caucalis parviflora

Caucalis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered caucalis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded locations.

Caucalis parviflora is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.
