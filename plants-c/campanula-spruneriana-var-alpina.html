Campanula spruneriana var. alpina

Campanula spruneriana var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

Campanula spruneriana var. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
