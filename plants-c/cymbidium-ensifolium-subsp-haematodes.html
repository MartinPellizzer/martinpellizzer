Cymbidium ensifolium subsp. haematodes

Cymbidium ensifolium subsp.

haematodes, commonly known as the blood-leaf orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often a deep red or maroon color, giving it its common name.

The plant produces tall spikes with clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists.
