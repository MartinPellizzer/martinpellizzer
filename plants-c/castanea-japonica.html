Castanea japonica

Castanea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its serrated leaves and spiny husks. The tree produces edible nuts encased in a thick, spiky shell, which are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritious nuts.

The plant plays an important role in both ecological systems and traditional cuisines in its native region.
