Convolvulus cantabrica subsp. medius

Convolvulus cantabrica subsp.

medius, commonly known as the Cantabrian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its hairy stems and leaves are characteristic features, contributing to its identification.

It is often found in coastal regions of Spain and Portugal, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
