Cola quinqueloba

Cola quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed cola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are typically yellowish-green in color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and for making beverages.
