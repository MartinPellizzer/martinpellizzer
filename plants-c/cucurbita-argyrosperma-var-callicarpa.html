Cucurbita argyrosperma var. callicarpa

Cucurbita argyrosperma var. callicarpa, commonly known as the golden cup squash, is a variant of the argyrosperma species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange fruit, which has a smooth, thin rind and a sweet, nutty flavor. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces large, pendant-shaped fruits, often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique taste, making it a popular choice among gardeners and chefs.
