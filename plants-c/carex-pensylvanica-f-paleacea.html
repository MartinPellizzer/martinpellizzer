Carex pensylvanica f. paleacea

Carex pensylvanica f. paleacea, commonly known as the pale-leaved sedge, is a variant of the Pennsylvania sedge.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive pale foliage and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
