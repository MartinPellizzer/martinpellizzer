Colubrina vitiensis

Colubrina vitiensis, commonly known as Vitiensis beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly the islands of the Lesser Antilles.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright purple berries that are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are simple, opposite, and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
