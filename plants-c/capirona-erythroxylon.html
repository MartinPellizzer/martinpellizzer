Capirona erythroxylon

Capirona erythroxylon, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Capirona erythroxylon is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
