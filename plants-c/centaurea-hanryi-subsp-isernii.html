Centaurea hanryi subsp. isernii

Centaurea hanryi subsp.

isernii, commonly known as the Isern's cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features striking blue to violet ray florets surrounded by pale yellow disk florets, making it visually distinctive. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
