Carex merinoi

Carex merinoi, commonly known as the Merino sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex merinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
