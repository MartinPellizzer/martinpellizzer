Chenopodium album var. striatiforme

Chenopodium album var. striatiforme, commonly known as goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual herb characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in various habitats.
