Cattleya aromatica

Cattleya aromatica, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Cattleya aromatica is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beautiful, long-lasting blooms.
