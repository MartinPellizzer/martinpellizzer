Carex hakkuensis

Carex hakkuensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex hakkuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
