Cyrtandra maesifolia

Cyrtandra maesifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered cyrtandra, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and is often found in humid, tropical climates.

Cyrtandra maesifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
