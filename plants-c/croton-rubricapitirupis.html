Croton rubricapitirupis

Croton rubricapitirupis, commonly known as the red-capped croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, palmate leaves that often display a striking red or orange coloration on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in tropical regions.
