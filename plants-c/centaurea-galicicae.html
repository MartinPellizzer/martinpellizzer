Centaurea galicicae

Centaurea galicicae, commonly known as the Galician centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though it is now considered endangered due to habitat loss.

Conservation efforts are crucial to preserve this rare and unique species.
