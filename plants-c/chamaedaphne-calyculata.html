Chamaedaphne calyculata

Chamaedaphne calyculata, commonly known as leatherleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern coast of North America.

It is characterized by its waxy, dark green leaves that remain leathery even when dry. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in swamps and bogs.

Chamaedaphne calyculata plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
