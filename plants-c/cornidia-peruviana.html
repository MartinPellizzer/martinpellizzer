Cornidia peruviana

Cornidia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cornidia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
