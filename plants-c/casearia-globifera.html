Casearia globifera

Casearia globifera, commonly known as the globular casearia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections in warm climates.
