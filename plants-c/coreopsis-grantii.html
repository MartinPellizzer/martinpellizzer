Coreopsis grantii

Coreopsis grantii, commonly known as the Texas star, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow to orange daisy-like flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Coreopsis grantii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
