Cyperus aethiops var. aberdarensis

Cyperus aethiops var. aberdarensis, commonly known as Aberdare flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to East Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its botanical features include slender stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled fruits.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential horticultural applications.
