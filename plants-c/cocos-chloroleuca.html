Cocos chloroleuca

Cocos chloroleuca, commonly known as the white-fruited coconut, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, fibrous fruits that are primarily used for their coconut water and oil. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments.

The plant plays an important role in both ecological and economic contexts in its native habitats.
