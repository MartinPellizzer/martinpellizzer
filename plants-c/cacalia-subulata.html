Cacalia subulata

Cacalia subulata, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, spiny stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and pointed, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Cacalia subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
