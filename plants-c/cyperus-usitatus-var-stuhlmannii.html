Cyperus usitatus var. stuhlmannii

Cyperus usitatus var. stuhlmannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers in spikelets. This species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

The plant is adapted to moist environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
