Cyathea concordia

Cyathea concordia, commonly known as the Concord fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived ornamental plant. Its fronds are typically bright green and maintain their color throughout the year, adding aesthetic value to gardens.

Cyathea concordia prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
