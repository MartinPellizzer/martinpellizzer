Corydalis procera

Corydalis procera, commonly known as the giant lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and have two lobes, which are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

Corydalis procera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
