Crambe filiformis

Crambe filiformis, commonly known as the slender crambe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Crambe filiformis is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is considered a herbaceous perennial.
