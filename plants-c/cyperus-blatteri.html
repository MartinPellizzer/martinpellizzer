Cyperus blatteri

Cyperus blatteri, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil.

Cyperus blatteri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and prevent erosion.
