Cereus calvus

Cereus calvus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its swollen, segmented stems that are typically green or red in color. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, often during the summer months. Its stems are covered in small, spiny tubercles, and it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
