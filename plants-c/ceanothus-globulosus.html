Ceanothus globulosus

Ceanothus globulosus, commonly known as the globe ceanothus, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus globulosus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
