Clematis chinensis var. vestita

Clematis chinensis var. vestita, commonly known as the Chinese vine or Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can grow up to several meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or as a hedge.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, though it can tolerate some shade.
