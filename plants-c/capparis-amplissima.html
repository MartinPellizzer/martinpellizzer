Capparis amplissima

Capparis amplissima, commonly known as the giant caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it thrives in coastal and rocky habitats.

Capparis amplissima is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
