Crataegus argyi

Crataegus argyi, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Crataegus argyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in temperate climates.
