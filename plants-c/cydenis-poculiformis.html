Cydenis poculiformis

Cydenis poculiformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and ragworts. The plant is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped flower head, which is composed of numerous small white to pale yellow florets. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Cydenis poculiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
