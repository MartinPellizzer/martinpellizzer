Campuloclinium macrocephalum var. strigosum

Campuloclinium macrocephalum var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy-headed tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with dark purple centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for native gardens and restoration projects.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in its habitat.
