Carex tabina

Carex tabina, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Carex tabina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
