Cydonia bourgeautii

Cydonia bourgeautii, commonly known as the African quince, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits that resemble quinces. Cydonia bourgeautii is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in its native regions.
