Copernicia hospita var. clarensis

Copernicia hospita var. clarensis, commonly known as the Clarense palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender trunk and a crown of pinnate, dark green leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in sandy or coastal soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
