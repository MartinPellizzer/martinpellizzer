Ctenitis falciculata

Ctenitis falciculata, commonly known as the sickle-crested fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, sickle-shaped appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
