Croton nephrophyllus

Croton nephrophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a combination of silver, green, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
