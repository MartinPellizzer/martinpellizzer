Cerastium vulgare var. holosteoides

Cerastium vulgare var. holosteoides, commonly known as the Holosteoides mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, oval leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a common species in horticultural settings and ecological restoration projects.
