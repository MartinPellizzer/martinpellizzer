Cattleya labiata var. trianae

Cattleya labiata var. trianae, commonly known as the Trianas Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically display a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
