Clematis grata var. likiangensis

Clematis grata var. likiangensis, commonly known as the Likiang Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale blue, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or walls.
