Coleus erici-rosenii

Coleus erici-rosenii, commonly known as the Ericaceous Coleus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are clustered in whorled inflorescences and come in a variety of colors, including red, purple, and white.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
