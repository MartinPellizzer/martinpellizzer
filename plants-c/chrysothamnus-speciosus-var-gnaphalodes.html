Chrysothamnus speciosus var. gnaphalodes

Chrysothamnus speciosus var. gnaphalodes, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branching.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
