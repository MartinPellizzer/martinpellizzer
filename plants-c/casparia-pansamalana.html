Casparia pansamalana

Casparia pansamalana, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, exhibiting a rosette-like arrangement of narrow, silver-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Casparia pansamalana plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
