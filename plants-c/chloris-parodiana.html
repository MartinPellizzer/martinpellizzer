Chloris parodiana

Chloris parodiana, commonly known as the Parodiana grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Chloris parodiana is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
