Carex × tornabenei

Carex × tornabenei, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex stricta and Carex lyngbyei, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and upright growth habit. The plant typically produces spikelets that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex × tornabenei is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecological communities.
