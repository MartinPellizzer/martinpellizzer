Calea tridactylita

Calea tridactylita, commonly known as the "Mexican snowflake," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Calea tridactylita is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
