Cypripedium humboldtii

Cypripedium humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's lady's slipper, is a perennial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It features a distinctive, showy flower with a pouch-like lip that is typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and grows in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
