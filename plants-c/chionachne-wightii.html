Chionachne wightii

Chionachne wightii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicle-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Chionachne wightii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
