Cyperus exaltatus var. iwasakii

Cyperus exaltatus var. iwasakii, commonly known as the Japanese marsh grass, is a variant of the common flatsedge.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water areas. It has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
