Columnea carinata

Columnea carinata, commonly known as the ridge-tongued flame flower, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

Columnea carinata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
