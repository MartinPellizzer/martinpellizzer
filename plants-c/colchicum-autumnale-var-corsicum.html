Colchicum autumnale var. corsicum

Colchicum autumnale var. corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican autumn crocus, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm. It is characterized by its toxic properties, containing colchicine, which is used in medicine.

This variety is often found in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in partial shade.
