Clarisia racemosa

Clarisia racemosa, commonly known as the evergreen trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Clarisia racemosa is also valued for its ability to produce small, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.
