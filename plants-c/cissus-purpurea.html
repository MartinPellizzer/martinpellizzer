Cissus purpurea

Cissus purpurea, commonly known as the purple cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus purpurea is often grown as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
