Crotalaria phylicoides

Crotalaria phylicoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also serves as a forage crop for livestock.

Crotalaria phylicoides is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
