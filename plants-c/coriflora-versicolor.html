Coriflora versicolor

Coriflora versicolor, commonly known as the variable flower plant, is a flowering species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored petals that range from white to deep purple, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Coriflora versicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
