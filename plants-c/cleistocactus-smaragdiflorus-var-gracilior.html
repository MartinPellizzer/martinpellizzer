Cleistocactus smaragdiflorus var. gracilior

Cleistocactus smaragdiflorus var. gracilior, commonly known as the emerald-flowered cleistocactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the dry regions of northern Argentina.

It features a short, rounded stem that produces clusters of bright green, ribbed segments. The plant is distinguished by its vibrant, yellow-green flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and rockeries.
