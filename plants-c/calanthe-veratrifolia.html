Calanthe veratrifolia

Calanthe veratrifolia, commonly known as the veined calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests. Its flowers have a distinctive veined pattern on the petals, which gives it its common name.

Calanthe veratrifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
