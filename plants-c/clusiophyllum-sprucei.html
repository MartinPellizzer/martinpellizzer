Clusiophyllum sprucei

Clusiophyllum sprucei, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy.
