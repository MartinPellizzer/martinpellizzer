Citrus × aurantium var. microcarpon

Citrus × aurantium var. microcarpon, commonly known as the pummelo, is a citrus plant characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is closely related to the grapefruit and orange. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub with dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often blooming in clusters.

The fruit is used for its juice, zest, and in various culinary and medicinal applications.
