Colchicum hierosolymitanum

Colchicum hierosolymitanum, commonly known as the Jerusalem colchicum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Levant region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
