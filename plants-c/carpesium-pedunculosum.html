Carpesium pedunculosum

Carpesium pedunculosum, commonly known as the clustered goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often branched and can grow up to several feet tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color.

Carpesium pedunculosum is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
