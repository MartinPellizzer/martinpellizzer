Chrysanthemum deserticola

Chrysanthemum deserticola, commonly known as the desert chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-green foliage, which helps it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It has adapted to survive in poor soil conditions and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a hardy ornamental plant in dry climates.
