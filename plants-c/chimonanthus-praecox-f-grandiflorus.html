Chimonanthus praecox f. grandiflorus

Chimonanthus praecox f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered witch hazel, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, red, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features include opposite, lance-shaped leaves and a woody, shrub-like structure.
