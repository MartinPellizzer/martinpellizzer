Cacalia obovata

Cacalia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Cacalia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
