Croton alabamensis var. texensis

Croton alabamensis var. texensis, commonly known as the Texas croton, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and can produce yellow or orange flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained soil.

It is often found in the coastal plain regions of Texas and Louisiana.
