Calypso pyriformis

Calypso pyriformis, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.

Calypso pyriformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
