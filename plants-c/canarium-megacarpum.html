Canarium megacarpum

Canarium megacarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces large, yellowish flowers followed by woody, orange-brown fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are rich in oil and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biodiesel.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
