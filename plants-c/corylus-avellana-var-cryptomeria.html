Corylus avellana var. cryptomeria

Corylus avellana var. cryptomeria, commonly known as the Japanese hazelnut, is a variant of the common hazelnut.

It is characterized by its elongated, clustered nuts and smooth, dark brown husks. The plant produces small, yellowish male catkins and female flowers that develop into the edible nuts. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
