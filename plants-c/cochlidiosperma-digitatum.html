Cochlidiosperma digitatum

Cochlidiosperma digitatum, commonly known as the fingerleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cochlidiosperma digitatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
