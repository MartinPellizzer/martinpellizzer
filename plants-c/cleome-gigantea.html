Cleome gigantea

Cleome gigantea, commonly known as the "Spider Flower," is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome gigantea is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
