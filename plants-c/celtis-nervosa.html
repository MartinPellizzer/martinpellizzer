Celtis nervosa

Celtis nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf box elder, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits that are dispersed by wind. Celtis nervosa typically grows in moist soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

It is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
