Clematis argentilucida var. likiangensis

Clematis argentilucida var. likiangensis, commonly known as the silver-leafed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. Its growth habit is climbing, and it produces tendrils that help it ascend supports.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
