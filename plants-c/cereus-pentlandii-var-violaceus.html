Cereus pentlandii var. violaceus

Cereus pentlandii var. violaceus, commonly known as the Purple Cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-pink flowers that bloom at night. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers warm, arid climates.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
