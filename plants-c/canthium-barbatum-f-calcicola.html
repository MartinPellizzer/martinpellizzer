Canthium barbatum f. calcicola

Canthium barbatum f. calcicola, commonly known as the calcicole canthium, is a variant of the genus Canthium native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, which is reflected in its subspecific name "calcicola," meaning "calcium dweller.". Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
