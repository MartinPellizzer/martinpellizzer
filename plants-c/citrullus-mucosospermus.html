Citrullus mucosospermus

Citrullus mucosospermus, commonly known as the watermelon, is a vine plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and large, lobed leaves. The fruit is typically green on the outside with a red or yellow flesh containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued for its high water content and nutritional value, particularly its high levels of vitamin C and lycopene.
