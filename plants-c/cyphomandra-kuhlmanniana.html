Cyphomandra kuhlmanniana

Cyphomandra kuhlmanniana, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits, which are typically red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
