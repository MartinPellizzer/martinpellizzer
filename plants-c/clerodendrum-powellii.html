Clerodendrum powellii

Clerodendrum powellii, commonly known as the Powell's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
