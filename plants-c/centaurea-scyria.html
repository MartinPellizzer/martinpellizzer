Centaurea scyria

Centaurea scyria, commonly known as the Turkish thistle, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly leaves and vibrant purple to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its height can range from 30 to 100 centimeters, and it produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea scyria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
