Canna sanguinea

Canna sanguinea, commonly known as the blood canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically appearing in late summer. Canna sanguinea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Its foliage and blooms add vibrant color and texture to both ornamental and aquatic landscapes.
