Chrysopteris phymatodes

Chrysopteris phymatodes, commonly known as the golden leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with yellow or gold on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
