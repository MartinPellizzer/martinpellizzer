Callicarpa bicolor var. subintegrifolia

Callicarpa bicolor var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of small, white flowers that give way to vibrant red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin, and the plant has a compact growth habit. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife.
