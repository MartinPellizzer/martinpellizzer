Caladium maculatum

Caladium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that display striking patterns of red, white, and green. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Caladium maculatum is typically propagated through tuberous roots and is popular in gardens and as houseplants.
