Carex rariflora f. rufescens

Carex rariflora f. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically reddish in color. This variety is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
