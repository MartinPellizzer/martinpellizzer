Ceropegia mahabalei var. hemalatae

Ceropegia mahabalei var. hemalatae, commonly known as the Hemalatae Ceropegia, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant exhibits a climbing habit, with slender stems that can twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.
