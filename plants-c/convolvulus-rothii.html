Convolvulus rothii

Convolvulus rothii, commonly known as the Roth's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, a characteristic shared with other morning glory species.

Convolvulus rothii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.
