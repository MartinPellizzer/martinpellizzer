Cissus porphyrophylla

Cissus porphyrophylla, commonly known as the red-leafed devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage and heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.

Cissus porphyrophylla is also valued for its ability to climb and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
