Cousinia capusii

Cousinia capusii, commonly known as the Kashmir copper grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Cousinia capusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
