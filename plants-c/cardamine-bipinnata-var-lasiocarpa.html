Cardamine bipinnata var. lasiocarpa

Cardamine bipinnata var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy water crowfoot, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal umbel, which bloom in early spring. This variety is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
