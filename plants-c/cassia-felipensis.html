Cassia felipensis

Cassia felipensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Cassia felipensis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
