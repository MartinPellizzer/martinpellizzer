Cyathea atahuallpa

Cyathea atahuallpa, commonly known as the Atahualpa tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
