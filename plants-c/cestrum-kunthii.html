Cestrum kunthii

Cestrum kunthii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum kunthii is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other night-blooming plants like the moonflower.
