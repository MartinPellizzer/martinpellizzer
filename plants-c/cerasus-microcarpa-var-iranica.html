Cerasus microcarpa var. iranica

Cerasus microcarpa var. iranica, commonly known as the Iranian cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to rocky and arid environments.
