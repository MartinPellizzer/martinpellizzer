Cerasus itosakura

Cerasus itosakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is closely related to other cherry species. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and turn yellow in autumn.

The fruit, a small cherry, is edible but not as widely cultivated as other cherry varieties.
