Coleus latifolius var. elatior

Coleus latifolius var. elatior, commonly known as the broadleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that can display shades of red, purple, pink, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are small and tubular, usually appearing in clusters at the leaf axils, and are typically white or pale purple in color.
