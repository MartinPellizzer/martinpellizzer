Cyperus michelianus f. elatus

Cyperus michelianus f. elatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes that are often subtended by bracts. This plant is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
