Coelogyne dichroantha

Coelogyne dichroantha, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, contrasting coloration on its petals and sepals, which often display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the winter months, adding to its ornamental value.

The orchid's unique morphology and color patterns make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
