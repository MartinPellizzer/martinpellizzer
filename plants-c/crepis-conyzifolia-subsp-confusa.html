Crepis conyzifolia subsp. confusa

Crepis conyzifolia subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawk's-beard, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with toothed margins, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
