Conoclinium dichotomum

Conoclinium dichotomum, commonly known as the Mexican beeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. These flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
