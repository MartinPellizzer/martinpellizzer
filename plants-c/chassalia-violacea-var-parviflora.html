Chassalia violacea var. parviflora

Chassalia violacea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered chassalia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
