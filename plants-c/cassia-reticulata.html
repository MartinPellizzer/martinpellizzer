Cassia reticulata

Cassia reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive reticulated pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Cassia reticulata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
