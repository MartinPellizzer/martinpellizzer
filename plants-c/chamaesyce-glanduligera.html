Chamaesyce glanduligera

Chamaesyce glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular purslane, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
