Chenopodium album

Chenopodium album, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and its small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa but has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium album is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
