Capsella nylandica

Capsella nylandica, commonly known as the Norwegian chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Capsella nylandica thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
