Chaetolepis gentianoides

Chaetolepis gentianoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
