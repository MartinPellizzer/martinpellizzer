Centaurea filarszkyi

Centaurea filarszkyi, commonly known as the Hungarian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Hungary and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and bright purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its growth habit is perennial, and it can form dense colonies, making it a potential weed in some areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
