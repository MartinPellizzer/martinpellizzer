Calystegia sepium var. fraterniflora

Calystegia sepium var. fraterniflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system and can spread aggressively through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
