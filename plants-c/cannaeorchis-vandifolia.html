Cannaeorchis vandifolia

Cannaeorchis vandifolia, commonly known as the Vandifolia orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, strap-like leaves and strikingly colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained, organic soil. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, lip-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cannaeorchis vandifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
