Citrus torosa

Citrus torosa, commonly known as the roughleaf mandarin, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, serrated leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange-colored fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained soils.

Citrus torosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible citrus fruits.
