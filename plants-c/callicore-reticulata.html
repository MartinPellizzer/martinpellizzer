Callicore reticulata

Callicore reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated swallowtail.

This plant is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. It produces small, white to greenish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are usually divided into lobes and have a distinctive net-like pattern on the underside.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or open areas with well-drained soil.
