Carex lyngbyei var. prionocarpa

Carex lyngbyei var. prionocarpa, commonly known as the Lyngby's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, typical of sedges. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are usually inconspicuous and clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
