Ceropegia racemosa

Ceropegia racemosa, commonly known as the string of hearts or wandering jew, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, which are pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular as a houseplant and in gardens.
