Crataegus monogyna f. rosiformis

Crataegus monogyna f. rosiformis, commonly known as the rose-forming hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rose-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated margins, and it produces red berries in the autumn.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
