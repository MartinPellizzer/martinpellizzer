Castanopsis cuspidata

Castanopsis cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscaping.
