Cardamine bellidifolia var. typica

Cardamine bellidifolia var. typica, commonly known as the typical watercress.

This perennial herb is native to wet, calcareous environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, typical of the Brassicaceae family. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

It is often found in stream margins and damp meadows, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
