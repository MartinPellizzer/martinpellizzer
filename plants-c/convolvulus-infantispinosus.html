Convolvulus infantispinosus

Convolvulus infantispinosus, commonly known as the spiny baby morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with tendrils that allow it to climb and twine around supports. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
