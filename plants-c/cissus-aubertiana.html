Cissus aubertiana

Cissus aubertiana, commonly known as the climbing devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It is a member of the grape family (Vitaceae) and is known for its ability to climb using tendrils.

Cissus aubertiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
