Clematis brevicaudata

Clematis brevicaudata, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, reddish-brown hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis brevicaudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
