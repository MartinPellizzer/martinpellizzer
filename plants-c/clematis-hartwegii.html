Clematis hartwegii

Clematis hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually blue or violet flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a desirable addition to gardens in suitable climates.

Clematis hartwegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
