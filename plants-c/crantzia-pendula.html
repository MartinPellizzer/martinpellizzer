Crantzia pendula

Crantzia pendula, commonly known as the hanging cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Crantzia pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
