Cyphostemma quinatum

Cyphostemma quinatum, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
