Carex ebracteata

Carex ebracteata, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It produces spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Carex ebracteata is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
