Chamaemelum coronopifolium

Chamaemelum coronopifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers are often harvested for use in teas and other remedies.
