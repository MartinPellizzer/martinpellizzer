Cotula aurea

Cotula aurea, commonly known as the golden cotula, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, often blooming in clusters. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cotula aurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
