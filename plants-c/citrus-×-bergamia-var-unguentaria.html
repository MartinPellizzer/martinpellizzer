Citrus × bergamia var. unguentaria

Citrus × bergamia var. unguentaria, commonly known as the bitter orange, is a citrus hybrid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, pebbled rind and oval-shaped fruit, which is typically harvested when fully mature. The plant produces a fragrant, yellowish-orange fruit with a high oil content in its peel, making it valuable in aromatherapy and perfumery. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and opposite, while the flowers are white and fragrant.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
