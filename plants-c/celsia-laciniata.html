Celsia laciniata

Celsia laciniata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance.

Native to regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high moisture levels. The plant features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense mats. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

Celsia laciniata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
