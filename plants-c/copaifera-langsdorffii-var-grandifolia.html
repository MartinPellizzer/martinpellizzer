Copaifera langsdorffii var. grandifolia

Copaifera langsdorffii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark is renowned for containing copaiba oil, which is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

This species plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
