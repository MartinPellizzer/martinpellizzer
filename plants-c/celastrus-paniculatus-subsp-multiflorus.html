Celastrus paniculatus subsp. multiflorus

Celastrus paniculatus subsp.

multiflorus, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or the multiflorous staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
