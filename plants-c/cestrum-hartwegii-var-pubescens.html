Cestrum hartwegii var. pubescens

Cestrum hartwegii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, typical of the nightshade family.
