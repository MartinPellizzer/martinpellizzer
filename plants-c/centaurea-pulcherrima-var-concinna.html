Centaurea pulcherrima var. concinna

Centaurea pulcherrima var. concinna, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has upright, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for dry gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and native plant landscapes.
