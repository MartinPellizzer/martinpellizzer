Crataegus oxyacantha glabra

Crataegus oxyacantha glabra, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
