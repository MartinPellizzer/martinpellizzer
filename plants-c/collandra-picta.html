Collandra picta

Collandra picta, commonly known as the painted collandra, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow, making it a visually appealing ornamental species. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves that are often divided into numerous leaflets. The flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in umbels, and are followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, Collandra picta is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its ornamental value.
