Croton magdalenae

Croton magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that display shades of red, orange, and yellow. It typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant and unique leaf patterns.
