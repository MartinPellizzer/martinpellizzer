Cerasus humilis

Cerasus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a small deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn red in the autumn.

Cerasus humilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and compact growth habit.
