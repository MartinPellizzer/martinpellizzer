Crataegus deltoides

Crataegus deltoides, commonly known as the deltoid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval to deltoid-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, stream banks, and open fields.

Crataegus deltoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
