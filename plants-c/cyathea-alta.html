Cyathea alta

Cyathea alta, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, sturdy trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forests or along riverbanks. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 5 meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.

Cyathea alta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
