Chloris aristata

Chloris aristata, commonly known as the spikeless grass or slender hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Chloris aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
