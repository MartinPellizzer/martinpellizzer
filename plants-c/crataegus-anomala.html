Crataegus anomala

Crataegus anomala, commonly known as the burr cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it typically grows in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Crataegus anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
