Catasetum brachybulbon

Catasetum brachybulbon, commonly known as the short-stalked orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers with distinctive sepals and petals. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
