Canavalia rockii

Canavalia rockii, commonly known as the Chinese burr legume, is a perennial climbing plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its characteristic burr-like fruits. Canavalia rockii is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed.
