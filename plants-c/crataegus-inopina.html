Crataegus inopina

Crataegus inopina, commonly known as the rare white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Crataegus inopina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in many regions.
