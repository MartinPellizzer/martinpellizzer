Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. commune

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. commune, commonly known as cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is widely used as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into oval, reddish berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct, slightly spicy aroma.

The tree thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
