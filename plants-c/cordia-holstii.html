Cordia holstii

Cordia holstii, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture with a distinctive scent.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
