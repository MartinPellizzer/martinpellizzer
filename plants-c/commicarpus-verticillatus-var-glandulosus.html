Commicarpus verticillatus var. glandulosus

Commicarpus verticillatus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular commicarpus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, glandular-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique leaf morphology and floral structure make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
