Clematis orientalis var. daurica

Clematis orientalis var. daurica, commonly known as the Daurian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, showy structure. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
