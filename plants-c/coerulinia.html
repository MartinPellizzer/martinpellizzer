Coerulinia

Coerulinia, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vivid blue petals and intricate floral structure. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Its unique coloration is due to the presence of specific pigments that are not commonly found in other orchid species.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Coerulinia is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
