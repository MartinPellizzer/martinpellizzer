Crucifera petrocallis

Crucifera petrocallis, commonly known as the rock crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the cruciferous plants. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to grow in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh, limestone-rich soils.
