Centaurea ruprechtii

Centaurea ruprechtii, commonly known as Ruprecht's cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Centaurea ruprechtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
