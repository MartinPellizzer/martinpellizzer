Carthamus corymbosus

Carthamus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, spiky seed heads that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and deep.

Carthamus corymbosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
