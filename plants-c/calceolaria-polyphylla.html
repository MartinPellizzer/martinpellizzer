Calceolaria polyphylla

Calceolaria polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Calceolaria polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
