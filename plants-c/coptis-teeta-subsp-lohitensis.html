Coptis teeta subsp. lohitensis

Coptis teeta subsp.

lohitensis, commonly known as the Lohitensis goldthread, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its rhizomatous growth and clustered yellow flowers.

Native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal, it thrives in high-altitude alpine environments. The plant's leaves are typically trifoliate, and its flowers have five sepals and numerous stamens.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its alkaloid content, which has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
