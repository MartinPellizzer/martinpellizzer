Cassia mayana

Cassia mayana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods containing flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia mayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
