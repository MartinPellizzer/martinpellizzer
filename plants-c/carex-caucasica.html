Carex caucasica

Carex caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex caucasica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
