Cleome hamiltoniana

Cleome hamiltoniana, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Cleome hamiltoniana is a hardy annual that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
