Collinia elegans

Collinia elegans, commonly known as the elegant collinia, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flower heads that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Collinia elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to attract pollinators.
