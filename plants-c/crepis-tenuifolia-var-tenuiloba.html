Crepis tenuifolia var. tenuiloba

Crepis tenuifolia var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, reaching a height of about 20 to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats where it can thrive in full sun.
