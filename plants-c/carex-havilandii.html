Carex havilandii

Carex havilandii, commonly known as Haviland's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads in late spring to early summer.

Carex havilandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
