Crassula exserta

Crassula exserta, commonly known as the "Pig's Ear" or "Lamb's Ear," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy texture, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Crassula exserta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
