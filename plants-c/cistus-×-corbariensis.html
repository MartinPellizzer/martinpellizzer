Cistus × corbariensis

Cistus × corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbari cistus, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the rockrose genus, characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus × corbariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
