Cerasus integerrima

Cerasus integerrima, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its beautiful spring blossoms.

Cerasus integerrima is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
