Carex laeta var. rostrata

Carex laeta var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its upright growth habit and subtle floral display.
