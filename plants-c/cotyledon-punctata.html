Cotyledon punctata

Cotyledon punctata, commonly known as the pebble plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a pebbly or bumpy texture. The leaves are typically green or reddish in color, and the plant produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant's unique appearance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among succulent enthusiasts.
