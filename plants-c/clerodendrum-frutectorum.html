Clerodendrum frutectorum

Clerodendrum frutectorum, commonly known as the "Bitterleaf" or "Wild Clerodendrum," is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
