Chromolaena ascendens

Chromolaena ascendens, commonly known as horseweed or chromolaena, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is known for its rapid growth and invasive potential in certain ecosystems. Chromolaena ascendens is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.

Its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions contributes to its widespread distribution and ecological impact.
