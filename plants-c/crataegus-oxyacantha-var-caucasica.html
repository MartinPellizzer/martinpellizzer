Crataegus oxyacantha var. caucasica

Crataegus oxyacantha var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
