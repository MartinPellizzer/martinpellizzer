Cyperus aromaticus var. elatus

Cyperus aromaticus var. elatus, commonly known as the fragrant flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ponds, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
