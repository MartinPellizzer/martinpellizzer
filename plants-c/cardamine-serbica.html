Cardamine serbica

Cardamine serbica, commonly known as Serbian watercress, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along streams, in forest understories, and in wet meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, rounded leaflets, and it produces small white or yellowish flowers.

The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in areas with rich, humusy soil.
