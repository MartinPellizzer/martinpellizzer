Convolvulus tuberifer

Convolvulus tuberifer, commonly known as the tuberous morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant produces tuberous roots, which are swollen underground storage organs used for propagating the species. Native to regions of southern Africa, it thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
