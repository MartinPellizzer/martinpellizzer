Camelina sativa subsp. dentata

Camelina sativa subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the serrated camelina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear, serrated leaves that give it its subspecies name.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in temperate climates. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other oilseed crops like mustard and rapeseed.

Its seeds are used for oil production and have potential applications in biodiesel and nutritional supplements.
