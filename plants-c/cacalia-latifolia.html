Cacalia latifolia

Cacalia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often broader than they are long. Its flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cacalia latifolia is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
