Cracca affinis

Cracca affinis, commonly known as the red brome, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, reddish spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. Cracca affinis typically thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean flora and a subject of study in ecological research.
