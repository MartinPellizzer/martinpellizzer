Cyperus ngothe

Cyperus ngothe, commonly known as the African flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus ngothe is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
