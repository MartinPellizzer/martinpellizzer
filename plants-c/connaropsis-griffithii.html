Connaropsis griffithii

Connaropsis griffithii, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a striking display of red or pink hues, which attract various pollinators.
