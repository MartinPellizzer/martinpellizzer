Crataegus pulchella

Crataegus pulchella, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its ornamental, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red fruits known as pomes, which are typically edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Crataegus pulchella thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open habitats.

It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing food for wildlife.
