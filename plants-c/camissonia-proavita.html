Camissonia proavita

Camissonia proavita, commonly known as the narrowleaf camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Camissoniaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Camissonia proavita is adapted to mild, coastal climates and is considered a rare and sensitive species.
