Campulosus concinnus

Campulosus concinnus, commonly known as the elegant campulose, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and clustered in a head, often displaying a mix of yellow and white petals.

Campulosus concinnus is often found in moist, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
