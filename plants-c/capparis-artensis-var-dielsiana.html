Capparis artensis var. dielsiana

Capparis artensis var. dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, citrus-like fruits that are used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
