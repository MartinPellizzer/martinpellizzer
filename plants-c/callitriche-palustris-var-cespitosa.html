Callitriche palustris var. cespitosa

Callitriche palustris var. cespitosa, commonly known as marsh stitchwort, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetland habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense mats. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually submerged or floating. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays an important role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
