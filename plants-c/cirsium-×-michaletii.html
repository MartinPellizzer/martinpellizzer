Cirsium × michaletii

Cirsium × michaletii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium undulatum, resulting in a plant with distinctive, spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Cirsium × michaletii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
