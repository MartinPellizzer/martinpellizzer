Careya lanceolata

Careya lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
