Carolinea marginata

Carolinea marginata, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often branched and covered in a fine, white coating.

Carolinea marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
