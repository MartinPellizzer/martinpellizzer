Crateva tapia subsp. odora

Crateva tapia subsp.

odora, commonly known as the sweet-scented cowpea, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and vibrant foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic and culinary uses.
