Coppensia ramosa

Coppensia ramosa, commonly known as the slender swamp rush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Australia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Coppensia ramosa plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland biodiversity and water quality.
