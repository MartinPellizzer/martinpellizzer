Cucumis melo var. maltensis

Cucumis melo var. maltensis, commonly known as the Maltese melon, is a variety of muskmelon native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Malta.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green rind and sweet, aromatic, yellow to orange flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
