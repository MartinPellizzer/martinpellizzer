Camaridium quercicola

Camaridium quercicola, commonly known as the oakleaf fern, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the temperate forests of North America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that resemble the shape of oak leaves, hence its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically a deep green, with a slightly glossy texture, and they can grow up to two feet in length.

Camaridium quercicola is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to add a lush, tropical feel to shaded landscapes.
