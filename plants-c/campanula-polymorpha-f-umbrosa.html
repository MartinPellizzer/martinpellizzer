Campanula polymorpha f. umbrosa

Campanula polymorpha f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

It is a compact, low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, shady environments, making it ideal for woodland gardens. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a slightly waxy texture. It produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms from late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
