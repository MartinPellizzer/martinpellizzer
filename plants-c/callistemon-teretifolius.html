Callistemon teretifolius

Callistemon teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Callistemon teretifolius is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects due to its nectar-rich flowers.
