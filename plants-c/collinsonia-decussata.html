Collinsonia decussata

Collinsonia decussata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland conditions.
