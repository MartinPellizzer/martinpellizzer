Clematis striata

Clematis striata, commonly known as the smooth clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with a hairy texture that becomes smoother as it matures. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Clematis striata is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
