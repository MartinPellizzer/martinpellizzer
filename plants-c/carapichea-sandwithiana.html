Carapichea sandwithiana

Carapichea sandwithiana, commonly known as the Sandwith's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Carapichea sandwithiana is often found in rocky soils and open grasslands, thriving in full sun.
