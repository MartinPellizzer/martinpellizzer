Clematis bojeri var. anethiflora

Clematis bojeri var. anethiflora, commonly known as the anise-leaved clematis, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that resemble anise, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or walls.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
