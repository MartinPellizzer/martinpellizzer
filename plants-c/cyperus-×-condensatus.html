Cyperus × condensatus

Cyperus × condensatus, commonly known as the dense flatsedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Cyperus × condensatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to various water conditions.
