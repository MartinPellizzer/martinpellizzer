Cassia barbata var. paraguayensis

Cassia barbata var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated seed pods that contain small, angular seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
