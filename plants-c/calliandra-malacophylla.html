Calliandra malacophylla

Calliandra malacophylla, commonly known as the scarlet calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange inflorescences, which consist of dense clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its woody stems are often covered in a distinctive powdery coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive blooms and drought tolerance, it is also used in ecological restoration projects.
