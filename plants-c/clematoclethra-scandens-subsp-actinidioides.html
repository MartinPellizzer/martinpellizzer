Clematoclethra scandens subsp. actinidioides

Clematoclethra scandens subsp.

actinidioides, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
