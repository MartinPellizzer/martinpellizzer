Campanula rotundifolia var. cochleariifolia

Campanula rotundifolia var. cochleariifolia, commonly known as the bellflower or bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features distinctive bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
