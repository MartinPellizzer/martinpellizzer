Clerodendrum erectum

Clerodendrum erectum, commonly known as the upright clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
