Campanula rotundifolia var. montana

Campanula rotundifolia var. montana, commonly known as mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features rounded, broad leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
