Cyperus atrosanguineus

Cyperus atrosanguineus, commonly known as blackroot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyperus atrosanguineus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
