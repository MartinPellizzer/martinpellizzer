Chamaepus

Chamaepus, commonly known as the common rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. The plant has small, linear leaves that are tightly packed along its stems, forming a dense mat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Chamaepus is characterized by its ability to tolerate harsh conditions, making it a hardy species in challenging landscapes.
