Coelogyne coronaria

Coelogyne coronaria, commonly known as the crowned orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant has a pseudobulb structure with fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is adorned with a distinctive crown-like structure, giving it its common name.

Coelogyne coronaria thrives in well-draining substrates and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.
