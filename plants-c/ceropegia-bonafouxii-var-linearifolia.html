Ceropegia bonafouxii var. linearifolia

Ceropegia bonafouxii var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved waxplant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a succulent vine characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and opposite, linear leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
