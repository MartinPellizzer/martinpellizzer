Cissus selloana var. glabra

Cissus selloana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, citrus-scented berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its ability to climb and its attractive foliage make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor spaces.
