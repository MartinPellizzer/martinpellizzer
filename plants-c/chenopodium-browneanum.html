Chenopodium browneanum

Chenopodium browneanum, commonly known as brown's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Chenopodium browneanum is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly tangy flavor.
