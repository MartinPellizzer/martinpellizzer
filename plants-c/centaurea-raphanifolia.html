Centaurea raphanifolia

Centaurea raphanifolia, commonly known as the radishleaf knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves that resemble the leaves of a radish. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Centaurea raphanifolia is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
