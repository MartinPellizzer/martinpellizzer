Cissus mirabilis

Cissus mirabilis, commonly known as the star ivy or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish fruits.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to climb trellises or walls.
