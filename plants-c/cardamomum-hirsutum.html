Cardamomum hirsutum

Cardamomum hirsutum, commonly known as hairy cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and produces small, black, peppery fruits that contain the spice used in various cuisines. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.

The plant's unique appearance and aromatic qualities make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
