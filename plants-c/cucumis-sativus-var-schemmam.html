Cucumis sativus var. schemmam

Cucumis sativus var. schemmam, commonly known as the Schemmam cucumber, is a cultivated variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elongated fruits with a thin, crisp skin. The plant typically has hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often grown for its tender, flavorful cucumbers used in fresh salads and pickling.
