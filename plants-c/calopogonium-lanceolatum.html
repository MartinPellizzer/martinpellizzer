Calopogonium lanceolatum

Calopogonium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf velvetbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Calopogonium lanceolatum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
