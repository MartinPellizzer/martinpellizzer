Camelina persistens

Camelina persistens, commonly known as yellow camellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Camelina persistens is adapted to drought conditions and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
