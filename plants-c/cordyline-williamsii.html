Cordyline williamsii

Cordyline williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, strap-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The leaves are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge on the underside, and they are arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to produce striking red or purple flowers in the spring.
