Carica quinqueloba

Carica quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and juicy flesh that contains seeds.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
