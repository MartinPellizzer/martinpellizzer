Chrysophyllum welwitschii

Chrysophyllum welwitschii, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
