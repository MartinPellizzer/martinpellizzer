Carex acutata

Carex acutata, commonly known as sharpleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and triangular stems, which are typically smooth and slightly ribbed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Carex acutata prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
