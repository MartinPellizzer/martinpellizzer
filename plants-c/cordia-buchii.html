Cordia buchii

Cordia buchii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fleshy flowers that bloom in winter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Cordia buchii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
