Cyperus helvus

Cyperus helvus, commonly known as the flatleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
