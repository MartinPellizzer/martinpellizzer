Campanula nejceffii

Campanula nejceffii, commonly known as the Nejceff's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
