Citrus × sinensis subsp. lusitanica

Citrus × sinensis subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lisbon lemon, is a subspecies of the common lemon. It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruit with a thin, aromatic peel.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is valued for its high vitamin C content and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
