Crinum longitubum

Crinum longitubum, commonly known as the long-tube crinum, is a striking herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are large and fleshy, adapted for storing water and nutrients.

Crinum longitubum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
