Cacalia silphiifolia

Cacalia silphiifolia, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a silvery or fuzzy underside.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
