Centaurea avilae

Centaurea avilae, commonly known as the Avila thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea avilae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
