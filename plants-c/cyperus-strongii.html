Cyperus strongii

Cyperus strongii, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus strongii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
