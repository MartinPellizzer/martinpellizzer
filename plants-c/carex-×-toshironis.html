Carex × toshironis

Carex × toshironis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It exhibits a variety of growth forms, including upright and spreading types, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Carex × toshironis is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
