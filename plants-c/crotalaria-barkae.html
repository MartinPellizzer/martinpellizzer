Crotalaria barkae

Crotalaria barkae, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various alkaloids, contributing to its medicinal and pesticidal properties.

Crotalaria barkae is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
