Cuscuta dentatasquamata

Cuscuta dentatasquamata, commonly known as the scarlet dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrition.

It produces small, yellow or orange flowers and has a unique, thread-like appearance due to its slender, branched stems. The plant is native to North America and is often found parasitizing a variety of herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it can form dense infestations on host plants, potentially causing significant damage.

Despite its parasitic nature, Cuscuta dentatasquamata plays a role in ecological systems by influencing host plant populations and providing food for certain insects and birds.
