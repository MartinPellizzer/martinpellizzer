Carludovica microphylla

Carludovica microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf cactus or Mexican cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compact, spiny stem and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
