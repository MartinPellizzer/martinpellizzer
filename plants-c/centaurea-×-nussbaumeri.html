Centaurea × nussbaumeri

Centaurea × nussbaumeri, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea × nussbaumeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
