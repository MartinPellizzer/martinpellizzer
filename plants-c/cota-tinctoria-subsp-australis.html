Cota tinctoria subsp. australis

Cota tinctoria subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian dyer's weed, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant is notable for its historical use in natural dye production, particularly for producing yellow dyes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.
