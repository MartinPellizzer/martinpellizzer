Caladium erythropus

Caladium erythropus, commonly known as the red-veined caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a lush green background. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Caladium erythropus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers that are not usually the focus of its ornamental value.
