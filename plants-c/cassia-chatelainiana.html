Cassia chatelainiana

Cassia chatelainiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces flat, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia chatelainiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
