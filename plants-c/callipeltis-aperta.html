Callipeltis aperta

Callipeltis aperta, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink.

The botanical structure of Callipeltis aperta includes a pseudobulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients and water.
