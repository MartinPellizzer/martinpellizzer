Cynanchum haruna-montanum

Cynanchum haruna-montanum, commonly known as the Japanese milkweed vine, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cynanchum haruna-montanum is also noted for its latex-containing stems, which can be toxic to livestock.
