Calamagrostis kengii

Calamagrostis kengii, commonly known as Keng's reed, is a perennial grass native to China.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, upright culms with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are usually pale green to golden in color.

Calamagrostis kengii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
