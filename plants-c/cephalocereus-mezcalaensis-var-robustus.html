Cephalocereus mezcalaensis var. robustus

Cephalocereus mezcalaensis var. robustus, commonly known as the robustus cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of white, tubular flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it resilient to drought. Its growth habit is slow, and it can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
