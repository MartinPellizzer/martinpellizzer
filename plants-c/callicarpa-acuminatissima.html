Callicarpa acuminatissima

Callicarpa acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant, white to light pink flower cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance.
