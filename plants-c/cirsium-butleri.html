Cirsium butleri

Cirsium butleri, commonly known as Butler's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or arid environments. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Cirsium butleri plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is a part of the Asteraceae family.
