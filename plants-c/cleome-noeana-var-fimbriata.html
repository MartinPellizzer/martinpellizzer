Cleome noeana var. fimbriata

Cleome noeana var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces distinctive, feathery bracts that give it its common name, enhancing its ornamental appeal. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Cleome noeana var. fimbriata is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and easy maintenance requirements.
