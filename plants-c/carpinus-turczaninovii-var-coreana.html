Carpinus turczaninovii var. coreana

Carpinus turczaninovii var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Korea.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, nut-like fruits that are important for wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable growing conditions.
