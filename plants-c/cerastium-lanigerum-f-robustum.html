Cerastium lanigerum f. robustum

Cerastium lanigerum f. robustum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cool, temperate climates.
