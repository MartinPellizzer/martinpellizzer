Convolvulus arvensis var. striatus

Convolvulus arvensis var. striatus, commonly known as the striped field bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive striped pattern on the underside, and its trumpet-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread aggressively and become invasive in certain regions. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ability to produce numerous seeds make it a challenging weed to control.
