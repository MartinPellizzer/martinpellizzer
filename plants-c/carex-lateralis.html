Carex lateralis

Carex lateralis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, characteristic of the sedge family. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex lateralis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
