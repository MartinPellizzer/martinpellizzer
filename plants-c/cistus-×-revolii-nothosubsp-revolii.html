Cistus × revolii nothosubsp. revolii

Cistus × revolii nothosubsp.

revolii, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ornamental value in horticulture.
