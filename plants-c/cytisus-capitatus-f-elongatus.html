Cytisus capitatus f. elongatus

Cytisus capitatus f. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated broom, is a variant of the common broom.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and Mediterranean climates.

This form is notable for its unique growth habit and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
