Cissus serrulatifolia

Cissus serrulatifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by translucent, waxy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus serrulatifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage.
