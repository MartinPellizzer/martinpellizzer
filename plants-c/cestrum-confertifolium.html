Cestrum confertifolium

Cestrum confertifolium, commonly known as the showy cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, and red. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum confertifolium is also known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
