Coreopsis major var. linearis

Coreopsis major var. linearis, commonly known as the linear coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in cone-shaped clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
