Carex longii

Carex longii, commonly known as the longleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, linear leaves that can reach several inches in length.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its habitat.
