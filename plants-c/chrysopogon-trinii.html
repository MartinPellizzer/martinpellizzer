Chrysopogon trinii

Chrysopogon trinii, commonly known as the three-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, erect leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with three flowers. It is characterized by its fibrous roots and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Chrysopogon trinii is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
