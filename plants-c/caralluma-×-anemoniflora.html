Caralluma × anemoniflora

Caralluma × anemoniflora, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant, colorful blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the spring season. Its leaves are usually modified into spines, and it has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
