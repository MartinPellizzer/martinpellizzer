Championella fulvihispida

Championella fulvihispida, commonly known as the yellow-hairy champion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its yellowish-green leaves covered in fine, hairy textures. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
