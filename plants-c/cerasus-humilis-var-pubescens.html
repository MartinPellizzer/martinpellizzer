Cerasus humilis var. pubescens

Cerasus humilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.

This variety is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental plants in horticulture.
