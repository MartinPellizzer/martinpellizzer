Conyza laciniata

Conyza laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf conyza, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It features a branching stem covered with fine hairs and has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often deeply divided. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Conyza laciniata is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in agricultural areas.
