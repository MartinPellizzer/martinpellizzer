Carex echinata var. perileia

Carex echinata var. perileia, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
