Cucumis figarei var. ficifolius

Cucumis figarei var. ficifolius, commonly known as the fig-leaf cucumber, is a unique variant of the Cucumis genus.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble the leaves of the fig tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces long, slender fruits that are typically green and have a mild, cucumber-like flavor. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

This variety thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires regular watering for optimal growth.
