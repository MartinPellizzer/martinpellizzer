Crataegus × lepida

Crataegus × lepida, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red or yellow berry that ripens in the fall.

The common hawthorn is often used in hedgerows and gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
