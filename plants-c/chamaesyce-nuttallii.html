Chamaesyce nuttallii

Chamaesyce nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically features a prostrate or ascending stem covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, bead-like fruits. Its leaves are usually linear to ovate, with a rough, sandpaper-like texture.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
