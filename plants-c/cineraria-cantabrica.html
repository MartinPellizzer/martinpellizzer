Cineraria cantabrica

Cineraria cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along cliffs and sea shores, thriving in coastal climates. Its leaves are usually greyish-green with a woolly texture, and it produces blue or violet flowers in the spring.

Cineraria cantabrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
