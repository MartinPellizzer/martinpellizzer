Coleus subscandens

Coleus subscandens, commonly known as the climbing coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a variety of colors, including green, red, and purple. It typically grows as a vine, often climbing or trailing along supports.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions.
