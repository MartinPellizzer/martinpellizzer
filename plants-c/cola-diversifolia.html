Cola diversifolia

Cola diversifolia, commonly known as African cola or kola nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces hard, woody pods that contain the aromatic kola nuts, which are rich in caffeine and used in traditional and commercial contexts. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant plays a significant role in both ecological and cultural systems in its native region.
