Cenchrus annuus

Cenchrus annuus, commonly known as the annual sandgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to warm temperate and tropical regions, particularly in Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a hairy texture.

Cenchrus annuus is adapted to thrive in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
