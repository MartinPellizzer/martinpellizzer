Ceropegia schinzii var. angolensis

Ceropegia schinzii var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
