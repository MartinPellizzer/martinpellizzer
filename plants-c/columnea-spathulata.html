Columnea spathulata

Columnea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Columnea spathulata is a member of the Gesneriaceae family, known for its showy blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
