Colchicum neapolitanum var. haynaldii

Colchicum neapolitanum var. haynaldii, commonly known as the Neapolitan autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are basal and often covered in a waxy coating. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.
