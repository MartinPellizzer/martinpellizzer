Cycnium bequaertii

Cycnium bequaertii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and riverbanks. The plant features compound leaves with finely divided lobes and produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts upon maturity.

Cycnium bequaertii is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
