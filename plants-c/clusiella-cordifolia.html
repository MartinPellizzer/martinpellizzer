Clusiella cordifolia

Clusiella cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf clusiella, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name, and they are often glossy and leathery in texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
