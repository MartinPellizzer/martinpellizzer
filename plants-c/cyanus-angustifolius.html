Cyanus angustifolius

Cyanus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
