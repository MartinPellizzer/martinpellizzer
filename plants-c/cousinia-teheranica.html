Cousinia teheranica

Cousinia teheranica, commonly known as Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Cousinia teheranica produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
