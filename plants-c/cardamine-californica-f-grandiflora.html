Cardamine californica f. grandiflora

Cardamine californica f. grandiflora, commonly known as the California watercress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are often submerged or floating in shallow water, adapting to wetland environments.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
