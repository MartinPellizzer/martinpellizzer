Crocus kirkii

Crocus kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family, Iridaceae.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a yellow center. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Crocus kirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
