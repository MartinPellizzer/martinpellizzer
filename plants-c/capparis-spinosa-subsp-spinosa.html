Capparis spinosa subsp. spinosa

Capparis spinosa subsp.

spinosa, commonly known as the common caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional landscaping.

The plant produces yellowish flowers that give way to green, spherical fruits called capers, which are harvested before ripening. These fruits are renowned for their pungent flavor and are widely used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is classified within the Capparaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural and chemical traits.
