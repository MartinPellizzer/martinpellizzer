Cyathea hierbabuena

Cyathea hierbabuena, commonly known as the Hierbabuena tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded, forested areas. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant is known for its slow growth rate.

Cyathea hierbabuena plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
