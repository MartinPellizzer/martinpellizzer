Cucumis melo convar. microcarpus

Cucumis melo convar. microcarpus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variety belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green and have a crisp, edible flesh. The plant is a vine that produces both male and female flowers, with the fruit developing from the female flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its refreshing, mild-tasting cucumbers used in various culinary applications.
