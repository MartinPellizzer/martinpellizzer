Cyathea boiviniiformis var. lobata

Cyathea boiviniiformis var. lobata, commonly known as the broad-lobed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive lobed shape, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
