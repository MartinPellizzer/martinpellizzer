Campanula rapunculoides f. ucrainica

Campanula rapunculoides f. ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
