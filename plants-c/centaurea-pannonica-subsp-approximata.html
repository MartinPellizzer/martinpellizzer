Centaurea pannonica subsp. approximata

Centaurea pannonica subsp.

approximata, commonly known as the Pannonian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with purple-tipped bracts, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, disturbed areas.
