Croton waterhouseae

Croton waterhouseae, commonly known as the Waterhouse croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and orange. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
