Convolvulus barbatus

Convolvulus barbatus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Convolvulus barbatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
