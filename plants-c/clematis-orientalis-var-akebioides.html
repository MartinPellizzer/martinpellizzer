Clematis orientalis var. akebioides

Clematis orientalis var. akebioides, commonly known as the Japanese climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or pergolas. Its showy blooms appear in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability in garden settings.
