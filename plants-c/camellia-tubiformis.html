Camellia tubiformis

Camellia tubiformis, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces large, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting central band. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

Camellia tubiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
