Crossandra horrida

Crossandra horrida, commonly known as the hairy crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Crossandra horrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
