Carex giovanniana

Carex giovanniana, commonly known as the Giant Sedge, is a tall, perennial herb native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth and long, narrow leaves that are usually a bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex giovanniana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
