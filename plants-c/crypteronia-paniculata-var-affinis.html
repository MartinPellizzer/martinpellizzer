Crypteronia paniculata var. affinis

Crypteronia paniculata var. affinis, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
