Cardiospermum corindum f. pechuelii

Cardiospermum corindum f. pechuelii, commonly known as the Pechuelii heartleaf, is a unique variant of the heartleaf morning glory.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. This plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. Its climbing habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.

The foliage and flowers contribute to its aesthetic appeal, making it a favored plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
