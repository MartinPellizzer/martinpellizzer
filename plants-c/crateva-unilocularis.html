Crateva unilocularis

Crateva unilocularis, commonly known as the Java apple or sour sop, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, spiny fruit that contains a single large seed. Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications despite its sharp taste.
