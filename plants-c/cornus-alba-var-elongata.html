Cornus alba var. elongata

Cornus alba var. elongata, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red stems that become more vivid in the winter, providing striking visual interest. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
