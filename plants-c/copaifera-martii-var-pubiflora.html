Copaifera martii var. pubiflora

Copaifera martii var. pubiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish to reddish resinous sap, which is highly valued for its medicinal properties. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The tree is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and its potential in the production of essential oils and phytochemicals.
