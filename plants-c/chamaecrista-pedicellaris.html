Chamaecrista pedicellaris

Chamaecrista pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by narrow, bean-like pods containing small, edible seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
