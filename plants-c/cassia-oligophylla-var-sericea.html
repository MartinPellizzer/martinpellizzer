Cassia oligophylla var. sericea

Cassia oligophylla var. sericea, commonly known as silver cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, papery flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its seeds are encased in flat, elongated pods, which are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
