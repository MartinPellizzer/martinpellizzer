Cephalaria cachemirica

Cephalaria cachemirica, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Cephalaria cachemirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
