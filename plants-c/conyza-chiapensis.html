Conyza chiapensis

Conyza chiapensis, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically small, daisy-like blooms. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Conyza chiapensis is sometimes considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
