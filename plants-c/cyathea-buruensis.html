Cyathea buruensis

Cyathea buruensis, commonly known as the Buru tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the island of Buru in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a layer of dead fronds. The plant produces large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Cyathea buruensis thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical rainforests.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
