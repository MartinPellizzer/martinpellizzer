Cupressus lusitanica var. epruinosa

Cupressus lusitanica var. epruinosa, commonly known as the Portuguese cypress, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dark green, scale-like foliage. The leaves are often covered in a whitish bloom, giving the plant its varietal name "epruinosa," which translates to "covered with powder.". It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by cylindrical cones.

This variety is highly valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
