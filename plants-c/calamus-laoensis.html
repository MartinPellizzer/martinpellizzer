Calamus laoensis

Calamus laoensis, commonly known as Lao bamboo or Lao calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Laos, Vietnam, and Cambodia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Calamus laoensis thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in local medicinal practices.
