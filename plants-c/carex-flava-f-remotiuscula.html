Carex flava f. remotiuscula

Carex flava f. remotiuscula, commonly known as the slender yellow sedge, is a variant of the yellow sedge species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in wet meadows or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikes, and it relies on wind pollination.

This subspecies is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
