Coleus lasianthus

Coleus lasianthus, commonly known as the silver leaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from silver to white in color, often with contrasting green veins. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its compact growth habit and ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant displays.
