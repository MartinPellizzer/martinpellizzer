Clypea tomentosa

Clypea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly clypea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Clypea tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and unique texture.
