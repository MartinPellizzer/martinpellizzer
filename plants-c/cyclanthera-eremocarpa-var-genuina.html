Cyclanthera eremocarpa var. genuina

Cyclanthera eremocarpa var. genuina, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a climbing vine native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It produces small, green, cucurbit-like fruits that are typically covered in a rough, spiny texture. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in hot, dry environments and is adapted to conserve water through its succulent stems.

The fruit is edible when mature, though it is often used in local cuisines for its mild flavor and crunchy texture.
