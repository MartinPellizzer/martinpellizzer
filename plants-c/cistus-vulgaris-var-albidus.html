Cistus vulgaris var. albidus

Cistus vulgaris var. albidus, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
