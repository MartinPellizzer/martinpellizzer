Cynoglossum olgae

Cynoglossum olgae, commonly known as the Greenland bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in summer. The plant thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cynoglossum olgae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in cold climates.
