Cissus roxburghii

Cissus roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan grape or rock fig, is a climbing plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy dots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like berries. It thrives in rocky or mountainous environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cissus roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
