Crataegus parvifolia

Crataegus parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus parvifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
