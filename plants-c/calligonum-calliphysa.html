Calligonum calliphysa

Calligonum calliphysa, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that thrives in sandy and saline soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots help stabilize dunes and prevent soil erosion.

Calligonum calliphysa is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
