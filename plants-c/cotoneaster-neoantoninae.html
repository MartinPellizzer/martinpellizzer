Cotoneaster neoantoninae

Cotoneaster neoantoninae, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.

Cotoneaster neoantoninae is relatively low maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types.
