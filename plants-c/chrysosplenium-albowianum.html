Chrysosplenium albowianum

Chrysosplenium albowianum, commonly known as the albowian nettle.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America, typically found in moist, shaded woodland environments. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are typically borne on slender, upright stalks and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
