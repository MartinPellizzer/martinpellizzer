Chamaecyparis squarrosa var. leptoclada

Chamaecyparis squarrosa var. leptoclada, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Japanese white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features a narrow, pyramidal shape with scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by round, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique foliage make it a distinctive addition to landscape designs.
