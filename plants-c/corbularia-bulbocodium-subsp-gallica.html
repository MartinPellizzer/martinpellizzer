Corbularia bulbocodium subsp. gallica

Corbularia bulbocodium subsp.

gallica, commonly known as the Gallic bulbous gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are tuberous, contributing to its common name.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
