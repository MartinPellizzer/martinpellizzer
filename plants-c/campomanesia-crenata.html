Campomanesia crenata

Campomanesia crenata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple or carambola, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that mature to a bright orange color, often resembling a star shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is commonly used in culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
