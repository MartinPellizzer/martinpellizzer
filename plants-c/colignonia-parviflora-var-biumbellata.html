Colignonia parviflora var. biumbellata

Colignonia parviflora var. biumbellata, commonly known as the small-flowered colignonia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
