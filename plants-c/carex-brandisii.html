Carex brandisii

Carex brandisii, commonly known as Brandis' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
