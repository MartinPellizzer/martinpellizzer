Cupressus goveniana var. pygmaea

Cupressus goveniana var. pygmaea, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, cool climates and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
