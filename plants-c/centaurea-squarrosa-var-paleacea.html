Centaurea squarrosa var. paleacea

Centaurea squarrosa var. paleacea, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a grayish appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful blooms.
