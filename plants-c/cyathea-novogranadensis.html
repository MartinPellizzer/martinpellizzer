Cyathea novogranadensis

Cyathea novogranadensis, commonly known as the Colombian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, supporting the growth of the fronds above.

Cyathea novogranadensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest structure and providing habitat for various organisms.
