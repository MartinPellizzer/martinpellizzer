Ceratostigma minus f. lasaense

Ceratostigma minus f. lasaense, commonly known as the variegated spreading bamboo, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features narrow, arching leaves that are typically variegated with white and green patterns, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, bright blue flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that persist into winter. It is native to China and thrives in partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
