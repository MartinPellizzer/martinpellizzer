Chamaecytisus supinus subsp. albus

Chamaecytisus supinus subsp.

albus, commonly known as the white broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the western Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
