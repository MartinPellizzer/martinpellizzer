Calamus opacus

Calamus opacus, commonly known as the obscure calamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers, which is typical of the Araceae family. Calamus opacus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies in its ecological role within wetland ecosystems.
