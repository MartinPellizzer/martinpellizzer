Clematis stanleyi var. hirsuta

Clematis stanleyi var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and compound leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
