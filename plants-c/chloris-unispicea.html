Chloris unispicea

Chloris unispicea, commonly known as the slender grassvetch, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish-brown flowers.

Chloris unispicea is often found in association with other grasses and forbs in its native ecosystems.
