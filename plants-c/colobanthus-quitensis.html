Colobanthus quitensis

Colobanthus quitensis, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the cold environments of Antarctica and sub-Antarctic regions.

It is a small, low-growing plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. This species is notable for its ability to survive in extreme conditions, including freezing temperatures and nutrient-poor soils. It plays a vital role in the ecosystems of its habitat by providing food for local wildlife.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is also studied for its potential in understanding plant resilience in harsh climates.
