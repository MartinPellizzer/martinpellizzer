Chenopodium album var. commune

Chenopodium album var. commune, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Lambsquarters is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides, fields, and gardens.

It is a common weed in agricultural settings and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
