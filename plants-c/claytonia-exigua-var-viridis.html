Claytonia exigua var. viridis

Claytonia exigua var. viridis, commonly known as the green mountain sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
