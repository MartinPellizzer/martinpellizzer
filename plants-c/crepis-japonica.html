Crepis japonica

Crepis japonica, commonly known as Japanese mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has hairy, lobed leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, hairy seeds.

Crepis japonica is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
