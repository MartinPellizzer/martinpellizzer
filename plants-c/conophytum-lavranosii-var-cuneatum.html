Conophytum lavranosii var. cuneatum

Conophytum lavranosii var. cuneatum, commonly known as the "Cape platter cactus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. Its leaves are typically triangular and fleshy, adapted for water retention.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
