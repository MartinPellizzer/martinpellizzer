Cerasus laurocerasus subvar. rotundifolia

Cerasus laurocerasus subvar. rotundifolia, commonly known as the common cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are often rounded at the base, giving it its subspecies name "rotundifolia.". The plant produces fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark red, edible berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
