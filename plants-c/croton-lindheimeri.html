Croton lindheimeri

Croton lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's croton, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
