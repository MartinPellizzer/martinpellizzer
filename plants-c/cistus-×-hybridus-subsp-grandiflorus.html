Cistus × hybridus subsp. grandiflorus

Cistus × hybridus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
