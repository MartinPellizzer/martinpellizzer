Cassia sparsa

Cassia sparsa, commonly known as the sparse cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are yellow and clustered, while the seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods.

Cassia sparsa is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
