Cordyline eschscholziana

Cordyline eschscholziana, commonly known as the red-flowered cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

Cordyline eschscholziana is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional landscaping.
