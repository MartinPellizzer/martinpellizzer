Cinnamomum kotoense

Cinnamomum kotoense, commonly known as Koto cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan, where it grows in humid, subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum kotoense is relatively rare and is often cultivated for its unique fragrance and ornamental value.
