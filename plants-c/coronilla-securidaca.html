Coronilla securidaca

Coronilla securidaca, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, twisted pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
