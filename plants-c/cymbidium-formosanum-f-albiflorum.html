Cymbidium formosanum f. albiflorum

Cymbidium formosanum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered formosan orchid, is a rare and striking cultivar of the Cymbidium genus.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is native to Taiwan and is prized for its elegant appearance and cold hardiness. It typically grows as an upright herb with lance-shaped leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This orchid requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.
