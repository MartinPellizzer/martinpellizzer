Carex spongiifolia

Carex spongiifolia, commonly known as the sponge-like sedge, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 5,000 species of grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, spongy-textured inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex spongiifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
