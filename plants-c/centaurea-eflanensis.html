Centaurea eflanensis

Centaurea eflanensis, commonly known as the Eflan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny leaves, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Centaurea eflanensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is considered a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar composite flower structures.
