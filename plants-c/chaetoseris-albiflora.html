Chaetoseris albiflora

Chaetoseris albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower bidens, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
