Centaurea cadmea

Centaurea cadmea, commonly known as the Greek clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive central disk, surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea cadmea is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
