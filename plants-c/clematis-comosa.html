Clematis comosa

Clematis comosa, commonly known as the fuzzy clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five oval leaflets that are hairy underneath.

Clematis comosa thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
