Cheirostylis liukiuensis

Cheirostylis liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with small, showy orchid flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
