Crataegus horrida var. aboriginum

Crataegus horrida var. aboriginum, commonly known as the Australian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
