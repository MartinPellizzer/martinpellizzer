Cymbidium aloifolium

Cymbidium aloifolium, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, producing white or pale yellow flowers with a subtle scent. It prefers cool, bright conditions and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Cymbidium aloifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown for its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms.
