Citrus medica subf. dulcis

Citrus medica subf.

dulcis, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a citrus fruit tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and dark green in color. The tree produces fragrant white flowers that give way to round, orange-colored fruits.

The fruit has a thin, leathery rind and a segmented pulp that is juicy and high in vitamin C. It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in food, beverages, and medicine.
