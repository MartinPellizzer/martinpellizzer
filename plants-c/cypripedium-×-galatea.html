Cypripedium × galatea

Cypripedium × galatea, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, showy flower that resembles a slipper, typically in shades of pink, white, or cream. The plant features a single, fleshy, greenish-brown rhizome and produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cypripedium × galatea is valued in horticulture for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation programs.
