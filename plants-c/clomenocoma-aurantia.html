Clomenocoma aurantia

Clomenocoma aurantia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Clomenocoma aurantia is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
