Carum delavayi

Carum delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's caraway, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its fruits are small, dry, and contain a single seed, typical of the Carum genus.

Carum delavayi is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
