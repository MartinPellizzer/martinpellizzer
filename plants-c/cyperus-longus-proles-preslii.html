Cyperus longus proles preslii

Cyperus longus proles preslii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense stands in wetlands. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus longus proles preslii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
