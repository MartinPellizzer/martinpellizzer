Crystallopollen serratuloides

Crystallopollen serratuloides, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb water efficiently from the soil.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
