Crataegus oxyacantha f. oxyacanthoides

Crataegus oxyacantha f. oxyacanthoides, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically used for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting wildlife, providing food and habitat for various birds and insects.
