Crataegus tenuis

Crataegus tenuis, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red to orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
