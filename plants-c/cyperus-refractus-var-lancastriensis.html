Cyperus refractus var. lancastriensis

Cyperus refractus var. lancastriensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas, pond margins, and along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of aquatic habitat conditions.
