Cardamine pratensis var. udicola

Cardamine pratensis var. udicola, commonly known as the meadow cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, with small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its reproductive structures are adapted for cross-pollination, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This variant is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
