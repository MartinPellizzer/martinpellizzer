Cyperus conservator-davidii

Cyperus conservator-davidii, commonly known as the David's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Cyperus conservator-davidii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
