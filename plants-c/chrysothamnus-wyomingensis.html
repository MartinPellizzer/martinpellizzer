Chrysothamnus wyomingensis

Chrysothamnus wyomingensis, commonly known as Wyoming goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Wyoming goldenbush is a perennial shrub with aromatic, silvery-green foliage.
