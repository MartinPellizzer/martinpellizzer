Casuarina montana

Casuarina montana, commonly known as the mountain casuarina, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland regions. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds.

Casuarina montana is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
