Chamaefistula barbinervis

Chamaefistula barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that have distinctive narrow veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its compact growth habit.

Chamaefistula barbinervis is relatively low-maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
