Carex nigra subsp. recta

Carex nigra subsp.

recta, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
