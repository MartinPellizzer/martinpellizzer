Carex diluta subsp. fissirostris

Carex diluta subsp.

fissirostris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
