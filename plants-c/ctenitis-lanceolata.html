Ctenitis lanceolata

Ctenitis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Ctenitis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
