Chailletia fasciculata

Chailletia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered chailletia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
