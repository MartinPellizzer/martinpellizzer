Crocanthemum glomeratum

Crocanthemum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It blooms in the summer and is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact, showy appearance.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
