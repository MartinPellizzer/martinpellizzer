Caragana jubata f. fastigiata

Caragana jubata f. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright form of the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit.

It features small, compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and its flowers are yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring. The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and is well adapted to harsh, arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
