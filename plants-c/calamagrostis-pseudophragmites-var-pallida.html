Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. pallida

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. pallida, commonly known as the silver reed or white reed, is a grass species characterized by its slender, silver-tipped foliage.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces panicles of pale to whitish flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that gives it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
