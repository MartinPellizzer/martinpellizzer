Carex aquatilis var. flavicans

Carex aquatilis var. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces bright yellow flowers, which are typically found in clusters along the central stem. It thrives in moist to aquatic conditions and is often found in marshes, ponds, and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
