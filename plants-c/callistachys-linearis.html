Callistachys linearis

Callistachys linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Callistachys linearis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
