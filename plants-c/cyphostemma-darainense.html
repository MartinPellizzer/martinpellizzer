Cyphostemma darainense

Cyphostemma darainense, commonly known as the Daraina moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
