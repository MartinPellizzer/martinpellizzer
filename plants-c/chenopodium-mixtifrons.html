Chenopodium mixtifrons

Chenopodium mixtifrons, commonly known as the mixed-frond goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on the environment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Chenopodium mixtifrons is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisine by some Native American tribes.
