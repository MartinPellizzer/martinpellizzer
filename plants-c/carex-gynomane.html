Carex gynomane

Carex gynomane, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This plant is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

Carex gynomane plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
