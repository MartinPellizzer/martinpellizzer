Carex stenophylla var. pellucida

Carex stenophylla var. pellucida, commonly known as the translucent sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a smooth, waxy surface that gives the plant its translucent appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
