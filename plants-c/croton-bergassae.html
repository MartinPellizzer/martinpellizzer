Croton bergassae

Croton bergassae, commonly known as the Bergassae croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are typically thick and have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Croton bergassae is also noted for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
