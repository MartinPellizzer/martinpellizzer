Capparis tonkinensis var. annamensis

Capparis tonkinensis var. annamensis, commonly known as the Annam bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that mature into a dark red or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
