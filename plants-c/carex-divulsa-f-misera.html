Carex divulsa f. misera

Carex divulsa f. misera, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of brownish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
