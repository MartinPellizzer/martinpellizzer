Cereus paniculatus

Cereus paniculatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its flowers are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Cereus paniculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.
