Coelandria coccinea

Coelandria coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet flame vine, is a striking tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody and twining, allowing it to climb and spread along supports.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
