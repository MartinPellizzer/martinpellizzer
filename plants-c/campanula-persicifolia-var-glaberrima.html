Campanula persicifolia var. glaberrima

Campanula persicifolia var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth texture, which gives the variety its name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
