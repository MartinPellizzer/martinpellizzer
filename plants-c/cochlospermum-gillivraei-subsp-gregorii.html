Cochlospermum gillivraei subsp. gregorii

Cochlospermum gillivraei subsp.

gregorii, commonly known as the Gregorii's flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean. It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near water sources.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
