Cochlearia sativa

Cochlearia sativa, commonly known as the common mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, rounded leaves that resemble mouse ears. Its flowers are bright yellow and are arranged in clusters, often blooming in early spring.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
