Crataegus × rubrinervis

Crataegus × rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking coloration and seasonal changes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
