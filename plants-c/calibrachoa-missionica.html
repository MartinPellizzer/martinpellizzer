Calibrachoa missionica

Calibrachoa missionica, commonly known as the Mission creeper, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a trailing or climbing vine that produces clusters of small, vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Calibrachoa missionica is often used in ornamental gardening for its colorful blooms and cascading growth habit.
