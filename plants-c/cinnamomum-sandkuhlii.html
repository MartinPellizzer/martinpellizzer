Cinnamomum sandkuhlii

Cinnamomum sandkuhlii, commonly known as the Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces reddish-brown bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct, spicy aroma, similar to cinnamon.

Cinnamomum sandkuhlii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
