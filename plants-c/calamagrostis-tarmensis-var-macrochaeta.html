Calamagrostis tarmensis var. macrochaeta

Calamagrostis tarmensis var. macrochaeta, commonly known as the Tarmian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to northern regions of Europe and Siberia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery plumes that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
