Clerodendrum amplius

Clerodendrum amplius, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Clerodendrum amplius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
