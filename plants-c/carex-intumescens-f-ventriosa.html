Carex intumescens f. ventriosa

Carex intumescens f. ventriosa, commonly known as the inflated sedge, is a variant of the Carex intumescens species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated bracts that give it a unique appearance. This plant is typically found in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. The leaves are generally grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.
