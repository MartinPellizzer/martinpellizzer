Croton bracteatus

Croton bracteatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Croton bracteatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
