Centaurea athoa subsp. athoa

Centaurea athoa subsp.

athoa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct central vein.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
