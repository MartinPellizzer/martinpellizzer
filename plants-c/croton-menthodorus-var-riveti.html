Croton menthodorus var. riveti

Croton menthodorus var. riveti, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often green with red or yellow veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it adaptable to various tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
