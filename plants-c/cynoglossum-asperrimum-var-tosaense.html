Cynoglossum asperrimum var. tosaense

Cynoglossum asperrimum var. tosaense, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
