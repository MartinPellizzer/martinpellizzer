Cissus auriculata

Cissus auriculata, commonly known as the earleaf fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often tinged with silver or white, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus auriculata is also valued for its ability to climb and its adaptability to various soil types.
