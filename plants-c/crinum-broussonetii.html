Crinum broussonetii

Crinum broussonetii, commonly known as the Broussonet's crinum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that form a rosette at the base, and its underground corm is used for propagation. Crinum broussonetii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its striking appearance and resilience make it a notable species in botanical collections.
