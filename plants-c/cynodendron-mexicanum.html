Cynodendron mexicanum

Cynodendron mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
