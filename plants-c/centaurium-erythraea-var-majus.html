Centaurium erythraea var. majus

Centaurium erythraea var. majus, commonly known as the greater centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
