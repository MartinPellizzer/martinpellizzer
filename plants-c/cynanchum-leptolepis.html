Cynanchum leptolepis

Cynanchum leptolepis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cynanchum leptolepis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
