Clusia callosa

Clusia callosa, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its roots are fibrous and can form a dense network, helping to stabilize soil in its native habitat.

Clusia callosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
