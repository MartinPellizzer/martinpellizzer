Cyanicula

Cyanicula, commonly known as the bluebell flower, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped blooms that range from pale blue to deep violet. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Cyanicula has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it is known for its early spring flowering.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
