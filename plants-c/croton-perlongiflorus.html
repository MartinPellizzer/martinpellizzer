Croton perlongiflorus

Croton perlongiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, spiny fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Croton perlongiflorus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant fruit.
