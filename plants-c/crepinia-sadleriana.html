Crepinia sadleriana

Crepinia sadleriana, commonly known as the Cape York lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are showy, with large, white petals that have a distinctive purple or pinkish hue at the base.

Crepinia sadleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important ecological role in its native environment.
