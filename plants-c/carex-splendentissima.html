Carex splendentissima

Carex splendentissima, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grass-like plants with triangular stems. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Carex splendentissima is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
