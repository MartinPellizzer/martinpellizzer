Cousinia sivasica

Cousinia sivasica, commonly known as the Sivas cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture in dry soils.

Cousinia sivasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
