Cephalaria mauritanica subsp. rifana

Cephalaria mauritanica subsp.

rifana, commonly known as the Rif mountain cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are usually pale blue or purple, bloom in summer and attract various pollinators.
