Creochiton kinabaluense

Creochiton kinabaluense, commonly known as the Kinabalu flatback slug, is a species of terrestrial pulmonate gastropod mollusk found in the high-altitude regions of Mount Kinabalu in Borneo.

It belongs to the family Limacidae and is characterized by its smooth, soft body and lack of a visible shell. The slug thrives in moist, shaded environments, often inhabiting leaf litter and moss-covered substrates. Its diet primarily consists of decaying plant matter, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of preserving its unique montane environment.
