Cardamine Dentata Subsp. Palustris

Cardamine dentata subsp. palustris is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to wetland environments in northern regions. It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. This subspecies thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in bogs and along stream banks. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it faces increasing threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique and ecologically important plant.
