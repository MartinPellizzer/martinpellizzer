Chirita reptans

Chirita reptans, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

Chirita reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.
