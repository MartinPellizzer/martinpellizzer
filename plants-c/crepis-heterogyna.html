Crepis heterogyna

Crepis heterogyna, commonly known as the variable mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant often grows in moist meadows and grasslands. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.

The species is known for its variability in leaf shape and flower size, which contributes to its common name.
