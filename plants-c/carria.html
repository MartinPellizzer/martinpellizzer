Carria

Carria, commonly known as the "Cape jasmine," is a genus of flowering plants in the Rubiaceae family.

It consists of several species, most notably Carria quadrifida, which is native to southern Africa. These plants are shrubs or small trees, often characterized by their fragrant, white or yellow flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a glossy surface.

Carria species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
