Centaurea variegata var. axillaris

Centaurea variegata var. axillaris, commonly known as the variable centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It produces small, bracteate flowers in late spring to early summer.

The variety axillaris is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form.
