Carum cordatum

Carum cordatum, commonly known as heartleaf caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves that resemble a heart shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their pungent aroma.

Carum cordatum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
