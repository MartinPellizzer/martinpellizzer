Chamaecrista goldmanii

Chamaecrista goldmanii, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, feather-like leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
