Centaurea myriostoma

Centaurea myriostoma, commonly known as the many-seedling centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
