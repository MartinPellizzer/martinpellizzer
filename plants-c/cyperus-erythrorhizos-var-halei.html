Cyperus erythrorhizos var. halei

Cyperus erythrorhizos var. halei, commonly known as Hale's purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale purple flowers. Native to parts of Southeast Asia, this species thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. Its root system is distinctive, featuring reddish-brown rhizomes that help it anchor in waterlogged soils.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
