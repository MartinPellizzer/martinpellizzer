Chilocarpus obtusifolius

Chilocarpus obtusifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved chilocarpus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically broad and rounded. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
