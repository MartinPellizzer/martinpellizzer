Convolvulus linearis

Convolvulus linearis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus linearis is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
