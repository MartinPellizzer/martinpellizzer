Carex pseudosadoensis

Carex pseudosadoensis, commonly known as the Sado sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
