Conyza squamata

Conyza squamata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though it is also considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Conyza squamata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
