Carissa haematocarpa

Carissa haematocarpa, commonly known as the blood orange carissa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible, with a sweet, tangy flavor and a high water content.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in its native habitat.
