Carpinus laxiflora var. fargesii

Carpinus laxiflora var. fargesii, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins that are typically wind-pollinated. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
