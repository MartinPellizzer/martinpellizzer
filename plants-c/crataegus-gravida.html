Crataegus gravida

Crataegus gravida, commonly known as the heavyhearted hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Crataegus gravida is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
