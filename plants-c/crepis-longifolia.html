Crepis longifolia

Crepis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Crepis longifolia is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a common weed in some regions.
