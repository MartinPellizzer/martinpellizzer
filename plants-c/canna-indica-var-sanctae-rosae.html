Canna indica var. sanctae-rosae

Canna indica var. sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the pink canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, with a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by bright pink or red bracts, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and ability to add texture and height to garden designs.
