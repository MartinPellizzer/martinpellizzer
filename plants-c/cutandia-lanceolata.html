Cutandia lanceolata

Cutandia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cutandia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
