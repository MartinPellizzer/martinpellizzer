Crotalaria adariensis

Crotalaria adariensis, commonly known as the Adari crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
