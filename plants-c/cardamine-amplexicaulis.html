Cardamine amplexicaulis

Cardamine amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream sides and woodland floors.
