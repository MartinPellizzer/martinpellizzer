Croton pellitus

Croton pellitus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that display a range of colors, from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
