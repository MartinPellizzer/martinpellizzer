Crataegus abstrusa

Crataegus abstrusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
