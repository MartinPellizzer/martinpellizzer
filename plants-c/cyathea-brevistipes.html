Cyathea brevistipes

Cyathea brevistipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea brevistipes plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
