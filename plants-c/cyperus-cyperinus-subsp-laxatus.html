Cyperus cyperinus subsp. laxatus

Cyperus cyperinus subsp.

laxatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of riparian ecosystems.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
