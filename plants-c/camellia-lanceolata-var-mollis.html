Camellia lanceolata var. mollis

Camellia lanceolata var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaf camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are softer and more plump compared to other camellia varieties. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and floral display.

Camellia lanceolata var. mollis prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
