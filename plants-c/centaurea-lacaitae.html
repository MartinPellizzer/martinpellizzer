Centaurea lacaitae

Centaurea lacaitae, commonly known as the Balkan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are arranged in compact, conical heads.

Centaurea lacaitae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
