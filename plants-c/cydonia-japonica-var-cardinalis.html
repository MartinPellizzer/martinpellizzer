Cydonia japonica var. cardinalis

Cydonia japonica var. cardinalis, commonly known as the Japanese quince.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, characterized by its ornamental value and unique fruit. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or red, adding visual appeal in spring. The fruit, which ripens in autumn, is edible when fully mature but has a tart flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
