Cactus coronaria

Cactus coronaria, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its adaptations include a shallow root system and a waxy coating on the stems to reduce water loss.

Cactus coronaria plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
