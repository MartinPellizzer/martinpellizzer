Cassia discolor

Cassia discolor, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the appearance of a flame. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged oppositely on the stem.

Cassia discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
