Calanda

Calanda, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Schlumbergera and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that resemble the appearance of a cactus. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, orange, and white during the winter months, typically around Christmas time. Calanda thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
