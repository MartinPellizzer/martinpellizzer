Crassula lanuginosa var. pachystemon

Crassula lanuginosa var. pachystemon, commonly known as the Woolly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in compact rosettes, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the summer months.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique texture and low maintenance requirements.
