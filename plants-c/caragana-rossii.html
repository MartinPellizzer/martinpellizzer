Caragana rossii

Caragana rossii, commonly known as the Ross's shrub vetch, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Caragana rossii is often used for soil stabilization and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
