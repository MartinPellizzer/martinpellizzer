Chaerophyllum ghilanicum

Chaerophyllum ghilanicum, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that appear in umbels. The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It has a short flowering period in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
