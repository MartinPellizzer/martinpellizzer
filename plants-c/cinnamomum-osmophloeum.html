Cinnamomum osmophloeum

Cinnamomum osmophloeum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Phoebe cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family.

It is native to southern China, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and bark, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
