Chenopodium album var. purpurascens

Chenopodium album var. purpurascens, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, deltoid leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, especially along the veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its edible young leaves, which can be used in salads or cooked like spinach.
