Carex vesicaria var. robusta

Carex vesicaria var. robusta, commonly known as the robust marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
