Cornus alba var. rutokensis

Cornus alba var. rutokensis, commonly known as the white dogwood or Siberian dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, reddish stems that become more vibrant in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn yellow or red in autumn.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
