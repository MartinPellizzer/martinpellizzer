Calystegia catesbeiana subsp. catesbeiana

Calystegia catesbeiana subsp.

catesbeiana, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

Botanically, it is notable for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
