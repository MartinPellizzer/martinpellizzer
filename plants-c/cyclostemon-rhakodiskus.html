Cyclostemon rhakodiskus

Cyclostemon rhakodiskus, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Cyclostemon rhakodiskus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
