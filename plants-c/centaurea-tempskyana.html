Centaurea tempskyana

Centaurea tempskyana, commonly known as the Tempskyana knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with prominent yellow centers, and it produces small, bristly seeds for dispersal.

Centaurea tempskyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
