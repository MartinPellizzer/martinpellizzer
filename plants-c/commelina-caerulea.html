Commelina caerulea

Commelina caerulea, commonly known as blue commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a white or pale blue spathe. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina caerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
