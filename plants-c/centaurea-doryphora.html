Centaurea doryphora

Centaurea doryphora, commonly known as the broad-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea doryphora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
