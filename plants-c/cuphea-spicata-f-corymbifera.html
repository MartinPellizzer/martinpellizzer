Cuphea spicata f. corymbifera

Cuphea spicata f. corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces clusters of flowers along the stems, giving it its "corymbifera" designation, which refers to its corymbose inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The foliage is often green with a slight hairy texture, adding to its visual appeal.
