Calamus hookerianus

Calamus hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet, marshy habitats. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers.

Calamus hookerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
