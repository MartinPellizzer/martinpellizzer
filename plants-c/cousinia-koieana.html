Cousinia koieana

Cousinia koieana, commonly known as the Tibetan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Cousinia koieana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
