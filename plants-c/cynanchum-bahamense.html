Cynanchum bahamense

Cynanchum bahamense, commonly known as Bahamian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lanceolate, with a somewhat hairy texture.

The species is part of the Asclepiadaceae family, known for its milkweed relatives and their latex-like sap.
