Catasetum laminatum var. eburneum

Catasetum laminatum var. eburneum, commonly known as the white-veined catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers with distinctive white veins on a pale green background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike bearing several showy, lip-like petals.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
