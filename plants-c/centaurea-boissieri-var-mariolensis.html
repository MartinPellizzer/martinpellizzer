Centaurea boissieri var. mariolensis

Centaurea boissieri var. mariolensis, commonly known as the Mariolensian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spiky bracts around the flower cluster.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
