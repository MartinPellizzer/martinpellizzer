Carex kinpokusanensis

Carex kinpokusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex kinpokusanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
