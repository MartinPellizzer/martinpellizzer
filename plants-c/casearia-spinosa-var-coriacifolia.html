Casearia spinosa var. coriacifolia

Casearia spinosa var. coriacifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus or coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a red or orange throat. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually pubescent, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
