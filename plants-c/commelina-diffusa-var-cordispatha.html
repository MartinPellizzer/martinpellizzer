Commelina diffusa var. cordispatha

Commelina diffusa var. cordispatha, commonly known as the heartleaf commelina, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Commelina genus. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
