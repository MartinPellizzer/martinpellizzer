Cousinia actinia

Cousinia actinia, commonly known as the Persian feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Cousinia actinia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
