Carex aquatilis var. stans

Carex aquatilis var. stans, commonly known as the aquatic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond margins, and stream edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy. Its flowering spikes are usually upright, bearing small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
