Campanula glomerata var. minor

Campanula glomerata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
