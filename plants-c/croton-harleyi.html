Croton harleyi

Croton harleyi, commonly known as the Harley's croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
