Cactus argenteus

Cactus argenteus, commonly known as the silver cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its silver-green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Cactus argenteus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
