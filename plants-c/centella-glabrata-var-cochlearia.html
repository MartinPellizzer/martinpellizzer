Centella glabrata var. cochlearia

Centella glabrata var. cochlearia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf centella, is a variant of the centella genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its small, rounded leaves and delicate white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of this variant include its distinctive leaf morphology and compact growth habit.
