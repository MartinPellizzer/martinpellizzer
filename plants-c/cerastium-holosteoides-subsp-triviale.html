Cerastium holosteoides subsp. triviale

Cerastium holosteoides subsp.

triviale, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems, and bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

This species is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
