Cyrtochilum gargantua

Cyrtochilum gargantua, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Cyrtochilum gargantua is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific care requirements.
