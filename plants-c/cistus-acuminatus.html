Cistus acuminatus

Cistus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small evergreen leaves, and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Cistus acuminatus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
