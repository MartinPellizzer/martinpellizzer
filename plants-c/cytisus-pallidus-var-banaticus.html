Cytisus pallidus var. banaticus

Cytisus pallidus var. banaticus, commonly known as the Banat whitethorn, is a shrub native to parts of southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and scrublands.

Cytisus pallidus var. banaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
