Calandrinia curvifolia

Calandrinia curvifolia, commonly known as the zigzag mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and features hairy stems that often curl in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Calandrinia curvifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
