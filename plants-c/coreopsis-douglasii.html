Coreopsis douglasii

Coreopsis douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
