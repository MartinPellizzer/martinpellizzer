Callianthemoides semiverticillata

Callianthemoides semiverticillata, commonly known as the creeping crown imperial, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has slender, branching stems that arise from a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it a distinctive, almost decorative appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
