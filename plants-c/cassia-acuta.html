Cassia acuta

Cassia acuta, commonly known as the acute cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and long, narrow leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, spindle-shaped pods containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Cassia acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
