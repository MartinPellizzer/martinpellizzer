Centaurium erythraea subsp. majus

Centaurium erythraea subsp.

majus, commonly known as the greater centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It has slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
