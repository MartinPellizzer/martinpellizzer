Calamagrostis purpurascens var. pumila

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. pumila, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinted foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray panicles of seeds in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, riverbanks, and other wetland environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
