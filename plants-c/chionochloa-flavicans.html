Chionochloa flavicans

Chionochloa flavicans, commonly known as the yellow hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Southern Hemisphere, particularly found in New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, delicate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowering heads. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and streambanks. Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
