Cladium mariscus subsp. chinense

Cladium mariscus subsp.

chinense, commonly known as the Chinese sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.
