Crocus imperati var. albiflorus

Crocus imperati var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered imperati crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in garden settings.
