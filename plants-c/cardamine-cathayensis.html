Cardamine cathayensis

Cardamine cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese water shield, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in shallow water environments.

Cardamine cathayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
