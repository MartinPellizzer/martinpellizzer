Cassinia complanata

Cassinia complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, surrounded by pale green bracts.

Cassinia complanata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
