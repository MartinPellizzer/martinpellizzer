Calliandra conzattiana

Calliandra conzattiana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Calliandra conzattiana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
