Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. vernicosa

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, often glossy bark and small, thorned branches.

The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
