Cyperus trichodes

Cyperus trichodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it helps in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Cyperus trichodes is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
