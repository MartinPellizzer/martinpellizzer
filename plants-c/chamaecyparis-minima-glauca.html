Chamaecyparis minima-glauca

Chamaecyparis minima-glauca, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or kishu-sugi, is a compact evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a ornamental shrub or tree. It produces small, cone-like structures that are inconspicuous and not typically showy.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
