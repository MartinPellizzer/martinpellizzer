Cocculus trilobus

Cocculus trilobus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often twisted fruit pods. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, which resembles a twisted horn, contains several seeds and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's unique morphology and historical use make it a notable species in botanical studies.
