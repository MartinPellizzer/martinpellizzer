Cacalia platensis

Cacalia platensis, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lobed, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cacalia platensis is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
