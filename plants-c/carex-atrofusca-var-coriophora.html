Carex atrofusca var. coriophora

Carex atrofusca var. coriophora, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, typically brownish or purplish flowers. This variety is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It is valued in wetland restoration projects for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
