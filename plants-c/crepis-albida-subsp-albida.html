Crepis albida subsp. albida

Crepis albida subsp.

albida, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and basal rosettes of linear, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which are surrounded by white bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
