Campanula trachelium subsp. urticifolia

Campanula trachelium subsp.

urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has hairy leaves and stems, and its leaves can cause a mild skin irritation due to the presence of stinging hairs. It typically grows in woodland areas and moist meadows, preferring partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
