Crataegus monogyna var. calycina

Crataegus monogyna var. calycina, commonly known as the common hawthorn or simply hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in a variety of soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
