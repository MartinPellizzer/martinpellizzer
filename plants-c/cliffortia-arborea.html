Cliffortia arborea

Cliffortia arborea, commonly known as the silver tree, is a unique species native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen tree that typically grows in rocky, coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant is characterized by its silvery-blue leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters during the winter months.

Cliffortia arborea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
