Cinnagrostis deserticola

Cinnagrostis deserticola, commonly known as the desert grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Cinnagrostis deserticola plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
