Crataegus opima

Crataegus opima, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible for birds and some mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
