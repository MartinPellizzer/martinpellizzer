Cirsium orizabaense

Cirsium orizabaense, commonly known as the Orizaba thistle, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high elevations. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh, arid conditions.

Cirsium orizabaense is often found in mountainous regions of central Mexico, such as the Sierra Madre Occidental.
