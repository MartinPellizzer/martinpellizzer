Croton guaraniticus f. genuinus

Croton guaraniticus f. genuinus, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It features striking, colorful leaves with a broad, ovate shape and a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

However, it is important to note that the plant can be toxic if ingested, requiring caution in handling and use.
