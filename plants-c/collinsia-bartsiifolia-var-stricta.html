Collinsia bartsiifolia var. stricta

Collinsia bartsiifolia var. stricta, commonly known as the Sierra Valley morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada region of California.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are characteristic of the Collinsia genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal.
