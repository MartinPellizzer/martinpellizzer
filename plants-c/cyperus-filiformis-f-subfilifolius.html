Cyperus filiformis f. subfilifolius

Cyperus filiformis f. subfilifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus Cyperus.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
