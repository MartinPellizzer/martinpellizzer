Coelogyne harana

Coelogyne harana, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Coelogyne harana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its showy, long-lasting blooms.
