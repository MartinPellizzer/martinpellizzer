Capsicum hamiltonii

Capsicum hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's pepper, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are usually harvested when they are fully mature. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it has a strong, spicy aroma.

Capsicum hamiltonii is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional cooking and medicinal practices.
