Cylicodaphne hookeri

Cylicodaphne hookeri, commonly known as the hooker's cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant with a short, stout trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
