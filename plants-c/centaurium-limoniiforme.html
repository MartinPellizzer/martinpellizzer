Centaurium limoniiforme

Centaurium limoniiforme, commonly known as the lemon-flowered centaurium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branching stems that typically reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurium limoniiforme is often found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.
