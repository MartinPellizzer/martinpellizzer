Clematis longiloba

Clematis longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color. Clematis longiloba thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be trained to grow on trellises or fences.
