Conilaria cretica

Conilaria cretica, commonly known as the Cretan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the island of Crete in Greece.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers and has fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Conilaria cretica is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in culinary applications.
