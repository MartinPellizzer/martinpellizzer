Cypripedium × conspicuum

Cypripedium × conspicuum, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy pouch-shaped lip that resembles a slipper, which is a key feature in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments and has a long, fleshy rhizome that helps it survive in poor soil conditions. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a white or pale yellow throat, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cypripedium × conspicuum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
