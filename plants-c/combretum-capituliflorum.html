Combretum capituliflorum

Combretum capituliflorum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and its striking clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
