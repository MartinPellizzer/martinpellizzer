Cousinia dichacantha

Cousinia dichacantha, commonly known as the two-branched cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears small, yellowish to reddish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has fibrous roots that help it survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Cousinia dichacantha is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
