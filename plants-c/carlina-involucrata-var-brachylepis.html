Carlina involucrata var. brachylepis

Carlina involucrata var. brachylepis, commonly known as the short-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical, spiky flower heads that are typically blue to violet in color. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lance-shaped, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American tribes.
