Cistus indicus

Cistus indicus, commonly known as the Indian rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Cistus indicus is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
