Chloris chloridea

Chloris chloridea, commonly known as slender grass or slender blue grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Australia.

It features fine, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a clump-forming growth habit.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
