Cardia alpina

Cardia alpina, commonly known as the alpine cardia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, commonly known as the buckwheat family. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration, especially on the underside.

Cardia alpina thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
