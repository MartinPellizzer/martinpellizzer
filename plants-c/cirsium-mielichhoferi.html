Cirsium mielichhoferi

Cirsium mielichhoferi, commonly known as the Mielichhofer's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
