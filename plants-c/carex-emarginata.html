Carex emarginata

Carex emarginata, commonly known as the notched sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 centimeters and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
