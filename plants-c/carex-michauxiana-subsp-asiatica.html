Carex michauxiana subsp. asiatica

Carex michauxiana subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

The subspecies asiatica is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering structure compared to the nominate subspecies.
