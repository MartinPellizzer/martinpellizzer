Calamintha corsica

Calamintha corsica, commonly known as Corsican mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Calamintha corsica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
