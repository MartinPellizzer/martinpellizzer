Centaurea sanctae-gadeae

Centaurea sanctae-gadeae, commonly known as the Holy Gadea's centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, dry environments. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, with bracts that resemble petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
