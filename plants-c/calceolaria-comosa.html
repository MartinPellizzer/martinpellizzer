Calceolaria comosa

Calceolaria comosa, commonly known as the hairy slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often hairy or velvety in texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and produces showy blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Calceolaria comosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
