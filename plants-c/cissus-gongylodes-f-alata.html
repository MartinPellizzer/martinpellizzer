Cissus gongylodes f. alata

Cissus gongylodes f. alata, commonly known as the creeping fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-gray underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a decorative plant or for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in suitable environments.
