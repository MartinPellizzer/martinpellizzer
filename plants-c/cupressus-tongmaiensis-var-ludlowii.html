Cupressus tongmaiensis var. ludlowii

Cupressus tongmaiensis var. ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's Chinese fir, is a rare and endemic coniferous tree native to China.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and narrow, conical shape. The plant exhibits typical features of the genus Cupressus, including scale-like leaves and seed cones. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
