Chrysopsis mixta

Chrysopsis mixta, commonly known as the hybrid golden coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy yellow to orange ray flowers with a prominent brown central disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.
