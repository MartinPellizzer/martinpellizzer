Ceropegia africana subsp. fortuita

Ceropegia africana subsp.

fortuita, commonly known as the African basket flower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a fibrous root system that helps it store water. It is often found growing along rocky outcrops and in open woodlands.

Its unique floral structure attracts specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
