Cephalanthus tetrandrus

Cephalanthus tetrandrus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its showy, purple-pink flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
