Chenopodium maroccanum

Chenopodium maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
