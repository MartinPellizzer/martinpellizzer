Cheirostylis sessanica

Cheirostylis sessanica, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Cheirostylis sessanica is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its unique floral structure and aesthetic appeal.
