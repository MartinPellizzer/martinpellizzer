Cheilanthes selinoides

Cheilanthes selinoides, commonly known as the rock fern or desert shield fern, is a terrestrial fern that thrives in arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant has thick, leathery fronds that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, protective layer of hair, which aids in reducing water loss.

It is native to regions of North America, including parts of the southwestern United States and Mexico.
