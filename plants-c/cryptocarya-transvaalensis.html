Cryptocarya transvaalensis

Cryptocarya transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal laurel, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries.

Cryptocarya transvaalensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
