Carex hypsophila var. havilandii

Carex hypsophila var. havilandii, commonly known as Haviland's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks. Its narrow, grassy leaves are typically a vibrant green, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
