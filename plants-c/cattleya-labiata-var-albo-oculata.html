Cattleya labiata var. albo-oculata

Cattleya labiata var. albo-oculata, commonly known as the White-eyed Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking appearance.

It features large, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellowish-orange lip that has a white eye marking. This variety is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and greenhouses.
