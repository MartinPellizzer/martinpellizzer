Chamaepitys mollis

Chamaepitys mollis, commonly known as the mountain pine, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its short, twisted trunk and dense, pyramidal shape. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, woody cones. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
