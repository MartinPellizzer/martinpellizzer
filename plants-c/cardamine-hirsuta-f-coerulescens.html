Cardamine hirsuta f. coerulescens

Cardamine hirsuta f. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-veined watercress, is a variant of the broadleaf watercress.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, blue-tinged bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
