Chromolaena quercetorum

Chromolaena quercetorum, commonly known as the tree of life, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
