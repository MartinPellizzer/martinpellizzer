Cephalaria charadzeae

Cephalaria charadzeae, commonly known as the Georgian cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
