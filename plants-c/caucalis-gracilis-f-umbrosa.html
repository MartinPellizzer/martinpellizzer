Caucalis gracilis f. umbrosa

Caucalis gracilis f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
