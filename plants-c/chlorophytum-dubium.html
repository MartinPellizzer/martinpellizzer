Chlorophytum dubium

Chlorophytum dubium, commonly known as the snake plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal houseplant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by narrow, grass-like seed pods.

Chlorophytum dubium is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
