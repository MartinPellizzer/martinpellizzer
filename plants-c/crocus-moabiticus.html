Crocus moabiticus

Crocus moabiticus, commonly known as the Moabitic crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which typically appear before the leaves emerge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens across temperate climates.
