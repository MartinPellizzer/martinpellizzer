Calanthe emarginata

Calanthe emarginata, commonly known as the notched calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring and summer months. Calanthe emmarginata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
