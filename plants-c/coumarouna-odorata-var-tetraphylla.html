Coumarouna odorata var. tetraphylla

Coumarouna odorata var. tetraphylla, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, green fruits that turn yellow when ripe. The fruit has a thick, fibrous rind and a starchy, edible pulp that is used in various culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
