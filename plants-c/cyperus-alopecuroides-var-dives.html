Cyperus alopecuroides var. dives

Cyperus alopecuroides var. dives, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in wet or moist soils, commonly found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Cyperaceae family.
