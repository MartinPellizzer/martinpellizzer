Conophallus konniaku

Conophallus konniaku, commonly known as the Konniaku mushroom, is a unique fungal species belonging to the family Tricholomataceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive cap, which is typically convex and often features a ring or annulus around the stem. The gills are closely spaced and usually exhibit a pale to yellowish coloration. This mushroom is typically found in humid, temperate forests, growing on decaying wood.

Its ecological role includes decomposing organic matter and contributing to nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems.
