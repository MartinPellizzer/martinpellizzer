Centaurea lingulata var. linearifolia

Centaurea lingulata var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its linear leaves and showy purple-blue flowers make it a striking addition to gardens and natural habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
