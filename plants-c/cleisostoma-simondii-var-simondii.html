Cleisostoma simondii var. simondii

Cleisostoma simondii var. simondii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
