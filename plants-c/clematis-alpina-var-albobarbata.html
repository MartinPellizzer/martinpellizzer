Clematis alpina var. albobarbata

Clematis alpina var. albobarbata, commonly known as the alpine white-barbed clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and showy, white flowers with a distinctive barbed appearance on the sepals. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is typically gray-green, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that mature in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to climb trellises or other structures.
