Carex papillosissima subsp. papillosissima

Carex papillosissima subsp.

papillosissima, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown and contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
