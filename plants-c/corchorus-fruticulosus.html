Corchorus fruticulosus

Corchorus fruticulosus, commonly known as the prickly leaf or wild cotton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, triangular leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal remedies.
