Clematis cylindrica var. walteri

Clematis cylindrica var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's cylindric clematis, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and vigorous growth.
