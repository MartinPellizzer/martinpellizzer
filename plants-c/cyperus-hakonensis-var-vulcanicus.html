Cyperus hakonensis var. vulcanicus

Cyperus hakonensis var. vulcanicus, commonly known as the volcanic flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to volcanic soils in Hawaii.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, disturbed areas such as streambanks and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
