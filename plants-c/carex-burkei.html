Carex burkei

Carex burkei, commonly known as the Burke's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex burkei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
