Caryota griffithii var. selebica

Caryota griffithii var. selebica, commonly known as the Sunda palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits that are covered in a fibrous husk.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
