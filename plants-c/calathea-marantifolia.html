Calathea marantifolia

Calathea marantifolia, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a member of the Marantaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that often feature intricate patterns and shades of green, silver, and pink. The plant is known for its unique leaf movement, where the leaves fold upwards at night, resembling a prayer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea marantifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
