Carex adusta var. sparsiflora

Carex adusta var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the dark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Carex adusta var. sparsiflora plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
