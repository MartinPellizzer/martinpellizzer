Chrysopsis oregona var. rudis

Chrysopsis oregona var. rudis, commonly known as the Oregon chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
