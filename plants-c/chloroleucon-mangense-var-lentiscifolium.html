Chloroleucon mangense var. lentiscifolium

Chloroleucon mangense var. lentiscifolium, commonly known as the mangrove hibiscus, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to saline and brackish environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
