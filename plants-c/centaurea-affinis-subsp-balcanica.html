Centaurea affinis subsp. balcanica

Centaurea affinis subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
