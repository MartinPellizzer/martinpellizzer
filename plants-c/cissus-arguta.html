Cissus arguta

Cissus arguta, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Cissus arguta is also noted for its ability to climb using adventitious roots that attach to surfaces.
