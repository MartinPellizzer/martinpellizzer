Chamissoa altissima f. longistyla

Chamissoa altissima f. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled chamissoa, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its elongated, showy stamens, which give it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
