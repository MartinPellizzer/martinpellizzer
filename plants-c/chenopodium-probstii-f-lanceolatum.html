Chenopodium probstii f. lanceolatum

Chenopodium probstii f. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in dry, three-lobed capsules. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

This variety is part of the Chenopodiaceae family, which includes many species adapted to saline or drought-prone habitats.
