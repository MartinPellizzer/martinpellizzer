Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. vaillantii

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. vaillantii, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is used to make cinnamon sticks and ground cinnamon spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its distinct flavor and is often preferred over other cinnamon types in culinary and medicinal applications.
