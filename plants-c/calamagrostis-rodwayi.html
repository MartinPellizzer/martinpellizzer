Calamagrostis rodwayi

Calamagrostis rodwayi, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to cool climates. It is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis rodwayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
