Cnicus willdenowii

Cnicus willdenowii, commonly known as the Willdenow's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Cnicus willdenowii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
