Cissus notabilis

Cissus notabilis, commonly known as the noble leaf or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus notabilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
