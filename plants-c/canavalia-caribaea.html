Canavalia caribaea

Canavalia caribaea, commonly known as the Carib bean or Caribbean morning glory, is a climbing legume native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in local cuisines.

Canavalia caribaea also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
