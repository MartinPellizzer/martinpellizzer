Cynorkis bimaculata

Cynorkis bimaculata, commonly known as the two-spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive two-spotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, showcasing vibrant colors that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming dense clumps that help retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Cynorkis bimaculata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
