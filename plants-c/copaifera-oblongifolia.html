Copaifera oblongifolia

Copaifera oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is often found in humid lowland forests. Its bark and sap contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both traditional and modern pharmacology.

The tree is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
