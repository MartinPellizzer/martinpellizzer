Cytisus incanus

Cytisus incanus, commonly known as the silver bush or creeping broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and profuse yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a sprawling growth habit and often forms dense mats or small bushes.

Cytisus incanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
