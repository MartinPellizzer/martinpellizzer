Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. consimilis

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

consimilis, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological communities it inhabits.
