Camissonia cheiranthifolia subsp. suffruticosa

Camissonia cheiranthifolia subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as the woolly camissonia, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
