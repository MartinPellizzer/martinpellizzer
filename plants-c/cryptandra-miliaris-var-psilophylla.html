Cryptandra miliaris var. psilophylla

Cryptandra miliaris var. psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cryptandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
