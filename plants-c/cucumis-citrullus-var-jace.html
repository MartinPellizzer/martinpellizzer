Cucumis citrullus var. jace

Cucumis citrullus var. jace, commonly known as the watermelon, is a vigorous vine that produces large, fleshy fruits with a high water content.

The plant is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers that are typically unisexual. Its fruit is typically red or yellow, with a thick rind and juicy, sweet flesh. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals.
