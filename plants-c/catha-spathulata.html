Catha spathulata

Catha spathulata, commonly known as African khat, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and gourds, though it is not closely related to them. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by green, edible fruits. It is cultivated for its leaves, which contain psychoactive compounds such as cathinone and cathine.

Due to its stimulating effects, it has cultural and medicinal significance in certain regions.
