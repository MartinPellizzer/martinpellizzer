Coriflora glaucophylla

Coriflora glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Coriflora glaucophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
