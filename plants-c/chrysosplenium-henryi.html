Chrysosplenium henryi

Chrysosplenium henryi, commonly known as Henry's golden spleenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
