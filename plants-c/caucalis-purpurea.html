Caucalis purpurea

Caucalis purpurea, commonly known as purple sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Montiaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves that are typically succulent. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to arid and rocky environments, it is adapted to survive in dry, nutrient-poor soils.

Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized in traditional herbal remedies.
