Cissus rhombia

Cissus rhombia, commonly known as the diamond leaf or rhombic fig.

It is a climbing plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive diamond pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and green walls.
