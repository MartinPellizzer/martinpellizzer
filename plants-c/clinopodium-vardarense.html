Clinopodium vardarense

Clinopodium vardarense, commonly known as the Balkan oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and have a strong, pungent aroma.

Clinopodium vardarense is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
