Coleosanthus wislizeni

Coleosanthus wislizeni, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive pappus-bearing seeds for wind dispersal. It is adapted to drought conditions with deep taproots and minimal water requirements.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
