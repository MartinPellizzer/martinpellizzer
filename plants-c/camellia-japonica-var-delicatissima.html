Camellia japonica var. delicatissima

Camellia japonica var. delicatissima, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, camellia-like flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.

The flowers typically appear in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape during the colder months.
