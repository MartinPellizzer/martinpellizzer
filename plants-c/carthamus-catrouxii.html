Carthamus catrouxii

Carthamus catrouxii, commonly known as the Kashmir safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, hairy seeds enclosed in a spiny capsule, which is a distinctive feature of the genus Carthamus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
