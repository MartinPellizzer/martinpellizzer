Carex confertospicata

Carex confertospicata, commonly known as the dense spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbed leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex confertospicata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
