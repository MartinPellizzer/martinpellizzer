Calamagrostis cephalantha

Calamagrostis cephalantha, commonly known as the bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Calamagrostis cephalantha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
