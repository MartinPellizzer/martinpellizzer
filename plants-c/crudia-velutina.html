Crudia velutina

Crudia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
