Clematis kamtschatica

Clematis kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Russian Far East.

It features slender, climbing stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

Clematis kamtschatica thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
