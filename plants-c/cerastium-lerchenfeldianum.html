Cerastium lerchenfeldianum

Cerastium lerchenfeldianum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to cold and rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
