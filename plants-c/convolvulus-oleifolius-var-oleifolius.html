Convolvulus oleifolius var. oleifolius

Convolvulus oleifolius var. oleifolius, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and along roadsides.
