Cyperus unioloides var. wightii

Cyperus unioloides var. wightii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

Cyperus unioloides var. wightii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wetland organisms.
