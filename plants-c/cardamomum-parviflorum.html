Cardamomum parviflorum

Cardamomum parviflorum, commonly known as small-flowered cardamom, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
