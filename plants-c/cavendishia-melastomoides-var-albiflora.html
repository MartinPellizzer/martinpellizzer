Cavendishia melastomoides var. albiflora

Cavendishia melastomoides var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Cavendishia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are showy and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
