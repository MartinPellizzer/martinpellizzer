Convolvulus verecundus subsp. waitaha

Convolvulus verecundus subsp.

waitaha, commonly known as Waitaha bindweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant typically produces small, pale purple or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
