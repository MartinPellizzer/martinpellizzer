Cyathocalyx prunifer

Cyathocalyx prunifer, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Cyathocalyx prunifer is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
