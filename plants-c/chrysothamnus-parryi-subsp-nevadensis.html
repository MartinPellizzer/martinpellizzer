Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. nevadensis

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Chrysothamnus parryi.
