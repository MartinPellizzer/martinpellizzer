Corytholoma douglasii

Corytholoma douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's corytholoma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that bear small, clustered flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The flowers are typically white or pale green, and the plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and ecosystems.
