Cirsium buergeri

Cirsium buergeri, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Cirsium buergeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
