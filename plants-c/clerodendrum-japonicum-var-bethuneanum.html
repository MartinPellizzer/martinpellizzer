Clerodendrum japonicum var. bethuneanum

Clerodendrum japonicum var. bethuneanum, commonly known as the Bethune's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
