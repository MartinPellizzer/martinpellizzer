Convolvulus martinicensis

Convolvulus martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open areas and along roadsides.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, giving the plant its common name.
