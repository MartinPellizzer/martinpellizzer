Calamus calicarpus

Calamus calicarpus, commonly known as the nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular, smooth, and often reddish stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in late summer, and its seeds are enclosed in three-sided, nut-like structures. Calamus calicarpus is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.

It is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
