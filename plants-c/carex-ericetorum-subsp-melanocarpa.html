Carex ericetorum subsp. melanocarpa

Carex ericetorum subsp.

melanocarpa, commonly known as blackstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or heathland environments. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
