Chamaesyce Puberula

Chamaesyce puberula is a small flowering plant native to the southeastern united states. it belongs to the asteraceae family and is commonly known as the hairy sand verbena. this plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. it thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
