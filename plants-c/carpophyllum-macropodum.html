Carpophyllum macropodum

Carpophyllum macropodum, commonly known as the broadleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and contributes to its ecological significance in native habitats.
