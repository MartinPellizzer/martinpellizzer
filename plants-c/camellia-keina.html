Camellia keina

Camellia keina, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is evergreen and thrives in partial shade with well-drained, acidic soil. Camellia keina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate colder climates compared to other camellia species.
