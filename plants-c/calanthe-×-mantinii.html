Calanthe × mantinii

Calanthe × mantinii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Calanthe indica and Calanthe delavayi.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Myanmar. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, lance-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically broad and elliptical, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Calanthe × mantinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings and as a potted plant.
