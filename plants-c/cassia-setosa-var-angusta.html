Cassia setosa var. angusta

Cassia setosa var. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved cassia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Cassia setosa var. angusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
