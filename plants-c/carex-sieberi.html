Carex sieberi

Carex sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. Native to temperate regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its upright growth habit and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
