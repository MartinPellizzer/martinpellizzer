Crassula petraea

Crassula petraea, commonly known as the stone rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often forming dense clusters. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in early spring.

Crassula petraea is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
