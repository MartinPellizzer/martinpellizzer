Calamus paulii

Calamus paulii, commonly known as the Paul's canes.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in basket weaving. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and requires a humid climate with well-draining soil.

Calamus paulii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional uses in various cultural practices.
