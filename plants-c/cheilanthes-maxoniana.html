Cheilanthes maxoniana

Cheilanthes maxoniana, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a species of fern native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, leathery leaves that help conserve water. Its sporangia are clustered in small, spore-producing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves.

Cheilanthes maxoniana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
