Ceraia lomatochila

Ceraia lomatochila, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems are erect and bear clusters of tiny blossoms.

Ceraia lomatochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
