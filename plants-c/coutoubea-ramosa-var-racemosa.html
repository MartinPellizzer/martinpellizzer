Coutoubea ramosa var. racemosa

Coutoubea ramosa var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose coutoubea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
