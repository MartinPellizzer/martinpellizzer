Coleus pobeguinii

Coleus pobeguinii, commonly known as the Madagascar coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that display a wide range of hues, including red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or lavender in color.

Coleus pobeguinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
