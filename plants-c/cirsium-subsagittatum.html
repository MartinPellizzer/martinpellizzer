Cirsium subsagittatum

Cirsium subsagittatum, commonly known as the sagittate thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves have a sagittate (arrowhead) shape, which is a key identifying feature.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
