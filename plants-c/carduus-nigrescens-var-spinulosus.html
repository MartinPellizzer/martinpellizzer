Carduus nigrescens var. spinulosus

Carduus nigrescens var. spinulosus, commonly known as the prickly sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a taproot system and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive spiny texture, contributing to its common name.
