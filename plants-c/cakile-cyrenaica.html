Cakile cyrenaica

Cakile cyrenaica, commonly known as the desert cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or desert environments. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system and a short growth cycle.

Cakile cyrenaica is also known for its ability to tolerate high salinity, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
