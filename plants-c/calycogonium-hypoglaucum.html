Calycogonium hypoglaucum

Calycogonium hypoglaucum, commonly known as the glaucous calycogonium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in arid environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
