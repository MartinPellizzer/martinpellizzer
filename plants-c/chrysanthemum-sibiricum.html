Chrysanthemum sibiricum

Chrysanthemum sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
