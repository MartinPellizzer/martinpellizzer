Chalybea calyptrata

Chalybea calyptrata, commonly known as the silverleaf candelabra, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, branched structure with multiple stems emerging from a single base. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking visual appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
