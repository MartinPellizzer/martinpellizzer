Chamaecrista rotundata var. grandistipula

Chamaecrista rotundata var. grandistipula, commonly known as the large-tipped ticktrefoil, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets and produces distinctive, yellow-orange pea-like flowers. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.
