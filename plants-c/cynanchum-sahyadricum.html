Cynanchum sahyadricum

Cynanchum sahyadricum, commonly known as the Sahyadri milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in this group. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its potential health benefits.
