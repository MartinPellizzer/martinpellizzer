Calendula magellanica

Calendula magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan marigold, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Calendula magellanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
