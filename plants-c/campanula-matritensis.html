Campanula matritensis

Campanula matritensis, commonly known as the Madrid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Campanula matritensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
