Crataegus monogyna var. media

Crataegus monogyna var. media, commonly known as the common hawthorn or medium hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological systems.
