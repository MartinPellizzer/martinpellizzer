Chasea clandestina

Chasea clandestina, commonly known as the hidden grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to the wetlands of South America, particularly Brazil and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and understudied species.
