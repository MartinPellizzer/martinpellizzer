Cucurbita argyrosperma subsp. sororia

Cucurbita argyrosperma subsp.

sororia, commonly known as the silver-seeded gourd, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds.

The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a distinctive silver-colored seed coat. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many cucurbit species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional use in local cuisines.
