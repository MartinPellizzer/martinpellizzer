Canavalia paraguayensis

Canavalia paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Canavalia paraguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
