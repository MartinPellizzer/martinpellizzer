Cyperus variegatus

Cyperus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive variegated patterns of green and white, often with a bluish tinge. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as ponds, ditches, and wetlands.

Cyperus variegatus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
