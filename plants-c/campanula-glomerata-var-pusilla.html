Campanula glomerata var. pusilla

Campanula glomerata var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features small, blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or small containers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along the stem.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, thriving in temperate climates.
