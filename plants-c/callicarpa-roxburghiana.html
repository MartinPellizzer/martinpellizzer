Callicarpa roxburghiana

Callicarpa roxburghiana, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to violet berries that appear in late summer and persist through winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and are used in traditional medicine.
