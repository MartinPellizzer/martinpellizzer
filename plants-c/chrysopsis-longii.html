Chrysopsis longii

Chrysopsis longii, commonly known as the longii everlasting, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is generally gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators.

Chrysopsis longii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
