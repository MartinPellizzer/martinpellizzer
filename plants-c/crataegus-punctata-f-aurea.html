Crataegus punctata f. aurea

Crataegus punctata f. aurea, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves that are often tinged with yellow or gold, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
