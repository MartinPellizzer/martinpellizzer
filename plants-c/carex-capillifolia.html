Carex capillifolia

Carex capillifolia, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. Carex capillifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and shaded forest floors.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
