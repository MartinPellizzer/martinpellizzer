Centaurea × matritensis

Centaurea × matritensis, commonly known as the Madrid centaury, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
