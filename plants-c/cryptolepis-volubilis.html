Cryptolepis volubilis

Cryptolepis volubilis, commonly known as the climbing cryptolepis, is a vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cryptolepis volubilis is often found in moist, shaded areas within forests and along stream banks.
