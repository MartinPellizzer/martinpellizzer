Cleome iberica

Cleome iberica, commonly known as the Spanish spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that resemble spiders. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome iberica is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
