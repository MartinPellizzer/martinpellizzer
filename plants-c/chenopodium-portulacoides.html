Chenopodium portulacoides

Chenopodium portulacoides, commonly known as purslane weed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically thrives in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant has prostrate or ascending stems covered with small, fleshy leaves that are often reddish in color. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Chenopodium portulacoides is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and animals.
