Cardamine incana

Cardamine incana, commonly known as slender watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or greenish flowers. Native to moist, shady environments, it thrives in temperate regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant has a short stem with alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
