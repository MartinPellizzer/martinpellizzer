Cosmos purpureus var. bicolor

Cosmos purpureus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bi-colored flowers with purple petals and a white center, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are gray-green in color, adding texture to the garden. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
