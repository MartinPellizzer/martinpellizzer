Chromatotriccum paspalifolium

Chromatotriccum paspalifolium, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a dense covering of fine hairs that contribute to its silvery sheen.

Chromatotriccum paspalifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
