Croton macrochlamys

Croton macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant croton, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often with vibrant green or variegated patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its size and showy appearance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
