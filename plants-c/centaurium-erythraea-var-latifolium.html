Centaurium erythraea var. latifolium

Centaurium erythraea var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent yellow stamens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
