Cyperus rhigiophyllus

Cyperus rhigiophyllus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowering spikes.

Cyperus rhigiophyllus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
