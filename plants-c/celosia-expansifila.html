Celosia expansifila

Celosia expansifila, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames, which are not petals but modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by the striking bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Celosia expansifila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its eye-catching appearance.
