Carex cockayneana

Carex cockayneana, commonly known as Cockayne's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
