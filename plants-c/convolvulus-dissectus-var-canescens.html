Convolvulus dissectus var. canescens

Convolvulus dissectus var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
