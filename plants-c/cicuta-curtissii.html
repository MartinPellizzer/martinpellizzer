Cicuta curtissii

Cicuta curtissii, commonly known as Curtiss' water hemlock, is a toxic member of the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the central and eastern United States and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers. The plant contains potent toxic compounds, including cicutoxin, which can be lethal to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wet meadows.

Due to its extreme toxicity, it is often considered one of the most dangerous plants in North America.
