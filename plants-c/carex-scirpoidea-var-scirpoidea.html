Carex scirpoidea var. scirpoidea

Carex scirpoidea var. scirpoidea, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
