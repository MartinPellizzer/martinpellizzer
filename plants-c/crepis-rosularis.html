Crepis rosularis

Crepis rosularis, commonly known as the rosette hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of basal leaves and a single, erect flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it withstands cold temperatures and strong winds.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
