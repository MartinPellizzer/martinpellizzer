Cistus crispus f. vestitus

Cistus crispus f. vestitus, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
