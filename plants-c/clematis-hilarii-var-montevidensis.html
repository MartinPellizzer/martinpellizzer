Clematis hilarii var. montevidensis

Clematis hilarii var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense Clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
