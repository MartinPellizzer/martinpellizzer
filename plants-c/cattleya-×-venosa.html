Cattleya × venosa

Cattleya × venosa, commonly known as the violet cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple, with a distinctive violet hue in the center. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cattleya × venosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty in both home gardens and horticultural exhibitions.
