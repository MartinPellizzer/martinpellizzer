Cyathea foxworthyi

Cyathea foxworthyi, commonly known as the Foxworth's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant is known for its slow growth rate.

Cyathea foxworthyi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various species.
