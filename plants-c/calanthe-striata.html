Calanthe striata

Calanthe striata, commonly known as the streaked calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or white with distinctive dark streaks along the petals.

Calanthe striata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
