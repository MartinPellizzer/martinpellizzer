Cestrum luridum

Cestrum luridum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, round seeds.

Cestrum luridum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
