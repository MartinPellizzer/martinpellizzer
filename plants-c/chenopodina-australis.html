Chenopodina australis

Chenopodina australis, commonly known as the Australian goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Chenopodina australis is adapted to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
