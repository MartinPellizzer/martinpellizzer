Coleus gibsonii

Coleus gibsonii, commonly known as the Gibson's coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Coleus gibsonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.
