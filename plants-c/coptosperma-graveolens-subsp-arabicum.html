Coptosperma graveolens subsp. arabicum

Coptosperma graveolens subsp.

arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian star anise, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, capsule-like fruits that resemble those of the star anise.

The plant produces small, white flowers and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to star anise.
