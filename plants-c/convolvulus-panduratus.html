Convolvulus panduratus

Convolvulus panduratus, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
