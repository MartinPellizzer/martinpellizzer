Clusia moaensis

Clusia moaensis, commonly known as the Moa River Clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, also known as the guttiferae, and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Clusia moaensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
