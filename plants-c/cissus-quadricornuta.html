Cissus quadricornuta

Cissus quadricornuta, commonly known as the four-angled devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
