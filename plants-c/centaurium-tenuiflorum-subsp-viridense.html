Centaurium tenuiflorum subsp. viridense

Centaurium tenuiflorum subsp.

viridense, commonly known as greenleaf lesser centaury, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a small herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
