Cyathea caesariana

Cyathea caesariana, commonly known as the blue hakea, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The fronds are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a distinctive blue tinge in some varieties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
