Clompanus tomentosa

Clompanus tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
