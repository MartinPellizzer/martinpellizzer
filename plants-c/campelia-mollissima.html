Campelia mollissima

Campelia mollissima, commonly known as the silky camelia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant blooms during the winter and early spring. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Campelia mollissima prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
