Cereus jajoianus

Cereus jajoianus, commonly known as the Jajoian candleplant, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, a feature common among many columnar cacti. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Cereus jajoianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
