Centropogon exsertus

Centropogon exsertus, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in native plant landscaping.
