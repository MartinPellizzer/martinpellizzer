Carex divulsa subsp. coriogyne

Carex divulsa subsp.

coriogyne, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshes, bogs, or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
