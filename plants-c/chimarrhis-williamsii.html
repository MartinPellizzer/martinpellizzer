Chimarrhis williamsii

Chimarrhis williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or pink bracts that surround small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
