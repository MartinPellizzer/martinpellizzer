Cleome chapalensis

Cleome chapalensis, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals that open in a spiral pattern.

Cleome chapalensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
