Colchicum longifolium var. micranthum

Colchicum longifolium var. micranthum, commonly known as the dwarf autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Western Asia.

It features striking violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a rhizome, and its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems. It is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils, often in woodland or alpine environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
