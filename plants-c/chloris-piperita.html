Chloris piperita

Chloris piperita, commonly known as peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale blue in color. Peppergrass is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is considered a weed in some agricultural areas but also serves as a food source for certain insects and birds.
