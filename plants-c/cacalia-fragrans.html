Cacalia fragrans

Cacalia fragrans, commonly known as sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a taproot that is often harvested for its aromatic, edible roots used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia fragrans thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
