Cerastium decalvans f. doerflerianum

Cerastium decalvans f. doerflerianum, commonly known as the Doerfler's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are small, rounded, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white or pink, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting insect life.
