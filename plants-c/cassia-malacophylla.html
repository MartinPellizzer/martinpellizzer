Cassia malacophylla

Cassia malacophylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is rough and fissured. Cassia malacophylla thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
