Clematis dioica subsp. americana

Clematis dioica subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American vine clematis, is a climbing perennial native to the eastern United States. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are follicles, containing seeds dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
