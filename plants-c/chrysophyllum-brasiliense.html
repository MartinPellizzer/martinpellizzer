Chrysophyllum brasiliense

Chrysophyllum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a green, spiky exterior and a sweet, creamy pulp containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
