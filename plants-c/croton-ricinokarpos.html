Croton ricinokarpos

Croton ricinokarpos, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of milky sap, which is a common trait in the Croton genus.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique fruiting structures.
