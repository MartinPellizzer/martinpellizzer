Curcuma phaeocaulis

Curcuma phaeocaulis, commonly known as the black ginger or purple ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines, and is characterized by its ornamental rhizomes and striking purple foliage. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and have a distinct earthy aroma.

Curcuma phaeocaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with partial shade.
