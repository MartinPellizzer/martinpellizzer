Clusia riedeliana

Clusia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Clusia riedeliana is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
