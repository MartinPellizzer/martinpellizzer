Cardamine divaricata

Cardamine divaricata, commonly known as the slender watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
