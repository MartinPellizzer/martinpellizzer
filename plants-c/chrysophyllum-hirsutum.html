Chrysophyllum hirsutum

Chrysophyllum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a yellow or orange skin, containing a sweet, creamy pulp and black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
