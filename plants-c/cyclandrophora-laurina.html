Cyclandrophora laurina

Cyclandrophora laurina, commonly known as the sweet-scented sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is highly valued for its rich, sweet scent and is used in traditional medicine and incense.

Cyclandrophora laurina thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
