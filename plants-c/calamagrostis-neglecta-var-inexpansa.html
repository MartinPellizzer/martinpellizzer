Calamagrostis neglecta var. inexpansa

Calamagrostis neglecta var. inexpansa, commonly known as the broadleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and wide, flat leaves that are often blue-green in color. This variety is known for its ornamental value, particularly in wet or moist soil conditions. It produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that add visual interest in the fall.

The plant is typically found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
