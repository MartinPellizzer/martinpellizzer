Cissus vitiginea var. cochinchinensis

Cissus vitiginea var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the creeping fig or silver vine, is a trailing evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy berries. It is often grown as a ground cover or trained on trellises due to its rapid growth and ornamental value.

Its adaptability to various soil types and tolerance to partial shade make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
