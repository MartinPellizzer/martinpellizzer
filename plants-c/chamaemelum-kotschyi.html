Chamaemelum kotschyi

Chamaemelum kotschyi, commonly known as Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with white ray florets, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
