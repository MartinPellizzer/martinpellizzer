Cardamine glacialis

Cardamine glacialis, commonly known as glacier buttercup, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to alpine and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface, adapted to withstand cold temperatures. It is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
