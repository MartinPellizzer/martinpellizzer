Cytisus danubialis

Cytisus danubialis, commonly known as the Danube broom, is a flowering shrub native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Cytisus danubialis is notable for its ornamental value and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
