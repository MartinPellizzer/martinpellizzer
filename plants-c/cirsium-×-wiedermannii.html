Cirsium × wiedermannii

Cirsium × wiedermannii, commonly known as the Wiedermann's thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, often reddish stems and large, purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Central Europe. It has been introduced to other regions, including North America, where it sometimes becomes invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
