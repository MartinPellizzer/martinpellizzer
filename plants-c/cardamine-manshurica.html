Cardamine manshurica

Cardamine manshurica, commonly known as Siberian watercress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and anemones.
