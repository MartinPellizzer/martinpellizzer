Crossopteryx kotschyana

Crossopteryx kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmiri rose or Kashmiri apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and ovate leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that are followed by edible, apple-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
