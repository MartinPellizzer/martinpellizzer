Centaurea huetii

Centaurea huetii, commonly known as the Huet's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in dry, rocky soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea huetii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
