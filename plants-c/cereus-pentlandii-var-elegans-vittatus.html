Cereus pentlandii var. elegans-vittatus

Cereus pentlandii var. elegans-vittatus, commonly known as the Zebra Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem covered with distinctive white and green vertical stripes. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance, it is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
