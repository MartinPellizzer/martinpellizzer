Carpinus hwai

Carpinus hwai, commonly known as the Hwai hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
