Chamaecrista cyclophylla

Chamaecrista cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophylla ticktree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, cylindrical pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
