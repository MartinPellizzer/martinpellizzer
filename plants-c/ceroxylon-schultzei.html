Ceroxylon schultzei

Ceroxylon schultzei, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its botanical features include woody stems and leathery, oval-shaped leaves.

Ceroxylon schultzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
