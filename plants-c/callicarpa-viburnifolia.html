Callicarpa viburnifolia

Callicarpa viburnifolia, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in panicles before the berries mature.

Callicarpa viburnifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and wildlife benefits.
