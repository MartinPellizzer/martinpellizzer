Calamintha caroliniana

Calamintha caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, hairy, and serrated leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. Its flowers are small, white to purple, and clustered in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas and stream banks.
