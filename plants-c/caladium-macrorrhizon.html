Caladium macrorrhizon

Caladium macrorrhizon, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf caladium, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, featuring a distinctive arrow-like shape with a prominent midrib. The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture and can exhibit variegation in some cultivars. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

This plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers.
