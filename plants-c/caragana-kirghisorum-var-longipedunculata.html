Caragana kirghisorum var. longipedunculata

Caragana kirghisorum var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate peashrub, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are elongated clusters with peduncles that are notably long in this variety. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and rocky soils.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
