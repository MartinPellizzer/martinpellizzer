Carduus pycnocephalus subsp. tenuiflorus

Carduus pycnocephalus subsp.

tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slenderflower thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a dense, spherical cluster of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in xerophytic vegetation.
