Cinnamomum multiflorum var. elongatum

Cinnamomum multiflorum var. elongatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils that have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The variety elongatum is distinguished by its elongated leaves and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
