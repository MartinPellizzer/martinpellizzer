Cyperus ivohibensis

Cyperus ivohibensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its morphology features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Cyperus ivohibensis is characterized by its distinctive flowering structure and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
