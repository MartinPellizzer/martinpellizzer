Cirsium undulatum var. tracyi

Cirsium undulatum var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flower heads, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Tracy's thistle is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing structural and genetic traits with other thistle species.
