Calea sororia

Calea sororia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are velvety and have a distinctive, sweet scent, especially when crushed.

Calea sororia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
