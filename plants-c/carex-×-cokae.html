Carex × cokae

Carex × cokae, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex brevicuspis and Carex ciliacea, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
