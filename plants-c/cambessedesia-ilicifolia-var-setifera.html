Cambessedesia ilicifolia var. setifera

Cambessedesia ilicifolia var. setifera, commonly known as the hairy buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for certain wildlife species.
