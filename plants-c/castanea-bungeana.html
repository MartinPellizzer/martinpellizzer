Castanea bungeana

Castanea bungeana, commonly known as the Bunge chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The tree produces edible, spiny nuts enclosed in a prickly husk, which ripen in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
