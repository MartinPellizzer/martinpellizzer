Cuphea hispida

Cuphea hispida, commonly known as the rough cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Cuphea hispida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
