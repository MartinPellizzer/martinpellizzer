Capparis angulata

Capparis angulata, commonly known as the angular caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Capparis angulata is also noted for its edible flowers and fruits, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
