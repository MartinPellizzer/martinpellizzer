Celastrus chiapensis

Celastrus chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and ecological interactions.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
