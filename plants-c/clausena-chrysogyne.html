Clausena chrysogyne

Clausena chrysogyne, commonly known as the golden-flowered Clausena, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-colored berries. Its fruit is edible, though often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
