Croton agrarius var. echinocarpus

Croton agrarius var. echinocarpus, commonly known as the spiny croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that are typically orange or red in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its striking foliage.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant may be toxic to humans and animals.
