Chytraculia chytraculia var. trichotoma

Chytraculia chytraculia var. trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy chytraculia, is a lesser-known species within the Chytraculaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance, which is most prominent on its stems and leaves. This variant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to ascend trees or shrubs.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological distinctiveness and potential ecological role in its native habitats.
