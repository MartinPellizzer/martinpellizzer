Cydonia cathayensis

Cydonia cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit that turns yellow or orange when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by its distinctive, fragrant fruit. Cydonia cathayensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
