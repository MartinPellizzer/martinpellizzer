Carex shanghangensis

Carex shanghangensis, commonly known as the Shanghang sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the Shanghang region, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Carex shanghangensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna.
