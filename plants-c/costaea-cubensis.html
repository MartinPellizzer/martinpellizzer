Costaea cubensis

Costaea cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban costaea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces showy, pinkish-white blooms.

Costaea cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
