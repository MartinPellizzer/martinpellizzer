Croton acutifolius

Croton acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability, it can be found in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and disturbed soils.
