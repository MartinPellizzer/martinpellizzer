Callisia elegans

Callisia elegans, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features trailing stems with fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale purple, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Callisia elegans is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its trailing growth habit.
