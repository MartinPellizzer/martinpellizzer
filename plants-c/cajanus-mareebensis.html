Cajanus mareebensis

Cajanus mareebensis, commonly known as the Mareeb wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important crop for drought-prone regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and soil improvement.
