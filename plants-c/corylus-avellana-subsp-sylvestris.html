Corylus avellana subsp. sylvestris

Corylus avellana subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the European hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It features slender, zigzagging branches and oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by edible hazelnuts in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts and cuisine.
