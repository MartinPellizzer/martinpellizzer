Chelonanthus frigidus

Chelonanthus frigidus, commonly known as the cold honeysuckle, is a perennial shrub native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
