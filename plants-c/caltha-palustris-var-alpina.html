Caltha palustris var. alpina

Caltha palustris var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold climates and high-altitude environments.
