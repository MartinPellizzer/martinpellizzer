Chloothamnus

Chloothamnus, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. Chloothamnus species are often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with full sun exposure.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and their ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
