Chaenocephalus suncho

Chaenocephalus suncho, commonly known as the suncho plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It typically features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
