Cissus quinquangularis

Cissus quinquangularis, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can be used for propagation through cuttings.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive foliage.
