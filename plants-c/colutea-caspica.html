Colutea caspica

Colutea caspica, commonly known as the Caspian colutea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Colutea caspica is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate foliage and blooming habit.
