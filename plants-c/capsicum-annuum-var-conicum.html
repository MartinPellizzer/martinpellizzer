Capsicum annuum var. conicum

Capsicum annuum var. conicum, commonly known as the horn pepper, is a variety of chili pepper characterized by its long, narrow, and often curved fruit shape.

It originates from Central and South America and is cultivated for both its culinary and ornamental value. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small white flowers that develop into the distinctive conical peppers. The fruit is usually red when mature, though it can also be yellow or orange, and contains numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and mild to medium heat level, making it popular in both home gardens and commercial cultivation.
