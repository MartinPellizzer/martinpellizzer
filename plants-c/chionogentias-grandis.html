Chionogentias grandis

Chionogentias grandis, commonly known as the silverleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of silvery-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Chionogentias grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
