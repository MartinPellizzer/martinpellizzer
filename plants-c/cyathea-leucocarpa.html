Cyathea leucocarpa

Cyathea leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruit tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are responsible for reproduction through spores. Cyathea leucocarpa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
