Chrysogonum peruvianum

Chrysogonum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Chrysogonum peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
