Coccothrinax spissa

Coccothrinax spissa, commonly known as the Cuban royal palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Cuba and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like fronds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical regions around the world.
