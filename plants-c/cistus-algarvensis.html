Cistus algarvensis

Cistus algarvensis, commonly known as the Algarve rockrose, is a shrub native to the southern region of Portugal.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, contributing to the unique flora of the region.

Cistus algarvensis is also valued for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
