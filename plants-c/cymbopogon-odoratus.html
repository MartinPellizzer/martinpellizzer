Cymbopogon odoratus

Cymbopogon odoratus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Poaceae family. Its rhizomes and roots are rich in essential oils, contributing to its widespread use in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Cymbopogon odoratus make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and natural product industries.
