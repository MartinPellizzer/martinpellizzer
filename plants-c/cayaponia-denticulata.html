Cayaponia denticulata

Cayaponia denticulata, commonly known as the jaguar bean, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its seeds are encased in tough, fibrous pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cayaponia denticulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
