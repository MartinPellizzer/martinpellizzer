Cousinia chrysacantha

Cousinia chrysacantha, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, tough seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across rocky and sandy soils.

Cousinia chrysacantha is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
