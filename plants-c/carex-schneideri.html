Carex schneideri

Carex schneideri, commonly known as Schneider's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, three-lobed flowers in spikelets. Native to Central and South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically clump-forming, with a dense, compact growth habit.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
