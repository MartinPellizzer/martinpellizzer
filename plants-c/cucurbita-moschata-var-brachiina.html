Cucurbita moschata var. brachiina

Cucurbita moschata var. brachiina, commonly known as the Mexican squash or chayote, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and hairy stems. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, with a thick, rough skin that varies in color from green to yellow. It produces a sweet, edible flesh that is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is well adapted to warm climates and is commonly cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in cooking.
