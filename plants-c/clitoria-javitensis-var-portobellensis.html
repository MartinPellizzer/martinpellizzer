Clitoria javitensis var. portobellensis

Clitoria javitensis var. portobellensis, commonly known as the Portobello butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant purple-blue flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The flowers are known to change color when exposed to acid or base, making it a popular choice for scientific demonstrations.
