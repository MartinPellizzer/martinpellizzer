Chrysophyllum deplanchei

Chrysophyllum deplanchei, commonly known as the African cherry or African sweet chestnut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Chrysophyllum deplanchei is also valued for its timber, which is hard and durable.
