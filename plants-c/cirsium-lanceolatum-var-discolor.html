Cirsium lanceolatum var. discolor

Cirsium lanceolatum var. discolor, commonly known as the variable thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The variety discolor is distinguished by its variable leaf coloration and the presence of a distinct, discolored patch on the leaf base.
