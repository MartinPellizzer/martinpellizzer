Coccinia adoensis var. aurantiaca

Coccinia adoensis var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green, with a striking golden or yellowish hue on the underside. The flowers are small, trumpet-shaped, and usually pale yellow to orange, blooming in clusters. The fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
