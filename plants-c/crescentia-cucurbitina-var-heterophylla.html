Crescentia cucurbitina var. heterophylla

Crescentia cucurbitina var. heterophylla, commonly known as the Caribbean bellflower, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a heavy, fleshy fruit that is typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on the environment.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
