Cycas lindstromii

Cycas lindstromii, commonly known as the Lindstrom's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the island of Palau in the western Pacific Ocean and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Cycas lindstromii is notable for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for hundreds of years.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
