Cuphea utriculosa var. donnell-smithii

Cuphea utriculosa var. donnell-smithii, commonly known as the Mexican Whistlingflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, red or orange flowers that resemble tiny whistles, which attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
