Carex macrostylos var. peregrina

Carex macrostylos var. peregrina, commonly known as the wandering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and are pollinated by wind.
