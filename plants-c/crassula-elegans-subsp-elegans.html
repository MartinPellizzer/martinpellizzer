Crassula elegans subsp. elegans

Crassula elegans subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the "Lamb's Ear" or "Elegant Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact growth habit and low water requirements.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it drought-tolerant and easy to care for.
