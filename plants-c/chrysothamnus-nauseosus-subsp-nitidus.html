Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. nitidus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

nitidus, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its structure and appearance make it well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
