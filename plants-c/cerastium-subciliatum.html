Cerastium subciliatum

Cerastium subciliatum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers with five petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in cooler climates.
