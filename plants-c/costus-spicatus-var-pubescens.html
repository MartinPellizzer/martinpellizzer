Costus spicatus var. pubescens

Costus spicatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy costus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces striking clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which are arranged in spiky inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
