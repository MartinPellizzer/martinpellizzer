Celtis multifolia

Celtis multifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and dark green on top. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple color. Celtis multifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
