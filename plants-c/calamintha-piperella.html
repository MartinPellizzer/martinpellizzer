Calamintha piperella

Calamintha piperella, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, small, gray-green leaves that emit a strong, minty scent. The plant produces tiny white or pale blue flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.

Calamintha piperella thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.
