Cocculus tamoides

Cocculus tamoides, commonly known as the Indian cocklebur, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and spiny fruits. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are woody, spherical capsules that contain several seeds.

Cocculus tamoides is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
