Cousinia scala

Cousinia scala, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia scala is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
