Cirsium griffithii var. crassispinum

Cirsium griffithii var. crassispinum, commonly known as the broadspined thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish-purple flower heads, which are composed of many small florets arranged in a composite flower structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
