Carex multiformis

Carex multiformis, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Carex multiformis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
