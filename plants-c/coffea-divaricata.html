Coffea divaricata

Coffea divaricata, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia and is known for its small, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that contain the seeds used to make coffee. It thrives in tropical climates with high altitudes and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is highly valued in specialty coffee markets.
