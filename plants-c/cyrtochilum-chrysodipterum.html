Cyrtochilum chrysodipterum

Cyrtochilum chrysodipterum, commonly known as the golden-winged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, showy flowers. The plant typically features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, golden-yellow sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Cyrtochilum chrysodipterum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
