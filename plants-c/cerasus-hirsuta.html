Cerasus hirsuta

Cerasus hirsuta, commonly known as the downy cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, downy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that ripen in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Cerasus hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
