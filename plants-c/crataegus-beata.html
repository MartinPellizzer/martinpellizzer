Crataegus beata

Crataegus beata, commonly known as the blessed hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses, apples, and cherries, and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy flowers. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits called haws, which are edible but often used in jams and wines.

Crataegus beata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
