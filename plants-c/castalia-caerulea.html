Castalia caerulea

Castalia caerulea, commonly known as the blue castalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, preferring rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Castalia caerulea is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in humid climates.
