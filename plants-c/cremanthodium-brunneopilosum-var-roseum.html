Cremanthodium brunneopilosum var. roseum

Cremanthodium brunneopilosum var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-colored cremanthodium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or rose-colored, with yellow central disks, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
