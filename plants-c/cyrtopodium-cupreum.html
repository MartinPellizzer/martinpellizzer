Cyrtopodium cupreum

Cyrtopodium cupreum, commonly known as the copper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a loose inflorescence. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered with a thick, leathery epidermis.

Cyrtopodium cupreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
