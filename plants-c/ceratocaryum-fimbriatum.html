Ceratocaryum fimbriatum

Ceratocaryum fimbriatum, commonly known as the African bristlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble brushes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers. It is often found in open woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
