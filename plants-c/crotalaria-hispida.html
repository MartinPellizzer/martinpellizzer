Crotalaria hispida

Crotalaria hispida, commonly known as the prickly crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, often spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria hispida is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
