Crataegus ellipticifolia

Crataegus ellipticifolia, commonly known as the ellipticleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves, which are typically 2 to 5 inches long and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sunlight.

Crataegus ellipticifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
