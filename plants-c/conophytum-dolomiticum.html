Conophytum dolomiticum

Conophytum dolomiticum, commonly known as the Dolomitic Pachyphytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
