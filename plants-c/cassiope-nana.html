Cassiope nana

Cassiope nana, commonly known as the dwarf mountain snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a compact and hardy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and full sun to partial shade.

Cassiope nana is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand cold climates.
