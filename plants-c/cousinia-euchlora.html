Cousinia euchlora

Cousinia euchlora, commonly known as the woolly globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of leaves and stems that rise from the base.

Cousinia euchlora is often found in desert environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
