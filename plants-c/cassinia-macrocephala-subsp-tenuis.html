Cassinia macrocephala subsp. tenuis

Cassinia macrocephala subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globemallow, is a shrub native to Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces spherical clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender leaves compared to other Cassinia species.
