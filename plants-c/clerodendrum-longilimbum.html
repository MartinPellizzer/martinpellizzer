Clerodendrum longilimbum

Clerodendrum longilimbum, commonly known as the long-leaved clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to white, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and flowering display.
