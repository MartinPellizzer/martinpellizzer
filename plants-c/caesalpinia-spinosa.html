Caesalpinia spinosa

Caesalpinia spinosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pea or tickbush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
