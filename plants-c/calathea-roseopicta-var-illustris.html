Calathea roseopicta var. illustris

Calathea roseopicta var. illustris, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves with intricate, colorful patterns in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage is often described as having a "velvet" texture, which enhances its ornamental appeal.

Due to its sensitivity to temperature changes and low light, it requires careful maintenance to flourish.
