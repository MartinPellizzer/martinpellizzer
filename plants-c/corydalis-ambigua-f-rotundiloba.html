Corydalis ambigua f. rotundiloba

Corydalis ambigua f. rotundiloba, commonly known as the rounded-leaf corydalis, is a variant of the broadleaf corydalis species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often rounded at the base, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters and blooming in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its delicate foliage and vibrant blooms.
