Conostegia macrantha

Conostegia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered crape myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its bark is smooth and grayish. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Conostegia macrantha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
