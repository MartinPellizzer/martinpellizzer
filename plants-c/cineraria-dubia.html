Cineraria dubia

Cineraria dubia, commonly known as the blue violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and fuzzy, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cineraria dubia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
