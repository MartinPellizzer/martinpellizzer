Cincinalis rugulosa

Cincinalis rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf cincinalis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and clusters of small, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
