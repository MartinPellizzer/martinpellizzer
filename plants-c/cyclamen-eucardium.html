Cyclamen eucardium

Cyclamen eucardium, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety foliage and clusters of delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.

Cyclamen eucardium is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.
