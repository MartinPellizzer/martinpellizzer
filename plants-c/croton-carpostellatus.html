Croton carpostellatus

Croton carpostellatus, commonly known as the black croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that can range from green to red, purple, or even black. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton carpostellatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
