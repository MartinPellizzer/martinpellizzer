Cyperus esculentus proles sativus

Cyperus esculentus proles sativus, commonly known as chufa or earth almond, is a tuberous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces edible tubers that are rich in nutrients, including carbohydrates, protein, and essential minerals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. Its growth habit includes tall, grass-like stems with narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The tubers, which are the main edible part, are typically harvested in the fall and can be roasted, ground, or used in various culinary applications.
