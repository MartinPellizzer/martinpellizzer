Cachrys gaubae

Cachrys gaubae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It produces small, yellow, egg-shaped fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
