Cerastium moesiacum f. rhodopeum

Cerastium moesiacum f. rhodopeum, commonly known as the Rhodope mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to rocky and arid environments. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, calcareous soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
