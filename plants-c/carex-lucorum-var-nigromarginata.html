Carex lucorum var. nigromarginata

Carex lucorum var. nigromarginata, commonly known as the black-margin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are usually dark green with a distinctive black or dark brown margin, giving it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
