Cyperus thomensis

Cyperus thomensis, commonly known as the Tomine sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grass-like plants with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus thomensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, grass-like foliage.
