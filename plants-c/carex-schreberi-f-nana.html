Carex schreberi f. nana

Carex schreberi f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a compact, herbaceous perennial that belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. This plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It is valued for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its ornamental appeal in water garden settings.

Carex schreberi f. nana is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its tolerance of varying moisture levels.
