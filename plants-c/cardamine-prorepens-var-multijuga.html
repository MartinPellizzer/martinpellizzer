Cardamine prorepens var. multijuga

Cardamine prorepens var. multijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water crowfoot, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and woodland floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale yellow, and consist of two sepals and two petals.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.
