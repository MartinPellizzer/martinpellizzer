Chailletia heudelotii

Chailletia heudelotii, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features tall, slender stems with large, showy white or pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical gardens.
