Cinnamomum pedunculatum var. lancifolium

Cinnamomum pedunculatum var. lancifolium, commonly known as the lanceleaf cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, reddish-brown fruits. Its aromatic bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
