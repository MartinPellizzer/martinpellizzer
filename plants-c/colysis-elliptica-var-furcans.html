Colysis elliptica var. furcans

Colysis elliptica var. furcans, commonly known as the forked colysis, is a variant of the colysis genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are often deeply lobed or divided, giving the plant a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its foliage is often a deep green, with some cultivars displaying variegation or reddish hues along the edges.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture to shaded garden spaces.
