Cactus aeruginosus

Cactus aeruginosus, commonly known as the blue cactus or the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that is typically blue-green in color, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically short and rounded, providing some shade to the stem.

Cactus aeruginosus is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
