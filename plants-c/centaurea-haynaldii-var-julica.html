Centaurea haynaldii var. julica

Centaurea haynaldii var. julica, commonly known as the Julica cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
