Coelachyrum induratum

Coelachyrum induratum, commonly known as the hard-stemmed grass or Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Coelachyrum induratum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
