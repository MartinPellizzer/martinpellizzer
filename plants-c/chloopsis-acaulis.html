Chloopsis acaulis

Chloopsis acaulis, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Chloopsis acaulis is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
