Ceiba crispiflora

Ceiba crispiflora, commonly known as the silky ceiba, is a large tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The tree has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces woody fruits containing fibrous seeds.

The plant is adapted to tropical climates and plays an important role in its native ecosystems.
