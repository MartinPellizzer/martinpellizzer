Celastrus richardii var. cochlearifolius

Celastrus richardii var. cochlearifolius, commonly known as the spiral-leaf staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its helical twisting growth habit. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a spiral arrangement, giving the plant its common name. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

This species is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
