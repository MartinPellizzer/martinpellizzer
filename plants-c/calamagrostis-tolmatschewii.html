Calamagrostis tolmatschewii

Calamagrostis tolmatschewii, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer, which are typically greenish or brownish in hue.

Calamagrostis tolmatschewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
