Colutea

Colutea, commonly known as sainfoin, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, many of which are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plants are characterized by their compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. They are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Some species are cultivated for their ornamental value or as forage for livestock.
