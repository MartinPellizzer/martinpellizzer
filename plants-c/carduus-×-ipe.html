Carduus × ipe

Carduus × ipe, commonly known as the common thistle.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its foliage is covered in stiff, prickly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
