Cocos nana

Cocos nana, commonly known as the dwarf coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its compact size compared to the typical coconut palm. The plant produces small, edible fruits that contain coconut water and flesh. Its leaves are long and feather-like, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Cocos nana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of coconut products.
