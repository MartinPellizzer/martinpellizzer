Cyanotis lanata var. gracilis

Cyanotis lanata var. gracilis, commonly known as the silky bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and it is native to parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
