Culcita straminea

Culcita straminea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are slender and branched.

Culcita straminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
