Combretum mossambicense

Combretum mossambicense, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight pubescence. It produces small, winged fruits that disperse its seeds by wind.

Combretum mossambicense is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
