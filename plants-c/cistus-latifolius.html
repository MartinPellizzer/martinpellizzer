Cistus latifolius

Cistus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grey-green, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is evergreen, with a woody stem and a dense, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Cistus latifolius is also known for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
