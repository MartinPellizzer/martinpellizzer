Campanula pelviformis

Campanula pelviformis, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Campanula pelviformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
