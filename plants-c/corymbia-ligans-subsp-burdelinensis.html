Corymbia ligans subsp. burdelinensis

Corymbia ligans subsp.

burdelinensis, commonly known as the Burdekin bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Queensland, Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often displays a reddish hue, and its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, urn-shaped fruits. It is adapted to dry, seasonal climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
