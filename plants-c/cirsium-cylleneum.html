Cirsium cylleneum

Cirsium cylleneum, commonly known as the Cyllene thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Cirsium cylleneum is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
