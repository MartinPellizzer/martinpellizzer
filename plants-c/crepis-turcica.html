Crepis turcica

Crepis turcica, commonly known as Turkish mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, with a distinctive shape that aids in identification.

Crepis turcica is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy environments.
