Calamintha caerulescens

Calamintha caerulescens, commonly known as bluebeard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Calamintha caerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
