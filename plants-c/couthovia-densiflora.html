Couthovia densiflora

Couthovia densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower shrub, is a perennial plant native to the temperate rainforests of the Pacific Northwest.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
