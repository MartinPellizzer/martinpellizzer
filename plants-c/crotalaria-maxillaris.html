Crotalaria maxillaris

Crotalaria maxillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and its seed pods are elongated and cylindrical. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria maxillaris is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
