Capparis membranacea var. puberula

Capparis membranacea var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, round, green fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in cuisine, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional settings.
