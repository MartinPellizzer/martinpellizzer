Carduus arabicus subsp. arabicus

Carduus arabicus subsp.

arabicus, commonly known as the Arab thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils.

The plant features a robust taproot and produces spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its composite flower heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to brown bracts.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
