Clematis biperrieri

Clematis biperrieri, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and attractive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for climbing structures in gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant blossoms and graceful growth habit.
