Calophyllum brasiliense subsp. mariae

Calophyllum brasiliense subsp.

mariae, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a member of the Clusiaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves. It produces yellow-orange flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
