Carex watsoniana

Carex watsoniana, commonly known as Watson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has triangular stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually brown or reddish in color.

Carex watsoniana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
