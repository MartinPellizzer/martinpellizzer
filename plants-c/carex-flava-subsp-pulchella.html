Carex flava subsp. pulchella

Carex flava subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or marshy habitats. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
