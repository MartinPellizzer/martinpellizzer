Citrullus vulgaris var. citroides

Citrullus vulgaris var. citroides, commonly known as the citron melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, yellow to green, rough-skinned fruits that contain a juicy, sweet, and aromatic pulp. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The citron melon is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
