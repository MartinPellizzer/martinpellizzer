Clusia organensis

Clusia organensis, commonly known as the Cuban glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often bright green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is adapted to grow in coastal areas and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Clusia organensis is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
