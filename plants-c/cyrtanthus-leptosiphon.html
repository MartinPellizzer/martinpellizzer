Cyrtanthus leptosiphon

Cyrtanthus leptosiphon, commonly known as the slender-siphon cyrtanthus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in late spring or early summer, depending on the climate.

Cyrtanthus leptosiphon is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
