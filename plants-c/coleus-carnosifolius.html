Coleus carnosifolius

Coleus carnosifolius, commonly known as the beefsteak plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. Its leaves are not only ornamental but also have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
