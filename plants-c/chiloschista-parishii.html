Chiloschista parishii

Chiloschista parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay glasswort, is a halophytic plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in estuaries and salt marshes. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding its survival in harsh environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white in color.

Chiloschista parishii plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
