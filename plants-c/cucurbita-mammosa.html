Cucurbita mammosa

Cucurbita mammosa, commonly known as the giant pumpkin or cushaw squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often heavily lobed leaves, which can reach several feet in diameter. The plant produces orange to yellow-orange, rough-skinned fruits that can weigh several kilograms. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive green throat, and it is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds and flesh.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season to mature.
