Carex platylepis

Carex platylepis, commonly known as flat-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex platylepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
