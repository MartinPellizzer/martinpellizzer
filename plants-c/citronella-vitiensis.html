Citronella vitiensis

Citronella vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitienna citronella, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the stalks. Citronella vitiensis is known for its strong, aromatic scent, which is often used in natural insect repellents.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
