Crataegus coccinea

Crataegus coccinea, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange fruit, which is typically produced in clusters and persists through winter. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus coccinea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological benefits to wildlife.
