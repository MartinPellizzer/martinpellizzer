Calamagrostis × submonticola

Calamagrostis × submonticola, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth and striking silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers in late summer, which are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a versatile ornamental grass.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it an excellent choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
