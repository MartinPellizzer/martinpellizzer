Cassia nictitans

Cassia nictitans, commonly known as the weeping cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble weeping strands, giving it its common name. The plant produces pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia nictitans thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
