Calcearia aristata

Calcearia aristata, commonly known as the desert star or desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a compact, mat-like growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are adapted to conserve water. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Calcearia aristata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
