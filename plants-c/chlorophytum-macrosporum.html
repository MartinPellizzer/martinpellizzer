Chlorophytum macrosporum

Chlorophytum macrosporum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with distinctive yellow or white stripes. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal indoor plant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by clusters of small, white berries.

Its air-purifying qualities make it a popular choice for both ornamental and functional indoor gardening.
