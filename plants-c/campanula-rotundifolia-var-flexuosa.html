Campanula rotundifolia var. flexuosa

Campanula rotundifolia var. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often slightly bent or flexed, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are rounded and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
