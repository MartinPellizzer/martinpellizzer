Cereus coerulescens f. melanacanthus

Cereus coerulescens f. melanacanthus, commonly known as the black spined night bloomer, is a rare and striking cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in sharp, black spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers at night, which open only during the evening hours. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique features, it is a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
