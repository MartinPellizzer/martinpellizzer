Cathartolinum medium var. texanum

Cathartolinum medium var. texanum, commonly known as the Texas cathartolinum, is a perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in native plant gardens.
