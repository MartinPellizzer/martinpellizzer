Calandrinia litoralis

Calandrinia litoralis, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy, succulent stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calandrinia litoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local wildlife.
