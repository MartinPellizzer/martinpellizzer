Carex bolanderi var. artemisiana

Carex bolanderi var. artemisiana, commonly known as Artemisian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
