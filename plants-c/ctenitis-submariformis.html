Ctenitis submariformis

Ctenitis submariformis, commonly known as the deep-sea fern, is a unique marine plant found in deep ocean environments.

It belongs to the family Ctenitidaceae and is characterized by its specialized fronds adapted to high-pressure and low-light conditions. The plant exhibits a complex structure with branching, ribbon-like fronds that help it capture nutrients from the water. Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal in the deep sea, often relying on currents for propagation.

Due to its extreme habitat, little is known about its full botanical characteristics and ecological role.
