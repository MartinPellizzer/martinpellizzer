Calamagrostis smirnowii

Calamagrostis smirnowii, commonly known as Smirnov's reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
