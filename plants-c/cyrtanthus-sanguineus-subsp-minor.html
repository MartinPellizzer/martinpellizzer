Cyrtanthus sanguineus subsp. minor

Cyrtanthus sanguineus subsp.

minor, commonly known as the dwarf rain lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
