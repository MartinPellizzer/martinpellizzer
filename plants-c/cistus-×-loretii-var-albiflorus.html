Cistus × loretii var. albiflorus

Cistus × loretii var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
