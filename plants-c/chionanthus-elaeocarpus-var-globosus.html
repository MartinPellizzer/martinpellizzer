Chionanthus elaeocarpus var. globosus

Chionanthus elaeocarpus var. globosus, commonly known as the globeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
