Carum microcarpum

Carum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited caraway, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as parsley and dill. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its fruit, a small schizocarp, is characteristic of the genus Carum.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its subtle aromatic properties.
