Cnicus × tartaricus

Cnicus × tartaricus, commonly known as the common sandthrift, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, sticky coating. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in sandy or disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Cnicus × tartaricus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh environments.
