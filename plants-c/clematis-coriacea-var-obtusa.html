Clematis coriacea var. obtusa

Clematis coriacea var. obtusa, commonly known as the smooth Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, usually in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its climbing habit and floral display.
