Cousinia gundelia

Cousinia gundelia, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads that resemble bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Cousinia gundelia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
