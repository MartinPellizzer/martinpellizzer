Cyperus hemisphaericus

Cyperus hemisphaericus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense mats in shallow water.

Cyperus hemisphaericus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
