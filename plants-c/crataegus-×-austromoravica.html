Crataegus × austromoravica

Crataegus × austromoravica, commonly known as the Austrian hawthorn, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically edible and attract various birds and wildlife. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Crataegus × austromoravica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
