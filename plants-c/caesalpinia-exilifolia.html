Caesalpinia exilifolia

Caesalpinia exilifolia, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its habitat. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

Caesalpinia exilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
