Crocus hittiticus

Crocus hittiticus, commonly known as the Hittite crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey and Syria. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils and can remain dormant for extended periods.

The plant is noted for its historical significance, having been cultivated in ancient times for its ornamental and possibly medicinal uses.
