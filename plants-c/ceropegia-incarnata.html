Ceropegia incarnata

Ceropegia incarnata, commonly known as the hooded milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, hood-like flowers that are often pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, white, egg-shaped fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ceropegia incarnata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
