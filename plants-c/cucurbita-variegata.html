Cucurbita variegata

Cucurbita variegata, commonly known as the ornamental gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage and elongated, often ribbed fruit. The plant produces yellow or white flowers and has a vining growth habit. Its leaves are typically broad and deeply lobed, adding visual interest to gardens.

While not typically cultivated for food, it is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance.
