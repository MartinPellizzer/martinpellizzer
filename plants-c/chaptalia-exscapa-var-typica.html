Chaptalia exscapa var. typica

Chaptalia exscapa var. typica, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
