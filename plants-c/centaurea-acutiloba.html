Centaurea acutiloba

Centaurea acutiloba, commonly known as the sharpleaf knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Centaurea acutiloba is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
