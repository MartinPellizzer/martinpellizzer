Crataegus macrosperma var. pastorum

Crataegus macrosperma var. pastorum, commonly known as the Pastoral hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often contain seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a hedging plant.
