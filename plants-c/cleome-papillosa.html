Cleome papillosa

Cleome papillosa, commonly known as the silky cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
