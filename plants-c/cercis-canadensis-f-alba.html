Cercis canadensis f. alba

Cercis canadensis f. alba, commonly known as the white maple, is a cultivar of the red maple that exhibits striking white to pale pink flowers.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The tree typically grows to a height of 20-30 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy spring blossoms and vibrant autumn foliage, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
