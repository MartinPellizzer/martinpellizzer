Clematis florida f. sieboldii

Clematis florida f. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's morning glory, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink to purple. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, slightly serrated edge. Its stems are vigorous climbers with thornless tendrils, making it suitable for trellises or arbors.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate.
