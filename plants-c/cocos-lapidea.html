Cocos lapidea

Cocos lapidea, commonly known as the Lapidea coconut, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces large, fibrous fruits that contain a single seed, which is used for propagation. Its leaves are long and feather-like, and the trunk is typically slender and unbranched.

The plant is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
