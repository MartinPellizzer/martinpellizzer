Coristospermum ferulaceum

Coristospermum ferulaceum, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, hairy pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, featuring deep root systems and succulent leaves.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
