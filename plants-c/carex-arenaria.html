Carex arenaria

Carex arenaria, commonly known as sand sedge, is a perennial plant native to sandy and coastal regions.

It has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or rocky areas.

Carex arenaria is valued for its ability to stabilize soils and its ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
