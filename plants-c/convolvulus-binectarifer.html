Convolvulus binectarifer

Convolvulus binectarifer, commonly known as the two-flowered convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive two-flowered inflorescences. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
