Calamus multirameus

Calamus multirameus, commonly known as the many-ramed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus multirameus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
