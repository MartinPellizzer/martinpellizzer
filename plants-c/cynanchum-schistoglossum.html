Cynanchum schistoglossum

Cynanchum schistoglossum, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or the devil's backbone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions and is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
