Calothamnus macrocarpus

Calothamnus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical inflorescences, which are composed of numerous bright red to orange tubular flowers. The plant produces woody, bottle-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Calothamnus macrocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
