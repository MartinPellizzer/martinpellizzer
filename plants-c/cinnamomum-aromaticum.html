Cinnamomum aromaticum

Cinnamomum aromaticum, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct cinnamon scent when crushed.

The bark is harvested and dried to produce the spice commonly referred to as cassia.
