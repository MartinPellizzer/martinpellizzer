Carapa angustifolia

Carapa angustifolia, commonly known as wild cashew or Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing seeds. The tree is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dietary fat. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
