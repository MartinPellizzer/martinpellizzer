Cissus tiliacea

Cissus tiliacea, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvet leaf, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Cissus tiliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
