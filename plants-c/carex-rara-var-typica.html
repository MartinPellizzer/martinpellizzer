Carex rara var. typica

Carex rara var. typica, commonly known as the rare sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, typical of many sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
