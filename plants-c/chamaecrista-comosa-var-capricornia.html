Chamaecrista comosa var. capricornia

Chamaecrista comosa var. capricornia, commonly known as the Capricorn Crownflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellow-orange blooms that appear in late summer. The plant has a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
