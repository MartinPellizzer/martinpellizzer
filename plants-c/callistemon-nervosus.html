Callistemon nervosus

Callistemon nervosus, commonly known as the narrow-veined bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson or red flower spikes that emerge from a dense cluster of leaves. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects due to its nectar-rich flowers.
