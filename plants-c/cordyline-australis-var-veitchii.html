Cordyline australis var. veitchii

Cordyline australis var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a fibrous, rough bark and large, strap-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant is adapted to coastal and subcoastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh conditions.

The variety is distinguished by its slightly shorter stature and more compact growth compared to the typical Cordyline australis.
