Calamus sordidus

Calamus sordidus, commonly known as the dirty reed, is a member of the Aroid family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in wet, swampy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among Aroids. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Calamus sordidus is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
