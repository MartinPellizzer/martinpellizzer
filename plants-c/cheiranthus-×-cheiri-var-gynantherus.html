Cheiranthus × cheiri var. gynantherus

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. gynantherus, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

The varietal name "gynantherus" refers to its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive gynostemon.
