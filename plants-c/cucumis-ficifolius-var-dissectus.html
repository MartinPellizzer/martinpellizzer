Cucumis ficifolius var. dissectus

Cucumis ficifolius var. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected cucumber gourd, is a unique variant of the species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that give it a striking, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a mild, slightly tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive foliage.

This variant is less commonly grown for food compared to other cucumis species, but it holds value in traditional horticulture and botanical studies.
