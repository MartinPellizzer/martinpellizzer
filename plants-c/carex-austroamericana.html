Carex austroamericana

Carex austroamericana, commonly known as the Southern American sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate various water conditions.
