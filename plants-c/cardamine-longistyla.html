Cardamine longistyla

Cardamine longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled watercress, is a flowering plant native to wetland and streamside habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like mustard and cress. The plant features small, clustered flowers with four white petals and prominent yellow stamens. Its leaves are alternate, basal, and have a distinctive lobed or divided shape.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing aquatic soils and providing habitat for invertebrates.
