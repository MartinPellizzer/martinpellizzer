Calanthe sandsii

Calanthe sandsii, commonly known as the sand calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks.

Calanthe sandsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a potted plant.
