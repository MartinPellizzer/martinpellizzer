Coelogyne apinnata

Coelogyne apinnata, commonly known as the naked-leaved orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves along the flower stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a contrasting red lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coelogyne apinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
