Carex tymphaea

Carex tymphaea, commonly known as swamp sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Carex tymphaea plays an important ecological role by stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
