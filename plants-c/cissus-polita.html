Cissus polita

Cissus polita, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a striking silver-green color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by translucent, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus polita is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and air-purifying properties.
