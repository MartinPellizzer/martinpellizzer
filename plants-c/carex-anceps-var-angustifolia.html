Carex anceps var. angustifolia

Carex anceps var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Carex anceps var. angustifolia is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
