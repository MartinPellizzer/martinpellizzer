Caraipa suaveolens

Caraipa suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented caraipa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
