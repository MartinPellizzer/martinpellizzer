Callista cathcartii

Callista cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Callista cathcartii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
