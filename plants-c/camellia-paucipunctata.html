Camellia paucipunctata

Camellia paucipunctata, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a waxy texture and a subtle fragrance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is also valued for its historical and cultural significance.
