Cassia williamsii

Cassia williamsii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, rectangular seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia williamsii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical areas.
