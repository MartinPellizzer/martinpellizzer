Ceratostylis purpurea

Ceratostylis purpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with purple veins on white petals, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
