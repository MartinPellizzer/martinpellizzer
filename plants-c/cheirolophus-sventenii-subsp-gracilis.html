Cheirolophus sventenii subsp. gracilis

Cheirolophus sventenii subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
