Cliffortia polycephala

Cliffortia polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed broom, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
