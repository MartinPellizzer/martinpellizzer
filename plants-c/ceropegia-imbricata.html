Ceropegia imbricata

Ceropegia imbricata, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia imbricata is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
