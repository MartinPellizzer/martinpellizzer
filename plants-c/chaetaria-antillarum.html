Chaetaria antillarum

Chaetaria antillarum, commonly known as the Caribbean sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a rough texture.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
