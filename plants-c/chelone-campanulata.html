Chelone campanulata

Chelone campanulata, commonly known as the white turtlehead, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features striking, tubular, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Chelone campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
