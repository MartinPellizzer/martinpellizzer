Circaea caulescens f. rosulata

Circaea caulescens f. rosulata, commonly known as the roseleaf wood sorrel, is a variant of the wood sorrel family with distinctive botanical features.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which are borne on slender stalks above the foliage. Its leaves are usually lobed or divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is native to regions of Europe and is often found in woodland or moist, shaded environments.
