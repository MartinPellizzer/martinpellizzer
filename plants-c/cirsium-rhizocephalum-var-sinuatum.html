Cirsium rhizocephalum var. sinuatum

Cirsium rhizocephalum var. sinuatum, commonly known as the Sinuous Thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, spiny stems and is characterized by its deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of many small florets arranged in a spherical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
