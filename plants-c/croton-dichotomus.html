Croton dichotomus

Croton dichotomus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often green with red or yellow veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
