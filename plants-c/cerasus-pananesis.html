Cerasus pananesis

Cerasus pananesis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamabuki, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Cerasus pananesis is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and cultural significance.
