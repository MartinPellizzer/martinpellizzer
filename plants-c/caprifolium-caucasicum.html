Caprifolium caucasicum

Caprifolium caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, often fragrant, simple leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in autumn, attracting various bird species.

Caprifolium caucasicum is often found in woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade and moist soils.
