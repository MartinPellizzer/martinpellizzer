Catasetum × altaflorestense

Catasetum × altaflorestense, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a hybrid species native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and specialized pollination mechanism involving wasps.

Catasetum × altaflorestense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
