Chrysanthemum fuscatum

Chrysanthemum fuscatum, commonly known as the gray chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost grayish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include rough, hairy leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads and diverse species.
