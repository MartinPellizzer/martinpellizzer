Capraria diffusa

Capraria diffusa, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Capraria diffusa is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal environments.
