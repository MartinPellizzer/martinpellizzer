Campanula persicifolia var. macrantha

Campanula persicifolia var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of large, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to landscapes.
