Cryptocarya oligocarpa

Cryptocarya oligocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal scrub habitats.

Cryptocarya oligocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
