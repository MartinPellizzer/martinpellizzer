Capsicum macrophyllum

Capsicum macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant capsicum or large-leaf chili, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and elongated, often vibrant red or yellow fruits. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruits are commonly used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor and nutritional value.
