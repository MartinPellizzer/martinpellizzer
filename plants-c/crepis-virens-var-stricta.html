Crepis virens var. stricta

Crepis virens var. stricta, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often arranged in a rosette. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This variety is distinguished by its more upright growth habit compared to other Crepis species.
