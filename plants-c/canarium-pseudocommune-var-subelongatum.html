Canarium pseudocommune var. subelongatum

Canarium pseudocommune var. subelongatum, commonly known as the long-fruited candlenut, is a variant of the candlenut tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovoid fruits that are typically yellow to brown in color when mature. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers clustered in panicles. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its oil-rich nuts, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.
