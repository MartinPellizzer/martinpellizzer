Calystegia sepium var. pubescens

Calystegia sepium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
