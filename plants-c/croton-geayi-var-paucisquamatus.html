Croton geayi var. paucisquamatus

Croton geayi var. paucisquamatus, commonly known as the sparse-scaly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often showy flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive, rough texture and sparse, short hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces capsule-like fruits.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
