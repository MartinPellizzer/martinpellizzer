Cotoneaster declinatus

Cotoneaster declinatus, commonly known as the hanging cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. The plant bears red or orange berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its adaptability to rocky and well-drained soils makes it a hardy plant suitable for various garden conditions.

Cotoneaster declinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural landscapes.
