Cytisus biflorus var. collinus

Cytisus biflorus var. collinus, commonly known as the purple flowering broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and clusters of small, bright purple flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
