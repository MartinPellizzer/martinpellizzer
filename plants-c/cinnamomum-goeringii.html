Cinnamomum goeringii

Cinnamomum goeringii, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or Kuzukami, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin and emit a strong, spicy fragrance.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though it is less common than cinnamon bark.
