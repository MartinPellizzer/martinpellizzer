Commiphora caerulea

Commiphora caerulea, commonly known as blue myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray leaves and produces a fragrant resin that has been used traditionally in incense and medicine. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with white or pale blue petals, and it is pollinated by insects.

The resin of Commiphora caerulea has a unique scent and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
