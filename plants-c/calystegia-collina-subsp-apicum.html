Calystegia collina subsp. apicum

Calystegia collina subsp.

apicum, commonly known as the alpine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a herbaceous vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom in the early morning.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
