Crataegus apiomorpha var. cyanophylla

Crataegus apiomorpha var. cyanophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is typically a shrub or small tree, growing to a height of about 3 to 6 meters.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
