Coussapoa araneosa

Coussapoa araneosa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance, with long, thin petals that resemble webbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered together in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Coussapoa araneosa is primarily grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique and intricate floral structure.
