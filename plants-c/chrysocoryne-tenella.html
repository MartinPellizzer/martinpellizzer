Chrysocoryne tenella

Chrysocoryne tenella, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a small herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
