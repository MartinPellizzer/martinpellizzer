Cacalia terminalis

Cacalia terminalis, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Cacalia terminalis is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
