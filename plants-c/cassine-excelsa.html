Cassine excelsa

Cassine excelsa, commonly known as the giant soursop or giant custard apple, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers. Its fruit, which can weigh up to 20 kilograms, has a thick, spiny rind and a sweet, creamy pulp.

Cassine excelsa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
