Calceolaria amplexicaulis

Calceolaria amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like calyx that encloses the flower, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often clasping, wrapping around the stem, which is a key identifying feature.

Calceolaria amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, tubular flowers.
