Ceropegia flavopurpurea

Ceropegia flavopurpurea, commonly known as the Purple-veined Ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, purple-veined flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
