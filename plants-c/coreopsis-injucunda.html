Coreopsis injucunda

Coreopsis injucunda, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and can reach heights of up to 2 feet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Coreopsis injucunda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
