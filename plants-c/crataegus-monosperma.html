Crataegus monosperma

Crataegus monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Crataegus monosperma is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
