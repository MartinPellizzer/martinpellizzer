Commelina montana

Commelina montana, commonly known as the mountain commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes plants known for their striking floral structures. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, blue or purple flowers surrounded by white bracts, which help attract pollinators.

Commelina montana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
