Cipura paludosa subsp. boliviensis

Cipura paludosa subsp.

boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian swamp cipura, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America. It thrives in marshy and boggy environments, often found in regions with high humidity and poor drainage.

The plant features elongated, grass-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a subtle fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
