Cyperus dichromeniformis

Cyperus dichromeniformis, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are often reddish or purplish in color.

Cyperus dichromeniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
