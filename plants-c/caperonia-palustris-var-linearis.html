Caperonia palustris var. linearis

Caperonia palustris var. linearis, commonly known as the slender marsh cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
