Carludovica marceana

Carludovica marceana, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Carludovica marceana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
