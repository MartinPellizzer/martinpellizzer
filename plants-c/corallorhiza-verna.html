Corallorhiza verna

Corallorhiza verna, commonly known as the early green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is a non-photosynthetic plant that relies entirely on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption, lacking chlorophyll and leaves. The plant produces a single, slender stem with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, which supply the plant with essential nutrients.

This orchid typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves of many trees have fully emerged.
