Colchicum eucomoides

Colchicum eucomoides, commonly known as the meadow colchicum, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with prominent yellow stamens, blooming in late summer. Its bulbs contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
