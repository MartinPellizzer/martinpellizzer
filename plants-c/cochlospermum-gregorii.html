Cochlospermum gregorii

Cochlospermum gregorii, commonly known as the Gregorii's glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dry seeds. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a rough or hairy texture.

Cochlospermum gregorii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or disturbed habitats.
