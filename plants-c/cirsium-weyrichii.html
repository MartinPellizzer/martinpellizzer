Cirsium weyrichii

Cirsium weyrichii, commonly known as Weyrich's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Cirsium weyrichii is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with a robust root system adapted to its harsh environment.
