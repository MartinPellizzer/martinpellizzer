Centaurea virgata

Centaurea virgata, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Centaurea virgata is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
