Crantzia congesta

Crantzia congesta, commonly known as the clustered crantzia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Crantzia congesta is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
