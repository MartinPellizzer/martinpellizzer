Cyperus tenerrimus

Cyperus tenerrimus, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Cyperus tenerrimus is often used in ornamental gardening for its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its attractive, upright growth habit.
