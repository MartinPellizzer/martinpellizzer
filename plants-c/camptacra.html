Camptacra

Camptacra, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance during the spring. Camptacra is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.
