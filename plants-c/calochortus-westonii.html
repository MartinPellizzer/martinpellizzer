Calochortus westonii

Calochortus westonii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley mariposa lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to California.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and sometimes purplish hues. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and meadows, often in areas with well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while the stem supports the flowering spike.

Calochortus westonii is a short-lived perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its native environment.
