Chaetium festucoides

Chaetium festucoides, commonly known as slender tuftgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its fine texture.

Botanically, it is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive floral structure.
