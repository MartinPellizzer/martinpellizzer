Cucumis abyssinicus

Cucumis abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian cucumber, is a rare and ancient species native to Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, elongated fruits. The plant produces small, cucurbit-like fruits that are typically harvested when immature for consumption. Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
