Celtis adolfi-friderici

Celtis adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the European hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, smooth fruits that are typically green when mature.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy and adaptable species in temperate climates.
