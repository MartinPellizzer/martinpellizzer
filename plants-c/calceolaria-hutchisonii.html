Calceolaria hutchisonii

Calceolaria hutchisonii, commonly known as the Chilean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the temperate and sometimes arid climates of its native habitat. It produces small, lance-shaped leaves and blooms during the spring and summer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
