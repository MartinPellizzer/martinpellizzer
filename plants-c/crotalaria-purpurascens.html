Crotalaria purpurascens

Crotalaria purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria purpurascens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
