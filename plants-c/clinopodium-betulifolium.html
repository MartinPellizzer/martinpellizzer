Clinopodium betulifolium

Clinopodium betulifolium, commonly known as buckthorn mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty fragrance when crushed.

The flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
