Carex lyngbyei var. variegata

Carex lyngbyei var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated lyngby's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are often variegated, featuring white or yellow markings that distinguish it from other Carex species.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
