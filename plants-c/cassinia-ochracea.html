Cassinia ochracea

Cassinia ochracea, commonly known as the golden cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom from spring to autumn.

Cassinia ochracea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
