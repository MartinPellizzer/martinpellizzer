Chromolaena pungens

Chromolaena pungens, commonly known as the hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiky fruits. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, slightly toxic sap.

Chromolaena pungens is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
