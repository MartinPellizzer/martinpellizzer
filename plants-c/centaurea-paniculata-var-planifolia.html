Centaurea paniculata var. planifolia

Centaurea paniculata var. planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged in a rosette pattern, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
