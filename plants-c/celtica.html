Celtica

Celtica, commonly known as the Celtic fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that unfurl in a distinctive spiral pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Celtica has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique appearance and historical significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
