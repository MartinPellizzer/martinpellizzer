Cynometra sessiliflora

Cynometra sessiliflora, commonly known as the African teak or African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, elliptical seeds.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and it also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
