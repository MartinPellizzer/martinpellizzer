Clematopsis grandifolia

Clematopsis grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered clematopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Berberidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often woody and somewhat thorny. Native to certain regions of Asia, it thrives in shady, moist environments.
