Campanula medium f. caesia

Campanula medium f. caesia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging from blue to violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
