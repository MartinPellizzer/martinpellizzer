Carex sphaerocarpa

Carex sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and distinctive, spherical spikelets that give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex sphaerocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
