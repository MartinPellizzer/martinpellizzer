Cassia cotinifolia var. percoriacea

Cassia cotinifolia var. percoriacea, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
