Colocasia esculenta f. ebiimo

Colocasia esculenta f. ebiimo, commonly known as the African elephant ear, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is cultivated for its ornamental value and edible tubers. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a velvety texture, and they can grow up to 30 inches in length. The tubers, which are starchy and edible, are a staple food in some African communities.

This variety is well-suited for humid climates and thrives in rich, well-draining soil.
