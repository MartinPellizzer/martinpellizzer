Carex dolichostachya f. imbecillis

Carex dolichostachya f. imbecillis, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a variant of the slender spike sedge, characterized by its reduced inflorescence.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike-like structure. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and the ability to form dense colonies.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological value and adaptability.
