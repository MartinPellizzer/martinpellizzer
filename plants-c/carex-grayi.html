Carex grayi

Carex grayi, commonly known as Gray's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences, typically bearing small, three-lobed flowers. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
