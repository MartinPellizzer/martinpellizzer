Cenchrus echinatus var. morisonii

Cenchrus echinatus var. morisonii, commonly known as the Morison's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil and low rainfall environments.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its erosion control capabilities and ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
