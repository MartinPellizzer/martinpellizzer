Carex chordorrhiza var. sphagnicola

Carex chordorrhiza var. sphagnicola, commonly known as the sphagnicolous sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Carex.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This plant is often found in wetland habitats, particularly in association with peat moss (sphagnum). Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it thrive in moist, acidic soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
