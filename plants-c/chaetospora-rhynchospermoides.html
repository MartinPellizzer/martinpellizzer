Chaetospora rhynchospermoides

Chaetospora rhynchospermoides, commonly known as the spiky bladdergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in fleshy, bladder-like structures called pseudocarps. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
