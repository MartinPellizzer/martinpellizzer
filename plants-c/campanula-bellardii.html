Campanula bellardii

Campanula bellardii, commonly known as the bellflower of Bellard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula bellardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
