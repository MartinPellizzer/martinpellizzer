Corpuscularia lehmannii

Corpuscularia lehmannii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces striking blue or violet flowers. The plant has small, linear leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
