Coelogyne flexuosa

Coelogyne flexuosa, commonly known as the wavy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers, which can be white, yellow, or orange, are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pleasant aroma.

Coelogyne flexuosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
