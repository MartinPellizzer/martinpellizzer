Cousinia yasujensis

Cousinia yasujensis, commonly known as Yasuj's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Cousinia yasujensis is considered a rare and endemic species.
