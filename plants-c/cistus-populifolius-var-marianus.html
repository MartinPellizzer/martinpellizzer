Cistus populifolius var. marianus

Cistus populifolius var. marianus, commonly known as the Marianus rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid, and sunny habitats. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to conserve water in its native environment.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in Mediterranean ecosystems.
