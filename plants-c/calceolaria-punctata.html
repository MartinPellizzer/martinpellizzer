Calceolaria punctata

Calceolaria punctata, commonly known as the spotted calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, elongated seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature. Calceolaria punctata thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners and botanists alike.
