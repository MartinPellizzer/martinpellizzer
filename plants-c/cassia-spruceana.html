Cassia spruceana

Cassia spruceana, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a bright green color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Cassia spruceana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
