Caesalpinia glabrata

Caesalpinia glabrata, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering.

Caesalpinia glabrata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses.
