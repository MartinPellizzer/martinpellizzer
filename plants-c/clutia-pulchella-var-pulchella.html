Clutia pulchella var. pulchella

Clutia pulchella var. pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Clutia pulchella var. pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
