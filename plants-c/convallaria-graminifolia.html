Convallaria graminifolia

Convallaria graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy lily of the valley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous structure. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.
