Conophytum viridicatum var. pisinnum

Conophytum viridicatum var. pisinnum, commonly known as the "Corky" or "Corky" conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
