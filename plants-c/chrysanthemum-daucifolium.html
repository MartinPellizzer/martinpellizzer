Chrysanthemum daucifolium

Chrysanthemum daucifolium, commonly known as the sand chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flower heads. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a compact growth form.

Its botanical features make it a hardy ornamental and a potential candidate for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
