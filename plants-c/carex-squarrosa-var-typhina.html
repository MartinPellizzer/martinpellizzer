Carex squarrosa var. typhina

Carex squarrosa var. typhina, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually gray-green in color and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The tufted sedge is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
