Comparettia sotoana

Comparettia sotoana, commonly known as the Soto's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it produces a unique, elongated fruit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
