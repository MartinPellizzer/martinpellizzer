Corynephorus deschampsioides

Corynephorus deschampsioides, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens. The plant produces small, pale green spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Corynephorus deschampsioides is drought-tolerant and low-maintenance, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.
