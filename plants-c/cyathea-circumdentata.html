Cyathea circumdentata

Cyathea circumdentata, commonly known as the toothed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are typically bright green and have a distinctive toothed margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
