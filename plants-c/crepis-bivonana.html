Crepis bivonana

Crepis bivonana, commonly known as the Bivona mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. It features a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, spoon-shaped leaves.
