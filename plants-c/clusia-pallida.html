Clusia pallida

Clusia pallida, commonly known as the white glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically light green to yellowish in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Clusia pallida is adapted to grow in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
