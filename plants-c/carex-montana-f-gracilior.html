Carex montana f. gracilior

Carex montana f. gracilior, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a variant of the common marsh sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
