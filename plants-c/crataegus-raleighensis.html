Crataegus raleighensis

Crataegus raleighensis, commonly known as Raleigh hawthorn, is a species of hawthorn native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and produces white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region.
