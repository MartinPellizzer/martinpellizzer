Carex illustranda

Carex illustranda, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
