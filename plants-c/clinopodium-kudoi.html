Clinopodium kudoi

Clinopodium kudoi, commonly known as Korean oregano, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Clinopodium kudoi is also valued in culinary applications for its distinctive, mint-like fragrance.
