Campanula rotundifolia var. dubia

Campanula rotundifolia var. dubia, commonly known as the "dubious bellflower," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, hairy seeds in its capsule fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
