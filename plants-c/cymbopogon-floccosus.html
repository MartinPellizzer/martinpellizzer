Cymbopogon floccosus

Cymbopogon floccosus, commonly known as the woolly grass or silver grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale flowers in clusters.

Cymbopogon floccosus is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
