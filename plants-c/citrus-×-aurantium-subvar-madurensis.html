Citrus × aurantium subvar. madurensis

Citrus × aurantium subvar. madurensis, commonly known as the Madurese kumquat, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit's skin is thin and edible, turning from green to orange as it ripens.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique culinary uses.
