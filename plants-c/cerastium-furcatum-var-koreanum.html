Cerastium furcatum var. koreanum

Cerastium furcatum var. koreanum, commonly known as the Korean mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Korea.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are hairy and often clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Caryophyllaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures.
