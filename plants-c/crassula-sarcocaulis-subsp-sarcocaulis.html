Crassula sarcocaulis subsp. sarcocaulis

Crassula sarcocaulis subsp.

sarcocaulis, commonly known as the stone rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves that form a rosette.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
