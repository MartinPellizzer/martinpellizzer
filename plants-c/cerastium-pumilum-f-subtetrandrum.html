Cerastium pumilum f. subtetrandrum

Cerastium pumilum f. subtetrandrum, commonly known as the dwarf mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and white flowers. The subspecies subtetrandrum is distinguished by its reduced number of stamens, giving it a unique floral morphology. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
