Campanula akuschensis

Campanula akuschensis, commonly known as the Akushan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms.

Campanula akuschensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
