Cyphia volubilis var. latipetala

Cyphia volubilis var. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petalled cyphia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
