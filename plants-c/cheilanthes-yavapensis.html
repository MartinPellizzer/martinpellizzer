Cheilanthes yavapensis

Cheilanthes yavapensis, commonly known as the Yavapai rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial, evergreen fern that typically grows on rocky outcrops and in arid environments. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth form with short, sturdy rhizomes and dense clusters of lanceolate, dark green leaves. Its foliage is often covered in a thin layer of white, waxy bloom, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

This species is well adapted to harsh conditions and is an important component of desert flora.
