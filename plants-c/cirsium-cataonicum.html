Cirsium cataonicum

Cirsium cataonicum, commonly known as the Cataonic thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cirsium cataonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
