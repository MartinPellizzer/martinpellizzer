Coryphantha bergeriana

Coryphantha bergeriana, commonly known as the Berger's hedgehog cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Coryphantha bergeriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
