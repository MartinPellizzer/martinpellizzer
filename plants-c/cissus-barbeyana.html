Cissus barbeyana

Cissus barbeyana, commonly known as the African dewberry, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are round, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Cissus barbeyana is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to climb trellises or walls.
