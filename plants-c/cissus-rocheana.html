Cissus rocheana

Cissus rocheana, commonly known as the rock fig or African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Cissus rocheana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
