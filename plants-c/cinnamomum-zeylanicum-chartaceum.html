Cinnamomum zeylanicum chartaceum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum chartaceum, commonly known as the paper cinnamon or cinnamon bark tree, is a species native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils rich in cinnamaldehyde. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lance-shaped leaves that are smooth on the upper surface and hairy underneath. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, followed by egg-shaped berries that turn red when mature.

The bark is often harvested for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
