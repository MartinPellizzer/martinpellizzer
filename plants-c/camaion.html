Camaion

Camaion, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Cyphomandra in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often mistaken for starfruit due to their shape. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
