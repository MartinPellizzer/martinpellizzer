Crepis nuda

Crepis nuda, commonly known as the naked thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairless and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellow, forming a cluster at the end of each stem.

Crepis nuda is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
