Campnosperma lepidotum

Campnosperma lepidotum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Campnosperma lepidotum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
