Cucumis melo var. keiseng

Cucumis melo var. keiseng, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruit with a thick, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic, yellow or orange flesh. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit develops from a single ovary after successful pollination.

This variety is prized for its flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
