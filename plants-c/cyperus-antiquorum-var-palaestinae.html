Cyperus antiquorum var. palaestinae

Cyperus antiquorum var. palaestinae, commonly known as the Palestine flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
