Cirsium × suspiciosum

Cirsium × suspiciosum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble thistle flowers. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It has a robust root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.

Due to its prickly foliage, it is often considered a weed but also serves as a food source for certain insects and wildlife.
