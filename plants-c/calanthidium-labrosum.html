Calanthidium labrosum

Calanthidium labrosum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Calanthidium labrosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
