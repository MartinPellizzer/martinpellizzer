Capsicum ligustrinum

Capsicum ligustrinum, commonly known as the wild pepper or Mexican wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are usually white or green.

Capsicum ligustrinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.
