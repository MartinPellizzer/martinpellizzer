Cyperus rotundus var. procerula

Cyperus rotundus var. procerula, commonly known as purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically develop in dense clusters. The plant thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, rice fields, and along waterways. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant's adaptability and reproductive strategies contribute to its widespread distribution and ecological significance.
