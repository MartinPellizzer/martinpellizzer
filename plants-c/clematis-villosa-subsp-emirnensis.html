Clematis villosa subsp. emirnensis

Clematis villosa subsp.

emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the Caucasus region. It features woody stems covered in soft, white pubescence, and its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges.

The plant produces fragrant, blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and showy blooms.
