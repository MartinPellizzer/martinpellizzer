Codiaeum variegatum f. appendiculatum

Codiaeum variegatum f. appendiculatum, commonly known as the flame leaf croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically large and glossy, adding a dramatic visual element to any garden or indoor space. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
