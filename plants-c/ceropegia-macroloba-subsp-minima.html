Ceropegia macroloba subsp. minima

Ceropegia macroloba subsp.

minima, commonly known as the miniature chain-of-hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This subspecies is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
