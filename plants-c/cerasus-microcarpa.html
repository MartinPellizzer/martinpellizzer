Cerasus microcarpa

Cerasus microcarpa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, sweet fruits and delicate pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Cerasus microcarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japanese tradition.
