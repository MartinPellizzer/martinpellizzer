Claytonia perfoliata var. exigua

Claytonia perfoliata var. exigua, commonly known as the dwarf spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in early season pollination.
