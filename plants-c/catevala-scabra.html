Catevala scabra

Catevala scabra, commonly known as the rough leaved cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Catevala scabra is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
