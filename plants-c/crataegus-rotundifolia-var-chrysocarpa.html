Crataegus rotundifolia var. chrysocarpa

Crataegus rotundifolia var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, often lobed leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
