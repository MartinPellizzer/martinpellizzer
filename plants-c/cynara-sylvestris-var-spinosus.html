Cynara sylvestris var. spinosus

Cynara sylvestris var. spinosus, commonly known as the Spanish needle or thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses. Its spines serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores, while its deep taproot allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

The plant is also valued for its potential in herbal remedies and as a source of natural fibers.
