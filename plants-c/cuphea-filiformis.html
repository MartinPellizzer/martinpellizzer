Cuphea filiformis

Cuphea filiformis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a branching stem, making it suitable for both ornamental and garden use. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as an upright shrub or trailing plant.

Cuphea filiformis is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
