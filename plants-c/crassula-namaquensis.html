Crassula namaquensis

Crassula namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua Crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the warmer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
