Calliandra nervosa

Calliandra nervosa, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
