Camellia euryoides

Camellia euryoides, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to Japan and Korea.

It is a flowering shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces showy, double or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Camellia euryoides prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
