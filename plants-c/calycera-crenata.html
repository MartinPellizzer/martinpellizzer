Calycera crenata

Calycera crenata, commonly known as the waxyleaf calycera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The leaves are usually waxy and have a distinctive crenate or scalloped edge, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
