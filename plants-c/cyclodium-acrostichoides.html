Cyclodium acrostichoides

Cyclodium acrostichoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf cyclodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer.

Cyclodium acrostichoides is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to wet or seasonally flooded environments.
