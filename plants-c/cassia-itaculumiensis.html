Cassia itaculumiensis

Cassia itaculumiensis, commonly known as the Itacolumi cassia, is a small shrub native to the Brazilian Cerrado region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to seasonal droughts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
