Calligonum lipskyi var. commutatum

Calligonum lipskyi var. commutatum, commonly known as the sand spurge, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It has small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots enable it to thrive in sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

This species is also known for its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent soil erosion in its native habitat.
