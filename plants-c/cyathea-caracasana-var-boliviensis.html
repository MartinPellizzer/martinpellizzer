Cyathea caracasana var. boliviensis

Cyathea caracasana var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, tropical rainforest environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its natural habitat.

The fronds are also used as thatching material in some regions.
