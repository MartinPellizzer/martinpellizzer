Crataegus × dipyrena

Crataegus × dipyrena, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

The fruit is an important food source for birds and mammals, contributing to its ecological significance.
