Callicarpa saccata

Callicarpa saccata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
