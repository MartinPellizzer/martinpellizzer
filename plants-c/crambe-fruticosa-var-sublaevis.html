Crambe fruticosa var. sublaevis

Crambe fruticosa var. sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are generally smooth and narrow, adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong winds. This variety is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in coastal environments.
