Cistus × bernardii

Cistus × bernardii, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which includes various species of evergreen shrubs characterized by their aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant produces tubular or bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow, typically blooming in spring and early summer. Its leaves are leathery and fragrant, often releasing a resinous scent when crushed.

Cistus × bernardii is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
