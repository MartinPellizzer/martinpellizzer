Calamagrostis schiedeana

Calamagrostis schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's reed, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often adorned with a soft, silky texture.

Calamagrostis schiedeana is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
