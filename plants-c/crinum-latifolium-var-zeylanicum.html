Crinum latifolium var. zeylanicum

Crinum latifolium var. zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including Sri Lanka, India, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, broad, strap-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
