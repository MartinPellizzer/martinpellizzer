Chamaemelum radiatum

Chamaemelum radiatum, commonly known as the field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant has aromatic leaves and stems, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinct scent. It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and roadsides.

Chamaemelum radiatum is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
