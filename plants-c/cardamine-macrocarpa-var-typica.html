Cardamine macrocarpa var. typica

Cardamine macrocarpa var. typica, commonly known as the broad-leaf watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its leaves are often used in salads, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
