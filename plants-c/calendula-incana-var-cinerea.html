Calendula incana var. cinerea

Calendula incana var. cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green to silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
