Canarium calophyllum

Canarium calophyllum, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
