Corylopsis kesakii

Corylopsis kesakii, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Corylopsis kesakii is often cultivated for its striking floral display and is relatively easy to care for.
