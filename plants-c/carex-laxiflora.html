Carex laxiflora

Carex laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets along the stem. Carex laxiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
