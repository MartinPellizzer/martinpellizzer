Coccinia cordifolia var. alceifolia

Coccinia cordifolia var. alceifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
