Crataegus texana

Crataegus texana, commonly known as the Texas hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and features thorny branches and dense, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often found in open woodlands, rocky slopes, and along streambanks.

The Texas hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
