Cordia jucunda

Cordia jucunda, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Cordia jucunda is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
