Carusia

Carusia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating scent.

Carusia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
