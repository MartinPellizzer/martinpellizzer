Cimicifuga cordifolia

Cimicifuga cordifolia, commonly known as American bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, white, lace-like flowers arranged in panicles. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are typically heart-shaped, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Cimicifuga cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
