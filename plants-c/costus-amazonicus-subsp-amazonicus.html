Costus amazonicus subsp. amazonicus

Costus amazonicus subsp.

amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian costus, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking, orange-red inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
