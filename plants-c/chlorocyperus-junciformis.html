Chlorocyperus junciformis

Chlorocyperus junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as sedges.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spikes of small, greenish flowers. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a useful plant for ecological restoration projects.
