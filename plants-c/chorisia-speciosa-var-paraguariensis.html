Chorisia speciosa var. paraguariensis

Chorisia speciosa var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan trumpet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bats. The plant has a woody, branching stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural propagation.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in tropical and subtropical regions.
