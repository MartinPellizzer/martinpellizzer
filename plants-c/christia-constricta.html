Christia constricta

Christia constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
