Clinopodium hohenackeri

Clinopodium hohenackeri, commonly known as Hohenacker's savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, clustered flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium hohenackeri is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
