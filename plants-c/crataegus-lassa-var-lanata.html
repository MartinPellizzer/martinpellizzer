Crataegus lassa var. lanata

Crataegus lassa var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
