Cardamine heptaphylla

Cardamine heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf watercress, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its unique leaf structure and ecological preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
