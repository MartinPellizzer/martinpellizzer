Campanula stefanoffii

Campanula stefanoffii, commonly known as the Stefanoff's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils. This species is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
