Corydalis persica

Corydalis persica, commonly known as Persian corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it often grows in rocky or alpine habitats. Corydalis persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its flowers are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
