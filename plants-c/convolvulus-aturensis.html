Convolvulus aturensis

Convolvulus aturensis, commonly known as the Turkish morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it adaptable to various garden settings.
