Clitoria javitensis

Clitoria javitensis, commonly known as the Java orchid vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant is a climbing vine that produces vibrant purple or pink blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation.

Clitoria javitensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
