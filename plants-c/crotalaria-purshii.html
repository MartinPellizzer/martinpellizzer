Crotalaria purshii

Crotalaria purshii, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also has medicinal properties.

Crotalaria purshii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
