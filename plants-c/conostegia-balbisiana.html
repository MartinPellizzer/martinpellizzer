Conostegia balbisiana

Conostegia balbisiana, commonly known as the Balbisian poinsettia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered bracts that resemble flowers, often mistaken for petals. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
