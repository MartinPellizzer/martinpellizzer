Crotalaria incana var. grandiflora

Crotalaria incana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent dark veins. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are notable for their showy, pea-like structure.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
