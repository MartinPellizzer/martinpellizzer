Cinnamomum fruticosum

Cinnamomum fruticosum, commonly known as the wild cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which can be used for propagation. Its bark and leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
