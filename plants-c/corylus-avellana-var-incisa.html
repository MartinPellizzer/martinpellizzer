Corylus avellana var. incisa

Corylus avellana var. incisa, commonly known as the European hazelnut or simply the hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and usually serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces catkins in early spring, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a cupule. The nuts are often used in cooking and baking, and the plant has historical significance in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates.
