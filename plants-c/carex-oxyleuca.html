Carex oxyleuca

Carex oxyleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in spike-like clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
