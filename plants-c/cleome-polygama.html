Cleome polygama

Cleome polygama, commonly known as the spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome polygama is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of growth in gardens.
