Cyperus immensus

Cyperus immensus, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a tall perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Cyperus immensus is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
