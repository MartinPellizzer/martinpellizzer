Calamus erinaceus subsp. daemonoropoides

Calamus erinaceus subsp.

daemonoropoides, commonly known as the spiny calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae) and is native to Southeast Asia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and elongated, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in small spines.

It produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is typically found in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
