Calophanes pulegium

Calophanes pulegium, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a strong, minty aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue and are arranged in dense spikes.

Calophanes pulegium is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
