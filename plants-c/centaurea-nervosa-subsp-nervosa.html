Centaurea nervosa subsp. nervosa

Centaurea nervosa subsp.

nervosa, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Centaurea nervosa subsp.

nervosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
