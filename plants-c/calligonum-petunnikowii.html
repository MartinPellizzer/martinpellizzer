Calligonum petunnikowii

Calligonum petunnikowii, commonly known as the Karakum sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for stabilization in desert and semi-arid regions.
