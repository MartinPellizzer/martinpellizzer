Calophyllum complanatum

Calophyllum complanatum, commonly known as the flat-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often flat and broad. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to reveal a single, hard-shelled seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
