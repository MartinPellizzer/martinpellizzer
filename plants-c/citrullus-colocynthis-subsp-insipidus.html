Citrullus colocynthis subsp. insipidus

Citrullus colocynthis subsp.

insipidus, commonly known as the bitter apple or wild cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is a trailing, herbaceous vine that produces pale green to yellowish fruits covered in spiny tubercles.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its fruits contain a toxic, bitter pulp and are not typically consumed by humans.

The species is characterized by its dioecious nature, with separate male and female plants.
