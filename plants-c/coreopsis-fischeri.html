Coreopsis fischeri

Coreopsis fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
