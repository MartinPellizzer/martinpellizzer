Caryota purpuracea

Caryota purpuracea, commonly known as the purple fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are bright green with a distinctive purple underside. Its inflorescences are compound and bear small, yellowish flowers, which eventually develop into orange-red fruits.

Caryota purpuracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
