Cynanchum louiseae

Cynanchum louiseae, commonly known as Louise's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its ecological importance to native pollinators.
