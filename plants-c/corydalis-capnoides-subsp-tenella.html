Corydalis capnoides subsp. tenella

Corydalis capnoides subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the slender corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It features delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, creeping stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological habitats.
