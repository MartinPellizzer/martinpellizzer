Chrysophyllum africanum

Chrysophyllum africanum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The fruit has a tough outer skin and a soft, sweet, custard-like pulp with a mild aroma. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
