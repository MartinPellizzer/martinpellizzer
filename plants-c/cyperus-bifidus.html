Cyperus bifidus

Cyperus bifidus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Cyperus bifidus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
