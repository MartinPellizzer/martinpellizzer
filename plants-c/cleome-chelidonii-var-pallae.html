Cleome chelidonii var. pallae

Cleome chelidonii var. pallae, commonly known as the pale cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Its flowers are often borne on branched inflorescences and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The pale cleome is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and easy cultivation requirements.
