Campanula carpatha

Campanula carpatha, commonly known as Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
