Coreopsis auriculata var. diversifolia

Coreopsis auriculata var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated tickseed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features daisy-like yellow flowers with distinctive orange centers and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for many climates.

Its compact growth habit and extended blooming period make it a popular choice for borders and containers.
