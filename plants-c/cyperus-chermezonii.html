Cyperus chermezonii

Cyperus chermezonii, commonly known as the flame grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, erect leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or brown, emerging from a spiky inflorescence. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cyperus chermezonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
