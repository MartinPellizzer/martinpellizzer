Cosmos subpubescens

Cosmos subpubescens, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from summer to fall. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slightly pubescent surface.

Cosmos subpubescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
