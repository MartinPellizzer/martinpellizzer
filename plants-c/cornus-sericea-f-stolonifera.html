Cornus sericea f. stolonifera

Cornus sericea f. stolonifera, commonly known as silky dogwood or silverleaf stolonifera, is a variant of the silky dogwood species.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features finely textured, silver-gray leaves that provide ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This form is notable for its stoloniferous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
