Caesalpinia bahamensis subsp. orientensis

Caesalpinia bahamensis subsp.

orientensis, commonly known as the Bahamian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas and parts of the Greater Antilles. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bright orange to red flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
