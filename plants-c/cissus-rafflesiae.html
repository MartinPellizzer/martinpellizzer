Cissus rafflesiae

Cissus rafflesiae, commonly known as the Raffles' devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus rafflesiae is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
