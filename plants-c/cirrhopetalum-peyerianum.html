Cirrhopetalum peyerianum

Cirrhopetalum peyerianum, commonly known as the Peyer's cirrhopetalum, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical Andes of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, elongated petals that resemble flowing hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Cirrhopetalum peyerianum is considered a conservation concern.
