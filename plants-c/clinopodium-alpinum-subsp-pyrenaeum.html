Clinopodium alpinum subsp. pyrenaeum

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

pyrenaeum, commonly known as alpine betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, blooming in late summer. Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

pyrenaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
