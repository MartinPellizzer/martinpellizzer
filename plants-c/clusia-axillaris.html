Clusia axillaris

Clusia axillaris, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in humid, shady environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Clusia axillaris is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is popular as a houseplant.
