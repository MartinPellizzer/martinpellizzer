Conyza deserticola

Conyza deserticola, commonly known as desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Conyza deserticola is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
