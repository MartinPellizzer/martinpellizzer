Carum erythrorhizum

Carum erythrorhizum, commonly known as red-root caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slender leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, brownish seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its root system is distinctive for its reddish color, which gives the plant its common name.
