Cladium rubiginosum var. subriparium

Cladium rubiginosum var. subriparium, commonly known as the red-stained sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland ecosystems in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like blades that are often reddish in color, especially at the base, and its ability to thrive in saturated soils. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize wetland areas effectively. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is adapted to periodic flooding and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
