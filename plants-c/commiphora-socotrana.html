Commiphora socotrana

Commiphora socotrana, commonly known as Socotra cinnamomum, is a unique plant native to the Socotra archipelago.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its habitat is limited to the arid and semi-arid regions of Socotra, where it has adapted to extreme environmental conditions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
