Cassia langsdorffii var. longipedicellata

Cassia langsdorffii var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the longpedicellate cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of this variety include its distinctive inflorescences and adaptability to various soil types.
