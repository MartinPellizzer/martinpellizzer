Chelone glabra f. rosea

Chelone glabra f. rosea, commonly known as the Roseate White Honeysuckle, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to rose-red in color, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
