Costus leucanthus

Costus leucanthus, commonly known as whiteflower costus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Costus leucanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
