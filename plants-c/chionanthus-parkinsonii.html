Chionanthus parkinsonii

Chionanthus parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, often with a faint fragrance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its growth habit is upright, with multiple branches forming a dense, rounded shape.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
