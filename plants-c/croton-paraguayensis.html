Croton paraguayensis

Croton paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and it produces capsule fruits containing seeds.

Croton paraguayensis is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
