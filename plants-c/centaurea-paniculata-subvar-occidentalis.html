Centaurea paniculata subvar. occidentalis

Centaurea paniculata subvar. occidentalis, commonly known as western panicled centaury, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
