Caralluma joannis

Caralluma joannis, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features fleshy, ribbed stems and produces small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in its native desert environment.

Caralluma joannis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
