Carex berteroana var. setifolia

Carex berteroana var. setifolia, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its wetland environment.
