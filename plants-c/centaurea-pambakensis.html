Centaurea pambakensis

Centaurea pambakensis, commonly known as the Pambakense knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea pambakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual contribution.
