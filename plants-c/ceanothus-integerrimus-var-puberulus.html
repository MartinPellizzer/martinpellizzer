Ceanothus integerrimus var. puberulus

Ceanothus integerrimus var. puberulus, commonly known as the softleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in coastal and inland regions. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are generally pubescent, giving it a soft, fuzzy texture.

This variety is often used in native plant landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
