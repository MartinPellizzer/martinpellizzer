Calamus kingianus

Calamus kingianus, commonly known as King's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Australia and is typically found in wetland or swampy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in moist areas.

Calamus kingianus is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
