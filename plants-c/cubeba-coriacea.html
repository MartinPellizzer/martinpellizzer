Cubeba coriacea

Cubeba coriacea, commonly known as cubeb, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its rough, bark-like stem and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, dark purple berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the berries are valued for their aromatic properties.

Cubeba coriacea is also cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and flavorings.
