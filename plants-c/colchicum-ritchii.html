Colchicum ritchii

Colchicum ritchii, commonly known as the Ritchie's colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a cluster of waxy, violet-blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often withering before flowering.

Colchicum ritchii is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
