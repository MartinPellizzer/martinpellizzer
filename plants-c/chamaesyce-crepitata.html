Chamaesyce crepitata

Chamaesyce crepitata, commonly known as the crack willow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
