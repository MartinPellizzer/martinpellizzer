Carduus hookerianus var. hesperius

Carduus hookerianus var. hesperius, commonly known as the Great Basin thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish-blue composite flower heads. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bristly, spherical seed heads.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
