Cavendishia micayensis

Cavendishia micayensis, commonly known as the Micayensis Cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage consists of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh climates.

Cavendishia micayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
