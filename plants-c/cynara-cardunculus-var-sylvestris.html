Cynara cardunculus var. sylvestris

Cynara cardunculus var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple flower heads. The plant produces edible inflorescences that are harvested before the flowers open. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it both a decorative plant and a potential source of edible vegetation.
