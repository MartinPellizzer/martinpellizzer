Croton mucronifolius

Croton mucronifolius, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often a deep green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
