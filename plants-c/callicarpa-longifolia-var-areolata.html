Callicarpa longifolia var. areolata

Callicarpa longifolia var. areolata, commonly known as the red-berried beauty or Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant purple to red berries in the fall. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a mild, sweet scent when crushed. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
