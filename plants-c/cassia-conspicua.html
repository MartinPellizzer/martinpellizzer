Cassia conspicua

Cassia conspicua, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. Cassia conspicua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
