Carex schedonautos

Carex schedonautos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences, which are typically brown or reddish in color.

Carex schedonautos plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
