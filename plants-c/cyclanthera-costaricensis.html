Cyclanthera costaricensis

Cyclanthera costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces elongated, pale green fruits with a warty surface and a bitter taste. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

Cyclanthera costaricensis is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
