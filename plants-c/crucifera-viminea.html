Crucifera viminea

Crucifera viminea, commonly known as the creeping wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or coastal areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
