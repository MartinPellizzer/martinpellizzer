Curcuma sylvatica

Curcuma sylvatica, commonly known as wild turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The rhizomes of Curcuma sylvatica are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
