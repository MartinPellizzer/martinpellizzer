Cosmos diversifolius var. deficiens

Cosmos diversifolius var. deficiens, commonly known as the Texas bluebonnet, is a species of flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and bipinnate leaves that are often divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces seeds in pods, which are dispersed by wind.

The Texas bluebonnet is the official state flower of Texas and is known for its resilience and beauty in the wild.
