Centaurea jacea var. lamyi

Centaurea jacea var. lamyi, commonly known as the Lamy's common knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy, adaptable species.
