Cynanchum argentinense

Cynanchum argentinense, commonly known as Argentine milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes many species of milkweeds. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a hairy texture.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is known for its ecological importance to certain pollinators.
