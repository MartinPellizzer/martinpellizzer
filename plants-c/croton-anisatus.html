Croton anisatus

Croton anisatus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Croton anisatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
