Canarium balsamiferum

Canarium balsamiferum, commonly known as the balsam apple or candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. The fruit's resinous pulp is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

It is also valued for its aromatic wood and oil, which have various industrial applications.
