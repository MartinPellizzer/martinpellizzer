Cornus hongkongensis subsp. gigantea

Cornus hongkongensis subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant cornus, is a striking plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
