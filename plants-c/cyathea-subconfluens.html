Cyathea subconfluens

Cyathea subconfluens, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's structure and enabling it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Cyathea subconfluens plays an important role in tropical ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
