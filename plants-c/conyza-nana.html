Conyza nana

Conyza nana, commonly known as dwarf ironweed, is a small annual herb native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are borne on slender peduncles. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza nana is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
