Crotalaria globulosa

Crotalaria globulosa, commonly known as the globe flower or African leadplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive globular seed pods. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, showy flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces spherical, spiky seed pods that split open when mature.

Crotalaria globulosa is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
