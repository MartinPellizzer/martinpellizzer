Chlorophora tinctoria f. miqueliana

Chlorophora tinctoria f. miqueliana, commonly known as the Miquel's dye wood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a yellow dye from its wood, which has been historically used in traditional crafts. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated for conservation and ornamental purposes.
