Combretum psidioides subsp. grandifolium

Combretum psidioides subsp.

grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leafed flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in summer, often attracting birds and insects.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
