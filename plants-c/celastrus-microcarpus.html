Celastrus microcarpus

Celastrus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited staff vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant's fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Celastrus microcarpus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
