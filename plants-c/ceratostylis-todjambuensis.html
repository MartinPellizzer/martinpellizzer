Ceratostylis todjambuensis

Ceratostylis todjambuensis, commonly known as the Tadjambean horned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive, horn-like spur. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a fragrant scent.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
