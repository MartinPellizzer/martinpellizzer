Calamagrostis × gracilescens

Calamagrostis × gracilescens, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and graceful, arching growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden and pond edge landscaping.
