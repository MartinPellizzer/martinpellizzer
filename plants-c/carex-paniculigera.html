Carex paniculigera

Carex paniculigera, commonly known as the panicled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a tall, branched spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

Carex paniculigera plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
