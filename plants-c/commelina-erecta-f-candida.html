Commelina erecta f. candida

Commelina erecta f. candida, commonly known as the white-veined spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with white petals and prominent yellow veins, which are typical of the genus Commelina. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The cultivar 'candida' is distinguished by its pale, almost white floral coloration, making it a popular ornamental plant.
