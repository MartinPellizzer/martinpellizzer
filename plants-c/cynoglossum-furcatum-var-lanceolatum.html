Cynoglossum furcatum var. lanceolatum

Cynoglossum furcatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hound's tongue, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped leaves that are lanceolate in this variety, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners.
