Crepis adenothrix

Crepis adenothrix, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it a common sight in ecological restoration projects.
