Crucianella fibrosa

Crucianella fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous crucianella, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, fibrous bark and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
