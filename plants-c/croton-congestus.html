Croton congestus

Croton congestus, commonly known as the clustered croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are palmate, with multiple lobes that give it a distinctive appearance.

Croton congestus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its bold foliage.
