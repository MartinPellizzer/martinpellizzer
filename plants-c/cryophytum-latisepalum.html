Cryophytum latisepalum

Cryophytum latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal cryophyte, is a unique alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and specialized adaptations to cold, harsh environments. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and often white or pale pink, with broad, fleshy sepals that provide structural support.

Cryophytum latisepalum is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
