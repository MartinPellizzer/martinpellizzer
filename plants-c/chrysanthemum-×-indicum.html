Chrysanthemum × indicum

Chrysanthemum × indicum, commonly known as the garden chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower forms, including pompon, anemone, and spider types. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of 30 to 150 centimeters. Its leaves are usually alternate, with lobed or serrated edges, and its flowers come in a wide array of colors.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
