Croton alceicornu

Croton alceicornu, commonly known as the horned croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, horn-like bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which are followed by distinctive orange or red fruits. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and vary in shape from ovate to lanceolate.

Croton alceicornu is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unusual inflorescences.
