Crotalaria cataractarum

Crotalaria cataractarum, commonly known as the water spinach or swamp cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Crotalaria cataractarum is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
