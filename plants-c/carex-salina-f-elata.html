Carex salina f. elata

Carex salina f. elata, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a variant of the salt marsh sedge native to coastal areas.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in wetlands and tidal zones. Its leaves are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a bluish-green hue that helps it tolerate high salt concentrations.

Carex salina f. elata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
