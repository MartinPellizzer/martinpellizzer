Cookia anisum-olens

Cookia anisum-olens, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit, which is typically a bell-shaped pepper, varies in color from green to red, yellow, or orange depending on maturity.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and is widely cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
