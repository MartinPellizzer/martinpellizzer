Crataegus crus-galli var. danielsii

Crataegus crus-galli var. danielsii, commonly known as the Daniel's hawthorn, is a rare variant of the hawthorn genus native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply pointed leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife species. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats.
