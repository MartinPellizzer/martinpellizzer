Cynanchum hoedimeerium subsp. timorense

Cynanchum hoedimeerium subsp.

timorense, commonly known as Timor milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the island of Timor. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
