Cylista polystachya

Cylista polystachya, commonly known as the common cylista, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Cylista polystachya thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
