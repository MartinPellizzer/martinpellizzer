Chabraea laciniata

Chabraea laciniata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

Chabraea laciniata is valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ability to purify air.
