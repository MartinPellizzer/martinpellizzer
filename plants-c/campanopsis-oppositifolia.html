Campanopsis oppositifolia

Campanopsis oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has opposite leaves that are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Campanopsis oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
