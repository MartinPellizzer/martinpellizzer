Centaurea reichenbachii

Centaurea reichenbachii, commonly known as the Reichenbach's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, spiky bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

Centaurea reichenbachii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
