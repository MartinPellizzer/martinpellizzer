Cynanchum daltonii

Cynanchum daltonii, commonly known as Dalton's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive hooded structure that attracts pollinators like butterflies and bees.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
