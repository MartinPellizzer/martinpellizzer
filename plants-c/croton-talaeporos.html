Croton talaeporos

Croton talaeporos, commonly known as the golden croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that often display shades of yellow, green, and red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
