Conyza tuberosa

Conyza tuberosa, commonly known as the hairy weed or false dandelion, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a taproot system and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, which help in reducing water loss. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Conyza tuberosa is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its invasive potential in some areas.
