Chloris distichophylla var. argentina

Chloris distichophylla var. argentina, commonly known as Argentine grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers.
