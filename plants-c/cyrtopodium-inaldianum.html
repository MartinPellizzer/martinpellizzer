Cyrtopodium inaldianum

Cyrtopodium inaldianum, commonly known as the Inaldia orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually pale yellow to greenish-white in color.

Cyrtopodium inaldianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
