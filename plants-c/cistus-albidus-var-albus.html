Cistus albidus var. albus

Cistus albidus var. albus, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and clusters of white, pink, or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
