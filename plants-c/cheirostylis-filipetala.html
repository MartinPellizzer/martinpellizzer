Cheirostylis filipetala

Cheirostylis filipetala, commonly known as the Philippine spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, slender flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, preferring well-drained soil. It produces showy, greenish-yellow to white flowers with a distinct, intricate structure.

Cheirostylis filipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
