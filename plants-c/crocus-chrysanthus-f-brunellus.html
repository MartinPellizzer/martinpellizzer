Crocus chrysanthus f. brunellus

Crocus chrysanthus f. brunellus, commonly known as the Brunell's crocus, is a botanical variant of the common crocus.

It is characterized by its unique, deep purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a corm as its underground storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, early-blooming flowers.
