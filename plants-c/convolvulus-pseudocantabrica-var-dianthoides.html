Convolvulus pseudocantabrica var. dianthoides

Convolvulus pseudocantabrica var. dianthoides, commonly known as the Spanish bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
