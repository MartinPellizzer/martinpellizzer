Centaurea jordaniana

Centaurea jordaniana, commonly known as Jordanian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Centaurea jordaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
