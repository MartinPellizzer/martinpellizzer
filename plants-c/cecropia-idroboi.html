Cecropia idroboi

Cecropia idroboi, commonly known as the Idrobo's ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences that attract various pollinators. The plant has a robust, straight trunk with a smooth, grayish bark that often develops fissures with age. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for numerous species in its native environment.

Cecropia idroboi is also valued for its durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
