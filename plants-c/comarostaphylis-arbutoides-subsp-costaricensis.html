Comarostaphylis arbutoides subsp. costaricensis

Comarostaphylis arbutoides subsp.

costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican strawberry tree, is a species native to Central America. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically red when ripe, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of ecological study.
