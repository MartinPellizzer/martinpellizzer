Croton rhamnifolius var. salviifolius

Croton rhamnifolius var. salviifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
