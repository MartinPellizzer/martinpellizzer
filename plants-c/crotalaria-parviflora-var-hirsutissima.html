Crotalaria parviflora var. hirsutissima

Crotalaria parviflora var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
