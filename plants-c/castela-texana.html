Castela texana

Castela texana, commonly known as Texas castela, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

Castela texana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
