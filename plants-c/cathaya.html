Cathaya

Cathaya, commonly known as the silver fir, is a genus of coniferous trees native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure, typical of gymnosperms. The genus includes several species, with Cathaya yahei being the most well-known. These trees are often found in mountainous regions and are valued for their ornamental and ecological significance.

Cathaya species are also important in the study of ancient plant lineages due to their evolutionary history.
