Cnicus tanakae

Cnicus tanakae, commonly known as Tanaka's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

Cnicus tanakae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
