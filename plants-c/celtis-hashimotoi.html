Celtis hashimotoi

Celtis hashimotoi, commonly known as the Japanese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, green berries. These berries turn red when mature and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Celtis hashimotoi is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.
