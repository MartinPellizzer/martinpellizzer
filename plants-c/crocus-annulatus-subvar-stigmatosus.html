Crocus annulatus subvar. stigmatosus

Crocus annulatus subvar. stigmatosus, commonly known as the Stigmatose Crocus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale yellow flowers with a prominent, dark brown stigma that protrudes prominently from the center. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

The unique floral morphology makes it a notable species in the genus Crocus.
