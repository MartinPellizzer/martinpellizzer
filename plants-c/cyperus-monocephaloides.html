Cyperus monocephaloides

Cyperus monocephaloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus monocephaloides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
