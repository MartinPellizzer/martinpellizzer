Cephalostachyum capitatum

Cephalostachyum capitatum, commonly known as the clustered spike grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces spikelets that are clustered at the tips of the stems, giving it its common name. It is native to regions in Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Cephalostachyum capitatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
