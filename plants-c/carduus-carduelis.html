Carduus carduelis

Carduus carduelis, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The common thistle is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
