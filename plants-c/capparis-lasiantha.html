Capparis lasiantha

Capparis lasiantha, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Capparis lasiantha is notable for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in its natural habitat.
