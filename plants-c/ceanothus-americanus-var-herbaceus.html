Ceanothus americanus var. herbaceus

Ceanothus americanus var. herbaceus, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as chaparral and coastal sage scrub. The plant features small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive blue-green color with a silvery underside.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
