Cabomba piauhiensis

Cabomba piauhiensis, commonly known as the Piauhi watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
