Cytisus nigricans var. australis

Cytisus nigricans var. australis, commonly known as the Australian broom, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
