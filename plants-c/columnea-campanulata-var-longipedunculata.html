Columnea campanulata var. longipedunculata

Columnea campanulata var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its elongated peduncles support clusters of flowers, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
