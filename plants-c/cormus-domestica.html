Cormus domestica

Cormus domestica, commonly known as the common bulbous plant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a corm, which is a modified stem that serves as a storage organ for nutrients. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Cormus domestica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
