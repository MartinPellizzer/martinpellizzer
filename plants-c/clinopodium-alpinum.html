Clinopodium alpinum

Clinopodium alpinum, commonly known as alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a mint-like aroma, while its small blue or purple flowers bloom in summer.

Clinopodium alpinum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
