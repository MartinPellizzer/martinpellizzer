Cyathea praecincta

Cyathea praecincta, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with finely divided segments that give it a delicate, lacy appearance.

Cyathea praecincta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
