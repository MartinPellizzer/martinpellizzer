Citronella moorei

Citronella moorei, commonly known as lemon grass, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by light brown, cylindrical fruits. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are widely used for their insect-repelling properties.

Citronella moorei thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
