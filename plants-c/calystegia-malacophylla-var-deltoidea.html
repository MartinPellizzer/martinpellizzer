Calystegia malacophylla var. deltoidea

Calystegia malacophylla var. deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive waxy coating. The flowers are typically pale blue or purple, with five petals that open in the morning. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

This plant thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
