Cleisostoma simondii

Cleisostoma simondii, commonly known as the white orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, showy flowers. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cleisostoma simondii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
