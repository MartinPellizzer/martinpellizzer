Cuphea thymoides

Cuphea thymoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Cuphea thymoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
