Carex lhotzkiana

Carex lhotzkiana, commonly known as the Lhotzkyan sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia, including parts of Siberia and the Himalayas. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that form dense clumps.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets at the end of each stem.
