Calochortus Clavatus Subsp. Gracilis

Calochortus clavatus subsp. gracilis is a delicate and rare flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the genus Calochortus, known for its showy, showy blooms and unique ecological adaptations. This subspecies is characterized by its slender stems and pale yellow to orange petals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral environments. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
