Campanula spruneriana

Campanula spruneriana, commonly known as the spruner's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula spruneriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
