Campylocentrum grisebachii

Campylocentrum grisebachii, commonly known as the purple-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Campylocentrum grisebachii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
