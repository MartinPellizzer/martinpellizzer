Cracca villosa var. incana

Cracca villosa var. incana, commonly known as the hairy cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
