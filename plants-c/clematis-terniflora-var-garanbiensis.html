Clematis terniflora var. garanbiensis

Clematis terniflora var. garanbiensis, commonly known as the garanbiensis clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a vigorous climber with compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a climbing plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rutaceae family.
