Calodracon heliconiifolia

Calodracon heliconiifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its rapid growth and dramatic floral display, it is often used in landscaping to add visual interest and color.
