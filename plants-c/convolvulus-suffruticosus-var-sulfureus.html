Convolvulus suffruticosus var. sulfureus

Convolvulus suffruticosus var. sulfureus, commonly known as the sulphur vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing herb with hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are small, tubular, and typically yellow or orange, with five lobes that open to reveal stamens and a pistil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is known for its bright, showy blooms and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental.
