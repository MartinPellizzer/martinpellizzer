Cachrys eriantha

Cachrys eriantha, commonly known as the woolly everlet, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
