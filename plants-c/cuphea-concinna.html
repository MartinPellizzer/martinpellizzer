Cuphea concinna

Cuphea concinna, commonly known as the "whorled cuphea," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual with striking, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea concinna is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
