Conchium mucronatum

Conchium mucronatum, commonly known as the cushion plant, is a low-growing succulent native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy rosettes of leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Conchium mucronatum is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
