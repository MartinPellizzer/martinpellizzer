Callicarpa mollis f. albifructa

Callicarpa mollis f. albifructa, commonly known as the white-fruited beautyberry, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by showy, pale pink to white berries. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant, slightly aromatic scent.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, particularly for its unique fruit color and compact growth habit.
