Crataegus sheridanii

Crataegus sheridanii, commonly known as Sheridan's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems, oval to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
