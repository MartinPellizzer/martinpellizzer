Centaurea parlatoris subsp. tenorei

Centaurea parlatoris subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Tuscan star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, glandular stems and striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
