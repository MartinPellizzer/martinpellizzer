Chamaecrista obcordata

Chamaecrista obcordata, commonly known as the obcordate burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by oblong, woody pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chamaecrista obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
