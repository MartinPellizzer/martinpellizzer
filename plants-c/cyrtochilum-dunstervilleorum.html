Cyrtochilum dunstervilleorum

Cyrtochilum dunstervilleorum, commonly known as the Dunsterville's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, elongated green leaves and a single, fragrant flower that typically blooms in the summer. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Cyrtochilum dunstervilleorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
