Celastrus andongensis

Celastrus andongensis, commonly known as the Andong bindweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Celastrus andongensis is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
