Celastrus semiarillatus

Celastrus semiarillatus, commonly known as the Chinese staff vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
