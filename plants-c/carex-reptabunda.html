Carex reptabunda

Carex reptabunda, commonly known as the creeping sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 5,000 species of grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and form dense, low-growing clumps. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which appear in late summer.

Carex reptabunda plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
