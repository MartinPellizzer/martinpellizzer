Caesalpinia enneaphylla

Caesalpinia enneaphylla, commonly known as the nine-leaf ticktree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with nine leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods. It is adapted to dry environments and has a shallow root system, making it suitable for arid conditions.

Caesalpinia enneaphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
