Centaurea duboisii

Centaurea duboisii, commonly known as Dubois' cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea duboisii is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
