Commiphora tomentosa

Commiphora tomentosa, commonly known as the Indian myrrh or kino tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers and is known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine. Its sap, known as kino, has been used for centuries for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
