Curcuma strobilina

Curcuma strobilina, commonly known as the gingerbread plant, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, aromatic rhizomes and bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture.

Curcuma strobilina is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its potential medicinal properties.
