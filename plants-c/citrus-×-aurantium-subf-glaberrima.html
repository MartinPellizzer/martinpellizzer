Citrus × aurantium subf. glaberrima

Citrus × aurantium subf.

glaberrima, commonly known as the sour orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically used for their high vitamin C content. It is often cultivated as a rootstock for other citrus varieties due to its hardiness and disease resistance.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
