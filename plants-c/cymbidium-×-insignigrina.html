Cymbidium × insignigrina

Cymbidium × insignigrina, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cymbidium goeringii and Cymbidium lowianum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China, characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dense clusters of long, narrow petals with a distinctive white or pale pink coloration. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely appreciated in horticulture for its beauty and resilience.
