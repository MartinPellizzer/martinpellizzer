Cleome tetrandra var. simplicifolia

Cleome tetrandra var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with four stamens, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
