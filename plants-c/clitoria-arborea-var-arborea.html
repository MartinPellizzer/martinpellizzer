Clitoria arborea var. arborea

Clitoria arborea var. arborea, commonly known as the tree legume or butterfly pea tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like blue or purple flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agroforestry systems.
