Corysanthes rotundifolia

Corysanthes rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf corysanthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its pollination.

Corysanthes rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
