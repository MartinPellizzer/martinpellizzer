Cynosurus callitrichus

Cynosurus callitrichus, commonly known as the tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with fine, soft leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hair. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Cynosurus callitrichus plays a role in maintaining soil stability and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
