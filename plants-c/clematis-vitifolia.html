Clematis vitifolia

Clematis vitifolia, commonly known as the grape-leaved clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves that resemble grape leaves, with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically in late spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis vitifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
