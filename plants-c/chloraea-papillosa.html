Chloraea papillosa

Chloraea papillosa, commonly known as the silky greenhood, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive green, hood-like labellum and pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understorey areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Chloraea papillosa is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological significance.
