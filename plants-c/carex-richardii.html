Carex richardii

Carex richardii, commonly known as Richard's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Carex richardii is a hardy species that thrives in wet to mesic conditions and is relatively low maintenance.
