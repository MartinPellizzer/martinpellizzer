Citrus chilensis

Citrus chilensis, commonly known as the chiltepe or Chilean lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It produces small, round, yellow-green fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. It is well adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its tart flavor and aromatic properties.
