Celosia peruviana

Celosia peruviana, commonly known as the flame flower or Peruvian celosia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, while its flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Celosia peruviana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
