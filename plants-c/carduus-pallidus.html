Carduus pallidus

Carduus pallidus, commonly known as the pale thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, silver-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
