Ceropegia lindenii

Ceropegia lindenii, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces unique, waxy, and often fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators, such as moths. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Ceropegia lindenii is a succulent, capable of storing water in its stems and leaves, making it adaptable to arid environments.
