Choriptera

Choriptera, commonly known as the springtail, is a small hexapod insect belonging to the order Collembola.

These tiny creatures are often found in moist environments such as soil, leaf litter, and under stones. They are characterized by their elongated bodies and a unique appendage called the furcula, which allows them to jump. Choriptera play an important role in soil ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and decomposition.

Their presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed habitats.
