Codonocroton triphyllum

Codonocroton triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
