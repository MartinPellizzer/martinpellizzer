Corispermum korovinii

Corispermum korovinii, commonly known as the Korovin's bladderwrack, is a halophytic plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in saline and coastal environments, often found in wet meadows and salt marshes. The plant is characterized by its small, bladder-like fruits that enclose the seeds, a feature typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain water in harsh, dry conditions.
