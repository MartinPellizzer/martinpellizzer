Cecropia ambaci

Cecropia ambaci, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, gray bark. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically alternate and can reach up to 30 cm in length. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

The tree is often found in disturbed habitats and plays a significant role in supporting local biodiversity.
