Callothlaspi lilacinum

Callothlaspi lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale lilac to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly rough texture. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine displays.

Callothlaspi lilacinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in cooler, arid environments.
