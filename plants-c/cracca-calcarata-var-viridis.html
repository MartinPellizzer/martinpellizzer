Cracca calcarata var. viridis

Cracca calcarata var. viridis, commonly known as the green cracca, is a variant of the cracca plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
