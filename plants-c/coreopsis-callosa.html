Coreopsis callosa

Coreopsis callosa, commonly known as the rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Coreopsis callosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
