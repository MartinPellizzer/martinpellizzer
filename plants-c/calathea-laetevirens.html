Calathea laetevirens

Calathea laetevirens, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It features striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns of green, silver, and rust-colored markings. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture that enhances their visual appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening.
