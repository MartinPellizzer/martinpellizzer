Cereus chiloensis var. lamprochlorus

Cereus chiloensis var. lamprochlorus, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a unique succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a tall, spindly stem covered in dense, white to silver-blue spines that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces large, night-blooming flowers that are typically pink or red, adding a striking visual contrast to its silver foliage. Its ability to store water in its stem allows it to survive in arid conditions with minimal rainfall.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in dry gardens or as a houseplant.
