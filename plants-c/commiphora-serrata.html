Commiphora serrata

Commiphora serrata, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves that are typically serrated, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces a valuable gum resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
