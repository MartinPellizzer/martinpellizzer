Crataegus engelmannii var. sinistra

Crataegus engelmannii var. sinistra, commonly known as the left-handed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native range.
