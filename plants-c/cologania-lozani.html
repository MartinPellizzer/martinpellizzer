Cologania lozani

Cologania lozani, commonly known as the Zambian orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Zambia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that often display a blend of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its botanical features include a pseudobulb structure and strap-like leaves, which help it store water and nutrients.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, Cologania lozani is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
