Cousinia malacophylla

Cousinia malacophylla, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and high-altitude environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

Cousinia malacophylla is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
