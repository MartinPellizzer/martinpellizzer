Callistemon recurvus

Callistemon recurvus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking crimson or red bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Callistemon recurvus is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
