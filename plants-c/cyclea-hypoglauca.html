Cyclea hypoglauca

Cyclea hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Cyclea hypoglauca is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
