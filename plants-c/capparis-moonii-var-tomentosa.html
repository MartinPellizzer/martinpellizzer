Capparis moonii var. tomentosa

Capparis moonii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly caper, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.

The species is part of the Capparaceae family, known for its diverse and often decorative plants.
