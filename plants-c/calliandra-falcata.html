Calliandra falcata

Calliandra falcata, commonly known as the sicklepod acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces distinctive, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Calliandra falcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
