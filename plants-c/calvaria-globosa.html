Calvaria globosa

Calvaria globosa, commonly known as the globe sphaerocarp, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Sphaerocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its spherical, spore-producing structures called sphaerocarps, which are typically found in freshwater environments. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead consisting of a simple, branching structure that floats on the water's surface. Its reproductive structures are highly specialized, with each sphaerocarp containing numerous spores that are released into the water when mature.

Calvaria globosa is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, where it plays a minor role in the ecosystem.
