Cariniana carajarum

Cariniana carajarum, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain the edible Brazil nuts. Its flowers are small and green, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
