Cyphostemma vandenbergheae

Cyphostemma vandenbergheae, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicle fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique growth habit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
