Calamagrostis crassifolia

Calamagrostis crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
