Chenopodium ficifolium var. indicola

Chenopodium ficifolium var. indicola, commonly known as Indian goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Asia, including India and China. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is sometimes cultivated as a leaf vegetable in traditional cuisines due to its edible leaves and nutritional value.
