Canarium macrophyllum

Canarium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing oily seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
