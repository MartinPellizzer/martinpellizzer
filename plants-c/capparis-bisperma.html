Capparis bisperma

Capparis bisperma, commonly known as the two-seeded caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits. Its leaves are usually small and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

The fruit contains two seeds, which is reflected in its scientific name.
