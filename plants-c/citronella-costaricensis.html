Citronella costaricensis

Citronella costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican citronella, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in natural insect repellents.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it holds significance in traditional herbal practices.
