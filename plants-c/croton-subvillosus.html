Croton subvillosus

Croton subvillosus, commonly known as the silky croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and leaves that vary in shape from elliptical to ovate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of light conditions.
