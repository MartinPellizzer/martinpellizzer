Catasetum bungerothii var. pottsianum

Catasetum bungerothii var. pottsianum, commonly known as the Potts' Catasetum, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often yellow or orange, flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by bees. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, spurred flowers, with the labellum being particularly elaborate.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
