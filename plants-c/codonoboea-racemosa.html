Codonoboea racemosa

Codonoboea racemosa, commonly known as the bluebell vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
