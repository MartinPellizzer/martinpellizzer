Calathea velutinifolia

Calathea velutinifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, dark green leaves with intricate, marbled patterns in shades of silver and green. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Calathea velutinifolia requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil to flourish.
