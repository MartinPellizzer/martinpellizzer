Clematis acuminata var. leschenaultiana

Clematis acuminata var. leschenaultiana, commonly known as the Leschenault's Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
