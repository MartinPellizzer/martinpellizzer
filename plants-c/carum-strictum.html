Carum strictum

Carum strictum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called schizocarps, which contain two mericarps each. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional cuisines for their distinctive flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
