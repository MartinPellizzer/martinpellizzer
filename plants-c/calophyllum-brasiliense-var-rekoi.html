Calophyllum brasiliense var. rekoi

Calophyllum brasiliense var. rekoi, commonly known as the Rekoi or Koa tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown to black bark and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
