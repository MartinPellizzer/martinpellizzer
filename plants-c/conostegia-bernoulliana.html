Conostegia bernoulliana

Conostegia bernoulliana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, round fruits.

Conostegia bernoulliana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.
