Calamagrostis jakutensis

Calamagrostis jakutensis, commonly known as the Yakut sedge, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Calamagrostis jakutensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
