Cyathea tristis

Cyathea tristis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor itself in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Cyathea tristis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
