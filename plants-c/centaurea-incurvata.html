Centaurea incurvata

Centaurea incurvata, commonly known as the bentleaf knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curled, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.
