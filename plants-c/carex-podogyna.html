Carex podogyna

Carex podogyna, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage. It is a perennial plant that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
