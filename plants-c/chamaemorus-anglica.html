Chamaemorus anglica

Chamaemorus anglica, commonly known as the common crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Chamaemorus anglica thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.
