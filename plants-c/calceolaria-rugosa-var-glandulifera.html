Calceolaria rugosa var. glandulifera

Calceolaria rugosa var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, while the flowers are usually violet or purple in color. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique floral structure and vibrant hues.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
