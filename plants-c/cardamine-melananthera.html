Cardamine melananthera

Cardamine melananthera, commonly known as the black-flowered watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and distinctive black-purple flowers. Native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is often found in rocky or streamside habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
