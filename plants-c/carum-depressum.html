Carum depressum

Carum depressum, commonly known as the bent caraway, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley, dill, and carrots, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Carum depressum is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its compact growth habit and delicate flowering.
