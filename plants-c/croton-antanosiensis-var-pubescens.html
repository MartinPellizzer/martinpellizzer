Croton antanosiensis var. pubescens

Croton antanosiensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
