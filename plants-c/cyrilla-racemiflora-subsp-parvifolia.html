Cyrilla racemiflora subsp. parvifolia

Cyrilla racemiflora subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, winged fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
