Cuphea radicans

Cuphea radicans, commonly known as the Whorled Snapdragon, is a trailing herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Cuphea radicans is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
