Cenchrus setaceus subsp. morronei

Cenchrus setaceus subsp.

morronei, commonly known as the Morrone's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in dryland gardens.
