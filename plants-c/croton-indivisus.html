Croton indivisus

Croton indivisus, commonly known as the single-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that can vary in color from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Croton indivisus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
