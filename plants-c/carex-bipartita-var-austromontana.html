Carex bipartita var. austromontana

Carex bipartita var. austromontana, commonly known as the Austromontana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This variety is native to Australia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in swampy or wetland habitats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
