Crocus hermoneus subsp. hermoneus

Crocus hermoneus subsp.

hermoneus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of central and eastern Iran. It produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cold climates.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
