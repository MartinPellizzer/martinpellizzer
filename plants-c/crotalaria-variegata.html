Crotalaria variegata

Crotalaria variegata, commonly known as the variegated spurred lupine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive spurred petals, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a velvety texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Crotalaria variegata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
