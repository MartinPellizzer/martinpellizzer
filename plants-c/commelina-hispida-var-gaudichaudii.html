Commelina hispida var. gaudichaudii

Commelina hispida var. gaudichaudii, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that are borne on short, terminal spikes. This variant is distinguished by its more robust growth and dense foliage compared to other members of the species.
