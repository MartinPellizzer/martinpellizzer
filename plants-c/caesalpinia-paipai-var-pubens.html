Caesalpinia paipai var. pubens

Caesalpinia paipai var. pubens, commonly known as the rough caesalpinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange pod-like fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces showy, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing food for various wildlife species.
