Carex acutiformis f. depauperata

Carex acutiformis f. depauperata, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
