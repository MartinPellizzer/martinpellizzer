Cyrtochilum simulans

Cyrtochilum simulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that resemble other orchid species, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs and produces fragrant blooms in shades of white and green. Cyrtochilum simulans thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
