Croton flavescens var. brandegeeanus

Croton flavescens var. brandegeeanus, commonly known as the Brandegee's croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that often display a vibrant yellow to orange hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.
