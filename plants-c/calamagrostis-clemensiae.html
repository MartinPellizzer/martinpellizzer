Calamagrostis clemensiae

Calamagrostis clemensiae, commonly known as the Clemens' reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display to its appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Calamagrostis clemensiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
