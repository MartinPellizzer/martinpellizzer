Clintonia ciliata

Clintonia ciliata, commonly known as the threadleaf caladium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
