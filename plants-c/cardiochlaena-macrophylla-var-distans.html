Cardiochlaena macrophylla var. distans

Cardiochlaena macrophylla var. distans, commonly known as the broad-leafed cardinal flower, is a striking perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
