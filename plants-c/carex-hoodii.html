Carex hoodii

Carex hoodii, commonly known as Hood's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Carex hoodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
