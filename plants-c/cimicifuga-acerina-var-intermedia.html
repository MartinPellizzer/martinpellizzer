Cimicifuga acerina var. intermedia

Cimicifuga acerina var. intermedia, commonly known as the red stem bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often turn red or purple in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in panicle inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in moist, wooded habitats.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
