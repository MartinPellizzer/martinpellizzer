Calliandra pilocarpa

Calliandra pilocarpa, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
