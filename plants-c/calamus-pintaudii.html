Calamus pintaudii

Calamus pintaudii, commonly known as the Pintaud's arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, commonly known as the palm family, though it is not a true palm. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Calamus pintaudii is often cultivated for its edible rhizomes, which are used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
