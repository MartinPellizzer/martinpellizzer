Cineraria glabra

Cineraria glabra, commonly known as the smooth cineraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to bloom throughout the year.

Cineraria glabra is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
