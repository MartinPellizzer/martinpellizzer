Callistachys lanceolata

Callistachys lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland areas and along stream banks. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, hence the species name, and are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in moist soil conditions.
