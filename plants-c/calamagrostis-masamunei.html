Calamagrostis masamunei

Calamagrostis masamunei, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-tipped plumes that create a striking visual display. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the fall.

Calamagrostis masamunei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
