Catha heyneana

Catha heyneana, commonly known as khat, is a flowering plant native to the horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in regions with low rainfall.

The leaves contain psychoactive compounds, particularly cathinone and cathine, which contribute to its traditional use as a stimulant.
