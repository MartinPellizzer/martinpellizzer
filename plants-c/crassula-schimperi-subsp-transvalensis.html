Crassula schimperi subsp. transvalensis

Crassula schimperi subsp.

transvalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal Crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features fleshy, rounded leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially when exposed to full sun. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
