Caesalpinia cacalaco

Caesalpinia cacalaco, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its bark is often rough and fissured. The fruits are elongated pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its striking floral display.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
