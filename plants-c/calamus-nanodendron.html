Calamus nanodendron

Calamus nanodendron, commonly known as the dwarf arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, ornamental palm characterized by its compact size and elegant, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are similar to those of the arrowroot plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and edible properties, it is cultivated both for horticultural display and culinary use.
