Celsia purpurea

Celsia purpurea, commonly known as the purple celsia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Celsia purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
