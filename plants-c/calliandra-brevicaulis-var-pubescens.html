Calliandra brevicaulis var. pubescens

Calliandra brevicaulis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy clusters of pink or red flowers, which are arranged in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets that are also covered in a dense pubescence.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
