Crataegus impervia

Crataegus impervia, commonly known as the rock crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, mountainous regions.

The species is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
