Cleisostoma simondii var. guangdongense

Cleisostoma simondii var. guangdongense, commonly known as the Guangdong waxflower, is a species of orchid native to southern China.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and the presence of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically has elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a waxy texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red streaks.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a potted plant.
