Calamagrostis halleriana var. pulchella

Calamagrostis halleriana var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and its striking purple to pinkish inflorescence that appears in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often in wet meadows or along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts beautifully with the colorful flowering spikes.
