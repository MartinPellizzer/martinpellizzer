Calpicarpum roxburghii

Calpicarpum roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a serrated edge and a slightly hairy surface.

Calpicarpum roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
