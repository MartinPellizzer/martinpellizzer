Cyperus selloanus

Cyperus selloanus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus selloanus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
