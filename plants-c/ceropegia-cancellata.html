Ceropegia cancellata

Ceropegia cancellata, commonly known as the laceleaf string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with a unique, tubular shape. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is popular as a houseplant and in hanging baskets.
