Carex platysperma var. platysperma

Carex platysperma var. platysperma, commonly known as the broad-fruit sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
