Croton tortilis

Croton tortilis, commonly known as the curly-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curling leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Croton tortilis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique foliage.
