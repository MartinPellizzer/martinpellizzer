Conyza pulsatilloides

Conyza pulsatilloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats including roadsides and fields. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish-green hue, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly.
