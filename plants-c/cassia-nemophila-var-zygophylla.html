Cassia nemophila var. zygophylla

Cassia nemophila var. zygophylla, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
