Chenopodium chilense var. denudata

Chenopodium chilense var. denudata, commonly known as the Chilean goosefoot, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by triangular, beak-like seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal areas and dry valleys.

This species is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
