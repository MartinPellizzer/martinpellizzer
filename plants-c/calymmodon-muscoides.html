Calymmodon muscoides

Calymmodon muscoides, commonly known as the moss-like liverwort, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the family Calymmodontaceae.

It is characterized by its small, leafy structure that resembles moss, with closely packed, overlapping leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including the female archegonia, are located on the lower surface of the leaves.

Calymmodon muscoides plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
