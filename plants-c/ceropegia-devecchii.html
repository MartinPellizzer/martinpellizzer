Ceropegia devecchii

Ceropegia devecchii, commonly known as the Cape ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
