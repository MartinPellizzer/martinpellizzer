Cyperus simpsonii

Cyperus simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Cyperus simpsonii is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
