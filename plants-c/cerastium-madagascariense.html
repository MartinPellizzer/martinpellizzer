Cerastium madagascariense

Cerastium madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often in moist or shaded environments. Its stems are prostrate, and it produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits.
