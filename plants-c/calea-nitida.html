Calea nitida

Calea nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance.

Calea nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
