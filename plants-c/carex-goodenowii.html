Carex goodenowii

Carex goodenowii, commonly known as Goodenow's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex goodenowii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
