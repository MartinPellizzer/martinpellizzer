Crepis graminifolia

Crepis graminifolia, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head, a common feature among members of its family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Crepis graminifolia is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed or marginal soils.
