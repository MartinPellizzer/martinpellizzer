Cynara scolymus var. pungens

Cynara scolymus var. pungens, commonly known as the Spanish artichoke, is a variant of the globe artichoke with distinctive botanical features.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces large, spiny, purple bracts that enclose the edible flower buds. Its leaves are large, lobed, and covered in a dense layer of fine, prickly hairs.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
