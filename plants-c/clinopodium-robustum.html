Clinopodium robustum

Clinopodium robustum, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Clinopodium robustum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
