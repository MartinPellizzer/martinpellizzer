Calanthe sasakii

Calanthe sasakii, commonly known as the Sasak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Japan and Taiwan.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the inflorescence is a raceme of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

Calanthe sasakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
