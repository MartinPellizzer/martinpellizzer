Clematis tangutica subsp. mongolica

Clematis tangutica subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian Clematis, is a perennial climbing vine native to regions of Central Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, often with a distinctive greenish hue.

The plant produces small, follicle-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
