Chlorophytum poricola

Chlorophytum poricola, commonly known as the porous snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender spikes during the growing season. Its leaves have a unique porous texture, which gives it its common name.

It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.
