Clematis sikkimensis

Clematis sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with slender, hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its significance in both horticulture and ecological studies.
