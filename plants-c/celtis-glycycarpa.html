Celtis glycycarpa

Celtis glycycarpa, commonly known as the Chinese sugar elm, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

Celtis glycycarpa is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
