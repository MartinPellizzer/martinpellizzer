Caucalis carnosa

Caucalis carnosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Caucalis carnosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
