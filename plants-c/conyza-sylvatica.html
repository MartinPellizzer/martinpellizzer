Conyza sylvatica

Conyza sylvatica, commonly known as woodland fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Conyza sylvatica is often found in moist, shaded environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
