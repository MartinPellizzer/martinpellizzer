Chenopodium scabricaule

Chenopodium scabricaule, commonly known as roughleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its seeds are small and dry, and it is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop.

The plant has a short life cycle and is adapted to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
