Crataegus monogyna var. borosii

Crataegus monogyna var. borosii, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
