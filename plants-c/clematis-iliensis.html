Clematis iliensis

Clematis iliensis, commonly known as the Eastern Persian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates. Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and vigorous growth habit.

Clematis iliensis is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in its native region.
