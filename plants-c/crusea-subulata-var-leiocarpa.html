Crusea subulata var. leiocarpa

Crusea subulata var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky crusea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover.
