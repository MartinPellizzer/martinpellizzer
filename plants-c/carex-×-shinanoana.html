Carex × shinanoana

Carex × shinanoana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes with wet or semi-wet conditions.
