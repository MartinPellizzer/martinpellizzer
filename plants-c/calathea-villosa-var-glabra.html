Calathea villosa var. glabra

Calathea villosa var. glabra, commonly known as the Velvet Leaf Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, waxy leaves that display intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. Its foliage is often marked by a subtle sheen and a unique, almost velvety texture.

Calathea villosa var. glabra is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
