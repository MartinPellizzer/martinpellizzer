Cyathea brownii

Cyathea brownii, commonly known as the Brown's tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is a large, evergreen fern characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, and the plant is known for its slow growth rate.

Cyathea brownii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
