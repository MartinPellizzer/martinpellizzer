Cynoglossum denticulatum var. ramosius

Cynoglossum denticulatum var. ramosius, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces violet or blue flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
