Cissus adnata var. pyrrhodasys

Cissus adnata var. pyrrhodasys, commonly known as the red-veined cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red veins on the leaves, which contrast with its glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus adnata var. pyrrhodasys is often used in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant due to its vigorous growth habit.
