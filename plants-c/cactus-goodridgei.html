Cactus goodridgei

Cactus goodridgei, commonly known as the Goodridge's cactus, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant typically produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. Its spines are short and often hidden beneath the thick, fleshy skin of the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
