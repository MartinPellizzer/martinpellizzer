Chlorophytum uhamense

Chlorophytum uhamense, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Chlorophytum uhamense is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
