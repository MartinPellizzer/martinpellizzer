Centaurea pseudospinulosa

Centaurea pseudospinulosa, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its slender, spiny stems and small, blue to purple flowers make it a distinctive member of the Centaurea genus.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
