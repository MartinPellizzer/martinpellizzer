Crepis lechleri

Crepis lechleri, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It has small, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Crepis lechleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
