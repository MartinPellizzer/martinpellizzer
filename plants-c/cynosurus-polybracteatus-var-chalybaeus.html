Cynosurus polybracteatus var. chalybaeus

Cynosurus polybracteatus var. chalybaeus, commonly known as the chalybeous hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.
