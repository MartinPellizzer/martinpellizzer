Centaurea picris

Centaurea picris, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with red veins and spiny, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowers bloom from June to August, and it is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant contains compounds such as hypericin and hyperforin, which have been studied for their potential antidepressant and antiviral effects.
