Carex magnifica var. lacunarum

Carex magnifica var. lacunarum, commonly known as the lacunar sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their narrow, linear leaves. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive, somewhat waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
