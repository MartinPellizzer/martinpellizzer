Clueria laciniata

Clueria laciniata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, fringed appearance.

Clueria laciniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
