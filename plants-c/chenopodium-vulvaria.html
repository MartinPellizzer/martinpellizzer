Chenopodium vulvaria

Chenopodium vulvaria, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically appearing in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium vulvaria is also known for its edible seeds, which have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
