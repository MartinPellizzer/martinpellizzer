Convolvulus amoenus

Convolvulus amoenus, commonly known as sweet bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber with slender, twining stems that can ascend various supports. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
