Crassula capensis var. promontorii

Crassula capensis var. promontorii, commonly known as the rock rose or Cape rock rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically forms a compact, bushy shrub with a rosette structure, and its leaves are often blue-green in color. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
