Carex nodiflora

Carex nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in inflorescences at the ends of the stems. Carex nodiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
