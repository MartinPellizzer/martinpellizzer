Carex Stricta Subsp. Cespitosa

Carex stricta subsp. cespitosa is a hardy sedge commonly found in wetland environments across northern regions. It forms dense, grass-like clumps and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife. Its narrow, dark green leaves give it a distinct appearance that blends well with surrounding vegetation. Despite its modest look, it is an essential component of many wetland ecosystems.
