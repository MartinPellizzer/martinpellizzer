Clethra suaveolens

Clethra suaveolens, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Clethra suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
