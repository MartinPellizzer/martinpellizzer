Carex khasiana

Carex khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Carex khasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
