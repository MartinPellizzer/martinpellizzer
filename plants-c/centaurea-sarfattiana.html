Centaurea sarfattiana

Centaurea sarfattiana, commonly known as the Sarfatt thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and striking purple to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought resistance.

Centaurea sarfattiana is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.
