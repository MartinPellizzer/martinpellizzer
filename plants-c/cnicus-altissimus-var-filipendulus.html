Cnicus altissimus var. filipendulus

Cnicus altissimus var. filipendulus, commonly known as the silverleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
