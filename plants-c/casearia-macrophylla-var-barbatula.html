Casearia macrophylla var. barbatula

Casearia macrophylla var. barbatula, commonly known as the silky casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
