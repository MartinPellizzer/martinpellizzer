Centaurea hortorum

Centaurea hortorum, commonly known as the garden centaury, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, bright blue or violet flowers arranged in dense, compact inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, with a hairy surface.

Centaurea hortorum is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
