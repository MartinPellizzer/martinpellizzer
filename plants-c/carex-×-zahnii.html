Carex × zahnii

Carex × zahnii, commonly known as the common sedge.

It is a hybrid species within the Carex genus, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex × zahnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
