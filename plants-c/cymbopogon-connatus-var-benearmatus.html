Cymbopogon connatus var. benearmatus

Cymbopogon connatus var. benearmatus, commonly known as the Benearmatus citron grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. Its essential oils, particularly citral, are used in the fragrance and flavor industries.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
