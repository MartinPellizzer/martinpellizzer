Calathea pumila

Calathea pumila, commonly known as the baby ivy or small-leaved calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, mottled leaves that display a range of colors, including green, silver, and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture in its soil. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns that change subtly in response to light conditions.

Calathea pumila is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
