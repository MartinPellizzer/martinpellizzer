Cristaria glomerulata

Cristaria glomerulata, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and ditches. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in groups, giving it its scientific name.

Cristaria glomerulata is a carnivorous plant that plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to the control of microorganism populations.
