Callerya atropurpurea

Callerya atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered callerya, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring a woody stem and compound leaves. The plant is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers, which bloom in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.
