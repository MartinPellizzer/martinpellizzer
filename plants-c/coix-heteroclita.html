Coix heteroclita

Coix heteroclita, commonly known as Chinese sedge, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers. Its seeds, known as coix seeds, are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The botanical characteristics of Coix heteroclita make it adaptable to moist soils and suitable for wetland habitats.
