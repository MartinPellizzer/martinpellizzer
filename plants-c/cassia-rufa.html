Cassia rufa

Cassia rufa, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround its yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia rufa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
