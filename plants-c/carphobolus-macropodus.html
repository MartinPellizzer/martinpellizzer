Carphobolus macropodus

Carphobolus macropodus, commonly known as the giant water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats on the water surface. It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

Its buoyant seeds allow it to spread easily through waterways, making it an invasive species in many regions.
