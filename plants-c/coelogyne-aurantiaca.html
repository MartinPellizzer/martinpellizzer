Coelogyne aurantiaca

Coelogyne aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers, which bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its tropical environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
