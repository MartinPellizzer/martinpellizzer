Carex spicata f. humilis

Carex spicata f. humilis, commonly known as the low sedges, is a compact, herbaceous perennial that belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark brown flowers, which are often subtended by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in water management and habitat support.
