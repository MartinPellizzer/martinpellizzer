Coffea engleri

Coffea engleri, commonly known as the Engleri coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia and is known for its small, red fruits that contain coffee beans. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy dark green surface and a slightly serrated edge.

While not widely cultivated for commercial coffee production, it holds significance in botanical research and local traditions.
