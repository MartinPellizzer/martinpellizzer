Conyza subsessilis

Conyza subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, and it has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Conyza subsessilis is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its potential as a source of bioactive compounds.
