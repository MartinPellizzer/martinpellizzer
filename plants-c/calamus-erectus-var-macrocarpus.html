Calamus erectus var. macrocarpus

Calamus erectus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.
