Chabertia dicranodendron

Chabertia dicranodendron, commonly known as the mossy bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its stems are short and creeping.

Chabertia dicranodendron plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
