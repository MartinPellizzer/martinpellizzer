Citrus × bigaradia var. racemosus

Citrus × bigaradia var. racemosus, commonly known as the sour orange.

This variety is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, yellowish-orange rind and a high acidity level. The leaves are glossy, dark green, and slightly serrated, while the flowers are fragrant and white with a yellow center. It is a hybrid citrus species that is often used as a rootstock in citrus cultivation due to its hardiness and resistance to certain diseases.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
