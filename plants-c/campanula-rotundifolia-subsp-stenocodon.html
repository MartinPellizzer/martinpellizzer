Campanula rotundifolia subsp. stenocodon

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

stenocodon, commonly known as narrow-corned bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
