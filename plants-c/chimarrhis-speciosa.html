Chimarrhis speciosa

Chimarrhis speciosa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value, with "speciosa" meaning showy.

It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
