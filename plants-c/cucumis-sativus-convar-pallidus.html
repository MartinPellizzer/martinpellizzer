Cucumis sativus convar. pallidus

Cucumis sativus convar. pallidus, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often pale green or white skin, which distinguishes it from typical cucumbers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a vine-like growth habit, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Its fruits are typically harvested when immature, offering a mild, crisp flavor.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and culinary use in various cuisines.
