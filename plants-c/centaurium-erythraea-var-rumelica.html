Centaurium erythraea var. rumelica

Centaurium erythraea var. rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelian red centaury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or are slightly ascending, with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and flies.

The plant is often found in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks.
