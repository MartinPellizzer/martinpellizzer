Crepis tectorum subsp. barkhausioides

Crepis tectorum subsp.

barkhausioides, commonly known as the rock crepis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its small, yellowish flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters, and it produces slender, hairy stems that help it withstand harsh environmental conditions.
