Chelone grandiflora

Chelone grandiflora, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Chelone grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
