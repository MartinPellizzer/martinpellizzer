Craterispermum capitatum

Craterispermum capitatum, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a source of nectar for pollinators.
