Cereus haitiensis

Cereus haitiensis, commonly known as the Haitian Christmas cactus, is a species native to the island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the holiday season, which is why it is popular as a festive ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cereus haitiensis is relatively low maintenance, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
