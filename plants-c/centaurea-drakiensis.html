Centaurea drakiensis

Centaurea drakiensis, commonly known as the Drakiensis thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and vibrant purple-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its growth habit is usually upright, with a height ranging from 30 to 100 centimeters.

Centaurea drakiensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
