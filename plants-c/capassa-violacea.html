Capassa violacea

Capassa violacea, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Capassa violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
