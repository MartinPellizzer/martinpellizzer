Cardamomum graminifolium

Cardamomum graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leafed cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually yield aromatic seeds. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The essential oils extracted from its seeds are known for their distinctive spicy and sweet aroma.
