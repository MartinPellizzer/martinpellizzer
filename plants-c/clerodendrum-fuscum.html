Clerodendrum fuscum

Clerodendrum fuscum, commonly known as the black elder or dark clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiny, globose fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
