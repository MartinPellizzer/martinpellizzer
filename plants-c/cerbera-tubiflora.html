Cerbera tubiflora

Cerbera tubiflora, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
