Cordia gerascanthus var. subcanescens

Cordia gerascanthus var. subcanescens, commonly known as the silverleaf cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
