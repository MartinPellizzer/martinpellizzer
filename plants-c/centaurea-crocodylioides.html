Centaurea crocodylioides

Centaurea crocodylioides, commonly known as the crocodile's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers and bristly, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
