Crataegus paddockeae

Crataegus paddockeae, commonly known as the Paddock's hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
