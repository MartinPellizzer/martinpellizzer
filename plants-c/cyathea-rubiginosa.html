Cyathea rubiginosa

Cyathea rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive dark reddish-brown coloration on its trunk, which becomes more pronounced with age. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Cyathea rubiginosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
