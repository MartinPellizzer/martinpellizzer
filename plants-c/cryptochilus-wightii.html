Cryptochilus wightii

Cryptochilus wightii, commonly known as the Wight's cryptochilus, is a species of orchid native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, fleshy, ovate leaf. The plant bears a spike of small, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent white calli on the column. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cryptochilus wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
