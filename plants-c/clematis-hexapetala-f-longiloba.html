Clematis hexapetala f. longiloba

Clematis hexapetala f. longiloba, commonly known as the long-petaled clematis, is a hybrid form of the Chinese clematis.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that are typically 6-8 cm in diameter. The plant is a deciduous vine with compound leaves that are trifoliate or pinnate, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant blooms.
