Chenopodium leucospermum

Chenopodium leucospermum, commonly known as white goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are small and contain a high amount of oil, making it a potential source for oil extraction.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
