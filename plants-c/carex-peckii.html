Carex peckii

Carex peckii, commonly known as Peck's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex peckii is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
