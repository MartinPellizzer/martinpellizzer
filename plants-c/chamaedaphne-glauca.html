Chamaedaphne glauca

Chamaedaphne glauca, commonly known as bog laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in sphagnum bogs and swampy areas.

Chamaedaphne glauca plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
