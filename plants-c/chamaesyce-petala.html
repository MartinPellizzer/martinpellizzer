Chamaesyce petala

Chamaesyce petala, commonly known as the purpleleaf sandwort, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
