Centaurea hymenolepis

Centaurea hymenolepis, commonly known as the silver centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spiny, bristly stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in compact, conical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Centaurea hymenolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
