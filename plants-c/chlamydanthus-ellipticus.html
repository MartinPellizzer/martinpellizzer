Chlamydanthus ellipticus

Chlamydanthus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed chlamydanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing yellow to orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
