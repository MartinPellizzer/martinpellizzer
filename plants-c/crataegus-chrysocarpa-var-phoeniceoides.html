Crataegus chrysocarpa var. phoeniceoides

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. phoeniceoides, commonly known as the golden-hued hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features showy, yellowish-orange fruits that are distinctive and often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant has pinnate leaves with serrated margins and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The fruit is edible but typically consumed by birds and small mammals rather than humans.
