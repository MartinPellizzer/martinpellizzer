Carex cognata var. congolensis

Carex cognata var. congolensis, commonly known as the Congo sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swampy forests and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green coloration, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the wet season.

This variant is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to humid tropical climates.
