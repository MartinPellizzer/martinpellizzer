Conophytum praecinctum

Conophytum praecinctum, commonly known as the "Cape Town beauty," is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, fleshy body with thick, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It is often grown as a decorative succulent due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Conophytum praecinctum prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure to flourish.
