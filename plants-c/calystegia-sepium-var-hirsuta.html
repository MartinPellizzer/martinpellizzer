Calystegia sepium var. hirsuta

Calystegia sepium var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its aggressive growth habit.

Due to its persistent nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
