Catasetum gnomus

Catasetum gnomus, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, brightly colored blossoms that range from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Catasetum gnomus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
