Crataegus oxyacantha var. laevigata

Crataegus oxyacantha var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked stems and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for garden planting.
