Catasetum × splendens

Catasetum × splendens, commonly known as the splendid orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns and contrasting markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their unique structure, including a specialized appendage called the gynostemium, which aids in pollination by specific insects.

Catasetum × splendens is prized in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
