Cacalia humifusa

Cacalia humifusa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia humifusa is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
