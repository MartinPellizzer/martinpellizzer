Clusia drouetiana

Clusia drouetiana, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia drouetiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its resilience to drought.
