Combretum roxburghii

Combretum roxburghii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant belongs to the family Combretaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions. Its bark is smooth and often peels off in strips, revealing a reddish inner layer.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
