Croton sertanejus

Croton sertanejus, commonly known as Brazilian croton, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
