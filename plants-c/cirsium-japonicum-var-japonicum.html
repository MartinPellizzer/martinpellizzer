Cirsium japonicum var. japonicum

Cirsium japonicum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese thistle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring rough, spiny leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant has a robust root system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The flowers are arranged in a loose panicle, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
