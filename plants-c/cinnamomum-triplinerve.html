Cinnamomum triplinerve

Cinnamomum triplinerve, commonly known as the three-veined cinnamon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are distinctly three-veined, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
