Chrysophyllum cainito var. caeruleum

Chrysophyllum cainito var. caeruleum, commonly known as the blue star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically blue or purple when ripe, though the variety caeruleum is noted for its bluish hue. The fruit's pulp is sweet and juicy, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
