Cymbopogon parkeri

Cymbopogon parkeri, commonly known as the African lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus-like scent. The plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in aromatherapy and perfumery. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cymbopogon parkeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
