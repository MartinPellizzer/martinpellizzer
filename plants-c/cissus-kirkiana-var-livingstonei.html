Cissus kirkiana var. livingstonei

Cissus kirkiana var. livingstonei, commonly known as the Livingstone's devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and horticultural purposes.
