Cestrum sphaerocarpum

Cestrum sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe-flowered trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers, typically in shades of white, yellow, or red. The plant's leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a spherical, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cestrum sphaerocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
