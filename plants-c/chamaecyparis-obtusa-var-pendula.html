Chamaecyparis obtusa var. pendula

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a variant of the Japanese cedar characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It is a coniferous evergreen with scale-like leaves that are bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones and is native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a desirable addition to landscaped areas.
