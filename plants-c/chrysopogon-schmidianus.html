Chrysopogon schmidianus

Chrysopogon schmidianus, commonly known as the Schmid's grass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Chrysopogon schmidianus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
