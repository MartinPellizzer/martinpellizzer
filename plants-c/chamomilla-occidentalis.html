Chamomilla occidentalis

Chamomilla occidentalis, commonly known as western chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal teas.

The flowers contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and calming effects.
