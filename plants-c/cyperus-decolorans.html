Cyperus decolorans

Cyperus decolorans, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curved, and the plant often grows in wet or marshy environments.

Cyperus decolorans is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
