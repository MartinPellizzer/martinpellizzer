Cynanchum anegadense

Cynanchum anegadense, commonly known as the Anegada milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the British Virgin Islands.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a woody stem with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The plant is also known for its latex-like sap, which can be toxic to some animals.
