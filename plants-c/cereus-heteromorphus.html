Cereus heteromorphus

Cereus heteromorphus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a strong fragrance. These flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours before closing in the morning.

Cereus heteromorphus is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
