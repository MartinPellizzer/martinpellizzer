Campanula glomerata subsp. stenosiphon

Campanula glomerata subsp.

stenosiphon, commonly known as narrow-siphon bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture.

The subspecies is characterized by its narrow, tubular corolla and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
