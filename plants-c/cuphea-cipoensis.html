Cuphea cipoensis

Cuphea cipoensis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, round, fuzzy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
