Cynanchum pauciflorum

Cynanchum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among milkweeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive structure that includes a corona surrounding the ovary.

Cynanchum pauciflorum is also noted for its medicinal properties, having been used in traditional herbal remedies.
