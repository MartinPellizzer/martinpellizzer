Caesalpinia oxycarpa

Caesalpinia oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-lobed coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, pointed leaflets, and its pods are woody, containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
