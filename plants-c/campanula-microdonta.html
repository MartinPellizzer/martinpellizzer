Campanula microdonta

Campanula microdonta, commonly known as the small bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula microdonta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
