Chamaedorea homomalla

Chamaedorea homomalla, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen palm, is a compact, ornamental plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk that remains relatively short throughout its life. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by dark, round berries. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This palm is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements.
