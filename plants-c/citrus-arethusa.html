Citrus arethusa

Citrus arethusa, commonly known as the Mexican lime or *Citrus arenaria*, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It produces small, round, green fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe and acidic. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in culinary applications, particularly in Mexican cuisine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for subtropical climates.
