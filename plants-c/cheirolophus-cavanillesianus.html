Cheirolophus cavanillesianus

Cheirolophus cavanillesianus, commonly known as the Cavanilles' bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
