Cerastium candicans

Cerastium candicans, commonly known as white mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are velvety and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cerastium candicans thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
