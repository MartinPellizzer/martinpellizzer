Chamaecrista nelsonii

Chamaecrista nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's burrlegume, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Chamaecrista nelsonii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
