Clutia lanceolata var. pubescens

Clutia lanceolata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, spreading foliage. The leaves are lanceolate in shape, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
