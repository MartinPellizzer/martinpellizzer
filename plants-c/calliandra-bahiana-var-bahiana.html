Calliandra bahiana var. bahiana

Calliandra bahiana var. bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like inflorescences. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening and for its nitrogen-fixing properties.
