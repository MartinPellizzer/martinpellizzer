Cissus cornifolia

Cissus cornifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in tiny, white, waxy dots. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when provided with adequate support. Its stems are succulent and can store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Cissus cornifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb.
