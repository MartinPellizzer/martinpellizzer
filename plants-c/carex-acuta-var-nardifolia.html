Carex acuta var. nardifolia

Carex acuta var. nardifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairless and have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
