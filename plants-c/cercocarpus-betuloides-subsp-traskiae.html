Cercocarpus betuloides subsp. traskiae

Cercocarpus betuloides subsp.

traskiae, commonly known as the Trask's curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are often curled at the edges.

The plant produces small, red or purplish flowers in spring, which are followed by distinctive, woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
