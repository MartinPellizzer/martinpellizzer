Chenopodium album f. leiospermum

Chenopodium album f. leiospermum, commonly known as lamb's quarters, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, succulent leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa but has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The f. leiospermum form is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves compared to the typical form.
