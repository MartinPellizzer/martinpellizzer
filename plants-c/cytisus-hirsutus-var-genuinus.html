Cytisus hirsutus var. genuinus

Cytisus hirsutus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Scottish broom, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
