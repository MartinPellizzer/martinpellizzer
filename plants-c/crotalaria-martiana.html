Crotalaria martiana

Crotalaria martiana, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive rattle-like structure at the base of the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Crotalaria martiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
