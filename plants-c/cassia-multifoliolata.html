Cassia multifoliolata

Cassia multifoliolata, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.

The seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds when mature.
