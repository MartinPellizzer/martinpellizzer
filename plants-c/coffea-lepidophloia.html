Coffea lepidophloia

Coffea lepidophloia, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee production.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
