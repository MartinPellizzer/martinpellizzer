Chamaecytisus ruthenicus var. syreiszczikowii

Chamaecytisus ruthenicus var. syreiszczikowii, commonly known as the Syreiszczikowii rock rose, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
