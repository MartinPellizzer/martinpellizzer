Centranthus angustifolius var. major

Centranthus angustifolius var. major, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
