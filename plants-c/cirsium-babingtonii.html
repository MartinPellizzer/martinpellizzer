Cirsium babingtonii

Cirsium babingtonii, commonly known as Babington's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
