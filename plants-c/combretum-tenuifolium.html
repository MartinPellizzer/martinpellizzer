Combretum tenuifolium

Combretum tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
