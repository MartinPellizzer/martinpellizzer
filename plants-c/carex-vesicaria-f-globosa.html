Carex vesicaria f. globosa

Carex vesicaria f. globosa, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of grass-like leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually bright green and stiff, with a smooth texture.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by spikelets that mature into brown, rounded structures.
