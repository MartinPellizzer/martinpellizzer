Cryptocarya smaragdina

Cryptocarya smaragdina, commonly known as the emerald cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Cryptocarya smaragdina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and evergreen nature.
