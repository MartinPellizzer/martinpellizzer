Cleome densifolia

Cleome densifolia, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching stems and clusters of small, often pink or white, showy flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.

Cleome densifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
