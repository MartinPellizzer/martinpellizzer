Convolvulus afanassievii

Convolvulus afanassievii, commonly known as the Afghan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant purple or pink blooms that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
