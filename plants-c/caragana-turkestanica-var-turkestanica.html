Caragana turkestanica var. turkestanica

Caragana turkestanica var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan shrub pea, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has compound leaves with 8 to 16 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, followed by legume pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppe regions.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.
