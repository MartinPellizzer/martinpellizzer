Cassia bauhinioides var. pilosior

Cassia bauhinioides var. pilosior, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the wet season. Its seed pods are elongated and contain numerous flat, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
