Crepis mollis subsp. hieracioides

Crepis mollis subsp.

hieracioides, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and often lobed, while the flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a dense cluster of disc florets.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
