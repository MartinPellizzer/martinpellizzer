Cynara humilis var. leucantha

Cynara humilis var. leucantha, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitats.
