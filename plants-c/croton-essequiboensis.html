Croton essequiboensis

Croton essequiboensis, commonly known as the Essequibo croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Guyana Shield region.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that range from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
