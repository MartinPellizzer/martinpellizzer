Carthamus arborescens

Carthamus arborescens, commonly known as yellow cockspur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disc of smaller florets, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces a distinctive, spiky seed head that is both ornamental and used in traditional medicine.
