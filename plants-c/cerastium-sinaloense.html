Cerastium sinaloense

Cerastium sinaloense, commonly known as the Mexican chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a woolly texture.

Cerastium sinaloense is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
