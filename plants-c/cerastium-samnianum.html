Cerastium samnianum

Cerastium samnianum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges. It is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar characteristics.

Cerastium samnianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
