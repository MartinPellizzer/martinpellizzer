Calamus caurinus

Calamus caurinus, commonly known as the golden orchid sedge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming during the rainy season. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
