Camara aculeata f. obtusifolia

Camara aculeata f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved nightshade, is a variant of the common nightshade species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, toxic berries that are a feature of its botanical profile. It typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
