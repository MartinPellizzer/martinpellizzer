Cestrum laurifolium var. intermedium

Cestrum laurifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade or golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
