Chionanthus tenuis

Chionanthus tenuis, commonly known as the slender fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its flowers have a distinctive, bell-like shape and emit a sweet, spicy scent.

Chionanthus tenuis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
