Conradina verticillata

Conradina verticillata, commonly known as the Florida stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
