Conyza canadensis var. pusilla

Conyza canadensis var. pusilla, commonly known as dwarf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is often found in disturbed sites and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
