Clerodendrum longitubum

Clerodendrum longitubum, commonly known as the long-tubed clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
