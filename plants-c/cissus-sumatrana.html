Cissus sumatrana

Cissus sumatrana, commonly known as the climbing fig-leaf goosefoot, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus sumatrana is also valued for its ability to climb and cover structures, making it a versatile plant in horticulture.
