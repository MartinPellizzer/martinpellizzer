Cyanus albofimbriatus

Cyanus albofimbriatus, commonly known as the white-edged blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces blue to violet flowers with white margins, which are often arranged in a radiate pattern. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soils in sunny locations.

Cyanus albofimbriatus is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
