Campanula rapunculoides var. reflexa

Campanula rapunculoides var. reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that hang downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
