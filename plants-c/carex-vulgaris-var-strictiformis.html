Carex vulgaris var. strictiformis

Carex vulgaris var. strictiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
