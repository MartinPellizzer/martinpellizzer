Cocculus laurifolius

Cocculus laurifolius, commonly known as the laurel-leafed cocculus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in green or reddish clusters.

The fruit is a smooth, indehiscent legume that contains a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine.
