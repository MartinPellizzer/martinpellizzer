Centaurea supina

Centaurea supina, commonly known as the prostrate cornflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Centaurea supina is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.
