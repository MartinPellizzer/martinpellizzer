Cryptomeria japonica

Cryptomeria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat sprays. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The wood of the Japanese cedar is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties.
