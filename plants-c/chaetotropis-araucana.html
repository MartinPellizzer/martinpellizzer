Chaetotropis araucana

Chaetotropis araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria tree, is a species of conifer native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves that form dense, pyramidal crowns. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red covering. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable and vulnerable species in its native habitat.
