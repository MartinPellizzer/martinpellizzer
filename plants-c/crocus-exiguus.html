Crocus exiguus

Crocus exiguus, commonly known as the dwarf crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its hardiness in colder climates. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during unfavorable conditions.

Crocus exiguus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of renewal and hope in many cultures.
