Caesalpinia savannarum

Caesalpinia savannarum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flame-like inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a long, woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

Caesalpinia savannarum is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
