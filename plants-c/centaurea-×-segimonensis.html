Centaurea × segimonensis

Centaurea × segimonensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × segimonensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in various soil conditions.
