Cereus pterocaulis

Cereus pterocaulis, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-colored stems, which are actually a different species grafted onto the main plant. The plant has spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
