Capsicum schottianum

Capsicum schottianum, commonly known as the Schott's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pale green flowers.

The fruit of Capsicum schottianum is often used in traditional cuisines for its mild to moderate heat and unique flavor profile.
