Cucurbita ciceraria

Cucurbita ciceraria, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant produces large, hollow fruits that can be used for storage or as containers. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, sunny environments.

The fruit is typically harvested when immature for culinary use or when mature for ornamental purposes.
