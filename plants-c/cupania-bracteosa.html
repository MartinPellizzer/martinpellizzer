Cupania bracteosa

Cupania bracteosa, commonly known as the Brazilian elder or Brazilian guava, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a small, edible drupe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.
