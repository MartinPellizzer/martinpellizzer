Clematis lasiantha subsp. fallax

Clematis lasiantha subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the fallax clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
