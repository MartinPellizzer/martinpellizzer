Chamaecrista planifolia

Chamaecrista planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
