Chenopodium berlandieri var. berlandieri

Chenopodium berlandieri var. berlandieri, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, ball-like seed heads that resemble small burs. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
