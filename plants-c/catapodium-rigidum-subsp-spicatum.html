Catapodium rigidum subsp. spicatum

Catapodium rigidum subsp.

spicatum, commonly known as the spicate catapodium, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features upright, rigid stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.
