Croton vittatus

Croton vittatus, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, multicolored foliage with shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
