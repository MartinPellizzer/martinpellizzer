Clitoria cerifera

Clitoria cerifera, commonly known as the wax flower or candle flower, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-like purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, round seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
