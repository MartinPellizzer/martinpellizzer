Calpurnia aurea subsp. sylvatica

Calpurnia aurea subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
