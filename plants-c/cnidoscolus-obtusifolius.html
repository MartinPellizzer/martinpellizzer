Cnidoscolus obtusifolius

Cnidoscolus obtusifolius, commonly known as the spiny leaf or prickly pear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, resembling a small, green pear, contains edible seeds and is used in some traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
