Ceratolobus laevigatus var. divaricatus

Ceratolobus laevigatus var. divaricatus, commonly known as the slender coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which are clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
