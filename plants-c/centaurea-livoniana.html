Centaurea livoniana

Centaurea livoniana, commonly known as Livonian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

Centaurea livoniana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
