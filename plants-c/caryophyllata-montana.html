Caryophyllata montana

Caryophyllata montana, commonly known as the mountain carnation, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its compact growth habit and resilience to cold temperatures make it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
