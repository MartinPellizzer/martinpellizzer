Carex panicea f. latifolia

Carex panicea f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved panicled sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a distinctive silver-white underside, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
