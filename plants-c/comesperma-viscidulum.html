Comesperma viscidulum

Comesperma viscidulum, commonly known as the sticky comesperma, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
