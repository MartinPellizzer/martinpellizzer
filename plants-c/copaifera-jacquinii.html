Copaifera jacquinii

Copaifera jacquinii, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and fragrant, resinous sap. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is often found in moist tropical forests. Its bark and sap are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also notable for its role in the production of essential oils and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
