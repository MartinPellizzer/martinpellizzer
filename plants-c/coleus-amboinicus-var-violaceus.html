Coleus amboinicus var. violaceus

Coleus amboinicus var. violaceus, commonly known as purple coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which is often variegated with shades of green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to their vibrant coloration.
