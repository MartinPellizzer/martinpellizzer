Chrysophyllum mexicanum var. politum

Chrysophyllum mexicanum var. politum, commonly known as the Mexican cherry tree, is a species native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit, which resembles a cherry, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
