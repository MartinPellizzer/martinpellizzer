Calytrix viscida

Calytrix viscida, commonly known as the sticky calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky resin, which gives it its common name. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

Calytrix viscida is typically shrub-like in growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
