Cleome chapalensis subsp. chapalensis

Cleome chapalensis subsp.

chapalensis, commonly known as the Mexican spiderflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, spiny flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, or white.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, colorful addition to their landscape.
