Capraria dulcis var. albiflora

Capraria dulcis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet pepper plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits in home gardens.
