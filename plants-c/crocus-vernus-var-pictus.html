Crocus vernus var. pictus

Crocus vernus var. pictus, commonly known as the painted crocus, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features elegant, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and purple, often with a distinctive painted pattern on the petals. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural habitats. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil under full sun to partial shade.

It is also known for its historical use in horticulture and as a symbol of renewal and the arrival of spring.
