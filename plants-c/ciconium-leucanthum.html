Ciconium leucanthum

Ciconium leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered ciconium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features striking white, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Ciconium leucanthum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
