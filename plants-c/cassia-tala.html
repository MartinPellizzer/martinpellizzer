Cassia tala

Cassia tala, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. Cassia tala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with a smooth, glossy surface and a slightly aromatic scent.
