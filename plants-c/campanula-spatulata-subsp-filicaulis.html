Campanula spatulata subsp. filicaulis

Campanula spatulata subsp.

filicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowers.

The subspecies filicaulis is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Campanula spatulata.
