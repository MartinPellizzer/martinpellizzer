Cestrum racemosum var. grande

Cestrum racemosum var. grande, commonly known as the large-flowered yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
