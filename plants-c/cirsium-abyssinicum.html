Cirsium abyssinicum

Cirsium abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian thistle, is a perennial plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or arid environments. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
