Carex arapahoensis

Carex arapahoensis, commonly known as Arapaho sedge, is a perennial plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and riparian zones. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Carex arapahoensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
