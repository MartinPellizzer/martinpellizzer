Centrosema angustifolium

Centrosema angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in agricultural settings for soil nitrogen fixation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
