Cyperus longus f. intermedius

Cyperus longus f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus Cyperus.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
