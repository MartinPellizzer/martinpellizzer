Carex × kurilensis

Carex × kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex × kurilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
