Calamus scutellaris

Calamus scutellaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a tropical plant characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wet, swampy environments.

Calamus scutellaris is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic materials.
