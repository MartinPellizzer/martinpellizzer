Cenchrus paniceus

Cenchrus paniceus, commonly known as panic grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers. The plant is adapted to drought and poor soils, making it a hardy species in arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

It is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
