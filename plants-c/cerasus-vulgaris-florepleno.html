Cerasus vulgaris florepleno

Cerasus vulgaris florepleno, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its showy, double flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded canopy. Its leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth and flowering.
