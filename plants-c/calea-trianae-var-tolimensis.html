Calea trianae var. tolimensis

Calea trianae var. tolimensis, commonly known as the Tolima calea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and forest edges, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
