Cinnagrostis rosea

Cinnagrostis rosea, commonly known as the rose-gold grass, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and vibrant, pinkish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and texture.

Cinnagrostis rosea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
