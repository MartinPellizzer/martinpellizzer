Crepis baldaccii subsp. baldaccii

Crepis baldaccii subsp.

baldaccii, commonly known as the Baldaccii's hairy cinquefoil, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

The species typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often on rocky or gravelly soils. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

The plant is notable for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions and its role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
