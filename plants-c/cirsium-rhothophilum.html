Cirsium rhothophilum

Cirsium rhothophilum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
