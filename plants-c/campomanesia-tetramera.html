Campomanesia tetramera

Campomanesia tetramera, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible and used in local cuisines.

Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its medicinal properties.
