Cimicifuga simplex var. racemosa

Cimicifuga simplex var. racemosa, commonly known as white bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, white, lacecap flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are often fuzzy or hairy. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.
