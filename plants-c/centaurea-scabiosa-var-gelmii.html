Centaurea scabiosa var. gelmii

Centaurea scabiosa var. gelmii, commonly known as the Alaskan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with purple to pink ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually gray-green, hairy, and arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
