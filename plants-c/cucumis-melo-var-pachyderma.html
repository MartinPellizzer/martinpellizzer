Cucumis melo var. pachyderma

Cucumis melo var. pachyderma, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a variant of the melon species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged rind and large, sweet, yellow or orange flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
