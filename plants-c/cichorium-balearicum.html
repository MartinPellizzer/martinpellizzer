Cichorium balearicum

Cichorium balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic chicory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and rosette-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines, while the flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cichorium balearicum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
