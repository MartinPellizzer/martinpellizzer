Cereus montezumae

Cereus montezumae, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce large, showy flowers at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Cereus montezumae is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
