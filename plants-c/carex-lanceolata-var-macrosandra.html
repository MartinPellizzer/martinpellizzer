Carex lanceolata var. macrosandra

Carex lanceolata var. macrosandra, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowering spikes consist of small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it produces distinctive, three-angled berries.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
