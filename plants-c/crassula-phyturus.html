Crassula phyturus

Crassula phyturus, commonly known as the "Cape snowflake," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
