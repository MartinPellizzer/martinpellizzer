Calopogon tuberosus

Calopogon tuberosus, commonly known as the pink marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands in the eastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, tall stem with a single, showy pink or lavender flower. The plant's leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette at the base. It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is adapted to moist, acidic soils.

Calopogon tuberosus is often found in bogs, swamps, and other wetland habitats.
