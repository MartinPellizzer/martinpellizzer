Crotalaria quinata

Crotalaria quinata, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure, and features trifoliate leaves with stipules. The plant produces clusters of yellow, spurred flowers that bloom from summer to autumn. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria quinata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for medicinal purposes.
