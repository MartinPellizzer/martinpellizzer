Cattleya × guatemalensis

Cattleya × guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical climates. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees in its natural habitat.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
