Caltha leptosepala var. macounii

Caltha leptosepala var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to moist, acidic soils in North America.

It features striking yellow flowers with reflexed petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
