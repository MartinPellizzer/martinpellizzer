Crassula gracilis

Crassula gracilis, commonly known as the string of pearls plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, bead-like leaves that form a cascading appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often used as a decorative indoor or outdoor plant.
