Calamintha pamphylica subsp. davisii

Calamintha pamphylica subsp.

davisii, commonly known as the Davis's calamint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The foliage is often used in herbal remedies due to its mild mint-like scent and potential medicinal properties.
