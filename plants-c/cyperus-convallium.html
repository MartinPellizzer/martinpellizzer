Cyperus convallium

Cyperus convallium, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus convallium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
