Chrysodium harlandii

Chrysodium harlandii, commonly known as the Harland's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
