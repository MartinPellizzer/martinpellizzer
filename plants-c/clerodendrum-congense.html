Clerodendrum congense

Clerodendrum congense, commonly known as the Congo flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
