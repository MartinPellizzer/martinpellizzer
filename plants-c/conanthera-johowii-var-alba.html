Conanthera johowii var. alba

Conanthera johowii var. alba, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to October. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a spiky, cone-shaped inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
