Colocasia vulgaris

Colocasia vulgaris, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its striking foliage.

The plant produces a spadix inflorescence, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.
