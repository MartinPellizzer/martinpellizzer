Cinnamomum pedunculatum

Cinnamomum pedunculatum, commonly known as the Java cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-like bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by oval, dark green fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are glossy, elliptical, and have a strong, spicy aroma.

The bark is harvested for its essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
