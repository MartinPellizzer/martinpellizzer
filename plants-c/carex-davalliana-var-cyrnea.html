Carex davalliana var. cyrnea

Carex davalliana var. cyrnea, commonly known as the pink sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pink or reddish in color, which distinguish it from other sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
