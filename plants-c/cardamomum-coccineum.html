Cardamomum coccineum

Cardamomum coccineum, commonly known as the red cardamom or crimson spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences.

Although not widely cultivated for its seeds like true cardamom, it is appreciated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
