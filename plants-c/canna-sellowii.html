Canna sellowii

Canna sellowii, commonly known as the yellow canna, is a striking perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Canna sellowii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
