Cyanus collinus

Cyanus collinus, commonly known as the ray floret or the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets that contrast with its yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cyanus collinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
