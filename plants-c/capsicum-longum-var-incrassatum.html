Capsicum longum var. incrassatum

Capsicum longum var. incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-fruit chili pepper, is a variant of the long pepper species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that typically mature to a dark red or orange color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by the fleshy, elongated pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its mild to medium heat level and is often used in culinary applications across various cuisines.
