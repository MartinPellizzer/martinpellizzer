Carex × paulii

Carex × paulii, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical and contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex × paulii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
