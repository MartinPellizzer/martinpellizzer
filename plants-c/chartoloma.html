Chartoloma

Chartoloma, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has a dense canopy and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Chartoloma is also known for its aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
