Cyathea caracasana

Cyathea caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas hakea, is a species of tree fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded areas of rainforests. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor deeply into the soil, providing stability in moist, nutrient-rich substrates.

Cyathea caracasana plays a significant role in its ecosystem by offering habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
