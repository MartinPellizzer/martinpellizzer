Cestrum psychotrifolium

Cestrum psychotrifolium, commonly known as the purple cestrum, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of purple and white. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, sweet taste. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable regions.
