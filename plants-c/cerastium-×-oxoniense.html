Cerastium × oxoniense

Cerastium × oxoniense, commonly known as the Oxford chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
