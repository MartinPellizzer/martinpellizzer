Chrysanthemum alabasicum

Chrysanthemum alabasicum, commonly known as the alabaster chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant is known for its striking, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
