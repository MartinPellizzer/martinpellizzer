Crassula obtusa

Crassula obtusa, commonly known as the Fat Plant or Little Baby Crassula, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish tinge, arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the growing season. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Crassula obtusa is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
