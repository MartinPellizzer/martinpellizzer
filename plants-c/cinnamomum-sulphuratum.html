Cinnamomum sulphuratum

Cinnamomum sulphuratum, commonly known as the sulfur cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-brown bark that emits a distinct cinnamon scent when broken. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries containing winged seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy fragrance.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
