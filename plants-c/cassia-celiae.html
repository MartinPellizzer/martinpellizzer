Cassia celiae

Cassia celiae, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant can grow up to several meters in height.

Cassia celiae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
