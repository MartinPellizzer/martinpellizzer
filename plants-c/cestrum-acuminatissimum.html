Cestrum acuminatissimum

Cestrum acuminatissimum, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.

Cestrum acuminatissimum is also noted for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
