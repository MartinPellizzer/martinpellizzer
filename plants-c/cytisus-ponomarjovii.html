Cytisus ponomarjovii

Cytisus ponomarjovii, commonly known as the Ponomarjov's broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus ponomarjovii is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
