Cornus poliophylla var. praelonga

Cornus poliophylla var. praelonga, commonly known as the long-leaved dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elongated leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
