Crotalaria prolongata

Crotalaria prolongata, commonly known as the long-stalked cowpea, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces yellow or orange flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and is known for its distinctive elongated pods. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Crotalaria prolongata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
