Colocasia antiquorum f. yamamotoi

Colocasia antiquorum f. yamamotoi, commonly known as the Yamamoto's elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter.

The foliage is typically a deep green with a waxy texture and often exhibits a reddish or purple tint along the veins. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in the summer months.
