Callicarpa oblongifolia

Callicarpa oblongifolia, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green on top and lighter in color underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa oblongifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
