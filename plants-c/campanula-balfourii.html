Campanula balfourii

Campanula balfourii, commonly known as the Sierra bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Campanula balfourii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
