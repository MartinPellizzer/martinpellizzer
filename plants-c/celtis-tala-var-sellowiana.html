Celtis tala var. sellowiana

Celtis tala var. sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Elm family (Ulmaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round fruits that are typically dispersed by wind.
