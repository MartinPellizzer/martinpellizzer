Cryptocarya acutifolia

Cryptocarya acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, acuminate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers and is typically found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Cryptocarya acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
