Corydalis × laxa

Corydalis × laxa, commonly known as the lax flowerpea, is a hybrid species within the genus Corydalis, belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, bean-like seed pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Corydalis × laxa is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a subject of botanical study due to its unique hybrid traits.
