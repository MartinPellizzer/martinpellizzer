Carex glauca var. leptostachys

Carex glauca var. leptostachys, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

Its blue-gray foliage and delicate flowers make it a visually appealing addition to wetland landscapes.
