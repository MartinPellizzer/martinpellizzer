Capparis obovata

Capparis obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Capparis obovata is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and rocky environments due to its drought tolerance.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
