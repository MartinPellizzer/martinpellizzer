Calceolaria incarum

Calceolaria incarum, commonly known as the "slipper orchid," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as along streams or in forest understories. Its flowers are often violet or purple in color and are pollinated by insects.

Calceolaria incarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
