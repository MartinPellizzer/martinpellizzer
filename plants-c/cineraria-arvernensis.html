Cineraria arvernensis

Cineraria arvernensis, commonly known as the French cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Cineraria arvernensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
