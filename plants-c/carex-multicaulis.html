Carex multicaulis

Carex multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex multicaulis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
