Columnea vinacea

Columnea vinacea, commonly known as the red-flowered coca, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Columnea vinacea is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
