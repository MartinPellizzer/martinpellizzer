Carex paniculata var. paradoxa

Carex paniculata var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Carex, is a striking sedge native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, upright spikes of white to pale green flowers that appear in late summer, adding a unique visual interest to gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a cool, fresh appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for wetland or bog gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
