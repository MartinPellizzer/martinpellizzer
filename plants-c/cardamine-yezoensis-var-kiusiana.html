Cardamine yezoensis var. kiusiana

Cardamine yezoensis var. kiusiana, commonly known as the Yezo Watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to parts of East Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is noted for its adaptability to cool, humid climates and its role in ecological systems as a food source for various insects and small animals.
