Clerodendrum excavatum var. cuneatum

Clerodendrum excavatum var. cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf champa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
