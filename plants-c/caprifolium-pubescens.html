Caprifolium pubescens

Caprifolium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its berries are red or orange and contain several seeds.

Caprifolium pubescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
