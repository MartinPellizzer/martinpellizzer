Castanea javanica var. fuscescens

Castanea javanica var. fuscescens, commonly known as the Javan chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by spiny, cup-like structures that enclose its edible nuts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
