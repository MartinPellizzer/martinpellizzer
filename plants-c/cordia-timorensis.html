Cordia timorensis

Cordia timorensis, commonly known as the Timor hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
