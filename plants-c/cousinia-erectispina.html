Cousinia erectispina

Cousinia erectispina, commonly known as the upright spiny couch grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense heads.

Cousinia erectispina is known for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
