Carduus argiroa

Carduus argiroa, commonly known as the silver thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Carduus argiroa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
