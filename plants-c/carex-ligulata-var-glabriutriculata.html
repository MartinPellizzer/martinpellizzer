Carex ligulata var. glabriutriculata

Carex ligulata var. glabriutriculata, commonly known as the smooth-ribbed sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The variety glabriutriculata is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and reduced leaf ribs.
