Calea liebmannii

Calea liebmannii, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day, a trait that gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, which resembles a cluster of small white petals. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Calea liebmannii is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
