Cousinia arbelensis var. pinnata

Cousinia arbelensis var. pinnata, commonly known as the pinnate desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The leaves are typically pinnately divided, giving the plant its common name, and are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
