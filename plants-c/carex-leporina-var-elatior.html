Carex leporina var. elatior

Carex leporina var. elatior, commonly known as the greater sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
