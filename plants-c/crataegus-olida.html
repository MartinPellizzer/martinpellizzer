Crataegus olida

Crataegus olida, commonly known as the Brazilian hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus olida is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
