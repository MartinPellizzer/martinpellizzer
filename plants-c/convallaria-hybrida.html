Convallaria hybrida

Convallaria hybrida, commonly known as Lily of the Valley, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Convallaria hybrida is also known for its toxic properties, containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful if ingested.
