Croton macrolepis

Croton macrolepis, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
