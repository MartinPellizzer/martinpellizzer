Choerospondias axillaris

Choerospondias axillaris, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn bright orange when mature, containing a single seed. It is valued for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The tree is also known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
