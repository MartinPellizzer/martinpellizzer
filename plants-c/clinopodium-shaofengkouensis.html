Clinopodium shaofengkouensis

Clinopodium shaofengkouensis, commonly known as Shaofengkou mint, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters.

Clinopodium shaofengkouensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
