Crocus albiflorus subsp. neapolitanus

Crocus albiflorus subsp.

neapolitanus, commonly known as the Naples crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and subtle floral display.
