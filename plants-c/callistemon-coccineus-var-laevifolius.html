Callistemon coccineus var. laevifolius

Callistemon coccineus var. laevifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking crimson spikes of flowers that resemble bottle brushes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract native birds and pollinators.
