Carex christyana

Carex christyana, commonly known as Christy's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered brownish flowers in late summer.

Carex christyana is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
