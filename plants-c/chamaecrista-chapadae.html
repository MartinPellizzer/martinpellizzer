Chamaecrista chapadae

Chamaecrista chapadae, commonly known as the Chapada velvetbean, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Chamaecrista chapadae is adapted to tropical and subtropical environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
