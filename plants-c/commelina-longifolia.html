Commelina longifolia

Commelina longifolia, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect stem and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are often clustered in a spathaceous bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina longifolia is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
