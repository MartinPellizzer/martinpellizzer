Coryphantha macromeris subsp. runyonii

Coryphantha macromeris subsp.

runyonii, commonly known as the Runyon's giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its large, spiny, globular stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in the axils of the spines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
