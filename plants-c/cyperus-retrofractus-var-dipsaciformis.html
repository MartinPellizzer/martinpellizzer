Cyperus retrofractus var. dipsaciformis

Cyperus retrofractus var. dipsaciformis, commonly known as the spike rush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in coastal areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
