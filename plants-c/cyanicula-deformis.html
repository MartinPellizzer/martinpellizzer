Cyanicula deformis

Cyanicula deformis, commonly known as the twisted blue orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unusual, curling leaves and vibrant blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology, which includes a compact growth habit and specialized root structures. Cyanicula deformis thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
