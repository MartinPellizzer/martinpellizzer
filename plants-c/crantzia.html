Crantzia

Crantzia, commonly known as sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal areas and are adapted to salty, well-drained soils. They produce showy, spiky flower heads that are often blue or purple in color. Crantzia species are perennial herbs with silver-gray foliage and robust stems.

They are valued in gardens for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
