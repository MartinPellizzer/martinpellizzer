Commiphora Rostrata Var. Reflexa

Reflexed myrrh, scientifically known as Commiphora rostrata var. reflexa, is a rare and unique variant of the myrrh tree native to parts of East Africa. This plant is characterized by its distinctive reflexed leaf margins, which give it a striking visual appearance. It produces a resin that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and aromatic practices. Despite its historical significance, it remains a little-known species in modern botanical studies. Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
