Cephalaria scabra

Cephalaria scabra, commonly known as roughhead goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture.

Cephalaria scabra is often used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
