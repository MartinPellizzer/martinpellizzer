Corchorus procumbens

Corchorus procumbens, commonly known as Marshmallow or Velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with hairy, fleshy leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are rich in mucilage, making it useful in traditional medicine for soothing sore throats and digestive issues.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its fibrous stems in some regions.
