Carex hornschuchiana f. atrata

Carex hornschuchiana f. atrata, commonly known as the Black-leaf sedge, is a striking perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, foliage that contrasts beautifully with its pale, grass-like blades. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, greenish-brown flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer.

Carex hornschuchiana f. atrata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
