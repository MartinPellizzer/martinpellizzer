Carex japonoalpina

Carex japonoalpina, commonly known as the Japanese alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex japonoalpina is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
