Calcitrapa pontica

Calcitrapa pontica, commonly known as the Pontic calcitrapa, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, showcasing its adaptability to alkaline conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense cluster.

Calcitrapa pontica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky slopes.
