Clompanus excelsa

Clompanus excelsa, commonly known as the towering marsh orchid, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Clompanus excelsa plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
