Chrysojasminum floridum

Chrysojasminum floridum, commonly known as the Florida golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
