Caryophyllus diminutus

Caryophyllus diminutus, commonly known as the dwarf carnation, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
