Calendula balansae

Calendula balansae, commonly known as the Balansae marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Calendula balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
