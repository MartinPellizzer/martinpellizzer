Celtis lactea

Celtis lactea, commonly known as the white-fruited hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy berries containing a single seed. Celtis lactea is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Its fruit is an important food source for various bird and mammal species.
