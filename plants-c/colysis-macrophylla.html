Colysis macrophylla

Colysis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant has broad, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, providing dense shade. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens and urban landscapes.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.
