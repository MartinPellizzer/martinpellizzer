Cissus okoutensis

Cissus okoutensis, commonly known as the African dewberry, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, greenish fruits that are often consumed by birds and other animals. Its stems are succulent and can be used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.

Cissus okoutensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
