Cytisus brasiliensis

Cytisus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus brasiliensis is adapted to various soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
