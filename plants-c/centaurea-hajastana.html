Centaurea hajastana

Centaurea hajastana, commonly known as the Armenian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has spiny leaves and a sturdy, upright stem, which helps it thrive in rocky and arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and mountain slopes.

Centaurea hajastana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
