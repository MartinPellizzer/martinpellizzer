Crocus annulatus subvar. caerulescens

Crocus annulatus subvar. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-crocus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded corms for storage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
