Crepis crithmifolia

Crepis crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, silver-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in maritime climates. Its flowers, which bloom in summer, are blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crepis crithmifolia is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
