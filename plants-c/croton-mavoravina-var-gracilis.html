Croton mavoravina var. gracilis

Croton mavoravina var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage with a combination of green, yellow, and red hues. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
