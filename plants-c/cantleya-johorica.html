Cantleya johorica

Cantleya johorica, commonly known as the Johor cantleya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Singapore.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to habitat loss, Cantleya johorica is considered a rare and endangered species.
