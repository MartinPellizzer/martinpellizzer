Conyza asperifolia

Conyza asperifolia, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to produce a large number of small seeds.

Conyza asperifolia is often considered a weed but has ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
