Convolvulus gortschakovii

Convolvulus gortschakovii, commonly known as the Gortschakov's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and hairy. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
