Commelina communis f. aureostriata

Commelina communis f. aureostriata, commonly known as the golden-striped spiderwort, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking, bright yellow stripes on its leaves.

It belongs to the Commelina genus and is part of the Commelinaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that are typically clustered in a spathe, a modified leaf-like structure. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
