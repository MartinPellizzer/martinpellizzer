Cerastium murbeckii

Cerastium murbeckii, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Cerastium murbeckii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
