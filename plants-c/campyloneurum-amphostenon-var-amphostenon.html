Campyloneurum amphostenon var. amphostenon

Campyloneurum amphostenon var. amphostenon, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value.
