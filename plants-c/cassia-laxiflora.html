Cassia laxiflora

Cassia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Cassia laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
