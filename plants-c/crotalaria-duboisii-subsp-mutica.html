Crotalaria duboisii subsp. mutica

Crotalaria duboisii subsp.

mutica, commonly known as the narrowleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellow flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It is typically found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, contributing to its ornamental appeal in certain regions.
