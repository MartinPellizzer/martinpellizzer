Campanula rhomboidalis

Campanula rhomboidalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula rhomboidalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
