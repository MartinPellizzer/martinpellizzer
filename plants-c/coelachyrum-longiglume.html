Coelachyrum longiglume

Coelachyrum longiglume, commonly known as the long glume grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with distinctive, elongated glumes. Its botanical features make it a common component of grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
