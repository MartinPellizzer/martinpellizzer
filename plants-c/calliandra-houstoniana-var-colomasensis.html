Calliandra houstoniana var. colomasensis

Calliandra houstoniana var. colomasensis, commonly known as the Mexican honeycomb tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, honeycomb-like pods that contain numerous small seeds. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
