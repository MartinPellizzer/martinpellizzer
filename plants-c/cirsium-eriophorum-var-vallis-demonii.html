Cirsium eriophorum var. vallis-demonii

Cirsium eriophorum var. vallis-demonii, commonly known as the Devil's-bit Scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive appearance and historical associations with the devil.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
