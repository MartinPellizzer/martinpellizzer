Crataegus inaudita

Crataegus inaudita, commonly known as the silent hawthorn, is a rare and little-studied species of hawthorn native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and red berries that appear in the autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and temperate climates. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Crataegus inaudita is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
