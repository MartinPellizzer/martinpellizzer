Ciponima

Ciponima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically divided into five to seven lobes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ciponima is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
