Chamaecrista cuneata

Chamaecrista cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or clay soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds.

Chamaecrista cuneata is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and spread.
