Clitoria gemina

Clitoria gemina, commonly known as the double butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Clitoria gemina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
