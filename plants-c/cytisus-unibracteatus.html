Cytisus unibracteatus

Cytisus unibracteatus, commonly known as the single-bract broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, characterized by its pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets, and it has a woody stem with thorn-like branches.

Cytisus unibracteatus is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
