Crataegus escalloniifolia

Crataegus escalloniifolia, commonly known as the Escalloniifolia hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorned branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
