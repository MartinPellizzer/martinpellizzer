Cytisus spinescens var. ciliatus

Cytisus spinescens var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, spiny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are compound, with leaflets that have hairy margins, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
