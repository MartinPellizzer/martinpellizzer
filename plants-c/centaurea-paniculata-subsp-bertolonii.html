Centaurea paniculata subsp. bertolonii

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, branched inflorescences of bright yellow to orange flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with serrated margins. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
