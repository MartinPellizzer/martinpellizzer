Croton horminoideus

Croton horminoideus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in yellow or greenish cyathia, typical of the genus Croton.

The plant is also notable for its latex, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
