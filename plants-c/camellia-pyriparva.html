Camellia pyriparva

Camellia pyriparva, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern Japan.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers are usually red or pink, with a distinctive cup-shaped structure and a central boss of stamens.

Camellia pyriparva prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
