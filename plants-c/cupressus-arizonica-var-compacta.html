Cupressus arizonica var. compacta

Cupressus arizonica var. compacta, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a compact, evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage that gives it a lush, green appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable growing conditions.
