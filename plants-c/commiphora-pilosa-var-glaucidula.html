Commiphora pilosa var. glaucidula

Commiphora pilosa var. glaucidula, commonly known as the woolly myrrh.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, and the species is known for its aromatic properties.

The varietal name 'glaucidula' refers to the bluish-gray coloration of its leaves.
