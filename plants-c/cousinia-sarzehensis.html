Cousinia sarzehensis

Cousinia sarzehensis, commonly known as the Karakoram dwarf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Karakoram Mountains in Pakistan.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, cold climates. Its small, yellowish-white flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia sarzehensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
