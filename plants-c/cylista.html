Cylista

Cylista, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 90 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Cylista is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, and it is known for its medicinal properties.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
