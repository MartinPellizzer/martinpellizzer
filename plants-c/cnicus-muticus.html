Cnicus muticus

Cnicus muticus, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and features small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Cnicus muticus is a hardy species that is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
