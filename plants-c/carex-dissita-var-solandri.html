Carex dissita var. solandri

Carex dissita var. solandri, commonly known as the Solander sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
