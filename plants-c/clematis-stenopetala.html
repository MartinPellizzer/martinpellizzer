Clematis stenopetala

Clematis stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, supporting its climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers with five narrow, elongated petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

Clematis stenopetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
