Calliandra obesiflora

Calliandra obesiflora, commonly known as the fatflower or coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its foliage. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stem is often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Calliandra obesiflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
