Crotalaria aspalathoides

Crotalaria aspalathoides, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
