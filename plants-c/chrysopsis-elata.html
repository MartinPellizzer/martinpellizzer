Chrysopsis elata

Chrysopsis elata, commonly known as the tall goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chrysopsis elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
