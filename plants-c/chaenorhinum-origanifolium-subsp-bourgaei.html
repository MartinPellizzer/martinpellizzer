Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp. bourgaei

Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp.

bourgaei, commonly known as the narrowleaf flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of oregano.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
