Cassia fasciculata var. macrosperma

Cassia fasciculata var. macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow to orange inflorescences, which are clustered and resemble flames, giving it its common name. Its flowers are followed by elongated, woody pods containing numerous ellipsoidal seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
