Citrus × aurantium subvar. margarita

Citrus × aurantium subvar. margarita, commonly known as the pomelo or sometimes the grapefruit, is a citrus hybrid with origins in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits that can weigh up to 10 pounds. The plant is a small evergreen tree with fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a mature size and a deep yellow or orange color.
