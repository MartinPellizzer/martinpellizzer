Commelina cucullata

Commelina cucullata, commonly known as the hooded dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with a hood-like structure over the reproductive parts. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Commelina cucullata is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
