Crataegus columbiana var. occidentalis

Crataegus columbiana var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a red, juicy berry that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
