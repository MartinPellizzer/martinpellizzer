Carex huangshanica

Carex huangshanica, commonly known as the Huangshan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, while its flowering structures consist of small, clustered spikes.

Carex huangshanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
