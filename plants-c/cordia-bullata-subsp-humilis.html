Cordia bullata subsp. humilis

Cordia bullata subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the low cordia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually gland-dotted and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated, orange to red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
