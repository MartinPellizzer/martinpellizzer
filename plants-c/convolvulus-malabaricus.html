Convolvulus malabaricus

Convolvulus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern and southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
