Calamagrostis pallida

Calamagrostis pallida, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name, and it typically grows in wet or moist environments. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in the summer. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Calamagrostis pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
