Coleus hymalis

Coleus hymalis, commonly known as the silverleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamentals.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, Coleus hymalis is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its ability to attract pollinators.
