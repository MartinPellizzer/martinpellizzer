Ceratogyne obionoides

Ceratogyne obionoides, commonly known as the slender spurred orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with distinctive spurred petals that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The leaves are linear and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal in cultivation.
