Cyclopogon trilineatus

Cyclopogon trilineatus, commonly known as the three-lined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate pseudobulbs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
