Crepinella gracilis

Crepinella gracilis, commonly known as the slender crepe jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in racemes.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
