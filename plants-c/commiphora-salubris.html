Commiphora salubris

Commiphora salubris, commonly known as the Indian frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been historically used for medicinal and incense purposes. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
