Chaenomeles japonica var. gigantea

Chaenomeles japonica var. gigantea, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince giant variety, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces ornamental red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to add color to gardens in different seasons.
