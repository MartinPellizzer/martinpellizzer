Cacalia firma var. pseudotaimingasa

Cacalia firma var. pseudotaimingasa, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
