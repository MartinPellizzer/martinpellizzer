Cestrum laetum

Cestrum laetum, commonly known as the happy trumpet plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum laetum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
