Chrysopteris peltidea

Chrysopteris peltidea, commonly known as the golden pelt fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, golden hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Chrysopteris peltidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
