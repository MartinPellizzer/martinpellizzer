Centaurea thessalonica

Centaurea thessalonica, commonly known as the Thessaly centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits.

Centaurea thessalonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
