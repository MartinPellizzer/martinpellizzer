Cryptolepis capensis

Cryptolepis capensis, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces smooth, orange-brown fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and forest margins.

Cryptolepis capensis is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
