Celastrus jeholensis

Celastrus jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol bittersweet, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional medicine, though its botanical properties remain under study.
