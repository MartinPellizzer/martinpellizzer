Coris monspeliensis var. maroccana

Coris monspeliensis var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan coris, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
