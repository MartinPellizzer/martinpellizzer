Cyperus karisimbiensis

Cyperus karisimbiensis, commonly known as the Karisimbi flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swampy areas and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
