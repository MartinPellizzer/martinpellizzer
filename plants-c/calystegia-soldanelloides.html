Calystegia soldanelloides

Calystegia soldanelloides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a trailing or climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, brown seeds.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.
