Cestrum pedunculare

Cestrum pedunculare, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Cestrum pedunculare is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
