Chamaecrista choriophylla

Chamaecrista choriophylla, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds are encased in a pod that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
