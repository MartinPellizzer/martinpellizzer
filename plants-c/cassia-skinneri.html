Cassia skinneri

Cassia skinneri, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia skinneri is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
