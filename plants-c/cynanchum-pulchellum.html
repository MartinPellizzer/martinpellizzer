Cynanchum pulchellum

Cynanchum pulchellum, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Cynanchum pulchellum is adapted to moist, woodland habitats and is known for its attractive appearance and ecological value to pollinators.
