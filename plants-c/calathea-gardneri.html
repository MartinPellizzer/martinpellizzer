Calathea gardneri

Calathea gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with intricate patterns of green, white, and silver. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture that enhances their visual appeal. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
