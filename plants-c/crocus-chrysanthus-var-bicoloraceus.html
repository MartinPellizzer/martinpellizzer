Crocus chrysanthus var. bicoloraceus

Crocus chrysanthus var. bicoloraceus, commonly known as the bicolor crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The flowers are characterized by their distinctive bicolor pattern, with a white or pale yellow center and a deeper yellow or orange outer petal. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its early blooming habit and ornamental appeal.
