Cinnamomum appelianum var. tripartitum

Cinnamomum appelianum var. tripartitum, commonly known as the three-parted cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by dark purple or black berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has economic significance in certain regions.
