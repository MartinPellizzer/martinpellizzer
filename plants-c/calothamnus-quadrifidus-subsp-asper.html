Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp. asper

Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp.

asper, commonly known as the rough-leaved waratah, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered with fine, rough hairs, giving the plant its subspecies name "asper.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
