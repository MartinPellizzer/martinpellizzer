Cydonia oblonga var. biserrulata

Cydonia oblonga var. biserrulata, commonly known as the Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit that turns a deep orange-red when mature. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The fruit is typically harvested in late autumn and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
