Chorizema baxteri

Chorizema baxteri, commonly known as the Baxter's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, branching stems that are covered in small, cylindrical segments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its arid habitat. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its thick, fleshy stems.

Chorizema baxteri plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
