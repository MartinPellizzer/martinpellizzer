Chorispora persica var. stipitatoglandulosa

Chorispora persica var. stipitatoglandulosa, commonly known as the stipitate glandular chorispora, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The variety is distinguished by its stipitate (stalked) glands on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique botanical classification.
