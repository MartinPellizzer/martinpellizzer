Cestrum nocturnum

Cestrum nocturnum, commonly known as nightshade or white star of night, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is also known for its toxic berries, which contain alkaloids and should not be ingested.
