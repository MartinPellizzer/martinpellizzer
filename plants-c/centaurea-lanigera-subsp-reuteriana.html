Centaurea lanigera subsp. reuteriana

Centaurea lanigera subsp.

reuteriana, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a single, tall stem bearing a cluster of small, tubular flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, coastal conditions.
