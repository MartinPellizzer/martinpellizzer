Calamus rhomboideus

Calamus rhomboideus, commonly known as the rhomboid arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its stout, rhizomatous stems and large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

The rhizomes of Calamus rhomboideus are often used as a starchy food source in traditional cuisines.
