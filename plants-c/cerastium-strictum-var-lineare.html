Cerastium strictum var. lineare

Cerastium strictum var. lineare, commonly known as the linear mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, linear leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in damp, temperate regions.
