Cotula abrotanifolia

Cotula abrotanifolia, commonly known as lesser crownflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Cotula abrotanifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
