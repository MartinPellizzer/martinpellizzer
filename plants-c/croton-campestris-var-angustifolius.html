Croton campestris var. angustifolius

Croton campestris var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red-orange berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
