Calanthe tuberculosa

Calanthe tuberculosa, commonly known as the wrinkled calanthe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Calanthe tuberculosa thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
