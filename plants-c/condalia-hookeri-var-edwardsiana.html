Condalia hookeri var. edwardsiana

Condalia hookeri var. edwardsiana, commonly known as the Edwards' condalia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower clusters typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
