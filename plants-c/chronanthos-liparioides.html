Chronanthos liparioides

Chronanthos liparioides, commonly known as the Lipari Island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
