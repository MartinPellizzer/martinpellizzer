Centaurea boissieri subsp. dufourii

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

dufourii, commonly known as the Dufour's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bright blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
