Carex zonata

Carex zonata, commonly known as the zoned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds.

Carex zonata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
