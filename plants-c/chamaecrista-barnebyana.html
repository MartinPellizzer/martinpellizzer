Chamaecrista barnebyana

Chamaecrista barnebyana, commonly known as the Barneby's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in disturbed habitats and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
