Cerastium caesareosylvaticum subf. eglandulosum

Cerastium caesareosylvaticum subf.

eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white flowers with five petals.

The plant is characterized by its glandular structures on the stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.
