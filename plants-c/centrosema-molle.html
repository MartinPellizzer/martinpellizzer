Centrosema molle

Centrosema molle, commonly known as hairy leadplant, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Centrosema molle is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
