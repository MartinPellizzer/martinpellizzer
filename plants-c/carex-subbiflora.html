Carex subbiflora

Carex subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct central vein, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers.

Carex subbiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
