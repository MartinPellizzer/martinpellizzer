Campanula rotundifolia f. glabrescens

Campanula rotundifolia f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy stems that become smooth with age, giving it its specific epithet "glabrescens.". The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are round and slightly lobed, with a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.
