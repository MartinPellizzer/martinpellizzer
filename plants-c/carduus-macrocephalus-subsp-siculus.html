Carduus macrocephalus subsp. siculus

Carduus macrocephalus subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust, upright stem covered in coarse, spiny hairs.

The plant produces large, showy purple to pink flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
