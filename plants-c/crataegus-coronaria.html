Crataegus coronaria

Crataegus coronaria, commonly known as the crownflower, is a species of hawthorn native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
