Campanula sibirica var. imeretina

Campanula sibirica var. imeretina, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in loose clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to cooler climates and well-drained soils.
