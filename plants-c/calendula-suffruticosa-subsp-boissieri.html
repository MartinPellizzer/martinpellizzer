Calendula suffruticosa subsp. boissieri

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Turkish calendula, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features woody stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with prominent central disks, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
