Cytisus biflorus subsp. elongatus

Cytisus biflorus subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as the long-flowered broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often arching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are compound, typically consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. This subspecies is adapted to sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
