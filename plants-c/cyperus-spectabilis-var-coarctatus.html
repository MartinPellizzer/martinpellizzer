Cyperus spectabilis var. coarctatus

Cyperus spectabilis var. coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
