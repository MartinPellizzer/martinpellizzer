Cassia bicapsularis var. tenuifolia

Cassia bicapsularis var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
