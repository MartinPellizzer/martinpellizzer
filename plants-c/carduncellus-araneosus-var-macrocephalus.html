Carduncellus araneosus var. macrocephalus

Carduncellus araneosus var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant spiny spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, and lobed leaves, which are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. This species is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation and is a defense mechanism against herbivores.
