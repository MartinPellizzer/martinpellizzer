Clematis polymorpha

Clematis polymorpha, commonly known as the climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple. The plant has a strong climbing habit, using its tendrils to attach to supports. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis polymorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth.
