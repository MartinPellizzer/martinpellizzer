Cassia psoraleopsis

Cassia psoraleopsis, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom in the summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Cassia psoraleopsis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
