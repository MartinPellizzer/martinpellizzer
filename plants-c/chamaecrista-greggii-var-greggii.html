Chamaecrista greggii var. greggii

Chamaecrista greggii var. greggii, commonly known as Gregg's ticktree, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring small, compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant has a taproot system, allowing it to survive in dry conditions by accessing deep water sources. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
