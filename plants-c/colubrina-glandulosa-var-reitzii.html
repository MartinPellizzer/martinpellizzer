Colubrina glandulosa var. reitzii

Colubrina glandulosa var. reitzii, commonly known as Reitz's glandular colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by oval, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in pine flatwood habitats.
