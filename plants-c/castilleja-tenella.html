Castilleja tenella

Castilleja tenella, commonly known as the Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its showy, red-orange bracts that resemble paintbrushes. Castilleja tenella blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
