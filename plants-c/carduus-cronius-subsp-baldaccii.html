Carduus cronius subsp. baldaccii

Carduus cronius subsp.

baldaccii, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
