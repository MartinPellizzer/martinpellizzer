Carex saltuensis

Carex saltuensis, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant typically grows in saline or brackish environments, such as coastal marshes and estuaries. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in three rows, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex saltuensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
