Combretum paniculatum subsp. microphyllum

Combretum paniculatum subsp.

microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
