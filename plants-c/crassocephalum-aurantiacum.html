Crassocephalum aurantiacum

Crassocephalum aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
