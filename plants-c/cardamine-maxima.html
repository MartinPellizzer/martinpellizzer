Cardamine maxima

Cardamine maxima, commonly known as broad-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like mustard and cabbage.
