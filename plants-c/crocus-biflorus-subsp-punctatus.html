Crocus biflorus subsp. punctatus

Crocus biflorus subsp.

punctatus, commonly known as the spotted crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It features striking, early spring blooms with violet-blue petals and distinctive yellow spots.

The plant produces two to three flowers per stem, which are often accompanied by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to temperate climates.
