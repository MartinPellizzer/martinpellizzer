Clinopodium dalmaticum

Clinopodium dalmaticum, commonly known as Dalmatian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval fruits called nutlets.

Clinopodium dalmaticum is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
