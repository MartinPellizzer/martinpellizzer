Cerastium alpinum lusus macrocarpum

Cerastium alpinum lusus macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas.
