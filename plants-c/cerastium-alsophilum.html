Cerastium alsophilum

Cerastium alsophilum, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants characterized by their showy blooms and succulent leaves. The plant typically features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
