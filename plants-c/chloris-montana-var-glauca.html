Chloris montana var. glauca

Chloris montana var. glauca, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
