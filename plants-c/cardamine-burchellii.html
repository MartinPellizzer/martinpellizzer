Cardamine burchellii

Cardamine burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive, peppery aroma, and it produces small white or greenish flowers arranged in racemes. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cardamine burchellii is also valued as a nutritious leafy green in various cuisines.
