Cactus regalis

Cactus regalis, commonly known as the royal cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height, covered in spines and areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, round fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Cactus regalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in xeriscaped gardens.
