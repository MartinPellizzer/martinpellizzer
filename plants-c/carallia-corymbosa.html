Carallia corymbosa

Carallia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered holly or Christmas holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are glossy and evergreen, providing ornamental value in gardens.

Carallia corymbosa thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.
