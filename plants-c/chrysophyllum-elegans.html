Chrysophyllum elegans

Chrysophyllum elegans, commonly known as the elegant chrysophyllum, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sweet, yellow-orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
