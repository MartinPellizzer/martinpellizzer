Chironia rubrocoerulea

Chironia rubrocoerulea, commonly known as the blue-and-red orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, bi-colored flowers that display a combination of deep red and bright blue hues. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, Chironia rubrocoerulea is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
