Caryota tremula

Caryota tremula, commonly known as the fishtail palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like fronds that resemble the tail of a fish, hence its common name. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that often develops a fibrous sheath. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow segments that give it a unique appearance.

Caryota tremula is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
