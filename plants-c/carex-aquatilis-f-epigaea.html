Carex aquatilis f. epigaea

Carex aquatilis f. epigaea, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a variant of the bog sedge species native to wetland environments.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to water-saturated soils, often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
