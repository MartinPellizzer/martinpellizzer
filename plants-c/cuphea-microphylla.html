Cuphea microphylla

Cuphea microphylla, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, tubular red or pink flowers that resemble wax. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Cuphea microphylla is also known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
