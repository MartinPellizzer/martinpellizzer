Clematis simsii var. micrantha

Clematis simsii var. micrantha, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded areas.
