Callistemon lanceolatus var. lophanthus

Callistemon lanceolatus var. lophanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush flowers that appear in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
