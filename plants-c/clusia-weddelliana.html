Clusia weddelliana

Clusia weddelliana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the year. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various light conditions.

Clusia weddelliana is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid environments and its resilience to poor soil conditions.
