Cucurbita ficifolia

Cucurbita ficifolia, commonly known as the figleaf gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble fig leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces yellow or orange fruits that are typically 10 to 30 centimeters in length. The fruit has a thick, fibrous rind and a sweet, edible pulp with small, edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in some cuisines.
