Cacalia marginata

Cacalia marginata, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually pale blue or white, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Cacalia marginata is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
