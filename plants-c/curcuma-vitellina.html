Curcuma vitellina

Curcuma vitellina, commonly known as the golden ginger or yellow turmeric, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The rhizomes of Curcuma vitellina are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other turmeric species.
