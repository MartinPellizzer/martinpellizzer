Campanula tridentata subsp. biebersteiniana

Campanula tridentata subsp.

biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and ability to withstand cold climates.
