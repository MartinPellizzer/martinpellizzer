Calea ovalis subsp. sabanensis

Calea ovalis subsp.

sabanensis, commonly known as the Saban Calea, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive central disc. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are usually ovate and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
