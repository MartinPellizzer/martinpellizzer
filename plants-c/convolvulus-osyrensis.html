Convolvulus osyrensis

Convolvulus osyrensis, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a trailing or climbing annual herb with hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Convolvulus osyrensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
