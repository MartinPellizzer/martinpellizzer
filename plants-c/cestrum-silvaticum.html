Cestrum silvaticum

Cestrum silvaticum, commonly known as the wild nightshade or silver trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often blooming at night. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Cestrum silvaticum is also known for its fragrant scent, which attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.
