Colea commersonii

Colea commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators.

Colea commersonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
