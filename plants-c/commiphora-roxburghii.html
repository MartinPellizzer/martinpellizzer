Commiphora roxburghii

Commiphora roxburghii, commonly known as Indian frankincense or guggul, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is tapped from its bark and used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The resin, known as guggul, has been used for centuries in Ayurvedic practices for its purported health benefits.
