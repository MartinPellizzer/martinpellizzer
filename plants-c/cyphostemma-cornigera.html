Cyphostemma cornigera

Cyphostemma cornigera, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
