Carex jamesii

Carex jamesii, commonly known as James' sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. Its appearance includes grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Carex jamesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
