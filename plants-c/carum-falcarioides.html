Carum falcarioides

Carum falcarioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved caraway, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, fern-like leaves and small white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Native to parts of southern Africa, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
