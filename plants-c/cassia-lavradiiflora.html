Cassia lavradiiflora

Cassia lavradiiflora, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences that appear in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that provide a lush green canopy.

Cassia lavradiiflora thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.
