Campanula linifolia f. latifrons

Campanula linifolia f. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. The leaves are broad and lanceolate, often with a soft, hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and foliage, which add visual interest to garden landscapes.
