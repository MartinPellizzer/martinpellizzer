Castela senticosa

Castela senticosa, commonly known as the sweet scented tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a sweet, pleasant fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Castela senticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its leaves in traditional medicine.
