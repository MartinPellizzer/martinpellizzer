Cuscuta platyloba var. platyloba

Cuscuta platyloba var. platyloba, commonly known as the broadleaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique life cycle that involves germinating on the surface of host plants and then developing specialized structures called haustoria to extract water and nutrients. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found growing on various host species such as legumes and other flowering plants. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive on multiple hosts make it an important ecological and agricultural concern.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a parasitic species and its impact on host plant health.
