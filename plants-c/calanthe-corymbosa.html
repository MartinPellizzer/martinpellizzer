Calanthe corymbosa

Calanthe corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces multiple upright flower spikes. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a showy, spurred lip that attracts pollinators. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage consists of broad, elliptical leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.
