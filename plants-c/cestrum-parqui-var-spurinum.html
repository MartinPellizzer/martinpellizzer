Cestrum parqui var. spurinum

Cestrum parqui var. spurinum, commonly known as the fragrant nightshade or evening glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening and emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
