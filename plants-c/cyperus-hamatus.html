Cyperus hamatus

Cyperus hamatus, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Cyperus hamatus often grows in wet or marshy areas, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Its unique spiny structures and adaptability to moist environments contribute to its ecological significance in these habitats.
