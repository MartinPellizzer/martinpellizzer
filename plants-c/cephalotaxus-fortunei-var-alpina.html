Cephalotaxus fortunei var. alpina

Cephalotaxus fortunei var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, scale-like leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
