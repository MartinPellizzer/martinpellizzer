Ceanothus papillosus var. roweanus

Ceanothus papillosus var. roweanus, commonly known as Rowean's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
