Carpesium nepalense var. lanatum

Carpesium nepalense var. lanatum, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and grasslands.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
