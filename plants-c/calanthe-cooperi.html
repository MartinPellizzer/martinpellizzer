Calanthe cooperi

Calanthe cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy texture.

Calanthe cooperi thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in woodland gardens.
