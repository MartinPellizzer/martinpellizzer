Chionanthus purpureus

Chionanthus purpureus, commonly known as the white fringetree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white flowers that appear in panicles during late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Chionanthus purpureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
