Carapa mangarevensis

Carapa mangarevensis, commonly known as the Mangareva copal tree, is a tropical tree native to the islands of the Tuamotu Archipelago in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, oval capsules containing aromatic resin. Its resin, known as copal, is highly valued for its use in traditional Polynesian crafts and as a natural adhesive.

The species is also of ecological importance, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
