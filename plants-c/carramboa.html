Carramboa

Carramboa, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Carramboa is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit.
