Copiapoa pseudocoquimbana var. vulgata

Copiapoa pseudocoquimbana var. vulgata, commonly known as the Coquimbo copiapoa, is a cactus native to northern Chile.

It is a small, globular plant with a short, thick stem and dense, white to grayish spines. The species typically grows in arid coastal environments, where it tolerates high salinity and extreme temperature fluctuations. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late winter or early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
