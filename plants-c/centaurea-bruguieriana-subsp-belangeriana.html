Centaurea bruguieriana subsp. belangeriana

Centaurea bruguieriana subsp.

belangeriana, commonly known as the Belanger's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and slender, upright stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish-gray hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
