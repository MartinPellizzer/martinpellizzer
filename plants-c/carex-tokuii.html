Carex tokuii

Carex tokuii, commonly known as the Tokui's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Carex tokuii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
