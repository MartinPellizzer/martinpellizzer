Chamaedorea bartlingiana

Chamaedorea bartlingiana, commonly known as the Bartling's bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used as an indoor plant due to its adaptability to indoor conditions.

Chamaedorea bartlingiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and air-purifying abilities.
