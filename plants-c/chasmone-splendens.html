Chasmone splendens

Chasmone splendens, commonly known as the splendor chasmone, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or greyish pubescence.

Chasmone splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought conditions.
