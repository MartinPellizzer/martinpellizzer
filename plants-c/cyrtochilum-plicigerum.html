Cyrtochilum plicigerum

Cyrtochilum plicigerum, commonly known as the pleated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pleated leaf structure that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Cyrtochilum plicigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptation to tropical forest conditions.
