Chamaecrista caribaea var. lucayana

Chamaecrista caribaea var. lucayana, commonly known as Lucayan burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a bushy habit with woody stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, bean-like pods that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are opposite and hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dune environments and its potential medicinal properties.
