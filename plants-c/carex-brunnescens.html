Carex brunnescens

Carex brunnescens, commonly known as the brownish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green with a reddish or brownish tint on the underside. Its flowering spikes are usually three-sided and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex brunnescens plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
