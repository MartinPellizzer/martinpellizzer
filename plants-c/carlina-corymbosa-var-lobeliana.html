Carlina corymbosa var. lobeliana

Carlina corymbosa var. lobeliana, commonly known as the Lobel's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy composite flower heads that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
