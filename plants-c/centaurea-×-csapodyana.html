Centaurea × csapodyana

Centaurea × csapodyana, commonly known as the Hungarian coneflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a conical shape at the top of the plant. The species is native to Hungary and surrounding regions, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
