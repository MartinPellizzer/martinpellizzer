Cyperus phleoides var. phleoides

Cyperus phleoides var. phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus phleoides var. phleoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
