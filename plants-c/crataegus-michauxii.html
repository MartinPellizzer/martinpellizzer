Crataegus michauxii

Crataegus michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions of the Appalachian Mountains. This deciduous shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
