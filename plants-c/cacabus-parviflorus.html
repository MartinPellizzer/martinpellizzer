Cacabus parviflorus

Cacabus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, compound leaves with a feathery appearance, typical of many acacia species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal regions.

The seeds of Cacabus parviflorus are encased in pods and can be used for ornamental or medicinal purposes.
