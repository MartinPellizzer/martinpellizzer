Crocus wilhelmii

Crocus wilhelmii, commonly known as the Wilhelm's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in southern Italy and Sicily. This plant is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The bulbs of Crocus wilhelmii are adapted to survive in rocky soils and harsh climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience in alpine environments.
