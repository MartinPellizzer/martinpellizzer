Croton drupaceus

Croton drupaceus, commonly known as the redroot croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by fleshy, orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structures.
