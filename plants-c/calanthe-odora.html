Calanthe odora

Calanthe odora, commonly known as the fragrant calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a raceme. Calanthe odora prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivated gardens and botanical collections.
