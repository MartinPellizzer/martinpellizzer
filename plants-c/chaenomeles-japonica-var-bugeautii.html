Chaenomeles japonica var. bugeautii

Chaenomeles japonica var. bugeautii, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thick, smooth bark and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal interest throughout the year.
