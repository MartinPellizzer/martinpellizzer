Crinum erubescens var. braziliense

Crinum erubescens var. braziliense, commonly known as the Brazilian red lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a pinkish or purplish hue. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
