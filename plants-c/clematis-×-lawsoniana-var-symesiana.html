Clematis × lawsoniana var. symesiana

Clematis × lawsoniana var. symesiana, commonly known as the Symes' Clematis, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and hardiness in temperate climates.
