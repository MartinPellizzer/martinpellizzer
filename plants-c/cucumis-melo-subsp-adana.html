Cucumis melo subsp. adana

Cucumis melo subsp.

adana, commonly known as the Adana melon, is a botanical variant of the cantaloupe. It is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

Native to the Mediterranean region, this subspecies thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows as a sprawling vine, producing yellow flowers and oval-shaped fruits.

Its genetic makeup contributes to its unique flavor profile and adaptability to various growing conditions.
