Carex scoparia var. lagopodioides

Carex scoparia var. lagopodioides, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated and produce tiny, triangular seeds.
