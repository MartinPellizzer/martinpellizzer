Cicuta californica

Cicuta californica, commonly known as western water hemlock, is a toxic member of the parsley family (Apiaceae) native to western North America.

It is a biennial herb that typically grows in wet, marshy areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters. It is highly poisonous due to the presence of cicutoxin, which can cause severe neurological symptoms in humans and animals.

Despite its dangers, it plays a role in certain ecological systems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine with extreme caution.
