Chenopodium rubrum var. vulgare

Chenopodium rubrum var. vulgare, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has alternate, ovate to triangular leaves that are usually red-veined and covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, erect inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

This species is often used as a weed in agricultural fields but also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
