Callipteris godmanii

Callipteris godmanii, commonly known as the Florida windmill palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, cold-hardy palm that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet. The plant features a slender trunk with a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive windmill-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in landscapes across the southeastern U.S.
