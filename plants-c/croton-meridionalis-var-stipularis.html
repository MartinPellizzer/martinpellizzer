Croton meridionalis var. stipularis

Croton meridionalis var. stipularis, commonly known as the Stipular Croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a textured surface, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by unique, spiky seed capsules.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
