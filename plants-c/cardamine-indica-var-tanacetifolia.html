Cardamine indica var. tanacetifolia

Cardamine indica var. tanacetifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces tiny white or greenish flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

The species is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its peppery flavor, making it a popular edible plant in certain cuisines.
