Carex distans f. appropinquata

Carex distans f. appropinquata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the sedge species Carex distans.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, three-lobed flowers arranged in a dense cluster.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly narrower leaves and shorter inflorescences compared to the typical form.
