Chamagrostis minima

Chamagrostis minima, commonly known as dwarf silvergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, silver-gray leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are arranged in panicle inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
