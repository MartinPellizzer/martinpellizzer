Camphora

Camphora, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The tree produces a valuable essential oil called camphor, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The camphora tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
