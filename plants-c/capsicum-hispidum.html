Capsicum hispidum

Capsicum hispidum, commonly known as the hairy pepper or wild chili, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, often yellow or orange fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
