Cnestis diffusa

Cnestis diffusa, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. Its flowers are tubular and are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
