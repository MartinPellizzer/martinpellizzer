Centaurea × enculescui

Centaurea × enculescui, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with erect, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and wildflower meadows.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, contributing to its adaptability and ornamental value.
