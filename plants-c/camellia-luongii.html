Camellia luongii

Camellia luongii, commonly known as the Vietnamese camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Camellia luongii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
