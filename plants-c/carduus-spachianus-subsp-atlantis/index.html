Carduus Spachianus Subsp. Atlantis

Carduus spachianus subsp. atlantis is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pyrenees. It is known for its distinctive purple flowers and robust, spiny leaves that help it survive in harsh mountain environments. This subspecies thrives in rocky soils and cool, moist climates, making it a symbol of resilience in alpine ecosystems. Its unique appearance has made it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. Despite its beauty, it remains a elusive and underappreciated member of the thistle family.
