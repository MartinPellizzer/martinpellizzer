Cytisus pulvinatus

Cytisus pulvinatus, commonly known as the purple broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of bright purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Cytisus pulvinatus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
