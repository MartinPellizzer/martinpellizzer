Cirsium allionii

Cirsium allionii, commonly known as the Allion's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a compact cluster.

Cirsium allionii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
