Carex handel-mazzettii

Carex handel-mazzettii, commonly known as the Handel-Mazzetti sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Carex handel-mazzettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
