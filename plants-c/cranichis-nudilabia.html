Cranichis nudilabia

Cranichis nudilabia, commonly known as the naked-lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, upright stem that bears a single, showy flower. The plant has a short, fleshy root system and typically grows in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive, elongated lip.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, often attracting specific insect pollinators.
