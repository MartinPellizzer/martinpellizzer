Carduus spinulosus subsp. lobulatus

Carduus spinulosus subsp.

lobulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features spiny, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine, bristly hairs.

The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
