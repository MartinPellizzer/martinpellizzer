Chamaecrista catolesensis

Chamaecrista catolesensis, commonly known as Catoles Mountain burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that are often hairy.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its role in soil stabilization.
