Colubrina alamanii

Colubrina alamanii, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody capsule that opens to release the seeds when dry.

Colubrina alamanii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
