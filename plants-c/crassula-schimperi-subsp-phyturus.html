Crassula schimperi subsp. phyturus

Crassula schimperi subsp.

phyturus, commonly known as the "Pothos Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative indoor plant.
