Chlorophytum tinneae

Chlorophytum tinneae, commonly known as the Snake Plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a lighter midrib. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on tall, slender stalks, which are followed by bright blue or purple berries. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil.

Chlorophytum tinneae is also known for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
