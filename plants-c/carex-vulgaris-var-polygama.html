Carex vulgaris var. polygama

Carex vulgaris var. polygama, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

Carex vulgaris var. polygama is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
