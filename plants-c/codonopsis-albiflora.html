Codonopsis albiflora

Codonopsis albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant features a tuberous root system, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are deeply lobed.

Codonopsis albiflora thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
