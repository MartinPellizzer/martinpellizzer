Crotalaria bhutanica

Crotalaria bhutanica, commonly known as Bhutan's crown flower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region, particularly Bhutan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at higher elevations. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a unique crown-like structure at the base of the petals.

Crotalaria bhutanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in local traditions.
