Crucifera cheiranthos

Crucifera cheiranthos, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, four-petaled flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
