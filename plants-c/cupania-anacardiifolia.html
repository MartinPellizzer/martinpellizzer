Cupania anacardiifolia

Cupania anacardiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that changes color from green to orange or red as it matures.
