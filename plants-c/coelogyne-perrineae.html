Coelogyne perrineae

Coelogyne perrineae, commonly known as the Purple-veined Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and large, fragrant, violet-blue flowers with distinctive purple veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It blooms during the late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its environment.

Coelogyne perrineae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
