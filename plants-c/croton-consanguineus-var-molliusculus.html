Croton consanguineus var. molliusculus

Croton consanguineus var. molliusculus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage.
