Centaurea phaeopappoides

Centaurea phaeopappoides, commonly known as the purplehead thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy pappus that aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Centaurea phaeopappoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
