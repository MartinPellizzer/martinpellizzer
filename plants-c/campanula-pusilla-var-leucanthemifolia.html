Campanula pusilla var. leucanthemifolia

Campanula pusilla var. leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white thimblebell bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, resembling those of Leucanthemum. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its delicate appearance and extended blooming period make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.
