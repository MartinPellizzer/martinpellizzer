Carludovica palmata var. humilis

Carludovica palmata var. humilis, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a compact, low-growing palm native to Mexico.

It features a short, sturdy trunk with a dense crown of feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive, fan-shaped appearance. The plant is characterized by its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The leaves are typically bright green and remain lush throughout the growing season.
