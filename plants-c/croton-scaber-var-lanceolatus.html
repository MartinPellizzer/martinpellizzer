Croton scaber var. lanceolatus

Croton scaber var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the rough croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are typically rough to the touch, with a serrated margin and a textured surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is commonly found in tropical gardens and landscapes.
