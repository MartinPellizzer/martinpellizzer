Crepis sancta subsp. sancta

Crepis sancta subsp.

sancta, commonly known as the holy grass or sacred hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its inflorescence consists of yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and its historical association with religious and medicinal uses.
