Crassula concinnella

Crassula concinnella, commonly known as the Little Carpet Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, rounded leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
