Cyathopappus abyssinicus

Cyathopappus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian cupflower, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cup-shaped inflorescence. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
