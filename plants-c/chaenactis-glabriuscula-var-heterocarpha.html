Chaenactis glabriuscula var. heterocarpha

Chaenactis glabriuscula var. heterocarpha, commonly known as the Sierra Pointleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its leaves are generally gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.
