Cnicus serratuloides

Cnicus serratuloides, commonly known as the prickly sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with a rosette of spiny, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by small, dry fruits called achenes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cnicus serratuloides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
