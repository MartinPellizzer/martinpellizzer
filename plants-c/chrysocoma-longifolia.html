Chrysocoma longifolia

Chrysocoma longifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
