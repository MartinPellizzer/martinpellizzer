Cyathea palaciosii

Cyathea palaciosii, commonly known as the Palacios's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive, evergreen fronds, which can reach several meters in length.

Cyathea palaciosii plays a significant ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
