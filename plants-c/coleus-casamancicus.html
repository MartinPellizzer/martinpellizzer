Coleus casamancicus

Coleus casamancicus, commonly known as the African coleus, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often ornamental, displaying a variety of colors such as red, purple, and green.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
