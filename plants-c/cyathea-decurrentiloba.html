Cyathea decurrentiloba

Cyathea decurrentiloba, commonly known as the decurrent lophosoria, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, shield-shaped fronds that grow in a dense, upright manner. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is often covered with remnants of old fronds, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Cyathea decurrentiloba is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
