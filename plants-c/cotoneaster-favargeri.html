Cotoneaster favargeri

Cotoneaster favargeri, commonly known as the favargeri cotoneaster, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, often thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers in the spring, followed by orange or red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Cotoneaster favargeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
