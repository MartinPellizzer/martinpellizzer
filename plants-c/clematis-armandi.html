Clematis armandi

Clematis armandi, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis armandi is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
