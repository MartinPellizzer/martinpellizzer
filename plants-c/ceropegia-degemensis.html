Ceropegia degemensis

Ceropegia degemensis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that resemble ghostly shapes, often appearing in shades of white and pink. The plant has fleshy, branching stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Ceropegia degemensis is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insects.
