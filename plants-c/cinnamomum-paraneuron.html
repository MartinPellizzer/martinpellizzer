Cinnamomum paraneuron

Cinnamomum paraneuron, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum paraneuron, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive fragrance, often used in traditional medicine.

The bark is the primary part used, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
