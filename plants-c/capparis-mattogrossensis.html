Capparis mattogrossensis

Capparis mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian caper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Capparis mattogrossensis is adapted to humid tropical climates and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
