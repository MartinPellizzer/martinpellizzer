Cousinia myrioglochis

Cousinia myrioglochis, commonly known as the many-flowered cushion plant, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing cushions of foliage that help it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are usually covered in a thick layer of fine hairs, which provide additional protection against desiccation.

Cousinia myrioglochis is adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient species found in rocky and sandy soils.
