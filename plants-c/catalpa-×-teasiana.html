Catalpa × teasiana

Catalpa × teasiana, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to China and widely cultivated in the United States.

It is a hybrid between Catalpa bignonioides and Catalpa ovata, showcasing a combination of traits from both parent species. The tree is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, with shallow fissures, and it produces long, bean-like pods that contain sticky, toxic seeds.

Catalpa × teasiana is often planted for its ornamental value and is relatively adaptable to various soil types.
