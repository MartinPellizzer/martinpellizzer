Cyrtosperma merkusii var. giganteum

Cyrtosperma merkusii var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, providing a lush, green canopy in wetland environments. The plant thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in marshes and flooded forests. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the water.

This species plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
