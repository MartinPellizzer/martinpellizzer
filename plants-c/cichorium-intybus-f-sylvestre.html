Cichorium intybus f. sylvestre

Cichorium intybus f. sylvestre, commonly known as wild chicory, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of blue to violet flowers and a taproot that is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is known for its bitter taste and is sometimes used as a coffee substitute.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
