Centaurea pontica

Centaurea pontica, commonly known as the Pontic centaury, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Centaurea pontica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic benefits.
