Campanula kotschyana

Campanula kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring cool and moist conditions.

Campanula kotschyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and hardy nature.
