Cousinia lipskyi

Cousinia lipskyi, commonly known as the Kyzylkum cousinia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. It features silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia lipskyi is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
