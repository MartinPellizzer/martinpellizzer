Centaurea ebenoides

Centaurea ebenoides, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright blue to purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a distinctive, spiny appearance.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
