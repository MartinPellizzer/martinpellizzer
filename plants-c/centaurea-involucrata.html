Centaurea involucrata

Centaurea involucrata, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bright yellow flowers with red or purple markings. Its inflorescence is a composite head surrounded by a series of bracts, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
