Carlina involucrata

Carlina involucrata, commonly known as the blueweed or purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features a central yellow disk surrounded by blue to purple ray flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Carlina involucrata thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in prairies and woodland edges.
