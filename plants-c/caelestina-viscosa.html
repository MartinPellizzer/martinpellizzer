Caelestina viscosa

Caelestina viscosa, commonly known as the sticky bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance from its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
