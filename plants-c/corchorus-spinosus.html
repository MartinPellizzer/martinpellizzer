Corchorus spinosus

Corchorus spinosus, commonly known as spiny smartweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are hairy and often tinged with red, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
