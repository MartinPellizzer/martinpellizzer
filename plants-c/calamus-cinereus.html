Calamus cinereus

Calamus cinereus, commonly known as the ash reed, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white spadices, which are typical of the Aroid family. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Calamus cinereus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
