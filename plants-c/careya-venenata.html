Careya venenata

Careya venenata, commonly known as the poisonwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing toxic seeds. Its roots are known to be poisonous, containing compounds that can cause severe illness if ingested.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its hard wood, though it requires caution due to its toxic properties.
