Cerasus ferruginea

Cerasus ferruginea, commonly known as the red cherry or rusty cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, edible fruits. The fruits are usually harvested in late summer and are known for their sweet, tart flavor.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy blossoms.
