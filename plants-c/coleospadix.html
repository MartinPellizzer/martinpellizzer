Coleospadix

Coleospadix, commonly known as the "spiny bladder" or "spiny cactus," is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Coleospadix is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
