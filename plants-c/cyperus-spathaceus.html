Cyperus spathaceus

Cyperus spathaceus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale purple to white flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus spathaceus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
