Convolvulus ocellatus var. ornatus

Convolvulus ocellatus var. ornatus, commonly known as the ornate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found twining around supports or other vegetation. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
