Carduus eriophorus

Carduus eriophorus, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
