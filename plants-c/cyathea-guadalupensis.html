Cyathea guadalupensis

Cyathea guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Cyathea guadalupensis plays a significant role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
