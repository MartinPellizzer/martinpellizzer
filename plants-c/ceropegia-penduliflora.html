Ceropegia penduliflora

Ceropegia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which attract specific pollinators like moths and flies. Its unique floral structure is adapted for specialized pollination, making it an interesting subject in botanical studies.

Ceropegia penduliflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
