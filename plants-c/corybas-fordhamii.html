Corybas fordhamii

Corybas fordhamii, commonly known as the Fordham's helmet orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that is typically deep red or orange with intricate patterns. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this botanical rarity.
