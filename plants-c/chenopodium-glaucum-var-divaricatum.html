Chenopodium glaucum var. divaricatum

Chenopodium glaucum var. divaricatum, commonly known as desert spinach or lamb's quarters, is a halophytic plant adapted to arid environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and its prostrate or spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in desert regions.

This species is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
