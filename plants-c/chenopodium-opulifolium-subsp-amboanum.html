Chenopodium opulifolium subsp. amboanum

Chenopodium opulifolium subsp.

amboanum, commonly known as the giant goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
