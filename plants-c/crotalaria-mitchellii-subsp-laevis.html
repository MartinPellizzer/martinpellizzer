Crotalaria mitchellii subsp. laevis

Crotalaria mitchellii subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the Mitchell's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a shallow root system that helps in nitrogen fixation.

This subspecies is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
