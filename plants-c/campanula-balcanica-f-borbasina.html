Campanula balcanica f. borbasina

Campanula balcanica f. borbasina, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Campanula balcanica f. borbasina is also noted for its resilience and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
