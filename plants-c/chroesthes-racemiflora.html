Chroesthes racemiflora

Chroesthes racemiflora, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Chroesthes racemiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
