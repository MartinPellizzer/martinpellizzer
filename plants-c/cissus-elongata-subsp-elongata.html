Cissus elongata subsp. elongata

Cissus elongata subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or white pubescence, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are flexible and can trail or climb, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained walls.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in semi-shaded environments.
