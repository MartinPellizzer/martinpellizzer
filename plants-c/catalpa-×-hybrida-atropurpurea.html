Catalpa × hybrida atropurpurea

Catalpa × hybrida atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking bronze-red in the fall. The tree produces long, drooping clusters of white or pale purple flowers in the spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in landscapes.
