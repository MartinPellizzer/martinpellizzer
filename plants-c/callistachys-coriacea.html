Callistachys coriacea

Callistachys coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The roughleaf hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
