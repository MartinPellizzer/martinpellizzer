Carex beringiana

Carex beringiana, commonly known as Bering's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in tundra or wetland habitats. Carex beringiana produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic and subarctic wildlife.
