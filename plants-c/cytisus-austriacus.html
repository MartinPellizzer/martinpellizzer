Cytisus austriacus

Cytisus austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It features slender, arching branches and is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cytisus austriacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
