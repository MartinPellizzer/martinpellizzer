Cupania Scrobiculata F. Reticulata

Reticulate cupania, scientifically known as Cupania scrobiculata f. reticulata, is a rare and unique variant of the cupania genus native to specific regions of South America. This plant is distinguished by its intricate, net-like leaf patterns that give it its common name. While not widely cultivated, it holds significance in botanical studies due to its distinct morphology and genetic traits. Its limited distribution and ornamental qualities make it a subject of interest for horticulturists and plant enthusiasts. Despite its beauty, reticulate cupania remains underappreciated and often overlooked in mainstream plant collections.
