Crotalaria exserta

Crotalaria exserta, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. Crotalaria exserta is known for its unique seed pods, which are elongated and often have a hooked or twisted appearance.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
