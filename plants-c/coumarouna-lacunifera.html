Coumarouna lacunifera

Coumarouna lacunifera, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a large deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing small, flat seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and montane regions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
