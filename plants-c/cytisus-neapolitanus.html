Cytisus neapolitanus

Cytisus neapolitanus, commonly known as the Naples broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe, particularly Italy.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Cytisus neapolitanus is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
