Celastrus venustus

Celastrus venustus, commonly known as the pretty bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
