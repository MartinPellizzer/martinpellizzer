Crepinia solstitialis

Crepinia solstitialis, commonly known as the summer solstice plant, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers bloom during the summer months, particularly around the time of the summer solstice, giving it its common name.

The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces a delicate, yellowish-pink flower that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
