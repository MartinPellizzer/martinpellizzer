Cistanche jodostoma

Cistanche jodostoma, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to members of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers and has a distinctive, branching stem structure. It is native to arid regions of China and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique biology and habitat, it is of interest in both ecological and medicinal studies.
