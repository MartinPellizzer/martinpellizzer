Callicarpa brevipes var. obovata

Callicarpa brevipes var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking violet to purple berries that ripen in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to obovate in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
