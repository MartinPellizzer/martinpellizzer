Cyperus giraudyi

Cyperus giraudyi, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, which is a defining feature of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus giraudyi is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
