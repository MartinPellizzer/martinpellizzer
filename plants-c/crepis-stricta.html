Crepis stricta

Crepis stricta, commonly known as the rigid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Crepis stricta is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
