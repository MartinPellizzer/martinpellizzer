Carex luridiformis

Carex luridiformis, commonly known as the yellowish sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.
