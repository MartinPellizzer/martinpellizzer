Cacalia delphiniphylla

Cacalia delphiniphylla, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, adapted to thrive in salty, windy environments. It is often found growing on cliffs and sandy shores, where it tolerates harsh conditions.

Cacalia delphiniphylla is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
