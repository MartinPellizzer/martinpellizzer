Cabralea lagoensis var. glabra

Cabralea lagoensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth cabralea, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly waxy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
