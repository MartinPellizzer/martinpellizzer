Carex virescens var. enormis

Carex virescens var. enormis, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and large, narrow leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces spikes of small, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found in riparian zones.

Its robust growth and ability to stabilize soil make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
