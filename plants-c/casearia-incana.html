Casearia incana

Casearia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and clustered in terminal or axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.
