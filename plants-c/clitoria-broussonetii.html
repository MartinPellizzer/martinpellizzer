Clitoria broussonetii

Clitoria broussonetii, commonly known as the flame bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pea-shaped flowers that are typically bright red or orange with a yellow throat. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces elongated pods that contain several bean-like seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Clitoria broussonetii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
