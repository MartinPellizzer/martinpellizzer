Cyananthus petiolatus var. minor

Cyananthus petiolatus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser petioled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the stems are often glandular.
