Cephalostachyum virulentum

Cephalostachyum virulentum, commonly known as the toxic grass or poison grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is notable for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though caution is advised due to its potential toxicity.

The plant's rapid growth and widespread distribution make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
