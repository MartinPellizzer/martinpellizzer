Carex duriuscula var. tenuispica

Carex duriuscula var. tenuispica, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike of tiny, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its wetland environment.
