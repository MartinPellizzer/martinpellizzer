Conyza vernonioides subsp. arborea

Conyza vernonioides subsp.

arborea, commonly known as the tree conyza, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, hairy seed heads. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

The species is occasionally found in disturbed areas and can spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
