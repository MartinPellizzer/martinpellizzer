Calophyllum leucocarpum

Calophyllum leucocarpum, commonly known as white fruit calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by large, white, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some traditional healing practices.
