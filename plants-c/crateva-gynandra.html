Crateva gynandra

Crateva gynandra, commonly known as African nightshade, is a tropical flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, with alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by spherical, greenish-black fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
