Carex tovarensis

Carex tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovar marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. Its grass-like appearance is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Carex tovarensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
