Centaurea leucophaea subsp. brunnescens

Centaurea leucophaea subsp.

brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-centred knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb with a robust root system and erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces showy, composite flower heads with purple to pink petals and a yellow center, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands.
