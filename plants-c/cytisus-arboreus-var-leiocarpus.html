Cytisus arboreus var. leiocarpus

Cytisus arboreus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the silky thorn acacia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
