Centaurea urvillei var. longicaulis

Centaurea urvillei var. longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed centaurea, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features erect, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, conical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a slight pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.
