Carex discoidea var. perangusta

Carex discoidea var. perangusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
