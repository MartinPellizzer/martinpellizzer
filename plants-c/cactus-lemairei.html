Cactus lemairei

Cactus lemairei, commonly known as the Lemaire cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a feature common in desert plants to minimize water loss.

Cactus lemairei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
