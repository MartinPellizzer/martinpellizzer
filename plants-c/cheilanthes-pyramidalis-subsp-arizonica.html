Cheilanthes pyramidalis subsp. arizonica

Cheilanthes pyramidalis subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona cliff brake, is a perennial fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This subspecies is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native habitat.
