Crabbea reticulata

Crabbea reticulata, commonly known as the nettleleaf dahlia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features a robust, tuberous root system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Crabbea reticulata is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
