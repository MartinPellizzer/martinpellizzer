Cynanchum tetragonum

Cynanchum tetragonum, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Cynanchum tetragonum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
