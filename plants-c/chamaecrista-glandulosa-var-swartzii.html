Chamaecrista glandulosa var. swartzii

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's ticktree, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with a woody stem and a dense, spreading form.
