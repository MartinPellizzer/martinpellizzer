Coronilla squamata

Coronilla squamata, commonly known as the scaly crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and scaly leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Coronilla squamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
