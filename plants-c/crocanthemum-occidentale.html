Crocanthemum occidentale

Crocanthemum occidentale, commonly known as western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water retention.

Crocanthemum occidentale is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, dunes, and rocky slopes.
