Canarium simplicifolium

Canarium simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaf candlenut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and glossy in appearance. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody capsules containing oily seeds.

The plant is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil for various industrial applications.
