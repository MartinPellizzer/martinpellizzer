Cinnamomum heudelotii

Cinnamomum heudelotii, commonly known as the African cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic fruits that contain small, round seeds. It is often cultivated for its essential oils and has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it distinct from the more commonly known cinnamon species like Cinnamomum verum.
