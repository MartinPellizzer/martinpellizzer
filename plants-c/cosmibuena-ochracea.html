Cosmibuena ochracea

Cosmibuena ochracea, commonly known as the golden cosmos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, vibrant yellow to orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a spherical or disc-like cluster. It thrives in full sunlight and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds to its ecological value.
