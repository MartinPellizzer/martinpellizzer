Calceolaria heterosepala

Calceolaria heterosepala, commonly known as the heterosepala slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique calyx-like structure that resembles a slipper, which is a distinguishing feature of the Calceolaria genus. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
