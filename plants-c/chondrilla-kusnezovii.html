Chondrilla kusnezovii

Chondrilla kusnezovii, commonly known as the Kusnezov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system that enables it to survive in dry environments by accessing groundwater. It is also known for producing a milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of many species in the genus Chondrilla.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
