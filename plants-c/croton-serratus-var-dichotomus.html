Croton serratus var. dichotomus

Croton serratus var. dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its branches often exhibit a distinctive dichotomous branching pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
