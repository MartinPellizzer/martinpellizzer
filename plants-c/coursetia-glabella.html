Coursetia glabella

Coursetia glabella, commonly known as the smooth coursetia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
