Chamaerhodos polygyna

Chamaerhodos polygyna, commonly known as the mountain bluet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Chamaerhodos polygyna plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
