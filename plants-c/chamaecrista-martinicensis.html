Chamaecrista martinicensis

Chamaecrista martinicensis, commonly known as the Caribbean coral bean, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Chamaecrista martinicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
