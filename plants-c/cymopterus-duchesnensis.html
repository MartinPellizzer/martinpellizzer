Cymopterus duchesnensis

Cymopterus duchesnensis, commonly known as the desert betony, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
