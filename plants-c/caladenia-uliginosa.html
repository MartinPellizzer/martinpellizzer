Caladenia uliginosa

Caladenia uliginosa, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy orchid flower with a distinctive, hood-like labellum. The plant typically grows in moist, boggy habitats, often in heathland or swampy areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Caladenia uliginosa is a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its natural habitat.
