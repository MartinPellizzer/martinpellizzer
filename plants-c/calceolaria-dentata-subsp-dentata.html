Calceolaria dentata subsp. dentata

Calceolaria dentata subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-shaped petals, which resemble small cups or bells.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
