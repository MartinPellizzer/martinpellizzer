Cereus cinerascens var. tenuior

Cereus cinerascens var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its growth habit and floral display make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical study.
