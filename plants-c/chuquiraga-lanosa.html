Chuquiraga lanosa

Chuquiraga lanosa, commonly known as the woolly chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Chuquiraga lanosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.
