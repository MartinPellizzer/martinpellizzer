Crepidiastrum quercus

Crepidiastrum quercus, commonly known as the oakleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem structure.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
