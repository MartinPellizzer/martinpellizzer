Cissus denticulata

Cissus denticulata, commonly known as the toothed cutleaf goosefoot, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine white coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
