Calceolaria adenocalyx

Calceolaria adenocalyx, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a member of the Calceolaria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique calyces, which resemble small hoods or cups, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to gardens and botanical collections.
