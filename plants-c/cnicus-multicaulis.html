Cnicus multicaulis

Cnicus multicaulis, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species often found in disturbed soils.
