Clidemia rubra var. cordifolia

Clidemia rubra var. cordifolia, commonly known as red-veined velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purplish on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
