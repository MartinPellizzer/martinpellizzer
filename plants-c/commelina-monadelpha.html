Commelina monadelpha

Commelina monadelpha, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows from 30 to 60 centimeters tall, with lance-shaped leaves and a basal rosette structure. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of three to five flowers, each with three petals and three sepals.

Commelina monadelpha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
