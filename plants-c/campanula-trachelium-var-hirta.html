Campanula trachelium var. hirta

Campanula trachelium var. hirta, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are also hairy.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
