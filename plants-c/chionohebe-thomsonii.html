Chionohebe thomsonii

Chionohebe thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's chionohebe, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
