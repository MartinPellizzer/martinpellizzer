Carex chrysolepis var. modesta

Carex chrysolepis var. modesta, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its foliage is often a striking golden-green, which distinguishes it from other sedges.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
