Croton verdickii

Croton verdickii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
