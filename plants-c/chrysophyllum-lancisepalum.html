Chrysophyllum lancisepalum

Chrysophyllum lancisepalum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features elliptical, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a round, yellowish berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
