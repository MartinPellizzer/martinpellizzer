Calceolaria montana

Calceolaria montana, commonly known as the mountain slipper orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like structure that resembles a slipper, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Calceolaria montana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
