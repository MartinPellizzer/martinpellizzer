Carex heribaudiana

Carex heribaudiana, commonly known as the Heribaud's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex heribaudiana is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
