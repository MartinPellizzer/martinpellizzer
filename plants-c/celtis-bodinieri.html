Celtis bodinieri

Celtis bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Celtis bodinieri is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
