Cacalia pilgeriana subsp. delphiniphylla

Cacalia pilgeriana subsp.

delphiniphylla, commonly known as the Delphinium-like Cacalia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple ray florets that resemble those of delphiniums.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a short, upright stem with few branches.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
