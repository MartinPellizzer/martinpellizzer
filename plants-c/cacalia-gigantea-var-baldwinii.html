Cacalia gigantea var. baldwinii

Cacalia gigantea var. baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin's giant cacalia, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
