Callisemaea grandiflora

Callisemaea grandiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional diets.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
