Camellia dalatensis

Camellia dalatensis, commonly known as the Dalat camellia, is a species native to Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in subtropical climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia dalatensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also appreciated for its historical significance in horticulture.
