Chamaerhodos erecta

Chamaerhodos erecta, commonly known as redstem dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce pale to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray appearance that helps it retain moisture.

Chamaerhodos erecta is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in native plant landscapes.
