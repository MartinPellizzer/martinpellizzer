Cotula vulgaris var. vulgaris

Cotula vulgaris var. vulgaris, commonly known as common cotula, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disc and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Cotula vulgaris var. vulgaris is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
