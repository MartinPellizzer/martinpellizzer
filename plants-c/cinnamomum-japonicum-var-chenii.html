Cinnamomum japonicum var. chenii

Cinnamomum japonicum var. chenii, commonly known as the Japanese cinnamon tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
