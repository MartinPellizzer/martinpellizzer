Chamaecrista adiantifolia

Chamaecrista adiantifolia, commonly known as the fernleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Chamaecrista adiantifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
