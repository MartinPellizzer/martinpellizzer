Conophytum pauperae

Conophytum pauperae, commonly known as the poor man's conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, paired leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Conophytum pauperae is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
