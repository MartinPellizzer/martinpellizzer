Centaurea × guichardii

Centaurea × guichardii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Centaurea × guichardii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
