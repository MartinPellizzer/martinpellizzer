Cirsium lokense

Cirsium lokense, commonly known as the Lokense thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its flowering period occurs during late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Cirsium lokense is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
