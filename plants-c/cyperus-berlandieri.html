Cyperus berlandieri

Cyperus berlandieri, commonly known as the broadleaf paperplant, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or brown in color. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in moist environments.

Cyperus berlandieri is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in wetland ecosystems.
