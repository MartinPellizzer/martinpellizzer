Cyperus spectabilis var. scaberrimus

Cyperus spectabilis var. scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is notable for its rough, scabrous texture on the leaves, which gives it its botanical name "scaberrimus.".
