Calycanthus praecox

Calycanthus praecox, commonly known as the early calycanthus or sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and hairy underneath. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Calycanthus praecox is valued in gardens for its unique fragrance and ornamental value.
