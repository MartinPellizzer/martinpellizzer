Carex canescens var. brunnescens

Carex canescens var. brunnescens, commonly known as the brownish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

This variant is distinguished by its darker, more bronzed foliage compared to the typical form of Carex canescens.
