Cirsium occidentale var. venustum

Cirsium occidentale var. venustum, commonly known as the showy milkweed or the western thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
