Crassula multiflora subsp. leucantha

Crassula multiflora subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as the silver crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, branching growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
