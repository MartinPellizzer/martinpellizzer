Cyananthus flavus subsp. montanus

Cyananthus flavus subsp.

montanus, commonly known as the alpine bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine, silvery down. Cyananthus flavus subsp.

montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its alpine habitat.
