Chloris monostachya

Chloris monostachya, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It is characterized by its ability to form dense clumps and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Chloris monostachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
