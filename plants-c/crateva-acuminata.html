Crateva acuminata

Crateva acuminata, commonly known as the Java bean or arrowhead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, spiny capsule. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
