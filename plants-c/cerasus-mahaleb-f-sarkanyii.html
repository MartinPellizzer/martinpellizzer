Cerasus mahaleb f. sarkanyii

Cerasus mahaleb f. sarkanyii, commonly known as the Hungarian cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus mahaleb species.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is native to the Carpathian Basin and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a pointed tip and a slightly serrated edge.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its attractive flowers and fruit.
