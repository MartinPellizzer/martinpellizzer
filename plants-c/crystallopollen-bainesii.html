Crystallopollen bainesii

Crystallopollen bainesii, commonly known as the glassy pollen plant, is a rare and unique species characterized by its translucent, glass-like pollen grains.

Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant's most distinguishing feature is its ability to produce pollen that appears almost solid due to its dense, crystalline structure. It is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly known as the grass family.

Due to its rarity and unusual morphology, it is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
