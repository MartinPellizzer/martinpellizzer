Cardamine hispidula

Cardamine hispidula, commonly known as hairy bittercress, is a small flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are white and consist of four petals, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

Cardamine hispidula is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
