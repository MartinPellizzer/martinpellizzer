Caesalpinia stuckertii

Caesalpinia stuckertii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and small, oblong leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its seeds.
