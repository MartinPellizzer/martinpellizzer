Centaurea herminii subsp. lusitana

Centaurea herminii subsp.

lusitana, commonly known as the Lusitanian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical form of Centaurea herminii.
