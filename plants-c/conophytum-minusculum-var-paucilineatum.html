Conophytum minusculum var. paucilineatum

Conophytum minusculum var. paucilineatum, commonly known as the dwarf conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, and often ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming a dense mat with multiple stems. Its flowering occurs in late winter to early spring, producing small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is prized for its compact size and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
