Corchorus hirtus var. pilosus

Corchorus hirtus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features sturdy, upright stems that are often hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in shades of greenish-yellow.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
