Cyperus neoschimperi

Cyperus neoschimperi, commonly known as the African sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to reddish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.

Cyperus neoschimperi is adapted to wet or marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas near water bodies.
