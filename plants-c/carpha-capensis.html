Carpha capensis

Carpha capensis, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pink or purple in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Carpha capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
