Crataegus retrusa

Crataegus retrusa, commonly known as the red chokecherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Crataegus retrusa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
