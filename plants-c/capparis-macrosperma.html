Capparis macrosperma

Capparis macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded caper, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems, thorny branches, and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils.

Capparis macrosperma is known for its ability to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
