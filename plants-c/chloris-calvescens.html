Chloris calvescens

Chloris calvescens, commonly known as slender grass or small-leaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chloris calvescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native fauna.
