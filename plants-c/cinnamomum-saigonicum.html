Cinnamomum saigonicum

Cinnamomum saigonicum, commonly known as the Saigon cinnamon or Vietnamese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark, which is harvested for its spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by egg-shaped berries that contain winged seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, camphor-like scent.

This species is closely related to other cinnamon trees but is distinguished by its unique bark and growth habits.
