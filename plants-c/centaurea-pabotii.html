Centaurea pabotii

Centaurea pabotii, commonly known as the Pabotii knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Centaurea pabotii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
