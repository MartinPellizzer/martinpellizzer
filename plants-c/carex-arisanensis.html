Carex arisanensis

Carex arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex arisanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
