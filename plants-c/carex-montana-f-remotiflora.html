Carex montana f. remotiflora

Carex montana f. remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered mountain sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose, cylindrical inflorescence.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological restoration projects in wetland environments.
