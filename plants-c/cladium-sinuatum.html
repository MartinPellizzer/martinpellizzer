Cladium sinuatum

Cladium sinuatum, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that have a serrated edge, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater wetlands and is often found in marshes and swamps. Cladium sinuatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, which help stabilize soil and reduce erosion in wetland areas.
