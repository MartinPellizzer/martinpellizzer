Celastrus flagellaris

Celastrus flagellaris, commonly known as the creeping celastroid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a woody vine that typically grows along the ground or climbing other vegetation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature. It has a rough, ridged stem and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is part of the Celastraceae family.
