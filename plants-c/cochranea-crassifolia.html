Cochranea crassifolia

Cochranea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved cochranea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
