Cymbopogon lecomtei

Cymbopogon lecomtei, commonly known as Lecomte's lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus-like scent. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The botanical features of Cymbopogon lecomtei make it a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
