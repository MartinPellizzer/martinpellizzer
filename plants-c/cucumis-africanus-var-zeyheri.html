Cucumis africanus var. zeyheri

Cucumis africanus var. zeyheri, commonly known as the African horned cucumber, is a unique vine species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, horn-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is edible but has a mild, cucumber-like flavor with a slightly bitter taste.
