Curcuma petiolaris

Curcuma petiolaris, commonly known as the white turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and other aromatic plants. The plant produces striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust growth and aromatic compounds similar to those found in other Curcuma species.
