Croton lasiopyrus

Croton lasiopyrus, commonly known as the red maple.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its vibrant red foliage in autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a samara, which aids in wind dispersal.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
