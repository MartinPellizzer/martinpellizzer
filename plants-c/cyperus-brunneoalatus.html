Cyperus brunneoalatus

Cyperus brunneoalatus, commonly known as the brown-hair sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, brownish hair. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically brown or reddish in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in wet or moist environments.

Cyperus brunneoalatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
