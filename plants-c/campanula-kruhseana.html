Campanula kruhseana

Campanula kruhseana, commonly known as the Kruhse's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Campanula kruhseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
