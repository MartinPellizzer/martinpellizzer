Centaurium umbellatum subsp. bernardii

Centaurium umbellatum subsp.

bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard's whitetop, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems that bear small, clustered inflorescences.

The flowers are typically pale to dark pink, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, characteristic of the Gentianaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.
