Clematis mauritiana subsp. strigillosa

Clematis mauritiana subsp.

strigillosa, commonly known as the Mauritius clematis, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius. It is characterized by its woody vines and showy, typically blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
