Capnorchis scandens

Capnorchis scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that climb along trees or rocky surfaces. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in loose clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Capnorchis scandens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
