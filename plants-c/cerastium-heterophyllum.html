Cerastium heterophyllum

Cerastium heterophyllum, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

The species is noted for its ability to form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
