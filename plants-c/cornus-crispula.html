Cornus crispula

Cornus crispula, commonly known as dwarf cornel.

This shrub is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, acidic soils. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn red in the autumn.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
