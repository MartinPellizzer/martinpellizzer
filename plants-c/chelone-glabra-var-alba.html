Chelone glabra var. alba

Chelone glabra var. alba, commonly known as white hound's tongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the base of the plant. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
