Centaurea jacea f. alvarensis

Centaurea jacea f. alvarensis, commonly known as the Alvares' cornflower, is a horticultural form of the common centaurea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is a perennial species that produces fluffy seed heads, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
