Chaptalia oblonga

Chaptalia oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact cluster.

Chaptalia oblonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
