Calamus platyspathus

Calamus platyspathus, commonly known as the broad-leafed calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are broad and elongated. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Calamus platyspathus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
