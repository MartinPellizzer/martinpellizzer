Campanula lehmanniana subsp. pseudohissarica

Campanula lehmanniana subsp.

pseudohissarica, commonly known as the Karakoram bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
