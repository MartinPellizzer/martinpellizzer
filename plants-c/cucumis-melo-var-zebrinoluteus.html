Cucumis melo var. zebrinoluteus

Cucumis melo var. zebrinoluteus, commonly known as the zebra melon, is a unique variant of the melon family characterized by its striking, striped rind.

This plant typically grows as a trailing vine, producing large, oblong fruits with a thick, waxy skin. The fruit's exterior features distinct, alternating light and dark green stripes, giving it a zebra-like appearance. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

The zebra melon is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its edible, sweet, and juicy flesh.
