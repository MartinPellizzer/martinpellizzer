Combretum semiadnatum

Combretum semiadnatum, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
