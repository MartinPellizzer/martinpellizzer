Casalea ascendens

Casalea ascendens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that can climb up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are elongated and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Casalea ascendens thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
