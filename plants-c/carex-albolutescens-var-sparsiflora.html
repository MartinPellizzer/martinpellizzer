Carex albolutescens var. sparsiflora

Carex albolutescens var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its sparse flowering and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
