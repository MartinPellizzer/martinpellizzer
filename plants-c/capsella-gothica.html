Capsella gothica

Capsella gothica, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, such as roadsides and fields. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The leaves are palmately divided, giving the plant a distinctive, delicate appearance.
