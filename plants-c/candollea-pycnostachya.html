Candollea pycnostachya

Candollea pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often hairy stems and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as under the canopy of rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Candollea pycnostachya is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
