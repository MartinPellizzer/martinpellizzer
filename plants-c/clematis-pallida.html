Clematis pallida

Clematis pallida, commonly known as the white clematis, is a perennial vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. Clematis pallida prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be a host plant for certain butterfly species.
