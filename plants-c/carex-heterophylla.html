Carex heterophylla

Carex heterophylla, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among its foliage.

Carex heterophylla is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
