Curcuma meraukensis

Curcuma meraukensis, commonly known as Java turmeric, is a rhizomatous herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish rhizomes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually orange or red, clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent, similar to other curcuma species.
