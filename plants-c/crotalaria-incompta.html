Crotalaria incompta

Crotalaria incompta, commonly known as the African cowpea or yellow crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
