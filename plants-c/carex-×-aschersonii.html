Carex × aschersonii

Carex × aschersonii, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like clusters of small, brownish flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
