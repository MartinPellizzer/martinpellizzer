Ceraia juncifolia

Ceraia juncifolia, commonly known as the swamp rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States. The plant has tall, grass-like stems that can grow up to several feet in height. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Ceraia juncifolia plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
