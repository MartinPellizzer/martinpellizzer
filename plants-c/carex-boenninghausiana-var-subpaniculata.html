Carex boenninghausiana var. subpaniculata

Carex boenninghausiana var. subpaniculata, commonly known as the panicled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
