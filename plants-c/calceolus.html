Calceolus

Calceolus, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-like structure that encloses the flower, giving it a unique and elegant appearance. These plants are primarily found in Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Calceolus species are typically small, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

They produce delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, making them popular among orchid enthusiasts.
