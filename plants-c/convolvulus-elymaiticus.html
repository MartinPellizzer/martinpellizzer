Convolvulus elymaiticus

Convolvulus elymaiticus, commonly known as the sand convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually prostrate or creeping.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
