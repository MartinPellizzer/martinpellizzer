Crataegus kelloggii

Crataegus kelloggii, commonly known as the Kellogg hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
