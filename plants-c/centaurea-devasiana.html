Centaurea devasiana

Centaurea devasiana, commonly known as the Deva's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue flower heads surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea devasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
