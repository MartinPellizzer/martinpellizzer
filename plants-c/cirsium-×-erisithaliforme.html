Cirsium × erisithaliforme

Cirsium × erisithaliforme, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cirsium arvense (creeping thistle) and Cirsium vulgare (common thistle), resulting in a plant with a complex genetic makeup. The species is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
