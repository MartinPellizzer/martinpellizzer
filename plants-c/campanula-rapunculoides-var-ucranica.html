Campanula rapunculoides var. ucranica

Campanula rapunculoides var. ucranica, commonly known as the Ukrainian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
