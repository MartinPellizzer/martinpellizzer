Crotalaria glauca var. humilis

Crotalaria glauca var. humilis, commonly known as the small blue morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces slender, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
