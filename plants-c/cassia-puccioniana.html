Cassia puccioniana

Cassia puccioniana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow, fern-like leaves that provide a vibrant visual display. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by long, slender pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. Cassia puccioniana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
