Cynoglossum hungaricum

Cynoglossum hungaricum, commonly known as Hungarian tongue-shaped flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers that resemble a tongue in shape, hence its common name. The plant features rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it contributes to biodiversity and supports pollinators.
