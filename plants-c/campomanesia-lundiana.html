Campomanesia lundiana

Campomanesia lundiana, commonly known as Brazilian rosewood or *pau-rosa*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

Its wood is highly valued for its rich color and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and musical instruments.
