Cinnamomum mezii

Cinnamomum mezii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum mezii, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to southern China, particularly in the provinces of Guangdong, Guangxi, and Yunnan. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The botanical features of Cinnamomum mezii include its evergreen nature, lance-shaped leaves, and its ability to thrive in subtropical climates.
