Cereus speciosissimus var. danielsii

Cereus speciosissimus var. danielsii, commonly known as the Daniel's Christmas Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented stems that branch into short, thick, fleshy segments, which are adapted for storing water. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular houseplant, especially during the winter months.
