Cornus multinervosa

Cornus multinervosa, commonly known as the slender cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its delicate, branching structure and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Cornus multinervosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
