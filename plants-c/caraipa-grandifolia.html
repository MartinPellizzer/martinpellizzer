Caraipa grandifolia

Caraipa grandifolia, commonly known as the Caraipa rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its density and fragrance. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and thrives in warm, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
