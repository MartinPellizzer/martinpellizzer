Celtis sinensis var. tetrandra

Celtis sinensis var. tetrandra, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
