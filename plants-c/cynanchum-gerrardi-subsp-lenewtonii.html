Cynanchum gerrardi subsp. lenewtonii

Cynanchum gerrardi subsp.

lenewtonii, commonly known as the slender milkweed vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to parts of eastern North America. It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, known for its milkweed species, and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
