Cyperus chilensis

Cyperus chilensis, commonly known as the Chile sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Cyperus chilensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
