Cajanus striatus

Cajanus striatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clusterbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Cajanus striatus thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in regions where legumes are a staple food source.
