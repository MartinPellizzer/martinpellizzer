Chloraea galeata

Chloraea galeata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Chloraea galeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
