Cissus mauritiana

Cissus mauritiana, commonly known as the African dewberry, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
