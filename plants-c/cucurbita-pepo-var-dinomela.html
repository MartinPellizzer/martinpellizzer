Cucurbita pepo var. dinomela

Cucurbita pepo var. dinomela, commonly known as the Mexican scalloped gourd, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its large, ribbed, and often warty fruits that can vary in color from green to orange or red. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and melons. It typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine with palmately lobed leaves.

The fruit is used in traditional cooking and is valued for its unique texture and flavor.
