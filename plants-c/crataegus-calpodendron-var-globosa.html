Crataegus calpodendron var. globosa

Crataegus calpodendron var. globosa, commonly known as the globular hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, often multi-branched form and produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant bears red to orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it has thorny branches typical of the Crataegus genus.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
