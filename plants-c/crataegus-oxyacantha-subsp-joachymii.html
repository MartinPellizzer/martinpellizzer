Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. joachymii

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

joachymii, commonly known as the Joachym's hawthorn, is a subspecies native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 8 meters.

The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red pome that ripens in the fall, attracting various bird species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
