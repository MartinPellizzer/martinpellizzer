Cassia flavicoma var. paraguariensis

Cassia flavicoma var. paraguariensis, commonly known as *Paraguariensis*, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves traditionally used in herbal remedies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
