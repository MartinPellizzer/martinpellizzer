Cereus piauhyensis

Cereus piauhyensis, commonly known as the Piauhy Cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil after rare rains.

Cereus piauhyensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
