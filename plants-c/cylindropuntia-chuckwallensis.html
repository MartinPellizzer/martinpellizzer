Cylindropuntia chuckwallensis

Cylindropuntia chuckwallensis, commonly known as Chuckwalla cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
