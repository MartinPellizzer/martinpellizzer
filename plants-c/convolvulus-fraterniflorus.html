Convolvulus fraterniflorus

Convolvulus fraterniflorus, commonly known as the brother's flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants characterized by their twining stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
