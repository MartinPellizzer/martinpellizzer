Chrysochosma lemmonii var. australis

Chrysochosma lemmonii var. australis, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking yellow foliage, which is particularly vivid during the summer months. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
