Corybas carinatus

Corybas carinatus, commonly known as the spurred helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped flower with a spurred lip that extends backward. The plant typically grows in forest understoreys, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a brownish tint, and it produces small, round seeds.

Corybas carinatus is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
