Cynorkis crispa

Cynorkis crispa, commonly known as the wavy-leaved cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and can grow quite large, reaching lengths of up to 2 meters.

Cynorkis crispa is native to southern Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
