Campanula dasyantha subsp. chamissonis

Campanula dasyantha subsp.

chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that are typically opposite or whorled in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
