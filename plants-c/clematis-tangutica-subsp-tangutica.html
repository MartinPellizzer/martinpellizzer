Clematis tangutica subsp. tangutica

Clematis tangutica subsp.

tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of western China. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated corolla. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
