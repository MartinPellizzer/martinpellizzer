Coronopus pectinatus

Coronopus pectinatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
