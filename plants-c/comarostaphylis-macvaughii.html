Comarostaphylis macvaughii

Comarostaphylis macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, typically found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Comarostaphylis macvaughii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
