Cirsium mexicanum

Cirsium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are arranged in a rounded cluster.

Cirsium mexicanum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
