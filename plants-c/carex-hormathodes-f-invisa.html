Carex hormathodes f. invisa

Carex hormathodes f. invisa, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

The silky sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
