Croton alchorneifolius

Croton alchorneifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
