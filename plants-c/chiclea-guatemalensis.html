Chiclea guatemalensis

Chiclea guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan chicle tree, is a species native to Central America, particularly Guatemala.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
