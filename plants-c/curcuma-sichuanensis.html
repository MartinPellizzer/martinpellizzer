Curcuma sichuanensis

Curcuma sichuanensis, commonly known as Sichuan turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, pale yellow rhizomes that contain curcuminoids, giving it its distinctive color and medicinal properties. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences.

This plant is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
