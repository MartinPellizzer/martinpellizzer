Calamagrostis silvatica

Calamagrostis silvatica, commonly known as the silvergrass or woodland reed, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, silver-tipped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden florets.

Calamagrostis silvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and biodiversity.
