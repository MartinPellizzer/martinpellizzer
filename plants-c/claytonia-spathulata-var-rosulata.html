Claytonia spathulata var. rosulata

Claytonia spathulata var. rosulata, commonly known as the rosette pink, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
