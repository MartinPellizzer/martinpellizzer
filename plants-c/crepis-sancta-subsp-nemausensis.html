Crepis sancta subsp. nemausensis

Crepis sancta subsp.

nemausensis, commonly known as the Nemausus bentgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in grassy areas and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and is often used in ornamental gardening.
