Capsicum chamaecerasus

Capsicum chamaecerasus, commonly known as the wild chili pepper, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
