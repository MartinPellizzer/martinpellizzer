Coleus pubescens

Coleus pubescens, commonly known as the woolly coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its vibrant coloration and texture.
