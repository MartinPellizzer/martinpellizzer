Cuphea trichochila

Cuphea trichochila, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of showy, tubular red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea trichochila is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
