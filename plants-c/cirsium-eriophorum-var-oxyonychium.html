Cirsium eriophorum var. oxyonychium

Cirsium eriophorum var. oxyonychium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
