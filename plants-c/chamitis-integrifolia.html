Chamitis integrifolia

Chamitis integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Chamitis integrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
