Chrysanthemum cadmeum

Chrysanthemum cadmeum, commonly known as the Cadmeum chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Chrysanthemum cadmeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
