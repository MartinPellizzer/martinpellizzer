Cyperus papyrus var. antiquorum

Cyperus papyrus var. antiquorum, commonly known as the ancient papyrus sedge, is a variant of the Cyperus papyrus species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Historically, it was used to make papyrus paper in ancient Egypt, making it a culturally significant plant.
