Clematis alpina var. macropetala

Clematis alpina var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five sepals that are often mistaken for petals, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clematis alpina var. macropetala prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
