Callista purpurea

Callista purpurea, commonly known as the purple callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
