Cardiolepis dentata

Cardiolepis dentata, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cardiolepis dentata thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
