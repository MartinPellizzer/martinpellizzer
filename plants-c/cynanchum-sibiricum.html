Cynanchum sibiricum

Cynanchum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Cynanchum sibiricum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
