Crataegus rhodella

Crataegus rhodella, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits known as pomes, which are typically eaten by birds. Crataegus rhodella typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
