Carex limosa subsp. planifolia

Carex limosa subsp.

planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved sedges, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are flat and smooth, typically ranging from green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender stems. It thrives in full to partial sun and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
