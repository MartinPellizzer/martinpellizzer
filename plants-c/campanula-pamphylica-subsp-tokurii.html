Campanula pamphylica subsp. tokurii

Campanula pamphylica subsp.

tokurii, commonly known as the Tokurii bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
