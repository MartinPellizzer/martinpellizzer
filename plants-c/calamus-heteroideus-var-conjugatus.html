Calamus heteroideus var. conjugatus

Calamus heteroideus var. conjugatus, commonly known as the paired reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
