Calanthe fimbriata

Calanthe fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or wooded slopes. Its flowers have a distinctive fringed appearance on the petals, which gives it its common name.

Calanthe fimbriata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
