Cheiridopsis tenuifolia

Cheiridopsis tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Cheiridopsis tenuifolia is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
