Cupressus doniana

Cupressus doniana, commonly known as the African cypress, is a coniferous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and a pyramidal or conical shape. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, and its cones are woody and typically contain two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has been used in traditional construction and as a source of timber.
