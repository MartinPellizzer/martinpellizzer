Calliandra codonandra

Calliandra codonandra, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, winged seeds in its pods. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
