Cavallium urens

Cavallium urens, commonly known as the common stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Cavallium urens is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

It is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
