Carex bolanderi var. sparsiflora

Carex bolanderi var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the Bolander's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
