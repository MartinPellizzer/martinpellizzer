Crataegus robur

Crataegus robur, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called haws, which are typically harvested in the fall. Crataegus robur is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedgerows and gardens.

It also has historical uses in herbal medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
