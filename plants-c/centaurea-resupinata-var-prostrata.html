Centaurea resupinata var. prostrata

Centaurea resupinata var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate cornflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, giving it a mat-like appearance. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
