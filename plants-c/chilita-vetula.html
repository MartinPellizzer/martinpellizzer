Chilita vetula

Chilita vetula, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked but play a role in its reproductive cycle.

Chilita vetula is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
