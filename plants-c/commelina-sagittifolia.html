Commelina sagittifolia

Commelina sagittifolia, commonly known as arrowleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white throat, often surrounded by three lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a pointed tip, and the flowers are clustered in umbels.

Commelina sagittifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and unique foliage.
