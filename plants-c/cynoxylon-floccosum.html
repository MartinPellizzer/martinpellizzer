Cynoxylon floccosum

Cynoxylon floccosum, commonly known as the woolly woodbine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy seed pods. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The woody stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving the plant its common name.
