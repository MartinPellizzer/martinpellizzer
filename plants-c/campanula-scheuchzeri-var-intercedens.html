Campanula scheuchzeri var. intercedens

Campanula scheuchzeri var. intercedens, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, soft down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Campanula scheuchzeri var. intercedens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
