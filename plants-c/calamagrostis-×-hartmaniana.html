Calamagrostis × hartmaniana

Calamagrostis × hartmaniana, commonly known as the silvergrass or purple moor grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of purple or pink flowers. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense stands.

Calamagrostis × hartmaniana is also noted for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
