Cardiolepis rubra

Cardiolepis rubra, commonly known as the red-leaved orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Cardiolepis rubra is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
