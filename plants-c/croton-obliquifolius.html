Croton obliquifolius

Croton obliquifolius, commonly known as the zigzag croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage.
