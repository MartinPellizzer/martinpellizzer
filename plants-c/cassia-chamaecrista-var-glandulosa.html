Cassia chamaecrista var. glandulosa

Cassia chamaecrista var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its glandular structures on the stems and leaves, which secrete a sticky substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
