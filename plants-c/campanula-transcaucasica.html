Campanula transcaucasica

Campanula transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula transcaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
