Clematis pashanensis var. latisepala

Clematis pashanensis var. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal clematis, is a flowering vine native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue flowers with broad, showy sepals that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and graceful growth habit.
