Centaurea intybacea var. glabrescens

Centaurea intybacea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
