Coreopsis ridens

Coreopsis ridens, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, linear to lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a central disk, blooming from late spring to early fall.

Coreopsis ridens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
