Cerasus prostrata

Cerasus prostrata, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
