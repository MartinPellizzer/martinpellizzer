Campylanthus ramosissimus

Campylanthus ramosissimus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features wiry, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a notable species in its native habitat.
