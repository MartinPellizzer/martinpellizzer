Clusia latipes

Clusia latipes, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Clusia latipes is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
