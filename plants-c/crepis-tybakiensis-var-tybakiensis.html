Crepis tybakiensis var. tybakiensis

Crepis tybakiensis var. tybakiensis, commonly known as the Tybakiensian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive achene fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
