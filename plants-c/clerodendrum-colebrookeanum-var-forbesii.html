Clerodendrum colebrookeanum var. forbesii

Clerodendrum colebrookeanum var. forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
