Chamaedorea pygmaea

Chamaedorea pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo palm, is a compact, evergreen plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in smooth, dark green leaves that resemble bamboo in appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate, making it ideal for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with consistent moisture.

Chamaedorea pygmaea is appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to purify the air.
