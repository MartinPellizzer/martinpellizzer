Castanea castanea

Castanea castanea, commonly known as the sweet chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It produces oval, spiny fruits called burrs, which enclose one or two edible nuts. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, typically measuring 5–12 cm in length. The tree can grow up to 25–30 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown.

Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
