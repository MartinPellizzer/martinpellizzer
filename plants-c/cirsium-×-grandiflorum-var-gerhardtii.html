Cirsium × grandiflorum var. gerhardtii

Cirsium × grandiflorum var. gerhardtii, commonly known as the Gerhardt's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
