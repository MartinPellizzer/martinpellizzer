Cepa alba

Cepa alba, commonly known as the white onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and broad, strap-like leaves. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor compared to other onions.

Cepa alba is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
