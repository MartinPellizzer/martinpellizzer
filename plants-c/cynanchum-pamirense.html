Cynanchum pamirense

Cynanchum pamirense, commonly known as the Pamir milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a unique corolla that features a hood-like structure.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ecological resilience.
