Calamagrostis sandiensis

Calamagrostis sandiensis, commonly known as the sand reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in sandy or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often adorned with silky, feathery bristles.

Calamagrostis sandiensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
