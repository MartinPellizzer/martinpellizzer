Crepidium chamaeorchis

Crepidium chamaeorchis, commonly known as the ground orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a slender rhizome with a rosette of linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a spike, and are typically pollinated by insects.

Crepidium chamaeorchis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
