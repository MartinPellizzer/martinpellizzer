Carduus × brunneri

Carduus × brunneri, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. Its floral structure includes both ray and disk flowers, typical of the composite flower head.

Carduus × brunneri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
