Convolvulus robertianus

Convolvulus robertianus, commonly known as the Robert's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale purple to pink, with five lobes, and bloom from spring to early summer.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
