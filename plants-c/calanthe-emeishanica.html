Calanthe emeishanica

Calanthe emeishanica, commonly known as the Emei Mountain calanthe, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the rich, organic soil in its native habitat.

Calanthe emeishanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is considered a flagship species for conservation efforts in its region.
