Cedrus deodara var. variegata

Cedrus deodara var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated deodar cedar, is a variant of the deodar cedar native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white or yellowish markings on the needles. This plant is a coniferous evergreen with a conical shape and can grow quite tall under favorable conditions. Its bark is typically reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The variegation in its leaves makes it a popular ornamental tree in horticulture.
