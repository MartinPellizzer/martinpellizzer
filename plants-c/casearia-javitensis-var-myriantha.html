Casearia javitensis var. myriantha

Casearia javitensis var. myriantha, commonly known as the white-flowered casearia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
