Cyperus flavescens subsp. fallaciosus

Cyperus flavescens subsp.

fallaciosus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
