Chlorospatha cedralensis

Chlorospatha cedralensis, commonly known as the Cedralensis Greenleaf, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
