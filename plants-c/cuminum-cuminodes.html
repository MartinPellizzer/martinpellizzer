Cuminum cuminodes

Cuminum cuminodes, commonly known as love-in-a-mist, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that create a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Cuminum cuminodes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and pleasant, mild fragrance.
