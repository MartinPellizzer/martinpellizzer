Callistemon speciosus

Callistemon speciosus, commonly known as the spotted gum bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
