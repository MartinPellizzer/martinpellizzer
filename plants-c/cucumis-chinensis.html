Cucumis chinensis

Cucumis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green when mature, with a smooth, thin rind. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blossoms, and it relies on pollination by insects.

The fruit is used both as a vegetable and for its seeds, which are edible and rich in nutrients.
