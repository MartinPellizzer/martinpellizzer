Citrus grandis var. kotokan

Citrus grandis var. kotokan, commonly known as the Kotokan grapefruit, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, round fruit with a thick, yellow-orange rind and a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy, elliptical, and have a strong citrus scent.

The fruit is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its high vitamin C content and aromatic properties.
