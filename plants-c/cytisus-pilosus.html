Cytisus pilosus

Cytisus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Cytisus pilosus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry areas.
