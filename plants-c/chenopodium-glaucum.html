Chenopodium glaucum

Chenopodium glaucum, commonly known as goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating called a glaucous bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in dry, three-lobed fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

Chenopodium glaucum is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines and as a source of nutrition.
