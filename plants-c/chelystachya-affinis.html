Chelystachya affinis

Chelystachya affinis, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, pale flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Chelystachya affinis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
