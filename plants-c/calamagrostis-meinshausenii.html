Calamagrostis meinshausenii

Calamagrostis meinshausenii, commonly known as the meinhausean reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, streambanks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds ornamental value.

Calamagrostis meinshausenii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
