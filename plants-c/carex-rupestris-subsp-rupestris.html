Carex rupestris subsp. rupestris

Carex rupestris subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, such as cliffs, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky bloom. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to well-drained soils.
