Craccina sulcata

Craccina sulcata, commonly known as the sulcate grass.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or saline environments.

Craccina sulcata is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
