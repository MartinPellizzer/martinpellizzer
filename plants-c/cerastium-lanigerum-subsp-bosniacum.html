Cerastium lanigerum subsp. bosniacum

Cerastium lanigerum subsp.

bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with dense, soft white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy surface and a truncate or notched base.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
