Carissa edulis var. comorensis

Carissa edulis var. comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian yellow custard apple, is a shrub native to the Comoros Islands.

It produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, creamy texture. The plant has dense, thorny foliage and is adapted to tropical climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
