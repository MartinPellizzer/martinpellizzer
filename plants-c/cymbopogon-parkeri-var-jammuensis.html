Cymbopogon parkeri var. jammuensis

Cymbopogon parkeri var. jammuensis, commonly known as Jammu grass, is a perennial grass native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct aromatic scent. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and alpine habitats.

Due to its unique fragrance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
