Centaurea × alexandri-borzae

Centaurea × alexandri-borzae, commonly known as the Alexandrian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open fields. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a notable ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural areas.
