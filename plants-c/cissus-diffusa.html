Cissus diffusa

Cissus diffusa, commonly known as the bonebrake or rock fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cissus diffusa thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
