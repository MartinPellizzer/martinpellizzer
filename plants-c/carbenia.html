Carbenia

Carbenia, commonly known as the common carbenia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often found in moist, woodland habitats. Carbenia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a distinctive flavor to dishes.
