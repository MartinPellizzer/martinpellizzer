Coleus esculentus

Coleus esculentus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are small, berry-like berries that contain seeds.

While it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes, it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
