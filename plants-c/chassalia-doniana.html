Chassalia doniana

Chassalia doniana, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in grasslands and has a bushy, compact habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disc florets, typical of the Compositae family.

Chassalia doniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils.
