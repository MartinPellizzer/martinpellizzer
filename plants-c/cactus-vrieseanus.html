Cactus vrieseanus

Cactus vrieseanus, commonly known as the Vriesia cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red, are a striking feature that attracts pollinators like bees and birds.

The Vriesia cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
