Croton chamelensis

Croton chamelensis, commonly known as the Cameroon croton, is a flowering plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse species with often milky sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive green or variegated appearance.

Croton chamelensis produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
