Calliandra comosa

Calliandra comosa, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Calliandra comosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
