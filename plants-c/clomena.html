Clomena

Clomena, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are usually palmately lobed and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, waxy appearance, blooming in late winter or early spring. Clomena is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.

It is often cultivated in shaded gardens for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.
