Convolvulus secundus var. latifolius

Convolvulus secundus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within the Convolvulaceae family.
