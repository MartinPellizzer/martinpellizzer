Carex campyloxys

Carex campyloxys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Native to wet or moist habitats, it thrives in environments with consistent moisture, such as marshes or along stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, dry berries.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
