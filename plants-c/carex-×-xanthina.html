Carex × xanthina

Carex × xanthina, commonly known as golden sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and damp woodlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex × xanthina is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
