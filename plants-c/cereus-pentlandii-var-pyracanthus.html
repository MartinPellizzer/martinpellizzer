Cereus pentlandii var. pyracanthus

Cereus pentlandii var. pyracanthus, commonly known as the Pyracanthus Cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and striking blooms make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
