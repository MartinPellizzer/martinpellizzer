Cassia mariquitensis

Cassia mariquitensis, commonly known as the Philippine cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in a long, slender pod, and the plant is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
