Crotalaria berteroana

Crotalaria berteroana, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria berteroana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
