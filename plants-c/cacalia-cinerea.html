Cacalia cinerea

Cacalia cinerea, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps it reflect sunlight and retain moisture. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cacalia cinerea is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
