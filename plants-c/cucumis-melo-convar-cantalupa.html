Cucumis melo convar. cantalupa

Cucumis melo convar. cantalupa, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It is a vine that typically grows on trellises or supports, producing large, yellow or orange fruits. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins and yellow flowers that are both male and female. The fruit develops from a single ovary and matures over several weeks, reaching peak sweetness during the summer months.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins A and C.
