Centaurea × beskidiana

Centaurea × beskidiana, commonly known as the Beskid centaury, is a hybrid plant species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and vibrant blue to purple flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and prefers well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea × beskidiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
