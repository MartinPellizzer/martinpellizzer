Cucurbita moschata var. minor

Cucurbita moschata var. minor, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin or casaba pumpkin, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita moschata.

It is characterized by its round, slightly ribbed fruit with a pale yellow to orange skin. The plant is a vine that produces large, fleshy fruits with a sweet, nutty flavor. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications due to its unique appearance and versatility.
