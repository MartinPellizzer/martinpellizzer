Chrysobraya glaricola

Chrysobraya glaricola, commonly known as the glaucous chrysobraya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its distinctive blue-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, typical of the family.

Chrysobraya glaricola is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
