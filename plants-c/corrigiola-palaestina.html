Corrigiola palaestina

Corrigiola palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, usually from April to May. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to thrive in arid and saline environments.

Corrigiola palaestina is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
