Cyperus altochrysocephalus

Cyperus altochrysocephalus, commonly known as the golden-headed flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often yellow or golden in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
