Conchocarpus concinnus

Conchocarpus concinnus, commonly known as the white ant plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, ant-like structures that cover its stems and leaves, which are actually specialized seed pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
