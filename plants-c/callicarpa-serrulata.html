Callicarpa serrulata

Callicarpa serrulata, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. These fruits are edible and often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.

Its vibrant foliage and showy fruits make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
