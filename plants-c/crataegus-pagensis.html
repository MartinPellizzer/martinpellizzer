Crataegus pagensis

Crataegus pagensis, commonly known as the Pagus hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to a range of soil types.
