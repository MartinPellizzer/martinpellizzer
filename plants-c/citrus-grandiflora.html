Citrus grandiflora

Citrus grandiflora, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green, and sour-tasting fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its botanical features include a branched root system and a dense, thorny crown.

Citrus grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in hybridization with other citrus species.
