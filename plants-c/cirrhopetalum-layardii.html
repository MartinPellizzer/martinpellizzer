Cirrhopetalum layardii

Cirrhopetalum layardii, commonly known as the Layard's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated petals that resemble flowing hair, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Cirrhopetalum layardii is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
