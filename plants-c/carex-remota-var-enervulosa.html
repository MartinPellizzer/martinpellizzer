Carex remota var. enervulosa

Carex remota var. enervulosa, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This variety is typically found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in moist environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
