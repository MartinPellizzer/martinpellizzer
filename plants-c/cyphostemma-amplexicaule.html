Cyphostemma amplexicaule

Cyphostemma amplexicaule, commonly known as the climbing cyphostemma, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, broad, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting succulent characteristics to store water.

Cyphostemma amplexicaule is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
