Campomanesia littoralis

Campomanesia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal guava, is a small evergreen tree native to coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
