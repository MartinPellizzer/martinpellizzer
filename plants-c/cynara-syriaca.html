Cynara syriaca

Cynara syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to its native arid and semi-arid environments.
