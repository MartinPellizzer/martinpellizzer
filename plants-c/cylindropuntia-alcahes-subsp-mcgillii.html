Cylindropuntia alcahes subsp. mcgillii

Cylindropuntia alcahes subsp.

mcgillii, commonly known as the McGill's barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features a cylindrical stem that is ribbed and covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological characteristics.
