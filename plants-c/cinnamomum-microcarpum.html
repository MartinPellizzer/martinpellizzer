Cinnamomum microcarpum

Cinnamomum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark berries. The plant produces essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent, which is similar to other cinnamon species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and commercial purposes.
