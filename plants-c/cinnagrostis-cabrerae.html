Cinnagrostis cabrerae

Cinnagrostis cabrerae, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across the island's terrain.

Cinnagrostis cabrerae is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
