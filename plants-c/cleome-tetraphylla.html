Cleome tetraphylla

Cleome tetraphylla, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome tetraphylla is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plant.
