Croton villosus var. rufescens

Croton villosus var. rufescens, commonly known as the rusty croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are typically green with reddish or brownish undersides. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
