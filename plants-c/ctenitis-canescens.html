Ctenitis canescens

Ctenitis canescens, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Tectariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a hairy, scaly texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are divided into broad, shield-shaped segments, giving it a unique appearance.

Ctenitis canescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
