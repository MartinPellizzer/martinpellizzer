Cotula filifolia

Cotula filifolia, commonly known as threadleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The leaves are narrow, thread-like, and covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

This plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
