Carex nubigena subsp. albata

Carex nubigena subsp.

albata, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
