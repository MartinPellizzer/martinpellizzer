Cyperus conglomeratus var. multiculmis

Cyperus conglomeratus var. multiculmis, commonly known as the clustered flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
