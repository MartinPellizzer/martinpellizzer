Cleome viscosa f. deglabrata

Cleome viscosa f. deglabrata, commonly known as the naked-stem cleome, is a variant of the common cleome.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its stems are often hairless or nearly so, giving it the "naked" appearance in its name.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
