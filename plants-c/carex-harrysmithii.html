Carex harrysmithii

Carex harrysmithii, commonly known as Harry Smith's sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, while the flowering spikes are often reddish or purplish in color.

Carex harrysmithii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
