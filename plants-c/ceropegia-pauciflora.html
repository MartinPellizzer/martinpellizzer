Ceropegia pauciflora

Ceropegia pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features trailing stems with fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pale green or white, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
