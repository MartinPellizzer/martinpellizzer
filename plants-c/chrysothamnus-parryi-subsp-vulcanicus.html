Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. vulcanicus

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

vulcanicus, commonly known as the Vulcan's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often found in areas with volcanic soil, reflecting its association with geologically active regions.
