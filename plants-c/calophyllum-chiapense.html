Calophyllum chiapense

Calophyllum chiapense, commonly known as Chiapan magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Calophyllum chiapense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
