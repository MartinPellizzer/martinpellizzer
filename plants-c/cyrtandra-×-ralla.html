Cyrtandra × ralla

Cyrtandra × ralla, commonly known as the hybrid Cyrtandra, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Cyrtandra carinata and Cyrtandra hirsuta, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology. The plant typically produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are adapted for pollination by birds and insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in botanical gardens and private collections.
