Ceropegia letestui

Ceropegia letestui, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy texture and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.
