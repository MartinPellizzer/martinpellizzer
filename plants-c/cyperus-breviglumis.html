Cyperus breviglumis

Cyperus breviglumis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often hidden among the foliage.

Cyperus breviglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
