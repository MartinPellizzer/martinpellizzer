Cassia × sturtii var. planipes

Cassia × sturtii var. planipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cassia, is a hybrid shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
