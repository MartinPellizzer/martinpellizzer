Clusia dickinsoniana

Clusia dickinsoniana, commonly known as the yellow hurricane, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a yellow or white underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.

Clusia dickinsoniana is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and air pollution.
