Crocus tournefortianus var. venereus

Crocus tournefortianus var. venereus, commonly known as the Venus crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, vivid yellow flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
