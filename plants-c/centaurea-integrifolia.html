Centaurea integrifolia

Centaurea integrifolia, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
