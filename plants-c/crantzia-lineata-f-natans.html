Crantzia lineata f. natans

Crantzia lineata f. natans, commonly known as the floating crantzia, is a rare aquatic plant characterized by its unique adaptations to a water environment.

It exhibits a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread across the water surface. Its leaves are typically narrow and ribbon-like, floating just above the water, which aids in photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikes, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often found in ponds and lakes.
