Cremanium elaeodendrum

Cremanium elaeodendrum, commonly known as the yellow stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause skin irritation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Cremanium elaeodendrum is known for its ecological importance, providing food for various insects and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
