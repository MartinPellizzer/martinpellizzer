Cyclopogon pamii

Cyclopogon pamii, commonly known as the Pampean cyclopogon, is a species of flowering plant native to the grasslands of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. Cyclopogon pamii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features that allow it to conserve water efficiently.
