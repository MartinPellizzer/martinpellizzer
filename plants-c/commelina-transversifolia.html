Commelina transversifolia

Commelina transversifolia, commonly known as the transverseleaf spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often with three petals and a distinctive three-lobed calyx. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina transversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
