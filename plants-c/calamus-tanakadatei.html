Calamus tanakadatei

Calamus tanakadatei, commonly known as the Japanese calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically found at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calamus tanakadatei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
