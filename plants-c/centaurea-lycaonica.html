Centaurea lycaonica

Centaurea lycaonica, commonly known as the Lycaonia knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright blue or purple flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea lycaonica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
