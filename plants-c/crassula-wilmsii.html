Crassula wilmsii

Crassula wilmsii, commonly known as the Wilmsii crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in a compact, rosette form, with leaves that are often tinged with red or purple under bright light. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer.

Crassula wilmsii is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
