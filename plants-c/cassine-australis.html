Cassine australis

Cassine australis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia, characterized by its smooth, papery bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, white, five-petaled flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
