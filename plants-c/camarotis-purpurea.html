Camarotis purpurea

Camarotis purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters along its twining stems. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for climbing.

Camarotis purpurea requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
