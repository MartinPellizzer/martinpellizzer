Cotula obscura

Cotula obscura, commonly known as the dark cotula, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cotula obscura is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
