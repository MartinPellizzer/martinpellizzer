Carex hudsonii f. stenocarpa

Carex hudsonii f. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-cupped sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is native to North America. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct midrib, while the flowers are usually pale green to brown.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
