Carex squarrosa f. robusta

Carex squarrosa f. robusta, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
