Centaurea paniculata subsp. paniculata

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the panicled centaury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers, which are surrounded by pale blue to white bracts.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
