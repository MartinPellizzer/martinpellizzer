Chenopodium desertorum subsp. virosum

Chenopodium desertorum subsp.

virosum, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in desert environments.
