Corylus avellana var. pectinata

Corylus avellana var. pectinata, commonly known as the curly hazel, is a variety of the common hazel that is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows. It produces small, edible nuts that are similar to those of other hazel species. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
