Cyathea pustulosa

Cyathea pustulosa, commonly known as the spiky tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive trunk covered with a thick, fibrous layer of old leaf bases. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea pustulosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
