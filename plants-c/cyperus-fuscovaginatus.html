Cyperus fuscovaginatus

Cyperus fuscovaginatus, commonly known as the dark sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and have a narrow, cylindrical shape. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense clusters, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus fuscovaginatus is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
