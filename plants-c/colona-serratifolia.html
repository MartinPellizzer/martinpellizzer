Colona serratifolia

Colona serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf colona, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a rough texture.

Colona serratifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
