Cuphea corymbifera

Cuphea corymbifera, commonly known as the coral cup or red cup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a distinctive corolla that is often swollen at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, round, red berries that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
