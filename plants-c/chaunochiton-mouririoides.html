Chaunochiton mouririoides

Chaunochiton mouririoides, commonly known as the Mouririoides sea snail, is a species of sea snail in the family Chaunoclitidae.

It is characterized by its small, elongated shell that is typically smooth and glossy. The shell coloration can vary from pale yellow to reddish-brown, often with faint radial markings. This species is found in shallow marine environments along the coasts of southern Brazil.

It plays a role in benthic ecosystems by contributing to the grazing pressure on algae and other microorganisms.
