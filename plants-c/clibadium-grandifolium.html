Clibadium grandifolium

Clibadium grandifolium, commonly known as the giant sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Due to its striking foliage, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
