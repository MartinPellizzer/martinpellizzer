Cleome gobica

Cleome gobica, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like blooms that appear in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome gobica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
