Cassia midas

Cassia midas, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of golden-yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia midas thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
