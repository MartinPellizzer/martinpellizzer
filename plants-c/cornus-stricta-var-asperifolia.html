Cornus stricta var. asperifolia

Cornus stricta var. asperifolia, commonly known as smooth cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth or slightly hairy, depending on the variety. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

This species is often used in wildlife habitats due to its ability to provide food and shelter for various animals.
