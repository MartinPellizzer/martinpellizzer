Conospermum coerulescens subsp. coerulescens

Conospermum coerulescens subsp.

coerulescens, commonly known as the Blueberry Bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking blue to purple flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in both natural and landscaped settings.
