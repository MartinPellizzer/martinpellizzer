Callicarpa paniculata

Callicarpa paniculata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its showy fruits attract birds and are a valuable food source in the ecosystem.
