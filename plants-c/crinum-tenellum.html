Crinum tenellum

Crinum tenellum, commonly known as the slender crinum, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a spiky, cylindrical shape, and bloom during the summer months.

Crinum tenellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
