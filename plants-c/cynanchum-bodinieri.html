Cynanchum bodinieri

Cynanchum bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or Bodinier's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds with silky appendages. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Cynanchum bodinieri is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
