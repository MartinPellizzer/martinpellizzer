Clematis × modesta

Clematis × modesta, commonly known as the Japanese vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive, slightly twisted appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × modesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
