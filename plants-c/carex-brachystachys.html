Carex brachystachys

Carex brachystachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. Native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, it often thrives in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
