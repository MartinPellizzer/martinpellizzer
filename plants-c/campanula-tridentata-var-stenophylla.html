Campanula tridentata var. stenophylla

Campanula tridentata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with three lobes, giving the plant its species name "tridentata.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
