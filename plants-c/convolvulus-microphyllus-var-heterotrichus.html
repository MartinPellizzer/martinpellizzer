Convolvulus microphyllus var. heterotrichus

Convolvulus microphyllus var. heterotrichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, ascending stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a spurred structure at the base. It produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry, sunny conditions.
