Chionodoxa grandiflora

Chionodoxa grandiflora, commonly known as the giant crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Chionodoxa grandiflora is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread over time, creating dense displays of color.
