Coussarea Odoratissima

Sweet bunchbone, scientifically known as Coussarea odoratissima, is a striking tropical plant celebrated for its fragrant, clustered flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within rainforests. The plant's unique appearance and sweet scent make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its flowers, which bloom in dense clusters, emit a delicate aroma reminiscent of vanilla and citrus. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in the broader horticultural world.
