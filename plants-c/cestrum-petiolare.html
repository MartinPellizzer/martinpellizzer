Cestrum petiolare

Cestrum petiolare, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cestrum petiolare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its nocturnal fragrance.
