Cissus pohlii

Cissus pohlii, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Cissus pohlii thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to trail from hanging baskets or shelves.
