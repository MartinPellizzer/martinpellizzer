Carex allomacros

Carex allomacros, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.

Carex allomacros is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
