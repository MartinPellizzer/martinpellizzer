Cattleya grandis

Cattleya grandis, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it one of the most striking orchids in the genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Cattleya grandis is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
