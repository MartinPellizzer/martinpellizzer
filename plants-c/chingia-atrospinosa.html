Chingia atrospinosa

Chingia atrospinosa, commonly known as the black-spined cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its spines are short and black, providing protection from herbivores.

Chingia atrospinosa is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant in xerophytic environments.
