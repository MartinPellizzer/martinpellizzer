Carex varia var. novae-angliae

Carex varia var. novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
