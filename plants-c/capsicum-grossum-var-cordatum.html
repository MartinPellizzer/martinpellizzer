Capsicum grossum var. cordatum

Capsicum grossum var. cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped chili or cordate pepper, is a unique cultivar of the Capsicum genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and elongated, tapered fruits that typically range in color from green to red when mature. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its mild to moderately hot flavor.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and culinary enthusiasts.
