Cassiope hypnoides

Cassiope hypnoides, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Cassiope hypnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
