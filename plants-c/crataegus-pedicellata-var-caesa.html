Crataegus pedicellata var. caesa

Crataegus pedicellata var. caesa, commonly known as the purple-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by distinctive purple or black berries in the fall. Its botanical features include thorned stems, pinnate leaf arrangement, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and along streambanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
