Cynanchum priorii

Cynanchum priorii, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed vine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive corona, which are typically clustered in umbels. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

Cynanchum priorii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
