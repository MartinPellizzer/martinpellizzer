Cyathea rumphiana

Cyathea rumphiana, commonly known as the Rumphian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in lowland forests. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's upright growth.

Cyathea rumphiana plays a role in providing habitat for various tropical organisms and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
