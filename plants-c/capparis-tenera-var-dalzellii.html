Capparis tenera var. dalzellii

Capparis tenera var. dalzellii, commonly known as the Dalzell's prickly custard apple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a rough, spiky berry that turns red when mature and contains a single large seed.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
