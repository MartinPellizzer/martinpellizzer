Callistemon viridiflorus

Callistemon viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like inflorescences, which are composed of numerous stamens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and evergreen, providing a glossy appearance.

It blooms in spring and summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.
