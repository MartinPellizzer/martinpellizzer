Chloris tenuis

Chloris tenuis, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue-green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
