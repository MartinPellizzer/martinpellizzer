Cissus serrulata

Cissus serrulata, commonly known as the rock fig or heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus serrulata is also valued for its ability to climb and its adaptability to various soil types.
