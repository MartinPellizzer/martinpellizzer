Convolvulus clementii var. biflorus

Convolvulus clementii var. biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or coastal areas. It blooms during the warmer months and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
