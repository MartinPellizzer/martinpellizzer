Carex stellulata var. ormantha

Carex stellulata var. ormantha, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
