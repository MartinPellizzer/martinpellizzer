Carex chorda

Carex chorda, commonly known as the threadleaf sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curved, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of North America.

Carex chorda plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
