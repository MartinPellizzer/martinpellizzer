Cyperus tenellus var. micromegas

Cyperus tenellus var. micromegas, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and parallel venation.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers.
