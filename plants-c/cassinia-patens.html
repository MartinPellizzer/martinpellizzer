Cassinia patens

Cassinia patens, commonly known as the slender cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It features small, yellowish flower heads surrounded by pale green bracts, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy.

Cassinia patens is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
