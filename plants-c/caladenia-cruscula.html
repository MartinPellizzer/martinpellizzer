Caladenia cruscula

Caladenia cruscula, commonly known as the red-stemmed spider-orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a single, slender stem that bears one or two showy, spider-like flowers with a distinctive red or orange hue. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly wasps, due to their specialized structure and scent.

Caladenia cruscula is a protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
