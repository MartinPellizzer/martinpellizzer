Cephalaria syriaca var. pedunculata

Cephalaria syriaca var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Syrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
