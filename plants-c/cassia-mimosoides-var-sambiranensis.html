Cassia mimosoides var. sambiranensis

Cassia mimosoides var. sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. It produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
