Cryptocarya glaucescens var. reticulata

Cryptocarya glaucescens var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and exhibits a dense, rounded canopy. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a glossy surface and a reticulate venation pattern, giving it its varietal name.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological role in seed dispersal.
