Cerasus erythrocarpa f. squarrosa

Cerasus erythrocarpa f. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose cherry, is a botanical variant of the cherry species characterized by its distinctive floral and foliar features.

This plant is native to regions of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, and the leaves are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence, giving the plant its "squarrosa" designation. The fruit is a small, edible cherry that is appreciated for its flavor and nutritional value.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and seasonal blooms.
