Cerasus officinarum

Cerasus officinarum, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits known as cherries, which are edible and used in various culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant colors in the fall, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

Cerasus officinarum is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its beauty and cultural significance.
