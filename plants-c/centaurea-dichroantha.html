Centaurea dichroantha

Centaurea dichroantha, commonly known as the two-colored centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered inflorescences.

Centaurea dichroantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
