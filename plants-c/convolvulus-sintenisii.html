Convolvulus sintenisii

Convolvulus sintenisii, commonly known as the Sicilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Convolvulus sintenisii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
