Cuphea lobophora var. lobophora

Cuphea lobophora var. lobophora, commonly known as the flame wheel cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a fiery appearance. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
