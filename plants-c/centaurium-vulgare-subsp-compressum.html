Centaurium vulgare subsp. compressum

Centaurium vulgare subsp.

compressum, commonly known as narrowleaf lesser centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bright red or orange, clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
