Cristaria carrizalensis

Cristaria carrizalensis, commonly known as the Carrizalensis sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique adaptations and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and dryland landscapes.
