Campanula × segusina

Campanula × segusina, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 30 inches and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
