Croton trombetensis

Croton trombetensis, commonly known as the Andean croton, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that can vary in color from green to reddish hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by unique, spiny seed capsules.

Croton trombetensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
