Carex pilulifera var. azorica

Carex pilulifera var. azorica, commonly known as the Azorean sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Azores.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
