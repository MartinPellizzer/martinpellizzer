Ceanothus dentatus subsp. lobbianus

Ceanothus dentatus subsp.

lobbianus, commonly known as the Sierra ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
