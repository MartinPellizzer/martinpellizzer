Camelina souliei

Camelina souliei, commonly known as the Siberian camelina, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has hairy, linear leaves. It is typically found in dry, sandy or gravelly soils.

Camelina souliei is often used in traditional medicine and has potential for oilseed cultivation.
