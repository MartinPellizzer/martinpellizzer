Columnea nicaraguensis

Columnea nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan flame flower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Columnea nicaraguensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along stream banks.
