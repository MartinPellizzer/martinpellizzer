Crataegus tanacetifolia var. leeana

Crataegus tanacetifolia var. leeana, commonly known as the Leeana hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of the yarrow plant, giving it its species name *tanacetifolia*. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.

The Leeana hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
