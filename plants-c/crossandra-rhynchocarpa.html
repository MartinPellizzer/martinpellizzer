Crossandra rhynchocarpa

Crossandra rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Crossandra rhynchocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting flowers.
