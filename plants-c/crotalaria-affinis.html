Crotalaria affinis

Crotalaria affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with spurred petals. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Crotalaria affinis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.
