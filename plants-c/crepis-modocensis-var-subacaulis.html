Crepis modocensis var. subacaulis

Crepis modocensis var. subacaulis, commonly known as the Modoc dwarf hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of basal leaves and small, yellowish composite flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. Its botanical features include a short, nearly stemless caudex and leaves that are typically linear to lanceolate.

It is considered a rare and endemic species, with limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.
