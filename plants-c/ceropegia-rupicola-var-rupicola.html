Ceropegia rupicola var. rupicola

Ceropegia rupicola var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hanging plant.

Its unique floral structure attracts specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
