Cyrtanthus galpinii

Cyrtanthus galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's bell-shaped flower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while the stems are usually short and sturdy.

Cyrtanthus galpinii is well-suited for rock gardens and dry climates due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
