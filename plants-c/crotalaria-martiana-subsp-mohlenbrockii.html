Crotalaria martiana subsp. mohlenbrockii

Crotalaria martiana subsp.

mohlenbrockii, commonly known as Mohlenbrock's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent veining.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its pods are elongated and contain several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in certain traditional herbal practices.
