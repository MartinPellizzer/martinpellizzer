Cornus nuttallii

Cornus nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its showy white bracts that appear in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in the fall.

Nuttall's dogwood is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
