Calamagrostis neglecta var. micrantha

Calamagrostis neglecta var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish to purplish in hue. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Calamagrostis neglecta var. micrantha is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
