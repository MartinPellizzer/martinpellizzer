Caryophyllus garciniifolius

Caryophyllus garciniifolius, commonly known as the Garcinia leaf caryophyllus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
