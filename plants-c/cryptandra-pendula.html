Cryptandra pendula

Cryptandra pendula, commonly known as the hanging cryptandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Cryptandra pendula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to dry conditions.
