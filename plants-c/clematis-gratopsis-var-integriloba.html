Clematis gratopsis var. integriloba

Clematis gratopsis var. integriloba, commonly known as the velvetleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its climbing habit is supported by tendrils, and it thrives in partial to full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
