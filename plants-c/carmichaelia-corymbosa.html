Carmichaelia corymbosa

Carmichaelia corymbosa, commonly known as the kōwhai tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Carmichaelia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Māori culture.
