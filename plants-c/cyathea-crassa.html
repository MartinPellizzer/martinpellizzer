Cyathea crassa

Cyathea crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.

Cyathea crassa plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
