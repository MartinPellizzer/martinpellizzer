Conyza kunthiana

Conyza kunthiana, commonly known as the common ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Conyza kunthiana is often considered a weed but has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
