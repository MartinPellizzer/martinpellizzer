Cyanicula caerulea

Cyanicula caerulea, commonly known as the bluebloom orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of delicate, sky-blue flowers with a striking violet-pink hue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and they are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and subtle fragrance.
