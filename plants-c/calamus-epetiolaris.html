Calamus epetiolaris

Calamus epetiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus epetiolaris is often found in wet, marshy habitats and is valued for its ornamental foliage in horticultural settings.
