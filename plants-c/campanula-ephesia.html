Campanula ephesia

Campanula ephesia, commonly known as the Ephesian bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula ephesia is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
