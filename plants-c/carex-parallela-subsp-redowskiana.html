Carex parallela subsp. redowskiana

Carex parallela subsp.

redowskiana, commonly known as the redowskian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
