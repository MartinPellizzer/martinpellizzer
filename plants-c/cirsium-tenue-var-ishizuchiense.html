Cirsium tenue var. ishizuchiense

Cirsium tenue var. ishizuchiense, commonly known as the Ishizuchi thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is covered in dense, bristly hairs, which provide protection against herbivores.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
