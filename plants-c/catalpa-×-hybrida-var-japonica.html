Catalpa × hybrida var. japonica

Catalpa × hybrida var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically 10–20 cm in length. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
