Canna indica var. patens

Canna indica var. patens, commonly known as the giant canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and vibrant blooms, making it a popular choice in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Canna indica var. patens is also known for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it suitable for water gardens and bog landscapes.
