Campanula coronopifolia

Campanula coronopifolia, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often used in erosion control.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive flowers and foliage, making it a popular choice in gardens.
