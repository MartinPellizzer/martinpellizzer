Cynanchica icarica

Cynanchica icarica, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a climbing plant native to various regions in Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides.

The plant is often found in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
