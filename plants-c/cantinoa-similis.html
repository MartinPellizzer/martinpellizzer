Cantinoa similis

Cantinoa similis, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that closely resemble those of other orchid species, a trait that aids in its pollination by insects. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Cantinoa similis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
