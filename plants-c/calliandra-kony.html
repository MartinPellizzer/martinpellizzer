Calliandra kony

Calliandra kony, commonly known as the pink coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The flowers are also a source of nectar for various pollinators, contributing to biodiversity in its ecosystem.
