Coleochloa pallidior

Coleochloa pallidior, commonly known as the pale leaved grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

The botanical features of Coleochloa pallidior make it an important component of its native ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
