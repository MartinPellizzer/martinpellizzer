Carex hirta f. hirtiformis

Carex hirta f. hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely covered with short, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
