Capparis bangii

Capparis bangii, commonly known as the Bangi's caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or white. The plant's leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a slight pubescence. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Capparis bangii is often found in moist, shaded environments within forest understories.
