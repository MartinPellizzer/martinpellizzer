Cassia pendula var. hemirostrata

Cassia pendula var. hemirostrata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed sickle cassia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that produce bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
