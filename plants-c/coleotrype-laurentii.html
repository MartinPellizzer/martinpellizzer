Coleotrype laurentii

Coleotrype laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian shield lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is characterized by its bright green to yellowish thallus, which is often covered in a fine, powdery layer of spores. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides the photosynthetic capability necessary for its survival. It is typically found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in northern regions.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for air quality and ecosystem health.
