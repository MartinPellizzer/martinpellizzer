Chamaecrista adamantina

Chamaecrista adamantina, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Chamaecrista adamantina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
