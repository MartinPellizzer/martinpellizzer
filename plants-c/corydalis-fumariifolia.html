Corydalis fumariifolia

Corydalis fumariifolia, commonly known as the smokeflower or fumariifolia corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that often have a pink or purple hue. The plant produces a unique, creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
