Carex atrofusca var. major

Carex atrofusca var. major, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves that form dense clumps, and it produces spike-like flower clusters in the summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in shaded areas. It has fibrous roots and is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
