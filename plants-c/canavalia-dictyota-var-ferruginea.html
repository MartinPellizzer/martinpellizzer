Canavalia dictyota var. ferruginea

Canavalia dictyota var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with alternating pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets, often tinged with a reddish or rusty hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to grow in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in traditional agriculture.
