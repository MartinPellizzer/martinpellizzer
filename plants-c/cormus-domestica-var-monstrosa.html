Cormus domestica var. monstrosa

Cormus domestica var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant bulbous plant, is a variant of the domesticated cormus species.

It is characterized by its enlarged, starchy corms that serve as storage organs for nutrients. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, often reaching several feet in length.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and culinary uses due to its unique appearance and nutritional content.
