Cestrum rugulosum

Cestrum rugulosum, commonly known as the rough cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the evening. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cestrum rugulosum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
