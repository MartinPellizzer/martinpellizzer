Cyperus laevigatus var. distachyos

Cyperus laevigatus var. distachyos, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a fibrous root system. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its morphology makes it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
