Cytisus leucanthus var. schipkaensis

Cytisus leucanthus var. schipkaensis, commonly known as the Schipka broom, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are generally hairy. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
