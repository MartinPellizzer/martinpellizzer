Cyclocarpa

Cyclocarpa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical heads of blue or purple flowers, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Cyclocarpa species are often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, and they are native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

The plant's unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
