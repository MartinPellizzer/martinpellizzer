Carum jahandiezii

Carum jahandiezii, commonly known as Persian cumin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers temperate climates with adequate sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitats.
