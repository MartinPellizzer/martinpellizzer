Campanumoea lancifolia

Campanumoea lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, which gives it its specific epithet "lancifolia.".
