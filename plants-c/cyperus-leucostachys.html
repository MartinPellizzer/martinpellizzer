Cyperus leucostachys

Cyperus leucostachys, commonly known as the white-tailed flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It typically grows in wet or marshy areas, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various waterlogged environments.
