Cardamine polyphylla

Cardamine polyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Cardamine polyphylla thrives in cool, humid conditions and is often found near streams, wet meadows, and forested areas.
