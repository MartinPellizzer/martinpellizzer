Cuphea campestris

Cuphea campestris, commonly known as the scarlet cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Cuphea campestris is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to add color to gardens throughout the growing season.
