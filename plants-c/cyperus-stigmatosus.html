Cyperus stigmatosus

Cyperus stigmatosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its reproductive structures, including spikelets and bracts, are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus stigmatosus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
