Cistus libanotis

Cistus libanotis, commonly known as the Lebanese rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
