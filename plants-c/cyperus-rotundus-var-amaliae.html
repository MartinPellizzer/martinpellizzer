Cyperus rotundus var. amaliae

Cyperus rotundus var. amaliae, commonly known as the spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and slender, cylindrical stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically emerging from the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
