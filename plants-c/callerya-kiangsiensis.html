Callerya kiangsiensis

Callerya kiangsiensis, commonly known as the Chinese callerya, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its attractive, pendant-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Callerya kiangsiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
