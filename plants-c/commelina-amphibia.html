Commelina amphibia

Commelina amphibia, commonly known as the aquatic spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers with three petals and a prominent yellow spot on the lower petal. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Commelina amphibia is known for its ability to thrive in both aquatic and terrestrial environments, making it a versatile and resilient species.
