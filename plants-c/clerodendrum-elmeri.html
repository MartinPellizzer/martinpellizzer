Clerodendrum elmeri

Clerodendrum elmeri, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often brightly colored in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clerodendrum elmeri is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
