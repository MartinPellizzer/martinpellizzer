Casearia emarginata

Casearia emarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
