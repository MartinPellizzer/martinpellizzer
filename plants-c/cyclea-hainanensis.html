Cyclea hainanensis

Cyclea hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan cycad, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Hainan Island in China.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with the female cones bearing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Cyclea hainanensis is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for conservation efforts.
