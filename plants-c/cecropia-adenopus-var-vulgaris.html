Cecropia adenopus var. vulgaris

Cecropia adenopus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and a massive, umbrella-shaped crown. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptic leaves that are smooth and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.
