Carex longiculmis

Carex longiculmis, commonly known as the long-beaked sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

Carex longiculmis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
