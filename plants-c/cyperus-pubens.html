Cyperus pubens

Cyperus pubens, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes, ponds, and along stream edges. Its cylindrical stems are usually hairless or slightly hairy, and it produces small, clustered flowers in spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus pubens plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
