Croton apostolon

Croton apostolon, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like orange to red inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often thick and woody. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The flowers are not showy but are followed by small, round fruits that contain seeds.
