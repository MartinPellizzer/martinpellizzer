Calamagrostis yukonensis

Calamagrostis yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers in late summer, which are typically greenish or purplish in color. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Yukon wild rye is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
