Cytisus hirsutus subsp. pumilus

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
