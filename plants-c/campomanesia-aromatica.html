Campomanesia aromatica

Campomanesia aromatica, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered together, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical uses in traditional medicine.
