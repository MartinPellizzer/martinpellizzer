Crataegus eatonia

Crataegus eatonia, commonly known as the English hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are edible but often tart.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
