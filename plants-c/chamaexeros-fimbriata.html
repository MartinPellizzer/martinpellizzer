Chamaexeros fimbriata

Chamaexeros fimbriata, commonly known as the fringe-leaved chamaexeros, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive fringed or serrated edge, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
