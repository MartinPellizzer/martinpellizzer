Cassia oligoclada var. goniodes

Cassia oligoclada var. goniodes, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban environments.
