Catasetum saccatum var. typum

Catasetum saccatum var. typum, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized column that facilitates pollination by specific wasps. The plant produces pendulous, showy flowers with a distinctive sac-like structure at the base of the ovary. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
