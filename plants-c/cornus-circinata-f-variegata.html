Cornus circinata f. variegata

Cornus circinata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a striking ornamental plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a compact shrub characterized by its unique foliage, featuring white and green variegation on its leaves. The plant produces showy white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
