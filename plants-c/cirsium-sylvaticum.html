Cirsium sylvaticum

Cirsium sylvaticum, commonly known as the wood thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland areas and moist meadows, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features large, spiny leaves with a rough texture and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
