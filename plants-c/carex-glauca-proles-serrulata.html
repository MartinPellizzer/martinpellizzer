Carex glauca proles serrulata

Carex glauca proles serrulata, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, pointed leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
