Camara aculeata f. nivea

Camara aculeata f. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered belladonna.

This variant of the common nightshade is characterized by its pale white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with the typical dark purple blooms of the species. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing tropane alkaloids.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and toxicology studies.
