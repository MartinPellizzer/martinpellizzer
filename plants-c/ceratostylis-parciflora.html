Ceratostylis parciflora

Ceratostylis parciflora, commonly known as the slender-leafed torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as under forest canopies. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers, each with a distinctive, elongated labellum.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
