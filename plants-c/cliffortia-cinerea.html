Cliffortia cinerea

Cliffortia cinerea, commonly known as the silverweed or white flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to rocky habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on cliffs, slopes, and rocky outcrops.

Cliffortia cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
