Crocus biflorus f. alexandri

Crocus biflorus f. alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian crocus, is a botanical variant of the genus Crocus.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-flowered inflorescence, which sets it apart from other crocus species. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a prominent, often yellow or orange center. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
