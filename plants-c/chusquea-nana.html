Chusquea nana

Chusquea nana, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, stout stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
