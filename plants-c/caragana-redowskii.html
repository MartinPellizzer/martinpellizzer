Caragana redowskii

Caragana redowskii, commonly known as the redowskii shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Caragana redowskii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.
