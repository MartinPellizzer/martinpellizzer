Cyclopia intermedia

Cyclopia intermedia, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cyclopia intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
