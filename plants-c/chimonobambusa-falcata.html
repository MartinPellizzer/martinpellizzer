Chimonobambusa falcata

Chimonobambusa falcata, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a species of flowering bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its culms are hollow and have a smooth, pale green surface.

Chimonobambusa falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
