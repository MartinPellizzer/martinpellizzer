Coleus cataractarum

Coleus cataractarum, commonly known as the waterfall coleus, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
