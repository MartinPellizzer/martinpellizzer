Cyperus confusus var. laxissimus

Cyperus confusus var. laxissimus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often in marshy areas or along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-sided flower clusters characteristic of the sedge family.
