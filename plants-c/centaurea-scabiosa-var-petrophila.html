Centaurea scabiosa var. petrophila

Centaurea scabiosa var. petrophila, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, cone-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, and its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate high altitudes and harsh weather conditions.

Centaurea scabiosa var. petrophila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
