Campanula piperi

Campanula piperi, commonly known as the pepper grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Campanula piperi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
