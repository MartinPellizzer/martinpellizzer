Candollea helianthemoides

Candollea helianthemoides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and grey-green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Candollea helianthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
