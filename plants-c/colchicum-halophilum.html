Colchicum halophilum

Colchicum halophilum, commonly known as the salt-tolerant autumn crocus, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in the autumn. The plant has adapted to thrive in soils with high salt content, making it a notable example of halophytic flora. Its leaves are typically basal and appear after the flowers, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties, though its toxicity requires careful handling.
