Crumenaria polygaloides var. discolor

Crumenaria polygaloides var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated crumenaria, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and yellow hues that create a visually appealing contrast. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
