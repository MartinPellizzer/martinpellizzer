Cardamine variabilis var. pinnatisecta

Cardamine variabilis var. pinnatisecta, commonly known as the variable watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance that aids in identification.

This variety is known for its variability in leaf shape and size, which contributes to its common name.
