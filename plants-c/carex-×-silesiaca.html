It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. The botanical features of Carex × silesiaca make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and botanical study.
