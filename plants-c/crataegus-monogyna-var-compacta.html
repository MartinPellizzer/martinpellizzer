Crataegus monogyna var. compacta

Crataegus monogyna var. compacta, commonly known as the common hawthorn or compact hawthorn, is a compact shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and grayish underneath. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a hedge plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil types.

This variety is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
