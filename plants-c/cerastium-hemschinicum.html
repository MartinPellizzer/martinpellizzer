Cerastium hemschinicum

Cerastium hemschinicum, commonly known as the Hemsch's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cerastium hemschinicum is often found in rocky soils and open meadows at high elevations.
