Carex ogawae

Carex ogawae, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Carex ogawae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
