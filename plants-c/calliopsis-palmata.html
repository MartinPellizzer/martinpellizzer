Calliopsis palmata

Calliopsis palmata, commonly known as the Florida bottle palm, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the coastal regions of Florida and the Bahamas.

It is a small, solitary palm with a thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Calliopsis palmata is considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
