Calamus brevispadix

Calamus brevispadix, commonly known as the short-clubbed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus brevispadix is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
