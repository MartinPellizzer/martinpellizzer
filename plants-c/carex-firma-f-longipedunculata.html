Carex firma f. longipedunculata

Carex firma f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of elongated, slender spikes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
