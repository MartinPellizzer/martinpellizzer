Chomelia atlantica

Chomelia atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic chomelia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by ornamental fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is also valued for its role in supporting local biodiversity.
