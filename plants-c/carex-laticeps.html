Carex laticeps

Carex laticeps, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex laticeps plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
