Cymbopogon rufus var. fulvicomus

Cymbopogon rufus var. fulvicomus, commonly known as the golden grass or yellow grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and narrow, bright yellow-green leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like, with small, pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
