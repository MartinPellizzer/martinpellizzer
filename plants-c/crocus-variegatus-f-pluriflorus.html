Crocus variegatus f. pluriflorus

Crocus variegatus f. pluriflorus, commonly known as the variegated crocus, is a cultivar of the crocus genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add early color to the garden.
