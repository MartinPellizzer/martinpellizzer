Croton gnidiaceus

Croton gnidiaceus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its showy, orange-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Croton gnidiaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
