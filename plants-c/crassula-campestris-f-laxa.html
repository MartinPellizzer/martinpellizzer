Crassula campestris f. laxa

Crassula campestris f. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the growing season. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
