Cuphea ramulosa var. virgata

Cuphea ramulosa var. virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and spikes of showy, tubular red flowers. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, making it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly habitats.
