Cerasus fruticosa f. varigata

Cerasus fruticosa f. varigata, commonly known as the Variegated Cherry Bush, is a unique ornamental plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry bush, known for its compact growth habit. The leaves exhibit a mix of green, white, and cream colors, creating a visually appealing contrast. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, adding to its ornamental value.

This plant is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively low maintenance.
