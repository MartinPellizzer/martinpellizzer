Calamintha langei

Calamintha langei, commonly known as Lange's calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features small, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.

Calamintha langei thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
