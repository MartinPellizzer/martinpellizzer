Camellia japonica var. picturata

Camellia japonica var. picturata, commonly known as the Painted Camellia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is prized for its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The variety is particularly noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in both residential and public gardens.
