Calendula pachysperma

Calendula pachysperma, commonly known as the African calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for gardens.
