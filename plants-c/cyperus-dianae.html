Cyperus dianae

Cyperus dianae, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Cyperus dianae thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife.
