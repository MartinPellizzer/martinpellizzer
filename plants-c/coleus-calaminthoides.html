Coleus calaminthoides

Coleus calaminthoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking color and texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
