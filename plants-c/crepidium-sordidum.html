Crepidium sordidum

Crepidium sordidum, commonly known as the dirty orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pale green to brownish pseudobulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces elongated spikes bearing numerous small, inconspicuous flowers with yellowish-green sepals and petals. It is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Crepidium sordidum is known for its low-growing habit and is often overlooked due to its inconspicuous appearance.
