Coccinia buikoensis

Coccinia buikoensis, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

The plant is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its potential medicinal properties.
