Cissus quadrangularis var. aculeatangula

Cissus quadrangularis var. aculeatangula, commonly known as the spiny devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, four-sided stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in tiny spines. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain three seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
