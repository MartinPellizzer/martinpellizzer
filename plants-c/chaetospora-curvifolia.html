Chaetospora curvifolia

Chaetospora curvifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaved moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth and distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its small, greenish-yellow sporophytes develop on the leaf surfaces, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Chaetospora curvifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
