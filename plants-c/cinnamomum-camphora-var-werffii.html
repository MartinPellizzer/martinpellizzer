Cinnamomum camphora var. werffii

Cinnamomum camphora var. werffii, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a variant of the camphor laurel native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, thick, and glossy leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and durability, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.
