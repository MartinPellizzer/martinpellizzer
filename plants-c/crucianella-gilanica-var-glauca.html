Crucianella gilanica var. glauca

Crucianella gilanica var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous bladderwort, is a rare aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap air and aid in buoyancy. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are slender and often bear small, translucent bladders that help it float.

The species is noted for its unique adaptations to aquatic life and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
