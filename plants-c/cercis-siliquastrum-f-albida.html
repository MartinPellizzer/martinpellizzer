Cercis siliquastrum f. albida

Cercis siliquastrum f. albida, commonly known as the white Persian lilac, is a hybrid form of the Persian lilac, characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region, featuring heart-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes. Its ornamental value lies in its showy spring blooms and attractive foliage.

It is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
