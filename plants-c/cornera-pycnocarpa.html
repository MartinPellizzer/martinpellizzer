Cornera pycnocarpa

Cornera pycnocarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited cornera, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, woody stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
