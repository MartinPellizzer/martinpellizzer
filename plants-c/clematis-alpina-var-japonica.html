Clematis alpina var. japonica

Clematis alpina var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese mountain clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and rocky landscapes.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
