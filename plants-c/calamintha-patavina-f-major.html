Calamintha patavina f. major

Calamintha patavina f. major, commonly known as the Greater Calamintha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong mint-like scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Calamintha patavina f. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
