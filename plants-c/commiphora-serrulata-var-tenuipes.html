Commiphora serrulata var. tenuipes

Commiphora serrulata var. tenuipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrrh.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

The species is valued for its unique fragrance and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries.
