Crotalaria pallidicaulis

Crotalaria pallidicaulis, commonly known as the pale-stemmed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm climates, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential agricultural resource.
