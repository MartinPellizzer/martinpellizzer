Celtis formosana

Celtis formosana, commonly known as the Formosan hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Celtis formosana is often found in moist forests and can tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
