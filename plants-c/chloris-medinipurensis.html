Chloris medinipurensis

Chloris medinipurensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green or pale blue in color.

Chloris medinipurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
