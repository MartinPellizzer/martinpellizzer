Crotalaria bequaertii

Crotalaria bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
