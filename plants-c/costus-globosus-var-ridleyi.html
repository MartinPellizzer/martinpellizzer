Costus globosus var. ridleyi

Costus globosus var. ridleyi, commonly known as the red ginger or red costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of ginger. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
