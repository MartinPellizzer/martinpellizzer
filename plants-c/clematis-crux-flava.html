Clematis crux-flava

Clematis crux-flava, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, often with a distinctive crux-shaped pattern. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering climbers.
