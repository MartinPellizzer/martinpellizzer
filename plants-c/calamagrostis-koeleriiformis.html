Calamagrostis koeleriiformis

Calamagrostis koeleriiformis, commonly known as Koeler's reed, is a grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-blue in color. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that are visually striking and attract various pollinators.

Calamagrostis koeleriiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
