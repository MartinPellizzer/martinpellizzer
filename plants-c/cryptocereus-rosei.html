Cryptocereus rosei

Cryptocereus rosei, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only a few hours. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique flowering habit, it is a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
