Coleocephalocereus buxbaumianus

Coleocephalocereus buxbaumianus, commonly known as the Buxbaum's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is typically green and covered in sparse, white spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
