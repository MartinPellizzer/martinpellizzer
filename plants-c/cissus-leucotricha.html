Cissus leucotricha

Cissus leucotricha, commonly known as the silver-leafed cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb and spread over surfaces.

Cissus leucotricha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
