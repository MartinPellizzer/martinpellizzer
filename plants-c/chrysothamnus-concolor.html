Chrysothamnus concolor

Chrysothamnus concolor, commonly known as the green coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, silvery-gray hairs. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Chrysothamnus concolor is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
