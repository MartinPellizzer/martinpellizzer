Cedrela toona var. vestita

Cedrela toona var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet tamanu, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
