Carex phalaroides subsp. crassiflora

Carex phalaroides subsp.

crassiflora, commonly known as broad-flowered sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges.

The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
