Carex subandrogyna

Carex subandrogyna, commonly known as the slender bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in bogs or marshes, and forms dense clumps. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex subandrogyna plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
