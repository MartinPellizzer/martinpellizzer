Cirsium spectabile var. araneosum

Cirsium spectabile var. araneosum, commonly known as the spider-thorned thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, showy purple to pinkish-blue flower heads. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, woolly texture on the underside. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
