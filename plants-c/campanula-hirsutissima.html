Campanula hirsutissima

Campanula hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.

Campanula hirsutissima is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
