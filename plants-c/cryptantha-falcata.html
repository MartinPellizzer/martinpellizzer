Cryptantha falcata

Cryptantha falcata, commonly known as the sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the fruit is a pod that curves downward, giving it its common name.

Cryptantha falcata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
