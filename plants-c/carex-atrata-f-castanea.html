Carex atrata f. castanea

Carex atrata f. castanea, commonly known as the chestnut sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
