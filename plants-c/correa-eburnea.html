Correa eburnea

Correa eburnea, commonly known as the white correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Correa eburnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
