Clematis villosa subsp. oligophylla

Clematis villosa subsp.

oligophylla, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States. It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
