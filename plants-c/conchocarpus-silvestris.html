Conchocarpus silvestris

Conchocarpus silvestris, commonly known as the forest goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and oblong, succulent leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated, spiny fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Conchocarpus silvestris make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
