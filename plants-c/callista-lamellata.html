Callista lamellata

Callista lamellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Callista lamellata is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
