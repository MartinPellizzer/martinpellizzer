Carex digitata f. speirostachys

Carex digitata f. speirostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant that typically thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This sedge is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in diverse wetland environments.
