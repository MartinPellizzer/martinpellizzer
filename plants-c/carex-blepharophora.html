Carex blepharophora

Carex blepharophora, commonly known as the hooded sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and streambanks.

Carex blepharophora is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
