Cannabis sativa f. afghanica

Cannabis sativa f. afghanica, commonly known as Afghan cannabis, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stature and can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces dense, resinous buds that are rich in cannabinoids such as THC. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a lighter green color compared to other cannabis varieties.

This subspecies is valued for its high potency and historical significance in traditional medicine and cultural practices.
