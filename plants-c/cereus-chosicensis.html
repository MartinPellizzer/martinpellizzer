Cereus chosicensis

Cereus chosicensis, commonly known as the Chosica cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. The plant produces large, showy night-blooming flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
