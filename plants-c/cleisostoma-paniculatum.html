Cleisostoma paniculatum

Cleisostoma paniculatum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with white petals and a yellow lip. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, and its flowers emit a sweet, spicy aroma that attracts pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cleisostoma paniculatum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
