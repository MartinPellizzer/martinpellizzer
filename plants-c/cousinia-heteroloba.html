Cousinia heteroloba

Cousinia heteroloba, commonly known as the woolly mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Cousinia heteroloba is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
