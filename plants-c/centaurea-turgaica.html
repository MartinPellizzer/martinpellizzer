Centaurea turgaica

Centaurea turgaica, commonly known as the Turgut's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its vibrant blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a silvery underside.

Centaurea turgaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
