Crinodendron eriocladum

Crinodendron eriocladum, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, velvety-textured leaflets, contributing to its unique visual appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens.

Due to its attractive foliage and blooms, it is often grown as an ornamental plant in subtropical regions.
