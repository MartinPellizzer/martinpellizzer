Cerasus dulcis proles duracina

Cerasus dulcis proles duracina, commonly known as the hardy cherry, is a species of Prunus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
