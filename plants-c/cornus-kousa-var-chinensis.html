Cornus kousa var. chinensis

Cornus kousa var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white bracts that surround clusters of small, yellowish flowers, which appear in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds. Its foliage is typically dark green with a smooth texture, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
