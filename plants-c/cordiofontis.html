Cordiofontis

Cordiofontis, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the warm seasons. Cordiofontis thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts and gardeners.
