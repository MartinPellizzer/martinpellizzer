Cassinia wilsoniae

Cassinia wilsoniae, commonly known as Wilson's cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in spherical clusters, and the leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Cassinia wilsoniae is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
