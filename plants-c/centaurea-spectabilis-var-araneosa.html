Centaurea spectabilis var. araneosa

Centaurea spectabilis var. araneosa, commonly known as the spider-throated centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, violet-blue flower heads that resemble a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
