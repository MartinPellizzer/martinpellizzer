Chapmannia reghidensis

Chapmannia reghidensis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse seeds via the wind. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and can survive with minimal water.

Chapmannia reghidensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing food for local wildlife.
