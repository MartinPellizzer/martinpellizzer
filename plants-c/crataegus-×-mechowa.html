Crataegus × mechowa

Crataegus × mechowa, commonly known as the Mechova hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges across Europe.

The Mechova hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
