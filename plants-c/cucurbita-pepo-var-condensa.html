Cucurbita pepo var. condensa

Cucurbita pepo var. condensa, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, bottle-shaped fruits with a rough, netted skin. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. Its fruits are typically harvested in the fall and are valued for their nutty flavor and nutritional content.

This variety is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
