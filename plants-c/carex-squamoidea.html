Carex squamoidea

Carex squamoidea, commonly known as the silver-scaled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a silvery-gray coating, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex squamoidea produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of its stems.
