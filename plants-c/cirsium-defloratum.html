Cirsium defloratum

Cirsium defloratum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's distinctive conical flower head is surrounded by bracts that give it its common name.
