Cynanchum oxyphyllum

Cynanchum oxyphyllum, commonly known as the arrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas.

Cynanchum oxyphyllum contains milky sap, which can be irritant to the skin.
