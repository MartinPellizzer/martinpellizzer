Cissus chontalensis

Cissus chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontalensis devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist undergrowth.

Cissus chontalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
