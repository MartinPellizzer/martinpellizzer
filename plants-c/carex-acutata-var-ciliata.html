Carex acutata var. ciliata

Carex acutata var. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliated carex, is a sedge species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

Carex acutata var. ciliata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
