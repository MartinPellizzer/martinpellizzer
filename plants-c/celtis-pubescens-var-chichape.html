Celtis pubescens var. chichape

Celtis pubescens var. chichape, commonly known as the Chichape hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, pubescent texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

The bark is often cracked and flaky, providing a distinctive appearance in mature trees.
