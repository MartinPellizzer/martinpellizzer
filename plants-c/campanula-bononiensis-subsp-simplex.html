Campanula bononiensis subsp. simplex

Campanula bononiensis subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the simple bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces upright spikes of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
