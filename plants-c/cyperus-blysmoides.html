Cyperus blysmoides

Cyperus blysmoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland areas in Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense stands near the water's edge. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes that are usually inconspicuous.

Cyperus blysmoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
