Crataegus pallidula

Crataegus pallidula, commonly known as the pale thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

Crataegus pallidula is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
