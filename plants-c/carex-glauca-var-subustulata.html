Carex glauca var. subustulata

Carex glauca var. subustulata, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
