Coelogyne ewae

Coelogyne ewae, commonly known as the Ewa's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters of 2 to 5 on a hairy, erect stem. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Coelogyne ewae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
