Colutea fistulosa

Colutea fistulosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with three oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive pod-like structures containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Colutea fistulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
