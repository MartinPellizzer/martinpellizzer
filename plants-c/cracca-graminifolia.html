Cracca graminifolia

Cracca graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy cracca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of the Mediterranean. It produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed fruits.

Cracca graminifolia is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
