Chaetaria

Chaetaria, commonly known as the spiky or bristly grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, often hairy stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture due to the presence of short, stiff hairs. Chaetaria species are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
