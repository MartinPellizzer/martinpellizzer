Cordyline rubella

Cordyline rubella, commonly known as the red cordyline, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-purple in color, especially when young. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor firmly in well-drained soil. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Cordyline rubella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various garden settings.
