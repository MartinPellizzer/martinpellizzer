Camellia maiana

Camellia maiana, commonly known as the Philippine camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the winter months. Camellia maiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
