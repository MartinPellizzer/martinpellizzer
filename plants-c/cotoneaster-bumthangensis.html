Cotoneaster bumthangensis

Cotoneaster bumthangensis, commonly known as the Bhutanese cotoneaster, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and Nepal.

It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant features dense, spreading foliage with small, oval leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are pinkish-white, appearing in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

Cotoneaster bumthangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
