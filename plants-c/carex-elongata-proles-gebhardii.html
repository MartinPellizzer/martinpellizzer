Carex elongata proles gebhardii

Carex elongata proles gebhardii, commonly known as the Gebhard's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

This sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
