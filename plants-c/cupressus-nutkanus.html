Cupressus nutkanus

Cupressus nutkanus, commonly known as the Nootka cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scaly, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin strips. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, giving it a dense, compact appearance.

The Nootka cypress is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
