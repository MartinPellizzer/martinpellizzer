Ceropegia whitesloaneana

Ceropegia whitesloaneana, commonly known as the White Sloane's African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and tubular, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
