Carex × anselmi

Carex × anselmi, commonly known as the Anselm's sedge, is a hybrid plant within the Carex genus, characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense growth habit.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and can vary in texture depending on the specific variety. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually brown or reddish in color, which appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

Carex × anselmi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
