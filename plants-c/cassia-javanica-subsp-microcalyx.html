Cassia javanica subsp. microcalyx

Cassia javanica subsp.

microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
