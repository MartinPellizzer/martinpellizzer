Cardamine skorpilii

Cardamine skorpilii, commonly known as the Skorpil's watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery aroma.

It produces small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
