Cucumis melo var. aegyptiacus

Cucumis melo var. aegyptiacus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variety of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and typically green fruit with a mild, crisp flavor. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a sprawling growth habit, often requiring support for its vines. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.

The leaves are broad and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
