Chlorophytum sylvestre

Chlorophytum sylvestre, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a yellowish edge. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by clusters of bright red berries. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Chlorophytum sylvestre is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
