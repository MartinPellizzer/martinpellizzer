Carex lupulina var. bella-villa

Carex lupulina var. bella-villa, commonly known as the Bella-Villa sedge, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spike-like cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
