Clematopsis simplicifolia

Clematopsis simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved clematopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematopsis simplicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
