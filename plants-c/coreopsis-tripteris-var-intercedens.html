Coreopsis tripteris var. intercedens

Coreopsis tripteris var. intercedens, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight zigzag pattern along their edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is known for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
