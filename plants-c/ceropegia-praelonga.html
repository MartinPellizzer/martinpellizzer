Ceropegia praelonga

Ceropegia praelonga, commonly known as the long-stemmed arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, tuberous roots. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia praelonga is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
