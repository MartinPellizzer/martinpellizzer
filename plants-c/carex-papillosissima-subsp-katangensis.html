Carex papillosissima subsp. katangensis

Carex papillosissima subsp.

katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower spikes. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
