Caragana korshinskii var. intermedia

Caragana korshinskii var. intermedia, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, legume-like pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for reclamation of degraded lands.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in sandy and saline soils.
