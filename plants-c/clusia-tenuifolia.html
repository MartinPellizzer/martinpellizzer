Clusia tenuifolia

Clusia tenuifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and often have a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia tenuifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and air pollution.
