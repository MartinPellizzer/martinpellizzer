Canavalia peruviana

Canavalia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bean or wild peanut, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Canavalia peruviana also has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
