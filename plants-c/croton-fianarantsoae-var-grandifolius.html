Croton fianarantsoae var. grandifolius

Croton fianarantsoae var. grandifolius, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
