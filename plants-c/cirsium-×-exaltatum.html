Cirsium × exaltatum

Cirsium × exaltatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and showy, purple-pink flower heads that resemble cones. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Cirsium × exaltatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
