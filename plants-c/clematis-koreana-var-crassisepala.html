Clematis koreana var. crassisepala

Clematis koreana var. crassisepala, commonly known as the Korean Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Korea.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
