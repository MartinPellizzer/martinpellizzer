Calceolaria arborescens

Calceolaria arborescens, commonly known as the tree calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and dense clusters of showy, pouch-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Calceolaria arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
