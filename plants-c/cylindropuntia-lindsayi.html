Cylindropuntia lindsayi

Cylindropuntia lindsayi, commonly known as Lindsay's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems, which are covered in spines and are modified for water storage. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits, known as tunas, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Cylindropuntia lindsayi is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in desert ecosystems.
