Carex blepharolepis

Carex blepharolepis, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly curled at the edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex blepharolepis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
