Commelina huntii

Commelina huntii, commonly known as the pink commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features striking violet to pink flowers with three petals and three sepals, often surrounded by bright green bracts. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Commelina huntii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
