Centaurea jungens

Centaurea jungens, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold, arid conditions.

Centaurea jungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, long-lasting blooms.
