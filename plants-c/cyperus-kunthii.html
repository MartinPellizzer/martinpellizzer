Cyperus kunthii

Cyperus kunthii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgewort, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedge plants, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Cyperus kunthii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
