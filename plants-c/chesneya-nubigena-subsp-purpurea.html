Chesneya nubigena subsp. purpurea

Chesneya nubigena subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple chesneya, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in compact clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
