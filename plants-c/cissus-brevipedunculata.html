Cissus brevipedunculata

Cissus brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in peduncles, giving it its species name. Its stems are succulent and can be used for propagation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.
