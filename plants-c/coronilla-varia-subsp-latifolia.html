Coronilla varia subsp. latifolia

Coronilla varia subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a deep root system, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for erosion control. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
