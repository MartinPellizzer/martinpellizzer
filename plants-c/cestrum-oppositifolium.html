Cestrum oppositifolium

Cestrum oppositifolium, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.

Cestrum oppositifolium is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
