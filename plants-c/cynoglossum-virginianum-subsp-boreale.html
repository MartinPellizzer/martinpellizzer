Cynoglossum virginianum subsp. boreale

Cynoglossum virginianum subsp.

boreale, commonly known as northern blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and Canada. It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a robust root system that helps it thrive in wet conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
