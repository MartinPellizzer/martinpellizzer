Caesalpinia atropunctata

Caesalpinia atropunctata, commonly known as the black thorn or black acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, black-punctuated leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often resembling those of the genus Acacia. Its fruit is a long, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Caesalpinia atropunctata is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
