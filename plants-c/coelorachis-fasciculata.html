Coelorachis fasciculata

Coelorachis fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered spikegrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fasciculate (bundled) growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Coelorachis fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
