Colea mauritiana

Colea mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius cobra lily, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often dark green in color. It produces striking, colorful flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a contrasting center.

Colea mauritiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also appreciated for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
