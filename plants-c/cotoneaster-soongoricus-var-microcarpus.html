Cotoneaster soongoricus var. microcarpus

Cotoneaster soongoricus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
