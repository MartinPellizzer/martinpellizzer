Cyperus lipopygmaeus

Cyperus lipopygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Cyperus lipopygmaeus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact size and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
