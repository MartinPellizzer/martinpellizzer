Corymbia watsoniana subsp.

watsoniana, commonly known as the Watson's red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to northern Western Australia and is characterized by its distinctive red to orange-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is a medium-sized tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, open crown.
