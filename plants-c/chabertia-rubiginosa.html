Chabertia rubiginosa

Chabertia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in association with other grasses and sedges.

Chabertia rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
