Centaurea montana subsp. lugdunensis

Centaurea montana subsp.

lugdunensis, commonly known as the French centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees and surrounding regions. It features upright, branching stems covered with glandular hairs, and its leaves are lanceolate with serrated edges.

The plant produces bright blue to purple flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
