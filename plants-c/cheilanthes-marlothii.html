Cheilanthes marlothii

Cheilanthes marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's rockcap, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and is typically found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry, sunny environments. The plant has thick, leathery fronds that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
