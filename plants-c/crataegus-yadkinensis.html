Crataegus yadkinensis

Crataegus yadkinensis, commonly known as Yadkin Crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, sour fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Yadkin Crabapple is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to support local wildlife.
