Cleyera japonica var. kaempferiana

Cleyera japonica var. kaempferiana, commonly known as Japanese holly or Japanese waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its year-round foliage.

The species is relatively low maintenance and thrives in partial shade to full sun conditions.
