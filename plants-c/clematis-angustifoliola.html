Clematis angustifoliola

Clematis angustifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial to local pollinators.
