Callistachys aciculifera

Callistachys aciculifera, commonly known as the acicular hollyleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to native plant landscapes.
