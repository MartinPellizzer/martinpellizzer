Convolvulus valentinus var. melillensis

Convolvulus valentinus var. melillensis, commonly known as the Melilla bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are heart-shaped and hairy, while the stems are slender and often covered in fine pubescence. This species thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
