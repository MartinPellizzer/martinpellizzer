Ceropegia munbyana subsp. hispanica

Ceropegia munbyana subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive corona structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral features.
