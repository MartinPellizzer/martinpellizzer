Cryptocarya pustulata

Cryptocarya pustulata, commonly known as the rough-leaved cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several elliptical seeds.

Cryptocarya pustulata is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
