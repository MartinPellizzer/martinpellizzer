Calochortus splendens var. atroviolaceus

Calochortus splendens var. atroviolaceus, commonly known as the dark violet glacier lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the Sierra Nevada mountains of California.

This perennial herb features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep violet to nearly black, with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

Calochortus splendens var. atroviolaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its high-altitude environment.
