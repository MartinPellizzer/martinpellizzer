Clinopodium flabellifolium

Clinopodium flabellifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves that are often deeply lobed and resemble a fan shape, giving it its species name *flabellifolium*, meaning "fan-leaved.". The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Clinopodium flabellifolium is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
