Cytisus ratisbonensis

Cytisus ratisbonensis, commonly known as the Ratisbon bush or golden broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Cytisus ratisbonensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in soil enrichment.
