Centaurea alba var. maluqueri

Centaurea alba var. maluqueri, commonly known as the Maluqueri Blushweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense colonies in disturbed soils.

It is often found in open woodlands, fields, and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
