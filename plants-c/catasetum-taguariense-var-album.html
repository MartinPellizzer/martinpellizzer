Catasetum taguariense var. album

Catasetum taguariense var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

The variety 'album' is distinguished by its pure white coloration, setting it apart from other varieties of Catasetum.
