Cassia cotinifolia

Cassia cotinifolia, commonly known as flame tree or bracken fern tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that turn red or orange in autumn. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and often exudes a fragrant, resinous sap.

Cassia cotinifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
