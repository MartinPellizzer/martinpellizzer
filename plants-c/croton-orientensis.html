Croton orientensis

Croton orientensis, commonly known as the Persian croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton orientensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
