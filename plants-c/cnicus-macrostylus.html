Cnicus macrostylus

Cnicus macrostylus, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cnicus macrostylus is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
