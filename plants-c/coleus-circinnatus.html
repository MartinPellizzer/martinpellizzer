Coleus circinnatus

Coleus circinnatus, commonly known as the fire plant or Hawaiian flame plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Coleus circinnatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf colors and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
