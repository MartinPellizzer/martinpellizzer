Centaurea seridis var. epapposa

Centaurea seridis var. epapposa, commonly known as the Egyptian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like structures at the base of its flower heads. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
