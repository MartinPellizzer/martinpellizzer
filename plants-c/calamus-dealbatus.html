Calamus dealbatus

Calamus dealbatus, commonly known as the white-stemmed arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, pale-green stems that are often mistaken for bamboo due to their similar appearance. Its rhizomes are used to produce a starchy root that serves as a food source.

Calamus dealbatus thrives in wet, swampy areas and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
