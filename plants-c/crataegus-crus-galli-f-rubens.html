Crataegus crus-galli f. rubens

Crataegus crus-galli f. rubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or reddish-brown stems, which are often more prominent than in the typical form. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
