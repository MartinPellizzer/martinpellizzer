Cistus × matritensis

Cistus × matritensis, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which is part of the family Ericaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is well adapted to Mediterranean climates, thriving in poor, well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Cistus × matritensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
