Carex tremula

Carex tremula, commonly known as slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex tremula plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
