Colocasia neoguineensis

Colocasia neoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are typically green with a yellow or white spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
