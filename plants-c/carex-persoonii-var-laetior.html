Carex persoonii var. laetior

Carex persoonii var. laetior, commonly known as the sweet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
