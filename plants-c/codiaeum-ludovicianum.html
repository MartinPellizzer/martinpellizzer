Codiaeum ludovicianum

Codiaeum ludovicianum, commonly known as the flameleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, heart-shaped leaves that range from red to orange and yellow, often with a metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
