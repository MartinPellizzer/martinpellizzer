Calophyllum macrophyllum

Calophyllum macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, which are followed by woody, orange-colored fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
