Capparis coccolobifolia f. parvifolia

Capparis coccolobifolia f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved coral tree, is a botanical variant of the coral tree species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its foliage is often dense and provides good shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
