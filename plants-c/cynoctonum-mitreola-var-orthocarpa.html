Cynoctonum mitreola var. orthocarpa

Cynoctonum mitreola var. orthocarpa, commonly known as the Mitreola orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
