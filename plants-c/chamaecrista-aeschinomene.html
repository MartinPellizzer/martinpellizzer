Chamaecrista aeschinomene

Chamaecrista aeschinomene, commonly known as the redroot cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
