Cassia scleroxylon

Cassia scleroxylon, commonly known as African sumac, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cassia scleroxylon is also valued for its hard wood and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
