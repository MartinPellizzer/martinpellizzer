Cardamine georgiana

Cardamine georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia watercress, is a small perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a terminal cluster, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
