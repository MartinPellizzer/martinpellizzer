Capparis sicula subsp. mesopotamica

Capparis sicula subsp.

mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian caper, is a shrub native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia. It features thorny stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its fruits are capsule-like and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to drought and its historical use in traditional medicine.
