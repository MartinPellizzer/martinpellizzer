Crataegus oxyacantha var. erioclada

Crataegus oxyacantha var. erioclada, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called haws, which are edible but often used in jams and wines.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
