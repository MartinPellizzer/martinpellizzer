Celtis lanceolata

Celtis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf boxelder, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family, which includes elms and ash trees. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are dioecious, with separate male and female flowers on different trees.

The fruit is a small, three-winged samara that aids in wind dispersal.
