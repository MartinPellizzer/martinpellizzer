Ceratochloa valdiviana

Ceratochloa valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ecological importance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
