Croton gaudichaudii

Croton gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Guadaloupe croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful leaves that display a variety of hues, including red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
