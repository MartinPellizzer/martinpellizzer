Centaurium perfoliatum

Centaurium perfoliatum, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and often have a distinctive perfoliate arrangement, where the base of the leaf appears to pass through the stem.

Centaurium perfoliatum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
