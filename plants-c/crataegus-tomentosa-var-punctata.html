Crataegus tomentosa var. punctata

Crataegus tomentosa var. punctata, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, velvety bark and small, rounded leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
