Chamaeriphes shatan

Chamaeriphes shatan, commonly known as the devil's thorn, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the dry season. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to habitat loss and climate change.

Botanists study it for its unique adaptations and potential ecological significance in its ecosystem.
