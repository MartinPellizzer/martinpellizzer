Calamagrostis arundinacea var. grandiflora

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. grandiflora, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, ornamental grass native to Europe and Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, with narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces showy, feathery panicles of pinkish-white flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
