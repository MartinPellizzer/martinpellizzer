Camellia amplexicaulis

Camellia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is an evergreen shrub that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

Camellia amplexicaulis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
