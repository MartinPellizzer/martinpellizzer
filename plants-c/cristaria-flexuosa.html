Cristaria flexuosa

Cristaria flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cristaria flexuosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
