Coffea canephora

Coffea canephora, commonly known as Robusta, is a species of coffee plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped leaves and produces red, fleshy berries containing two seeds each. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It is known for its higher caffeine content compared to other coffee species.

Robusta is primarily cultivated for its use in instant coffee and espresso blends.
