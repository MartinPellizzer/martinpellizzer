Copaifera paupera

Copaifera paupera, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood and heartwood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Copaifera paupera is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
