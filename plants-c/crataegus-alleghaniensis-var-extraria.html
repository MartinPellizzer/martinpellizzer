Crataegus alleghaniensis var. extraria

Crataegus alleghaniensis var. extraria, commonly known as the Allegheny crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
