Crataegus brunetiana var. fernaldii

Crataegus brunetiana var. fernaldii, commonly known as Fernald's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy upper surface.

This variety is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
