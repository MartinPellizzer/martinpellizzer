Carex glauca var. sylvatica

Carex glauca var. sylvatica, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and produces spike-like inflorescences that range from green to brown. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and naturalistic plantings.
