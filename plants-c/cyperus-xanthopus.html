Cyperus xanthopus

Cyperus xanthopus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along riverbanks.

Cyperus xanthopus is an important species in wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
