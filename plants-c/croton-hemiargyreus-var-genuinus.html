Croton hemiargyreus var. genuinus

Croton hemiargyreus var. genuinus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
