Centaurea calolepis

Centaurea calolepis, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea calolepis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
