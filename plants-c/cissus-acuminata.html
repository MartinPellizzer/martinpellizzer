Cissus acuminata

Cissus acuminata, commonly known as the rock fig or stone plant, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. Its adaptability to rocky and arid environments makes it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.

Cissus acuminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
