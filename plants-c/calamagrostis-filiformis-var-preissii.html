Calamagrostis filiformis var. preissii

Calamagrostis filiformis var. preissii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
