Couratari macrosperma

Couratari macrosperma, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or Brazilian arrowroot, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. Its roots are highly valued for their starchy, edible qualities, making it an important plant in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The species is also noted for its ecological significance in supporting diverse local wildlife.
