Crassula vaillantii

Crassula vaillantii, commonly known as the "Bottle Tree" or "Coral Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and become more pronounced during periods of drought.

Crassula vaillantii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
