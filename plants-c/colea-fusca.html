Colea fusca

Colea fusca, commonly known as the dark coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Colea fusca is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
