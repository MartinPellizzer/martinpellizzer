Cousinia handelii

Cousinia handelii, commonly known as the Kashmir snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Cousinia handelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
