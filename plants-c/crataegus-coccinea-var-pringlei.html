Crataegus coccinea var. pringlei

Crataegus coccinea var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorned branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local wildlife and a subject of ecological study.
