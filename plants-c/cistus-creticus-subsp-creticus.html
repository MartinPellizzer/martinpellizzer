Cistus creticus subsp. creticus

Cistus creticus subsp.

creticus, commonly known as the Cretan rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Crete. It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

It plays a significant role in the local flora and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
