Centaurea Margaritacea Subsp. Paczoskyi

Centaurea margaritacea subsp. paczoskyi is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is known for its delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. This subspecies is closely related to the common centaury but exhibits unique morphological features. Its habitat is often limited to rocky slopes and dry meadows, making it a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this beautiful and ecologically important plant.
