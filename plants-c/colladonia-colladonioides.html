Colladonia colladonioides

Colladonia colladonioides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, hairy, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
