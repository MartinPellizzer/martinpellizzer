Cyathea oldhamii

Cyathea oldhamii, commonly known as the Oldham's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while the fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea oldhamii plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
