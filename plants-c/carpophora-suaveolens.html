Carpophora suaveolens

Carpophora suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its pleasant aroma.
