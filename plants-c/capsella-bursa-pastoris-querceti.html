Capsella bursa-pastoris querceti

Capsella bursa-pastoris querceti, commonly known as the common chickweed with oak affinity, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. The species is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
