Centaurea kochii

Centaurea kochii, commonly known as Koch's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

Centaurea kochii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
