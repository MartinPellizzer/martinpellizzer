Chimarrhis cymosa

Chimarrhis cymosa, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found along coastal areas.

Chimarrhis cymosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
