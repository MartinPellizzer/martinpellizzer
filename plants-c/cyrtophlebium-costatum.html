Cyrtophlebium costatum

Cyrtophlebium costatum, commonly known as the mountain holly.

It is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red or orange berries.

Cyrtophlebium costatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
