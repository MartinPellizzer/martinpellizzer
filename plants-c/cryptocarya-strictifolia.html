Cryptocarya strictifolia

Cryptocarya strictifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cryptocarya, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with narrow, leathery leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
