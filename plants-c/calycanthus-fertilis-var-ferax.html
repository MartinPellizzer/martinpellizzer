Calycanthus fertilis var. ferax

Calycanthus fertilis var. ferax, commonly known as the Florida sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that provide attractive foliage throughout the growing season. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small gardens or as a specimen plant.

The botanical characteristics of this variety include its distinctive floral scent and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
