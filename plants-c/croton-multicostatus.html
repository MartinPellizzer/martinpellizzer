Croton multicostatus

Croton multicostatus, commonly known as the multibract croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
