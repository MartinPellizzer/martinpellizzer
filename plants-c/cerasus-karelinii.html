Cerasus karelinii

Cerasus karelinii, commonly known as the Korean cherry or Karelin's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Cerasus karelinii is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for various pollinators and wildlife.
