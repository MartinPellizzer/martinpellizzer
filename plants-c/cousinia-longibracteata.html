Cousinia longibracteata

Cousinia longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers, which appear in the summer, are usually yellow or pink and are arranged in clusters.

Cousinia longibracteata is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
