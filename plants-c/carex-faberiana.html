Carex faberiana

Carex faberiana, commonly known as the Faber's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often dark green in color.

Carex faberiana produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
