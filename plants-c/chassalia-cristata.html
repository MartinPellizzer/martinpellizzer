Chassalia cristata

Chassalia cristata, commonly known as the crown flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped inflorescence that resembles a crown, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually blue or purple in color.

Chassalia cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
