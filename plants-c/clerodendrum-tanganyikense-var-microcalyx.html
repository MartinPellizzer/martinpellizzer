Clerodendrum tanganyikense var. microcalyx

Clerodendrum tanganyikense var. microcalyx, commonly known as the small-leaved glorybower, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically small and covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in the summer months. Its inflorescences are often surrounded by bracts that are brightly colored, enhancing its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
