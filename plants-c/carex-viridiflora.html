Carex viridiflora

Carex viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on tall, slender inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
