Centaurea leucophaea subsp. pseudocoerulescens

Centaurea leucophaea subsp.

pseudocoerulescens, commonly known as the pale blue knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in composite heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
