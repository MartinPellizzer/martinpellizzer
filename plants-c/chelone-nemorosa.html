Chelone nemorosa

Chelone nemorosa, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 60 centimeters tall, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped, toothed, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Chelone nemorosa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
