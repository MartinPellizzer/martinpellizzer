Chaenactis glabriuscula var. aurea

Chaenactis glabriuscula var. aurea, commonly known as the golden foothills dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
