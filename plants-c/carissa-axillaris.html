Carissa axillaris

Carissa axillaris, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to arid conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
