Coix lacryma-jobi

Coix lacryma-jobi, commonly known as Job's tears, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical seed spike that resembles a tear, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by the distinctive, hard, inedible seeds. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient in various cultures.

Botanically, it is valued for its high nutritional content and unique chemical composition.
