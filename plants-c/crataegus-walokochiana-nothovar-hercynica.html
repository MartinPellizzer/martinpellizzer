Crataegus walokochiana nothovar. hercynica

Crataegus walokochiana nothovar. hercynica, commonly known as the Hercynian hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus walokochiana and another Crataegus species.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions with mild climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural purposes.
