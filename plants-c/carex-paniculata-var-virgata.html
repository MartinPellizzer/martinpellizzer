Carex paniculata var. virgata

Carex paniculata var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's tears sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
