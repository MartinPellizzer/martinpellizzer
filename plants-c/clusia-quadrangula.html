Clusia quadrangula

Clusia quadrangula, commonly known as the four-cornered glory bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a quadrangular shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white, which bloom in clusters. Clusia quadrangula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
