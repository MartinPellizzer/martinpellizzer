Crocus purpureus

Crocus purpureus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, spicy fragrance, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its bulbs are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of saffron-like pigments.

The species is well-suited to rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.
