Coriaria lurida

Coriaria lurida, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Coriariaceae family and is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
