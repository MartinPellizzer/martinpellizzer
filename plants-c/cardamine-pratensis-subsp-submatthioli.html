Cardamine pratensis subsp. submatthioli

Cardamine pratensis subsp.

submatthioli, commonly known as the lesser watercress, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive basal rosette and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its flowers have four petals and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its reproductive success.

The subspecies is characterized by its smaller stature and more compact growth compared to the typical form of Cardamine pratensis.
