Calliandra peckoltii

Calliandra peckoltii, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a woody stem and compound leaves. The plant is renowned for its vibrant, showy inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
