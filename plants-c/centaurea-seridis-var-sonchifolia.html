Centaurea seridis var. sonchifolia

Centaurea seridis var. sonchifolia, commonly known as the desert knapweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
