Chrysocoryne hugelii

Chrysocoryne hugelii, commonly known as the Hugel's golden crown, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
