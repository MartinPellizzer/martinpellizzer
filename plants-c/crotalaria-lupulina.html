Crotalaria lupulina

Crotalaria lupulina, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are often used as a legume crop, while the plant itself has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Crotalaria lupulina is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
