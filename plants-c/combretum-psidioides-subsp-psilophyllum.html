Combretum psidioides subsp. psilophyllum

Combretum psidioides subsp.

psilophyllum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
