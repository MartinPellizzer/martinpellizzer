Campanula glomerata subsp. farinosa

Campanula glomerata subsp.

farinosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Campanula glomerata subsp.

farinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rocky landscapes.
