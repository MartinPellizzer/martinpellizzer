Cinnamomum malayanum

Cinnamomum malayanum, commonly known as Malayan cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is used to produce a spice similar to cinnamon. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it produces oval, woody fruits.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
