Crambe tataria var. hungarica

Crambe tataria var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian crambe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other economically important plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant features bi-lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in racemes, typical of the family. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The seeds of this plant are small and have a high oil content, making them of interest for potential agricultural use.
