Convolvulus arvensis var. multiflorus

Convolvulus arvensis var. multiflorus, commonly known as the field bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It is known for its aggressive growth and is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings.
