Celastrus stenophyllus

Celastrus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
