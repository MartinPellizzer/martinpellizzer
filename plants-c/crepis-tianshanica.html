Crepis tianshanica

Crepis tianshanica, commonly known as the Tianshan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Tianshan mountain range in China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in late summer.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.
