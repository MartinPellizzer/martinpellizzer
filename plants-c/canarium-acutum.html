Canarium acutum

Canarium acutum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a thick, oily pulp. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a natural adhesive.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its potential economic uses in traditional medicine and industry.
