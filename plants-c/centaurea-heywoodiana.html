Centaurea heywoodiana

Centaurea heywoodiana, commonly known as Heywood's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has spiny leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its purple to pinkish flower heads consist of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea heywoodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
