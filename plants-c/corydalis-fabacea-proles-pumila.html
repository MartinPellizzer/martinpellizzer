Corydalis fabacea proles pumila

Corydalis fabacea proles pumila, commonly known as the dwarf corydalis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
