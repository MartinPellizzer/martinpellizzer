Capparis brenesii

Capparis brenesii, commonly known as the Hawaiian caper, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats, characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

Capparis brenesii is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii, making it an important component of the local flora.
