Cytisus malacitanus subsp. catalaunicus

Cytisus malacitanus subsp.

catalaunicus, commonly known as the Catalan broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
