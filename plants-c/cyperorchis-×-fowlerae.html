Cyperorchis × fowlerae

Cyperorchis × fowlerae, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Cyperorchis mitis and Cyperorchis bulbosus.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance.

Cyperorchis × fowlerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
