Crataegus oxyacantha var. concolor

Crataegus oxyacantha var. concolor, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valued species in horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
