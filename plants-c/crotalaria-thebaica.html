Crotalaria thebaica

Crotalaria thebaica, commonly known as the Ethiopian crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring upright stems and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom from late summer to autumn. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Crotalaria thebaica is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
