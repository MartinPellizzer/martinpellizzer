Christophoriana arguta

Christophoriana arguta, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a rough, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

Christophoriana arguta is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and has a slow growth rate.
