Chlorophytum elongatofusiforme

Chlorophytum elongatofusiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spider plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that form a rosette pattern, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and moderate light conditions.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
