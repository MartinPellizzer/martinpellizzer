Carum seselifolium

Carum seselifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved caraway, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces oval, ridged fruits that contain essential oils, which are responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Carum seselifolium is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
