Cyrtomidictyum lepidocaulon

Cyrtomidictyum lepidocaulon, commonly known as the velvetleaf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
