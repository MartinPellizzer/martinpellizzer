Citharexylum sessaei

Citharexylum sessaei, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
