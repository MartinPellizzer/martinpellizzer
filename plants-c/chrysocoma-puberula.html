Chrysocoma puberula

Chrysocoma puberula, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, and fuzzy foliage, which gives it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and foliage.
