Cheilanthes pseudofarinosa

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with clustered, lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curled. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.
