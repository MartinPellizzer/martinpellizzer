Carex boliviensis

Carex boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a dark green coloration, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Carex boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
