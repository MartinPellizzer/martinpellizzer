Chamaecrista serpens var. oaxacana

Chamaecrista serpens var. oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and distinctive, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and potential ecological roles in native habitats.
