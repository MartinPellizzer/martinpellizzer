Chamaecyparis excelsa

Chamaecyparis excelsa, commonly known as the giant cedar or Alaska cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and its distinctive, layered foliage that resembles a rounded crown. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
