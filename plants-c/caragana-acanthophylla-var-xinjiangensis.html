Caragana acanthophylla var. xinjiangensis

Caragana acanthophylla var. xinjiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang barbed bush willow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, barbed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to poor soil conditions.

This species is often used for windbreaks and soil stabilization in its native habitat.
