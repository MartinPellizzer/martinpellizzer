Croton salviformis

Croton salviformis, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton salviformis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
