Cryptolappa salicifolia

Cryptolappa salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved cryptolappa, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are alternately arranged along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.
