Carex scotica

Carex scotica, commonly known as Scottish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or heathland habitats. It is often found in shaded areas and is adapted to temperate climates.

Carex scotica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
