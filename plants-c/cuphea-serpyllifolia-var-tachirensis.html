Cuphea serpyllifolia var. tachirensis

Cuphea serpyllifolia var. tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachirensis Cuphea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from red to pink. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is relatively easy to grow and prefers full sun to partial shade.
