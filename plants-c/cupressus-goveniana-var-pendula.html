Cupressus goveniana var. pendula

Cupressus goveniana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Alberta cedar, is a cultivar of the Colorado spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive weeping habit, with long, pendulous branches that cascade downward. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing an ornamental appearance. It is a hardy evergreen that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its unique visual appeal and adaptability to various climates.
