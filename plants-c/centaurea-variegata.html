Centaurea variegata

Centaurea variegata, commonly known as the variegated centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and serrated, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea variegata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
