Clethra confertifolia

Clethra confertifolia, commonly known as the denseflower clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Clethra confertifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
