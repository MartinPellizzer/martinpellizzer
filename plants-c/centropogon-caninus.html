Centropogon caninus

Centropogon caninus, commonly known as the dog's nose plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.
