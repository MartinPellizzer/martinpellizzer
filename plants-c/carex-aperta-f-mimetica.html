Carex aperta f. mimetica

Carex aperta f. mimetica, commonly known as the mimic sedge, is a variant of the broader Carex aperta species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America, particularly in wetland and riparian habitats. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its ability to mimic the appearance of other sedges, which may aid in its survival through camouflage.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
