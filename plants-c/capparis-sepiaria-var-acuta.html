Capparis sepiaria var. acuta

Capparis sepiaria var. acuta, commonly known as the sharp caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, thorn-like stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible, tart berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, preferring full sun and well-drained conditions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
