Citrus × aurantium subsp. bergamia

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

bergamia, commonly known as bergamot, is a citrus hybrid native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature.

The plant produces dark green, glossy leaves and has a thornless stem, making it suitable for ornamental and cultivation purposes. Bergamot is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from the fruit's peel and used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and the fragrance industry.
