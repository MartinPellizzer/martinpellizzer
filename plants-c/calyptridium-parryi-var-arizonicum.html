Calyptridium parryi var. arizonicum

Calyptridium parryi var. arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping in desert landscapes.
