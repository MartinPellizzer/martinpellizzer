Calamus pseudomollis

Calamus pseudomollis, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus pseudomollis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
