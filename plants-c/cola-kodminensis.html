Cola kodminensis

Cola kodminensis, commonly known as African cola, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
