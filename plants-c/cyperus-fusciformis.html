Cyperus fusciformis

Cyperus fusciformis, commonly known as the brown spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Cyperus fusciformis thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to humid conditions makes it a common component of wetland ecosystems.
