Campomanesia bracteolata

Campomanesia bracteolata, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed raw or used in local cuisine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
