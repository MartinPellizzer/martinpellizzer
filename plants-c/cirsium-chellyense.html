Cirsium chellyense

Cirsium chellyense, commonly known as the Chelly thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the western Mediterranean, particularly in Spain and northern Africa. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and open woodlands.

Cirsium chellyense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
