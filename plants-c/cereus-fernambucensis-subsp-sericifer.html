Cereus fernambucensis subsp. sericifer

Cereus fernambucensis subsp.

sericifer, commonly known as the white-spined cactus, is a rare and specialized cactus native to the high-altitude regions of Argentina. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered with white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid, rocky environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
