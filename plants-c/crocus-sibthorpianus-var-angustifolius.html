Crocus sibthorpianus var. angustifolius

Crocus sibthorpianus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, particularly in Namibia and South Africa. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, nodding shape, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
