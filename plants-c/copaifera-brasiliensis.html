Copaifera brasiliensis

Copaifera brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

The species plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat and is also a source of biodiversity.
