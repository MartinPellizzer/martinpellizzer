Chromolaena uromeres

Chromolaena uromeres, commonly known as crofton weed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Chromolaena uromeres is known for its rapid growth and invasive nature, making it a significant weed in many tropical and subtropical ecosystems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and ability to thrive in disturbed areas contribute to its widespread distribution.
