Chamaecrista takhtajanii

Chamaecrista takhtajanii, commonly known as the Afghan ticktree, is a leguminous shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It is often used for erosion control due to its deep root system and drought tolerance.

The pods are woody and contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.
