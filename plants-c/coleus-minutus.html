Coleus minutus

Coleus minutus, commonly known as the dwarf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage, often featuring shades of red, purple, and green. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue or purple and appear in clusters.

Coleus minutus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaves and easy care requirements.
