Cyananthus sericeus

Cyananthus sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cyananthus sericeus is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
