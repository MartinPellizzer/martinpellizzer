Calea camargoana

Calea camargoana, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

Calea camargoana is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
