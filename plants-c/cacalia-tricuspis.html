Cacalia tricuspis

Cacalia tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, branching stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have three distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name.
