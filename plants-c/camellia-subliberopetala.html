Camellia subliberopetala

Camellia subliberopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth texture. The plant produces camellia-like flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia subliberopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.
