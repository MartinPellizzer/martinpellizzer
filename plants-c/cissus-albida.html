Cissus albida

Cissus albida, commonly known as the silver cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white variegated leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, pale green flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cissus albida is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
