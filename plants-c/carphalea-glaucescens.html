Carphalea glaucescens

Carphalea glaucescens, commonly known as the bluestem ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet to moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, pink to purple flower heads appear in late summer and are clustered in panicles.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
