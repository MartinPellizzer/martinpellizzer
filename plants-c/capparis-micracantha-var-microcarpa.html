Capparis micracantha var. microcarpa

Capparis micracantha var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
