Citrus ichangensis

Citrus ichangensis, commonly known as the ichang mandarin, is a species of citrus native to China.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces fragrant, orange-colored fruits that are similar in appearance to mandarins but have a more intense flavor. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, and it thrives in subtropical climates.

The fruit is valued for its high vitamin C content and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
