Cinnagrostis cabrerae var. cabrerae

Cinnagrostis cabrerae var. cabrerae, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid and coastal environments of its native habitat. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
