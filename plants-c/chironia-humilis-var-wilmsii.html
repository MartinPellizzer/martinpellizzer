Chironia humilis var. wilmsii

Chironia humilis var. wilmsii, commonly known as the Wilmsii sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
