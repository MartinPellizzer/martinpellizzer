Cryptocarya sulcata

Cryptocarya sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag sycamore, is a species of evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging veins on the leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in coastal and lowland forests, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Cryptocarya sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and resilience.
