Crataegus brevipedicellata

Crataegus brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-peduncled hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus brevipedicellata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
