Ceiba sipolisii

Ceiba sipolisii, commonly known as the Sipaliwani ceiba, is a tropical tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces showy, white flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it plays an important role in the rainforest ecosystem.

The species is also valued for its timber and is considered a keystone species in its native habitat.
