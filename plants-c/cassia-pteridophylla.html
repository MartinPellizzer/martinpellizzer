Cassia pteridophylla

Cassia pteridophylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from December to March. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia pteridophylla is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
