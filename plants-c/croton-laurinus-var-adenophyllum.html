Croton laurinus var. adenophyllum

Croton laurinus var. adenophyllum, commonly known as the Christmas croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and glandular dots, which give it its specific epithet "adenophyllum.". It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange or red berries.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
