Capellia

Capellia, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble a crown. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Capellia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
