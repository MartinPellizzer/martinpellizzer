Citrus × aurantium var. japonica

Citrus × aurantium var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese lemon or yuzu, is a small evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It produces small, fragrant, yellow citrus fruits that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is known for its distinctive aroma and is often used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal remedies.
