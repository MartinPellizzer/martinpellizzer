Cistus croceus

Cistus croceus, commonly known as the yellow rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus croceus is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
