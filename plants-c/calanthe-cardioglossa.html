Calanthe cardioglossa

Calanthe cardioglossa, commonly known as the heart-lipped calanthe, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and striking, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its flowers are typically pink or white with a distinctive, heart-shaped lip.

Calanthe cardioglossa prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
