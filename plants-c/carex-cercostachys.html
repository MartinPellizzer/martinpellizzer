Carex cercostachys

Carex cercostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex cercostachys plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
