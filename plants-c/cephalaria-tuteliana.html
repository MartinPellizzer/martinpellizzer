Cephalaria tuteliana

Cephalaria tuteliana, commonly known as the mountain cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Cephalaria tuteliana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
