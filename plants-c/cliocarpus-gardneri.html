Cliocarpus gardneri

Cliocarpus gardneri, commonly known as the mangrove apple or white mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a small tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water and birds.

Cliocarpus gardneri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
