Carex oederi var. philocrena

Carex oederi var. philocrena, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that ranges from silver-gray to bluish-green, with slender, triangular leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, which are clustered in spikes and typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
