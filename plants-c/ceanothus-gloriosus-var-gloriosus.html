Ceanothus gloriosus var. gloriosus

Ceanothus gloriosus var. gloriosus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to coastal gardens. Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
