Castratella rosea

Castratella rosea, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Castratella rosea prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
