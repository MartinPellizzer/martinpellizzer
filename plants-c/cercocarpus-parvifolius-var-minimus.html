Cercocarpus parvifolius var. minimus

Cercocarpus parvifolius var. minimus, commonly known as the small-leaved curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in spring, which are followed by distinctive, curling seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
