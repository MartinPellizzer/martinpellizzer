Cornus alba f. baileyi

Cornus alba f. baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's dogwood, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features striking red stems that add vibrant color to gardens, especially in winter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and adaptability to various growing conditions.
