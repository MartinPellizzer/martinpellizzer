Corydalis simplicifolia

Corydalis simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often growing in woodland or rocky habitats.

Corydalis simplicifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
