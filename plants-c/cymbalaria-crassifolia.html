Cymbalaria crassifolia

Cymbalaria crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and it is often found in moist, grassy areas.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
