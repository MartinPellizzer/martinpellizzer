Cynanchum Canescens

Cynanchum canescens is a hardy perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is known for its distinctive gray-green foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it an important component of its ecosystem. Despite its beauty, it is also considered a potential invasive species in some areas.
