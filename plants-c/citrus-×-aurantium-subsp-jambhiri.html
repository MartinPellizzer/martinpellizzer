Citrus × aurantium subsp. jambhiri

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

jambhiri, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive wrinkled texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, yellow-green fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. The fruit's zest is widely used in cooking for its intense, aromatic flavor.

Botanically, it is a hybrid of Citrus aurantium and Citrus jambhiri, with distinctive features that set it apart from other citrus species.
