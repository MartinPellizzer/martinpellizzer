Centaurea reyesii

Centaurea reyesii, commonly known as Reyes' cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features blue to violet flowers with yellow centers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea reyesii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
