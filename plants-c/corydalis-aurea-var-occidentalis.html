Corydalis aurea var. occidentalis

Corydalis aurea var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western golden corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
