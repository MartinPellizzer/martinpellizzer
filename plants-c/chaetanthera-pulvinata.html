Chaetanthera pulvinata

Chaetanthera pulvinata, commonly known as the purple beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features hairy, upright stems and produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Its unique floral structure and attractive color make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
