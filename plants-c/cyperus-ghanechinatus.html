Cyperus ghanechinatus

Cyperus ghanechinatus, commonly known as the Ghana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or brown in color. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Cyperus ghanechinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
