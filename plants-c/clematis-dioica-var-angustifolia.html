Clematis dioica var. angustifolia

Clematis dioica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures such as trellises or fences.
