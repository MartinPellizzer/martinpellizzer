Castanea crenata f. pleiocarpa

Castanea crenata f. pleiocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese horse chestnut, is a variant of the chestnut genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which are a notable feature of its botanical profile. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in horticulture.
