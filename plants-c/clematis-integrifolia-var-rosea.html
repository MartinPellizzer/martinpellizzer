Clematis integrifolia var. rosea

Clematis integrifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, single or double pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 20 feet long, making it an excellent choice for trellises or arbors. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
