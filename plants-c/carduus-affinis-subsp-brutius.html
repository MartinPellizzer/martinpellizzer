Carduus affinis subsp. brutius

Carduus affinis subsp.

brutius, commonly known as the bristly cardueus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and wastelands.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces purple to reddish composite flowers. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
