Cardamine hayneana var. iliciana

Cardamine hayneana var. iliciana, commonly known as the western watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features small, alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are white or pale green, arranged in clusters and appearing in early spring.

The species is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
