Clematis paniculata var. lathyroides

Clematis paniculata var. lathyroides, commonly known as the ivy-leaved clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitat.
