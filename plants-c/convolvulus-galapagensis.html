Convolvulus galapagensis

Convolvulus galapagensis, commonly known as the Galápagos morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue or violet flowers with five petals. The plant has a climbing habit, using its tendrils to attach to surrounding vegetation. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers open in the morning and close by midday.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for local pollinators.
