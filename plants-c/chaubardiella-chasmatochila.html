Chaubardiella chasmatochila

Chaubardiella chasmatochila, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant has a low-growing habit with linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
