Centaurea boissieri subsp. calvescens

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

calvescens, commonly known as the Cretan star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like structures at the base of its inflorescence. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy-like plants.
