Conospermum acerosum subsp. hirsutum

Conospermum acerosum subsp.

hirsutum, commonly known as the Hairy Waratah, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking red or pink inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are borne on tall, erect stems and are visited by birds and insects.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
