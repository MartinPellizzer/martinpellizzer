Cousinia carlinoides

Cousinia carlinoides, commonly known as the Carpathian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, elongated fruits known as achenes.

Cousinia carlinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
