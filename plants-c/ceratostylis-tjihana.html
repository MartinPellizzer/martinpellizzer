Ceratostylis tjihana

Ceratostylis tjihana, commonly known as the Tjihana orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with slender, erect stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests or swampy areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered with a fine layer of hairs, which helps in retaining moisture.

The orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
