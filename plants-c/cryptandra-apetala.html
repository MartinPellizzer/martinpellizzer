Cryptandra apetala

Cryptandra apetala, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Cryptandra apetala is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
