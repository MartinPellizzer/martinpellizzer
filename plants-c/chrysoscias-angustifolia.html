Chrysoscias angustifolia

Chrysoscias angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysoscia, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
