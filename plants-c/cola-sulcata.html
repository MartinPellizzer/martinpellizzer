Cola sulcata

Cola sulcata, commonly known as the African kola nut tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces hard, elongated fruits that contain the aromatic kola nuts used in traditional ceremonies and beverages. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
