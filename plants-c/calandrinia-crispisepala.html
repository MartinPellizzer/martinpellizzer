Calandrinia crispisepala

Calandrinia crispisepala, commonly known as the hairy pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a distinctive hairy texture. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Calandrinia crispisepala thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
