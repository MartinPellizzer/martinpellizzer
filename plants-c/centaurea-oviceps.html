Centaurea oviceps

Centaurea oviceps, commonly known as the sheep's beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that are often surrounded by white bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea oviceps is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
