Commelina undulata

Commelina undulata, commonly known as the wavy commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy edge, contributing to its common name.

Commelina undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
