Convolvulus turrillianus

Convolvulus turrillianus, commonly known as the rock convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its vines can climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
