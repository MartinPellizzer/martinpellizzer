Comanthera curralensis

Comanthera curralensis, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are often pink or purple, with a unique structure that resembles a small orchid.

Comanthera curralensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
