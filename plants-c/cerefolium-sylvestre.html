Cerefolium sylvestre

Cerefolium sylvestre, commonly known as wild sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.
