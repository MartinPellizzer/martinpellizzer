Chamaejasme stelleriana

Chamaejasme stelleriana, commonly known as Steller's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It features dense clusters of yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
