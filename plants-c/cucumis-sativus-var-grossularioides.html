Cucumis sativus var. grossularioides

Cucumis sativus var. grossularioides, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often spiny fruit, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, a common trait in the Cucurbitaceae family.

This variety is valued for its crisp texture and mild flavor, making it a popular choice for fresh consumption.
