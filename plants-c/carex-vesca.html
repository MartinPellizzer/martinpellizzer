Carex vesca

Carex vesca, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its flowers are usually brown or greenish and lack showy petals.

Carex vesca is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
