Calliandra luetzelburgii

Calliandra luetzelburgii, commonly known as the yellow calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, fluffy bracts. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that provide a lush, green backdrop to its vibrant flowers.
