Convolvulus pruinosus

Convolvulus pruinosus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
