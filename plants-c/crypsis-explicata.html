Crypsis explicata

Crypsis explicata, commonly known as the translucent leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves that appear almost see-through.

Native to the humid understory of tropical rainforests, it thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. Its leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern, allowing for efficient light absorption while minimizing water loss. The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates or decaying wood.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is noted for its intricate venation and slow growth rate.
