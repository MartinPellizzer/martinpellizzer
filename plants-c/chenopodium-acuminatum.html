Chenopodium acuminatum

Chenopodium acuminatum, commonly known as sharpleaf goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often used as a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium acuminatum is also known for its high nutritional value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
