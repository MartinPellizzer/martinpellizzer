Coreopsis discoidea

Coreopsis discoidea, commonly known as the disc coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like appearance, featuring bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coreopsis discoidea is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
