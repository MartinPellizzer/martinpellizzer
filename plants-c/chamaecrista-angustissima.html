Chamaecrista angustissima

Chamaecrista angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with slender, linear leaflets, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Chamaecrista angustissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
