Calamus griseus

Calamus griseus, commonly known as gray rattan, is a climbing plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, slender stems that are often used in the production of rattan furniture. The plant has pinnate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly grayish underside, giving it its common name. Calamus griseus produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue to black berries.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, lowland areas with rich soil.
