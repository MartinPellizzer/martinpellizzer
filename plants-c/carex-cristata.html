Carex cristata

Carex cristata, commonly known as the cristate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

Carex cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
