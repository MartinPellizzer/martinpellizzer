Celtis brownii

Celtis brownii, commonly known as the white ash, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa, including South Africa.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by small, round fruits that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

Celtis brownii is often found in woodland areas and can grow up to 15 meters tall, making it a notable component of local ecosystems.
