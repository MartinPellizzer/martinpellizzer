Cromidon gracile

Cromidon gracile, commonly known as the slender cromidon, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and appear in clusters.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
