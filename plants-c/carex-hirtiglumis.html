Carex hirtiglumis

Carex hirtiglumis, commonly known as the hairy-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish flowers, which are often enclosed in bracts.

Carex hirtiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
