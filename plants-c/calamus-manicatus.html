Calamus manicatus

Calamus manicatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender reed-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its rhizomes are also utilized in some local medicinal practices.

Calamus manicatus thrives in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.
