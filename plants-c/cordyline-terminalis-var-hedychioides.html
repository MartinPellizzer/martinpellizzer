Cordyline terminalis var. hedychioides

Cordyline terminalis var. hedychioides, commonly known as the red-veined dracaena, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are predominantly green with prominent red veins. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and thrives in bright, indirect light. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
