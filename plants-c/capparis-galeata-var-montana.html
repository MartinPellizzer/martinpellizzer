Capparis galeata var. montana

Capparis galeata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain caper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, green, spherical fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
