Carex tuberosa

Carex tuberosa, commonly known as tuberous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and produces dense, grass-like foliage with triangular stems. The plant is notable for its tuberous roots, which are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex tuberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
