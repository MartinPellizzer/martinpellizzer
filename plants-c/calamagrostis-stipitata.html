Calamagrostis stipitata

Calamagrostis stipitata, commonly known as the slender sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features slender, arching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are often white or pale green, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
