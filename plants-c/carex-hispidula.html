Carex hispidula

Carex hispidula, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or brown, and it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
