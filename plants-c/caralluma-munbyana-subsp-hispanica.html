Caralluma munbyana subsp. hispanica

Caralluma munbyana subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish devil's tongue, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It features fleshy, spine-tipped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a notable species in the Crassulaceae family.
