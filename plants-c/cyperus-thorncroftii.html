Cyperus thorncroftii

Cyperus thorncroftii, commonly known as the Thorncroft flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, pale green to brown flowers, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas.

Cyperus thorncroftii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
