Cardamine hirsuta var. formosana

Cardamine hirsuta var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan hairy watercress, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
