Cyperus flavescens var. fontanesii

Cyperus flavescens var. fontanesii, commonly known as the yellow flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
