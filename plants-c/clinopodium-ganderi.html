Clinopodium ganderi

Clinopodium ganderi, commonly known as Gander's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance, while its small flowers are typically white or pale purple.

Clinopodium ganderi is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.
