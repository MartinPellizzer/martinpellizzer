Chloropsis crinita

Chloropsis crinita, commonly known as the silver hair vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Chloropsis crinita is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
