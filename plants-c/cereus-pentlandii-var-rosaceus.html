Cereus pentlandii var. rosaceus

Cereus pentlandii var. rosaceus, commonly known as the Rosy Cereus, is a cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, large, white flowers that bloom at night, typically during the summer months. Its stems are covered in fine, white spines, and it is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
