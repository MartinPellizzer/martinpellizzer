Chaenomeles trichogyna

Chaenomeles trichogyna, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during late spring. The plant's fruit, which is edible when ripe, turns from green to a deep red or orange color. It has a rugged, ornamental appearance with thick, rough bark and small, dark green leaves.

Chaenomeles trichogyna is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
