Calamus oreophilus

Calamus oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain calamus, is a perennial herb native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Calamus oreophilus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
