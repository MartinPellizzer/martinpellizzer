Chrysobalanus icaco

Chrysobalanus icaco, commonly known as the coco plum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalanoideae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, plum-like fruits.

It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
