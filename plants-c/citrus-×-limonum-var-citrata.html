Citrus × limonum var. citrata

Citrus × limonum var. citrata, commonly known as the lemon tree, is a citrus species characterized by its evergreen shrub or small tree form.

It produces oval, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic peel and a juicy, acidic pulp. The leaves are glossy, ovate, and have a distinct citrus scent, while the flowers are white and fragrant. The plant is native to Southeast Asia but is widely cultivated in warm climates for its fruit and essential oils.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth and fruit production.
