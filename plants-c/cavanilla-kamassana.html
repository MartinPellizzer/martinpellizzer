Cavanilla kamassana

Cavanilla kamassana, commonly known as the Kamassan pepper plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, spicy fruits used in traditional cuisine. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it is cultivated for both its ornamental value and its pungent fruit.

The botanical characteristics of Cavanilla kamassana make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical environments.
