Campderia floribunda

Campderia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered campderia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules as fruit, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
