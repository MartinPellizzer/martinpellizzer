Cirsium toraiense

Cirsium toraiense, commonly known as the Torai thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
