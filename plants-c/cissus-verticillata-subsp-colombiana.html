Cissus verticillata subsp. colombiana

Cissus verticillata subsp.

colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America. It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and yellow markings.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
