Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. plattensis

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. plattensis, commonly known as Platt's yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are adaptations to arid environments. The plant typically grows in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
