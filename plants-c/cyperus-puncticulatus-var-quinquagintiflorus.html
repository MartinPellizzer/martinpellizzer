Cyperus puncticulatus var. quinquagintiflorus

Cyperus puncticulatus var. quinquagintiflorus, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, typically greenish or brownish in color.

It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.
