Chrysolaena sceptrum

Chrysolaena sceptrum, commonly known as the golden rosette, is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. This plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and vibrant, golden-yellow foliage. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
