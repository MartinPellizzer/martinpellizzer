Cirsium austrosatsumense

Cirsium austrosatsumense, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and its flowers are composed of ray florets that bloom in late summer.

Cirsium austrosatsumense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
