Clematis petriei

Clematis petriei, commonly known as the New Zealand vine, is a climbing perennial native to New Zealand.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length, allowing it to climb trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin.

Clematis petriei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.
