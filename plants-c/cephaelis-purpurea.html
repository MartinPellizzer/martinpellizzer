Cephaelis purpurea

Cephaelis purpurea, commonly known as the purple flame or pink fairy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting bees and butterflies, contributing to pollination.

Cephaelis purpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
