Caltha palustris f. transsilvanica

Caltha palustris f. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian marsh marigold, is a botanical variant of the marsh marigold native to Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are dark green, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
