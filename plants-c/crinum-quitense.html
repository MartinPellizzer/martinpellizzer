Crinum quitense

Crinum quitense, commonly known as the Quitense Glory-of-the-Snow, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often resembling lilies. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically evergreen and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can produce multiple stems and flowers per season.

Crinum quitense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
