Clematoclethra cordifolia

Clematoclethra cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf clematoclethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy spring blooms.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
