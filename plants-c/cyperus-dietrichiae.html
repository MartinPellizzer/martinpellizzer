Cyperus dietrichiae

Cyperus dietrichiae, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wetland environments. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with whorled leaves that give it a compact, rounded appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, and it often grows in shallow water or moist soil.

Cyperus dietrichiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
