Carex inaequabilis

Carex inaequabilis, commonly known as the unequal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex inaequabilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
