Carex atherodes var. vix-vaginans

Carex atherodes var. vix-vaginans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheathing at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
