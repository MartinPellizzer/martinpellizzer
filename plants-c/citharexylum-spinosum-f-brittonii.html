Citharexylum spinosum f. brittonii

Citharexylum spinosum f. brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's spiny citharexylum, is a variant of the spiny citharexylum, a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a leathery texture and a slightly serrated margin. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
