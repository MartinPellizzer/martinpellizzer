Cymbidium paucifolium

Cymbidium paucifolium, commonly known as the few-leaved orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces upright, racemose inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale pink with a yellow lip, and they bloom in late winter to early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
