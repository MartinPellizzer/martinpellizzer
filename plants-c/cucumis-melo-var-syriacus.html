Cucumis melo var. syriacus

Cucumis melo var. syriacus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit, which typically measures between 15 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for trellising or ground planting. Its fruit is usually harvested when still green, though some varieties may develop a slight yellow or orange tint.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
