Cephalaria ambrosioides var. angustifolia

Cephalaria ambrosioides var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose panicles, and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in less fertile conditions.
