Callista marginata

Callista marginata, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong winds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found growing near the shoreline.

Callista marginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
