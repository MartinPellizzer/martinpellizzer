Centaurea xylobasis

Centaurea xylobasis, commonly known as the woodland centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and woodlands. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are bright blue to purple, with ray florets that resemble daisies, and are arranged in dense, conical clusters.

Centaurea xylobasis is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soils.
