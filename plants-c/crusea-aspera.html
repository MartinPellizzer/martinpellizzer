Crusea aspera

Crusea aspera, commonly known as the rough crusea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Crusea aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
