Cercanthemum baronii

Cercanthemum baronii, commonly known as the Baron's thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Cercanthemum baronii is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
