Chamaecrista aurivilla

Chamaecrista aurivilla, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Chamaecrista aurivilla is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
