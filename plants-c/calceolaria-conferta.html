Calceolaria conferta

Calceolaria conferta, commonly known as the woolly slipper orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and striking, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, slender stems and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Calceolaria conferta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
