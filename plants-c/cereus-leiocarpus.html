Cereus leiocarpus

Cereus leiocarpus, commonly known as the white night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting moths for pollination. It has spiny areoles and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
