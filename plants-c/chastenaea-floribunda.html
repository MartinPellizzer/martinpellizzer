Chastenaea floribunda

Chastenaea floribunda, commonly known as the showy chastetree, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Chastenaea floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
