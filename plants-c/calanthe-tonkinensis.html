Calanthe tonkinensis

Calanthe tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and striking, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of several showy flowers with a distinctive lip, which is a key feature in orchid identification.

Calanthe tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
