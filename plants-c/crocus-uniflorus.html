Crocus uniflorus

Crocus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bulbous structure and produces a single, showy flower with three petals and three sepals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its flowers are usually a vibrant yellow or orange, and the plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

Crocus uniflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
