Cassia ciliata

Cassia ciliata, commonly known as the sicklepod or golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental species.

Cassia ciliata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
