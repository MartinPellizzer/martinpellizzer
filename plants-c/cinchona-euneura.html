Cinchona euneura

Cinchona euneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is notable for its historical significance in the production of quinine, a compound used in the treatment of malaria.

However, it is less commonly cultivated today compared to other cinchona species.
