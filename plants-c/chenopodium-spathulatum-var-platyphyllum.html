Chenopodium spathulatum var. platyphyllum

Chenopodium spathulatum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as broadleaf goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to regions of North America and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is sometimes used as a food source for livestock and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
