Clethra pumila

Clethra pumila, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a small shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Clethra pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
