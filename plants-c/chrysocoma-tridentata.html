Chrysocoma tridentata

Chrysocoma tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Chrysocoma tridentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
