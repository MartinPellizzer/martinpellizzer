Chusquea pilgeri

Chusquea pilgeri, commonly known as the Pilger's bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Chusquea pilgeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
