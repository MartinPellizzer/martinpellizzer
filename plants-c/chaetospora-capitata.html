Chaetospora capitata

Chaetospora capitata, commonly known as the purple sea lettuce, is a marine green alga found in coastal areas.

It has a distinctive morphology with a dense, cushion-like growth form and a bluish-green to purple coloration. The plant consists of a thallus that is flattened and covered with small, branched filaments. It is often found attached to rocks or other substrates in intertidal zones.

Its structure supports a variety of marine life, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
