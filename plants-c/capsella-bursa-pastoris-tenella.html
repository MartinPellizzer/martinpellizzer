Capsella bursa-pastoris tenella

Capsella bursa-pastoris tenella, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a basal rosette structure. Its fruits are short, club-shaped pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
