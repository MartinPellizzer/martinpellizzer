Calceolaria grandipinnata

Calceolaria grandipinnata, commonly known as the large-leaf slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Calceolaria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette or spiral pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
