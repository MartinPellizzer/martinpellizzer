Clematis orientalis var. baluchistanica

Clematis orientalis var. baluchistanica, commonly known as the Baluchistan clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It is a deciduous vine that produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a climbing plant in gardens.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
