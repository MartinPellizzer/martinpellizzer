Ceratophyllum verrucosum

Ceratophyllum verrucosum, commonly known as the spiked hornwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, spiky leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is often found in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. It does not require soil to grow, as it can anchor itself using its rhizoids.

Ceratophyllum verrucosum is known for its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life and improve water quality by absorbing nutrients.
