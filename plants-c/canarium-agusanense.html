Canarium agusanense

Canarium agusanense, commonly known as the Agusan nut or Agusan canarium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Burseraceae.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the provinces of Agusan and Davao. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
