Cyperus versicolor

Cyperus versicolor, commonly known as the variable flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus versicolor is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
