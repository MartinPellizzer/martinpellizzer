Capparis umbonata var. latifolia

Capparis umbonata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round, green fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The variety latifolia is distinguished by its wider leaflets compared to the typical form of Capparis umbonata.
