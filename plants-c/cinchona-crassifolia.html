Cinchona crassifolia

Cinchona crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its bark contains alkaloids, including quinine, which has historically been used in medicine.
