Cadaba glandulosa

Cadaba glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cadaba, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in tiny glands, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use.
