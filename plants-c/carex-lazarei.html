Carex lazarei

Carex lazarei, commonly known as the Lazarei sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex lazarei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
