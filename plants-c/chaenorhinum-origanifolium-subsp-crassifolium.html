Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp. crassifolium

Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf sand rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
