Carex gracilis f. personata

Carex gracilis f. personata, commonly known as the slender sedge.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is native to parts of North America. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to aquatic environments.
