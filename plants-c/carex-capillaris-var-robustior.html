Carex capillaris var. robustior

Carex capillaris var. robustior, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
