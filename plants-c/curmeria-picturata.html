Curmeria picturata

Curmeria picturata, commonly known as the painted curmeria, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored flowers that range from yellow to orange and red, often resembling intricate paintings. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Curmeria picturata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional medicine in some cultures.
