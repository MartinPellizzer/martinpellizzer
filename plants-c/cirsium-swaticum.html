Cirsium swaticum

Cirsium swaticum, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are spiny and covered in fine hairs, while its purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Cirsium swaticum plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
