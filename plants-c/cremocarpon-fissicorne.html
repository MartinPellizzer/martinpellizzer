Cremocarpon fissicorne

Cremocarpon fissicorne, commonly known as the fissured cup lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, leaf-like structure.

It typically grows on bark, soil, or rock surfaces in temperate forest environments. The thallus is usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a finely wrinkled surface. It has a complex structure with a medulla composed of loosely aggregated hyphae and a cortex that is often differentiated.

This lichen is known for its ability to tolerate varying environmental conditions, making it a common species in shaded, moist habitats.
