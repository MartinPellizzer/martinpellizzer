Calligonum griseum

Calligonum griseum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and gray-green foliage that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Its adaptability to extreme conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
