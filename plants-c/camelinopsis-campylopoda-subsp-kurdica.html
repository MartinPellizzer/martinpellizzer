Camelinopsis campylopoda subsp. kurdica

Camelinopsis campylopoda subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish camelinopsis, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces delicate, pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
