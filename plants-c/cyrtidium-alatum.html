Cyrtidium alatum

Cyrtidium alatum, commonly known as the winged fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its unique, frond-like leaves that are divided into narrow, winged segments. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cyrtidium alatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
