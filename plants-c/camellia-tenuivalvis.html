Camellia tenuivalvis

Camellia tenuivalvis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces striking red-orange flowers with prominent yellow stamens, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Camellia tenuivalvis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal floral display.
