Convolvulus pseudosoldanella

Convolvulus pseudosoldanella, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky soils.

This species is often found growing in dry, sunny habitats, including cliffs and gravelly slopes.
