Cyathea singalanensis

Cyathea singalanensis, commonly known as the Singalana tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea singalanensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various local species.
