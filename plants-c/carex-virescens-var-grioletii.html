Carex virescens var. grioletii

Carex virescens var. grioletii, commonly known as the Griolet's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
