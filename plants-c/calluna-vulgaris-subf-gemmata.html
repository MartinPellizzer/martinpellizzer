Calluna vulgaris subf. gemmata

Calluna vulgaris subf.

gemmata, commonly known as the gemma-type heather, is a subshrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. It is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its ability to form dense mats, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded areas.
