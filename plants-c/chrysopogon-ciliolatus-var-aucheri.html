Chrysopogon ciliolatus var. aucheri

Chrysopogon ciliolatus var. aucheri, commonly known as the Aucher's tussock grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

This grass is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.
