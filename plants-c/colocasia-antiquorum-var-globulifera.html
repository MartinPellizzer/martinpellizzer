Colocasia antiquorum var. globulifera

Colocasia antiquorum var. globulifera, commonly known as the elephant ear or giant taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, and often glossy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking purple or red spathes that enclose bright yellow to orange spadices, creating an eye-catching floral display. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is widely cultivated for its dramatic foliage and is also used in traditional cuisine in some regions.
