Clinopodium colchicum var. sotzinum

Clinopodium colchicum var. sotzinum, commonly known as the Sotzina mint, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a minty scent, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Andean medicine for its purported healing benefits.
