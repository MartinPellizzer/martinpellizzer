Carex wahuensis subsp. rubiginosa

Carex wahuensis subsp.

rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru.

This sedge is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.
