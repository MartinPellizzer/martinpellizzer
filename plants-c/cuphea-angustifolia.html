Cuphea angustifolia

Cuphea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea angustifolia is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
