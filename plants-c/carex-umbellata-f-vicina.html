Carex umbellata f. vicina

Carex umbellata f. vicina, commonly known as the slender flat-sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
