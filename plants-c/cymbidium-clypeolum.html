Cymbidium clypeolum

Cymbidium clypeolum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with tall, erect stems that bear numerous lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent white lips. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light.

Cymbidium clypeolum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
