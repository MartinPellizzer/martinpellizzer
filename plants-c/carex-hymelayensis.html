Carex hymelayensis

Carex hymelayensis, commonly known as the Hymelayensis sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically green or brown in color.
