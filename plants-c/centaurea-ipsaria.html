Centaurea ipsaria

Centaurea ipsaria, commonly known as the Balkan star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea ipsaria is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
