Cyrtomium pseudocaudipinnum

Cyrtomium pseudocaudipinnum, commonly known as the false tail pine, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.
