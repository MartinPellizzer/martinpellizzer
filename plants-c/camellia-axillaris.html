Camellia axillaris

Camellia axillaris, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Camellia axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic beauty.
