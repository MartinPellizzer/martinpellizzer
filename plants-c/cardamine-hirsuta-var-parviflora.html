Cardamine hirsuta var. parviflora

Cardamine hirsuta var. parviflora, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to the typical form of Cardamine hirsuta.
