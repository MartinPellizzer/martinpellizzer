Carex panicea f. binata

Carex panicea f. binata, commonly known as the panicle sedge, is a variant of the panicled sedge native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and dark green. The plant produces dense, fluffy panicles of small, white or pale green flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
