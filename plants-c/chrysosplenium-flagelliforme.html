Chrysosplenium flagelliforme

Chrysosplenium flagelliforme, commonly known as the creeping chrysosplenium, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and delicate, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
