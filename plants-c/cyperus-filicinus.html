Cyperus filicinus

Cyperus filicinus, commonly known as the water flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often form dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. Cyperus filicinus is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it thrives in shallow water or saturated soil.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
