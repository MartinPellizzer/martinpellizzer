Centaurea procurrens subsp. sharonensis

Centaurea procurrens subsp.

sharonensis, commonly known as Sharon's prostrate cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The species is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential horticultural value.
