Chrysanthemum multicaule

Chrysanthemum multicaule, commonly known as the clustered chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flower heads that range in color from yellow to white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disc florets.

Chrysanthemum multicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
