Cucurbita maxima var. leucadica

Cucurbita maxima var. leucadica, commonly known as the white-fruited pumpkin, is a variety of the giant pumpkin species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, round, pale yellow to white-colored fruits, which can weigh several kilograms. The plant produces broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. The fruit is used in both culinary and ornamental contexts due to its unique appearance and nutritional value.

It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season for optimal development.
