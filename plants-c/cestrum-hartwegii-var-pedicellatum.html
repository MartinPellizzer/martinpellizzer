Cestrum hartwegii var. pedicellatum

Cestrum hartwegii var. pedicellatum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
