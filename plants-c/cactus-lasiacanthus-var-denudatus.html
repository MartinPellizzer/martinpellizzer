Cactus lasiacanthus var. denudatus

Cactus lasiacanthus var. denudatus, commonly known as the naked cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and reduced leaves, which are adaptations to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
