Carex fusca subsp. intricata

Carex fusca subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the intricate sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream edges. Its leaves are generally dark green and have a slightly rough texture.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
