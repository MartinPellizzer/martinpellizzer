Cymbidium × ballianum

Cymbidium × ballianum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cymbidium goeringii and Cymbidium lowianum.

It is a tall, evergreen herbaceous plant with broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces tall spikes bearing numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It is native to China and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

This orchid thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
