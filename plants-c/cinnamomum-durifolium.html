Cinnamomum durifolium

Cinnamomum durifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved cinnamon, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, reddish-brown fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is aromatic and has been used traditionally in various medicinal and culinary applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
