Cissus microdonta f. bemarahensis

Cissus microdonta f. bemarahensis, commonly known as the Bemarahensis grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor plants.
