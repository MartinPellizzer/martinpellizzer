Croton suaveolens var. oblongifolius

Croton suaveolens var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the fragrant croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of bloom. Its stems are typically thick and woody, with a tendency to branch out profusely.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
