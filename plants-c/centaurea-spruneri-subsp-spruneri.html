Centaurea spruneri subsp. spruneri

Centaurea spruneri subsp.

spruneri, commonly known as the Balkan cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
