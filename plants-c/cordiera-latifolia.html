Cordiera latifolia

Cordiera latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Cordiera latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
