Cachrys pterochlaena var. leiocarpa

Cachrys pterochlaena var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded cashew, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a drupe with a hard, woody shell enclosing the edible seed. The plant is cultivated for its seed, which is processed into the popular nut known as cashew.

It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
