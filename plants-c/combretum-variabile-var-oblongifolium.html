Combretum variabile var. oblongifolium

Combretum variabile var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf combretum, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the rainy season. Its bark is thick and often flaky, and it is commonly found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
