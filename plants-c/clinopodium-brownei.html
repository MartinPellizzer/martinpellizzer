Clinopodium brownei

Clinopodium brownei, commonly known as Brown's mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Clinopodium brownei is also valued for its ornamental qualities in native plant gardens.
