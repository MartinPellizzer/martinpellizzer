Cicer arietinum subsp. reticulatum

Cicer arietinum subsp.

reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate chickpea, is a subspecies of the chickpea plant characterized by its distinctive reticulate pattern on the pod and seeds. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that develop into pods containing 2–4 angular seeds. This subspecies is valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions and is cultivated for both its nutritional value and agricultural resilience.

Its genetic diversity contributes to breeding programs aimed at improving chickpea varieties for higher yields and disease resistance.
