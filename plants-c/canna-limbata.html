Canna limbata

Canna limbata, commonly known as the broadleaf canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Canna limbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
