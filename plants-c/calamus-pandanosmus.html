Calamus pandanosmus

Calamus pandanosmus, commonly known as the pandan-like reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves.

It typically grows in wet, tropical environments, often near water bodies or in swampy areas. The plant's leaves are broad and can be used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. Its rhizomes are also valued for their aromatic properties.

However, it is important to note that this species is sometimes confused with other similar-looking palms due to its morphological similarities.
