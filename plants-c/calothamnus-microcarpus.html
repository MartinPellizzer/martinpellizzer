Calothamnus microcarpus

Calothamnus microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Calothamnus microcarpus is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
