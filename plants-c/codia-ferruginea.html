Codia ferruginea

Codia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty codia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Codia ferruginea is native to parts of Africa and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
