Convolvulus luteolus var. purpuratus

Convolvulus luteolus var. purpuratus, commonly known as the purple-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, blooming throughout the summer months.

The variety is distinguished by its vibrant purple petals, which contrast with its delicate foliage.
