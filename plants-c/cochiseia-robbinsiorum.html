Cochiseia robbinsiorum

Cochiseia robbinsiorum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Cochiseia robbinsiorum plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
