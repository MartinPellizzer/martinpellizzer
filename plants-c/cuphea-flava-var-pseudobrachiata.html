Cuphea flava var. pseudobrachiata

Cuphea flava var. pseudobrachiata, commonly known as the golden flame cuphea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Central America.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and ornamental value in gardens.
