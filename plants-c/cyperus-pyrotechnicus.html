Cyperus pyrotechnicus

Cyperus pyrotechnicus, commonly known as the flame grass, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright red or orange bracts that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and it typically grows in wet or marshy environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the inflorescence being the most visually distinctive feature.

Cyperus pyrotechnicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
