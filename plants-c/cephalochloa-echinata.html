Cephalochloa echinata

Cephalochloa echinata, commonly known as the spiny-headed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive spiny bracts at the base of its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.
