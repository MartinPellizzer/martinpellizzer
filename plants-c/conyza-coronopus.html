Conyza coronopus

Conyza coronopus, commonly known as common ironweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Conyza coronopus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and waste areas.
