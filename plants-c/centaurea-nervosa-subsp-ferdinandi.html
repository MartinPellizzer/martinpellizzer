Centaurea nervosa subsp. ferdinandi

Centaurea nervosa subsp.

ferdinandi, commonly known as Ferdinand's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It features erect, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange composite flower heads, which consist of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.
