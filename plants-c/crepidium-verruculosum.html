Crepidium verruculosum

Crepidium verruculosum, commonly known as the疣茎兰, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest floors, where it thrives in rich, organic soil. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with fleshy, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale green, with a distinct lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under suitable conditions.
