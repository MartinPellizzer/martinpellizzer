Copernicia barbadensis

Copernicia barbadensis, commonly known as the Barbados wax palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, waxy fruits that contain a single seed. It is adapted to coastal and dry environments, often growing in sandy soils.

The wax palm is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
