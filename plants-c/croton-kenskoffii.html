Croton kenskoffii

Croton kenskoffii, commonly known as the golden croton, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental members. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green with a yellow or orange midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton kenskoffii is valued in horticulture for its vibrant foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
