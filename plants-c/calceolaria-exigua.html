Calceolaria exigua

Calceolaria exigua, commonly known as the dwarf slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique and delicate appearance.

Calceolaria exigua is a member of the Calceolarieae tribe within the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
