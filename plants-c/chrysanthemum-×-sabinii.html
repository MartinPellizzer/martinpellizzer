Chrysanthemum × sabinii

Chrysanthemum × sabinii, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its foliage consists of dark green, lobed leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Chrysanthemum × sabinii is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
