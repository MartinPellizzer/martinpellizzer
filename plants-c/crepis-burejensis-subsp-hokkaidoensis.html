Crepis burejensis subsp. hokkaidoensis

Crepis burejensis subsp.

hokkaidoensis, commonly known as Hokkaido crested grass, is a flowering plant species native to Japan, specifically Hokkaido. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence with bracts that resemble a crown.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a corymb-like structure.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
