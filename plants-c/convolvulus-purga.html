Convolvulus purga

Convolvulus purga, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its vines can grow rapidly, often climbing or trailing along surfaces.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the seeds are enclosed in a spiral-shaped capsule.
