Commelina humilis

Commelina humilis, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with striking floral structures. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes and shaded understories of forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers consist of three blue or violet petals and three white sepals.

Commelina humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
