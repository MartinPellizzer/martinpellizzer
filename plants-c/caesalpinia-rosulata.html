Caesalpinia rosulata

Caesalpinia rosulata, commonly known as the rose-apple tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release small, round, red seeds.

Caesalpinia rosulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
