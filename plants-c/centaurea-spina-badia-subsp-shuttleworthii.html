Centaurea spina-badia subsp. shuttleworthii

Centaurea spina-badia subsp.

shuttleworthii, commonly known as the Shuttleworth's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
