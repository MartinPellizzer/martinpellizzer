Cirsium clavatum

Cirsium clavatum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with rough, prickly edges, and its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Cirsium clavatum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
