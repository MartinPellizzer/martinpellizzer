Chloraea odontoglossa

Chloraea odontoglossa, commonly known as the toothed tongue orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive, toothed lip. Its growth habit is characterized by its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or mounted displays.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate beauty, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
