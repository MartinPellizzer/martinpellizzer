Calamagrostis scopulorum var. bakeri

Calamagrostis scopulorum var. bakeri, commonly known as Baker's rock grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that are often lined with fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish-brown, adding visual interest in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
