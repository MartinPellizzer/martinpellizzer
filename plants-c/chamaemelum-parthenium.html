Chamaemelum parthenium

Chamaemelum parthenium, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-white flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces tiny, spherical flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets, which are arranged in a composite flower structure. Feverfew is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
