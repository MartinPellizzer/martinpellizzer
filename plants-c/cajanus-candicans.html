Cajanus candicans

Cajanus candicans, commonly known as the evergreen pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several angular seeds, which are often used as a food source. Cajanus candicans is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
