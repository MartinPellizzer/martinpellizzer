Chenopodium brandegeeae

Chenopodium brandegeeae, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with edible leaves and seeds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually succulent, with a rough texture, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its potential as a drought-resistant crop.
