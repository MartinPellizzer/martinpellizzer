Camaridium polyphyllum

Camaridium polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features multiple stems that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Camaridium polyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
