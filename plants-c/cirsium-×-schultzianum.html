Cirsium × schultzianum

Cirsium × schultzianum, commonly known as the Schultze thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant's robust growth and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological studies.

Its botanical features contribute to its role as a potential weed in agricultural settings.
