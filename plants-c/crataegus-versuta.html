Crataegus versuta

Crataegus versuta, commonly known as the nodding hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Crataegus versuta typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.
