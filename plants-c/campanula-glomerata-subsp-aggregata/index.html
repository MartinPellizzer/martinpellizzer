Campanula Glomerata Subsp. Aggregata

Campanula glomerata subsp. aggregata is a charming member of the bellflower family known for its clusters of blue or violet flowers. This subspecies thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas across Europe. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance ornamental plant. The plant's ability to bloom reliably in late spring adds a touch of color to natural or cultivated landscapes. With its delicate appearance and hardy nature, it remains a favorite among botanists and garden enthusiasts alike.
