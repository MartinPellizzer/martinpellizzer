Capura fruticosa

Capura fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub of the Capura genus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, often growing in tropical and subtropical environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Capura fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
