Crupinastrum centaureum

Crupinastrum centaureum, commonly known as the purple cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures around its flower heads. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.

Crupinastrum centaureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
