Centaurium turcicum

Centaurium turcicum, commonly known as Turkish centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Centaurium turcicum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
