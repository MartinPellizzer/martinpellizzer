Carex sareptana

Carex sareptana, commonly known as the Sareptan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering structure consists of small, clustered spikes that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex sareptana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
