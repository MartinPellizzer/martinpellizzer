Clematis japonica var. obvallata

Clematis japonica var. obvallata, commonly known as the Japanese vine or obvallate clematis, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to climb trellises or fences.
