Cinnamomum dominii

Cinnamomum dominii, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, slightly waxy texture.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elliptical, dark brown fruits containing a single seed.
