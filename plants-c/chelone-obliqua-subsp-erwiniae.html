Chelone obliqua subsp. erwiniae

Chelone obliqua subsp.

erwiniae, commonly known as the pink mason's flower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
