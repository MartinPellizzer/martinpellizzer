Calliandra novaesii

Calliandra novaesii, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range from pink to deep red in color. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are narrow and lance-shaped.
