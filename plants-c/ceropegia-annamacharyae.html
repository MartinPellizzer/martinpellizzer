Ceropegia annamacharyae

Ceropegia annamacharyae, commonly known as the Annamacharyan Crown Vetch, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to white in color, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
