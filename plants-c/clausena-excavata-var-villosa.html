Clausena excavata var. villosa

Clausena excavata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, acidic fruits that are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
