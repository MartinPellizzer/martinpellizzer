Cyrtanthus mckenii var. cooperi

Cyrtanthus mckenii var. cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to Mediterranean-style landscapes.
