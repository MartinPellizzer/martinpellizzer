Carex albursina

Carex albursina, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in dense spikes. Native to the northeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
