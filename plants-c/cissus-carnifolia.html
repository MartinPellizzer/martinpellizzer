Cissus carnifolia

Cissus carnifolia, commonly known as the bleeding heart vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often red-veined and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus carnifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
