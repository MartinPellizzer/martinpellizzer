Croton alloeophyllus

Croton alloeophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated, waxy leaves that range from green to white and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems and dense foliage.

Croton alloeophyllus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
