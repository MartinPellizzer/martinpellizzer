Carex brevis

Carex brevis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges.

Carex brevis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
