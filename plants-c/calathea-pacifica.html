Calathea pacifica

Calathea pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific calathea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns and variegated leaf surfaces. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.
