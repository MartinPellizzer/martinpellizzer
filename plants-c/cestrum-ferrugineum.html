Cestrum ferrugineum

Cestrum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in summer. These flowers are adapted for pollination by night-flying insects such as moths.

The plant is also known for its toxicity, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
