Centropogon pamplonensis var. peruvianus

Centropogon pamplonensis var. peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
