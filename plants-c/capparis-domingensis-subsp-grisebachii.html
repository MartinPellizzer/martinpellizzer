Capparis domingensis subsp. grisebachii

Capparis domingensis subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-leaved caper, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas and the Greater Antilles. It features woody stems with thorny branches and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are usually gray-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant produces small, edible berries that are yellow when ripe.

It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sandy soils, showing adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
