Chorizotheca micrantheoides

Chorizotheca micrantheoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its flowers are usually pale green to white in color.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
