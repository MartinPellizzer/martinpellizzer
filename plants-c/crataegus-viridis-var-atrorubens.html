Crataegus viridis var. atrorubens

Crataegus viridis var. atrorubens, commonly known as the black-red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along streambanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
