Cyperus procerus var. vanderystii

Cyperus procerus var. vanderystii, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually found in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The botanical features of this variety include its narrow leaves and ability to thrive in humid, nutrient-rich soils.
