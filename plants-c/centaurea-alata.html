Centaurea alata

Centaurea alata, commonly known as the winged thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale blue to purple flowers and its spiny, winged bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea alata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
