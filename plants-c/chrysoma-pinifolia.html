Chrysoma pinifolia

Chrysoma pinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
