Chichaea

Chichaea, commonly known as the mountain beauty, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae.

These plants are native to South Africa and are characterized by their striking, often brightly colored flowers. Chichaea species typically grow in rocky or sandy soils and prefer full sun to partial shade. Their foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often variegated or spotted.

The genus includes several species, such as Chichaea flava and Chichaea violacea, which are prized for their ornamental value in gardens.
