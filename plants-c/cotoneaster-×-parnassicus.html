Cotoneaster × parnassicus

Cotoneaster × parnassicus, commonly known as the Parnassian cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features dense, rounded foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its winter interest and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster × parnassicus is also known for its adaptability to different climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
