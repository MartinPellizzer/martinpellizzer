Centaurea urvillei subsp. deinacantha

Centaurea urvillei subsp.

deinacantha, commonly known as the Urville's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and dense, spherical clusters of purple to pink bracts that resemble flowers.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by leaf-like bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
