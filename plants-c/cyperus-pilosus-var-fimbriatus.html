Cyperus pilosus var. fimbriatus

Cyperus pilosus var. fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringe flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common wetland species. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often adorned with fringe-like hairs.

The leaves are usually hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance and aiding in its identification.
