Carex valbrayi

Carex valbrayi, commonly known as the Valbray sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex valbrayi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native habitat.
