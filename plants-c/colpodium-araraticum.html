Colpodium araraticum

Colpodium araraticum, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream sides. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Colpodium araraticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
