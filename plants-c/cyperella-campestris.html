Cyperella campestris

Cyperella campestris, commonly known as field flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically form in dense clusters. The plant produces spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, and its roots system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, disturbed soils. It is commonly found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperella campestris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
