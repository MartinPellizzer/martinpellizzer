Clematis ibarensis

Clematis ibarensis, commonly known as the Ibar Valley Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Ibar Valley in Serbia.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its interest among horticulturists and botanists.
