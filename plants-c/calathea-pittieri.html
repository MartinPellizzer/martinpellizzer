Calathea pittieri

Calathea pittieri, commonly known as the "prince of Wales" calathea, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated, and often patterned foliage. The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and display intricate color patterns that change with light exposure. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea pittieri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
