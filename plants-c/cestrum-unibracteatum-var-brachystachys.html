Cestrum unibracteatum var. brachystachys

Cestrum unibracteatum var. brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that ripens in the summer months, containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
