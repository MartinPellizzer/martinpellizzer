Chenopodium platyphyllum

Chenopodium platyphyllum, commonly known as broadleaf goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Chenopodium platyphyllum is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly tart flavor and are used in traditional cuisines.
