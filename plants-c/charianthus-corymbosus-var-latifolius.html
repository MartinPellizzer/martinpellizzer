Charianthus corymbosus var. latifolius

Charianthus corymbosus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
