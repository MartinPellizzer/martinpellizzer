Cyrtorchis chailluana

Cyrtorchis chailluana, commonly known as the Chaillu's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that often feature a mix of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter, which is essential for its survival in its natural habitat.

Cyrtorchis chailluana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
