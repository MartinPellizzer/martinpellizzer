Cymbopogon flexuosus var. assamensis

Cymbopogon flexuosus var. assamensis, commonly known as Assam grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices in its native regions.
