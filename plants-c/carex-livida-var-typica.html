Carex livida var. typica

Carex livida var. typica, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flower clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex livida var. typica is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
