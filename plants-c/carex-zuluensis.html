Carex zuluensis

Carex zuluensis, commonly known as the Zulu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Carex zuluensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
