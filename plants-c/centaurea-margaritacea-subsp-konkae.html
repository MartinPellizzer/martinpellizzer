Centaurea margaritacea subsp. konkae

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

konkae, commonly known as the Konkae cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to gardens and botanical collections.
