Cacalia decurrens

Cacalia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. Cacalia decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
