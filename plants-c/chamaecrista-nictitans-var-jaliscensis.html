Chamaecrista nictitans var. jaliscensis

Chamaecrista nictitans var. jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican ticktree, is a species of shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
