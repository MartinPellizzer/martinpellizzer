Calochortus venustus var. sanguineus

Calochortus venustus var. sanguineus, commonly known as the bloodroot lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink petals and a single, prominent yellow stamen. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating.

The species is noted for its early spring blooming period, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
