Croton lundianus var. latifolius

Croton lundianus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
