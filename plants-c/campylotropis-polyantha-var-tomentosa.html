Campylotropis polyantha var. tomentosa

Campylotropis polyantha var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its softly hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its trifoliate leaves and papilionaceous flowers are typical of the Fabaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
