Crepis foetida subsp. rhoeadifolia

Crepis foetida subsp.

rhoeadifolia, commonly known as the stinkcup, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists alike.
