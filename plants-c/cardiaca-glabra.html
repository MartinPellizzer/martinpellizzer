Cardiaca glabra

Cardiaca glabra, commonly known as the smooth heartleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
