Carya cordiformis var. latifolia

Carya cordiformis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are typically harvested in the fall. Its botanical features include alternate, palmately lobed leaves and male and female flowers that appear on separate trees.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
