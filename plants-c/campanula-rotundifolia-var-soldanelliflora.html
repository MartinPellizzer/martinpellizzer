Campanula rotundifolia var. soldanelliflora

Campanula rotundifolia var. soldanelliflora, commonly known as the blue bell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are rounded and often hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
