Celtis occidentalis var. grandidentata

Celtis occidentalis var. grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed boxelder, is a variant of the boxelder tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominently lobed margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by spherical, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. Its bark is typically smooth with a light grayish-brown color, and it can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, woodland environments.
