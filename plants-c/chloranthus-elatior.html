Chloranthus elatior

Chloranthus elatior, commonly known as the greater chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
