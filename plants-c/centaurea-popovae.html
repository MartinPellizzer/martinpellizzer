Centaurea popovae

Centaurea popovae, commonly known as the Popov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea popovae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
