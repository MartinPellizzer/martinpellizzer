Carex superata

Carex superata, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex superata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
