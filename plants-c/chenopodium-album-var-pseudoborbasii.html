Chenopodium album var. pseudoborbasii

Chenopodium album var. pseudoborbasii, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a variant of the common lambsquarters.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant features serrated, spiny leaves and small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

This plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a food source.
