Collinsia parviflora var. minima

Collinsia parviflora var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf fairy bellflower, is a low-growing perennial native to the coastal regions of California.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a bluish tinge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered blooms throughout the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
