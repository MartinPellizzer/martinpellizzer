Casimiroa sapota f. comitana

Casimiroa sapota f. comitana, commonly known as the Mexican sapote, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a smooth, yellowish fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in desserts and beverages due to its unique flavor and texture.
