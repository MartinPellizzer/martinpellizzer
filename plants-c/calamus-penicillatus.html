Calamus penicillatus

Calamus penicillatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, which is typically enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in wetland gardens and conservation areas.
