Cnicus gmelinii

Cnicus gmelinii, commonly known as the common groundsel, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields. It has a taproot system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cnicus gmelinii is considered a weed in some regions but is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
