Cleome coluteoides

Cleome coluteoides, commonly known as the purple cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge.

Cleome coluteoides is often found in disturbed soils and is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
