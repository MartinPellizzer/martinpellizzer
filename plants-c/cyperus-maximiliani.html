Cyperus maximiliani

Cyperus maximiliani, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Cyperus maximiliani is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
