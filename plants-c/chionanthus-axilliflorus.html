Chionanthus axilliflorus

Chionanthus axilliflorus, commonly known as the western chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a bluish-green hue with a fuzzy texture.

The western chionanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
