Capsicum tetramerum

Capsicum tetramerum, commonly known as the four-petaled chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its unique four-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

Capsicum tetramerum is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in culinary applications.
