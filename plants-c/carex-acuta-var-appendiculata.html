Carex acuta var. appendiculata

Carex acuta var. appendiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, three-stamened flowers.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic insects and birds.
