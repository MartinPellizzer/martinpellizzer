Cassia claussenii

Cassia claussenii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, feathery inflorescences that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual display. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Cassia claussenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and tropical appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.
