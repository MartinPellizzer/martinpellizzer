Camellia

Camellia, commonly known as the camellia plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Theaceae.

It is native to eastern and southern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. Camellias are known for their showy, evergreen leaves and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. They thrive in acidic, well-drained soil and prefer partial shade to full sun, depending on the species.

Camellias are often cultivated as ornamental plants in gardens and are also valued for their tea leaves, which are used to produce green tea.
