Coelogyne banksii

Coelogyne banksii, commonly known as the Banks' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with fleshy leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms in summer, with long, narrow petals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Coelogyne banksii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
