Cyperochloa hirsuta

Cyperochloa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. Cyperochloa hirsuta thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways.

Its hairy texture and robust growth make it a common species in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
