Calamintha hispidula

Calamintha hispidula, commonly known as hairy calamintha, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive member of the mint family.

Calamintha hispidula is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
