Cissus clematidifolia

Cissus clematidifolia, commonly known as the climbing goosefoot, is a vining plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are succulent and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread easily.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, lemon-scented berries.
