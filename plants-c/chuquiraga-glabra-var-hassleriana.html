Chuquiraga glabra var. hassleriana

Chuquiraga glabra var. hassleriana, commonly known as the Hassler's chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant is shrub-like, with slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and attracting various pollinators.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
