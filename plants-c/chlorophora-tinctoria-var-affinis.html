Chlorophora tinctoria var. affinis

Chlorophora tinctoria var. affinis, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood with a fine, even texture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
