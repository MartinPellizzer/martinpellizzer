Cornus excelsa var. beyrichiana

Cornus excelsa var. beyrichiana, commonly known as the golden cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly hairy underside.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
