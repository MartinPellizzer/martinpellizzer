Cystopteris fragilis subsp. dentata

Cystopteris fragilis subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the fragrant water fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific. It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are often translucent and finely divided.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in humid forests. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive serrated or toothed edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are released into the air for dispersal.
