Cassipourea plumosa

Cassipourea plumosa, commonly known as the silky cassipourea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage, with leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Cassipourea plumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf structure.
