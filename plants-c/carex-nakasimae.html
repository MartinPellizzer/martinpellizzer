Carex nakasimae

Carex nakasimae, commonly known as the Nakasima sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typically green to brown in color, which appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex nakasimae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
