Caluera

Caluera, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of night-blooming plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Caluera is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flowering patterns.
