Cereus × fulgidus

Cereus × fulgidus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its striking nocturnal blooms.
