Cotoneaster integerrimus var. pyrenaicus

Cotoneaster integerrimus var. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the Pyrenees mountain region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
