Crotalaria rotundifolia

Crotalaria rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf cowpea, is a tropical and subtropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers with prominent, spurred petals. The plant is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also has medicinal properties in some cultures.

Crotalaria rotundifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
