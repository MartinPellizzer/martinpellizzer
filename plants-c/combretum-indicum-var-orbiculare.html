Combretum indicum var. orbiculare

Combretum indicum var. orbiculare, commonly known as the round-leaf flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to orbiculate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or yellowish coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and seasonal flowering.
