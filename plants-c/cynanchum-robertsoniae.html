Cynanchum robertsoniae

Cynanchum robertsoniae, commonly known as Robertson's milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands.

Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it unpalatable to most herbivores.
