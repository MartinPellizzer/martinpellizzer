Carex limosa var. painei

Carex limosa var. painei, commonly known as the Pain's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Carex limosa var. painei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
