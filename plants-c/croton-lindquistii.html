Croton lindquistii

Croton lindquistii, commonly known as the Lindquist's croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often brightly colored. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules.

Croton lindquistii is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid environments.
