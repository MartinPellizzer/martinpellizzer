Chamaecrista fodinarum

Chamaecrista fodinarum, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
