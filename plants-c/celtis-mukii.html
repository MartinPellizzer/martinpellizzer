Celtis mukii

Celtis mukii, commonly known as the African boxthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Celtis mukii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood.
