Crotalaria acicularis

Crotalaria acicularis, commonly known as the needlepod cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, needle-like pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred shape, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds.

Crotalaria acicularis is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in agricultural systems for soil improvement.
