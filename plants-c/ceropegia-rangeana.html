Ceropegia rangeana

Ceropegia rangeana, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia rangeana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
