Cryptocarya elegans

Cryptocarya elegans, commonly known as the slender cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Cryptocarya elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
