Carex rigida f. drejeriana

Carex rigida f. drejeriana, commonly known as the rigid sedge, is a variant of the rigid sedge species native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and upright, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, fens, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive, spike-like inflorescence that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
