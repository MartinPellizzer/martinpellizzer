Cissus curvipoda

Cissus curvipoda, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a vigorous trailing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Cissus curvipoda is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
