Cyperus brachiatus

Cyperus brachiatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or brown in color. Cyperus brachiatus is characterized by its upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
