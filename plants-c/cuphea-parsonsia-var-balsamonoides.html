Cuphea parsonsia var. balsamonoides

Cuphea parsonsia var. balsamonoides, commonly known as the Balsamodendron cuphea, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
