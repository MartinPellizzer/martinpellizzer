Chassalia tsaratanensis

Chassalia tsaratanensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana chassalia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers arranged in a composite head. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in montane forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
