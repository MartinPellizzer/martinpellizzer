Campanula rotundifolia var. alcoiana

Campanula rotundifolia var. alcoiana, commonly known as the Alcoa bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its round, lobed leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Campanulaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures.
