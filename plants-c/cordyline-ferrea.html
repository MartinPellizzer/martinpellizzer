Cordyline ferrea

Cordyline ferrea, commonly known as the red cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often bright red or purple in color. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its unique foliage makes it a standout feature in both indoor and outdoor settings.
