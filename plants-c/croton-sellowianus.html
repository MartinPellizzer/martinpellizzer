Croton sellowianus

Croton sellowianus, commonly known as the Sellow's croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
