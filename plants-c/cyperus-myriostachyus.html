Cyperus myriostachyus

Cyperus myriostachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The botanical features of Cyperus myriostachyus make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
