Cryophytum suaveolens

Cryophytum suaveolens, commonly known as the fragrant snowplant, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
