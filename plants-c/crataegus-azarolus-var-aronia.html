Crataegus azarolus var. aronia

Crataegus azarolus var. aronia, commonly known as the blackthorn or mackerel tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is typically gray with a rough texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
