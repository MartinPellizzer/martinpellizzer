Carex flaccosperma var. glaucodea

Carex flaccosperma var. glaucodea, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are usually a bluish-green color, giving it its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early to mid-summer.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
