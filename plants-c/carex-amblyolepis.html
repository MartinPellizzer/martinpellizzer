Carex amblyolepis

Carex amblyolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges.

Carex amblyolepis is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
