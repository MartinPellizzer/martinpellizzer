Cyperus viscosus

Cyperus viscosus, commonly known as sticky flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus viscosus is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for wetland habitats.
