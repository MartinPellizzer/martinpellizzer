Centaurea hybrida var. rodiei

Centaurea hybrida var. rodiei, commonly known as the Rodiei's cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces achenes as its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
