Conchium epiglottis

Conchium epiglottis, commonly known as the epiglottic conch, is a specialized anatomical structure found in certain amphibians, particularly in the larynx.

It is a cartilaginous or bony projection that helps in directing air and food into the appropriate pathways. The epiglottic conch plays a role in preventing food from entering the trachea during swallowing. Its shape and position vary among species, reflecting adaptations to different feeding and respiratory behaviors.

This structure is less commonly discussed in botanical contexts, as it is primarily an anatomical feature rather than a plant.
