Carex arida

Carex arida, commonly known as the desert sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Carex genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Carex arida plays a role in stabilizing soils in dry environments and provides habitat for various small organisms.
