Calamus bifacialis

Calamus bifacialis, commonly known as the two-faced reed, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically green on one side and lighter on the other. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or white, followed by fibrous, palm-like fruits. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in Southeast Asia.

Due to its unique leaf coloration and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
