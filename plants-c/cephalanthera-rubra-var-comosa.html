Cephalanthera rubra var. comosa

Cephalanthera rubra var. comosa, commonly known as the hairy pink lady's slipper, is a striking orchid with unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that are adorned with a hairy, pouch-like structure called a labellum. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often forming a basal rosette.

This variety is notable for its delicate appearance and specific ecological requirements.
