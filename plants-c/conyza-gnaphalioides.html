Conyza gnaphalioides

Conyza gnaphalioides, commonly known as common ironweed, is an annual flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and serrated, alternate leaves. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late summer, producing copious amounts of wind-pollinated seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain areas.
