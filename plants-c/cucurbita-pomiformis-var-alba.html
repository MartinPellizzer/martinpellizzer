Cucurbita pomiformis var. alba

Cucurbita pomiformis var. alba, commonly known as the white pumpkin, is a variety of the genus Cucurbita native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, round, white to pale yellow fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant is a vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are typically separate from the female flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is used in various culinary and ornamental applications due to its unique appearance and mild flavor.
