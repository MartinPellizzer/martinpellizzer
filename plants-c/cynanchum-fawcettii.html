Cynanchum fawcettii

Cynanchum fawcettii, commonly known as Fawcett's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its milky sap, which is toxic, is a notable botanical feature.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
