Canarium papuanum

Canarium papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan candlenut, is a tropical tree native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea and the Moluccas.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while the tree's wood is valued for its durability.

Canarium papuanum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
