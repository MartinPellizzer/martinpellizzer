Carex volkensii

Carex volkensii, commonly known as the East African sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Carex volkensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
