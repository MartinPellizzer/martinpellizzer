Chirita barbata

Chirita barbata, commonly known as the hairy chirita, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

The flowers, which range in color from red to orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
