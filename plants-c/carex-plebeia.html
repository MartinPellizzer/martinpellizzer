Carex plebeia

Carex plebeia, commonly known as common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating.

Carex plebeia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
