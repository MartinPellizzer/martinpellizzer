Campanulorchis pseudoleiophylla

Campanulorchis pseudoleiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bell orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers, which are typically pale green to yellowish in color, adapted for specific pollinators. The plant exhibits a sympodial growth habit, with its roots forming a dense network that helps it survive in its often humid and shaded habitats. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in its native environment.
