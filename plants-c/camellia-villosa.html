Camellia villosa

Camellia villosa, commonly known as the hairy camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are alternate, leathery, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Camellia villosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
