Citrus × javanica

Citrus × javanica, commonly known as the Javanese lime or kaffir lime, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a bumpy, green exterior and a strong, aromatic scent. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic zest and juice, commonly used in cooking and traditional medicine.
