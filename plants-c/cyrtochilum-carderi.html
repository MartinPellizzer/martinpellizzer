Cyrtochilum carderi

Cyrtochilum carderi, commonly known as the Carder's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. Cyrtochilum carderi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
