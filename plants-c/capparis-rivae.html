Capparis rivae

Capparis rivae, commonly known as the river caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in umbels, and its leaves are typically alternate and oval in shape. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
