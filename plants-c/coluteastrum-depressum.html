Coluteastrum depressum

Coluteastrum depressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
