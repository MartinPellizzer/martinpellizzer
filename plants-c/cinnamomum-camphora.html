Cinnamomum camphora

Cinnamomum camphora, commonly known as the camphor laurel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds. Its leaves and bark contain essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
