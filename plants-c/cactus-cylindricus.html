Cactus cylindricus

Cactus cylindricus, commonly known as the fishhook cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its adaptations include a thick, water-storing stem and reduced leaves, which help it survive in harsh, dry environments.

The fishhook cactus is often used in traditional medicine and is also popular as a decorative plant in arid climates.
