Carex hectorii

Carex hectorii, commonly known as Hector's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Hector's sedge plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
