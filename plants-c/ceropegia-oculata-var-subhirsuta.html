Ceropegia oculata var. subhirsuta

Ceropegia oculata var. subhirsuta, commonly known as the velvet leaf or silver vine, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
