Cyperus balfourii

Cyperus balfourii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has grass-like leaves that are linear and tapering, with a distinctive cylindrical stem. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus balfourii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
