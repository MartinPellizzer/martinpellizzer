Carduus lappoides

Carduus lappoides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
