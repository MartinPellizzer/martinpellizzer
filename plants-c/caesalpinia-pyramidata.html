Caesalpinia pyramidata

Caesalpinia pyramidata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, pyramid-shaped clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in long, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.
