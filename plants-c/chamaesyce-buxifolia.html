Chamaesyce buxifolia

Chamaesyce buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to grayish hairs, giving them a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by tiny, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can become a nuisance in natural habitats if not managed carefully.
