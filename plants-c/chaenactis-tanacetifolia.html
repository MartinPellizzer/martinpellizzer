Chaenactis tanacetifolia

Chaenactis tanacetifolia, commonly known as the Alpine Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

Chaenactis tanacetifolia is often found in open meadows and is an important part of the alpine ecosystem.
