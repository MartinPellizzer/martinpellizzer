Cremolobus pinnatifidus var. integrifolius

Cremolobus pinnatifidus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved cremolobus, is a unique variant of the cremolobus species characterized by its large, lobed leaves.

The leaves are typically pinnatifid, meaning they are deeply divided into narrow segments, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered together, and it is valued for its ornamental foliage rather than its blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and requires careful cultivation.
