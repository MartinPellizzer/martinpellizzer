Cyperus pennatus var. armstrongii

Cyperus pennatus var. armstrongii, commonly known as the Armstrong's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
