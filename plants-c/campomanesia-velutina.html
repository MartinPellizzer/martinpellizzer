Campomanesia velutina

Campomanesia velutina, commonly known as the velvet apple or Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries with a sweet, honey-like flavor.

Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
