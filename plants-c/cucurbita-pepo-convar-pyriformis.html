Cucurbita pepo convar. pyriformis

Cucurbita pepo convar. pyriformis, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, pear-shaped fruits with a rough, orange to deep yellow skin. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbita genus. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and are widely cultivated for culinary use.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Native American agriculture.
