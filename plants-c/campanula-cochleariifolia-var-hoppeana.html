Campanula cochleariifolia var. hoppeana

Campanula cochleariifolia var. hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
