Chloryllis

Chloryllis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Chloryllis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
