Campanula tyrolensis

Campanula tyrolensis, commonly known as the Tyrol bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula tyrolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
