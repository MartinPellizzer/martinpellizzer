Carex livida var. grayana

Carex livida var. grayana, commonly known as gray's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable environments.
