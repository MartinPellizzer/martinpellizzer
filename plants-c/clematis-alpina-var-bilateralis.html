Clematis alpina var. bilateralis

Clematis alpina var. bilateralis, commonly known as the alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white or pale blue blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
