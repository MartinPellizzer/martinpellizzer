Calceolaria bicrenata

Calceolaria bicrenata, commonly known as the two-crown calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Calceolaria bicrenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, colorful blooms.
