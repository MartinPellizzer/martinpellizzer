Chloris uliginosa

Chloris uliginosa, commonly known as purple mat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, purple or pinkish flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or poorly drained soils, often found in swampy areas or along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more pronounced in dry conditions.

Chloris uliginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
