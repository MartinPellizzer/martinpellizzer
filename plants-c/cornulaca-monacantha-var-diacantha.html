Cornulaca monacantha var. diacantha

Cornulaca monacantha var. diacantha, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has succulent, fleshy leaves that are used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and are often consumed cooked or raw.

It is also valued for its ability to survive in harsh environments, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
