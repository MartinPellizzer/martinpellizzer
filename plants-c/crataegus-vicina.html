Crataegus vicina

Crataegus vicina, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features thorny branches and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red or orange-pink berry that ripens in the fall.

Crataegus vicina is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
