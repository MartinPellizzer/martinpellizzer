Cinnamomum peruvianum

Cinnamomum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian cinnamom, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for their essential oils.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
