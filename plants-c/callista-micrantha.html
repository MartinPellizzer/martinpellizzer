Callista micrantha

Callista micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered callista, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and open woodlands.

The flowers are borne in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
