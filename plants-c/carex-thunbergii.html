Carex thunbergii

Carex thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

Carex thunbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
