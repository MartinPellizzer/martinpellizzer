Cycas siamensis

Cycas siamensis, commonly known as the Siam cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with the female cones being larger and more elongated than the male ones. Cycas siamensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and conservation efforts.
