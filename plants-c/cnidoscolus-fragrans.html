Cnidoscolus fragrans

Cnidoscolus fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant stinging leaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its stems and leaves contain irritant compounds, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Despite its defensive properties, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
