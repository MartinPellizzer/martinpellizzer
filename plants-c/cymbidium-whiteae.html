Cymbidium whiteae

Cymbidium whiteae, commonly known as the White's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Cymbidium whiteae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
