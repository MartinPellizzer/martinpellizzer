Carex × treverica

Carex × treverica, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, often dark green leaves arranged in dense, grass-like clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are borne on cylindrical inflorescences. Carex × treverica is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ability to thrive in wet environments makes it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
