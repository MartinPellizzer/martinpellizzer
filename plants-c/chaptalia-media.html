Chaptalia media

Chaptalia media, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Chaptalia media produces small purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
