Commelina cardiosepala

Commelina cardiosepala, commonly known as the heart-sepal commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, clustered flowers with three sepals and two petals. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
