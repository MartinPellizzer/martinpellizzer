Centaurea micrantha var. fastigiata

Centaurea micrantha var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched, with linear, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically pale blue to purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
