Crassula filamentosa

Crassula filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula filamentosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
