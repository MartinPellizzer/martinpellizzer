Cavendishia endresii

Cavendishia endresii, commonly known as the Endresii orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces pendant, spurred flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cavendishia endresii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
