Crotalaria peschiana

Crotalaria peschiana, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a spurred petal, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate and often trifoliate, with a hairy texture.

Crotalaria peschiana is sometimes used in ecological restoration due to its ability to improve soil fertility.
