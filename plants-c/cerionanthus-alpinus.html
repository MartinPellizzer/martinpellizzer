Cerionanthus alpinus

Cerionanthus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

Cerionanthus alpinus thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats.
