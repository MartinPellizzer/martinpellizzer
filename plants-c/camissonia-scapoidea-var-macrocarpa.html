Camissonia scapoidea var. macrocarpa

Camissonia scapoidea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a dry, segmented capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
