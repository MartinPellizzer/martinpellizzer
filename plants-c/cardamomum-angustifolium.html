Cardamomum angustifolium

Cardamomum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leafed cardamom, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which eventually give way to aromatic, dark brown pods containing numerous seeds. Its botanical classification places it in the family Zingiberaceae, which includes other economically important spices like ginger and turmeric.

The plant's unique scent and flavor make it a valuable ingredient in both culinary and medicinal applications.
