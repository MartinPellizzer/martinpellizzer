Campanula rotundifolia subsp. euxina

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

euxina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
