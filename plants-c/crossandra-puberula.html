Crossandra puberula

Crossandra puberula, commonly known as the velvet flower or hairy crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a velvety appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Crossandra puberula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
