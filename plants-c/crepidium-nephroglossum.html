Crepidium nephroglossum

Crepidium nephroglossum, commonly known as the kidney-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, kidney-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within the orchid family.
