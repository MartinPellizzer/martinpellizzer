Cacoucia chinensis

Cacoucia chinensis, commonly known as Chinese cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its pods contain edible seeds that are widely used in Asian cuisines.

Cacoucia chinensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
