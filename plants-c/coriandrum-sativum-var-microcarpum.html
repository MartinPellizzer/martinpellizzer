Coriandrum sativum var. microcarpum

Coriandrum sativum var. microcarpum, commonly known as dwarf coriander, is a variant of the coriander plant characterized by its smaller, more compact growth habit.

It produces small, spherical fruits known as seeds, which are typically harvested before they fully mature. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is aromatic, with a distinct licorice-like flavor, making it suitable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible parts.
