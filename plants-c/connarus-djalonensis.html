Connarus djalonensis

Connarus djalonensis, commonly known as the West African ironwood tree, is a member of the Rhamnaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture with prominent veins. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This plant is native to tropical Africa and is adapted to dry, rocky soils.
