Capparis brevipes

Capparis brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, characterized by its small, thorny stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native habitat.

Capparis brevipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
