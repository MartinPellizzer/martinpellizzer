Colchicum psammophilum

Colchicum psammophilum, commonly known as the sandhill colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsules that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
