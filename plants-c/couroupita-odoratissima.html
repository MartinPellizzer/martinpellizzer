Couroupita odoratissima

Couroupita odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, sweet aroma. The tree produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically large and glossy. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
