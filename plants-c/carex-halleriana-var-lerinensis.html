Carex halleriana var. lerinensis

Carex halleriana var. lerinensis, commonly known as the Lerins sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds. This variant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
