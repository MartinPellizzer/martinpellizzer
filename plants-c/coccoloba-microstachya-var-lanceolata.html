Coccoloba microstachya var. lanceolata

Coccoloba microstachya var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf coralbean, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, glossy green leaves with a waxy surface that reflects light, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for coastal and sandy soils.
