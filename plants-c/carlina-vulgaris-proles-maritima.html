Carlina vulgaris proles maritima

Carlina vulgaris proles maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its structure is adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray, making it a hardy coastal species.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
