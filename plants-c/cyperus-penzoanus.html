Cyperus penzoanus

Cyperus penzoanus, commonly known as the Amazonian flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus penzoanus is characterized by its small, triangular leaves and produces spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.
