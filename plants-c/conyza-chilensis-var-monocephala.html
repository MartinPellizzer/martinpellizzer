Conyza chilensis var. monocephala

Conyza chilensis var. monocephala, commonly known as the Chilean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
