Cupressus nootkatensis var. pendula

Cupressus nootkatensis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Sitka cedar, is a variant of the Sitka cedar native to Alaska and western Canada.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, typical of the cypress family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
