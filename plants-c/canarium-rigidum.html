Canarium rigidum

Canarium rigidum, commonly known as the rigid candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. The seeds are used for oil extraction and have traditional applications in various cultures.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
