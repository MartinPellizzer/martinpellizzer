Chamaecytisus leucotrichus

Chamaecytisus leucotrichus, commonly known as the silver broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has silvery-white hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden planting, though it can be invasive in some regions.
