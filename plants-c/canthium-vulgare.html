Canthium vulgare

Canthium vulgare, commonly known as the common spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in sticky, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Canthium vulgare is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and various ecological roles.
