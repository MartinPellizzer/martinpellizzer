Chamira cornuta

Chamira cornuta, commonly known as the horned chamira, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of South America.

It features a cylindrical stem with a distinctive horn-like protrusion at the top, which gives it its common name. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months.

Chamira cornuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
