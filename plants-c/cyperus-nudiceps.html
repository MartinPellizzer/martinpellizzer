Cyperus nudiceps

Cyperus nudiceps, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along water edges or in flooded soils. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in water gardens.
