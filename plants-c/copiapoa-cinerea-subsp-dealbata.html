Copiapoa cinerea subsp. dealbata

Copiapoa cinerea subsp.

dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered copiapoa, is a succulent cactus native to northern Chile. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered with white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces pale yellow to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
