Caprifolium conjugiale

Caprifolium conjugiale, commonly known as the clustered honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in the fall and are edible for both humans and wildlife.

Caprifolium conjugiale is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
