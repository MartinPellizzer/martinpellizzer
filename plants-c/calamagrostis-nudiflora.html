Calamagrostis nudiflora

Calamagrostis nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually leafless, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis nudiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
