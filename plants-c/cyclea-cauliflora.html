Cyclea cauliflora

Cyclea cauliflora, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, white to purple flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is known for its toxic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
