Crataegus neobaxteri

Crataegus neobaxteri, commonly known as the Neobaxter hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern China, specifically the Yunnan province, where it grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in the fall, providing food for various wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
