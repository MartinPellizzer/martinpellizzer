Cota macrantha var. vitellina

Cota macrantha var. vitellina, commonly known as the yellow cota, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is often found in disturbed soils. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
