Crataegus monogyna f. flore-roseo-pleno

Crataegus monogyna f. flore-roseo-pleno, commonly known as the rose-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its showy, double pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a small to medium-sized shrub with thorny branches and dark green leaves. It produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used for ornamental purposes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its role in supporting local wildlife.
