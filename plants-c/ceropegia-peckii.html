Ceropegia peckii

Ceropegia peckii, commonly known as the Peck's African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
