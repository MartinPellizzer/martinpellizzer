Cousinia platyptera

Cousinia platyptera, commonly known as the flat-leaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and flat, with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Cousinia platyptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens for its unique foliage and modest blooms.
