Corymbium cymosum

Corymbium cymosum, commonly known as the clustered everlasting or everlastings, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense inflorescences of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain intact for extended periods, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Corymbium cymosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its aesthetic and hardy nature.
