Caprifolium hypoleucum

Caprifolium hypoleucum, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
