Crataegus aestivalis f. luculenta

Crataegus aestivalis f. luculenta, commonly known as the early summer hawthorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in late summer. It is native to the northeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
