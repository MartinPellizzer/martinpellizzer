Celastrus mauritianus

Celastrus mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
