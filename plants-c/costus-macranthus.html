Costus macranthus

Costus macranthus, commonly known as the broad-leaved costus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, spadix-like inflorescences are surrounded by bright yellow bracts, making it visually striking.

Costus macranthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
