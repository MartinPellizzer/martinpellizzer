Carduus latisquamus

Carduus latisquamus, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a rosette of spiny, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

Carduus latisquamus is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils, and it has been used historically in traditional medicine.
