Chrysochosma schaffneri var. nealleyi

Chrysochosma schaffneri var. nealleyi, commonly known as the Nealley's goldenbush, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
