Calcatrippa

Calcatrippa, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, prickly texture.

Calcatrippa plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
