Carex × montanoaltaica

Carex × montanoaltaica, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to high-altitude regions of the Alps, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is often used in ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
