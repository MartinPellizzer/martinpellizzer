Campanula pusilla var. stenocodon

Campanula pusilla var. stenocodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
