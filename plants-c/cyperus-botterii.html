Cyperus botterii

Cyperus botterii, commonly known as the African sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its small, triangular leaves and inconspicuous flowers are adapted for efficient water propagation and survival in aquatic environments.

Cyperus botterii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
