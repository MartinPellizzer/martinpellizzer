Crataegus oxyacantha eriocarpa

Crataegus oxyacantha eriocarpa, commonly known as the hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
