Campanula pusilla var. jaubertiana

Campanula pusilla var. jaubertiana, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
