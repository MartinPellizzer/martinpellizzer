Centaurea panormitana subsp. todaroi

Centaurea panormitana subsp.

todaroi, commonly known as the Maltese star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Malta and parts of Sicily. It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers, which are arranged in dense, spherical heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are upright with spiny ridges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
