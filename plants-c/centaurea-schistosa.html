Centaurea schistosa

Centaurea schistosa, commonly known as the rock-centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Centaurea schistosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
