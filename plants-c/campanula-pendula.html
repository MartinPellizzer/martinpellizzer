Campanula pendula

Campanula pendula, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that hang from long, slender stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Campanula pendula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
