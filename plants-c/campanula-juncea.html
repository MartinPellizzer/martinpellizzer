Campanula juncea

Campanula juncea, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula juncea is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
