Cousinia gigantoptera

Cousinia gigantoptera, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Cousinia gigantoptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun.
