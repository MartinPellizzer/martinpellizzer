Cissus woodii

Cissus woodii, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Cissus woodii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
