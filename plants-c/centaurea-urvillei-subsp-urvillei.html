Centaurea urvillei subsp. urvillei

Centaurea urvillei subsp.

urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
