Capsella bursa-pastoris lenaensis

Capsella bursa-pastoris lenaensis, commonly known as the common chickweed or mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with small, rounded leaves and white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in gardens. Its fruit is a hairy, bladder-like capsule, which gives it the common name "bursa pastoris," meaning "shepherd's bag.".

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
