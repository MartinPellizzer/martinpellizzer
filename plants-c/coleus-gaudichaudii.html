Coleus gaudichaudii

Coleus gaudichaudii, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to orange and yellow, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Coleus gaudichaudii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and is used in landscaping and garden design.
