Carex colchica subsp. ligerica

Carex colchica subsp.

ligerica, commonly known as the Ligerica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or streamside habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
