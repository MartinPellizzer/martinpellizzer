Cirsium edule

Cirsium edule, commonly known as the edible thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves, when cooked, are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, while the flowers can be used in culinary applications.

Cirsium edule is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive issues and inflammation.
