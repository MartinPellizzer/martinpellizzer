Carex argyrantha var. aenea

Carex argyrantha var. aenea, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that have a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
