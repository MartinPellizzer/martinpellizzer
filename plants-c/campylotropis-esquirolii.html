Campylotropis esquirolii

Campylotropis esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's climbing bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and cylindrical flower clusters. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
