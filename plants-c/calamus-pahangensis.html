Calamus pahangensis

Calamus pahangensis, commonly known as the Pahang calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in wet, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by wind.

Calamus pahangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some applications in local medicine and handicraft industries.
