Cousinia persarum

Cousinia persarum, commonly known as Persian cushion grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage with hairy leaves, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across harsh landscapes.

Cousinia persarum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
