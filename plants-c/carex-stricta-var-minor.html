Carex stricta var. minor

Carex stricta var. minor, commonly known as slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
