Coelogyne hookeriana

Coelogyne hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

Coelogyne hookeriana is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
