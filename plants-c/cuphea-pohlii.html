Cuphea pohlii

Cuphea pohlii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Cuphea pohlii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add vibrant color to landscapes.
