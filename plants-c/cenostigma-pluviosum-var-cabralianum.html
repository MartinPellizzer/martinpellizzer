Cenostigma pluviosum var. cabralianum

Cenostigma pluviosum var. cabralianum, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
