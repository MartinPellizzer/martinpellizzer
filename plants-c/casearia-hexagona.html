Casearia hexagona

Casearia hexagona, commonly known as the hexagon leaf casearia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that often have a distinctive hexagonal shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
