Calytrix simplex subsp. simplex

Calytrix simplex subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the silky tea-tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across its native range.
