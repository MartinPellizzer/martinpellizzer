Calluna vulgaris subf. pendula

Calluna vulgaris subf.

pendula, commonly known as the weeping heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

This subshrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
