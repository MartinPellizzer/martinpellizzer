Cyclamen persicum var. cristatum

Cyclamen persicum var. cristatum, commonly known as the crinkled cyclamen, is a unique variant of the Persian cyclamen characterized by its distinctive, ruffled foliage.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a spurred petal that adds to its ornamental appeal. The crinkled leaves are a result of a genetic mutation that causes the leaves to curl and form a crinkled pattern.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for indoor or outdoor use during the winter months.
