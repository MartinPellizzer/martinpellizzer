Careya valida

Careya valida, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
