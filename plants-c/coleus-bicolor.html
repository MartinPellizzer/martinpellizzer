Coleus bicolor

Coleus bicolor, commonly known as the spotted coleus, is a vibrant tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a variety of red, purple, and green patterns. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often used in floral arrangements.

Coleus bicolor thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.
