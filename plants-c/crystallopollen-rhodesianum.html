Crystallopollen rhodesianum

Crystallopollen rhodesianum, commonly known as the Rhodesian crystal pollen plant, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly Zimbabwe and Mozambique, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, translucent, and often colorful pollen grains, which are a key feature in its classification. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a delicate, orchid-like appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
