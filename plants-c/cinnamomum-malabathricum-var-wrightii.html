Cinnamomum malabathricum var. wrightii

Cinnamomum malabathricum var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's cinnamonwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
