Ceratonia siliqua var. siccata

Ceratonia siliqua var. siccata, commonly known as the Aleppo date, is a variety of the carob tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. The plant thrives in dry, arid climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The Aleppo date is valued not only for its fruit but also for its historical and cultural significance in traditional agriculture.
