Cephalangraecum braunii

Cephalangraecum braunii, commonly known as the Braun's star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
