Cousinia eurylepis

Cousinia eurylepis, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia eurylepis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
