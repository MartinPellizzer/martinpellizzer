Copiapoa esmeraldana

Copiapoa esmeraldana, commonly known as the Emerald Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of bright green, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in arid conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
