Cytisus ruthenicus var. zingeri

Cytisus ruthenicus var. zingeri, commonly known as the Zinger's broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs. This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal floral display.
