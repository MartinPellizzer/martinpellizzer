Carex psilostachya

Carex psilostachya, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green color.

Carex psilostachya is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
