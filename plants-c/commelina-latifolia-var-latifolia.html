Commelina latifolia var. latifolia

Commelina latifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking blue-violet bracts that enclose its small, white flowers. The plant has slender, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.
