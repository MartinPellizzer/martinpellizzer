Clematis lasiantha subsp. pseudoligusticifolia

Clematis lasiantha subsp.

pseudoligusticifolia, commonly known as the fragrant clematis, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often with a finely toothed margin.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and twining, allowing it to climb and support itself on structures.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
