Cleomella perennis

Cleomella perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant is perennial, meaning it lives for more than two years, and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture.

Cleomella perennis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
