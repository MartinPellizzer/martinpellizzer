Caroxylon indicum

Caroxylon indicum, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to southern Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit, which resembles a date, is edible and has been used for food and medicinal purposes for centuries.

The plant also plays a significant role in traditional horticulture and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
