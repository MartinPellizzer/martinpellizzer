Chenopodium rubricaule

Chenopodium rubricaule, commonly known as redstem goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features succulent, reddish stems that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name. The leaves are ovate to deltoid in shape, with a rough, sandpapery texture and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
