Cephaloschefflera crassissima

Cephaloschefflera crassissima, commonly known as the broad-leafed umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like canopy of broad, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
