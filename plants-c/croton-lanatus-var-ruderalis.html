Croton lanatus var. ruderalis

Croton lanatus var. ruderalis, commonly known as the rough croton, is a variant of the croton plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and branching stems, often growing in disturbed or ruderal habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its latex sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It is often found in open areas such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
