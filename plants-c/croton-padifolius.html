Croton padifolius

Croton padifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often bright green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
