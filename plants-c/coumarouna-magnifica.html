Coumarouna magnifica

Coumarouna magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent coumarouna, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
