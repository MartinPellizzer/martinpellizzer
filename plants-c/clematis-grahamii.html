Clematis grahamii

Clematis grahamii, commonly known as Graham's clematis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Clematis grahamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
