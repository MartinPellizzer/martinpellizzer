Cyperus marlothii

Cyperus marlothii, commonly known as the African sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense clumps with slender, triangular stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Cyperus marlothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
