Conchocarpus cauliflorus

Conchocarpus cauliflorus, commonly known as the calico tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant produces striking, colorful fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.

The calico tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
