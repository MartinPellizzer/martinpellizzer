Campanula hongii

Campanula hongii, commonly known as Hong's bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Campanula hongii is also noted for its ability to thrive in woodland settings, making it a suitable choice for naturalized landscapes.
