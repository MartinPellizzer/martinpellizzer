Cestrum calycosum

Cestrum calycosum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Cestrum calycosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blooms.
