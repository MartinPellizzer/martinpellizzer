Cystopteris fragilis var. regularis

Cystopteris fragilis var. regularis, commonly known as the brittle shield fern, is a delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, fragile fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant produces small, round spore cases called sori on the underside of its leaves. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, airy appearance.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
