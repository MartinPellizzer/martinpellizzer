Cattleya × johnstoniana

Cattleya × johnstoniana, commonly known as the Johnston's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from various Cattleya species.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, often with intricate patterns. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
