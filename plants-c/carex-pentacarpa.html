Carex pentacarpa

Carex pentacarpa, commonly known as the five-fruit sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are wind-pollinated, and its fruiting structures are distinctive, consisting of five to six elongated, spiky capsules.
