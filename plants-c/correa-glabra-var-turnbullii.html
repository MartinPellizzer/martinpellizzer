Correa glabra var. turnbullii

Correa glabra var. turnbullii, commonly known as Turnbull's correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
