Cecropia multisecta

Cecropia multisecta, commonly known as the jaguar's tears, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of woody, three-lobed fruits.
