Crepidium laeve

Crepidium laeve, commonly known as the smooth lady's-tresses, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, branching stem with numerous small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a loose, loose spiral. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often emerging from the base of the plant.

Crepidium laeve is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
