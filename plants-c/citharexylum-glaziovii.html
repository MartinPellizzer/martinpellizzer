Citharexylum glaziovii

Citharexylum glaziovii, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and may exude a mild fragrance when crushed.
