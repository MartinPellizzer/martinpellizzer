Crocus ruksansii

Crocus ruksansii, commonly known as the Karst crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, mountainous environments.

Crocus ruksansii blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
