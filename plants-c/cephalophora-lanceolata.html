Cephalophora lanceolata

Cephalophora lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate headplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
