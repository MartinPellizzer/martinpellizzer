Calamintha villosa

Calamintha villosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Calamintha villosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing and healing properties.
