Cylindropuntia fulgida

Cylindropuntia fulgida, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and bright yellow spines, which give it its common name. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

The cactus is well adapted to desert conditions, with a shallow root system and a waxy coating to reduce water loss.
