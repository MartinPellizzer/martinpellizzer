Chromolaena suratensis

Chromolaena suratensis, commonly known as Surat weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough texture and often bear small, white or pale green spots.

Chromolaena suratensis is known for its rapid growth and is considered an invasive species in many areas where it is introduced.
