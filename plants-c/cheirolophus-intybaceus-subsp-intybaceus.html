Cheirolophus intybaceus subsp. intybaceus

Cheirolophus intybaceus subsp.

intybaceus, commonly known as the Tuscan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
