Clematis orientalis subvar. thomsonii

Clematis orientalis subvar. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's oriental clematis, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale blue, often with a yellow center. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically thorny. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively easy maintenance.
