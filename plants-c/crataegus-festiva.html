Crataegus festiva

Crataegus festiva, commonly known as the festive hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, compound leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing food for birds.
