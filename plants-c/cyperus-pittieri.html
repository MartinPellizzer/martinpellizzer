Cyperus pittieri

Cyperus pittieri, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in wetland and marshy habitats. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Cyperus pittieri is often found in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common species in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
