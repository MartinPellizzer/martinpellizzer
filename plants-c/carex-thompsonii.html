Carex thompsonii

Carex thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Thompson's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
