Croton nitidulus var. cinereus

Croton nitidulus var. cinereus, commonly known as the silver croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and silver. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
