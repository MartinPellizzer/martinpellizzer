Cissampelos pareira var. orbiculata

Cissampelos pareira var. orbiculata, commonly known as the broadleaf moonvine, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that are borne in clusters along the stems. Its fruits are round, woody capsules that split open to release small, flat seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
