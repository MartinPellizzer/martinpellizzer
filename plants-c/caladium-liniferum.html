Caladium liniferum

Caladium liniferum, commonly known as the red-veined caladium, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, brightly colored leaves that often display vibrant red, pink, and white veins against a lush green background. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Caladium liniferum thrives in warm, humid conditions and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, as it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.
