Carduus tuberosus

Carduus tuberosus, commonly known as artichoke thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, spiny fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its root system is extensive and can reach deep into the soil, making it a hardy and resilient species.

The plant is cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of medicinal compounds.
