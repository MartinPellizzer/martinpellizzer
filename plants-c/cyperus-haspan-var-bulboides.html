Cyperus haspan var. bulboides

Cyperus haspan var. bulboides, commonly known as the bulbous flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among its foliage. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common species in marshy or aquatic environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
