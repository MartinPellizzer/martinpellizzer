Crotalaria assamica var. keralensis

Crotalaria assamica var. keralensis, commonly known as Keralan crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in the wetlands and marshy areas of Kerala.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive crown-like structure.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
