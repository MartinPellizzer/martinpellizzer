Comanthosphace hakonensis

Comanthosphace hakonensis, commonly known as the deep-sea green alga, is a unicellular organism belonging to the class Chlorophyceae.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in extreme marine environments, including high-pressure and low-light conditions. The plant possesses a simple morphology with a single nucleus and chloroplasts that contain chlorophyll a and c. It plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to primary productivity.
