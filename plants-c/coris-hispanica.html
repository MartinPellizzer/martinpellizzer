Coris hispanica

Coris hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish coris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it is valued for its medicinal properties.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
