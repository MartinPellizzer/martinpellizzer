Cirsium diversifolium

Cirsium diversifolium, commonly known as the diverse-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a variety of leaf shapes, contributing to its common name.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
