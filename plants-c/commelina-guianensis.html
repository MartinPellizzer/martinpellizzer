Commelina guianensis

Commelina guianensis, commonly known as the giant spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are slender and can climb or trail along the ground.

Commelina guianensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
