Centaurea ispahanica subsp. macrocarpa

Centaurea ispahanica subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the Persian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
