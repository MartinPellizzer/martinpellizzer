Chamitea

Chamitea, commonly known as the marsh hawksbeard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and is often found in wetland habitats. The plant has linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces achenes as its fruit. Chamitea is typically found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
