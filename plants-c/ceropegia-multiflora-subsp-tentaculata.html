Ceropegia multiflora subsp. tentaculata

Ceropegia multiflora subsp.

tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, trailing stems that often bear small, tubular flowers with a distinctive hooded structure.

The leaves are typically opposite and have a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces pale yellow or greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by specific moths.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
