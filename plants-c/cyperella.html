Cyperella

Cyperella, commonly known as watergrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

These plants are typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Cyperella species are characterized by their grass-like appearance, with narrow leaves and spike-like inflorescences. They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants in wetland gardens.
