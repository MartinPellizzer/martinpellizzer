Croton potabilis

Croton potabilis, commonly known as the water croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

While not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
