Campanula autraniana

Campanula autraniana, commonly known as the Autran's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula autraniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
