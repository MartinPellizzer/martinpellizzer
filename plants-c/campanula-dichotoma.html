Campanula dichotoma

Campanula dichotoma, commonly known as the forked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, two-branched stems that give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Campanula dichotoma thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
