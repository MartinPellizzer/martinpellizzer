Calamagrostis rosea var. viridula

Calamagrostis rosea var. viridula, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features fine, silver-blue foliage that transitions to a soft green in the summer, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant produces feathery, pinkish-red plumes in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often used in wetland restoration or as a ornamental in water feature areas.

Its upright growth habit and drought tolerance make it a versatile choice for landscaping.
