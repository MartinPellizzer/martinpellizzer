Cerinthe gymnandra var. oranensis

Cerinthe gymnandra var. oranensis, commonly known as the Algerian toadflax, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with four petals, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
