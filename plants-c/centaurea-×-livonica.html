Centaurea × livonica

Centaurea × livonica, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and gardens.

Centaurea × livonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
