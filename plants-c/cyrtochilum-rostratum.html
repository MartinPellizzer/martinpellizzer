Cyrtochilum rostratum

Cyrtochilum rostratum, commonly known as the rostrated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique rosette-shaped growth habit and distinctive, elongated flower spikes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with a striking yellow and green coloration, often with a red or purple lip. Cyrtochilum rostratum thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

It is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
