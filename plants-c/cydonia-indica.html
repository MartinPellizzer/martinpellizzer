Cydonia indica

Cydonia indica, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and the Himalayas.

It produces rough, yellowish fruits that turn orange-red when ripe, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has a rugged, thorny appearance with pinnately compound leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit contains a large, hard seed and is valued for its aromatic, slightly tart flavor.

The plant is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
