Chaetopappa exilis

Chaetopappa exilis, commonly known as the slenderleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to harsh, drought-prone habitats.
