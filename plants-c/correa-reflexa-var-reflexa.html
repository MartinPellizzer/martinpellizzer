Correa reflexa var. reflexa

Correa reflexa var. reflexa, commonly known as the pink correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
