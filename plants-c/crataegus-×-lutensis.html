Crataegus × lutensis

Crataegus × lutensis, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible for birds and some mammals.

Crataegus × lutensis is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and ecological diversity.
