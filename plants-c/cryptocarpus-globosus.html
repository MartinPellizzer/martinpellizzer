Cryptocarpus globosus

Cryptocarpus globosus, commonly known as the globe arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, green leaves that are typically rounded and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Cryptocarpus globosus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
