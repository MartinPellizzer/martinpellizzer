Cinnamomum percoriaceum

Cinnamomum percoriaceum, commonly known as the fragrant camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils rich in compounds like camphor and cineole. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture on the underside and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The tree is valued in traditional medicine and is also used in the production of perfumes and insect repellents.
