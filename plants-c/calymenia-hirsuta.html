Calymenia hirsuta

Calymenia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy calymenia, is a member of the family Calymariaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Calymenia hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and along stream banks.
