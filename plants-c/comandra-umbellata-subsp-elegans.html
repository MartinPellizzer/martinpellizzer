Comandra umbellata subsp. elegans

Comandra umbellata subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant comandra, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in an umbel, giving it its species name.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
