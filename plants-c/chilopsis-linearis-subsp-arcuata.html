Chilopsis linearis subsp. arcuata

Chilopsis linearis subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the desert four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to orange and are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds. This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its evolutionary adaptation to the harsh conditions of its environment.
