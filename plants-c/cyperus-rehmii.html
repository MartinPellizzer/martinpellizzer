Cyperus rehmii

Cyperus rehmii, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. Cyperus rehmii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
