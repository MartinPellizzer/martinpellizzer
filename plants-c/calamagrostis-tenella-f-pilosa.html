Calamagrostis tenella f. pilosa

Calamagrostis tenella f. pilosa, commonly known as silky blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its slender, grass-like leaves are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silky texture.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
