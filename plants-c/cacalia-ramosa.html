Cacalia ramosa

Cacalia ramosa, commonly known as the branching bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has a slender, branching stem and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are alternate, usually lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Cacalia ramosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
