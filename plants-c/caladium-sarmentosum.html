Caladium sarmentosum

Caladium sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, brightly colored leaves that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in a sprawling, vine-like manner, often forming dense mats on the ground. Its leaves are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant coloration and unique patterns.

Caladium sarmentosum prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
