Cornus suffruticosa

Cornus suffruticosa, commonly known as white sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. Its fruit is a dark blue to black drupe, which is edible but often consumed by birds.

Cornus suffruticosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
