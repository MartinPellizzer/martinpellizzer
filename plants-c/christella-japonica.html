Christella japonica

Christella japonica, commonly known as Japanese rock rose, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round interest in gardens.
