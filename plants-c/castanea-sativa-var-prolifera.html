Castanea sativa var. prolifera

Castanea sativa var. prolifera, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces edible nuts encased in spiny husks. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible nuts.

This variety is notable for its ability to form dense thickets, making it a valuable species in reforestation efforts.
