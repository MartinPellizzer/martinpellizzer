Cissus perrieri

Cissus perrieri, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Cissus perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
