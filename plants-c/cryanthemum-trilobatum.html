Cryanthemum trilobatum

Cryanthemum trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has finely divided, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color and bloom in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
