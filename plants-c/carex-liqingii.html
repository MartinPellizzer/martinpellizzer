Carex liqingii

Carex liqingii, commonly known as the Liqing sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes during the growing season.

Carex liqingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
