Calceolaria lanigera

Calceolaria lanigera, commonly known as the velvety calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and striking, hooded flowers that resemble small pouches. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, purple, and white, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Calceolaria lanigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
