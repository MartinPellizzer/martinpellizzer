Centaurea orthoacantha

Centaurea orthoacantha, commonly known as the upright thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and bright yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula.

Centaurea orthoacantha is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
