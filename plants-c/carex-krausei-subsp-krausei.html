Carex krausei subsp. krausei

Carex krausei subsp.

krausei, commonly known as Krause's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
