Chondrosum hirsutum var. glandulosum

Chondrosum hirsutum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy chondrosum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky secretions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
