Citrus × aurantium subsp. suntra

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

suntra, commonly known as the Pummelo, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature.

The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has evergreen leaves that are oval and glossy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The subspecies is closely related to other citrus species and is valued for its high juice content and aromatic properties.
