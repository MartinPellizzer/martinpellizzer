Capparis thorelii

Capparis thorelii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.
