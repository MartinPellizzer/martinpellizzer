Conophytum minutum

Conophytum minutum, commonly known as the dwarf conophytum, is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. Its unique morphology and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.

Conophytum minutum is typically grown in well-draining soil and requires minimal watering to prevent root rot.
