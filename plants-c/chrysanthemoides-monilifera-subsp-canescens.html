Chrysanthemoides monilifera subsp. canescens

Chrysanthemoides monilifera subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the coastal chrysanthemum, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
