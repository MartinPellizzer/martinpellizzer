Chionodoxa luciliae var. forbesii

Chionodoxa luciliae var. forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It produces clusters of delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained, fertile soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
