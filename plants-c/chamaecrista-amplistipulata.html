Chamaecrista amplistipulata

Chamaecrista amplistipulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved Crown Vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.
