Carex rutilus

Carex rutilus, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces a showy inflorescence of greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Carex rutilus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
