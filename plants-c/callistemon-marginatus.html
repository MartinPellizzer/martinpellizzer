Callistemon marginatus

Callistemon marginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Callistemon marginatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
