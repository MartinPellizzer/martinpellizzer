Carex gifuensis

Carex gifuensis, commonly known as the Gifu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Carex gifuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
