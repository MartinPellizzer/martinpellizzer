Chamaecrista cotinifolia var. percoriacea

Chamaecrista cotinifolia var. percoriacea, commonly known as the coral bean or red coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
