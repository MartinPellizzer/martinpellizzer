Celastrus angularis

Celastrus angularis, commonly known as the angular staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Celastrus angularis is often found in forest understories and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
