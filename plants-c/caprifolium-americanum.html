Caprifolium americanum

Caprifolium americanum, commonly known as American honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often appearing in clusters. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Its berries, which are red or orange when mature, are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
