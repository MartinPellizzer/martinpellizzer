Crataegus ambigua

Crataegus ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus ambigua typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
