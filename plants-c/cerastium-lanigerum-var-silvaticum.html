Cerastium lanigerum var. silvaticum

Cerastium lanigerum var. silvaticum, commonly known as the woodland mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. Its leaves are hairy and closely spaced, contributing to its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

It is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.
