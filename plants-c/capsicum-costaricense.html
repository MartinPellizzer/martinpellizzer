Capsicum costaricense

Capsicum costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican chili or the red chile, is a species native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant with erect stems and ovate, glossy leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it produces bright red, elongated fruits that are often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its edible fruits.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other economically important crops like tomatoes and potatoes.
