Campanula decumbens subsp. decumbens

Campanula decumbens subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
