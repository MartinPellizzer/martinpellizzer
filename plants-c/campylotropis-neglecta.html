Campylotropis neglecta

Campylotropis neglecta, commonly known as the neglected bottlebrush.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, characterized by its dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
