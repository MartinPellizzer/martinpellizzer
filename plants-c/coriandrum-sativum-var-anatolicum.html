Coriandrum sativum var. anatolicum

Coriandrum sativum var. anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian cumin, is a cultivated variant of the coriander plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow seeds that are highly aromatic and used extensively in culinary applications. The plant produces umbel-shaped flower clusters and has feathery, finely divided leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is particularly valued for its distinct flavor profile and is widely used in traditional cuisines across Turkey and the Middle East.
