Capparis macropoda

Capparis macropoda, commonly known as the large-fruited caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal areas or on limestone soils. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. Its leaves are thick and succulent, with a slightly bitter taste, and the plant is known for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Capparis macropoda make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
