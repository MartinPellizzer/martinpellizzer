Camptotheca acuminata

Camptotheca acuminata, commonly known as the Chinese fringe tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree's leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with serrated margins. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Camptotheca acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
