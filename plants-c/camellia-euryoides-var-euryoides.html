Camellia euryoides var. euryoides

Camellia euryoides var. euryoides, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
