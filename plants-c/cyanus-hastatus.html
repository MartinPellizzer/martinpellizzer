Cyanus hastatus

Cyanus hastatus, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Cyanus hastatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
