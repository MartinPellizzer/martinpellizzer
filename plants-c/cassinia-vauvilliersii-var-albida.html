Cassinia vauvilliersii var. albida

Cassinia vauvilliersii var. albida, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, pale green leaves beneath the flower heads.

This species is often found in coastal dunes and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and wind.
