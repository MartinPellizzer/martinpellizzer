Cynanchum maeoticum

Cynanchum maeoticum, commonly known as the Crimean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of southern Russia and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces distinctive yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, spiny fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
