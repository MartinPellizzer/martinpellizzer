Castanopsis tribuloides

Castanopsis tribuloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, India, and the Philippines. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
