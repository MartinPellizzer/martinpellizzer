Cuphea cuernavacana

Cuphea cuernavacana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea cuernavacana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its eye-catching blooms and heat tolerance.
