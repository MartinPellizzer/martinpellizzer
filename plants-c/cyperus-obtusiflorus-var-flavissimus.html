Cyperus obtusiflorus var. flavissimus

Cyperus obtusiflorus var. flavissimus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often yellow or golden in color.

This variant is distinguished by its vibrant floral display, making it a notable species in ecological and ornamental contexts.
