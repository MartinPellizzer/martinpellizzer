Celastrus pubescens

Celastrus pubescens, commonly known as the roughleaf staffvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by elongated, woody fruits.

Celastrus pubescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
