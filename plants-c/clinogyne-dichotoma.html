Clinogyne dichotoma

Clinogyne dichotoma, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a stout, branching stem that becomes woody with age, and its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs. Clinogyne dichotoma thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
