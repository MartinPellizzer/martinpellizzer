Citrus × aurantium var. vulgaris

Citrus × aurantium var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common orange.

It is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its fragrant, orange-colored fruit, which is widely cultivated for its juice and culinary uses. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring dark green, glossy leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are white and fragrant, often preceding the fruit development.

The fruit contains a segmented, juicy pulp surrounded by a thin, leathery rind that is rich in essential oils.
