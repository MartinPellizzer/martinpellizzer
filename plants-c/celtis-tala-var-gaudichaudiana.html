Celtis tala var. gaudichaudiana

Celtis tala var. gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's boxwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring a compact, rounded form with dense foliage. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, often with a slight pubescence on the underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round, orange-red fruits that are edible for birds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and evergreen characteristics in certain climates.
