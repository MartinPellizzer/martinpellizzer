Cattleya × mantinix

Cattleya × mantinix, commonly known as the mantinix orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Cattleya mossiae and Cattleya labiata.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features large, showy flowers with vibrant colors, often in shades of pink, orange, and red, and a distinctive lip that is typically darker and more contrasting. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The mantinix orchid is prized by horticulturists for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
