Cadetia trigonocarpa

Cadetia trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant features trifoliate leaves, which are three-lobed and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
