Catalpa bignonioides f. purpurea

Catalpa bignonioides f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple catalpa, is a striking ornamental tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The flowers are typically a deep purple or violet color, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape. The tree produces long, bean-like pods that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is well-suited for gardens and parks due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
