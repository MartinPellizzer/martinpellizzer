Convolvulus ruprechtii

Convolvulus ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are twining and capable of climbing other vegetation.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
