Cyathea microphylloides

Cyathea microphylloides, commonly known as the small-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea microphylloides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
