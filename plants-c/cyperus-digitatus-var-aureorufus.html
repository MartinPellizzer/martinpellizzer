Cyperus digitatus var. aureorufus

Cyperus digitatus var. aureorufus, commonly known as the golden rush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
