Chironia angularis var. angustifolia

Chironia angularis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chironia, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, angular stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
