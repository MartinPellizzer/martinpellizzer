Crocus kurdistanicus

Crocus kurdistanicus, commonly known as the Kurdish crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are underground and serve as storage organs for nutrients.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
