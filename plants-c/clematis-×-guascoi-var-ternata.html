Clematis × guascoi var. ternata

Clematis × guascoi var. ternata, commonly known as the Ternate clematis, is a hybrid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is compound, with palmately arranged leaves that are often deeply lobed.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
