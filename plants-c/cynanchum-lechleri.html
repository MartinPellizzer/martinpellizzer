Cynanchum lechleri

Cynanchum lechleri, commonly known as bullet wood, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its latex contains compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their potential healing properties.

However, the plant is also known for its toxicity, with some parts containing harmful substances.
