Clidemia laxiflora var. longipetiolata

Clidemia laxiflora var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant has a trailing or climbing growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The varietal form longipetiolata is distinguished by its elongated petioles, which contribute to its unique appearance.
