Carex bipartita var. glareosa

Carex bipartita var. glareosa, commonly known as the shining sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-sided capsules that contain its seeds.

This variety is noted for its glossy, dark green foliage that adds ornamental value to wetland gardens.
