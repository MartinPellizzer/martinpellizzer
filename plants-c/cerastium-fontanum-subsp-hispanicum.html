Cerastium fontanum subsp. hispanicum

Cerastium fontanum subsp.

hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish chickweed, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for ecological restoration projects and garden use in shady areas.
