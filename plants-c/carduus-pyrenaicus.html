Carduus pyrenaicus

Carduus pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
