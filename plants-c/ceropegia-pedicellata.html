Ceropegia pedicellata

Ceropegia pedicellata, commonly known as the string of hearts or angel's wings, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular as a houseplant and in rock gardens.
