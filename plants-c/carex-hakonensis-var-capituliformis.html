Carex hakonensis var. capituliformis

Carex hakonensis var. capituliformis, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet or mesic habitats. Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The varietal form 'capituliformis' refers to its distinctive, clustered flowering structure.
