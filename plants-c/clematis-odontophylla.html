Clematis odontophylla

Clematis odontophylla, commonly known as the tooth-leaved clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis odontophylla is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
