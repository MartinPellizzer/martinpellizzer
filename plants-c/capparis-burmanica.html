Capparis burmanica

Capparis burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
