Cissus succulenta

Cissus succulenta, commonly known as the stonecrop cissus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, rounded leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. Its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and hanging baskets.

Cissus succulenta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
