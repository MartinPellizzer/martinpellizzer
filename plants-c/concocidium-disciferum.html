Concocidium disciferum

Concocidium disciferum, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance in its habitat.
