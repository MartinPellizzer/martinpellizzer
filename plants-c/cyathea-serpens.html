Cyathea serpens

Cyathea serpens, commonly known as the serpent fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea serpens plays a role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
