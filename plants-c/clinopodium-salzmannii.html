Clinopodium salzmannii

Clinopodium salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are small, ovate, and often have a mint-like scent, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Clinopodium salzmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
