Carex ferruginea var. caudata

Carex ferruginea var. caudata, commonly known as the red stem sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
