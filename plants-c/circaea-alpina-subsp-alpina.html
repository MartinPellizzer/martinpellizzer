Circaea alpina subsp. alpina

Circaea alpina subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and arctic regions. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
