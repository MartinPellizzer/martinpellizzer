Coleus cucullatus

Coleus cucullatus, commonly known as the hooded coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. Its leaves are often deeply lobed and can display a range of colors, including green, red, and purple.

Coleus cucullatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
