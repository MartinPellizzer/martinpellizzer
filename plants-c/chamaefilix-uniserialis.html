Chamaefilix uniserialis

Chamaefilix uniserialis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Chamaefilix uniserialis is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
