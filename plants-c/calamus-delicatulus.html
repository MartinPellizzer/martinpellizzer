Calamus delicatulus

Calamus delicatulus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often forming dense colonies. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
