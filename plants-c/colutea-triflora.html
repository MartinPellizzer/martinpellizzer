Colutea triflora

Colutea triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically and agriculturally significant.
