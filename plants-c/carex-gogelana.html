Carex gogelana

Carex gogelana, commonly known as the Gogelana sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Carex gogelana plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
