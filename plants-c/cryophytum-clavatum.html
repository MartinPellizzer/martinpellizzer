Cryophytum clavatum

Cryophytum clavatum, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Cryophytum clavatum is adapted to survive in harsh alpine conditions, often growing at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species within its ecosystem.
