Croftia

Croftia, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant that thrives in marshy and wetland environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that often float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Croftia is known for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical classification places it in the family Poaceae, making it a member of the grass family.
