Cenchrus mutabilis

Cenchrus mutabilis, commonly known as the variable grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its adaptability and drought tolerance make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
