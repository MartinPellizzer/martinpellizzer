Cynanchum wrightianum

Cynanchum wrightianum, commonly known as Wright's dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Cynanchum wrightianum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
