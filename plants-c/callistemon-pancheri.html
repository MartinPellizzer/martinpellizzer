Callistemon pancheri

Callistemon pancheri, commonly known as the pink bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically bright pink or red in color. The plant produces small, nectar-rich flowers that attract birds and insects, making it an important species in local ecosystems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
