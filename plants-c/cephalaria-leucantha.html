Cephalaria leucantha

Cephalaria leucantha, commonly known as the whitehead cephalaria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Cephalaria leucantha is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
