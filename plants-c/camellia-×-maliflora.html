Camellia × maliflora

Camellia × maliflora, commonly known as the pompon camellia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Camellia sasanqua and Camellia japonica.

It is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental qualities, particularly its compact, pompon-like flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, and produces elliptical, leathery leaves.

Camellia × maliflora thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes and container planting.
