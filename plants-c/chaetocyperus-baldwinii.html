Chaetocyperus baldwinii

Chaetocyperus baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin's flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
