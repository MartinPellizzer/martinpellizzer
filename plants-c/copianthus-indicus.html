Copianthus indicus

Copianthus indicus, commonly known as the Indian broom.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Rutaceae family, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a glossy surface, while its stems are typically woody and branched.

Copianthus indicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
