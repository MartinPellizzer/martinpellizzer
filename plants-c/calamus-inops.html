Calamus inops

Calamus inops, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the Araceae family.

Calamus inops is often found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
