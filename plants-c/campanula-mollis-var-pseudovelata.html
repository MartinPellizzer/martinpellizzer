Campanula mollis var. pseudovelata

Campanula mollis var. pseudovelata, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, charming blooms.
