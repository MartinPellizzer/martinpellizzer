Caltha ranunculoides

Caltha ranunculoides, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves and grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in areas with rich, loamy soil and requires consistent moisture to thrive.

Caltha ranunculoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
