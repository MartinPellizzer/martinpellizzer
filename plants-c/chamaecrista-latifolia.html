Chamaecrista latifolia

Chamaecrista latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Chamaecrista latifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
