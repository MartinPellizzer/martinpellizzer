Centaurea paniculata subsp. cossoniana

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

cossoniana, commonly known as the Algerian knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and striking purple to pink flower heads.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, composite flowers arranged in panicles. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
