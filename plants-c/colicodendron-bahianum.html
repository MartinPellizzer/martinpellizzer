Colicodendron bahianum

Colicodendron bahianum, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
