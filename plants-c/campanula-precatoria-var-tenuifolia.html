Campanula precatoria var. tenuifolia

Campanula precatoria var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
