Cassia stowardii

Cassia stowardii, commonly known as the silver cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Cassia stowardii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
