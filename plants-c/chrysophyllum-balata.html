Chrysophyllum balata

Chrysophyllum balata, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible fruits with a creamy, sweet pulp surrounded by a thick, inedible layer.

Its fruits are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and timber.
