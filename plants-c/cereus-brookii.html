Cereus brookii

Cereus brookii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often sparsely covered with white or pale green spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. These flowers emit a sweet fragrance and are pollinated by moths and bats.

Cereus brookii is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and thriving in full sun.
