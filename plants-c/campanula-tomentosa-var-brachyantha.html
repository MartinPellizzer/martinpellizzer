Campanula tomentosa var. brachyantha

Campanula tomentosa var. brachyantha, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense, woolly, silver-gray foliage that provides a soft texture to garden landscapes. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
