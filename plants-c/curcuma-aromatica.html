Curcuma aromatica

Curcuma aromatica, commonly known as turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange rhizomes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, clustered fruits.

The rhizomes are rich in curcumin, a compound responsible for its distinctive color and medicinal properties.
