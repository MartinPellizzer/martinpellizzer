Chamaefistula hayesiana

Chamaefistula hayesiana, commonly known as Hayes' thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
