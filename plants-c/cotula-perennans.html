Cotula perennans

Cotula perennans, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and is also valued in traditional medicine.

Cotula perennans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
