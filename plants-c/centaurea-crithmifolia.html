Centaurea crithmifolia

Centaurea crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centaurea crithmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
