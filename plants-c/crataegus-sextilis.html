Crataegus sextilis

Crataegus sextilis, commonly known as the six-lobed hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant often has thorny branches.

Crataegus sextilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
