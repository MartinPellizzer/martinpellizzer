Centaurea pratensis subsp. nigrescens

Centaurea pratensis subsp.

nigrescens, commonly known as the darkening field centaury, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets, and they bloom from June to August.

The subspecies is characterized by its darkening flower heads, which turn brown as they mature.
