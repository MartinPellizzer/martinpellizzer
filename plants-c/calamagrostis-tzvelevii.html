Calamagrostis tzvelevii

Calamagrostis tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tzvelev's reed, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, branching pattern.

Calamagrostis tzvelevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
