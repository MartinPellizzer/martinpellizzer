Clinopodium abyssinicum

Clinopodium abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its small, pink to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, four-lobed nutlets as fruits.

Clinopodium abyssinicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
