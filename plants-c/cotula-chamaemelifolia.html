Cotula chamaemelifolia

Cotula chamaemelifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive silvery or grayish hue.

Cotula chamaemelifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
