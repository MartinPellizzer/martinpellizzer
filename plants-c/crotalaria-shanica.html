Crotalaria shanica

Crotalaria shanica, commonly known as the Shan velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
