Carissa pichoniana

Carissa pichoniana, commonly known as the Pichon's moonvine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its dense, thorny branches. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.
