Citrus × virgata

Citrus × virgata, commonly known as the hybrid mandarin orange, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Citrus reticulata (mandarin) and Citrus sinensis (sweet orange).

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with thin, loose peels that separate easily from the pulp. The fruit typically has a sweet and tangy flavor, with a high juice content and a mild aroma. Botanically, it is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.
