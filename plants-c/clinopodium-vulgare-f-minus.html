Clinopodium vulgare f. minus

Clinopodium vulgare f. minus, commonly known as narrow-leaved savory, is a compact perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical form. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink or purple, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.
