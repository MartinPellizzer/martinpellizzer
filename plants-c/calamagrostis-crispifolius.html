Calamagrostis crispifolius

Calamagrostis crispifolius, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in gardens and natural habitats. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or folded. It produces feathery, plume-like inflorescences in the summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Calamagrostis crispifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
