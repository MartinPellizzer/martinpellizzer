Cereus speciosissimus var. merckii

Cereus speciosissimus var. merckii, commonly known as the Merck's night bloomer, is a succulent cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and striking floral display.
