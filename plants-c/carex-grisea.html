Carex grisea

Carex grisea, commonly known as gray sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its pale grayish-green foliage gives it a distinctive appearance in natural settings.
