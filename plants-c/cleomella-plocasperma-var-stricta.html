Cleomella plocasperma var. stricta

Cleomella plocasperma var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
