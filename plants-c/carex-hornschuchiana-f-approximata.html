Carex hornschuchiana f. approximata

Carex hornschuchiana f. approximata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat for invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.
