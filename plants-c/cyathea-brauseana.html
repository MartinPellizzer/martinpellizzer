Cyathea brauseana

Cyathea brauseana, commonly known as the Brause's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and helps in nutrient absorption from the soil.

Cyathea brauseana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
