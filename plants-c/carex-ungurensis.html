Carex ungurensis

Carex ungurensis, commonly known as the Moldavian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex ungurensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
