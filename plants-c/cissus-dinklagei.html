Cissus dinklagei

Cissus dinklagei, commonly known as the African snowball vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that resemble snowballs, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
