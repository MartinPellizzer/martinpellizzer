Centaurea rouyi var. suffrutescens

Centaurea rouyi var. suffrutescens, commonly known as the Kashmir centaury, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It features wiry, often hairy stems that are typically less than 30 cm in height. The plant produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers, which are arranged in compact, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
