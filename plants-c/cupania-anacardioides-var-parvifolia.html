Cupania anacardioides var. parvifolia

Cupania anacardioides var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sugar-apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers that are borne in clusters. The plant bears small, round, yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
