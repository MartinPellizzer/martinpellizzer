Caladium bicolor f. haematostigma

Caladium bicolor f. haematostigma, commonly known as the bloodleaf, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and display a range of red, pink, and white patterns, often with a striking red margin. It is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant prefers well-draining soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, as it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are not typically noticed.
