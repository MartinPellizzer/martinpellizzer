Campelia

Campelia, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late winter to early spring.

Campelia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
