Calicotome infesta subsp. intermedia

Calicotome infesta subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the African broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
