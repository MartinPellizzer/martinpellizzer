Cyathea trindadensis

Cyathea trindadensis, commonly known as the Trinidad tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the plant's upright structure.

Cyathea trindadensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
