Carex hilairei

Carex hilairei, commonly known as the Hilaire's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure typical of sedges.

The flowering spikes are usually greenish or brownish, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
