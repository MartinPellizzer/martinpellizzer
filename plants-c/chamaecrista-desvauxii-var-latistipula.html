Chamaecrista desvauxii var. latistipula

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. latistipula, commonly known as the broad-tipped clusterbean, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and distinctive, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
