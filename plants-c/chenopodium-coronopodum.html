Chenopodium coronopodum

Chenopodium coronopodum, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often heart-shaped or triangular in outline. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are minute, oval, and typically covered in a fine layer of hair. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
