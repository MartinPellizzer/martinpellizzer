Cineraria integrifolia var. umbellata

Cineraria integrifolia var. umbellata, commonly known as the Umbellate Cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
