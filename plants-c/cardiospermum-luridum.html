Cardiospermum luridum

Cardiospermum luridum, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a heart shape, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along roadsides or in disturbed areas.

The yellow shower vine is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail along supports.
