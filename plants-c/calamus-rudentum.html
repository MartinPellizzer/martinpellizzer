Calamus rudentum

Calamus rudentum, commonly known as the fragrant calamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically green and glossy, with a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowering occurs in terminal panicles, producing small, inconspicuous white flowers.

Calamus rudentum is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.
