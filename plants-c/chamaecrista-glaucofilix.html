Chamaecrista glaucofilix

Chamaecrista glaucofilix, commonly known as blueleaf burr legume, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
