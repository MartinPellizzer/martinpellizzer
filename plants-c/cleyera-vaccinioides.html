Cleyera vaccinioides

Cleyera vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are glossy and leathery. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for certain bird species.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
