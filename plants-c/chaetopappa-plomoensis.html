Chaetopappa plomoensis

Chaetopappa plomoensis, commonly known as the Plomo's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
