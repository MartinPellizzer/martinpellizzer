Cinnagrostis ciliata var. glabrescens

Cinnagrostis ciliata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early summer, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, attractive lawns.
