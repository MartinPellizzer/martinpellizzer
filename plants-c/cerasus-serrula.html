Cerasus serrula

Cerasus serrula, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its serrated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
