Cyperus swartzii var. granularis

Cyperus swartzii var. granularis, commonly known as the granular flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets, which are often pale green to brown in color.

Cyperus swartzii var. granularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
