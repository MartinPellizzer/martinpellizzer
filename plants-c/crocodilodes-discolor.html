Crocodilodes discolor

Crocodilodes discolor, commonly known as the spotted crocodile plant, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive mottled green and white pattern. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, clustered in showy inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
