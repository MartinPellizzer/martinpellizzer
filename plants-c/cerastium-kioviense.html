Cerastium kioviense

Cerastium kioviense, commonly known as the Kioviene chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are oval and hairy, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Cerastium kioviense is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
