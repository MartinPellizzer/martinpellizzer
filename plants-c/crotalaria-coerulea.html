Crotalaria coerulea

Crotalaria coerulea, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, and the plant often grows as an annual herb.

Crotalaria coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
