Canna variegatifolia

Canna variegatifolia, commonly known as the variegated canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that often display vibrant variegation of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage and floral display make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
