Cyrta serrulata

Cyrta serrulata, commonly known as the serrated cyrta, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cyrta serrulata is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
