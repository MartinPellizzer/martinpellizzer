Crotalaria acuminatissima

Crotalaria acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-pointed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and pointed leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria acuminatissima is also notable for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
