Carex canescens var. oregana

Carex canescens var. oregana, commonly known as the Oregon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and streamside habitats. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-gray hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
