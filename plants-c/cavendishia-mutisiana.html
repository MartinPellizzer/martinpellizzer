Cavendishia mutisiana

Cavendishia mutisiana, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Cavendishia mutisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
