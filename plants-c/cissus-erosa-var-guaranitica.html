Cissus erosa var. guaranitica

Cissus erosa var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian stonecrop, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its stems are succulent and can store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to trail or climb.
