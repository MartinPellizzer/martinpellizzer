Cyperus zollingeri var. permacer

Cyperus zollingeri var. permacer, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

Cyperus zollingeri var. permacer is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
