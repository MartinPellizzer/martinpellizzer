Cerastium latifolium proles uniflorum

Cerastium latifolium proles uniflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from April to June, and its leaves are broad and heart-shaped. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and garden beds. It has a fibrous root system and spreads through both seeds and stolons, making it a common weed in cultivated areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
