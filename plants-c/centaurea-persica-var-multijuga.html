Centaurea persica var. multijuga

Centaurea persica var. multijuga, commonly known as Persian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
