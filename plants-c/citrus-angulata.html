Citrus angulata

Citrus angulata, commonly known as the angular citrus or sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically small, oval-shaped, and has a thick, yellowish rind with a sour, acidic flavor. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils due to its aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
