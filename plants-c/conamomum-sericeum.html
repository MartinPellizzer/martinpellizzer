Conamomum sericeum

Conamomum sericeum, commonly known as silver banana, is a tropical plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces a fleshy, elongated fruit that is typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance, it is popular in horticulture and can be found in botanical gardens and private collections.
