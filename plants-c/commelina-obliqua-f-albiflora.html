Commelina obliqua f. albiflora

Commelina obliqua f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered commelina, is a variant of the Commelina obliqua species, which belongs to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with its broad, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

The plant exhibits rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
