Clutia alaternoides f. leptophylla

Clutia alaternoides f. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clutia, is a botanical variant of the clutia plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
