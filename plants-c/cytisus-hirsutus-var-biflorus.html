Cytisus hirsutus var. biflorus

Cytisus hirsutus var. biflorus, commonly known as the hairy broom or twin-flowered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
