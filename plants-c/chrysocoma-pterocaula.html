Chrysocoma pterocaula

Chrysocoma pterocaula, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Chrysocoma pterocaula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
