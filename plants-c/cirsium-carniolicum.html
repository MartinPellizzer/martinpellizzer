Cirsium carniolicum

Cirsium carniolicum, commonly known as the Carniolan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Alps and adjacent regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cirsium carniolicum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
