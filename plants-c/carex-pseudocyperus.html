Carex pseudocyperus

Carex pseudocyperus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with stamens and pistils that facilitate wind pollination.
