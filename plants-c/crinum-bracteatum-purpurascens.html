Crinum bracteatum purpurascens

Crinum bracteatum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-tinged crinum, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its bulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as storage organs for nutrients.

The species is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
