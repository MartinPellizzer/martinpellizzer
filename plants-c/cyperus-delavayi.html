Cyperus delavayi

Cyperus delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. Cyperus delavayi produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland areas.
