Crepis sicula

Crepis sicula, commonly known as Sicilian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis sicula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
