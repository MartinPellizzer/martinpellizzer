Carex vulpinoidea var. triangularis

Carex vulpinoidea var. triangularis, commonly known as the triangular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value lies in its distinctive foliage and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
