Callista venusta

Callista venusta, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a tall, branched stem that supports its showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Callista venusta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
