Cardiandra alternifolia var. hakonensis

Cardiandra alternifolia var. hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakonensis False Flax, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are alternately arranged along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
