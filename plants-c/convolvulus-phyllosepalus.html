Convolvulus phyllosepalus

Convolvulus phyllosepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy flowers. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals, and bloom in the morning.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
