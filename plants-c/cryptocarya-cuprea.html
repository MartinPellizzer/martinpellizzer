Cryptocarya cuprea

Cryptocarya cuprea, commonly known as the red-flowered cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically reddish in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cryptocarya cuprea is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens for its visual appeal and unique botanical features.
