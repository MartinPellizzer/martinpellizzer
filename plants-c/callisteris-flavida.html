Callisteris flavida

Callisteris flavida, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it has a low, spreading growth habit.

Callisteris flavida is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
