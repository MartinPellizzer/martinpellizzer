Carex amurensis var. abbreviata

Carex amurensis var. abbreviata, commonly known as the Amur sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more condensed inflorescence compared to other Carex species. It typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
