Cymbopogon foliosus

Cymbopogon foliosus, commonly known as the broadleaf grass or silver grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, elongated leaves with a silver-gray hue due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its aromatic properties make it a potential candidate for essential oil extraction and ornamental use.
