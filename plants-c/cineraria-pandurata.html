Cineraria pandurata

Cineraria pandurata, commonly known as the Mexican cineraria, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features hairy, blue-green leaves and produces white to pale purple blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Cineraria pandurata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
