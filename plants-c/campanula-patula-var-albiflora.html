Campanula patula var. albiflora

Campanula patula var. albiflora, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers with a subtle lavender tint. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft, grayish-white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
