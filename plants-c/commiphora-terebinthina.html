Commiphora terebinthina

Commiphora terebinthina, commonly known as the Indian myrrh or terebinth.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to regions of India and Sri Lanka. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy surface and a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.
