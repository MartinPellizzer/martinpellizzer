Crocus speciosus var. transylvanicus

Crocus speciosus var. transylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that appear in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
