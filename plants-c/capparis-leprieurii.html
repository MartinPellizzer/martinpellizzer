Capparis leprieurii

Capparis leprieurii, commonly known as the rock caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Capparis leprieurii is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant.
