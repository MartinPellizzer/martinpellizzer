Crocus scardicus

Crocus scardicus, commonly known as the Cardigan crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
