Camellia lanceoleosa

Camellia lanceoleosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and vibrant, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant is evergreen and thrives in partial shade with well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a source of tea.

Camellia lanceoleosa is also valued for its ability to produce edible seeds and its role in traditional medicine.
