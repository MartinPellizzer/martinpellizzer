Crocus pulchricolor

Crocus pulchricolor, commonly known as the showy crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The showy crocus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
