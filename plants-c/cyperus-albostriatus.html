Cyperus albostriatus

Cyperus albostriatus, commonly known as the white-striated flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands, rice fields, and along water bodies.

Cyperus albostriatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
