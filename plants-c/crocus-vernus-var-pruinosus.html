Crocus vernus var. pruinosus

Crocus vernus var. pruinosus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize over time.
