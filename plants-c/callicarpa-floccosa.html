Callicarpa floccosa

Callicarpa floccosa, commonly known as the woolly beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa floccosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and autumn foliage.
