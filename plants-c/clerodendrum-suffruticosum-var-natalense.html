Clerodendrum suffruticosum var. natalense

Clerodendrum suffruticosum var. natalense, commonly known as Natal spiderflower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red and purple. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
