Ceraia compressicaulis

Ceraia compressicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ceraia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that form dense mats on the ground. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually white or pale green in color.

Ceraia compressicaulis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
