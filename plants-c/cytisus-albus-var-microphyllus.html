Cytisus albus var. microphyllus

Cytisus albus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, giving it its distinguishing name. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
