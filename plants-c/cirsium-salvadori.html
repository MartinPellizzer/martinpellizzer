Cirsium salvadori

Cirsium salvadori, commonly known as the Salvador's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Cirsium salvadori is known for its hardy nature and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.
