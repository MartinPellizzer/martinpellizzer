Celtis shortii

Celtis shortii, commonly known as the shortii hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. Celtis shortii is often found in moist bottomlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
