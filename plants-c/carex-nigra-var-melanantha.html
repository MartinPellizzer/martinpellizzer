Carex nigra var. melanantha

Carex nigra var. melanantha, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of dark brown to black, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
