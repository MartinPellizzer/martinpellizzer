Centromadia pungens subsp. laevis

Centromadia pungens subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth centromadia, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in composite inflorescences.

The subspecies laevis is distinguished by its smooth, less hairy leaves compared to other subspecies of Centromadia pungens. It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia.

This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
