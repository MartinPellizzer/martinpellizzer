Chiococca brachiata var. petiolaris

Chiococca brachiata var. petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled chiococca, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent petioles. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
