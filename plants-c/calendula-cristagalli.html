Calendula cristagalli

Calendula cristagalli, commonly known as the fringed calendula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery petals that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat succulent.

Calendula cristagalli is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.
