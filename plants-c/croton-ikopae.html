Croton ikopae

Croton ikopae, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that exhibit a wide range of shapes and patterns. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Croton ikopae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
