Cyperus procerulus

Cyperus procerulus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage. Its rhizomes help it spread and establish dense colonies in moist soils.

Cyperus procerulus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
