Colubrina glandulosa subsp. reitzii

Colubrina glandulosa subsp.

reitzii, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dune habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
