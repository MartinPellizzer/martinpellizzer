Cyathea physolepidota

Cyathea physolepidota, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the plant in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea physolepidota plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
