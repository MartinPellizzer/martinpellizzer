Ceropegia pilifera

Ceropegia pilifera, commonly known as the threadleaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It produces nectar that attracts specific pollinators, such as certain species of moths and flies.

Ceropegia pilifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in semi-shade conditions.
