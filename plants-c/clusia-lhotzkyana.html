Clusia lhotzkyana

Clusia lhotzkyana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used as an ornamental plant in landscaping.

Clusia lhotzkyana is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
