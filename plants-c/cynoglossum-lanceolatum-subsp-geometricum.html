Cynoglossum lanceolatum subsp. geometricum

Cynoglossum lanceolatum subsp.

geometricum, commonly known as the geometric bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its geometrically patterned leaves, which give it its common name.
