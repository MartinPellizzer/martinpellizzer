Callicarpa triloba

Callicarpa triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have three lobes, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
