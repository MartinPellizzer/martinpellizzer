Calceolaria flexuosa subsp. chrysocalyx

Calceolaria flexuosa subsp.

chrysocalyx, commonly known as the golden cupula, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive calyces that resemble small cups, which are typically bright yellow or orange.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
