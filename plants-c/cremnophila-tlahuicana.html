Cremnophila tlahuicana

Cremnophila tlahuicana, commonly known as the Mexican rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Cremnophila tlahuicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
