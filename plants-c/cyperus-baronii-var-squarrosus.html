Cyperus baronii var. squarrosus

Cyperus baronii var. squarrosus, commonly known as the tufted flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
