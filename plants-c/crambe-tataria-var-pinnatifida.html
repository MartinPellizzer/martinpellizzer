Crambe tataria var. pinnatifida

Crambe tataria var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Siberian mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. The leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest edges.

The plant is notable for its potential as a biofuel crop due to its high oil content in its seeds.
