Clutia polifolia var. teretifolia

Clutia polifolia var. teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clutia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
