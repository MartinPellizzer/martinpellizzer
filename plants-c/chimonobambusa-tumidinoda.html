Chimonobambusa tumidinoda

Chimonobambusa tumidinoda, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of over 20 meters. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its culms are hollow and have a smooth, sometimes woody texture.

This bamboo is valued for its strength and is used in construction and traditional crafts.
