Cuphea epilobiifolia f. gollmeri

Cuphea epilobiifolia f. gollmeri, commonly known as the Gollmer's flame flower, is a cultivar of the Cuphea genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
