Casparya brevipetala

Casparya brevipetala, commonly known as the short-petalled caspary, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that help it conserve water in its high-altitude environment. Casparya brevipetala thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its unique floral structure makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
