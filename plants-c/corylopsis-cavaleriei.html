Corylopsis cavaleriei

Corylopsis cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese flame candle.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, characterized by its bright yellow, candle-like flowers that appear in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has aromatic, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Corylopsis cavaleriei prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable addition to gardens in temperate climates.
