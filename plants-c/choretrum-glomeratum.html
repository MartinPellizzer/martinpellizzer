Choretrum glomeratum

Choretrum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has delicate, branching stems. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered together in dense groups.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms.
