Calycogonium rosmarinifolium subsp. moanum

Calycogonium rosmarinifolium subsp.

moanum, commonly known as the Moanum stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the Mediterranean region. It features fleshy, upright stems covered in small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
