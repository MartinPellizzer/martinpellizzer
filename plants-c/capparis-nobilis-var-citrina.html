Capparis nobilis var. citrina

Capparis nobilis var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and woody shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, green, edible buds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly succulent texture.

The yellow caper is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
