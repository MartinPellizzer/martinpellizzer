Cassia glaucofilix

Cassia glaucofilix, commonly known as the blue-green cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia glaucofilix is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
