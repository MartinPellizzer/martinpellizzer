Castanea crenata f. imperfecta

Castanea crenata f. imperfecta, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or Chinese chestnut, is a variant of the chestnut tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smaller, less lobed leaves compared to the typical species, and its nuts are often smaller and more oval in shape. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes around the world.
