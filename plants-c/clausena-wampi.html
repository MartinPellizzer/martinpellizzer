Clausena wampi

Clausena wampi, commonly known as the wampi or wild mango, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small white flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically green when unripe and turning yellow or red when mature. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
