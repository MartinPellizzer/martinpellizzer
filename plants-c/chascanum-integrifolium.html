Chascanum integrifolium

Chascanum integrifolium, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are broad and have smooth, undivided edges.

Chascanum integrifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
