Carex lagunensis

Carex lagunensis, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland habitats and thrives in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
