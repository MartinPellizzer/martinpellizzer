Carex coacta

Carex coacta, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex coacta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
