Carex laxiflora var. plantaginea

Carex laxiflora var. plantaginea, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow-green flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
