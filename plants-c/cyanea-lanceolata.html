Cyanea lanceolata

Cyanea lanceolata, commonly known as the Hawaiian sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Hawaii.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Cyanea lanceolata is an important species in its native ecosystem, contributing to local biodiversity and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
