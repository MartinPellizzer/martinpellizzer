Cassia bicapsularis var. indecora

Cassia bicapsularis var. indecora, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Cassia. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
