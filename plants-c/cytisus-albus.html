Cytisus albus

Cytisus albus, commonly known as the white broom, is a deciduous shrub native to western Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus albus is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and sandy soils.
