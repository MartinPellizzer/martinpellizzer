Cleistocactus baumannii subsp. anguinus

Cleistocactus baumannii subsp.

anguinus, commonly known as the angular cleistocactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its columnar stems that are ribbed and often covered with spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that open only briefly during the day. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, preferring full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
