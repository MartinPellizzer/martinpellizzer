Crocus vernus var. reticulatus

Crocus vernus var. reticulatus, commonly known as the reticulated crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its flowers have a distinctive reticulate pattern on the petals, giving it its scientific name.

The plant is well-suited for rock gardens and well-drained soils, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty.
