Carthamus caeruleus var. tingitanus

Carthamus caeruleus var. tingitanus, commonly known as the blue crocus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and historical use in traditional medicine.
