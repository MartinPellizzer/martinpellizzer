Ceropegia bhutanica

Ceropegia bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Ceropegia bhutanica is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
