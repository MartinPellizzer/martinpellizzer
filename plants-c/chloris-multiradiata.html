Chloris multiradiata

Chloris multiradiata, commonly known as the silver grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Chloris multiradiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
