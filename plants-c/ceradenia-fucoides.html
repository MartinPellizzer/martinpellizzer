Ceradenia fucoides

Ceradenia fucoides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and a basal rosette of silver-gray leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Ceradenia fucoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salty air and coastal conditions.
