Calamus dioicus

Calamus dioicus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been historically used in herbal remedies.

Calamus dioicus is also an important plant in the ecological makeup of wetland ecosystems.
