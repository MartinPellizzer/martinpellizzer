Conophytum uviforme f. meleagris

Conophytum uviforme f. meleagris, commonly known as the "spotted rock fig," is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique spotted leaves and compact growth habit make it a striking addition to rock gardens and succulent collections.
