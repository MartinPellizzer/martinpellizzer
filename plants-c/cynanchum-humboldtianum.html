Cynanchum humboldtianum

Cynanchum humboldtianum, commonly known as Humboldt's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its toxic sap, which contains cardiac glycosides.

Cynanchum humboldtianum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.
