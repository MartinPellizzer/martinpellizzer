Carex umbellata

Carex umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
