Catevala vittata

Catevala vittata, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and spotted, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Catevala vittata is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
