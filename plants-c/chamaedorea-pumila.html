Chamaedorea pumila

Chamaedorea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo palm, is a compact, evergreen plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features slender, gracefully arching leaves that give it a lush, bamboo-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, making it ideal for small spaces or as a tabletop plant. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with a texture that resembles bamboo, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Chamaedorea pumila prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, thriving in warm, moist environments.
