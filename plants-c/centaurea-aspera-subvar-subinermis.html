Centaurea aspera subvar. subinermis

Centaurea aspera subvar. subinermis, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom from summer to autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its slender, branched stems and finely divided leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.
