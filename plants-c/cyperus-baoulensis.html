Cyperus baoulensis

Cyperus baoulensis, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as seasonally flooded areas and swampy soils. Cyperus baoulensis produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
