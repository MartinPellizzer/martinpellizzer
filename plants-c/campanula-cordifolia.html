Campanula cordifolia

Campanula cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It has a compact growth habit with a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Campanula cordifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
