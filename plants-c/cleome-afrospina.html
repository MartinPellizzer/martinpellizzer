Cleome afrospina

Cleome afrospina, commonly known as the African spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spindly stems and clusters of small, yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome afrospina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
