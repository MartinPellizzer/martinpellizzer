Cestrum arvense

Cestrum arvense, commonly known as field jessamine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum arvense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
