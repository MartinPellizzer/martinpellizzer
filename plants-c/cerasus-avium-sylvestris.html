Cerasus avium sylvestris

Cerasus avium sylvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible cherries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it has a smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
