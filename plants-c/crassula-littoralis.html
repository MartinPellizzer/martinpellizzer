Crassula littoralis

Crassula littoralis, commonly known as the coastal crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and coastal environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun exposure. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Crassula littoralis is a low-maintenance plant that is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
