Chelone glabra

Chelone glabra, commonly known as white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Chelone glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
