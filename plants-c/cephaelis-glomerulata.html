Cephaelis glomerulata

Cephaelis glomerulata, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cephaelis glomerulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
