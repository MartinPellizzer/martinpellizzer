Coelostylis glandulosa

Coelostylis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular coelostylis, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is a small, terrestrial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. Its flowers are usually borne on short, erect stems and are characterized by their glandular surfaces.

Coelostylis glandulosa is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
