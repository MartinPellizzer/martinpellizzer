Crataegus locuples

Crataegus locuples, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features thorny branches and produces white or pink flowers in early spring. Its fruit, known as a haw, is typically red or yellow and contains a single seed.

Crataegus locuples is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
