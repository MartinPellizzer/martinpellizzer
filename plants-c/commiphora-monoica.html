Commiphora monoica

Commiphora monoica, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a perfume. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various insect and bird species.
