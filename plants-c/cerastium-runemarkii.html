Cerastium runemarkii

Cerastium runemarkii, commonly known as the Runemark chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, hairy, and egg-shaped, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Cerastium runemarkii is often found in open habitats such as tundra, gravelly slopes, and rocky shores.
