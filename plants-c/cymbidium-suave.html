Cymbidium suave

Cymbidium suave, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, producing blossoms in shades of white, pink, and yellow. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Cymbidium suave is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
