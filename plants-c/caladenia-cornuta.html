Caladenia cornuta

Caladenia cornuta, commonly known as the horned spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with a single, erect flower that has a long, slender spur. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive horn-shaped structure at the base of the column.

Caladenia cornuta is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological specificity.
