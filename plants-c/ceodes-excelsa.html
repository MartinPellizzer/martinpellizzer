Ceodes excelsa

Ceodes excelsa, commonly known as the giant ceodes, is a striking evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which appear in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ceodes excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
