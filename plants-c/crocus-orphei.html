Crocus orphei

Crocus orphei, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a symbol of renewal and the arrival of warmer weather. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The Persian crocus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and traditional medicine.
