Campe orthoceras

Campe orthoceras, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its striking, horn-like floral structures that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to specific soil conditions. Its flowers are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Campe orthoceras is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
