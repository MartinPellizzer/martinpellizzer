Crotalaria perfoliata

Crotalaria perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often fused around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, releasing the seeds to the ground.

Crotalaria perfoliata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including disturbed soils and open woodlands.
