Cotoneaster polonicus

Cotoneaster polonicus, commonly known as the Polish cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Cotoneaster polonicus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
