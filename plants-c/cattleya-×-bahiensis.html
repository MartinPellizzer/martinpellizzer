Cattleya × bahiensis

Cattleya × bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahian Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
