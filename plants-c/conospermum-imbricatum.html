Conospermum imbricatum

Conospermum imbricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a species of shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.

Conospermum imbricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
