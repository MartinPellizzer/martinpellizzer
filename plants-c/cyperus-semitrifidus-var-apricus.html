Cyperus semitrifidus var. apricus

Cyperus semitrifidus var. apricus, commonly known as the apricot sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist to slightly saline soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This variety is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
