Cousinia uncinata

Cousinia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is herbaceous, with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or brown and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Cousinia uncinata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
