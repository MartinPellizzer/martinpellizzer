Caltha palustris subsp. thracica

Caltha palustris subsp.

thracica, commonly known as the Thrace marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, fens, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
