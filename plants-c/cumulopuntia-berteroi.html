Cumulopuntia berteroi

Cumulopuntia berteroi, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white or pale blue spines and its ability to form large, rounded clumps. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to hot, dry environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Cumulopuntia berteroi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
