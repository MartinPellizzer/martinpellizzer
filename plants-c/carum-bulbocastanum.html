Carum bulbocastanum

Carum bulbocastanum, commonly known as the bulbous caraway, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its clustered, bulbous roots and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, spicy aroma similar to caraway.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
