Coreopsis trifida

Coreopsis trifida, commonly known as the tickseed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with dark centers, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Coreopsis trifida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
