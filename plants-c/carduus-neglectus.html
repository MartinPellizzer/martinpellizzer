Carduus neglectus

Carduus neglectus, commonly known as the common cocklebur.

It is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae, native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and large, spherical fruit that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and weed management.
