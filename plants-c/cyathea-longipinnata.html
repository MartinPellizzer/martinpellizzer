Cyathea longipinnata

Cyathea longipinnata, commonly known as the long-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate fronds that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea longipinnata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
