Craspedia fimbriata var. marginata

Craspedia fimbriata var. marginata, commonly known as the coastal flax or silver flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

This plant is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
