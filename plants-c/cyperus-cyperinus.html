Cyperus cyperinus

Cyperus cyperinus, commonly known as the nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, tuberous roots and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often mistaken for grasses or sedges.

Cyperus cyperinus is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and disturbed soils, and it can become invasive in certain environments.
