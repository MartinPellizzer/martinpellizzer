Celtis vandervoetiana

Celtis vandervoetiana, commonly known as the Vandervoet's hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to parts of Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
