Cynoglossum natolicum

Cynoglossum natolicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Cynoglossum natolicum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
