Calanthe nephroidea

Calanthe nephroidea, commonly known as the kidney orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive kidney-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Calanthe nephroidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
