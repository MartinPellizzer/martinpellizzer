Croton longicolumellus

Croton longicolumellus, commonly known as the long-stemmed croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, often brightly colored leaves that can vary in shape and texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage.
