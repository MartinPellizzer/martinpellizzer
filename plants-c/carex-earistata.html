Carex earistata

Carex earistata, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex earistata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
