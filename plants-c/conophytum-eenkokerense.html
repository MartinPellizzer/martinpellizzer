Conophytum eenkokerense

Conophytum eenkokerense, commonly known as the "Cape Karoo stone plant," is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
