Carex laciniata

Carex laciniata, commonly known as the shredded sedgeland, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex laciniata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
