Cuphea glaziovii

Cuphea glaziovii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched and woody. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea glaziovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
