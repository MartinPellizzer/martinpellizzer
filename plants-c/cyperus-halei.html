Cyperus halei

Cyperus halei, commonly known as the Hale's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Cyperus halei is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various water conditions.
