Cabomba piauhiensis f. albida

Cabomba piauhiensis f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered watermilfoil, is a striking aquatic plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate beauty to its aquatic habitat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
