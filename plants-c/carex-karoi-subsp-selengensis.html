Carex karoi subsp. selengensis

Carex karoi subsp.

selengensis, commonly known as the Selengian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
