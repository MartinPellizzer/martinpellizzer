Crataegus × silvicola

Crataegus × silvicola, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a hybrid species native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–10 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
