Carex vaginata var. petersii

Carex vaginata var. petersii, commonly known as Peters' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate stalks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
