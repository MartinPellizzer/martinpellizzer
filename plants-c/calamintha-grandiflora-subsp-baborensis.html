Calamintha grandiflora subsp. baborensis

Calamintha grandiflora subsp.

baborensis, commonly known as Baborensis calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a mint-like scent, and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its subspecific distinction highlights its unique ecological and botanical characteristics within the Calamintha genus.
