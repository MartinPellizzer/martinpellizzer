Carex capillaris f. krausei

Carex capillaris f. krausei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
