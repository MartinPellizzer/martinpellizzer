Cirsium occidentale var. occidentale

Cirsium occidentale var. occidentale, commonly known as the western thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, serrated leaves and purple to pinkish-purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is often used in native plant landscaping.
