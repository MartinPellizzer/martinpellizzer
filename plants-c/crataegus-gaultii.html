Crataegus gaultii

Crataegus gaultii, commonly known as the Gault's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red pome that ripens in the fall, providing food for wildlife.

Crataegus gaultii is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
