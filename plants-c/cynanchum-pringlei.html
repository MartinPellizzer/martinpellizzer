Cynanchum pringlei

Cynanchum pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various ailments.
