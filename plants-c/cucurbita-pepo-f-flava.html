Cucurbita pepo f. flava

Cucurbita pepo f. flava, commonly known as the yellow squash, is a variant of the common gourd belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. The plant is an annual vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamins and nutrients.
