Crambe teretifolia

Crambe teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crambe, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or rocky areas.

Crambe teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
