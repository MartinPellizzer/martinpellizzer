Cheilanthes glauca

Cheilanthes glauca, commonly known as the blue hawksbeard, is a species of fern native to arid and rocky habitats in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cheilanthes glauca plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is often found in desert and semi-desert regions.
