Cyperus subanceps

Cyperus subanceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Cyperus subanceps produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
