Calymenia nyctaginea

Calymenia nyctaginea, commonly known as the nightshade calymenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
