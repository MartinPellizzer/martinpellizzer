Clinopodium giresunicum

Clinopodium giresunicum, commonly known as the Alpine mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like aroma, while its small blue or purple flowers bloom in summer.

Clinopodium giresunicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
