Campanula loeflingii var. occidentalis

Campanula loeflingii var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
