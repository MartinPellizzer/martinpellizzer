Carex longebrachiata

Carex longebrachiata, commonly known as the long-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex longebrachiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
