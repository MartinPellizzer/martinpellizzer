Capparis sinclairii

Capparis sinclairii, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red capparis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits. Its roots are known to contain compounds with medicinal properties, and the wood is valued for its durability and color.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland areas.
