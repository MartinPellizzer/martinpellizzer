Cephalophora scaposa

Cephalophora scaposa, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
