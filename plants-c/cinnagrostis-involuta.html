Cinnagrostis involuta

Cinnagrostis involuta, commonly known as silverleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cinnagrostis involuta is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
