Cyperus cunninghamii subsp. cunninghamii

Cyperus cunninghamii subsp.

cunninghamii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats such as swampy areas, marshes, and along waterways.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in coastal and inland wetlands.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
