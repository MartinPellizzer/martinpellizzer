Chrysanthemum lanceolatum var. heterophyllum

Chrysanthemum lanceolatum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, which bloom in the late summer to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
