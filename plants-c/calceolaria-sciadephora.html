Calceolaria sciadephora

Calceolaria sciadephora, commonly known as the shadow cup, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble small cups, with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in cloud forests. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Calceolaria sciadephora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific humidity and light conditions.
