Castanea crenata var. japonica

Castanea crenata var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its oval, hairy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which mature in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often growing in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary use, it is cultivated in gardens and for chestnut production.
