Centaurea divaricata

Centaurea divaricata, commonly known as the divided blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature.

Centaurea divaricata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
