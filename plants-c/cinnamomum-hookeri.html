Cinnamomum hookeri

Cinnamomum hookeri, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or small-leaved cinnamon, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.
