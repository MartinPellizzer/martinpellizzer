Croton monachinoensis

Croton monachinoensis, commonly known as the white croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
