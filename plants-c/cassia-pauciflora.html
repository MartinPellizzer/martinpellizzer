Cassia pauciflora

Cassia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. Cassia pauciflora thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
