Campanula rotundifolia f. monstrosa

Campanula rotundifolia f. monstrosa, commonly known as the purple marsh bell, is a striking perennial herb native to moist meadows and wetlands in northern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped purple or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy floral displays.

This cultivar is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in damp conditions.
