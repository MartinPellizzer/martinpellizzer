Concocidium ottonis

Concocidium ottonis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or goldenrod, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of yellow or golden flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to the southern United States and thrives in moist, sunny environments. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
