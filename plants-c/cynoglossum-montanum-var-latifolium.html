Cynoglossum montanum var. latifolium

Cynoglossum montanum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
