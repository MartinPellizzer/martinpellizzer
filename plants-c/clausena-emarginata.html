Clausena emarginata

Clausena emarginata, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The plant also has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
