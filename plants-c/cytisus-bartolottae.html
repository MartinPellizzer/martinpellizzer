Cytisus bartolottae

Cytisus bartolottae, commonly known as the Canary Islands broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, thorned stems. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Cytisus bartolottae is often found in coastal areas and rocky landscapes.
