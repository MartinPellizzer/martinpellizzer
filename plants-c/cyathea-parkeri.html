Cyathea parkeri

Cyathea parkeri, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Indian Ocean islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. Cyathea parkeri thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and play a key role in its reproductive cycle.
