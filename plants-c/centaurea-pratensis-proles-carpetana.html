Centaurea pratensis proles carpetana

Centaurea pratensis proles carpetana, commonly known as the Carpathian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
