Coriandrum sativum var. vulgare

Coriandrum sativum var. vulgare, commonly known as cilantro, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its aromatic properties are due to the presence of compounds like linalool and terpenes.
