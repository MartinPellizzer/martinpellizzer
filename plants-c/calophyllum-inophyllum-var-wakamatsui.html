Calophyllum inophyllum var. wakamatsui

Calophyllum inophyllum var. wakamatsui, commonly known as the Japanese sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its resin, which is used in varnishes and as a natural dye.

The variant wakamatsui is distinguished by its specific geographical distribution and slightly different morphological features compared to the typical form.
