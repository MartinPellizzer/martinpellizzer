Centaurea tenuiflora

Centaurea tenuiflora, commonly known as narrowflower cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea tenuiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
