Camelina sativa var. microcarpa

Camelina sativa var. microcarpa, commonly known as dwarf camelina, is a variety of the oilseed plant Camelina sativa.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and reduced seed size compared to the standard variety. The plant typically produces yellow flowers and has a short growing season, making it suitable for certain climates. Its seeds are rich in oil, which can be used for culinary or industrial purposes.

This variety is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its low input requirements.
