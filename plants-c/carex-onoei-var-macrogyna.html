Carex onoei var. macrogyna

Carex onoei var. macrogyna, commonly known as the large-fruit sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive, three-sided spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands, making it a common sight in riparian areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it both ecologically and aesthetically significant.
