Campanula tauricola

Campanula tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Campanula tauricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
