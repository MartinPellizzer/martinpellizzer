Chersodoma diclina

Chersodoma diclina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions. Its woody stems are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.

Chersodoma diclina plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
