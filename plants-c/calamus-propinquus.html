Calamus propinquus

Calamus propinquus, commonly known as the common calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Calamus propinquus is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
