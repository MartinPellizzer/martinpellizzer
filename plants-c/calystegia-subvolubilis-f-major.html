Calystegia subvolubilis f. major

Calystegia subvolubilis f. major, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a vigorous, climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and can grow up to several meters in length. Its stems are covered in fine, hairy strands, and it thrives in moist, sunny environments.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive if not properly managed.
