Crocus reticulatus subsp. hittiticus

Crocus reticulatus subsp.

hittiticus, commonly known as the Hittite crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils.

The plant produces striking purple or violet flowers with a distinctive reticulate pattern on the tepals. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
