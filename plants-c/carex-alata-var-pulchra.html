Carex alata var. pulchra

Carex alata var. pulchra, commonly known as the silky flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that typically bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands and marshy areas.

Its delicate, silky appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in water features and bog gardens.
