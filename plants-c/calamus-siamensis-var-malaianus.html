Calamus siamensis var. malaianus

Calamus siamensis var. malaianus, commonly known as the Siam cane or Malayan calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swamps or along riverbanks.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.
