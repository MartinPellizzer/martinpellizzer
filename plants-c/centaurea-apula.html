Centaurea apula

Centaurea apula, commonly known as the Apulian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and bright yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a rough texture.

Centaurea apula is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
