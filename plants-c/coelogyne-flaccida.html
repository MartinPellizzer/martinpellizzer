Coelogyne flaccida

Coelogyne flaccida, commonly known as the limp orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences and pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Coelogyne flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
