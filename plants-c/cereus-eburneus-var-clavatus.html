Cereus eburneus var. clavatus

Cereus eburneus var. clavatus, commonly known as the Clavate Cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth habit, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
