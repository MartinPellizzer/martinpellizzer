Costus wilsonii

Costus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Costus wilsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
