Cassia annunciata

Cassia annunciata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia annunciata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
