Campanula tridentata

Campanula tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with three distinct teeth, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Campanula tridentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
