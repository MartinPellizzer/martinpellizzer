Cyperus microbolbos

Cyperus microbolbos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus microbolbos plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
