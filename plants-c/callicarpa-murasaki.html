Callicarpa murasaki

Callicarpa murasaki, commonly known as Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant purple berries in the fall. The plant is valued for its ornamental value, particularly the striking color of its fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Callicarpa murasaki is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract birds.
