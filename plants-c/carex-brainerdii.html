Carex brainerdii

Carex brainerdii, commonly known as Brainerd's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among the foliage.

Brainerd's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
