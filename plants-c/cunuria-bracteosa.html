Cunuria bracteosa

Cunuria bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Cunuria bracteosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
