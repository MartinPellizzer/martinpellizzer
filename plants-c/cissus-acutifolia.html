Cissus acutifolia

Cissus acutifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its stems are succulent and can be used for propagation through cuttings.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
