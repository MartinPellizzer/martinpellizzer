Campanula hakkiarica

Campanula hakkiarica, commonly known as the Hakkiaric bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Campanula hakkiarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
