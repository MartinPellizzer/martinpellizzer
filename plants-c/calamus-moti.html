Calamus moti

Calamus moti, commonly known as the Moti reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Calamus moti produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Its rhizomes are sometimes utilized in local medicine for their purported healing properties.
