Cassia goensis

Cassia goensis, commonly known as the African senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it suitable for various ecological and agricultural applications.
