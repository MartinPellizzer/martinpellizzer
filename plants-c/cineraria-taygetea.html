Cineraria taygetea

Cineraria taygetea, commonly known as the Greek cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms in the spring.

Cineraria taygetea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
