Caccinia crassifolia f. obtusifolia

Caccinia crassifolia f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved caccinia, is a botanical variant of the species Caccinia crassifolia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities.

This variety is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
