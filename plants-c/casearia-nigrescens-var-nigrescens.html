Casearia nigrescens var. nigrescens

Casearia nigrescens var. nigrescens, commonly known as black casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy dark green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is characterized by its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
