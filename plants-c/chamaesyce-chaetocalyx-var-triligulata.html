Chamaesyce chaetocalyx var. triligulata

Chamaesyce chaetocalyx var. triligulata, commonly known as the three-lobed sandweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind or insects. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, providing additional protection against desiccation.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.
