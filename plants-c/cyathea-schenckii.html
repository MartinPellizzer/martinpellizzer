Cyathea schenckii

Cyathea schenckii, commonly known as the Schenck's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in rainforests or along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can reach significant lengths, supporting its tall growth.

Cyathea schenckii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
