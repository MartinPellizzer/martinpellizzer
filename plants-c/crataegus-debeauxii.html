Crataegus debeauxii

Crataegus debeauxii, commonly known as Beaux's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals but are generally not consumed by humans.

Crataegus debeauxii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
