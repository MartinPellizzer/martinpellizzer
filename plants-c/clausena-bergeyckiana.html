Clausena bergeyckiana

Clausena bergeyckiana, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sour fruits resembling small, round olives.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
