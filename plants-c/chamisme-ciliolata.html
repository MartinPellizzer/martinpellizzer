Chamisme ciliolata

Chamisme ciliolata, commonly known as the silky chamise, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Chamisme ciliolata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
