Cynoglossum racemosum

Cynoglossum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
