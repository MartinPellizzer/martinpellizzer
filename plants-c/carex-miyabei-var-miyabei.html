Carex miyabei var. miyabei

Carex miyabei var. miyabei, commonly known as Miyabe's sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Miyabe's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland environments.
