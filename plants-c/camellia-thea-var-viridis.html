Camellia thea var. viridis

Camellia thea var. viridis, commonly known as the emerald camellia, is a variant of the tea plant characterized by its distinctive green foliage.

It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and emit a rich, emerald hue throughout the growing season.

While not commonly cultivated for tea production, it is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
