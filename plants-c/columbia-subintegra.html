Columbia subintegra

Columbia subintegra, commonly known as the Columbia Mountain stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to the high elevations of the Columbia Mountains in British Columbia, Canada.

It features fleshy, upright leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Columbia subintegra is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine environments.
