Cleistanthus tomentosus

Cleistanthus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly cleistanthus, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cleistanthus tomentosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
