Calendula monardii

Calendula monardii, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and branched.

Calendula monardii is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
