Crepis virgata

Crepis virgata, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and it produces achenes as its seed type.

Crepis virgata is generally herbaceous, with a low-growing habit and hairy stems.
