Carex fernandezensis

Carex fernandezensis, commonly known as the Fernandez sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
