Clematis gouriana var. malaiana

Clematis gouriana var. malaiana, commonly known as the Malayan clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, allowing it to climb trees and shrubs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a heart-shaped base, with a velvety texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
