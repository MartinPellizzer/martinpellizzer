Crataegus gattingeri

Crataegus gattingeri, commonly known as the Gattinger’s hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in late summer, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Crataegus gattingeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
