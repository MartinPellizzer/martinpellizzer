Clibadium

Clibadium, commonly known as the burr cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Clibadium is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
