Carex lobata

Carex lobata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit makes it a common component of wetlands and woodland edges.
