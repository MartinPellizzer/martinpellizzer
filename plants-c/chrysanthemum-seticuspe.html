Chrysanthemum seticuspe

Chrysanthemum seticuspe, commonly known as the rough chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
