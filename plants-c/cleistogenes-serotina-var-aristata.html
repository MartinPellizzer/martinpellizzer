Cleistogenes serotina var. aristata

Cleistogenes serotina var. aristata, commonly known as the silverleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of compact spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is often found in grasslands and prairies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
