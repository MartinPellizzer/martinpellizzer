Crepis vuralii

Crepis vuralii, commonly known as the Vural's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of silver-gray leaves and small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces achene fruits with pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
