Carex goodenoughii

Carex goodenoughii, commonly known as Goodenough's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex goodenoughii is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
