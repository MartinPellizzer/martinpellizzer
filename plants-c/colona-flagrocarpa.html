Colona flagrocarpa

Colona flagrocarpa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of Australian identity.

Colona flagrocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
