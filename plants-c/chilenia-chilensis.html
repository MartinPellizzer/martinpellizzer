Chilenia chilensis

Chilenia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Chilenia chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
