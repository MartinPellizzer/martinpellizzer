Chrysopsis coronopifolia

Chrysopsis coronopifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its silver-gray foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Chrysopsis coronopifolia produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to its native habitat.
