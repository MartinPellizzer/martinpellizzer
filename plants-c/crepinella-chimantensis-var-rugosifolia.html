Crepinella chimantensis var. rugosifolia

Crepinella chimantensis var. rugosifolia, commonly known as the rugged leaf crepinella, is a rare plant species native to the humid forests of northern South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces berries that are edible for certain wildlife.
