Crossopetalum macrocarpum

Crossopetalum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited crossopetalum, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying vibrant colors such as pink or red. The plant has a woody stem with thick, leathery leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern. Its fruit is large and fleshy, containing several seeds enclosed in a thick, protective covering.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its natural environment.
