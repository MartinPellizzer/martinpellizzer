Chrysophyllum reticulatum

Chrysophyllum reticulatum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that turn red when ripe, containing a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, white pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is popular in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
