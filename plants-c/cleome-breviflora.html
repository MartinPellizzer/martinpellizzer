Cleome breviflora

Cleome breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered cleome, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Cleome breviflora is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as fields and along stream banks.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
