Carex dolichostachya subsp. trichosperma

Carex dolichostachya subsp.

trichosperma, commonly known as the long-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces distinctive, three-angled berries.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of North America and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
