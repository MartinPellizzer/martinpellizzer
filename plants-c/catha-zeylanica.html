Catha zeylanica

Catha zeylanica, commonly known as kath, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring elliptical leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, three-lobed fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds like cathinone and cathine.
