Crataegus chrysocarpa var. subrotundifolia

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. subrotundifolia, commonly known as the golden-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, orange to yellow fruits that are edible but often have a sour taste. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
