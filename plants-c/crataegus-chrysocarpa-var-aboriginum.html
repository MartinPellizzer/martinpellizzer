Crataegus chrysocarpa var. aboriginum

Crataegus chrysocarpa var. aboriginum, commonly known as the golden-fruited hawthorn, is a variety of hawthorn native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange berries that mature in autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pinkish flowers in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
