Carex subantarctica

Carex subantarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic sedge, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subantarctic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its ecosystem.
