Carex acuta f. minor

Carex acuta f. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a smooth texture, while the flowering spikes are usually pale green to brownish in color.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
