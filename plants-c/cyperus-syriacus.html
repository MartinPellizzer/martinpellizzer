Cyperus syriacus

Cyperus syriacus, commonly known as flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. Native to regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common weed in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of wetland conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in these ecosystems.
