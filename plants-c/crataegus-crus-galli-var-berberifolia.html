Crataegus crus-galli var. berberifolia

Crataegus crus-galli var. berberifolia, commonly known as the berberifoliate hawthorn, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are usually small, ovate, and have a rough texture with a yellowish-green underside. This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It produces red berries in the fall, which are a food source for various wildlife species.
