Centaurea × domsae

Centaurea × domsae, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Centaurea × domsae is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
