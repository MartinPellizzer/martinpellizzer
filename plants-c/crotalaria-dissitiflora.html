Crotalaria dissitiflora

Crotalaria dissitiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flower cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a spurred structure. The plant typically grows as an erect annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria dissitiflora is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or ornamental purposes.
