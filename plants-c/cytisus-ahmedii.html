Cytisus ahmedii

Cytisus ahmedii, commonly known as the Sudanese broom, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions with its deep root system and waxy leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in reclamation projects in degraded landscapes.
