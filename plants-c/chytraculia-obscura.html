Chytraculia obscura

Chytraculia obscura, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and elusive species of fern found primarily in tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that appear almost ethereal in sunlight. The plant's spores are released in a unique manner, often through specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves. Its growth is slow and requires a humid, shaded environment to thrive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat needs, Chytraculia obscura is considered a conservation concern.
