Centaurea serratulifolia

Centaurea serratulifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in dry, rocky soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea serratulifolia is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
