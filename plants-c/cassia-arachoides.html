Cassia arachoides

Cassia arachoides, commonly known as the African candle bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of yellow flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cassia arachoides is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
