Chamaecrista sophoroides

Chamaecrista sophoroides, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
