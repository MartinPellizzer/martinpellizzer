Chrysophyllum mortehanii

Chrysophyllum mortehanii, commonly known as the African cherry or African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also valued for its timber and traditional uses.
