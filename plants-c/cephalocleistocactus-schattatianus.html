Cephalocleistocactus schattatianus

Cephalocleistocactus schattatianus, commonly known as the Schattatianus cactus, is a species of cactus native to Bolivia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy appearance. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cactus is relatively rare and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate.
