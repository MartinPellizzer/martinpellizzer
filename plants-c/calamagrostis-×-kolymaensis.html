Calamagrostis × kolymaensis

Calamagrostis × kolymaensis, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a perennial grass native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to brown in hue.

Calamagrostis × kolymaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
