Coccoloba paraguariensis f. intermedia

Coccoloba paraguariensis f. intermedia, commonly known as the Paraguayan glorybower, is a variant of the Coccoloba genus native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
