Crescentia arborea

Crescentia arborea, commonly known as the sweet sop or custard apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are large, showy, and typically white or pale yellow, with a strong fragrance.

The fruit is a large, spiny capsule that contains several large, fleshy, seed-filled segments.
