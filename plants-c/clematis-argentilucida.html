Clematis argentilucida

Clematis argentilucida, commonly known as the silver lace vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage that resembles lace, with silvery-white undersides on the leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis argentilucida is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
