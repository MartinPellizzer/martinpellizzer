Chamaesenna didymobotrya

Chamaesenna didymobotrya, commonly known as the two-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five lobes, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an attractive species for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
