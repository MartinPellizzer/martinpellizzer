Citrullus lanatus var. senegalicus

Citrullus lanatus var. senegalicus, commonly known as the watermelon, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its sprawling vines and large, lobed leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces bright green rind with a red or yellow flesh that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its high water content and sweet, juicy flesh.
