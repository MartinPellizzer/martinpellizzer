Centaurea aladaghensis

Centaurea aladaghensis, commonly known as the Aladagh thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from blue to purple in color.

Centaurea aladaghensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
