Chaetochloa longipila

Chaetochloa longipila, commonly known as the long-leaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marsh environments in Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dense mats, forming part of the vegetation in freshwater ecosystems. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.

Its adaptability to waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of wetland plant communities.
