Croton caudatus var. denticulatus

Croton caudatus var. denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a reddish or bronze hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some regions for their purported healing properties.
