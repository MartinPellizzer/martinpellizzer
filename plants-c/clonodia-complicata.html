Clonodia complicata

Clonodia complicata, commonly known as the twisted clonoid, is a perennial flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clonodia complicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
