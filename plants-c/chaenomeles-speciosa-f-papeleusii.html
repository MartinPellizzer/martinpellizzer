Chaenomeles speciosa f. papeleusii

Chaenomeles speciosa f. papeleusii, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to red flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has thick, reddish-brown bark and produces small, yellowish fruits in the autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and seasonal display of flowers and fruit make it a valuable addition to temperate landscapes.
