Crocus lageniflorus var. striatus

Crocus lageniflorus var. striatus, commonly known as the striated crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often striped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
