Crocus moesiacus subvar. aureus

Crocus moesiacus subvar. aureus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped blooms that are often adorned with a purple or brown stripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
