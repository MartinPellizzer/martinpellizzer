Caladium laucheanum

Caladium laucheanum, commonly known as the red-veined caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant shades of red, pink, and white, with contrasting veins. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.

Caladium laucheanum is typically propagated through tuberous roots, which can be planted in spring for optimal growth.
