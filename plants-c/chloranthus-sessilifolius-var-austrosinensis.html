Chloranthus sessilifolius var. austrosinensis

Chloranthus sessilifolius var. austrosinensis, commonly known as the Chinese chloranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, sessile leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
