Coleus schizophyllus

Coleus schizophyllus, commonly known as the mountain coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
