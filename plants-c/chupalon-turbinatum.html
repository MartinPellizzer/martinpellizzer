Chupalon turbinatum

Chupalon turbinatum, commonly known as the turban lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, turban-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red in color. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical name reflects its unique floral structure, which resembles a turban in shape.
