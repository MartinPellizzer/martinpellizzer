Chionanthus sumatranus

Chionanthus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran whiteflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Sumatra and Borneo.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and lance-shaped, while its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels.

The Sumatran whiteflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, sweet-scented blooms.
