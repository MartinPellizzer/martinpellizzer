Cerastium variabile

Cerastium variabile, commonly known as variable mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium variabile is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in moist areas.
