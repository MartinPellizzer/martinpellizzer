Commiphora angustifoliolata

Commiphora angustifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Its flowering is typically followed by the production of small, red berries that contain the seeds.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential medicinal applications.
