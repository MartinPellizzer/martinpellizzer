Cestrum albopunctatum

Cestrum albopunctatum, commonly known as the white-spotted cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive white or pale green spots, and they bloom in clusters during the summer months.
