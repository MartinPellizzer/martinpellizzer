Carex flava var. brevirostris

Carex flava var. brevirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
