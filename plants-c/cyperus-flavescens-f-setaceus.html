Cyperus flavescens f. setaceus

Cyperus flavescens f. setaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
