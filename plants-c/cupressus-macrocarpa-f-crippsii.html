Cupressus macrocarpa f. crippsii

Cupressus macrocarpa f. crippsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kahikatea, is a unique form of the Australian lacebark tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often twisted and arranged in a spiral pattern. This cultivar exhibits a compact, columnar growth habit, making it suitable for small spaces. The foliage is typically a deep green, with a silvery-blue tinge on the underside, adding visual interest.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
