Cruciata × grecescui

Cruciata × grecescui, commonly known as the hybrid cruciata, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two species of the Cruciata genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often displaying a range of vibrant colors depending on the cultivar. The plant typically features small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticultural settings.
