Carex cespitosa var. tristigmatica

Carex cespitosa var. tristigmatica, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
