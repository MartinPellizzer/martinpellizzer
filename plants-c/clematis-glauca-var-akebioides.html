Clematis glauca var. akebioides

Clematis glauca var. akebioides, commonly known as the Akebioid Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with persistent, waxy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage remains green throughout the growing season, adding visual interest in both summer and autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
