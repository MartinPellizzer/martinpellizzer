Crataegus calpodendron var. mollicula

Crataegus calpodendron var. mollicula, commonly known as the soft-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
