Cymbidium triste

Cymbidium triste, commonly known as the sad orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and fragrant, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a delicate appearance that contributes to its melancholic common name.

Cymbidium triste is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral fragrance.
