Carduus cirsioides

Carduus cirsioides, commonly known as the common cockleburs, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
