Carex ericetorum

Carex ericetorum, commonly known as the heath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils, often found in heathlands and woodland edges. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and grass-green, and it produces small, clustered flower heads in the summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
