Caryopteris × clandonensis

Caryopteris × clandonensis, commonly known as blue mist spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, blue-black berries in the fall. It is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9 and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Caryopteris × clandonensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
