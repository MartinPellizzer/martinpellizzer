Cissus pentandra

Cissus pentandra, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy dots. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used as a houseplant.

Cissus pentandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying properties.
