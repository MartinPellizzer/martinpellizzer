Centaurea aspera nothovar. conica

Centaurea aspera nothovar. conica, commonly known as the conical centaury, is a variant of the rough centaury, characterized by its distinctive conical flower head.

This plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The flowers are usually a vibrant red or orange, with bracts that give the appearance of a cone.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
