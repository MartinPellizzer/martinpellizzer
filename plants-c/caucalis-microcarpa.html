Caucalis microcarpa

Caucalis microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has slender, upright stems with opposite leaves. Its small, capsule-like fruits contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caucalis microcarpa is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
