Colchicum multiflorum subsp. micranthum

Colchicum multiflorum subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in the autumn, typically from September to November.

The plant has a corm-like structure and produces leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in a waxy coating. Its botanical characteristics include a short stem, and the flowers are borne on short pedicels.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking fall blooms.
