Cleistopholis albida var. longipedicellata

Cleistopholis albida var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the white-flowered cleistopholis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that are typically borne on slender pedicels. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
