Christella semisagittata

Christella semisagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf christella, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly notched at the base. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded, wetland habitats.
