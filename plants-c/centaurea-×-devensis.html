Centaurea × devensis

Centaurea × devensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea jacea (red cornflower). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and bright blue to purple ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
