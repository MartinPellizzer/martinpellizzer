Campanula baskilensis

Campanula baskilensis, commonly known as the Baskil bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers during the summer months.

Campanula baskilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
