Cotoneaster racemiflorus var. typicus

Cotoneaster racemiflorus var. typicus, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats and as a hardy, low-maintenance garden plant.
