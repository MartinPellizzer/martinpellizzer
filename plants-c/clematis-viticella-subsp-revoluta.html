Clematis viticella subsp. revoluta

Clematis viticella subsp.

revoluta, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a vigorous climbing plant native to Europe. It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, often with a faint scent. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental vine.
