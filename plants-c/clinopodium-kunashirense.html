Clinopodium kunashirense

Clinopodium kunashirense, commonly known as the Ryukyu mint, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium kunashirense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
