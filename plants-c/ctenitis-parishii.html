Ctenitis parishii

Ctenitis parishii, commonly known as the Parry's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its distinctive, spirally arranged fronds.

Ctenitis parishii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
