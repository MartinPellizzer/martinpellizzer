Cerastium holosteoides var. hallaisanense

Cerastium holosteoides var. hallaisanense, commonly known as Hallaisan chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
