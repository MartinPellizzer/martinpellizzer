Calytrix

Calytrix, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their small, showy flowers that often bloom during the winter months. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Calytrix species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
