Calycanthemum leucanthemum

Calycanthemum leucanthemum, commonly known as white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Calycanthemum leucanthemum plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
