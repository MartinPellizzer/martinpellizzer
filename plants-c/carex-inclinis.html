Carex inclinis

Carex inclinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions, Carex inclinis typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, three-rowed berries.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
