Caesalpinia burmanica

Caesalpinia burmanica, commonly known as Burmese crownflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
