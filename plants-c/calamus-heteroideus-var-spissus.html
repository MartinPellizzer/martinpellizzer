Calamus heteroideus var. spissus

Calamus heteroideus var. spissus, commonly known as the dense-stemmed arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, ridged stems and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional basketry and thatching. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are a staple in some local cuisines. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is cultivated in both horticultural and agricultural settings.
