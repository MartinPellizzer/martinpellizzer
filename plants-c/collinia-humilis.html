Collinia humilis

Collinia humilis, commonly known as the low collinia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its reproductive strategy relies on wind pollination, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in loose, organic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Collinia humilis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
