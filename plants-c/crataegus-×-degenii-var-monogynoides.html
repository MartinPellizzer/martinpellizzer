Crataegus × degenii var. monogynoides

Crataegus × degenii var. monogynoides, commonly known as the red sand cherry, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, red fruits that are edible for birds and mammals. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and moist soils, and it is valued for its ornamental value due to its vibrant fall foliage. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other hawthorn species.

The varietal name "monogynoides" refers to its predominantly single-seeded fruits, distinguishing it from other varieties in the species.
