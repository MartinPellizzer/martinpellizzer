Cytisus maderensis

Cytisus maderensis, commonly known as the Madeira broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Azores.

It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, with a leathery texture.

Cytisus maderensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
