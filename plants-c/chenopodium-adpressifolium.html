Chenopodium adpressifolium

Chenopodium adpressifolium, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its small green flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
