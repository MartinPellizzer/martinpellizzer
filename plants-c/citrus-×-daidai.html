Citrus × daidai

Citrus × daidai, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or daidai, is a hybrid citrus fruit native to Japan.

It is a small, round fruit with a thick, leathery rind that is typically green or yellowish in color. The fruit contains a segmented, juicy pulp that is sweet and slightly acidic, making it popular for both fresh consumption and culinary use. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruit.
