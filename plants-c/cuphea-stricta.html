Cuphea stricta

Cuphea stricta, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright, branching stems covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and are often borne in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cuphea stricta is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
