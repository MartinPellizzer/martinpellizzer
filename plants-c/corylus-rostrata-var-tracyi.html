Corylus rostrata var. tracyi

Corylus rostrata var. tracyi, commonly known as the Tracy's hazelnut, is a variant of the hazelnut species native to specific regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated nuts with a pronounced rostrum, or beak-like projection, which sets it apart from other hazelnut varieties. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for both its ecological role and its potential for cultivation in horticultural settings.
