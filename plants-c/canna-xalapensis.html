Canna xalapensis

Canna xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral display.

Canna xalapensis is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10.
