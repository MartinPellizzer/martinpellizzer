Cocos procopiana

Cocos procopiana, commonly known as the coconut of the Philippines, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces a distinctive fruit that contains a hard, fibrous husk surrounding a white, fleshy interior. The coconut is valued for its edible flesh, oil, and water, as well as its fibrous husk used in various industries.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
