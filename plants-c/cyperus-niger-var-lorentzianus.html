Cyperus niger var. lorentzianus

Cyperus niger var. lorentzianus, commonly known as black rush or swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually brown or black, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus niger var. lorentzianus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
