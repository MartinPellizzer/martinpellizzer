Chamaefistula anconis

Chamaefistula anconis, commonly known as the Anconis thornapple, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
