Convallaria japonica var. minor

Convallaria japonica var. minor, commonly known as Japanese lily of the valley, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that grow in a basal rosette. It produces red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, shaded environments.
