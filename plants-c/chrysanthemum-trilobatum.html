Chrysanthemum trilobatum

Chrysanthemum trilobatum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
