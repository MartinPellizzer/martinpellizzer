Carex capillata

Carex capillata, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex capillata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
