Campylospermum deltoideum

Campylospermum deltoideum, commonly known as the deltoid flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Campylospermum deltoideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
