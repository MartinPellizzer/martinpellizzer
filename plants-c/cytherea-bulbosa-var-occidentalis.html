Cytherea bulbosa var. occidentalis

Cytherea bulbosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western bulbous cytherea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its bulbous root system and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water requirements.
