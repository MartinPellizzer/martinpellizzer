Croton ceanothifolius

Croton ceanothifolius, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton ceanothifolius is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
