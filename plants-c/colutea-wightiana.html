Colutea wightiana

Colutea wightiana, commonly known as Wight's shieldflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Colutea wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
