Clethra oleoides

Clethra oleoides, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Clethra oleoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
