Crossoliparis

Crossoliparis, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem with opposite leaves that are ovate in shape and have a smooth texture. Its roots are fibrous and capable of anchoring the plant in well-drained soil.

Crossoliparis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny environments.
