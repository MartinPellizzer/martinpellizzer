Crataegus pruinosa f. dissona

Crataegus pruinosa f. dissona, commonly known as the variegated hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, which features white or yellow margins on the leaves. This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing to a height of 3 to 6 meters. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, followed by small, red berries in the autumn.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
