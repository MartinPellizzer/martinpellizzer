Campanula rotundifolia subsp. fennica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

fennica, commonly known as the northern bellflower, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has rounded, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as tundra and mountain meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
