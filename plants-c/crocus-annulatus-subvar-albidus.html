Crocus annulatus subvar. albidus

Crocus annulatus subvar. albidus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early spring blooming habit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced pigmentation compared to other crocus varieties.
