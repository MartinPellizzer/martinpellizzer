Carex popovii subsp. brunneola

Carex popovii subsp.

brunneola, commonly known as the brownish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow, rough, and have a distinctive brownish hue, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky environments.
