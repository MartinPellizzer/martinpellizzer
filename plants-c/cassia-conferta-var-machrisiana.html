Cassia conferta var. machrisiana

Cassia conferta var. machrisiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its rapid growth rate.
