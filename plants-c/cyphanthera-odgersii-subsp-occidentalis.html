Cyphanthera odgersii subsp. occidentalis

Cyphanthera odgersii subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
