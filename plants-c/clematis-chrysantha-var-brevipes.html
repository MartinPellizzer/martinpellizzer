Clematis chrysantha var. brevipes

Clematis chrysantha var. brevipes, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems that climb using twining tendrils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-blooming vines.
