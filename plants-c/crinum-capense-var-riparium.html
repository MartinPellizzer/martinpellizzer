Crinum capense var. riparium

Crinum capense var. riparium, commonly known as the river crinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows near water bodies such as rivers and wetlands. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its flowers are large, showy, and usually white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

It is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
