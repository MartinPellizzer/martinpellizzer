Conyza canadensis var. incisa

Conyza canadensis var. incisa, commonly known as rough ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields.

Conyza canadensis var. incisa is often found in the eastern and central United States.
