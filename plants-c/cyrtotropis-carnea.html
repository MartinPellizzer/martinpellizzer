Cyrtotropis carnea

Cyrtotropis carnea, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the dry environment.

Cyrtotropis carnea is an important species for pollinators and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.
