Crataegus amicalis

Crataegus amicalis, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, oval leaves and is part of the Rosaceae family.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
