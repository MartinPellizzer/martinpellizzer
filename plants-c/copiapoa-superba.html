Copiapoa superba

Copiapoa superba, commonly known as the royal cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Copiapoa superba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
