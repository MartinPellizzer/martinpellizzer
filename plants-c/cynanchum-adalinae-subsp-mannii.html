Cynanchum adalinae subsp. mannii

Cynanchum adalinae subsp.

mannii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive milky sap and showy, white, night-blooming flowers.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
