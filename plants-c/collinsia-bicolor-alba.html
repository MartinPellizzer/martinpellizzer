Collinsia bicolor alba

Collinsia bicolor alba, commonly known as the white fairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive, early spring blooms.
