Campanula glomerata var. heterophylla

Campanula glomerata var. heterophylla, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and vary in shape, giving rise to its varietal name "heterophylla," which means "different leaves.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
