Croton lobatus var. seemannii

Croton lobatus var. seemannii, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with a white or cream-colored margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that give way to orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
