Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp. purpurascens

Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or boggy habitats, such as heaths and marshes, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and distinctive purple-tinted foliage.

The plant produces panicles of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it a useful plant for stabilizing soils in wetland environments. Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp.

purpurascens is also valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
