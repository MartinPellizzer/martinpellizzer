Coeloglossum bracteatum

Coeloglossum bracteatum, commonly known as the velvet tassel orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to thrive. Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.

Coeloglossum bracteatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
