Crudia bracteata

Crudia bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate thistle, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is covered in dense, bract-like structures that provide protection against herbivores.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
