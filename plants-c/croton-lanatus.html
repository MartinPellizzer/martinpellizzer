Croton lanatus

Croton lanatus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and striking, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
