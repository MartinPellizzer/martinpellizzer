Calyptranthes oblongifolia

Calyptranthes oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblongleaf calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Calyptranthes oblongifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
