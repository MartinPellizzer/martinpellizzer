Cissus boliviana

Cissus boliviana, commonly known as the climbing grape ivy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its stems are flexible and can climb using tendrils, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

Cissus boliviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to purify indoor air.
