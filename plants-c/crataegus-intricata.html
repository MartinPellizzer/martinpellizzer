Crataegus intricata

Crataegus intricata, commonly known as the intricate hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern North America, where it typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with finely serrated margins, and the bark is often scaly and grayish in color.

Crataegus intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
