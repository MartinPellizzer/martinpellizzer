Cordyline haageana

Cordyline haageana, commonly known as the New Zealand cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features a tall, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, spongy bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its resilience and unique appearance make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
