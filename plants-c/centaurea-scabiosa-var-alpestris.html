Centaurea scabiosa var. alpestris

Centaurea scabiosa var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, purple-red flower heads and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, bract-like leaves and is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold climates and harsh environmental conditions.
