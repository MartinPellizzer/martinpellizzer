Colutea arborescens var. melanocalyx

Colutea arborescens var. melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-flowered broom.

This variant is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and compound leaves. It produces dense clusters of small, blackish-purple flowers in early spring, which are followed by distinctive bean-like pods. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often found in woodland edges and open fields, contributing to local biodiversity.
