Chamaefilix lunulata var. harpeodes

Chamaefilix lunulata var. harpeodes, commonly known as the Harpe's rockfoxtail, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-angled, bristly seed capsules. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought make it a resilient plant in its native range.
