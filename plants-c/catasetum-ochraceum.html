Catasetum ochraceum

Catasetum ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a bee, a feature that aids in pollination by bees. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their sweet, fragrant scent.

Catasetum ochraceum is prized in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
