Cleisostoma isuaravanum

Cleisostoma isuaravanum, commonly known as the Ivory Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
