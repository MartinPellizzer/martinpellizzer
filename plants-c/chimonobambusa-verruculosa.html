Chimonobambusa verruculosa

Chimonobambusa verruculosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it grows in moist, temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks covered in a fuzzy, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly wavy edge, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in various climates.
