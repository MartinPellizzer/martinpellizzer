Carex acutiformis

Carex acutiformis, commonly known as the sharp-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark brown, nut-like seeds.

Carex acutiformis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
