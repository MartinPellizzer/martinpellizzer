Cerasus kumanoensis

Cerasus kumanoensis, commonly known as the Kumano cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Japan, particularly in the Kumano area. This deciduous shrub produces small, delicate pink flowers in early spring, which bloom before its leaves emerge. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
