Carex dasycarpa var. tenax

Carex dasycarpa var. tenax, commonly known as the stiff-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it thrives in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
