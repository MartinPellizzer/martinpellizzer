Cistus × psilosepalus

Cistus × psilosepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, resinous scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
