Croton betaceus var. genuinus

Croton betaceus var. genuinus, commonly known as the white croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with contrasting white or yellow veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
