Cassia tristicula

Cassia tristicula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cassia tristicula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
