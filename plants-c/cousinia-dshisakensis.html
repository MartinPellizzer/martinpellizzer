Cousinia dshisakensis

Cousinia dshisakensis, commonly known as the Dshisakense thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Cousinia dshisakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
