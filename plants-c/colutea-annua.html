Colutea annua

Colutea annua, commonly known as common senna, is an annual legume native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces lens-shaped pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Colutea annua has a shallow root system and thrives in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and medicinal studies.
