Chrysanthemum fruticulosum

Chrysanthemum fruticulosum, commonly known as the hairy chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
