Campanula carpatica var. subpilosa

Campanula carpatica var. subpilosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are ovate and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
