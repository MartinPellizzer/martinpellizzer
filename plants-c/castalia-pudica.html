Castalia pudica

Castalia pudica, commonly known as the shy plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, pale yellow to white flowers that resemble small daisies. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Castalia pudica is known for its ability to close its flowers in response to touch, a trait that gives it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
