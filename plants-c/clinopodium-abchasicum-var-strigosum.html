Clinopodium abchasicum var. strigosum

Clinopodium abchasicum var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
