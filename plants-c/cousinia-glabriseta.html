Cousinia glabriseta

Cousinia glabriseta, commonly known as the smoothleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia glabriseta is often found in steppe and desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
