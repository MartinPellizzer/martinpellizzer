Cordia ferruginea

Cordia ferruginea, commonly known as the red cedar or ironwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
