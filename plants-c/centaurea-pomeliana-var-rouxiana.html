Centaurea pomeliana var. rouxiana

Centaurea pomeliana var. rouxiana, commonly known as the Roux's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces a single spike of flowers at the top of its stem. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
