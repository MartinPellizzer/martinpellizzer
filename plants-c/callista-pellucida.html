Callista pellucida

Callista pellucida, commonly known as the glassy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are often translucent or glassy in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Callista pellucida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
