Caesia parviflora var. minor

Caesia parviflora var. minor, commonly known as the small-flowered caesia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and spreads by rhizomes, forming dense clumps.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native garden landscaping.
