Calamus strictus

Calamus strictus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing compounds with medicinal properties.

Calamus strictus thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along riverbanks.
