Cnicus

Cnicus, commonly known as sandspurry, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

These plants are typically low-growing herbs with hairy stems and alternate leaves. They produce small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Cnicus species are often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.
