Crassocephalum sambamense

Crassocephalum sambamense, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Crassocephalum sambamense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
