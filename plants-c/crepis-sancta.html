Crepis sancta

Crepis sancta, commonly known as the sacred hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tiny disk florets, forming a distinctive floral structure. It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Crepis sancta is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
