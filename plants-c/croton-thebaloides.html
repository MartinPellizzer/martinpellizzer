Croton thebaloides

Croton thebaloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
