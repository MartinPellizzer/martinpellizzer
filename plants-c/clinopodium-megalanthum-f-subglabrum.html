Clinopodium megalanthum f. subglabrum

Clinopodium megalanthum f. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth giant betony, is a variant of the giant betony plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and stems, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are clustered in dense whorls and are typically blue to purple in color.

The subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and its preference for well-drained soils.
