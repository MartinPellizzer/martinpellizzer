Carex recurvirostra

Carex recurvirostra, commonly known as the recurved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and the edges of ponds.

Carex recurvirostra is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
