Coleus laxiflorus

Coleus laxiflorus, commonly known as the laxiflorous coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
