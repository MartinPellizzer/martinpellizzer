Carex divisa subsp. setifolia

Carex divisa subsp.

setifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

The subspecies setifolia is distinguished by its slender, elongated leaves and is often found in temperate regions of North America.
