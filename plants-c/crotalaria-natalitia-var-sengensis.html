Crotalaria natalitia var. sengensis

Crotalaria natalitia var. sengensis, commonly known as the Natal spurred crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive spurred shape, and it produces oblong pods that contain several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
