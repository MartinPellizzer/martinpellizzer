Carex aquatilis var. sphagnophila

Carex aquatilis var. sphagnophila, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in peat bogs and marshes. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and narrow, with a dense root system adapted to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
