Chromolaena mattogrossensis

Chromolaena mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian chromolaena, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges due to its adaptability and rapid growth.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a challenge to native vegetation.
