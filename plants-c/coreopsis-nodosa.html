Coreopsis nodosa

Coreopsis nodosa, commonly known as the clustered coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis nodosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
