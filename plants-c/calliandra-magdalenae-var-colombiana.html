Calliandra magdalenae var. colombiana

Calliandra magdalenae var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is part of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from pink to red, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various insects and small animals.
