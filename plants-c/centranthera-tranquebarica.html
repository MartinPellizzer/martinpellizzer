Centranthera tranquebarica

Centranthera tranquebarica, commonly known as the Transquebar orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor it in moist, well-drained soils, often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique floral display.
