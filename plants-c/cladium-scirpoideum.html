Cladium scirpoideum

Cladium scirpoideum, commonly known as the marsh cordgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

Cladium scirpoideum is well adapted to brackish and freshwater conditions, making it a key component of coastal and marsh ecosystems.
