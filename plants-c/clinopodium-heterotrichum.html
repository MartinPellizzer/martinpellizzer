Clinopodium heterotrichum

Clinopodium heterotrichum, commonly known as narrow-leaf mountain mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a minty fragrance when crushed.

The small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
