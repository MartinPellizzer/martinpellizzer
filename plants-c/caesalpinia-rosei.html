Caesalpinia rosei

Caesalpinia rosei, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like, bright red and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia rosei is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
