Chrysothamnus formosus

Chrysothamnus formosus, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The golden coneflower produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
