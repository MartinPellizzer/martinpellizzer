Clematis hispanica

Clematis hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive, tubular shape. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis hispanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be trained to climb trellises or walls.
