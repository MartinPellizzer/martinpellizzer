Canarium manii

Canarium manii, commonly known as the white oil palm, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are pollinated by insects. Its fruits are drupes that contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, yellowish pulp.

The tree is valued for its oil-rich seeds and is often cultivated for its economic and ecological benefits.
