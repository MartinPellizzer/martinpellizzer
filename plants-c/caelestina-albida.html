Caelestina albida

Caelestina albida, commonly known as the white moonflower, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The flowers emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
