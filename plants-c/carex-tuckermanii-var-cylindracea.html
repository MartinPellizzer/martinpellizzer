Carex tuckermanii var. cylindracea

Carex tuckermanii var. cylindracea, commonly known as Tuckerman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
