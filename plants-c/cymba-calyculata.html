Cymba calyculata

Cymba calyculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, pale green fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cymba calyculata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
