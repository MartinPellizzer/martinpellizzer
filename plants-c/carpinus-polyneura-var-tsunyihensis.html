Carpinus polyneura var. tsunyihensis

Carpinus polyneura var. tsunyihensis, commonly known as the Tsunyih mountain hornbeam, is a variant of the hornbeam genus native to eastern China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, round berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
