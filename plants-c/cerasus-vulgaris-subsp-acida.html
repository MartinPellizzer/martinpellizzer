Cerasus vulgaris subsp. acida

Cerasus vulgaris subsp.

acida, commonly known as sour cherry, is a subspecies of the cherry tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, tart fruits that are widely used in culinary applications such as jams, pies, and alcoholic beverages.

The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it well-suited for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
