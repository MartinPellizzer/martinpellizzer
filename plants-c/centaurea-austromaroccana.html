Centaurea austromaroccana

Centaurea austromaroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan centaury, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea austromaroccana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
