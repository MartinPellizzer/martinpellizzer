Cirsium grumosum

Cirsium grumosum, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Cirsium grumosum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
