Crambe hedgei

Crambe hedgei, commonly known as the hairy crampgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Crambe hedgei is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
