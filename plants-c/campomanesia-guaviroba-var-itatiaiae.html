Campomanesia guaviroba var. itatiaiae

Campomanesia guaviroba var. itatiaiae, commonly known as the Itatiaia guava, is a tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface. The fruit has a smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp, making it a valued species for both local consumption and potential commercial cultivation.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
