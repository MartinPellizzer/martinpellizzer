Cytisus major

Cytisus major, commonly known as the greater bird's-briar, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Cytisus major is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
