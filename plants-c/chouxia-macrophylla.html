Chouxia macrophylla

Chouxia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform on the water's surface. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support its massive foliage.

Chouxia macrophylla plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter to various aquatic organisms.
