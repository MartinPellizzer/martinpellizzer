Cordyline umbraculifera

Cordyline umbraculifera, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, palm-like leaves that form a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The plant produces striking purple flowers that appear in clusters at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Cordyline umbraculifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
