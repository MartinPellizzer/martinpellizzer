Cucurbita maxima var. microcina

Cucurbita maxima var. microcina, commonly known as the miniature pumpkin, is a compact variety of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is characterized by its small, round or oval-shaped fruits, typically ranging from 2 to 5 inches in diameter. The plant is a vine that produces lobed, palmately compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and suitability for small-space cultivation.
