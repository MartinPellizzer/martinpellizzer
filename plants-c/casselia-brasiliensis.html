Casselia brasiliensis

Casselia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cassia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Casselia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
