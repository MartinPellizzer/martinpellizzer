Cacalia tussilaginoides

Cacalia tussilaginoides, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly leaves and showy purple or pink composite flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant is often used in herbal medicine for its purported respiratory benefits.
