Calamagrostis epigejos subsp. glomerata

Calamagrostis epigejos subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the silvergrass or bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
