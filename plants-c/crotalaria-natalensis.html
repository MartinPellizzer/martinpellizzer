Crotalaria natalensis

Crotalaria natalensis, commonly known as Natal Crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Crotalaria natalensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
