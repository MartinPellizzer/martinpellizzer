Cerberiopsis neriifolia

Cerberiopsis neriifolia, commonly known as the white soursop or white custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, spiny fruits resembling a green, round melon. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching structure that supports its growth in humid, tropical climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruits.
