Chenopodium hircinum f. acutatum

Chenopodium hircinum f. acutatum, commonly known as the sharpleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant characterized by its acute, serrated leaf margins.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually alternate and lanceolate, with a rough texture that distinguishes it from other members of the genus.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
