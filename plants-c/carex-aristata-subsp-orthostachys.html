Carex aristata subsp. orthostachys

Carex aristata subsp.

orthostachys, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, erect leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
