Convolvulus agrophilus

Convolvulus agrophilus, commonly known as the field bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Convolvulus agrophilus is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain environments.
