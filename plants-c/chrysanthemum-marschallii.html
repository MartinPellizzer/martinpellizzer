Chrysanthemum marschallii

Chrysanthemum marschallii, commonly known as the marsh chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Chrysanthemum marschallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
