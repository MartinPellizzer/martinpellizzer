Casearia inaequilatera

Casearia inaequilatera, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright blue berries that are edible for birds and mammals. The plant has opposite, simple leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
