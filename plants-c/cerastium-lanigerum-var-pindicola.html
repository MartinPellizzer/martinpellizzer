Cerastium lanigerum var. pindicola

Cerastium lanigerum var. pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
