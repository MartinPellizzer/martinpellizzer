Coreopsis pubescens var. debilis

Coreopsis pubescens var. debilis, commonly known as the softleaf coreopsis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically reaches a height of 6 to 12 inches and has a spreading growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
