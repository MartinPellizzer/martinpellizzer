Cinna setifolia

Cinna setifolia, commonly known as the slender cinnamon fern, is a member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has a distinctive sterile frond that is typically lighter in color compared to the fertile fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Cinna setifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
