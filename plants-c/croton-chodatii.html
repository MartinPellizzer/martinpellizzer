Croton chodatii

Croton chodatii, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated foliage. The leaves are typically alternate and can be broadly ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
