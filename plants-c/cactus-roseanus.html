Cactus roseanus

Cactus roseanus, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Cactus roseanus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
