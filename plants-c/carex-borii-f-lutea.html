Carex borii f. lutea

Carex borii f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are often a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wet or moist environments. It is often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
