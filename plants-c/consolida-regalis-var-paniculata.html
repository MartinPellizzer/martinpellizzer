Consolida regalis var. paniculata

Consolida regalis var. paniculata, commonly known as the royal poppy or panicled consolida, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, arranged in loose, branching clusters called paniculata. The plant has slender, ridged stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, colorful blooms.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to wildflower gardens and natural landscapes.
