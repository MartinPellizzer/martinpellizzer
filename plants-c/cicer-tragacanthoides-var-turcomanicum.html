Cicer tragacanthoides var. turcomanicum

Cicer tragacanthoides var. turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
