Campomanesia dichotoma

Campomanesia dichotoma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making it popular in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic qualities.
