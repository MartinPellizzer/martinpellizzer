Cirsium alpini

Cirsium alpini, commonly known as the alpine thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Cirsium alpini has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
