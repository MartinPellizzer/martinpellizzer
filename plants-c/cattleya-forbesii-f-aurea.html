Cattleya forbesii f. aurea

Cattleya forbesii f. aurea, commonly known as the Golden Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces large, fragrant blooms that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
