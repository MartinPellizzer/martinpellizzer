Carex filiformis var. occultans

Carex filiformis var. occultans, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage remains green throughout the growing season, providing year-round interest in wetland gardens.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
