Carex pseudocyperus f. brachystachya

Carex pseudocyperus f. brachystachya, commonly known as the short-stalked sedges, is a variant of the broader Carex pseudocyperus species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure supports its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
