Cactus ceratocentrus

Cactus ceratocentrus, commonly known as the fishhook cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that are often ribbed and covered in white or grayish hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
