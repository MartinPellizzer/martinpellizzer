Crataegus cordifolia var. matura

Crataegus cordifolia var. matura, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features elliptic to ovate leaves that are heart-shaped at the base, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red, juicy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
