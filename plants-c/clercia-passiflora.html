Clercia passiflora

Clercia passiflora, commonly known as the passion flower vine, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its striking, intricate flowers.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is native to regions of Central and South America. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, colorful blooms with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of stamens. Its fruit, known as a passion fruit, is edible and contains a juicy, seed-filled pulp.

Clercia passiflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
