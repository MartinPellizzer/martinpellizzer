Canna ventricosa

Canna ventricosa, commonly known as the giant cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and often brightly colored leaf-like bracts that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which are typically borne on spikes. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed.

Canna ventricosa thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic foliage.
