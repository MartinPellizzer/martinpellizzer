Coaxana

Coaxana, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, often with a rosette of leaves at the base. Coaxana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a favorite among indoor gardeners worldwide.
