Cineraria cruenta

Cineraria cruenta, commonly known as the blueweed or cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cineraria cruenta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
