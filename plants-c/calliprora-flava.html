Calliprora flava

Calliprora flava, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Calliprora flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
