Cyclamen rhodium subsp. rhodium

Cyclamen rhodium subsp.

rhodium, commonly known as the silver cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers with a showy, spurred petal structure typical of the cyclamen genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its elegant foliage and blooms. Cyclamen rhodium subsp.

rhodium is well-suited for shady garden areas and indoor cultivation.
