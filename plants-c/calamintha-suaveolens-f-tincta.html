Calamintha suaveolens f. tincta

Calamintha suaveolens f. tincta, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are often used in herbal medicine for their mild sedative and digestive properties.

The variety 'tincta' is distinguished by its more intense fragrance and slightly different floral structure compared to the typical form.
