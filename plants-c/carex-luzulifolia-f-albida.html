Carex luzulifolia f. albida

Carex luzulifolia f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its striking white floral display, making it a desirable ornamental plant in aquatic gardens.
