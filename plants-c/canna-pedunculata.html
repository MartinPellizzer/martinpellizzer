Canna pedunculata

Canna pedunculata, commonly known as the giant canna, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the Americas.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often with a reddish or purple tint along the veins. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Canna pedunculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
