Clusia mutica

Clusia mutica, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers that are often clustered in spikes. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Clusia mutica is also known for its resilience, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
