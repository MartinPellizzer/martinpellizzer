Cynorkis siederi

Cynorkis siederi, commonly known as the Sieder's cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate to flourish.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
