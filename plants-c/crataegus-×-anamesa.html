Crataegus × anamesa

Crataegus × anamesa, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna (common hawthorn) and Crataegus laevigata (smooth hawthorn).

It typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters and features thorned branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
