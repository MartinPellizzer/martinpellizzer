Carex ivanoviae

Carex ivanoviae, commonly known as Ivanov's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Carex ivanoviae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
