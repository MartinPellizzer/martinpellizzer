Caesalpinia torquata

Caesalpinia torquata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that are borne in long, pendulous clusters, giving it a striking appearance during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is often fissured and rough in texture. Caesalpinia torquata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

It also has cultural and medicinal significance in some traditional practices.
