Columnea steyermarkii

Columnea steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a waxy texture, and its stems are often covered in a fine, hairy layer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Columnea steyermarkii is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for hummingbirds and other pollinators.
