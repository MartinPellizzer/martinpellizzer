Clinopodium ascendens

Clinopodium ascendens, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, nut-like fruits.

Clinopodium ascendens is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
