Cymbidium elegans var. lushuiense

Cymbidium elegans var. lushuiense, commonly known as the Lushui orchid, is a rare and elegant species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
