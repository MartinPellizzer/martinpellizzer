Centaurea dissecta

Centaurea dissecta, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance, and it produces striking blue to purple flowers.

Centaurea dissecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
