Carex nemarosa

Carex nemarosa, commonly known as the water sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, usually green or brownish flowers that are clustered at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Carex nemarosa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
