Chrysopsis sessiliflora var. echioides

Chrysopsis sessiliflora var. echioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
