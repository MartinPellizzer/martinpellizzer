Cymbachne guineensis var. auriculata

Cymbachne guineensis var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden ear grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, elongated leaves with a distinctive golden sheen on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
