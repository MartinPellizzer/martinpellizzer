Cineraria malvifolia

Cineraria malvifolia, commonly known as the woolly cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cineraria malvifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.
