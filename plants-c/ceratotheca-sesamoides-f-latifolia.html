Ceratotheca sesamoides f. latifolia

Ceratotheca sesamoides f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sesamoid sesamum, is a variant of the sesamoid sesamum plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, arid climates.
