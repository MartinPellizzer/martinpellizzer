Carex helodes var. maurusia

Carex helodes var. maurusia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. This sedge typically grows in marshy areas, stream banks, and other damp habitats, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
