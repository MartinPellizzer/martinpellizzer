Crotalaria novae-hollandiae f. oblongifolia

Crotalaria novae-hollandiae f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silverleaf, is a variant of the silverleaf crotalaria, characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves.

It is a herbaceous plant native to Australia, typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a unique, spurred structure. Its foliage is covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it resilient in harsh environments.
