Carex dahurica

Carex dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex dahurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
