Crepinia psilophylla

Crepinia psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Crepinia psilophylla plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
