Cirsium ferox var. costae

Cirsium ferox var. costae, commonly known as the spiked thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its prickly nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
