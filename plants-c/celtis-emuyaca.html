Celtis emuyaca

Celtis emuyaca, commonly known as the Mexican hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, white, fleshy berries that attract various wildlife.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in arid and semi-arid regions.
