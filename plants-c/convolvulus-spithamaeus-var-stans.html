Convolvulus spithamaeus var. stans

Convolvulus spithamaeus var. stans, commonly known as the pale morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its rapid growth habit and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various horticultural applications.
