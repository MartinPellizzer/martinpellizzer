Celosia taitoensis

Celosia taitoensis, commonly known as the Taitoensis plume, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, feathery bracts that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are usually red or orange, adding a striking visual element to gardens.

Celosia taitoensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and resilience.
