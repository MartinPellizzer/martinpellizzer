Cuscuta acuta

Cuscuta acuta, commonly known as the slender dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a distinctive appearance with thin, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to various regions of the world, including North America, and is often found on grasses and other herbaceous plants. Cuscuta acuta has a rapid growth rate and can quickly infest large areas, making it a troublesome weed in agricultural settings.

Its life cycle involves producing seeds that can remain viable in the soil for many years.
