Carduus aemilii

Carduus aemilii, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Carduus aemilii is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
