Clematis viticella var. atrorubens

Clematis viticella var. atrorubens, commonly known as the red-flowered Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are showy, with a distinctive red and purple coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white fruits that mature into dry, round berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
