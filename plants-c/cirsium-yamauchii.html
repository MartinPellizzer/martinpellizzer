Cirsium yamauchii

Cirsium yamauchii, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Cirsium yamauchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
