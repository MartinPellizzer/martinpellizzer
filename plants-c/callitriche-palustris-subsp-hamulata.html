Callitriche palustris subsp. hamulata

Callitriche palustris subsp.

hamulata, commonly known as the water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant found in wetlands and shallow water habitats. It has delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are typically borne on short stalks. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which can help stabilize sediments in aquatic environments.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to fluctuating water levels.
