Crataegus crus-galli var. pyracanthifolia

Crataegus crus-galli var. pyracanthifolia, commonly known as the red-berried hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
