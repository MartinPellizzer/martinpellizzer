Cycnium albersii

Cycnium albersii, commonly known as the yellow wood nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Cycnium albersii is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
