Cyperus bernieri

Cyperus bernieri, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies. Its slender, grass-like stems are often mistaken for sedges, but it has distinct floral structures typical of the sedge family.

Cyperus bernieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a decorative plant in humid environments.
