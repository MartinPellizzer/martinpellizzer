Ceratolobus kingianus

Ceratolobus kingianus, commonly known as the King Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of King Island in Tasmania.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on long, slender stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in coastal and sandy soils. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, and it is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.

The King Island hibiscus is also known for its fragrant blooms, which attract various pollinators such as birds and insects.
