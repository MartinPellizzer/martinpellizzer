Cryptocarya oblonga

Cryptocarya oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

The plant has some ornamental value and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
