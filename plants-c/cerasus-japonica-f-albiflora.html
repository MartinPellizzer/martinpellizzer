Cerasus japonica f. albiflora

Cerasus japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white blossoms that appear in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a pointed tip, turning yellow in autumn.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
