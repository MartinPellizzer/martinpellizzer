Carex canescens var. dubia

Carex canescens var. dubia, commonly known as the gray sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish-brown flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
