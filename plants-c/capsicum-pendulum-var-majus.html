Capsicum pendulum var. majus

Capsicum pendulum var. majus, commonly known as the giant hanging pepper, is a unique variant of the pepper plant characterized by its long, pendulous fruit clusters.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces elongated, finger-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often turning red or yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and mild, sweet flavor, making it a popular choice for both gardens and culinary use.
