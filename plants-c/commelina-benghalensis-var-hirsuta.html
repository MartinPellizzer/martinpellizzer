Commelina benghalensis var. hirsuta

Commelina benghalensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
