Campanula elegans

Campanula elegans, commonly known as the elegant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
