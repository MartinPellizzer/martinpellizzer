Crepis albida subsp. grosii

Crepis albida subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, growing in rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces achenes with pappus, which aid in wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
