Crucifera corvini

Crucifera corvini, commonly known as the Blackleaf Mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or white, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is also known for its peppery taste, making it a potential candidate for culinary or medicinal use.
