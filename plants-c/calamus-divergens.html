Calamus divergens

Calamus divergens, commonly known as the divergent reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional basket-making and weaving. Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties.

Calamus divergens thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
