Cordylanthus pilosus subsp. pilosus

Cordylanthus pilosus subsp.

pilosus, commonly known as the hairy beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
