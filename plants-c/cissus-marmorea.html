Cissus marmorea

Cissus marmorea, commonly known as the marble vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often variegated leaves that resemble marble. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus marmorea is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
