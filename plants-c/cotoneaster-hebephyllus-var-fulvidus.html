Cotoneaster hebephyllus var. fulvidus

Cotoneaster hebephyllus var. fulvidus, commonly known as the golden cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries in the autumn. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
