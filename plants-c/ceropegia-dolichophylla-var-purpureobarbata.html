Ceropegia dolichophylla var. purpureobarbata

Ceropegia dolichophylla var. purpureobarbata, commonly known as the purple-barred African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to purple in color. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a waxy texture and often bear a purple or reddish tinge on the underside. This species thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
