Chloraea disoides var. disoides

Chloraea disoides var. disoides, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like structure with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the southern hemisphere's summer, usually from December to February.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
