Ceroxylon mooreanum

Ceroxylon mooreanum, commonly known as the Moorean wax tree, is a tall, evergreen tree native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are adapted to the humid and often high-rainfall conditions of its habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, woody, cone-like fruits. Its trunk is often covered in a thick layer of resin, which gives it a unique appearance and helps protect it from pests and environmental stress.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in its native region.
