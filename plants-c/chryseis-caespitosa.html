Chryseis caespitosa

Chryseis caespitosa, commonly known as the golden tufted grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, with slender, thread-like bracts.

Chryseis caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
