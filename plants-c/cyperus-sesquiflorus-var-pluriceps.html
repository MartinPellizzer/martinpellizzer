Cyperus sesquiflorus var. pluriceps

Cyperus sesquiflorus var. pluriceps, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in spikelets at the base of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
