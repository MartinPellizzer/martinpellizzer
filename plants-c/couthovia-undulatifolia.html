Couthovia undulatifolia

Couthovia undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its delicate, undulating fronds.

Native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically bi-colored, with a pale underside that contrasts with the darker upper surface.

Couthovia undulatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
