Campanula mairei var. anremerica

Campanula mairei var. anremerica, commonly known as the Anremerica bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula mairei var. anremerica is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and its ability to attract pollinators.
