Chlorocyperus cimicinus

Chlorocyperus cimicinus, commonly known as the golden flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and rhizomes, which aid in its spread.

It is often found in wetlands, riparian zones, and other waterlogged habitats.
