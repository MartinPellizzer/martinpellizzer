Callopisma perfoliatum var. angustifolium

Callopisma perfoliatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cow-weed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters. The leaves are lance-shaped and narrow, with a prominent midrib that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
