Cactus dillenii

Cactus dillenii, commonly known as the Dillen's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, waxy spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a notable example of succulent evolution in desert ecosystems.
