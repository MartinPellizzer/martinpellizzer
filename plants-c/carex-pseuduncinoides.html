Carex pseuduncinoides

Carex pseuduncinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Carex pseuduncinoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
