Carex jaluensis

Carex jaluensis, commonly known as the Jalu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along streams. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex jaluensis produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
