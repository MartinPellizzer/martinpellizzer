Cassandra angustifolia

Cassandra angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cassandra, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
