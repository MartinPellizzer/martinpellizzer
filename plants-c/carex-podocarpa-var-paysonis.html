Carex podocarpa var. paysonis

Carex podocarpa var. paysonis, commonly known as Payson sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish in color.

Payson sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
