Cotoneaster coadunatus

Cotoneaster coadunatus, commonly known as the Cabbage Tree Cotoneaster, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cotoneaster coadunatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
