Campanula gmelinii

Campanula gmelinii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Campanula gmelinii has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
