Canna coccinea

Canna coccinea, commonly known as the red canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange, which are often mistaken for petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its broad, lance-shaped leaves are a deep green and can grow up to 1 meter in length.

Canna coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a tropical foliage plant.
