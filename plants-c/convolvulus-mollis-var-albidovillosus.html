Convolvulus mollis var. albidovillosus

Convolvulus mollis var. albidovillosus, commonly known as the white hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens.
