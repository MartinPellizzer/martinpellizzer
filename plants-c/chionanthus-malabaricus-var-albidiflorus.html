Chionanthus malabaricus var. albidiflorus

Chionanthus malabaricus var. albidiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
