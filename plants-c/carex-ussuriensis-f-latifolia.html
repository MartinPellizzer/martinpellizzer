Carex ussuriensis f. latifolia

Carex ussuriensis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It is native to regions of Asia, including parts of Russia and China.

The broad-leaved sedge is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various conditions.
