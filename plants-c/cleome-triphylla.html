Cleome triphylla

Cleome triphylla, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, spindly flowers that resemble spiders, typically blooming in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and it is often found in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and marshes. Cleome triphylla is an annual plant that thrives in warm climates and is popular in gardens for its ornamental value.
