Calceolaria forgetii

Calceolaria forgetii, commonly known as the Chilean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Calceolaria forgetii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
