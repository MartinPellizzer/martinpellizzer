Curcuma petiolata

Curcuma petiolata, commonly known as the black ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with green and white patterns.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange and are borne on terminal inflorescences, adding ornamental value to the plant.
