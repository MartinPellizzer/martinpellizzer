Chelone obliqua var. speciosa

Chelone obliqua var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy heartleaf twinflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
