Cyphomandra verruculosa

Cyphomandra verruculosa, commonly known as the rough sour apple, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The fruit is a small, sour-tasting berry that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
