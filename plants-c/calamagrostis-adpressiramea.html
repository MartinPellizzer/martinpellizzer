Calamagrostis adpressiramea

Calamagrostis adpressiramea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis adpressiramea is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
