Chimonocalamus bicorniculatus

Chimonocalamus bicorniculatus, commonly known as the two-horned bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
