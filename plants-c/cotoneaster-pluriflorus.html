Cotoneaster pluriflorus

Cotoneaster pluriflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Cotoneaster pluriflorus is often used in landscaping for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
