Campanula lingulata

Campanula lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
