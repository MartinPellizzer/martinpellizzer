Cynoglossum amplifolium var. subalpinum

Cynoglossum amplifolium var. subalpinum, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variant is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
