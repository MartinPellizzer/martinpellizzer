Centaurium spicatum f. elegans

Centaurium spicatum f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant mouse-ear.

This plant is a herbaceous species characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The flowers are usually pink or red, and the leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or red flowers.
