Campanula hispanica subsp. catalanica

Campanula hispanica subsp.

catalanica, commonly known as Catalan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
