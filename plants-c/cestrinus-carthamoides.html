Cestrinus carthamoides

Cestrinus carthamoides, commonly known as the common groundsel, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stem and basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world.

Cestrinus carthamoides is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
