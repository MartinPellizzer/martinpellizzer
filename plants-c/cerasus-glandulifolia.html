Cerasus glandulifolia

Cerasus glandulifolia, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glandular underside, giving it its scientific name.

Cerasus glandulifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms and hardy nature.
