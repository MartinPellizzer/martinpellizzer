Castanea crenata var. pendula

Castanea crenata var. pendula, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or weeping chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that give it a weeping appearance, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and serrated. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that enclose edible nuts, which are typically harvested in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
