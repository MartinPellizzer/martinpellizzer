Chasseloupia montana

Chasseloupia montana, commonly known as mountain chasseloupia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Chasseloupia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
