Cephalocereus machrisii

Cephalocereus machrisii, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, spherical flowers that bloom at night, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and produces small, pale green leaves during its juvenile stage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

The flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.
