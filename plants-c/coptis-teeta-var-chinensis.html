Coptis teeta var. chinensis

Coptis teeta var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China and Nepal.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its yellow rhizomes, which contain the alkaloid berberine. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in moist, rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to elliptic leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
