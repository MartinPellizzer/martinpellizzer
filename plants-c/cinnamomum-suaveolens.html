Cinnamomum suaveolens

Cinnamomum suaveolens, commonly known as the fragrant cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
