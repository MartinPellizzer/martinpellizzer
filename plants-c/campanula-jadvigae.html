Campanula jadvigae

Campanula jadvigae, commonly known as the Jadviga bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, clustered blooms during the summer months.

Campanula jadvigae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
