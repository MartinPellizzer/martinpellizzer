Crocus fragrans

Crocus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-colored blooms with a distinctive, sweet scent. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Crocus fragrans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
