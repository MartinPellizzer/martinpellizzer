Celtis cinnamomea

Celtis cinnamomea, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Elm family (Ulmaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
