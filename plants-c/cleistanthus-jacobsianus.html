Cleistanthus jacobsianus

Cleistanthus jacobsianus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple center, and they bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and disturbed areas.
