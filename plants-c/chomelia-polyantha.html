Chomelia polyantha

Chomelia polyantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chomelia polyantha is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its use in traditional medicine.
