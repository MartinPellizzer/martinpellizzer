Carex scopulorum

Carex scopulorum, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer months. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Carex scopulorum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
