Cyperus glaber var. subchoristachys

Cyperus glaber var. subchoristachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. This variety is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
