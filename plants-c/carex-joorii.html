Carex joorii

Carex joorii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.

Carex joorii prefers partial to full shade and is relatively low maintenance once established.
