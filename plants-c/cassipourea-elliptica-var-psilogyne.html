Cassipourea elliptica var. psilogyne

Cassipourea elliptica var. psilogyne, commonly known as the red-veined cassipourea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its distinctive elliptical leaves with prominent red veins. The leaves are usually glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the reddish undersides. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
