Cyanocephalus incanus

Cyanocephalus incanus, commonly known as the blue-headed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a succulent cactus characterized by its blue-gray stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
