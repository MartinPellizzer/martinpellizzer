Cycas rumphii f. seemannii

Cycas rumphii f. seemannii, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique variant of the cycad genus Cycas.

It is characterized by its compact size and distinctive foliage, featuring pinnately compound leaves with a bluish-green hue. Native to the Philippines, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a popular choice for conservation and horticultural purposes.

The plant's cycadophyta lineage reflects its ancient evolutionary history, dating back to the Mesozoic era.
