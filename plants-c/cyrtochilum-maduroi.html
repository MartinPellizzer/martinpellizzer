Cyrtochilum maduroi

Cyrtochilum maduroi, commonly known as the Maduroi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers that bloom during the dry season. Cyrtochilum maduroi prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
