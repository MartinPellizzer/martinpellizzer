Caryocar microcarpum

Caryocar microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited cupuacu, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are encased in a thick, woody shell. The fruit's interior contains a single large seed, which is surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

This species is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
