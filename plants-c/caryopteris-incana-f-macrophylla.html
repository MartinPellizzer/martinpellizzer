Caryopteris incana f. macrophylla

Caryopteris incana f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved blue mistflower, is a cultivar of the silverblue mistflower.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers in late summer, which are arranged in whorled inflorescences. Its leaves are larger than those of the typical species, often reaching up to 3 inches in length.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil types.
