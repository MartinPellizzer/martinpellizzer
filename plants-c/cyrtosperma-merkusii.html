Cyrtosperma merkusii

Cyrtosperma merkusii, commonly known as the Malabar arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and parts of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in an inflorescence, with a spathe that encloses the spadix.

The plant is cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a source of carbohydrates.
