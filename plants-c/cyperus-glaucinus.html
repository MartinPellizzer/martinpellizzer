Cyperus glaucinus

Cyperus glaucinus, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among its foliage. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Cyperus glaucinus is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and adaptability.
