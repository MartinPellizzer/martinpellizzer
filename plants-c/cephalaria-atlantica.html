Cephalaria atlantica

Cephalaria atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
