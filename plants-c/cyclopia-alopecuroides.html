Cyclopia alopecuroides

Cyclopia alopecuroides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine.

Cyclopia alopecuroides is also cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
