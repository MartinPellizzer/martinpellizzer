Carex myosurus var. floribunda

Carex myosurus var. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

The flowering sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
