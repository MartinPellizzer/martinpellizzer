Carex acuta subsp. intermedia

Carex acuta subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures consist of small, three-parted flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
