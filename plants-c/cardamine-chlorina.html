Cardamine chlorina

Cardamine chlorina, commonly known as the green watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
