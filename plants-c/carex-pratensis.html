Carex pratensis

Carex pratensis, commonly known as the meadow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

Carex pratensis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
