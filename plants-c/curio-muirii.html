Curio muirii

Curio muirii, commonly known as the Muir's curio, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or white. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its leaves and stems, making it drought-tolerant.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
