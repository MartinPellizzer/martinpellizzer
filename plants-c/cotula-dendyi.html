Cotula dendyi

Cotula dendyi, commonly known as the tree cotula, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Cotula dendyi has a compact growth habit with woody stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
