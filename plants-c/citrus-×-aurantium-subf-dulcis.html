Citrus × aurantium subf. dulcis

Citrus × aurantium subf.

dulcis, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a species within the Rutaceae family. It is characterized by its smooth, thick rind and segmented, juicy interior filled with aromatic pulp.

The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a leathery texture.

The flowers are fragrant, white, and clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by the characteristic citrus fruit.
