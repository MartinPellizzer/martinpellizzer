Cyperus maranguensis var. ferrugineoviridis

Cyperus maranguensis var. ferrugineoviridis, commonly known as the rusty-green flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy tissue. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside.

This variation is notable for its unique pigmentation and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
