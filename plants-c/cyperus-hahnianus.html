Cyperus hahnianus

Cyperus hahnianus, commonly known as the Hahn's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it produces small, three-sided spikelets that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Cyperus hahnianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
