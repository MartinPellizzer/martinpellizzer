Crotalaria remotiflora

Crotalaria remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent black markings. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Crotalaria remotiflora is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
