Conophytum altile

Conophytum altile, commonly known as the rock tulip, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Conophytum altile is prized for its unique appearance and ability to bloom despite its harsh environment.
