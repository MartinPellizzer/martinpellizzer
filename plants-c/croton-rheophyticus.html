Croton rheophyticus

Croton rheophyticus, commonly known as the stream croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments near streams or in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits.

Croton rheophyticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
