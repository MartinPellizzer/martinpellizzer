Calamus tumidus

Calamus tumidus, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its underground stems produce starchy tubers, which are edible and have been used as a food source.

Calamus tumidus also plays a role in traditional crafts, with its leaves used for making mats and thatching.
