Coleus suffruticosus

Coleus suffruticosus, commonly known as the creeping coleus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves ranging from red, purple, and maroon to green and variegated patterns. The plant typically grows in a low, spreading habit, often forming dense mats or trailing vines.

Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the cultivar. Coleus suffruticosus thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking leaf colors.
