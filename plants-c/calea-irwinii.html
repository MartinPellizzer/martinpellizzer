Calea irwinii

Calea irwinii, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescence that opens at night. The plant features dark, velvety leaves with a distinctive pattern of white veins, which are more visible under moonlight. Its flowers, which bloom in the evening, are often described as having a ghostly or ethereal appearance.

Calea irwinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
