Ceradenia comosa

Ceradenia comosa, commonly known as the woolly ceradenia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
