Clinopodium argenteum

Clinopodium argenteum, commonly known as silver betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in silvery-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Silver betony thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
