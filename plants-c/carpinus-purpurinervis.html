Carpinus purpurinervis

Carpinus purpurinervis, commonly known as the purple-veined hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with prominent purple veins. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
