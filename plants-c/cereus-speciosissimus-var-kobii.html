Cereus speciosissimus var. kobii

Cereus speciosissimus var. kobii, commonly known as the Kobii night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, funnel-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to drought conditions.

The plant is well-suited for cultivation in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
