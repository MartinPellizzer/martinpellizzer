Chenopodium longidjawense

Chenopodium longidjawense, commonly known as the long-beaked goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Chenopodium longidjawense is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
