Cardamomum anthodiodes

Cardamomum anthodiodes, commonly known as the Indian cardamom or small cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Zingiberaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, pale green flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes, which are the primary part used, contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive spicy aroma.

Cardamomum anthodiodes is valued both for its culinary uses and traditional medicinal properties.
