Conophytum

Conophytum, commonly known as the "living stones," is a genus of succulent plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing them to thrive in arid environments. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps minimize water loss through evaporation. Conophytum species produce small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, usually during the warmer months.

Their unique appearance and ability to survive in harsh conditions make them popular among succulent enthusiasts.
