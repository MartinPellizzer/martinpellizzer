Conchograecum cribbianum

Conchograecum cribbianum, commonly known as the Caribbean star cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in arid environments.

Conchograecum cribbianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical climates.
