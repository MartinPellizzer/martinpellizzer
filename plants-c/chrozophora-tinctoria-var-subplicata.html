Chrozophora tinctoria var. subplicata

Chrozophora tinctoria var. subplicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in loose inflorescences. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a waxy coating. This variety is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in dyeing fabrics.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
