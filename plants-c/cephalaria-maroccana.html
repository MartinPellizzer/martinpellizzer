Cephalaria maroccana

Cephalaria maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flower heads that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
