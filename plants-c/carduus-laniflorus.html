Carduus laniflorus

Carduus laniflorus, commonly known as the downy thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces tall, spiny stems that bear clusters of purple to pinkish flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its flowering heads are composed of many tiny florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Carduus laniflorus is often found in dry grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in disturbed soils.
