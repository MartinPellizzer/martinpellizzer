Chamaesyce ammatotricha

Chamaesyce ammatotricha, commonly known as the woolly sand mat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Chamaesyce ammatotricha plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
