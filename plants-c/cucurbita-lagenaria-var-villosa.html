Cucurbita lagenaria var. villosa

Cucurbita lagenaria var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, inflated, pale green to yellowish fruit covered in coarse, hairy bumps. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. Its flowers are typically yellow with five lobes, and the plant produces both male and female flowers on the same vine.

The hairy gourd is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
