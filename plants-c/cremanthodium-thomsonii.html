Cremanthodium thomsonii

Cremanthodium thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, globe-shaped inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Cremanthodium thomsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
