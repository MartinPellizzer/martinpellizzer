Cephalanthus africanus

Cephalanthus africanus, commonly known as the African candlewood, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Cephalanthus africanus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
