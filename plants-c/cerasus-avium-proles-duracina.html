Cerasus avium proles duracina

Cerasus avium proles duracina, commonly known as the duracina cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus avium species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit that matures to a deep red color. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring pinnate leaves and showy white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
