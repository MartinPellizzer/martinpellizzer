Coreocarpus arizonicus var. typicus

Coreocarpus arizonicus var. typicus, commonly known as the Arizona coreocarpus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, elongated fruit capsules. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
