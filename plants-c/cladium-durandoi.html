Cladium durandoi

Cladium durandoi, commonly known as the dwarf sawgrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize the soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Cladium durandoi plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitat.
