Corymbia dunlopiana

Corymbia dunlopiana, commonly known as the Dunlop's bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears flaky or peeling, and its small, elliptical leaves that are usually grey-green in color. The tree produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the winter months.

Its hard, dense wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in traditional and contemporary woodworking applications.
