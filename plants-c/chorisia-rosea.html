Chorisia rosea

Chorisia rosea, commonly known as the silk floss tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, woolly pods that resemble floss, which give it its common name. The plant has a tall, slender trunk with smooth, pale bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used for medicinal purposes. It produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The silk floss tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
