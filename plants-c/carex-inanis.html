Carex inanis

Carex inanis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. Native to wetlands and moist soils in North America, it thrives in shallow water or damp environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
