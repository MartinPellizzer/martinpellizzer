Carex parvigluma

Carex parvigluma, commonly known as the small-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually a dark green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Carex parvigluma is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and related plants.
