Cyperus obtusiflorus var. rigidus

Cyperus obtusiflorus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
