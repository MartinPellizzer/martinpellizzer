Cassia indecora var. pendula

Cassia indecora var. pendula, commonly known as weeping cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. The leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
