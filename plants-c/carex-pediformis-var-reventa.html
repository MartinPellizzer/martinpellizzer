Carex pediformis var. reventa

Carex pediformis var. reventa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly folded along the midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
