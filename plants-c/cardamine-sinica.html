Cardamine sinica

Cardamine sinica, commonly known as Chinese watercress, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Cardamine sinica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.
