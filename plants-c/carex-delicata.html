Carex delicata

Carex delicata, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex delicata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
