Chrysocyathus distortus

Chrysocyathus distortus, commonly known as the distorted cup coral.

It is a species of stony coral characterized by its unique, branched morphology and vibrant coloration. Native to shallow tropical waters, it thrives in clear, warm marine environments. The coral's structure consists of calcareous skeletal elements formed by the deposition of calcium carbonate.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various marine organisms and contributing to reef biodiversity.
