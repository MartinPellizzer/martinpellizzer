Ceanothus griseus

Ceanothus griseus, commonly known as gray ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a dense, fuzzy texture that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Ceanothus griseus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
