Carex ochrostachya

Carex ochrostachya, commonly known as the white-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often white or pale in color.

Carex ochrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
