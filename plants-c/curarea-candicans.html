Curarea candicans

Curarea candicans, commonly known as the golden arrow poison plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers and is notable for containing curare, a potent neurotoxin used historically by indigenous peoples.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural toxins for research.
