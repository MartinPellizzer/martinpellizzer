Croton antisyphiliticus var. hypoleucus

Croton antisyphiliticus var. hypoleucus, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, variegated foliage.

The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a pale green to almost white coloration contrasting with darker veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
