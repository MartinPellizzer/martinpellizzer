Chiogenes japonica

Chiogenes japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
