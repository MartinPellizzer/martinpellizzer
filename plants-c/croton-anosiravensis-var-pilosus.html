Croton anosiravensis var. pilosus

Croton anosiravensis var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
