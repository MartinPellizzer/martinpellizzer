Cephalaria maroccana var. glabrescens

Cephalaria maroccana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Moroccan goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, though the variety glabrescens is distinguished by its smoother, glabrous foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
