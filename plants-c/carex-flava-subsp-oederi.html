Carex flava subsp. oederi

Carex flava subsp.

oederi, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spicate inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
