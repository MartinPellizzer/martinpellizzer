Colubrina faraloatra var. trichocarpa

Colubrina faraloatra var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a dense, bushy form.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal hammocks and dry woodlands.
