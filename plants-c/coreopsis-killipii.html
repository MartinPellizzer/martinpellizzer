Coreopsis killipii

Coreopsis killipii, commonly known as the Killip's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with ray florets that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Coreopsis killipii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
