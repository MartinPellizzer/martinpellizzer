Campanula matritensis var. nevadensis

Campanula matritensis var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Nevada.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
