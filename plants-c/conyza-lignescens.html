Conyza lignescens

Conyza lignescens, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Conyza lignescens is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
