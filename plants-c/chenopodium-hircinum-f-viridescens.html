Chenopodium hircinum f. viridescens

Chenopodium hircinum f. viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
