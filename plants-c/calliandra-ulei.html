Calliandra ulei

Calliandra ulei, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces bright red or pink flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, delicate leaflets.

Calliandra ulei is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
