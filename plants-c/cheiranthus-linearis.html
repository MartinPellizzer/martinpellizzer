Cheiranthus linearis

Cheiranthus linearis, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is often found growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
