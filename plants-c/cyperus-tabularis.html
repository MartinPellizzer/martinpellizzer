Cyperus tabularis

Cyperus tabularis, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Cyperus tabularis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
