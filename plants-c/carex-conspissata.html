Carex conspissata

Carex conspissata, commonly known as the upland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex conspissata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
