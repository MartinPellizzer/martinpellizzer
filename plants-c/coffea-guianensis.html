Coffea guianensis

Coffea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese coffee or Brazilian coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and white with a sweet fragrance.
