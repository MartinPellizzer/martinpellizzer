Callianthemum rutifolium subsp. coriandrifolium

Callianthemum rutifolium subsp.

coriandrifolium, commonly known as the Corsican carnation, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and branched. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
