Cissus micradenia

Cissus micradenia, commonly known as the creeping fig or silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is characterized by its ability to climb using adventitious roots that form along its stems.

Cissus micradenia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
