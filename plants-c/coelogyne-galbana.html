Coelogyne galbana

Coelogyne galbana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with red and purple streaks, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in seasonal climates. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne galbana is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
