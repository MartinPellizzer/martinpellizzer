Crepis gillii var. bellidifolia

Crepis gillii var. bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifolia mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are visited by various pollinators. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is part of the genus Crepis, which includes many species with similar morphological features.
