Convolvulus filiformis

Convolvulus filiformis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and a distinctive spur.

Convolvulus filiformis is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
