Carex hoozanensis

Carex hoozanensis, commonly known as Hoozan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

Carex hoozanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
