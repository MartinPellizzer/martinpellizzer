Cheiranthus longisiliquus

Cheiranthus longisiliquus, commonly known as the long silique wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods called siliques that contain its seeds. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.
