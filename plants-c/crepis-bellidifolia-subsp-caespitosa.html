Crepis bellidifolia subsp. caespitosa

Crepis bellidifolia subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, while the stem is usually shorter than the leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in early summer.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
