Centaurea cilicica

Centaurea cilicica, commonly known as Cilician centaury, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and Cyprus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Centaurea cilicica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
