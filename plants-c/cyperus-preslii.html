Cyperus preslii

Cyperus preslii, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus preslii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
