Chloranthus oldhamii

Chloranthus oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's spikenard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and the Philippines. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Chloranthus oldhamii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
