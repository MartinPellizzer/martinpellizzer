Columnea hirta var. hirta

Columnea hirta var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy whorled coca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
