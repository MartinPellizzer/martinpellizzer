Cryptantha glomerulifera

Cryptantha glomerulifera, commonly known as the clustered flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in late spring.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
