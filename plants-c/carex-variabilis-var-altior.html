Carex variabilis var. altior

Carex variabilis var. altior, commonly known as the tall variable sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its tall stature, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

This sedge is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
