Chenopodium sancta-maria

Chenopodium sancta-maria, commonly known as Saint Mary's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the seeds are small and clustered in dense heads.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported antiparasitic and digestive properties.
