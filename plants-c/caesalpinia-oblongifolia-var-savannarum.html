Caesalpinia oblongifolia var. savannarum

Caesalpinia oblongifolia var. savannarum, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble peacock feathers, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
