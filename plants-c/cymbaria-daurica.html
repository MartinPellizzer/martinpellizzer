Cymbaria daurica

Cymbaria daurica, commonly known as Daurian cymbaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cymbaria daurica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
