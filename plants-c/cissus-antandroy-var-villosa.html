Cissus antandroy var. villosa

Cissus antandroy var. villosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in size, while the plant produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This species is often used in ornamental horticulture for its unique foliage and ability to climb.
