Conophytum dilatatum

Conophytum dilatatum, commonly known as the "Cape Flax" or "Cape Flaxflower," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like structures that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. Conophytum dilatatum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
