Cerasus juliana var. muscatella

Cerasus juliana var. muscatella, commonly known as the Muscat cherry or Muscatella cherry, is a variety of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 2 to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring.

The plant is native to Japan and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and edible fruits.
