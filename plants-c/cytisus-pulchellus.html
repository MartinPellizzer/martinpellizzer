Cytisus pulchellus

Cytisus pulchellus, commonly known as the purple broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its slender, thornless stems are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value.

Cytisus pulchellus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
