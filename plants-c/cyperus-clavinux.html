Cyperus clavinux

Cyperus clavinux, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Cyperus clavinux is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
