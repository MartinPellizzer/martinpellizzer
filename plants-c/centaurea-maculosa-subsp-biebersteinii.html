Centaurea maculosa subsp. biebersteinii

Centaurea maculosa subsp.

biebersteinii, commonly known as the spotted knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with distinctive black markings and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides. It is an annual or biennial species, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
