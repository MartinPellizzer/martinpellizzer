Crataegus aronia proles ruscinonensis

Crataegus aronia proles ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Russian blackthorn, is a shrub native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and thorny branches. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, preferring moist, well-drained conditions. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical uses in traditional medicine.
