Caryocar dentatum

Caryocar dentatum, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitats.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
