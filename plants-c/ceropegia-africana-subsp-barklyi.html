Ceropegia africana subsp. barklyi

Ceropegia africana subsp.

barklyi, commonly known as the Cape ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, often hairy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
