Crotalaria orientalis subsp. allenii

Crotalaria orientalis subsp.

allenii, commonly known as the Allen's morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers with a prominent dark center.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in the early morning, giving it the common name "morning glory," and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
