Conophytum parvimarinum

Conophytum parvimarinum, commonly known as the small marine conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
