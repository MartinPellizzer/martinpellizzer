Campanula gracilis var. littoralis

Campanula gracilis var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along rocky shores. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

Campanula gracilis var. littoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
