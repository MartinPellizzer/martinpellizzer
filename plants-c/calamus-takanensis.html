Calamus takanensis

Calamus takanensis, commonly known as the Takanensis rattan, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its long, flexible stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered fruits containing edible seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Calamus takanensis is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
