Convolvulus eremophilus

Convolvulus eremophilus, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Convolvulus eremophilus is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.
