Cerastium moesiacum f. dimoniei

Cerastium moesiacum f. dimoniei, commonly known as the pink snowbell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its frost-resistant nature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
