Calamagrostis cuspidata

Calamagrostis cuspidata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades that are typically blue-green in color, with a sharp, pointed tip. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis cuspidata is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
