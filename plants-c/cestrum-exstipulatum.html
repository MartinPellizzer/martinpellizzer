Cestrum exstipulatum

Cestrum exstipulatum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
