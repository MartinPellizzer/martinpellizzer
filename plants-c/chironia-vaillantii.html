Chironia vaillantii

Chironia vaillantii, commonly known as the Vaillant's orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as pink, red, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, moist soils.

Chironia vaillantii is often found in humid, shaded environments such as cloud forests.
