Cirsium × subalpinum subsp. rivulariforme

Cirsium × subalpinum subsp.

rivulariforme, commonly known as the alpine thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink composite flowers. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its subspecies status reflects its geographic and morphological distinctions from other Cirsium species.
