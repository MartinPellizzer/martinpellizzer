Carex harmsiana

Carex harmsiana, commonly known as the Harms' sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex harmsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
