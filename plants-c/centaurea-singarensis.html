Centaurea singarensis

Centaurea singarensis, commonly known as the Singarensis knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-based flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea singarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
