Cochleanthes ionoleuca

Cochleanthes ionoleuca, commonly known as the Ionoleuca orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
