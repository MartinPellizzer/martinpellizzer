Cnemidophacos sophoroides

Cnemidophacos sophoroides, commonly known as the silky bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for nitrogen fixation, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
