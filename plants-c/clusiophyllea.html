Clusiophyllea

Clusiophyllea, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
