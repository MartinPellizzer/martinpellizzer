Cirsium vaseyi

Cirsium vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Cirsium vaseyi is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in arid environments.
