Carex × krajinae

Carex × krajinae, commonly known as the Krajina sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, which belongs to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in wetland or moist soil environments. It exhibits typical sedge morphology with three-sided stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex × krajinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
