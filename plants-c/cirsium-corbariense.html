Cirsium corbariense

Cirsium corbariense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a compact head.

Cirsium corbariense is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
