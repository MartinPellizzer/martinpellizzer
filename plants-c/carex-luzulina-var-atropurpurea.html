Carex luzulina var. atropurpurea

Carex luzulina var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in bogs, heaths, and along stream banks. Its foliage is usually a striking deep purple or reddish hue, especially in the autumn, making it a visually distinctive species.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
