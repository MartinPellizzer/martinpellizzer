Clerodendrum nuxioides

Clerodendrum nuxioides, commonly known as the African bitters or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and are borne in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
