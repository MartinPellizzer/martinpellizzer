Cynoglossum creticum f. micranthum

Cynoglossum creticum f. micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a botanical variant of the genus Cynoglossum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, particularly found in rocky and limestone soils. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to violet in color, and bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
