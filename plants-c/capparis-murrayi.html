Capparis murrayi

Capparis murrayi, commonly known as the Murray's caper, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in culinary applications.

Capparis murrayi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
