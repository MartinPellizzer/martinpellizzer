Cerastium parvum

Cerastium parvum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees.

Cerastium parvum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its rapid spread through stolons.
