Cerastium decalvans f. adamovicii

Cerastium decalvans f. adamovicii, commonly known as the Adamovicii mouse-ear chickweed, is a variant of the mouse-ear chickweed species.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.
