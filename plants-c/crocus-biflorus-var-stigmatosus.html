Crocus biflorus var. stigmatosus

Crocus biflorus var. stigmatosus, commonly known as the Stigmatose Crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow stigmatic area that distinguishes it from other crocus varieties. The plant produces two to three narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and compact size.
