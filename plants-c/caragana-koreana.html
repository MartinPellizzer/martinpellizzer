Caragana koreana

Caragana koreana, commonly known as the Korean peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and parts of northeastern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils, and is drought-tolerant. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caragana koreana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
