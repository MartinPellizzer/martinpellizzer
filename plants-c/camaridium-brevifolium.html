Camaridium brevifolium

Camaridium brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved camaridium, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle beauty to its habitat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid forests or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
