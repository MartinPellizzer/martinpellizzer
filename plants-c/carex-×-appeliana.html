Carex × appeliana

Carex × appeliana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex appello and Carex inops, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often hidden among the foliage.
