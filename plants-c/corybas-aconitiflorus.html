Corybas aconitiflorus

Corybas aconitiflorus, commonly known as the blue hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flower that resembles a small, dark blue bell. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its flowers are usually solitary and have a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Corybas aconitiflorus is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance in New Zealand's flora.
