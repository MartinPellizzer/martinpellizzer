Capsicum indicum macrocarpon

Capsicum indicum macrocarpon, commonly known as the Indian eggplant or bird's eye chili, is a species native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and relatively small, while the fruit is a bright red, elongated capsule that contains numerous small, pointed seeds.

This variety is valued for its pungent flavor and is widely used in traditional cuisines across its native regions.
