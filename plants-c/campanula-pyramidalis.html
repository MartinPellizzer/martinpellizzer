Campanula pyramidalis

Campanula pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula pyramidalis is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
