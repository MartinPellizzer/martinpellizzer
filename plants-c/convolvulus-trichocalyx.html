Convolvulus trichocalyx

Convolvulus trichocalyx, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
