Caustis dioica

Caustis dioica, commonly known as the water nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Caustis dioica thrives in moist, shady environments such as along riverbanks and in wetlands.
