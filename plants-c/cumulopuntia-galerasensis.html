Cumulopuntia galerasensis

Cumulopuntia galerasensis, commonly known as the Galeras Mountain cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its arid habitat. It is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments with limited rainfall.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
