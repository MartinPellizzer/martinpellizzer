Convallaria maxima

Convallaria maxima, commonly known as lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a distinctive veining pattern.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
