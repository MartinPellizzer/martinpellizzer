Calamus siphonospathus var. polylepis

Calamus siphonospathus var. polylepis, commonly known as the broad-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, broad leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in aquatic ecosystems.
