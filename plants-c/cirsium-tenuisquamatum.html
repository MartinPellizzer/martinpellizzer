Cirsium tenuisquamatum

Cirsium tenuisquamatum, commonly known as the slender leaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
