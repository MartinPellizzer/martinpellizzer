Campanula scheuchzeri var. luxurians

Campanula scheuchzeri var. luxurians, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural requirements.
