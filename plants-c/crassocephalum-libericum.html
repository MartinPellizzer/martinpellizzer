Crassocephalum libericum

Crassocephalum libericum, commonly known as the African blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
