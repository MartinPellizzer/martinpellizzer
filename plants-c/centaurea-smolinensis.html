Centaurea smolinensis

Centaurea smolinensis, commonly known as Smolin's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its slender stems and narrow leaves are adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
