Celastrus pyrifolius

Celastrus pyrifolius, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance during the flowering season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical and often have a slightly serrated edge. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Celastrus pyrifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
