Cryptanthus incrassatus

Cryptanthus incrassatus, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that ranges from silver to deep green, and its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a compact, low-growing perennial that is popular in rock gardens and containers due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a favored choice for both gardeners and botanists.
