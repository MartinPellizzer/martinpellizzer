Chuquiraga kuschelii

Chuquiraga kuschelii, commonly known as the Andean burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its unique morphology includes a creeping growth habit and specialized structures for seed dispersal.
