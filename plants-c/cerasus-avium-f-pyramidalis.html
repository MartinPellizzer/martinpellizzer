Cerasus avium f. pyramidalis

Cerasus avium f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It features small, dark red to black fruits that are typically harvested in late spring. The plant has a dense, pyramidal shape, making it suitable for small gardens or as a specimen tree. It requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth and fruit production.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit.
