Carex vulgaris var. intermedia

Carex vulgaris var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked spikelets.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
