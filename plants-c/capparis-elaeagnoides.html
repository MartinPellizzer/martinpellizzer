Capparis elaeagnoides

Capparis elaeagnoides, commonly known as the prickly date caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems covered in sharp, spiny thorns and has small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Capparis elaeagnoides is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat various ailments.
