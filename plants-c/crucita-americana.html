Crucita americana

Crucita americana, commonly known as the American crucifix, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Crucita americana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
