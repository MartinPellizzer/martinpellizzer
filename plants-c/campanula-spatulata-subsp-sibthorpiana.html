Campanula spatulata subsp. sibthorpiana

Campanula spatulata subsp.

sibthorpiana, commonly known as the Sibthorpian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and slightly hairy, with a prominent basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
