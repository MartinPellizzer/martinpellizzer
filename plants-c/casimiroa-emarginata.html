Casimiroa emarginata

Casimiroa emarginata, commonly known as the Mexican lime or calamondin, is a small evergreen citrus tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit is a small, round citrus with a thick, leathery rind and a juicy, acidic pulp. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
