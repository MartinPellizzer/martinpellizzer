Carex oederi var. exigua

Carex oederi var. exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. This plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Carex oederi var. exigua is also known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
