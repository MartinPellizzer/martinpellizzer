Cebatha virginica

Cebatha virginica, commonly known as the Virginia cucumber vine, is a climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant's fruits are edible, resembling small cucumbers, and are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and trailing habit make it a popular choice for trellises and garden borders.
