Colletia ferox var. intricata

Colletia ferox var. intricata, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often at the base of the plant. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within the Solanaceae family.
