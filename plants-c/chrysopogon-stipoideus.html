Chrysopogon stipoideus

Chrysopogon stipoideus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale blue or greenish in color. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.

Chrysopogon stipoideus is also used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
