Convolvulus latiflorus

Convolvulus latiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to autumn.

Convolvulus latiflorus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
