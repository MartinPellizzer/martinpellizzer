Celtis cerasifera

Celtis cerasifera, commonly known as the European hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals but not for humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and urban environments.

Celtis cerasifera is known for its moderate growth rate and adaptability to different climates.
