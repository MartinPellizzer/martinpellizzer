Centaurea salonitana var. lanceolata

Centaurea salonitana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue to purple composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
