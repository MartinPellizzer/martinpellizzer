Convolvulus ferrugineus

Convolvulus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a reddish-brown underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Convolvulus ferrugineus is a perennial species that can spread rapidly, making it both a decorative plant and a potential weed.
