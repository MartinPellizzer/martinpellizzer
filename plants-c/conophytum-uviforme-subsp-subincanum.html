Conophytum uviforme subsp. subincanum

Conophytum uviforme subsp.

subincanum, commonly known as the "rock orchid," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, rounded leaves that are often covered in a whitish or pale blue waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires bright sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
