Catasetum × revolutum

Catasetum × revolutum, commonly known as the orchid with a unique floral structure, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that often feature a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange, with a complex arrangement that includes a specialized column for pollination.

Catasetum × revolutum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
