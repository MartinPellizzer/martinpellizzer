Crepidium acuminatum

Crepidium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It typically grows at high altitudes in rocky or forested areas.

Crepidium acuminatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
