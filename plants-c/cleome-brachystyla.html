Cleome brachystyla

Cleome brachystyla, commonly known as the dwarf cleome or false spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, branching stems with alternate, heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, with five petals and stamens that are longer than the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cleome brachystyla is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
