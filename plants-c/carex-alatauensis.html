Carex alatauensis

Carex alatauensis, commonly known as the Alatau sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex alatauensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
