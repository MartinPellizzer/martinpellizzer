Camaridium scalariforme

Camaridium scalariforme, commonly known as the scalloped leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scalloped leaf margins and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

Camaridium scalariforme is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
