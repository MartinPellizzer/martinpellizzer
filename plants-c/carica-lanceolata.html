Carica lanceolata

Carica lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit, which is similar to the common papaya, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
