Celtis balansae

Celtis balansae, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas.

Celtis balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in urban and natural landscapes.
