Cassia chamaecrista var. nictitans

Cassia chamaecrista var. nictitans, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
