Cardamine pratensis var. carnosa

Cardamine pratensis var. carnosa, commonly known as the meadow cress or broad-leaved water cress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and stems that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. It has a short life cycle, flowering and setting seed within a single growing season.

The species is known for its peppery taste, making it a traditional ingredient in some culinary applications.
