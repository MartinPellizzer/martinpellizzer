Campanula hercegovina var. fallens

Campanula hercegovina var. fallens, commonly known as the falling bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
