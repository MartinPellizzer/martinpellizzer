Cistus monspeliensis f. flavescens

Cistus monspeliensis f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
