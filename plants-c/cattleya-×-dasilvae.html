Cattleya × dasilvae

Cattleya × dasilvae, commonly known as the Dorsal Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Cattleya bowringiana and Cattleya labiata.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates with bright indirect light. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and lavender, often with a distinctive dorsal sepal that is broader than the others. It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively ease of cultivation.

This hybrid is known for its fragrant blooms and is often used in floral arrangements and as a decorative plant.
