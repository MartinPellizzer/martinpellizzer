Crataegus chamaemespilus discolor

Crataegus chamaemespilus discolor, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
