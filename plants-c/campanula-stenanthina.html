Campanula stenanthina

Campanula stenanthina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
