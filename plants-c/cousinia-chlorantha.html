Cousinia chlorantha

Cousinia chlorantha, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and silvery-blue, providing a striking visual contrast to its yellow flowers.

Cousinia chlorantha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
