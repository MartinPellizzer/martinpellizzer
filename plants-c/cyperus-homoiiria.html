Cyperus homoiiria

Cyperus homoiiria, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
