Claytonia spathulata subsp. exigua

Claytonia spathulata subsp.

exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf springbeauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows.

The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to spatulate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and have five petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Portulacaceae family.
