Campanula graminifolia var. major

Campanula graminifolia var. major, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
