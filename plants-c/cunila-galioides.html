Cunila galioides

Cunila galioides, commonly known as marsh woundwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features slender, upright stems covered with fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in loose, terminal clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and other water bodies.

Cunila galioides is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
