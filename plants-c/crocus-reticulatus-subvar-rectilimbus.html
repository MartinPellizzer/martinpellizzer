Crocus reticulatus subvar. rectilimbus

Crocus reticulatus subvar. rectilimbus, commonly known as the straight-stemmed crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a reticulate (net-like) pattern on the petals. This subvariety is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.
