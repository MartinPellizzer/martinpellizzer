Conghas zeylonensis

Conghas zeylonensis, commonly known as the Ceylon bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas.

Due to its rapid growth and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
