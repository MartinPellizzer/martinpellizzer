Cousinia tenella

Cousinia tenella, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

Cousinia tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
