Chrysanthemum leiophyllum

Chrysanthemum leiophyllum, commonly known as the smoothleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white or pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Chrysanthemum leiophyllum is often found in wetland habitats and is a valuable component of native plant communities.
