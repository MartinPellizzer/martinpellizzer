Campanula napuligera

Campanula napuligera, commonly known as the Naples bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula napuligera is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
