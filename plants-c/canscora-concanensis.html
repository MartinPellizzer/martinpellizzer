Canscora concanensis

Canscora concanensis, commonly known as the Indian celandine, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and the plant has a short, rhizomatous root system.

Canscora concanensis is often found in forest understories and along riverbanks, where it thrives in humid conditions.
