Cerastium alpinum subsp. glabratum

Cerastium alpinum subsp.

glabratum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers with five petals, blooming in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
