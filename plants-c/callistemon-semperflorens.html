Callistemon semperflorens

Callistemon semperflorens, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a species of flowering shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom throughout the year, giving it the common name "semperflorens," which means "ever-flowering" in Latin. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color and are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract birds and insects.
