Calamus jaherianus

Calamus jaherianus, commonly known as the Persian canes or Persian arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the southern regions of Iran and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often used for crafting. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its rhizomes are harvested for their starchy, edible roots, which have been used in traditional cuisine.

Calamus jaherianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
