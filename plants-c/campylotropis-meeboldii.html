Campylotropis meeboldii

Campylotropis meeboldii, commonly known as the Meebold's false indigo, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

The species is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
