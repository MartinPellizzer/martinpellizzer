Campanula tracheliifolia

Campanula tracheliifolia, commonly known as the hare's foot bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, hairy leaves that are often lobed, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula tracheliifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
