Centaurea montana var. nigrofimbria

Centaurea montana var. nigrofimbria, commonly known as the dark-fringed blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with dark purple or black margins on the petals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant has upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
