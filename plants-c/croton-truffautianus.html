Croton truffautianus

Croton truffautianus, commonly known as the Truffaut's croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
