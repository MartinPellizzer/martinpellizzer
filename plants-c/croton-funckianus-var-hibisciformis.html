Croton funckianus var. hibisciformis

Croton funckianus var. hibisciformis, commonly known as the Hibisciform Croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of hibiscus, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent red stamens, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
