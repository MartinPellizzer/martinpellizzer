Cannaeorchis

Cannaeorchis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature bright yellow or orange hues. Cannaeorchis species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitat.

These plants require high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making them popular among orchid enthusiasts.
