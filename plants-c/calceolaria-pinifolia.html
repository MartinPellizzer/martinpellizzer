Calceolaria pinifolia

Calceolaria pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and needle-like, resembling pine needles, which is reflected in its common name.

Calceolaria pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
