Cardamine rocheliana

Cardamine rocheliana, commonly known as Rochel's watercress, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers in early spring.

The species is often found in calcareous soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.
