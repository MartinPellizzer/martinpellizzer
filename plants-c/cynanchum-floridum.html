Cynanchum floridum

Cynanchum floridum, commonly known as Florida milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Cynanchum floridum typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies.
