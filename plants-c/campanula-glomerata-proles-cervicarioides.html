Campanula glomerata proles cervicarioides

Campanula glomerata proles cervicarioides, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, low-growing stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
