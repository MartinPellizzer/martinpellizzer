Consolida tuntasiana

Consolida tuntasiana, commonly known as the Tuntasian sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and slightly hairy.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
