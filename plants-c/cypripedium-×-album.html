Cypripedium × album

Cypripedium × album, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of white to pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in shaded forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
