Chrysanthemum flavovirens

Chrysanthemum flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers consist of ray florets and disk florets, which contribute to its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
