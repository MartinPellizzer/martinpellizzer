Cocculus tomentosus

Cocculus tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Cocculus tomentosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
