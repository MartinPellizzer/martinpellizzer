Cupressus lusitanica subsp. benthamii

Cupressus lusitanica subsp.

benthamii, commonly known as the Portuguese cypress, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across southern Europe.
