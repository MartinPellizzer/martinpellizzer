Cyanotis minima

Cyanotis minima, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers. Cyanotis minima is characterized by its compact size and striking floral coloration.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
