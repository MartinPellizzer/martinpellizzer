Chaetanthera linearis var. taltalensis

Chaetanthera linearis var. taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal chaetanthera, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It is often found in dune ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in arid coastal environments.
