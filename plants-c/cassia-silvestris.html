Cassia silvestris

Cassia silvestris, commonly known as wild cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Cassia silvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
