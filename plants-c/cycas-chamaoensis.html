Cycas chamaoensis

Cycas chamaoensis, commonly known as the Chamao cycad, is a species of cycad native to China.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Cycas chamaoensis is considered a slow-growing plant with a long lifespan, often reaching several decades in age.
