Cereus coerulescens var. longispinus

Cereus coerulescens var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined night bloomer, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in long, sharp spines. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
