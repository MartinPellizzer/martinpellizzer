Cyperus tanganyicanus

Cyperus tanganyicanus, commonly known as the African umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Cyperus tanganyicanus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various water conditions.
