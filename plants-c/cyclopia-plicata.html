Cyclopia plicata

Cyclopia plicata, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation. Its succulent leaves help it retain moisture, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Cyclopia plicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in horticulture.
