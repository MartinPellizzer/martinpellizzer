Clinogyne sulphurea

Clinogyne sulphurea, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, hairy leaves and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Clinogyne sulphurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
