Crataegus tenuifolia

Crataegus tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Crataegus tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
