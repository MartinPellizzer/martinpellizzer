Corylus mandshurica

Corylus mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk, similar to other hazel species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Corylus mandshurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
