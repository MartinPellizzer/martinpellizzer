Catasetum wredeanum

Catasetum wredeanum, commonly known as the Wrede's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a unique, spurred lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
