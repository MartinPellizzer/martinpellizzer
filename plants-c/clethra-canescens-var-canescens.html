Clethra canescens var. canescens

Clethra canescens var. canescens, commonly known as the gray alder, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
