Carduus tenuiflorus var. albidus

Carduus tenuiflorus var. albidus, commonly known as the white thistle, is a variant of the common thistle characterized by its pale, almost white flower heads.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features spiny leaves and a robust, upright stem, with the flower heads composed of numerous small, white florets. Its botanical structure includes a central disc surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
