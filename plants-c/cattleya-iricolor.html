Cattleya iricolor

Cattleya iricolor, commonly known as the Pink Cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink petals and white sepals, which give it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their fragrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cattleya iricolor is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
