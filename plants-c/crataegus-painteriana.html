Crataegus painteriana

Crataegus painteriana, commonly known as the Painter's Hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in partial to full sun.

The Painter's Hawthorn is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
