Carduus lanceolatus

Carduus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
