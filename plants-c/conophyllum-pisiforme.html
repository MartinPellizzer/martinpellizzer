Conophyllum pisiforme

Conophyllum pisiforme, commonly known as the heartleaf conophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is often found in coastal dunes and is adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds.
