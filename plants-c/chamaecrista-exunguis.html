Chamaecrista exunguis

Chamaecrista exunguis, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to full sun.

Chamaecrista exunguis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
