Camellia imbricata

Camellia imbricata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with a compact and bushy growth habit. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Camellia imbricata prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in temperate climates.
