Crocus insularis

Crocus insularis, commonly known as the Sicilian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is closely related to other species in the Crocus genus, sharing similar floral structures and growth habits.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
