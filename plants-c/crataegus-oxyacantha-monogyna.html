Crataegus oxyacantha monogyna

Crataegus oxyacantha monogyna, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and other disturbed habitats, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
