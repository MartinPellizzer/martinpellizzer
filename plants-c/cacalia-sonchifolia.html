Cacalia sonchifolia

Cacalia sonchifolia, commonly known as the common cacalia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is also known as the daisy or sunflower family. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
