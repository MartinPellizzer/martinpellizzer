Carex distans var. rigidifolia

Carex distans var. rigidifolia, commonly known as the stiff-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, narrow leaves that are typically stiff and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.
