Cissus vagans

Cissus vagans, commonly known as the wandering begonia, is a trailing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cissus vagans is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and easy maintenance.
