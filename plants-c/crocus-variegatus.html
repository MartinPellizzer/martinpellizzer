Crocus variegatus

Crocus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Iridaceae family.

The corms of Crocus variegatus are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
