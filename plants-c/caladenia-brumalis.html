Caladenia brumalis

Caladenia brumalis, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a species of orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a single, erect stem that bears a single or occasionally two showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms during late winter to early spring, hence its common name. It has a distinctive floral structure with a narrow, hood-like labellum and elongated petals.

Caladenia brumalis is adapted to grow in sandy or loamy soils in open woodland or heathland environments.
