Crepinia leucographa

Crepinia leucographa, commonly known as the white-veined cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves with distinctive white veins. The plant produces cones, with the female cones being larger and more elongated than the male ones. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
