Crinum australe var. pedunculatum

Crinum australe var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the Australian swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces large, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a range of environmental conditions.
