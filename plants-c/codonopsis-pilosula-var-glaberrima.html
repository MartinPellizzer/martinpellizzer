Codonopsis pilosula var. glaberrima

Codonopsis pilosula var. glaberrima, commonly known as the hairy ginseng or the Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bell-shaped and usually white or pale pink.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, including adaptogenic and immunostimulant effects.
