Carduus × borderei

Carduus × borderei, commonly known as the French thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is native to southern Europe and has been introduced to other regions as an ornamental or for its medicinal properties.

Due to its prickly nature, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a deterrent to livestock.
