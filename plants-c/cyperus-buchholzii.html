Cyperus buchholzii

Cyperus buchholzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyperus buchholzii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various water conditions.
