Clematis terniflora f. maximowicziana

Clematis terniflora f. maximowicziana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has a distinct, twisted stem that often has a reddish or purple hue. It blooms in the early morning, hence its common name, and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
