Clematis tsengiana

Clematis tsengiana, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five to seven petals. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that ripen to red, containing seeds covered in a sticky coating. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis tsengiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
