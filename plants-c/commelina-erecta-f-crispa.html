Commelina erecta f. crispa

Commelina erecta f. crispa, commonly known as the crisped spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers with white markings. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This variety is distinguished by its crisped or curled leaf margins, which give it its botanical name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially sunny environments.
