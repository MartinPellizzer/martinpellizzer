Cyathea ars

Cyathea ars, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive growth pattern with a single trunk that develops a thick, fibrous outer layer over time. Its fronds are typically arching and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cyathea ars thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
