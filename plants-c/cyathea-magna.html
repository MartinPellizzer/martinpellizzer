Cyathea magna

Cyathea magna, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The trunk is composed of a thick, fibrous mass of dead leaves, which provides structural support.

Cyathea magna plays an important ecological role in its habitat, offering shelter and food for various organisms.
