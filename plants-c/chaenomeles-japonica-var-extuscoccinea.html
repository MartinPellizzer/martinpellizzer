Chaenomeles japonica var. extuscoccinea

Chaenomeles japonica var. extuscoccinea, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, orange to red fruits in the fall, which are edible but often tart. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
