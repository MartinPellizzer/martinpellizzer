Chloranthus mandshuricus

Chloranthus mandshuricus, commonly known as the Manchurian angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountainous regions.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
