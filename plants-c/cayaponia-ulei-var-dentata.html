Cayaponia ulei var. dentata

Cayaponia ulei var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated cayaponia, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
