Cyphostemma masukuense subsp. ferrugineovelutinum

Cyphostemma masukuense subsp.

ferrugineovelutinum, commonly known as the red-stemmed moonvine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Central Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in a reddish-brown, velvety pubescence.

The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface, while the flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in clusters. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

Its unique appearance and growth habit make it a notable ornamental and potentially useful plant in ecological studies.
