Centaurea parlatoris

Centaurea parlatoris, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Centaurea parlatoris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
