Capparis ferruginea subsp. cubensis

Capparis ferruginea subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban red caper, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba. It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
