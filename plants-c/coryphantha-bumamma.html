Coryphantha bumamma

Coryphantha bumamma, commonly known as the Bumamma cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and typically features a crown of white or pale flowers. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
