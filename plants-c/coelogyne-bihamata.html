Coelogyne bihamata

Coelogyne bihamata, commonly known as the two-hued orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing large, colorful orchid flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne bihamata is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
