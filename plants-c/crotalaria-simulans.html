Crotalaria simulans

Crotalaria simulans, commonly known as the false cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria simulans is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

However, some species within the genus are toxic, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
