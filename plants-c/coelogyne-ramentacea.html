Coelogyne ramentacea

Coelogyne ramentacea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and long, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of fragrant, showy blooms that appear during the dry season.

Coelogyne ramentacea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its use in traditional medicine.
