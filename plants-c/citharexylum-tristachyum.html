Citharexylum tristachyum

Citharexylum tristachyum, commonly known as the fragrant crape myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
