Cavendishia adenophora

Cavendishia adenophora, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple lip, and it blooms during the warm season.

Cavendishia adenophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
