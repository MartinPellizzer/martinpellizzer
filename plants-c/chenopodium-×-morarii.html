Chenopodium × morarii

Chenopodium × morarii, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a naturally occurring hybrid species resulting from the cross between Chenopodium album and Chenopodium berlandieri.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive bluish-green color. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces dry, three-angled fruits.

Chenopodium × morarii is often found in temperate regions and is considered a weed in some agricultural contexts.
