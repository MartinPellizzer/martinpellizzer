Clerodendrum dubium

Clerodendrum dubium, commonly known as the wild chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its bark is rough and dark, often with a reddish or brownish hue, and it contains various bioactive compounds.

Clerodendrum dubium is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
