Coreopsis guineensis

Coreopsis guineensis, commonly known as the African coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers with prominent central disks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
