Coptis quinquefolia var. pedatoquinquefolia

Coptis quinquefolia var. pedatoquinquefolia, commonly known as the eastern goldthread, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's leaves are typically divided into five parts, with the lower leaves having a distinctive broad base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its alkaloid content, which has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
