Cissampelos pareira var. wildei

Cissampelos pareira var. wildei, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
