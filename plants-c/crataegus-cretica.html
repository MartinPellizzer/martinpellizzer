Crataegus cretica

Crataegus cretica, commonly known as the Cretan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruits are edible but often have a tart flavor, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Crataegus cretica is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
