Crocus biflorus subsp. argenteus

Crocus biflorus subsp.

argenteus, commonly known as the silver crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It produces striking, early spring flowers with pale yellow petals and a distinctive, often darker throat.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open woodland and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
