Cyperus diphyllus var. triangularis

Cyperus diphyllus var. triangularis, commonly known as the triangular flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and have a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its triangular-shaped leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it recognizable in its natural habitat.
