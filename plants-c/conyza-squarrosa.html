Conyza squarrosa

Conyza squarrosa, commonly known as the woollyleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Conyza squarrosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
