Cyperus serotinus f. grandis

Cyperus serotinus f. grandis, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb with tall, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
