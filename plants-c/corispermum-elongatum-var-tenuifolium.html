Corispermum elongatum var. tenuifolium

Corispermum elongatum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, typical of plants in arid or coastal regions. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often found in salt marshes, dunes, and other saline habitats across the northern hemisphere.

Its botanical structure reflects its ability to thrive in high-salinity conditions.
