Carex disticha var. pedunculata

Carex disticha var. pedunculata, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and flowering spikes, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
