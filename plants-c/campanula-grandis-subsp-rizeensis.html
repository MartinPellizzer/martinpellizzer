Campanula grandis subsp. rizeensis

Campanula grandis subsp.

rizeensis, commonly known as the Rize bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
