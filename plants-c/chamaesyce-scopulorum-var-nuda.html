Chamaesyce scopulorum var. nuda

Chamaesyce scopulorum var. nuda, commonly known as the naked rock spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient and hardy species.
