Coleus coerulescens

Coleus coerulescens, commonly known as the blue coleus, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage.

This plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is prized for its striking leaves that display shades of blue, purple, and green. The leaves are often adorned with intricate patterns and are used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties, with leaves emitting a pleasant scent when crushed.
