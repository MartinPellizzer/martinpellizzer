Cyphanthera albicans

Cyphanthera albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered legume, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Cyphanthera albicans is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
