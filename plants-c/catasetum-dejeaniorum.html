Catasetum dejeaniorum

Catasetum dejeaniorum, commonly known as the Dejean's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and often showy floral structures. The plant produces specialized flowers with a unique mechanism for pollination, involving a structure called the galea, which facilitates the transfer of pollen to specific pollinators. Its leaves are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern, and the plant thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Catasetum dejeaniorum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitat.
