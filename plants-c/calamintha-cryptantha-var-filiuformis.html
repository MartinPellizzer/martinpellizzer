Calamintha cryptantha var. filiuformis

Calamintha cryptantha var. filiuformis, commonly known as the filiform calamintha, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild aromatic properties.
