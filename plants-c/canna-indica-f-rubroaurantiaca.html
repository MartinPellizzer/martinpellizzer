Canna indica f. rubroaurantiaca

Canna indica f. rubroaurantiaca, commonly known as the red and gold canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green, often with a reddish or purplish tinge along the veins. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by brightly colored bracts, with the inflorescence often displaying a combination of red and gold hues. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its vibrant foliage and floral display in gardens and landscapes.
