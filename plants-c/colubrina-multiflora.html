Colubrina multiflora

Colubrina multiflora, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along streambanks. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Colubrina multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
