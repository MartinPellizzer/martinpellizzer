Corybas diemenicus

Corybas diemenicus, commonly known as the Tasmanian hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that resembles a cup or a helmet, which is a distinctive feature among orchids. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and undergrowth. Its flowers are usually green or reddish-brown, with a prominent central spur.

Corybas diemenicus is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
