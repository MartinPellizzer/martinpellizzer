Chaetogastra hieracioides

Chaetogastra hieracioides, commonly known as the common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern and produces tiny white flowers with five petals. The plant has a hairy stem and is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in temperate regions.
