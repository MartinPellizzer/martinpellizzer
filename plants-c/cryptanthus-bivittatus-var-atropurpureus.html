Cryptanthus bivittatus var. atropurpureus

Cryptanthus bivittatus var. atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple carpet plant, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to Brazil.

It features dense rosettes of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with purple or reddish hues, especially under bright light. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
