Cirsium royleanum

Cirsium royleanum, commonly known as the Afghan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, seed-bearing structures called achenes.

Cirsium royleanum is often found in alpine meadows and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
