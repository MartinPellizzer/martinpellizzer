Cyclosorus dissitus

Cyclosorus dissitus, commonly known as the fluted water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often curled or twisted, giving it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are long and creeping, allowing it to spread across the substrate.

Cyclosorus dissitus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
