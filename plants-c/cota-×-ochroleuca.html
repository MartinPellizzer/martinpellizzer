Cota × ochroleuca

Cota × ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow cota, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and sturdy, upright stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
