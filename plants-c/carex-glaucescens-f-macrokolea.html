Carex glaucescens f. macrokolea

Carex glaucescens f. macrokolea, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a variant of the glaucous sedge species.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color. This plant is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
