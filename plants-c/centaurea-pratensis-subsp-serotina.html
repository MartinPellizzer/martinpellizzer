Centaurea pratensis subsp. serotina

Centaurea pratensis subsp.

serotina, commonly known as the late centaury, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features slender stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to purple composite flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its delayed flowering compared to other centaury species.
