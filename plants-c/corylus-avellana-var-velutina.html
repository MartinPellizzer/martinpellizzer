Corylus avellana var. velutina

Corylus avellana var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet hazel, is a variant of the hazel tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety bark and its ability to produce edible nuts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its catkins are yellowish and appear in early spring.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and potential for nut production.
