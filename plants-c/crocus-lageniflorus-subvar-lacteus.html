Crocus lageniflorus subvar. lacteus

Crocus lageniflorus subvar. lacteus, commonly known as the milk crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive milky or waxy appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to the landscape during the transition from winter to summer.
