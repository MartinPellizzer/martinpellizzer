Carex nigra

Carex nigra, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. It produces small, brownish spikelets that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Black sedge thrives in wet or moist soil and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
