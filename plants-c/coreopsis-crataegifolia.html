Coreopsis crataegifolia

Coreopsis crataegifolia, commonly known as the sand coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for sandy or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
