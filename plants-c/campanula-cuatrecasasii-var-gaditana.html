Campanula cuatrecasasii var. gaditana

Campanula cuatrecasasii var. gaditana, commonly known as the Galician bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
