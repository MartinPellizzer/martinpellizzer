Chrysosplenium glechomifolium

Chrysosplenium glechomifolium, commonly known as the creeping chrysosplenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive serrated margin. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
