Cirsium pinetorum

Cirsium pinetorum, commonly known as the pine thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, rough, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish-purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas and open meadows.

Cirsium pinetorum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
