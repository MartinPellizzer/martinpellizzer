Cytisus pendulinus

Cytisus pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, typical of the Fabaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.
