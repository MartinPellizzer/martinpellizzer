Clematis fusca var. flabellata

Clematis fusca var. flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate clematis, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and compound leaves that are often trifoliate or pinnate. The flowers are small, white to pale blue, and typically appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer. This variety is known for its vigorous growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
