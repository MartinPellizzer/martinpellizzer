Candollea longifolia

Candollea longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, elongated inflorescence and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it produces fragrant, colorful blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Candollea longifolia is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
