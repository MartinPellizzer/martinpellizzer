Convolvulus hookerianus

Convolvulus hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features slender, winding stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its small, five-petaled flowers are usually pale pink to white, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
