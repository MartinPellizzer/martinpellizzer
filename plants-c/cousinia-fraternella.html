Cousinia fraternella

Cousinia fraternella, commonly known as the fraternella globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that helps retain moisture and protect against strong winds. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow to orange, are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cousinia fraternella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
