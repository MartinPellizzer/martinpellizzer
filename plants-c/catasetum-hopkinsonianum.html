Catasetum hopkinsonianum

Catasetum hopkinsonianum, commonly known as the Hopkinson's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature bright yellow or orange petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant produces a unique structure called a "catasetum," which is a specialized organ used for pollination. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Catasetum hopkinsonianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
