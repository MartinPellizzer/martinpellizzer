Carex vagans

Carex vagans, commonly known as the wandering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Carex vagans thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
