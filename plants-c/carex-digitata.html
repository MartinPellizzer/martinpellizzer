Carex digitata

Carex digitata, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

Carex digitata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
