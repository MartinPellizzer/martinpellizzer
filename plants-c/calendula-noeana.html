Calendula noeana

Calendula noeana, commonly known as the Armenian marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, narrow leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula noeana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
