Chenopodium album var. coronatum

Chenopodium album var. coronatum, commonly known as common lambsquarters with a crown, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields, roadsides, and gardens. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive white or silver-colored underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

This plant is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
