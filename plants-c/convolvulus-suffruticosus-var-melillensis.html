Convolvulus suffruticosus var. melillensis

Convolvulus suffruticosus var. melillensis, commonly known as the Melillan convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing stems and small, funnel-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry conditions.
