Coleus leiperi

Coleus leiperi, commonly known as the Leiper's coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Coleus leiperi is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
