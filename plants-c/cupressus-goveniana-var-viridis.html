Cupressus goveniana var. viridis

Cupressus goveniana var. viridis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cypress, is a variant of the Mexican cypress native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and its foliage provides dense shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting moderate drought tolerance once established.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to add vertical interest to gardens and natural landscapes.
