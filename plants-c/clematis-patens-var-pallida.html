Clematis patens var. pallida

Clematis patens var. pallida, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
