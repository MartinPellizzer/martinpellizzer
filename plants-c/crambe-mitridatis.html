Crambe mitridatis

Crambe mitridatis, commonly known as the Mitridatis crambe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Crambe mitridatis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
