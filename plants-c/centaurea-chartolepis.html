Centaurea chartolepis

Centaurea chartolepis, commonly known as the silky centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
