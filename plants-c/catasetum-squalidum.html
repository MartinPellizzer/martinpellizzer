Catasetum squalidum

Catasetum squalidum, commonly known as the dirty orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of dark, often reddish or brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by bees, with a distinctive structure that facilitates pollen transfer.

Catasetum squalidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
