Calamagrostis longirostris

Calamagrostis longirostris, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Calamagrostis longirostris is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
