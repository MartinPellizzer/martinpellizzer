Citrus limonimedica

Citrus limonimedica, commonly known as the lemon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces oval-shaped fruits with a thick, yellow rind and juicy, acidic pulp. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
