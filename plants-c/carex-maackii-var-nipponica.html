Carex maackii var. nipponica

Carex maackii var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or wetlands. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
