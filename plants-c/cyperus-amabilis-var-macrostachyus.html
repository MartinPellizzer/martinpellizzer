Cyperus amabilis var. macrostachyus

Cyperus amabilis var. macrostachyus, commonly known as the broad-stalked flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in floodplains, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of soil moisture conditions.
