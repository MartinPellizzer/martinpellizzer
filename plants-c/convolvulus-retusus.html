Convolvulus retusus

Convolvulus retusus, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus retusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
