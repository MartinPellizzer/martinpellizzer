Carex venusta

Carex venusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers in the summer. Carex venusta typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
