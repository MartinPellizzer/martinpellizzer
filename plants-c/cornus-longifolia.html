Cornus longifolia

Cornus longifolia, commonly known as sweetgum or American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky, spherical fruit capsules. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming furrowed with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cornus longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
