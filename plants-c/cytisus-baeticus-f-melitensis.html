Cytisus baeticus f. melitensis

Cytisus baeticus f. melitensis, commonly known as the Maltese broom, is a horticultural form of the broom plant native to the island of Malta.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features small, leathery leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Due to its restricted natural range, it is considered a rare and valuable plant in botanical collections.
