Chlorophytum papillososcapum

Chlorophytum papillososcapum, commonly known as the woolly sword plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
