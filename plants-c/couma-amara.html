Couma amara

Couma amara, commonly known as bitter couma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested without proper preparation.
