Commiphora dalzielii

Commiphora dalzielii, commonly known as the Dalziel's myrrh tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, red or orange fruits containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
