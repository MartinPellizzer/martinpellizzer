Clarisia biflora

Clarisia biflora, commonly known as the double-flowered clarisia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clarisia biflora is relatively low-maintenance and can be grown in containers or in garden beds with well-drained soil.
