Costus albus

Costus albus, commonly known as white costus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Costus albus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
