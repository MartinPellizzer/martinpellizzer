Cardamine patagonica

Cardamine patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian watercress, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are small, oval, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in certain gardens.
