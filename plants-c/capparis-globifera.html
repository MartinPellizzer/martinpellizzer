Capparis globifera

Capparis globifera, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular flower clusters, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal or sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are usually yellow or white.

Capparis globifera is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
