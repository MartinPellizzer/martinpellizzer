Carex sylvicola

Carex sylvicola, commonly known as the forest sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist forested areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the ends of its stems. Carex sylvicola thrives in shaded, wet environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
