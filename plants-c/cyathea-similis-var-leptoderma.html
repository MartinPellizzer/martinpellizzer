Cyathea similis var. leptoderma

Cyathea similis var. leptoderma, commonly known as the slender-leaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its rhizome system supports the development of its distinctive, fibrous trunk.

The fronds are typically bright green and remain evergreen throughout the year.
