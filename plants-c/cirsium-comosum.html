Cirsium comosum

Cirsium comosum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cirsium comosum has a robust root system and is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.
