Calamintha sylvatica f. boveana

Calamintha sylvatica f. boveana, commonly known as the Bovean wood betony, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the wood betony species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Europe, particularly in the Alps. The plant features slender, aromatic stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which appear in early summer, are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in dense whorls along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
