Centaurea alba subsp. caliacrae

Centaurea alba subsp.

caliacrae, commonly known as the Cilicia centaury, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
