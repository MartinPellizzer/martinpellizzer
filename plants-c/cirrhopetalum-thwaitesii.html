Cirrhopetalum thwaitesii

Cirrhopetalum thwaitesii, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique, waxy appearance that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
