Cousinia syrdarjensis

Cousinia syrdarjensis, commonly known as the Turkestan gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as steppes and rocky soils. It has hairy leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cousinia syrdarjensis is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
