Carex echinata subsp. townsendii

Carex echinata subsp.

townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's bulblet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies is distinguished by its dense, bulbous base and small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
