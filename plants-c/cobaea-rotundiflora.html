Cobaea rotundiflora

Cobaea rotundiflora, commonly known as the round-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a climbing vine that produces large, fragrant blooms, often opening in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cobaea rotundiflora is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
