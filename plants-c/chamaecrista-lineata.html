Chamaecrista lineata

Chamaecrista lineata, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. The plant is often found in sandy soils along roadsides and in open woodlands. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for nitrogen fixation.

Chamaecrista lineata is also known for its hard, woody seed pods that persist through winter, providing food for birds and small mammals.
