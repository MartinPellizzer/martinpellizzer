Columbia aequilateralis

Columbia aequilateralis, commonly known as the Pacific Northwest bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, providing it with some drought resistance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along rocky slopes.

Columbia aequilateralis is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local pollinators.
