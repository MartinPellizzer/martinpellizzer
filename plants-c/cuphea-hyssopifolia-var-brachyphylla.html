Cuphea hyssopifolia var. brachyphylla

Cuphea hyssopifolia var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed cigar plant, is a compact shrub native to Central America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
