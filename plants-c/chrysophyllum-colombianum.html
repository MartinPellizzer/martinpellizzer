Chrysophyllum colombianum

Chrysophyllum colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The fruit is a sweet, aromatic berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe. It is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
