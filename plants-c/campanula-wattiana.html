Campanula wattiana

Campanula wattiana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Campanula wattiana has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
