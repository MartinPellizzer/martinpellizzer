Celtis japonica

Celtis japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

Celtis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
