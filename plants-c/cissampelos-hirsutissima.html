Cissampelos hirsutissima

Cissampelos hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy shield vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been utilized in various herbal remedies.
