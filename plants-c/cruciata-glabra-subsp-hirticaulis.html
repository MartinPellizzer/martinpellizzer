Cruciata glabra subsp. hirticaulis

Cruciata glabra subsp.

hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed crosswort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
