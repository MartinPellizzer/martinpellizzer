Cerasus caproniana var. griotta

Cerasus caproniana var. griotta, commonly known as the red-flowered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
