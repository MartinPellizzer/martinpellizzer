Cytisus ratisbonensis var. virgatus

Cytisus ratisbonensis var. virgatus, commonly known as the silver bush broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, alternate, and pinnately compound, with each leaflet having a slight serration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
