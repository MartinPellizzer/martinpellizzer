Caesalpinia erianthera

Caesalpinia erianthera, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers clustered in panicles, which bloom in spring and summer. Its seed pods are elongated and often contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caesalpinia erianthera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is a symbol of Australian national identity.
