Carex × connectens

Carex × connectens, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, usually green or brown flowers, which are clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Carex × connectens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
