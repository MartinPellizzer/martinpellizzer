Commelina erecta var. hamipila

Commelina erecta var. hamipila, commonly known as the upright spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
