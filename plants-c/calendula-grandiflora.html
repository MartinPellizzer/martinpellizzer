Calendula grandiflora

Calendula grandiflora, commonly known as the common marigold, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Calendula grandiflora is also used in herbal remedies and as a natural dye.
