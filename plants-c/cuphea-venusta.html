Cuphea venusta

Cuphea venusta, commonly known as the velvet cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of red, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea venusta is known for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season.
