Conophytum meridianum

Conophytum meridianum, commonly known as the "Sunstone" or "Little Sun," is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, triangular-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It prefers full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
