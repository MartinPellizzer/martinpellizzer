Cyrtopodium flavescens

Cyrtopodium flavescens, commonly known as the golden-stem orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, fleshy rhizome and produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant blooms with fragrant, yellowish-green to orange flowers that are clustered in a raceme. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Cyrtopodium flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
