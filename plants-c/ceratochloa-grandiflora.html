Ceratochloa grandiflora

Ceratochloa grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered grass, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, showy inflorescences. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
