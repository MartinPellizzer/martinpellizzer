Cerastium banaticum f. velessanum

Cerastium banaticum f. velessanum, commonly known as the Velessanum chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, white flowers. The plant features opposite, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a groundcover plant in ecological restoration projects.
