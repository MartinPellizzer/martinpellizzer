Camaridium falcatum

Camaridium falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white, emerging from the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in water gardens for its ornamental value.

Camaridium falcatum plays a role in improving water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
