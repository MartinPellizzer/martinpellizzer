Cyathea vilhelmii

Cyathea vilhelmii, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant grows in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters and are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Cyathea vilhelmii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
