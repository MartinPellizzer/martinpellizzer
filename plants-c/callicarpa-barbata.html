Callicarpa barbata

Callicarpa barbata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet to purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, aromatic bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and are edible for humans when ripe.
