Chavica miniata

Chavica miniata, commonly known as the dwarf chavica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Chavica miniata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
