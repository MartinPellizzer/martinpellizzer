Carex hanensis

Carex hanensis, commonly known as the Han River sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in marshy areas, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
