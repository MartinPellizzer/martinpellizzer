Carex tracyi

Carex tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open habitats. Its botanical features include triangular stems and spike-like inflorescences, characteristic of many sedges.

Tracy's sedge is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler, humid environments.
