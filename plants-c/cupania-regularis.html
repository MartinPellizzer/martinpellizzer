Cupania regularis

Cupania regularis, commonly known as the small-flowered cupania, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Cupania regularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and delicate blooms.
