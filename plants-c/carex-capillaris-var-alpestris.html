Carex capillaris var. alpestris

Carex capillaris var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to mountainous regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It typically grows in alpine meadows and is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine flora and fauna.
