Crataegus amabilis

Crataegus amabilis, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. These berries are often used in culinary applications and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

Crataegus amabilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
