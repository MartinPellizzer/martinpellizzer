Cerasus itosakura var. koshiensis

Cerasus itosakura var. koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi cherry, is a variety of Prunus species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that ripen in early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
