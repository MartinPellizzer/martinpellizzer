Commiphora karibensis

Commiphora karibensis, commonly known as the Caribbean myrrh tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resins, which are exuded from its bark and leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
