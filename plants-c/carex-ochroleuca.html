Carex ochroleuca

Carex ochroleuca, commonly known as yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a distinctive yellowish hue on the underside.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
