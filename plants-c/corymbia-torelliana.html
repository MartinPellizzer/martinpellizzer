Corymbia torelliana

Corymbia torelliana, commonly known as the Red Mahogany, is a species of flowering tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark red to black bark and its distinctive, spreading canopy. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, providing an attractive appearance throughout the year.

Corymbia torelliana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
