Cyperus subumbellatus var. sublimis

Cyperus subumbellatus var. sublimis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often subtended by bracts. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
