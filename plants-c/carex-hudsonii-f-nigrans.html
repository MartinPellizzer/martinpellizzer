Carex hudsonii f. nigrans

Carex hudsonii f. nigrans, commonly known as the black-stemmed sedges, is a sedge species characterized by its dark, often purplish stems.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, producing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
