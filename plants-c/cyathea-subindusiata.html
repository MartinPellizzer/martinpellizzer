Cyathea subindusiata

Cyathea subindusiata, commonly known as the narrow-ridge tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.

The fronds are also sometimes used as thatching material in local communities.
