Clematis affinis

Clematis affinis, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often climbing by means of its twining stems. Its botanical characteristics include a long flowering period and the ability to climb various structures.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
