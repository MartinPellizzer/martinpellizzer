Cenchrus polystachios subsp. atrichus

Cenchrus polystachios subsp.

atrichus, commonly known as the blackgrass or African grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth surface and is typically found in open, disturbed habitats. It is often used for erosion control due to its rapid growth and extensive root system.

Additionally, it serves as a forage plant for livestock in some regions.
