Caesalpinia barbon

Caesalpinia barbon, commonly known as the flame bean, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flower clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves, and its pods are long, slender, and often covered in small spines. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant floral display.
