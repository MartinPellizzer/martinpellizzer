Crepis gaditana

Crepis gaditana, commonly known as the southern mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Crepis gaditana is a perennial herb that is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in temperate climates.
