Caryopteris sinensis

Caryopteris sinensis, commonly known as Chinese blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of blue-lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving them a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
