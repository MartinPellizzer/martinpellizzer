Codiaeum variegatum var. pictum

Codiaeum variegatum var. pictum, commonly known as the flame leaf or crocodile plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, green, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns resembling flames or crocodile skin. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its striking foliage. It is typically propagated through stem cuttings and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.

The leaves are usually leathery and can grow quite large, adding a dramatic visual impact to any indoor space.
