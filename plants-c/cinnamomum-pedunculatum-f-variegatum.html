Cinnamomum pedunculatum f. variegatum

Cinnamomum pedunculatum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated cinnamon tree, is a unique variant of the cinnamon plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues, making it visually appealing. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and aromatic properties.
