Copernicia × burretiana

Copernicia × burretiana, commonly known as the hybrid copernicia, is a species native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
