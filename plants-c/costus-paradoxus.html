Costus paradoxus

Costus paradoxus, commonly known as the false arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of yellow to orange, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a waxy appearance.

Costus paradoxus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
