Carex fuscidula

Carex fuscidula, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a yellowish or reddish hue, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
