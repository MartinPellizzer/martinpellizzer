Cardamine leucantha f. koreana

Cardamine leucantha f. koreana, commonly known as the Korean watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the genus Cardamine. It thrives in moist, shaded areas such as stream banks and wet meadows, and is often found in cool, temperate climates.

Its foliage is edible and has been used in traditional Korean cuisine and herbal medicine.
