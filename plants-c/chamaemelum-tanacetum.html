Chamaemelum tanacetum

Chamaemelum tanacetum, commonly known as chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, and its aromatic, feathery leaves. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and thrives in temperate climates. Chamomile is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in herbal medicine and aromatherapy.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and medicinal purposes.
