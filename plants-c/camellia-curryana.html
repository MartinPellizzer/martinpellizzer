Camellia curryana

Camellia curryana, commonly known as the Kurunegala camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Camellia curryana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
