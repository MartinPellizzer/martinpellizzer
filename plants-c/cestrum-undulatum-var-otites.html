Cestrum undulatum var. otites

Cestrum undulatum var. otites, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open until dawn. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
