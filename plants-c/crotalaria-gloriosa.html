Crotalaria gloriosa

Crotalaria gloriosa, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small crowns. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Crotalaria gloriosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
