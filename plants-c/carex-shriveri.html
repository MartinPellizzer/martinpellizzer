Carex shriveri

Carex shriveri, commonly known as Shriver's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its appearance features grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads.

Carex shriveri is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
