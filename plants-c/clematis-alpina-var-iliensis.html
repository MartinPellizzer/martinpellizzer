Clematis alpina var. iliensis

Clematis alpina var. iliensis, commonly known as the Ili clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which are often accompanied by distinctive, palmate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a climbing plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature in cooler climates.
