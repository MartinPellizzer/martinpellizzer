Cymbidium insigne subsp. seidenfadenii

Cymbidium insigne subsp.

seidenfadenii, commonly known as the Seidenfaden's noble orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This subspecies is particularly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its elegance and rarity.
