Cheilanthes nitidula

Cheilanthes nitidula, commonly known as the glossy lip fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and distinctive, leathery, dark green fronds. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Cheilanthes nitidula is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is well adapted to harsh, sunny conditions.
