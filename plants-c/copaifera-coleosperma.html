Copaifera coleosperma

Copaifera coleosperma, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemate inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Copaifera coleosperma is valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
