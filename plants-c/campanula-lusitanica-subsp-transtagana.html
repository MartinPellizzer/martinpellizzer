Campanula lusitanica subsp. transtagana

Campanula lusitanica subsp.

transtagana, commonly known as the Transylvania bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
