Chilita trichacantha

Chilita trichacantha, commonly known as the woolly chilita, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
