Cephalostachyum chevalieri

Cephalostachyum chevalieri, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces distinctive golden spikelets that are clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
