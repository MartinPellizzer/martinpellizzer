Carex festiva var. ebenea

Carex festiva var. ebenea, commonly known as the silver-spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to reddish in color and appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in water gardens or as a ground cover in shady, wet areas.
