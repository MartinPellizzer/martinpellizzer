Cissus goudotii

Cissus goudotii, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Its succulent stems and leaves help it store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Cissus goudotii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
