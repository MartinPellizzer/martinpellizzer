Canna pallida var. maculata

Canna pallida var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central America.

It features large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery-white underside. The plant produces showy, orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

Canna pallida var. maculata is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and ornamental value in gardens.
