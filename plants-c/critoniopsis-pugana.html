Critoniopsis pugana

Critoniopsis pugana, commonly known as the Pugana orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
