Cladium teretifolium var. planifolium

Cladium teretifolium var. planifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, flat, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense stands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to the structure of wetland ecosystems.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
