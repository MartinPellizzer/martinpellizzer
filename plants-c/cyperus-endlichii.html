Cyperus endlichii

Cyperus endlichii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Cyperus endlichii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the base of the plant.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
