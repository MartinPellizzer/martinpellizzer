Calamagrostis fulva

Calamagrostis fulva, commonly known as the golden reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and bright golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts with the golden flowers.

Calamagrostis fulva plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
