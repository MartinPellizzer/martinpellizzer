Cirsium drummondii var. acaulescens

Cirsium drummondii var. acaulescens, commonly known as Drummond's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
