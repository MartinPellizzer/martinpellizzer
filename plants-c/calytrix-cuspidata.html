Calytrix cuspidata

Calytrix cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Calytrix cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
