Cyrilla pulchella

Cyrilla pulchella, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Cyrilla pulchella is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial to full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
