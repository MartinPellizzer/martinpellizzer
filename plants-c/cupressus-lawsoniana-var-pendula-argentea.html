Cupressus lawsoniana var. pendula-argentea

Cupressus lawsoniana var. pendula-argentea, commonly known as the silver weeping cedar, is a cultivar of the Lawson cypress.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a weeping habit, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are a silvery-blue color, adding visual interest to the landscape. This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
