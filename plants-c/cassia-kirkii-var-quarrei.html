Cassia kirkii var. quarrei

Cassia kirkii var. quarrei, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
