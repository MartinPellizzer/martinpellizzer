Cardiocrinum cathayanum

Cardiocrinum cathayanum, commonly known as the Chinese lily or the giant lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 cm in diameter. The plant features a robust rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Cardiocrinum cathayanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
