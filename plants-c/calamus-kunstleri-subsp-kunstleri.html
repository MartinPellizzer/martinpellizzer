Calamus kunstleri subsp. kunstleri

Calamus kunstleri subsp.

kunstleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

This plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typically greenish in color.

It thrives in wet, swampy areas and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
