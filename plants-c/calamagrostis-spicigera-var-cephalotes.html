Calamagrostis spicigera var. cephalotes

Calamagrostis spicigera var. cephalotes, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of silvery-blue flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a silver-gray sheen, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
