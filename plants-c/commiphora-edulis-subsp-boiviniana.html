Commiphora edulis subsp. boiviniana

Commiphora edulis subsp.

boiviniana, commonly known as the Madagascar incense tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar. It produces a fragrant resin, known as olibanum, which has been used traditionally for incense and medicinal purposes.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and bears yellowish-green flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its resinous sap is exuded from cracks in the bark, particularly during the dry season.

This subspecies is valued for its unique aromatic properties and ecological role in its native habitat.
