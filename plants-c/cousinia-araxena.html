Cousinia araxena

Cousinia araxena, commonly known as the Armenian grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Cousinia araxena is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
