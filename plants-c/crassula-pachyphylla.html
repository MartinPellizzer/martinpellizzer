Crassula pachyphylla

Crassula pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form with a rosette structure. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months.

Due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
