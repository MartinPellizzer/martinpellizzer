Conyza pinnatilobata

Conyza pinnatilobata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed bluemink, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

This plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its wind-dispersed seeds.
