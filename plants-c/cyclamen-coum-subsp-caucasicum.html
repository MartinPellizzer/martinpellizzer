Cyclamen coum subsp. caucasicum

Cyclamen coum subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features heart-shaped, fleshy leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of bright pink to purple flowers with curled petals.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its tuberous roots help it survive harsh winters, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens. Cyclamen coum subsp.

caucasicum is also noted for its ability to bloom throughout the winter months.
