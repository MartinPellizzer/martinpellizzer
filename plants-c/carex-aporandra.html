Carex aporandra

Carex aporandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.
