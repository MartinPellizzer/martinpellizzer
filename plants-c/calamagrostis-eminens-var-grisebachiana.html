Calamagrostis eminens var. grisebachiana

Calamagrostis eminens var. grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray beard grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-gray inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest in gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
