Canavalia madagascariensis

Canavalia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bean or tara bean, is a leguminous plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and erect, branching stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and agricultural potential.

Canavalia madagascariensis also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
