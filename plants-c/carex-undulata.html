Carex undulata

Carex undulata, commonly known as the wavy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly undulating, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Carex undulata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
