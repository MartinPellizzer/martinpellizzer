Colubrina fermenta

Colubrina fermenta, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along waterways. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Colubrina fermenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist soils.
