Cephalostachyum scandens

Cephalostachyum scandens, commonly known as the climbing bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Cephalostachyum scandens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
