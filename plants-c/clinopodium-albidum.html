Clinopodium albidum

Clinopodium albidum, commonly known as white hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, white flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Clinopodium albidum is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
