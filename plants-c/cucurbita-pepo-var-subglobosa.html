Cucurbita pepo var. subglobosa

Cucurbita pepo var. subglobosa, commonly known as the Turk's Turban gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a trailing vine that produces large, spherical, green fruits with a thick, warty rind. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit can be harvested when immature for use in cooking or when fully mature for decorative purposes.
