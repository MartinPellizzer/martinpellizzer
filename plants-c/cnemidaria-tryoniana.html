Cnemidaria tryoniana

Cnemidaria tryoniana, commonly known as the Tryon's grasshopperweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are elongated with a slightly wavy margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Cnemidaria tryoniana is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
