Clerodendrum cephalanthum

Clerodendrum cephalanthum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that produce numerous yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
