Carex saxatilis var. infuscata

Carex saxatilis var. infuscata, commonly known as the dark-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green with a reddish or brownish hue along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spike of small, greenish flowers that mature to a reddish color.

Carex saxatilis var. infuscata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
