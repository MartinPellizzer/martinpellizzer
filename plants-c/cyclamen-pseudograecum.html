Cyclamen pseudograecum

Cyclamen pseudograecum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bowl-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Cyclamen pseudograecum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
