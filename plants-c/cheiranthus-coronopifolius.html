Cheiranthus coronopifolius

Cheiranthus coronopifolius, commonly known as the crown-leaf candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its flowers are often arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
