Centropogon peruvianus

Centropogon peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian torchflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by vibrant, showy bracts that attract pollinators.

Centropogon peruvianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
