Citrus × omikanto

Citrus × omikanto, commonly known as the hybrid citrus plant, is a cross between sweet orange and mandarin orange.

It is characterized by its small, round, and thick-skinned fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub with dense, evergreen foliage. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often blooming in clusters.

The fruit is prized for its high juice content and sweet flavor, making it popular in both culinary and commercial applications.
