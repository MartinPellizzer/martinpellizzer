Crepidiastrum tenuicaule

Crepidiastrum tenuicaule, commonly known as the slender-stemmed spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Small, yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
