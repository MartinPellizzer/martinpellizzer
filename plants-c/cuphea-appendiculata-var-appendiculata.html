Cuphea appendiculata var. appendiculata

Cuphea appendiculata var. appendiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, violet to purple bracts that surround the small, yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
