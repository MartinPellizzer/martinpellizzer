Chionanthus virginicus

Chionanthus virginicus, commonly known as the white fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
