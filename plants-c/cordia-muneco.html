Cordia muneco

Cordia muneco, commonly known as the red cedar or Mexican red cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. Cordia muneco is often found in dry to moist forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and timber.

It also has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of dye.
