Ceropegia bikitaensis

Ceropegia bikitaensis, commonly known as the Bikita's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia bikitaensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
