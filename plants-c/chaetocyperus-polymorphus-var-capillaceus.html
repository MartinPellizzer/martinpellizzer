Chaetocyperus polymorphus var. capillaceus

Chaetocyperus polymorphus var. capillaceus, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Its morphology is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
