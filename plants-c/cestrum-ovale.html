Cestrum ovale

Cestrum ovale, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Cestrum ovale is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, with opposite, ovate leaves.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nocturnal beauty and ornamental value.
