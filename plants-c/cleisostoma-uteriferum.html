Cleisostoma uteriferum

Cleisostoma uteriferum, commonly known as the "Mother's Flower," is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Cleisostoma uteriferum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
