Chloris notocoma

Chloris notocoma, commonly known as the southern wickbush, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or pale in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Chloris notocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
