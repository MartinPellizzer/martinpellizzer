Chaenostoma fastigiatum

Chaenostoma fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often floating or submerged, with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in late summer.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
