Centaurea ovina subsp. steveniana

Centaurea ovina subsp.

steveniana, commonly known as Steven's hoary centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
