Convolvulus triflorus

Convolvulus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pale purple or white, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Convolvulus triflorus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sunny, open habitats.
