Croton curassavicus

Croton curassavicus, commonly known as the red maple or bloodwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored foliage, which can range from red to yellow and orange. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Croton curassavicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
