Cactus geminatus

Cactus geminatus, commonly known as the twin-cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that often branch into two or more stems at the base, giving it its common name. The plant has spiny areoles that bear both flowers and spines, and it produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to its harsh environment.

Cactus geminatus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
