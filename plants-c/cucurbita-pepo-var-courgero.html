Cucurbita pepo var. courgero

Cucurbita pepo var. courgero, commonly known as the courgette or zucchini, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Americas.

It is a trailing annual vine that produces elongated, tender fruits with a typically green, smooth skin. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow, monoecious flowers, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The fruit is primarily cultivated for its edible, nutrient-rich flesh, which is used in various culinary preparations.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
