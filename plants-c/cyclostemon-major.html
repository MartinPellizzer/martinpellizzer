Cyclostemon major

Cyclostemon major, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America and Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cyclostemon major is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
