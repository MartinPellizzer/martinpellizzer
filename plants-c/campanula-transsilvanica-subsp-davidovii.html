Campanula transsilvanica subsp. davidovii

Campanula transsilvanica subsp.

davidovii, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
