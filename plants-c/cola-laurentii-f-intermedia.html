Cola laurentii f. intermedia

Cola laurentii f. intermedia, commonly known as the African cola or African coffee tree, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into hard, ridged pods containing stimulant-rich seeds. These seeds are used to make a caffeine-containing beverage similar to coffee.

It is valued for its ornamental foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
