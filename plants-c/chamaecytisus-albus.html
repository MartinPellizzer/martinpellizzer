Chamaecytisus albus

Chamaecytisus albus, commonly known as the white broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Chamaecytisus albus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
