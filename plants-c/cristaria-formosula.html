Cristaria formosula

Cristaria formosula, commonly known as the Chinese horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright orange or red, saucer-shaped flowers with prominent, horn-like projections. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cristaria formosula is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
