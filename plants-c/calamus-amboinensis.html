Calamus amboinensis

Calamus amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered, fragrant inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into oval, fibrous fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional uses.

Calamus amboinensis is valued in perfumery and traditional medicine for its essential oils and medicinal compounds.
