Conoclinium ballotifolium

Conoclinium ballotifolium, commonly known as the showy coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular, purple to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
