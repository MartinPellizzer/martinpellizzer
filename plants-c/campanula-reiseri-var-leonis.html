Campanula reiseri var. leonis

Campanula reiseri var. leonis, commonly known as the Leon's bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
