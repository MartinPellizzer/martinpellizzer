Cleome potamophila

Cleome potamophila, commonly known as the river cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Cleome potamophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
