Cynoglossum parviflorum

Cynoglossum parviflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms from late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.

The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
