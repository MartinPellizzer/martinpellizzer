Centaurea jacea var. serotina

Centaurea jacea var. serotina, commonly known as late centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features erect stems with narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with reddish bracts. Its flowers bloom late in the growing season, usually from August to October, hence its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
