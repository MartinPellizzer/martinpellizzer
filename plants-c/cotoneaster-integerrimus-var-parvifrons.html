Cotoneaster integerrimus var. parvifrons

Cotoneaster integerrimus var. parvifrons, commonly known as the small-fruited cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, compact growth with reddish-brown bark. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
