Carex pseudobrizoides

Carex pseudobrizoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, three-sided spikelets.

Carex pseudobrizoides plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
