Cyperus semidives

Cyperus semidives, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyperus semidives is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
