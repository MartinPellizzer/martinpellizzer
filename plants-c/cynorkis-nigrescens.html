Cynorkis nigrescens

Cynorkis nigrescens, commonly known as the black-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

This perennial herbaceous plant is characterized by its dark violet to black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat requirements, Cynorkis nigrescens is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
