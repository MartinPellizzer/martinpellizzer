Congolanthus

Congolanthus, commonly known as the Congo flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention. Its growth habit is typically tree-like, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy.

Congolanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
