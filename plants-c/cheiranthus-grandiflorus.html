Cheiranthus grandiflorus

Cheiranthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces showy, four-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Its foliage consists of oval, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.
