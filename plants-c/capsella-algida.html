Capsella algida

Capsella algida, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a small annual herb with hairy, alternate leaves and produces small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in cold, moist environments.

Capsella algida is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
