Cladium arthrophyllum

Cladium arthrophyllum, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss in its moist environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the structure of wetland ecosystems.

Cladium arthrophyllum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland species.
