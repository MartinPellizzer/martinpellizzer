Coccothrinax miraguama subsp. roseocarpa

Coccothrinax miraguama subsp.

roseocarpa, commonly known as the rose-pod palm, is a tropical plant native to the Greater Antilles. It is a small, slow-growing palm with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by distinctive red or pink fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal and limestone soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
