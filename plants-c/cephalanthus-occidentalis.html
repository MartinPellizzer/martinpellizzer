Cephalanthus occidentalis

Cephalanthus occidentalis, commonly known as the sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, sweet taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
