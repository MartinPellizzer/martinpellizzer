Celmisia macmahonii var. macmahonii

Celmisia macmahonii var. macmahonii, commonly known as the MacMahon's celmisia, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, often hairy foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in native forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
