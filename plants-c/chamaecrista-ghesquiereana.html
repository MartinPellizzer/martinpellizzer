Chamaecrista ghesquiereana

Chamaecrista ghesquiereana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny seed pods that encase its small, yellowish seeds. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or open habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
