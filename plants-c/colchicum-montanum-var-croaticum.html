Colchicum montanum var. croaticum

Colchicum montanum var. croaticum, commonly known as the Croatian meadow colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces unique, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by insects. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
