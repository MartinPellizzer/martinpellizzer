Cucumis sativus var. rossicus

Cucumis sativus var. rossicus, commonly known as the Russian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit, which typically measures around 15 to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a vining growth habit, requiring support for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its crisp, mild flavor and is commonly used in fresh salads and pickling.
