Cucumis microspermus var. rugosus

Cucumis microspermus var. rugosus, commonly known as the rough-seeded cucumber, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive, warty surface texture.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Africa. The plant produces small, elongated fruits with a pale green color and a rough, uneven surface. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
