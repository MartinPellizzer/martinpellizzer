Cucumis turbinatus

Cucumis turbinatus, commonly known as the turban cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that are typically yellow or green in color, with a thick, waxy skin. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The turban cucumber is valued for its edible fruit, which has a mild flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
