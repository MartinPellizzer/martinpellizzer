Cassia acapulcensis

Cassia acapulcensis, commonly known as Mexican cassia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Cassia acapulcensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
