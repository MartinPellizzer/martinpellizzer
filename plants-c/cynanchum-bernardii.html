Cynanchum bernardii

Cynanchum bernardii, commonly known as Bernard's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Cynanchum bernardii thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
