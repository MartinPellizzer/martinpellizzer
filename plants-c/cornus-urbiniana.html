Cornus urbiniana

Cornus urbiniana, commonly known as the Urbinian dogwood, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, often accompanied by bracts that resemble petals.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that matures in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
