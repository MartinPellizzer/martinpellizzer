Carex longicaulis

Carex longicaulis, commonly known as the long-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex longicaulis thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in riparian zones and pond margins.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
