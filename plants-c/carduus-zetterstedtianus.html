Carduus zetterstedtianus

Carduus zetterstedtianus, commonly known as the Zetterstedt's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, prickly hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, spherical head.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
