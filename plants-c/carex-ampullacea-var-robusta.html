Carex ampullacea var. robusta

Carex ampullacea var. robusta, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and stiff, with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
