Cydonia oblonga var. obpyricarpa

Cydonia oblonga var. obpyricarpa, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces rough, yellowish-brown fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature, although they can be harvested earlier for culinary use. The fruit has a thick, inedible rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp when ripe. It is often used in jams, jellies, and traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
