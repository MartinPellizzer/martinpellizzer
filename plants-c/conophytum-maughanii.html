Conophytum maughanii

Conophytum maughanii, commonly known as the Maughan's conophytum, is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is often grown as a rock garden plant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.

Conophytum maughanii requires well-draining soil and minimal water to prevent root rot.
