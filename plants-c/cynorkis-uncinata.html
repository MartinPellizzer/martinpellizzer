Cynorkis uncinata

Cynorkis uncinata, commonly known as the hooked cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with the female cones being larger and more robust than the male ones. Its growth is slow, and it can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitats.
