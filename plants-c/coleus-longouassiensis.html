Coleus longouassiensis

Coleus longouassiensis, commonly known as the Longouassien coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a variety of hues, including red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
