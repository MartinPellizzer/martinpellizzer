Corydalis buschii

Corydalis buschii, commonly known as the Busch's fumariopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, often reddish or purple flowers that attract pollinators.

Corydalis buschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
