Chukrasia chickrassa

Chukrasia chickrassa, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood or Chandan, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in religious ceremonies and traditional medicine.
