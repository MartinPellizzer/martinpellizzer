Carex pallescens f. elatior

Carex pallescens f. elatior, commonly known as the tall pale sedge, is a variant of the pale sedge species native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in early summer, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in temperate regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity in aquatic ecosystems.
