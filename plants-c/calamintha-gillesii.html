Calamintha gillesii

Calamintha gillesii, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its fragrance is reminiscent of mint and it is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Calamintha gillesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
