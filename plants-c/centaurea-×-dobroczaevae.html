Centaurea × dobroczaevae

Centaurea × dobroczaevae, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Carpathian region of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring vibrant blue to purple flower heads with yellow centers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
