Cotoneaster melanocarpus proles laxiflorus

Cotoneaster melanocarpus proles laxiflorus, commonly known as the laxiflorous cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and winter interest.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
