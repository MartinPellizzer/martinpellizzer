Commicarpus pilosus

Commicarpus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy commicarpus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like structure, a feature typical of the Fabaceae family.

Commicarpus pilosus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
