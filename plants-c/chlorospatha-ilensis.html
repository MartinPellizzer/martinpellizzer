Chlorospatha ilensis

Chlorospatha ilensis, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a rare and distinctive member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright green, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it produces spores rather than seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
