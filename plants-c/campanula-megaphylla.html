Campanula megaphylla

Campanula megaphylla, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Campanula megaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
