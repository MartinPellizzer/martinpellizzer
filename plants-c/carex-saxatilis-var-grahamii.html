Carex saxatilis var. grahamii

Carex saxatilis var. grahamii, commonly known as Graham's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This variety typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
