Chautemsia calcicola

Chautemsia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone chautemsia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
