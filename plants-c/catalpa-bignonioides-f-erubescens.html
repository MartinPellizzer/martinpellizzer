Catalpa bignonioides f. erubescens

Catalpa bignonioides f. erubescens, commonly known as the scarlet catalpa, is a cultivar of the catalpa tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically a vibrant red or pink, contrasting with its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces long, bean-like pods that contain sticky, fluffy seeds, typical of the Bignoniaceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique floral display makes it a standout in gardens and parks during the spring and early summer.
