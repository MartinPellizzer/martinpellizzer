Cathedra

Cathedra, commonly known as the sacred chair, is a plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Cathedra is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical name, *Cathedra drupifera*, reflects its historical association with religious settings.
