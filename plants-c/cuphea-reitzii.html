Cuphea reitzii

Cuphea reitzii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea reitzii is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
