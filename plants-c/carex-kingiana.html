Carex kingiana

Carex kingiana, commonly known as the King's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Carex kingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural landscapes.
