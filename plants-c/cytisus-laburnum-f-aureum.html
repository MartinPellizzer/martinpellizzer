Cytisus laburnum f. aureum

Cytisus laburnum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden laburnum, is a cultivar of the common laburnum, characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, belonging to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants. The plant features slender, arching branches and small, compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers, which appear in early summer, are clustered in drooping racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and is relatively easy to care for.
