Cnicus wallichii var. platylepis

Cnicus wallichii var. platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features succulent, lobed leaves that are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
