Crotalaria tunguensis

Crotalaria tunguensis, commonly known as the Tungu cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are often accompanied by distinctive leaflets. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Crotalaria tunguensis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
