Crolocos calycina

Crolocos calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Crolocos calycina is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
