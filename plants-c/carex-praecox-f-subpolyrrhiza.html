Carex praecox f. subpolyrrhiza

Carex praecox f. subpolyrrhiza, commonly known as the early sedge, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
