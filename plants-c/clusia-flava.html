Clusia flava

Clusia flava, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically bright yellow to green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters. Clusia flava is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
