Calycanthus glaucus

Calycanthus glaucus, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often gland-dotted and have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Calycanthus glaucus is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
