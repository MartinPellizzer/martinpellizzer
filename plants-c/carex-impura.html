Carex impura

Carex impura, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex impura is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
