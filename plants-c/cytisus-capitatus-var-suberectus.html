Cytisus capitatus var. suberectus

Cytisus capitatus var. suberectus, commonly known as the upright broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often upright stems covered in small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or sandy landscapes.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
