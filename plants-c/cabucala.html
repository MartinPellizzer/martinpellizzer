Cabucala

Cabucala, commonly known as the white lily of the Nile, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that grow along its slender, upright stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Cabucala is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some African communities.
