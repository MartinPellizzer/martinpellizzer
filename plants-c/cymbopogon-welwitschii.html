Cymbopogon welwitschii

Cymbopogon welwitschii, commonly known as African citron grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its essential oils, which are used in various traditional and commercial applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in perfumery and as a natural insect repellent.
