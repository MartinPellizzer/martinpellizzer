Cyathodes straminea var. macrantha

Cyathodes straminea var. macrantha, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
