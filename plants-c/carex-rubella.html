Carex rubella

Carex rubella, commonly known as redstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive reddish stems that give it its common name.

Carex rubella plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
