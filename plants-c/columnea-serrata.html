Columnea serrata

Columnea serrata, commonly known as the serrated flame flower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Columnea serrata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
