Crepis canariensis

Crepis canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal inflorescence, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Crepis canariensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and arid environments.
