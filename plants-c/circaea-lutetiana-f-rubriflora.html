Circaea lutetiana f. rubriflora

Circaea lutetiana f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
