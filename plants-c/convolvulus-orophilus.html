Convolvulus orophilus

Convolvulus orophilus, commonly known as the alpine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing or trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Convolvulus orophilus is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
