Cardamine purpurea

Cardamine purpurea, commonly known as purple crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are divided into several lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cardamine purpurea is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
