Campanulorchis pulverulenta

Campanulorchis pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered bell orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that resemble miniature bells, typically in shades of white or pale blue. The plant has a compact growth habit with small, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation due to its specific ecological needs.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
