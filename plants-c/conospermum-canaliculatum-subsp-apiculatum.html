Conospermum canaliculatum subsp. apiculatum

Conospermum canaliculatum subsp.

apiculatum, commonly known as the serrated conospermum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces striking, showy inflorescences of yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
