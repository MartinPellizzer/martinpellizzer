Calappa oleracea

Calappa oleracea, commonly known as the edible amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are often consumed as a leafy green vegetable, valued for their nutritional content and mild flavor.

Calappa oleracea is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and versatile crop.
