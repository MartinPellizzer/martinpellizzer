Ceanothus × pulchellus

Ceanothus × pulchellus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are aromatic and often have a leathery texture, while its root system is deep and drought-tolerant.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
