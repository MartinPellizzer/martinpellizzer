Coursetia apantensis

Coursetia apantensis, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green in color. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
