Cissus planchonii

Cissus planchonii, commonly known as the rock fig or creeping fig, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Its ability to climb and adapt to various substrates makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
