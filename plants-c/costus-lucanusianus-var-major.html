Costus lucanusianus var. major

Costus lucanusianus var. major, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with pale yellow or white flowers arranged in a spadix, surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, used in traditional medicine and as a starchy food source.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
