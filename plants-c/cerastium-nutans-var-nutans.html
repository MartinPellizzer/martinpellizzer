Cerastium nutans var. nutans

Cerastium nutans var. nutans, commonly known as the hanging chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that bear small, white flowers with five petals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and stolons.
