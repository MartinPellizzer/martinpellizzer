Carpodinus flavidiflora

Carpodinus flavidiflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered carpodine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
