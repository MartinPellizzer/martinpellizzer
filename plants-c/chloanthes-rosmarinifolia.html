Chloanthes rosmarinifolia

Chloanthes rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the fragrant chloanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often grown as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
