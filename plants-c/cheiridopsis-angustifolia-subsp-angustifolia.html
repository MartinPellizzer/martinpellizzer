Cheiridopsis angustifolia subsp. angustifolia

Cheiridopsis angustifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, ribbed stems and small, spoon-shaped leaves that help it store water.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
