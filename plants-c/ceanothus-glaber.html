Ceanothus glaber

Ceanothus glaber, commonly known as the smooth ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus glaber is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
