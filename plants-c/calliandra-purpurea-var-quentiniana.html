Calliandra purpurea var. quentiniana

Calliandra purpurea var. quentiniana, commonly known as the Quentin's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters, providing ornamental value. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

This species is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is used in landscaping and as a cut flower.
