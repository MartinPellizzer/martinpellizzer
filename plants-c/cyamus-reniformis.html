Cyamus reniformis

Cyamus reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves that are often kidney-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with specialized mechanisms to conserve water.

Cyamus reniformis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
