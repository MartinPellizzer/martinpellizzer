Callicarpa reticulata

Callicarpa reticulata, commonly known as the red-berried beauty or Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red berries in the fall, which are edible for humans and birds. Callicarpa reticulata thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
