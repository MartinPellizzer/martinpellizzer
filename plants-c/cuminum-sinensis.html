Cuminum sinensis

Cuminum sinensis, commonly known as Chinese cumin, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has feathery, aromatic leaves. Its seeds are widely used in traditional Chinese cuisine for their pungent, spicy flavor.

The botanical features of Cuminum sinensis make it valuable both in culinary and medicinal applications.
