Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. californicus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. californicus, commonly known as the California goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
