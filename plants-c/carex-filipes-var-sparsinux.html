Carex filipes var. sparsinux

Carex filipes var. sparsinux, commonly known as the sparse-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, sparse hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks.

This variant is notable for its delicate foliage and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
