Chlorophytum colubrinum var. upembense

Chlorophytum colubrinum var. upembense, commonly known as the African Spear Plant, is a succulent perennial native to southern Africa.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a white midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
