Campanula laciniata

Campanula laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are deeply lobed, giving the plant its common name, and they are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
