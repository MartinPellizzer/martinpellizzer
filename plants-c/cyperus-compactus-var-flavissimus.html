Cyperus compactus var. flavissimus

Cyperus compactus var. flavissimus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a yellowish hue, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often golden in color. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique appearance.

Additionally, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
