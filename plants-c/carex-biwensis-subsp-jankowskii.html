Carex biwensis subsp. jankowskii

Carex biwensis subsp.

jankowskii, commonly known as Jankowski's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
