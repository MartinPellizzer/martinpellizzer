Claoxylon crassifolium

Claoxylon crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
