Chenopodium blomianum

Chenopodium blomianum, commonly known as the Blomianum goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Chenopodium blomianum is also known for its edible leaves, which are consumed in some traditional cuisines.
