Chelidonium murale

Chelidonium murale, commonly known as greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, ribbed flowers and has a distinctive, often foul-smelling odor. The plant contains alkaloids, including chelidonine, which have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically lobed and can be hairy, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Chelidonium murale is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
