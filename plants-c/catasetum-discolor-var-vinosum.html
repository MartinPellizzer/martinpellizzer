Catasetum discolor var. vinosum

Catasetum discolor var. vinosum, commonly known as the vinous catasetum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This orchid is characterized by its striking, often violet or reddish-brown flowers that are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant produces a unique type of pollination mechanism involving specialized structures called gynostemium, which facilitate the transfer of pollen to specific pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright but indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its exotic appearance and rarity in the wild.
