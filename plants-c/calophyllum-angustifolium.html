Calophyllum angustifolium

Calophyllum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
