Cereus candicans var. dumesnilianus

Cereus candicans var. dumesnilianus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its growth habit is columnar, with ribbed stems that store water efficiently.

The species is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and unique blooming cycle.
