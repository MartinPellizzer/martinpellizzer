Chenopodium payco

Chenopodium payco, commonly known as desert spinach, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are rich in nutrients and often used in traditional diets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its growth habit includes upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.
