Cyperus panamensis

Cyperus panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Cyperus panamensis has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing wetland soils.
