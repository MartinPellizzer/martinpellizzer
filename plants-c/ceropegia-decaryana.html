Ceropegia decaryana

Ceropegia decaryana, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia decaryana is popular as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
