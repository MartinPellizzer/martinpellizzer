Cymbopogon nardus var. goeringii

Cymbopogon nardus var. goeringii, commonly known as citronella grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, pale yellow flowers. It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and as a natural insect repellent.

The variety goeringii is particularly noted for its strong citronella scent and high essential oil content.
