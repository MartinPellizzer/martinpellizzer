Cavendishia jardinensis

Cavendishia jardinensis, commonly known as the Jardinensis Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny, segmented leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
