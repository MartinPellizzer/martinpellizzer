Casearia javitensis f. grandifolia

Casearia javitensis f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered casearia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
