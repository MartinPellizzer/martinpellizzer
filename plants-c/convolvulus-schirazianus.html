Convolvulus schirazianus

Convolvulus schirazianus, commonly known as the Persian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant has a trailing growth habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
