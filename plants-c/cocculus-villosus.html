Cocculus villosus

Cocculus villosus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its flowers are typically pale blue or purple. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the early morning. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Cocculus villosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
