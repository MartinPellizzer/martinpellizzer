Cracca plumosa

Cracca plumosa, commonly known as the silky cracca, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it a soft, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle touch of color to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique foliage, Cracca plumosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
