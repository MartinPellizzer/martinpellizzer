Chlora perfoliata

Chlora perfoliata, commonly known as the common perfoliate, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in soft, grayish-green leaves. The leaves are typically opposite, with a distinctive perfoliate base that appears to perforate the stem, giving the plant its name. It produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in the summer months.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
