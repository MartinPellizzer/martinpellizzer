Cynanchum rubens

Cynanchum rubens, commonly known as red milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release tiny, hairy seeds attached to silky filaments, aiding in wind dispersal. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cynanchum rubens thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially sunny environments.
