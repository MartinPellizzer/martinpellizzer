Cladanthus eriolepis

Cladanthus eriolepis, commonly known as the woolly bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and evolutionary botany.
