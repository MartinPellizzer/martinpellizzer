Cryptocarya pusilla

Cryptocarya pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Cryptocarya pusilla is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
