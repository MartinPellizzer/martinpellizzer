Clusia monocarpa

Clusia monocarpa, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Clusia monocarpa is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to tolerate urban environments.
