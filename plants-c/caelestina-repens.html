Caelestina repens

Caelestina repens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and vibrant color.
