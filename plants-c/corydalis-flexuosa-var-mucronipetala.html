Corydalis flexuosa var. mucronipetala

Corydalis flexuosa var. mucronipetala, commonly known as the bentstem fumitory, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are somewhat succulent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
