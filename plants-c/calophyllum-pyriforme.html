Calophyllum pyriforme

Calophyllum pyriforme, commonly known as the pear-shaped sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, pear-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. Its sap, known as sandarac, is traditionally used in varnishes and as a medicinal remedy.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has economic significance in traditional medicine and industry.
