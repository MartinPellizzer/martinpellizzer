Cyperus confertus var. bidentatus

Cyperus confertus var. bidentatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
