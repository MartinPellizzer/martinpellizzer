Carex vulgaris var. chlorostachya

Carex vulgaris var. chlorostachya, commonly known as the green-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually a vibrant green, and the inflorescences may have a bluish or greenish hue.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
