Croton boinensis var. tomentosus

Croton boinensis var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and are covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid conditions.
