Celtis occidentalis subsp. georgiana

Celtis occidentalis subsp.

georgiana, commonly known as the Southern Hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, green berries that mature to a dark purple color. It is often found in moist bottomlands, along stream banks, and in urban environments.

The Southern Hackberry is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in various soil types.
