Codonopsis pilosula subsp. pilosula

Codonopsis pilosula subsp.

pilosula, commonly known as the hairy ginseng, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that resemble those of ginseng.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are bell-shaped and usually white or pale purple.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is often cultivated for its medicinal roots.
