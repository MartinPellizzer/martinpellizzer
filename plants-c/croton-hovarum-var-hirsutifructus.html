Croton hovarum var. hirsutifructus

Croton hovarum var. hirsutifructus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often green with reddish or purple undersides. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by distinctive, hairy fruits that are orange to red in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its striking foliage and unique fruiting structures.
