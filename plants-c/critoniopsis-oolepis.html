Critoniopsis oolepis

Critoniopsis oolepis, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hair. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
