Centaurea adeana

Centaurea adeana, commonly known as the Adeana thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea adeana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
