Cuphea linarioides var. communis

Cuphea linarioides var. communis, commonly known as the "whirling heels" or "whorled cuphea," is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces bracted inflorescences that resemble whorls. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

Cuphea linarioides var. communis is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping in warm climates.
