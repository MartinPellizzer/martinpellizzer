Cassia pseudonictitans

Cassia pseudonictitans, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia pseudonictitans is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
