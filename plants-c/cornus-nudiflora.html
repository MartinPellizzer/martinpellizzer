Cornus nudiflora

Cornus nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-stalk dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cornus nudiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
