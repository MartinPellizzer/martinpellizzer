Campanula medium var. bourdiniana

Campanula medium var. bourdiniana, commonly known as the Bourdin's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
