Cynara scolymus subsp. cardunculus

Cynara scolymus subsp.

cardunculus, commonly known as the giant cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments. It is cultivated for its edible stems and leaves, though it requires careful preparation due to its prickly texture.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a decorative ornamental.
