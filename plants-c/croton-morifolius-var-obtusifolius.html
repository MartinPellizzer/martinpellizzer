Croton morifolius var. obtusifolius

Croton morifolius var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves.

The foliage is typically a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying variegation or reddish tints. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with bright, indirect light.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.
