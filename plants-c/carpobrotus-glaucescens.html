Carpobrotus glaucescens

Carpobrotus glaucescens, commonly known as the silver iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
