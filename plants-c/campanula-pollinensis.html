Campanula pollinensis

Campanula pollinensis, commonly known as the Pollinensis bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Campanula pollinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
