Carex carlantoniana

Carex carlantoniana, commonly known as the Carlanton sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers in early summer, which are often green or brown in color.

Carex carlantoniana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
