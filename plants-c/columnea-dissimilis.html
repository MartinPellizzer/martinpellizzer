Columnea dissimilis

Columnea dissimilis, commonly known as the red-tailed columnea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that are often adorned with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant produces showy, pendulous inflorescences that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its foliage consists of elongated, glossy leaves that are typically dark green.

Columnea dissimilis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
