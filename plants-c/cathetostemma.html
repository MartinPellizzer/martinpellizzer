Cathetostemma

Cathetostemma, commonly known as the "blue bell" or "blue bell vine," is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant has slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and trail along supports or other vegetation. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Cathetostemma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical environments.
