Croton siderophyllus var. hirsutus

Croton siderophyllus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hirsute croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture, while the flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
