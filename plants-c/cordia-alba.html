Cordia alba

Cordia alba, commonly known as white cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Cordia alba is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
