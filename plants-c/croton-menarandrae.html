Croton menarandrae

Croton menarandrae, commonly known as the Red-veined Croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues, often with prominent veins. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant produces distinctive, capsule-like fruits.
