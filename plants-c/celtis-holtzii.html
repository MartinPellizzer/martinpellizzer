Celtis holtzii

Celtis holtzii, commonly known as the Mexican hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to central and southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, followed by small, round berries that mature to a dark blue or black color.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, rocky areas, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
