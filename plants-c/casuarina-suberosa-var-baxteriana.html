Casuarina suberosa var. baxteriana

Casuarina suberosa var. baxteriana, commonly known as the baxter's she-oak, is a woody shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous foliage and distinctive cone-like structures that resemble pine cones. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal vegetation and a valuable plant for ecological restoration.
