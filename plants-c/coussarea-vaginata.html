Coussarea vaginata

Coussarea vaginata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
