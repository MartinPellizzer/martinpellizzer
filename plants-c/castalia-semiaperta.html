Castalia semiaperta

Castalia semiaperta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved castalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple and are arranged in spikes, attracting various pollinators.

Castalia semiaperta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
