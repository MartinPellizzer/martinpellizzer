Commiphora campestris subsp. magadiensis

Commiphora campestris subsp.

magadiensis, commonly known as the Magadenis Commiphora, is a small shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and ecological traits compared to other Commiphora species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered plant.
