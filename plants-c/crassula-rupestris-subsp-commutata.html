Crassula rupestris subsp. commutata

Crassula rupestris subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the rock crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, typical of succulents.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its slow growth and drought tolerance, it is a low-maintenance plant for arid environments.
