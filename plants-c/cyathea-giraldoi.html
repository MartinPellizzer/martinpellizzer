Cyathea giraldoi

Cyathea giraldoi, commonly known as the Giraldo's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, persistent layer of old leaf bases. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Cyathea giraldoi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
