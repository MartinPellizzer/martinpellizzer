Crassula sylvatica

Crassula sylvatica, commonly known as the rock rose or forest crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Crassula sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
