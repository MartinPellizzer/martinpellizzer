Caccinia macranthera f. linearifolia

Caccinia macranthera f. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caccinia, is a variant of the species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is a woody shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and horticultural interest in suitable climates.
