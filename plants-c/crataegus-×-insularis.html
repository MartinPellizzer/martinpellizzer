Crataegus × insularis

Crataegus × insularis, commonly known as the island hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. It is native to the British Isles and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
