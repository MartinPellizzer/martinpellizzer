Cyclopogon micranthus

Cyclopogon micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cyclopogon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Cyclopogon micranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
