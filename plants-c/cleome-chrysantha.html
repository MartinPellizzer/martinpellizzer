Cleome chrysantha

Cleome chrysantha, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that resemble spider legs, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Cleome chrysantha is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
