Citrus warburgina

Citrus warburgina, commonly known as the Natal orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange-colored fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe. Citrus warburgina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to the citrus genus.
