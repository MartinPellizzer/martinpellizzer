Cespedesia bonplandii

Cespedesia bonplandii, commonly known as the silver bush or white grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a silvery appearance.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
