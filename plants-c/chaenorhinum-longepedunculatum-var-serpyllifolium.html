Chaenorhinum longepedunculatum var. serpyllifolium

Chaenorhinum longepedunculatum var. serpyllifolium, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
