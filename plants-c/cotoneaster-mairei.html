Cotoneaster mairei

Cotoneaster mairei, commonly known as the Maire's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Cotoneaster mairei typically grows in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its aesthetic value in gardens.
