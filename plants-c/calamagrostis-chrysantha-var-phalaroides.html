Calamagrostis chrysantha var. phalaroides

Calamagrostis chrysantha var. phalaroides, commonly known as the golden reed, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow to golden inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
