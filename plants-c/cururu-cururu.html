Cururu cururu

Cururu cururu, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are clustered in pendulous racemes.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
