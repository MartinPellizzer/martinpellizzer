Campanula tridentata var. argunensis

Campanula tridentata var. argunensis, commonly known as the Argun Mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
