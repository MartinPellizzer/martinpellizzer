Carex barrosii

Carex barrosii, commonly known as the Barros sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color.

The flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated.
