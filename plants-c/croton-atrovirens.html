Croton atrovirens

Croton atrovirens, commonly known as the black-leaf croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, almost black, velvety leaves and its clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, round, green to reddish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Croton atrovirens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
