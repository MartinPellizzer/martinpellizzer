Canna seleriana

Canna seleriana, commonly known as the red bamboo canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features tall, sturdy stalks that can reach up to 6 feet in height, topped with large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by brightly colored bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna seleriana is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral display in gardens and water features.
