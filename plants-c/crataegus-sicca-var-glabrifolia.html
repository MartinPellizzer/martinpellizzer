Crataegus sicca var. glabrifolia

Crataegus sicca var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and simple, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
