Calamagrostis pisinna

Calamagrostis pisinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and upright, with a bluish-green hue. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

Calamagrostis pisinna is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
