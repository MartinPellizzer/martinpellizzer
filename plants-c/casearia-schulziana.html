Casearia schulziana

Casearia schulziana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and mammals but toxic to humans. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture.

Casearia schulziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
