Cyperus brunneoater

Cyperus brunneoater, commonly known as the brownish sand reed, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and riverbanks, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often appearing in summer, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus brunneoater is also sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
