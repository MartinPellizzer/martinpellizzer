Campanula balansae

Campanula balansae, commonly known as the Balansae bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, flowering display.
