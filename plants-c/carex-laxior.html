Carex laxior

Carex laxior, commonly known as the soft bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are generally narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Carex laxior plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
