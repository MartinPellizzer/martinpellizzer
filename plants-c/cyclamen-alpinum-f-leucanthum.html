Cyclamen alpinum f. leucanthum

Cyclamen alpinum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered alpine cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with prominent red or purple markings. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its blooms typically appear in late winter to early spring, adding early color to gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant.
