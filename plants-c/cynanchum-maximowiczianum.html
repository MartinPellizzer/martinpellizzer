Cynanchum maximowiczianum

Cynanchum maximowiczianum, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds, is a notable botanical feature.

Cynanchum maximowiczianum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
