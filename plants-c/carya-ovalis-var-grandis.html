Carya ovalis var. grandis

Carya ovalis var. grandis, commonly known as the large-seeded hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its oval, edible nuts enclosed in a thick, spiny husk. The tree produces alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and yellowish-green flowers in catkins. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in furniture and tool-making.

The plant also plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
