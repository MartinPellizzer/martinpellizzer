Cupania schizoneura

Cupania schizoneura, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries.
