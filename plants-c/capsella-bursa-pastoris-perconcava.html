Capsella bursa-pastoris perconcava

Capsella bursa-pastoris perconcava, commonly known as the common chickweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by distinctive triangular or heart-shaped seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many temperate regions.
