Callista griffithiana

Callista griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's callista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, making it visually striking. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

Callista griffithiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability.
