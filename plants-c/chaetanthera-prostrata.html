Chaetanthera prostrata

Chaetanthera prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate chaetanthera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
