Chrysothamnus parryi var. howardii

Chrysothamnus parryi var. howardii, commonly known as the Howard's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
