Carum lemmonii

Carum lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's caraway, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, white to greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

Carum lemmonii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
