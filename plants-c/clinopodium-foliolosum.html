Clinopodium foliolosum

Clinopodium foliolosum, commonly known as narrow-leaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Clinopodium foliolosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
