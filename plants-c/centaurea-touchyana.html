Centaurea touchyana

Centaurea touchyana, commonly known as the Armenian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from pink to purple.

Centaurea touchyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
