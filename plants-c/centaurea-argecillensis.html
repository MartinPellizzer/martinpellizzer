Centaurea argecillensis

Centaurea argecillensis, commonly known as the Argecillensis thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Centaurea argecillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
