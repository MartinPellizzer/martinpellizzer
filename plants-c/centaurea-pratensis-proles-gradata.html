Centaurea pratensis proles gradata

Centaurea pratensis proles gradata, commonly known as the clustered knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically pink to purple in color.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
