Campanula serrata

Campanula serrata, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are serrated and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
