Carex panicea subsp. pelia

Carex panicea subsp.

pelia, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are typically arranged in dense, paniculate inflorescences, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
