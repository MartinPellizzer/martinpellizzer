Capparis iltisiana

Capparis iltisiana, commonly known as the Iltis's caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, lowland environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Capparis iltisiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
