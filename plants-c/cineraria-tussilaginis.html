Cineraria tussilaginis

Cineraria tussilaginis, commonly known as the hoary cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its medicinal properties have been historically used to treat respiratory ailments, though it is not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes.

The plant's unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valued addition to native plant collections.
