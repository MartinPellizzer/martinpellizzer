Capsicum indicum var. aviculare

Capsicum indicum var. aviculare, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its small, fiery red fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and the fruits are pungent, containing capsaicin, which gives them their heat.

This variety is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
