Cercanthemum hoffmannii

Cercanthemum hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, spiky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
