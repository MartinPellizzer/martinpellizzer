Chrysothamnus parryi var. imulus

Chrysothamnus parryi var. imulus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silver-green foliage and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
