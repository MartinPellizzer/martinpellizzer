Cladium mariscus var. californicum

Cladium mariscus var. californicum, commonly known as California sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of California.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like blades that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water. The plant thrives in marshy environments and is adapted to tolerate both flooding and saline conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to the structure of wetland ecosystems.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
