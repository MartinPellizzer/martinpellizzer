Cerinthodes sibiricum

Cerinthodes sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its cold tolerance, it is a hardy species adapted to harsh, temperate climates.
