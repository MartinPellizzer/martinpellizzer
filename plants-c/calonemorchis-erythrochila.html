Calonemorchis erythrochila

Calonemorchis erythrochila, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
