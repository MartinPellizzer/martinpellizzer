Candollea enervia

Candollea enervia, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking plant. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Candollea enervia is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is appreciated in both horticultural and botanical collections.
