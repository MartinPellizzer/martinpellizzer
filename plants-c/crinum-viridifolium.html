Crinum viridifolium

Crinum viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leafed crinum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Crinum viridifolium is also noted for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
