Calamagrostis vicunarum var. abscondita

Calamagrostis vicunarum var. abscondita, commonly known as the hidden panicgrass, is a rare and lesser-known grass species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.

Its botanical study contributes to the understanding of grassland ecosystems and plant adaptation strategies.
