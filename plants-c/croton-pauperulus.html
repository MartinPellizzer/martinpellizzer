Croton pauperulus

Croton pauperulus, commonly known as the poor man's croton, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
