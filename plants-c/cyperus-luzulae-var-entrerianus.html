Cyperus luzulae var. entrerianus

Cyperus luzulae var. entrerianus, commonly known as the Argentine flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered and bear small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
