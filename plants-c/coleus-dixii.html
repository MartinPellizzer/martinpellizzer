Coleus dixii

Coleus dixii, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its foliage is dense and textured, with a velvety feel that distinguishes it from other coleus species.

Coleus dixii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
