Carex acuta f. personata

Carex acuta f. personata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge.

This plant is a variant of Carex acuta, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. The inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats across temperate regions.
