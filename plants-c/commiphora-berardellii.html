Commiphora berardellii

Commiphora berardellii, commonly known as the Berardell's myrrh, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for its aromatic and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The resin exuded by Commiphora berardellii is similar to myrrh and has been used in various cultural and therapeutic practices.
