Calamintha candidissima

Calamintha candidissima, commonly known as white honeysuckle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

Calamintha candidissima is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
