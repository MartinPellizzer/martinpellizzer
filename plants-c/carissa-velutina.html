Carissa velutina

Carissa velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. Its thick, velvety stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance.

Carissa velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
