Chamarea esterhuyseniae

Chamarea esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysen's chamarea, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family.

Native to South Africa, this plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. It typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding color to gardens during the cooler months. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, grass-like foliage and showy inflorescences.
