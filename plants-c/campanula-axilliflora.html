Campanula axilliflora

Campanula axilliflora, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
