Clidemia bifaria

Clidemia bifaria, commonly known as the silverleaf false mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Clidemia bifaria thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
