Clitoria pozuzoensis

Clitoria pozuzoensis, commonly known as the Pozuzo legume, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features striking violet to purple pea-like flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Clitoria pozuzoensis is notable for its ornamental value and potential ecological importance in its native habitat.
