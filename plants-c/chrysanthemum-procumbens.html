Chrysanthemum procumbens

Chrysanthemum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically spreads along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, often blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
