Cuphea setosa var. glabrescens

Cuphea setosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive, fluffy, purple-pink bracts that surround the small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to magenta in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea setosa var. glabrescens is also known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
