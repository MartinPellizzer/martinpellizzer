Cereus candicans var. tenuispinus

Cereus candicans var. tenuispinus, commonly known as the slender-spined night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, white spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
