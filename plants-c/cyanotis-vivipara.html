Cyanotis vivipara

Cyanotis vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its submerged leaves that are often ribbon-like and have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the water surface, and it is known for its unique reproductive method, viviparity, where the seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. Cyanotis vivipara thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
