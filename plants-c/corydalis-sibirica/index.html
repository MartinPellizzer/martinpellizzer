Corydalis Sibirica

Corydalis sibirica is a delicate flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia. It is known for its vibrant, spurred petals that bloom in shades of pink and purple during the early summer months. This species belongs to the genus Corydalis, which includes over 500 species of flowering plants. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
