Cassia chrysophylla

Cassia chrysophylla, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces long, slender pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia chrysophylla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
