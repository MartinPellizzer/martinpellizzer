Cerosora argentea

Cerosora argentea, commonly known as silverleaf cerosora, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shrublands or open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
