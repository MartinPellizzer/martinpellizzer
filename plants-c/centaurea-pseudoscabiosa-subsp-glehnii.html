Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp. glehnii

Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp.

glehnii, commonly known as Glehn's false knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and composite flowers.

The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often ornamental species in its native range.
