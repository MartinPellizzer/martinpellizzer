Cousinia taldykensis

Cousinia taldykensis, commonly known as the Taldyk Tansy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia taldykensis is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
