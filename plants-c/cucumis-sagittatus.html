Cucumis sagittatus

Cucumis sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf cucumber, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, edible fruits with a mild, cucumber-like flavor. The plant has heart-shaped or arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are distinctive and contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a mature green color, though it can be left to ripen to a yellowish hue.
