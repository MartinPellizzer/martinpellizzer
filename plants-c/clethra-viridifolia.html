Clethra viridifolia

Clethra viridifolia, commonly known as greenleaf clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green on both sides. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Clethra viridifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
