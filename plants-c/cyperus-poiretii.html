Cyperus poiretii

Cyperus poiretii, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically purple or reddish in color. Cyperus poiretii is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
