Chamaeplium runcinatum

Chamaeplium runcinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain-avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
