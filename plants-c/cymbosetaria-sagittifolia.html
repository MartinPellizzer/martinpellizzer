Cymbosetaria sagittifolia

Cymbosetaria sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf cymbosetaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
