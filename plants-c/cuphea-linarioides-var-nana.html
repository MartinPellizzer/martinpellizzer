Cuphea linarioides var. nana

Cuphea linarioides var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a sprawling growth habit, making it suitable for containers or as a ground cover. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant, long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
