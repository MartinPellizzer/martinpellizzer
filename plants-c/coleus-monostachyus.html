Coleus monostachyus

Coleus monostachyus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including purple, red, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Coleus monostachyus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
