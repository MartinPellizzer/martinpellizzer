Carex leporina var. argyroglochin

Carex leporina var. argyroglochin, commonly known as silverleaf sedges, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
