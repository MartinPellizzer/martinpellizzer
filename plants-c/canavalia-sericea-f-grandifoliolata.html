Canavalia sericea f. grandifoliolata

Canavalia sericea f. grandifoliolata, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species characterized by its large, velvety leaves.

It is a leguminous plant that belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its ecological role in soil enrichment and pollination.
