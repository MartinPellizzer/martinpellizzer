Cucurbita

Cucurbita, commonly known as pumpkin, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cucurbitaceae.

These plants are characterized by their large, lobed leaves and tendrils that help them climb. The most notable feature of Cucurbita is its fruit, which can vary in shape, size, and color. They are typically annual plants that thrive in warm climates and require well-drained soil.

The genus includes both edible and ornamental species, making it a versatile and widely cultivated group.
