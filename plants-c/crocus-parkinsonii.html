Crocus parkinsonii

Crocus parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces striking violet or blue flowers with a prominent yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bulbs of Crocus parkinsonii are toxic and should be handled with care.
