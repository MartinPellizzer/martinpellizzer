Campanula glomerata f. alba

Campanula glomerata f. alba, commonly known as the white clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its dense clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to Europe, particularly the Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
