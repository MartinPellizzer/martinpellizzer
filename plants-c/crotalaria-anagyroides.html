Crotalaria anagyroides

Crotalaria anagyroides, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed, and it produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in grassland habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

This species is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its role in promoting biodiversity.
