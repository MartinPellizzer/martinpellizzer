Cyathea dubia

Cyathea dubia, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its rhizome system supports the development of its massive fronds, which are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea dubia plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
