Capsicum indicum var. cordiforme

Capsicum indicum var. cordiforme, commonly known as the heart-shaped chili pepper, is a variant of the Capsicum indicum species.

It is native to India and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit develops from a single ovary and matures from green to red, depending on the variety and environmental conditions.
