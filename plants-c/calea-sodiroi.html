Calea sodiroi

Calea sodiroi, commonly known as the "Mexican ghost flower," is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pale blue or white, almost translucent petals. The plant features a tall, slender stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most notable feature is its intricate, showy inflorescence that resembles a ghostly face, giving it its common name.

Calea sodiroi is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and is often grown in shaded, humid environments.
