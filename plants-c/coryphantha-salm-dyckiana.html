Coryphantha salm-dyckiana

Coryphantha salm-dyckiana, commonly known as the Salm-Dyck's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are often green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Coryphantha salm-dyckiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
