Convolvulus quinqueflorus

Convolvulus quinqueflorus, commonly known as the fiveflower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, five-petaled, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The leaves are heart-shaped and oppositely arranged, while the stems are hairy and twining. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
