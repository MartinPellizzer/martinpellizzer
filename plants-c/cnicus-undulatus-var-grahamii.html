Cnicus undulatus var. grahamii

Cnicus undulatus var. grahamii, commonly known as Graham's bentthorn, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often curved spines along the stems and leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
