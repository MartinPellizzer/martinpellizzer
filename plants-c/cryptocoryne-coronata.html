Cryptocoryne coronata

Cryptocoryne coronata, commonly known as the Crown Cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and is popular among aquarium enthusiasts for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal. It produces small, white flowers that are typically short-lived and not very showy.

Cryptocoryne coronata is often used in planted tanks to provide a natural, lush background for other aquatic plants and fish.
