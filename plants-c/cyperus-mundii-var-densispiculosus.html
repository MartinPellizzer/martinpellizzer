Cyperus mundii var. densispiculosus

Cyperus mundii var. densispiculosus, commonly known as the dense-bristled flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered with dense, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful pioneer species and a potential weed in certain ecosystems.
