Chenopodium × pseudoficifolium

Chenopodium × pseudoficifolium, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
