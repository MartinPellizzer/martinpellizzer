Cupania xylocarpa var. nervosa

Cupania xylocarpa var. nervosa, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowleaf cupania, is a species native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent venation patterns. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in greenish-yellow clusters.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
