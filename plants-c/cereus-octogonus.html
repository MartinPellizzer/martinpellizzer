Cereus octogonus

Cereus octogonus, commonly known as the eight-spined cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, a common trait among many cacti. Its spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
