Campanula ericoides

Campanula ericoides, commonly known as the heather bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in woodland or heathland environments. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Campanula ericoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
