Campanula scheuchzeri var. rhodii

Campanula scheuchzeri var. rhodii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typical of the Campanulaceae family, and are arranged in loose clusters.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and slopes.
