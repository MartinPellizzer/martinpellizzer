Ceanothus × roseus

Ceanothus × roseus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of violet or blue-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun. It is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its use in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal.

Ceanothus × roseus is often used in xeriscaping because of its drought tolerance once established.
