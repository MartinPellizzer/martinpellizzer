Convolvulus glaber

Convolvulus glaber, commonly known as the smooth morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Convolvulus glaber is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and rhizomes.
