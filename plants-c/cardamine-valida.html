Cardamine valida

Cardamine valida, commonly known as the spring cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery scent when crushed.
