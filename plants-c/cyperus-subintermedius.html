Cyperus subintermedius

Cyperus subintermedius, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on slender stalks, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus subintermedius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
