Centaurea ucriae subsp. umbrosa

Centaurea ucriae subsp.

umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in shaded, rocky or woodland habitats, showcasing its adaptability to low-light conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
