Cinchona caribbeana

Cinchona caribbeana, commonly known as the Caribbeana quinine tree, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It is notable for its historical use in the extraction of quinine, a compound used to treat malaria.

The tree typically grows in moist, shaded environments in tropical forests.
