Cassia disperma

Cassia disperma, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Cassia disperma thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
