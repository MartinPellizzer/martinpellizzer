Cinnamomum polderi

Cinnamomum polderi, commonly known as the Polder's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which are responsible for its aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
