Ceratocaryum xerophilum

Ceratocaryum xerophilum, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with warm, dry climates.
