Costus ramosus

Costus ramosus, commonly known as the fragrant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces striking, orange-red flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant's aromatic qualities make it a valuable species in both horticulture and herbal practices.
