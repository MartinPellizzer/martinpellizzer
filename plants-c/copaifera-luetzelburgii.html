Copaifera luetzelburgii

Copaifera luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant is notable for producing a valuable resin, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
