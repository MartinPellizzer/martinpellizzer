Campylospermum nigrinerve

Campylospermum nigrinerve, commonly known as the black-veined campylospermum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It features small, opposite leaves with dark, prominent veins that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, showing some tolerance to salty environments.
