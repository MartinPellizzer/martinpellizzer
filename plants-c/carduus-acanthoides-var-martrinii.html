Carduus acanthoides var. martrinii

Carduus acanthoides var. martrinii, commonly known as the Matrinii thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branched stem that bears purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
