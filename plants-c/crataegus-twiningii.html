Crataegus twiningii

Crataegus twiningii, commonly known as the twining hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan. This shrub or small tree is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, oval leaves. The flowers are typically white or pink and appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
