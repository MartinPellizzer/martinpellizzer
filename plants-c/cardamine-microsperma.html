Cardamine microsperma

Cardamine microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded water crowfoot, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a basal rosette structure, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five sepals and numerous stamens. Cardamine microsperma produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect populations.
