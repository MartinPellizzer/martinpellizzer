Cantua megapotamica

Cantua megapotamica, commonly known as the Andean bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cantua megapotamica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
