Commelina sylvatica

Commelina sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to anchor well in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.

Commelina sylvatica plays a role in supporting local insect populations and contributing to ecosystem biodiversity.
