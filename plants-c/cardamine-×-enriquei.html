Cardamine × enriquei

Cardamine × enriquei, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Cardamine bulbifera and Cardamine hirsuta, characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short rhizomatous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural habitat.
