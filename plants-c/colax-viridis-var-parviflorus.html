Colax viridis var. parviflorus

Colax viridis var. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved green colax, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
