Carex neopaleacea

Carex neopaleacea, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Carex neopaleacea plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
