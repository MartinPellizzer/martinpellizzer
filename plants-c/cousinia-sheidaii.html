Cousinia sheidaii

Cousinia sheidaii, commonly known as the Kashmiri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia sheidaii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
