Carex malipoensis

Carex malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in moist environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in humid conditions.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various small organisms.
