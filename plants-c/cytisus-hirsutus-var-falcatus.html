Cytisus hirsutus var. falcatus

Cytisus hirsutus var. falcatus, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
