Carex neurochlamys

Carex neurochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a prominent central vein. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex neurochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
