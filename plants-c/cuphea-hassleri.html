Cuphea hassleri

Cuphea hassleri, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Cuphea hassleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
