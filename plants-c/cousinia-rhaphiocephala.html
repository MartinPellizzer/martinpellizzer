Cousinia rhaphiocephala

Cousinia rhaphiocephala, commonly known as the woolly mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its resilience to extreme environmental conditions.

Cousinia rhaphiocephala is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
