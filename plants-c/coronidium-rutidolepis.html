Coronidium rutidolepis

Coronidium rutidolepis, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grayish-green leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a crown-like pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Coronidium rutidolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
