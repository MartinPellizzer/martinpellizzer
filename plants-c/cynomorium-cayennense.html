Cynomorium cayennense

Cynomorium cayennense, commonly known as the greater cactus apple, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces large, showy yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant has succulent, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
