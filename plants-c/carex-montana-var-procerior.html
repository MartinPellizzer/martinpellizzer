Carex montana var. procerior

Carex montana var. procerior, commonly known as the tall marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its tall, slender inflorescence that produces small, three-stamened flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
