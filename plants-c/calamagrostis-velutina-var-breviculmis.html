Calamagrostis velutina var. breviculmis

Calamagrostis velutina var. breviculmis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its culms are slender and often form dense clumps, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle shape.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
