Cissus compressicaulis

Cissus compressicaulis, commonly known as the compressed shield fern, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its succulent, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
