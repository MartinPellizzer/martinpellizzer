Calamus cumingianus

Calamus cumingianus, commonly known as Cuming's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are pollinated by insects.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
