Centaurea haynaldii

Centaurea haynaldii, commonly known as Haynald's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. This perennial herb typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
