Cornus paniculata var. albida

Cornus paniculata var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white panicles that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, greyish bark. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators, and its berries provide food for birds and small mammals.
