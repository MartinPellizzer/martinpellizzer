Ceanothus hybridus

Ceanothus hybridus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are evergreen and provide dense foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Ceanothus hybridus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
