Crithmum silaus

Crithmum silaus, commonly known as sea fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its foliage is commonly used in culinary applications, adding a distinctive flavor to dishes.

Additionally, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
