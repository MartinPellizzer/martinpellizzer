Chamaecrista chamaecristoides var. brandegeei

Chamaecrista chamaecristoides var. brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's coral bean, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, orange to red flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.
