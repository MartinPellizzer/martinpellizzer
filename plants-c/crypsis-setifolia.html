Crypsis setifolia

Crypsis setifolia, commonly known as the slender cryptomeria, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to release seeds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
