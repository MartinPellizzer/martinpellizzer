Carex subcompressa

Carex subcompressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage, contributing to its subtle appearance.
