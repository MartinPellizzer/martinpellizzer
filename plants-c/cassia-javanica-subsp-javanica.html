Cassia javanica subsp. javanica

Cassia javanica subsp.

javanica, commonly known as the Java cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods that split open when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
