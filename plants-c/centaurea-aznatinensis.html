Centaurea aznatinensis

Centaurea aznatinensis, commonly known as the Aznatenian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called cypselae.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
