Cytinus hypocistis subsp. kermesinus

Cytinus hypocistis subsp.

kermesinus, commonly known as the crimson sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a small, annual herb that grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open habitats.

The plant produces striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its unique structure includes a fleshy, inflated stem that resembles a seed pod, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Cytinus.

The subspecies kermesinus is distinguished by its more intense coloration and specific geographic distribution.
