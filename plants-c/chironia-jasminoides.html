Chironia jasminoides

Chironia jasminoides, commonly known as the white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and aromatic properties.
