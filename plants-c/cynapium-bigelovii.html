Cynapium bigelovii

Cynapium bigelovii, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, round seeds enclosed in pods, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications in dry regions.
