Cypholophus velutinus

Cypholophus velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a slightly lobed base.

Cypholophus velutinus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in some areas.
