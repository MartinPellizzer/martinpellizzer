Cotoneaster acutifolius var. ambiguus

Cotoneaster acutifolius var. ambiguus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often used in gardens and landscapes.
