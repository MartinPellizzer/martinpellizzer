Carduus defloratus subsp. defloratus

Carduus defloratus subsp.

defloratus, commonly known as the common carducus, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant features a tall, spiny stem with alternate, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy composite flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by pale green to brown ray florets.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
