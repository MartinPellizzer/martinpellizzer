Campylospermum denudatum

Campylospermum denudatum, commonly known as the naked-leaved campylospermum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forest understories. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive coloration that attracts pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
