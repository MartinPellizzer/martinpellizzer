Cordatifolium

Cordatifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its foliage is thick and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Cordatifolium is also noted for its small, inconspicuous flowers that grow in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.
