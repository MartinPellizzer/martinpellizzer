Cicer floribundum

Cicer floribundum, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often used as a cover crop due to its nitrogen-fixing ability and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
