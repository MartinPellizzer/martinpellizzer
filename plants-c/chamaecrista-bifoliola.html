Chamaecrista bifoliola

Chamaecrista bifoliola, commonly known as the two-leaved burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with two opposite leaflets and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
