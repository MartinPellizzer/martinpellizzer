Carex delongii

Carex delongii, commonly known as Delong's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex delongii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
