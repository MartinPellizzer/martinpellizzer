Cephalaria salicifolia

Cephalaria salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose panicles, often appearing in shades of pink to purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
