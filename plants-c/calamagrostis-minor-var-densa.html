Calamagrostis minor var. densa

Calamagrostis minor var. densa, commonly known as dense bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
