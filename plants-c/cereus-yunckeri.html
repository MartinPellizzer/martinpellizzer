Cereus yunckeri

Cereus yunckeri, commonly known as the Yuncker's torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant produces large, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Cereus yunckeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering habits.
