Carex oederoides

Carex oederoides, commonly known as Oeder's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Oeder's sedge is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
