Calanthe patsinensis

Calanthe patsinensis, commonly known as the Patsinon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, compact inflorescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe patsinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
