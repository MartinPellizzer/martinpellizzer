Carex picea

Carex picea, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, stream sides, and woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
