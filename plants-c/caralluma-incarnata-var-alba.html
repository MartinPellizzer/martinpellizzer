Caralluma incarnata var. alba

Caralluma incarnata var. alba, commonly known as white columnar cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
