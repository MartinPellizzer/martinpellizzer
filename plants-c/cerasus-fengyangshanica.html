Cerasus fengyangshanica

Cerasus fengyangshanica, commonly known as the Fengyangshan cherry, is a species of Prunus native to China.

It is characterized by its small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late spring. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
