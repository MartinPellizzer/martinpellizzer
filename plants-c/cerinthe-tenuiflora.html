Cerinthe tenuiflora

Cerinthe tenuiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
