Calamus gonospermus

Calamus gonospermus, commonly known as the fragrant calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The botanical characteristics of Calamus gonospermus make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
