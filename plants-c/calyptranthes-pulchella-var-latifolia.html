Calyptranthes pulchella var. latifolia

Calyptranthes pulchella var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf calyptranthes, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
