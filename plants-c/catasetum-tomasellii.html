Catasetum tomasellii

Catasetum tomasellii, commonly known as the Tomasek's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that feature a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate morphology, with specialized pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum tomasellii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
