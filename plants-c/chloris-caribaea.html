Chloris caribaea

Chloris caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, flat leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant for livestock.
