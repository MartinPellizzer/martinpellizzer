Coreopsis mutica var. multiligulata

Coreopsis mutica var. multiligulata, commonly known as the hairy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow ligule, typical of the genus Coreopsis. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from early summer to fall.
