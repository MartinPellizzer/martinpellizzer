Carduus crispus var. valentinus

Carduus crispus var. valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
