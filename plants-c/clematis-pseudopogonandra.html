Clematis pseudopogonandra

Clematis pseudopogonandra, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support, such as trellises or fences, for optimal display.
