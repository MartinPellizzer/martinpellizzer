Crotalaria durandiana

Crotalaria durandiana, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, stipules at the base of the leaves, and a unique structure called a calyx that persists after flowering.

Crotalaria durandiana is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
