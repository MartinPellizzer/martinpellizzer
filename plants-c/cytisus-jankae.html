Cytisus jankae

Cytisus jankae, commonly known as the Janka's broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical bean-like pods and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus jankae is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to salt spray.
