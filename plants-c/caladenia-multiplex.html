Caladenia multiplex

Caladenia multiplex, commonly known as the multiform spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and intricate, spider-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, narrow spur. Caladenia multiplex is often found in heathlands and open forests, growing in well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and threatened species, requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
