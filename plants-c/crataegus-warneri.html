Crataegus warneri

Crataegus warneri, commonly known as Warner's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and is characterized by its spiny branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open thickets.

Warner's hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
