Cystopora

Cystopora, commonly known as the bladder wort, is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the family Cystoporidae.

These plants are characterized by their unique bladder-like structures, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. Cystopora species typically grow in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plants have a simple morphology, with leaf-like structures that float on the water surface.

They play a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
