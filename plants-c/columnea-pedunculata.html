Columnea pedunculata

Columnea pedunculata, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically dark green with prominent red veins, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
