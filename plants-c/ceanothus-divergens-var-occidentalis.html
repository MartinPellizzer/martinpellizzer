Ceanothus divergens var. occidentalis

Ceanothus divergens var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland chaparral habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
