Coccoloba geniculata

Coccoloba geniculata, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils. The plant features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in the morning.

Coccoloba geniculata is well adapted to saline environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
