Centaurea gallica f. herminii

Centaurea gallica f. herminii, commonly known as the Herminii centaury, is a botanical variant of the common centaury.

It is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to southern Europe, this plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
