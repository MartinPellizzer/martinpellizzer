Ceanothus × lobbianus

Ceanothus × lobbianus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing year-round visual interest.

Ceanothus × lobbianus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
