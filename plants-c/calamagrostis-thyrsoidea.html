Calamagrostis thyrsoidea

Calamagrostis thyrsoidea, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silvery-blue foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in panicles, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

Calamagrostis thyrsoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
