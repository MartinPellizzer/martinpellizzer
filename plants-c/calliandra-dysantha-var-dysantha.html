Calliandra dysantha var. dysantha

Calliandra dysantha var. dysantha, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red bract-like flowers that are arranged in dense, showy clusters. The plant has a woody stem with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
