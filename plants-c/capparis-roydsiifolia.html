Capparis roydsiifolia

Capparis roydsiifolia, commonly known as the Royal's foliage caper, is a species of flowering plant in the Capparaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.
