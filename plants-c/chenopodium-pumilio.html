Chenopodium pumilio

Chenopodium pumilio, commonly known as dwarf goosefoot, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, often thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Chenopodium pumilio is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
