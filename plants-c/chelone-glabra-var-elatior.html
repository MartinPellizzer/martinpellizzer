Chelone glabra var. elatior

Chelone glabra var. elatior, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features striking, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It prefers moist, rich soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
