Cardiospermum cuchujaquense

Cardiospermum cuchujaquense, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
