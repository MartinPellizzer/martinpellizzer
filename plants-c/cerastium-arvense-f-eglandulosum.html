Cerastium arvense f. eglandulosum

Cerastium arvense f. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandless mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Its fleshy stems and leaves are adapted to retain moisture, making it resilient in various environmental conditions.
