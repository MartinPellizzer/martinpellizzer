Camptocarpus crassifolius

Camptocarpus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf camptocarpus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems and elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated fruit capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
