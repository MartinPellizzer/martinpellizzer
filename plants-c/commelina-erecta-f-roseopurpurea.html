Commelina erecta f. roseopurpurea

Commelina erecta f. roseopurpurea, commonly known as the purple rose commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features striking violet to purple leaves with a distinctive white midrib, and its flowers are typically small, with three blue or purple petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or near water sources.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
