Canna occidentalis

Canna occidentalis, commonly known as the red bamboo canna, is a striking tropical plant native to the Americas.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a striking coloration of green or red. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna occidentalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
