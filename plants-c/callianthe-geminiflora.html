Callianthe geminiflora

Callianthe geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two closely spaced flowers on each stem. The plant produces showy, often fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Callianthe geminiflora is considered a valuable but endangered species in conservation efforts.
