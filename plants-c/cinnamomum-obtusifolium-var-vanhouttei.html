Cinnamomum obtusifolium var. vanhouttei

Cinnamomum obtusifolium var. vanhouttei, commonly known as the Vanhoutte's cinnamon tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. Its aromatic bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
