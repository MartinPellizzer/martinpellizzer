Carlina longifolia

Carlina longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features a robust stem with lance-shaped, rough, and hairy leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a spherical inflorescence, consisting of purple to pink bracts and yellow disk flowers.

Carlina longifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
