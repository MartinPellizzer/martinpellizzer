Campanula bononiensis var. concolor

Campanula bononiensis var. concolor, commonly known as the concolor bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
