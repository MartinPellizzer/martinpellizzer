Corynephorus articulatus proles gracilis

Corynephorus articulatus proles gracilis, commonly known as the slender chain grass, is a grass species characterized by its delicate, slender blades and compact growth habit.

It typically forms dense, low-growing tufts with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly rolled at the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are arranged in a chain-like pattern along the stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
