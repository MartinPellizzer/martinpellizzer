Caprifolium thomsonii

Caprifolium thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dry seeds.

Thomson's honeysuckle is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
