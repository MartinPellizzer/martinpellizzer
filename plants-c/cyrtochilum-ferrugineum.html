Cyrtochilum ferrugineum

Cyrtochilum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of South America.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Cyrtochilum ferrugineum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
