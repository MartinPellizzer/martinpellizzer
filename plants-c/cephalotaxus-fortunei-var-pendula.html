Cephalotaxus fortunei var. pendula

Cephalotaxus fortunei var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive cascading form.

It features dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is native to China and thrives in shaded, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique shape and foliage.
