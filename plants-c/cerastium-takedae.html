Cerastium takedae

Cerastium takedae, commonly known as the Takeda's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding delicate beauty to its natural habitat.

Cerastium takedae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
