Cotoneaster raboutensis

Cotoneaster raboutensis, commonly known as the Malagasy cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
