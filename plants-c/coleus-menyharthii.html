Coleus menyharthii

Coleus menyharthii, commonly known as the African coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct veining pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Coleus menyharthii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
