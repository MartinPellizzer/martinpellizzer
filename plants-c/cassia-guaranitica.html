Cassia guaranitica

Cassia guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a woody stem with a smooth, grayish bark.

Cassia guaranitica is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
