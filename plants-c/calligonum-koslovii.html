Calligonum koslovii

Calligonum koslovii, commonly known as the Koslov's tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing desertification.

Calligonum koslovii is also valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
