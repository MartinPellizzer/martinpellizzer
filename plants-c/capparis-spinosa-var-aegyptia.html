Capparis spinosa var. aegyptia

Capparis spinosa var. aegyptia, commonly known as the Egyptian caper, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, green, sour fruits known as capers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
