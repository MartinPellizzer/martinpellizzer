Cirrhopetalum koordersii

Cirrhopetalum koordersii, commonly known as the Koord's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers, which can be pink, purple, or white, are adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.

Cirrhopetalum koordersii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
