Combretum apiculatum subsp. leutweinii

Combretum apiculatum subsp.

leutweinii, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce showy, pink to white flowers.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture. This species is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
