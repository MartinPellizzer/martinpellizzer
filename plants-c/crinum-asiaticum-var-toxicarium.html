Crinum asiaticum var. toxicarium

Crinum asiaticum var. toxicarium, commonly known as the toxic crinum, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces tall, showy spikes of white to lilac-colored flowers. The plant is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its bulbs contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
