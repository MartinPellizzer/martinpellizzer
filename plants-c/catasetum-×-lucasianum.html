Catasetum × lucasianum

Catasetum × lucasianum, commonly known as the orchid with a unique floral structure.

This plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. It is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Catasetum caudatum and Catasetum saccatum. The plant features specialized structures called "labellum" and "column," which are adaptations for its pollination mechanism.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
