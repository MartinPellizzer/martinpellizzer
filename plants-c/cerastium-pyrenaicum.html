Cerastium pyrenaicum

Cerastium pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, opposite, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cerastium pyrenaicum thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
