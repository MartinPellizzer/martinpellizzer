Chusquea spicata

Chusquea spicata, commonly known as the spiculate bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Chusquea spicata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
