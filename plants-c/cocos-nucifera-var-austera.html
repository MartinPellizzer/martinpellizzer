Cocos nucifera var. austera

Cocos nucifera var. austera, commonly known as the Australian coconut palm, is a variant of the coconut tree native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and long, pinnate leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces large, fibrous fruits containing coconut water and meat, which are used for food and beverages. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and abundant sunlight.

This variety is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
