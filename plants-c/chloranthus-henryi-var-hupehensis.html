Chloranthus henryi var. hupehensis

Chloranthus henryi var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh chloranthus, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a short stem with few branches and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
