Calochortus elegans var. major

Calochortus elegans var. major, commonly known as the showy globemallow, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
