Corokia buddleioides var. linearis

Corokia buddleioides var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf corokia, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and inland forest habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
