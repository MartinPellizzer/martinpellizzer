Crocus kangalensis

Crocus kangalensis, commonly known as the Kangal crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming period, often in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are typically borne on short stems. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Crocus kangalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
