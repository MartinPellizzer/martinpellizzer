Cathormion polyanthum

Cathormion polyanthum, commonly known as the mountain glory of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and is known for its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique appearance and resilience.
