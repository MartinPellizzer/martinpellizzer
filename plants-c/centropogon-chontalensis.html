Centropogon chontalensis

Centropogon chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontal grass, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
