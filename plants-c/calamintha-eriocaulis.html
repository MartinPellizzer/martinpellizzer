Calamintha eriocaulis

Calamintha eriocaulis, commonly known as wooly calamintha, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calamintha eriocaulis is often used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties and is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
