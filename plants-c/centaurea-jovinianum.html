Centaurea jovinianum

Centaurea jovinianum, commonly known as the Jovinian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bracts that resemble petals. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea jovinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
