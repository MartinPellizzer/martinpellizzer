Cercis racemosa

Cercis racemosa, commonly known as the Chinese cliff brake, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in racemes during spring, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
