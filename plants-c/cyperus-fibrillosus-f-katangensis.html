Cyperus fibrillosus f. katangensis

Cyperus fibrillosus f. katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikelets that bear small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in its spread and survival in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
