Cyperus teneriffae var. succulentus

Cyperus teneriffae var. succulentus, commonly known as the Canary Island flatsedge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and compact, rounded growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
