Calceolaria integrifolia f. robusta

Calceolaria integrifolia f. robusta, commonly known as the robust calceolaria, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, resembling small lanterns. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is native to South America, particularly Chile, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom profusely during the summer months.
