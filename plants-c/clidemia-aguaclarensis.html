Clidemia aguaclarensis

Clidemia aguaclarensis, commonly known as the waterleaf plant, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Clidemia aguaclarensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
