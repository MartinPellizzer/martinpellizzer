Chamaedorea gracilis

Chamaedorea gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo palm, is a compact evergreen plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often mistaken for bamboo, and its foliage consists of long, narrow, dark green leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its low maintenance requirements. It is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to add a tropical aesthetic to any space.

Chamaedorea gracilis is typically propagated through division or seed, and it prefers well-draining soil with consistent moisture.
