Campanula pubiflora

Campanula pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula pubiflora is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
