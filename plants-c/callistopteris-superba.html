Callistopteris superba

Callistopteris superba, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lush fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, showcasing a complex and elegant structure. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found along riverbanks and in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically located on the undersides of the fronds.

Callistopteris superba plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various forest organisms.
