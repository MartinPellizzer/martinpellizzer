Calamus oblongus

Calamus oblongus, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a tropical perennial plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy rhizome that is used as a food source in some regions. Its growth habit includes forming dense clumps, often in wet or marshy environments.

Calamus oblongus is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
