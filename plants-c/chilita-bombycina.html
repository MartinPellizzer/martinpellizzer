Chilita bombycina

Chilita bombycina, commonly known as the woolly chilita, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
