Chamaesyce portucasadiana

Chamaesyce portucasadiana, commonly known as the Texas sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in coastal dunes and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
