Cissus trianae var. martiniana

Cissus trianae var. martiniana, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue hue due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by green, edible berries that turn red when ripe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
