Convolvulus betonicifolius subsp. peduncularis

Convolvulus betonicifolius subsp.

peduncularis, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments.

The plant features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, blooming in summer.

It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
