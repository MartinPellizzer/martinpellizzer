Cassia uniflora var. latifolia

Cassia uniflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with broad, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
