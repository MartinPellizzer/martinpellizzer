Conyza bampsiana

Conyza bampsiana, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as fields and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Conyza bampsiana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
