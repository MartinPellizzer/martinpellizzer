Carex conicoides

Carex conicoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually green or brown in color.

Carex conicoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
