Clinopodium alpinum subsp. dinaricum

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

dinaricum, commonly known as Dinaric mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in the region.
