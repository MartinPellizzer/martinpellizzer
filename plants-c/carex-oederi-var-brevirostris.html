Carex oederi var. brevirostris

Carex oederi var. brevirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beaked sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces distinctive, three-sided seed capsules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
