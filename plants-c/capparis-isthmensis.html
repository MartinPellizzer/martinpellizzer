Capparis isthmensis

Capparis isthmensis, commonly known as the Isthmus caper, is a flowering plant native to the Isthmus of Panama.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
