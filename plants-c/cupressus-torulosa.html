Cupressus torulosa

Cupressus torulosa, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar form and dark green, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes around the world.
