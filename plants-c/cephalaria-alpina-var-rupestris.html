Cephalaria alpina var. rupestris

Cephalaria alpina var. rupestris, commonly known as the Alpine cephalaria, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for alpine gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
