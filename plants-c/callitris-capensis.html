Callitris capensis

Callitris capensis, commonly known as the coastal gimlet, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, round cones. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. It has a slow growth rate and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid conditions.
