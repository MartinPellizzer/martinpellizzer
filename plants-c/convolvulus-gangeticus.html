Convolvulus gangeticus

Convolvulus gangeticus, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in wastelands, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule and dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
