Chirita urticifolia

Chirita urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its common name derives from the resemblance of its leaves to those of stinging nettle.
