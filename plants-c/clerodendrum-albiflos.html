Clerodendrum albiflos

Clerodendrum albiflos, commonly known as the white-flowered glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
