Coreopsis imbricata

Coreopsis imbricata, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Coreopsis imbricata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
