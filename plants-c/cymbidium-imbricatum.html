Cymbidium imbricatum

Cymbidium imbricatum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, India, and the Himalayas.

It is an evergreen herbaceous plant with tall, upright stems that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive ruffled lip that sets it apart from other orchid species. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
