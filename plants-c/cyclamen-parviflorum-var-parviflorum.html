Cyclamen parviflorum var. parviflorum

Cyclamen parviflorum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Cyclamen parviflorum var. parviflorum is known for its ability to go dormant during summer, making it a popular choice for seasonal decoration.
