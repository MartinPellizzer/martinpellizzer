Cyanus roseus

Cyanus roseus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and a central cone-like structure composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cyanus roseus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
