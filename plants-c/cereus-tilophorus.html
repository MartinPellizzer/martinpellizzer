Cereus tilophorus

Cereus tilophorus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance and can remain open for several days.

Cereus tilophorus is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
