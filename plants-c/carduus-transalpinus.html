Carduus transalpinus

Carduus transalpinus, commonly known as the Transalpine thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads make it easily identifiable.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
