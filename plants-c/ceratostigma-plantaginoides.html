Ceratostigma plantaginoides

Ceratostigma plantaginoides, commonly known as the creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
