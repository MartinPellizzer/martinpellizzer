Cyperus widgrenii

Cyperus widgrenii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and floodplains, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus widgrenii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
