Chabertia vasconica

Chabertia vasconica, commonly known as the Iberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Chabertia vasconica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
