Carex cladostachya var. oerstedii

Carex cladostachya var. oerstedii, commonly known as Oersted’s sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along streams. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

Oersted’s sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
