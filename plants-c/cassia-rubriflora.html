Cassia rubriflora

Cassia rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking red-orange inflorescences that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cassia rubriflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
