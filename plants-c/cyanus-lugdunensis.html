Cyanus lugdunensis

Cyanus lugdunensis, commonly known as the Edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its white petals with a central yellow disk, and its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies.

Due to its rarity and ecological sensitivity, it is protected in many regions.
