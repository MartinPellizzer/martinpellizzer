Clematis acuminata subsp. andersonii

Clematis acuminata subsp.

andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's Clematis, is a perennial climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
