Cissus sicyoides f. parvifolia

Cissus sicyoides f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved rock fig, is a unique variant of the rock fig plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry, sunny environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a houseplant.
