Caroxylon tetrandrum

Caroxylon tetrandrum, commonly known as the four-threaded grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Caroxylon tetrandrum is adapted to survive in harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small fauna in its native range.
