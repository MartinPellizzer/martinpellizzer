Camaridium paleatum

Camaridium paleatum, commonly known as the pale leaf fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pale green fronds that give it a distinctive, ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in humid tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Camaridium paleatum is considered a conservation concern.
