Cucurbita pepo var. genevensis

Cucurbita pepo var. genevensis, commonly known as the genevan pumpkin, is a variant of the common gourd.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical shape and smooth, waxy skin that ranges from light green to pale yellow. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is typically harvested when the fruit is still immature. It is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.

This variety is native to Europe and has been historically used in decorative displays and traditional festivals.
