Chrysopogon hackelii

Chrysopogon hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna habitats.

Chrysopogon hackelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
