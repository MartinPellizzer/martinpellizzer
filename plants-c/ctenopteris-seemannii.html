Ctenopteris seemannii

Ctenopteris seemannii, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor and absorb nutrients from the soil. Its fronds are divided into segments called pinnae, which are arranged in a distinctive, cascading pattern.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
