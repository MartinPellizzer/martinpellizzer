Centaurea spruneri subsp. lineariloba

Centaurea spruneri subsp.

lineariloba, commonly known as the linear-lobed knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces purple to pink flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
