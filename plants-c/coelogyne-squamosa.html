Coelogyne squamosa

Coelogyne squamosa, commonly known as the scaly orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange, with a distinctive, scaly appearance on the petals.

Coelogyne squamosa is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
