Chamaecrista macambensis var. pubescens

Chamaecrista macambensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crown flower, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fluffy, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
