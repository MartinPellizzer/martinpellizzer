Cyphostemma pachypus

Cyphostemma pachypus, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
