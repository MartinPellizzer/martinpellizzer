Cynosurus polydactylon

Cynosurus polydactylon, commonly known as the many-toed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.

Cynosurus polydactylon plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
