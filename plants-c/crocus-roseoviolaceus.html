Crocus roseoviolaceus

Crocus roseoviolaceus, commonly known as the purple-rose crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a rose-like hue, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its flowers are also a source of nectar for early pollinators like bees and butterflies.
