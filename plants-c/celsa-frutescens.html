Celsa frutescens

Celsa frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf red-hot poker, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, spiky red flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
