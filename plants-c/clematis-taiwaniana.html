Clematis taiwaniana

Clematis taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Taiwan.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin.

Clematis taiwaniana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
