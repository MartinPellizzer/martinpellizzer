Catasetum rondonense

Catasetum rondonense, commonly known as the Rondonense Catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with a specialized pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers have a distinct lip that aids in the transfer of pollinia, a feature common in the Catasetum genus.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
