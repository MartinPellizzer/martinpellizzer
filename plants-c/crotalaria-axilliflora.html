Crotalaria axilliflora

Crotalaria axilliflora, commonly known as the axillary-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
