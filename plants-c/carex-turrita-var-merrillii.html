Carex turrita var. merrillii

Carex turrita var. merrillii, commonly known as the Merrill's tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its tall, dense flowering spikes that produce small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
