Calamus petiolaris

Calamus petiolaris, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is often found growing in wet, swampy areas or along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and ecological role.

The plant plays an important part in the biodiversity of its native habitats.
