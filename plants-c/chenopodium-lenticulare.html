Chenopodium lenticulare

Chenopodium lenticulare, commonly known as the broadleaf pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can thrive in a variety of soil types. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

The plant is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein and minerals.
