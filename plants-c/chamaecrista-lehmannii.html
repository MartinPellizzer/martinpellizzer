Chamaecrista lehmannii

Chamaecrista lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

Chamaecrista lehmannii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
