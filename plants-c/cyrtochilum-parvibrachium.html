Cyrtochilum parvibrachium

Cyrtochilum parvibrachium, commonly known as the small-bract cyclopia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with prominent lip petals. Cyrtochilum parvibrachium thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
