Clematis lasiantha

Clematis lasiantha, commonly known as the large-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis lasiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
