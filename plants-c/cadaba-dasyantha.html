Cadaba dasyantha

Cadaba dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
