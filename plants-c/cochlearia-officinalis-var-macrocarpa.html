Cochlearia officinalis var. macrocarpa

Cochlearia officinalis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The species is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to harsh conditions.
