Centaurea regia var. purpurea

Centaurea regia var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple gladiola, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flower heads with vibrant purple petals and bright yellow centers. The plant produces feathery, bristly seed heads that are characteristic of the Centaurea genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from summer into early autumn.
