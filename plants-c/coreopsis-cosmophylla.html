Coreopsis cosmophylla

Coreopsis cosmophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with prominent yellow centers and ray florets, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis cosmophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
