Commelina longicapsa

Commelina longicapsa, commonly known as the long-caps bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the inflorescence consists of a spire of small, tubular flowers.

Commelina longicapsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
