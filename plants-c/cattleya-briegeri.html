Cattleya briegeri

Cattleya briegeri, commonly known as the Brieger's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with dark purple or violet markings. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

This orchid thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
