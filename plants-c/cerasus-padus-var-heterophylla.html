Cerasus padus var. heterophylla

Cerasus padus var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable cherry, is a lesser-known variant of the common bird cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are typically pinkish-white and appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries.

The plant has ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its visual appeal in gardens.
