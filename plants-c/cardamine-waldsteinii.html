Cardamine waldsteinii

Cardamine waldsteinii, commonly known as Waldstein's watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often have a peppery scent when crushed.

The small white or greenish flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.
