Campanula garganica subsp. istriaca

Campanula garganica subsp.

istriaca, commonly known as the Istrian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in Croatia and Slovenia. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and forest edges.

The plant features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
