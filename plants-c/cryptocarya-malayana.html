Cryptocarya malayana

Cryptocarya malayana, commonly known as the Malay cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a woody pulp.

Cryptocarya malayana is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
