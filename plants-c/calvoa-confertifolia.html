Calvoa confertifolia

Calvoa confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved calvoa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Calvoa confertifolia is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
