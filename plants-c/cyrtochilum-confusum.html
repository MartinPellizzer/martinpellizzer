Cyrtochilum confusum

Cyrtochilum confusum, commonly known as the confused slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like.

Cyrtochilum confusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
