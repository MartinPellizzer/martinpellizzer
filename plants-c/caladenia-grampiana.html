Caladenia grampiana

Caladenia grampiana, commonly known as the Grampian spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in open habitats, often in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinct, elongated spur that resembles a spider's leg.

Caladenia grampiana is a short-lived perennial, with a life cycle that includes a dormant phase during unfavorable conditions.
