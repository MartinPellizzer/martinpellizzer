Clematis brasiliana var. laxa

Clematis brasiliana var. laxa, commonly known as the Brazilian laxa clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of several meters, allowing it to climb trees and other structures. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are usually oval and slightly hairy.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
