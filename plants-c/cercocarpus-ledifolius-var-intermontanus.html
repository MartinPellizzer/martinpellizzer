Cercocarpus ledifolius var. intermontanus

Cercocarpus ledifolius var. intermontanus, commonly known as mountain curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing form and its distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, red fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
