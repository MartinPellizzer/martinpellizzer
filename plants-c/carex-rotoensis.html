Carex rotoensis

Carex rotoensis, commonly known as the Rotoenga sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in coastal areas or freshwater wetlands. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on tall, slender stems, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex rotoensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
