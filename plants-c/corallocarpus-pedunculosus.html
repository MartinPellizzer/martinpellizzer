Corallocarpus pedunculosus

Corallocarpus pedunculosus, commonly known as the slender-fruited corallocarpus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Malvaceae family.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.
