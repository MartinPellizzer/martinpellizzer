Cactus subcroceus

Cactus subcroceus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its adaptations include succulent stems that store water and a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

Overall, it is a resilient plant well-suited to desert environments.
