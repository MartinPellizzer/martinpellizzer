Crataegus succulenta var. pertomentosa

Crataegus succulenta var. pertomentosa, commonly known as the fragrant crataegus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves and flowers have a distinctive, aromatic scent, which contributes to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
