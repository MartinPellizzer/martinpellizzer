Cereus chiloensis var. brevispinulus

Cereus chiloensis var. brevispinulus, commonly known as the short-spined chilote cactus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of spines. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is well adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
