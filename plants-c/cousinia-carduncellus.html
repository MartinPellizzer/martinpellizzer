Cousinia carduncellus

Cousinia carduncellus, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are broad and covered in dense, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in summer and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
