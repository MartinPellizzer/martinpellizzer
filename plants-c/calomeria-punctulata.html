Calomeria punctulata

Calomeria punctulata, commonly known as the spotted dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features a hairy, upright stem and produces small, oval-shaped seeds with white pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Calomeria punctulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for gardening purposes.
