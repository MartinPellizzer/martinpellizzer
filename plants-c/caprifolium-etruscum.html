Caprifolium etruscum

Caprifolium etruscum, commonly known as the Etruscan honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to two meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
