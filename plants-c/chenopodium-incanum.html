Chenopodium incanum

Chenopodium incanum, commonly known as silver goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Chenopodium incanum is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
