Cassia florifera

Cassia florifera, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia florifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
