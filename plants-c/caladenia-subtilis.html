Caladenia subtilis

Caladenia subtilis, commonly known as the slender spider-orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to southeastern Australia.

This terrestrial herbaceous plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are characterized by their slender, spider-like appearance, with a single, erect pink or lilac-colored flower that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces a single, narrow leaf and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, reflecting its specialized ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this unique species.
