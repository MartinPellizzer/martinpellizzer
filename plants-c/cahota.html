Cahota

Cahota, commonly known as the yellow balsa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its palm-like appearance with large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.
