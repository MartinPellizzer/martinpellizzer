Cyathea engleri

Cyathea engleri, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to 3 meters in length, and they are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive zigzag pattern.

Cyathea engleri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
