Chrysosplenium amabile

Chrysosplenium amabile, commonly known as the sweetleaf saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in early spring. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Chrysosplenium amabile is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity in its native habitats.
