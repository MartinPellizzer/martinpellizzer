Cassia coquimbensis

Cassia coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo cassia, is a shrub native to Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in coastal areas.

Cassia coquimbensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
