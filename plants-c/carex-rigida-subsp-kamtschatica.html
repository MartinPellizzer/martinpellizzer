Carex rigida subsp. kamtschatica

Carex rigida subsp.

kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
