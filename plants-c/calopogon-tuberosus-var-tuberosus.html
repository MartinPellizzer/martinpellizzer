Calopogon tuberosus var. tuberosus

Calopogon tuberosus var. tuberosus, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has tuberous roots that are often harvested for their ornamental and medicinal value. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in wetlands and pine barrens.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
