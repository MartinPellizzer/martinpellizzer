Carex praecox var. monostachya

Carex praecox var. monostachya, commonly known as early sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
