Chrysophyllum cearaense

Chrysophyllum cearaense, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or ceara cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and resemble cherries in appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.
