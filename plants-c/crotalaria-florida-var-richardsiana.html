Crotalaria florida var. richardsiana

Crotalaria florida var. richardsiana, commonly known as the Florida crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with orange markings, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is also known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its role in supporting local pollinators.
