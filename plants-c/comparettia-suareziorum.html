Comparettia suareziorum

Comparettia suareziorum, commonly known as the Suarez's orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that often display a mix of purple, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain water in humid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
