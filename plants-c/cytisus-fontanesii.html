Cytisus fontanesii

Cytisus fontanesii, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Cytisus fontanesii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility in degraded areas.
