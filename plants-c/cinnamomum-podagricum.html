Cinnamomum podagricum

Cinnamomum podagricum, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by oval-shaped berries. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and dense foliage.

The botanical features of Cinnamomum podagricum make it well adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.
