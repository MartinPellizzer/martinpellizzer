Caragana campanulata

Caragana campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower caragana, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant for rocky or sandy soils.

Caragana campanulata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
