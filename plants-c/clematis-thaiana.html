Clematis thaiana

Clematis thaiana, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Clematis thaiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
