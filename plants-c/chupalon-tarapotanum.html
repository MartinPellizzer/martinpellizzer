Chupalon tarapotanum

Chupalon tarapotanum, commonly known as the Philippine starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces edible yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to starfruits, with a distinctive star-shaped base. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, typically measuring 10-20 cm in length.

The tree thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
