Ceropegia shrirangii

Ceropegia shrirangii, commonly known as the Shrirang Ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a trailing or climbing herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically opposite or whorled. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
