Coelodiscus hirsutulus

Coelodiscus hirsutulus, commonly known as the hairy disc fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fronds that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
