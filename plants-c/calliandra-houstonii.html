Calliandra houstonii

Calliandra houstonii, commonly known as the Houstonia tree or the Mexican orchid tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of orchids, typically blooming in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calliandra houstonii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
