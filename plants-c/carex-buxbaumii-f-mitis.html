Carex buxbaumii f. mitis

Carex buxbaumii f. mitis, commonly known as the Mitis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

The Mitis sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
