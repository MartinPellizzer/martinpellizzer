Corypha macrophylla

Corypha macrophylla, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces large, yellow flowers followed by dark purple fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or lowland forests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is cultivated in many tropical gardens and botanical collections.
