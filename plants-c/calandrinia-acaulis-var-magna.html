Calandrinia acaulis var. magna

Calandrinia acaulis var. magna, commonly known as the giant sand verbena, is a strikingly tall variant of the sand verbena species.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This plant features showy, clustered pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its stems are typically erect and branched, with narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss.

The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
