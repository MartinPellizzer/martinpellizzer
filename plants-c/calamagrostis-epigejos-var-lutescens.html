Calamagrostis epigejos var. lutescens

Calamagrostis epigejos var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features tall, slender stalks that produce fluffy, golden-brown plumes in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, attractive clumps in gardens and natural landscapes.
