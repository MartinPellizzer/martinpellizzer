Conostegia glabra

Conostegia glabra, commonly known as the smooth conostegia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Conostegia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
