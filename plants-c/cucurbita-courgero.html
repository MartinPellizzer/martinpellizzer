Cucurbita courgero

Cucurbita courgero, commonly known as the Courgeron pumpkin, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, round fruits with a thick, warty skin that can range in color from green to orange. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many cucurbit species.

The Courgeron pumpkin is primarily grown for ornamental purposes, though it can also be used in cooking.
