Convolvulus subsessilis

Convolvulus subsessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its slender stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while the flowers range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is part of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and rhizomes.
