Cyperus laxus subsp. buchholzii

Cyperus laxus subsp.

buchholzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 meter in height, with a distinctive triangular cross-section. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often borne on long, slender stalks.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
