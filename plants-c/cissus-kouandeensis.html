Cissus kouandeensis

Cissus kouandeensis, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its thick, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocks or trees.

Cissus kouandeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
