Comptonia peregrina

Comptonia peregrina, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, smooth, oval fruits that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Comptonia peregrina is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
