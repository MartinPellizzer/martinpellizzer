Cassia silvestris f. ferruginea

Cassia silvestris f. ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its distinctive reddish-brown markings on the leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of this plant are also used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
