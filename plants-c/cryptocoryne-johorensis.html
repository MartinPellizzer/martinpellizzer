Cryptocoryne johorensis

Cryptocoryne johorensis, commonly known as the Johore cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate light conditions. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, making it a popular choice for pond and aquarium landscaping.

Cryptocoryne johorensis is also valued for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing excess nutrients.
