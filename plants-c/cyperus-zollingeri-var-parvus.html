Cyperus zollingeri var. parvus

Cyperus zollingeri var. parvus, commonly known as the dwarf flat sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its compact size and adaptability, it is often used in ornamental landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
