Carex krebsiana

Carex krebsiana, commonly known as the Krebs' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, typical of many sedges. Carex krebsiana typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a valuable plant for soil stabilization and ecological restoration projects.
