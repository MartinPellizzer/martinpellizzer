Calamagrostis × ponojensis

Calamagrostis × ponojensis, commonly known as the blue moor grass, is a perennial grass native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage and tall, slender inflorescences that bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats. It plays a significant role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native environment.

Calamagrostis × ponojensis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
