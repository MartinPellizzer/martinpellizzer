Chaetogastra cordeiroi

Chaetogastra cordeiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian feather grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

It is native to Brazil and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open, disturbed areas. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a fibrous texture, and its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
