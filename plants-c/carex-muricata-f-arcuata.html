Carex muricata f. arcuata

Carex muricata f. arcuata, commonly known as the curly sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish itself in suitable habitats.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.
