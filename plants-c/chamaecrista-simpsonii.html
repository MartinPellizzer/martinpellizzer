Chamaecrista simpsonii

Chamaecrista simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's burrbean, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
