Cirsium halleri

Cirsium halleri, commonly known as the Haller's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is notable for its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or grassland habitats.

Cirsium halleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
