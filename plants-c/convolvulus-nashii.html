Convolvulus nashii

Convolvulus nashii, commonly known as Nash's bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Convolvulus nashii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
