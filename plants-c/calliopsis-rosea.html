Calliopsis rosea

Calliopsis rosea, commonly known as the pink rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small, pink, and clustered in racemes, adding a delicate touch to its habitat.

Calliopsis rosea is generally low-maintenance and well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
