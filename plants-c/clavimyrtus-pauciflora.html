Clavimyrtus pauciflora

Clavimyrtus pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, with a glossy surface and a strong, pleasant scent.

Clavimyrtus pauciflora is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
