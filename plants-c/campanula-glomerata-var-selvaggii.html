Campanula glomerata var. selvaggii

Campanula glomerata var. selvaggii, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
