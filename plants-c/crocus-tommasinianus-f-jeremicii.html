Crocus tommasinianus f. jeremicii

Crocus tommasinianus f. jeremicii, commonly known as the Jeremicii crocus, is a cultivar of the crocus genus characterized by its distinctive floral features.

This plant is a hybrid form that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species, often displaying vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma. It is a bulbous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, typical of many Crocus species. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit and striking flower color.
