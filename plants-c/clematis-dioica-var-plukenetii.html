Clematis dioica var. plukenetii

Clematis dioica var. plukenetii, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
