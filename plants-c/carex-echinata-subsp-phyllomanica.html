Carex echinata subsp. phyllomanica

Carex echinata subsp.

phyllomanica, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Its subspecies distinction is based on morphological differences, particularly in leaf and inflorescence structure.
