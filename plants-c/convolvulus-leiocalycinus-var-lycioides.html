Convolvulus leiocalycinus var. lycioides

Convolvulus leiocalycinus var. lycioides, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a sprawling perennial with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste ground.

Its botanical features make it both a resilient weed and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
