Cissus darik

Cissus darik, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, red berries. It is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Cissus darik is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
