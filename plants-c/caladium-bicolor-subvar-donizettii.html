Caladium bicolor subvar. donizettii

Caladium bicolor subvar. donizettii, commonly known as the "Picotee" caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of white, pink, and green hues. The leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy texture and can grow up to 12 inches in diameter. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is popular as a ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and is often used in gardens and as houseplants.
