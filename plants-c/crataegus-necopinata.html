Crataegus necopinata

Crataegus necopinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus necopinata is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.
