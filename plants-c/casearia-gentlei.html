Casearia gentlei

Casearia gentlei, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking purple to black berries that are both ornamental and edible, though they are typically consumed by birds rather than humans. Casearia gentlei thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage is glossy and dark green, adding visual interest throughout the year.
