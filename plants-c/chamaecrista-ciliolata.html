Chamaecrista ciliolata

Chamaecrista ciliolata, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in raceme clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are encased in slender, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Chamaecrista ciliolata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
