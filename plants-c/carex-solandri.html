Carex solandri

Carex solandri, commonly known as the Solander sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex solandri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
