Clematis bojeri var. macrophylla

Clematis bojeri var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved Clematis, is a flowering vine native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support, such as a trellis or fence, to climb and flourish.
