Cardamine flaccida

Cardamine flaccida, commonly known as the drooping watercress, is a delicate perennial herb native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant features trifoliate basal leaves and smaller, alternate leaves along its slender stems. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are borne in terminal racemes and attract various pollinators.

The entire plant is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland areas where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
