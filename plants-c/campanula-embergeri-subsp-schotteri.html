Campanula embergeri subsp. schotteri

Campanula embergeri subsp.

schotteri, commonly known as the Schotteri bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine layer of white or grayish pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
