Cyperorchis longifolia

Cyperorchis longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered clubmoss, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually green or brown and lack showy petals, which is characteristic of many Cyperaceae species.
