Calotropis sussuela

Calotropis sussuela, commonly known as the Persian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing compounds like cardiac glycosides.

Calotropis sussuela is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
