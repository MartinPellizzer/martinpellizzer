Costus ciliatus

Costus ciliatus, commonly known as the fragrant costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Costus ciliatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
