Chartolepis libanotica

Chartolepis libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Chartolepis libanotica is often found in open habitats such as hillsides and wastelands.
