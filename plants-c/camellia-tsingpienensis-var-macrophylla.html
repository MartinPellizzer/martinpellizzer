Camellia tsingpienensis var. macrophylla

Camellia tsingpienensis var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
