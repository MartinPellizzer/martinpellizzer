Cerasus asplenifolia

Cerasus asplenifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are typically white or pink in color. Its fruits are small, yellow plums that ripen in summer, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Cerasus asplenifolia prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
