Cheirostylis merrillii

Cheirostylis merrillii, commonly known as the Malay orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive spurred lip that is a key identifying feature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
