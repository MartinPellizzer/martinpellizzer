Cestrum diurnum var. fasciatiflorum

Cestrum diurnum var. fasciatiflorum, commonly known as the spotted flower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white with prominent yellow or red markings. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is evergreen, with dark green leaves that have a slightly rough texture.
