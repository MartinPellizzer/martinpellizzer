Carex rigida var. macedonica

Carex rigida var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

This variety is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
