Cynanchum jacquemontianum

Cynanchum jacquemontianum, commonly known as the Jacquemont's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
