Crinum erubescens var. caraccense

Crinum erubescens var. caraccense, commonly known as the Caraccan Crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its robust, fleshy bulbs and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Crinum erubescens var. caraccense is also known for its strong fragrance, which can be both enchanting and overwhelming.
