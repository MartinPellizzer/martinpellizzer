Cousinia butkovii

Cousinia butkovii, commonly known as the Armenian snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are white to pale blue and are surrounded by bracts that resemble petals.

Cousinia butkovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
