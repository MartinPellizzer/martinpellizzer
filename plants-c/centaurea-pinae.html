Centaurea pinae

Centaurea pinae, commonly known as the Pina's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Centaurea pinae is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
