Ceiba speciosa

Ceiba speciosa, commonly known as the "angel's trumpet" or "celestial tree," is a towering tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its massive trunk and broad, spreading crown. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous, woody capsule that splits open when mature.

Ceiba speciosa is also valued for its strong, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.
