Croton mamillatus

Croton mamillatus, commonly known as the spiny croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, often spiny bark and alternate, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes grown in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
