Calophanoides xerobatica

Calophanoides xerobatica, commonly known as the desert saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in drought-resistant gardens.
