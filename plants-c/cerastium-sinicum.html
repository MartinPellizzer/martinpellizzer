Cerastium sinicum

Cerastium sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Cerastium sinicum is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
