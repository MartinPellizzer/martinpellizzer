Crepis leontodontoides

Crepis leontodontoides, commonly known as the golden hawk's-bill, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, hairy growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
