Cereus sargentianus

Cereus sargentianus, commonly known as the Sargent's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its succulent structure is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and resilience.
