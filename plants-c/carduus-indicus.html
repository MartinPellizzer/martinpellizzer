Carduus indicus

Carduus indicus, commonly known as the Indian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
