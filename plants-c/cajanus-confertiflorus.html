Cajanus confertiflorus

Cajanus confertiflorus, commonly known as the cluster-flowered pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pod-shaped fruits containing several seeds, which are used for both human consumption and animal feed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Cajanus confertiflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to various environmental conditions.
