Chenopodium atrovirens

Chenopodium atrovirens, commonly known as black goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind. Chenopodium atrovirens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, arid environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
