Celastrus bilocularis

Celastrus bilocularis, commonly known as the two-chambered staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, orange-red berries containing several seeds.

Its growth habit and ornamental berries make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
