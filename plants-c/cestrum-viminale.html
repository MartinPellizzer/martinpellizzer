Cestrum viminale

Cestrum viminale, commonly known as the silver trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or vine that can grow up to 15 feet in length, featuring slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
