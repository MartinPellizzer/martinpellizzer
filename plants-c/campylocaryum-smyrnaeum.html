Campylocaryum smyrnaeum

Campylocaryum smyrnaeum, commonly known as the Smyrna thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

It is considered a hardy species that can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
