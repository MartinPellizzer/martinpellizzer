Cuphea tetrapetala var. pallida

Cuphea tetrapetala var. pallida, commonly known as the pale cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring through early summer.
