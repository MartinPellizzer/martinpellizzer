Copaifera mopane

Copaifera mopane, commonly known as mopane wood or mopani, is a tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. Its bark and wood have been traditionally used for various purposes, including fuel and construction.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
