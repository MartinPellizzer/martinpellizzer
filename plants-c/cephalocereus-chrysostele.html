Cephalocereus chrysostele

Cephalocereus chrysostele, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a spiny exterior and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are pollinated by bats and moths, which are attracted to its nectar.

The cactus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
