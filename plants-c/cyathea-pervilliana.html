Cyathea pervilliana

Cyathea pervilliana, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of multiple frond clusters, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyathea pervilliana plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
