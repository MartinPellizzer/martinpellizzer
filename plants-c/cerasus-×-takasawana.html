Cerasus × takasawana

Cerasus × takasawana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, red or black fruits that are edible but less commonly consumed.

The species is native to Japan and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.
