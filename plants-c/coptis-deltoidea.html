Coptis deltoidea

Coptis deltoidea, commonly known as the delta buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and palmately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Coptis deltoidea is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
