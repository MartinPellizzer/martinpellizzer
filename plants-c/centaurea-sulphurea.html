Centaurea sulphurea

Centaurea sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur-centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in summer and are surrounded by narrow, spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea sulphurea is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
