Cordyline imperialis

Cordyline imperialis, commonly known as the imperial cordyline, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear at the tips of the stems, followed by red berries. It is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for landscaping and as a houseplant in warm regions.
