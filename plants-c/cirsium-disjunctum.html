Cirsium disjunctum

Cirsium disjunctum, commonly known as the disjunct thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a distinctive appearance with its purple to pinkish flower heads. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Cirsium disjunctum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and wildlife.
