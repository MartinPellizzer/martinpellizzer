Cornutia

Cornutia, commonly known as the "milkweed of the south," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves and showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among members of the Apocynaceae family. Cornutia species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including forests and open areas.
