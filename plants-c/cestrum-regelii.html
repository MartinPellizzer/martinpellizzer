Cestrum regelii

Cestrum regelii, commonly known as the red-flowered trumpet vine, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Cestrum regelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and vibrant floral display.
