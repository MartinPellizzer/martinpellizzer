Columbea angustifolia

Columbea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed columbea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along forest floors or in damp undergrowth. It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
