Clematis hirsuta var. gallaensis

Clematis hirsuta var. gallaensis, commonly known as the Gallaean Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
