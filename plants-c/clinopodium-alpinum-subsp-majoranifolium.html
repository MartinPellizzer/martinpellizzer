Clinopodium alpinum subsp. majoranifolium

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

majoranifolium, commonly known as alpine oregano, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and hairy, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, mint-like scent.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their pungent fragrance and potential medicinal properties.

This subspecies is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in rocky soils and cold climates.
