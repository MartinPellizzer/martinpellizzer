Carex glauca subsp. dinarica

Carex glauca subsp.

dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage is often a bluish-green, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
