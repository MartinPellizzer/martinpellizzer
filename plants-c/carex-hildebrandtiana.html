Carex hildebrandtiana

Carex hildebrandtiana, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-sided, triangular berries.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, wet environments.
