Carex phalaroides var. crassiflora

Carex phalaroides var. crassiflora, commonly known as broad-flowered sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers in early summer. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
