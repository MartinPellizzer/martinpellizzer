Crataegus resecta

Crataegus resecta, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red drupes that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
