Carex mollicula var. hupehensis

Carex mollicula var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. This variety is distinguished by its soft, hairy texture and compact growth habit, making it suitable for moist, shady environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that add visual interest.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration and as an ornamental in water gardens due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
