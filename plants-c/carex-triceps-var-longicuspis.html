Carex triceps var. longicuspis

Carex triceps var. longicuspis, commonly known as the long-awned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
