Calamintha alpina var. nebrodensis

Calamintha alpina var. nebrodensis, commonly known as alpine calamint, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional herbal medicine for their calming properties.
