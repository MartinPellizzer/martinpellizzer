Cissus macrobotrys

Cissus macrobotrys, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white veins on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular ornamental plant.
