Carex purdiei

Carex purdiei, commonly known as the Purdie's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex purdiei plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
