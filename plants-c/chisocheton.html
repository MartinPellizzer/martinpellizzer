Chisocheton

Chisocheton, commonly known as the flame tree or *Chisocheton lobatus*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
