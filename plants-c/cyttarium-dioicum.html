Cyttarium dioicum

Cyttarium dioicum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cyttarium dioicum is often found in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides.
