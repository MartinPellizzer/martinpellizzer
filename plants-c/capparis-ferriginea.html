Capparis ferriginea

Capparis ferriginea, commonly known as the red-rooted caper, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis ferriginea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
