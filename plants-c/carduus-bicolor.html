Carduus bicolor

Carduus bicolor, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

Carduus bicolor is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
