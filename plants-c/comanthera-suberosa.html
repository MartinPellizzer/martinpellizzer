Comanthera suberosa

Comanthera suberosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
