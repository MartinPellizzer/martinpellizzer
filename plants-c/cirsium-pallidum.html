Cirsium pallidum

Cirsium pallidum, commonly known as the pale thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are spiny and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually purple or pink in color.

Cirsium pallidum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
