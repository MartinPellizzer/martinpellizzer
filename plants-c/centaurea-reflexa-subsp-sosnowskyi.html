Centaurea reflexa subsp. sosnowskyi

Centaurea reflexa subsp.

sosnowskyi, commonly known as Sosnowsky's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes cultivated or considered a weed.
