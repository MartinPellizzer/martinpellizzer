Calamintha abyssinica

Calamintha abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian germander, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Calamintha abyssinica is often grown for its ornamental value and has a mild, mint-like fragrance.
