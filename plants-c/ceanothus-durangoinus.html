Ceanothus durangoinus

Ceanothus durangoinus, commonly known as Durango ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid landscapes. It is part of the Rutaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like citrus.

Ceanothus durangoinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
