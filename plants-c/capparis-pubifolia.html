Capparis pubifolia

Capparis pubifolia, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Capparis pubifolia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
