Celtis hildebrandii

Celtis hildebrandii, commonly known as the Hildebrand's hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

It is often found in moist bottomlands, along streams, and in upland forests, showcasing its adaptability to various soil types.
