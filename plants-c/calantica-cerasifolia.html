Calantica cerasifolia

Calantica cerasifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaved calantica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, elliptic leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
