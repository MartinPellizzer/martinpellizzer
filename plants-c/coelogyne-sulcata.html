Coelogyne sulcata

Coelogyne sulcata, commonly known as the wavy-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, wavy-edged leaves and vibrant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow with red or orange markings, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne sulcata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
