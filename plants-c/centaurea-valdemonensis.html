Centaurea valdemonensis

Centaurea valdemonensis, commonly known as the Valdemonica centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spiky bracts around its composite flowers.

Centaurea valdemonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows.
