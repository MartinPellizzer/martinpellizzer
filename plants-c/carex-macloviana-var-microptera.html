Carex macloviana var. microptera

Carex macloviana var. microptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
