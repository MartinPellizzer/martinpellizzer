Ceratocarpus maritimus

Ceratocarpus maritimus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring silver-gray, scale-like leaves and spiky, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. Its unique morphology makes it a striking addition to coastal landscapes.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
