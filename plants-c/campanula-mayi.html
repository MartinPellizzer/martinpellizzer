Campanula mayi

Campanula mayi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Campanula mayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
