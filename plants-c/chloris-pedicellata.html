Chloris pedicellata

Chloris pedicellata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a blue-green hue, while the flowers are small and clustered.

Chloris pedicellata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
