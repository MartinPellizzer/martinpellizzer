Campanula polymorpha f. mentiens

Campanula polymorpha f. mentiens, commonly known as the Mentha-leaved bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a mint-like scent, giving it its common name. The flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet, and bloom in late summer.

It is a perennial plant that prefers full sun to partial shade and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
