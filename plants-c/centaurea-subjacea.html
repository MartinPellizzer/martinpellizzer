Centaurea subjacea

Centaurea subjacea, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
