Calamagrostis epigejos var. huebneriana

Calamagrostis epigejos var. huebneriana, commonly known as the European reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate texture to the landscape. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
