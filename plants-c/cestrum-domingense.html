Cestrum domingense

Cestrum domingense, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.
