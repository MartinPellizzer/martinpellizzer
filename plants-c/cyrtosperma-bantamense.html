Cyrtosperma bantamense

Cyrtosperma bantamense, commonly known as the Bantam arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant produces edible tubers that are used in local cuisines for their starchy texture. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and home gardens.
