Calliandra salvadorensis

Calliandra salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, pink or red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and can contribute to nitrogen fixation in the soil.
