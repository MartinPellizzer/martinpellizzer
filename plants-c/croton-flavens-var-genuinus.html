Croton flavens var. genuinus

Croton flavens var. genuinus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that range from yellow to red and orange hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
