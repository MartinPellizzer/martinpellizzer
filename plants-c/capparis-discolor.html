Capparis discolor

Capparis discolor, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that contain seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
