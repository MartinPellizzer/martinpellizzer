Cenchrus ciliaris var. pennisetiformis

Cenchrus ciliaris var. pennisetiformis, commonly known as buffelgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to dry, semi-arid environments and is often used for forage and soil stabilization.

Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it a resilient species in challenging climates.
