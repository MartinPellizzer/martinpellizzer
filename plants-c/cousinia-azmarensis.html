Cousinia azmarensis

Cousinia azmarensis, commonly known as the Azmaran cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, featuring succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Cousinia azmarensis is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
