Chorisia pubiflora

Chorisia pubiflora, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-colored bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant has a distinctive, tree-like structure with a thick, woody trunk and spreading branches. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its unique appearance.
