Cactus caronatus

Cactus caronatus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to absorb water quickly from infrequent rains. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

The flowers are often pollinated by nocturnal insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
