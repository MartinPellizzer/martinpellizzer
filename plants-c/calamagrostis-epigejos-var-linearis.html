Calamagrostis epigejos var. linearis

Calamagrostis epigejos var. linearis, commonly known as slender bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of blue-gray florets that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
