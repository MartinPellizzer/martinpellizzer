Cyperus indecorus

Cyperus indecorus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or brown. Cyperus indecorus typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

It is often considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
