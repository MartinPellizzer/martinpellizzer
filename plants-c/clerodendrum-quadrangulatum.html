Clerodendrum quadrangulatum

Clerodendrum quadrangulatum, commonly known as the four-angle clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, quadrangular (four-sided) stems. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with bright yellow to orange flowers, which are often accompanied by small, greenish bracts. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
