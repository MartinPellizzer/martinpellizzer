Chamaesyce theriaca

Chamaesyce theriaca, commonly known as goatweed, is a flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

The plant contains latex and has been used traditionally in some cultures for various purposes.
