Carex guffroyana

Carex guffroyana, commonly known as the Guffroy's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats. It produces small, greenish flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Carex guffroyana is often found in areas with partial shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
