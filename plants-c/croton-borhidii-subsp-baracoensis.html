Croton borhidii subsp. baracoensis

Croton borhidii subsp.

baracoensis, commonly known as the Baracoa croton, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually alternate and can vary in shape, sometimes being ovate or elliptic.
