Colutea spinosa

Colutea spinosa, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Colutea spinosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
