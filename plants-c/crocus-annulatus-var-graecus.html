Crocus annulatus var. graecus

Crocus annulatus var. graecus, commonly known as the Greek crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and early blooming period, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and contribution to early season pollinator activity.
