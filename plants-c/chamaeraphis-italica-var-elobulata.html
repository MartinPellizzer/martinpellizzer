Chamaeraphis italica var. elobulata

Chamaeraphis italica var. elobulata, commonly known as the Italian sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in arid or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping.
