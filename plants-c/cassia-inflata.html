Cassia inflata

Cassia inflata, commonly known as the inflated cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces inflated, seed-filled pods that split open when mature.

Cassia inflata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
