Coronilla rostrata var. pauciflora

Coronilla rostrata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the scarlet vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and spikes of small, bright red or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and scrublands. It has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
