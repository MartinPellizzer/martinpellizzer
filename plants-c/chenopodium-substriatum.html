Chenopodium substriatum

Chenopodium substriatum, commonly known as smooth goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium substriatum is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and spread.
