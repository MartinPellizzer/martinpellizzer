Capsicum muticum

Capsicum muticum, commonly known as the mountain pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into green, fleshy fruits.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and is known for its mild spicy flavor.
