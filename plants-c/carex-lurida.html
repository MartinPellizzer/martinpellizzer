Carex lurida

Carex lurida, commonly known as the purple sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with a reddish or purplish tint on the underside. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in a spike-like structure and are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex lurida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
