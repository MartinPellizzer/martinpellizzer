Crinum vanillodorum

Crinum vanillodorum, commonly known as the vanilla crinum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

Crinum vanillodorum is cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique aromatic properties.
