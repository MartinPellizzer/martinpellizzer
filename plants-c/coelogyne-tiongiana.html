Coelogyne tiongiana

Coelogyne tiongiana, commonly known as the Tiongian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne tiongiana thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
