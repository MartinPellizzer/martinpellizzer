Cotoneaster giraldii

Cotoneaster giraldii, commonly known as the Giant Cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside.

Cotoneaster giraldii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
