Cereus olivaceus

Cereus olivaceus, commonly known as the olive-cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Cereus olivaceus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
