Chrysanthemum transcaucasicum

Chrysanthemum transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
