Calceolaria salicifolia subsp. salicifolia

Calceolaria salicifolia subsp.

salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, willow-like leaves and striking, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats. It blooms in the spring and is often found in the Andes region.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
