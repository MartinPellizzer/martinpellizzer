Cuphea heterophylla

Cuphea heterophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea heterophylla is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
