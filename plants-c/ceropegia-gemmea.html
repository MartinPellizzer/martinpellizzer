Ceropegia gemmea

Ceropegia gemmea, commonly known as the gemmed arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, tuberous stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
