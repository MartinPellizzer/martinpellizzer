Crepis dubia

Crepis dubia, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters.

Crepis dubia is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes where few other plants can survive.
