Cassia centiflora

Cassia centiflora, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cassia centiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
