Carex alliiformis

Carex alliiformis, commonly known as the garlic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically hidden within the leafy foliage. Carex alliiformis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
