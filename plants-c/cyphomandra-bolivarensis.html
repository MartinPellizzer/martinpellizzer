Cyphomandra bolivarensis

Cyphomandra bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian tomato, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines, though they are not as widely cultivated as other tomato species. It has heart-shaped leaves and typically grows as an annual herb.

The plant's botanical characteristics include hairy stems and showy, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.
