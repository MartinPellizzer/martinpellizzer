Chamaecrista spodiotricha

Chamaecrista spodiotricha, commonly known as the hairy crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, which aid in seed dispersal.

Chamaecrista spodiotricha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
