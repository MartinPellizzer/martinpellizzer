Calathea reginae

Calathea reginae, commonly known as the Queen Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is prized for its striking, colorful foliage that features intricate patterns and vibrant hues of green, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a velvety texture and a waxy sheen. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This plant is known for its ability to purify the air and is often used as a decorative indoor plant.
