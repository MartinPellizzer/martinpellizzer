Caelestina ageratoides

Caelestina ageratoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Caelestina ageratoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
