Cotoneaster lacteus

Cotoneaster lacteus, commonly known as the white cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval in shape with a slightly serrated edge. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and fruit.

Cotoneaster lacteus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
