Caryophyllus racemosus

Caryophyllus racemosus, commonly known as the Indian clove or wild clove, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some culinary contexts.
