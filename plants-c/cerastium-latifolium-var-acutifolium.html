Cerastium latifolium var. acutifolium

Cerastium latifolium var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a distinctive acuminate tip, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
