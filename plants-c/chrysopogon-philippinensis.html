Chrysopogon philippinensis

Chrysopogon philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine grass or Philippine galingale, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale yellow to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and forest margins.

Chrysopogon philippinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
