Catonia blattarioides

Catonia blattarioides, commonly known as the leafy bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient absorption. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves. It is a carnivorous plant that relies on its specialized structures to supplement its nutrient intake.

Despite its carnivorous nature, it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling microorganism populations.
