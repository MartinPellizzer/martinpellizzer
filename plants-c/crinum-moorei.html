Crinum moorei

Crinum moorei, commonly known as the Moore's crinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish flowers. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Crinum moorei is known for its strong, spicy fragrance, which is released when the flowers open.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
