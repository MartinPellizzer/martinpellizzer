Clianthus puniceus

Clianthus puniceus, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain fireweed, is a striking member of the Fabaceae family native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, flame-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

Clianthus puniceus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for native pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
