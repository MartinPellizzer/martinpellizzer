Crepis tenerrima

Crepis tenerrima, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, open habitats. Its small, pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Crepis tenerrima is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
