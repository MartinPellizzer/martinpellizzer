Chamaecyparis obtusa var. erecta

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. erecta, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Japanese white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dense foliage, and scale-like leaves that are often a deep green. The tree produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
