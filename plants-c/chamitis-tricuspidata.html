Chamitis tricuspidata

Chamitis tricuspidata, commonly known as three-lobed chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three distinct lobes, and it produces small, aromatic flowers that bloom in the summer.

Chamitis tricuspidata is valued for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
