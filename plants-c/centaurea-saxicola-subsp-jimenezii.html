Centaurea saxicola subsp. jimenezii

Centaurea saxicola subsp.

jimenezii, commonly known as Jimenez's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
