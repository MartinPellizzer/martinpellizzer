Cordyla calycandra

Cordyla calycandra, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Cordyla calycandra is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture.
