Caladium petiolatum

Caladium petiolatum, commonly known as the "Cape Cod" caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that come in vibrant shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its petioles, or leaf stalks, are notably long and sturdy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Caladium petiolatum is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
