Craspedia fimbriata var. major

Craspedia fimbriata var. major, commonly known as the broad-fringed everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
