Crotalaria hexaptera

Crotalaria hexaptera, commonly known as the six-winged cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with six petals. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop and has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

Crotalaria hexaptera is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
