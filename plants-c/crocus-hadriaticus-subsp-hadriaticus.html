Crocus hadriaticus subsp. hadriaticus

Crocus hadriaticus subsp.

hadriaticus, commonly known as the Adriatic crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical corms for storage and reproduction. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or calcareous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
