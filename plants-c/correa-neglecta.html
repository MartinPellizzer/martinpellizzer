Correa neglecta

Correa neglecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive blue-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
