Centaurea davisii

Centaurea davisii, commonly known as Davis's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea davisii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
