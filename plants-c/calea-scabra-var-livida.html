Calea scabra var. livida

Calea scabra var. livida, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet ray florets that resemble a misty cloud. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
