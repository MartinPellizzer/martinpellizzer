Cassia cytisoides var. decora

Cassia cytisoides var. decora, commonly known as the ornamental cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.
