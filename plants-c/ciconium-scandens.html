Ciconium scandens

Ciconium scandens, commonly known as the climbing ciconium, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a woody vine characterized by its long, trailing stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ciconium scandens is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
