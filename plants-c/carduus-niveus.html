Carduus niveus

Carduus niveus, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and striking white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are often spiny, and the flower heads are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Carduus niveus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
