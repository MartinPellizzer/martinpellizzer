Calathea cofaniorum

Calathea cofaniorum, commonly known as the Cofan flame plant, is a tropical species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns of red, green, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that enhances its aesthetic appeal.

Calathea cofaniorum requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
