Callirhoe involucrata var. palmata

Callirhoe involucrata var. palmata, commonly known as the palmate coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a hand. The plant produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in arid landscape designs.
