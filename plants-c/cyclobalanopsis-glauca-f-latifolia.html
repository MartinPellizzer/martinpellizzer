Cyclobalanopsis glauca f. latifolia

Cyclobalanopsis glauca f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen oak, is a variant of the cyclobalanopsis glauca species, characterized by its broader leaf morphology.

It is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a leathery texture and a glossy, dark green upper surface. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and mountainous forest ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
