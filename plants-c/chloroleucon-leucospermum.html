Chloroleucon leucospermum

Chloroleucon leucospermum, commonly known as the white starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
