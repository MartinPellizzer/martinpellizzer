Cirsium opacum

Cirsium opacum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Cirsium opacum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
