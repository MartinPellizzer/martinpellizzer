Calceolaria ericoides

Calceolaria ericoides, commonly known as the heath calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Calceolaria ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
