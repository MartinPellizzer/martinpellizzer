Chrysophyllum grandifolium

Chrysophyllum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf chrysophyllum, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic use in agriculture and pharmaceuticals.
