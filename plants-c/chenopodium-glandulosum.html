Chenopodium glandulosum

Chenopodium glandulosum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and leaves with a rough, sometimes prickly texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Chenopodium glandulosum is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste.
