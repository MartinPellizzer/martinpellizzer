Clompanus arabica

Clompanus arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia and Sudan.

It is a small evergreen shrub that produces red berries containing the seeds used to make coffee. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It requires a specific range of temperatures and careful cultivation to ensure optimal growth.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season.
