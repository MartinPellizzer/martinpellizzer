Conoclinium subviscosum

Conoclinium subviscosum, commonly known as sticky trumpetweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its tubular, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has hairy, often sticky stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Conoclinium subviscosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
