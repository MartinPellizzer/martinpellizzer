Chamaemelum montanum

Chamaemelum montanum, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its flat, feathery clusters of small white or pinkish flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Chamaemelum montanum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
