Clethra conzattiana

Clethra conzattiana, commonly known as the Conzatti clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Clethra conzattiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
