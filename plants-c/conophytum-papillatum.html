Conophytum papillatum

Conophytum papillatum, commonly known as the "Cushion Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy, and often brightly colored leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Conophytum papillatum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
