Cenocline

Cenocline, commonly known as the "flowering plant of the genus Cenocline," is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate and lanceolate, with a rough texture.

Cenocline species are often found in meadows and along roadsides, contributing to the biodiversity of these habitats.
