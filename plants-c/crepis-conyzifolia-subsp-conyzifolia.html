Crepis conyzifolia subsp. conyzifolia

Crepis conyzifolia subsp.

conyzifolia, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.
