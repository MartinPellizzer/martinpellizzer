Collomia cavanillesii f. coccinea

Collomia cavanillesii f. coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or open habitats. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
