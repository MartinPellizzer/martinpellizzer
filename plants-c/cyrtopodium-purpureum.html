Cyrtopodium purpureum

Cyrtopodium purpureum, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall compact appearance.

Cyrtopodium purpureum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
