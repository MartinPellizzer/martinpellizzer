Calophyllum andersonii

Calophyllum andersonii, commonly known as the Andes mountain tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
