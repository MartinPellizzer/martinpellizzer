Centaurea scopulicola

Centaurea scopulicola, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vivid blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea scopulicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and dry landscaping.
