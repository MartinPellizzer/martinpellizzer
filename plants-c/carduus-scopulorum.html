Carduus scopulorum

Carduus scopulorum, commonly known as the rock thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its robust root system allows it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat inflammation and digestive issues.
