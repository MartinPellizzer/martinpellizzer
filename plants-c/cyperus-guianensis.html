Cyperus guianensis

Cyperus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus guianensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
