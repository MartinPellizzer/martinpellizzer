Campanula americana f. tubuliflora

Campanula americana f. tubuliflora, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
