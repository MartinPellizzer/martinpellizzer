Calamus nitidus

Calamus nitidus, commonly known as the smooth calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, often forming dense colonies. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Calamus nitidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
