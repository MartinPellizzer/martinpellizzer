Celastrus rosthornianus var. loeseneri

Celastrus rosthornianus var. loeseneri, commonly known as the Loesener's staffvetch, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the genus Celastrus.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in small umbels, followed by orange-red berries that are visually striking. It is native to specific regions in Central Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant but under-studied species.
