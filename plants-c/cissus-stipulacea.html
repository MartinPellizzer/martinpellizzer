Cissus stipulacea

Cissus stipulacea, commonly known as the leafy creeper or tropical arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in traditional cuisine. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions make it a popular ornamental plant.

Cissus stipulacea is also valued for its medicinal properties in some traditional systems of medicine.
