Chenopodium luteum

Chenopodium luteum, commonly known as yellow goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular shell. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium luteum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
