Chasmone lanceolata

Chasmone lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chasmone, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
