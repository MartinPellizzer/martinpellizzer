Clematis vietnamensis

Clematis vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Clematis vietnamensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
