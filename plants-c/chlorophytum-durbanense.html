Chlorophytum durbanense

Chlorophytum durbanense, commonly known as the Durban spider plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. It is a hardy, evergreen perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Chlorophytum durbanense is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
