Centaurea monticola var. orthocantha

Centaurea monticola var. orthocantha, commonly known as the mountain centaury, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features upright stems with spiny leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by pale blue to purple bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
