Croton phaenodon

Croton phaenodon, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
