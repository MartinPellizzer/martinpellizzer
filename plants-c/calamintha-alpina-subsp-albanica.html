Calamintha alpina subsp. albanica

Calamintha alpina subsp.

albanica, commonly known as alpine calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their mild aromatic and possibly anti-inflammatory properties.
