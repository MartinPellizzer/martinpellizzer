Curcuma andersonii

Curcuma andersonii, commonly known as the Andes turmeric, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Curcuma andersonii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal uses.
