Ceanothus × rugosus

Ceanothus × rugosus, commonly known as the coast ceanothus, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
