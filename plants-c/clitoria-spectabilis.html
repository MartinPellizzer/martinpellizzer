Clitoria spectabilis

Clitoria spectabilis, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
