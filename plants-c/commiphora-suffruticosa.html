Commiphora suffruticosa

Commiphora suffruticosa, commonly known as Indian myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
