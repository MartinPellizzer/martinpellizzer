Coffea canephora var. oka

Coffea canephora var. oka, commonly known as the Oka coffee bean, is a variant of the robusta coffee species native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped beans and a dark, almost black color when dried. The plant produces dense foliage and has a robust growth habit, thriving in tropical climates with high humidity. It is known for its strong, bold flavor profile with notes of nuts and chocolate.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its high caffeine content and resilience to pests and diseases.
