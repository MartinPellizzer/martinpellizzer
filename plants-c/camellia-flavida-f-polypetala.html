Camellia flavida f. polypetala

Camellia flavida f. polypetala, commonly known as the pink-flowered camellia, is a rare and ornamental variant of the camellia genus.

It is characterized by its showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is native to southern China and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. It has glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, red seed pods after flowering.

This cultivar is valued for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
