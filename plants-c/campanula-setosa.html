Campanula setosa

Campanula setosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Campanula setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
