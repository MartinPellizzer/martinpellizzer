Cyperus mapanioides

Cyperus mapanioides, commonly known as the African bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus mapanioides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
