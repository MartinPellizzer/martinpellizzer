Carex concava

Carex concava, commonly known as the concave sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curved, with a distinctive concave shape along the midrib.

Carex concava is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
