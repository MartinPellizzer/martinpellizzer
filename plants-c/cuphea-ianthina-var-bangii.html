Cuphea ianthina var. bangii

Cuphea ianthina var. bangii, commonly known as the flamebeard plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, creating a fiery visual effect. The plant produces showy, long-lasting inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cuphea ianthina var. bangii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
