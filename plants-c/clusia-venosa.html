Clusia venosa

Clusia venosa, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are typically leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia venosa is also known for its ability to tolerate various soil types and poor drainage, making it a resilient choice for landscaping.
