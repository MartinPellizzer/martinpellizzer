Calamagrostis epigejos subsp. extremorientalis

Calamagrostis epigejos subsp.

extremorientalis, commonly known as the eastern Asian reed, is a perennial grass native to regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

This subspecies is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
