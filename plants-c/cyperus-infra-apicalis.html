Cyperus infra-apicalis

Cyperus infra-apicalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in a panicle-like structure.

Cyperus infra-apicalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
