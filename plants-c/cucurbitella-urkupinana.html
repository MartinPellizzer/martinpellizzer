Cucurbitella urkupinana

Cucurbitella urkupinana, commonly known as the Urkupina gourd, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit with a rough, warty surface. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a hairy texture.

The flowering pattern is monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.
