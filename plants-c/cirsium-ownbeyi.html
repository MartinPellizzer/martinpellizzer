Cirsium ownbeyi

Cirsium ownbeyi, commonly known as the Ownbey's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium ownbeyi is often found in areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.
