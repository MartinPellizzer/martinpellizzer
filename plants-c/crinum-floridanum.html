Crinum floridanum

Crinum floridanum, commonly known as the Florida rain lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy scent. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Crinum floridanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
