Callipeltis cucullaris var. ciliata

Callipeltis cucullaris var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and they are arranged oppositely along the stems. The flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.
