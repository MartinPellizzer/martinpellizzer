Castela leonis

Castela leonis, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Castela leonis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
