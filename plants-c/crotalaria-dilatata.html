Crotalaria dilatata

Crotalaria dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Crotalaria dilatata is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
