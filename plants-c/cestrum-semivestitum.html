Cestrum semivestitum

Cestrum semivestitum, commonly known as the red cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum semivestitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance.
