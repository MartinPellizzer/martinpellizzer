Colea ambrensis

Colea ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrensis Colea, is a rare and unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and requires full sunlight to thrive. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, making it a notable species for botanical study.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Colea ambrensis is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
