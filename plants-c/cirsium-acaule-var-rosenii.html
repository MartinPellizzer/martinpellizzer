Cirsium acaule var. rosenii

Cirsium acaule var. rosenii, commonly known as the Rosen's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite flower structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
