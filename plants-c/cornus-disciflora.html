Cornus disciflora

Cornus disciflora, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. Its stems are typically reddish-brown, especially in the winter, which adds ornamental value.

Cornus disciflora is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
