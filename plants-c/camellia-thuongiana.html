Camellia thuongiana

Camellia thuongiana, commonly known as the Vietnamese camellia, is a species native to Vietnam and southern China.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, with a waxy texture. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval in shape, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Camellia thuongiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
