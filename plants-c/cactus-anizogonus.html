Cactus anizogonus

Cactus anizogonus, commonly known as the bird's nest cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching structure that resembles a bird's nest, with clusters of spiny stems forming a dense, protective covering. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warm season, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting it to harsh desert conditions.

Cactus anizogonus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water environments.
