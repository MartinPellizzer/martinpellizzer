Ceanothus andersonii

Ceanothus andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus andersonii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
