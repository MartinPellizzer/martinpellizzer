Clematis tatarinowii

Clematis tatarinowii, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis tatarinowii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
