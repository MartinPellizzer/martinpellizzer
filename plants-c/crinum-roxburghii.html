Crinum roxburghii

Crinum roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian lily or Roxburgh's crinum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces large, white or pale pink flowers with a strong, often unpleasant fragrance. Its leaves are broad, strap-shaped, and arranged in a rosette at the base.

Crinum roxburghii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
