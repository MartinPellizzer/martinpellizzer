Chimonobambusa jainii

Chimonobambusa jainii, commonly known as the Jain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Chimonobambusa jainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
