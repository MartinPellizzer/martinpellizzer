Celsia barnadesii

Celsia barnadesii, commonly known as the Brazilian cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a small, branching cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring ribbed stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with spines that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Celsia barnadesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique form and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
