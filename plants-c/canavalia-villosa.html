Canavalia villosa

Canavalia villosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it adaptable to various environments. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Canavalia villosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities in agricultural settings.
