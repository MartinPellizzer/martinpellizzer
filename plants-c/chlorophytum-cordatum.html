Chlorophytum cordatum

Chlorophytum cordatum, commonly known as the Tiger's Tail or Spider Plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often striped with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers on long, slender stalks, which are followed by clusters of bright green, arrowhead-shaped berries. It is a hardy, evergreen perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and moderate light conditions.

Chlorophytum cordatum is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to purify indoor air.
