Chamaemelum heterolepis

Chamaemelum heterolepis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Chamaemelum heterolepis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
