Centaurea obtusiloba

Centaurea obtusiloba, commonly known as the bluntleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Centaurea obtusiloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
