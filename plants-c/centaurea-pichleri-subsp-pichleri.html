Centaurea pichleri subsp. pichleri

Centaurea pichleri subsp.

pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It produces small, bracteate flower heads that are arranged in a composite structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
