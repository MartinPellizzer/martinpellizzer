Chassalia acutiflora

Chassalia acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense cluster, and bloom during the warmer months.

Chassalia acutiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
