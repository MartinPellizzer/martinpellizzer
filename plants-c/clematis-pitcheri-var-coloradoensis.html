Clematis pitcheri var. coloradoensis

Clematis pitcheri var. coloradoensis, commonly known as the Colorado clematis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing white or pale blue flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in arid conditions.
