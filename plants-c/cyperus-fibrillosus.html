Cyperus fibrillosus

Cyperus fibrillosus, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils, it is often found in areas with high humidity and frequent rainfall.
