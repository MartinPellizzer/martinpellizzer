Curcuma zedoaria

Curcuma zedoaria, commonly known as galangal or wild ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, knotted rhizomes. The plant produces tall, leafy stems and small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, valued for their distinctive flavor and potential health benefits.

The botanical features of C. zedoaria make it an important species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical practices.
