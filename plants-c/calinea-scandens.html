Calinea scandens

Calinea scandens, commonly known as the climbing calinea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a vine-like species that typically grows along the ground or climbs on other structures using its tendrils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Calinea scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
