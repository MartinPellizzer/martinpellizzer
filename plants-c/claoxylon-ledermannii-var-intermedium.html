Claoxylon ledermannii var. intermedium

Claoxylon ledermannii var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate claoxylon, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
