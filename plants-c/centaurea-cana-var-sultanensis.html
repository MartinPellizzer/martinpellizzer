Centaurea cana var. sultanensis

Centaurea cana var. sultanensis, commonly known as Sultan's Blush, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, bright pink to red flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
