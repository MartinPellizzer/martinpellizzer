Campanula pannonica

Campanula pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula pannonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
