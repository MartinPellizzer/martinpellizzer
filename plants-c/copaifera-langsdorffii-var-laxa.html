Copaifera langsdorffii var. laxa

Copaifera langsdorffii var. laxa, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.
