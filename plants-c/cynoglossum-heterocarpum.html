Cynoglossum heterocarpum

Cynoglossum heterocarpum, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct blue or purple coloration. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet, with five lobes, and bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
