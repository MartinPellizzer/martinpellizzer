Corydalis adunca subsp. scaphopetala

Corydalis adunca subsp.

scaphopetala, commonly known as the narrowleaf corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It features slender, branching stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped.

The flowers are typically pale pink to purple, with two upper petals that curve back and two lower petals that are spurred. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland settings.
