Cardamine × dubia

Cardamine × dubia, commonly known as the hybrid bittercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are usually basal, with a distinctive fern-like structure, and it produces small, yellow flowers in early spring.

Cardamine × dubia is often found in areas with rich, organic soil and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
