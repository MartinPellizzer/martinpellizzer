Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. ceruminosus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. ceruminosus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features yellow, fluffy flower heads that bloom in the spring and early summer. The plant has silvery-green, aromatic foliage that is often covered in a fine, whitish coating. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its structure is low-growing with multiple stems, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and erosion control.
