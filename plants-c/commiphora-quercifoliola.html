Commiphora quercifoliola

Commiphora quercifoliola, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of an oak. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in traditional medicine.
