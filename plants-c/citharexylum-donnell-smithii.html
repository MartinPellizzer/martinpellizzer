Citharexylum donnell-smithii

Citharexylum donnell-smithii, commonly known as the New Zealand honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and along stream banks.
