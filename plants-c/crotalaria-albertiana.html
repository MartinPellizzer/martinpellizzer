Crotalaria albertiana

Crotalaria albertiana, commonly known as the Albert's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red veins and palmate leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It grows as an annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Crotalaria albertiana is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
