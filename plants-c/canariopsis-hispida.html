Canariopsis hispida

Canariopsis hispida, commonly known as the rough canaryweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its hairy leaves and stems give it a distinctive appearance, contributing to its common name.
