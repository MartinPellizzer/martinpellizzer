Cissus diversilobata

Cissus diversilobata, commonly known as the variable-lobed cutleaf, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lobed leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and unique foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
