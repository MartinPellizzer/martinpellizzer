Chenopodium album subsp. opulifolium

Chenopodium album subsp.

opulifolium, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and has alternate, ovate to triangular leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its fruits are dry, three-angled capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is also known for its edible young leaves, which are used in some culinary traditions.
