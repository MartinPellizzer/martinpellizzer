Cyperus tomaiophyllus

Cyperus tomaiophyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-ranked flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly rough texture.

Cyperus tomaiophyllus is typically found in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands.
