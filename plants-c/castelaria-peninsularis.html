Castelaria peninsularis

Castelaria peninsularis, commonly known as the Canary Islands spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Castelaria peninsularis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

It is notable for its ability to withstand drought and saline conditions, making it adapted to the unique island environment.
