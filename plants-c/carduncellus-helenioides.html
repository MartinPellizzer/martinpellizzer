Carduncellus helenioides

Carduncellus helenioides, commonly known as the Helen's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and silvery-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to Mediterranean gardens.
