Cologania humifusa

Cologania humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel or common groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cologania humifusa is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
