Capparis palmeri

Capparis palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's caper, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, reddish-purple fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is characterized by its ability to withstand drought and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
