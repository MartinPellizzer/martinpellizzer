Carex conica var. lanceolata

Carex conica var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-brown spikelets that mature into dark brown seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
