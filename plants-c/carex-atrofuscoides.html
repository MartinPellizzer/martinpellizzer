Carex atrofuscoides

Carex atrofuscoides, commonly known as blackroot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective plant for stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in wetland environments.
