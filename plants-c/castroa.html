Castroa

Castroa, commonly known as the coastal lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to the southern hemisphere.

It typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces showy, often white or pink, bell-shaped flowers. Castroa species are generally low-growing and are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
