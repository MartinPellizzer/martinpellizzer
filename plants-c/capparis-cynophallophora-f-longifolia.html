Capparis cynophallophora f. longifolia

Capparis cynophallophora f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-stalked caper, is a variant of the caper plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, waxy leaves that are typically hairy in texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, round, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
