Carex parvirufa

Carex parvirufa, commonly known as the slender redstem sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex parvirufa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
