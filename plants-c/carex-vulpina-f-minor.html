Carex vulpina f. minor

Carex vulpina f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf moor grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along stream banks. Its compact form and ability to form dense mats make it a valuable plant for erosion control and habitat creation.

The inflorescence consists of small, three-ranked spikelets that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
