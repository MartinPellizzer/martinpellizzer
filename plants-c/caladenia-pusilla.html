Caladenia pusilla

Caladenia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flower spike. The flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, narrow spur. It blooms during the spring and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate appearance and specialized pollination mechanisms.
