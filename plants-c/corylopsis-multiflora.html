Corylopsis multiflora

Corylopsis multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese lantern bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal interest. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy ornamental choice for gardens.

Its striking flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
