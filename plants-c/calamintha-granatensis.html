Calamintha granatensis

Calamintha granatensis, commonly known as granate calamint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features square, hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. Its aromatic leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Calamintha granatensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
