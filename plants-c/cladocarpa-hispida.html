Cladocarpa hispida

Cladocarpa hispida, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Cladocarpa hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
