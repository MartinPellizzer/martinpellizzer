Cladium mariscus var. ferruginescens

Cladium mariscus var. ferruginescens, commonly known as the rusty spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by bracts. The plant has fibrous roots and grass-like leaves that are often covered with a rusty-colored bloom, giving it its common name. It thrives in marshy areas and is adapted to periodic flooding.

Cladium mariscus var. ferruginescens plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
