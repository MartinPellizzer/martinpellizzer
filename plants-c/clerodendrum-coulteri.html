Clerodendrum coulteri

Clerodendrum coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's chaste tree, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a globose capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
