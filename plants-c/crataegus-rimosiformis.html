Crataegus rimosiformis

Crataegus rimosiformis, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a woody, often multi-stemmed structure.

Crataegus rimosiformis is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
