Chascanum angolense subsp. zambesiacum

Chascanum angolense subsp.

zambesiacum, commonly known as the Zambesia chascanum, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its succulent stems and reduced leaf surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
