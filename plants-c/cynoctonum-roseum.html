Cynoctonum roseum

Cynoctonum roseum, commonly known as the pink hibiscus mosquito, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that resemble hibiscus blooms. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found near water sources.

The plant is also known for its role in the life cycle of the Aedes aegypti mosquito, which is a vector for diseases like dengue and Zika.
