Crotalaria isaloensis

Crotalaria isaloensis, commonly known as the Ile-Ile plant, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It has small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
