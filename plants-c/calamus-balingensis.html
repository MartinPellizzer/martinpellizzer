Calamus balingensis

Calamus balingensis, commonly known as the Balingen bamboo or Balingen calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense clumps. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered fruits.

Calamus balingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in tropical gardens.
