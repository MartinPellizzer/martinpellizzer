Campanula bayerniana var. trautvetteri

Campanula bayerniana var. trautvetteri, commonly known as the Trautvetter's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
