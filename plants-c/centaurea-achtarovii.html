Centaurea achtarovii

Centaurea achtarovii, commonly known as the Achtarovii knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in mountainous areas. It features bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is relatively drought-tolerant.
