Caprifolium semperflorens

Caprifolium semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming honeysuckle, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
