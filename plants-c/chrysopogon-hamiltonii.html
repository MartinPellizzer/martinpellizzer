Chrysopogon hamiltonii

Chrysopogon hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to brownish inflorescences. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Chrysopogon hamiltonii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
