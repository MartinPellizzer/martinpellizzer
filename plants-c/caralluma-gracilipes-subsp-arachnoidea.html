Caralluma gracilipes subsp. arachnoidea

Caralluma gracilipes subsp.

arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-tailed gem, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that bear small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems. Its unique appearance, with leaf-like structures resembling spider legs, gives it its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and resilience.
