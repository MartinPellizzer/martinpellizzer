Cyperus laevigatus subsp. laevigatus

Cyperus laevigatus subsp.

laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its root system is fibrous, and it often forms dense colonies, which can influence local ecosystems.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, making it a common plant in regions with high humidity and rainfall.
