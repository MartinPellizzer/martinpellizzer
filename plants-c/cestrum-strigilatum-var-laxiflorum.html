Cestrum strigilatum var. laxiflorum

Cestrum strigilatum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorum cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has a trailing or prostrate growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often used in ornamental gardening for its aromatic blooms.
