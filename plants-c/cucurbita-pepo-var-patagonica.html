Cucurbita pepo var. patagonica

Cucurbita pepo var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian squash, is a variant of the common gourd native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruits that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant is an annual vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, nutty flavor.
