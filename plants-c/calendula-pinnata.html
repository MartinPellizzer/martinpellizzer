Calendula pinnata

Calendula pinnata, commonly known as the horse lungwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Calendula pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
