Cinnamomum aggregatum

Cinnamomum aggregatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum loureiroi, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
