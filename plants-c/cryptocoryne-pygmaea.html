Cryptocoryne pygmaea

Cryptocoryne pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that often exhibit a bluish tint. This plant thrives in low-light conditions and is popular among aquarium enthusiasts for its ease of care and aesthetic appeal. It is a hardy species that can tolerate a range of water parameters, making it suitable for various aquatic environments.

Cryptocoryne pygmaea also plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
