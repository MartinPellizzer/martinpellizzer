Cleisostoma pachyphyllum

Cleisostoma pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leafed wax plant, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its humid, low-light environment. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinct lip that attracts pollinators.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.
