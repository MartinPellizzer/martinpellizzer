Conospermum affine

Conospermum affine, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight blue or greyish hue.
