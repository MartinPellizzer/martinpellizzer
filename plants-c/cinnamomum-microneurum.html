Cinnamomum microneurum

Cinnamomum microneurum, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or small-veined cinnamon, is a species of tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties. Its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown bark make it distinct within the genus.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
