Cardamine axillaris var. tucumanensis

Cardamine axillaris var. tucumanensis, commonly known as the Tucuman slender watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are small, alternate, and often have a distinctive kidney-shaped form.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes, contributing to its ornamental value in certain horticultural settings.
