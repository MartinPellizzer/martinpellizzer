Citrus × vulgaris var. myrtifolia

Citrus × vulgaris var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves that resemble those of myrtle.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically smaller than standard lemons but have a similar tart flavor. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and traditional medicine applications.
