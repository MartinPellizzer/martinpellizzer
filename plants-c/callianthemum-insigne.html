Callianthemum insigne

Callianthemum insigne, commonly known as the noble rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Callianthemum insigne is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
