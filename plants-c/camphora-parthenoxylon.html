Camphora parthenoxylon

Camphora parthenoxylon, commonly known as the Indian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood that contains camphor, a volatile compound used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The tree has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing smooth, black seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in various cultural and commercial applications for centuries.
