Cerasus cerasus

Cerasus cerasus, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and serrated along the edges. The plant flowers with pink to white blossoms in early spring, followed by the development of sweet, fleshy fruits. The fruits are usually red or dark red when ripe, containing a single hard pit.

It is cultivated for its edible fruit and is often grown in temperate climates.
