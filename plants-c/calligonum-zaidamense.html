Calligonum zaidamense

Calligonum zaidamense, commonly known as the Zaidam sandmat, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has adapted to harsh environmental conditions with deep roots and a succulent structure to store water. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration in desert environments.
