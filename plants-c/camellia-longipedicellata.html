Camellia longipedicellata

Camellia longipedicellata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features large, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are borne on pedicels that are elongated and often adorned with small, leaf-like bracts.

Camellia longipedicellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
