Calytrix muricata

Calytrix muricata, commonly known as the rough-leaved calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name.

Calytrix muricata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
