Canavalia regalis

Canavalia regalis, commonly known as the royal bean or crown bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several edible, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible seeds.

Canavalia regalis is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.
