Carex exigua

Carex exigua, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
