Capparis chinensis

Capparis chinensis, commonly known as Chinese caper, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant's leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
