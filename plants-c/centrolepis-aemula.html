Centrolepis aemula

Centrolepis aemula, commonly known as the slender centrolepis, is a unique plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to southern Africa, this species is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a resilient plant in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
