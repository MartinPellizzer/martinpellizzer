Cordylanthus mollis var. viridis

Cordylanthus mollis var. viridis, commonly known as greenleaf flaxvetch, is a perennial legume native to California.

It features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
