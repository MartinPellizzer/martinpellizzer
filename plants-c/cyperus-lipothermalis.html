Cyperus lipothermalis

Cyperus lipothermalis, commonly known as the heat-loving sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus lipothermalis is adapted to high temperatures and is often found in regions with warm climates.
