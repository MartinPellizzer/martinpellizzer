Carex doisutepensis

Carex doisutepensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle.

Carex doisutepensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
