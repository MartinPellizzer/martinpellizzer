Crotalaria drummondii

Crotalaria drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and soil enrichment properties.
