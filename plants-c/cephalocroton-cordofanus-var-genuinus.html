Cephalocroton cordofanus var. genuinus

Cephalocroton cordofanus var. genuinus, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
