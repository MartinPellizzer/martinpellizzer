Cytisus grandiflorus

Cytisus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a hedge.
