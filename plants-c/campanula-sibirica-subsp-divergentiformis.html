Campanula sibirica subsp. divergentiformis

Campanula sibirica subsp.

divergentiformis, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold climates and poor soils.
