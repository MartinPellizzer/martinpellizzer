Caralluma aaronis

Caralluma aaronis, commonly known as the Aaron's nightshade, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. It produces small, toxic berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, Caralluma aaronis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
