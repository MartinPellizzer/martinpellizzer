Cryptocarya elliptifolia

Cryptocarya elliptifolia, commonly known as the oval-leafed cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.
