Clausena austroindica

Clausena austroindica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn bright orange when mature, containing a single seed. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves and fruits are also used in culinary applications, particularly in Indian cuisine.
