Crataegus errata

Crataegus errata, commonly known as the red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
