Calceolaria cantensis

Calceolaria cantensis, commonly known as the Peruvian slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Calceolaria cantensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
