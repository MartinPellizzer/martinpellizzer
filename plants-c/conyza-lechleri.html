Conyza lechleri

Conyza lechleri, commonly known as "velvetleaf," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Conyza lechleri is considered an invasive species in many areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
