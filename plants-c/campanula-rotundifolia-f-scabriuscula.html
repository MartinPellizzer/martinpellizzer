Campanula rotundifolia f. scabriuscula

Campanula rotundifolia f. scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features oval, rough-textured leaves that are typically hairy and slightly scalloped at the edges. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
