Ceropegia ciliatioris

Ceropegia ciliatioris, commonly known as the hairy ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia ciliatioris is a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a trailing plant in gardens due to its ornamental appeal.
