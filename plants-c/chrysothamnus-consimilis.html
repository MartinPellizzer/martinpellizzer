Chrysothamnus consimilis

Chrysothamnus consimilis, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It has small, needle-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Chrysothamnus consimilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
