Centaurea × aliena

Centaurea × aliena, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with bright blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Centaurea × aliena is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
