Camelina

Camelina, commonly known as false flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. Camelina has small, yellow flowers and produces slender, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant is known for its high oil content, making it a valuable source of edible oil.

It is also used in agricultural systems for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
