Chenopodium saxatile

Chenopodium saxatile, commonly known as mountain goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Chenopodium saxatile is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
