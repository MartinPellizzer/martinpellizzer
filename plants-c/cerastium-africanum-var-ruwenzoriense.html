Cerastium africanum var. ruwenzoriense

Cerastium africanum var. ruwenzoriense, commonly known as the Ruwenzori mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It is notable for its compact growth habit and is often found in the Ruwenzori Mountains.
