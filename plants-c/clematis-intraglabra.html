Clematis intraglabra

Clematis intraglabra, commonly known as the smoothstem clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are usually smooth and can be twining or trailing, allowing it to climb various supports.

Clematis intraglabra prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
