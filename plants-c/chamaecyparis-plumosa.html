Chamaecyparis plumosa

Chamaecyparis plumosa, commonly known as the lacey cedar, is a species of cypress native to Japan.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in flat, spreading sprays, creating a unique visual texture. It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Chamaecyparis plumosa prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
