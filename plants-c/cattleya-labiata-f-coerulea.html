Cattleya labiata f. coerulea

Cattleya labiata f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue-lipped cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking floral display.

This cultivar is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive blue-violet lip, which sets it apart from other cattleya varieties. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Its vibrant colors and elegant form make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and collectors.
