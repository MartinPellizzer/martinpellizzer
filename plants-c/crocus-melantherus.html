Crocus melantherus

Crocus melantherus, commonly known as the dark violet crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in harsh environments, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sunny locations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
