Canthium gaertneri

Canthium gaertneri, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Canthium gaertneri is also known for its latex-like sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
