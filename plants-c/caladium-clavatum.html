Caladium clavatum

Caladium clavatum, commonly known as the red-veined caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant patterns of red, white, and green. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-draining soil. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for garden displays.

Caladium clavatum is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
