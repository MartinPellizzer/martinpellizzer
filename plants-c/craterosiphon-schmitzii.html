Craterosiphon schmitzii

Craterosiphon schmitzii, commonly known as the Schmitz's rockweed, is a marine green alga found in coastal regions.

It thrives in intertidal zones, where it attaches itself to rocks using a holdfast. The plant has a filamentous thallus with a branching structure, and its cells are rich in chlorophyll, allowing it to perform photosynthesis efficiently. It plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Craterosiphon schmitzii is also used in scientific research due to its unique biochemical properties.
