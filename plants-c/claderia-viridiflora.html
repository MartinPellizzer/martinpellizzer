Claderia viridiflora

Claderia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered claderia, is a unique species belonging to the family Claderiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, foliage-like structure that resembles a cluster of leaves, often mistaken for a moss or lichen. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and ecological role in nutrient cycling within its habitat.
