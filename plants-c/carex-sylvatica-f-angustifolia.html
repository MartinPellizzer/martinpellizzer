Carex sylvatica f. angustifolia

Carex sylvatica f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and stiff, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
