Conoclinium oligolepis

Conoclinium oligolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
