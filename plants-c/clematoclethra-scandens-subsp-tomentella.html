Clematoclethra scandens subsp. tomentella

Clematoclethra scandens subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the woolly honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features slender, arching stems that often climb or trail along the ground.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy underside, giving it a distinctive, velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
