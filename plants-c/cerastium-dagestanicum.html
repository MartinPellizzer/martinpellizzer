Cerastium dagestanicum

Cerastium dagestanicum, commonly known as the Dagestani mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
