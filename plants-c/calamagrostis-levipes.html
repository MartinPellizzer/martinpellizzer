Calamagrostis levipes

Calamagrostis levipes, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, slender culms that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence is a panicle composed of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or pink.

Calamagrostis levipes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
