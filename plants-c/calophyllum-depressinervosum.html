Calophyllum depressinervosum

Calophyllum depressinervosum, commonly known as the low-veined calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, orange-red berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacology.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its attractive foliage and growth habit.
