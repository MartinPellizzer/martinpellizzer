Commelina pubescens

Commelina pubescens, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, pubescent surface, and it produces small, three-lobed bracts that surround the flowers.

Commelina pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
