Convolvulus valentinus var. transfretanus

Convolvulus valentinus var. transfretanus, commonly known as the Valentine's convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and delicate appearance.
