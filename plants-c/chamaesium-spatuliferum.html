Chamaesium spatuliferum

Chamaesium spatuliferum, commonly known as the broad-leafed chamaesium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to spatulate in shape, often with a velvety texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

Chamaesium spatuliferum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments.
