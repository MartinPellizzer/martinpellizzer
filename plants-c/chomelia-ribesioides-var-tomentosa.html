Chomelia ribesioides var. tomentosa

Chomelia ribesioides var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly chomelia, is a tropical shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in shaded gardens.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
