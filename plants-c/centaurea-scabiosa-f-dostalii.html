Centaurea scabiosa f. dostalii

Centaurea scabiosa f. dostalii, commonly known as the Dostal's spiked thistle, is a botanical variant of the common centaurea.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of Central Asia. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
