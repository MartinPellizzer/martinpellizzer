Carex vicaria var. costata

Carex vicaria var. costata, commonly known as the ribbed sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant features prominent longitudinal ridges on its leaves, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes and pond margins.

Carex vicaria var. costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
