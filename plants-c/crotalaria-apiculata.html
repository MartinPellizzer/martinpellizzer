Crotalaria apiculata

Crotalaria apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source.

Crotalaria apiculata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
