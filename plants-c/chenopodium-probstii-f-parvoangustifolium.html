Chenopodium probstii f. parvoangustifolium

Chenopodium probstii f. parvoangustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
