Cucubalus italicus

Cucubalus italicus, commonly known as the Italian cucumber, is a climbing vine native to southern Europe.

It produces elongated, edible fruits that are typically harvested when immature and tender. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth habit, it is often used in ornamental gardening and as a decorative vine.
