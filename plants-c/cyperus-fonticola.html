Cyperus fonticola

Cyperus fonticola, commonly known as the water umbrella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and pond edges.

Cyperus fonticola plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
