Capsicum longum var. latum

Capsicum longum var. latum, commonly known as the bird pepper or long pepper, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It produces elongated, slender fruits that range in color from green to red when mature. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The fruits are typically harvested when they are fully mature, and they are used both as a spice and for their medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
