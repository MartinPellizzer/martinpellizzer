Cotula sphaeranthus

Cotula sphaeranthus, commonly known as the globe cotula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cotula sphaeranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and colorful display.
