Cistus crispus f. warburgii

Cistus crispus f. warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, woolly leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is well adapted to rocky, dry environments and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
