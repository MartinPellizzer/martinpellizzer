Cereus elegantissimus

Cereus elegantissimus, commonly known as the elegant night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for drought conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
