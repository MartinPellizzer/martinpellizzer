Convolvulus nil

Convolvulus nil, commonly known as the Egyptian bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is a climbing or trailing plant with hairy stems and is often found growing along fences or in rocky areas.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to withstand drought conditions.
