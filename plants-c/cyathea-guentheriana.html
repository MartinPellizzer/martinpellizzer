Cyathea guentheriana

Cyathea guentheriana, commonly known as the Guenther's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system allows it to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, contributing to its growth in dense tropical forests.

Cyathea guentheriana plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
