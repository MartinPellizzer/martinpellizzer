Capparis crotonoides

Capparis crotonoides, commonly known as the African cheeseplant, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. The plant is characterized by its pungent, often unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in its flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Capparis crotonoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
