Coreopsis lineariloba

Coreopsis lineariloba, commonly known as the slender coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is a perennial herb that is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
