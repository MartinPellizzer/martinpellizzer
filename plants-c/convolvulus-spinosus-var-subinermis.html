Convolvulus spinosus var. subinermis

Convolvulus spinosus var. subinermis, commonly known as the softleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are heart-shaped and slightly hairy, while the flowers range in color from pale yellow to pink.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced spines compared to the typical Convolvulus spinosus.
