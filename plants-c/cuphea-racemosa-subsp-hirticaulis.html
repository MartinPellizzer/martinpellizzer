Cuphea racemosa subsp. hirticaulis

Cuphea racemosa subsp.

hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, showy clusters of tubular, red or pink flowers.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
