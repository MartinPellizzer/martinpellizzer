Crotalaria otoptera

Crotalaria otoptera, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with pinnately compound leaves. Its seed pods are cylindrical and dehiscent, releasing seeds when mature.

Crotalaria otoptera is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.
