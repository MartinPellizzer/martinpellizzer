Cistus myrthifolius

Cistus myrthifolius, commonly known as the Mediterranean rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.

The plant also has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
