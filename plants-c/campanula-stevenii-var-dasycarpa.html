Campanula stevenii var. dasycarpa

Campanula stevenii var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
