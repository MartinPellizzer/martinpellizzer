Convolvulus striatus

Convolvulus striatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in spring and summer. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and sandy environments.
