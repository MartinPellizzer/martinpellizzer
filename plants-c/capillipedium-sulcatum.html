Capillipedium sulcatum

It is characterized by its leafy, thallus-like structure with a distinctive wrinkled or plicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its morphology includes a central axis with closely arranged, dorsiventral leaves.
