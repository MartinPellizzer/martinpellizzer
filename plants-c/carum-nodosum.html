Carum nodosum

Carum nodosum, commonly known as nodding parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used as a culinary herb, similar to parsley. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its aromatic foliage contains volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and flavor.

Carum nodosum is typically grown in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
