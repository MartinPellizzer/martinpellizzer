Celastrus esquirolianus

Celastrus esquirolianus, commonly known as the Esquiroli's staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
