Cactus obtusipetalus

Cactus obtusipetalus, commonly known as the broad-petalled cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Overall, Cactus obtusipetalus exemplifies adaptations for desert survival through its structural and physiological features.
