Cyathea squarrosa

Cyathea squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to its role in forest ecosystems.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.
