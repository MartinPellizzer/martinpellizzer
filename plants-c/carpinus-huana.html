Carpinus huana

Carpinus huana, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
