Cinnamomum kinabaluense

Cinnamomum kinabaluense, commonly known as the Kinabalu cinnamon, is a species of tree native to the high-altitude regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces dark brown, ellipsoid fruits that contain a single seed. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

This species is often found in cloud forests and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat.
