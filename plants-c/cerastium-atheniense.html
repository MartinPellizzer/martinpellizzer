Cerastium atheniense

Cerastium atheniense, commonly known as the Athens mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
