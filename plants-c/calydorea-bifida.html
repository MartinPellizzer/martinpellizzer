Calydorea bifida

Calydorea bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf calydorea, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifid (split) leaves, which are often deeply divided into two lobes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Calydorea bifida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
