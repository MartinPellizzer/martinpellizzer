Chenopodium album subf. ovalifolium

Chenopodium album subf.

ovalifolium, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in compact clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine.
