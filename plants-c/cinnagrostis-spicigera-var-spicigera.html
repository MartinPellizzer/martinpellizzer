Cinnagrostis spicigera var. spicigera

Cinnagrostis spicigera var. spicigera, commonly known as the slender false foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
