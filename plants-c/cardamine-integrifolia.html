Cardamine integrifolia

Cardamine integrifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to other members of the cabbage family.

Cardamine integrifolia plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
