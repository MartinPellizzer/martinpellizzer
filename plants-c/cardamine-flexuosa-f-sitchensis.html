Cardamine flexuosa f. sitchensis

Cardamine flexuosa f. sitchensis, commonly known as the Sitka buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the cool, humid climates of its native habitat.
