Cleomella oxystyloides

Cleomella oxystyloides, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, often pink or white, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cleomella oxystyloides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
