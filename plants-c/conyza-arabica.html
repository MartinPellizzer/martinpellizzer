Conyza arabica

Conyza arabica, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. It has a branched stem and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
