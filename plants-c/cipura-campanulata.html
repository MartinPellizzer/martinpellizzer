Cipura campanulata

Cipura campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Cipura campanulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
