Clematis brachyura f. hexasepala

Clematis brachyura f. hexasepala, commonly known as the six-lobed clematis, is a variant of the species Clematis brachyura.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its woody stems and climbing habit. The flowers are typically blue to purple, with six sepals that are often mistaken for petals. This form exhibits a unique floral structure compared to the typical form of the species, with the sepals being more prominent and lobed.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
