Chilocarpus ceylanicus

Chilocarpus ceylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
