Cucumis melo convar. adzhur

Cucumis melo convar. adzhur, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a botanical variant of the melon species characterized by its distinct fruit morphology.

The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, juicy interior. It is typically cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in water and nutrients. The leaves are broad and lobed, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

This variety is well-suited to warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
