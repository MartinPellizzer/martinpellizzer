Cyrtochilum trigibbum

Cyrtochilum trigibbum, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces inflorescences with three to five flowers.

Cyrtochilum trigibbum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
