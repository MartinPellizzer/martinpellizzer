Cyperus cyperinus var. laxatus

Cyperus cyperinus var. laxatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and an important component of wetland ecosystems.
