Cissampelos bojeriana

Cissampelos bojeriana, commonly known as the Bojer's woodbine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional medicine for various ailments.
