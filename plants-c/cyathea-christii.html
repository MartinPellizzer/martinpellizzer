Cyathea christii

Cyathea christii, commonly known as the Christ's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea christii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
