Chrysoma domingensis var. obtusifolia

Chrysoma domingensis var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
