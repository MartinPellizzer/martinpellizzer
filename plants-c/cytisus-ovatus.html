Cytisus ovatus

Cytisus ovatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and leathery, typically appearing in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
