Croton brevispicatus var. isalensis

Croton brevispicatus var. isalensis, commonly known as the Isalensis Croton, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and may exhibit a wide range of colors, including shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
