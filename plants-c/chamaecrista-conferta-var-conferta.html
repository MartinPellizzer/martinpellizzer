Chamaecrista conferta var. conferta

Chamaecrista conferta var. conferta, commonly known as the hairy palmera or hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The seeds are encased in a hairy pod and are often dispersed by wind or animals.
