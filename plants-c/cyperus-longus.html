Cyperus longus

Cyperus longus, commonly known as the common flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the base. Cyperus longus is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it a common sight in various aquatic ecosystems.
