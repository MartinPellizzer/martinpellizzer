Chrysoglossum robinsonii

Chrysoglossum robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's chrysoglossum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
