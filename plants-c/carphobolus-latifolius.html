Carphobolus latifolius

Carphobolus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf carphobolus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through vegetative propagation.

While not widely cultivated, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its ecological adaptations.
