Citrus × depressa

Citrus × depressa, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, fragrant fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces white, fragrant flowers. The fruit's outer skin is thick and edible, while the inner segments are juicy and tart.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citrus fruits.
