Celastrus tristis

Celastrus tristis, commonly known as the bitter apple or yellow elder, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit contains several seeds enclosed in a fleshy, edible pericarp.

Celastrus tristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
