Cercis chingii

Cercis chingii, commonly known as the Chinese kerria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces showy, red-purple flowers in the spring, which are borne on the previous year's wood. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Cercis chingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
