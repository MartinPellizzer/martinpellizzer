Chamelaucium marchantii

Chamelaucium marchantii, commonly known as the pink ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to mauve flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a low, spreading growth habit with narrow, linear leaves.

Chamelaucium marchantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its native conditions.
