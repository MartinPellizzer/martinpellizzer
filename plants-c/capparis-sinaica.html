Capparis sinaica

Capparis sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Capparis sinaica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
