Crataegus resima

Crataegus resima, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
