Capparis erythrocarpos

Capparis erythrocarpos, commonly known as the red-fruited caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and aromatic, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional practices.
