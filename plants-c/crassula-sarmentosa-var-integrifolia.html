Crassula sarmentosa var. integrifolia

Crassula sarmentosa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot or the Stringy Fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that grow horizontally along the ground, often forming dense mats. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, typically green with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
