Centaurea nudicaulis

Centaurea nudicaulis, commonly known as the mouse-eared chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species with narrow, hairy leaves and small, bright blue or purple flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
