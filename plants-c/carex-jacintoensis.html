Carex jacintoensis

Carex jacintoensis, commonly known as the Jacinto's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that are often dark green and narrow.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
