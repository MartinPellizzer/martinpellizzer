Clematis buchananii

Clematis buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Clematis buchananii is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
