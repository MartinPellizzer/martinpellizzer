Carex tereticaulis

Carex tereticaulis, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.
