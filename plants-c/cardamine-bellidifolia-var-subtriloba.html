Cardamine bellidifolia var. subtriloba

Cardamine bellidifolia var. subtriloba, commonly known as the three-lobed water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streams, ponds, and wet meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
