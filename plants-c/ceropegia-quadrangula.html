Ceropegia quadrangula

Ceropegia quadrangula, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which are pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
