Cyperus hamiltonii

Cyperus hamiltonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist soils and full sun to partial shade. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Cyperus hamiltonii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
