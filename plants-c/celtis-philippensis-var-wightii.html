Celtis philippensis var. wightii

Celtis philippensis var. wightii, commonly known as the Philippine boxwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact foliage and smooth, dark bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
