Centaurea × antoniae

Centaurea × antoniae, commonly known as Antonia's cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
