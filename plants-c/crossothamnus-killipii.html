Crossothamnus killipii

Crossothamnus killipii, commonly known as the Killip's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Crossothamnus killipii is often found in coastal scrub and dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
