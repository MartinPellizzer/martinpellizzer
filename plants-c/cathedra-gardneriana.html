Cathedra gardneriana

Cathedra gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered with a thick, fibrous rind. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance.

This ancient plant is characterized by its slow growth and ability to survive in low-nutrient soils.
