Chionanthus densiflorus

Chionanthus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds.

The denseflower viburnum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
