Cylindrolobus carneus

Cylindrolobus carneus, commonly known as the red-stemmed devil's claw, is a unique plant species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical, red-tinged stems that are covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, which help protect it from intense sunlight.

Cylindrolobus carneus is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments, making it a resilient and fascinating example of botanical adaptation.
