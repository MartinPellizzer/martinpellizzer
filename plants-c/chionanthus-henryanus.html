Chionanthus henryanus

Chionanthus henryanus, commonly known as the Chinese Fringeflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
