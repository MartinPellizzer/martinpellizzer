Centaurea jacea subsp. debeauxii

Centaurea jacea subsp.

debeauxii, commonly known as the Beaux's Bladder Campion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers in compact, spherical clusters called cymes. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in the Asteraceae family.
