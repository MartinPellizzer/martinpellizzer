Comparettia pacensium

Comparettia pacensium, commonly known as the Pacentia orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically produces pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
