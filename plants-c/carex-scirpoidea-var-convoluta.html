Carex scirpoidea var. convoluta

Carex scirpoidea var. convoluta, commonly known as the curled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants with jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cylindrical spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important species in riparian and wetland ecosystems.

The curled sedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
