Caladenia carnea

Caladenia carnea, commonly known as the pink-streaked spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a single, slender stem that bears a single, strikingly pink and white flower with intricate, spider-like markings. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly wasps, due to their specialized floral structures.

Caladenia carnea is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
