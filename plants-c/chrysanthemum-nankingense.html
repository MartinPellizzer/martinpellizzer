Chrysanthemum nankingense

Chrysanthemum nankingense, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
