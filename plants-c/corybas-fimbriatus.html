Corybas fimbriatus

Corybas fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flower that is often brightly colored, typically in shades of red or orange. The plant produces a single, showy bloom that sits atop a slender stem, and its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along streambanks.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
