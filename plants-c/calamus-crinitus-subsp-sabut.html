Calamus crinitus subsp. sabut

Calamus crinitus subsp.

sabut, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae. It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-white spadices and yellow staminates. It thrives in wet, tropical environments, typically found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
