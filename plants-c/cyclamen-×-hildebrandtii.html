Cyclamen × hildebrandtii

Cyclamen × hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's cyclamen, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species, Cyclamen persicum and Cyclamen coum.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, heart-shaped leaves with intricate patterns and vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.

Cyclamen × hildebrandtii requires well-draining soil and moderate watering to flourish.
