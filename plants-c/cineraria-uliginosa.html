Cineraria uliginosa

Cineraria uliginosa, commonly known as bog cineraria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in damp, acidic soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture.

Cineraria uliginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in garden settings with proper moisture conditions.
