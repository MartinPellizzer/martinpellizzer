Cydonia maliformis

Cydonia maliformis, commonly known as the Persian apple or white damask, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It produces large, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The fruit, which resembles a large, fuzzy pear, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and edible plant in temperate climates.
