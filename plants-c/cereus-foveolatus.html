Cereus foveolatus

Cereus foveolatus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces a unique, swollen fruit that contains seeds.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is a hardy plant that requires minimal water and care.
