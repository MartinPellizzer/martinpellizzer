Carlina stricta

Carlina stricta, commonly known as the rigid coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, stiff stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy blue to purple flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina stricta is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
