Capparis corymbosa var. sansibarensis

Capparis corymbosa var. sansibarensis, commonly known as the East African sour fig, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in cymes, followed by edible, greenish-yellow fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional cuisine and have a tart, acidic flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.
