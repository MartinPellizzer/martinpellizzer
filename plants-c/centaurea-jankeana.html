Centaurea jankeana

Centaurea jankeana, commonly known as the Janke's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea jankeana is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
