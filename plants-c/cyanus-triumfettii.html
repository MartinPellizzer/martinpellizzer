Cyanus triumfettii

Cyanus triumfettii, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its vibrant blue or violet petals are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets, creating a striking visual display.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
