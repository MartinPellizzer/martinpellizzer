Cephaelis altsonii

Cephaelis altsonii, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

Cephaelis altsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
