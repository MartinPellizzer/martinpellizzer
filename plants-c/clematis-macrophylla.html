Clematis macrophylla

Clematis macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese forest clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically 5 to 10 inches in length. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis macrophylla is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
