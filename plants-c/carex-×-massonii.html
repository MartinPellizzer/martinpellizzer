Carex × massonii

Carex × massonii, commonly known as the Chinese sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spikelets that bear small, three-lobed flowers. Native to East Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
