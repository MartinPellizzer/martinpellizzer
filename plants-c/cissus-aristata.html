Cissus aristata

Cissus aristata, commonly known as the arrowhead fig or arrowleaf cissus, is a trailing, woody vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often adorned with small, white, waxy spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Cissus aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
