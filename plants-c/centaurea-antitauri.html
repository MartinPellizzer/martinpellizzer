Centaurea antitauri

Centaurea antitauri, commonly known as the Tauric centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, upright growth habit. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with ray florets that are often longer than the disk florets.

Centaurea antitauri is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
