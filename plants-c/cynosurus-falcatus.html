Cynosurus falcatus

Cynosurus falcatus, commonly known as the bent grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curved, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is usually erect and slightly drooping.

Cynosurus falcatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
