Carex sororia f. subdivulsa

Carex sororia f. subdivulsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It is often found in shaded areas and can thrive in both temperate and subtropical climates.

Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
