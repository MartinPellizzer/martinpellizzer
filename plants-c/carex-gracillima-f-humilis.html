Carex gracillima f. humilis

Carex gracillima f. humilis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This subspecies is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. It thrives in full to partial sunlight and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
