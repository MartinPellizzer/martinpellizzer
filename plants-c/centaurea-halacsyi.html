Centaurea halacsyi

Centaurea halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its spiky, purple to red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.
