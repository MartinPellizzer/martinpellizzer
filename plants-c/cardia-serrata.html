Cardia serrata

Cardia serrata, commonly known as the serrated cardia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cardia serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
