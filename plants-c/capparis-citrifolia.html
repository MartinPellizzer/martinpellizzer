Capparis citrifolia

Capparis citrifolia, commonly known as the sour cabbage or vinegar tree, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and pungent, sour-smelling leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are followed by small, green, fleshy fruits. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, particularly in the Canary Islands, where they are valued for their unique flavor and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to arid and saline environments.
