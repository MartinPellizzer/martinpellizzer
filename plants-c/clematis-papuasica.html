Clematis papuasica

Clematis papuasica, commonly known as the Papuan Clematis, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Papua and nearby islands.

It features woody stems with thorn-like projections and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Clematis papuasica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
