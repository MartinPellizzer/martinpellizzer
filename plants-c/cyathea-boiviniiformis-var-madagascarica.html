Cyathea boiviniiformis var. madagascarica

Cyathea boiviniiformis var. madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizome system helps it anchor firmly in the soil while absorbing nutrients.

The fronds are often used in traditional practices and are also valued for their ornamental appearance in horticulture.
