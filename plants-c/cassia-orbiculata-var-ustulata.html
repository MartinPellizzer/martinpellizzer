Cassia orbiculata var. ustulata

Cassia orbiculata var. ustulata, commonly known as the flame tree or torch tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red inflorescences that resemble torches, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
