Cyperus paraguayensis

Cyperus paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous, and it is adapted to thrive in water-saturated soils.

Cyperus paraguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
