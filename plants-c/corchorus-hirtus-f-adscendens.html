Corchorus hirtus f. adscendens

Corchorus hirtus f. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending prickly mallows, is a variant of the prickly mallow species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India. The plant features hairy, branched stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a rough texture.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
