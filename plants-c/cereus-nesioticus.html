Cereus nesioticus

Cereus nesioticus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the cactus family, Cactaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only a few hours. Its stems are typically cylindrical and covered with areoles, which bear spines and flowers.

Cereus nesioticus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
