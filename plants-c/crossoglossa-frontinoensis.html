Crossoglossa frontinoensis

Crossoglossa frontinoensis, commonly known as the Frontino orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces intricate floral structures with a unique lip shape. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
