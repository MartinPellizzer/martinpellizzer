Centaurea nigra subsp. aterrima

Centaurea nigra subsp.

aterrima, commonly known as black-centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its gray-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spiny bracts surrounding the flower head.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
