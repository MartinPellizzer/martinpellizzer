Calyptranthes cristalensis

Calyptranthes cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystal Mountain calyptranthes, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests, where it thrives in cooler, humid conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

Calyptranthes cristalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
