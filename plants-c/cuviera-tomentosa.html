Cuviera tomentosa

Cuviera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cuviera, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
