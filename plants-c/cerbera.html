Cerbera

Cerbera, commonly known as the poison apple or cerbera odora, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and includes species such as Cerbera odora, which is notable for its striking orange flowers and red berries. The plant contains toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides, making it potentially dangerous if ingested. Cerbera species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant flowers and unique foliage.

However, care must be taken to avoid confusion with edible plants, as some parts of the plant can be highly poisonous.
