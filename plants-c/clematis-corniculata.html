Clematis corniculata

Clematis corniculata, commonly known as the horned clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has thin, twining stems that climb using small tendrils, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis corniculata is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and vigorous growth habit.
