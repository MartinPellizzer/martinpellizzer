Camellia gauchowensis

Camellia gauchowensis, commonly known as the Gauchow camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, and the flowers bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia gauchowensis is also noted for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in acidic soils.
