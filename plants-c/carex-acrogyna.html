Carex acrogyna

Carex acrogyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex acrogyna plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
