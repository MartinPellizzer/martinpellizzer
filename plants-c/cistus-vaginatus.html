Cistus vaginatus

Cistus vaginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in spring. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cistus vaginatus is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
