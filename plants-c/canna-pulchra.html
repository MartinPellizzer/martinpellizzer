Canna pulchra

Canna pulchra, commonly known as the pink canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, stout stalks and large, vibrant leaves that can reach up to 2 feet in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of pink, red, or orange. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Canna pulchra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
