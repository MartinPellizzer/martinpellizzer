Celastrus articulatus var. orbiculatus

Celastrus articulatus var. orbiculatus, commonly known as the round-leaf staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, orbicular to ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in axillary clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. Its stems are woody and often branched, with a tendency to climb or trail along the ground.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
