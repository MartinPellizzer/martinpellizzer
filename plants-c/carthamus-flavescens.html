Carthamus flavescens

Carthamus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow safflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed heads that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
