Conchocarpus mastigophorus

Conchocarpus mastigophorus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that resemble showers, hence its common name. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this species make it a distinctive addition to tropical gardens and landscapes.
