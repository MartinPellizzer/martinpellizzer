Chloroleucon caraboboense

Chloroleucon caraboboense, commonly known as the Carabobo goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid regions.
