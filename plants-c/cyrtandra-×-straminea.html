Cyrtandra × straminea

Cyrtandra × straminea, commonly known as the yellow heliconia, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a bird's nest. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spiral arrangement that attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats.

Cyrtandra × straminea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
