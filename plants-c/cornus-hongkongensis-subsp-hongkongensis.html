Cornus hongkongensis subsp. hongkongensis

Cornus hongkongensis subsp.

hongkongensis, commonly known as Hong Kong cornel. It is a flowering plant in the family Cornaceae, native to eastern Asia, particularly Hong Kong and southern China.

The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.
