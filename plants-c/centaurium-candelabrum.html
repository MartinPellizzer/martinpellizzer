Centaurium candelabrum

Centaurium candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Centaurium candelabrum thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
