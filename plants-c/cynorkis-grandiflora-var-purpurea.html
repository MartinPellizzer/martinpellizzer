Cynorkis grandiflora var. purpurea

Cynorkis grandiflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a striking species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are known for their intricate structure and long-lasting color.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
