Carlemannia tetragona

Carlemannia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its square or four-angled stems and small, star-shaped white or purple flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.
