Chrysobalanus ellipticus

Chrysobalanus ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic chrysobalanus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small apples. The fruit has a hard, woody exterior and contains a single seed.

It is often found in coastal and lowland rainforests, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
