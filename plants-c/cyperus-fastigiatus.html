Cyperus fastigiatus

Cyperus fastigiatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along waterways.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system, which aids in its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
