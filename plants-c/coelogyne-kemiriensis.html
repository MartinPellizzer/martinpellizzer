Coelogyne kemiriensis

Coelogyne kemiriensis, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coelogyne kemiriensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
