Cicer incisum

Cicer incisum, commonly known as the cutleaf vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
