Calendula incana subsp. algarbiensis

Calendula incana subsp.

algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbi calendula, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
