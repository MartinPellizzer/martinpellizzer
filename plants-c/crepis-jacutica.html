Crepis jacutica

Crepis jacutica, commonly known as the jacutian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Crepis jacutica is adapted to arid environments and is typically found in steppe and semi-desert regions.
