Crataegus curvisepala

Crataegus curvisepala, commonly known as the curved-sepaled hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and wildlife but generally not for humans.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
