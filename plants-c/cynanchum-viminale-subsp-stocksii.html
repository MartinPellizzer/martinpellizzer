Cynanchum viminale subsp. stocksii

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

stocksii, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy or rocky soils.
