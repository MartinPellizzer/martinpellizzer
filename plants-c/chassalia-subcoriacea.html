Chassalia subcoriacea

Chassalia subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough chassalia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "subcoriacea," meaning "subcoriaceous" or slightly leathery. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Chassalia subcoriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
