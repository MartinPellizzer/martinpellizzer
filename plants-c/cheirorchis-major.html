Cheirorchis major

Cheirorchis major, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vivid colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its unique morphology includes a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in challenging conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
