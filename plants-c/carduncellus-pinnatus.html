Carduncellus pinnatus

Carduncellus pinnatus, commonly known as the spiny cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its robust growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
