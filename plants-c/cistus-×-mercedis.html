Cistus × mercedis

Cistus × mercedis, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Cistus × mercedis is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
