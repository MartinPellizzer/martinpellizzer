Chionanthus ramiflorus var. palembanicus

Chionanthus ramiflorus var. palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembang chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
