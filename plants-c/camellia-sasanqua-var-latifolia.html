Camellia sasanqua var. latifolia

Camellia sasanqua var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf camellia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that provide a lush green backdrop for its showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a specimen plant.
