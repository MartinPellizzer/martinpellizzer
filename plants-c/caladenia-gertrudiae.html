Caladenia gertrudiae

Caladenia gertrudiae, commonly known as the Gertrude's spider-orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, spider-like appearance with a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats, often in areas with low rainfall. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a spider.

Caladenia gertrudiae is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
