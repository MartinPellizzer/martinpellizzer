Campanula suanetica var. appendiculata

Campanula suanetica var. appendiculata, commonly known as the Suanetica bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.
