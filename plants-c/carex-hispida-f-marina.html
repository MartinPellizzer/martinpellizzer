Carex hispida f. marina

Carex hispida f. marina, commonly known as the saltmarsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline or brackish wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, coastal environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in early spring, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing shoreline soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to tolerate high salinity and frequent tidal inundation.
