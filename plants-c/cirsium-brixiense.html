Cirsium brixiense

Cirsium brixiense, commonly known as the Brixiense thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium brixiense is often found in mountainous regions and is considered a rare species in some areas.
