Calamagrostis beyrichiana

Calamagrostis beyrichiana, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and silky, silver-blue foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces panicles of small, pale green to golden flowers in late summer, which are attractive to pollinators.

Calamagrostis beyrichiana is often used in water features and wetland restoration due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
