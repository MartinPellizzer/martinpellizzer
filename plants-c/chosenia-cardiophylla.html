Chosenia cardiophylla

Chosenia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, thriving in swampy or marshy environments. Its leaves are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name, and it is known for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Chosenia cardiophylla plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
