Carlina corymbosa subsp. globosa

Carlina corymbosa subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking spherical blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
