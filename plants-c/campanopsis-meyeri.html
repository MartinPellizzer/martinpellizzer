Campanopsis meyeri

Campanopsis meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Campanopsis meyeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
