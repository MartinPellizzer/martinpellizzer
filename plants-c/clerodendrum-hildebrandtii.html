Clerodendrum hildebrandtii

Clerodendrum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
