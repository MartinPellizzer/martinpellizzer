Centaurea tauscheri

Centaurea tauscheri, commonly known as Tauscher's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea tauscheri is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
