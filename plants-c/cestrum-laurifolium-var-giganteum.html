Cestrum laurifolium var. giganteum

Cestrum laurifolium var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant laurel bell, is a striking flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are oval and pointed, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 10 feet tall, making it a dramatic addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in warm climates for its beauty and fragrance.
