Crotalaria pulchella

Crotalaria pulchella, commonly known as the pretty crotalaria, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a prominent black spot. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicine practices.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain environments.
