Campanopsis androsacea var. rigida

Campanopsis androsacea var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
