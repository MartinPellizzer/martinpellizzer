Calea robinsoniana

Calea robinsoniana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, velvety petals that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique nocturnal blooming habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Calea robinsoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in gardens for its ethereal appearance.
