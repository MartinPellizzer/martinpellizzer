Calopogon pulchellus var. latifolius

Calopogon pulchellus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and marshes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it has a short, rhizomatous stem.

This variety is distinguished by its wider leaves compared to the typical form of Calopogon pulchellus.
