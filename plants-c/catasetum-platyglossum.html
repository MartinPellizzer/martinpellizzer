Catasetum platyglossum

Catasetum platyglossum, commonly known as the flat-tongue orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a tongue. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic.

Catasetum platyglossum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
