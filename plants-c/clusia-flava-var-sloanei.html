Clusia flava var. sloanei

Clusia flava var. sloanei, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically bright green, and it produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant is evergreen and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salty air, making it suitable for seaside gardens.

Clusia flava var. sloanei is also known for its ability to produce copious amounts of nectar, attracting a variety of pollinators.
