Convolvulus philomega

Convolvulus philomega, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial.
