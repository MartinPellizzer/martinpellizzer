Ceratostema urbanianum

Ceratostema urbanianum, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ceratostema urbanianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
