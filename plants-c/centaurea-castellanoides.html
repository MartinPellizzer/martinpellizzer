Centaurea castellanoides

Centaurea castellanoides, commonly known as the Castellanos' cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

Centaurea castellanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
