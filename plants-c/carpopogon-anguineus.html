Carpopogon anguineus

Carpopogon anguineus, commonly known as the blackseed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its coarse, dark green leaves and produces small, dark purple to black seeds that are highly nutritious. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for both humans and animals.

The plant's resilience and nutritional value contribute to its ecological and agricultural significance.
