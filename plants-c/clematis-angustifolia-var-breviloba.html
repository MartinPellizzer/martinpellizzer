Clematis angustifolia var. breviloba

Clematis angustifolia var. breviloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, supporting its climbing habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
