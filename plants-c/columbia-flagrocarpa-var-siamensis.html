Columbia flagrocarpa var. siamensis

Columbia flagrocarpa var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam golden fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling small figs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
