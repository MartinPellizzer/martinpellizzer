Carlina rhodopea

Carlina rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope aster, is a flowering plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky, sunny habitats and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous environment. Its violet-blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina rhodopea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
