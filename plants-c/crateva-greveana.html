Crateva greveana

Crateva greveana, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
