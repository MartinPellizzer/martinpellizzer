Carpinus turczaninovii f. coreana

Carpinus turczaninovii f. coreana, commonly known as the Korean hornbeam, is a variant of the hornbeam genus native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
