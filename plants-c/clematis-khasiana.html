Clematis khasiana

Clematis khasiana, commonly known as the Himalayan clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Clematis khasiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
