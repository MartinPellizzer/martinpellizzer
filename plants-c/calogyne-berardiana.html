Calogyne berardiana

Calogyne berardiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a distinctive lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Calogyne berardiana is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and relatively easy care requirements.
