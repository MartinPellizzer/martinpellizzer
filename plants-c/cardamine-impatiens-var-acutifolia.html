Cardamine impatiens var. acutifolia

Cardamine impatiens var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the small white flowers appear in clusters.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for invertebrates.
