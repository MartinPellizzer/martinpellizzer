Calea caracasana var. pilosior

Calea caracasana var. pilosior, commonly known as the velvetleaf calea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
