Canarium sapidum

Canarium sapidum, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a hard, oily seed used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, has been historically used for various purposes, including as a chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in tropical forests.
