Cyperus aristatus var. versicolor

Cyperus aristatus var. versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays shades of green, yellow, and white, creating a vibrant visual effect. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in water gardens or as a decorative plant in shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and indoor cultivation.
