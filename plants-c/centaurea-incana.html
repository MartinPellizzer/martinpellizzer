Centaurea incana

Centaurea incana, commonly known as hoary centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spiny, branched stems. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Centaurea incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
