Cornus malifolia

Cornus malifolia, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that become prominent in winter. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant yellow or red in the fall.

Cornus malifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
