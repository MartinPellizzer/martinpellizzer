Commelina polhillii subsp. polhillii

Commelina polhillii subsp.

polhillii, commonly known as the Polhill's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Africa. It belongs to the Commelina genus in the family Commelinaceae, characterized by its distinct leaf and flower structures.

The plant typically features arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture.
