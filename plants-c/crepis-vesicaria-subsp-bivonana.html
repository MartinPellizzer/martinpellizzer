Crepis vesicaria subsp. bivonana

Crepis vesicaria subsp.

bivonana, commonly known as the two-lobed mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates.

Its subspecies name, *bivonana*, refers to the distinctive two-lobed shape of its leaves.
