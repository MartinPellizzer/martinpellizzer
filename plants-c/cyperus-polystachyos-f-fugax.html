Cyperus polystachyos f. fugax

Cyperus polystachyos f. fugax, commonly known as the fugax flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its small, three-ranked flowers are usually green or reddish, and it produces three-sided, triangular seeds.

This subspecies is often found in the southeastern United States and is adapted to moist, sandy soils.
