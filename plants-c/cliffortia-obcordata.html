Cliffortia obcordata

Cliffortia obcordata, commonly known as the heart's-ease or the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found on coastal cliffs and mountain slopes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, with some varieties displaying a striking red hue. Its leaves are typically succulent, fleshy, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Cliffortia obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic beauty.
