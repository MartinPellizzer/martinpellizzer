Carex allivescens

Carex allivescens, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and often slightly hairy, with a dense cluster of spike-like flowers at the top. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Carex allivescens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
