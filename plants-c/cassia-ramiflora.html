Cassia ramiflora

Cassia ramiflora, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and compound leaves with bright yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing numerous flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Cassia ramiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
