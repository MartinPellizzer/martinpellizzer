Cynanchum carnosum

Cynanchum carnosum, commonly known as the fat-horned milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is known for its milky sap.

Cynanchum carnosum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
