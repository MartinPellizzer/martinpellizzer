Capparis calvescens

Capparis calvescens, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright orange, berry-like fruits. The fruit is often used in culinary applications due to its sweet and tangy flavor.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
