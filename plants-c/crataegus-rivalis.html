Crataegus rivalis

Crataegus rivalis, commonly known as the Rival hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Crataegus rivalis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
