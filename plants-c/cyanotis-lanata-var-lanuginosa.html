Cyanotis lanata var. lanuginosa

Cyanotis lanata var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, often with a white or pale blue throat. The plant has hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
