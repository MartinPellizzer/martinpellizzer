Chorizanthe viridis

Chorizanthe viridis, commonly known as green burclover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides.

Chorizanthe viridis is an annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for certain insects.
