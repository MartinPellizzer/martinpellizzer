Coleus blumei var. marshallii

Coleus blumei var. marshallii, commonly known as the Red Dragon coleus, is a vibrant ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep red to maroon hues, often featuring intricate patterns of white or yellow. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and containers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its bold coloration and texture.
