Calanthe arcuata subsp. caudatilabella

Calanthe arcuata subsp.

caudatilabella, commonly known as the long-lipped calanthe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, strap-like leaves.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with three petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a prized specimen in both horticulture and botanical research.
