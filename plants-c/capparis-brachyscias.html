Capparis brachyscias

Capparis brachyscias, commonly known as the small-fruited caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and edible, similar to other caper species.

Capparis brachyscias is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
