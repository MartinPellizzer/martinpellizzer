Cissus connivens

Cissus connivens, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
