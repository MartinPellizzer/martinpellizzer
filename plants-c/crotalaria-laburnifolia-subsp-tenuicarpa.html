Crotalaria laburnifolia subsp. tenuicarpa

Crotalaria laburnifolia subsp.

tenuicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Ghana.

The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a slender, elongated pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
