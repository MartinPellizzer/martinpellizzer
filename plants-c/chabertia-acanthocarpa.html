Chabertia acanthocarpa

Chabertia acanthocarpa, commonly known as the spinyfruit chabertia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a dry, spiny capsule that contains several seeds, aiding in its dispersal by wind or animals.
