Carex subumbellata var. subumbellata

Carex subumbellata var. subumbellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flower heads known as umbels, which are usually green to reddish in color.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
