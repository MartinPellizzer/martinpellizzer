Campanula patula var. vajdae

Campanula patula var. vajdae, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are slender and often branched. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.
