Carex × sakaguchii

Carex × sakaguchii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is native to Japan and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland areas.

Carex × sakaguchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural and cultivated landscapes.
