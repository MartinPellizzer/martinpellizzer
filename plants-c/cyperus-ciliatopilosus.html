Cyperus ciliatopilosus

Cyperus ciliatopilosus, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Cyperus ciliatopilosus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
