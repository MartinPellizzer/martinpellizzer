Crataegus × cogswellii

Crataegus × cogswellii, commonly known as the Cogswell hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant features thorned branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. Its fruit is a small, red, juicy berry that ripens in late summer.

The Cogswell hawthorn is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
