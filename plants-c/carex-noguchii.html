Carex noguchii

Carex noguchii, commonly known as Noguchi's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex noguchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
