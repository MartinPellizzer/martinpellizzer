Chartolepis kotschyi

Chartolepis kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi's rockrose, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access underground water. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, hardy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xerophytic gardens.
