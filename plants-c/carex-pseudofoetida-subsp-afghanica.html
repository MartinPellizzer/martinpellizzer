Carex pseudofoetida subsp. afghanica

Carex pseudofoetida subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
