Campanopsis krebsii var. transvalica

Campanopsis krebsii var. transvalica, commonly known as the Transvaal bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals and a prominent spur. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
