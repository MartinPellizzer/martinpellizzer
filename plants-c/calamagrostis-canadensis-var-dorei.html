Calamagrostis canadensis var. dorei

Calamagrostis canadensis var. dorei, commonly known as Dore's bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
