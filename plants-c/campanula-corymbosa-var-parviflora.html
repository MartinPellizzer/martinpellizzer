Campanula corymbosa var. parviflora

Campanula corymbosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
