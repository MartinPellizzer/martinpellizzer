Colutea arborescens f. bullata

Colutea arborescens f. bullata, commonly known as the bullate tree clover, is a botanical variant of the tree clover species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often hairy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
