Convolvulus sagittatus var. ulosepalus

Convolvulus sagittatus var. ulosepalus, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
