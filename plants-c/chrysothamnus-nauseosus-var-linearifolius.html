Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. linearifolius

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. linearifolius, commonly known as the linearleaf goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
