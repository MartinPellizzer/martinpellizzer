Campanula besenginica

Campanula besenginica, commonly known as the Besengin bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula besenginica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
