Cirsium bolocephalum

Cirsium bolocephalum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Cirsium bolocephalum typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
