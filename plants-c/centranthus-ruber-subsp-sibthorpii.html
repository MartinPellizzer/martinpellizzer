Centranthus ruber subsp. sibthorpii

Centranthus ruber subsp.

sibthorpii, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, pink, or white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Centranthus genus, sharing similar botanical features and ecological preferences.
