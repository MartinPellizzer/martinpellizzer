Cestrum cancellatum

Cestrum cancellatum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has opposite, simple, elliptic leaves that are often hairy. It produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but should be consumed in moderation.

Cestrum cancellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
