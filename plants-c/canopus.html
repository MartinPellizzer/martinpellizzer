Canopus

Canopus, commonly known as the "Ship's Star," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Capparis and is characterized by its woody shrub structure and thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often followed by small, edible berries. Canopus thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Its historical use in traditional medicine adds to its botanical significance.
