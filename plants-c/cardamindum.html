Cardamindum

Cardamindum, commonly known as the false cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Zingiberaceae, which also includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are typically clustered in spikes and can range in color from white to pale yellow.

The rhizomes of Cardamindum are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to true cardamom.
