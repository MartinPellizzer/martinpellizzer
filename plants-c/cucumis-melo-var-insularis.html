Cucumis melo var. insularis

Cucumis melo var. insularis, commonly known as the Canary Island melon, is a botanical variant of the cantaloupe.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, honey-flavored flesh. The plant produces large, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, aromatic skin. It thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor and ornamental value.
