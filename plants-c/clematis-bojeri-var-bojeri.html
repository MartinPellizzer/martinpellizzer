Clematis bojeri var. bojeri

Clematis bojeri var. bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
