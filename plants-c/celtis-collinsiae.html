Celtis collinsiae

Celtis collinsiae, commonly known as the Collins' hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but not typically for humans.

Celtis collinsiae is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and riparian areas.
