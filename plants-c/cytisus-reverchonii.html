Cytisus reverchonii

Cytisus reverchonii, commonly known as the Canary Islands broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to the island's unique climate. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and covered in fine hairs.

Cytisus reverchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
