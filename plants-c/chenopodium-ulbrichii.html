Chenopodium ulbrichii

Chenopodium ulbrichii, commonly known as wild spinach, is an annual flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its triangular, smooth, and edible leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, greenish flowers and has a fibrous root system.

Chenopodium ulbrichii is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also valued as a nutritious vegetable.
