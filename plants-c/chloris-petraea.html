Chloris petraea

Chloris petraea, commonly known as the rock grass or mountain grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
