Clarisia volubilis

Clarisia volubilis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Clarisia volubilis is often used in gardens as a decorative climber due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
