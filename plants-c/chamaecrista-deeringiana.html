Chamaecrista deeringiana

Chamaecrista deeringiana, commonly known as Deering's burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and restoration projects.
