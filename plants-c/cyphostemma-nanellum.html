Cyphostemma nanellum

Cyphostemma nanellum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
