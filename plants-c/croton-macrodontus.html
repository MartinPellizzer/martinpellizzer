Croton macrodontus

Croton macrodontus, commonly known as the large-toothed croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves with prominently veined, lobed or toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
