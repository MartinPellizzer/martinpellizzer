Campanula sivasica

Campanula sivasica, commonly known as the Sivas bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it blooms during the summer months.

Campanula sivasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
