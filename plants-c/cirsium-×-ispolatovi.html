Cirsium × ispolatovi

Cirsium × ispolatovi, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, spiny seed heads.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
