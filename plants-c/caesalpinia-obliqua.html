Caesalpinia obliqua

Caesalpinia obliqua, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that display vibrant red and yellow hues. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are borne in dense, branched clusters. Caesalpinia obliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
