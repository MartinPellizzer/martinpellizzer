Crataegus alleghaniensis var. mira

Crataegus alleghaniensis var. mira, commonly known as the Allegheny crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers in spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but generally not consumed by humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
