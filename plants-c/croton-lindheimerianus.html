Croton lindheimerianus

Croton lindheimerianus, commonly known as Lindheimer's croton, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage, which adds visual interest to gardens and landscapes.
