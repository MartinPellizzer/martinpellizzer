Carex × oenensis

Carex × oenensis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges. Its leaves are grass-like, usually dark green with a waxy coating, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Carex × oenensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife in its native range.
