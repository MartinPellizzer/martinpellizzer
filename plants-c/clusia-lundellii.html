Clusia lundellii

Clusia lundellii, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Clusia lundellii is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine.
