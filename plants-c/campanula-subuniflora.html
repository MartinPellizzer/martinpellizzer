Campanula subuniflora

Campanula subuniflora, commonly known as the single-flower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula subuniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
