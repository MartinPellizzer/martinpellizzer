Cordia blancoi

Cordia blancoi, commonly known as the white cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cordia blancoi is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
