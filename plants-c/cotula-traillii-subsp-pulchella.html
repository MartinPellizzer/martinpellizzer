Cotula traillii subsp. pulchella

Cotula traillii subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits. Cotula traillii subsp.

pulchella is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
