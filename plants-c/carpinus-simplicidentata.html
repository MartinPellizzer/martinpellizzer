Carpinus simplicidentata

Carpinus simplicidentata, commonly known as the smooth-toothed hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Carpinus simplicidentata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
