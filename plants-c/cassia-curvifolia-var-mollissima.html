Cassia curvifolia var. mollissima

Cassia curvifolia var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which are typical of the legume family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
