Crepis incarnata var. dinarica

Crepis incarnata var. dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric eyebright, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
