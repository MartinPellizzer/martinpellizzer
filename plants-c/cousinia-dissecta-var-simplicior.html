Cousinia dissecta var. simplicior

Cousinia dissecta var. simplicior, commonly known as the simpleleaf desert cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often densely covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperature fluctuations.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
