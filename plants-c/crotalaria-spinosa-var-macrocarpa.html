Crotalaria spinosa var. macrocarpa

Crotalaria spinosa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded cowpea or sand vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves, with yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several elliptical, flat seeds.

This species is often used for soil stabilization and has some traditional medicinal uses.
