Carex spicigera var. rubella

Carex spicigera var. rubella, commonly known as the red-fruited sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its reddish-brown inflorescences that distinguish it from other sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

The variety rubella is notable for its distinctive coloration and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
