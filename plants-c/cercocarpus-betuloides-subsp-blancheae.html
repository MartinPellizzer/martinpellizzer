Cercocarpus betuloides subsp. blancheae

Cercocarpus betuloides subsp.

blancheae, commonly known as Blanche's curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
