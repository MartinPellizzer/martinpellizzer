Centranthus calcitrapae var. pumila

Centranthus calcitrapae var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf red centranthus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is aromatic and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its resilience in harsh environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
