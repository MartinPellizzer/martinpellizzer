Christella flaccida

Christella flaccida, commonly known as the slender rock-rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a succulent structure to store water.

Christella flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
