Cissus allenii

Cissus allenii, commonly known as the bone leaf or creeping fig, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish layer of trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, jelly-like fruits that are popular in local cuisines. Cissus allenii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
