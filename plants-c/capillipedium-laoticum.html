Capillipedium laoticum

Capillipedium laoticum, commonly known as the Laotian water lily, is a rare aquatic plant native to the Mekong River basin in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are thick and waxy, helping to support the weight of the plant and retain moisture.

Capillipedium laoticum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for aquatic insects and serving as a food source for various waterfowl.
