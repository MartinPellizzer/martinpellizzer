Crocus boissieri

Crocus boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and dry soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Crocus boissieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
