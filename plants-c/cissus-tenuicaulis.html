Cissus tenuicaulis

Cissus tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cissus tenuicaulis thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
