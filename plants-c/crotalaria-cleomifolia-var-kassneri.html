Crotalaria cleomifolia var. kassneri

Crotalaria cleomifolia var. kassneri, commonly known as the Kassner's cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are often used in traditional agriculture for their nitrogen-fixing properties.

This variant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
