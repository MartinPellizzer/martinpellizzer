Calonemorchis remota

Calonemorchis remota, commonly known as the remote calonemorchis, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing in association with specific host trees. Its reproductive system relies on specialized symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a critically endangered species.
