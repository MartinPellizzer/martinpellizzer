Carex × peraffinis

Carex × peraffinis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Carex × peraffinis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
