Commiphora arussensis

Commiphora arussensis, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, aromatic resinous exudate, which is used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface.

Commiphora arussensis typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.
