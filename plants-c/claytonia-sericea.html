Claytonia sericea

Claytonia sericea, commonly known as silky springbeauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Claytonia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
