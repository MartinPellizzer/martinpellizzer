Chavica sylvatica

Chavica sylvatica, commonly known as the European snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland floors and shaded areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing before the flowers.

Chavica sylvatica is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
