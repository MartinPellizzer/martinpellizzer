Croton lachnostephanus

Croton lachnostephanus, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Croton lachnostephanus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
