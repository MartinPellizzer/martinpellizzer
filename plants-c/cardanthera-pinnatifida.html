Cardanthera pinnatifida

Cardanthera pinnatifida, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant features pinnately divided leaves and produces showy, typically pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Cardanthera pinnatifida is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its ecological importance.
