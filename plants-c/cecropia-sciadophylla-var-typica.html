Cecropia sciadophylla var. typica

Cecropia sciadophylla var. typica, commonly known as the typical ceiba, is a fast-growing, tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and a distinctive, buttressed trunk. The plant is a member of the Malvaceae family and is often found in disturbed habitats such as forest edges and clearings. It produces showy, yellowish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
