Cereus pentlandii var. lateritius

Cereus pentlandii var. lateritius, commonly known as the rock cactus or laterite cereus, is a unique species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial cactus that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a short, thick stem with spines and produces large, showy flowers that bloom at night. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system to access water in dry soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
