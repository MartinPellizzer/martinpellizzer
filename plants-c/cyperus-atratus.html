Cyperus atratus

Cyperus atratus, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dark purple or blackish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically found in wet or disturbed soils. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Cyperus atratus is an important plant in wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
