Carthamus tauricus

Carthamus tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric safflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in summer and are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces seed heads that are rich in oils, which have been historically used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Carthamus tauricus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
