Carum ajawain

Carum ajawain, commonly known as Ajawain, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and cooking, valued for their pungent, caraway-like flavor.

The essential oils extracted from Ajawain contain compounds like limonene and phellandrene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
