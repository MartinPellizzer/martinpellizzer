Calceolaria latifolia

Calceolaria latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its broad, velvety leaves are usually dark green, and the flowers come in shades of purple, pink, and white.

Calceolaria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
