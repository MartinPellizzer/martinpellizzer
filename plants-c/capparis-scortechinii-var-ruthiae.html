Capparis scortechinii var. ruthiae

Capparis scortechinii var. ruthiae, commonly known as the Ruth's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and distinctive, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
