Cucubalus venosus

Cucubalus venosus, commonly known as the veined cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a elongated, cylindrical berry that turns from green to orange when mature.

The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
