Cirsium eatonii var. viperinum

Cirsium eatonii var. viperinum, commonly known as the viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species characterized by its spiny, upright stems and blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and features compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with venomous snakes due to its resemblance to their markings.
