Camptotheca

Camptotheca, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive, serrated edge and emits a fragrant, spicy aroma. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in the spring.

Camptotheca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
