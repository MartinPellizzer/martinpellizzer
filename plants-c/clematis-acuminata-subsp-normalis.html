Clematis acuminata subsp. normalis

Clematis acuminata subsp.

normalis, commonly known as the common clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to parts of Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures for optimal display.
