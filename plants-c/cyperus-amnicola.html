Cyperus amnicola

Cyperus amnicola, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. Cyperus amnicola is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
