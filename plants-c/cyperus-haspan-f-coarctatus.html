Cyperus haspan f. coarctatus

Cyperus haspan f. coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its small, three-lobed flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, three-angled fruits.

Cyperus haspan f. coarctatus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
