Croton pycnadenius

Croton pycnadenius, commonly known as the dense-leaved croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
