Crocus vernus var. glorianella

Crocus vernus var. glorianella, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas or gardens. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate.

The variety is distinguished by its more intense coloration compared to the typical crocus vernus.
