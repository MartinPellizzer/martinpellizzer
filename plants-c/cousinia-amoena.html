Cousinia amoena

Cousinia amoena, commonly known as the Kashmiri poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bract-like leaves around the flower head.

Cousinia amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
