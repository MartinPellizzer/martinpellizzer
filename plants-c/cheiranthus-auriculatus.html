Cheiranthus auriculatus

Cheiranthus auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cheiranthus auriculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or orange flowers.
