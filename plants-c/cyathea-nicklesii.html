Cyathea nicklesii

Cyathea nicklesii, commonly known as the Nickles' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome system supports its growth, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

Cyathea nicklesii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
