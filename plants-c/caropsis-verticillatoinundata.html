Caropsis verticillatoinundata

Caropsis verticillatoinundata, commonly known as the spiral-leaved nut.

It is a unique plant characterized by its spirally arranged leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, hard-shelled fruits that enclose its seeds. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in moist, tropical environments.

Due to its rare occurrence and specialized habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying plant morphology and adaptation.
