Calamus simplicifolius

Calamus simplicifolius, commonly known as the simple-leaved reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. Calamus simplicifolius is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential medicinal uses.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
