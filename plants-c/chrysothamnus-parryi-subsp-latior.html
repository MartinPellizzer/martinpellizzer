Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. latior

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

latior, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic foliage, which are typical features of the Asteraceae family.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of native plant communities and its use in ecological restoration projects.
