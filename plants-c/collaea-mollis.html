Collaea mollis

Collaea mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Collaea mollis is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage.
