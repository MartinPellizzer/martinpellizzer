Carex chinensis var. tsushimensis

Carex chinensis var. tsushimensis, commonly known as the Tsushimense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense arrangement that helps it thrive in shaded conditions.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
