Campanula oenipontana

Campanula oenipontana, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula oenipontana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
