Cuphea affinitatum

Cuphea affinitatum, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea affinitatum is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
