Carex omeiensis var. multifascula

Carex omeiensis var. multifascula, commonly known as the multi-flowered sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and flowering structures called spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in clusters, which are often hidden among the leaf-like bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
