Crotalaria longirostrata var. macrophylla

Crotalaria longirostrata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, though caution is advised due to its toxic alkaloids.
