Calea subcordata var. hirtella

Calea subcordata var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy calea, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its flowers are known for their unique, spiral-like structure and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
