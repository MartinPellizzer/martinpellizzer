Crataegus dodgei var. flavida

Crataegus dodgei var. flavida, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers, which bloom in early spring, and its small, red or orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy green surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
