Cnidoscolus macrandrus

Cnidoscolus macrandrus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, stinging hairs called trichomes. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking appearance and unique defensive mechanisms. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.
