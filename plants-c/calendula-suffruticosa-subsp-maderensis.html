Calendula suffruticosa subsp. maderensis

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan calendula, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
