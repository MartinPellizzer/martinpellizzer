Chamaesphacos afghanicus

Chamaesphacos afghanicus, commonly known as Afghan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
