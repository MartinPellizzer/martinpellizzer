Crataegus viridis var. nitida

Crataegus viridis var. nitida, commonly known as the glossy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are glossy on both sides, giving it its common name. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for hedges or garden borders.
