Cerasus bigarella rocmontis

Cerasus bigarella rocmontis, commonly known as the Rocmont's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its small, cherry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
