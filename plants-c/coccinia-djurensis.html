Coccinia djurensis

Coccinia djurensis, commonly known as the African leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Coccinia djurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
