Cucurbita texana

Cucurbita texana, commonly known as the Texas pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, followed by orange to red, elongated fruits. It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The fruit is edible when mature, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Cucurbita species.
