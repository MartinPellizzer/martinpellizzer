Cucubalus noctiflorus

Cucubalus noctiflorus, commonly known as the night-flowering moonflower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The night-flowering moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
