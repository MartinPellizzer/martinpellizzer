Calamagrostis villosa var. krupae

Calamagrostis villosa var. krupae, commonly known as the Krup's hairy reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and silky, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. This variety thrives in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetland restoration and water garden settings. Its feathery plumes bloom in late summer, providing visual interest and attracting pollinators.

The plant is hardy and resilient, capable of surviving in a range of environmental conditions.
