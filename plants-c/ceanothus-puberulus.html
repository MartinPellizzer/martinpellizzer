Ceanothus puberulus

Ceanothus puberulus, commonly known as the silky ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Oregon.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus puberulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
