Cerionanthus tataricus

Cerionanthus tataricus, commonly known as the Tatarian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Cerionanthus tataricus is also known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
