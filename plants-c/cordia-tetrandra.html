Cordia tetrandra

Cordia tetrandra, commonly known as the four-stamen cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Cordia tetrandra is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
