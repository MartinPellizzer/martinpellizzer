Cochlearia officinalis var. typica

Cochlearia officinalis var. typica, commonly known as the common cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear to oblanceolate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate and is sometimes grown in gardens for its ornamental value.
