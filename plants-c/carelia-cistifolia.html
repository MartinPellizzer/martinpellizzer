Carelia cistifolia

Carelia cistifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, leathery leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It is also known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Carelia cistifolia is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
