Cornus hemsleyi var. gracilipes

Cornus hemsleyi var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife species.
