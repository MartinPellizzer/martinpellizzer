Conothamnus neglectus

Conothamnus neglectus, commonly known as the slender everlasting or slender bluebells, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats.

Conothamnus neglectus is well adapted to dry conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
