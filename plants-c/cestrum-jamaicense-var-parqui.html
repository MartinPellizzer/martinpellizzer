Cestrum jamaicense var. parqui

Cestrum jamaicense var. parqui, commonly known as the Jamaican trumpetbush, is a flowering shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, a common trait among members of its family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant flowers and fragrant scent, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm regions.
