Calceolaria patens

Calceolaria patens, commonly known as the showy calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Calceolaria patens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
