Conyza montigena subsp. kilimanjarica

Conyza montigena subsp.

kilimanjarica, commonly known as Kilimanjaro fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa, particularly Tanzania. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and narrow, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
