Cerasus bifrons f. glandulosa

Cerasus bifrons f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cherry, is a variant of the cherry species characterized by its distinctive glandular features on its leaves and stems.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in summer. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and bear small, sticky glands.

The glandular cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
