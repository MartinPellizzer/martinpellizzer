Chlorophora tinctoria var. mora

Chlorophora tinctoria var. mora, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a medium to large tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable, heavy wood used in furniture and construction.
