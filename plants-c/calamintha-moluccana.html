Calamintha moluccana

Calamintha moluccana, commonly known as Moluccan calamint, is a perennial herb native to the Molucca Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue to purple and are arranged in whorls along the stems.

Calamintha moluccana is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive benefits.
