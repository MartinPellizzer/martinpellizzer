Cereus phaeacanthus var. breviflorus

Cereus phaeacanthus var. breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique blooming habit and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
