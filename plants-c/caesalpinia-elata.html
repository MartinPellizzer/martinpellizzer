Caesalpinia elata

Caesalpinia elata, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia elata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm, tropical climates.
