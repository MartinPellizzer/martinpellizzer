Crocus persicus

Crocus persicus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a single, showy bloom on a slender stem, with leaves that are narrow and grass-like. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and cool climates.

Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to return year after year in suitable conditions.
