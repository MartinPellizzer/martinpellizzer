Caragana pygmaea var. ouratensis

Caragana pygmaea var. ouratensis, commonly known as the dwarf peashrub, is a low-growing shrub native to the arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
