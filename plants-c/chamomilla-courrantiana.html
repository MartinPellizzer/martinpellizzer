Chamomilla courrantiana

Chamomilla courrantiana, commonly known as the Mexican chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellowish-white flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Chamomilla courrantiana is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
