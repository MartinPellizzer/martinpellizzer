Crambe orientalis

Crambe orientalis, commonly known as the oriental crambe, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a distinctive serrated edge. Crambe orientalis is often found in rocky or dry habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
