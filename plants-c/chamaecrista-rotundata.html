Chamaecrista rotundata

Chamaecrista rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
