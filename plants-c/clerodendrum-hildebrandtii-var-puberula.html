Clerodendrum hildebrandtii var. puberula

Clerodendrum hildebrandtii var. puberula, commonly known as the velvet clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
