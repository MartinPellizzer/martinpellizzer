Cymbidium aloifolium f. album

Cymbidium aloifolium f. album, commonly known as the white-leafed orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically white or pale green, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces tall, erect flower spikes bearing numerous white or pale pink flowers with a pleasant fragrance. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in suitable environments.
