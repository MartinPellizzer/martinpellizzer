Chamaecytisus triflorus subsp. leucotrichus

Chamaecytisus triflorus subsp.

leucotrichus, commonly known as the silver broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its silver-gray foliage and long blooming period make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
