Cassia gemina

Cassia gemina, commonly known as the twin cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with two pairs of leaflets, giving it the name "twin.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Cassia gemina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
