Cassia tetraphylla var. ventuarensis

Cassia tetraphylla var. ventuarensis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to landscapes in warm climates.
