Chionanthus spicatus

Chionanthus spicatus, commonly known as the spicate chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and autumn foliage color.
