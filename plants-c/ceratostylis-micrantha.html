Ceratostylis micrantha

Ceratostylis micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a short rhizome and produces a single spike of flowers with narrow, lance-shaped petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
