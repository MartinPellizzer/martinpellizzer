Cyperus antiquorum

Cyperus antiquorum, commonly known as the ancient sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Cyperus antiquorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
