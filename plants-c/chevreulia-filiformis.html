Chevreulia filiformis

Chevreulia filiformis, commonly known as the slender silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often appear to be made of silk, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
