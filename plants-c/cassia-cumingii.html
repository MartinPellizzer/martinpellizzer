Cassia cumingii

Cassia cumingii, commonly known as Chinese cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, spindle-shaped pods that contain numerous seeds. Cassia cumingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
