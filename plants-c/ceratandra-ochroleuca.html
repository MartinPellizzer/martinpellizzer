Ceratandra ochroleuca

Ceratandra ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and can bloom throughout the year in suitable conditions.

Ceratandra ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
