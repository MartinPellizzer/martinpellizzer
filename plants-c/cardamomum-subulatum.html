Cardamomum subulatum

Cardamomum subulatum, commonly known as the slender cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes, which are the primary part used, have a distinct aromatic and spicy flavor.

This species is valued both for its culinary uses and its traditional medicinal properties.
