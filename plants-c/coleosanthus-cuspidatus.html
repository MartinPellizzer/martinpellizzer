Coleosanthus cuspidatus

Coleosanthus cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
