Chamaecrista nictitans var. pilosa

Chamaecrista nictitans var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy gladecress, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by narrow, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
