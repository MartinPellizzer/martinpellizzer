Cinnamomum inconspicuum

Cinnamomum inconspicuum, commonly known as the little cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that contain a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and have a mild aromatic scent similar to cinnamon.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
