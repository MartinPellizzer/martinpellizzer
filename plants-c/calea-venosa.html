Calea venosa

Calea venosa, commonly known as the "flower of the evening," is a striking plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, violet-blue flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Calea venosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional healing practices.
