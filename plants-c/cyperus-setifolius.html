Cyperus setifolius

Cyperus setifolius, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus setifolius is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
