Chrysanthemum coronarium var. discolor

Chrysanthemum coronarium var. discolor, commonly known as the African daisy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive discoloration in the center, giving it its scientific name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
