Croton chapelieri var. longepetiolata

Croton chapelieri var. longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves with long petioles.

The foliage is often a vibrant mix of green, red, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its decorative value in gardens and indoor spaces.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.
