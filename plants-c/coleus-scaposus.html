Coleus scaposus

Coleus scaposus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf color and compact growth habit.

Coleus scaposus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
