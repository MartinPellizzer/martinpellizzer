Carex macrophylla

Carex macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Carex macrophylla typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
