Canna fulgida

Canna fulgida, commonly known as the fiery canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that come in vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow, often mistaken for petals. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna fulgida is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display and ability to add color to landscapes.
