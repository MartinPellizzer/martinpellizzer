Centaurea karamianiae

Centaurea karamianiae, commonly known as Karaman's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
