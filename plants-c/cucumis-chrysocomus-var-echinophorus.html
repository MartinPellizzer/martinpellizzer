Cucumis chrysocomus var. echinophorus

Cucumis chrysocomus var. echinophorus, commonly known as the spiny cucumber, is a rare and unique variant of the Cucumis genus.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny rind and small, elongated fruits that are typically green when mature. Native to the arid regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in hot, dry climates with well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a trailing or climbing growth habit and small, lobed leaves.

The plant is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its distinct morphology and potential for cultivation in suitable environments.
