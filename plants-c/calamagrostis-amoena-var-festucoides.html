Calamagrostis amoena var. festucoides

Calamagrostis amoena var. festucoides, commonly known as the slender bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
