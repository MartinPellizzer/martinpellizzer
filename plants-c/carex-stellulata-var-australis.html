Carex stellulata var. australis

Carex stellulata var. australis, commonly known as the Southern star sedge, is a perennial plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark brown flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often dark green in color.

The inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets, which are arranged in a distinctive star-like pattern, giving the plant its common name.
