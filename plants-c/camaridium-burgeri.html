Camaridium burgeri

Camaridium burgeri, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Camaridium burgeri is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure.
