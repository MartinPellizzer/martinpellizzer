Carex goodenowii f. zonata

Carex goodenowii f. zonata, commonly known as the zonate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value.

The zonate sedge is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
