Centaurea jacea subsp. vinyalsii

Centaurea jacea subsp.

vinyalsii, commonly known as the Catalan knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and striking purple to pink flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
