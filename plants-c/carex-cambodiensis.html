Carex cambodiensis

Carex cambodiensis, commonly known as the Cambodian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, spikelike flower clusters.

Carex cambodiensis is characterized by its robust rhizomes and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
