Centrostachys bidentata

Centrostachys bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed centrostachys, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and have distinct, toothed margins, which contribute to its common name.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in the summer months.
