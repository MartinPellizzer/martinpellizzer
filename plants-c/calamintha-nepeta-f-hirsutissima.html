Calamintha nepeta f. hirsutissima

Calamintha nepeta f. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy catmint, is a cultivar of the catmint plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is a compact, low-maintenance plant that is often used in ornamental gardens for its attractive appearance and mild scent.

The hairy texture of its leaves gives it a distinctive look and makes it a popular choice for borders and containers.
