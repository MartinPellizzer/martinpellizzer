Combretum mamehanko

Combretum mamehanko, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
