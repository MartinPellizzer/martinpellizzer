Calanthe amamiana

Calanthe amamiana, commonly known as the Amami calanthe, is a rare orchid species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive purple lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, thriving in humid and cool conditions. Its rhizomes are short and thick, anchoring it in the soil while supporting its foliage.

Calanthe amamiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
