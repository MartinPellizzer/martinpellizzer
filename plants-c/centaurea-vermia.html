Centaurea vermia

Centaurea vermia, commonly known as the Vermeil centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea vermia is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
