Citrus × nobilis subf. miyagawa-wase

Citrus × nobilis subf.

miyagawa-wase, commonly known as the Miyagawa wase mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broadly elliptical with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for both culinary and decorative purposes.
