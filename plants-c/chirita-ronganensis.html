Chirita ronganensis

Chirita ronganensis, commonly known as the Rongan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Chirita ronganensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its delicate flowers.
