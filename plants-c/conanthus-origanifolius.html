Conanthus origanifolius

Conanthus origanifolius, commonly known as the lemon verbena plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional medicine.
