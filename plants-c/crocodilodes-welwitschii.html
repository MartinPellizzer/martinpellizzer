Crocodilodes welwitschii

Crocodilodes welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's crocodile plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that resemble the scales of a crocodile, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
