Cirsium × paradoxum

Cirsium × paradoxum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a distinctive conical seed head, which is a key feature in its common name. It is native to North America and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Cirsium × paradoxum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
