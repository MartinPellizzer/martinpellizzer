Cynoglossum virginianum var. boreale

Cynoglossum virginianum var. boreale, commonly known as the northern forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
