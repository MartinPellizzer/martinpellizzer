Cupania diplopetala

Cupania diplopetala, commonly known as the Brazilian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Cupania diplopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
