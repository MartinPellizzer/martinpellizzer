Campanula patula subsp. debilis

Campanula patula subsp.

debilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming floral display.
