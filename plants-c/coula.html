Coula

Coula, commonly known as African mango or wild mango, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Coula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
