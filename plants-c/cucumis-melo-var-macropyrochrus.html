Cucumis melo var. macropyrochrus

Cucumis melo var. macropyrochrus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivated variety of the melon plant characterized by its large, elongated fruits.

The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough, hairy texture, while the stems are hairy and trailing. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and the fruit develops from the female flowers.

This variety is prized for its sweet, aromatic flesh and is commonly used in culinary applications.
