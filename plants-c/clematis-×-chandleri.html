Clematis × chandleri

Clematis × chandleri, commonly known as the Sweet autumn clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × chandleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
