Campomanesia mollicarpa

Campomanesia mollicarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are typically harvested when they turn red, containing a sweet, juicy pulp with a single seed.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
