Camellia wardii var. muricatula

Camellia wardii var. muricatula, commonly known as the Shikumen Camellia, is a species native to China, particularly the Shikumen region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive pink or red center. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in partially shaded environments with well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia wardii var. muricatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.
