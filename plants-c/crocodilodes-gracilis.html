Crocodilodes gracilis

Crocodilodes gracilis, commonly known as the slender crocodile fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive, almost reptilian appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores produced in clusters on the undersides of its fronds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
