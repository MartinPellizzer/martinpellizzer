Caesalpinia fisheriana

Caesalpinia fisheriana, commonly known as the Fisher's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bright yellow or orange inflorescences. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Caesalpinia fisheriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
