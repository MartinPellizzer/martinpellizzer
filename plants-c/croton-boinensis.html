Croton boinensis

Croton boinensis, commonly known as the Borneo croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, simple leaves with a rough texture and prominent venation. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow, and it produces toxic, orange-red berries.

Croton boinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
