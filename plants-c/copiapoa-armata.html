Copiapoa armata

Copiapoa armata, commonly known as the armed copiapoa, is a cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its columnar stems that are often covered with white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by cylindrical, ribbed fruits. It is adapted to extreme aridity, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Copiapoa armata is a popular specimen in desert gardens due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
