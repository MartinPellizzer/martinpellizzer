Carex lepidocarpa f. rectirostris

Carex lepidocarpa f. rectirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beaked sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a distinct, smooth texture.

The inflorescence consists of two types of spikelets, with the lower one being longer and more robust than the upper one.
