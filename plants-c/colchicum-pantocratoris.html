Colchicum pantocratoris

Colchicum pantocratoris, commonly known as the Pantocrator lily, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Colchicum, which belongs to the family Colchicaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a cluster of waxy, purple or pink flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
