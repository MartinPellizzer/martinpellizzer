Croton greveanus

Croton greveanus, commonly known as the Natal croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and vibrant green color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
