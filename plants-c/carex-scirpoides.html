Carex scirpoides

Carex scirpoides, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in aquatic habitats.

Carex scirpoides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to fluctuating water levels.
