Cirsium × hugueninii

Cirsium × hugueninii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture.

Cirsium × hugueninii is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in various environments.
