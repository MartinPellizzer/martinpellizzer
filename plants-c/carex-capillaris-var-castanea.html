Carex capillaris var. castanea

Carex capillaris var. castanea, commonly known as the chestnut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, dark green leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish to brownish flowers, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
