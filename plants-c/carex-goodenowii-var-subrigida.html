Carex goodenowii var. subrigida

Carex goodenowii var. subrigida, commonly known as the rigid sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, stiff leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, and its rhizomes are relatively short.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
