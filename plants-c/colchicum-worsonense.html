Colchicum worsonense

Colchicum worsonense, commonly known as the Worson's colchicum, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are usually purple or reddish in color.

Colchicum worsonense is also known for containing toxic alkaloids, which make it potentially dangerous if ingested.
