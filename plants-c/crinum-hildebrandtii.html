Crinum hildebrandtii

Crinum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's crinum, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Crinum hildebrandtii is also noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions once established.
