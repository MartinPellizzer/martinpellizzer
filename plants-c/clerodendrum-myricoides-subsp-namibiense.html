Clerodendrum myricoides subsp. namibiense

Clerodendrum myricoides subsp.

namibiense, commonly known as the Namibian flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and striking floral display make it a notable species in its native habitat.
