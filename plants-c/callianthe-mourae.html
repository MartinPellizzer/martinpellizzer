Callianthe mourae

Callianthe mourae, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with deep purple veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
