Crepis taraxacifolia var. pectinata

Crepis taraxacifolia var. pectinata, commonly known as the woolly hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lanceolate with a distinctive pinnate arrangement, and the inflorescence consists of a single central flower surrounded by bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
