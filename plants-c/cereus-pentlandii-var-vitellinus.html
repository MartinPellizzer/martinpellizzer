Cereus pentlandii var. vitellinus

Cereus pentlandii var. vitellinus, commonly known as the Yellow Cereus, is a succulent cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming at night. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
