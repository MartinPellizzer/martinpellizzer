Cyrtandra bungahijau

Cyrtandra bungahijau, commonly known as the golden flame ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the year in suitable conditions. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Cyrtandra bungahijau is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
