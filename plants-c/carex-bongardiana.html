Carex bongardiana

Carex bongardiana, commonly known as Bongard’s sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Bongard’s sedge produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
