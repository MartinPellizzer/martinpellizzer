Cirsium aduncum

Cirsium aduncum, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has a basal rosette of leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs.

Cirsium aduncum is an important food source for certain insects and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
