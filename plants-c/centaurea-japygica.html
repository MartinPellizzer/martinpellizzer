Centaurea japygica

Centaurea japygica, commonly known as the Kashmir centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Centaurea japygica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
