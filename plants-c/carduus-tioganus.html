Carduus tioganus

Carduus tioganus, commonly known as the Tioga thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
