Carex glauca f. corcyrensis

Carex glauca f. corcyrensis, commonly known as the Corcyrene blue sedge, is a variant of the blue sedge species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically bluish-green, with a waxy coating that gives them a distinct appearance. This form is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges or stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
