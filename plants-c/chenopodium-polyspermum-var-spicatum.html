Chenopodium polyspermum var. spicatum

Chenopodium polyspermum var. spicatum, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated inflorescences. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Chenopodium polyspermum var. spicatum is often found in sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
