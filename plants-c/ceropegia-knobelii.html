Ceropegia knobelii

Ceropegia knobelii, commonly known as the Cape fuchsia, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers that are often visited by specific pollinators, such as the hawk moth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, white hairs.

Ceropegia knobelii thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
