Capnoides straminea

Capnoides straminea, commonly known as the yellow balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in terminal racemes, blooming during the rainy season.

Capnoides straminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
