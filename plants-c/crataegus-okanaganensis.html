Crataegus okanaganensis

Crataegus okanaganensis, commonly known as Okanagan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
