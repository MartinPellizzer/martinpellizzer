Cyperus baronii var. mannii

Cyperus baronii var. mannii, commonly known as the African umbrella plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and dense, umbrella-like clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish hue at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water gardens or as a landscape plant in humid climates.
