Cacalia similis

Cacalia similis, commonly known as the similar cacalia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in a composite head.
