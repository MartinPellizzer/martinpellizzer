Calamagrostis epigejos var. alpestris

Calamagrostis epigejos var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage that turns golden in autumn. The plant produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
