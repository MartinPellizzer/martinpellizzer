Corybas callosus

Corybas callosus, commonly known as the hairy hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that is often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually dark purple or brown, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Corybas callosus is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific insect pollinators.
