Citronella mucronata

Citronella mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-point citronella, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is closely related to other citronella species and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its essential oil contains citral, which is known for its insect-repelling properties.
