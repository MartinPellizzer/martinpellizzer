Cynanchum wilfordii var. amamianum

Cynanchum wilfordii var. amamianum, commonly known as the Amamianum milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweed species, and is characterized by its opposite leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a latex sap that is milky and can be toxic, a common trait among milkweeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
