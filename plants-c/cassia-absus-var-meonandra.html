Cassia absus var. meonandra

Cassia absus var. meonandra, commonly known as the meadow cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow, papery flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
