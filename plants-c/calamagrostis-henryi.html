Calamagrostis henryi

Calamagrostis henryi, commonly known as Henry's reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to China.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
