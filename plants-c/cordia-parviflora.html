Cordia parviflora

Cordia parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Cordia parviflora is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
