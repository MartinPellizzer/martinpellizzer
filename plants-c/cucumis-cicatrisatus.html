Cucumis cicatrisatus

Cucumis cicatrisatus, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique species of flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is notable for its ability to survive extreme desiccation, entering a state of dormancy when dry and reviving when water becomes available. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cucumbers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are succulent and can store water.

This adaptation makes it well-suited for arid environments and has earned it the nickname "resurrection plant.".
