Ceratosanthes parviflora

Ceratosanthes parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered horned poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy, orange-red flowers in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
