Calyptrella tristis

Calyptrella tristis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a small, mat-forming perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Calyptrella tristis is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
