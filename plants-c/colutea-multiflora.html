Colutea multiflora

Colutea multiflora, commonly known as the sweet caraway or field bean.

It is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae, native to Europe and western Asia. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in spherical heads. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent pod that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
