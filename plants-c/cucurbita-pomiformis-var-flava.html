Cucurbita pomiformis var. flava

Cucurbita pomiformis var. flava, commonly known as the buttercup squash, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange, pear-shaped fruits with a smooth, waxy skin. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, particularly in traditional dishes from its native regions.
