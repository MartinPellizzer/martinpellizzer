Convolvulus equitans var. lindheimeri

Convolvulus equitans var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

The leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
