Cephalaria sumbuliana

Cephalaria sumbuliana, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.
