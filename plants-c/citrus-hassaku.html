Citrus hassaku

Citrus hassaku, commonly known as Japanese mandarin or kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It produces small, orange-colored fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green, though they ripen to a bright orange hue. The fruit has a thin, edible peel and a sweet, segmented pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruits.

The plant thrives in subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
