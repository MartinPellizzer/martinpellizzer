Cyrilla microareolata subsp. microareolata

Cyrilla microareolata subsp.

microareolata, commonly known as the small-areola cyrilla, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with prominent, raised veins.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks.
