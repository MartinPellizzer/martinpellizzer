Croton claraensis

Croton claraensis, commonly known as the Clara's croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and vibrant green or red hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

Croton claraensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
