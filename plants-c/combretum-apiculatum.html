Combretum apiculatum

Combretum apiculatum, commonly known as the red-flowered combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are borne in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
