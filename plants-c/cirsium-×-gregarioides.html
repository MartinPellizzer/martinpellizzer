Cirsium × gregarioides

Cirsium × gregarioides, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. This plant is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, rocky soils. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The botanical features of Cirsium × gregarioides make it an important plant in ecological studies and native landscaping.
