Calamus javensis var. tetrastichus

Calamus javensis var. tetrastichus, commonly known as the four-rowed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and pinnate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by dark, round fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
