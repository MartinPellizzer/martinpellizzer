Cnicus rothrockii var. diffusus

Cnicus rothrockii var. diffusus, commonly known as the desert sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a mat-forming growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh conditions.

It is also known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes.
