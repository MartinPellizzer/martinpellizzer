Corydalis solida var. hispida

Corydalis solida var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Corydalis solida var. hispida is valued in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
