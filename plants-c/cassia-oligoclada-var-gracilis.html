Cassia oligoclada var. gracilis

Cassia oligoclada var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with slender, acuminate leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which are typical of the legume family, Fabaceae. Its flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
