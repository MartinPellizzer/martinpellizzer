Cestrum densiflorum

Cestrum densiflorum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring thick, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its flowers are typically clustered in panicles and have a waxy texture.

The plant is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
