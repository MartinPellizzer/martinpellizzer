Cnicus eriophorus subsp. spathulatus

Cnicus eriophorus subsp.

spathulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf gooseweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and yellowish to orange disk flowers.

The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a low-growing habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

The subspecies spathulatus is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other subspecies of Cnicus eriophorus.
