Cistus × cyprius f. albiflorus

Cistus × cyprius f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a low-maintenance ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its aromatic leaves and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
