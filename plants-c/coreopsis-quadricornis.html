Coreopsis quadricornis

Coreopsis quadricornis, commonly known as the four-horned coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four distinct petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Coreopsis quadricornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
