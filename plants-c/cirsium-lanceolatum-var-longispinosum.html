Cirsium lanceolatum var. longispinosum

Cirsium lanceolatum var. longispinosum, commonly known as the long-spined thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers are typically clustered in a single head, and the bracts are long and spiny, providing protection for the flower buds.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
