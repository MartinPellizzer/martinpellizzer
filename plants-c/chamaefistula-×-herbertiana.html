Chamaefistula × herbertiana

Chamaefistula × herbertiana, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, fragrant, lilac-pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge.

The botanical name reflects its hybrid origin between Chamaefistula and Vitex.
