Cyperus japonicus

Cyperus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets that are often reddish in color.

Cyperus japonicus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
