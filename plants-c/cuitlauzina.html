Cuitlauzina

Cuitlauzina, commonly known as the "flower of the gods," is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a strong, fragrant aroma. Its leaves are typically ovate and glossy, while its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Cuitlauzina has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
