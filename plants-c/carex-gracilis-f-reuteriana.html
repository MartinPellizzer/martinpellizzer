Carex gracilis f. reuteriana

Carex gracilis f. reuteriana, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
