Carex macloviana f. involucrata

Carex macloviana f. involucrata, commonly known as the velvet sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This fern-like sedge is valued for its ability to thrive in wet environments and its aesthetic qualities in garden settings.
