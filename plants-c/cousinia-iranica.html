Cousinia iranica

Cousinia iranica, commonly known as the Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, thriving in dry and semi-arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in a head, and the leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Cousinia iranica is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
