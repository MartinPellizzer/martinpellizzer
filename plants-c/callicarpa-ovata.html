Callicarpa ovata

Callicarpa ovata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and are edible for humans when ripe.
