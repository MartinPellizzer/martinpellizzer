Centaurea × beckiana

Centaurea × beckiana, commonly known as the Beck's cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which consist of both ray florets and tubular disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
