Cymbopogon proximus var. sennarensis

Cymbopogon proximus var. sennarensis, commonly known as the Sennarensis lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of southern India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong citrus scent when crushed. This variety is closely related to other members of the Cymbopogon genus, which includes several species used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable resource in both traditional and modern applications.
