Cremolobus pinnatifidus

Cremolobus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and have a serrated edge, while its stems are erect and branched.

Cremolobus pinnatifidus is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
