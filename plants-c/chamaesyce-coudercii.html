Chamaesyce coudercii

Chamaesyce coudercii, commonly known as the smallleaf sandmat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, fleshy, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in desert or semi-desert regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to harsh, dry conditions, and it is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
