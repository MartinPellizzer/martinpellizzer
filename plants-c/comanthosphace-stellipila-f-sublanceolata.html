Comanthosphace stellipila f. sublanceolata

Comanthosphace stellipila f. sublanceolata, commonly known as the star-shaped liverwort, is a member of the Marchantiophyta division.

It is a non-vascular plant characterized by its flattened, leaf-like structures called thalli. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as on rocks or soil in temperate regions. Its sublanceolate leaf shape contributes to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its role in ecological succession.
