Calamus javensis subvar. mollispinus

Calamus javensis subvar. mollispinus, commonly known as the Java calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is known for its aromatic rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. Its growth habit is typically clumping, with the stems forming dense clusters near the ground.

The leaves are long and narrow, often reaching several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
