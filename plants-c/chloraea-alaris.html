Chloraea alaris

Chloraea alaris, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils.
