Cereus tacuaralensis

Cereus tacuaralensis, commonly known as the Tacuaralensis Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, spiny stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, a characteristic feature of many cacti in the Cereus genus. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system to access water in its dry habitat.

This plant is well adapted to extreme environmental conditions and is often found in rocky soils.
