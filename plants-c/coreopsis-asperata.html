Coreopsis asperata

Coreopsis asperata, commonly known as rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves with rough, hairy textures. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis asperata is often found in prairies, open woods, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
