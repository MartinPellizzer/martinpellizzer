Clematis lushuiensis

Clematis lushuiensis, commonly known as the Lushui Clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody vines and showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
