Chenopodium auricomum

Chenopodium auricomum, commonly known as golden goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often subtended by bracts. It is native to North America and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Chenopodium auricomum is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also a food source for some wildlife species.
