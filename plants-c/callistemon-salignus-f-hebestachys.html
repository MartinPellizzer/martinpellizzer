Callistemon salignus f. hebestachys

Callistemon salignus f. hebestachys, commonly known as the golden bottlebrush, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red, bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting birds and insects. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving them a distinctive appearance. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal or sunny garden locations.
