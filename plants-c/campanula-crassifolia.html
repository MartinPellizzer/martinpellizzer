Campanula crassifolia

Campanula crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
