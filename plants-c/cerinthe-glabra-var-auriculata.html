Cerinthe glabra var. auriculata

Cerinthe glabra var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate cerinthe, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, spurred flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
