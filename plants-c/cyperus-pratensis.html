Cyperus pratensis

Cyperus pratensis, commonly known as marsh flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus pratensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
