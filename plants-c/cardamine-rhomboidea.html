Cardamine rhomboidea

Cardamine rhomboidea, commonly known as the kidneyleaf buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are typically divided into three lobes, giving it a rhomboid appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Cardamine rhomboidea is often found in temperate regions of North America and is known for its delicate, ephemeral growth cycle.
