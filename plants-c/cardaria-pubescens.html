Cardaria pubescens

Cardaria pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods and are often used as a food source.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
