Cyperus friesii

Cyperus friesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus friesii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
