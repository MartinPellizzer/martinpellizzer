Campanula × lundstroemii

Campanula × lundstroemii, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Campanula × lundstroemii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
