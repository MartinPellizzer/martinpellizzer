Cyperus rotundus var. taylorii

Cyperus rotundus var. taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-lobed, triangular fruits.

This variant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
