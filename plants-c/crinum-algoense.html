Crinum algoense

Crinum algoense, commonly known as the African lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a strong fragrance. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Crinum algoense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its bulbous roots store nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of drought.
