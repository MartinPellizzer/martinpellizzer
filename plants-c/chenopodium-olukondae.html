Chenopodium olukondae

Chenopodium olukondae, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, featuring a shallow root system and thick, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional diets for their nutritional value.

Chenopodium olukondae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
