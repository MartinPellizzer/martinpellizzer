Cojoba arborea var. angustifolia

Cojoba arborea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cojoba, is a tropical plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
