Cassia orenocensis

Cassia orenocensis, commonly known as the Orenoco cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia orenocensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
