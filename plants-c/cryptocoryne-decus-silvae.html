Cryptocoryne decus-silvae

Cryptocoryne decus-silvae, commonly known as the forest cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to the shaded, slow-moving waters of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that often display a striking pattern of light and dark green. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and prefers soft, slightly acidic water with a high nutrient content. Its rhizomes form dense clusters, helping to stabilize substrates and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Cryptocoryne decus-silvae is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value and ease of care.
