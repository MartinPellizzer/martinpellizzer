Carludovica costaricensis

Carludovica costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Central America.

It is a low-growing, spineless shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of fleshy, green stems that store water, adapted to its arid habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and rocky areas.
