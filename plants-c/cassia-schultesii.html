Cassia schultesii

Cassia schultesii, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cassia schultesii thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
