Campanula pangea

Campanula pangea, commonly known as the Pangea bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Campanula pangea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
