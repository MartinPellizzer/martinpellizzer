Clutia phyllanthifolia

Clutia phyllanthifolia, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
