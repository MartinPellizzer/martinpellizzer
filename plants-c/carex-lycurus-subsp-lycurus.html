Carex lycurus subsp. lycurus

Carex lycurus subsp.

lycurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
