Cryptantha ramosissima

Cryptantha ramosissima, commonly known as the desert flax or desert bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Cryptantha ramosissima plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
