Carex neomexicana

Carex neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Carex neomexicana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
