Carex heteroclita

Carex heteroclita, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Carex heteroclita is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
