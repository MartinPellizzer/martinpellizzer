Cuphea llavea var. barbigera

Cuphea llavea var. barbigera, commonly known as the bearded cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces distinctive, beard-like bracts beneath its flowers, which are a key identifying feature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
