Commelina mannii

Commelina mannii, commonly known as the white-veined spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Commelina genus within the family Commelinaceae, which includes many herbaceous plants. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with prominent white veins and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
