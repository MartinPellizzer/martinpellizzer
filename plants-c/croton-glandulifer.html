Croton glandulifer

Croton glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often green with a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
