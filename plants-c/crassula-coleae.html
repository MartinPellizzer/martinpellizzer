Crassula coleae

Crassula coleae, commonly known as the "Cape Town Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight red or purple tinge, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive dry conditions.

Crassula coleae is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
