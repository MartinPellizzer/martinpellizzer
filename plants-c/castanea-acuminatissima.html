Castanea acuminatissima

Castanea acuminatissima, commonly known as the Chinese sweet chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain one or two edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. Castanea acuminatissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and its nuts, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
