Carex diplasiocarpa

Carex diplasiocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a dark green coloration. Its flowering spikes are usually unisexual, with separate male and female flowers, and it produces small, inconspicuous seeds.

Carex diplasiocarpa is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
