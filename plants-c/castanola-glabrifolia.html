Castanola glabrifolia

Castanola glabrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Castanola glabrifolia is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
