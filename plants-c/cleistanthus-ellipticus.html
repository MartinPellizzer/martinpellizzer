Cleistanthus ellipticus

Cleistanthus ellipticus, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
