Cucurbita melopepo var. caseolaris

Cucurbita melopepo var. caseolaris, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin or cascarilla, is a botanical variety belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often deeply lobed leaves, which are typically dark green with a rough texture. The plant produces orange to yellow fruits that are usually oval or spherical in shape. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary and ornamental uses in various regions of Mexico and Central America.
