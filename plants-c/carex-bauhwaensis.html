Carex bauhwaensis

Carex bauhwaensis, commonly known as the Hainan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Hainan Island, where it grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in forest understories and wetlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
