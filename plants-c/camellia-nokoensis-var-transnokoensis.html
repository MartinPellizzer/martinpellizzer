Camellia nokoensis var. transnokoensis

Camellia nokoensis var. transnokoensis, commonly known as the Transnoko camellia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces striking, camellia-like flowers in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
