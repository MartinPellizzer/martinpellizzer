Campomanesia fusca

Campomanesia fusca, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to bright red, edible berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitat.
