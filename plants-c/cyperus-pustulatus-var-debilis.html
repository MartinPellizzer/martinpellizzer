Cyperus pustulatus var. debilis

Cyperus pustulatus var. debilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often adorned with small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled capsules.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
