Colutea halepica

Colutea halepica, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with several leaflets and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Colutea halepica is also sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
