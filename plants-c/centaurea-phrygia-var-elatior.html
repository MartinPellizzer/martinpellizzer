Centaurea phrygia var. elatior

Centaurea phrygia var. elatior, commonly known as the tall Armenian centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces showy, pink to red composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight bluish-gray hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
