Cistus × crispatus

Cistus × crispatus, commonly known as the wrinkled rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which is part of the family Ericaceae, known for its evergreen shrubs and heath-like plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Cistus × crispatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
