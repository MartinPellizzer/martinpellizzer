Cardamine × zahlbruckneriana

Cardamine × zahlbruckneriana, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces capsules containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
