Colubrina texensis var. pedunculata

Colubrina texensis var. pedunculata, commonly known as Texas coralbean, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It features showy, bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, spherical berries that turn from green to black as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
