Chrysophyllum pruniferum

Chrysophyllum pruniferum, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, yellowish rind and a sour, acidic pulp. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible pulp, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
