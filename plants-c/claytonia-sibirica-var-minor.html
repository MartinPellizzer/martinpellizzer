Claytonia sibirica var. minor

Claytonia sibirica var. minor, commonly known as the Siberian spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features rosettes of ovate, hairy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
