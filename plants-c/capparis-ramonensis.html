Capparis ramonensis

Capparis ramonensis, commonly known as the Ramon's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
