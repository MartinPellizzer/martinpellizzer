Chrysophyllum peninsulare

Chrysophyllum peninsulare, commonly known as the Cuban star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and limestone soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
