Cyclamen purpurascens subsp. ponticum

Cyclamen purpurascens subsp.

ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive purplish tinge, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a spurred shape.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. It produces underground tubers that store nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of dormancy. Cyclamen purpurascens subsp.

ponticum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
