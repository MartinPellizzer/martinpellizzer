Croton sagranus

Croton sagranus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from red to orange and yellow, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
