Camellia oligophlebia

Camellia oligophlebia, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant is closely related to the tea plant, Camellia sinensis, and shares similar botanical features. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Camellia oligophlebia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
