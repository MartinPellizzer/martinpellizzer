Cassia centijuga

Cassia centijuga, commonly known as golden shower tree or Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. Cassia centijuga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
