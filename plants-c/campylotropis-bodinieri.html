Campylotropis bodinieri

Campylotropis bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier's climbing bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
