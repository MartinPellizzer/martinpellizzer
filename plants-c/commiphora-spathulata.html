Commiphora spathulata

Commiphora spathulata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers and is known for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes.

The species is valued for its ecological role in arid and semi-arid regions.
