It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, heart-shaped fronds covered in fine, hairy structures. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Cyclosorus callosus is often found in temperate regions and plays a minor role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
