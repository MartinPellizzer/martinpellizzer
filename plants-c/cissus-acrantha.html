Cissus acrantha

Cissus acrantha, commonly known as the African devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Cissus acrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
