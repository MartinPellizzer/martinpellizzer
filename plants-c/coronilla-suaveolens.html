Coronilla suaveolens

Coronilla suaveolens, commonly known as sweet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to violet. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
