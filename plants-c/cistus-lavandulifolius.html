Cistus lavandulifolius

Cistus lavandulifolius, commonly known as sweet rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a fragrant essential oil similar to lavender, which contributes to its use in perfumery and aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions makes it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
