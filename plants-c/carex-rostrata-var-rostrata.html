Carex rostrata var. rostrata

Carex rostrata var. rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a reddish or purplish hue at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
