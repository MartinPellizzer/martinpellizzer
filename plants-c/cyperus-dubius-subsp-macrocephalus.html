Cyperus dubius subsp. macrocephalus

Cyperus dubius subsp.

macrocephalus, commonly known as the broad-headed sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in disturbed or flooded soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
