Coleus marshallii

Coleus marshallii, commonly known as the red-leaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that range from deep red to purple and green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Coleus marshallii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
