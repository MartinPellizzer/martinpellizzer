Cyrtandra × adine

Cyrtandra × adine, commonly known as the hybrid Cyrtandra, is a flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Cyrtandra adinonii and Cyrtandra falcata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
