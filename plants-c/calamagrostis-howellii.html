Calamagrostis howellii

Calamagrostis howellii, commonly known as Howell's reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of streams and rivers. The plant features tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist environments.
