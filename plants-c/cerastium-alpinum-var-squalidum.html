Cerastium alpinum var. squalidum

Cerastium alpinum var. squalidum, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is adapted to cold climates and has a compact growth habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for ecological restoration in mountainous environments.
