Cyathea ochroleuca

Cyathea ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are typically bright green with prominent, yellowish veins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Cyathea ochroleuca plays a role in providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
