Centaurea aeolica

Centaurea aeolica, commonly known as the Aeolian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea aeolica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
