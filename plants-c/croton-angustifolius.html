Croton angustifolius

Croton angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, narrow leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Croton angustifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
