Cerastium chilense

Cerastium chilense, commonly known as Chilean mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
