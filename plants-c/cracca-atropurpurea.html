Cracca atropurpurea

Cracca atropurpurea, commonly known as purple cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its stems are hairy and often reddish in color, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Cracca atropurpurea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
