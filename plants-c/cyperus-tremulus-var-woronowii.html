Cyperus tremulus var. woronowii

Cyperus tremulus var. woronowii, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense clusters.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats and is often found in disturbed or wetland areas.
