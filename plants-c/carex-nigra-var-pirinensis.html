Carex nigra var. pirinensis

Carex nigra var. pirinensis, commonly known as the Pirin black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its foliage is dark green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
