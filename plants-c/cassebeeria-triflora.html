Cassebeeria triflora

Cassebeeria triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
