Centaurea jacea subsp. oxylepis

Centaurea jacea subsp.

oxylepis, commonly known as the oxylepis thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are typically covered in grayish or whitish hairs.

The plant produces purple to reddish-blue flower heads, which are surrounded by sharp, spiny bracts. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
