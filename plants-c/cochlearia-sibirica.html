Cochlearia sibirica

Cochlearia sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are often lobed or toothed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cochlearia sibirica is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability in harsh climates.
