Carica papaya var. jimenezii

Carica papaya var. jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez papaya, is a less common variant of the papaya plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange fruits that are typically smaller than the common papaya. The plant produces a distinctive latex sap, which is a feature shared with other members of the Caricaceae family. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
