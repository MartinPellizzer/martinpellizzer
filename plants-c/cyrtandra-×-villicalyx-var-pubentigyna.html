Cyrtandra × villicalyx var. pubentigyna

Cyrtandra × villicalyx var. pubentigyna, commonly known as the hairy Cyrtandra, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, clustered in umbels, and are often fragrant.

This variant is notable for its distinctive pubescence, which contributes to its ecological adaptations in humid, shaded environments.
