Cephalostachyum chinense

Cephalostachyum chinense, commonly known as the Chinese spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces distinctive spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale yellow to white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
