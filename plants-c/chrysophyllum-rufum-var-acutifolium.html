Chrysophyllum rufum var. acutifolium

Chrysophyllum rufum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the red-leafed sour orange, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically reddish when young and turn dark green with age. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits with a tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often cultivated in gardens and tropical landscapes.
