Cyperus redolens

Cyperus redolens, commonly known as the sweet grass or fragrant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves emit a pleasant, sweet aroma, especially when crushed, which contributes to its common name.

Cyperus redolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
