Crotalaria griquensis

Crotalaria griquensis, commonly known as the Grootfontein burrbean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bean-like pods. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Crotalaria griquensis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
