Cupaniopsis serrata f. vestita

Cupaniopsis serrata f. vestita, commonly known as the silky cupaniopsis, is a variant of the cupaniopsis genus native to Australia.

This plant is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It produces small, white flowers followed by dark, spherical fruits, making it a visually interesting plant for horticultural purposes.
