Cereus coerulescens var. landbeckii

Cereus coerulescens var. landbeckii, commonly known as the Landbeck's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
