Corylus heterophylla var. thunbergii

Corylus heterophylla var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually toothed or lobed. The plant produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers in separate clusters. Its edible nuts are enclosed in a spiny husk and are often used in traditional cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
