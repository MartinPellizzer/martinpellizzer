Centaurea carystea

Centaurea carystea, commonly known as the Cyprus star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, star-shaped flowers surrounded by bracts.

Centaurea carystea is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
