Carex stenantha var. yatsugatakensis

Carex stenantha var. yatsugatakensis, commonly known as the Yatsugatakensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
