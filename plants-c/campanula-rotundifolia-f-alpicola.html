Campanula rotundifolia f. alpicola

Campanula rotundifolia f. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
