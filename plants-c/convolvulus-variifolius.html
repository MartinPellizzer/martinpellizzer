Convolvulus variifolius

Convolvulus variifolius, commonly known as the variable leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are variable in shape, hence the species name "variifolius," and can be heart-shaped or ovate.

The vine-like stems allow it to climb or trail, making it a common ornamental plant in gardens.
