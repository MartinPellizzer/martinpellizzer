Cytisus purpureus var. atropurpureus

Cytisus purpureus var. atropurpureus, commonly known as the dark purple broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, multi-branched stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, with a pale green color and a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, typically appearing in shades of deep purple or violet.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and rocky areas.
