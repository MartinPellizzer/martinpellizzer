Carthamus carthamoides

Carthamus carthamoides, commonly known as the brown thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with purple centers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
