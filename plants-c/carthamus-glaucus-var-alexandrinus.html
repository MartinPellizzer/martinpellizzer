Carthamus glaucus var. alexandrinus

Carthamus glaucus var. alexandrinus, commonly known as Alexandrian safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, spiky seed heads that contain numerous seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and practical purposes.
