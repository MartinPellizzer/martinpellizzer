Cienfuegosia sulfurea var. integrifolia

Cienfuegosia sulfurea var. integrifolia, commonly known as the golden cienfuegosia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly found in parts of Cuba and the Isle of Youth.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, entire leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and arid environments.

Its unique coloration and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
