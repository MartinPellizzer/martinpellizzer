Cupressus arizonica var. glabra

Cupressus arizonica var. glabra, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a deciduous conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are often glabrous, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in desert regions.
