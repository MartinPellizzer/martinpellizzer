Crotalaria subtilis

Crotalaria subtilis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used as a food source in some regions.

Crotalaria subtilis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
