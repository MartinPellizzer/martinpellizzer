Casearia sylvestris var. myricoides

Casearia sylvestris var. myricoides, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. The plant has opposite, simple leaves with a rough texture and a faintly aromatic scent. It produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
