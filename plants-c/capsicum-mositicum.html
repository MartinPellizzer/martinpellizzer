Capsicum mositicum

Capsicum mositicum, commonly known as the wild pepper or mountain pepper, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and typically grows in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Capsicum mositicum is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
