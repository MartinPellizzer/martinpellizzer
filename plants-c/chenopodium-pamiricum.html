Chenopodium pamiricum

Chenopodium pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Chenopodium pamiricum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
