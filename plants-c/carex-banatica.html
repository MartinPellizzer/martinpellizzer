Carex banatica

Carex banatica, commonly known as the Banat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in wetland areas.

Carex banatica is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its aesthetic appeal in wetland gardens.
