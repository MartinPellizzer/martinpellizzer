Cardamine polemonioides

Cardamine polemonioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often used in salads, and it is valued for its peppery flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbage and mustard.
