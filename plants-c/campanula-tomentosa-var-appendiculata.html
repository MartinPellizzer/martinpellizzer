Campanula tomentosa var. appendiculata

Campanula tomentosa var. appendiculata, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety appendiculata is distinguished by its elongated, hairy bracts that give it a unique appearance.
