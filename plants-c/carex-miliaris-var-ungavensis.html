Carex miliaris var. ungavensis

Carex miliaris var. ungavensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like structures that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
