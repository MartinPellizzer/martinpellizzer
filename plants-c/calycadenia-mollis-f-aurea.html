Calycadenia mollis f. aurea

Calycadenia mollis f. aurea, commonly known as the golden foothills globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and foothills.
