Cyrtochilum weberbauerianum

Cyrtochilum weberbauerianum, commonly known as the Weberbauer's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, pouch-like flowers. The plant typically produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Cyrtochilum weberbauerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
