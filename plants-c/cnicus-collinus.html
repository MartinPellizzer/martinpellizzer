Cnicus collinus

Cnicus collinus, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Cnicus collinus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a common sight in disturbed environments.
