Carex melastoma

Carex melastoma, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering structures consist of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex melastoma plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
