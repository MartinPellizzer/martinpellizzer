Cytisus elongatus var. multiflorus

Cytisus elongatus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the long-stemmed broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that often grow in a sprawling or upright habit. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky environments.
