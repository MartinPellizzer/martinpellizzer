Chimonocalamus longiligulatus

Chimonocalamus longiligulatus, commonly known as the long-leaf arrow bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces panicles of small, lance-shaped flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
