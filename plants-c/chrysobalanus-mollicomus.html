Chrysobalanus mollicomus

Chrysobalanus mollicomus, commonly known as the silky chrysobalanus, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
