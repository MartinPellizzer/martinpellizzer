Chrysolaena campestris

Chrysolaena campestris, commonly known as the field chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
