Carex glauca var. tenuicula

Carex glauca var. tenuicula, commonly known as the slender blue sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
