Cyttaranthus congolensis

Cyttaranthus congolensis, commonly known as the Congo flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Cyttaranthus congolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
