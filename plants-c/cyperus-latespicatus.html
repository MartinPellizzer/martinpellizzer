Cyperus latespicatus

Cyperus latespicatus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. Its leaves are broad and flat, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Cyperus latespicatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
