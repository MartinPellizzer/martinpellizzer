Carex satakeana

Carex satakeana, commonly known as the Satake's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typically in shades of green or brown, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex satakeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
