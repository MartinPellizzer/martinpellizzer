Carex multifiliculmis

Carex multifiliculmis, commonly known as the many-branched sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological stability and habitat diversity.
