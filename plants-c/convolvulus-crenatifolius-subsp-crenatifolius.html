Convolvulus crenatifolius subsp. crenatifolius

Convolvulus crenatifolius subsp.

crenatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a climbing plant with heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and ability to cover trellises or fences.
