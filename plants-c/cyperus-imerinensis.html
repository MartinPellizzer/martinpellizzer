Cyperus imerinensis

Cyperus imerinensis, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyperus imerinensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
