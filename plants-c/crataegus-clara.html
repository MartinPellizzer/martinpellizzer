Crataegus clara

Crataegus clara, commonly known as the clearthorn crabapple, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 feet and features thorny branches, which are a characteristic feature of the genus Crataegus. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

Crataegus clara is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
