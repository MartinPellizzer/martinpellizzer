Carex lancifructus

Carex lancifructus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowering structures consist of small, three-lobed spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex lancifructus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
