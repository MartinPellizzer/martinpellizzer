Cucubalus scaber

Cucubalus scaber, commonly known as the rough gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are typically round or oval, with a thick, warty skin.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which can be used in various culinary applications.
