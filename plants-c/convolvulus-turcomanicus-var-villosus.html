Convolvulus turcomanicus var. villosus

Convolvulus turcomanicus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid regions.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain environments.
