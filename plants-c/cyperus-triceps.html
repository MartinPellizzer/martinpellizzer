Cyperus triceps

Cyperus triceps, commonly known as the tri-color sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus triceps produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.
