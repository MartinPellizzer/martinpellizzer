Cercocarpus betulifolius var. blancheae

Cercocarpus betulifolius var. blancheae, commonly known as Blanche's curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
