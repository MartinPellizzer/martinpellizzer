Cinnamomum penninervium

Cinnamomum penninervium, commonly known as cinnamon tree or Chinese cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a prominent midrib, giving it the species name "penninervium," meaning "feather-veined.".
