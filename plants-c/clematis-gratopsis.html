Clematis gratopsis

Clematis gratopsis, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis gratopsis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
