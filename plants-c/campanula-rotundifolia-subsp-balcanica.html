Campanula rotundifolia subsp. balcanica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
