Centaurea paniculata var. maculosa

Centaurea paniculata var. maculosa, commonly known as the spotted bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers with prominent white centers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

The variety 'maculosa' is distinguished by its spotted or mottled appearance on the flower heads.
