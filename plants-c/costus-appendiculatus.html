Costus appendiculatus

Costus appendiculatus, commonly known as the golden costus, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its most distinctive feature is the inflorescence, which consists of bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in a spike.

Costus appendiculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
