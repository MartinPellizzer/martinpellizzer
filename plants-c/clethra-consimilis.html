Clethra consimilis

Clethra consimilis, commonly known as the similar clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the plant family Clethraceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in late summer, which are clustered in elongated inflorescences. Clethra consimilis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance to various soil conditions.
