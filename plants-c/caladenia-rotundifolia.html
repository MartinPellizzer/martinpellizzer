Caladenia rotundifolia

Caladenia rotundifolia, commonly known as the Round-leafed Spider Orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with long, slender sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive, narrow spur that houses the reproductive structures.

Caladenia rotundifolia is a protected species due to habitat loss and is often found in conservation reserves.
