Citrus × vulgaris var. chinensis

Citrus × vulgaris var. chinensis, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a citrus fruit native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded fruits with a thin, colorful rind and segmented, juicy pulp. The plant is a hybrid of Citrus sinensis and Citrus reticulata, resulting in a variety that is highly valued for its sweet, less acidic flavor. It thrives in warm climates and is commonly cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, while the flowers are fragrant and white, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
