Centaurea deustiformis subsp. ptarmicifolia

Centaurea deustiformis subsp.

ptarmicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
