Cornus foemina subsp. microcarpa

Cornus foemina subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf cornel, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its small, white, bracteate flowers that appear in late spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in shaded areas.
