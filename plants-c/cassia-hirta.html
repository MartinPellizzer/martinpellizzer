Cassia hirta

Cassia hirta, commonly known as sickle pod or Indian cassia, is a shrub native to Africa, southern Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain small, flat seeds. Cassia hirta is often found in dry, disturbed areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
