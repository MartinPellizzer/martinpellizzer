Cassia tomentosa var. albida

Cassia tomentosa var. albida, commonly known as white silky cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets that contribute to its distinctive silvery-green foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
