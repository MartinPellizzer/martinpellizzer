Chosenia maximoviczii

Chosenia maximoviczii, commonly known as the giant reed or giant grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color and panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along rivers, showing adaptability to moist environments.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
