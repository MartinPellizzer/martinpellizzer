Corysanthes moluccana

Corysanthes moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, and it thrives in humid, shady environments.

Corysanthes moluccana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
