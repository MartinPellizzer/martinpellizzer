Capparis sodada

Capparis sodada, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains a single, large seed.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
