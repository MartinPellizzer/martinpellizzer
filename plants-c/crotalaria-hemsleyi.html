Crotalaria hemsleyi

Crotalaria hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria hemsleyi is also notable for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
