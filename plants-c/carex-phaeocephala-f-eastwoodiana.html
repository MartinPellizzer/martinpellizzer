Carex phaeocephala f. eastwoodiana

Carex phaeocephala f. eastwoodiana, commonly known as the Eastwood's black-headed sedge, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that turn a striking dark brown at the tips, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered brownish-green flowers in early summer, which are followed by maturing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories.

This sedges is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
