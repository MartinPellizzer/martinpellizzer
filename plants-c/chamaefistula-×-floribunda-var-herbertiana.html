Chamaefistula × floribunda var. herbertiana

Chamaefistula × floribunda var. herbertiana, commonly known as the Herbert's weeping willow, is a hybrid ornamental tree characterized by its cascading, willow-like foliage.

It features long, slender leaves that are typically bright green and emit a subtle fragrance. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
