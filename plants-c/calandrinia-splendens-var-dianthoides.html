Calandrinia splendens var. dianthoides

Calandrinia splendens var. dianthoides, commonly known as the showy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
