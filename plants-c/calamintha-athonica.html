Calamintha athonica

Calamintha athonica, commonly known as alpine germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its small, pink or white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Calamintha athonica is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
