Chrysophyllum monachinoanum

Chrysophyllum monachinoanum, commonly known as the sacred apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits resembling apples in appearance. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in humid climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
