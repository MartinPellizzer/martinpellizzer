Carex varia

Carex varia, commonly known as variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex varia produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
