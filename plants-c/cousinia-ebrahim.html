Cousinia ebrahim

Cousinia ebrahim, commonly known as the Ebrahim's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia ebrahim produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
