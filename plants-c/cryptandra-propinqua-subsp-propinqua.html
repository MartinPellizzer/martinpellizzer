Cryptandra propinqua subsp. propinqua

Cryptandra propinqua subsp.

propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptandra, is a small shrub native to southern Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is found in coastal and inland regions. It is adapted to dry conditions and can survive in nutrient-poor environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
