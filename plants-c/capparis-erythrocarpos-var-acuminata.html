Capparis erythrocarpos var. acuminata

Capparis erythrocarpos var. acuminata, commonly known as the red-fruited caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
