Centropogon glandulosus var. ovatus

Centropogon glandulosus var. ovatus, commonly known as the woolly centropogon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and flies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
