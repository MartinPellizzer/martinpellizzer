Crocus banaticus var. scepusiensis

Crocus banaticus var. scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian crocus, is a rare and ornamental species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or forested habitats. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
