Cinnamomum baitelloanum

Cinnamomum baitelloanum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Chinese camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has oval-shaped leaves with a fragrant, waxy texture. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
