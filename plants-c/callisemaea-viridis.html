Callisemaea viridis

Callisemaea viridis, commonly known as greenleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in a low, spreading habit. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.
