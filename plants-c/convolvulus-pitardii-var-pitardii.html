Convolvulus pitardii var. pitardii

Convolvulus pitardii var. pitardii, commonly known as the Pitard's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is often found in dry, sunny environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
