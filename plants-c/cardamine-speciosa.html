Cardamine speciosa

Cardamine speciosa, commonly known as the showy watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to moist, temperate regions, it thrives in shaded, wet environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has a short stem and produces a peppery, edible foliage similar to watercress.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
