Cinnamomum saxatile

Cinnamomum saxatile, commonly known as the rock cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens.
