Carex azuayae

Carex azuayae, commonly known as the Azuay sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Carex azuayae produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
