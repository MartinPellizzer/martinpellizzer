Cervispina villarsii

Cervispina villarsii, commonly known as the Spiny-headed Cactus, is a unique succulent native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged structure. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature that helps reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Cervispina villarsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
