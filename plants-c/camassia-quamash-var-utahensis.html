Camassia quamash var. utahensis

Camassia quamash var. utahensis, commonly known as Utah camas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and woodland areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy inflorescences.

Utah camas is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance to indigenous peoples.
