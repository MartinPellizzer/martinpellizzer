Chrysopsis mariana f. efulgens

Chrysopsis mariana f. efulgens, commonly known as the Hawaiian golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal habitats, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its ability to withstand salty sea air.

This endemic species is an important part of Hawaii's unique flora and is often found in coastal dunes and shorelines.
