Citrus × aurantium subf. ampullacea

Citrus × aurantium subf.

ampullacea, commonly known as the sour orange, is a citrus plant characterized by its thick, aromatic peel and yellow-orange fruit. It is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is typically bitter and not consumed raw but is used in marmalades and perfumes. The subsp.

ampullacea is notable for its high essential oil content, which contributes to its aromatic properties.
