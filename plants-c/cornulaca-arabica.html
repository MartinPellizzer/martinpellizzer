Cornulaca arabica

Cornulaca arabica, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically inconspicuous, followed by seeds enclosed in a spiny capsule. Its leaves are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their nutritional value.

Cornulaca arabica is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in desert landscapes.
