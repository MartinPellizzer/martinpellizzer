Cyrtandra × ferrea

Cyrtandra × ferrea, commonly known as the ironwood plant, is a hybrid species native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its robust, woody stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
