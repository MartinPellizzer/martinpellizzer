Carex viridula subsp. nevadensis

Carex viridula subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small animals.
