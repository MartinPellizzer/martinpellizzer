Carex caryophyllea f. pedunculata

Carex caryophyllea f. pedunculata, commonly known as the pink sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking pink to purple spikelets that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
