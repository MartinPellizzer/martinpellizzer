Capnoides tenella

Capnoides tenella, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, often with a distinctive purple calyx. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Capnoides tenella is widely cultivated as a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
