Croton rhamnifolius var. genuinus

Croton rhamnifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, narrow, and often glossy leaves that can vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
