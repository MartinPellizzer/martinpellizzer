Carex × inaensis

Carex × inaensis, commonly known as the Inaensis sedge, is a hybrid species within the Carex genus, known for its ornamental value in wetland and moist garden settings.

It features dense, grass-like foliage that typically ranges from green to blue-gray in color, with slender, cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for water gardens and bog landscapes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming attractive clumps over time.

Carex × inaensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
