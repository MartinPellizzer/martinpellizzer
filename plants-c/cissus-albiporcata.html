Cissus albiporcata

Cissus albiporcata, commonly known as the white-pelted devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
