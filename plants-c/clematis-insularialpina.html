Clematis insularialpina

Clematis insularialpina, commonly known as the island alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant features compound leaves with palmate lobes and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
