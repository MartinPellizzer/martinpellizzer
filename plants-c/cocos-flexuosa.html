Cocos flexuosa

Cocos flexuosa, commonly known as the flexuous coconut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes other palms. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. It produces coconuts that are typically smaller and more elongated compared to those of the common coconut.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
