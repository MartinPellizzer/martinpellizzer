Clematis davurica

Clematis davurica, commonly known as the Siberian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 15 feet long, making it suitable for trellises or fences.

Clematis davurica is relatively hardy and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
