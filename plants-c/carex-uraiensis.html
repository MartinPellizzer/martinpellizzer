Carex uraiensis

Carex uraiensis, commonly known as the Uraeiensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy or swampy areas. Its flowering spikes are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex uraiensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
