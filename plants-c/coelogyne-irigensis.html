Coelogyne irigensis

Coelogyne irigensis, commonly known as the Iriomote orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Iriomote Island of Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it produces multiple inflorescences during the flowering season.

Coelogyne irigensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
