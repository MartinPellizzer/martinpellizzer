Carduus pycnocephalus var. acicularis

Carduus pycnocephalus var. acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and a tall, branching stem that bears purple to pink flower heads. The leaves are narrow and sharply pointed, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
