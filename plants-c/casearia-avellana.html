Casearia avellana

Casearia avellana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
