Celsia carmanica

Celsia carmanica, commonly known as the Carmel celsia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its attractive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate with a smooth texture, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Celsia carmanica thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
