Ceradenia warmingii

Ceradenia warmingii, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and is often found growing in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
