Citrofortunella microcarpa

Citrofortunella microcarpa, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen citrus tree native to China.

It produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible both when ripe and when unripe, with the latter being used in candied form. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The flowers are fragrant and white, typically appearing in clusters during the spring.
