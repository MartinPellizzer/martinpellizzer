Centranthus longiflorus var. latifolius

Centranthus longiflorus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf red valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
