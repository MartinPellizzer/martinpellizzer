Campulosus aromaticus

Campulosus aromaticus, commonly known as the fragrant mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its distinctive scent and therapeutic properties.
