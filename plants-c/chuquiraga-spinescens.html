Chuquiraga spinescens

Chuquiraga spinescens, commonly known as the Andean burr bush, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical fruit capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
