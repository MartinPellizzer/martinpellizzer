Cuphea nitidula var. donkelaarii

Cuphea nitidula var. donkelaarii, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a compact, upright shrub native to South America.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are highly attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery wax, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its striking foliage and long blooming period.
