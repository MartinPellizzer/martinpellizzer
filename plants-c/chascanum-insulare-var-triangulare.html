Chascanum insulare var. triangulare

Chascanum insulare var. triangulare, commonly known as the triangular chascanum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
