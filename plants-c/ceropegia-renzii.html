Ceropegia renzii

Ceropegia renzii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a waxy, silver sheen, which helps reduce water loss.

Ceropegia renzii is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
