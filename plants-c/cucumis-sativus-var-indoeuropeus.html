Cucumis sativus var. indoeuropeus

Cucumis sativus var. indoeuropeus, commonly known as the European cucumber, is a cultivated variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that are typically harvested when immature and tender. The plant is a herbaceous vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and it relies on pollination for fruit development.

This variety is widely grown for its crisp, refreshing flavor and is commonly used in salads and pickling.
