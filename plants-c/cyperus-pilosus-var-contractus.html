Cyperus pilosus var. contractus

Cyperus pilosus var. contractus, commonly known as the woolly flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
