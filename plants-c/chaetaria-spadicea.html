Chaetaria spadicea

Chaetaria spadicea, commonly known as the yellow chaetaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
