Crataegus cuneata f. tangchungchangii

Crataegus cuneata f. tangchungchangii, commonly known as the Tangchungchang hawthorn, is a unique hawthorn species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often twisted leaves, which are a key identifying feature. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is typically found in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural collections.
