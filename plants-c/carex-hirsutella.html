Carex hirsutella

Carex hirsutella, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex hirsutella plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
