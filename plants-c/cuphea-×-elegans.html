Cuphea × elegans

Cuphea × elegans, commonly known as the flame bean or whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy green leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant colors and unique flower structure.

Cuphea × elegans is relatively low maintenance and prefers well-drained soil.
