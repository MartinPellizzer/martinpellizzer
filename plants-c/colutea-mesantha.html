Colutea mesantha

Colutea mesantha, commonly known as the lesser broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a slender, branched stem. It is often found growing on the roots of grasses and other plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can be a nuisance in agricultural settings.
