Crotalaria parva

Crotalaria parva, commonly known as small cowpea or small bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pairs of elliptical leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
