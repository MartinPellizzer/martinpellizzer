Callaeum septentrionale

Callaeum septentrionale, commonly known as the northern callaeum, is a rare flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Callaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy coating.

The species is noted for its limited distribution and is considered a conservation concern due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
