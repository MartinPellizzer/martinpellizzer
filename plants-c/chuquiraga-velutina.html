Chuquiraga velutina

Chuquiraga velutina, commonly known as the velvet chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-green, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Chuquiraga velutina thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
