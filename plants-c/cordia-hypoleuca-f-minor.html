Cordia hypoleuca f. minor

Cordia hypoleuca f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf white cordia, is a compact shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
