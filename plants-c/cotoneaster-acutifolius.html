Cotoneaster acutifolius

Cotoneaster acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a pointed tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Cotoneaster acutifolius is also valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
