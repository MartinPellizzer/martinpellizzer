Callicarpa minutiflora

Callicarpa minutiflora, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that transition from white to pink as they mature. The plant produces vibrant purple berries that are edible for birds but generally considered toxic to humans. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its seasonal color changes and unique fruit make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
