Cylindropuntia californica subsp. californica

Cylindropuntia californica subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to desert and semi-desert habitats.
