Cheilotheca crocea

Cheilotheca crocea, commonly known as the red-flowered cheilotheca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink to red flowers during the spring and early summer. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Cheilotheca crocea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
