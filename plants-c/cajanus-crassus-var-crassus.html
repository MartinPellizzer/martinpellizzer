Cajanus crassus var. crassus

Cajanus crassus var. crassus, commonly known as the rough sida, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing small, round seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
