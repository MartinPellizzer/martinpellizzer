Chaetanthera ramosissima

Chaetanthera ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, glandular trichomes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, blooming in the spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.
