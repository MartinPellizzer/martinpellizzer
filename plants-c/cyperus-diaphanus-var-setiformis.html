Cyperus diaphanus var. setiformis

Cyperus diaphanus var. setiformis, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of water quality.
