Crateva laeta

Crateva laeta, commonly known as the Indian buttercup, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Crateva laeta is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
