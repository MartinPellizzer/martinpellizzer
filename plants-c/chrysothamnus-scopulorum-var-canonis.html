Chrysothamnus scopulorum var. canonis

Chrysothamnus scopulorum var. canonis, commonly known as the Canon Canyon goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-lying mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
