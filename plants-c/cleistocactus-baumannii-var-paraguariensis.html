Cleistocactus baumannii var. paraguariensis

Cleistocactus baumannii var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguay apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
