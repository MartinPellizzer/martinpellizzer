Clematis aristata subsp. normalis

Clematis aristata subsp.

normalis, commonly known as the western clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue to purple, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often climbing along fences or trees.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
