Ceropegia steudneri

Ceropegia steudneri, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, which are pollinated by specific insect species. Ceropegia steudneri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
