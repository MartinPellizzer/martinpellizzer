Chelonespermum majus

Chelonespermum majus, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
