Crepis litardierei

Crepis litardierei, commonly known as the Litardiere's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
