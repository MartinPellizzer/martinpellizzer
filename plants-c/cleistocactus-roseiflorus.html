Cleistocactus roseiflorus

Cleistocactus roseiflorus, commonly known as the pink-flowered barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is covered in spines and features a distinctive pink or rose-colored flower that blooms during the rainy season. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening only briefly before closing, which is a unique feature of its reproductive strategy.

Cleistocactus roseiflorus is well adapted to desert conditions, storing water in its thick stem and minimizing water loss through its waxy surface.
