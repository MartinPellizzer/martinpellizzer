Crassula decumbens

Crassula decumbens, commonly known as the "Lucky Jade" or "Panda Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge on the edges. The plant grows in a low, spreading habit, often forming a rosette that resembles a panda's face. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months.

Crassula decumbens is drought-tolerant and thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.
