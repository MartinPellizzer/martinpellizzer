Chuquiraga insignis var. lancifolia

Chuquiraga insignis var. lancifolia, commonly known as the Andean thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, giving it the specific epithet *lancifolia*.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
