Croton ambovombensis var. lepidotus

Croton ambovombensis var. lepidotus, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a striking shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, yellow, and red hues, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
