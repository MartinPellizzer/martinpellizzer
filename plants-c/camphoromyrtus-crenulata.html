Camphoromyrtus crenulata

Camphoromyrtus crenulata, commonly known as the New Zealand native tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant has a rounded, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge, and it produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers.

Camphoromyrtus crenulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
