Convolvulus soldanella

Convolvulus soldanella, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, funnel-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in the morning, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
