Crotalaria anthyllopsis

Crotalaria anthyllopsis, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble a crown. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
