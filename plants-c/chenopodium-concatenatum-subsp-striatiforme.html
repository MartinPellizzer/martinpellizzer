Chenopodium concatenatum subsp. striatiforme

Chenopodium concatenatum subsp.

striatiforme, commonly known as the striped goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a rough, sometimes hairy texture. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
