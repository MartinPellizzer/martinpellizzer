Crotalaria fulva

Crotalaria fulva, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be a pioneer species in ecological succession.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its role in supporting biodiversity.
