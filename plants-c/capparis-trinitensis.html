Capparis trinitensis

Capparis trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Capparis trinitensis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
