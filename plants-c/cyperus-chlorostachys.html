Cyperus chlorostachys

Cyperus chlorostachys, commonly known as the blue rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Cyperus chlorostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a decorative plant in water gardens.
