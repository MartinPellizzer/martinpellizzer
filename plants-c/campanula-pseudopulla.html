Campanula pseudopulla

Campanula pseudopulla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate.
