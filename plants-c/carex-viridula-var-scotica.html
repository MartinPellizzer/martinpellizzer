Carex viridula var. scotica

Carex viridula var. scotica, commonly known as the Scottish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and somewhat glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
