Chrysopsis columbiana

Chrysopsis columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. Chrysopsis columbiana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
