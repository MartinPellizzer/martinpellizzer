Calophyllum brasiliense var. brasiliense

Calophyllum brasiliense var. brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
