Clusia oblanceolata

Clusia oblanceolata, commonly known as the fatshedder or broadleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. Clusia oblanceolata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
