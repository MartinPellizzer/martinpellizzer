Campanula carpatica f. dasycarpa

Campanula carpatica f. dasycarpa, commonly known as the Wooly Bellflower, is a compact, flowering perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, and its bell-shaped flowers are typically blue to purple in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a favored addition to many garden landscapes.
