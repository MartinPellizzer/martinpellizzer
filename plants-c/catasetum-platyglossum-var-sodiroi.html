Catasetum platyglossum var. sodiroi

Catasetum platyglossum var. sodiroi, commonly known as the Sodiro's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by bees, which are attracted by the flower's structure and scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
