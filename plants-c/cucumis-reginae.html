Cucumis reginae

Cucumis reginae, commonly known as the queen cucumber, is a rare and ancient species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, elongated fruits with a pale green skin and a mild, sweet flavor. The plant thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are yellow and are typically pollinated by insects, leading to the development of its unique fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and historical significance, it is considered a valuable subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
