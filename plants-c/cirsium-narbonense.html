Cirsium narbonense

Cirsium narbonense, commonly known as the Narbonne thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly southern France and parts of Italy. The plant is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

Cirsium narbonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
