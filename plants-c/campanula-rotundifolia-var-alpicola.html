Campanula rotundifolia var. alpicola

Campanula rotundifolia var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of rounded, silver-gray leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
