Clusia cooperi

Clusia cooperi, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Clusia cooperi is well adapted to humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its low maintenance requirements.
