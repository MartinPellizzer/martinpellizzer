Calytrix divergens

Calytrix divergens, commonly known as the spreading calytrix, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Calytrix divergens is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
