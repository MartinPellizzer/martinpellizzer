Carex vallis-rosetto f. ramosa

Carex vallis-rosetto f. ramosa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil.

The leaves are usually narrow and sword-shaped, with a distinct midrib and a waxy coating that aids in water retention.
