Coprosma discolor

Coprosma discolor, commonly known as the variable coprosma, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a waxy, dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes reddish underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is often found in coastal and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
