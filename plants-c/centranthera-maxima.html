Centranthera maxima

Centranthera maxima, commonly known as the giant centranthera, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Centranthera maxima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
