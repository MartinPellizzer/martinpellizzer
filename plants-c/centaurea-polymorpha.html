Centaurea polymorpha

Centaurea polymorpha, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom from summer to autumn. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Centaurea polymorpha is known for its adaptability and has been introduced to various regions as an ornamental plant.
