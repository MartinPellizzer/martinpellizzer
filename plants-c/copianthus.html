Copianthus

Copianthus, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Copianthus species are valued for their ornamental value and are often cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
