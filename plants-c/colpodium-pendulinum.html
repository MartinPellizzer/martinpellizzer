Colpodium pendulinum

Colpodium pendulinum, commonly known as the hanging bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems that often hang from the vegetation in water bodies. The plant produces small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient absorption. It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and ditches.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
