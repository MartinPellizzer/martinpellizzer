Carex hassiana

Carex hassiana, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle.

Carex hassiana plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
