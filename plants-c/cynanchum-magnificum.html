Cynanchum magnificum

Cynanchum magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including humans. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Cynanchum magnificum is often found in meadows and forest edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
