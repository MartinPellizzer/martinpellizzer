Carex distans f. virescens

Carex distans f. virescens, commonly known as the green-tongued sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is predominantly green, with a distinctive bluish-green hue on the upper surface, giving it its common name.

It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.
