Calymmodon pendens

Calymmodon pendens, commonly known as the hanging calymmodon, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous, delicate flowers that hang in clusters, creating an ornamental display. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a decorative plant and its ecological interactions within its native habitat.
