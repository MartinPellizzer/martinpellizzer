Ceropegia arcuata subsp. mirkinii

Ceropegia arcuata subsp.

mirkinii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often visited by specific pollinators, such as moths. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The subspecies mirkinii is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the species.
