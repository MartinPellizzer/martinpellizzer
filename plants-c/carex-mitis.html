Carex mitis

Carex mitis, commonly known as softleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces wind-pollinated seeds.

Carex mitis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
