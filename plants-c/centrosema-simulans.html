Centrosema simulans

Centrosema simulans, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used as a forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
