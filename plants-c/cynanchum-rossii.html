Cynanchum rossii

Cynanchum rossii, commonly known as Chinese milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive seed pods containing silky-haired seeds. It is often found in moist or wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Cynanchum rossii is also known for its latex-containing sap, which can be toxic to animals and humans.
