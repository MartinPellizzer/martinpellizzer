Callitris robusta var. microcarpa

Callitris robusta var. microcarpa, commonly known as the coastal or sand pine, is a member of the Cupressaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely arranged along the branches, giving it a dense appearance. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to harsh coastal environments and is often used in dune stabilization due to its hardy nature.
