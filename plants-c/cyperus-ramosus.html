Cyperus ramosus

Cyperus ramosus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus ramosus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
