Cestrum glaucescens

Cestrum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
