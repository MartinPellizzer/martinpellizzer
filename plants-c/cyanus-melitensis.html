Cyanus melitensis

Cyanus melitensis, commonly known as the Maltese bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
