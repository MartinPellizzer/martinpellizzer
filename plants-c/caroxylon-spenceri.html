Caroxylon spenceri

Caroxylon spenceri, commonly known as the Spencer's carob tree, is a fossilized plant that provides insight into ancient ecosystems.

It belongs to the legume family and is characterized by its woody structure and pod-like fruits. The plant is primarily found in sedimentary rock formations from the Cretaceous period. Its botanical features include compound leaves and seeds encased in thick, leathery pods.

This species is significant for understanding the evolution of plant life during the Mesozoic era.
