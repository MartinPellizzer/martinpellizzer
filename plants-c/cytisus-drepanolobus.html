Cytisus drepanolobus

Cytisus drepanolobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a woody stem with thorn-like branches.

Cytisus drepanolobus is adapted to dry and sunny environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
