Cistus populifolius f. marianus

Cistus populifolius f. marianus, commonly known as the Marianus rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
