Celtis riparia

Celtis riparia, commonly known as the river elm, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. Celtis riparia thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

It is a hardy species that can tolerate various environmental conditions.
