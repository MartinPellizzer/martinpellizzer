Colchicum montanum var. pleiophyllum

Colchicum montanum var. pleiophyllum, commonly known as the mountain colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with reflexed petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features a short, stout rhizome and possesses lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine down. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
