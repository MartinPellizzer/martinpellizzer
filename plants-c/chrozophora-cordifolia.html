Chrozophora cordifolia

Chrozophora cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
