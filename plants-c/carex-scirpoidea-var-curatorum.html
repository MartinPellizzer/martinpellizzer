Carex scirpoidea var. curatorum

Carex scirpoidea var. curatorum, commonly known as the Curator's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are pollinated by wind.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
