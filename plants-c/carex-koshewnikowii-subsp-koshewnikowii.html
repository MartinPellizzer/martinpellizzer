Carex koshewnikowii subsp. koshewnikowii

Carex koshewnikowii subsp.

koshewnikowii, commonly known as the Koshewnikow's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
