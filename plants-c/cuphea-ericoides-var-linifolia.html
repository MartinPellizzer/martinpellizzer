Cuphea ericoides var. linifolia

Cuphea ericoides var. linifolia, commonly known as the silky cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
