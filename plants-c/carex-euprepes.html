Carex euprepes

Carex euprepes, commonly known as the white sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex euprepes plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
