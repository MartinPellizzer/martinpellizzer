Clerodendrum indeniense

Clerodendrum indeniense, commonly known as the purple flame or Indian flame bush, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and serrated edges. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
