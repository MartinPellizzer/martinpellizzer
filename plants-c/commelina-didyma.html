Commelina didyma

Commelina didyma, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often surrounded by three white or pale green bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows from 30 to 120 centimeters tall, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and forest floors.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
