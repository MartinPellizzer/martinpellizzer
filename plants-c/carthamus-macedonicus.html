Carthamus macedonicus

Carthamus macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and cone-shaped disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

The plant also plays a role in pollination and supports local biodiversity.
