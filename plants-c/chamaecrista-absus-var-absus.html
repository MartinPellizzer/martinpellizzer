Chamaecrista absus var. absus

Chamaecrista absus var. absus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
