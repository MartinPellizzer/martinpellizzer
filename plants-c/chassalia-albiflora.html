Chassalia albiflora

Chassalia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered chassalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
