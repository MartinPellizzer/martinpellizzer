Cockaynea × elymoides

Cockaynea × elymoides, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often blue-green-colored leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
