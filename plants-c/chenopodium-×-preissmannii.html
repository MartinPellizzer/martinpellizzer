Chenopodium × preissmannii

Chenopodium × preissmannii, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is native to parts of Central Asia and has adapted to thrive in harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
