Coronidium fulvidum

Coronidium fulvidum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
