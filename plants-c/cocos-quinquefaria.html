Cocos quinquefaria

Cocos quinquefaria, commonly known as the five-finger date palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The five-finger date palm is also valued for its ability to withstand coastal conditions.
