Cinnamodendron brasiliense

Cinnamodendron brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazil cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other plants like cashew and sumac. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits.
