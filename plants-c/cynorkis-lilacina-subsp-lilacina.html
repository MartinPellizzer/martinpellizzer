Cynorkis lilacina subsp. lilacina

Cynorkis lilacina subsp.

lilacina, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This subspecies is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
