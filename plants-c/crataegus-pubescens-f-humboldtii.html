Crataegus pubescens f. humboldtii

Crataegus pubescens f. humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable regions.
