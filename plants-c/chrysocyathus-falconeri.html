Chrysocyathus falconeri

Chrysocyathus falconeri, commonly known as the Falconer's sea fan, is a species of gorgonian coral found in the tropical Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its branching, fan-like structure composed of calcium carbonate, which provides a habitat for various marine organisms. The coral exhibits a pale yellow to orange coloration, often with intricate patterns that enhance its visual appeal. It thrives in shallow, clear waters with moderate to strong currents.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic value, it is often cultivated in marine aquariums.
