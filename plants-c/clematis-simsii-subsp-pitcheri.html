Clematis simsii subsp. pitcheri

Clematis simsii subsp.

pitcheri, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
