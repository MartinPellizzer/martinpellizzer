Curcuma arida

Curcuma arida, commonly known as the desert turmeric, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellowish to orange flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its adaptability to arid environments and its unique chemical composition.
