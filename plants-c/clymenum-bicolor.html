Clymenum bicolor

Clymenum bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a contrasting central stripe. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Clymenum bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
