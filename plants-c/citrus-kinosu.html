Citrus kinosu

Citrus kinosu, commonly known as the Japanese lemon or kinkan, is a small evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It produces small, round fruits with a thick, textured rind and a tart, aromatic pulp. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to its high vitamin C content.
