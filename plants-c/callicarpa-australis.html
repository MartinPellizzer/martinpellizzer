Callicarpa australis

Callicarpa australis, commonly known as the Australian beautyberry, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Callicarpa australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
