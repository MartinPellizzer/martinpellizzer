Chlorocyperus phymatodes

Chlorocyperus phymatodes, commonly known as the broadleaf umbrella sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often surrounded by leaf-like bracts. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure is adapted to waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
