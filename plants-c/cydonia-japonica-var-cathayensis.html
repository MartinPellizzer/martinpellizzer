Cydonia japonica var. cathayensis

Cydonia japonica var. cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and pinnately compound leaves. The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters before the fruit develops.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which has a sweet, aromatic flavor when ripe.
