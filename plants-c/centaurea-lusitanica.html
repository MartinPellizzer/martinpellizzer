Centaurea lusitanica

Centaurea lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually sparsely branched.

Centaurea lusitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
