Cassia seleri

Cassia seleri, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia seleri is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
