Carex sylvatica var. polyarrhenostachya

Carex sylvatica var. polyarrhenostachya, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, narrow leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in garden settings for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
