Carduus kerneri subsp. kerneri

Carduus kerneri subsp.

kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branched stem that bears purple to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
