Crepis amplexifolia

Crepis amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant is recognized for its basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Crepis amplexifolia produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
