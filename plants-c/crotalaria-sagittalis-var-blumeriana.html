Crotalaria sagittalis var. blumeriana

Crotalaria sagittalis var. blumeriana, commonly known as the Blumer's spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which are typically bright yellow with orange markings. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
