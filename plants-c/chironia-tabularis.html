Chironia tabularis

Chironia tabularis, commonly known as the flatleaf chironia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils. Its leaves are broad and flat, which is an adaptation to its aquatic environment.

Chironia tabularis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
