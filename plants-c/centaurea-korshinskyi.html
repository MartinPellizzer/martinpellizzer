Centaurea korshinskyi

Centaurea korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its striking blue-violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea korshinskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
