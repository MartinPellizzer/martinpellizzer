Calytrix wickllamiana

Calytrix wickllamiana, commonly known as the Wickham's bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.
