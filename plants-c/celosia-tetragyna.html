Celosia tetragyna

Celosia tetragyna, commonly known as the spikesalad, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is herbaceous, with upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Celosia tetragyna is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
