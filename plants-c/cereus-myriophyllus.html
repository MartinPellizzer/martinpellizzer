Cereus myriophyllus

Cereus myriophyllus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce large, fragrant flowers at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and open only during the evening.

The plant is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades.
