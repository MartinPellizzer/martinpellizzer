Cymopterus nivalis

Cymopterus nivalis, commonly known as the snow larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its beauty and ecological importance, it is a notable species in high-elevation ecosystems.
