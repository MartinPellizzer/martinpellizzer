Capparis jamaicensis var. siliquosa

Capparis jamaicensis var. siliquosa, commonly known as the Jamaican caper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in coastal ecosystems and traditional medicine.
