Canthium mundianum

Canthium mundianum, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Canthium mundianum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
