Cleretum clavatum

Cleretum clavatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cleretum clavatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
