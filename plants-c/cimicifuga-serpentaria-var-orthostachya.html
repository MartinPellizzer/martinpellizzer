Cimicifuga serpentaria var. orthostachya

Cimicifuga serpentaria var. orthostachya, commonly known as black cohosh, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of white to greenish-white flowers. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves with toothed margins. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
