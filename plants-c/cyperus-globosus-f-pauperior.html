Cyperus globosus f. pauperior

Cyperus globosus f. pauperior, commonly known as the poorflower sedge, is a variant of the globosus form of Cyperus globosus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along waterways. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
