Calamus cristatus

Calamus cristatus, commonly known as the crown reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive, feather-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense stands.

Calamus cristatus is also valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
