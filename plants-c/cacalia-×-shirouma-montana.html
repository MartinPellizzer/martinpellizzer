Cacalia × shirouma-montana

Cacalia × shirouma-montana, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
