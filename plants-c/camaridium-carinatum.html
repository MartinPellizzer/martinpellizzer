Camaridium carinatum

Camaridium carinatum, commonly known as the spiny leaf orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its botanical significance lies in its specialized adaptations to its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
