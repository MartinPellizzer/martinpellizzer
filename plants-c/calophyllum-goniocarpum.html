Calophyllum goniocarpum

Calophyllum goniocarpum, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Its bark and wood are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
