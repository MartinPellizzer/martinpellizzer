Chiococca alba var. parvifolia

Chiococca alba var. parvifolia, commonly known as the white cacao tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small evergreen tree with smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are alternate and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its genetic relationship to cacao, making it a subject of study for cocoa cultivation and biodiversity conservation.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the rich, nutrient-dense soils of the rainforest.
