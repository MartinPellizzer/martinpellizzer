Cirsium × sennholzii

Cirsium × sennholzii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × sennholzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
