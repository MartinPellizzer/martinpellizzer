Carex aequata

Carex aequata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex aequata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
