Citrus × papillaris var. chrysocarpa

Citrus × papillaris var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden kumquat, is a unique hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits.

The fruit's outer skin is typically a vibrant yellow-orange, while the pulp is sweet and aromatic, often used in culinary applications. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental and edible plant in subtropical regions.
