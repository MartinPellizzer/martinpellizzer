Coffea merguensis var. orientalis

Coffea merguensis var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental coffee.

This variant is native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Africa, particularly Ethiopia and Eritrea. It is a shrub or small tree that produces small, red, fleshy fruits containing seeds used for coffee. The plant thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires a humid environment.

It is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often cultivated for specialty coffee production.
