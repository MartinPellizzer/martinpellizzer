Cyperus cyperoides subsp. pseudoflavus

Cyperus cyperoides subsp.

pseudoflavus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
