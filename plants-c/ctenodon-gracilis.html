Ctenodon gracilis

Ctenodon gracilis, commonly known as the slender thread-leaf plant, is a small, herbaceous species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
