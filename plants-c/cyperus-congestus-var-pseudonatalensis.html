Cyperus congestus var. pseudonatalensis

Cyperus congestus var. pseudonatalensis, commonly known as the Natal flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
