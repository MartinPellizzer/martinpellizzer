Crotalaria breviflora

Crotalaria breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent veining. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often grown as a forage crop and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
