Caesia calliantha

Caesia calliantha, commonly known as the fragrant caesia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Caesia calliantha is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
