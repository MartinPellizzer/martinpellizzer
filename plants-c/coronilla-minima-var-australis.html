Coronilla minima var. australis

Coronilla minima var. australis, commonly known as the Australian dwarf sainfoin, is a low-growing perennial legume native to southern Australia.

It features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
