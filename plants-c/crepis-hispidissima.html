Crepis hispidissima

Crepis hispidissima, commonly known as the hispid mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by achene fruits.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, rough appearance.
