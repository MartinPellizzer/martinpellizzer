Centaurea transcaucasica subsp. alexandri

Centaurea transcaucasica subsp.

alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian knapweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to pinkish-red flower heads.

The species features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bracteate involucres that enclose the composite flowers. Native to the Transcaucasus region, it thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils.

This subspecies is often found in steppe and forest-steppe habitats, showcasing its adaptability to temperate climates.
