Conophytum pellucidum subsp. saueri

Conophytum pellucidum subsp.

saueri, commonly known as the "Saucer Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa. It features a compact, rosette-shaped body with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The leaves are typically pale green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months.

This subspecies is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
