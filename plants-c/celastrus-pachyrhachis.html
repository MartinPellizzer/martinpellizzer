Celastrus pachyrhachis

Celastrus pachyrhachis, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features a woody stem with a thick, fleshy root system that helps it anchor in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to orange or red berries containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
