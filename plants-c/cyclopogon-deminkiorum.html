Cyclopogon deminkiorum

Cyclopogon deminkiorum, commonly known as the Demink's cyclopogon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
