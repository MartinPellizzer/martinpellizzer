Cubeba costulata

Cubeba costulata, commonly known as cubeb or java pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple, elongated berries. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Cubeb is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and as a spice.
