Colocasia peregrina

Colocasia peregrina, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking purple or white spathes that enclose its small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

Colocasia peregrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
