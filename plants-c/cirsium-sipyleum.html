Cirsium sipyleum

Cirsium sipyleum, commonly known as the Sipylean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its spiny leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads make it a distinctive member of the thistle genus.

Cirsium sipyleum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
