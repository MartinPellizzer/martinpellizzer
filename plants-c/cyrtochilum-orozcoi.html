Cyrtochilum orozcoi

Cyrtochilum orozcoi, commonly known as the Orozco's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high Andes of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and a single, fragrant flower with a distinctive pouch-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky, humid environments at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns on the lip.

Cyrtochilum orozcoi is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanical conservation efforts.
