Cirsium praeteriens

Cirsium praeteriens, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in fields, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Cirsium praeteriens is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structure.
