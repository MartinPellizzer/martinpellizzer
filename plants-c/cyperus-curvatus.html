Cyperus curvatus

Cyperus curvatus, commonly known as the curly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like stems are often curved, giving it its common name.

Cyperus curvatus is characterized by its small, triangular leaves and produces spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers.
