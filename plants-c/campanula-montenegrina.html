Campanula montenegrina

Campanula montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula montenegrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
