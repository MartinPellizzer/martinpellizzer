Commelina sulcata

Commelina sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
