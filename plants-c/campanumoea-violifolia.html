Campanumoea violifolia

Campanumoea violifolia, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically violet or blue, with five petals that curve backward, creating a distinctive bell-like shape.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
