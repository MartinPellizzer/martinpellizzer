Clinopodium bulgaricum

Clinopodium bulgaricum, commonly known as Bulgarian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant has square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small purple or pink flowers bloom in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their aromatic and slightly minty flavor.
