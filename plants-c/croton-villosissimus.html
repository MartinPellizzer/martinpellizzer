Croton villosissimus

Croton villosissimus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
