Callicarpa subintegra

Callicarpa subintegra, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Callicarpa subintegra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and autumn foliage.
