Carex chilosacca

Carex chilosacca, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a valuable plant for wetland restoration.

Carex chilosacca is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens with damp conditions.
