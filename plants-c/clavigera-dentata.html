Clavigera dentata

Clavigera dentata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers with distinctive toothed edges. The leaves are lanceolate, with serrated margins that contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
