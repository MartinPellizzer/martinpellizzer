Chaerophyllum nodosum

Chaerophyllum nodosum, commonly known as nodding parsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system and is known for its aromatic properties.

It is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
