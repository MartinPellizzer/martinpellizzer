Cissus dahomensis

Cissus dahomensis, commonly known as the African Stonecrop, is a climbing vine native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, coral-red tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
