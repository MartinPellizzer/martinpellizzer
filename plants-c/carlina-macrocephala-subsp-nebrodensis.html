Carlina macrocephala subsp. nebrodensis

Carlina macrocephala subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodensis Carlina, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more robust flower heads compared to other Carlina species.
