Capparis macleishii

Capparis macleishii, commonly known as the MacLeish's caper, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various caper species known for their edible flowers and fruits. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, small green leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Capparis macleishii is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
