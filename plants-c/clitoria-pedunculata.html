Clitoria pedunculata

Clitoria pedunculata, commonly known as the winged bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three oval leaflets and distinctive butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically purple or pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

The roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
