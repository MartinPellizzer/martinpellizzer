Camellia pyxidiacea var. rubituberculata

Camellia pyxidiacea var. rubituberculata, commonly known as the red tubercled camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a unique tuberculate texture on the petals. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
