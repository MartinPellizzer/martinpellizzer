Carex × limula

Carex × limula, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and often forms dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces triangular, three-sided berries.

Carex × limula is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
