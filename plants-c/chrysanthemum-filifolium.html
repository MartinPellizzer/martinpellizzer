Chrysanthemum filifolium

Chrysanthemum filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both landscape use and cut flower arrangements.
