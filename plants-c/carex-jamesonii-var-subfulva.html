Carex jamesonii var. subfulva

Carex jamesonii var. subfulva, commonly known as the golden bulrush, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a yellowish or golden hue, especially at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
