Cassia myrtifolia

Cassia myrtifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth texture. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
