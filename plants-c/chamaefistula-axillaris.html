Chamaefistula axillaris

Chamaefistula axillaris, commonly known as the axillary frangipani, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, ovate leaves that are opposite or clustered at the leaf axils, giving it its common name. Its showy, fragrant flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and blooming during the dry season.

The bark is smooth and grayish, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
