Chamaefilix platyneuros var. serrata

Chamaefilix platyneuros var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically serrated, giving the species its scientific name, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This hardy plant is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological resilience.
