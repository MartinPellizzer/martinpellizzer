Campanula macrorhiza var. pubescens

Campanula macrorhiza var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall textured look.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
