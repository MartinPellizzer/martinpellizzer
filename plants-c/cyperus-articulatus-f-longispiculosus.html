Cyperus articulatus f. longispiculosus

Cyperus articulatus f. longispiculosus, commonly known as the long-bristled flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus characterized by its distinctive morphological features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has slender, erect stems that often root at the nodes, and its leaves are linear with a smooth surface. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are elongated and covered with long, bristly hairs, giving it its common name.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
