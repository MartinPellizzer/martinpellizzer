Colchicum paschei

Colchicum paschei, commonly known as Pasche's colchicum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, showy blooms in shades of pink, white, and red, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. It has a short, rhizomatous root system and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.

Colchicum paschei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
