Campanula javanica

Campanula javanica, commonly known as the Javan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Campanula javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
