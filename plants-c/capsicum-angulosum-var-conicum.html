Capsicum angulosum var. conicum

Capsicum angulosum var. conicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chili or the narrow-leafed pepper, is a unique variant of the capsicum genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, conical-shaped fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, sunny climates.

This variety is valued for its distinct flavor and is often used in traditional cuisines.
