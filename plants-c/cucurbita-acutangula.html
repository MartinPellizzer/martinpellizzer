Cucurbita acutangula

Cucurbita acutangula, commonly known as the prickly pear or desert fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and broad, paddle-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the fleshy, green fruit that develops from the flower, which can be harvested when mature.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has a texture similar to a pear, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor.
