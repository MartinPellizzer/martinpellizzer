Crataegus stephanostyla

Crataegus stephanostyla, commonly known as the stephanostyle hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
