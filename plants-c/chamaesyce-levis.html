Chamaesyce levis

Chamaesyce levis, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous spreading or ascending branches. The leaves are generally smooth, ovate to elliptical in shape, and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
