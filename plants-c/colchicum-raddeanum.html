Colchicum raddeanum

Colchicum raddeanum, commonly known as the Siberian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, bulbous corms that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
