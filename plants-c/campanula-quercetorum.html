Campanula quercetorum

Campanula quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Campanula quercetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
