Canavalia munda

Canavalia munda, commonly known as the white bean or slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Canavalia munda is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It also has medicinal uses and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agricultural systems.
