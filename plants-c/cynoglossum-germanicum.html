Cynoglossum germanicum

Cynoglossum germanicum, commonly known as German mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves that resemble mouse ears. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Cynoglossum germanicum is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
