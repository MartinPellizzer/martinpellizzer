Cytisus argyreius

Cytisus argyreius, commonly known as the golden bush or yellow broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
