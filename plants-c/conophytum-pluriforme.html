Conophytum pluriforme

Conophytum pluriforme, commonly known as the "Cape Rainplant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rock-like appearance. The plant features fleshy, triangular leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the rainy season.

Conophytum pluriforme is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
