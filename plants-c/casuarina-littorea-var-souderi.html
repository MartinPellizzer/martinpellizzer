Casuarina littorea var. souderi

Casuarina littorea var. souderi, commonly known as the coastal she-oak, is a variant of the coastal she-oak species native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves and distinctive, cone-like fruits. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its root system helps stabilize dunes and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in coastal ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
