Cassia notabilis

Cassia notabilis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves and has a fibrous, woody root system. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cassia notabilis is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
