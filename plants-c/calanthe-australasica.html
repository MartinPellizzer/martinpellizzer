Calanthe australasica

Calanthe australasica, commonly known as the Australian calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically produces compact, showy inflorescences with small, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Calanthe australasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
