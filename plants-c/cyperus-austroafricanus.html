Cyperus austroafricanus

Cyperus austroafricanus, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in riparian zones or along water bodies.

Cyperus austroafricanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
