Calceolaria pendula

Calceolaria pendula, commonly known as the hanging slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, slipper-shaped flowers that hang from long, slender stems. The plant produces vibrant, often purple or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Calceolaria pendula thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
