Crassula nodulosa var. longisepala

Crassula nodulosa var. longisepala, commonly known as the Long-sepaled Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
