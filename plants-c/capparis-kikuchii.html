Capparis kikuchii

Capparis kikuchii, commonly known as the Japanese caper, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves, which are typical of its genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Capparis kikuchii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
