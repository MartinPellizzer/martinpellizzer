Calliandra fuscipila

Calliandra fuscipila, commonly known as the dark-petaled calliandra, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

Calliandra fuscipila also has nitrogen-fixing properties, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
