Centaurea margaritacea

Centaurea margaritacea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that resemble small holly berries, hence its common name. The plant has spiny, grass-like leaves and thrives in coastal environments, often growing on sandy or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Centaurea margaritacea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
