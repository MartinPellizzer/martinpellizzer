Cynoglossum capusii

Cynoglossum capusii, commonly known as the Persian mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cynoglossum capusii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
