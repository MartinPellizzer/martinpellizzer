Carex straminea var. indivisa

Carex straminea var. indivisa, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically light green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
