Cordia lauta

Cordia lauta, commonly known as the large-leaved cordia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Cordia lauta is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
