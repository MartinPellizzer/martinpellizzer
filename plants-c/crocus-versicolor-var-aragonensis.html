Crocus versicolor var. aragonensis

Crocus versicolor var. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often bicolor flowers that range from yellow to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in harsh environments, allowing it to bloom in early spring.
