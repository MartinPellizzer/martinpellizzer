Convolvulus hirsutus

Convolvulus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Convolvulus hirsutus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to climb or trail along supports.
