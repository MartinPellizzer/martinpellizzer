Correa

Correa, commonly known as the mountain bottlebrush, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to the vibrant floral displays. Correa species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

They thrive in well-drained soils and require full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
