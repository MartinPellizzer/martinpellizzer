Centaurea glastifolia subsp. intermedia

Centaurea glastifolia subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate glastifolia centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower heads.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
