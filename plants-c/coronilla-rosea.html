Coronilla rosea

Coronilla rosea, commonly known as the rose crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.
