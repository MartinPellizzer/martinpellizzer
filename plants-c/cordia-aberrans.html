Cordia aberrans

Cordia aberrans, commonly known as the red cordia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive central column. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
