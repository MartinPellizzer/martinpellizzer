Cucumis colocynthoides

Cucumis colocynthoides, commonly known as the prickly cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, spiny fruits. Its botanical features include a Cucurbitaceae family classification, typical of the cucumber and melon family.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
