Calceolaria verbascifolia

Calceolaria verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and striking, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insects, such as bees and flies.

Calceolaria verbascifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its exotic appearance.
