Carex echinata var. radiata

Carex echinata var. radiata, commonly known as the radiate sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
