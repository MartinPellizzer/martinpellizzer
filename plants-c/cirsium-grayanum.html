Cirsium grayanum

Cirsium grayanum, commonly known as the Grayanum thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
