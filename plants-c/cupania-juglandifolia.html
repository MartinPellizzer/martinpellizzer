Cupania juglandifolia

Cupania juglandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or Brazilian pecan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
