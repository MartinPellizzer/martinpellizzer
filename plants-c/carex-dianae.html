Carex dianae

Carex dianae, commonly known as the Dianae sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to certain regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
