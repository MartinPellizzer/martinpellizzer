Crocus laevigatus subsp. pumilus

Crocus laevigatus subsp.

pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It produces delicate, early spring flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pale pink.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and early blooming habit make it a valuable addition to gardens seeking early floral interest.
