Castela tweediei var. typica

Castela tweediei var. typica, commonly known as the red sandalwood.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its woody structure and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its bark is thick and often fissured.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique aesthetic features.
