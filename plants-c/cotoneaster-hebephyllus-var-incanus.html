Cotoneaster hebephyllus var. incanus

Cotoneaster hebephyllus var. incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Cotoneaster hebephyllus var. incanus is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats and erosion control.
