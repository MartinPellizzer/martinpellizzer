Cyperus ciliatopilosus var. rhizomatosus

Cyperus ciliatopilosus var. rhizomatosus, commonly known as the hairy fringed flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats or colonies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with hairy margins, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
