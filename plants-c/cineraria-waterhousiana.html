Cineraria waterhousiana

Cineraria waterhousiana, commonly known as the Waterhouse cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and grayish-green, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cineraria waterhousiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
