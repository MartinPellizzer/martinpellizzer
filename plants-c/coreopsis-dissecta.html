Coreopsis dissecta

Coreopsis dissecta, commonly known as the Canadian aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features finely dissected, fern-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
