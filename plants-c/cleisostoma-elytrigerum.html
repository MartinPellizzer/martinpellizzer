Cleisostoma elytrigerum

Cleisostoma elytrigerum, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
