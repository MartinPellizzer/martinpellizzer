Crotalaria obovata

Crotalaria obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf cowpea or yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Crotalaria obovata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
