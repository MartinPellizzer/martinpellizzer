Coronilla occidentalis

Coronilla occidentalis, commonly known as western crownvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
