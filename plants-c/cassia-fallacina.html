Cassia fallacina

Cassia fallacina, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has feathery, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Cassia fallacina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
