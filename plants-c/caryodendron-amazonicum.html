Caryodendron amazonicum

Caryodendron amazonicum, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are pollinated by specific bees, and its fruit is a large, woody capsule containing several edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The plant plays a vital ecological role in the rainforest, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are also economically important, being harvested for their high nutritional value and commercial use.
