Ctenopteris podocarpa

Ctenopteris podocarpa, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate fronds that are often used in horticultural displays due to their ornamental appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Ctenopteris podocarpa plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
