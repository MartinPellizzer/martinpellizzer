Capsicum conicum var. orientale

Capsicum conicum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental bird pepper, is a variant of the bird pepper species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often brightly colored fruits, which can range from red to yellow or orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The fruit is used in various culinary and medicinal applications due to its capsaicin content.
