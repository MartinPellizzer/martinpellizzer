Crataegus reverchonii var. reverchonii

Crataegus reverchonii var. reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorned branches, which are a characteristic feature of the Crataegus genus. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and turn yellow in the autumn.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
