Corchorus argutus var. longicarpus

Corchorus argutus var. longicarpus, commonly known as the long-seeded prickly lettuce, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features hairy, alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces yellow flowers in a composite head. The plant is characterized by its elongated, spiny fruits that contain small, flat seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is noted for its fibrous stems and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
