Calea lucida

Calea lucida, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Calea lucida is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
