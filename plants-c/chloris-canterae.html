Chloris canterae

Chloris canterae, commonly known as slender grass or Canterbury grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Chloris canterae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
