Cercanthemum auriculatum

Cercanthemum auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

The plant's unique leaf shape and striking flowers make it a notable species in its native habitat.
