Carex fuscula

Carex fuscula, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex fuscula produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
