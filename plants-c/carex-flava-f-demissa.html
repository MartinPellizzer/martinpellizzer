Carex flava f. demissa

Carex flava f. demissa, commonly known as the slender yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, yellowish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive yellow-green coloration that distinguishes it from other sedges.
