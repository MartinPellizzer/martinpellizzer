Cryptomeria japonica spiralites-falcata

Cryptomeria japonica spiralites-falcata, commonly known as the Japanese fir with falcate spirals, is a rare variant of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of needles, which form a unique falcate (sickle-shaped) pattern along the branches. The plant exhibits a coniferous structure with needle-like leaves and cone-bearing reproductive structures. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
