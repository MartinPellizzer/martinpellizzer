Cracca candida

Cracca candida, commonly known as the white cracca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and grassy areas. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. It is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.

Cracca candida is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
