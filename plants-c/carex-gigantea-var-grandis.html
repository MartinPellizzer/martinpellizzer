Carex gigantea var. grandis

Carex gigantea var. grandis, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a tall, grass-like perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features dense, cylindrical spikes of pale yellow-brown flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
