Coccothrinax torrida

Coccothrinax torrida, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in warm climates.
