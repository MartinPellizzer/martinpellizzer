Calamus paspalanthus

Calamus paspalanthus, commonly known as the grass-like sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Calamus paspalanthus is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
