Clematis sonneratii

Clematis sonneratii, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

Clematis sonneratii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
