Cinnamomum arfakense

Cinnamomum arfakense, commonly known as the Arfak cinnamon or West Papua cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is the source of its essential oil. The plant thrives in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a distinct cinnamon scent when crushed.

The tree is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
