Cicerbita leucophaea

Cicerbita leucophaea, commonly known as the white-flowered cicerbita, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
