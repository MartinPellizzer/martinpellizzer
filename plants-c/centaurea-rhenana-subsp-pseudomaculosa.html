Centaurea rhenana subsp. pseudomaculosa

Centaurea rhenana subsp.

pseudomaculosa, commonly known as the red-centred centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and strikingly colored flowers, typically with a red center and yellow or orange petals.

The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
