Cirsium angulosum

Cirsium angulosum, commonly known as angular thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are covered in stiff hairs and have a distinctive angular appearance, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium angulosum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
