Castanella

Castanella, commonly known as the chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae and is characterized by its lobed leaves and prickly husks surrounding its edible nuts. The tree produces small, sweet, and nutritious nuts that are widely consumed both raw and cooked. Castanella thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate rainfall.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
