Crotalaria bifaria

Crotalaria bifaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bean-like pods. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has trifoliate leaves with narrow leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used as a protein-rich food source in some cultures.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
