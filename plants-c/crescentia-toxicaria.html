Crescentia toxicaria

Crescentia toxicaria, commonly known as the "Coco de Mer" or "Double coconut," is a flowering plant native to the Seychelles and parts of the Indian Ocean.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a thick, buttressed trunk and broad, palm-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive, large, double coconuts that are encased in a fibrous husk and contain a single, large seed. Crescentia toxicaria is notable for its unique morphology and is considered a keystone species in its native ecosystems.

Due to its rarity and ecological importance, it is also a symbol of the Seychelles.
