Codonoraphia

Codonoraphia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
