Carex trisperma var. billingsii

Carex trisperma var. billingsii, commonly known as the Billings sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
