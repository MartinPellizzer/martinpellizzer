Chamaecytisus smyrnaeus

Chamaecytisus smyrnaeus, commonly known as the Turkish broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, thorny stems that often grow in dense, bushy formations. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
