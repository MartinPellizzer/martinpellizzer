Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. latifolia

Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pale green or reddish-brown. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
