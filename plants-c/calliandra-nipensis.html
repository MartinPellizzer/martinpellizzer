Calliandra nipensis

Calliandra nipensis, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calliandra nipensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
