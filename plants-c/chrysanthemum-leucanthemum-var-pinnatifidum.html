Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. pinnatifidum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and finely divided, pinnatifid leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

This variety is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
