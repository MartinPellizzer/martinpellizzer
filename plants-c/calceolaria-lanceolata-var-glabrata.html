Calceolaria lanceolata var. glabrata

Calceolaria lanceolata var. glabrata, commonly known as the hairy calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its "hairy" common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
