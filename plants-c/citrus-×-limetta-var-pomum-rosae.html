Citrus × limetta var. pomum-rosae

Citrus × limetta var. pomum-rosae, commonly known as the blood orange.

This variety is a hybrid citrus plant that exhibits unique botanical features, including a thick, leathery rind that is often reddish in color. The fruit is characterized by its deep red or maroon flesh, which is segmented and juicy. It typically grows on shrubs or small trees, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The plant prefers warm, sunny climates and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for Mediterranean and subtropical regions.
