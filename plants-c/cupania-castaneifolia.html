Cupania castaneifolia

Cupania castaneifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or sweet chestnut tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. Its botanical features include smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped, dark green leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
