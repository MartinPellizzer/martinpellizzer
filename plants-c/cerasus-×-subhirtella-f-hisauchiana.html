Cerasus × subhirtella f. hisauchiana

Cerasus × subhirtella f. hisauchiana, commonly known as the Higan cherry, is a deciduous ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is prized for its vibrant spring blossoms, which appear in clusters of pink or white flowers before the leaves emerge. The plant has a compact, rounded form with dense foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers are also a source of nectar for pollinators, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
