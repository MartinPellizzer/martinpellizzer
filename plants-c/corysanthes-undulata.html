Corysanthes undulata

Corysanthes undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf candlewood, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, lance-shaped leaves that have a wavy margin. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a fragrant aroma. Corysanthes undulata is typically found in rainforest or coastal habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
