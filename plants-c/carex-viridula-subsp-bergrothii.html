Carex viridula subsp. bergrothii

Carex viridula subsp.

bergrothii, commonly known as Bergroth's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Bergroth's sedge is often found in shaded environments and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
