Coelogyne pandurata

Coelogyne pandurata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow pseudobulbs and striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding substrate.

Coelogyne pandurata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
