Crataegus monogyna var. rosiformis

Crataegus monogyna var. rosiformis, commonly known as the roseleaf hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rose-like leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
