Calceolaria tinctoria

Calceolaria tinctoria, commonly known as the dye plant or scarlet cup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
