Carduus baeticus

Carduus baeticus, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its hardy nature makes it a common sight in natural habitats across southern Europe.
