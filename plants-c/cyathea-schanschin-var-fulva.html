Cyathea schanschin var. fulva

Cyathea schanschin var. fulva, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are typically a vibrant green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
