Camelina microcarpa subsp. pilosa

Camelina microcarpa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and distinctive, bladder-like seed pods.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
