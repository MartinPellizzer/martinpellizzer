Chasmanthium

Chasmanthium, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Chasmanthium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a biofuel crop.
