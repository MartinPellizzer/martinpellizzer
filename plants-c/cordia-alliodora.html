Cordia alliodora

Cordia alliodora, commonly known as the sweet gum or alligator tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the petiole. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
