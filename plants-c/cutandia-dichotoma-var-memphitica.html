Cutandia dichotoma var. memphitica

Cutandia dichotoma var. memphitica, commonly known as the Memphis cutandia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
