Crassula galunkensis

Crassula galunkensis, commonly known as the Cape Town Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and has a compact, bushy appearance. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
