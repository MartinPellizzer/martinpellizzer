Ceanothus endlichii

Ceanothus endlichii, commonly known as the Endlicher's ceanothus, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, often fragrant foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Ceanothus endlichii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
