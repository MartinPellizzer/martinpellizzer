Cerastium brachypetalum subsp. atheniense

Cerastium brachypetalum subsp.

atheniense, commonly known as the Athens mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, rounded leaves.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
