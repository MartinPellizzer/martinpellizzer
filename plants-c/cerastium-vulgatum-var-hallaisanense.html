Cerastium vulgatum var. hallaisanense

Cerastium vulgatum var. hallaisanense, commonly known as the Hallaisan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense mat of small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
