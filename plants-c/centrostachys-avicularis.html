Centrostachys avicularis

Centrostachys avicularis, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or disturbed coastal soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
