Cayratia cardiospermoides

Cayratia cardiospermoides, commonly known as the heartseed vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive seed pods that contain a single large, fleshy seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique seed structure, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
