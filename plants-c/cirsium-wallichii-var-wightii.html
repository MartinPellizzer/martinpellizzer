Cirsium wallichii var. wightii

Cirsium wallichii var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
