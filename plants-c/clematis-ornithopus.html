Clematis ornithopus

Clematis ornithopus, commonly known as the silverleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically woody and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Clematis ornithopus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
