Comandra pallida var. angustifolia

Comandra pallida var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf comandra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats. It has a taproot system and is adapted to arid environments.
