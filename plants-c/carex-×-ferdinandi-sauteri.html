Carex × ferdinandi-sauteri

Carex × ferdinandi-sauteri, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often a striking green or bronze color.

Carex × ferdinandi-sauteri is valued in wetland restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in water gardens.
