Cliocarpus

Cliocarpus, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rhizophoraceae.

It is characterized by its salt-excreting leaves, which help it survive in coastal and brackish water environments. The plant has a robust root system, including pneumatophores that allow it to breathe in waterlogged soils. Cliocarpus species are often found in mangrove forests, playing a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
