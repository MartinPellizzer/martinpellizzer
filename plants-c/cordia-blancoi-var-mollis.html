Cordia blancoi var. mollis

Cordia blancoi var. mollis, commonly known as the soft cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or limestone soils.
