Crambe cordifolia subsp. kotschyana

Crambe cordifolia subsp.

kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir cabbage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant ecology and horticulture.
