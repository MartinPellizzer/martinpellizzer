Clusia rosea

Clusia rosea, commonly known as the waxleaf croton, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Clusia rosea is relatively low-maintenance and can be grown in containers or in gardens with well-draining soil.
