Coris monspeliensis var. annua

Coris monspeliensis var. annua, commonly known as the annual coris, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a wildflower in gardens and naturalized areas.
