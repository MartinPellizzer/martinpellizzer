Carduus pycnocephalus subsp. cinereus

Carduus pycnocephalus subsp.

cinereus, commonly known as the gray thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features robust, spiny stems and large, silver-gray, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in dense, spherical heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and recognizable species within the Carduus genus.
