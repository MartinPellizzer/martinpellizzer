Cyclopogon eldorado

Cyclopogon eldorado, commonly known as the Eldorado cyclopogon, is a rare and striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the canopy layer of the forest. Its leaves are long and strap-like, forming a rosette that holds water, a feature common among bromeliads.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts.
