Cinnamomum philippinense

Cinnamomum philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or Luzon cinnamon, is a species of tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that resembles true cinnamon. The plant typically grows in lowland and montane forests, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a fragrant aroma.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, dark purple berries containing a single seed.
