Cissampelos tropaeolifolia var. fluminensis

Cissampelos tropaeolifolia var. fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian moon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often adorned with waxy coatings. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and greenery in shaded areas.
