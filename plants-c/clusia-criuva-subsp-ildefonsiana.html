Clusia criuva subsp. ildefonsiana

Clusia criuva subsp.

ildefonsiana, commonly known as the Ildefonso's glory bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
