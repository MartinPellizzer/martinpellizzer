Cyathodes oxycedrus

Cyathodes oxycedrus, commonly known as the Mediterranean boxwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Cyathodes. These flowers are followed by small, red berries that contain the plant's seeds.

The shrub is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
