Cordia lutea

Cordia lutea, commonly known as the yellow cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, pale bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
