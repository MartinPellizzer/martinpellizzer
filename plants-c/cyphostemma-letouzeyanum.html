Cyphostemma letouzeyanum

Cyphostemma letouzeyanum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to arid conditions.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
