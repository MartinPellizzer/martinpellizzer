Cyrtomium caryotideum

Cyrtomium caryotideum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, arching fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery-white tomentum, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, rounded pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

Cyrtomium caryotideum is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
