Cynanchum brevipedunculatum

Cynanchum brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the short-stalked dogbane, is a perennial herb native to Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
