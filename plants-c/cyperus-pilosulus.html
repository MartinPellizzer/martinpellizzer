Cyperus pilosulus

Cyperus pilosulus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, along waterways, or in disturbed sites. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus pilosulus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
