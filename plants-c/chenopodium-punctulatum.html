Chenopodium punctulatum

Chenopodium punctulatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its seeds are edible and have been used by Indigenous peoples for food and medicine.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability make it a common weed in various ecosystems.
