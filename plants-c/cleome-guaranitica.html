Cleome guaranitica

Cleome guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian spider flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome guaranitica is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique floral display.
