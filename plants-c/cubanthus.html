Cubanthus

Cubanthus, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Mexico and Central America. The plants typically have showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with contrasting yellow or white markings. They are herbaceous perennials that thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

Cubanthus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
