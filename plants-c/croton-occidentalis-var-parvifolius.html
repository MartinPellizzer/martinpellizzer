Croton occidentalis var. parvifolius

Croton occidentalis var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are typically small and ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf colors and adaptability to various growing conditions.
