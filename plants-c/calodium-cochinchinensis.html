Calodium cochinchinensis

Calodium cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid or Caladium cochinchinensis, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Calodium cochinchinensis is valued for its vibrant leaf patterns, which can range from white and pink to red and green.
