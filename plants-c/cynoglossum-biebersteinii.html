Cynoglossum biebersteinii

Cynoglossum biebersteinii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a bell-like shape. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cynoglossum biebersteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
