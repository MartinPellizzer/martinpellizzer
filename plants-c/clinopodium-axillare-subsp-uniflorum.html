Clinopodium axillare subsp. uniflorum

Clinopodium axillare subsp.

uniflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer.

The species is characterized by its single-flower arrangement on each stem, distinguishing it from other members of the genus.
