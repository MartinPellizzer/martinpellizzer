Cerasus prostrata var. concolor

Cerasus prostrata var. concolor, commonly known as the Chinese flowering cherry or the dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is relatively hardy, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
