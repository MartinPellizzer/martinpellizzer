Clethra williamsii

Clethra williamsii, commonly known as Williams' clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Clethra williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
