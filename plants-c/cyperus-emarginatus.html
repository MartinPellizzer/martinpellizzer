Cyperus emarginatus

Cyperus emarginatus, commonly known as the spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are often reddish or purplish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus emarginatus is often used in ornamental gardening and can also serve as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.
