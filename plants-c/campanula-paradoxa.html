Campanula paradoxa

Campanula paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Campanula paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
