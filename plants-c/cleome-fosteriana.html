Cleome fosteriana

Cleome fosteriana, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, spindly stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome fosteriana is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
