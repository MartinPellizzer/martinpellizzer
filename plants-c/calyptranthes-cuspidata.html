Calyptranthes cuspidata

Calyptranthes cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive sharp tip on each leaf. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Calyptranthes cuspidata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.
