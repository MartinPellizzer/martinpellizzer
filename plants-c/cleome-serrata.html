Cleome serrata

Cleome serrata, commonly known as the cutleaf cleome, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Cleome serrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
