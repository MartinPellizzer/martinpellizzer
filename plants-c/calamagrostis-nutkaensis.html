Calamagrostis nutkaensis

Calamagrostis nutkaensis, commonly known as Nutka reed, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, saline environments, such as tidal marshes and coastal dunes. Its flowering heads are slender and spike-like, producing small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Calamagrostis nutkaensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
