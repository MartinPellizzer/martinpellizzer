Chamaesyce herbstii

Chamaesyce herbstii, commonly known as Herbst's sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Chamaesyce herbstii is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a characteristic species of desert scrub habitats.
