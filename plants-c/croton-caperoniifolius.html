Croton caperoniifolius

Croton caperoniifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often showy flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by colorful bracts.

Croton caperoniifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
