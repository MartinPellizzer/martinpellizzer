Cordia chaetodonta

Cordia chaetodonta, commonly known as the hairy cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland areas. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a hairy texture, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.
