Chamaecyparis pisifera var. variegata

Chamaecyparis pisifera var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Japanese cedar, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green stripes on its needle-like leaves. This evergreen conifer is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.

The plant is also valued for its slow growth and ability to maintain a compact form over time.
