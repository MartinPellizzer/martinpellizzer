Callistemon pauciflorus

Callistemon pauciflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in winter and spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Callistemon pauciflorus is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
