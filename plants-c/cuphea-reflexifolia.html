Cuphea reflexifolia

Cuphea reflexifolia, commonly known as the zigzag cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Cuphea reflexifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
