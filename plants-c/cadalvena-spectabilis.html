Cadalvena spectabilis

Cadalvena spectabilis, commonly known as the showy cadalvena, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
