Chaerophyllum bifidum

Chaerophyllum bifidum, commonly known as the forked water parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.
