Corydalis fumariifolia f. albiflora

Corydalis fumariifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered fumariifolia corydalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that are predominantly white, with a pale pink or red tinge at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and early spring blooms.
