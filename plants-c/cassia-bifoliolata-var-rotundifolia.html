Cassia bifoliolata var. rotundifolia

Cassia bifoliolata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in panicles, which bloom during the wet season. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
