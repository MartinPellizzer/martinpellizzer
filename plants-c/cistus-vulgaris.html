Cistus vulgaris

Cistus vulgaris, commonly known as rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus vulgaris is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
