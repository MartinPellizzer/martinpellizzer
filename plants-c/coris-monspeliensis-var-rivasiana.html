Coris monspeliensis var. rivasiana

Coris monspeliensis var. rivasiana, commonly known as the Rivasian rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
