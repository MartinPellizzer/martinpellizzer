Carissa edulis var. horrida

Carissa edulis var. horrida, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. The plant features waxy, dark green leaves and hairy stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Despite its ornamental value, the plant can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
