Clinopodium brasilicum

Clinopodium brasilicum, commonly known as Brazilian mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Clinopodium brasilicum is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
