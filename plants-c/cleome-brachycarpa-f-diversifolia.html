Cleome brachycarpa f. diversifolia

Cleome brachycarpa f. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinctive variegation pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
