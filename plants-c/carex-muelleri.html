Carex muelleri

Carex muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
