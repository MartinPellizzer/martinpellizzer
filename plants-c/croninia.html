Croninia

Croninia, commonly known as the "Firecracker" or "Cape gold," is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

These plants are characterized by their striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. Croninia species are typically shrubs or subshrubs with woody stems and narrow, linear leaves. They bloom during the warmer months, often producing clusters of flowers that resemble small lanterns.

The genus is valued in horticulture for its vibrant displays and drought tolerance.
