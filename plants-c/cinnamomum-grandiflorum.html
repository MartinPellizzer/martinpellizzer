Cinnamomum grandiflorum

Cinnamomum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered cinnamon or Cinnamomum grandiflorum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant's botanical features make it valuable for both commercial and ecological purposes.
