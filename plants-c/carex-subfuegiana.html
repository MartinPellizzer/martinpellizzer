Carex subfuegiana

Carex subfuegiana, commonly known as the Fuegian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, specifically the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of streams.

Carex subfuegiana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
