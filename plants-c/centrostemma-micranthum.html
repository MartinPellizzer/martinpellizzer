Centrostemma micranthum

Centrostemma micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered centrostemma, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale green flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
