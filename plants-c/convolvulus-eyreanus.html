Convolvulus eyreanus

Convolvulus eyreanus, commonly known as Eyre's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows from 30 to 100 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are wiry and often trailing.

Convolvulus eyreanus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
