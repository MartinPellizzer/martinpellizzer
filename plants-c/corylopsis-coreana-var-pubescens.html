Corylopsis coreana var. pubescens

Corylopsis coreana var. pubescens, commonly known as the Korean yellowflower winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in late autumn to early winter, providing vibrant color when most other plants are dormant. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique flowering period and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to winter gardens.
