Chrysophyllum pyriforme

Chrysophyllum pyriforme, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a yellow or orange drupe, typically pear-shaped, with a thick, edible pulp surrounding a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and the edible fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
