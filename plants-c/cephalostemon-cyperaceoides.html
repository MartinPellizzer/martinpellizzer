Cephalostemon cyperaceoides

Cephalostemon cyperaceoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often brown or reddish in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Cephalostemon cyperaceoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
