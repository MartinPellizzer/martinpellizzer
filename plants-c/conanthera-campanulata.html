Conanthera campanulata

Conanthera campanulata, commonly known as the bluebell of the prairies, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Conanthera campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
