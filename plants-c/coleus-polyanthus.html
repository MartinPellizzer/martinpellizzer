Coleus polyanthus

Coleus polyanthus, commonly known as the shoal fin or red curl, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often features shades of red, purple, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its trailing growth habit, making it popular in hanging baskets and containers. Its leaves are often variegated, with contrasting patterns that add visual interest to gardens.

Coleus polyanthus thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
