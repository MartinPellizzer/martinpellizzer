Cicerbita nuristanica

Cicerbita nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species in botanical research.
