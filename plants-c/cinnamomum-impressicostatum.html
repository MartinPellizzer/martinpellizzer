Cinnamomum impressicostatum

Cinnamomum impressicostatum, commonly known as the Guangxi cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive fragrance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
