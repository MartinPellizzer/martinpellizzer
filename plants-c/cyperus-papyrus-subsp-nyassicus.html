Cyperus papyrus subsp. nyassicus

Cyperus papyrus subsp.

nyassicus, commonly known as the African papyrus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like foliage.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers, thriving in waterlogged or moist soils. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant's historical significance includes its use in ancient Egyptian papermaking and as a symbol of the Nile.
