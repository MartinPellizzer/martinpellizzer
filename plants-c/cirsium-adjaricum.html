Cirsium adjaricum

Cirsium adjaricum, commonly known as the Adjara thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silvery-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spherical shape. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
