Cucumis esculentus

Cucumis esculentus, commonly known as the cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces elongated, spiky fruits with a high water content. The plant has palmate leaves and yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. The fruit is typically harvested before it fully matures, when it is still tender and crisp.

It is widely cultivated for its refreshing, edible fruit used in various culinary applications.
