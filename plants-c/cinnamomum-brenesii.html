Cinnamomum brenesii

Cinnamomum brenesii, commonly known as the Guatemalan cinnamon or *Cinnamomum brenesii*, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America, particularly Guatemala and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant's essential oils are also valued for their aromatic and potential therapeutic uses.
