Cassinia affinis

Cassinia affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and the leaves are narrow and often hairy.

Cassinia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
