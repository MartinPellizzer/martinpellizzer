Chaetochloa glauca var. purpurea

Chaetochloa glauca var. purpurea, commonly known as purple meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage, which becomes more pronounced under certain environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive coloration and ease of cultivation.
