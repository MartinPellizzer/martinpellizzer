Clavimyrtus marginata

Clavimyrtus marginata, commonly known as the marginated myrtle, is a shrub native to the humid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale margin. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pink and are arranged in clusters.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
