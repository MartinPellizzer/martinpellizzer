Chusquea acutigluma

Chusquea acutigluma, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a sharp, acuminate tip, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous forests.

Chusquea acutigluma plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
