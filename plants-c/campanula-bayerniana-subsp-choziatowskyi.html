Campanula bayerniana subsp. choziatowskyi

Campanula bayerniana subsp.

choziatowskyi, commonly known as the Choziatowski's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and dark green in color.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, blooming during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
