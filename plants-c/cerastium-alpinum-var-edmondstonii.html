Cerastium alpinum var. edmondstonii

Cerastium alpinum var. edmondstonii, commonly known as the Edmondston's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and clusters of small, white flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
