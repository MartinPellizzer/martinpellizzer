Cannabis sativa var. vulgaris

Cannabis sativa var. vulgaris, commonly known as hemp, is a variety of the cannabis plant characterized by its low tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) content and high cannabidiol (CBD) levels.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, with long, narrow leaves and flowering buds that develop at the nodes. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires a significant amount of sunlight and well-drained soil. Its fibrous stalks are used for industrial purposes, while its leaves and flowers are utilized in various medicinal and nutritional applications.

The botanical features of Cannabis sativa var. vulgaris make it distinct from its psychoactive counterparts.
