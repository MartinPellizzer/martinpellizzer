Cirsium yoshizawae

Cirsium yoshizawae, commonly known as the Yoshizawa thistle, is a rare flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern.
