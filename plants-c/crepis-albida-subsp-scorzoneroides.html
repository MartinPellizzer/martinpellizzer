Crepis albida subsp. scorzoneroides

Crepis albida subsp.

scorzoneroides, commonly known as the white cock's-comb, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow ray florets that resemble a cock's comb, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic regions, preferring rocky or gravelly soils. It has a compact growth habit with upright, hairy stems and small, alternate leaves.

The subspecies is notable for its frost resistance and is often found in high-altitude environments across Europe and parts of Asia.
