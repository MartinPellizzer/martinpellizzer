Carex macrandrolepis

Carex macrandrolepis, commonly known as the large-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swamps, and prefers full to partial sunlight. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple hues.

Carex macrandrolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
