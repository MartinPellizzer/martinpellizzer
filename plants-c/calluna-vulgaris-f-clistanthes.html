Calluna vulgaris f. clistanthes

Calluna vulgaris f. clistanthes, commonly known as the purple heather, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its leaves are evergreen, needle-like, and retain their color throughout the year.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
