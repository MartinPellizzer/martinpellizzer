Cyperus hildra

Cyperus hildra, commonly known as Hildra flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus hildra is often found in wetland habitats, including swampy areas and along waterways.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
