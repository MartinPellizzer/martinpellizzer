Clerodendrum megasepalum

Clerodendrum megasepalum, commonly known as the giant flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody with a tendency to branch profusely. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different garden environments.
