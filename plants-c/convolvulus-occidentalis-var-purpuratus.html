Convolvulus occidentalis var. purpuratus

Convolvulus occidentalis var. purpuratus, commonly known as the Western morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, purplish-pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy annual in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
