Cordia fischeri

Cordia fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's glorybower, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry forest habitats.

Cordia fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
