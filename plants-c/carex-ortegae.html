Carex ortegae

Carex ortegae, commonly known as the Ortegae sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or brown in color.

Carex ortegae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
