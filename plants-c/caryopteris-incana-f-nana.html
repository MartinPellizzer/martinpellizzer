Caryopteris incana f. nana

Caryopteris incana f. nana, commonly known as dwarf silver-blue mistflower, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bluish-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive, almost frosty appearance. The plant produces clusters of delicate, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is well-suited for rock gardens, borders, or as a ground cover due to its hardiness and drought tolerance.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and the subtle, airy display of its blooms.
