Cassia heptanthera

Cassia heptanthera, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia heptanthera thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
