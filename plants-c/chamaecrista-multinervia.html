Chamaecrista multinervia

Chamaecrista multinervia, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or saline soils along the coastline. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the morning. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern.

Chamaecrista multinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
