Cryptocarya wiedensis

Cryptocarya wiedensis, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Cryptocarya wiedensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
