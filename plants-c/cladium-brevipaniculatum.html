Cladium brevipaniculatum

Cladium brevipaniculatum, commonly known as the short-bracted sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and swampy environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the base of the stems. Cladium brevipaniculatum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its ability to thrive in saturated soils makes it a key component of marsh and swamp habitats.
