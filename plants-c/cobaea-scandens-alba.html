Cobaea scandens alba

Cobaea scandens alba, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and tropical landscapes.
