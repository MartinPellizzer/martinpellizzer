Commelina densiflora

Commelina densiflora, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Commelina densiflora is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
