Chaetopappa imberbis

Chaetopappa imberbis, commonly known as the smooth-haired dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.
