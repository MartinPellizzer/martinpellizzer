Columnea wendlandisma

Columnea wendlandisma, commonly known as the Wendland's flame flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Columnea wendlandisma is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its ornamental value and unique floral display.
