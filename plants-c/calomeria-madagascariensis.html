Calomeria madagascariensis

Calomeria madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the island's unique climate. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-filled capsules as fruit.

Calomeria madagascariensis is of interest to botanists due to its endemic status and ecological significance in its native habitat.
