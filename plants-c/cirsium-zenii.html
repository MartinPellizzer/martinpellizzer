Cirsium zenii

Cirsium zenii, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene fruits.

Cirsium zenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
