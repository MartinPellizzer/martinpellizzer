Centaurea calcitrapa subsp. calcitrapa

Centaurea calcitrapa subsp.

calcitrapa, commonly known as the limestone cudweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
