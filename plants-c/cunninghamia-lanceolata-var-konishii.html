Cunninghamia lanceolata var. konishii

Cunninghamia lanceolata var. konishii, commonly known as the Konishii Chinese fir, is a variant of the Chinese fir native to southern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The leaves are needle-like, typically arranged in spirals, and are dark green with a slight bluish tinge. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of landscaping applications.
