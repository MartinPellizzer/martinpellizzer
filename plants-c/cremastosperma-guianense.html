Cremastosperma guianense

Cremastosperma guianense, commonly known as the Guianese cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Guianas and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers.

Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
