Crataegus pseudoheterophylla

Crataegus pseudoheterophylla, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with some leaves being simple and others compound, a trait that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and produces white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red or orange berry that ripens in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
