Calamagrostis vicunarum

Calamagrostis vicunarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a grass species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Calamagrostis vicunarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
