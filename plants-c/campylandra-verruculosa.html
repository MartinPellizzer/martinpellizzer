Campylandra verruculosa

Campylandra verruculosa, commonly known as the rough campylandra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
