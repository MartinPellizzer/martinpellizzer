Cajanus marmoratus

Cajanus marmoratus, commonly known as the marble bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive marbled patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

Cajanus marmoratus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
