Coccothrinax baileyana

Coccothrinax baileyana, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Caribbean.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
