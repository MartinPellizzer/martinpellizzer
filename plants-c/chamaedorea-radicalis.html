Chamaedorea radicalis

Chamaedorea radicalis, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen palm, is a species native to Central America.

It is a slow-growing, compact palm that typically reaches a height of about 3 to 5 feet. The plant features slender, unbranched stems and broad, pinnate leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Chamaedorea radicalis is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
