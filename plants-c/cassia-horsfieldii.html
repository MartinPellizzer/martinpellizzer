Cassia horsfieldii

Cassia horsfieldii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it an important food source for pollinators like bees and birds.

Cassia horsfieldii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
