Carphobolus umbellulatus

Carphobolus umbellulatus, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. Its fruit is a capsule that releases seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes many daisy and sunflower relatives.
