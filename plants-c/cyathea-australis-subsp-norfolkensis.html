Cyathea australis subsp. norfolkensis

Cyathea australis subsp.

norfolkensis, commonly known as the Norfolk Island tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Norfolk Island in the South Pacific. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

This subspecies is of conservation interest due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes.
