Crepidium flavovirens

Crepidium flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, yellow-green flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are often clustered in loose inflorescences and are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
