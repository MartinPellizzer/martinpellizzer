Copaifera copallifera

Copaifera copallifera, commonly known as the copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood and leaves. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits.

The plant is valued for its copaiba oil, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
