Courimari

Courimari, commonly known as the "brazilian nightshade," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Courimari is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
