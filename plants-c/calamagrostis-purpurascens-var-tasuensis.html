Calamagrostis purpurascens var. tasuensis

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. tasuensis, commonly known as the Tasmanian purple grass, is a perennial grass native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple-tinged foliage that turns golden in the fall. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with small, delicate flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
