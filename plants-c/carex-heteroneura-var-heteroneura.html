Carex heteroneura var. heteroneura

Carex heteroneura var. heteroneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
