Chaetochloa polystachya

Chaetochloa polystachya, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and often bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Chaetochloa polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
