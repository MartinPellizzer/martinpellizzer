Cerastium furcatum var. chiisanense

Cerastium furcatum var. chiisanense, commonly known as the Japanese mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white pubescence. It produces pink to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and floral structure compared to other Cerastium species.
