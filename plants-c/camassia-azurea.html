Camassia azurea

Camassia azurea, commonly known as blue camas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowering stems rise above the foliage.

Camassia azurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by Indigenous peoples for both food and cultural purposes.
