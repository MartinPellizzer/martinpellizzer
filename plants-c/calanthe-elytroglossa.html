Calanthe elytroglossa

Calanthe elytroglossa, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, showy flowers with golden-yellow petals and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Calanthe elytroglossa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
