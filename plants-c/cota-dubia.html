Cota dubia

Cota dubia, commonly known as the false mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Cota dubia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
