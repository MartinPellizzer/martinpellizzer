Cerastium banaticum var. kosaninii

Cerastium banaticum var. kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and delicate appearance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
