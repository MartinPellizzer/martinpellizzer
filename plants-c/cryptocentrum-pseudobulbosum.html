Cryptocentrum pseudobulbosum

Cryptocentrum pseudobulbosum, commonly known as the false bulb orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are small and often clustered in inflorescences.

Cryptocentrum pseudobulbosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
