Carludovica palmata

Carludovica palmata, commonly known as the Mexican palm or the chonta palm, is a tropical plant native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits containing black seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a distinctive feature in tropical landscapes.
