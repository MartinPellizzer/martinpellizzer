Carex hoppneri

Carex hoppneri, commonly known as Hoppner's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex hoppneri is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
