Cyperus haspan var. sphaerospermoides

Cyperus haspan var. sphaerospermoides, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
