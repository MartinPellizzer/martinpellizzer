Clematis koreana var. biternata

Clematis koreana var. biternata, commonly known as the Korean Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Korea.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
