Clompanus gracilis

Clompanus gracilis, commonly known as the slender clompanus, is a delicate flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
