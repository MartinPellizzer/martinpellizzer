Cacalia taliensis

Cacalia taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia taliensis is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
