Cyperus fasciculatus

Cyperus fasciculatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus fasciculatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
