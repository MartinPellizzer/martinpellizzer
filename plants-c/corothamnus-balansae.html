Corothamnus balansae

Corothamnus balansae, commonly known as Balansae thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, narrow leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow to orange disc flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets.

Corothamnus balansae is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
