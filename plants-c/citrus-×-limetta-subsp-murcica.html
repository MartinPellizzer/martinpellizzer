Citrus × limetta subsp. murcica

Citrus × limetta subsp.

murcica, commonly known as the Murcian lemon, is a citrus hybrid native to southern Spain. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, yellowish peel and a high juice content.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The fruit is used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its high vitamin C content and aromatic properties.
