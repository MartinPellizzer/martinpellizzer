Carex hudsonii var. homalocarpa

Carex hudsonii var. homalocarpa, commonly known as the Hudson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
