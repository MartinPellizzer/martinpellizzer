Camellia cuspidata var. grandiflora

Camellia cuspidata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often fragrant, blooming in late winter to early spring. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its winter blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
