Cadaba apiculata

Cadaba apiculata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small, lantern-like structure. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
