Cleome affinis

Cleome affinis, commonly known as the common cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, pink, or white. The leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are often hairy on both sides. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its delicate appearance and ease of growth.
