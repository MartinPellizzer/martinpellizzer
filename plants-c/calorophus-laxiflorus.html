Calorophus laxiflorus

Calorophus laxiflorus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss.

Calorophus laxiflorus is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.
