Campanula adhaerens

Campanula adhaerens, commonly known as the clinging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to purple in color, and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula adhaerens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
