Caylusea hexagyna var. rigida

Caylusea hexagyna var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid caylusea, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rigid, upright growth habit and distinctive, hexagonal leaf structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variant is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
