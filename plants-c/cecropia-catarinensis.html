Cecropia catarinensis

Cecropia catarinensis, commonly known as the Catarina ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, pentagonal leaves and distinctive, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats and is an important component of the local ecosystem.

Cecropia catarinensis plays a role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
