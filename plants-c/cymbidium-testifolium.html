Cymbidium testifolium

Cymbidium testifolium, commonly known as the testicle orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous inflorescence and elongated, fleshy sepals that resemble testicles, giving it its unusual common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Cymbidium testifolium is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
