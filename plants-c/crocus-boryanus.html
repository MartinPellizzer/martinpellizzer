Crocus boryanus

Crocus boryanus, commonly known as the Bory's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. This perennial herb produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
