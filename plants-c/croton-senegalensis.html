Croton senegalensis

Croton senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegal croton, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and bright, showy flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and traditional uses.
