Caucalis cretica

Caucalis cretica, commonly known as lesser burnet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant has a creeping rootstock and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Caucalis cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
