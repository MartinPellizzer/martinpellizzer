Chaenocephalus propinquus

Chaenocephalus propinquus, commonly known as the common sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are encased in spiny burs, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing.

The plant is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and persistence in various environments.
