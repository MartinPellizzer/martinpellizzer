Commiphora cinerea

Commiphora cinerea, commonly known as the gray myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its pale gray bark and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces a resinous gum, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is noted for its slow growth and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
