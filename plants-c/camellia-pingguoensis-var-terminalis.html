Camellia pingguoensis var. terminalis

Camellia pingguoensis var. terminalis, commonly known as the Guangxi camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or yellow base. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
