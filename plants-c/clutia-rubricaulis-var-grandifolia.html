Clutia rubricaulis var. grandifolia

Clutia rubricaulis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the red-stemmed clutia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often red-veined, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue, which contributes to its common name.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
