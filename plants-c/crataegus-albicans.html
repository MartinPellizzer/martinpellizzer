Crataegus albicans

Crataegus albicans, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist woodlands or along streambanks.

Crataegus albicans is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
