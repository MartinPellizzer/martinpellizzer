Chenopodium atriplicis

Chenopodium atriplicis, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Chenopodium atriplicis is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
