Cissus triloba

Cissus triloba, commonly known as the bone climbers, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive leaf shapes. The leaves are usually heart-shaped with a serrated margin and can vary in size. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
