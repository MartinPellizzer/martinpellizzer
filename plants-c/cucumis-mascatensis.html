Cucumis mascatensis

Cucumis mascatensis, commonly known as the Muscat cucumber, is a rare and unique species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a slightly musky aroma. The plant produces yellow flowers and thrives in arid environments, adapting to the harsh climate of its native region. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
