Catasetum atratum

Catasetum atratum, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique floral structure. It produces fragrant flowers that are often pollinated by specific species of bees.

Catasetum atratum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses.
