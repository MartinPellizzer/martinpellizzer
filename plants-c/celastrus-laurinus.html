Celastrus laurinus

Celastrus laurinus, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright purple berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Celastrus laurinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
