Chamomilla vulgaris

Chamomilla vulgaris, commonly known as German chamomile, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a slender, branched stem and produces daisy-like yellow flowers with white petals. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties. The essential oil extracted from its flowers contains compounds such as bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and calming effects.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for herbal gardens.
