Cynanchum mairei

Cynanchum mairei, commonly known as the Maire's dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans.

It is often found in moist woodlands and rocky slopes, thriving in temperate climates.
