Cousinia eryngioides

Cousinia eryngioides, commonly known as the silverleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spiny, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia eryngioides is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in steppe and desert regions.
