Chrysanthemum alpinum f. pyrenaicum

Chrysanthemum alpinum f. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean alpine chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to alpine environments.
