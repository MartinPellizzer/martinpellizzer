Calamus lakshmanae

Calamus lakshmanae, commonly known as the Lakshman's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically arched or curved. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by dark, fleshy berries. Native to the tropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
