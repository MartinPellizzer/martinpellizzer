Carex verae

Carex verae, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Carex verae plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
