Calceolaria patagonica

Calceolaria patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like petals that resemble a small flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its blooms, which are usually violet or purple, appear in late summer and early autumn.

Calceolaria patagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
