Campylocentrum flavum

Campylocentrum flavum, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Campylocentrum flavum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
