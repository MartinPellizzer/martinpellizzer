Chordifex

Chordifex, commonly known as the sword fern, is a type of fern characterized by its long, sword-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is fibrous and helps anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Chordifex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
