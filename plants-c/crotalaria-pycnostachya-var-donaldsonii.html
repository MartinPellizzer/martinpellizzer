Crotalaria pycnostachya var. donaldsonii

Crotalaria pycnostachya var. donaldsonii, commonly known as the narrow-stalked crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
