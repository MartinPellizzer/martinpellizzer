Cereus spegazzinii var. ebenacanthus

Cereus spegazzinii var. ebenacanthus, commonly known as the Red Hook Cereus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming at night. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a robust root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates with minimal water requirements.
