Coronaria tomentosa

Coronaria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly coronaria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its hairy foliage helps to reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
