Cheiloclinium podostemma

Cheiloclinium podostemma, commonly known as the river waterplant, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It thrives in fast-flowing freshwater environments, such as rivers and streams, where it attaches itself to submerged rocks or vegetation. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water resistance and facilitate nutrient absorption. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or yellowish and bloom near the water's surface.

Cheiloclinium podostemma plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality maintenance.
