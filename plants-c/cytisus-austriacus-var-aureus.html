Cytisus austriacus var. aureus

Cytisus austriacus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has slender, often thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
