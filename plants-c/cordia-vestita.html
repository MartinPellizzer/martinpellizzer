Cordia vestita

Cordia vestita, commonly known as the white cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Cordia vestita is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
