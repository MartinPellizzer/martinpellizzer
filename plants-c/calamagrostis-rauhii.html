Calamagrostis rauhii

Calamagrostis rauhii, commonly known as Rauh's reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, light green to golden yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis rauhii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
