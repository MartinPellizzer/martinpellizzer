Chrysopogon verticillatus

Chrysopogon verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled grass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Chrysopogon verticillatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
