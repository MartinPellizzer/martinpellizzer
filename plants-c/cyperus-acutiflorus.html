Cyperus acutiflorus

Cyperus acutiflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus acutiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
