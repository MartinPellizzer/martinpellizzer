Campanula floridana

Campanula floridana, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Campanula floridana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
