Colladonia heptaptera

Colladonia heptaptera, commonly known as the seven-lobed water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that float on the water's surface, with long, slender stems that often extend into the water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and requires adequate sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
