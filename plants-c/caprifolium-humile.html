Caprifolium humile

Caprifolium humile, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Caprifolium humile is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for birds and wildlife.
