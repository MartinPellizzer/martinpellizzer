Citrus × aurantium var. bergamia

Citrus × aurantium var. bergamia, commonly known as bergamot, is a citrus hybrid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruit. The fruit's peel, or zest, contains high concentrations of limonene and other essential oils, making it valuable in perfumery and food flavoring. The plant thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and aromatic properties.
