Comarostaphylis microcarpa

Comarostaphylis microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is typically less than 10 inches tall.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
