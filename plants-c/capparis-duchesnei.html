Capparis duchesnei

Capparis duchesnei, commonly known as the desert caper, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and can withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, tart berries. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
