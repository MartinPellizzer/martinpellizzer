Cardamine heterophylla var. hirtella

Cardamine heterophylla var. hirtella, commonly known as hairy watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, alternate leaves that are often hairy, especially along the veins, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. It produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
