Cocculus lucidus

Cocculus lucidus, commonly known as the shining cow-plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and distinctive, waxy, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red in color. Its roots and stems contain alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Cocculus lucidus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
