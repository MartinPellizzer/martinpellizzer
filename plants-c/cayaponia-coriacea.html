Cayaponia coriacea

Cayaponia coriacea, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a woody plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Cayaponia coriacea is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
