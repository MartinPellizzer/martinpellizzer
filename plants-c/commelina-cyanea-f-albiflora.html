Commelina cyanea f. albiflora

Commelina cyanea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bluebell, is a variant of the Commelina cyanea species characterized by its striking white flowers.

This plant belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The white petals contrast sharply with the blue-violet bracts, making it visually distinctive.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
