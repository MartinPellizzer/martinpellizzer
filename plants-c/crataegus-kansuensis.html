Crataegus kansuensis

Crataegus kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
