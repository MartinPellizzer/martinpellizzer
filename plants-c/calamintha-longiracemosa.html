Calamintha longiracemosa

Calamintha longiracemosa, commonly known as longracemose germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in long, branched clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a fragrant, mint-like scent.

Calamintha longiracemosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
