Callothlaspi antitauricum

Callothlaspi antitauricum, commonly known as the Tauric thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Taurus Mountains in Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish composite flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Callothlaspi antitauricum is often found in open habitats such as steppes and mountain slopes.
