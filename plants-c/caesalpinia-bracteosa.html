Caesalpinia bracteosa

Caesalpinia bracteosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
