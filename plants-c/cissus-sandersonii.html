Cissus sandersonii

Cissus sandersonii, commonly known as the sanderson's devil's ivy, is a tropical vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It is a member of the family Convulvulaceae and is well adapted to humid, shaded environments.
