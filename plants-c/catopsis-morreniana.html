Catopsis morreniana

Catopsis morreniana, commonly known as the Morren's catopsis, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant's foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the rosette.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
