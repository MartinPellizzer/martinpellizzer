Clarkia purpurea var. hirsuta

Clarkia purpurea var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers and hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The hairy clarkia is often found in the Sierra Nevada and other mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
