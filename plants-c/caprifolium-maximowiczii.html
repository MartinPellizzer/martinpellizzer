Caprifolium maximowiczii

Caprifolium maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Far East of Russia and parts of northern China.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red berries that ripen in late summer and are edible for birds and small mammals.

Caprifolium maximowiczii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
