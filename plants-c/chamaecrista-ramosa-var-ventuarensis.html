Chamaecrista ramosa var. ventuarensis

Chamaecrista ramosa var. ventuarensis, commonly known as the Texas coralbean, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
