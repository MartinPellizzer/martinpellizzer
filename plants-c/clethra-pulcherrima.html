Clethra pulcherrima

Clethra pulcherrima, commonly known as the fragrant sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter beneath. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. These flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
