Calceolaria tenuifolia

Calceolaria tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender slipperflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
