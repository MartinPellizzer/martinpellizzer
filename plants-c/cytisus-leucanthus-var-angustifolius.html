Cytisus leucanthus var. angustifolius

Cytisus leucanthus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it both ecologically and horticulturally significant.
