Candollea corymbosa

Candollea corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bristly corymbosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in corymbs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Candollea corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
