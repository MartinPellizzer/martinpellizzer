Cyanotis longifolia var. bakeriana

Cyanotis longifolia var. bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
