Cupressus benthamii var. arizonica

Cupressus benthamii var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or sandy soils.
