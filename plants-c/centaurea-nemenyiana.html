Centaurea nemenyiana

Centaurea nemenyiana, commonly known as the Nemenyian's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking blue to purple ray flowers surrounding a yellow disk, typical of the genus Centaurea. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
