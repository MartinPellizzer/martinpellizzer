Cyperus aureus

Cyperus aureus, commonly known as golden flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with whorled leaves that are often bright green or reddish in color. Its flowering spikes are usually triangular and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus aureus is often found in wetland habitats and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.
