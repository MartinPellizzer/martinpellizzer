Cladium filiforme

Cladium filiforme, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in saturated soils. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cladium filiforme plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
