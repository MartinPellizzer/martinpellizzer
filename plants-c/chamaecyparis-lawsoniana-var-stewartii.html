Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. stewartii

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. stewartii, commonly known as the Lawson cypress, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive pyramidal shape.

It features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its elegant form and resistance to pests and diseases.
