Calliandra penduliflora

Calliandra penduliflora, commonly known as the weeping calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra penduliflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
