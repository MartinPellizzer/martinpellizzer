Carex × josephi-schmittii

Carex × josephi-schmittii, commonly known as the Schmitt's sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces cylindrical spikes of pale to brownish flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
