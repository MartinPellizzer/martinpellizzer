Cirsium erisithales subsp. rivulare

Cirsium erisithales subsp.

rivulare, commonly known as the water thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
