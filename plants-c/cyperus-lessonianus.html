Cyperus lessonianus

Cyperus lessonianus, commonly known as the marsh clubmoss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus lessonianus thrives in moist soils and is often found in swampy areas, riverbanks, and wet meadows.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
