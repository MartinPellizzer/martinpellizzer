Cestrum dumetorum

Cestrum dumetorum, commonly known as the "Whitewash" or "Chuparosa," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the evening and emit a strong, sweet scent. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

However, it is also considered invasive in some regions and can have toxic effects on livestock and humans.
