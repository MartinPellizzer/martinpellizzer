Carex alterniflora var. rubrovaginata

Carex alterniflora var. rubrovaginata, commonly known as the red-barked sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. The plant is notable for its distinctive reddish-brown sheathing leaves at the base, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and streambanks, and is often found in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
