Cassia keyensis

Cassia keyensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces long, narrow, dark green leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cassia keyensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
