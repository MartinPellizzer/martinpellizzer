Cucubalus macrophyllus

Cucubalus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf cucumber gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and elongated, pale green fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the plant is known for its vigorous vine growth habit.
