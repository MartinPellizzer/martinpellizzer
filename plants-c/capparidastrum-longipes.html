Capparidastrum longipes

Capparidastrum longipes, commonly known as the longipes spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Capparaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and fleshy, often hairy leaves. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable plant in various environments.
