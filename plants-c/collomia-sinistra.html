Collomia sinistra

Collomia sinistra, commonly known as the left-side bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Collomia sinistra is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
