Cichorium intybus f. album

Cichorium intybus f. album, commonly known as white endive, is a cultivar of the chicory plant characterized by its pale, blanched leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves, which are used in salads and cooked dishes. Its leaves are crisp and slightly bitter, with a mild, nutty flavor.

This variety is often cultivated in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
