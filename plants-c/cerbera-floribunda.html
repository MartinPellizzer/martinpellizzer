Cerbera floribunda

Cerbera floribunda, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a glossy appearance.

Cerbera floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
