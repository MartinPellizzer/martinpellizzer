Cyperus niloticus

Cyperus niloticus, commonly known as the Nile sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Cyperus niloticus is often used in ornamental horticulture and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
