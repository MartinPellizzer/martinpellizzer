Crataegus × curonica

Crataegus × curonica, commonly known as the Curonic hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus crus-galli.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and dense foliage. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically oval or ovate, with serrated edges, and the plant is adapted to a variety of soil types.
