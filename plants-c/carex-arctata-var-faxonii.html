Carex arctata var. faxonii

Carex arctata var. faxonii, commonly known as the Faxon's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, and its rhizomes contribute to its ability to spread and form dense colonies.

This species is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to waterlogged soils.
