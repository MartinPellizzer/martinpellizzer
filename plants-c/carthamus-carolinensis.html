Carthamus carolinensis

Carthamus carolinensis, commonly known as the blackweed or Carolina thistle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in wet or disturbed soils, often near waterways or in moist meadows. The plant features upright stems with spiny leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers. Its seeds are small, dark, and have a distinctive crown-like structure, which aids in wind dispersal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing characteristics with other sunflowers and daisies.
