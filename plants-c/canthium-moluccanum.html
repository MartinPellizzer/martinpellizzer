Canthium moluccanum

Canthium moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its production of a milky sap. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in cup-shaped structures called cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
