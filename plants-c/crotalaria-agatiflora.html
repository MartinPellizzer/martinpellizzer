Crotalaria agatiflora

Crotalaria agatiflora, commonly known as the African leadplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
