Cerastium subpilosum var. takasagomontana

Cerastium subpilosum var. takasagomontana, commonly known as the Takasago snowdrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
