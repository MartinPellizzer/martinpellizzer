Cynorkis aurantiaca

Cynorkis aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
