Celtis kajewskii

Celtis kajewskii, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn yellow or red when mature.

Celtis kajewskii is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
