Combretum sublancifolium

Combretum sublancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Combretum sublancifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
