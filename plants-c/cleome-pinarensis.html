Cleome pinarensis

Cleome pinarensis, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with simple, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cleome pinarensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
