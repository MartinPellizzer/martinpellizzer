Clerodendrum angolense

Clerodendrum angolense, commonly known as the Angola flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically opposite and have a slightly serrated margin.
