Cleisostoma demangei

Cleisostoma demangei, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers that resemble flames, making it a visually appealing plant. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are borne on slender inflorescences and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
