Carissa edulis subsp. continentalis

Carissa edulis subsp.

continentalis, commonly known as the continental yellowball, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems and oval, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange to red berries, which are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
