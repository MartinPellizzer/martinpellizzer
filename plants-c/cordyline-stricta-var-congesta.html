Cordyline stricta var. congesta

Cordyline stricta var. congesta, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features a tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer that resembles bark. The plant produces a dense crown of long, narrow, dark green leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
