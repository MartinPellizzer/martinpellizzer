Cymbidium sutepense

Cymbidium sutepense, commonly known as the Sutep orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems that bear several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with numerous fragrant, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lip being prominently marked.

Cymbidium sutepense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
