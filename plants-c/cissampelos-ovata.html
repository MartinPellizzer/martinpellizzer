Cissampelos ovata

Cissampelos ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn orange when mature. It is often found growing in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
