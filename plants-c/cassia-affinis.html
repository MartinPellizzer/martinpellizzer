Cassia affinis

Cassia affinis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds. Cassia affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.
