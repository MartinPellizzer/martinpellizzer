Citharexylum ellipticum

Citharexylum ellipticum, commonly known as the broadleaf cithara or sweet cithara, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features opposite, elliptic-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a slightly waxy texture. Its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom in summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
