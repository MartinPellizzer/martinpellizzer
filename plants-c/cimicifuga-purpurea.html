Cimicifuga purpurea

Cimicifuga purpurea, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features tall, unbranched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are followed by bright red berries. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in herbal medicine.

Cimicifuga purpurea is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating menopausal symptoms and musculoskeletal disorders.
