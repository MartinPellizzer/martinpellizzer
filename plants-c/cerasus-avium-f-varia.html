Cerasus avium f. varia

Cerasus avium f. varia, commonly known as the variable cherry, is a botanical variant of the sweet cherry.

It is characterized by its diverse flower and fruit colors, which can range from white to pink and red. This form exhibits genetic variability, making it a subject of interest in horticultural research. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with dense foliage and showy blooms.

Its adaptability to different climates contributes to its cultivation in various regions.
