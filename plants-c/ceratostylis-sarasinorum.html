Ceratostylis sarasinorum

Ceratostylis sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, hooded flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
