Chamaelirium hisauchianum subsp. kurohimense

Chamaelirium hisauchianum subsp.

kurohimense, commonly known as the Japanese tansy, is a perennial herb native to Japan. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin, and the flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbrella-like structures.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
