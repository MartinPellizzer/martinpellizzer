Calea harlingii

Calea harlingii, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night. The plant has a short, upright stem with broad, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive central disk.

Calea harlingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
