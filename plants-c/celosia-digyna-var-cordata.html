Celosia digyna var. cordata

Celosia digyna var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf celosia, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like flower spikes that come in shades of red, pink, and orange. The leaves are heart-shaped, giving the plant its common name, and they are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This variety is prized for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
