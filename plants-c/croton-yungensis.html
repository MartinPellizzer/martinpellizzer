Croton yungensis

Croton yungensis, commonly known as Yung's croton, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to the highland regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton yungensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
