Catasetum bungerothii var. aurantiacum

Catasetum bungerothii var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden Catasetum, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers, which are often adorned with dark red or purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with showy, spurred petals that attract specific pollinators.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
