Collabium ovalifolium

Collabium ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf collabium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a preference for well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Collabium ovalifolium is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
