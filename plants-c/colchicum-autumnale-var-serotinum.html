Colchicum autumnale var. serotinum

Colchicum autumnale var. serotinum, commonly known as the autumn crocus or meadow saffron, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spurred flowers that resemble those of true crocuses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
