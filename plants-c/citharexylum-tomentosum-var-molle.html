Citharexylum tomentosum var. molle

Citharexylum tomentosum var. molle, commonly known as the silky cithara, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
