Calamus microcarpus var. diminutus

Calamus microcarpus var. diminutus, commonly known as the dwarf calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a compact, herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features a stout, creeping rhizome with narrow, linear leaves that are often a glossy green. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
