Cleome radula

Cleome radula, commonly known as the red cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers with prominent stamens. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy, branching stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome radula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
