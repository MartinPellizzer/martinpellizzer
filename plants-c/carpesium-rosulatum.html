Carpesium rosulatum

Carpesium rosulatum, commonly known as the rose-like thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
