Chaenostoma hereroense

Chaenostoma hereroense, commonly known as the Herero waterwheel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in a whorled pattern, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
