Chrysothamnus elegans

Chrysothamnus elegans, commonly known as the elegant goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with woody stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
