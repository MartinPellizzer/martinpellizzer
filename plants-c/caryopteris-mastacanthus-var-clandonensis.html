Caryopteris mastacanthus var. clandonensis

Caryopteris mastacanthus var. clandonensis, commonly known as the Clandonensis bluemouth, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.
