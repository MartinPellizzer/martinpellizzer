Callisia cordifolia

Callisia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a groundcover or trailing plant, often found in moist, shady environments. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and silver, adding visual interest to gardens.

Callisia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
