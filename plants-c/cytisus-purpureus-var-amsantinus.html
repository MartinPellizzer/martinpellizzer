Cytisus purpureus var. amsantinus

Cytisus purpureus var. amsantinus, commonly known as the Amsantinus Scotch broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to various arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
