Centaurea gerhardii

Centaurea gerhardii, commonly known as Gerhard's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly foliage. This perennial species thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Centaurea gerhardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
