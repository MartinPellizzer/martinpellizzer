Cornutia grandifolia var. quadrangularis

Cornutia grandifolia var. quadrangularis, commonly known as the quadrangular cornutia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
