Crataegus grossulariifolia

Crataegus grossulariifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptical leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
