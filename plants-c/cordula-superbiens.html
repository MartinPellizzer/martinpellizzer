Cordula superbiens

Cordula superbiens, commonly known as the grand cordula, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, round, green berries that contain the seeds. Cordula superbiens thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
