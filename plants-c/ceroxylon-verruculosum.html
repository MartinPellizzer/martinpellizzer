Ceroxylon verruculosum

Ceroxylon verruculosum, commonly known as the Andean wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that give it its common name, and it can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has a solitary trunk and produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for thatching.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
