Cerefolium sativum

Cerefolium sativum, commonly known as the edible cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its peppery taste, which is used in salads and as a garnish.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to reseed itself make it a popular choice for home gardens.
