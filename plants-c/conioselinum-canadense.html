Conioselinum canadense

Conioselinum canadense, commonly known as Canadian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and along roadsides.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
