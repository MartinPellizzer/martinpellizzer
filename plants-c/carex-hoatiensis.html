Carex hoatiensis

Carex hoatiensis, commonly known as the Hoatiensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Carex hoatiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
