Cyanotis pauciflora

Cyanotis pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream sides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Cyanotis pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
