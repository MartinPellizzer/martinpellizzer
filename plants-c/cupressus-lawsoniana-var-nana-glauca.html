Cupressus lawsoniana var. nana-glauca

Cupressus lawsoniana var. nana-glauca, commonly known as the dwarf blue-cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree.

It features dense, scale-like foliage that is a striking blue-gray color, giving it a unique ornamental appearance. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 3 meters, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting good drought tolerance once established.

Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in landscapes.
