Chamaecytisus spinescens subsp. creticus

Chamaecytisus spinescens subsp.

creticus, commonly known as the Cretan broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
