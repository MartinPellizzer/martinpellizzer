Castanea margaretta var. angustifolia

Castanea margaretta var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chestnut, is a variant of the chestnut genus native to specific regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and produces edible nuts encased in spiny husks. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and small, inconspicuous flowers that precede the nut development.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local ecosystems.
