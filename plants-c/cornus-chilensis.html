Cornus chilensis

Cornus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its attractive white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Cornus chilensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
