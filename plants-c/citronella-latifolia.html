Citronella latifolia

Citronella latifolia, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks covered in fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are known for their strong lemon scent and mosquito-repelling qualities.

Citronella latifolia is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
