Corydalis bulbosa subsp. cava

Corydalis bulbosa subsp.

cava, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to purple flowers with two prominent upper petals. Its bulbous root system is adapted for survival in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments.
