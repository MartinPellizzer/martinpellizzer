Coleus seretii

Coleus seretii, commonly known as the African shield leaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often velvety foliage, which can display shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped or ovate, with a glossy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
