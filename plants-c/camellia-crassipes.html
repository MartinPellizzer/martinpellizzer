Camellia crassipes

Camellia crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture in the soil.

Camellia crassipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
