Crypsinus quasidivaricatus

Crypsinus quasidivaricatus, commonly known as the variable twining vine, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its twining habit and can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
