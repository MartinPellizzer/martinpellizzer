Calceolaria nivalis subsp. nivalis

Calceolaria nivalis subsp.

nivalis, commonly known as the snow calceolaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that resemble small cups, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather conditions. Calceolaria nivalis subsp.

nivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
