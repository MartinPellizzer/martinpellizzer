Chorizanthe vaginata

Chorizanthe vaginata, commonly known as desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
