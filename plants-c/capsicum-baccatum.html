Capsicum baccatum

Capsicum baccatum, commonly known as the "Peru cherry" or "Andean pepper," is a species of chili pepper native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, green, fleshy fruits that mature to a vibrant red or orange.

Capsicum baccatum is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional Andean cuisine and herbal medicine.
