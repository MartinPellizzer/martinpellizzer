Cephalotaxus drupacea f. sphaeralis

Cephalotaxus drupacea f. sphaeralis, commonly known as the spherical yew, is a unique variant of the species characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant features scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, red berries are an important feature, though the plant is toxic and should be handled with care.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
