Croton serratifolius

Croton serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves that are usually a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in regions with suitable climates.
