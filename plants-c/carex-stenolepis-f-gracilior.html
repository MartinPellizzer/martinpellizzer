Carex stenolepis f. gracilior

Carex stenolepis f. gracilior, commonly known as the slender-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex stenolepis species, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, typically found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wetland environments.

This form is distinguished by its more slender leaves compared to the typical form of the species.
