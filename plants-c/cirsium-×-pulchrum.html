Cirsium × pulchrum

Cirsium × pulchrum, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue flower heads and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × pulchrum is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
