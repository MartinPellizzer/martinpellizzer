Chrysanthellum abyssinicum

Chrysanthellum abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, velvety leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
