Canna iridiflora var. ehemannii

Canna iridiflora var. ehemannii, commonly known as the Ehemann's canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
