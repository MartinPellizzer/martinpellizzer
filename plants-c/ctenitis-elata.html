Ctenitis elata

Ctenitis elata, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its towering structure, while its fronds play a crucial role in photosynthesis.

Ctenitis elata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
