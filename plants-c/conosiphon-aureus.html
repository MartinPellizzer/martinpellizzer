Conosiphon aureus

Conosiphon aureus, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to store water in the humid, shaded environments it prefers.

Conosiphon aureus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
