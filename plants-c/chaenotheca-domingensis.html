Chaenotheca domingensis

Chaenotheca domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican cup lichen, is a member of the family Verrucariaceae.

It is a saxicolous lichen, typically found growing on rocky surfaces in tropical and subtropical regions. The thallus is crustose, with a dark, leathery appearance, and it often forms dense colonies on limestone or granite. Its morphology includes a central, cup-like structure with a smooth, dark brown to black surface.

The species is characterized by its unique chemical composition, containing various secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological niche.
