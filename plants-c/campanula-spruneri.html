Campanula spruneri

Campanula spruneri, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. Campanula spruneri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
