Carya mexicana f. polyneura

Carya mexicana f. polyneura, commonly known as the Mexican hickory, is a botanical variant of the Mexican hickory species.

It is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing one or two nuts enclosed in a thick, spiny husk.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
