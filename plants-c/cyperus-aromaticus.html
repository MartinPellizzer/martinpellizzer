Cyperus aromaticus

Cyperus aromaticus, commonly known as sweet grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, clustered spikelets that produce tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight fragrance when crushed.

Sweet grass is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
