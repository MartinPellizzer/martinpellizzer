Convolvulus arvensis var. cherleri

Convolvulus arvensis var. cherleri, commonly known as the field bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to pink flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has a robust root system and can spread aggressively through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in many regions. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

Its rapid growth and persistent nature make it a challenging weed to control in agricultural and natural settings.
