Conyza obovata

Conyza obovata, commonly known as the whiteleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides. The plant features lobed leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils.

Conyza obovata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
