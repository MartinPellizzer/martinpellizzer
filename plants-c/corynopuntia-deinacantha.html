Corynopuntia deinacantha

Corynopuntia deinacantha, commonly known as the Mexican blue cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Corynopuntia deinacantha is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
