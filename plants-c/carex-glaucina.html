Carex glaucina

Carex glaucina, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among the foliage, making it less showy than some other sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex glaucina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
