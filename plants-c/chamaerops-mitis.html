Chamaerops mitis

Chamaerops mitis, commonly known as the windmill palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of the Middle East. This palm is characterized by its slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, wind-swept appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is relatively drought-tolerant and can adapt to various climate conditions, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
