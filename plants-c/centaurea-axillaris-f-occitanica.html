Centaurea axillaris f. occitanica

Centaurea axillaris f. occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
