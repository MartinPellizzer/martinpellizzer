Citrus × canaliculata

Citrus × canaliculata, commonly known as the canary bell orange.

It is a hybrid citrus species that exhibits characteristics of both sweet and sour oranges, with a distinctive elongated, bell-shaped fruit. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, glossy, and dark green, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns orange, and it is valued for its edible pulp and aromatic properties.
