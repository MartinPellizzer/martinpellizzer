Chamaecyparis pisifera var. plumosa-aurea

Chamaecyparis pisifera var. plumosa-aurea, commonly known as the golden thread cedar, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery foliage that gives it a soft, airy appearance. The leaves are a striking golden-yellow color, especially in full sun, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. It is a slow-growing evergreen with a pyramidal shape that becomes more spreading with age.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and is relatively cold-hardy, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
