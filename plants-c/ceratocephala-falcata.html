Ceratocephala falcata

Ceratocephala falcata, commonly known as the sicklehead fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with a feathery appearance, and it produces small, clustered spores for reproduction.

Ceratocephala falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
