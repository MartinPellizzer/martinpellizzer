Carduus undulatus

Carduus undulatus, commonly known as the undulating thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often branched.

The undulating thistle is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
