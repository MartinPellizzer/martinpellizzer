Cassine bupleuroides

Cassine bupleuroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

Cassine bupleuroides is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
