Chamaemespilus ambigua

Chamaemespilus ambigua, commonly known as the common snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its foliage is characterized by ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it has a distinctive, aromatic scent.
