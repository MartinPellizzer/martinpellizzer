Cyperus tenax

Cyperus tenax, commonly known as zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshy areas or along waterways. Cyperus tenax produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
