Carex buxbaumii

Carex buxbaumii, commonly known as the Buxbaum's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-gray hue that adds visual interest to gardens.

Carex buxbaumii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
