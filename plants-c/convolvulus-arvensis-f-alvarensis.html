Convolvulus arvensis f. alvarensis

Convolvulus arvensis f. alvarensis, commonly known as the Alvares' field bindweed, is a variant of the common field bindweed, characterized by its distinct floral morphology.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It has a robust root system that allows it to spread aggressively, making it a challenging weed to control.

Its botanical traits make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
