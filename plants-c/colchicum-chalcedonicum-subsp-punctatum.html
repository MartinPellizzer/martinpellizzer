Colchicum chalcedonicum subsp. punctatum

Colchicum chalcedonicum subsp.

punctatum, commonly known as the spotted autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking purple to lilac flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted with white or green markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal and toxic properties, containing colchicine, a compound used in medical treatments.
