Ceanothus chloroxylon

Ceanothus chloroxylon, commonly known as the bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Ceanothus chloroxylon is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
