Convolvulus calvertii var. tauricus

Convolvulus calvertii var. tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth forms.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
