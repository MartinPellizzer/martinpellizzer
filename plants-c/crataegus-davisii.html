Crataegus davisii

Crataegus davisii, commonly known as Davis hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly found in Arizona and New Mexico. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that matures in the summer, attracting various bird species.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
