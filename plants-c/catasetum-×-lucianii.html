Catasetum × lucianii

Catasetum × lucianii, commonly known as the orchid with the unique floral structure, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that feature a specialized column for pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store moisture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
