Cucumis anguria var. longaculeatus

Cucumis anguria var. longaculeatus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a unique variant of the anguria cucumber species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, horn-like projections along its stem and fruit. The plant is a climbing vine that produces elongated, thin fruits with a mild, slightly bitter flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing traits with other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons.

Its structure and growth habit make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and cultivation.
