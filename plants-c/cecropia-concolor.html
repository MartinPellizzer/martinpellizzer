Cecropia concolor

Cecropia concolor, commonly known as the common ceiba, is a fast-growing tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, swollen, hollow stems that provide habitat for various animals. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

Cecropia concolor is often used in reforestation projects due to its rapid growth and ecological benefits.
