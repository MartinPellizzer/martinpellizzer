Cnemidostachys salicifolia

Cnemidostachys salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in spikes, with a delicate, greenish-yellow color.

Cnemidostachys salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
