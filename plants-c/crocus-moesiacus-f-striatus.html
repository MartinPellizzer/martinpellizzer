Crocus moesiacus f. striatus

Crocus moesiacus f. striatus, commonly known as the Striped Crocus, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, multi-colored flowers.

This cultivar features petals with distinctive stripes, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red, creating a visually appealing display. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the Balkan region of Europe. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering crocuses.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.
