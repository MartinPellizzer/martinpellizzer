Campomanesia fusca var. subtriflora

Campomanesia fusca var. subtriflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tart flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
