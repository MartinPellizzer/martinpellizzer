Carex chrysoleuca

Carex chrysoleuca, commonly known as the golden sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, slender, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex chrysoleuca plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
