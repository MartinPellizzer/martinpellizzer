Cotoneaster kansuensis

Cotoneaster kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces clusters of bright red berries in the fall. Cotoneaster kansuensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in marginal environments.
