Cupressus lusitanica glauca-pendula

Cupressus lusitanica glauca-pendula, commonly known as the blue-gray weeping cedar, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to Portugal.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a weeping appearance, with small, scale-like leaves that are a striking blue-gray color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
