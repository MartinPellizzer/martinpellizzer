Ceropegia penicillata

Ceropegia penicillata, commonly known as the spiral-shaped milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-arranged leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Asclepiadaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia penicillata is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
