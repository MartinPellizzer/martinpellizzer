Clidemia cinerea

Clidemia cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clidemia cinerea is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
