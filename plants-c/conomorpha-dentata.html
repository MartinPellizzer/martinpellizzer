Conomorpha dentata

Conomorpha dentata, commonly known as the serrated leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Conomorpha dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
