Cyperus wrightii

Cyperus wrightii, commonly known as Wright's flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic plants.

Cyperus wrightii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
