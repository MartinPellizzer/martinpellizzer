Cupania inelegans

Cupania inelegans, commonly known as the Brazilian elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
