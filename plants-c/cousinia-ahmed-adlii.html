Cousinia ahmed-adlii

Cousinia ahmed-adlii, commonly known as the Kashmiri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while the flowers are small and yellowish, often arranged in a cluster.

Cousinia ahmed-adlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
