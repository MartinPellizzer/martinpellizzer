Cirsium ledebourii

Cirsium ledebourii, commonly known as the Le-debour's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish-purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cirsium ledebourii is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Asteraceae family.
