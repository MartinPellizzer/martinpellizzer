Crocus angustifolius

Crocus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant produces striking purple or violet flowers with six petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a corm, which is a type of underground storage organ.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
