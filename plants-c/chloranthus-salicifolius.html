Chloranthus salicifolius

Chloranthus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leafed chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, willow-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has a short stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
