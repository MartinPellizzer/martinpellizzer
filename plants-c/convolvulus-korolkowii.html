Convolvulus korolkowii

Convolvulus korolkowii, commonly known as the Korolkow's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its flowers bloom in the morning and close by afternoon, hence its common name.
