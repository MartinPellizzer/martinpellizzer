Caucalis lappula

Caucalis lappula, commonly known as bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or white flowers clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caucalis lappula thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.
