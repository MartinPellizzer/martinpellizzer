Cayaponia calycina

Cayaponia calycina, commonly known as the Cayaponia flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.
