Cylindropuntia davisii

Cylindropuntia davisii, commonly known as the Davis hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically followed by red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
