Cynanchum rusbyi

Cynanchum rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pink or reddish.

The plant produces seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
