Crataegus praearmata

Crataegus praearmata, commonly known as the prickly pear or thorned hawthorn, is a species of hawthorn native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Crataegus praearmata plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
