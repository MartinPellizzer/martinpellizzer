Campomanesia apaensis

Campomanesia apaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and are often used in jams, juices, and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental foliage and fruit.
