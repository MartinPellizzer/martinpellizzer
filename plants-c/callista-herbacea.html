Callista herbacea

Callista herbacea, commonly known as the common callista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green ray florets, creating a distinctive appearance.

Callista herbacea is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
