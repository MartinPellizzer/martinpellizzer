Clerodendrum schultzei

Clerodendrum schultzei, commonly known as the Schultze's flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Its stem is woody and can develop a reddish hue, especially in full sun.

Clerodendrum schultzei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
