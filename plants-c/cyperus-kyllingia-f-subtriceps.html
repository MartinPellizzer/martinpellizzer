Cyperus kyllingia f. subtriceps

Cyperus kyllingia f. subtriceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
