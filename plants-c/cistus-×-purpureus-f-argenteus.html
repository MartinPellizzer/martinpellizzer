Cistus × purpureus f. argenteus

Cistus × purpureus f. argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to purple flowers in late spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
