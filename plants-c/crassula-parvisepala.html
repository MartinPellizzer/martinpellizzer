Crassula parvisepala

Crassula parvisepala, commonly known as the miniature crassula or small-leaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of small, fleshy, silver-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its low maintenance and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
