Campanula rotundifolia var. pubescens

Campanula rotundifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
