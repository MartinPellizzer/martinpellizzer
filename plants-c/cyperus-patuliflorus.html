Cyperus patuliflorus

Cyperus patuliflorus, commonly known as the broadflower sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering stems are usually tall and slender, bearing small, clustered flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus patuliflorus is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.
