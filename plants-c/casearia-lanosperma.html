Casearia lanosperma

Casearia lanosperma, commonly known as the woolly casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, shiny surface.

Casearia lanosperma is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
