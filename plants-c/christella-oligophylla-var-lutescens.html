Christella oligophylla var. lutescens

Christella oligophylla var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Christella, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
