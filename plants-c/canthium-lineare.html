Canthium lineare

Canthium lineare, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and linear in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a characteristic structure of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Canthium lineare is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
