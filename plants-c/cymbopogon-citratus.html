Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus, commonly known as lemongrass, is a tropical perennial grass native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant contains essential oils rich in citral, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and therapeutic uses.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
