Ceropegia virescens

Ceropegia virescens, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Ceropegia virescens thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
