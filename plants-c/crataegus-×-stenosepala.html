Crataegus × stenosepala

Crataegus × stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, often white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a red or yellow berry that matures in the fall, and the plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
