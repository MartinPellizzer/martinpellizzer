Chrysophyllum splendens

Chrysophyllum splendens, commonly known as the golden apple or sweet apple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a bright orange or red, resembling small apples. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, tangy flavor, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
