Carex laxiflora var. minor

Carex laxiflora var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
