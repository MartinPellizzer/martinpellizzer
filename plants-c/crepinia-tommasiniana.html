Crepinia tommasiniana

Crepinia tommasiniana, commonly known as the Tommasin's sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor aquatic environments.

Crepinia tommasiniana plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
