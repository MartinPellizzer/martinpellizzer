Camelina laxa

Camelina laxa, commonly known as the slender camelina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Camelina laxa is also noted for its potential as a source of oilseed for agricultural use.
