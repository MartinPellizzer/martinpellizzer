Cordia sellowiana

Cordia sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Sowell's cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Cordia sellowiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
