Cestrum humboldtii var. calycinum

Cestrum humboldtii var. calycinum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters along the stems. The plant is a vigorous climber with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
