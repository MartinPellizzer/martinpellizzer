Cirsium blumeri

Cirsium blumeri, commonly known as Blumer's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring spiny leaves and a robust, upright stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing purple to pinkish hues.

Cirsium blumeri is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
