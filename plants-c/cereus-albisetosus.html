Cereus albisetosus

Cereus albisetosus, commonly known as the white-bristled cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and dense clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Cereus albisetosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
