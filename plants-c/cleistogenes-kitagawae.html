Cleistogenes kitagawae

Cleistogenes kitagawae, commonly known as the Korean false brome, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and forest margins.

Cleistogenes kitagawae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
