Clianthus puniceus var. magnificus

Clianthus puniceus var. magnificus, commonly known as the "New Zealand flax" or "mountain flax," is a striking member of the Fabaceae family native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink bracts that surround its small, pea-like flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting native pollinators and stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
