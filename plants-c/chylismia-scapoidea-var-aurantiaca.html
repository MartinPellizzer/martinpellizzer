Chylismia scapoidea var. aurantiaca

Chylismia scapoidea var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden chylismia, is a strikingly colorful variant of the chylismia genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems that rise from a basal rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its vibrant colors and unique appearance.
