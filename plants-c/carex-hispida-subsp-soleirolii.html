Carex hispida subsp. soleirolii

Carex hispida subsp.

soleirolii, commonly known as the soleirolii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in moist or wet environments.

The plant features dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in shaded, damp areas such as woodland floors or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
