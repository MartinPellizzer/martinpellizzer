Carya alba subsp. ovata

Carya alba subsp.

ovata, commonly known as the white hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow catkins and female flowers, followed by edible, sweet, and nut-like fruits.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
