Cymbachne guineensis subvar. viicola

Cymbachne guineensis subvar. viicola, commonly known as the African grass or Guinea grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, narrow leaves, and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
