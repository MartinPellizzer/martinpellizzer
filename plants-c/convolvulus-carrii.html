Convolvulus carrii

Convolvulus carrii, commonly known as the Carri's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its vines can spread aggressively, often climbing or trailing over other vegetation.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
