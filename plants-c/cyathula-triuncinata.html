Cyathula triuncinata

Cyathula triuncinata, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble those of the buttercup, though it is not related to the true buttercups. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Cyathula triuncinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
