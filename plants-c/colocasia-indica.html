Colocasia indica

Colocasia indica, commonly known as elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its striking foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The tubers of Colocasia indica are also used in some traditional cuisines.
