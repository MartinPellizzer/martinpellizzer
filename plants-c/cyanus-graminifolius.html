Cyanus graminifolius

Cyanus graminifolius, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its striking blue or purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving the plant its scientific name, which translates to "grass-like blue flower.".
