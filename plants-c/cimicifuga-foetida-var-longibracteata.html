Cimicifuga foetida var. longibracteata

Cimicifuga foetida var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract bugbane, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of white, lacecap flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, partially shaded environments.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
