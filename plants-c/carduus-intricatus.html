Carduus intricatus

Carduus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
