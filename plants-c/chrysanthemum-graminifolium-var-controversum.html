Chrysanthemum graminifolium var. controversum

Chrysanthemum graminifolium var. controversum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value has also made it a popular choice in horticulture.
