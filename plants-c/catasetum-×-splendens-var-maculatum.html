Catasetum × splendens var. maculatum

Catasetum × splendens var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers with distinctive spots or markings on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple showy flowers arranged in a spike, and it produces a unique, elongated ovary that is characteristic of the Catasetum genus.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
