Cytisus albus var. pallidus

Cytisus albus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
