Cleisostoma longifolium

Cleisostoma longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved hooded orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces intricate, hooded flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
