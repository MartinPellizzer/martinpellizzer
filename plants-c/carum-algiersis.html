Carum algiersis

Carum algiersis, commonly known as Algerian caraway, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, aromatic scent.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
