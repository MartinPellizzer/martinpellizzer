Callicarpa bonplandiana

Callicarpa bonplandiana, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant red berries in the fall. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
