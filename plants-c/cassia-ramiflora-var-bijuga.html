Cassia ramiflora var. bijuga

Cassia ramiflora var. bijuga, commonly known as golden flame cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for landscape design in warm climates.
