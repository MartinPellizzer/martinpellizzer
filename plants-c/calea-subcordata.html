Calea subcordata

Calea subcordata, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Calea subcordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
