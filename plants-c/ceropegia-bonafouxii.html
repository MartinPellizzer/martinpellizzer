Ceropegia bonafouxii

Ceropegia bonafouxii, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, with a unique corona structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in tropical and subtropical regions.
