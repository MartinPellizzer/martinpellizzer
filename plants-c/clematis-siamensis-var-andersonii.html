Clematis siamensis var. andersonii

Clematis siamensis var. andersonii, commonly known as the Siam Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
