Cyperus compressotriqueter

Cyperus compressotriqueter, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, often playing a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Cyperus compressotriqueter is widely distributed across tropical and subtropical regions.
