Croton glandulosus var. septentrionalis

Croton glandulosus var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern croton, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and palmate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to wetland environments and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
