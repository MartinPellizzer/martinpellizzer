Calliandra purpurea

Calliandra purpurea, commonly known as the purple calliandra, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to purple inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
