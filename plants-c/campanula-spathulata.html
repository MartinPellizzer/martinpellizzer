Campanula spathulata

Campanula spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped to broadly ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Campanula spathulata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

It is relatively low maintenance and suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
