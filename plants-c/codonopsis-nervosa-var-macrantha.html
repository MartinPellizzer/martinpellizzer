Codonopsis nervosa var. macrantha

Codonopsis nervosa var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered codonopsis, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clustered flowers. The plant produces bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.
