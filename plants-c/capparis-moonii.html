Capparis moonii

Capparis moonii, commonly known as the moon's cress, is a flowering plant native to the western Pacific region, including parts of Australia and the Philippines.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are usually small, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Capparis moonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
