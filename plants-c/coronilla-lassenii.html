Coronilla lassenii

Coronilla lassenii, commonly known as the Lassen's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, and it has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Coronilla lassenii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
