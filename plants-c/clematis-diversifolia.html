Clematis diversifolia

Clematis diversifolia, commonly known as the variable clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually white or pink flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis diversifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various garden conditions.
