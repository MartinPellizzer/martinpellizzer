Callisia tripogandra

Callisia tripogandra, commonly known as the zigzag hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a zigzag pattern along the midrib, giving it its common name. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the summer.

Callisia tripogandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and blooms.
