Carex scoparia f. peracuta

Carex scoparia f. peracuta, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often sharply pointed. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
