Cercinia wightii

Cercinia wightii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Cercinia wightii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
