Cowania subintegra

Cowania subintegra, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowania, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
