Capparis lankesteri

Capparis lankesteri, commonly known as the Lankester's caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and features small, often yellow or white, fragrant flowers. The plant has green, spiny leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
