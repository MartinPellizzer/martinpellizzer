Crypteronia laxa

Crypteronia laxa, commonly known as the woolly crypteronia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Crypteronia laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
