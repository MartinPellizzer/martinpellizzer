Cryptocarya cordata

Cryptocarya cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in umbels, and the fruit is a small, woody drupe.

Cryptocarya cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
