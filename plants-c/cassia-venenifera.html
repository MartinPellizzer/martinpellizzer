Cassia venenifera

Cassia venenifera, commonly known as the poison cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing numerous flat, bean-like seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and woodlands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
