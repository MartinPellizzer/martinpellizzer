Cheilanthes chrysophylla

Cheilanthes chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden frond fern, is a species of fern native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, golden-yellow fronds that give it its common name. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cheilanthes chrysophylla is a perennial plant that displays a unique adaptation to its harsh environment, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
