Cerastium eriophorum f. glandulosovillosum

Cerastium eriophorum f. glandulosovillosum, commonly known as the hairy cushion mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas, and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal habitats.
