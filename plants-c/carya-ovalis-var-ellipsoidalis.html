Carya ovalis var. ellipsoidalis

Carya ovalis var. ellipsoidalis, commonly known as the ellipsoidalis hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow catkins in early spring, followed by edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks.

Its fruit is typically harvested in the fall and is valued for its nutty flavor and culinary uses.
