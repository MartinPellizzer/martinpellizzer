Coreocarpus involutus

Coreocarpus involutus, commonly known as the spiral-leaf coreocarpus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive spiral-shaped fruits. Its leaves are narrow and often curl inward, giving the plant its characteristic appearance.

Coreocarpus involutus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
