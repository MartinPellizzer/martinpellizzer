Centaurea polyclada var. subciliata

Centaurea polyclada var. subciliata, commonly known as the silky centaurea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers, which are surrounded by pale blue or white ray florets. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
