Caperonia castaneifolia f. succulenta

Caperonia castaneifolia f. succulenta, commonly known as the succulent form of the chestnut-leaf caperonia, is a unique variant of the base species.

It exhibits a more compact growth habit with thickened, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture, enhancing its drought tolerance. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value.

This form is prized in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic appeal in dry garden settings.
