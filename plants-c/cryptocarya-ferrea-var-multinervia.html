Cryptocarya ferrea var. multinervia

Cryptocarya ferrea var. multinervia, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It is often found in coastal and sclerophyllous forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The leaves and bark contain essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
