Cirsium × haynaldii

Cirsium × haynaldii, commonly known as the Haynald's thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive tendencies, it is sometimes cultivated but requires careful management.
