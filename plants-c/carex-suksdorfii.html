Carex suksdorfii

Carex suksdorfii, commonly known as Saksdorf's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
