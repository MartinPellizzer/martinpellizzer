Chamaecrista schmitzii

Chamaecrista schmitzii, commonly known as Schmitz's coralbean, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Chamaecrista schmitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
