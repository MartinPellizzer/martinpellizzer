Clematis stenosepala

Clematis stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a woody stem with thorns and is known for its vigorous growth habit.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
