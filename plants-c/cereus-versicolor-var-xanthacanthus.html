Cereus versicolor var. xanthacanthus

Cereus versicolor var. xanthacanthus, commonly known as the yellow-spined cactus, is a hybrid species belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often exhibit a pale green to blue-gray coloration. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its spines are typically yellow or orange, giving it its common name.

This cactus is native to South America and is cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
