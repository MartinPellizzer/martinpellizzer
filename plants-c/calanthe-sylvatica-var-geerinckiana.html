Calanthe sylvatica var. geerinckiana

Calanthe sylvatica var. geerinckiana, commonly known as the Geerinck's forest calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the understory of dense vegetation. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in specialty orchid collections.
