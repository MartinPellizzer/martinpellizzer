Cephaelis cyanocarpa

Cephaelis cyanocarpa, commonly known as blue-fruited cephaelis, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
