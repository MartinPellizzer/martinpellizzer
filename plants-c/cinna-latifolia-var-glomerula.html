Cinna latifolia var. glomerula

Cinna latifolia var. glomerula, commonly known as the clustered cinnamon fern, is a variant of the cinnamon fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features a stout, club-shaped fertile stalk that bears spore-bearing spikes at the tip. Its leaves are typically broad and have a distinctive, slightly curled appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
