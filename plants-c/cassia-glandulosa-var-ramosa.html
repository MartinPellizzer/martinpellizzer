Cassia glandulosa var. ramosa

Cassia glandulosa var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, glandular structures that secrete a sticky substance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in terminal spikes, which are typical of the legume family. Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
