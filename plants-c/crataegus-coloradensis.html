Crataegus coloradensis

Crataegus coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in Colorado and adjacent regions. The plant is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
