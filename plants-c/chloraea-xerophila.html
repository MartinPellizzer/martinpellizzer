Chloraea xerophila

Chloraea xerophila, commonly known as the sandhill bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, featuring small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or inland sandplains.
