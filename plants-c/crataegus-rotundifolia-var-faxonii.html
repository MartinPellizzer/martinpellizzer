Crataegus rotundifolia var. faxonii

Crataegus rotundifolia var. faxonii, commonly known as the Faxon's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, often lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different light conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
