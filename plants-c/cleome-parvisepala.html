Cleome parvisepala

Cleome parvisepala, commonly known as the small-sepaled cleome, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and landscapes.

Cleome parvisepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
