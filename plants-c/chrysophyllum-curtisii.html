Chrysophyllum curtisii

Chrysophyllum curtisii, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Chrysophyllum curtisii is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
