Crataegus austrina

Crataegus austrina, commonly known as the Australian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and features thorny branches. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Crataegus austrina is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
