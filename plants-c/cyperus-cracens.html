Cyperus cracens

Cyperus cracens, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Cyperus cracens produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
