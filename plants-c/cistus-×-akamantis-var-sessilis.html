Cistus × akamantis var. sessilis

Cistus × akamantis var. sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
