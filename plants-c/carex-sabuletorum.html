Carex sabuletorum

Carex sabuletorum, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as dunes, roadsides, and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to reddish-brown flowers. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Carex sabuletorum is adapted to coastal and inland environments, often thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions.
