Conyza myrsinites

Conyza myrsinites, commonly known as white cudweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped with toothed margins. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza myrsinites is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
