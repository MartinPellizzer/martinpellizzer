Campanula gilanica

Campanula gilanica, commonly known as the Gilan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caspian region of Iran.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Campanula gilanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
