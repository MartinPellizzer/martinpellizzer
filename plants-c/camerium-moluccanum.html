Camerium moluccanum

Camerium moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan camellia, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in spring and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.
