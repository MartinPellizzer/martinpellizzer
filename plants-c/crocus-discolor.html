Crocus discolor

Crocus discolor, commonly known as the variable crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking, colorful flowers that range from white to deep purple, often with contrasting markings. The plant produces a single, showy bloom on a slender stem, typically in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

Crocus discolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early flowering.
