Colubrina asiatica var. pubescens

Colubrina asiatica var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy colubrina, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
