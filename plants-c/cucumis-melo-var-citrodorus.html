Cucumis melo var. citrodorus

Cucumis melo var. citrodorus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit with a distinctive yellow or greenish skin. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing small, yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its crisp, mildly sweet flavor and is commonly cultivated for fresh consumption.
