Clematis flammula var. roseopurpurea

Clematis flammula var. roseopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-rose Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender stems that climb using twining tendrils, and its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
