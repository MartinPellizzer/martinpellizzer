Conyza retirensis

Conyza retirensis, commonly known as desert composite or sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows low to the ground, with hairy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. Its small, white or yellow flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Conyza retirensis is known for its resilience, thriving in sandy soils and with minimal water requirements.
