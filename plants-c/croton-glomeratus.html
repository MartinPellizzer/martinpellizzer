Croton glomeratus

Croton glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Croton glomeratus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
