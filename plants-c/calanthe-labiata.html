Calanthe labiata

Calanthe labiata, commonly known as the pink-stemmed calanthe, is a perennial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulbous structure with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy bloom. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Calanthe labiata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
