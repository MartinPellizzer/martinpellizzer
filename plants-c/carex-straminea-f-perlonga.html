Carex straminea f. perlonga

Carex straminea f. perlonga, commonly known as the long-leaved sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
