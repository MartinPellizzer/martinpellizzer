Campanula cephalotes f. alba

Campanula cephalotes f. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is native to regions of Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is often a deep green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
