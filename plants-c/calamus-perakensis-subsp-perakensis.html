Calamus perakensis subsp. perakensis

Calamus perakensis subsp.

perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers.

This subspecies is often found in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich environments.
