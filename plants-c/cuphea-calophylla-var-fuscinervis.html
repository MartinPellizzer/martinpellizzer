Cuphea calophylla var. fuscinervis

Cuphea calophylla var. fuscinervis, commonly known as the red-veined cuphea, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent, reddish veins that give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
