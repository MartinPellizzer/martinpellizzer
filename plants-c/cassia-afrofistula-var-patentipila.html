Cassia afrofistula var. patentipila

Cassia afrofistula var. patentipila, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features large, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and often exudes a fragrant resin, which has traditional uses in medicine and industry.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
