Cleomella jonesii

Cleomella jonesii, commonly known as Jones' cleome, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cleomella jonesii is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through seeds or stem cuttings.
