Crataegus cognata

Crataegus cognata, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small red to orange berry, ripens in the fall and is an important food source for birds and wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and landscapes.
