Coelogyne ecarinata

Coelogyne ecarinata, commonly known as the "snake orchid," is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender inflorescence and striking, downward-facing flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves, which help it store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne ecarinata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
