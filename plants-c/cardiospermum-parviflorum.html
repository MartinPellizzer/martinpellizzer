Cardiospermum parviflorum

Cardiospermum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower heartseed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit, a hairy pod, contains seeds enclosed in a heart-shaped structure, giving it its common name.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique seed pods.
