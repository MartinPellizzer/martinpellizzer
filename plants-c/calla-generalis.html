Calla generalis

Calla generalis, commonly known as the giant calla, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and showy, white spathes that enclose the true flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a dramatic visual impact to its surroundings.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
