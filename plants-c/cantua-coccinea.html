Cantua coccinea

Cantua coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cantua coccinea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from summer to autumn.
