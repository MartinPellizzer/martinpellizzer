Clusia cochlitheca

Clusia cochlitheca, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Clusia cochlitheca is also valued for its air-purifying properties and resilience to urban conditions.
