Catasetum × splendens var. aliciae

Catasetum × splendens var. aliciae, commonly known as the Alicia's Catasetum, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red, often with contrasting patterns. The plant produces a unique structure called a "labellum," which is highly specialized and plays a role in pollination. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
