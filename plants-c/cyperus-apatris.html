Cyperus apatris

Cyperus apatris, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Cyperus apatris plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
