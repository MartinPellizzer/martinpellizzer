Cissus tinctoria

Cissus tinctoria, commonly known as the "sugar plant" or "crimson root," is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, translucent glands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's ability to thrive in poor soil conditions makes it adaptable to various environments.
