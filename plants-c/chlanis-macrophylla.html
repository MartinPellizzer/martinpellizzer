Chlanis macrophylla

Chlanis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are typically pale yellow or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as beetles and flies. Its roots system helps stabilize the sediment in water bodies, contributing to water quality.

Chlanis macrophylla plays an important ecological role in its native environment, supporting biodiversity and maintaining ecosystem balance.
