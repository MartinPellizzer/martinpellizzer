Celmisia verbascifolia

Celmisia verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly celmisia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

Celmisia verbascifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
