Centaurea × emilii-popii

Centaurea × emilii-popii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Centaurea × emilii-popii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
