Covellia courtallensis

Covellia courtallensis, commonly known as the Malabar thornapple, is a climbing plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.
