Cotoneaster japonicus

Cotoneaster japonicus, commonly known as Japanese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It grows to a height of about 3 to 6 feet and has a compact, bushy form.

Cotoneaster japonicus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
