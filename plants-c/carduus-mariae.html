Carduus mariae

Carduus mariae, commonly known as Mary's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a bitter taste, while its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Carduus mariae is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive issues and liver ailments.
