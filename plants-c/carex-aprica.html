Carex aprica

Carex aprica, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex aprica is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
