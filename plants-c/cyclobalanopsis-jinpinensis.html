Cyclobalanopsis jinpinensis

Cyclobalanopsis jinpinensis, commonly known as the Jinpin Chinese ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed acorns, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mixed forests.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a habitat provider and its contribution to soil stabilization.
