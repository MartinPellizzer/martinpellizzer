Chastenaea merianiae

Chastenaea merianiae, commonly known as the Merian's candleplant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, candle-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid native environment.

Chastenaea merianiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
