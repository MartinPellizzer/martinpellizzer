Campanula petrophila var. linoides

Campanula petrophila var. linoides, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in crevices and on limestone, showcasing its adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and linear, contributing to its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
