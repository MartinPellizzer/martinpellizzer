Callicarpa prolifera var. rubroglandulosa

Callicarpa prolifera var. rubroglandulosa, commonly known as the red glandular beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that turn a striking red when ripe. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to ecological diversity.
