Carex chorgosica

Carex chorgosica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a distinctive cylindrical inflorescence. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Carex chorgosica is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
