Cousinia bamianica

Cousinia bamianica, commonly known as the Bamian mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

Cousinia bamianica is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
