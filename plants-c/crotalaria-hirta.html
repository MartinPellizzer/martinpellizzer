Crotalaria hirta

Crotalaria hirta, commonly known as hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and palmately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, and it is often found in dry, disturbed habitats.

Crotalaria hirta is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
