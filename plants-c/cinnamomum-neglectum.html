Cinnamomum neglectum

Cinnamomum neglectum, commonly known as the lesser cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like true cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its aromatic properties.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
