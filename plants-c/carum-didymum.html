Carum didymum

Carum didymum, commonly known as two-flowered caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender stems and small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, aromatic scent.

Carum didymum is often grown for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
