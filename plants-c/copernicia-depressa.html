Copernicia depressa

Copernicia depressa, commonly known as the Amazonian palm or the wax palm, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials.

Copernicia depressa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
