Ceratandra parviflora

Ceratandra parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered spider-orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Ceratandra parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various pollinators.
