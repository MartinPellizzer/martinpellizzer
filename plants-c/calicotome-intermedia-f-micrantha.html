Calicotome intermedia f. micrantha

Calicotome intermedia f. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil and low rainfall environments.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and forage for wildlife, and its wood is used in traditional crafts and construction.
