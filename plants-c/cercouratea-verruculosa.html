Cercouratea verruculosa

Cercouratea verruculosa, commonly known as the roughleaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, delicate fronds that are covered in small, rough projections, giving it a textured appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, forming dense colonies that help stabilize the soil.

Cercouratea verruculosa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its natural filtration processes.
