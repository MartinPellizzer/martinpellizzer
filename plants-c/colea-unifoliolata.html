Colea unifoliolata

Colea unifoliolata, commonly known as the single leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, thick, fleshy leaf that serves as both a photosynthetic organ and a water storage structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent nature allows it to thrive in dry environments by minimizing water loss through reduced leaf surface area.

Colea unifoliolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
