Calamagrostis × subepigeios

Calamagrostis × subepigeios, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a hybrid grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and fluffy, silver-gray panicles that create a striking visual effect in gardens. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adding a soft texture to the landscape.

Calamagrostis × subepigeios is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
