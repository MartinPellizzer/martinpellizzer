Chenopodium album var. virescens

Chenopodium album var. virescens, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
