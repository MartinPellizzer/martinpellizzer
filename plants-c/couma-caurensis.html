Couma caurensis

Couma caurensis, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
