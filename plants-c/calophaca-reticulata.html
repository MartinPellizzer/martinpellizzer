Calophaca reticulata

Calophaca reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated calophaca, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, net-like venation patterns on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
