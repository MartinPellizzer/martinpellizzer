Cicercula cicera var. coromandelica

Cicercula cicera var. coromandelica, commonly known as the Coromandel vetch, is a perennial legume native to South Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found growing in dry, sandy soils. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its seeds are edible and have been traditionally used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
