Campomanesia sessiliflora var. lanuginosa

Campomanesia sessiliflora var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the wooly campomanesia, is a species native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its hairy, or lanuginose, leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The variety lanuginosa is distinguished by its dense pubescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
