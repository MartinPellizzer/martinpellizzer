Crataegus valida

Crataegus valida, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches, which are a characteristic feature of the genus Crataegus. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The Pacific crabapple is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
