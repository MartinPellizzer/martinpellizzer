Campanula rotundifolia subsp. eurotundifolia

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

eurotundifolia, commonly known as the European bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are typically opposite or whorled along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
