Chrysanthemum remotipinnum

Chrysanthemum remotipinnum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
