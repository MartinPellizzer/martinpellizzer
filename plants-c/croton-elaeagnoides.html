Croton elaeagnoides

Croton elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silver croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, silver-gray foliage and branching habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for arid landscapes.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
