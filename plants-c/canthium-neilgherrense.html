Canthium neilgherrense

Canthium neilgherrense, commonly known as the Nilgiri spurge, is a shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Canthium neilgherrense is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
