Cliffortia × crenulata

Cliffortia × crenulata, commonly known as the Cape bell or rock bell, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink blooms that appear in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Cliffortia × crenulata is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
