Calothamnus arcuatus

Calothamnus arcuatus, commonly known as the wavy-leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often curve in a wavy pattern. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and inland regions.

Calothamnus arcuatus is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
