Cerbera obovata

Cerbera obovata, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has broad, oval-shaped leaves and produces bright orange or yellow fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

However, due to the toxicity of its parts, it requires careful handling and is not recommended for use in areas with small children or pets.
