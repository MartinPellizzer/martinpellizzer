Calamagrostis arundinacea var. subvaria

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. subvaria, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that produce feathery, silver-green plumes during the summer months. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
