Caryocar brasiliense

Caryocar brasiliense, commonly known as the "pequi" tree, is a tropical species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a deep root system that helps stabilize the soil. Its fruits are valued for their edible seeds and oil, which are used in traditional cuisine and local industries.

The plant also plays a role in supporting biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
