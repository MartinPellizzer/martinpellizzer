Caltha violacea

Caltha violacea, commonly known as the violet buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to wet or moist soil conditions.

Caltha violacea is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
