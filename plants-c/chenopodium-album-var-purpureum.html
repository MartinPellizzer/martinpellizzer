Chenopodium album var. purpureum

Chenopodium album var. purpureum, commonly known as purple goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to rhombic leaves that are usually green with reddish or purple veins and margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dry, triangular seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

The species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
