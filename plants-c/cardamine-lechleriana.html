Cardamine lechleriana

Cardamine lechleriana, commonly known as the Lechler's watercress, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
