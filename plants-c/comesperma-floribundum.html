Comesperma floribundum

Comesperma floribundum, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, typically pink or red in color. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to salty air and well-drained soils.

Comesperma floribundum is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
