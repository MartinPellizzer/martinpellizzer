Cassia parkeriana

Cassia parkeriana, commonly known as Mexican cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It features compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of ovate, serrated leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

Cassia parkeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
