Crocus serotinus subsp. salzmannii

Crocus serotinus subsp.

salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, early spring blooms with violet-blue petals and a distinctive yellow stamen.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in dry conditions, and it is often found in alpine and coastal habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking flowers.
