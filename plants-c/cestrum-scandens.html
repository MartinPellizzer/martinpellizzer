Cestrum scandens

Cestrum scandens, commonly known as night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a vigorous, climbing shrub that produces fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and can grow up to several meters in height if supported.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
