Corydalis pseudomucronata var. cristata

Corydalis pseudomucronata var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are characterized by their unique, elongated upper petals and a distinctive, spurred lower petal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
