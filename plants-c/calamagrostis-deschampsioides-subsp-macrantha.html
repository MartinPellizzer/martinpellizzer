Calamagrostis deschampsioides subsp. macrantha

Calamagrostis deschampsioides subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of blue-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
