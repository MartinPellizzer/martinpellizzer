Cissus erecta

Cissus erecta, commonly known as the climbing gooseberry, is a vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Cissus erecta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
