Carduus transcaspicus

Carduus transcaspicus, commonly known as the Transcaspian thistle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and desert regions.

Its structure and growth habits make it a resilient species in harsh climates.
