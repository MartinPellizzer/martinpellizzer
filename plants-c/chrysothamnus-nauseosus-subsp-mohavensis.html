Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. mohavensis

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

mohavensis, commonly known as Mohave goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic foliage, which are typical features of the genus Chrysothamnus.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
