Calawaya aequiloba

Calawaya aequiloba, commonly known as the Hawaiian mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is often found at higher elevations. Calawaya aequiloba has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various insect and bird species.
