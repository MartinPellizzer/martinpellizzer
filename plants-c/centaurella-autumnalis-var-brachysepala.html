Centaurella autumnalis var. brachysepala

Centaurella autumnalis var. brachysepala, commonly known as the autumn centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
