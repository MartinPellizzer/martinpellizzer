Cyperus submicrolepis

Cyperus submicrolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water.

Cyperus submicrolepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
