Colchicum maraschicum

Colchicum maraschicum, commonly known as the Maraschino lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It blooms in late summer, typically from August to September.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical significance in horticulture.
