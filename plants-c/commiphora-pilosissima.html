Commiphora pilosissima

Commiphora pilosissima, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its aromatic, resinous sap. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
