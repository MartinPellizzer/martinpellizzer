Centaurea fischeri subsp. cheiranthifolia

Centaurea fischeri subsp.

cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the Fischer's centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in rocky or arid environments. It produces small, seed-like fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
