Crepis tristis

Crepis tristis, commonly known as the weedy mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a single head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands.

Crepis tristis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed.
