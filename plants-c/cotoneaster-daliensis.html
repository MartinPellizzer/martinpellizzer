Cotoneaster daliensis

Cotoneaster daliensis, commonly known as Dali cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
