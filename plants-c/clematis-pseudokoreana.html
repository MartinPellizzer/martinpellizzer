Clematis pseudokoreana

Clematis pseudokoreana, commonly known as the Korean clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with five petals. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis pseudokoreana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
