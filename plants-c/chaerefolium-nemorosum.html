Chaerefolium nemorosum

Chaerefolium nemorosum, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.

The wood anemone is an important early bloomer in forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing nectar for early pollinators.
