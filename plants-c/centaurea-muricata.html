Centaurea muricata

Centaurea muricata, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with prominent central disc flowers surrounded by ray florets.

The species is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
