Centrosema lucia-helenae

Centrosema lucia-helenae, commonly known as the Helen's Crown.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The species belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important component of nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
