Corydalis mira subsp. mira

Corydalis mira subsp.

mira, commonly known as the Fairy Slipper Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
