Crataegus × maritima

Crataegus × maritima, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a hybrid shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and antioxidants. The plant typically grows in saline or sandy soils and is well adapted to harsh maritime environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a rough texture, while its branches are often thorny.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits.
