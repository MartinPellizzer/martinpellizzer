Cupania asperula

Cupania asperula, commonly known as roughcup hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Cupania asperula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
