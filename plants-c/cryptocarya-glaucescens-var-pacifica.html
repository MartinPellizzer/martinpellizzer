Cryptocarya glaucescens var. pacifica

Cryptocarya glaucescens var. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific cryptocarya, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a whitish or bluish underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are often greenish-white in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
