Coreopsis aristosa

Coreopsis aristosa, commonly known as the aristate coreopsis, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
