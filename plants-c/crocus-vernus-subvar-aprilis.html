Crocus vernus subvar. aprilis

Crocus vernus subvar. aprilis, commonly known as the April crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and prefers well-drained soil in a sunny to partially shaded location. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subvariety is distinguished by its earlier flowering time compared to other crocus species.
