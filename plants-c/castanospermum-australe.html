Castanospermum australe

Castanospermum australe, commonly known as the Sydney apple or broad-leaved ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, oval-shaped leaves and its distinctive, heavy, woody fruits that contain a single, hard seed. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its unique, round, apple-like fruits. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in rainforests and coastal regions.

The plant has ecological significance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
