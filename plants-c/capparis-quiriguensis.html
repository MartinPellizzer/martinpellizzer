Capparis quiriguensis

Capparis quiriguensis, commonly known as the Quechua caper, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which is characterized by its often thorny stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Capparis quiriguensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
