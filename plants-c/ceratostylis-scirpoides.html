Ceratostylis scirpoides

Ceratostylis scirpoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated flower spike that bears a single, strikingly hooded orchid bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its flowers are often pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, narrow labellum.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in association with specific fungal symbionts.
