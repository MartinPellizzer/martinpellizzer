Crocodilina humilis

Crocodilina humilis, commonly known as the dwarf crocodile plant, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, waxy leaves that often display a vibrant green color with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts.
