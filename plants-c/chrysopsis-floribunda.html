Chrysopsis floribunda

Chrysopsis floribunda, commonly known as the golden aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Chrysopsis floribunda is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
