Cleome uncifera subsp. microphylla

Cleome uncifera subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States. It features slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
