Calotropis wallichii

Calotropis wallichii, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in clusters. Its latex sap is milky and contains toxic compounds, making it a defensive mechanism against herbivores.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine systems.
