Craccina leptocaulis

Craccina leptocaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed craccus, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and sandy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is characterized by its short stature and rapid life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
