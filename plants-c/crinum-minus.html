Crinum minus

Crinum minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crinum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Crinum minus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
