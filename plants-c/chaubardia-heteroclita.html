Chaubardia heteroclita

Chaubardia heteroclita, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Chaubardia heteroclita is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
