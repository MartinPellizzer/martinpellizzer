Chimonobambusa szechuanensis f. flexuosa

Chimonobambusa szechuanensis f. flexuosa, commonly known as the Sichuan flexuous bamboo, is a unique variant of the Sichuan bamboo species.

It is characterized by its gracefully arching stems that exhibit a flexible growth habit, distinguishing it from the more rigid forms of its parent species. The plant typically grows in the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

This bamboo is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
