Capparis baducca

Capparis baducca, commonly known as the Cape Tribulation caper, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in coastal and sandy soils.

Capparis baducca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in coastal gardens.
