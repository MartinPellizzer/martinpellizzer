Cordia poliophylla

Cordia poliophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Cordia poliophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
