Caladenia integra

Caladenia integra, commonly known as the Crimson Spider Orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower that features a vivid red or orange hue with intricate, spider-like markings. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom during the cooler months, often from late autumn to early spring.

Caladenia integra is a protected species due to habitat loss and is often cultivated in conservation programs to ensure its survival.
