Chassalia pubescens

Chassalia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy chassalia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Chassalia pubescens is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it serves as a food source for various pollinators.
