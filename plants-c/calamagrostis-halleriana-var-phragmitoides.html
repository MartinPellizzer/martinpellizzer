Calamagrostis halleriana var. phragmitoides

Calamagrostis halleriana var. phragmitoides, commonly known as the tall reed or giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This species is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
