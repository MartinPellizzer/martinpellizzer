Cleyera japonica var. tonkinensis

Cleyera japonica var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
