Chassalia magnificens

Chassalia magnificens, commonly known as the magnificent chassalia, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its vibrant yellow flowers, surrounded by white ray florets, bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
