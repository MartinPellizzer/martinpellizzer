Casselia simplicissima

Casselia simplicissima, commonly known as the simple cassia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, unbranched inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are typically simple and alternate. Casselia simplicissima is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
