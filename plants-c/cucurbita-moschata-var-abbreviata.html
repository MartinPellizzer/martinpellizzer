Cucurbita moschata var. abbreviata

Cucurbita moschata var. abbreviata, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a cultivated variety of squash native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often yellow or orange fruits with a thick, smooth rind. The plant produces broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is valued for its edible flesh, which is sweet and nutty in flavor.

This variety is often used in traditional cuisines and is also grown for ornamental purposes.
