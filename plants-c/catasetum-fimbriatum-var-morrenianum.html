Catasetum fimbriatum var. morrenianum

Catasetum fimbriatum var. morrenianum, commonly known as the Morren's Catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that can range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, spurred flowers, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
