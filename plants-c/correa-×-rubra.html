Correa × rubra

Correa × rubra, commonly known as the red correa, is a hybrid shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Correa × rubra is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
