Convolvulus subspathulatus

Convolvulus subspathulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, ascending stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habits.
