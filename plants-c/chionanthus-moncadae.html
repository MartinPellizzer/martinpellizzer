Chionanthus moncadae

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as moths and bees, which play a crucial role in its reproductive cycle.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Chionanthus moncadae is considered vulnerable, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
