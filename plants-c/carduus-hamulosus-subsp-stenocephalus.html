Carduus hamulosus subsp. stenocephalus

Carduus hamulosus subsp.

stenocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branched stem that bears purple to pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact, narrow flower heads compared to other varieties within the Carduus genus.
