Celtis audibertiana

Celtis audibertiana, commonly known as the Audibert's hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a dark purple color. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.

Celtis audibertiana is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
