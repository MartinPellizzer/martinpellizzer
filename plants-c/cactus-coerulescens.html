Cactus coerulescens

Cactus coerulescens, commonly known as the blue cactus or blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often with a bluish-gray hue due to its dense coating of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Cactus coerulescens is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
