Crotalaria pycnostachya subsp. tropeae

Crotalaria pycnostachya subsp.

tropeae, commonly known as the Cape yellow bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
