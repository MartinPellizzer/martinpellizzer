Campanula homallanthina

Campanula homallanthina, commonly known as the Sierra Madre bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Campanula homallanthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
