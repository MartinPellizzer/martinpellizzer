Crataegus × cispontica

Crataegus × cispontica, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiac benefits.

The species is often used in hedgerows and gardens due to its hardiness and adaptability.
