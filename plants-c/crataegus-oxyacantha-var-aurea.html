Crataegus oxyacantha var. aurea

Crataegus oxyacantha var. aurea, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a variety of hawthorn native to Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a glossy green surface.
