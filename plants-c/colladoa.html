Colladoa

Colladoa, commonly known as the "Spanish broom," is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is often found in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Colladoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
