Ceratochloa pendula

Ceratochloa pendula, commonly known as the hanging grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that dangle from the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swamps or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
