Chionanthus stenurus

Chionanthus stenurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate ornamental value to gardens. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Chionanthus stenurus is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
