Coprosmanthus japonicus

Coprosmanthus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Coprosmanthus japonicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
