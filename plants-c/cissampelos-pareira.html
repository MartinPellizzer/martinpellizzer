Cissampelos pareira

Cissampelos pareira, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, yellowish fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
