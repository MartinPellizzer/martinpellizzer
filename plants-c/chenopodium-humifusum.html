Chenopodium humifusum

Chenopodium humifusum, commonly known as pigweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Chenopodium humifusum is also considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature.
