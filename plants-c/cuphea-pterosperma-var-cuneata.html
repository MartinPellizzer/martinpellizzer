Cuphea pterosperma var. cuneata

Cuphea pterosperma var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers. Its unique flower structure attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators, enhancing its ecological value.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
