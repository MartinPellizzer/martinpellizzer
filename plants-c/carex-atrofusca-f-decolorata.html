Carex atrofusca f. decolorata

Carex atrofusca f. decolorata, commonly known as the black sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex atrofusca.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that contains small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This sedgeland plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
