Cedrus cedrus

Cedrus cedrus, commonly known as the Atlas cedar, is a coniferous tree native to the mountains of North Africa.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cedrus cedrus is valued for its durable wood and has been used historically in construction and religious contexts.
