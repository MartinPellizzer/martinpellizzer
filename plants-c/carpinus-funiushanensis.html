Carpinus funiushanensis

Carpinus funiushanensis, commonly known as the Funiu Shan hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are male flowers, while female flowers are found in separate catkins. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
