Cassia orbiculata

Cassia orbiculata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and light-colored. Cassia orbiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
