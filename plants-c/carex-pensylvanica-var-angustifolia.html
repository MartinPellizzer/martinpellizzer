Carex pensylvanica var. angustifolia

Carex pensylvanica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its flowering spikes are usually borne on slender stems, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.
