Croton scouleri var. grandifolius

Croton scouleri var. grandifolius, commonly known as the Pacific Northwest croton, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest region of North America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often bright green with a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple branches forming a dense, bushy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, seasonal color changes.
