Castalia tetragona var. leibergii

Castalia tetragona var. leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's castalia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The species produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant species.
