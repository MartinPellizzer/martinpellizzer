Cheiranthus lanceolatus

Cheiranthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wallflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
