Charlwoodia rubra

Charlwoodia rubra, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a unique species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, spreading crown, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its bark is thick and flaky, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Charlwoodia rubra plays an important ecological role in its habitat, offering shelter and food to various wildlife species.
