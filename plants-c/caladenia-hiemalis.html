Caladenia hiemalis

Caladenia hiemalis, commonly known as the winter spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. The plant produces a single, slender stem with a single, slender flower that is usually pale green to yellow in color. Its flowering period occurs in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earlier blooming orchids in the region.

The species is characterized by its intricate floral structure and is often pollinated by specific insect species.
