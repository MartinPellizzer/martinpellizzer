Cyperus incompletus

Cyperus incompletus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Cyperus incompletus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
