Carex flacca f. chlorocarpa

Carex flacca f. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the chlorocarpic sedges, is a variant of the common sedge, Carex flacca.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are generally broader and more chlorophyll-rich compared to the typical form, giving it a more vibrant green appearance.

This sedge plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
