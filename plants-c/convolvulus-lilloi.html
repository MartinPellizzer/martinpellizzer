Convolvulus lilloi

Convolvulus lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and open fields, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Convolvulus lilloi is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other genera like Ipomoea and Pharbitis.
