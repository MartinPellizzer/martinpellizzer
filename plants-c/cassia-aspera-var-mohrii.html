Cassia aspera var. mohrii

Cassia aspera var. mohrii, commonly known as Mohri's cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, arid environments and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
