Campanula rotundifolia var. verlotii

Campanula rotundifolia var. verlotii, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
