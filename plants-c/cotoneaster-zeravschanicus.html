Cotoneaster zeravschanicus

Cotoneaster zeravschanicus, commonly known as the Pamir cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, orange-red berries that are edible for birds. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Cotoneaster zeravschanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
