Campanula portenschlagiana var. pumila

Campanula portenschlagiana var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial that features small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
