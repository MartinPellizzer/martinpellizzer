Ctenopteris gradata

Ctenopteris gradata, commonly known as the ladder fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into segments resembling steps or a ladder, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with each pinna having a serrated edge.

Ctenopteris gradata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
