Cerastium strictum subsp. ciliatum

Cerastium strictum subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.
