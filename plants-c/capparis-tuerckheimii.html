Capparis tuerckheimii

Capparis tuerckheimii, commonly known as the desert caper, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Capparis tuerckheimii is well adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
