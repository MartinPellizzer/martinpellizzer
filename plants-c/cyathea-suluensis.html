Cyathea suluensis

Cyathea suluensis, commonly known as the Sulu tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea suluensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and shelter for various organisms.
