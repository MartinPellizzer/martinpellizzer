Citrus grandis var. dulcis

Citrus grandis var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces fragrant, round fruits with thick, leathery rinds. The fruit contains a segmented, juicy pulp that is rich in vitamin C and essential oils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary, medicinal, and commercial applications.
