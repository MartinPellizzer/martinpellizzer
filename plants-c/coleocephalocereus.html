Coleocephalocereus

Coleocephalocereus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a type of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful crown, which is actually a grafted variety of the Hylocereus genus. The main body of the plant is a slow-growing columnar cactus with green, spiny stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
