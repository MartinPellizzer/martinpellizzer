Cicer grossum

Cicer grossum, commonly known as the broadleaf vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a hairy stem and produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Cicer grossum is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
