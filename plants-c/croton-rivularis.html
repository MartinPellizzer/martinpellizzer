Croton rivularis

Croton rivularis, commonly known as the river croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows along riverbanks and in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Croton rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
