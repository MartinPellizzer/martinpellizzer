Carex chalaros

Carex chalaros, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex chalaros is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
