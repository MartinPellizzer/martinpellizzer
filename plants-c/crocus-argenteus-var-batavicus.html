Crocus argenteus var. batavicus

Crocus argenteus var. batavicus, commonly known as the silver crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, early spring blooms with pale yellow to white petals and a distinctive, dark purple to black center. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears one to three flowers, often appearing before the leaves fully emerge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early blooming display.
