Corynopuntia clavata

Corynopuntia clavata, commonly known as the clustered cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Corynopuntia clavata is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability.
