Cucurbita leucantha

Cucurbita leucantha, commonly known as the white-fleshed pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its pale-colored, edible fruit. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of many cucurbits. Its fruit is typically harvested when mature, though it can be used at various stages of development.

The species is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses in traditional Mexican cuisine.
