Crassula natans f. filiformis

Crassula natans f. filiformis, commonly known as the thread-leaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, narrow, thread-like leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This cultivar is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
