Crateva speciosa

Crateva speciosa, commonly known as the Java apple or the sour sop, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a smooth, green, oblong berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a juicy, aromatic pulp with a sour taste.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
