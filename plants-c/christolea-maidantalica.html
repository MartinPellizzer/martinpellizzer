Christolea maidantalica

Christolea maidantalica, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct dark green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
