Cestrum cuneifolium

Cestrum cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange-red in color. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and nocturnal fragrance.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
