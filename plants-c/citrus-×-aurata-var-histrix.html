Citrus × aurata var. histrix

Citrus × aurata var. histrix, commonly known as the dwarf lemon or citronella shrub, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, lemon-scented leaves.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically not eaten but are valued for their essential oils. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and citrus aroma. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The leaves and flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
