Cylindrolobus carunculosus

Cylindrolobus carunculosus, commonly known as the hairy globular thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny head of yellow flowers surrounded by dense, woolly bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
