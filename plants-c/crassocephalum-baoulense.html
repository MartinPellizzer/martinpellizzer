Crassocephalum baoulense

Crassocephalum baoulense, commonly known as the African blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy composite flowers that attract pollinators. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
