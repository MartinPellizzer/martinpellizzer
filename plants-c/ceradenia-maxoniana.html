Ceradenia maxoniana

Ceradenia maxoniana, commonly known as the Maxon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate and often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ceradenia maxoniana is considered a rare and endangered species.
