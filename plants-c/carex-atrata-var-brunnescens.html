Carex atrata var. brunnescens

Carex atrata var. brunnescens, commonly known as the brownish tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green to brownish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
