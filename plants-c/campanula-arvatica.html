Campanula arvatica

Campanula arvatica, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Campanula arvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
