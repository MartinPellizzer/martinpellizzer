Cistus quinquenervis

Cistus quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and aromatic, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Cistus quinquenervis is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
