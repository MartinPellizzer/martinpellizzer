Chionanthus confertus

Chionanthus confertus, commonly known as the Chinese whiteflower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, racemose inflorescences that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles and are often accompanied by small, yellowish bracts.

Chionanthus confertus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
