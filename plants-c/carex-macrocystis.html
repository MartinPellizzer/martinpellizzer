Carex macrocystis

Carex macrocystis, commonly known as the large-cyst sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
