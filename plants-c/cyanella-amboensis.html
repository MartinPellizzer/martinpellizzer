Cyanella amboensis

Cyanella amboensis, commonly known as the Blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to Madagascar, characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and striking blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are clustered in spikelets and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.

Cyanella amboensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting biodiversity.
