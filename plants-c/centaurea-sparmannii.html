Centaurea sparmannii

Centaurea sparmannii, commonly known as the Sparrmann's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea sparmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
