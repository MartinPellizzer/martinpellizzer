Cyanus moschatus

Cyanus moschatus, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
