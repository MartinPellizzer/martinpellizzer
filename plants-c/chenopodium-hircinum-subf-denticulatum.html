Chenopodium hircinum subf. denticulatum

Chenopodium hircinum subf.

denticulatum, commonly known as the curly goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
