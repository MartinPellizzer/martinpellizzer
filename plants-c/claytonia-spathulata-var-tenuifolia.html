Claytonia spathulata var. tenuifolia

Claytonia spathulata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, narrow leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas. It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is known for its early spring blooming habit.
