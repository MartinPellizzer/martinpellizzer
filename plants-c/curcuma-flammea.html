Curcuma flammea

Curcuma flammea, commonly known as the flame curcuma, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, orange-red rhizomes and large, showy leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces inflorescences with bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
