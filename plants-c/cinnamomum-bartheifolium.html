Cinnamomum bartheifolium

Cinnamomum bartheifolium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum bartheifolium, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red berries. Its essential oils, rich in cinnamaldehyde, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
