Cochlearia officinalis subsp. aestuaria

Cochlearia officinalis subsp.

aestuaria, commonly known as the sea kale, is a coastal plant adapted to salty and windy environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a raceme. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shores of the British Isles and northern Europe.

Its subspecies status reflects its distinct ecological and morphological adaptations to maritime conditions.
