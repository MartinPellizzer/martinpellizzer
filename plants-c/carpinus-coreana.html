Carpinus coreana

Carpinus coreana, commonly known as the Korean hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
