Cassia schinifolia

Cassia schinifolia, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. Cassia schinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
