Calanthe montana

Calanthe montana, commonly known as the mountain calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, lance-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinct three-lobed lip. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette and are usually dark green with a waxy texture.

Calanthe montana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest habitats.
