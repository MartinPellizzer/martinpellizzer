Corysanthes calophylla

Corysanthes calophylla, commonly known as the satinwood or blackwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a significant resource in traditional crafts.

Corysanthes calophylla plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining forest structure.
