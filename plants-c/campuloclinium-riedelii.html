Campuloclinium riedelii

Campuloclinium riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Campuloclinium riedelii typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
