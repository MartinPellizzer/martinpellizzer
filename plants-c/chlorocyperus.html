Chlorocyperus

Chlorocyperus, commonly known as the green flatsedge, is a genus of flowering plants in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plants typically grow in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Chlorocyperus species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, and they play a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Their botanical features make them important for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
