Ceropegia nubica

Ceropegia nubica, commonly known as the mountain ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and pairs of opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white with a distinctive, waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia nubica is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and interesting floral structure.
