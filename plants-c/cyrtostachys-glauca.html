Cyrtostachys glauca

Cyrtostachys glauca, commonly known as the blue glauca or blue-leafed cycad, is a unique cycad species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, dark green leaves that have a bluish tint, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a tropical or subtropical climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
