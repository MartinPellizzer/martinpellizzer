Cyathea rheosora

Cyathea rheosora, commonly known as the wetland tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in nutrient-rich soil.

Cyathea rheosora plays an important ecological role in maintaining the structure and biodiversity of its habitat.
