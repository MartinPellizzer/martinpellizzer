Ceanothus myrianthus

Ceanothus myrianthus, commonly known as the silverleaf ceanothus, is a deciduous shrub native to California and Oregon in the United States.

It features small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Ceanothus myrianthus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
