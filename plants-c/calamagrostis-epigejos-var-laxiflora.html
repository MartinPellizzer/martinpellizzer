Calamagrostis epigejos var. laxiflora

Calamagrostis epigejos var. laxiflora, commonly known as the silvergrass or blue joint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, pale blue or silver flowers in late summer, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
