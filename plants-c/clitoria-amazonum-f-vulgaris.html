Clitoria amazonum f. vulgaris

Clitoria amazonum f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of natural dyes.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in beverages.
