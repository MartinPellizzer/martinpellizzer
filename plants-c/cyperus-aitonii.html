Cyperus aitonii

Cyperus aitonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus aitonii typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps, ditches, and flooded meadows.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a common wetland plant.
