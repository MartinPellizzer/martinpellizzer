Carex breviculmis f. wardiana

Carex breviculmis f. wardiana, commonly known as the Ward's short-bracted sedge, is a compact, clump-forming perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets along the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
