Clerodendrum discolor var. duemmeri

Clerodendrum discolor var. duemmeri, commonly known as the Duemmer's flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar aromatic properties.
