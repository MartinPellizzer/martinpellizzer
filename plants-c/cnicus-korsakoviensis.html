Cnicus korsakoviensis

Cnicus korsakoviensis, commonly known as the Korsakov thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are spiny and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Cnicus korsakoviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
