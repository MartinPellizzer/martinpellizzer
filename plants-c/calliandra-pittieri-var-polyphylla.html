Calliandra pittieri var. polyphylla

Calliandra pittieri var. polyphylla, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its distinctive pink to red bract-like flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
