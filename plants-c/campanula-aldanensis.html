Campanula aldanensis

Campanula aldanensis, commonly known as the Altai bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and sturdy.

Campanula aldanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
