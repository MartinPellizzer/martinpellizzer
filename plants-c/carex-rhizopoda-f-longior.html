Carex rhizopoda f. longior

Carex rhizopoda f. longior, commonly known as the long-leaved sedges, is a variant of the sedge species Carex rhizopoda.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinct appearance compared to other sedge varieties. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical structures.

The plant has fibrous roots and rhizomes, which help it thrive in waterlogged soils and contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
