Citrus × tardiferax

Citrus × tardiferax, commonly known as the trifoliate orange.

This hybrid citrus plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Citrus sinensis (sweet orange) and Citrus trifoliata (trifoliate orange). The plant is known for its cold hardiness and is often used as a rootstock in citrus cultivation.

Its fruit is typically small, sour, and not suitable for direct consumption but is valued for its ornamental qualities and agricultural utility.
