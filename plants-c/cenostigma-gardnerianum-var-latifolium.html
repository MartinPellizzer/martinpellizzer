Cenostigma gardnerianum var. latifolium

Cenostigma gardnerianum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cenostigma, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
