Conyza undulata

Conyza undulata, commonly known as the wavycup, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a wavy or undulating edge, giving it its common name.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
