Catha lanceolata

Catha lanceolata, commonly known as khat, is a flowering plant native to the horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense shrubs. Its leaves contain cathinone and cathine, which are stimulant compounds responsible for its psychoactive effects.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.
