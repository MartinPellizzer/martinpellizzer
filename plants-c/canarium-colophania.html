Canarium colophania

Canarium colophania, commonly known as the African canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its resin and timber.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
