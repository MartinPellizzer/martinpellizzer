Conamomum vietnamense

Conamomum vietnamense, commonly known as Vietnamese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the humid forests of Vietnam.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Conamomum vietnamense thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
