Corybas puberulus

Corybas puberulus, commonly known as the hairy helmet orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that is often adorned with hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under dense vegetation or in rocky areas. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow or reddish in color, with a distinct, intricate structure.

Corybas puberulus is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insects for reproduction.
