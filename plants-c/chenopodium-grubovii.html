Chenopodium grubovii

Chenopodium grubovii, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in the summer. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Chenopodium grubovii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.
