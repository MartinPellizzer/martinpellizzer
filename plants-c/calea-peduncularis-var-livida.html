Calea peduncularis var. livida

Calea peduncularis var. livida, commonly known as the purple calea, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique flower structure and long blooming period.
