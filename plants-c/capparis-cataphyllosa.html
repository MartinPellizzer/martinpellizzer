Capparis cataphyllosa

Capparis cataphyllosa, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that mature into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and cliffs.

Capparis cataphyllosa is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
