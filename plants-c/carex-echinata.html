Carex echinata

Carex echinata, commonly known as spiny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond edges.

Carex echinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
