Carpha eminii

Carpha eminii, commonly known as the Armenian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spikelets during the summer months. Carpha eminii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
