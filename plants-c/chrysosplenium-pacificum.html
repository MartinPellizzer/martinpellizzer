Chrysosplenium pacificum

Chrysosplenium pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific chrysosplenium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, kidney-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
