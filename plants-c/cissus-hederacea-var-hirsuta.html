Cissus hederacea var. hirsuta

Cissus hederacea var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy money plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its vigorous growth makes it suitable for trellises or as a hanging plant in indoor settings.
