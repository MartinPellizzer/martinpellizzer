Cinnamomum melastomaceum

Cinnamomum melastomaceum, commonly known as the Indian cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is harvested and used as a spice. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its wood is valued for its durability and distinctive scent, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
