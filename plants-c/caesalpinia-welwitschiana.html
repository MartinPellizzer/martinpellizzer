Caesalpinia welwitschiana

Caesalpinia welwitschiana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which are borne in dense, terminal clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and striking appearance make it a notable species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
