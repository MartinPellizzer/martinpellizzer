Croton campanulatus

Croton campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton campanulatus is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
