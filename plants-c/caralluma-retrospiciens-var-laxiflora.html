Caralluma retrospiciens var. laxiflora

Caralluma retrospiciens var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Desert Candelabra, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Caralluma retrospiciens var. laxiflora is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
