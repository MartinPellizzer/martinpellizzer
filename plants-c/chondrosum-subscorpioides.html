Chondrosum subscorpioides

Chondrosum subscorpioides, commonly known as the scorpionweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

The species is known for its unique, spiky seed pods that resemble a scorpion's tail, giving it its common name.
