Celastrus cordatus

Celastrus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf celastre, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red berries that attract birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
