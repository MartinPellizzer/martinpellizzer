Ciminalis capitata

Ciminalis capitata, commonly known as the spiked centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, blue or violet flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Ciminalis capitata is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
