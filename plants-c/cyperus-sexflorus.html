Cyperus sexflorus

Cyperus sexflorus, commonly known as the six-flowered flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa and Asia. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, exhibiting a clumping growth habit with slender, triangular stems. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with the distinctive feature of six stamens in each flower.

Cyperus sexflorus is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for wetland habitats.
