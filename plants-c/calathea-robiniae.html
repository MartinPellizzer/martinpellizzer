Calathea robiniae

Calathea robiniae, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature intricate patterns of green, silver, and brown. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are often described as having a velvety texture and a unique, almost leopard-like appearance.

Calathea robiniae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
