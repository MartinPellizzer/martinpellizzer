Cinnamomum pedatinervium

Cinnamomum pedatinervium, commonly known as the mountain cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Cambodia, and Laos. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions with rich soil.
