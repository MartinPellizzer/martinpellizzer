Crocus vittatus var. candidus

Crocus vittatus var. candidus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its elegant, nodding flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped blooms that are often fragrant. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its early spring display.
