Cynanchum sublanceolatum var. auriculatum

Cynanchum sublanceolatum var. auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are small and clustered in racemes, often with a pale yellow to greenish hue, and are followed by cylindrical, ribbed fruits. This plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
