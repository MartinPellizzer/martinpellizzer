Caltha palustris var. enkoso

Caltha palustris var. enkoso, commonly known as the marsh marigold variant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an early bloomer and its ecological value to pollinators.
