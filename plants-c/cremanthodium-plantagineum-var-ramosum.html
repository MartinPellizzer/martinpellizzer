Cremanthodium plantagineum var. ramosum

Cremanthodium plantagineum var. ramosum, commonly known as the hairy leaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of hairy, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
