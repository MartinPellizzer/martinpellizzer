Conospermum filifolium subsp. filifolium

Conospermum filifolium subsp.

filifolium, commonly known as the silky conospermum, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, silky appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
