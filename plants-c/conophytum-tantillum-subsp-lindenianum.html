Conophytum tantillum subsp. lindenianum

Conophytum tantillum subsp.

lindenianum, commonly known as the "Linden's conophytum," is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like structures that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
