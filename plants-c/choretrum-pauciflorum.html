Choretrum pauciflorum

Choretrum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered choretrum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
