Crassula glabrifolia

Crassula glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Crassula glabrifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
