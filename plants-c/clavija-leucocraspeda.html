Clavija leucocraspeda

Clavija leucocraspeda, commonly known as the white-fringed clavija, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white flowers with prominent yellow centers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
