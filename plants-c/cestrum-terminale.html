Cestrum terminale

Cestrum terminale, commonly known as night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture.

Cestrum terminale thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant nocturnal fragrance.
