Coryphopteris stereophylla

Coryphopteris stereophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. This fern is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and Southeast Asia. The leaves are typically arching and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to shaded, moist environments.

It thrives in humid conditions and is often found growing in dense forests or on rocky outcrops.
