Cissampelos robertsonii

Cissampelos robertsonii, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, orange-red fruits. Its stems are woody and often twine around supporting structures, while its roots are tuberous, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional African medicine.
