Centaurea paniculata subsp. biformis

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

biformis, commonly known as the two-formed bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, thriving in full sun. Its blue to purple flowers are arranged in paniculate inflorescences, and the subspecies is distinguished by its two distinct forms in the flower heads.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
