Cirsium racemiforme

Cirsium racemiforme, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Cirsium racemiforme is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
