Centaurea × prodanii

Centaurea × prodanii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Centaurea × prodanii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
