Carex elongata var. pauciflora

Carex elongata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly ribbed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and swamps.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable indicator of soil moisture conditions.
