Calorhabdos formosana

Calorhabdos formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan flame orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange floral display. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators with their vivid colors and fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
