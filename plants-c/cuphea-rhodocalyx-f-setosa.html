Cuphea rhodocalyx f. setosa

Cuphea rhodocalyx f. setosa, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that give it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
