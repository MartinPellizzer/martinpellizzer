Chenolea convallis

Chenolea convallis, commonly known as the creeping chenopod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous and adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.

Chenolea convallis is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
