Cleome amazonica

Cleome amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Cleome amazonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
