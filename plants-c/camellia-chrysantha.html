Camellia chrysantha

Camellia chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant blooms.

Camellia chrysantha prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.
