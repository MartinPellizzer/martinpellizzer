Carex vespertina

Carex vespertina, commonly known as evening sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swamps, and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its spikelets are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Carex vespertina plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
