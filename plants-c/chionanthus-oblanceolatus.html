Chionanthus oblanceolatus

Chionanthus oblanceolatus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The Chinese fringeflower is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its delicate, cascading blooms.
