Centaurea jacea subsp. gaudinii

Centaurea jacea subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as Gaudin's knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking purple to pink flower heads. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
