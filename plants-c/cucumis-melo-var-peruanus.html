Cucumis melo var. peruanus

Cucumis melo var. peruanus, commonly known as the Peruvian melon, is a variant of the melon genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical shape and thick, rugged rind that varies in color from pale green to yellowish. The fruit typically has a mild, sweet flavor with a high water content, making it suitable for fresh consumption. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is less commonly cultivated than other melon types but is valued for its unique appearance and taste.
