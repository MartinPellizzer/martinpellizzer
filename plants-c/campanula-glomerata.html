Campanula glomerata

Campanula glomerata, commonly known as clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Campanula glomerata is valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its suitability for rock gardens and borders.
