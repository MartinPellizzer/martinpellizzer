Celosia micrantha

Celosia micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces vibrant, spike-like inflorescences in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Celosia micrantha is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
