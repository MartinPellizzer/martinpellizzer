Coutarea hexandra var. calycina

Coutarea hexandra var. calycina, commonly known as the Amazonian trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
