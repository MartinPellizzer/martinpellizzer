Ctenitis spectabilis

Ctenitis spectabilis, commonly known as the spectabilis ctenitis, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate fronds with a distinctive, fern-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its leaves are bioluminescent under certain conditions, which is a rare trait among ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
