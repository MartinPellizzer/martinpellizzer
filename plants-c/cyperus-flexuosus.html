Cyperus flexuosus

Cyperus flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it often grows in wetland or marshy environments. Cyperus flexuosus is adapted to moist soils and can thrive in both aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.

Its presence is often indicative of healthy wetland ecosystems.
