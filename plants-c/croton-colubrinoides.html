Croton colubrinoides

Croton colubrinoides, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from red to orange and yellow, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
