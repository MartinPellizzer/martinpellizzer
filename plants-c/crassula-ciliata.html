Crassula ciliata

Crassula ciliata, commonly known as the Woolly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and dense clusters of fleshy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically pale green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive periods of drought.

Crassula ciliata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
