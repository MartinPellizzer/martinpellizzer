Calceolaria palustris subsp. santanderensis

Calceolaria palustris subsp.

santanderensis, commonly known as the marsh calceolaria, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America. It features delicate, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure, characteristic of the Calceolaria genus.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
