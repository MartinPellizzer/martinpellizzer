Calamagrostis conspicua

Calamagrostis conspicua, commonly known as the showy grass or silver feather reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of silvery-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-white or bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis conspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
