Cereus spegazzinii

Cereus spegazzinii, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a columnar stem with a ribbed structure and spines that are often short and soft. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and floral arrangements.
