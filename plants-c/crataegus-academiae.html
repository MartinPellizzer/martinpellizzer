Crataegus academiae

Crataegus academiae, commonly known as the academic hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it produces thorny branches.

Crataegus academiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
