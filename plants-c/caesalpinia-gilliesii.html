Caesalpinia gilliesii

Caesalpinia gilliesii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Caesalpinia gilliesii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
