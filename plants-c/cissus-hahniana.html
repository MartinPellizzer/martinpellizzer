Cissus hahniana

Cissus hahniana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus hahniana is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
