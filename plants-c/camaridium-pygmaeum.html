Camaridium pygmaeum

Camaridium pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf camara, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Camaridaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a short, compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed around its central stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
