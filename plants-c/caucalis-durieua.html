Caucalis durieua

Caucalis durieua, commonly known as the Durieu's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Caucalis durieua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
