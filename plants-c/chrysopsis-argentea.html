Chrysopsis argentea

Chrysopsis argentea, commonly known as silverleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chrysopsis argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
