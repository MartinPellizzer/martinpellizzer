Collomia biflora var. erythaeoides

Collomia biflora var. erythaeoides, commonly known as the red-flowered flaxvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
