Cicerbita cyanea var. paniculata

Cicerbita cyanea var. paniculata, commonly known as the blue thistle or blue hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a robust, upright stem with hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in a panicle inflorescence, giving it a distinctive, branching appearance.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
