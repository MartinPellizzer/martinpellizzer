Conospermum capitatum

Conospermum capitatum, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests.

Conospermum capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
