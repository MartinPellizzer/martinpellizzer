Crinum venustum

Crinum venustum, commonly known as the showy crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in a rosette at the base. Its flowers are characterized by a prominent central spadix surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Crinum venustum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils.
