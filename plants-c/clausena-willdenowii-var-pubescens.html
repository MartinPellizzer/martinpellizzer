Clausena willdenowii var. pubescens

Clausena willdenowii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wax apple, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and oval, glossy leaves. The fruit is a smooth, yellowish drupe that matures in late summer, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant is notable for its hairy texture on young shoots and leaves, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Clausena.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.
