Chlorocyperus amuricus

Chlorocyperus amuricus, commonly known as the Amur sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Chlorocyperus amuricus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
