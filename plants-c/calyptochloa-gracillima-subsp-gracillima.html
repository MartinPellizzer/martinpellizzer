Calyptochloa gracillima subsp. gracillima

Calyptochloa gracillima subsp.

gracillima, commonly known as the slender leafed grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive sheath that encloses the stem, giving it its scientific name.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
