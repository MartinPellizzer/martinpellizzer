Cephalocereus swartzii

Cephalocereus swartzii, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive crown of spines and produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
