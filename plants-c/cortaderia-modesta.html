Cortaderia modesta

Cortaderia modesta, commonly known as fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, arching blades that form a fountain-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces feathery, silver-gray plumes that add ornamental value in late summer and autumn. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics include narrow leaves and a clumping growth habit, which contribute to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
