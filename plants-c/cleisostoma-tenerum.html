Cleisostoma tenerum

Cleisostoma tenerum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Cleisostoma tenerum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
