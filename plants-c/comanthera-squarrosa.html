Comanthera squarrosa

Comanthera squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in corymbs. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

Comanthera squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
