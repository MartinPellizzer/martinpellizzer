Carex laxiculmis var. copulata

Carex laxiculmis var. copulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This variety is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

The plant is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate wet conditions and its aesthetic appeal in natural landscapes.
