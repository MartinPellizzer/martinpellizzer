Claytonia vancouverii

Claytonia vancouverii, commonly known as Vancouver's spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Claytonia vancouverii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
