Cistus creticus subsp. corsicus

Cistus creticus subsp.

corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia. It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its resinous leaves emit a strong, distinctive scent, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
