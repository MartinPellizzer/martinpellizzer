Coreopsis testudinea

Coreopsis testudinea, commonly known as the painted daisy, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white or pink petals. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, meadows, and along stream banks. It has feathery, hairy leaves and produces seeds in a fluffy, pinkish-pubescent capsule.

Coreopsis testudinea is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
