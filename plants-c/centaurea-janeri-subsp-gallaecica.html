Centaurea janeri subsp. gallaecica

Centaurea janeri subsp.

gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
