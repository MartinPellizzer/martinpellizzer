Crepis praemorsa subsp. praemorsa

Crepis praemorsa subsp.

praemorsa, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette, aiding in water retention and protection from extreme weather.
