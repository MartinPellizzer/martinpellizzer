Convolvulus chilensis

Convolvulus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds that are sometimes used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a popular garden plant and an invasive species in some areas.
