Crepis cylindrica

Crepis cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features upright, cylindrical flower heads that are composed of many small yellow florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Crepis cylindrica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
