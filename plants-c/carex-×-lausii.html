Carex × lausii

Carex × lausii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically forms clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic environments.

Carex × lausii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wetland biodiversity.
