Centaurea paniculata var. isernii

Centaurea paniculata var. isernii, commonly known as the Isern's plumeless thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense, spiny leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, purple to pink composite flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
