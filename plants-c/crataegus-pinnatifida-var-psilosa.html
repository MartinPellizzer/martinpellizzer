Crataegus pinnatifida var. psilosa

Crataegus pinnatifida var. psilosa, commonly known as the slender thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, slender leaflets and thorny branches. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
