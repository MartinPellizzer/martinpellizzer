Campylotheca helianthoides

Campylotheca helianthoides, commonly known as the golden candlewood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The golden candlewood is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
