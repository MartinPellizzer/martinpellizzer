Campanula japonica

Campanula japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
