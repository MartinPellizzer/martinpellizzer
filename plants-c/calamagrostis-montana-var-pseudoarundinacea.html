Calamagrostis montana var. pseudoarundinacea

Calamagrostis montana var. pseudoarundinacea, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
