Cuphea petiolata

Cuphea petiolata, commonly known as the whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

Cuphea petiolata thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
