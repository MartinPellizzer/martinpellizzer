Camellia furfuracea

Camellia furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. Camellia furfuracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
