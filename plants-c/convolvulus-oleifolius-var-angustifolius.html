Convolvulus oleifolius var. angustifolius

Convolvulus oleifolius var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution across its native range.
