Cistus inconspicuus

Cistus inconspicuus, commonly known as the lesser rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has greyish, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on rocky slopes or coastal areas.

Cistus inconspicuus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
