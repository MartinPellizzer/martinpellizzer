Cissus furcifera

Cissus furcifera, commonly known as the sandalwood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cissus furcifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
