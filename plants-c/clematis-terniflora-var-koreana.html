Clematis terniflora var. koreana

Clematis terniflora var. koreana, commonly known as Korean vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-7 cm in diameter. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
