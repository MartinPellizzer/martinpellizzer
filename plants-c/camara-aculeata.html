Camara aculeata

Camara aculeata, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in sharp spines. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its berries, which are toxic, are often mistaken for edible fruits.

Camara aculeata thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.
