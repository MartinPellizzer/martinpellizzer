Capsicum annuum var. fingerhuthii

Capsicum annuum var. fingerhuthii, commonly known as the Hungarian Wax Pepper, is a cultivar of the common chili pepper.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that typically range from 10 to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow or orange fruits that turn red when fully mature, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates. It is a member of the Solanaceae family, sharing botanical traits with other peppers such as tomatoes and potatoes.

This variety is often used in culinary applications for its mild heat and distinct flavor.
