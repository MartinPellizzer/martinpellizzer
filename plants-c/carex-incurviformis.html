Carex incurviformis

Carex incurviformis, commonly known as the bent-form sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that curve inward, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Carex incurviformis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
