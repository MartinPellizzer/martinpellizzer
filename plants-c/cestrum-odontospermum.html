Cestrum odontospermum

Cestrum odontospermum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
