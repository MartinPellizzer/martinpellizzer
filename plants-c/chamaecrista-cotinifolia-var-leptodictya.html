Chamaecrista cotinifolia var. leptodictya

Chamaecrista cotinifolia var. leptodictya, commonly known as the slender leaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The varietal name "leptodictya" refers to its slender, delicate leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties of Chamaecrista.
