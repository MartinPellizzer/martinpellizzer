Carex × almii

Carex × almii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid between Carex echinata and Carex lyngbyei, resulting in a unique combination of characteristics from both parents. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, erect leaves that form dense clumps, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, Carex × almii is often used in ecological landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
