Crataegus roanensis var. fluviatilis

Crataegus roanensis var. fluviatilis, commonly known as the river hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit with thorned branches. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it has a shallow root system adapted to wet environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
