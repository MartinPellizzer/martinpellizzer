Cyperus vegetus var. trigonus

Cyperus vegetus var. trigonus, commonly known as the triangular flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in non-native habitats.
