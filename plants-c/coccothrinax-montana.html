Coccothrinax montana

Coccothrinax montana, commonly known as the mountain palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Isle of Pines.

It is a small, evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters, characterized by its stiff, clustered leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or mountainous regions.

Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
