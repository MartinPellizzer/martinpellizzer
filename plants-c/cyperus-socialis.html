Cyperus socialis

Cyperus socialis, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with three stamens and a single pistil.

Cyperus socialis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
