Coccoloba ruiziana

Coccoloba ruiziana, commonly known as the red-leaved glorybower, is a tropical plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red, velvety leaves that turn a deep crimson in full sun. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Coccoloba ruiziana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
