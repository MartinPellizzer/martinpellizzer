Centaurea balbisiana subsp. aemilii

Centaurea balbisiana subsp.

aemilii, commonly known as the Emile's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
