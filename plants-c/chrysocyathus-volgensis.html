Chrysocyathus volgensis

Chrysocyathus volgensis, commonly known as the golden cup coral, is a unique marine organism belonging to the order Caryophyllales.

It is characterized by its intricate, branching structure that resembles a coral-like formation, though it is not a true coral but a type of calcareous algae. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for certain marine ecosystems and its potential applications in biotechnology.
