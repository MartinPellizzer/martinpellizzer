Convolvulus igneus

Convolvulus igneus, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a desirable ornamental plant.
