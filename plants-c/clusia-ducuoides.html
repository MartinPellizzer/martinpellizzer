Clusia ducuoides

Clusia ducuoides, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is a member of the Clusiaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Clusia ducuoides is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and attractive foliage.
