Cirsium × bernadense

Cirsium × bernadense, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with rough textures and serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
