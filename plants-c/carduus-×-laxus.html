Carduus × laxus

Carduus × laxus, commonly known as the common cockleburs, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and spherical, prickly fruit heads that contain numerous seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its flowers are usually yellow and are arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family.

Carduus × laxus is an annual or biennial plant that can become invasive in certain regions.
