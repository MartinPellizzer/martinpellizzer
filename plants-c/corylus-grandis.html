Corylus grandis

Corylus grandis, commonly known as the large hazelnut or Chinese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature in the fall. The plant has alternate, simple, and serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Corylus grandis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its nut production.
