Carex spectabilis f. chrysantha

Carex spectabilis f. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a variant of the common sedge that exhibits striking golden-yellow foliage.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for ecological restoration and landscape design in moist environments.
