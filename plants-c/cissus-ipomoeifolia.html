Cissus ipomoeifolia

Cissus ipomoeifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by pale yellow berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus ipomoeifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
