Crataegus viburnifolia

Crataegus viburnifolia, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and wildlife. It typically grows in moist, woodland areas and can reach heights of up to six feet.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
