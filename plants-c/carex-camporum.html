Carex camporum

Carex camporum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a bluish-green color. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex camporum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
