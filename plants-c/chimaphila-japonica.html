Chimaphila japonica

Chimaphila japonica, commonly known as Japanese pinyon, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically white or pink, and appear in clusters during the spring.

Chimaphila japonica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, moist woodland environments.
