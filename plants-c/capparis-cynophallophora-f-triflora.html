Capparis cynophallophora f. triflora

Capparis cynophallophora f. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered caper, is a unique variant of the caper plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and distinctive, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters of three. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in local cuisines. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

This variety is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its culinary and medicinal uses.
