Cirsium pyramidale

Cirsium pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramidal thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flowers and a robust, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with stiff, prickly margins, and the plant produces a tall, branched stem.

Cirsium pyramidale is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
