Carex lacerans

Carex lacerans, commonly known as the shredded sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often frayed or shredded at the edges, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Carex lacerans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
