Cyperus cruentus

Cyperus cruentus, commonly known as blood grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically reddish or purple in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Cyperus cruentus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.
