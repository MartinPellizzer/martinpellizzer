Colletia foliosa

Colletia foliosa, commonly known as the leafless cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lack of visible leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green, spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise sparsely vegetated structure. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Colletia foliosa is an important species in desert ecosystems.
