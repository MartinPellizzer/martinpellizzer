Ceradenia praeclara

Ceradenia praeclara, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals that form a bell-shaped structure, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and hummingbirds, showcasing a unique adaptation to its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss, Ceradenia praeclara is considered endangered, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts for this rare species.
