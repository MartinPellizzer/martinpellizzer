Centaurea pratensis subsp. carpetana

Centaurea pratensis subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the carpet cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers in late summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its common name reflects its tendency to form dense, carpet-like patches in its habitat.
