Carduus nigrescens subsp. martrinii

Carduus nigrescens subsp.

martrinii, commonly known as the blackthorn sowthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, often along roadsides and in meadows.

The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its fruits are small, spiny spheres that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
