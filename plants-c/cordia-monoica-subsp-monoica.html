Cordia monoica subsp. monoica

Cordia monoica subsp.

monoica, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
