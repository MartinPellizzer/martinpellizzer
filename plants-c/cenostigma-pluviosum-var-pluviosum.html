Cenostigma pluviosum var. pluviosum

Cenostigma pluviosum var. pluviosum, commonly known as the rain lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
