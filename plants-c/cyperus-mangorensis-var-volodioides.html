Cyperus mangorensis var. volodioides

Cyperus mangorensis var. volodioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, thriving in marshy areas and shallow water. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
