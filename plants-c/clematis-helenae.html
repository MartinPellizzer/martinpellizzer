Clematis helenae

Clematis helenae, commonly known as the Helen's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis helenae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
