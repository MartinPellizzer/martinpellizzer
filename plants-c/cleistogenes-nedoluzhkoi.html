Cleistogenes nedoluzhkoi

Cleistogenes nedoluzhkoi, commonly known as the Khabarovsk feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Far East of Russia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

Cleistogenes nedoluzhkoi is adapted to temperate climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
