Camderia

Camderia, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, erect stems rising from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots are submerged in aquatic environments. Camderia is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures, often representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Nymphaeaceae and is closely related to other water lilies.
