Celosia caudata

Celosia caudata, commonly known as the flame flower or cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a comb or flame. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, with flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Celosia caudata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
