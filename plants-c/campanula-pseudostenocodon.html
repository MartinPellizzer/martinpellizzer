Campanula pseudostenocodon

Campanula pseudostenocodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
