Crocus × nubigenoides

Crocus × nubigenoides, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a hybrid species within the Crocus genus.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and has adapted to cold, alpine environments. It produces bulbous roots and has narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
