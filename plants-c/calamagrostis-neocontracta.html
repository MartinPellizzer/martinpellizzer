Calamagrostis neocontracta

Calamagrostis neocontracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage that adds ornamental value to landscapes. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Calamagrostis neocontracta is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
