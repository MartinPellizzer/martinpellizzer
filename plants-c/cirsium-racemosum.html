Cirsium racemosum

Cirsium racemosum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a racemose inflorescence, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Cirsium racemosum is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
