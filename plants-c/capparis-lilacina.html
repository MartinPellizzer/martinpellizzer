Capparis lilacina

Capparis lilacina, commonly known as the lilac sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, lilac-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Capparis lilacina is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
