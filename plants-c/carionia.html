Carionia

Carionia, commonly known as the death flower, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a woody stem and thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Carionia is a member of the Boraginaceae family, which includes other plants with similar medicinal properties.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
