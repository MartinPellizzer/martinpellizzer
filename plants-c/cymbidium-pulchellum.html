Cymbidium pulchellum

Cymbidium pulchellum, commonly known as the orchid of the highlands, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Cymbidium pulchellum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
