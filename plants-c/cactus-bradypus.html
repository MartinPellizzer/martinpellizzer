Cactus bradypus

Cactus bradypus, commonly known as the slow-moving cactus, is a unique species that thrives in arid environments.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom during the warmer months.

Despite its name, the cactus bradypus is not a true cactus but belongs to the genus Bradypus, which is more closely related to the legumes.
