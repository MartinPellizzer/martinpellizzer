Carpinus betulus var. virginiana

Carpinus betulus var. virginiana, commonly known as the American hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, round nuts enclosed in a spiky husk.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats, where it plays a role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
