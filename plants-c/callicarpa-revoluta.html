Callicarpa revoluta

Callicarpa revoluta, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that transition into vibrant red berries in the fall. These berries are edible for humans and birds, contributing to its ecological importance.

Callicarpa revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
