Caucalis chrysocarpa

Caucalis chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically found in moist, grassy habitats. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a yellowish or greenish throat. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
