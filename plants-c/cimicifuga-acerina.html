Cimicifuga acerina

Cimicifuga acerina, commonly known as the red-barked cimicifuga, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
