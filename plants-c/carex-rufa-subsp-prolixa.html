Carex rufa subsp. prolixa

Carex rufa subsp.

prolixa, commonly known as the long-awned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
