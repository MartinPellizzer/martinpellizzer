Cirsium viperinum

Cirsium viperinum, commonly known as the viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
