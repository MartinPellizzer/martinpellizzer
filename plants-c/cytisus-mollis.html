Cytisus mollis

Cytisus mollis, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Cytisus mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
