Cattleya × gransabanensis

Cattleya × gransabanensis, commonly known as the Gransaban hybrid, is a hybrid orchid resulting from crosses between Cattleya mossiae and Cattleya bicolor.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow, often with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. It is a popular choice for horticulture due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.

The species is also valued for its genetic diversity, contributing to the development of many modern Cattleya hybrids.
