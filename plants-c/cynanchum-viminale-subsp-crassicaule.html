Cynanchum viminale subsp. crassicaule

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East. It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces showy, yellowish-white flowers in clusters.

Its milky sap, containing toxic compounds, is a notable botanical feature, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
