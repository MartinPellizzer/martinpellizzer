Chrysanthemum naktongense

Chrysanthemum naktongense, commonly known as the Naktong chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both horticultural and traditional medicinal purposes.

Its flowering period usually occurs in the fall, adding vibrant color to gardens during the autumn season.
