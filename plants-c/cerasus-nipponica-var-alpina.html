Cerasus nipponica var. alpina

Cerasus nipponica var. alpina, commonly known as the Japanese mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers cool, temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
