Campanula cochleariifolia var. gracilis

Campanula cochleariifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
