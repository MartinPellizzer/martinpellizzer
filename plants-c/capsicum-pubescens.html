Capsicum pubescens

Capsicum pubescens, commonly known as the Andean hot pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, often colorful fruits that range from yellow to red and can be spicy or mild depending on the variety. Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves and yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruits.

Capsicum pubescens is cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional Andean cuisine.
