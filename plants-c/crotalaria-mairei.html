Crotalaria mairei

Crotalaria mairei, commonly known as the Maire's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers with prominent spurred petals, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets. Crotalaria mairei is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which can remain on the plant for an extended period.
