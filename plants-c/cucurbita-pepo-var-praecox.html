Cucurbita pepo var. praecox

Cucurbita pepo var. praecox, commonly known as the early pumpkin, is a cultivated variant of the common gourd.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and early maturation. The plant produces cylindrical, smooth-skinned fruits that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often used for both ornamental purposes and as a source of edible seeds and flesh.
