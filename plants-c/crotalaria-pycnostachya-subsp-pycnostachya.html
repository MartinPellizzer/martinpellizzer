Crotalaria pycnostachya subsp. pycnostachya

Crotalaria pycnostachya subsp.

pycnostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed cowpea, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are typically yellow with dark veins and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It produces elliptical to oblong pods that contain several seeds, which are used for forage and human consumption.

This plant is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.
