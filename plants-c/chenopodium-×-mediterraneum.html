Chenopodium × mediterraneum

Chenopodium × mediterraneum, commonly known as Mediterranean goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically dry and winged, aiding in wind dispersal. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in disturbed soils and arid conditions.

Chenopodium × mediterraneum is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and birds.
