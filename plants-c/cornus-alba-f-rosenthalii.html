Cornus alba f. rosenthalii

Cornus alba f. rosenthalii, commonly known as the Rosenthal's Cornus or the Red Osier Dogwood.

This plant is a compact, shrub-like form of the common dogwood, characterized by its vibrant red stems that persist throughout the winter. It features small white flowers in clusters, which appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
