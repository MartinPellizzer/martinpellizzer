Carex cyanea

Carex cyanea, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or blue in color, giving it its common name. Carex cyanea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
