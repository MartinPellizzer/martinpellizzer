Capparis tetrasperma

Capparis tetrasperma, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, alternate leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
