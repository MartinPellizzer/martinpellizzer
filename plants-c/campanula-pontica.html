Campanula pontica

Campanula pontica, commonly known as the Pontic bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. Campanula pontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
