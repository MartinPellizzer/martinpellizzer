Crassula sarmentosa var. sarmentosa

Crassula sarmentosa var. sarmentosa, commonly known as the stringy crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and bushy appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
