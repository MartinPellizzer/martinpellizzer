Chuquiraga leucoxylon

Chuquiraga leucoxylon, commonly known as the Andean burr.

It is a shrub native to the Andes, characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, bur-like structure. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystem and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
