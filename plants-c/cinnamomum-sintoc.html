Cinnamomum sintoc

Cinnamomum sintoc, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or sintoc, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval-shaped berries containing dark brown seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
