Cissus ellenbeckii

Cissus ellenbeckii, commonly known as the rock fig or African grape, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling grapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Cissus ellenbeckii is often used in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability.
