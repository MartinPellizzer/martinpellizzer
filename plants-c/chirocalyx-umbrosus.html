Chirocalyx umbrosus

Chirocalyx umbrosus, commonly known as the shadow calyx, is a rare and enigmatic plant species characterized by its unique foliage and floral structure.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reflect excess light in its shaded habitat. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a deep shade of violet or purple, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in dense forest understories where light is limited.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Chirocalyx umbrosus is considered an endangered species in its native range.
