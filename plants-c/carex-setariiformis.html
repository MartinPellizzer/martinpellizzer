Carex setariiformis

Carex setariiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spike-like structure, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex setariiformis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
