Crotalaria peguana

Crotalaria peguana, commonly known as the Pepperyweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branched stems and palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and are followed by distinctive, spiny pods containing several seeds.

Crotalaria peguana is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
