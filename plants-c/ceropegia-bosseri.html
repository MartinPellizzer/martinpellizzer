Ceropegia bosseri

Ceropegia bosseri, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a sprawling, trailing growth habit with tuberous roots and fleshy, pale green to blue-green stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or white, with intricate patterns resembling spider webs. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
