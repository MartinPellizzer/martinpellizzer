Chionochloa pallens subsp. pallens

Chionochloa pallens subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
