Camarea ericoides

Camarea ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers in a spadix, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Camarea ericoides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.
