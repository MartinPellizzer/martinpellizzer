Centaurea attica subsp. pateraea

Centaurea attica subsp.

pateraea, commonly known as the Greek cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its small, bract-like leaves and showy inflorescence make it a notable species in its native habitat.
