Cyclopogon aphyllus

Cyclopogon aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless cyclopogon, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with the majority of its structure consisting of a dense cluster of bracts. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its specialized morphology, it is a rare and intriguing subject for botanical study.
