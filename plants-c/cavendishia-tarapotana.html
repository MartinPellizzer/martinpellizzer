Cavendishia tarapotana

Cavendishia tarapotana, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display a mix of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

Cavendishia tarapotana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and private collections.
