Carex akiensis

Carex akiensis, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has triangular, grass-like stems that are typically reddish or purple in color, especially at the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, three-parted flowers in spikelets.

Carex akiensis thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
