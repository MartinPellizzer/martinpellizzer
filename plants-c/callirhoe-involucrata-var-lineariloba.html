Callirhoe involucrata var. lineariloba

Callirhoe involucrata var. lineariloba, commonly known as the linearleaf blanketflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
