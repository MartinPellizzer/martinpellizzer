Catalpa

Catalpa, commonly known as the catalpa tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Bignoniaceae.

It is native to the Americas and includes several species, such as Catalpa bignonioides and Catalpa ovata. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Catalpa trees are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also known for their large, edible seeds.

Additionally, the bark of the catalpa tree has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
