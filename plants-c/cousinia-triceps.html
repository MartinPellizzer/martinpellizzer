Cousinia triceps

Cousinia triceps, commonly known as the three-lobed hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia triceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
