Cupania uraguensis

Cupania uraguensis, commonly known as the Uraguay cupania, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Cupania uraguensis is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
