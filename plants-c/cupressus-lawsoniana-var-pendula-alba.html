Cupressus lawsoniana var. pendula-alba

Cupressus lawsoniana var. pendula-alba, commonly known as the white weeping lacey cypress, is a variant of the Lawson cypress characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage.

This cultivar features long, pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The leaves are scale-like and light green, contributing to its ethereal look. It is a slow-growing evergreen that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique form and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in landscape design for its visual interest and adaptability.
