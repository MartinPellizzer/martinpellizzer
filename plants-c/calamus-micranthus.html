Calamus micranthus

Calamus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Calamus micranthus is often found in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for aquatic gardens and conservation efforts.
