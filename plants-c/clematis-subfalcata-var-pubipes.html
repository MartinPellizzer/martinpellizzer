Clematis subfalcata var. pubipes

Clematis subfalcata var. pubipes, commonly known as the pubescent clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it its varietal name.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, woodland environments.
