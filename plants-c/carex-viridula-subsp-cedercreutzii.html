Carex viridula subsp. cedercreutzii

Carex viridula subsp.

cedercreutzii, commonly known as the Cedercreutz sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

The plant is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in ecological gardens.
