Cyperus × ogawai

Cyperus × ogawai, commonly known as the Japanese flatsedge, is a wetland plant native to East Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus × ogawai is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
