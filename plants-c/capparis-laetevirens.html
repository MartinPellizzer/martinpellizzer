Capparis laetevirens

Capparis laetevirens, commonly known as the sweet caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, spherical berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Capparis laetevirens is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showing resilience to salt spray and drought.
