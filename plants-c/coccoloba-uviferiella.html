Coccoloba uviferiella

Coccoloba uviferiella, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, often with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.
