Colchicum burchellii subsp. burchellii

Colchicum burchellii subsp.

burchellii, commonly known as the African autumn crocus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It produces striking violet-blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant features a bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients and supports its growth cycle. Its leaves are lance-shaped and appear after the flowers, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
