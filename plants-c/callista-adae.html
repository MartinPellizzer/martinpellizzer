Callista adae

Callista adae, commonly known as the rainbow flower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms. Callista adae thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.
