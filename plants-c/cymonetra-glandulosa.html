Cymonetra glandulosa

Cymonetra glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cymonetra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits. Its leaves are often covered in small glands, which secrete a sticky substance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
