Cardamine circaeoides var. diversifolia

Cardamine circaeoides var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, wet environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The variety *diversifolia* is notable for its variable leaf morphology, which can differ significantly across populations.
