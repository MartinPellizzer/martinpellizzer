Centaurea appendicata

Centaurea appendicata, commonly known as the appendiculate cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea appendicata is often found in open habitats like grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.
