Citrus × depressa var. voangasay

Citrus × depressa var. voangasay, commonly known as the Voangasay kumquat, is a hybrid citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to orange as they mature. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications.
