Croton standleyanus

Croton standleyanus, commonly known as the red croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically broad and have a velvety texture, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
