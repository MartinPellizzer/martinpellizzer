Centrolepis caespitosa

Centrolepis caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted centrolepis, is a unique succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical stems that form a rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
