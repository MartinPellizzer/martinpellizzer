Cyanus velenovskyi

Cyanus velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its compact, bushy growth habit. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters, and it is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Cyanus velenovskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
