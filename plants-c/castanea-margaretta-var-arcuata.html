Castanea margaretta var. arcuata

Castanea margaretta var. arcuata, commonly known as the Margaretta chestnut, is a rare and ancient variant of the chestnut genus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated acorns and dense, spiny foliage. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
