Calophyllum hirtellum

Calophyllum hirtellum, commonly known as the hirtellum calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
