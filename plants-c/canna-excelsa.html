Canna excelsa

Canna excelsa, commonly known as the giant cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green color and a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces showy, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Canna excelsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in water features.
