Cardamine porphyroneura

Cardamine porphyroneura, commonly known as the purple-stemmed watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in woodland areas. Its stems are often tinged with a distinctive purple hue, which is a notable botanical feature.

The species is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
