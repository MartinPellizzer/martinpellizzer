Chenopodiastrum fasciculosum var. schimperi

Chenopodiastrum fasciculosum var. schimperi, commonly known as the African goosefoot, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or erect growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

This plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
