Conyza redolens

Conyza redolens, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

Conyza redolens is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
