Cyperus usitatus var. sanguinolentus

Cyperus usitatus var. sanguinolentus, commonly known as the red-veined flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically found in wetland habitats. Its reddish veins in the leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is often used in water gardens and as a decorative element in moist environments.
