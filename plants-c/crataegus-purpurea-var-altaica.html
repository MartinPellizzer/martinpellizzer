Crataegus purpurea var. altaica

Crataegus purpurea var. altaica, commonly known as the Altaian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but generally tart, often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates, making it a hardy and resilient species.
