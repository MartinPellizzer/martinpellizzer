Cupania hispida

Cupania hispida, commonly known as the roughcup plant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Cupania hispida is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
