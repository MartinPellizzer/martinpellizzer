Cleome spinifolia

Cleome spinifolia, commonly known as prairie cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 to 3 feet tall, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Cleome spinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
