Cheirolophus intybaceus subsp. lagunae

Cheirolophus intybaceus subsp.

lagunae, commonly known as the Almond-leaved Thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
