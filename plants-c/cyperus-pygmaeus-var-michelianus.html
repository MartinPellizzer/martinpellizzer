Cyperus pygmaeus var. michelianus

Cyperus pygmaeus var. michelianus, commonly known as the dwarf sedgeland sedge, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its low maintenance and adaptability, it is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping.
