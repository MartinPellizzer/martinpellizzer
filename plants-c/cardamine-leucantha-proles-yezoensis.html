Cardamine leucantha proles yezoensis

Cardamine leucantha proles yezoensis, commonly known as the Yezo white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It has a short rhizomatous root system and produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
