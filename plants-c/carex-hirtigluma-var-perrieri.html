Carex hirtigluma var. perrieri

Carex hirtigluma var. perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier’s sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous roots and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely arranged around the stem. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical, bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
