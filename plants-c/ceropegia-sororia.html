Ceropegia sororia

Ceropegia sororia, commonly known as the string of turtles, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. The plant has succulent, trailing stems that produce small, rounded leaves resembling turtle shells, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia sororia is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
