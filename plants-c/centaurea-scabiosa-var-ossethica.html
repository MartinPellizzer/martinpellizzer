Centaurea scabiosa var. ossethica

Centaurea scabiosa var. ossethica, commonly known as the Ossetian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, bright red to orange flower heads with surrounding blue to violet bracts, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often glandular and emit a mild scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
