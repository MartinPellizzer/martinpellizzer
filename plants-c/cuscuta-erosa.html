Cuscuta erosa

Cuscuta erosa, commonly known as yellow dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellow-orange flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to North America and can be found growing on a variety of plants, including crops and wild vegetation. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, and human activity, allowing it to spread widely.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
