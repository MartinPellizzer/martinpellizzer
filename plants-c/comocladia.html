Comocladia

Comocladia, commonly known as the sea grapes or *Ceratophyllum demersum*, is a freshwater plant characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, rounded leaves.

It is an aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving water, often found in ponds and lakes. The plant is notable for its ability to produce oxygen through photosynthesis, making it beneficial for aquatic ecosystems. Comocladia lacks true roots and instead relies on its stems for anchoring and nutrient absorption.

It is also used in aquariums and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
