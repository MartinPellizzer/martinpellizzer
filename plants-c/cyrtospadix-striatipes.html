Cyrtospadix striatipes

Cyrtospadix striatipes, commonly known as the striped spadix plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It features a spadix, a fleshy spike of small flowers, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, with a glossy surface, while the spadix is often striped with contrasting colors.

Cyrtospadix striatipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
