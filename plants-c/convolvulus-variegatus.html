Convolvulus variegatus

Convolvulus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, often colorful flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its flowers typically come in shades of purple, pink, and white, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
