Cacalia helophila

Cacalia helophila, commonly known as the marsh bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Cacalia helophila plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
