Calluna vulgaris f. praecox

Calluna vulgaris f. praecox, commonly known as early heather, is a subspecies of the common heather.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms earlier than the typical form of Calluna vulgaris, often in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found in heathlands and moorlands.

Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year.
