Carex × prostii

Carex × prostii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex × prostii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
