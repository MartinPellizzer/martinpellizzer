Colax palmifolius

Colax palmifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf colax, is a unique plant species characterized by its large, palm-like leaves.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes true palms, and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with a crown of broad, pinnately divided leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to their striking appearance.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
