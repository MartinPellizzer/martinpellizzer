Cirsium ochrocentrum subsp. martinii

Cirsium ochrocentrum subsp.

martinii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow to orange flower heads.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or serrated, with a rough texture. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.
