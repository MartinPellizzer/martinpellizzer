Cassia adenosperma

Cassia adenosperma, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia adenosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
