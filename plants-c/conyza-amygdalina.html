Conyza amygdalina

Conyza amygdalina, commonly known as goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can grow in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Conyza amygdalina is also known for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in treating ailments such as inflammation and skin conditions.
