Carex vexans

Carex vexans, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
