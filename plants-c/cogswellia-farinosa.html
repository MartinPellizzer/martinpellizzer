Cogswellia farinosa

Cogswellia farinosa, commonly known as the farinosa cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by fruit containing seeds. Its adaptations to dry environments include thick, fleshy stems and reduced leaves.

Cogswellia farinosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
