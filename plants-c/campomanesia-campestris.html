Campomanesia campestris

Campomanesia campestris, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Campomanesia campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
