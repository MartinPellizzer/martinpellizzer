Croton rufescens

Croton rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and vibrant, often yellow or orange, foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a waxy coating.

Croton rufescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
