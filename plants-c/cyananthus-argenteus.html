Cyananthus argenteus

Cyananthus argenteus, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Cyananthus argenteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
