Citrus medica f. tamurana

Citrus medica f. tamurana, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or kinkan, is a citrus fruit characterized by its small, round, and smooth skin.

It is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its sweet, aromatic fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit ripens in late autumn and is prized for its high vitamin C content and unique flavor.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
