Clematis tenii

Clematis tenii, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis tenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
