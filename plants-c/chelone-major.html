Chelone major

Chelone major, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Chelone major is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
