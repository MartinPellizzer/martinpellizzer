Clematis cordata subsp. pitcheri

Clematis cordata subsp.

pitcheri, commonly known as the Pitcher's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside and produces small, fragrant, blue-violet flowers in late spring.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
