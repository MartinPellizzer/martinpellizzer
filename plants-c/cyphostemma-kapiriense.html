Cyphostemma kapiriense

Cyphostemma kapiriense, commonly known as the Kapiriense moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by orange to red fruits containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
