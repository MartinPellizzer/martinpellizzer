Cinna ovata

Cinna ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, oval-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open meadows or forest edges.

Cinna ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
