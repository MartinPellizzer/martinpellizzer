Calliandra portoricensis

Calliandra portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican calliandra, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
