Cerastium arisanense

Cerastium arisanense, commonly known as the Arisan chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, fleshy leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cerastium arisanense is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
