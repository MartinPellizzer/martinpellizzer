Conioselinum schugnanicum

Conioselinum schugnanicum, commonly known as the Schugnan endemic wormwood, is a flowering plant species native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is aromatic, with finely divided leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
