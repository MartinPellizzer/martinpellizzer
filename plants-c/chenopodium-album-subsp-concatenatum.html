Chenopodium album subsp. concatenatum

Chenopodium album subsp.

concatenatum, commonly known as fat hen, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae. It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas.

The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

The species is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
