Clusia virgata

Clusia virgata, commonly known as the broadleaf poinsettia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are surrounded by brightly colored bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Clusia virgata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
