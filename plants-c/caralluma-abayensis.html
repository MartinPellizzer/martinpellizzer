Caralluma abayensis

Caralluma abayensis, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of India and parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom at night. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through features such as succulent leaves and deep root systems.

Caralluma abayensis is also known for its traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
