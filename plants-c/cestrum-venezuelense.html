Cestrum venezuelense

Cestrum venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan jessamine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white or yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Cestrum venezuelense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
