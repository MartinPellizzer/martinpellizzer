Chenopodium nitidispermum

Chenopodium nitidispermum, commonly known as the shining goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving them a shiny appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense, compact clusters.

Chenopodium nitidispermum is typically found in disturbed soils and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
