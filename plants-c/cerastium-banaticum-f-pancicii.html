Cerastium banaticum f. pancicii

Cerastium banaticum f. pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
