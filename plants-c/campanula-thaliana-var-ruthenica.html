Campanula thaliana var. ruthenica

Campanula thaliana var. ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
