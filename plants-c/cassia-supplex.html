Cassia supplex

Cassia supplex, commonly known as the mourning cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, flat pods containing numerous seeds. Cassia supplex thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
