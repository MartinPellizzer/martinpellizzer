Cymbopogon bassacensis

Cymbopogon bassacensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong citrus scent when crushed. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its rhizomes and leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their fragrance and potential therapeutic properties.
