Cyperus aureus subsp. esculentus

Cyperus aureus subsp.

esculentus, commonly known as water chestnut, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, spiky, pale yellow to brown flowers.

The plant is characterized by its spongy, edible underground tubers, which are a key part of its botanical significance. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
