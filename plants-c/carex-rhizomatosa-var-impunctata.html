Carex rhizomatosa var. impunctata

Carex rhizomatosa var. impunctata, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, typical of many Cyperaceae species. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It prefers full to partial sun and moist to wet soil conditions, often playing a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
