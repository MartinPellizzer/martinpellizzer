Convolvulus incanus

Convolvulus incanus, commonly known as the silverweed or white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Convolvulus incanus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in full sun and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its medicinal properties.
