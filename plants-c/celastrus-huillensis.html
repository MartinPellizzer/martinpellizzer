Celastrus huillensis

Celastrus huillensis, commonly known as the Huilliche staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of South American flora.
