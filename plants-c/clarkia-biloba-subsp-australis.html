Clarkia biloba subsp. australis

Clarkia biloba subsp.

australis, commonly known as the coastal clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and supporting local pollinators.
