Cocos acrocomioides

Cocos acrocomioides, commonly known as the coconut palm, is a flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces a distinctive fruit, the coconut, which contains both water and a white, fleshy mesocarp. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its economic and culinary uses.

The plant's ability to grow in coastal areas makes it an important species in many island ecosystems.
