Caragana maimanensis

Caragana maimanensis, commonly known as the Maiman's shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caragana maimanensis is often used in landscaping and for soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.
