Cynanchum riometense

Cynanchum riometense, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes many species of milkweeds. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive hooded structure.

Cynanchum riometense is known for its latex sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
