Carpodinus gentilii

Carpodinus gentilii, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a rare and exquisite flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the thin soil of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Carpodinus gentilii is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
