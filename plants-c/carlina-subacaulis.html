Carlina subacaulis

Carlina subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blossoms. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Carlina subacaulis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
