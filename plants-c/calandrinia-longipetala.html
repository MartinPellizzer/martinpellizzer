Calandrinia longipetala

Calandrinia longipetala, commonly known as the long-petioled portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, a characteristic known as nyctinasty.

Calandrinia longipetala is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
