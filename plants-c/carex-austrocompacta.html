Carex austrocompacta

Carex austrocompacta, commonly known as the southern compact sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces viable seeds that contribute to its ecological role in wetland habitats.

Carex austrocompacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
