Convolvulus hermanniae var. albidovillosus

Convolvulus hermanniae var. albidovillosus, commonly known as the white-hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale yellow or white flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
