Campanopsis riparia

Campanopsis riparia, commonly known as the river bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands and riverbanks of eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Campanopsis riparia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
