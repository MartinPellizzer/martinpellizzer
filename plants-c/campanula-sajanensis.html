Campanula sajanensis

Campanula sajanensis, commonly known as the Sajansky bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Campanula sajanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
