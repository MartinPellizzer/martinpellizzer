Coleus adenophorus

Coleus adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in whorls, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Coleus adenophorus is cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
