Chamaecrista ramosa var. curvifolia

Chamaecrista ramosa var. curvifolia, commonly known as the curlyleaf coral bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that curve inward, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in elongated racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its toxic seeds, it should be handled with care.
