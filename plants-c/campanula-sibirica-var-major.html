Campanula sibirica var. major

Campanula sibirica var. major, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
