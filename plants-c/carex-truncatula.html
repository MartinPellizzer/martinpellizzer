Carex truncatula

Carex truncatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along water edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually a dark green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the summer. Carex truncatula is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and related plants.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
