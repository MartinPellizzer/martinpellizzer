Cyathea traillii

Cyathea traillii, commonly known as the Traill's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in fibrous, persistent roots. The plant features a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea traillii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
