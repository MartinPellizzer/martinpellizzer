Carex hexinensis

Carex hexinensis, commonly known as the Hexin sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to China and is typically found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
