Convolvulus cataonicus

Convolvulus cataonicus, commonly known as the eastern bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its vines can spread aggressively.

It is native to regions across Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
