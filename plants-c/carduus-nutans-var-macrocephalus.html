Carduus nutans var. macrocephalus

Carduus nutans var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple flower heads that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it a notable ornamental plant. The plant features robust, spiny stems and broad, gray-green leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical structure includes a taproot system and a composite flower head composed of both ray and disk florets.
