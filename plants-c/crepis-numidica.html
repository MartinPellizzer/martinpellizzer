Crepis numidica

Crepis numidica, commonly known as the Numidian hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by numerous disk flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Crepis numidica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
