Calandrinia pygmaea

Calandrinia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a small, flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Calandrinia pygmaea is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it an ideal plant for xeriscaping.
