Convolvulus vidalii f. immaculatus

Convolvulus vidalii f. immaculatus, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a cultivated form of the species Convolvulus vidalii.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or blue hues of the species. This plant is a trailing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The varietal form 'immaculatus' is prized for its unique coloration and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
