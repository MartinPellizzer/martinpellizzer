Circaea lutetiana f. ericetorum

Circaea lutetiana f. ericetorum, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has slender, ascending stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges, heathlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
