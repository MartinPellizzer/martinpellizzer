Crataegus × atrorubella

Crataegus × atrorubella, commonly known as the blackthorn hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus monogyna (common blackthorn) and Crataegus laevigata (whitebeam). The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, small dark green leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. It produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

This species is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges across Europe.
