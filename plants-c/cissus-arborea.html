Cissus arborea

Cissus arborea, commonly known as the climbing fig or rock fig, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Cissus arborea is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or coastal environments, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
