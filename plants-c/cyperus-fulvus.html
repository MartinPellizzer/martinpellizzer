Cyperus fulvus

Cyperus fulvus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its root system helps stabilize soil in wetland habitats, contributing to ecological balance.

Cyperus fulvus is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
