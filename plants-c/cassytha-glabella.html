Cassytha glabella

Cassytha glabella, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a creeping, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features slender, serpentine stems that root at the nodes and produce small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has finely hairy leaves that are opposite and oval in shape. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
