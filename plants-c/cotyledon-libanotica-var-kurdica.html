Cotyledon libanotica var. kurdica

Cotyledon libanotica var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish arrowleaf, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces striking, violet or purple flowers in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
