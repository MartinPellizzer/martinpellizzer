Campanula andrewsii subsp. breueri

Campanula andrewsii subsp.

breueri, commonly known as the Breuer's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

The plant features erect, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to violet in color, and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
