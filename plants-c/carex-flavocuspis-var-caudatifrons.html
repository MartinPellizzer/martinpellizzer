Carex flavocuspis var. caudatifrons

Carex flavocuspis var. caudatifrons, commonly known as the golden-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The variety caudatifrons is distinguished by its elongated, drooping flower spikes, which add to its ornamental appeal.
