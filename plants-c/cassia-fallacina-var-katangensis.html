Cassia fallacina var. katangensis

Cassia fallacina var. katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine due to their potential pharmacological properties.
