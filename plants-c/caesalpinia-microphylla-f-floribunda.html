Caesalpinia microphylla f. floribunda

Caesalpinia microphylla f. floribunda, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, pod-like fruits. Its ornamental value is enhanced by the showy, clustered flowers that appear in spring.

The species is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
