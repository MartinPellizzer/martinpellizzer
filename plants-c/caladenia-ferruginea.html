Caladenia ferruginea

Caladenia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a single, strikingly colored flower with a distinctive, spider-like appearance. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a yellowish or brownish dorsal sepal.

Caladenia ferruginea is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
