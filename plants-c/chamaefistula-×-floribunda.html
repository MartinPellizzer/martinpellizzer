Chamaefistula × floribunda

Chamaefistula × floribunda, commonly known as the flowering laburnum, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its rapid growth and showy appearance, it is often used as a hedge or screening plant.
