Chamaecrista arboae

Chamaecrista arboae, commonly known as the tree burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its compound leaves and its role in nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in its native environments.
