Chrysoma solidaginoides

Chrysoma solidaginoides, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
