Cleistopholis grandiflora

Cleistopholis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a slow growth rate and is known for its hard, durable wood, which is resistant to decay. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cleistopholis grandiflora is an important species in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
