Carex pinetorum

Carex pinetorum, commonly known as the pine flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its grass-like leaves are usually dark green and have a rough texture, while its flowering spikes are usually reddish or brownish.

Carex pinetorum plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
