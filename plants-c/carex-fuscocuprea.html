Carex fuscocuprea

Carex fuscocuprea, commonly known as the brownish copper sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Carex fuscocuprea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
