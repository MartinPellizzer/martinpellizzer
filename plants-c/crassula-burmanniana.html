Crassula burmanniana

Crassula burmanniana, commonly known as the Burmann's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically forms dense clusters of rosettes, with leaves that are often blue-green in color. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring or early summer.

Crassula burmanniana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
