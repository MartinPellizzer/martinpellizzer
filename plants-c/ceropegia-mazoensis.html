Ceropegia mazoensis

Ceropegia mazoensis, commonly known as the Malawi moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as moths and bees. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Ceropegia mazoensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering pattern.
