Carex verna f. monostachya

Carex verna f. monostachya, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
