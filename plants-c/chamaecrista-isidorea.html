Chamaecrista isidorea

Chamaecrista isidorea, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. Its showy, yellow-orange flowers are arranged in dense, globular clusters, and it produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
