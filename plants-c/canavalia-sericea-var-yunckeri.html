Canavalia sericea var. yunckeri

Canavalia sericea var. yunckeri, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with silky, hairy surfaces. The flowers are typically pale purple or white, and the plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often used in traditional agriculture.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
