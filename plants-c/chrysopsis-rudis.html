Chrysopsis rudis

Chrysopsis rudis, commonly known as the rough chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late summer to early fall.
