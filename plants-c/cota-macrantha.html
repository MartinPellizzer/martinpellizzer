Cota macrantha

Cota macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered cota, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy, umbel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cota macrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
