Cupaniopsis hypodermatica

Cupaniopsis hypodermatica, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and silver-blue foliage. The plant produces showy, star-shaped white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments, featuring thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Cupaniopsis hypodermatica is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
