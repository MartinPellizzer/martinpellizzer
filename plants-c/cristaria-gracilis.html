Cristaria gracilis

Cristaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with its inflorescence bearing several white or pale pink flowers. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Cristaria gracilis is often found in association with other wetland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of these habitats.
