Cypripedium montanum

Cypripedium montanum, commonly known as the mountain lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rocky or loamy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Cypripedium montanum is a perennial plant that blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a unique ornamental value to its natural habitat.
