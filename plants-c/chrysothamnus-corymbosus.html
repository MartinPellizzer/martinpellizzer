Chrysothamnus corymbosus

Chrysothamnus corymbosus, commonly known as the golden bunch grass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots and produces slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
