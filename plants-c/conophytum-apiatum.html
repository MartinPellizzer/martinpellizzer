Conophytum apiatum

Conophytum apiatum, commonly known as the "Cape gold" or "Cape plant," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss through evaporation.

Conophytum apiatum is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
