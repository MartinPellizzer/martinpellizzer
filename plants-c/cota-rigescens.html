Cota rigescens

Cota rigescens, commonly known as the rigid cota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cota rigescens is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
