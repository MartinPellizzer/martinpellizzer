Cardamine raphanifolia subsp. gallaecica

Cardamine raphanifolia subsp.

gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician water cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small white flowers.

Native to the coastal regions of Galicia in northwestern Spain, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant produces siliques, which contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
