Cordia domingensis

Cordia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitats.
