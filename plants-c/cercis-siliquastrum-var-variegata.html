Cercis siliquastrum var. variegata

Cercis siliquastrum var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often display variegation with white or yellow margins. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in early spring, which appear before the leaves emerge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it an attractive addition to landscapes.
