Caladenia reniformis

Caladenia reniformis, commonly known as the Round-leafed Spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with long, thin sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open woodlands and heathlands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, elongated labellum.

Caladenia reniformis is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological specificity.
