Citrus tachibana

Citrus tachibana, commonly known as Japanese mandarin or tachibana, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Japan and southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested in late autumn. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
