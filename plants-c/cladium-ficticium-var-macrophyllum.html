Cladium ficticium var. macrophyllum

Cladium ficticium var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant sawgrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a dominant species in marsh ecosystems. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help filter water and reduce erosion.

Cladium ficticium var. macrophyllum is also an important component of the food web, supporting a wide range of wildlife.
