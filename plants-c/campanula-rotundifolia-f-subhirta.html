Campanula rotundifolia f. subhirta

Campanula rotundifolia f. subhirta, commonly known as the rounded-leaved bellflower, is a cultivar of the bellflower species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive round, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its charming appearance.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
