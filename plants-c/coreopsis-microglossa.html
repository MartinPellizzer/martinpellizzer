Coreopsis microglossa

Coreopsis microglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to diverse landscapes.
