Consolida divaricata var. gracillima

Consolida divaricata var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender sweetpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their delicate appearance.

This variety is noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
