Cynodendron bicolor

Cynodendron bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored grass tree, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking appearance, featuring tall, grass-like leaves that are often green on one side and white or pale on the other. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, which bloom in the dry season. Cynodendron bicolor is adapted to arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its distinctive coloration and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitats.
