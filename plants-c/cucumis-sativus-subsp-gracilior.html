Cucumis sativus subsp. gracilior

Cucumis sativus subsp.

gracilior, commonly known as the slender cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber. It is characterized by its thinner, more elongated fruits compared to standard cucumber varieties.

This subspecies typically grows on trailing vines and produces small, often pale green fruits. It is native to regions of southern and eastern Asia, where it thrives in warm climates.

The plant's flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, a common trait in the Cucurbitaceae family.
