Crataegus scabrida var. hadleyana

Crataegus scabrida var. hadleyana, commonly known as the Hadley's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, thriving in temperate climates.
