Croton cupreolepis

Croton cupreolepis, commonly known as the copperleaf or red copperleaf, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, copper-colored leaves that turn reddish in some varieties. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Croton cupreolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
