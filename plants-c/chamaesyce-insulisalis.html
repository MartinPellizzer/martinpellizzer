Chamaesyce insulisalis

Chamaesyce insulisalis, commonly known as the island sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous species that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Chamaesyce insulisalis is often found in coastal areas and is well-suited to rocky or gravelly environments.
