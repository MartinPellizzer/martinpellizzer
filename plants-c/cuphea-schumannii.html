Cuphea schumannii

Cuphea schumannii, commonly known as the Schumann's candleplant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea schumannii is known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators due to its nectar-rich flowers.
