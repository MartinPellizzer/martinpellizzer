Capparis florida

Capparis florida, commonly known as the sweetbriar or common caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Capparis florida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
