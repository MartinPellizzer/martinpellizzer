Calophyllum mariae

Calophyllum mariae, commonly known as the Mary's candlewood, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its bark is thick and often flaky, while its leaves are simple and alternate.

Calophyllum mariae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
