Campanula myosotidifolia

Campanula myosotidifolia, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula myosotidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
