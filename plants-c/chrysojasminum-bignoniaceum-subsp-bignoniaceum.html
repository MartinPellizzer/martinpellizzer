Chrysojasminum bignoniaceum subsp. bignoniaceum

Chrysojasminum bignoniaceum subsp.

bignoniaceum, commonly known as the golden jasmines, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bignoniaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or gold in color.

The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
