Calispermum scandens

Calispermum scandens, commonly known as the climbing cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial vine that typically grows in moist, temperate regions, often found along stream banks and in woodland areas. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy. Its fruit is a slender, cylindrical silique that contains several seeds.

Calispermum scandens is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
