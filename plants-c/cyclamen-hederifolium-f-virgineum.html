Cyclamen hederifolium f. virgineum

Cyclamen hederifolium f. virgineum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a hybrid form of the European cyclamen.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bowl-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers cool, moist environments and is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its popularity in horticulture, though it requires careful attention to avoid overwatering.
