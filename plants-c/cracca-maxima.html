Cracca maxima

Cracca maxima, commonly known as the giant cracca, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features a tall, branching stem with rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, with both ray and disc florets contributing to its vibrant appearance.

Cracca maxima thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.
