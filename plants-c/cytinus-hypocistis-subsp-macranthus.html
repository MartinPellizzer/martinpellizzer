Cytinus hypocistis subsp. macranthus

Cytinus hypocistis subsp.

macranthus, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family. It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has a short lifespan and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The subspecies macranthus is distinguished by its larger flowers compared to other subspecies of Cytinus.
