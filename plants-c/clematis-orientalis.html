Clematis orientalis

Clematis orientalis, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to purple, often with a distinctive violet or blue hue. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
