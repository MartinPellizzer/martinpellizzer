Cordylanthus pilosus var. bolanderi

Cordylanthus pilosus var. bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's bushvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has finely hairy foliage and produces leguminous pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
