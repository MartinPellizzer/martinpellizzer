Calamus pseudozebrinus

Calamus pseudozebrinus, commonly known as the false zebra plant, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a distinctive striped pattern that resembles the zebra's markings.

Calamus pseudozebrinus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
