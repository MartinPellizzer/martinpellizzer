Chenopodium ambrosioides var. suffruticosum

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. suffruticosum, commonly known as pigweed or lambsquarters, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
