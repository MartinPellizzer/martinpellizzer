Convolvulus frankeanus

Convolvulus frankeanus, commonly known as the Franke's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing or trailing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant.
