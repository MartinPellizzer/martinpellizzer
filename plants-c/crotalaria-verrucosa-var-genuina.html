Crotalaria verrucosa var. genuina

Crotalaria verrucosa var. genuina, commonly known as the woolly crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
