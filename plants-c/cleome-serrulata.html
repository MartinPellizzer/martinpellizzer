Cleome serrulata

Cleome serrulata, commonly known as the cutleaf spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that resemble spiderwebs. The leaves are deeply lobed and serrated, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
