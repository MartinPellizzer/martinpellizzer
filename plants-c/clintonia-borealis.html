Clintonia borealis

Clintonia borealis, commonly known as the northern star or white star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, often in areas with rich, acidic soil. The plant features lance-shaped, waxy leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers in early spring. Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin and distinctive floral appearance.

Clintonia borealis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
