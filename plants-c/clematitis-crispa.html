Clematitis crispa

Clematitis crispa, commonly known as the crinkled clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and clusters of fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is a deciduous vine that typically grows along forest edges or in shaded areas. It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Clematitis crispa prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
