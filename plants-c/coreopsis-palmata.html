Coreopsis palmata

Coreopsis palmata, commonly known as the palmate coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features palmately divided leaves, which give it its common name, and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
