Calluna vulgaris subf. adscendens

Calluna vulgaris subf.

adscendens, commonly known as the ascending heather, is a subspecies of the common heather native to western Europe. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and dark green, providing year-round structure to its habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
