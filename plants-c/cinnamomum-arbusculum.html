Cinnamomum arbusculum

Cinnamomum arbusculum, commonly known as the Indian cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic bark that is often used as a spice. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits.

The bark is harvested for its aromatic qualities and is closely related to the more well-known cinnamon species.
