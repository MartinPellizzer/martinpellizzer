Calyptranthes chrysophylloides

Calyptranthes chrysophylloides, commonly known as the golden-leaf calyptranthes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying a yellowish tinge. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
