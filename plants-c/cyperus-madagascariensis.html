Cyperus madagascariensis

Cyperus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the base of the plant. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens or used in wetland restoration projects.
