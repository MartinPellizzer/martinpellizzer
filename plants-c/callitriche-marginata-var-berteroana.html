Callitriche marginata var. berteroana

Callitriche marginata var. berteroana, commonly known as the Mexican water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly notched at the edges. Its flowers are tiny, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom throughout the growing season.

It is an important part of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
