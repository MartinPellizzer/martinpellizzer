Chrysocephalum apiculatum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flower heads that are highly ornamental. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
