Chrysothamnus californicus var. occidentalis

Chrysothamnus californicus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
