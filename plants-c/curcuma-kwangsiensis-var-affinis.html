Curcuma kwangsiensis var. affinis

Curcuma kwangsiensis var. affinis, commonly known as the Guangxi turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish rhizomes. The plant produces erect, leafy stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically orange or reddish, clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stems.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
