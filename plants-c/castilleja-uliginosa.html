Castilleja uliginosa

Castilleja uliginosa, commonly known as the marsh cinquefoil, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes.

It has bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. It forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi to obtain nutrients.

Castilleja uliginosa is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
