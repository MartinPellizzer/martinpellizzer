Crinum cochinchinense

Crinum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese lily, is a bulbous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in humid climates. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette around the bulb.

The flowers, which can be white, yellow, or pink, bloom in summer and are pollinated by insects.
