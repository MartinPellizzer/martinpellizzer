Calliandra gilbertii

Calliandra gilbertii, commonly known as the Gilbert's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Calliandra gilbertii is often used in ornamental gardening and can also serve as a nitrogen-fixing plant in soil improvement.
