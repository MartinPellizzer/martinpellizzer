Chaerophyllum colensoi

Chaerophyllum colensoi, commonly known as the Colenso's hogweed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions like KwaZulu-Natal. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or riverbanks. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and it produces small white or greenish flowers in clustered umbels.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
