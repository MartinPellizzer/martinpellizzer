Cardamine loxostemonoides var. luxurians

Cardamine loxostemonoides var. luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators. Its roots are typically fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from water.

This variety is often found in slow-moving streams and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions.
