Calycanthus acuminatus

Calycanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the sourwood or redroot candleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often appearing in late spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental value.

Calycanthus acuminatus is valued in gardens for its unique floral scent and adaptability to various soil types.
