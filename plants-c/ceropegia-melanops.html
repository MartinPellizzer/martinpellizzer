Ceropegia melanops

Ceropegia melanops, commonly known as the silver leaf string plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features trailing stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
