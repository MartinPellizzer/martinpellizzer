Clidemia rubra

Clidemia rubra, commonly known as red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with showy bracts that add to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clidemia rubra is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ornamental and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
