Casearia parviflora var. microphylla

Casearia parviflora var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
