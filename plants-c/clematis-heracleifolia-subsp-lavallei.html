Clematis heracleifolia subsp. lavallei

Clematis heracleifolia subsp.

lavallei, commonly known as the Lavalle's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Argentina. It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or walls.
