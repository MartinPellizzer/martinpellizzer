Comparettia ottonis

Comparettia ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's flame orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright orange and red flowers that resemble flames. The plant has aerial roots that help it attach to trees and absorb moisture from the air. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Comparettia ottonis thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
