Cardamine heleniae

Cardamine heleniae, commonly known as Helen's watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery aroma.

The small white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.
