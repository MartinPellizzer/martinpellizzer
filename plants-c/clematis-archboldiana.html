Clematis archboldiana

Clematis archboldiana, commonly known as the Florida clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows along coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Clematis archboldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
