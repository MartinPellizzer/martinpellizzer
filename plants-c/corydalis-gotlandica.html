Corydalis gotlandica

Corydalis gotlandica, commonly known as Gotland carnation, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Baltic region.

It features delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular species among gardeners interested in alpine and perennial flora.
