Caroxylon omaruruense

Caroxylon omaruruense, commonly known as the Namib Desert date palm, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and sparse, drooping leaves. The plant is well adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its trunk and having a slow growth rate.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important subject of botanical study in desert ecosystems.
