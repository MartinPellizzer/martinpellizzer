Corchorus pilolobus

Corchorus pilolobus, commonly known as the African hibiscus or kalahari mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming from summer to autumn. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savannas.
