Camellia megasepala

Camellia megasepala, commonly known as the giant camellia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and a woody stem, typical of the Camellia genus. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its potential in horticultural research.
