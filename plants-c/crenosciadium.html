Crenosciadium

Crenosciadium, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique, spiraling growth pattern.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively across moist, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Crenosciadium thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
