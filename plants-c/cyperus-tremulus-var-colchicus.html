Cyperus tremulus var. colchicus

Cyperus tremulus var. colchicus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches.

Its growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
