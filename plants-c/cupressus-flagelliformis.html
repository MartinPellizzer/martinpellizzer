Cupressus flagelliformis

Cupressus flagelliformis, commonly known as the fishtail cypress, is a species of conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in a dense, fibrous mat. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique visual appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
