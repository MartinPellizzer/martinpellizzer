Caladenia carnea f. calliniger

Caladenia carnea f. calliniger, commonly known as the pink-streaked spider orchid, is a unique orchid species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and striking, pink-tinged flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or pale red with intricate markings, and it blooms during the cooler months of spring and early summer.

This rare and beautiful orchid is often found in specific habitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
