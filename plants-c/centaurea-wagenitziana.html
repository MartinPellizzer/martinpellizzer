Centaurea wagenitziana

Centaurea wagenitziana, commonly known as the Wagenitz's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
