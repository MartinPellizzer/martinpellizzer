Cenchrus taitensis

Cenchrus taitensis, commonly known as the Tait's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia, Hawaii, and the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Cenchrus taitensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in coastal or sandy environments.
