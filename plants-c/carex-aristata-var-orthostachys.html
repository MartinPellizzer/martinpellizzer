Carex aristata var. orthostachys

Carex aristata var. orthostachys, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, upright leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-brown spikelets that mature into dark brown berries. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas, stream banks, and wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
