Calamagrostis austroscaberula

Calamagrostis austroscaberula, commonly known as the eastern tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage that turns golden in the fall. The plant produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Calamagrostis austroscaberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
