Cornus controversa var. variegata

Cornus controversa var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
