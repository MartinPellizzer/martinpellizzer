Chamaeleon speciosus

Chamaeleon speciosus, commonly known as the colorful chameleon plant, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, often blue or purple, tubular flowers. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Chamaeleon speciosus is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
