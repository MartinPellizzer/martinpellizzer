Crinum × letitiae

Crinum × letitiae, commonly known as the hybrid Crinum luteum × Crinum bulbisacrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a bulbous stem that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and distinctive foliage.
