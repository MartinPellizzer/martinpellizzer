Chenopodium murale var. schimperi

Chenopodium murale var. schimperi, commonly known as red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or dry environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
