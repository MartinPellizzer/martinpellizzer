Carex manca subsp. takasagoana

Carex manca subsp.

takasagoana, commonly known as the Takasago sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
