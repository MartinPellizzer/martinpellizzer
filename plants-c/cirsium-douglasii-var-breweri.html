Cirsium douglasii var. breweri

Cirsium douglasii var. breweri, commonly known as Douglas's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into bristly, spiny seed heads.

This plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
