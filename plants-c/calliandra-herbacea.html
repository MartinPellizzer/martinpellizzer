Calliandra herbacea

Calliandra herbacea, commonly known as the coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red, pink, or yellow flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calliandra herbacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
