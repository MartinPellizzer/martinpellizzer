Carex hebecarpa subsp. ligulata

Carex hebecarpa subsp.

ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. It has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated.
