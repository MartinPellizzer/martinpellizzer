Chenopodium album var. hastatum

Chenopodium album var. hastatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it often grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It has a short life cycle and is commonly found as a weed in agricultural areas.

The species is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked like spinach.
