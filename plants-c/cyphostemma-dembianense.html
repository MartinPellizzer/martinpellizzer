Cyphostemma dembianense

Cyphostemma dembianense, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
