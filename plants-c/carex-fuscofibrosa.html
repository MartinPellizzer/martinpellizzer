Carex fuscofibrosa

Carex fuscofibrosa, commonly known as the dark fiber sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, where it forms dense colonies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark brown, fibrous seeds.

Carex fuscofibrosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
