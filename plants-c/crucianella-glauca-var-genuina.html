Crucianella glauca var. genuina

Crucianella glauca var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in saline or sandy soils. It is often found in dune environments and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
