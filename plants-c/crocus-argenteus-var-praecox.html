Crocus argenteus var. praecox

Crocus argenteus var. praecox, commonly known as the early white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms early in the spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early flowering habit.
