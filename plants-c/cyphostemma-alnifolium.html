Cyphostemma alnifolium

Cyphostemma alnifolium, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
