Crassula peculiaris

Crassula peculiaris, commonly known as the "Peculiar Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically forms a low-growing, compact rosette with a woolly texture, and its leaves are often tinged with purple or red hues. Crassula peculiaris produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
