Charybdis maura

Charybdis maura, commonly known as the yellow trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Charybdis maura is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
