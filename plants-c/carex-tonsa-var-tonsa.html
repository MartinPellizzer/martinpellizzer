Carex tonsa var. tonsa

Carex tonsa var. tonsa, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex tonsa var. tonsa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
