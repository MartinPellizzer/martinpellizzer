Carex spaniocarpa

Carex spaniocarpa, commonly known as the upland sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
