Cerastium arvense var. verticellare

Cerastium arvense var. verticellare, commonly known as the upright mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in temperate regions and a useful plant for ecological studies.
