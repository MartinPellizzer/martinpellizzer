Calamagrostis magadanica

Calamagrostis magadanica, commonly known as the Magadan reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the cold climates of Eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It thrives in wet, boggy environments and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including frost and poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native range.

Due to its unique ecological role and aesthetic appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in specialty gardens in cooler regions.
