Carex athrostachya var. unilateralis

Carex athrostachya var. unilateralis, commonly known as the one-sided sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a spike-like arrangement of flowering structures. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.
