Cnicus asiaticus

Cnicus asiaticus, commonly known as the Asiatic sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features a rosette of smooth, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a single, terminal cluster. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cnicus asiaticus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
