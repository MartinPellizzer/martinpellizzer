Cestrum atrovirens

Cestrum atrovirens, commonly known as the black-eyed jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a dark green or black center. These flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climates.
