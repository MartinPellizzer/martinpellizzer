Cytisus balansae subsp. nevadensis

Cytisus balansae subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada broom, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
