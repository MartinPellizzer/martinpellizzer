Calothamnus graniticus subsp. leptophyllus

Calothamnus graniticus subsp.

leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaved golden everlasting, is a low-growing shrub native to the southern regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils, often found on rocky outcrops and granite hills. It is a hardy species adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it drought-tolerant.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important component of local flora.
