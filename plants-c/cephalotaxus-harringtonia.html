Cephalotaxus harringtonia

Cephalotaxus harringtonia, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to eastern Asia.

It features a distinctive, compact growth habit with dense, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that contain seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and longevity.

Cephalotaxus harringtonia is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
