Coelogyne parishii

Coelogyne parishii, commonly known as the Purple Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters on tall, slender inflorescences. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Coelogyne parishii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
