Ceratostylis mayrii

Ceratostylis mayrii, commonly known as the Mayri's spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, elongated spike bearing numerous small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
