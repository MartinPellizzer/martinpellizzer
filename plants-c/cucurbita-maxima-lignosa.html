Cucurbita maxima lignosa

Cucurbita maxima lignosa, commonly known as the hardy pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to North America.

It is characterized by its robust, thick-walled fruits that can vary in color from green to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as a vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is used for both culinary and ornamental purposes, and it is valued for its hardiness in cooler climates.

The plant's botanical features include a strong, woody stem and a long growing season.
