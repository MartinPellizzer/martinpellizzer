Carex paracheniana

Carex paracheniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to wet or moist habitats, it thrives in environments with consistent moisture, such as marshes or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in suitable conditions.
