Coptis chinensis var. brevisepala

Coptis chinensis var. brevisepala, commonly known as the broad-leaved goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow rhizomes, which contain the alkaloid berberine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has palmate leaves with a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in cymes, often blooming in early spring.

This variant is valued both for its medicinal properties and its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
