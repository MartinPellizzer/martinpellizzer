Commelina calandrinioides

Commelina calandrinioides, commonly known as the slender dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers bloom during the day and close at night, a feature that gives it its common name.

Commelina calandrinioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
