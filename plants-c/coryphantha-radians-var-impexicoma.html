Coryphantha radians var. impexicoma

Coryphantha radians var. impexicoma, commonly known as the Mexican ghost cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
