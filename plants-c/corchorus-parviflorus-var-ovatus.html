Corchorus parviflorus var. ovatus

Corchorus parviflorus var. ovatus, commonly known as the broad-flowered dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features robust, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with soft, hairy foliage. The leaves are broadly ovate, with a waxy surface and serrated margins, and they are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is known for its tolerance to poor soil conditions and its ability to thrive in full sun.
