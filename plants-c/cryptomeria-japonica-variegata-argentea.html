Cryptomeria japonica variegata-argentea

Cryptomeria japonica variegata-argentea, commonly known as the silver-variegated Japanese cedar, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and yellow markings on its needle-like leaves. This plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The leaves are typically shorter and more densely packed compared to the standard species, contributing to its unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for horticultural displays.
