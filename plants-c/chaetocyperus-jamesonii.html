Chaetocyperus jamesonii

Chaetocyperus jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
