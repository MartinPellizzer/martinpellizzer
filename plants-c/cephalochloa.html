Cephalochloa

Cephalochloa, commonly known as the "head grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plants typically have a basal rosette of leaves with a dense cluster of flowering heads at the center. Cephalochloa species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

They play a role in soil stabilization and provide habitat for various small invertebrates.
