Convolvulus althaeoides var. angustisectus

Convolvulus althaeoides var. angustisectus, commonly known as narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Convolvulaceae family.
