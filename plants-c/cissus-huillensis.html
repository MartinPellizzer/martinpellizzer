Cissus huillensis

Cissus huillensis, commonly known as the Huilliche devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its adaptability to various soil types and drought resistance make it a resilient species.

Cissus huillensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
