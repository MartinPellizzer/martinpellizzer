Cucumis melo var. gourbek

Cucumis melo var. gourbek, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It requires well-drained soil and regular watering for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary uses.
