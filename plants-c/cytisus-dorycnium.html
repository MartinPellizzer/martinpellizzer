Cytisus dorycnium

Cytisus dorycnium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and woodland edges. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
