Carex olivacea var. angustior

Carex olivacea var. angustior, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne on separate flowering stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
