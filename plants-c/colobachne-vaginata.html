Colobachne vaginata

Colobachne vaginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Colobachne vaginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
