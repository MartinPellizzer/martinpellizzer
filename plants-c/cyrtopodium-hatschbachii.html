Cyrtopodium hatschbachii

Cyrtopodium hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatschbach's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It features a slender, erect stem with several fleshy, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale green flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cyrtopodium hatschbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
