Claoxylon occidentale

Claoxylon occidentale, commonly known as the western claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Claoxylaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its rarity and limited distribution, it is considered an endangered species in its natural habitat.
