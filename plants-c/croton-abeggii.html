Croton abeggii

Croton abeggii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that vary in shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its vibrant foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical and subtropical regions.
