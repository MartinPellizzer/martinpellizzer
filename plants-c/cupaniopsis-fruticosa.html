Cupaniopsis fruticosa

Cupaniopsis fruticosa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant produces edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
