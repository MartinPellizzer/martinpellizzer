Chaenomeles maulei var. alpina

Chaenomeles maulei var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thick, corky bark and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible but not commonly cultivated for consumption.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
