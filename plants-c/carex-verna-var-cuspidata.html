Carex verna var. cuspidata

Carex verna var. cuspidata, commonly known as early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex verna var. cuspidata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
