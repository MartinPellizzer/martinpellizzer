Carex brizoides f. remota

Carex brizoides f. remota, commonly known as the remote sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a distinctive flowering structure.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
