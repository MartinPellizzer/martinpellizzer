Calocarpum mammosum var. ovoideum

Calocarpum mammosum var. ovoideum, commonly known as the egg-shaped milk tree, is a unique variant of the milk tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, milk-producing structures that resemble small eggs, giving it its common name. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a fleshy, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is known for its slow growth and ornamental value.
