Carlina vulgaris var. orophila

Carlina vulgaris var. orophila, commonly known as the alpine carline thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray, woolly leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Carlina vulgaris var. orophila is a perennial species that plays an important role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
