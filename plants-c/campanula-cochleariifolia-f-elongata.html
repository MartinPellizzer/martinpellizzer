Campanula cochleariifolia f. elongata

Campanula cochleariifolia f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated bellflower, is a cultivar of the bellflower species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged oppositely along the stem. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
