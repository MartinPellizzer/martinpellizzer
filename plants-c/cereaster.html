Cereaster

Cereaster, commonly known as the starflower, is a member of the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its star-shaped flowers, which typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, sandy soils. Cereaster has small, opposite leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
