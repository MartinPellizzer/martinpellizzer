Cornus kweichowensis

Cornus kweichowensis, commonly known as the Yunnan cornel, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cornus kweichowensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
