Carex liparocarpos subsp. bordzilowskii

Carex liparocarpos subsp.

bordzilowskii, commonly known as the Balkan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
