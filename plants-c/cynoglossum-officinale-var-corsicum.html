Cynoglossum officinale var. corsicum

Cynoglossum officinale var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are bell-shaped and arranged in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
