Chrysocoma coronopifolia

Chrysocoma coronopifolia, commonly known as the white thorn tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, round fruits that are edible for some animals. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
