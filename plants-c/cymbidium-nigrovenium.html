Cymbidium nigrovenium

Cymbidium nigrovenium, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces tall, upright inflorescences bearing clusters of dark purple to black flowers with contrasting white veins. Its leaves are strap-shaped, long, and often covered in a waxy coating.

Cymbidium nigrovenium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
