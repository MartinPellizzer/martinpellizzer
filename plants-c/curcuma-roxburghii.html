Curcuma roxburghii

Curcuma roxburghii, commonly known as turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange rhizomes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces pale yellow to white flowers. Its rhizomes are rich in curcumin, a compound responsible for its vibrant color and medicinal properties.

Curcuma roxburghii is widely cultivated for both culinary and traditional medicinal uses.
