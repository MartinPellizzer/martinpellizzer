Cota tinctoria var. gaudium-solis

Cota tinctoria var. gaudium-solis, commonly known as the golden solis cota, is a variant of the Mexican sunflower.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Central America and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is an annual plant that blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and a distinct, upright growth habit.
