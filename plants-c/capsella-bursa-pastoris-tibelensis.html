Capsella bursa-pastoris tibelensis

Capsella bursa-pastoris tibelensis, commonly known as the Turkish chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and oval, hairy leaves. The plant produces distinctive heart-shaped pods that enclose its seeds, a feature that gives it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
