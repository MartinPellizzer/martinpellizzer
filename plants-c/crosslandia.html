Crosslandia

Crosslandia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Schlumbergera and is characterized by its segmented stems that resemble the branches of a tree. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season, typically between December and February. Crosslandia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for indoor cultivation.
