Crocus gunae

Crocus gunae, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of saffron-like stigmas.

Crocus gunae is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
