Cucumis grossularoides

Cucumis grossularoides, commonly known as the wild cucumber or African horned cucumber, is a vine species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It produces elongated, spiny fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn yellow or orange. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, cucumber-like flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique appearance.
