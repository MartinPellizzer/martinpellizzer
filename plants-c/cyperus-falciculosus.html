Cyperus falciculosus

Cyperus falciculosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant has slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus falciculosus is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
