Cucumis globosus

Cucumis globosus, commonly known as the globe cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant produces round, green fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and large, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The globe cucumber is often cultivated for its edible, crisp fruit, which has a mild flavor and high water content.
