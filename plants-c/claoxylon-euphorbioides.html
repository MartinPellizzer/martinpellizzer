Claoxylon euphorbioides

Claoxylon euphorbioides, commonly known as the African milkwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, three-lobed capsules containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in savanna ecosystems.

The tree is also notable for its latex, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
