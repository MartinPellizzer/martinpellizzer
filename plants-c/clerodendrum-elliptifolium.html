Clerodendrum elliptifolium

Clerodendrum elliptifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in summer. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
