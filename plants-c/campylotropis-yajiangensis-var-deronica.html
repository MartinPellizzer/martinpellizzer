Campylotropis yajiangensis var. deronica

Campylotropis yajiangensis var. deronica, commonly known as the Yajiang bush clover, is a flowering plant native to the Yajiang region of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky and mountainous habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
