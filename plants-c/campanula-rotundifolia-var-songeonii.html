Campanula rotundifolia var. songeonii

Campanula rotundifolia var. songeonii, commonly known as the Songeonii bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive round, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The varietal form, songeonii, is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and flower coloration compared to the typical variety.
