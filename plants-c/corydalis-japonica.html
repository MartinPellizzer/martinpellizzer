Corydalis japonica

Corydalis japonica, commonly known as the Japanese candytuft, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has a fibrous root system and features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland edges.

Corydalis japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
