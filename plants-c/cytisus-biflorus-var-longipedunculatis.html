Cytisus biflorus var. longipedunculatis

Cytisus biflorus var. longipedunculatis, commonly known as the long peduncled broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, thorny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are small, trifoliate, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in ecological restoration projects.
