Cybianthus dussii

Cybianthus dussii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its deep taproot system and succulent leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Cybianthus dussii is often found in sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
