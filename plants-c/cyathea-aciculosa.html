Cyathea aciculosa

Cyathea aciculosa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense network, aiding in its ability to anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are bi-directionally arranged, with the lower fronds often being shorter and more compact.

Cyathea aciculosa plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to the forest's biodiversity.
