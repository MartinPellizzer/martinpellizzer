Centropogon ferrugineus var. parviflorus

Centropogon ferrugineus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, purple to pink ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

The small, clustered flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
