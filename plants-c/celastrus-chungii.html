Celastrus chungii

Celastrus chungii, commonly known as the Chinese staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
