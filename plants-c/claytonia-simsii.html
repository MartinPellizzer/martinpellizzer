Claytonia simsii

Claytonia simsii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and native plant collections.
