Convolvulus eichwaldii

Convolvulus eichwaldii, commonly known as the Eichwald's bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to regions of southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
