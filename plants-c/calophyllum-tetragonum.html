Calophyllum tetragonum

Calophyllum tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and its leaves are typically alternate and simple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
