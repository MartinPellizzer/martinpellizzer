Cleome kerberi

Cleome kerberi, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, spindly flowers that resemble spiders. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Cleome kerberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
