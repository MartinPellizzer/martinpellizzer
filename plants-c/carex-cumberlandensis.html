Carex cumberlandensis

Carex cumberlandensis, commonly known as Cumberland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Carex cumberlandensis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
