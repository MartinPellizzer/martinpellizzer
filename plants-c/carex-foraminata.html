Carex foraminata

Carex foraminata, commonly known as the threadleaf sedgeland sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and riparian zones. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm in width, giving it a delicate appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex foraminata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native wetland habitats.
