Centaurea sibthorpii

Centaurea sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads.
