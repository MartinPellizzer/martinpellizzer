Croton guadeloupensis

Croton guadeloupensis, commonly known as the Guadeloupe croton, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the island of Guadeloupe.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, colorful flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Croton guadeloupensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
