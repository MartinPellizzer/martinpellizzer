Calceolaria caerulescens

Calceolaria caerulescens, commonly known as the blue slipper flower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-shaped petals that resemble tiny slippers, often in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, thriving in rocky soils. It produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
