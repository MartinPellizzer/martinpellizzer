Carex deflexa var. deflexa

Carex deflexa var. deflexa, commonly known as the drooping sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a distinct sheathing base.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
