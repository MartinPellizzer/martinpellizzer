Carex forsteri var. cockayneana

Carex forsteri var. cockayneana, commonly known as the Cockayne's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
