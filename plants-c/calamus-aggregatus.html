Calamus aggregatus

Calamus aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features long, slender rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that are often green to reddish in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in spikes, and it produces berries that are dispersed by water.

Calamus aggregatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
