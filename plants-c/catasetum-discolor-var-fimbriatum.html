Catasetum discolor var. fimbriatum

Catasetum discolor var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the Fimbriated Spotted Catasetum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This orchid is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves and its unique, spurred flowers that typically display a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant is epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces a distinctive, long, twisted column that bears the pollinia, a feature typical of many orchids.

The varietal name "fimbriatum" refers to the fringed or serrated edges of its petals and sepals, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.
