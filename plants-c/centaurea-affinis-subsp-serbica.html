Centaurea affinis subsp. serbica

Centaurea affinis subsp.

serbica, commonly known as the Serbian knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas.

The plant features striking blue to purple composite flowers arranged in dense, conical inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette structure.

It is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
