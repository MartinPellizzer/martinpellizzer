Calopogonium ferrugineum

Calopogonium ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty calopogonium, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant in sustainable farming practices.
