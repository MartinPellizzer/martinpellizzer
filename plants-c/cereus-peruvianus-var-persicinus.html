Cereus peruvianus var. persicinus

Cereus peruvianus var. persicinus, commonly known as the Persian night bloomer, is a succulent cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its sparsely branched stems and small, sharp spines. The plant produces striking, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique blooming habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
