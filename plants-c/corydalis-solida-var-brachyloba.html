Corydalis solida var. brachyloba

Corydalis solida var. brachyloba, commonly known as the short-stemmed Corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, nodding flowers. The flowers typically range in color from pale pink to lavender, and they bloom in early spring. This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for shady garden spaces.
