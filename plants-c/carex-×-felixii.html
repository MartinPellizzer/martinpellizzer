Carex × felixii

Carex × felixii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Carex × felixii is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
