Convolvulus divisus

Convolvulus divisus, commonly known as the divided morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are deeply divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus divisus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
