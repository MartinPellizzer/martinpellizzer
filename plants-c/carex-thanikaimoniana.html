Carex thanikaimoniana

Carex thanikaimoniana, commonly known as the Thanikai Mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of southern India.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
