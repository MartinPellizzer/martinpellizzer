Clematis potaninii

Clematis potaninii, commonly known as the Siberian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant's foliage consists of compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Clematis potaninii is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
