Carex leporina var. americana

Carex leporina var. americana, commonly known as the American marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swales, and pond edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-ranked brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
