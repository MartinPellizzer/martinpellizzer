Chrysophyllum guerelianum

Chrysophyllum guerelianum, commonly known as the African cherry or Guereliana, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange to yellow in color when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for local use.
