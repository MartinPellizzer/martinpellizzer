Croton sublepidotus

Croton sublepidotus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth or slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
