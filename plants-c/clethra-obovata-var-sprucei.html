Clethra obovata var. sprucei

Clethra obovata var. sprucei, commonly known as Spruce's sweet pepperbush, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in elongated inflorescences. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
