Calanthe philippinensis

Calanthe philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

Calanthe philippinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
