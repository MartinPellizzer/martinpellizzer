Cucurbita maxima var. grossa

Cucurbita maxima var. grossa, commonly known as the scallop pumpkin, is a large, vining squash native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and deeply lobed leaves, which are typically broad and heart-shaped. The fruit is usually oblong or slightly spherical, with a thick, edible rind and a sweet, nutty-flavored pulp. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and culinary use in various cuisines.
