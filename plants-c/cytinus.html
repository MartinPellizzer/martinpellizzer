Cytinus

Cytinus, commonly known as the "buttercup of the desert," is a unique flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on other plants for water and nutrients through its root system. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically small and scale-like, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Cytinus is also known for its ability to survive in arid environments, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
