Cinnamomum wilsonii

Cinnamomum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The leaves are often used in perfumery and as a culinary herb.
