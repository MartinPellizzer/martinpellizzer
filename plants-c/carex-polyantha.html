Carex polyantha

Carex polyantha, commonly known as the pink sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pink or reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
