Caryophyllus laxeracemosus

Caryophyllus laxeracemosus, commonly known as the wild clove or fragrant caryophyll.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers emit a subtle, spicy fragrance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
