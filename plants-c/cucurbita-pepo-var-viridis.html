Cucurbita pepo var. viridis

Cucurbita pepo var. viridis, commonly known as the zucchini or summer squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual vine that produces elongated, cylindrical fruits with a smooth, dark green skin. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow male and female flowers that bloom on separate stems. Its fruit is typically harvested when immature, before it fully matures into a hard, dry form.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible flesh, which is used in various culinary preparations.
