Carex × halophila var. flavicans

Carex × halophila var. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow flowering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. It is often found in regions with high humidity and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for wetland restoration and garden landscapes.
