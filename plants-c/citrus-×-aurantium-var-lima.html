Citrus × aurantium var. lima

Citrus × aurantium var. lima, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically 2-5 cm in length. The fruit has a thin, edible peel that is rich in vitamin C and essential oils. The pulp inside is sweet and juicy, often eaten fresh or used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and its unique, flavorful fruit.
