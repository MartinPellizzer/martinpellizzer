Crocus hermoneus

Crocus hermoneus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are typically borne on short, sturdy stems. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crocus hermoneus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
