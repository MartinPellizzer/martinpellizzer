Cirsium falcatum

Cirsium falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flowers and deeply lobed, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed or marginal habitats.

Cirsium falcatum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
