Clinopodium pamphylicum subsp. piperelloides

Clinopodium pamphylicum subsp.

piperelloides, commonly known as Piperello's woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
