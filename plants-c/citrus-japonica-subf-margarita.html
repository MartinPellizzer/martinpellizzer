Citrus japonica subf. margarita

Citrus japonica subf.

margarita, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or kumquat, is a small evergreen shrub native to East Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, orange-colored fruits that are edible both in their skin and pulp.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to humid, subtropical climates. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
