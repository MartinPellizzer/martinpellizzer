Cassebeeria glaberrima

Cassebeeria glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often arranged in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.
