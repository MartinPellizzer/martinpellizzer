Centaurea graelsii

Centaurea graelsii, commonly known as the Greek cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flower heads with red-brown bracts, surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea graelsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
