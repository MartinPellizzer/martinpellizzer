Christella porphyrophlebia

Christella porphyrophlebia, commonly known as the purple-veined bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Christella porphyrophlebia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
