Corryocactus erici-marae

Corryocactus erici-marae, commonly known as the Maraean corryocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
