Cepa Ascalonica

Cepa ascalonica is a unique plant known for its unusual appearance and historical significance. It is often referred to as the "Devil's Onion" due to its association with dark folklore and superstitions. This plant thrives in damp, shaded environments and is commonly found in regions with temperate climates. Its bulbous structure and strong odor have made it both feared and revered throughout history. Despite its ominous reputation, it remains a subject of fascination for botanists and historians alike.
