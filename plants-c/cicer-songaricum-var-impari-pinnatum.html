Cicer songaricum var. impari-pinnatum

Cicer songaricum var. impari-pinnatum, commonly known as the unequal-pinnate vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets, typically arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

This variety is valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
