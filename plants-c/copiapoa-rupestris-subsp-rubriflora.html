Copiapoa rupestris subsp. rubriflora

Copiapoa rupestris subsp.

rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered copiapoa, is a cacti species native to Chile. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
