Centaurea castellanoides subsp. arundana

Centaurea castellanoides subsp.

arundana, commonly known as the Sierra foothill star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its spiky, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and forms compact, upright clumps.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
