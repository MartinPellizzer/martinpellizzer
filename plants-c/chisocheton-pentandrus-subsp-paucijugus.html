Chisocheton pentandrus subsp. paucijugus

Chisocheton pentandrus subsp.

paucijugus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chisocheton, is a member of the Clusiaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
