Ceropegia glenensis

Ceropegia glenensis, commonly known as the Glen's African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
