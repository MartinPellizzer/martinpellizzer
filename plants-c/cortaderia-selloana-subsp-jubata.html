Cortaderia selloana subsp. jubata

Cortaderia selloana subsp.

jubata, commonly known as the purple fountain grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to South America. It features tall, arching inflorescences that are typically a deep purple or bronze color, adding visual interest to gardens.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally blue-green in color, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of climates.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds and butterflies.
