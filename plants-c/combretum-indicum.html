Combretum indicum

Combretum indicum, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The bark is smooth and often exudes a milky sap when wounded.
