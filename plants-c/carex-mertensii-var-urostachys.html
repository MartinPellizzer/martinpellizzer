Carex mertensii var. urostachys

Carex mertensii var. urostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
