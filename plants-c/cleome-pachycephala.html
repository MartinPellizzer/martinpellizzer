Cleome pachycephala

Cleome pachycephala, commonly known as the thick-headed spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces striking, spider-like flowers with five petals and prominent stamens, typically in shades of pink to red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Cleome pachycephala thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
