Cucurbita moschata var. barbarica

Cucurbita moschata var. barbarica, commonly known as the Siberian pumpkin, is a hardy, cold-tolerant variety native to Central Asia and Siberia.

It is characterized by its rugged, ribbed, and often hairy stems, which help it survive in harsh climates. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a pale to dark orange rind and a sweet, nutty flavor. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is often used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
