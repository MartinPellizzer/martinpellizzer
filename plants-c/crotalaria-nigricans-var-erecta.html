Crotalaria nigricans var. erecta

Crotalaria nigricans var. erecta, commonly known as black-seed cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, hairy pods containing black or dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in crop rotation systems.
