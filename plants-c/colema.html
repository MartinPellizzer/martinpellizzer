Colema

Colema, commonly known as the "Mexican turnip," is a root vegetable native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which also includes broccoli and cabbage. The plant features a thick, edible root that is typically purple or white in color. It has small, green, lobed leaves that grow close to the ground.

Colema is often used in traditional Mexican dishes and is valued for its slightly sweet and nutty flavor.
