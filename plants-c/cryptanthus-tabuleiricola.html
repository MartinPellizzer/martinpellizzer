Cryptanthus tabuleiricola

Cryptanthus tabuleiricola, commonly known as the coastal cryptanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is a member of the amaranth family (Amaranthaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blooms in shades of red, pink, and purple, which appear during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salt exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
