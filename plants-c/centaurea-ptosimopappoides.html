Centaurea ptosimopappoides

Centaurea ptosimopappoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf starthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its flowers are yellow with purple markings.

It produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind.
