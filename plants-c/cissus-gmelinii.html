Cissus gmelinii

Cissus gmelinii, commonly known as the climbing goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by edible, sour-tasting berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Cissus gmelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
