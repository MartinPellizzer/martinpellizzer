Caesalpinia dauensis

Caesalpinia dauensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, peacock-like flower clusters that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
