Chalcas koenigii

Chalcas koenigii, commonly known as Indian cumin or koenigii cumin, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces oval, wrinkled seeds that are widely used as a spice in Indian cuisine. Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and a taproot system.
