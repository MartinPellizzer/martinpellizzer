Ceropegia dioscoridis

Ceropegia dioscoridis, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has succulent leaves that are often heart-shaped, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia dioscoridis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
