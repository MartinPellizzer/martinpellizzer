Campanopsis madagascariensis

Campanopsis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
