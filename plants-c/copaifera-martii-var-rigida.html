Copaifera martii var. rigida

Copaifera martii var. rigida, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense canopy, with elongated, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, resinous sap, which is often tapped for its medicinal and aromatic properties. Its bark and leaves contain compounds such as alpha-pinene and beta-caryophyllene, contributing to its use in traditional medicine.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
