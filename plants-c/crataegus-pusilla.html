Crataegus pusilla

Crataegus pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its compact size and ornamental value in gardens.

Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
