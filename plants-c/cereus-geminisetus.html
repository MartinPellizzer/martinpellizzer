Cereus geminisetus

Cereus geminisetus, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering make it a striking addition to desert gardens.
