Calamintha × conillii

Calamintha × conillii, commonly known as California mountain mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white to pale blue flowers in the summer. Calamintha × conillii is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It also attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
