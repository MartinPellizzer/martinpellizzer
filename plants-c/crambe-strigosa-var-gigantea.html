Crambe strigosa var. gigantea

Crambe strigosa var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant napowee, is a striking member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
