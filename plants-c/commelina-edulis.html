Commelina edulis

Commelina edulis, commonly known as the arrowleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and small, blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

While it is not widely cultivated for food, some indigenous communities use its leaves in traditional cuisine.
