Cornus hemsleyi

Cornus hemsleyi, commonly known as Hemsley's dogwood, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its showy white bracts that surround small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and is known for its ornamental value. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, often turning red in the fall.

Cornus hemsleyi is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
