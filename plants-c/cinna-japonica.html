Cinna japonica

Cinna japonica, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or Japanese sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and emit a mild, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Cinna japonica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
