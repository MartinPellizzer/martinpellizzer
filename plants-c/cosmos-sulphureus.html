Cosmos sulphureus

Cosmos sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur cosmos, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often mistaken for those of other cosmos species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
