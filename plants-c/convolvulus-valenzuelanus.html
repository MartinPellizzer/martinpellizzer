Convolvulus valenzuelanus

Convolvulus valenzuelanus, commonly known as the Valenzuelan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed in certain regions.
