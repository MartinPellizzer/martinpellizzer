Conophyllum niveum

Conophyllum niveum, commonly known as the white conophyllum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal or inland regions.

Conophyllum niveum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
