Cornus stolonifera var. angustipetala

Cornus stolonifera var. angustipetala, commonly known as narrow-leaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically 2-5 cm in length, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its stems are often reddish or purplish, and it has a compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
