Calamintha latiuscula

Calamintha latiuscula, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
