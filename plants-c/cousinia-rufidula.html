Cousinia rufidula

Cousinia rufidula, commonly known as the red-flowered cushion-cush.

It is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, forming a low-growing cushion of foliage. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cousinia rufidula is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
