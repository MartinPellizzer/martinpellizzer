Colysis triphylla

Colysis triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Colysis triphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
