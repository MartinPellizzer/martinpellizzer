Campanula glomerata subsp. oblongifolia

Campanula glomerata subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a compact growth habit. This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
