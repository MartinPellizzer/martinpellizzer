Calliandra fulgens

Calliandra fulgens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra fulgens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
