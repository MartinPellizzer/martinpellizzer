Chaetocalyx hebecarpus var. oblongifolius

Chaetocalyx hebecarpus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and pointed. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warm seasons. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
