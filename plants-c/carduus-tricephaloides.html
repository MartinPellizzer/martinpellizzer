Carduus tricephaloides

Carduus tricephaloides, commonly known as the three-headed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a composite flower head that consists of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing purple to pinkish flowers.

The species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
