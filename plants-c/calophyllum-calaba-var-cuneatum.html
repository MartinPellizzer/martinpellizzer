Calophyllum calaba var. cuneatum

Calophyllum calaba var. cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
