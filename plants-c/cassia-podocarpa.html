Cassia podocarpa

Cassia podocarpa, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous mesh. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it is often found in woodland and open forests.

Cassia podocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses by Indigenous Australians.
