Catinga aromatica

Catinga aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant catinga, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of northern Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand prolonged drought. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Catinga aromatica plays a vital role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
