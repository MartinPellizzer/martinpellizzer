Convolvulus selloi

Convolvulus selloi, commonly known as the Sello's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, funnel-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant features hairy, slender stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
