Campanula scheuchzeri subsp. witasekiana

Campanula scheuchzeri subsp.

witasekiana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Alps. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
