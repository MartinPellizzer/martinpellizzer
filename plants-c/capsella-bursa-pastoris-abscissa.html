Capsella bursa-pastoris abscissa

Capsella bursa-pastoris abscissa, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It features small, white flowers with four petals and produces distinctive heart-shaped seed pods. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along pathways.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in many temperate regions.
