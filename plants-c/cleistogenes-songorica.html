Cleistogenes songorica

Cleistogenes songorica, commonly known as the Songor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in areas with high humidity and moderate temperatures. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Cleistogenes songorica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
