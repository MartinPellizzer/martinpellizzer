Callistachys procumbens

Callistachys procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate flamebrush, is a low-growing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has slender, woody stems that often spread along the ground, forming a dense mat. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.
