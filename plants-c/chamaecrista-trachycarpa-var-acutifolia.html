Chamaecrista trachycarpa var. acutifolia

Chamaecrista trachycarpa var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf burr legume, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
