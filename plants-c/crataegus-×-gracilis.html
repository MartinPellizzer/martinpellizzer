Crataegus × gracilis

Crataegus × gracilis, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, thorned branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and often used in culinary or medicinal contexts. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden cultivation.
