Calamagrostis neglecta var. fallax

Calamagrostis neglecta var. fallax, commonly known as autumn bluestem, is a grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue-gray foliage that turns golden in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and wetlands. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
