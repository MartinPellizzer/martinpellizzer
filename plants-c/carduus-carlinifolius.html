Carduus carlinifolius

Carduus carlinifolius, commonly known as the Carlini-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

The plant is not only ornamental but also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
