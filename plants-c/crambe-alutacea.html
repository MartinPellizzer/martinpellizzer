Crambe alutacea

Crambe alutacea, commonly known as the slender crambe, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated pods containing seeds.

Crambe alutacea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
