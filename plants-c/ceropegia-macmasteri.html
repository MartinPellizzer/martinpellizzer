Ceropegia macmasteri

Ceropegia macmasteri, commonly known as the MacMaster's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent vine characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found growing along rocky outcrops or in dry woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
