Cerasus takasagomontana

Cerasus takasagomontana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy, early spring blossoms. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in autumn.

Cerasus takasagomontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic beauty.
