Crepidium breviscapum

Crepidium breviscapum, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base of the plant.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
