Carpodinus tenuifolia

Carpodinus tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed carpodine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
