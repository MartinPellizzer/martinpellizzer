Calophyllum brasiliense var. gardneri

Calophyllum brasiliense var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's torch tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
