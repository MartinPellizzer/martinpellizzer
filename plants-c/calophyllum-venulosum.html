Calophyllum venulosum

Calophyllum venulosum, commonly known as the veined calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
