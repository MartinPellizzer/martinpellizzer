Croton dasygyne

Croton dasygyne, commonly known as the spiny croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often hidden within cup-like structures called cyathia.

Croton dasygyne is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
