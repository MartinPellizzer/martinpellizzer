Cymbopogon exaltatus var. ambiguus

Cymbopogon exaltatus var. ambiguus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that emit a strong citronella scent. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and natural insect repellents. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it is valued in traditional herbal practices.
