Chondrilla latifolia

Chondrilla latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf chondrilla, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert environments.

Chondrilla latifolia is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
