Calendula suffruticosa subsp. vejerensis

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

vejerensis, commonly known as the Vejerensis marigold, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, rounded fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
