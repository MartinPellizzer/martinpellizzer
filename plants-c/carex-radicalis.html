Carex radicalis

Carex radicalis, commonly known as the swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often found in wet or marshy environments. Carex radicalis thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
