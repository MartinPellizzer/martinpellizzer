Ceratopetalum succirubrum

Ceratopetalum succirubrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed mountain heath, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems that turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glandular surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
