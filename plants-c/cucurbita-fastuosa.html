Cucurbita fastuosa

Cucurbita fastuosa, commonly known as the ornamental gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often striped or variegated fruits that come in a variety of shapes and sizes. The plant is typically cultivated for its decorative value rather than for consumption. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring a long growing season.

The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.
