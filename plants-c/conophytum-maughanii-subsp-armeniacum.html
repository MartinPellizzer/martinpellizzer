Conophytum maughanii subsp. armeniacum

Conophytum maughanii subsp.

armeniacum, commonly known as the Armenian conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
