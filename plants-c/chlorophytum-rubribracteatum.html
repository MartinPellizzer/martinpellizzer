Chlorophytum rubribracteatum

Chlorophytum rubribracteatum, commonly known as the red-bracted spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that often have a reddish hue at the tips. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it easy to cultivate and maintain.
