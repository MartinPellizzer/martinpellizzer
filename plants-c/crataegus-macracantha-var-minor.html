Crataegus macracantha var. minor

Crataegus macracantha var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring thorned branches and dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
