Crotalaria acervata

Crotalaria acervata, commonly known as the clustered crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive yellow flowers with spurred petals. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, angular seeds.

Crotalaria acervata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
