Carex dinarica

Carex dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland and riparian habitats.

Carex dinarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions to biodiversity.
