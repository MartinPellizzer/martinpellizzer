Coprosma spathulata subsp. spathulata

Coprosma spathulata subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical to ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
