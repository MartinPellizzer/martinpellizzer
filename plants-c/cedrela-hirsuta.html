Cedrela hirsuta

Cedrela hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cedrela, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often fragrant bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Cedrela hirsuta is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
