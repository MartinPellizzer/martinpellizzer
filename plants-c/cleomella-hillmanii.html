Cleomella hillmanii

Cleomella hillmanii, commonly known as the Hillman's cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly known as "spiderworts.". The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Cleomella hillmanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
