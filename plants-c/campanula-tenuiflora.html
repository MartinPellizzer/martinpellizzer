Campanula tenuiflora

Campanula tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a grayish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
