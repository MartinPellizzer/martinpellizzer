Cerasus ilicifolia

Cerasus ilicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yuzu cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its slender, often thorny branches and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, yellow-orange fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

The species is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
