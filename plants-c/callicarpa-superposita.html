Callicarpa superposita

Callicarpa superposita, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning reddish in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental berries and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
