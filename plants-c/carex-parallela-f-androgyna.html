Carex parallela f. androgyna

Carex parallela f. androgyna, commonly known as the Androgyna sedge, is a variant of the parallel sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its androgynous flowers, which contain both male and female reproductive structures.
