Centaurea paucijuga var. melanolepis

Centaurea paucijuga var. melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, rayless florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive dark green color with a contrasting purplish underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
