Carex praecox subsp. mixta

Carex praecox subsp.

mixta, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and other wetland plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
