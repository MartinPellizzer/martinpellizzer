Chassalia moramangensis

Chassalia moramangensis, commonly known as the Moramanga chassalia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
