Celastrus jackianus

Celastrus jackianus, commonly known as the Chinese bindweed, is a woody climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and often trifoliate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
