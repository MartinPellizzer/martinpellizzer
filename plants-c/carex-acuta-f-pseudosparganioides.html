Carex acuta f. pseudosparganioides

Carex acuta f. pseudosparganioides, commonly known as the sharp beak sedge, is a variant of the common sedges, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system, which allows it to spread and form dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of two distinct spikelets, with the lower one being shorter and more compact.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
