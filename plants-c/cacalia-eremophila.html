Cacalia eremophila

Cacalia eremophila, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cacalia eremophila is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
