Carex arnottiana

Carex arnottiana, commonly known as Arnott's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to the northern regions of North America, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
