Chukrasia velutina var. microcarpa

Chukrasia velutina var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange-red fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rutaceae family.
