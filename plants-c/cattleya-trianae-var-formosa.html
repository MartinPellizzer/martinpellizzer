Cattleya trianae var. formosa

Cattleya trianae var. formosa, commonly known as the Formosa Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in both greenhouses and gardens.
