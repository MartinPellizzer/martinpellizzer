Convolvulus mollis var. hasslerianus

Convolvulus mollis var. hasslerianus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, hence its association with the term "morning glory.".
