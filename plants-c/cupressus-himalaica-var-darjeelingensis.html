Cupressus himalaica var. darjeelingensis

Cupressus himalaica var. darjeelingensis, commonly known as the Darjeeling cypress, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
