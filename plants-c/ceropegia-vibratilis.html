Ceropegia vibratilis

Ceropegia vibratilis, commonly known as the rattled vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, delicate, pale green flowers. The plant has opposite, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia vibratilis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
