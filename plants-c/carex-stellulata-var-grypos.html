Carex stellulata var. grypos

Carex stellulata var. grypos, commonly known as the gray sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a grayish-green hue, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in water gardens.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
