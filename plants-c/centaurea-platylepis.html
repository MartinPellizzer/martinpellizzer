Centaurea platylepis

Centaurea platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and distinctive, spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Centaurea platylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
