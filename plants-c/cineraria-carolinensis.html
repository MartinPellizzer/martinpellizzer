Cineraria carolinensis

Cineraria carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet and features gray-green, fuzzy leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in the spring and early summer.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.
