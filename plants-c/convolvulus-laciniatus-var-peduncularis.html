Convolvulus laciniatus var. peduncularis

Convolvulus laciniatus var. peduncularis, commonly known as the fringed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports such as fences or trellises. Its flowers have five lobes and are often adorned with fine, fringed edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative and resilient plant.
