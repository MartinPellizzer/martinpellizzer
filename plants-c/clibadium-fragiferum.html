Clibadium fragiferum

Clibadium fragiferum, commonly known as the fragrant cucumber vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, green, edible fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its strong, pleasant aroma. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The vine is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
