Callista geminata

Callista geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and cloud forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in pairs, giving the plant its common name.

Callista geminata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
