Calamagrostis macloviana

Calamagrostis macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian reed, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Calamagrostis macloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
