Cercopetalum dasyanthum var. longeacuminatum

Cercopetalum dasyanthum var. longeacuminatum, commonly known as the long-lobed mountain mahogany, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
