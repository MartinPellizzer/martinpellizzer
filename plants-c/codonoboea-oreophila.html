Codonoboea oreophila

Codonoboea oreophila, commonly known as the mountain codonopsis, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
