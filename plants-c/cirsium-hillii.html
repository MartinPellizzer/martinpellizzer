Cirsium hillii

Cirsium hillii, commonly known as Hill's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and tall, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering stems can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, making it a striking feature in its natural environment.

Cirsium hillii is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
