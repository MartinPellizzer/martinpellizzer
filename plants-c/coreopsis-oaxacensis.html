Coreopsis oaxacensis

Coreopsis oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Coreopsis oaxacensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
