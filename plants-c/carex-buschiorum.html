Carex buschiorum

Carex buschiorum, commonly known as the Buschiorum sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers in early summer, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Carex buschiorum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
