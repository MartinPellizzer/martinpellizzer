Camellia japonica var. macrocarpa

Camellia japonica var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-sepaled camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, with deep pink to red petals. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The camellia is also valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its winter blooming period.
