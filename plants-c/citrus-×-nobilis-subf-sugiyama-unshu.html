Citrus × nobilis subf. sugiyama-unshu

Citrus × nobilis subf.

sugiyama-unshu, commonly known as the Unshu mandarin. It is a hybrid citrus species originating from Japan, characterized by its small, sweet, and seedless fruit.

The plant typically has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The fruit is prized for its high vitamin C content and distinct flavor.
