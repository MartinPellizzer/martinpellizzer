Calanthe caudatilabella var. latiloba

Calanthe caudatilabella var. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved tail orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a unique, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
