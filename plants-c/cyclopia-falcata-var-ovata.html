Cyclopia falcata var. ovata

Cyclopia falcata var. ovata, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive basal rosette of leaves that are ovate in shape, giving it its varietal name. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
