Coptis groenlandica

Coptis groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Coptis groenlandica is also valued for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids similar to those found in other members of the genus.
