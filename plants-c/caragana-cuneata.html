Caragana cuneata

Caragana cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf shrub or Siberian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions.

Caragana cuneata is also used in landscaping and for erosion control due to its hardy nature.
