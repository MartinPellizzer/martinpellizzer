Carex tenera

Carex tenera, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered spikelets at the tips. Its leaves are usually pale green and have a soft, grassy texture, making it a common sight in moist soils.

Carex tenera plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
