Capparis dolichopoda

Capparis dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-stemmed caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, characterized by its thorny stems and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, capsaicin-like berries. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a unique ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

The species is part of the Capparaceae family, which is known for its distinctive floral structures and ecological adaptations.
