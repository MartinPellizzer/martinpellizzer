Cyclobalanopsis semiserratoides

Cyclobalanopsis semiserratoides, commonly known as the narrow-toothed ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, characterized by its hard, durable wood.

It is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Malaysia. The plant produces small, chestnut-like fruits that are encased in a spiky husk, typical of the genus Cyclobalanopsis. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
