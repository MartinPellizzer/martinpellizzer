Cyperus longus f. badiiformis

Cyperus longus f. badiiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a variant of the Cyperus longus species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically broader than those of the typical form, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
