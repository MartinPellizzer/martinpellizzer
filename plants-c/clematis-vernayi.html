Clematis vernayi

Clematis vernayi, commonly known as the vernay's clematis, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, usually white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Clematis vernayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blooms.
