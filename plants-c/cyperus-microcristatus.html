Cyperus microcristatus

Cyperus microcristatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in wetland environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily in moist soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various water conditions.
