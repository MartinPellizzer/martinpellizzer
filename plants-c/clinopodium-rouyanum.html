Clinopodium rouyanum

Clinopodium rouyanum, commonly known as Rouyan's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters.

Clinopodium rouyanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
