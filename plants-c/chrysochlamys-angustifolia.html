Chrysochlamys angustifolia

Chrysochlamys angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysochlamys, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Chrysochlamys angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and unique flowering habit.
