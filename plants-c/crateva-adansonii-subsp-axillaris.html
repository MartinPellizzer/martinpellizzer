Crateva adansonii subsp. axillaris

Crateva adansonii subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
