Ctenitis calcicola

Ctenitis calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with a creeping rhizome and small, shield-shaped fronds. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in dry conditions.

Ctenitis calcicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to limestone substrates.
