Coleus anamudianus

Coleus anamudianus, commonly known as the Anamudi mountain coleus, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, preferring well-drained soil. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
