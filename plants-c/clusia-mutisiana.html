Clusia mutisiana

Clusia mutisiana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Clusia mutisiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
