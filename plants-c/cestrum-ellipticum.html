Cestrum ellipticum

Cestrum ellipticum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, tubular flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Cestrum ellipticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nighttime blooming habit.
