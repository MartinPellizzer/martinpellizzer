Chamaecrista ustulata

Chamaecrista ustulata, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a leguminous shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a distinctive, woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its seeds are encased in small, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.
