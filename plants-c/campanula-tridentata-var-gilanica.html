Campanula tridentata var. gilanica

Campanula tridentata var. gilanica, commonly known as the Gilan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color, which bloom in summer. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike.
