Carex pallidula

Carex pallidula, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
