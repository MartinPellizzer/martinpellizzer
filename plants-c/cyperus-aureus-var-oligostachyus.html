Cyperus aureus var. oligostachyus

Cyperus aureus var. oligostachyus, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, or in swampy habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
