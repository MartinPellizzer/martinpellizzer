Curio cuneifolius

Curio cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed curio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a spherical shape, often referred to as a "cap" or "head.". Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Curio cuneifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
