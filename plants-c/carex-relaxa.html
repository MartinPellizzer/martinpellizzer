Carex relaxa

Carex relaxa, commonly known as the looseleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in spikelets at the top of the plant.

Carex relaxa thrives in wet or damp environments and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.
