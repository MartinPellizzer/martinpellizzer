Cyperus melanospermus var. imerinensis

Cyperus melanospermus var. imerinensis, commonly known as the blackseed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces dark, oily seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in certain regions.
