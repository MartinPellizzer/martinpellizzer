Chimonocalamus pallens

Chimonocalamus pallens, commonly known as the pale reed bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a light green color and a slight whitish bloom on the underside.

Chimonocalamus pallens is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
