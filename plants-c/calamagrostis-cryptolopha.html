Calamagrostis cryptolopha

Calamagrostis cryptolopha, commonly known as the cryptic feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife.

Calamagrostis cryptolopha is valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding and its aesthetic appeal in wetland landscapes.
