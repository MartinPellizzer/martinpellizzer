Casearia suaveolens

Casearia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Casearia suaveolens is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
