Chloroleucon acacioides

Chloroleucon acacioides, commonly known as the acacia-like shrub, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid to arid environments.

Chloroleucon acacioides is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
