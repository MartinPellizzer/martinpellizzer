Chamaecrista aristata

Chamaecrista aristata, commonly known as the silverleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Chamaecrista aristata thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant adapted to dry conditions.
