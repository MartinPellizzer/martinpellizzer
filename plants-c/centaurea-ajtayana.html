Centaurea ajtayana

Centaurea ajtayana, commonly known as the Ajtay's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Hungary.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often found in regions with a continental climate.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
