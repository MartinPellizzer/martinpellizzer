Cucurbita maxima var. macrocina

Cucurbita maxima var. macrocina, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita maxima, characterized by its exceptionally large fruits.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the genus Cucurbita. Its fruit can weigh several hundred pounds, making it a popular choice for competitions and festivals.

The flesh is typically orange, sweet, and dense, suitable for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
