Calliandra exsudans

Calliandra exsudans, commonly known as the bleeding elder, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to exude a reddish sap, which gives it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
