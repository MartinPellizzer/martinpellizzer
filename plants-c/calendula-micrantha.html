Calendula micrantha

Calendula micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, narrow corolla and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and serrated. It blooms from late spring to early autumn and is known for its medicinal properties.

Calendula micrantha is a member of the Asteraceae family, sharing characteristics with other members of the genus Calendula.
