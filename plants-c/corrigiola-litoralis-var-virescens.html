Corrigiola litoralis var. virescens

Corrigiola litoralis var. virescens, commonly known as sea lentil or sea spinach, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean and other temperate regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is adapted to salt spray and high sunlight exposure.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their mild, peppery flavor.
