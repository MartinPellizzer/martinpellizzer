Crypsis

Crypsis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent flowers that emit a faint glow in the dark. The plant's botanical features include delicate, translucent petals and a short, compact stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense tropical forests.

Due to its rare occurrence and unusual appearance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research.
