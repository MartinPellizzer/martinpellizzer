Chaetotropis imberbis var. bonariensis

Chaetotropis imberbis var. bonariensis, commonly known as the smooth chaetotropis, is a variant of the chaetotropis genus native to South America.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

The plant is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to climb using its tendrils.
