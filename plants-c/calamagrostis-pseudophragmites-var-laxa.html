Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. laxa

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf reed, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. This variety thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are typically brown or purplish in hue.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
