Cereus geometrizans var. quadrangularispinus

Cereus geometrizans var. quadrangularispinus, commonly known as the angular spine cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are ribbed and often have a reddish or greenish hue. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its spines are thick and angular, providing protection from herbivores.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
