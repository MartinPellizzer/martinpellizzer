Campanula saxifraga subsp. meyeriana

Campanula saxifraga subsp.

meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are usually lanceolate or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
