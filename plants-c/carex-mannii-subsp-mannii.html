Carex mannii subsp. mannii

Carex mannii subsp.

mannii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
