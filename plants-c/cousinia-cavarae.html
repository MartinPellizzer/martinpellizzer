Cousinia cavarae

Cousinia cavarae, commonly known as the Armenian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss.

Cousinia cavarae produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by tightly packed disc florets, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
