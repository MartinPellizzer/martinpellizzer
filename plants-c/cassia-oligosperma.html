Cassia oligosperma

Cassia oligosperma, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia oligosperma thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.
