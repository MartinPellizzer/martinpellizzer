Callicarpa longipes var. longipes

Callicarpa longipes var. longipes, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant violet to purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, aromatic bark. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often found in forest understories.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.
