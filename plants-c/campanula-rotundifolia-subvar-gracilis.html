Campanula rotundifolia subvar. gracilis

Campanula rotundifolia subvar. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are rounded and often hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
