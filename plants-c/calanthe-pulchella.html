Calanthe pulchella

Calanthe pulchella, commonly known as the elegant calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant, flowers that display a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with upright, strap-like leaves and a short, stocky rhizome. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose panicle and bloom during the spring or early summer.

Calanthe pulchella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
