Calopsis oxylepis

Calopsis oxylepis, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and woody.

Calopsis oxylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
