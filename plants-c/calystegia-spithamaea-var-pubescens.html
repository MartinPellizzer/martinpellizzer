Calystegia spithamaea var. pubescens

Calystegia spithamaea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming throughout the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions due to its rapid growth and extensive root system.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
