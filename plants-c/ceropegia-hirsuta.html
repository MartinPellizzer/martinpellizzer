Ceropegia hirsuta

Ceropegia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia hirsuta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
