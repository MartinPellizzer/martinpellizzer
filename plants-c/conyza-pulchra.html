Conyza pulchra

Conyza pulchra, commonly known as the pretty weed or common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to violet flower heads that bloom from late summer to early autumn. The plant produces numerous small, white to pale blue ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, with a grayish-green color, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Conyza pulchra is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
