Calamagrostis neglecta f. violacea

Calamagrostis neglecta f. violacea, commonly known as the violet false panicgrass, is a cool-season grass that exhibits striking purple foliage.

It is a compact, clump-forming perennial that thrives in moist to wet soils, making it ideal for wetland gardens or pond edges. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a soft pink to purple hue to the landscape. Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, offering year-round visual interest.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
