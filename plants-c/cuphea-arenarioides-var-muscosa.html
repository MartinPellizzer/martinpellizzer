Cuphea arenarioides var. muscosa

Cuphea arenarioides var. muscosa, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
