Cyperus confusus

Cyperus confusus, commonly known as the confused flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Cyperus confusus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in parts of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
