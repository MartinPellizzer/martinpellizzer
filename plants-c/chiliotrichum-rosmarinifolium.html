Chiliotrichum rosmarinifolium

Chiliotrichum rosmarinifolium, commonly known as rosemary, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. Rosemary is known for its strong, woody scent and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
