Cassia cruckshanksii

Cassia cruckshanksii, commonly known as the Cruckshanks' senna, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptic and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
