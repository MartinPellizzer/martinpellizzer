Centaurea × sagarrae

Centaurea × sagarrae, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with erect, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Centaurea × sagarrae is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
