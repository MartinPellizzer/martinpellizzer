Calamus prattianus

Calamus prattianus, commonly known as the Pratt's canescent palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its tropical habitat. Its inflorescences are typically clustered at the base of the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Calamus prattianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
