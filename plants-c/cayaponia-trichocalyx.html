Cayaponia trichocalyx

Cayaponia trichocalyx, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.
