Camellia parafurfuracea

Camellia parafurfuracea, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting central stripe.

Camellia parafurfuracea prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
