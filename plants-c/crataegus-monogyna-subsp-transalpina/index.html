Crataegus Monogyna Subsp. Transalpina

Crataegus monogyna subsp. transalpina is a lesser-known but fascinating member of the hawthorn family. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, this subspecies thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its delicate white flowers appear in early spring, followed by red berries that attract various wildlife. The plant is often overlooked despite its ecological importance and ornamental value. Due to habitat loss, it faces threats that warrant greater conservation attention.
