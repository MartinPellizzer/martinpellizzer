Carex hostiana

Carex hostiana, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Carex hostiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
