Commelina heterosperma

Commelina heterosperma, commonly known as the heterogeneous commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
