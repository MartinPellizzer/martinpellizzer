Calamagrostis urelytra var. macrantha

Calamagrostis urelytra var. macrantha, commonly known as the tall golden grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and golden-yellow plumes that appear in late summer. The plant prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife and stabilizing soil in wet environments.
