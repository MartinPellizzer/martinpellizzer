Calendula officinalis f. pleniflora

Calendula officinalis f. pleniflora, commonly known as the double-flowered calendula, is a cultivar of the common marigold.

It is characterized by its densely packed, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is a herbaceous annual with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers are also used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
