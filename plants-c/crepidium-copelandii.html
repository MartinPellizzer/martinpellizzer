Crepidium copelandii

Crepidium copelandii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulb-like stems and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

Crepidium copelandii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
