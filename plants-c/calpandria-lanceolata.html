Calpandria lanceolata

Calpandria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf calpandria, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its flowers, which appear in the summer, are small and yellow, contributing to its role in local pollination networks.
