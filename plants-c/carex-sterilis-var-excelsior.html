Carex sterilis var. excelsior

Carex sterilis var. excelsior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This variety is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets at the top of the stems.
