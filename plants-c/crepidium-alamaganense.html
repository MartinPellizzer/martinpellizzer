Crepidium alamaganense

Crepidium alamaganense, commonly known as the Alamagan orchid, is a rare endemic species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
