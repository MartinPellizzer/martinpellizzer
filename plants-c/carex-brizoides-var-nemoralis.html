Carex brizoides var. nemoralis

Carex brizoides var. nemoralis, commonly known as the wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
