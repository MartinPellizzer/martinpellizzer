Cleome werdermannii

Cleome werdermannii, commonly known as the Werdermann's cleome, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
