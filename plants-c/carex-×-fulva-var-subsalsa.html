Carex × fulva var. subsalsa

Carex × fulva var. subsalsa, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to golden green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
