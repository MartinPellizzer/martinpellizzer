Cratoxylum sumatranum subsp. neriifolium

Cratoxylum sumatranum subsp.

neriifolium, commonly known as the black-leaved tamanu, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces woody capsules that contain oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used for their astringent properties, and the oil extracted from the seeds has various applications in skincare and industry.

The species is valued for both its medicinal and economic significance in its native regions.
