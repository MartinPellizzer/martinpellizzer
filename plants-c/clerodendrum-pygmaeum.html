Clerodendrum pygmaeum

Clerodendrum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf champa or small champa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly pubescent underside.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
