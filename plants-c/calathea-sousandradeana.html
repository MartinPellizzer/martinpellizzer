Calathea sousandradeana

Calathea sousandradeana, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, intricately patterned leaves that display a combination of deep green, silver, and maroon hues. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often described as velvety to the touch, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calathea sousandradeana requires consistent moisture and indirect light to flourish, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
