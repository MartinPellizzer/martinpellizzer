Cyrilla racemiflora var. subglobosa

Cyrilla racemiflora var. subglobosa, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and red or orange berries. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
