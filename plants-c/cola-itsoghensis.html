Cola itsoghensis

Cola itsoghensis, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant. Cola itsoghensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
