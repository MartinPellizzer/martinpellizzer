Cephalocereus robinii var. keyensis

Cephalocereus robinii var. keyensis, commonly known as the Key Island cactus, is a species native to the Florida Keys.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinct rib structure and spines that are typically pale yellow to brown. The plant produces white flowers that bloom in the summer months, often at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal environments.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a notable species in the region's flora.
