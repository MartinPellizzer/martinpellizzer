Cyclosorus × intermedius

Cyclosorus × intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate water clover, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant features delicate, floating leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with finely divided, thread-like lobes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Cyclosorus × intermedius plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
