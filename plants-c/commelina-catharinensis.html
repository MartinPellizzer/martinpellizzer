Commelina catharinensis

Commelina catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features slender stems and produces small, blue or purple flowers with white markings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
