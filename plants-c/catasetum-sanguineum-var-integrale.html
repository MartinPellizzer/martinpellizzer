Catasetum sanguineum var. integrale

Catasetum sanguineum var. integrale, commonly known as the red orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are often adorned with contrasting yellow or green markings. The plant produces a unique structure called a "catasetum," which is a specialized pollen sac that adheres to the pollinator. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.

This orchid is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
