Cactus praelii

Cactus praelii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of bright yellow spines. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
