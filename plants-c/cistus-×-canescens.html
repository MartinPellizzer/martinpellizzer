Cistus × canescens

Cistus × canescens, commonly known as the whitethorn rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grayish-white to silver-green foliage with a woolly texture, and its flowers are typically pink to reddish-purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous scent and its ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Cistus × canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
