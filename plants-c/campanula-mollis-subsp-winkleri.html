Campanula mollis subsp. winkleri

Campanula mollis subsp.

winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
