Cyathea subincisa

Cyathea subincisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of arching fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea subincisa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
