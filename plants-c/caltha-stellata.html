Caltha stellata

Caltha stellata, commonly known as the star flower or lesser marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Caltha stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and also has historical uses in herbal medicine.
