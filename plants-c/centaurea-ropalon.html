Centaurea ropalon

Centaurea ropalon, commonly known as the Balkan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has erect, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
