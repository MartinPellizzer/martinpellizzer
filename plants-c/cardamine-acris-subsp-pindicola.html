Cardamine acris subsp. pindicola

Cardamine acris subsp.

pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a delicate, fern-like appearance, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
