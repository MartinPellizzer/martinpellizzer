Comanthera vernonioides var. minor

Comanthera vernonioides var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
