Cyperus persicus

Cyperus persicus, commonly known as Persian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.

Cyperus persicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
