Crotalaria filifolia

Crotalaria filifolia, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure. The plant is native to Central and South America and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste lands. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also has medicinal properties in some cultures.

Crotalaria filifolia is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
