Crotalaria perlaxa

Crotalaria perlaxa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with prominent purple or black markings, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Crotalaria perlaxa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
