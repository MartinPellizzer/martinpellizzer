Cistanthe picta var. frigida

Cistanthe picta var. frigida, commonly known as the frigid cistanthe, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold regions of central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pale blue or white, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in colder climates.
