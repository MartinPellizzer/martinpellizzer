Calceolaria truxillensis

Calceolaria truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillense slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or sandy soils, and prefers cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, and it blooms during the cooler months of the year.

Calceolaria truxillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
