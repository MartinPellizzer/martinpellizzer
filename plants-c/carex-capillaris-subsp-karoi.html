Carex capillaris subsp. karoi

Carex capillaris subsp.

karoi, commonly known as the Karoi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
