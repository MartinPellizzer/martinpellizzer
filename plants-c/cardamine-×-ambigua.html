Cardamine × ambigua

Cardamine × ambigua, commonly known as the broad-leaved watercress, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
