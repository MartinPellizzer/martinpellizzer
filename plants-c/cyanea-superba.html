Cyanea superba

Cyanea superba, commonly known as the Hawaiian skyflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Cyanea superba thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forested areas.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a notable species in Hawaiian botany.
