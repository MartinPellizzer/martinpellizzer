Chrysanthemum przewalskii

Chrysanthemum przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
