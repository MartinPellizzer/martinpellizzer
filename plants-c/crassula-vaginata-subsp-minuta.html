Crassula vaginata subsp. minuta

Crassula vaginata subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the Baby Necklace Plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa. It features small, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance.

The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant and well-suited for arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the growing season.

Its slow growth rate and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
