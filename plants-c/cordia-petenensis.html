Cordia petenensis

Cordia petenensis, commonly known as the Mexican rubber tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its latex, which has been historically used in the production of rubber and other industrial applications.
