Coleus decurrens

Coleus decurrens, commonly known as the spreading coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and exhibit a wide range of colors, including red, purple, and green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
