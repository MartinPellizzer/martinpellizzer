Cryptocoryne cordata

Cryptocoryne cordata, commonly known as the broad-leaf cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant thrives in soft, slightly acidic water and is often found in slow-moving freshwater environments. It is a popular choice for aquariums due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

Cryptocoryne cordata is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions, making it a versatile addition to aquatic ecosystems.
