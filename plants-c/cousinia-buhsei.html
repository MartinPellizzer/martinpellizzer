Cousinia buhsei

Cousinia buhsei, commonly known as the Buhsei's cottongrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cold climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia buhsei is often found in rocky or gravelly areas and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
