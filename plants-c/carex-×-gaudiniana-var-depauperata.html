Carex × gaudiniana var. depauperata

Carex × gaudiniana var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, showcasing narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
