Chamaesyce atrococca

Chamaesyce atrococca, commonly known as the blackleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dark green, egg-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
