Cordia nodosa f. glabrior

Cordia nodosa f. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved coralwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry forest environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive appearance.
