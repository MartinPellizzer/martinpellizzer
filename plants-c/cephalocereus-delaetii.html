Cephalocereus delaetii

Cephalocereus delaetii, commonly known as the Lafeo cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
