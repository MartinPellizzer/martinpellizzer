Cypripedium × euryale

Cypripedium × euryale, commonly known as the Chinese lily or the yellow root orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, often yellow or greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is usually darker in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces fleshy, sometimes hairy leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also threatened by habitat loss and over-collection.
