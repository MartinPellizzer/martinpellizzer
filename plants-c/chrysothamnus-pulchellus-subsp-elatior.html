Chrysothamnus pulchellus subsp. elatior

Chrysothamnus pulchellus subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow ray flowers and a distinctive conical shape, with numerous yellow disk flowers at the center.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.
