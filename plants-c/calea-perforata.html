Calea perforata

Calea perforata, commonly known as the perforate calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive perforations along the midrib, giving it its common name. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calea perforata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
