Crataegus × hudsonica

Crataegus × hudsonica, commonly known as the Hudson thornapple, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, often pink or white flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas.

Crataegus × hudsonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
