Citrus × aurantium var. grandis

Citrus × aurantium var. grandis, commonly known as the grapefruit.

It is a hybrid citrus plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America. The plant is characterized by its thick, rough, and often spiny rind, which contains a high concentration of essential oils. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and it produces large, oval-shaped fruits with a segmented interior.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its juice, which is rich in vitamin C and used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
