Carpopogon niveus

Carpopogon niveus, commonly known as the white panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in dense, paniculate inflorescences, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Carpopogon niveus is an important plant for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
