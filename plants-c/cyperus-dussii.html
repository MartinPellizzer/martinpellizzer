Cyperus dussii

Cyperus dussii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Cyperus dussii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
