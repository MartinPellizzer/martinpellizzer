Corydalis curviflora

Corydalis curviflora, commonly known as the twisted-flower larkspur, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Corydalis curviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
