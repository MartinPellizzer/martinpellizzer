Ceanothus divergens subsp. occidentalis

Ceanothus divergens subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-green foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
