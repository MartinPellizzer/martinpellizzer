Calamagrostis sublanceolata

Calamagrostis sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and its ability to thrive in wet or moist environments. The plant typically grows in marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

Calamagrostis sublanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
