Clinopodium vulgare subsp. arundanum

Clinopodium vulgare subsp.

arundanum, commonly known as meadow betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its flowering period extends from July to September, and it is often found in association with other wetland plants.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
