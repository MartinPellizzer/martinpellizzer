Cirsium × hallerianum

Cirsium × hallerianum, commonly known as the haller's thistle, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium horridulum.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowering heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has a robust root system and can form dense colonies, which helps it thrive in various environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
