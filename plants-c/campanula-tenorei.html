Campanula tenorei

Campanula tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Italy.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula tenorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
