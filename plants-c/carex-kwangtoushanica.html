Carex kwangtoushanica

Carex kwangtoushanica, commonly known as the Kwangtoushan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
