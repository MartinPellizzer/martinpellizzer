Calamus latus

Calamus latus, commonly known as broad calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender reed-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The rhizomes of Calamus latus are sometimes used in traditional medicine and for crafting purposes.
