Cystacanthus paniculatus

Cystacanthus paniculatus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of yellow flowers that resemble cascading showers, hence its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cystacanthus paniculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
