Castalis

Castalis, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Castalis has oval, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
