Cyathea pukuana

Cyathea pukuana, commonly known as the Pukalani tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in coastal forests and wetlands. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which can reach lengths of up to six feet.

Cyathea pukuana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various native species.
