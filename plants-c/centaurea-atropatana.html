Centaurea atropatana

Centaurea atropatana, commonly known as the Atropatana knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or reddish, with ray florets that attract pollinators.
