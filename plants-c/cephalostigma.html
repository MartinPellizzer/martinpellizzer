Cephalostigma

Cephalostigma, commonly known as the "head shield" or "cap plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, shield-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature common in many cucurbit species. The fruit is a small, round berry that contains several seeds.
