Cotoneaster talgaricus

Cotoneaster talgaricus, commonly known as the Talgaric cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Cotoneaster talgaricus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
