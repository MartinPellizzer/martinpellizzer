Cirsium lanceolatum subsp. hypoleucum

Cirsium lanceolatum subsp.

hypoleucum, commonly known as the paleleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down.

The plant produces purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
