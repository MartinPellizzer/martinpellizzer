Cephalocereus robustus

Cephalocereus robustus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and prominent spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
