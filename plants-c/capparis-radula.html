Capparis radula

Capparis radula, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, green berries. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Capparaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
