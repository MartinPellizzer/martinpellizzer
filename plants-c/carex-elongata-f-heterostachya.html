Carex elongata f. heterostachya

Carex elongata f. heterostachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The foliage is usually dark green, with a smooth texture and a distinct midrib.
