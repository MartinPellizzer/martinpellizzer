Cedrela mahagoni

Cedrela mahagoni, commonly known as mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its straight grain and rich, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and workability. The plant belongs to the family Meliaceae and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with large, simple, alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and usually yellow-green, while the fruit is a capsule containing several winged seeds.

Cedrela mahagoni is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
