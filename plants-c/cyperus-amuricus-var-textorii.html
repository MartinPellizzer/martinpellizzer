Cyperus amuricus var. textorii

Cyperus amuricus var. textorii, commonly known as the Amur flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that often form dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
