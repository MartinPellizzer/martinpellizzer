Cirrhopetalum albidum

Cirrhopetalum albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered cirrhopetalum, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a unique floral structure that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
