Chrysophtalmum leptocladum

Chrysophtalmum leptocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, tubular disk flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
