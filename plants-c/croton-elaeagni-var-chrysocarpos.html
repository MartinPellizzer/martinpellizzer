Croton elaeagni var. chrysocarpos

Croton elaeagni var. chrysocarpos, commonly known as the golden-fruited croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are clustered in small umbels, making it visually striking. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
