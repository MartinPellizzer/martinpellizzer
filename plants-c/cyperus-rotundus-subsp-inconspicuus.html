Cyperus rotundus subsp. inconspicuus

Cyperus rotundus subsp.

inconspicuus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes.

This subspecies is often found in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, ditches, and along waterways. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily and form dense mats.

The plant is adapted to a wide range of soil conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic settings.
