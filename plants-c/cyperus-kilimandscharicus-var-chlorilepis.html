Cyperus kilimandscharicus var. chlorilepis

Cyperus kilimandscharicus var. chlorilepis, commonly known as the green-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and spikelets that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its predominantly green foliage, setting it apart from other varieties of Cyperus kilimandscharicus.
