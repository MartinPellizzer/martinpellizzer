Carex rostrata var. maxima

Carex rostrata var. maxima, commonly known as the giant beak sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to wetland habitats. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
