Canavalia galeata

Canavalia galeata, commonly known as the white cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible beans and as a forage crop.

Canavalia galeata has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
