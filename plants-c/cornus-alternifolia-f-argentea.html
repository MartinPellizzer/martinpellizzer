Cornus alternifolia f. argentea

Cornus alternifolia f. argentea, commonly known as the white elderberry or white dogwood, is a botanical variant of the flowering dogwood.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.
