Ceratostylis sima

Ceratostylis sima, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a slender rhizome and produces small, oval-shaped pseudobulbs.

The flowers are often clustered and have a unique, spurred shape that distinguishes it from other orchid species.
