Casuarina cunninghamiana subsp. cunninghamiana

Casuarina cunninghamiana subsp.

cunninghamiana, commonly known as the coastal she-oak, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves that are arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, red or yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.

This species is well adapted to saline and wind-swept conditions, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
