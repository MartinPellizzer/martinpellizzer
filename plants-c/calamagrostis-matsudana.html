Calamagrostis matsudana

Calamagrostis matsudana, commonly known as Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or golden in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Calamagrostis matsudana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
