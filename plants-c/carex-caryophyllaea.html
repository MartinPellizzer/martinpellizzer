Carex caryophyllaea

Carex caryophyllaea, commonly known as the scarlet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, reddish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex caryophyllaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
