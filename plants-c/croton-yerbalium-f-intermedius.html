Croton yerbalium f. intermedius

Croton yerbalium f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate croton, is a variant of the Croton genus characterized by its unique foliage and growth habits.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant features broad, often variegated leaves that can range in color from green to yellow and red, adding visual interest to gardens. It typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage rather than its flowers.
