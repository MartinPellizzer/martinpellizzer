Conyza pampeana

Conyza pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands and shrublands, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Conyza pampeana is also known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand dry conditions.
