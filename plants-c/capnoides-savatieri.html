Capnoides savatieri

Capnoides savatieri, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include showy inflorescences and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
