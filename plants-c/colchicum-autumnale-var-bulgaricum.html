Colchicum autumnale var. bulgaricum

Colchicum autumnale var. bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces a corm-like structure that serves as its storage organ and enables it to survive harsh winter conditions. Its leaves are basal, broad, and arrow-shaped, typically appearing after the flowers.

This variant is closely related to the more widely known Colchicum autumnale, sharing similar toxic properties due to the presence of colchicine.
