Chrysaea

Chrysaea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the green calyx. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Chrysaea is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to care for.
