Cestrum salicifolium

Cestrum salicifolium, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often mistaken for willow leaves, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Cestrum salicifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in warm climates.
