Cyperus uncinatus

Cyperus uncinatus, commonly known as the hookleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus uncinatus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
