Capparis oblongifolia

Capparis oblongifolia, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has woody stems and alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Capparis oblongifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
