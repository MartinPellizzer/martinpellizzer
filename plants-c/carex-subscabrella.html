Carex subscabrella

Carex subscabrella, commonly known as slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it often grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
