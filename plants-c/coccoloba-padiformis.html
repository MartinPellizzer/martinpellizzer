Coccoloba padiformis

Coccoloba padiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf coccoloba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, tropical environments, often found in marshes and along stream banks. The plant has broad, fleshy leaves that are usually green and may have a waxy coating. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are often pollinated by insects.

The plant is known for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
