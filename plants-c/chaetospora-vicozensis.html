Chaetospora vicozensis

Chaetospora vicozensis, commonly known as the Vicozensis Haircap, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, turfil-like growth and small, elongated leaves with a distinct midrib. The plant produces peristome teeth that are typically short and robust, aiding in spore dispersal. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized moss species.
