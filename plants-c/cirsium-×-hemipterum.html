Cirsium × hemipterum

Cirsium × hemipterum, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
