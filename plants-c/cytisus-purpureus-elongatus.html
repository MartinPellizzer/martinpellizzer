Cytisus purpureus elongatus

Cytisus purpureus elongatus, commonly known as the purple flowering broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, bright purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, yellow pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
