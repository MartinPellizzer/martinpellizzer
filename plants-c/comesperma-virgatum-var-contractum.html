Comesperma virgatum var. contractum

Comesperma virgatum var. contractum, commonly known as narrowleaf bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, organic soil. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a flowering stem that is usually less than 30 cm in height.

It is an important component of the understory vegetation in its native ecosystems.
