Cousinia cordifolia

Cousinia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Cousinia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
