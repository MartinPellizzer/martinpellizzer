Cassia confusa

Cassia confusa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that turn from green to brown as they mature.

Cassia confusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
