Cerasus jamasakura

Cerasus jamasakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yamazakura, is a species of flowering shrub native to Japan.

It is celebrated for its early blooming pink or white flowers that appear before the leaves in spring. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other cherry species. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, a small cherry, is edible but less commonly consumed compared to other cherry varieties.
