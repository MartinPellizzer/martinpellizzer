Clusia williamsii

Clusia williamsii, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and light conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its ability to tolerate drought and pollution also makes it a common urban plant.
