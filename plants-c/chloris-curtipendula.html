Chloris curtipendula

Chloris curtipendula, commonly known as slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
