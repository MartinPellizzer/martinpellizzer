Campanula pinnatifida var. robusta

Campanula pinnatifida var. robusta, commonly known as the robust bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive, lace-like appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
