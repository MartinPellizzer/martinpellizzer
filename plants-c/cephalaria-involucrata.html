Cephalaria involucrata

Cephalaria involucrata, commonly known as the clustered cephalaria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically wind-pollinated flowers. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cephalaria involucrata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
