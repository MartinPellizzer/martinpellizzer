Chassalia tsaratanensis var. tsaratanensis

Chassalia tsaratanensis var. tsaratanensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana chassalia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the rainforests of the eastern part of the island. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and endemic status.
