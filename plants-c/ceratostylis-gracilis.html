Ceratostylis gracilis

Ceratostylis gracilis, commonly known as the slender spathephylla, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
