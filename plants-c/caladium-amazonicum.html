Caladium amazonicum

Caladium amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often displaying vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas. Its foliage is not only ornamental but also plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.

Caladium amazonicum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
