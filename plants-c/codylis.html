Codylis

Codylis, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. Codylis species often have opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produce small, capsule-like fruits.

Its botanical features make it a notable addition to gardens and natural ecosystems.
