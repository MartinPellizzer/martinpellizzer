Campanumoea maximowiczii

Campanumoea maximowiczii, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Campanumoea maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
