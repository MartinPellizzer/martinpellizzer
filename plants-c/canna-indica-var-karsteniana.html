Canna indica var. karsteniana

Canna indica var. karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to orange. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
