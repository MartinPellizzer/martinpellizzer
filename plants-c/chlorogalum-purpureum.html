Chlorogalum purpureum

Chlorogalum purpureum, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of waxy, purple-tinged leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with purple veins. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils. Its leaves are succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
