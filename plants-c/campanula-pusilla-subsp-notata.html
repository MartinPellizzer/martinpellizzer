Campanula pusilla subsp. notata

Campanula pusilla subsp.

notata, commonly known as the small bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm, with hairy, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
