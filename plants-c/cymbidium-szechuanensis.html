Cymbidium szechuanensis

Cymbidium szechuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan orchid, is a species native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and long-lasting, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, lance-shaped petals and a distinctive, often hairy lip. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often grown as a decorative ornamental plant.

Cymbidium szechuanensis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
