Carex tenejapensis

Carex tenejapensis, commonly known as the Tenejapan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes. It is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Carex tenejapensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
