Cryptocarya floribunda

Cryptocarya floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant ash or sweet ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are fragrant and attract pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Cryptocarya floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
