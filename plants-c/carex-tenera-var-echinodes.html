Carex tenera var. echinodes

Carex tenera var. echinodes, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
