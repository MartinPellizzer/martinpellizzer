Calamus reyesianus

Calamus reyesianus, commonly known as the Reyes' canescent palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, pinnate leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish bloom. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, yellowish flowers.

Calamus reyesianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
