Carmichaelia juncea var. parviflora

Carmichaelia juncea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Carmichaelia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
