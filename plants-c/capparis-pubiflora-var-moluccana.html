Capparis pubiflora var. moluccana

Capparis pubiflora var. moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan caper, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers with five petals, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
