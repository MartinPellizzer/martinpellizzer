Caletia wilhelmi

Caletia wilhelmi, commonly known as the Wilhelmine's spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring upright stems covered in sticky, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, often with a bluish or purplish hue.

Caletia wilhelmi is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
