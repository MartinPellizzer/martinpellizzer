Candelabria micrantha

Candelabria micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered candelabrum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that are usually pink or lavender in color. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The plant's unique floral structure and compact growth habit make it a desirable ornamental species in botanical gardens.
