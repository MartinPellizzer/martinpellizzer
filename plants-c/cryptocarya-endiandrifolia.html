Cryptocarya endiandrifolia

Cryptocarya endiandrifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeech, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits.

Cryptocarya endiandrifolia is often found in coastal and lowland forests, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
