Carex tarumensis

Carex tarumensis, commonly known as the Tarumense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually have a distinct midrib, while its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

Carex tarumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
