Centaurea solstitialis var. pygmaea

Centaurea solstitialis var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf burning bush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact size and vibrant color make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
