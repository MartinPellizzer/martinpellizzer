Cistus × corbariensis var. lanceolatus

Cistus × corbariensis var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local flora.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
