Crotalaria lachnoclada

Crotalaria lachnoclada, commonly known as the sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped seed pods. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with compound leaves and yellow flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in various environmental conditions.

Crotalaria lachnoclada is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
