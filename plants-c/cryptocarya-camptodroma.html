Cryptocarya camptodroma

Cryptocarya camptodroma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and bay trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. It produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round berries.

Cryptocarya camptodroma is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soils.
