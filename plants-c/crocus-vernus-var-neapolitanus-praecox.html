Crocus vernus var. neapolitanus-praecox

Crocus vernus var. neapolitanus-praecox, commonly known as the Naples early crocus, is a cultivar of the common crocus.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pale yellow, or cream. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to southern Europe.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
