Cyperus denudatus f. singularis

Cyperus denudatus f. singularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a unique variant of the Cyperus denudatus species.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, making it a distinctive member of the Cyperaceae family. This plant typically thrives in wetland or marshy environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, which are typical of sedges.

Cyperus denudatus f. singularis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological value.
