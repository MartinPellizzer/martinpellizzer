Chamaecrista bifoliolata

Chamaecrista bifoliolata, commonly known as the twin-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often divided into small, paired leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, branching clusters, followed by elongated, spiny pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
