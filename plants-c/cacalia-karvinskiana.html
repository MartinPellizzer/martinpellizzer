Cacalia karvinskiana

Cacalia karvinskiana, commonly known as the Karvinskian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Cacalia karvinskiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
