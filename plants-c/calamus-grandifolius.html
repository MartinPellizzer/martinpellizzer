Calamus grandifolius

Calamus grandifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by dark, edible fruits containing angular seeds.

Its rhizomes are also valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
