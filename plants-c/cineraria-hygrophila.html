Cineraria hygrophila

Cineraria hygrophila, commonly known as the water cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Cineraria hygrophila is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
