Cyrtomium reflexosquamatum

Cyrtomium reflexosquamatum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are pale green to silver in color, with a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyrtomium reflexosquamatum is native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.
