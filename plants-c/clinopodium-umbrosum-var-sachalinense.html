Clinopodium umbrosum var. sachalinense

Clinopodium umbrosum var. sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma, while its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
