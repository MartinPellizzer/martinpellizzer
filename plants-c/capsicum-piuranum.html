Capsicum piuranum

Capsicum piuranum, commonly known as the Peruvian chili or yellow chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in warm, dry climates.

The fruit is used in local cuisines and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
