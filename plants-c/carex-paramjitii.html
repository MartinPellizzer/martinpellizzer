Carex paramjitii

Carex paramjitii, commonly known as Paramjit's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
