Chamaesyce maritima

Chamaesyce maritima, commonly known as sea sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are typically succulent and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are inconspicuous, producing small, yellowish-green blooms.

Chamaesyce maritima plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
