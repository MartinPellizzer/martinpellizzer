Cassinia wyberbensis

Cassinia wyberbensis, commonly known as the Cape mountain cassinia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, bract-like leaves and has a compact, shrub-like growth habit.

Cassinia wyberbensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
