Calophyllum nubicola

Calophyllum nubicola, commonly known as the African sandpaper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
