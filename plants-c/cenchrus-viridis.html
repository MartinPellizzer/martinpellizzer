Cenchrus viridis

Cenchrus viridis, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
