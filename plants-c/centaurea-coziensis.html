Centaurea coziensis

Centaurea coziensis, commonly known as the Cozi's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its striking blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
