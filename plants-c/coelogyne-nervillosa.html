Coelogyne nervillosa

Coelogyne nervillosa, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne nervillosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
