Cineraria collina

Cineraria collina, commonly known as the rock cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cineraria collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
