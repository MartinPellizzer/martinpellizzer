Cocculus timorensis

Cocculus timorensis, commonly known as the Timor devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
