Coffea canephora f. sankuruensis

Coffea canephora f. sankuruensis, commonly known as the Sankuru coffee.

It is a wild form of Coffea canephora, native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. This variety is characterized by its small, dark red berries and high caffeine content. It is primarily found in the Sankuru River basin, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less cultivated species within the Coffea genus.
