Carelia albida

Carelia albida, commonly known as the white carelia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens with adequate drainage.

Carelia albida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be propagated through seeds or cuttings.
