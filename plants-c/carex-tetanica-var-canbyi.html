Carex tetanica var. canbyi

Carex tetanica var. canbyi, commonly known as Canby's sedges, is a variant of the sedge species Carex tetanica.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. It has fibrous roots and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
