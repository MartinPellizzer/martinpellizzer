Croton riedelianus

Croton riedelianus, commonly known as the Riedel's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red, purple, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not commonly found in cultivation outside its native range.
