Cyperus phillipsiae

Cyperus phillipsiae, commonly known as the pink flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus phillipsiae is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
