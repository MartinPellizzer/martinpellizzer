Croton coriaceus

Croton coriaceus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Croton coriaceus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
