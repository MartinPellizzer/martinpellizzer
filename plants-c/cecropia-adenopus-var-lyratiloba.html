Cecropia adenopus var. lyratiloba

Cecropia adenopus var. lyratiloba, commonly known as the lyrate-leaf ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are often lyrate in shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically has a straight, unbranched stem with a smooth, greyish bark. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
