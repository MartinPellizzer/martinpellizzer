Crataegus robbinsiana

Crataegus robbinsiana, commonly known as the Robbins hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorned branches, which are a distinguishing botanical characteristic. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it has a generally rugged, upright growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
