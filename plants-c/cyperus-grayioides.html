Cyperus grayioides

Cyperus grayioides, commonly known as the gray rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Cyperus grayioides produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
