Chorispermum exscapum

Chorispermum exscapum, commonly known as the slender chorisperm, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family, which it was previously classified under. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
