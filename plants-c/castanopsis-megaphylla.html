Castanopsis megaphylla

Castanopsis megaphylla, commonly known as the giant tanoak, is a tall evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts, and is characterized by its hard, durable wood. The tree produces oval, edible nuts enclosed in a woody cupule, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its broad, leathery leaves are dark green on top and lighter underneath, providing dense shade in its natural habitat.

Castanopsis megaphylla plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its forest ecosystems.
