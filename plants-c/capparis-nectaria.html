Capparis nectaria

Capparis nectaria, commonly known as the sweet caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its edible flower buds, which are used in culinary applications.

Capparis nectaria thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
