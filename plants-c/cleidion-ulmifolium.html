Cleidion ulmifolium

Cleidion ulmifolium, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas. Its bark is smooth and gray, while its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive palmate venation.

The white mulberry is also valued for its role in supporting silkworm cultivation.
