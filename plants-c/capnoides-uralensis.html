Capnoides uralensis

Capnoides uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and slightly woody at the base.

Capnoides uralensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
