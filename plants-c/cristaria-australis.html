Cristaria australis

Cristaria australis, commonly known as the Southern starwort, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped inflorescence, which consists of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a radial pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats that help stabilize the soil.

Cristaria australis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
