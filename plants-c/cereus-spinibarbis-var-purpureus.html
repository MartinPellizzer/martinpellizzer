Cereus spinibarbis var. purpureus

Cereus spinibarbis var. purpureus, commonly known as the Purple Thorn Cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers at night, a feature typical of many cacti adapted to nocturnal pollination. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

The varietal name 'purpureus' refers to the purple coloration often observed in its flowers or stems.
