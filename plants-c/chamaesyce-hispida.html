Chamaesyce hispida

Chamaesyce hispida, commonly known as the hispid sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in drought-prone areas.
