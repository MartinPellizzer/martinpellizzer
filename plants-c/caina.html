Caina

Caina, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a low-growing perennial, often forming dense clumps. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and blue.

Caina thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
