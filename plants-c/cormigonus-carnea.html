Cormigonus carnea

Cormigonus carnea, commonly known as the redstem corm, is a striking plant characterized by its vibrant red stems and clusters of small, showy flowers.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features a corm-like structure at its base, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Cormigonus carnea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions.
