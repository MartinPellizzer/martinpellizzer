Conyza cinerarioides

Conyza cinerarioides, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy leaves with a rough texture and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Conyza cinerarioides is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas.
