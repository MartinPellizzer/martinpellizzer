Cerasus laurocerasus capensis

Cerasus laurocerasus capensis, commonly known as the Cape cherry, is a species of Prunus native to South Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and fruit.
