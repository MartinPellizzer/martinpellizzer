Cucumis melo var. dinodaim

Cucumis melo var. dinodaim, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a variant of the cantaloupe species characterized by its distinctive netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit develops from a single pistil and is usually harvested when the rind turns a deep orange or yellow hue.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
