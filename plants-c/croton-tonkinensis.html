Croton tonkinensis

Croton tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed margins and produces small, greenish flowers in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The sap contains toxic compounds, so caution is advised when handling the plant.
