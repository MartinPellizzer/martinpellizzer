Cyrtochilum melanthes

Cyrtochilum melanthes, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique coloration that ranges from deep purples to blacks.

Cyrtochilum melanthes is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
