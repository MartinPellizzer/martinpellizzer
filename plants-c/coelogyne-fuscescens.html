Coelogyne fuscescens

Coelogyne fuscescens, commonly known as the black orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and white.

Coelogyne fuscescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
