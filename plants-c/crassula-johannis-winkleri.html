Crassula johannis-winkleri

Crassula johannis-winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically forms a dense, compact rosette, with leaves that are often blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual appearance. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the warmer months.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
