Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. hololeucus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

hololeucus, commonly known as yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are adaptations to its arid environment.

The plant produces small, cylindrical seed heads that are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

Its growth form and flowering patterns make it an important component of its ecosystem.
