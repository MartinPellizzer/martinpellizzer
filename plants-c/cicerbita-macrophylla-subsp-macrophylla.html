Cicerbita macrophylla subsp. macrophylla

Cicerbita macrophylla subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed bittercress, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bitter taste. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, producing a mild mustard-like odor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
