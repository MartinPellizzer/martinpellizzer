Croton paxianus

Croton paxianus, commonly known as the Paxian croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
