Carex riparia f. flagelliformis

Carex riparia f. flagelliformis, commonly known as the long-beard sedge, is a variant of the riparia sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. Its most distinctive feature is the elongated, hanging bracts that resemble a flowing beard, giving it its common name.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
