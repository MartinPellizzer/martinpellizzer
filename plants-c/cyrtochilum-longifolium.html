Cyrtochilum longifolium

Cyrtochilum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower structure, which is a defining feature of the Cypripedium subfamily. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Cyrtochilum longifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
