Capparis elaeagnoides var. longipedicellata

Capparis elaeagnoides var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stalked caper, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thorny stems and dense clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
