Clematis florida var. flore-plena

Clematis florida var. flore-plena, commonly known as the Japanese garden clematis, is a vigorous climbing plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, double flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features sturdy, thornless stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, making it ideal for trellises or arches. Its foliage is heart-shaped and provides a lush green backdrop to the showy blooms.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
