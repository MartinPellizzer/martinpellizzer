Carex tetanica var. carteri

Carex tetanica var. carteri, commonly known as Carter's tussock sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikelets that are typically clustered in a tussock-like formation. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It is native to North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of wildlife, including insects and small vertebrates.
