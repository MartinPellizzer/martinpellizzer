Cattleya pumila var. mirabilis

Cattleya pumila var. mirabilis, commonly known as the miniature cattleya, is a compact orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
