Cordia bracteata

Cordia bracteata, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Cordia bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
