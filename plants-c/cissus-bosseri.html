Cissus bosseri

Cissus bosseri, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Cissus bosseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
