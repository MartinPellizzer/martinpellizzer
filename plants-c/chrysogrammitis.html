Chrysogrammitis

Chrysogrammitis, commonly known as the golden moss fern, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Chrysogonaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, fern-like foliage that often displays a striking golden hue. They typically thrive in moist, shaded environments, making them common in temperate forests and woodland areas. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Chrysogrammitis species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their unique aesthetic.
