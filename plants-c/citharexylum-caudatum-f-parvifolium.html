Citharexylum caudatum f. parvifolium

Citharexylum caudatum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved citharexylum, is a compact shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, elliptical leaves that are smaller than those of the typical species, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

The foliage is evergreen, providing year-round interest in gardens.
