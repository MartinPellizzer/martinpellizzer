Chrysurus echinatus var. purpurascens

Chrysurus echinatus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-spined hawthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin and a glossy green surface. The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall and serves as a food source for various wildlife.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
