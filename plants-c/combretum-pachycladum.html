Combretum pachycladum

Combretum pachycladum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and distinctive, clustered, flame-like red flowers that appear in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
