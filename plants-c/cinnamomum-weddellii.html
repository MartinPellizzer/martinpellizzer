Cinnamomum weddellii

Cinnamomum weddellii, commonly known as Weddell's cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum weddellii typically grows in humid, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.
