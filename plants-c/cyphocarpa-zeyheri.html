Cyphocarpa zeyheri

Cyphocarpa zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's hakea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water. It is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal areas.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
