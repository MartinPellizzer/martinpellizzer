Camellia glabriperulata

Camellia glabriperulata, commonly known as the smooth-ribbed camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, double pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is evergreen and thrives in partial shade with well-drained, acidic soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

Camellia glabriperulata is also noted for its cold hardiness, making it suitable for temperate climates.
