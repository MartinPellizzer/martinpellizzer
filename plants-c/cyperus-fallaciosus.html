Cyperus fallaciosus

Cyperus fallaciosus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and flooded soils, and is often found in disturbed areas.

Cyperus fallaciosus is primarily an ornamental plant but can also serve as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.
