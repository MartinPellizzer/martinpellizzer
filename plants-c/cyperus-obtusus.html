Cyperus obtusus

Cyperus obtusus, commonly known as bluntleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

Cyperus obtusus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
