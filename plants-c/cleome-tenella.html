Cleome tenella

Cleome tenella, commonly known as spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. Cleome tenella thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique floral display and ease of cultivation.
