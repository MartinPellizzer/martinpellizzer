Carex dissitiflora subsp. taiwanensis

Carex dissitiflora subsp.

taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Taiwan. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical features.
