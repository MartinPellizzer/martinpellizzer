Cattleya longipes var. lucasiana

Cattleya longipes var. lucasiana, commonly known as the Lucasiana Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, typically in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, making it a striking addition to any collection. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both home and greenhouse settings.
