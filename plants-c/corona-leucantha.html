Corona leucantha

Corona leucantha, commonly known as the white crown orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive crown-like structure in its flower. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
