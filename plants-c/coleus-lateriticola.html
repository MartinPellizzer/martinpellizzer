Coleus lateriticola

Coleus lateriticola, commonly known as the lateritic coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are often variegated with shades of green, red, and purple, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in whorled inflorescences, and bloom in shades of pink or white.
