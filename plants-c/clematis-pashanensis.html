Clematis pashanensis

Clematis pashanensis, commonly known as the Himalayan clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five to seven petals. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for gardeners looking to add ornamental value to their landscapes.
