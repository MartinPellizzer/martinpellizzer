Cedrelinga

Cedrelinga, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued in the furniture and musical instrument industries. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume-like pods containing flat, elongated seeds.

Cedrelinga is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively fast growth rate.
