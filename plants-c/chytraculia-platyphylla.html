Chytraculia platyphylla

Chytraculia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf chytracul, is a unique plant species characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

It belongs to the family Chytraculaceae and is native to the arid regions of southern Africa. The plant exhibits a succulent growth habit, allowing it to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or green, and it produces distinctive, spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Chytraculia platyphylla is considered an important species for studying plant survival in extreme environments.
