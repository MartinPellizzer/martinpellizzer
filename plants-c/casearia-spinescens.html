Casearia spinescens

Casearia spinescens, commonly known as the spiny casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
