Cota palaestina

Cota palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
