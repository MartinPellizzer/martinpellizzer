Cyperus fuligineus

Cyperus fuligineus, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous and lack showy flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus fuligineus is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic ecosystems due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
