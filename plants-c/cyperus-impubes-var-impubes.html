Cyperus impubes var. impubes

Cyperus impubes var. impubes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common species in marshy areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
