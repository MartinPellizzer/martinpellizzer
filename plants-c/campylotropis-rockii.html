Campylotropis rockii

Campylotropis rockii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or mountainous areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Campylotropis rockii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it valuable in soil enrichment.
