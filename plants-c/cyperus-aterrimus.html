Cyperus aterrimus

Cyperus aterrimus, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale or brown in color, and it blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers. Cyperus aterrimus is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common species in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
