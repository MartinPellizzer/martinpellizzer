Crambe caspica

Crambe caspica, commonly known as the Caspian crambe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with glandular hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a rough texture.

Crambe caspica is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
