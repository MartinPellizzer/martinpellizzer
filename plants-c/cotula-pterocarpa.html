Cotula pterocarpa

Cotula pterocarpa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, winged fruits called pappus, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Cotula pterocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
