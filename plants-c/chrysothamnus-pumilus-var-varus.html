Chrysothamnus pumilus var. varus

Chrysothamnus pumilus var. varus, commonly known as the dwarf golden coneflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, including slopes and open woodlands.

Its compact size and vibrant flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
