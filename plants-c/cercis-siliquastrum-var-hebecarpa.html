Cercis siliquastrum var. hebecarpa

Cercis siliquastrum var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the Persian Lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during early spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
