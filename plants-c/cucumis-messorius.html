Cucumis messorius

Cucumis messorius, commonly known as the burr cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, temperate climates. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it has a trailing growth habit.

The burr cucumber is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
