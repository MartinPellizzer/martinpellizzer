Carlina salicifolia subsp. lancerottensis

Carlina salicifolia subsp.

lancerottensis, commonly known as the Lancerottensis carline, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and produces striking yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
