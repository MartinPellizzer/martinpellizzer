Colubrina celtidifolia var. minuteserrata

Colubrina celtidifolia var. minuteserrata, commonly known as the dwarf serrated colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in native plant gardens.
