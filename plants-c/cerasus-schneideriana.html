Cerasus schneideriana

Cerasus schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, bright red berries. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
