Centaurea limbata

Centaurea limbata, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are often surrounded by pale yellow bracts.

Centaurea limbata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
