Calliandra canescens

Calliandra canescens, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink or red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It is often used in reforestation and erosion control due to its deep root system and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Calliandra canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and as a source of forage for livestock.
