Campanula scheuchzeri var. typica

Campanula scheuchzeri var. typica, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
