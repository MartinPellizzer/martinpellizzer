Columbia burretii

Columbia burretii, commonly known as the giant goldenrod, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant can grow up to 6 feet tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important species for pollinators.

Columbia burretii thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
