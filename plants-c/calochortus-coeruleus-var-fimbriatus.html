Calochortus coeruleus var. fimbriatus

Calochortus coeruleus var. fimbriatus, commonly known as the blue globe lily, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the genus Calochortus, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as mariposa lilies. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in well-drained soils in open habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its finely fringed petals, which give it its scientific name "fimbriatus," meaning fringed.
