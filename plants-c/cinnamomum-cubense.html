Cinnamomum cubense

Cinnamomum cubense, commonly known as Cuban cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that resembles true cinnamon. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. Its bark is harvested for its fragrant, spicy essential oils, which are used in perfumes, food, and traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
