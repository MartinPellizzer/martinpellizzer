Cochranea florida

Cochranea florida, commonly known as the Florida cochranea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cochranea florida prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
