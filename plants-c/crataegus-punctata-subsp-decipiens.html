Crataegus punctata subsp. decipiens

Crataegus punctata subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the false hawthorn, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf and fruit morphology compared to other hawthorn species.
