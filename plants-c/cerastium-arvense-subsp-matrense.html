Cerastium arvense subsp. matrense

Cerastium arvense subsp.

matrense, commonly known as matren's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a woolly appearance.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
