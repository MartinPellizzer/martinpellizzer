Calamus hispidulus

Calamus hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Calamus hispidulus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
