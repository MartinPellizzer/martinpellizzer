Calophyllum vitiense

Calophyllum vitiense, commonly known as the Vitiense Mahoe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed. It is often found in coastal forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also has cultural significance in some Pacific island communities.
