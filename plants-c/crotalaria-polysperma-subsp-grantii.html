Crotalaria polysperma subsp. grantii

Crotalaria polysperma subsp.

grantii, commonly known as the Grant's crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
