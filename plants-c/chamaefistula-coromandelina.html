Chamaefistula coromandelina

Chamaefistula coromandelina, commonly known as the Coromandel boxwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a compact, rounded shape, giving it a boxwood-like appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
