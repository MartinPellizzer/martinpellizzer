Clematis crenata

Clematis crenata, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis crenata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
