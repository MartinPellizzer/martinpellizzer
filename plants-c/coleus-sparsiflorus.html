Coleus sparsiflorus

Coleus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
