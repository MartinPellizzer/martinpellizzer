Campanula betonicifolia var. byzantina

Campanula betonicifolia var. byzantina, commonly known as the Byzantine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
