Carex burangensis

Carex burangensis, commonly known as the Burang sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex burangensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
