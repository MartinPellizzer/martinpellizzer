Croton trichotomus

Croton trichotomus, commonly known as the three-branched croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage.
