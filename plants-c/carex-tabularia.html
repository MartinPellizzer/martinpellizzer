Carex tabularia

Carex tabularia, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and pond edges. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex tabularia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
