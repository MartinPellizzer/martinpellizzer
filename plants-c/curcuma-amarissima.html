Curcuma amarissima

Curcuma amarissima, commonly known as bitter turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark brown rhizomes, which contain high levels of curcuminoids, giving it its distinctive color and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Due to its potential health benefits, it is increasingly being studied for its use in traditional and modern medicine.
