Claytonia robusta

Claytonia robusta, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially under stress or in cooler conditions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland areas.

Claytonia robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
