Capparis acutifolia

Capparis acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by edible, round, green berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Capparis acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
