Carex complanata var. hirsuta

Carex complanata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy flat sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
