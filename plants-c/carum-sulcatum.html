Carum sulcatum

Carum sulcatum, commonly known as the grooved caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, spicy aroma.

The fruit, or seed, is small and dry, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
