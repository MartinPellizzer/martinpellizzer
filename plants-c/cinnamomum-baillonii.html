Cinnamomum baillonii

Cinnamomum baillonii, commonly known as the Bailloni cinnamon or Baillon's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, spindle-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests.
