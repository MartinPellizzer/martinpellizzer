Coccinia jatrophifolia var. australis

Coccinia jatrophifolia var. australis, commonly known as the Australian morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Australia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

It is also a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
