Chukrasia tabularis var. macrocarpa

Chukrasia tabularis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and provide dense shade. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
