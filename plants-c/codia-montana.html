Codia montana

Codia montana, commonly known as the mountain codia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in forested or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly aromatic scent.

Codia montana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
