Comparettia hirtzii

Comparettia hirtzii, commonly known as the Hirtz's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are often covered in a dense, hairy coating, which helps in moisture retention.

Comparettia hirtzii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
