Centaurea pinnatispina

Centaurea pinnatispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea pinnatispina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
