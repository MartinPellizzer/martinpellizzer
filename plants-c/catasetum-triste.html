Catasetum triste

Catasetum triste, commonly known as the sad orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant, often colorful blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are known for their intricate morphology, with specialized structures that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Catasetum triste is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
