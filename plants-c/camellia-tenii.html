Camellia tenii

Camellia tenii, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Camellia tenii prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
