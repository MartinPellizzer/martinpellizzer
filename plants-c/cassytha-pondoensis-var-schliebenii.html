Cassytha pondoensis var. schliebenii

Cassytha pondoensis var. schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's twining vine, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that often grow along the ground or climbing other vegetation. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that dehisces to release tiny seeds.
