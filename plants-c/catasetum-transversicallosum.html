Catasetum transversicallosum

Catasetum transversicallosum, commonly known as the Transversicallosum Catasetum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces intricate, often fragrant blooms with a distinctive lip that is specialized for pollination by specific insect species. Its leaves are alternate and lanceolate, often with a waxy texture.

Catasetum transversicallosum is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
