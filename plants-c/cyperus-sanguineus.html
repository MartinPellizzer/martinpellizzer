Cyperus sanguineus

Cyperus sanguineus, commonly known as red flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically reddish or pink in color, which give it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus sanguineus is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
