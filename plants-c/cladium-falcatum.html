Cladium falcatum

Cladium falcatum, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland ecosystems in the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often sword-shaped leaves that are typically green and have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense mats that help stabilize the soil. Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to the structure of wetland habitats.

Cladium falcatum plays a crucial role in providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
