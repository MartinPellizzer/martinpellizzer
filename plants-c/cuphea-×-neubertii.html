Cuphea × neubertii

Cuphea × neubertii, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant is a member of the Lythraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped, dark green leaves and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and unique floral display.

Cuphea × neubertii is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.
