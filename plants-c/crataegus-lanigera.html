Crataegus lanigera

Crataegus lanigera, commonly known as the hairy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and oval, hairy leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus lanigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
