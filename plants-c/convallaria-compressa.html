Convallaria compressa

Convallaria compressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily of the valley, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found in woodlands and mountainous regions.

The plant is part of the Convallariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
