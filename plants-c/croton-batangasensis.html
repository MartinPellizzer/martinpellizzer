Croton batangasensis

Croton batangasensis, commonly known as the Batangas croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by unique, spiky fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
