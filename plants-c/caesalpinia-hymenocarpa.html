Caesalpinia hymenocarpa

Caesalpinia hymenocarpa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant floral display.

The botanical features of Caesalpinia hymenocarpa make it a valuable species for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
