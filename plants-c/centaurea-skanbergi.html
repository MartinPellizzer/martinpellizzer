Centaurea skanbergi

Centaurea skanbergi, commonly known as the Skanberg's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Centaurea skanbergi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
