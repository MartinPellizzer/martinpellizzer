Chirita asperifolia

Chirita asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf chinaberry, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are rough to the touch due to their pubescent surface. Its small, yellow flowers are typically clustered in panicles, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
