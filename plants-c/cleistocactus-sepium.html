Cleistocactus sepium

Cleistocactus sepium, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and bright, often colorful flowers that bloom at night. The plant is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually tubular and come in shades of red, yellow, and orange.

Cleistocactus sepium is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
