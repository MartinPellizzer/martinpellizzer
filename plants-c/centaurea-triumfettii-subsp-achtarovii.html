Centaurea triumfettii subsp. achtarovii

Centaurea triumfettii subsp.

achtarovii, commonly known as the Achtarovii knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
