Crataegus × palmstruchii

Crataegus × palmstruchii, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus oxyacantha.

It typically features thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant is known for its ornamental value, with showy fruit clusters that turn red or orange in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
