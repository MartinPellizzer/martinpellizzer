Cactus parkinsonii

Cactus parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features a globular stem with a distinctive green color and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and botanical gardens.
