Carduus tauricus

Carduus tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, pale purple to blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and mountainous areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in coarse hairs, while the flowers are surrounded by a series of bracts that form a distinctive crown.

Carduus tauricus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
