Ceropegia carnosa subsp. glabra

Ceropegia carnosa subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
