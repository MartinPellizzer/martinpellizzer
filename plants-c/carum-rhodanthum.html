Carum rhodanthum

Carum rhodanthum, commonly known as the red-flowered caraway, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and umbels of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Carum rhodanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its visual interest.

Its fragrance is subtle and slightly spicy, similar to other members of its family.
