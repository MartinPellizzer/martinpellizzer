Croton pycnocephalus var. intermedius

Croton pycnocephalus var. intermedius, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often striking foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique leaf patterns and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
