Cousinia alpina f. subtilis

Cousinia alpina f. subtilis, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, typically from June to August. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
