Caladenia suaveolens

Caladenia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in open forests and heathlands. The plant features a single, erect stem with a single or occasionally two flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their spider-like appearance and a distinctive sweet fragrance.

Caladenia suaveolens is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
