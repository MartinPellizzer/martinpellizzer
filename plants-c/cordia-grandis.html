Cordia grandis

Cordia grandis, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils, making it a common species in coastal mangrove forests. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries containing numerous seeds. Its roots form a complex network that helps stabilize coastal sediments and prevent erosion.

Cordia grandis plays a vital role in marine and coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
