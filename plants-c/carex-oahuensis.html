Carex oahuensis

Carex oahuensis, commonly known as Oahu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Oahu in Hawaii.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex oahuensis is a rare species, facing threats from habitat loss and invasive species.
