Copernicia × textilis

Copernicia × textilis, commonly known as the carnauba wax palm, is a hybrid species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, feather-like leaves. The plant is renowned for producing a high-quality wax used in various industrial applications. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its economic importance, it is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
