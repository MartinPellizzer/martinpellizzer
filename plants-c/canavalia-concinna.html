Canavalia concinna

Canavalia concinna, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are a good source of protein and minerals.

Additionally, Canavalia concinna has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
