Croton serratifolius var. silvaticus

Croton serratifolius var. silvaticus, commonly known as the rough-leaved croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
