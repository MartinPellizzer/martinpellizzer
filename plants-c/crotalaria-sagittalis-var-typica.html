Crotalaria sagittalis var. typica

Crotalaria sagittalis var. typica, commonly known as the arrowhead cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is an annual herb with a hairy stem and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

The seeds are typically small and can be dispersed by wind or animals.
