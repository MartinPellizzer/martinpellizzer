Caladenia valida

Caladenia valida, commonly known as the Western River Orchid, is an orchid species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which bloom during the autumn season. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along riverbanks and open woodlands. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 40 centimeters.

Caladenia valida is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological specificity.
