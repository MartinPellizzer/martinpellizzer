Citrus × nobilis subf. keraji

Citrus × nobilis subf.

keraji, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a citrus hybrid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, greenish-yellow fruit.

The fruit has a distinctive bumpy rind and a strong, aromatic scent. It is widely used in cooking, particularly in Thai and other Southeast Asian cuisines.

Botanically, it is valued for its essential oils and aromatic compounds, which contribute to its culinary and aromatic significance.
