Crambe scoparia

Crambe scoparia, commonly known as the hairy crambe, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, hairy leaves, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces elongated pods containing spherical seeds, which are often used in agricultural settings.
