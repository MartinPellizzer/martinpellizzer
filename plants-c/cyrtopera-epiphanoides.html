Cyrtopera epiphanoides

Cyrtopera epiphanoides, commonly known as the golden sun orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant golden-yellow flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open woodland or heathland habitats. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, usually from September to November.

Cyrtopera epiphanoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
