Clusia dixonii

Clusia dixonii, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia dixonii is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
