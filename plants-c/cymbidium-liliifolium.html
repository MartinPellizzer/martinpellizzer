Cymbidium liliifolium

Cymbidium liliifolium, commonly known as the Lily-leaved Orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often described as resembling lily leaves. The plant produces tall, upright inflorescences bearing clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium liliifolium is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
