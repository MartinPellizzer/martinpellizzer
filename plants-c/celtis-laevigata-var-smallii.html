Celtis laevigata var. smallii

Celtis laevigata var. smallii, commonly known as the smallleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by green, berry-like fruits that ripen to a dark blue or black color. These fruits are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

The plant is also valued for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to thrive in urban environments.
