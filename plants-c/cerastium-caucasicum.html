Cerastium caucasicum

Cerastium caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Cerastium caucasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
