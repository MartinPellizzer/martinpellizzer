Capsella bursa-pastoris subrhombea

Capsella bursa-pastoris subrhombea, commonly known as the heart-shaped weed or shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular or heart-shaped pods that contain numerous small seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens. It has small, alternate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

Its botanical features make it a common weed with ecological and historical significance.
