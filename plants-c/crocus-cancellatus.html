Crocus cancellatus

Crocus cancellatus, commonly known as the checkered crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun or partial shade. The plant produces small, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a distinctive pattern of darker markings. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Crocus cancellatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and naturalized areas throughout its native range.
