Croton glandulosus var. lundianus

Croton glandulosus var. lundianus, commonly known as the Lundianus croton, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
