Cactus ottonis

Cactus ottonis, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which protect it from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Cactus ottonis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
