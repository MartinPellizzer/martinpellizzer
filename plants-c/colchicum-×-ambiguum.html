Colchicum × ambiguum

Colchicum × ambiguum, commonly known as the hybrid meadow colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces a tall stem with a single, spurred flower that can be pink, red, or white in color. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
