Callicocca tomentosa

Callicocca tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly callicocca, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in regions with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

The woolly callicocca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
