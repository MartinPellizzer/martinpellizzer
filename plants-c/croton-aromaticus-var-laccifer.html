Croton aromaticus var. laccifer

Croton aromaticus var. laccifer, commonly known as the lacquer tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and striking, colorful inflorescences that range from yellow to red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Its sap is known for its thick, sticky consistency and has been historically used in traditional crafts and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
