Cereus huilunchu

Cereus huilunchu, commonly known as the Huilunchu cactus, is a species native to Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, spiny stem and a grayish-green color. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for cultivation in dry climates.

Its structure and growth habits are typical of many cacti in the Cereus genus.
