Carex hirta var. glabrata

Carex hirta var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
