Cicer tetraspermum

Cicer tetraspermum, commonly known as the four-seeded vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces four-seeded pods, which is a distinctive feature contributing to its common name. It is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Cicer tetraspermum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
