Cymbidium tortisepalum var. tortisepalum

Cymbidium tortisepalum var. tortisepalum, commonly known as the twisted-sepal cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, narrow pseudobulbs. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, dark green to brownish-purple flowers. Its leaves are strap-shaped, glossy, and often have a waxy coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
