Calceolaria cymbiflora

Calceolaria cymbiflora, commonly known as the cup-shaped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Calceolaria cymbiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
