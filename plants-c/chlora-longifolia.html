Chlora longifolia

Chlora longifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf chlorine, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are known for their high concentration of alkaloids, making it a subject of botanical and pharmacological interest.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is often studied for potential medicinal applications.
