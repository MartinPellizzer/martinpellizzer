Clematis villosa var. poggei

Clematis villosa var. poggei, commonly known as the Pogge's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 20 feet, supported by hooked tendrils. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and the plant is often found in woodland and moist habitats.

Clematis villosa var. poggei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
