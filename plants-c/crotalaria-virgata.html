Crotalaria virgata

Crotalaria virgata, commonly known as the smallflower cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria virgata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
