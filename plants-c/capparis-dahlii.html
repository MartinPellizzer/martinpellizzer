Capparis dahlii

Capparis dahlii, commonly known as the Mexican sour fig, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, edible berry that turns red when mature.

Capparis dahlii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
