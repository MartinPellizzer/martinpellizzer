Centaurium erythraea var. sublitorale

Centaurium erythraea var. sublitorale, commonly known as the sea centaury, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. The plant produces small, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a compact growth habit.

This variety is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
