Carum imbricatum

Carum imbricatum, commonly known as the Persian cumin or Persian coriander, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, known for their distinct aroma and flavor.

Carum imbricatum thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
