Coffea congensis var. micrantha

Coffea congensis var. micrantha, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee plant, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that produces red berries containing the seeds used for coffee. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high altitudes and well-drained soil. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and dark green color.

The flowers are white and fragrant, typically blooming in the spring.
