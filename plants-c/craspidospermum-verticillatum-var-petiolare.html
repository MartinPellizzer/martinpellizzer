Craspidospermum verticillatum var. petiolare

Craspidospermum verticillatum var. petiolare, commonly known as the petioled craspidospermum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
