Cucumis melo var. chloris

Cucumis melo var. chloris, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit with a pale green skin. The plant typically grows on a trailing vine, producing small, pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are usually harvested when immature, before they fully mature into the typical melon form.

This variety is prized for its crisp texture and mild, sweet flavor.
