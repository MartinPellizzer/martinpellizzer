Coffea acuminata

Coffea acuminata, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee plant, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia and surrounding regions.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain two seeds, which are the coffee beans used for brewing. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a pointed tip, giving it its scientific name.

Coffea acuminata is known for its aromatic and flavorful beans, which are often used in specialty coffee blends.
