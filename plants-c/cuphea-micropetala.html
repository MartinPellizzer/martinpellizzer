Cuphea micropetala

Cuphea micropetala, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its unique appearance and easy care requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.

Cuphea micropetala is also known for attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators due to its nectar-rich flowers.
