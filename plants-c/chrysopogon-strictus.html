Chrysopogon strictus

Chrysopogon strictus, commonly known as narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in open areas, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to different environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. It is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grass species.
