Calanthe brevicornu

Calanthe brevicornu, commonly known as the short-horned calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with distinctive, short, rounded sepals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Calanthe brevicornu is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
