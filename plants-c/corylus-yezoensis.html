Corylus yezoensis

Corylus yezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the hazelnut family, Corylaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by oval-shaped nuts that mature in the fall. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Corylus yezoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible nuts.
