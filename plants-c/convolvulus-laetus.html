Convolvulus laetus

Convolvulus laetus, commonly known as the spreading bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
