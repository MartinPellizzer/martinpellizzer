Ceanothus verrucosus var. rigidus

Ceanothus verrucosus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid thimbleweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
