Carex elbertiana

Carex elbertiana, commonly known as Elbert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Carex elbertiana produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, and it often forms dense colonies in its natural environment.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
