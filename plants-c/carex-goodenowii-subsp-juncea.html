Carex goodenowii subsp. juncea

Carex goodenowii subsp.

juncea, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in riparian zones.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
