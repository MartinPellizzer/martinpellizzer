Convolvulus boissieri subsp. boissieri

Convolvulus boissieri subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in the morning.

The plant has slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
