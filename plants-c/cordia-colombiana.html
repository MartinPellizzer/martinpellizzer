Cordia colombiana

Cordia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
