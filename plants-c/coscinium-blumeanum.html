Coscinium blumeanum

Coscinium blumeanum, commonly known as the Indian black nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, violet flowers that turn into dark purple berries. Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in dense vegetation.
