Campanula antiatlantica

Campanula antiatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
