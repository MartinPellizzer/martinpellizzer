Cinnamomum semecarpifolium

Cinnamomum semecarpifolium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or camphor laurel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that contain seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
