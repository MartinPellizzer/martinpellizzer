Conyza crispata

Conyza crispata, commonly known as the white sandmat, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, white to pale yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The leaves are usually grayish-green with a fuzzy texture and are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize areas with minimal water.
