Coryphantha salinensis

Coryphantha salinensis, commonly known as the salt flat cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. Coryphantha salinensis is adapted to survive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy plant in harsh desert environments.

Its structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptations to extreme drought and high salinity conditions.
