Carex decidua

Carex decidua, commonly known as the deciduous sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green color.

Carex decidua produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
