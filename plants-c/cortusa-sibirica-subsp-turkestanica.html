Cortusa sibirica subsp. turkestanica

Cortusa sibirica subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to purple in color.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its showy blooms and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
