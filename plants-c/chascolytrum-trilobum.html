Chascolytrum trilobum

Chascolytrum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed milkweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are deeply divided into three lobes, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including livestock. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
