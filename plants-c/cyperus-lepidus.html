Cyperus lepidus

Cyperus lepidus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in a dense, compact cluster. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Cyperus lepidus is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
