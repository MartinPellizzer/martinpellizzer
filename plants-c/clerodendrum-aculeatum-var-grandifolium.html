Clerodendrum aculeatum var. grandifolium

Clerodendrum aculeatum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the giant flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, red-orange flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or screen in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
