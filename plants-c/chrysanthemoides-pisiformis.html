Chrysanthemoides pisiformis

Chrysanthemoides pisiformis, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often around the holiday season. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
