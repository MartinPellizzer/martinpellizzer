Cocculus burmanni

Cocculus burmanni, commonly known as the burr cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruit that resembles a cucumber. The plant produces yellowish flowers and has a thick, hairy stem that helps it climb. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a pointed tip and have a rough texture.

The fruit contains several seeds and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
