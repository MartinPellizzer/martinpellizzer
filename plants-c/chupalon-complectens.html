Chupalon complectens

Chupalon complectens, commonly known as the tangled mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the moist woodlands of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, feathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its slender stems. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and spreads horizontally, allowing it to thrive in shaded, nutrient-rich soils.

Chupalon complectens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
