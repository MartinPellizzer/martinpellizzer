Campanula × digenea

Campanula × digenea, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula × digenea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
