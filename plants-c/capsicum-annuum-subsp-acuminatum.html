Capsicum annuum subsp. acuminatum

Capsicum annuum subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the acute-lobed chili pepper, is a botanical variant of the common bell pepper. It is characterized by its elongated, pointed fruits that typically range in color from green to red, orange, or yellow when mature.

The plant is an annual herb with simple, alternate leaves that have serrated margins. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its spicy fruits, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
