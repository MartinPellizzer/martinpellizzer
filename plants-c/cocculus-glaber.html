Cocculus glaber

Cocculus glaber, commonly known as the rough cocculus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, woody pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
