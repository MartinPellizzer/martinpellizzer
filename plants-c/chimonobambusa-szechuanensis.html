Chimonobambusa szechuanensis

Chimonobambusa szechuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to China, particularly the Sichuan province, and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. This bamboo is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
