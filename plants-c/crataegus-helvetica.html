Crataegus helvetica

Crataegus helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used for making jams and wines. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Crataegus helvetica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
