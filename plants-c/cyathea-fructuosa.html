Cyathea fructuosa

Cyathea fructuosa, commonly known as the fruit-bearing tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Cyathea fructuosa produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are involved in its reproductive cycle.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
