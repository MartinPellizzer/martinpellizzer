Cassebeeria robusta

Cassebeeria robusta, commonly known as the robust cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The robust cassia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
