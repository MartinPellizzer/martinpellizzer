Commelina sabatieri

Commelina sabatieri, commonly known as the white-veined dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Commelina sabatieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
