Chadsia grevei var. latifolia

Chadsia grevei var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved chadsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its wide, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
