Calamagrostis hsinganensis

Calamagrostis hsinganensis, commonly known as the Chinese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to northern China.

It features slender, silver-tipped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it grows in clumps with a tall, upright habit. The plant produces panicles of small, feathery flowers in late summer, which are often used in ornamental landscaping. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Calamagrostis hsinganensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife and improving soil stability.
