Cissus patellicalyx

Cissus patellicalyx, commonly known as the Patellifolius cissus, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can form a dense network when grown in suitable conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
