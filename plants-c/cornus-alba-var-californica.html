Cornus alba var. californica

Cornus alba var. californica, commonly known as the California dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its foliage changes color throughout the seasons, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
