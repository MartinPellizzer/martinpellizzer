Calophyllum carrii var. carrii

Calophyllum carrii var. carrii, commonly known as the Caribbean mahogany, is a species of flowering plant in the family Clusiaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean region, including parts of the Greater Antilles and the Lesser Antilles. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and smooth, dark reddish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
