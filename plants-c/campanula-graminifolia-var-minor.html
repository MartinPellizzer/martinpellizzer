Campanula graminifolia var. minor

Campanula graminifolia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bluebell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
