Croton nobilis

Croton nobilis, commonly known as the Indian croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from bright red to deep orange and green. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its showy, spiky flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Croton nobilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
