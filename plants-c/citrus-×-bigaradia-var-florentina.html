Citrus × bigaradia var. florentina

Citrus × bigaradia var. florentina, commonly known as the sweet lime or Persian lime, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from a cross between the citron and the mandarin.

It is characterized by its small, round, green fruit with a thin, smooth rind and a juicy, sweet white pulp. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The fruit is particularly valued for its high vitamin C content and refreshing flavor.
