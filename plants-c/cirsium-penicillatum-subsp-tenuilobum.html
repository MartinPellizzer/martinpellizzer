Cirsium penicillatum subsp. tenuilobum

Cirsium penicillatum subsp.

tenuilobum, commonly known as narrow-lobed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spiny, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has deeply lobed, silver-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaf lobes compared to other members of the Cirsium penicillatum species complex.
