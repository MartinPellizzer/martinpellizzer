Chamaecyparis thyoides pendula

Chamaecyparis thyoides pendula, commonly known as the hanging cedar or creeping cedar, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green and emit a subtle, pleasant aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its unique form and ability to provide dense coverage in shaded garden settings.
