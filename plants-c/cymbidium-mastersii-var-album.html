Cymbidium mastersii var. album

Cymbidium mastersii var. album, commonly known as the white cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its elegant appearance.

This variety is relatively easy to care for, requiring moderate light and consistent moisture to thrive.
