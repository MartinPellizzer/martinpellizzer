Colchicum longifolium var. castrense

Colchicum longifolium var. castrense, commonly known as the Castrense autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
