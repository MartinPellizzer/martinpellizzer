Ceratolobus plicatus

Ceratolobus plicatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the midrib, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Ceratolobus plicatus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
