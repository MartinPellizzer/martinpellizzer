Camellia × kamoshibori

Camellia × kamoshibori, commonly known as the Kamoshibori camellia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Camellia sasanqua and Camellia reticulata.

It is native to Japan and is prized for its showy, double-flowered blooms that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia × kamoshibori is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and winter blooming habit.
