Centaurea palaestinica

Centaurea palaestinica, commonly known as Palestinian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel, Palestine, and southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant features erect, branching stems with narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with distinctive purple or red bracts. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea palaestinica is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
