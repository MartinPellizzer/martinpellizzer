Chrysocactus

Chrysocactus, commonly known as the gold cactus, is a type of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Chrysocactus is popular as a decorative plant due to its compact size and striking appearance.
