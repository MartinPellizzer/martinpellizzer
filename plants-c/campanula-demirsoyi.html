Campanula demirsoyi

Campanula demirsoyi, commonly known as the Demirsoy bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has hairy leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula demirsoyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
