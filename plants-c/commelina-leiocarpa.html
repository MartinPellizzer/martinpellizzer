Commelina leiocarpa

Commelina leiocarpa, commonly known as the white commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes a variety of flowering plants known for their ornamental and ecological significance. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typically white or pale blue in color, and are accompanied by three bracts.

Commelina leiocarpa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands, forests, and along stream banks.
