Carex killickii

Carex killickii, commonly known as the killick's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on a single, erect stem. Carex killickii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
