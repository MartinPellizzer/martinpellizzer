Cyclamen brevifrons

Cyclamen brevifrons, commonly known as the short-stemmed cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland and rocky habitats. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a sought-after ornamental plant.

Cyclamen brevifrons is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
