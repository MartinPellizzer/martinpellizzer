Callista affinis

Callista affinis, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and daisy-like yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both cultivated and natural settings.

Callista affinis is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
