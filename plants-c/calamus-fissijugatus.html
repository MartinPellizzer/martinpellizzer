Calamus fissijugatus

Calamus fissijugatus, commonly known as the split-leaf calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Aroid family, Araceae.

It is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often exhibit a distinct split or groove along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
