Centaurea holophylla

Centaurea holophylla, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and showy, spiky purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance, and it is often found in coastal areas.

Centaurea holophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
