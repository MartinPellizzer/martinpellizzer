Cyathea meridionalis

Cyathea meridionalis, commonly known as the southern tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall, woody trunk, while the fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Cyathea meridionalis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
