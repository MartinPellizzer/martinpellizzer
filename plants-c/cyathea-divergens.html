Cyathea divergens

Cyathea divergens, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

Cyathea divergens plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various organisms.
