Cyathea lindeniana

Cyathea lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant is known for its slow growth rate.

Cyathea lindeniana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
