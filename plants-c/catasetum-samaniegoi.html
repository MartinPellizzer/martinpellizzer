Catasetum samaniegoi

Catasetum samaniegoi, commonly known as the Samaniego's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant mix of red, yellow, and purple hues. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surface of trees and other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers have a unique structure with a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Catasetum samaniegoi is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized collections.
