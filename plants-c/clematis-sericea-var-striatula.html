Clematis sericea var. striatula

Clematis sericea var. striatula, commonly known as the striated Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, typically with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability in garden settings.
