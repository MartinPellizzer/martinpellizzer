Croton scouleri var. darwinii

Croton scouleri var. darwinii, commonly known as the Darwin's croton, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features vibrant, palmate leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and wildlife support purposes.
