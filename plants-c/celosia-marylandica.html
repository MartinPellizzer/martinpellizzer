Celosia marylandica

Celosia marylandica, commonly known as the Maryland cockscomb, is a striking annual plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and pink. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its upright growth habit and compact size. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Celosia marylandica thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
