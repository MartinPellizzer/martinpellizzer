Chenopodium album subsp. pedunculare

Chenopodium album subsp.

pedunculare, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to triangular leaves that are often covered with fine, prickly hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, tangy seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a versatile and resilient plant with both ecological and practical significance.
