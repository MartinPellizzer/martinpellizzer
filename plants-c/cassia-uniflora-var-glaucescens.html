Cassia uniflora var. glaucescens

Cassia uniflora var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
