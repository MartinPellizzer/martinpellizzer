Convallaria majalis f. prolificans

Convallaria majalis f. prolificans, commonly known as the fragrant lily of the valley, is a variant of the traditional lily of the valley plant.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that form a dense, low-growing rosette. It is native to Europe and thrives in shaded, moist environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
