Ceropegia malawiensis

Ceropegia malawiensis, commonly known as the Malawi pipevine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble pipes. The plant typically grows as a succulent perennial, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually opposite and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The flowers are pollinated by specific moths, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
