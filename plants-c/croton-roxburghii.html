Croton roxburghii

Croton roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian croton, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that can range from bright yellow to deep red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface with prominent veins.

Croton roxburghii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
