Cirsium vlassovianum var. lineare

Cirsium vlassovianum var. lineare, commonly known as the linear thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, compound leaves with a distinctive linear shape. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
