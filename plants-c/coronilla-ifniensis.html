Coronilla ifniensis

Coronilla ifniensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its creeping growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Coronilla ifniensis is often found in open grasslands and coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
