Caryocar toxiferum

Caryocar toxiferum, commonly known as the poison nut or Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, green flowers that develop into woody fruits. The fruit contains a single, hard-shelled seed surrounded by a toxic, fleshy pulp. The plant is part of the Burseraceae family and is known for its ecological importance in its native habitat.

However, its seeds are highly toxic and should never be consumed.
