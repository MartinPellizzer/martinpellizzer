Cyperus longus var. badius

Cyperus longus var. badius, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish or purplish in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Cyperus longus var. badius is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability.
