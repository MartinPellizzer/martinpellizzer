Carex convoluta

Carex convoluta, commonly known as the curled sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has slender, triangular stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. Carex convoluta produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
