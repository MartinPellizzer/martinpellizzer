Cyperus niederleinianus

Cyperus niederleinianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinct midrib, contributing to its structural identity.

Cyperus niederleinianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
