Chamaesyce punctulata

Chamaesyce punctulata, commonly known as the dotted spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, green, succulent leaves with distinctive white or yellow dots along the veins. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of tiny yellow flowers clustered in dense, branched inflorescences.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
