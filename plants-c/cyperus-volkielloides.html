Cyperus volkielloides

Cyperus volkielloides, commonly known as the Volkielloides flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and along riverbanks. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, with small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus volkielloides is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
