Collinsonia verticillata

Collinsonia verticillata, commonly known as the whorled loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy and bear opposite or whorled leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
