Combretum pincianum

Combretum pincianum, commonly known as the Pincian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange floral displays during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are clustered in panicles. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant seasonal blooms.
