Carex limosa var. pauciflora

Carex limosa var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and triangular stems. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced flowering structures, contributing to its unique ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
