Croton apetalus

Croton apetalus, commonly known as the naked croton, is a unique plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of petals, giving it its scientific name "apetalus," which means "without petals.". The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often green or variegated with yellow or white markings. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green.

Croton apetalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
