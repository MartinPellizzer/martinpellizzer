Cordia expansa

Cordia expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Cordia expansa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
