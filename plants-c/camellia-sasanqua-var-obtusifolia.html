Camellia sasanqua var. obtusifolia

Camellia sasanqua var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of deep red, camellia-like flowers in late autumn to early winter. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
