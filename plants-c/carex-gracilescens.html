Carex gracilescens

Carex gracilescens, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps that help stabilize the surrounding ecosystem.

Its small, greenish-brown flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a vital role in wetland habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
