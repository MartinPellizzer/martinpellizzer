Campanula pusilla subsp. excisa

Campanula pusilla subsp.

excisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
