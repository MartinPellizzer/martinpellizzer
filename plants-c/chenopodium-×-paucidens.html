Chenopodium × paucidens

Chenopodium × paucidens, commonly known as the common goosefoot, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, succulent leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to North America and thrives in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its growth habit is typically prostrate or spreading, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
