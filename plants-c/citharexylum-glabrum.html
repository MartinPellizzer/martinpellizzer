Citharexylum glabrum

Citharexylum glabrum, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Citharexylum glabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
