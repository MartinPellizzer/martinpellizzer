Cousinia kermanensis

Cousinia kermanensis, commonly known as Kerman's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a deep root system to access water. It is often found in mountainous areas and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

Cousinia kermanensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
