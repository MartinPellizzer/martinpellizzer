Croton curviflorus

Croton curviflorus, commonly known as the curlleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often brightly colored leaves that display a wide range of hues, including green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Croton curviflorus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
