Clematis cirrhosa f. semitriloba

Clematis cirrhosa f. semitriloba, commonly known as the fragrant clematis, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a trifoliate or semitrilobate pattern, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, often with a slight pinkish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
