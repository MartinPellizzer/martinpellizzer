Carex capillaris var. parvirostris

Carex capillaris var. parvirostris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to droop slightly.

This variety is often found in moist soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
