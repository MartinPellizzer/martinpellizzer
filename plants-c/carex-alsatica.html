Carex alsatica

Carex alsatica, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils at high elevations. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex alsatica is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
