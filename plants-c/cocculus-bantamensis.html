Cocculus bantamensis

Cocculus bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam devil's fig, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are toxic and contain a single large seed, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates.
