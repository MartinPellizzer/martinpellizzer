Carex capillaris f. major

Carex capillaris f. major, commonly known as the largehair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a distinctive hairy texture, especially along the edges.
