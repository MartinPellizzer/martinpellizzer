Coccothrinax pseudorigida

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the islands of Cuba and Hispaniola. The plant is characterized by its robust, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of pale, waxy hair. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, three-angled fruits.
