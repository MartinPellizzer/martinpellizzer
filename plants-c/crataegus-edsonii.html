Crataegus edsonii

Crataegus edsonii, commonly known as Edson's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Crataegus edsonii is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
