Carissa bispinosa subsp. zambesiensis

Carissa bispinosa subsp.

zambesiensis, commonly known as the Zambesi moonvine, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, orange-yellow berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
