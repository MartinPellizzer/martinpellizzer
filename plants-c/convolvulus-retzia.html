Convolvulus retzia

Convolvulus retzia, commonly known as the red balsam, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Convolvulus retzia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
