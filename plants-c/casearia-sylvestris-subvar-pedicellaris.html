Casearia sylvestris subvar. pedicellaris

Casearia sylvestris subvar. pedicellaris, commonly known as the red-flowered casearia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
