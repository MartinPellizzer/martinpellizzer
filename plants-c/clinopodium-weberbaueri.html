Clinopodium weberbaueri

Clinopodium weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are small, opposite, and often have a minty scent, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from white to purple.

Clinopodium weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
