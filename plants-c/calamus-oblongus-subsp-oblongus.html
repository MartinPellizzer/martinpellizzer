Calamus oblongus subsp. oblongus

Calamus oblongus subsp.

oblongus, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia, the Philippines, and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family.

This subspecies is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
