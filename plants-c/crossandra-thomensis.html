Crossandra thomensis

Crossandra thomensis, commonly known as the Tomontsisi or Tomontsis, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sao Tome in West Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Crossandra thomensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
