Callistemon longifolius

Callistemon longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like inflorescences, which are composed of numerous tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight blue or grayish hue.

Callistemon longifolius is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it thrives in full sun.
