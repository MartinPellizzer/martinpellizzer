Clerodendrum angustipetalum

Clerodendrum angustipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and feature tubular corollas with two lips, a common trait in the genus Clerodendrum.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
