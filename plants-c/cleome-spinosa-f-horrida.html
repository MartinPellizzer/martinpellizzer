Cleome spinosa f. horrida

Cleome spinosa f. horrida, commonly known as the rough cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it a rugged appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
