Cupania rugosa

Cupania rugosa, commonly known as the roughcup plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cupania rugosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
