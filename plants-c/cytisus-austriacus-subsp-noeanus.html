Cytisus austriacus subsp. noeanus

Cytisus austriacus subsp.

noeanus, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
