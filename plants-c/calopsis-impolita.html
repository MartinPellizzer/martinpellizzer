Calopsis impolita

Calopsis impolita, commonly known as the rough calopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
