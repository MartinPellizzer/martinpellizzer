Chamaerops hystrix

Chamaerops hystrix, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a striking evergreen plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of stiff, spiny leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits, which are edible for some bird species. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.

Chamaerops hystrix thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.
