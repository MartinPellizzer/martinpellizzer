Crepis scorzoneroides

Crepis scorzoneroides, commonly known as the scorzoneroid dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and surrounded by bracts.

Crepis scorzoneroides is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
