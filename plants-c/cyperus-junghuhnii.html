Cyperus junghuhnii

Cyperus junghuhnii, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Cyperus junghuhnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
