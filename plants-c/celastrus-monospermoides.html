Celastrus monospermoides

Celastrus monospermoides, commonly known as the smooth-seed staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
