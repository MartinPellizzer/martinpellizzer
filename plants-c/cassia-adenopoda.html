Cassia adenopoda

Cassia adenopoda, commonly known as golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its showy, yellow, fluffy inflorescences that appear in spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets.

Cassia adenopoda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
