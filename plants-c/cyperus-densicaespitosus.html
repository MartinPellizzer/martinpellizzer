Cyperus densicaespitosus

Cyperus densicaespitosus, commonly known as the dense bristly flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, bristly foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it often serves as a habitat for various small organisms.

Cyperus densicaespitosus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and filtering water in wetland ecosystems.
