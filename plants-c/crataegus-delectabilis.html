Crataegus delectabilis

Crataegus delectabilis, commonly known as the edible hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries known as haws, which are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Crataegus delectabilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
