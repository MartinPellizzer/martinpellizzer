Chenopodium hircinum var. densum

Chenopodium hircinum var. densum, commonly known as sheep's spinach or woolly chenopodium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its edible leaves, which have a slightly salty taste and are used in traditional cuisines.
