Coelogyne siamensis

Coelogyne siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has a pseudobulb structure with long, strap-like leaves, and it thrives in humid, warm environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.

Coelogyne siamensis is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
