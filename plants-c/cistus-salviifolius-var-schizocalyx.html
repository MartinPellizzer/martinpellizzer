Cistus salviifolius var. schizocalyx

Cistus salviifolius var. schizocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hirsute stems and fragrant foliage contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
