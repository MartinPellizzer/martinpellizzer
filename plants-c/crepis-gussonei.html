Crepis gussonei

Crepis gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
