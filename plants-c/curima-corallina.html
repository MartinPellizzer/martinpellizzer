Curima corallina

Curima corallina, commonly known as coral bush, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the arid conditions of its native environment.

Curima corallina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
