Critamus latifolius

Critamus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
