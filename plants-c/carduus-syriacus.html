Carduus syriacus

Carduus syriacus, commonly known as Syrian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, upright stem with spiny leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
