Coleataenia beyeri

Coleataenia beyeri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to eastern and southern Africa, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
