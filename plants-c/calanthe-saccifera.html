Calanthe saccifera

Calanthe saccifera, commonly known as the sac-like calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive sac-like structure at the base of the petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calanthe saccifera is valued in horticulture for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
