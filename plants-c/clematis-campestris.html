Clematis campestris

Clematis campestris, commonly known as the field clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.

Clematis campestris is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
