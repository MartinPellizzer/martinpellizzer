Clinopodium alpinum subsp. albanicum

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

albanicum, commonly known as Albanian mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
