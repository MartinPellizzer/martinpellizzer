Clinopodium Thymbra

Clinopodium thymbra, commonly known as wild thyme, is a fragrant perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its lush green foliage. Its aromatic leaves are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Despite its modest appearance, Clinopodium thymbra holds a special place in traditional herbal practices and garden landscapes.
