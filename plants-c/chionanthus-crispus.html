Chionanthus crispus

Chionanthus crispus, commonly known as the crinkled whiteflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, reflexed flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often crinkled or serrated along the edges. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and prefers partial to full shade.

Chionanthus crispus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
