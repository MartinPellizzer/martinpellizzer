Cytisopsis dorycniifolia subsp. reeseana

Cytisopsis dorycniifolia subsp.

reeseana, commonly known as the Reese's cytisopsis, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
