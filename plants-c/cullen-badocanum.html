Cullen badocanum

Cullen badocanum, commonly known as the Badocanum bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
