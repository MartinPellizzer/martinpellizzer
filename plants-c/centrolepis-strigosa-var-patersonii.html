Centrolepis strigosa var. patersonii

Centrolepis strigosa var. patersonii, commonly known as the Paterson's centrolepis, is a rare and unique plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the ancient gymnosperm group, characterized by its ability to retain water in its leaves, which helps it survive in arid environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in rocky outcrops and is considered a relict species with a long evolutionary history.
