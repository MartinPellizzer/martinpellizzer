Cratylia floribunda var. typica

Cratylia floribunda var. typica, commonly known as the clustered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

Botanically, it is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
