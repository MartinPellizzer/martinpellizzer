Centaurea × ludovici

Centaurea × ludovici, commonly known as the French centaury, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and vibrant blue to purple flower heads that resemble the classic centaur's tail. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to southern Europe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Centaurea × ludovici is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in herbal remedies.
