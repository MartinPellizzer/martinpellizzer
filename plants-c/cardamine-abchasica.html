Cardamine abchasica

Cardamine abchasica, commonly known as Abkhazian watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia and Russia.
