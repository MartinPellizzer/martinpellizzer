Chamaebatia australis

Chamaebatia australis, commonly known as the coastal buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture and a slightly serrated margin.

Chamaebatia australis thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
