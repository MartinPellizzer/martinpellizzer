Carex emarcida

Carex emarcida, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ecological importance in wetland ecosystems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carex emarcida plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
