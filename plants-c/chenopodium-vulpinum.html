Chenopodium vulpinum

Chenopodium vulpinum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. Lambsquarters thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of minerals such as potassium and magnesium.
