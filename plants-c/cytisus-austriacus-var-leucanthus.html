Cytisus austriacus var. leucanthus

Cytisus austriacus var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that are slightly smaller and more clustered compared to the typical cytisus species. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
