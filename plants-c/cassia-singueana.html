Cassia singueana

Cassia singueana, commonly known as flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and striking, bright orange to red flower clusters that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a fluffy, white coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a notable feature in botanical gardens and urban landscapes.
