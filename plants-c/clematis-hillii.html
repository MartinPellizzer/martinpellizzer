Clematis hillii

Clematis hillii, commonly known as the alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer. The plant has slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with finely toothed edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
