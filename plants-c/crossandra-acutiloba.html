Crossandra acutiloba

Crossandra acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-leaved crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has narrow, pointed leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Crossandra acutiloba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking floral display and relatively low maintenance requirements.
