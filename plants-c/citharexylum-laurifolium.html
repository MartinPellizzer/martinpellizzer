Citharexylum laurifolium

Citharexylum laurifolium, commonly known as the Australian rosewood or silky lauriferous, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
