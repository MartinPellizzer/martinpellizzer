Calluna vulgaris f. lutescens

Calluna vulgaris f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow heather, is a variant of the common heather.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its yellowish to golden-colored flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in heathlands and moorlands. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
