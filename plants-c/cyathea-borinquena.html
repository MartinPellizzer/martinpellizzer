Cyathea borinquena

Cyathea borinquena, commonly known as the Puerto Rican tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome system supports its growth and helps anchor it to the forest floor.

Cyathea borinquena plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
