Chaetochloa scandens

Chaetochloa scandens, commonly known as the climbing bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats or climb over other vegetation. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
