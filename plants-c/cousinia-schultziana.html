Cousinia schultziana

Cousinia schultziana, commonly known as the Schultze's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Cousinia schultziana is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.
