Caladenia patersonii var. lindleyana

Caladenia patersonii var. lindleyana, commonly known as the Paterson's spider orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spider-like appearance with a single, elongated flower that often displays a vivid red or orange hue. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats, such as grasslands and woodlands. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by thynnid wasps, which are attracted to the orchid's unique scent and coloration.

This variety is part of the Caladenia genus, which is known for its diverse and often intricate floral structures.
