Catha buxifolia

Catha buxifolia, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its stimulating effects due to the presence of cathinone and cathine, alkaloids found in its leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also known by various common names such as khat and is used in traditional practices across its native regions.
