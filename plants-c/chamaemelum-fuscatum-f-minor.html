Chamaemelum fuscatum f. minor

Chamaemelum fuscatum f. minor, commonly known as the lesser fumitory, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, often appearing in pairs along the stem.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller size and more delicate appearance compared to the typical form of Chamaemelum fuscatum.
