Crucifera pannonica

Crucifera pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
