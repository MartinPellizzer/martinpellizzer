Crataegus leioclada

Crataegus leioclada, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
