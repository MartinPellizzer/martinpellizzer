Columnea cristata

Columnea cristata, commonly known as the cristate flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Columnea cristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
