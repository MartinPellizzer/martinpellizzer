Coccothrinax inaguensis

Coccothrinax inaguensis, commonly known as the Inagua palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Bahamas and is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit. The plant features long, pinnate leaves that are often used for thatching and other traditional purposes. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, demonstrating a high degree of salt tolerance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
