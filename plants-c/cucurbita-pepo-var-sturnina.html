Cucurbita pepo var. sturnina

Cucurbita pepo var. sturnina, commonly known as the yellow crookneck squash, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellowish-green fruit with a slight curve at the end. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible young fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
