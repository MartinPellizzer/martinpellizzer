Crocus variegatus f. leptanthus

Crocus variegatus f. leptanthus, commonly known as the slender crocus, is a botanical variant of the variegated crocus.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and delicate, early spring blooms. The flowers typically feature a white or pale yellow perianth with a distinctive purple or violet center. This species is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
