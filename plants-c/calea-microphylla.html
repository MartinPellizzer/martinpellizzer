Calea microphylla

Calea microphylla, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, waxy flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

Calea microphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
