Cassia shaferi

Cassia shaferi, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Cassia shaferi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.
