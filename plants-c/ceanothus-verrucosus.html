Ceanothus verrucosus

Ceanothus verrucosus, commonly known as the woolly ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus verrucosus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
