Carex dubia

Carex dubia, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland and marsh environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex dubia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
