Capparis erythrocarpos var. rosea

Capparis erythrocarpos var. rosea, commonly known as the rose coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
