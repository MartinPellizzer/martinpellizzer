Capparis viridis

Capparis viridis, commonly known as green caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, yellow flowers and edible, green buds that are used in culinary applications. The plant has a distinctive scent and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and gastronomic purposes.

The fruit is a small, capsule that contains several seeds.
