Cistus creticus subsp. eriocephalus

Cistus creticus subsp.

eriocephalus, commonly known as the Cretan rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
