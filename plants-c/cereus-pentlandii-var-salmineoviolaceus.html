Cereus pentlandii var. salmineoviolaceus

Cereus pentlandii var. salmineoviolaceus, commonly known as the Purple-veined Cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
