Convolvulus racemosus

Convolvulus racemosus, commonly known as the racemose bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems that climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

Native to southern Africa, it is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
