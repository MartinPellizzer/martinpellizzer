Codonocalyx lanatus

Codonocalyx lanatus, commonly known as the hairy urd or the Indian bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and as a leguminous crop.

Botanically, it is notable for its compound leaves and the presence of nitrogen-fixing nodules on its roots.
