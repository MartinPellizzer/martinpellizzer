Cinnamomum oblongifolium

Cinnamomum oblongifolium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum oblongifolium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and aromatic bark. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.
