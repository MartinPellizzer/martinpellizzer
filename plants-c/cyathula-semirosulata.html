Cyathula semirosulata

Cyathula semirosulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
