Crataegus brainerdii var. asperifolia

Crataegus brainerdii var. asperifolia, commonly known as the brainerdii hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated margins, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in native habitats.
