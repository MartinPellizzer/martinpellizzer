Comolia angustifolia

Comolia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf comolia, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It is often found in the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region.

Comolia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
