Crotalaria malaissei

Crotalaria malaissei, commonly known as the Malayan crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodland or disturbed soils. It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.

Its seeds contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
