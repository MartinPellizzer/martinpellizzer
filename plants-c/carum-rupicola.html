Carum rupicola

Carum rupicola, commonly known as mountain caraway, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, finely divided leaves, and small white or greenish flowers. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing at high altitudes.
