Cornus officinalis var. koreana

Cornus officinalis var. koreana, commonly known as Korean cornel, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
