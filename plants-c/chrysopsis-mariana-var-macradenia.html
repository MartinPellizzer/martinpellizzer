Chrysopsis mariana var. macradenia

Chrysopsis mariana var. macradenia, commonly known as the Macradenia chrysopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams.
