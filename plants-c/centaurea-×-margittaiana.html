Centaurea × margittaiana

Centaurea × margittaiana, commonly known as the Margitta hybrid, is a hybrid species within the genus Centaurea, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts, which are typical features of the Centaurea genus. The plant is perennial and often grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense, conical inflorescences, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for horticultural use.
