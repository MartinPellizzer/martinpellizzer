Costus acanthocephalus

Costus acanthocephalus, commonly known as the spiked costus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and other aromatic plants. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
