Cactus karwinskianus

Cactus karwinskianus, commonly known as the Karwin's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines and sometimes adorned with small, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and xeriscaping.
