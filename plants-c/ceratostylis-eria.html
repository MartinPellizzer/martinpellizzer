Ceratostylis eria

Ceratostylis eria, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, and it often forms dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
