Caralluma caudata var. chibensis

Caralluma caudata var. chibensis, commonly known as the Chibensis prickly pear, is a succulent plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped stems and clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its fruit, which resembles a small pear, is edible and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and resilience.
