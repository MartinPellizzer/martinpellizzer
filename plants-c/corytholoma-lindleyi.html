Corytholoma lindleyi

Corytholoma lindleyi, commonly known as the Lepidodendron, is a type of ancient tree-like plant that thrived during the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its tall, trunk-like structure and its distinctive, scale-like leaves. This plant is part of the Lepidodendron family and is often found in fossil records due to its widespread presence in ancient swamp forests. Corytholoma lindleyi played a significant role in the formation of coal deposits through its rapid growth and high carbon content.
