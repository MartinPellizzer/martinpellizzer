Coffea canephora var. maclaudii

Coffea canephora var. maclaudii, commonly known as the Maclaudii coffee.

It is a variant of the robusta coffee species, characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, oval-shaped red berries, each containing two seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is less commonly cultivated than other coffee types but is valued for its unique flavor profile.
