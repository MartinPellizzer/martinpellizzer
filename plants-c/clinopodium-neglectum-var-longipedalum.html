Clinopodium neglectum var. longipedalum

Clinopodium neglectum var. longipedalum, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct, aromatic scent. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

The variety longipedalum is distinguished by its longer pedicels compared to the typical form, contributing to its unique appearance.
