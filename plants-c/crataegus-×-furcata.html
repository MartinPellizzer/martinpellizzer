Crataegus × furcata

Crataegus × furcata, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata, resulting in a plant with a distinctive appearance. The species features thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for wildlife.

Crataegus × furcata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
