Cyperus alba-purpureus

Cyperus alba-purpureus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by water.

Cyperus alba-purpureus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
