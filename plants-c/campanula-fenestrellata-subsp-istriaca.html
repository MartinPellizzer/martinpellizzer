Campanula fenestrellata subsp. istriaca

Campanula fenestrellata subsp.

istriaca, commonly known as the Istrian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
