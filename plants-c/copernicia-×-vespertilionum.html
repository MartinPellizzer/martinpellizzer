Copernicia × vespertilionum

Copernicia × vespertilionum, commonly known as the bat palm, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Copernicia cerifera and Copernicia melchiori.

It is a small, slow-growing palm native to the coastal regions of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, feather-like fronds that form a compact, umbrella-shaped crown. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The bat palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts due to its fibrous leaves.
