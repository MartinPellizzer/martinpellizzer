Cerastium montioides

Cerastium montioides, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Cerastium montioides thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
