Cassia neglecta var. entreriana

Cassia neglecta var. entreriana, commonly known as the Argentine cassia, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are yellow and clustered, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.
