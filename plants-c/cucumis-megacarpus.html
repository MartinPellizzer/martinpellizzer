Cucumis megacarpus

Cucumis megacarpus, commonly known as the giant cucumber, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is a vine that produces flowers and fruits in a similar manner to other cucurbits. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the fruit has a thick, edible rind with a mild, cucumber-like flavor.

This species is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
