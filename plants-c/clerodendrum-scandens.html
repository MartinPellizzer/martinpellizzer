Clerodendrum scandens

Clerodendrum scandens, commonly known as the climbing clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a vigorous climber with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. These flowers are followed by distinctive, spiny, egg-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
