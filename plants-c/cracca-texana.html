Cracca texana

Cracca texana, commonly known as Texas cracca, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cracca texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
