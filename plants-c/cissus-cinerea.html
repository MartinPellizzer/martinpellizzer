Cissus cinerea

Cissus cinerea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in loose inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread easily.

Cissus cinerea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
