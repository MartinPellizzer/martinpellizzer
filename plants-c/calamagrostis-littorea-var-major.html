Calamagrostis littorea var. major

Calamagrostis littorea var. major, commonly known as the sea reed, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in brackish or saltwater environments, often found in marshes and tidal zones. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, fluffy panicles of flowers in late summer. It is well adapted to withstand salt spray and fluctuating water levels.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
