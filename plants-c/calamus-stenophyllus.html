Calamus stenophyllus

Calamus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 10 cm wide. It grows in wetlands and swampy areas, often forming dense stands.

The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of many palms.
