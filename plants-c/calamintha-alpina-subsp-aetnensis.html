Calamintha alpina subsp. aetnensis

Calamintha alpina subsp.

aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna germander, is a perennial herb native to the volcanic regions of Sicily. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
