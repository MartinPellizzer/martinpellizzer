Chrysophyllum akusae

Chrysophyllum akusae, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow mango, is a small evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellow, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction.
