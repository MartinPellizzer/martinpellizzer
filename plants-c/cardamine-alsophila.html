Cardamine alsophila

Cardamine alsophila, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white or greenish flowers. Native to mountainous regions in Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a short growing season and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
