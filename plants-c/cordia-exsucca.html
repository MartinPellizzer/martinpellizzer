Cordia exsucca

Cordia exsucca, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and thick, waxy leaves.

Cordia exsucca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
