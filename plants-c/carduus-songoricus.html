Carduus songoricus

Carduus songoricus, commonly known as the Songor thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, appearing in spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
