Chusquea rugoloana

Chusquea rugoloana, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with a fine layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
