Clematis orientalis var. longicaudata

Clematis orientalis var. longicaudata, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a decorative element in landscapes.
