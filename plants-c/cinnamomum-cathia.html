Cinnamomum cathia

Cinnamomum cathia, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or Kalamansan, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and emit a mild cinnamon scent when crushed.

The bark, which is the primary source of its commercial value, contains essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
