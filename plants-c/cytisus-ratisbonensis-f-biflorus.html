Cytisus ratisbonensis f. biflorus

Cytisus ratisbonensis f. biflorus, commonly known as the Ratisbon broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers and is native to southern Europe. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its foliage consists of small, trifoliate leaves, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
