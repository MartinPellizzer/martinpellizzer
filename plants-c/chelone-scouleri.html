Chelone scouleri

Chelone scouleri, commonly known as the Canadian twinflower, is a perennial herb native to the western regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves and its clusters of pale pink to white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental members.

Chelone scouleri is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic appeal in native plant gardens.
