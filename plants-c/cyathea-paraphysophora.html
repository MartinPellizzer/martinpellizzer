Cyathea paraphysophora

Cyathea paraphysophora, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, pinnate fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to grow in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking feature in tropical landscapes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
