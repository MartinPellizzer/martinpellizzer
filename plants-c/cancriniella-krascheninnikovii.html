Cancriniella krascheninnikovii

Cancriniella krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in steppe and desert environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

The species is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to stabilize sandy soils through its extensive root system.
