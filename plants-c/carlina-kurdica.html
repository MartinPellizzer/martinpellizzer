Carlina kurdica

Carlina kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Carlina kurdica is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
