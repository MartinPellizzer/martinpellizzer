Conostomium gazense

Conostomium gazense, commonly known as the Zanzibar waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It features a distinctive whorl of leaves that radiate from a central stem, giving it a wheel-like appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, brackish waters and is often found in estuaries and lagoons. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its saline environment.

Conostomium gazense plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
