Cissus rubromarginata

Cissus rubromarginata, commonly known as the red-edged jade vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cascading inflorescences that feature bright red bracts surrounding small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture with a slight reddish margin. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

This plant is popular in ornamental horticulture for its unique and colorful appearance.
