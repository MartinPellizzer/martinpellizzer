Cuscuta megalantha

Cuscuta megalantha, commonly known as the giant dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly specialized structure for attaching to host plants, using haustoria to penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant is native to parts of the Americas and can be found on a variety of host species, including shrubs and trees. Its life cycle involves producing seeds that can remain viable in the soil for many years.
