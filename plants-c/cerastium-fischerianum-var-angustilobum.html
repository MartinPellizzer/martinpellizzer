Cerastium fischerianum var. angustilobum

Cerastium fischerianum var. angustilobum, commonly known as narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pink, forming in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
