Campanula lobelioides

Campanula lobelioides, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 3 feet, often forming dense clusters.

Campanula lobelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
