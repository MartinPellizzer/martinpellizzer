Cordia martii

Cordia martii, commonly known as the red cedar or Mexican cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other tropical hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Cordia martii is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making, and it also has traditional medicinal uses.
