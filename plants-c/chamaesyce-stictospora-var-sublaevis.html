Chamaesyce stictospora var. sublaevis

Chamaesyce stictospora var. sublaevis, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a characteristic plant of desert ecosystems.
