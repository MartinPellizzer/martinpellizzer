Chelone rosea

Chelone rosea, commonly known as the white hound's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, tongue-like shape. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Chelone rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
