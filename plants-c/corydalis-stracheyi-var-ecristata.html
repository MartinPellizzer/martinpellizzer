Corydalis stracheyi var. ecristata

Corydalis stracheyi var. ecristata, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine and woodland habitats.
