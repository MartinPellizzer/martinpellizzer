Cyperus scabricaulis

Cyperus scabricaulis, commonly known as rough-stemmed sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it forms dense clusters. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus scabricaulis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
