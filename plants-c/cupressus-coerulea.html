Cupressus coerulea

Cupressus coerulea, commonly known as the Blue Atlas Cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which is dense and needle-like, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and as a specimen plant.
