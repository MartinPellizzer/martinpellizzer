Clematis douglasii f. pulsatilloides

Clematis douglasii f. pulsatilloides, commonly known as the Douglas clematis, is a compact shrub-like vine native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability in gardens.
