Cymbopogon chevalieri

Cymbopogon chevalieri, commonly known as citron grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Citron grass is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional and modern applications.
