Carex monotropa

Carex monotropa, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Carex monotropa is often found in association with other shade-tolerant species, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
