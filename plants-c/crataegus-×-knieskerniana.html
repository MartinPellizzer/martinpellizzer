Crataegus × knieskerniana

Crataegus × knieskerniana, commonly known as the Knieskern hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping due to its attractive spring blooms and autumn foliage.
