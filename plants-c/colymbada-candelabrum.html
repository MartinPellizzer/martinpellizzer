Colymbada candelabrum

Colymbada candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick lily, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, candle-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a dramatic visual effect in gardens.

Colymbada candelabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
