Colchicum ninae

Colchicum ninae, commonly known as the dwarf autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm, which serves as its primary storage organ. Colchicum ninae is part of the Colchicaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to rock gardens and woodland settings.
