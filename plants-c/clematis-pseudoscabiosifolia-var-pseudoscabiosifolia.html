Clematis pseudoscabiosifolia var. pseudoscabiosifolia

Clematis pseudoscabiosifolia var. pseudoscabiosifolia, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or trellises. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
