Corythea

Corythea, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. Corythea is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
