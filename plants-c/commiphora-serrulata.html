Commiphora serrulata

Commiphora serrulata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed.
