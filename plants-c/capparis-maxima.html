Capparis maxima

Capparis maxima, commonly known as the large-flowered caper, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are often used in cooking. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Capparis maxima is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
