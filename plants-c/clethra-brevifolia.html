Clethra brevifolia

Clethra brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers. Clethra brevifolia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
