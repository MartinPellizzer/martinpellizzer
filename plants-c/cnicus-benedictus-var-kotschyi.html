Cnicus benedictus var. kotschyi

Cnicus benedictus var. kotschyi, commonly known as the Kashmir thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are generally grayish-green with a woolly texture, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
