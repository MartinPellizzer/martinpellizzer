Chelonanthera clypeata

Chelonanthera clypeata, commonly known as the shield-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
