Carduus meonanthus var. ammophilus

Carduus meonanthus var. ammophilus, commonly known as the Ammophila thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize sand dunes, making it ecologically significant in coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, windy conditions typical of its habitat.
