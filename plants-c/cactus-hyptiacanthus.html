Cactus hyptiacanthus

Cactus hyptiacanthus, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often resembling a ghostly silhouette against the desert landscape. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Despite its delicate appearance, the ghost cactus is highly resilient, capable of surviving extreme drought conditions.
