Cucurbita pyridaris

Cucurbita pyridaris, commonly known as the bitter gourd or karela, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces long, ridged, and typically bitter fruits. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is used in various cuisines, particularly in South Asian and Southeast Asian countries.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content and medicinal properties.
