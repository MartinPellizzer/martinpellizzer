Chamaecrista souzana

Chamaecrista souzana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
