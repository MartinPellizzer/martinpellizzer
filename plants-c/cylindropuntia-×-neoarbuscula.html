Cylindropuntia × neoarbuscula

Cylindropuntia × neoarbuscula, commonly known as the rainbow cactus, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless columnar cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems and vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping due to its striking appearance and low water requirements.
