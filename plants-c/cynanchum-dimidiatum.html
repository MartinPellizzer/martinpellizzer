Cynanchum dimidiatum

Cynanchum dimidiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which contains latex. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Cynanchum dimidiatum is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
