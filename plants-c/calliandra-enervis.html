Calliandra enervis

Calliandra enervis, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink or red inflorescences that resemble cascading showers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
