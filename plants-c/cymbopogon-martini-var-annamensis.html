Cymbopogon martini var. annamensis

Cymbopogon martini var. annamensis, commonly known as Vietnamese lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong citrus scent. The plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
