Cirrhopetalum zygoglossum

Cirrhopetalum zygoglossum, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color. Its leaves are broad and lanceolate, often with a waxy surface.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
