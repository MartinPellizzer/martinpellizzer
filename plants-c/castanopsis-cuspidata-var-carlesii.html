Castanopsis cuspidata var. carlesii

Castanopsis cuspidata var. carlesii, commonly known as the Formosan tanoak, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, spiky nuts enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
