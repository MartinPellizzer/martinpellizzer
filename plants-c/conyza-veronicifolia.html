Conyza veronicifolia

Conyza veronicifolia, commonly known as the blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, blue to purple composite flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Conyza veronicifolia is known for its ability to spread rapidly through wind-dispersed seeds.
