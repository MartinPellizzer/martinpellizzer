Cestrum macbridei

Cestrum macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Its berries, which are usually red or orange when mature, contain toxic compounds and should not be consumed by humans or animals.
