Coryphantha cubensis

Coryphantha cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban cactus or the golden cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Cuba.

It is a small, globular plant with a short, thick stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is adapted to arid environments, with succulent leaves that store water.

Coryphantha cubensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
