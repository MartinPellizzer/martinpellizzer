Campanula leptosiphon

Campanula leptosiphon, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula leptosiphon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
