Cytisus austriacus var. albus

Cytisus austriacus var. albus, commonly known as the white Austrian broom, is a flowering shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens despite its limited distribution in the wild.
