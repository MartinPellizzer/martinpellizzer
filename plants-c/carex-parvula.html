Carex parvula

Carex parvula, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a small perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland environments. Carex parvula has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
