Cereus denudatus

Cereus denudatus, commonly known as the naked cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a ribbed stem with prominent spines and produces large, showy white flowers at night. Its flowers are usually short-lived, opening only briefly before closing.

Cereus denudatus is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water requirements.
