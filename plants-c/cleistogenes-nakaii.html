Cleistogenes nakaii

Cleistogenes nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or grassy areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Cleistogenes nakaii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
