Crataegus prona

Crataegus prona, commonly known as the prickly pear or dog rose, is a species of hawthorn native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant features thorny branches and produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the autumn.

The botanical characteristics of Crataegus prona make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
