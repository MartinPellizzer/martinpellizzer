Cochlearia kamtschatica

Cochlearia kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatka cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features small, white, cruciform flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Cochlearia kamtschatica is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates.
