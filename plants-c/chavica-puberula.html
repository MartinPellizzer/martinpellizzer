Chavica puberula

Chavica puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf chavica, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Chavica puberula is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
