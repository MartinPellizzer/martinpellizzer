Clerodendrum ramosissimum

Clerodendrum ramosissimum, commonly known as the fragrant clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its branched, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and emit a mild, sweet scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
