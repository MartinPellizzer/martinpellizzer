Cheiranthus scaber

Cheiranthus scaber, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are rough to the touch, giving it the species name "scaber," meaning rough.

It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.
