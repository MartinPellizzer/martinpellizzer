Campanula rubra

Campanula rubra, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
