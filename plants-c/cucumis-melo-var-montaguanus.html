Cucumis melo var. montaguanus

Cucumis melo var. montaguanus, commonly known as the Mexican moonmelon, is a unique variant of the melon genus characterized by its elongated, pale green fruit with a rough, spiky rind.

The fruit typically measures around 20 to 30 centimeters in length and contains a soft, sweet, and aromatic flesh. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is native to Mexico and parts of Central America.
