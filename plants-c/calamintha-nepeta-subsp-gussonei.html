Calamintha nepeta subsp. gussonei

Calamintha nepeta subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as Sicilian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often wrinkled and have a distinct mint-like fragrance.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its subspecies status highlights its unique genetic and morphological characteristics compared to other forms of Calamintha nepeta.
