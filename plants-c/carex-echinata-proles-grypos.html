Carex echinata proles grypos

Carex echinata proles grypos, commonly known as the spiny sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind or water. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
