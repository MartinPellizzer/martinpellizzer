Chamaeraphis setosa f. biconvexa

Chamaeraphis setosa f. biconvexa, commonly known as the biconvex sea rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is distinguished by its doubly convex leaf shape, which gives it its specific epithet.
