Crypteronia paniculata var. leptostachys

Crypteronia paniculata var. leptostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked crypteronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
