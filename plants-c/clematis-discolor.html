Clematis discolor

Clematis discolor, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in early summer. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis discolor is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant, fragrant blooms.
