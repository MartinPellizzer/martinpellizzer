Campanula barbata f. pusilla

Campanula barbata f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
