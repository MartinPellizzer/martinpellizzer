Carex hordeistichos var. elongata

Carex hordeistichos var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated beak sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The varietal form elongata is distinguished by its longer, more slender leaves compared to the typical species.
