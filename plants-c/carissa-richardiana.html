Carissa richardiana

Carissa richardiana, commonly known as the Natal plumbago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
