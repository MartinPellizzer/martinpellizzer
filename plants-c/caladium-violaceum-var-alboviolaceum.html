Caladium violaceum var. alboviolaceum

Caladium violaceum var. alboviolaceum, commonly known as the white-veined caladium, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that display a striking combination of deep purple and white veins. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and container gardening.

Caladium violaceum var. alboviolaceum is typically propagated through rhizome division and is known for its ability to add vibrant color to indoor and outdoor spaces.
