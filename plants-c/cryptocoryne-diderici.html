Cryptocoryne diderici

Cryptocoryne diderici, commonly known as the Diderici waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged in freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and rivers. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, which help stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Cryptocoryne diderici is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive under moderate lighting conditions.
