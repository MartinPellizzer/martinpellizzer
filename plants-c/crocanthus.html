Crocanthus

Crocanthus, commonly known as the golden crown or yellow bell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Crocanthus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
