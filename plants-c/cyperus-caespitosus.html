Cyperus caespitosus

Cyperus caespitosus, commonly known as spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a common component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Cyperus caespitosus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
