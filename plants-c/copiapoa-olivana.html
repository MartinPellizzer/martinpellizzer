Copiapoa olivana

Copiapoa olivana, commonly known as the Olivana cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive greenish-gray color and a spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall and high sunlight exposure.

Copiapoa olivana is often grown as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
