Calonemorchis reticulata

Calonemorchis reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

The orchid's intricate floral structure includes a spurred lip, which is a distinguishing feature of many orchid species.
