Calligonum palibinii

Calligonum palibinii, commonly known as the Palibinii's tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high salinity and drought.

Calligonum palibinii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
