Crotalaria lisowskii

Crotalaria lisowskii, commonly known as the Lisowskii's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers in raceme clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

Crotalaria lisowskii is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
