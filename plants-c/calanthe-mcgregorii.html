Calanthe mcgregorii

Calanthe mcgregorii, commonly known as the McGregor's calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy rhizomes and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, and its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems. It prefers shaded, moist habitats with well-drained soil, often growing in forest understories.

Calanthe mcgregorii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
