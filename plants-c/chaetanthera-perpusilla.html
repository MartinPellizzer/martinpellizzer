Chaetanthera perpusilla

Chaetanthera perpusilla, commonly known as the small hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, often with a faint fragrance. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
