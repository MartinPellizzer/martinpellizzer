Cousinia subtilis

Cousinia subtilis, commonly known as the slender cushion gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Cousinia subtilis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
