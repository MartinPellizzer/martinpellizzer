Cistus × glaucifolius

Cistus × glaucifolius, commonly known as the purple heath, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Additionally, it has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
