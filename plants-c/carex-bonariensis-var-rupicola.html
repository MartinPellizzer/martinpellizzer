Carex bonariensis var. rupicola

Carex bonariensis var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock carex, is a perennial sedge native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers in the summer, which are followed by triangular, grass-like seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
