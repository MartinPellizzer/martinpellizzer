Cocculus nudiflorus

Cocculus nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower moonseed, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are usually hidden among the foliage, giving it its "nakedflower" common name. Its fruits are distinctive, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Cocculus nudiflorus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
