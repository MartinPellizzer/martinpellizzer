Chenopodium album var. lanceolatum

Chenopodium album var. lanceolatum, commonly known as narrowleaf lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by pale green to yellowish seed capsules. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed due to its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
