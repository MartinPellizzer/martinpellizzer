Carex novae-selandiae

Carex novae-selandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and streamside habitats.

Carex novae-selandiae plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
