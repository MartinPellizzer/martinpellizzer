Crataegus pringlei var. lobulata

Crataegus pringlei var. lobulata, commonly known as the lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and open slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
