Campanula lanceolata var. hirsuta

Campanula lanceolata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to purple in color. The leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "hirsuta" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
