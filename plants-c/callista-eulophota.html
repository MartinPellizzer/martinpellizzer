Callista eulophota

Callista eulophota, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Callista eulophota is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
