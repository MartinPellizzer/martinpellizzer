Clinopodium coreanum

Clinopodium coreanum, commonly known as Korean oregano, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, opposite leaves with a minty scent. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink or purple in color.

Clinopodium coreanum is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
