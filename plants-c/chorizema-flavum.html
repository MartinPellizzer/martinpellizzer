Chorizema flavum

Chorizema flavum, commonly known as the yellow chorizema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Chorizema flavum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
