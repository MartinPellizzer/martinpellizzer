Cirsium salmanticum

Cirsium salmanticum, commonly known as the Salmantic thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its spiny leaves and showy purple flowers make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
