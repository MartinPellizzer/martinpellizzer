Commiphora sphaerocarpa

Commiphora sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves. It produces a yellowish resin that has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The fruit is spherical and contains a single seed, making it distinctive in appearance.
