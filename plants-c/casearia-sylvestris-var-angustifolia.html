Casearia sylvestris var. angustifolia

Casearia sylvestris var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features simple, elliptic to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural use in subtropical climates.
