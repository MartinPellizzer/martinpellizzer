Commiphora ornifolia var. glabra

Commiphora ornifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed myrrh tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with a dense canopy and a preference for tropical or subtropical climates.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
