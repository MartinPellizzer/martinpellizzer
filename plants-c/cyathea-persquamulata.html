Cyathea persquamulata

Cyathea persquamulata, commonly known as the squamulate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that form a broad, umbrella-like shape, contributing to its ornamental value. Its fronds are covered with small, scale-like structures, giving it the common name "squamulate.".

Cyathea persquamulata is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
