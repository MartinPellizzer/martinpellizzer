Carex megalandra

Carex megalandra, commonly known as the giant marsh sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex megalandra plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
