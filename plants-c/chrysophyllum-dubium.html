Chrysophyllum dubium

Chrysophyllum dubium, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellow or orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, though it is less commercially significant compared to other members of its genus.
