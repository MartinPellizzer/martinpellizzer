Canavalia centralis

Canavalia centralis, commonly known as the central legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is often used for forage or traditional medicine.

Canavalia centralis is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it an important species in ecological restoration.
