Chaetostoma hexapetalum

Chaetostoma hexapetalum, commonly known as the six-petaled water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface, typically in the morning. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
