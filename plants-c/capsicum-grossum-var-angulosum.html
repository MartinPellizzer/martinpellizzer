Capsicum grossum var. angulosum

Capsicum grossum var. angulosum, commonly known as the angular capsicum, is a variant of the wild pepper species native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, angular fruits that typically range in color from green to red when mature. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, serrated edge. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and potential use in both ornamental and culinary applications.
