Carduus chamaecephalus

Carduus chamaecephalus, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and deeply lobed, serrated leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
