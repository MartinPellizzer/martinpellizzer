Clusia polystigma

Clusia polystigma, commonly known as the broadleaf glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Clusia polystigma is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
