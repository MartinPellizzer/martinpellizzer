Calystegia japonica f. albiflora

Calystegia japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese morning glory, is a variant of the species native to East Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, distinguishing it from the more common purple-flowered varieties. It blooms in the summer months and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun.
