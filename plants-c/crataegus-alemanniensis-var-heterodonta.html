Crataegus alemanniensis var. heterodonta

Crataegus alemanniensis var. heterodonta, commonly known as the Alemanni cherry or the heterodonta hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered astringent.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
