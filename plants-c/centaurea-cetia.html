Centaurea cetia

Centaurea cetia, commonly known as the Cetian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves, which are typical of the genus Centaurea. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Centaurea cetia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
