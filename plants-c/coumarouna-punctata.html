Coumarouna punctata

Coumarouna punctata, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
