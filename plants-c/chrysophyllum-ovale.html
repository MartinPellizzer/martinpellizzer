Chrysophyllum ovale

Chrysophyllum ovale, commonly known as the oval star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a large, oval-shaped fruit with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, white, sponge-like pulp. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
