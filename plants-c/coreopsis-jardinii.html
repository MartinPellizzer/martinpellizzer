Coreopsis jardinii

Coreopsis jardinii, commonly known as the garden coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds ecological value to its habitat.
