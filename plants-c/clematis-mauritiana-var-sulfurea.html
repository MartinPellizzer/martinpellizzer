Clematis mauritiana var. sulfurea

Clematis mauritiana var. sulfurea, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
