Cassia artemisiifolia

Cassia artemisiifolia, commonly known as sicklepod, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into distinctive, spiny pods. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny conditions. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a shallow root system.

Cassia artemisiifolia is sometimes considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
