Cyperus aster var. andringitrensis

Cyperus aster var. andringitrensis, commonly known as the Andringitra sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowering spikes that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and restricted geographic distribution.
