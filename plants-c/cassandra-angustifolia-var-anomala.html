Cassandra angustifolia var. anomala

Cassandra angustifolia var. anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassandra, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity with limited horticultural use.
