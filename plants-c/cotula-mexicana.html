Cotula mexicana

Cotula mexicana, commonly known as Mexican daisies, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a daisy-like pattern, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. Cotula mexicana is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
