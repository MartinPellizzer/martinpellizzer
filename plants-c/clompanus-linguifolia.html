Clompanus linguifolia

Clompanus linguifolia, commonly known as the tongueleaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its elongated, tongue-like leaves.

Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Clompanus linguifolia is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
