Cistus atlanticus

Cistus atlanticus, commonly known as Atlantic rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of western Europe.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and aromatic, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Cistus atlanticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
