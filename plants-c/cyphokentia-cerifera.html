Cyphokentia cerifera

Cyphokentia cerifera, commonly known as the candle palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a dense crown of pinnate leaves. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Cyphokentia cerifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
