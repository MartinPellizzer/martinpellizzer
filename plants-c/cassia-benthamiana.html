Cassia benthamiana

Cassia benthamiana, commonly known as the bentham's cassia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cassia benthamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
