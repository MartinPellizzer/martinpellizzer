Carduus tenuiflorus subsp. sardous

Carduus tenuiflorus subsp.

sardous, commonly known as the Sardinian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or arid soils and features a tall, spiny stem with lobed leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spiky, spherical seed heads. It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
