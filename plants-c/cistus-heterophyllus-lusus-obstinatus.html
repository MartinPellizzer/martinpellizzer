Cistus heterophyllus lusus obstinatus

Cistus heterophyllus lusus obstinatus, commonly known as the Portuguese rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it a hardy ornamental plant.
