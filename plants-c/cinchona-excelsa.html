Cinchona excelsa

Cinchona excelsa, commonly known as the Andean quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its production of alkaloids, particularly quinine, which has historically been used in the treatment of malaria. It thrives in humid, high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its medicinal significance, it is also cultivated in various tropical regions for commercial purposes.
