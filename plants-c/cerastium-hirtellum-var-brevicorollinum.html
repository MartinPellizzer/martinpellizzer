Cerastium hirtellum var. brevicorollinum

Cerastium hirtellum var. brevicorollinum, commonly known as the short-stalked mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
