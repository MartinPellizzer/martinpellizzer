Citrus medica subf. kizu

Citrus medica subf.

kizu, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or kizu mandarin, is a citrus species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, round, and often segmented fruits with a thin, smooth peel.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flesh and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
