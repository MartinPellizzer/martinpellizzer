Crinum declinatum

Crinum declinatum, commonly known as the drooping lily, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms, which appear in summer.
