Cracca cathartica

Cracca cathartica, commonly known as the yellow hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as cardiac glycosides.

Due to its toxicity, it is not recommended for cultivation in gardens or for medicinal use without professional guidance.
