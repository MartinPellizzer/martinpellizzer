Calceolaria chubutensis

Calceolaria chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color. The plant produces small, hairy leaves and thrives in rocky, arid environments. It is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Calceolaria chubutensis is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance in its region.
