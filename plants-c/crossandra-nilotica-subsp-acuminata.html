Crossandra nilotica subsp. acuminata

Crossandra nilotica subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
