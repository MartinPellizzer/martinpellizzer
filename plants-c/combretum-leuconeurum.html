Combretum leuconeurum

Combretum leuconeurum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and turn red or orange before falling. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
