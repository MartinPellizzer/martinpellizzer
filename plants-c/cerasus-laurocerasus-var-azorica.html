Cerasus laurocerasus var. azorica

Cerasus laurocerasus var. azorica, commonly known as the Azores cherry, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Azores Islands.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a glossy texture and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its ornamental value, with attractive red or black berries that appear in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
