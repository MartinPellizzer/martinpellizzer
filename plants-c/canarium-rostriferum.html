Canarium rostriferum

Canarium rostriferum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody shell. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.

The species is also known for its aromatic resins, which have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
