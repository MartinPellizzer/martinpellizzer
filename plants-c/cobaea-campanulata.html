Cobaea campanulata

Cobaea campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces showy, pendulous blooms that are often used in gardens for their ornamental value. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, adding a lush foliage effect.

Cobaea campanulata thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
