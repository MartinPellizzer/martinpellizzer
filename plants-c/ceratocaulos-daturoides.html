Ceratocaulos daturoides

Ceratocaulos daturoides, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a broad, umbrella-like canopy and can grow up to 30 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
