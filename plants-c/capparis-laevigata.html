Capparis laevigata

Capparis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Capparis laevigata is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
