Carum pimpinelloides

Carum pimpinelloides, commonly known as mountain caraway, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its aromatic seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their digestive and carminative properties.

Carum pimpinelloides is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
