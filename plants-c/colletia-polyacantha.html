Colletia polyacantha

Colletia polyacantha, commonly known as the many-thorned candelabra cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its adaptations include succulent stems for water storage and spines for protection against herbivores.

Colletia polyacantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
