Chrysodium zollingeri

Chrysodium zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its vibrant yellow to orange petals. It has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
