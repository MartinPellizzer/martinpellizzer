Conyza timorensis

Conyza timorensis, commonly known as Timor fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Conyza timorensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
