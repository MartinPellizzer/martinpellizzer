Caladenia magniclavata

Caladenia magniclavata, commonly known as the large-veined spider-orchid, is an Australian orchid species native to the south-eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with long, slender petals and a distinctive, narrow labellum. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually from late winter to early spring.

Caladenia magniclavata is a protected species due to habitat loss and is often found in conservation reserves.
