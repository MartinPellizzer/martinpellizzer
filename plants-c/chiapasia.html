Chiapasia

Chiapasia, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its fruits are fleshy berries containing several seeds.

Chiapasia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
