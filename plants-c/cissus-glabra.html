Cissus glabra

Cissus glabra, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cissus glabra is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
