Crinum ornatum var. insigne

Crinum ornatum var. insigne, commonly known as the showy crinum, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are narrow and often tinged with red at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
