Cyperus robustior

Cyperus robustior, commonly known as the broadleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Cyperus robustior thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, making it both a common wetland plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
