Centaurea cylindrocarpa

Centaurea cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking blue to purple conical flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea cylindrocarpa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
