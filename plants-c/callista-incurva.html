Callista incurva

Callista incurva, commonly known as the spreading bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
