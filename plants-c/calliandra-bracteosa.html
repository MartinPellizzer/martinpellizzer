Calliandra bracteosa

Calliandra bracteosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive branching pattern and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.
