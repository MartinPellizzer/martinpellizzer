Cyperus vaginatus

Cyperus vaginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems, with each spikelet containing several tiny flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus vaginatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native wetland habitats.
