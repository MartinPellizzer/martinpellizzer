Campanula fischeriana

Campanula fischeriana, commonly known as Fischer's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula fischeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
