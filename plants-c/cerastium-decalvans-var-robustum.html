Cerastium decalvans var. robustum

Cerastium decalvans var. robustum, commonly known as the robust mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
