Calamagrostis pappophorea

Calamagrostis pappophorea, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces feathery, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to spread in open, disturbed habitats. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis pappophorea is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
