Chamaedrys ovalifolia

Chamaedrys ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leafed chamaedrys, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
