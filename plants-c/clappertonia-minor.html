Clappertonia minor

Clappertonia minor, commonly known as the lesser clappertonia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and are borne in clusters near the base of the plant.

Clappertonia minor is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
