Cacalia harveyi

Cacalia harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Harvey's bluebell is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
