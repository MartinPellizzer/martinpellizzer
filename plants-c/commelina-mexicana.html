Commelina mexicana

Commelina mexicana, commonly known as Mexican spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features striking blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, often surrounded by two whorls of white or green bracts. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
