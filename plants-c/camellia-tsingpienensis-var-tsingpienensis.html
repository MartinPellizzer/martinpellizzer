Camellia tsingpienensis var. tsingpienensis

Camellia tsingpienensis var. tsingpienensis, commonly known as the Tsingpien camellia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces striking, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable addition to gardens during the colder months. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
