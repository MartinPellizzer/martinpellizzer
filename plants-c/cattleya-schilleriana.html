Cattleya schilleriana

Cattleya schilleriana, commonly known as the Schiller's Cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, and showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and violet. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya schilleriana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral displays.
