Capparis racemulosa

Capparis racemulosa, commonly known as the coastal caper, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

The fruit is a small, spherical capsule that contains several seeds.
