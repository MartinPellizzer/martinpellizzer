Chrysaspis erubescens

Chrysaspis erubescens, commonly known as the red velvet fern, is a unique plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from reddish to purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
