Carex glaucodea

Carex glaucodea, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas.

Carex glaucodea produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.
