Cicer arietinum var. nigrum

Cicer arietinum var. nigrum, commonly known as black chickpea, is a variant of the chickpea species native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant is an annual legume that produces pods containing small, dark-colored seeds used in various cuisines. The botanical features include trifoliate leaves, yellow flowers, and a robust root system that aids in nitrogen fixation. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds are rich in protein and are widely cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
