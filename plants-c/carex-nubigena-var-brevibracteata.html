Carex nubigena var. brevibracteata

Carex nubigena var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
