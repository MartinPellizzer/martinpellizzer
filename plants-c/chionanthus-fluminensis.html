Chionanthus fluminensis

Chionanthus fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble snowflakes, which bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Chionanthus fluminensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its elegant floral display.
