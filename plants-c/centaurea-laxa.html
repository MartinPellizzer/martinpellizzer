Centaurea laxa

Centaurea laxa, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale pink seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Centaurea laxa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
