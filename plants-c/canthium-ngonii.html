Canthium ngonii

Canthium ngonii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Canthium ngonii is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
