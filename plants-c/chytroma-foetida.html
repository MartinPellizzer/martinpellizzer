Chytroma foetida

Chytroma foetida, commonly known as the stinking sprout, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a distinctive, often unpleasant odor, which is a key feature in its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique scent and its role in local ecosystems.
