Crotalaria incana subsp. purpurascens

Crotalaria incana subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its lanceolate, often hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow, spurred flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, inflated pod, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

It is also noted for containing toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
