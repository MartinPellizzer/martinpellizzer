Cleomella oocarpa

Cleomella oocarpa, commonly known as the yellow cleome, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmate leaves and showy, clustered blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
