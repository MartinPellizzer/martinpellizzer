Clematis simensis

Clematis simensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian clematis, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or violet in color. Clematis simensis thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
