Cucumis microcarpus

Cucumis microcarpus, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its vines grow trailing or climbing.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a characteristic known as monoecious.
