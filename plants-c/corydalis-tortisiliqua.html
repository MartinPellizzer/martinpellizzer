Corydalis tortisiliqua

Corydalis tortisiliqua, commonly known as the rock corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Corydalis tortisiliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
