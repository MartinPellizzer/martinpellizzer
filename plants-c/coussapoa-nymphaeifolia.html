Coussapoa nymphaeifolia

Coussapoa nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, which open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are anchored in the muddy bottom of slow-moving water bodies.

Coussapoa nymphaeifolia plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
