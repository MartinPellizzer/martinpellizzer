Cyperus minutulus

Cyperus minutulus, commonly known as the minute flatsedge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous. Cyperus minutulus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish habitats.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
