Cyathea emilei

Cyathea emilei, commonly known as the Emile's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a distinctive tropical appearance. Cyathea emilei is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in humid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of Madagascar's diverse flora.
