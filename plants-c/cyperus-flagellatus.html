Cyperus flagellatus

Cyperus flagellatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyperus flagellatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in moist environments.
