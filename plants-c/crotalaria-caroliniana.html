Crotalaria caroliniana

Crotalaria caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces striking yellow-orange flowers with five petals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along creek banks.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it an invasive species in many regions.
