Commicarpus fallacissimus f. pilosus

Commicarpus fallacissimus f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy false mallow, is a variant of the Commicarpus fallacissimus species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to dry conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive coloration that aids in pollination.

This form is notable for its unique morphological features, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
