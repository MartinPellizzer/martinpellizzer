Cathea pulchella

Cathea pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful cathea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that collect water, creating a small pool for its insect larvae. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cathea pulchella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
