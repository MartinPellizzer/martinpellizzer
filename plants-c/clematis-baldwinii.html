Clematis baldwinii

Clematis baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis baldwinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
