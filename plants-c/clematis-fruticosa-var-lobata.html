Clematis fruticosa var. lobata

Clematis fruticosa var. lobata, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
