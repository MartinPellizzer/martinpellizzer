Cocculus sagittifolius

Cocculus sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its woody stems and persistent foliage make it a distinctive feature in woodland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
