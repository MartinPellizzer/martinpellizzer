Carex geihokuensis

Carex geihokuensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex geihokuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet areas.
