Convolvulus purpuratus var. solanensis

Convolvulus purpuratus var. solanensis, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
