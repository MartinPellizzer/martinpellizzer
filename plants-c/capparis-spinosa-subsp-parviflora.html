Capparis spinosa subsp. parviflora

Capparis spinosa subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
