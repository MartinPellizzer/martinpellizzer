Caryophyllus armeria

Caryophyllus armeria, commonly known as the common honesty, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits that split open to release seeds. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a symbol of sincerity in literature adds to its cultural significance.
