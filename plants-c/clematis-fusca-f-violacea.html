Clematis fusca f. violacea

Clematis fusca f. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered clematis, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail in garden settings.
