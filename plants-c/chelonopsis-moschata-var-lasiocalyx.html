Chelonopsis moschata var. lasiocalyx

Chelonopsis moschata var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is notable for its fruit, which is a fleshy, edible pod containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
