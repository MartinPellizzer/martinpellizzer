Clarkia williamsonii var. incerta

Clarkia williamsonii var. incerta, commonly known as the Williamson's clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is part of the evening primrose family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
