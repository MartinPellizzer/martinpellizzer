Cinnamomum splendens

Cinnamomum splendens, commonly known as the golden cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that contain a single seed, and its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum splendens is also notable for its ability to produce a unique, sweet-smelling resin.
