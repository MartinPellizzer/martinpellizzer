Cichorium endivia var. crispum

Cichorium endivia var. crispum, commonly known as frisee, is a leafy green vegetable characterized by its curly, pale green leaves.

It is a cultivar of the chicory plant, which is native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves, which are harvested before they become too bitter. It thrives in cool weather and is often cultivated in temperate climates.

The leaves are rich in nutrients and have a slightly bitter, nutty flavor.
