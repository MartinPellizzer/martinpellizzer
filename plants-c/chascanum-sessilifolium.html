Chascanum sessilifolium

Chascanum sessilifolium, commonly known as the Andean pepper or purple cornflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value and traditional use in local cultures make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
