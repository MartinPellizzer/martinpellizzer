Cassinia aureonitens

Cassinia aureonitens, commonly known as the golden button bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble buttons. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Cassinia aureonitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
