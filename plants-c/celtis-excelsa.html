Celtis excelsa

Celtis excelsa, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. Celtis excelsa is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in temperate climates.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common ornamental and shade tree in urban landscapes.
