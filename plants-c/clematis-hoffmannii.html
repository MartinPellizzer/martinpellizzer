Clematis hoffmannii

Clematis hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
