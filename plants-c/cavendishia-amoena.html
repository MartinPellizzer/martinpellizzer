Cavendishia amoena

Cavendishia amoena, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink and white petals, which resemble a slipper, giving it its common name. The plant features a dense, hairy root system that helps it thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cavendishia amoena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
