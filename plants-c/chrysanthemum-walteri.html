Chrysanthemum walteri

Chrysanthemum walteri, commonly known as Walter's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and orange.

Walter's chrysanthemum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
