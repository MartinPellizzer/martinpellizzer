Carruthersia brassii

Carruthersia brassii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, typically blooming in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Carruthersia brassii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
