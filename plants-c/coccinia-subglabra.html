Coccinia subglabra

Coccinia subglabra, commonly known as the smooth cucumber or Indian cheese fruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, yellow, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are slender and twining.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
