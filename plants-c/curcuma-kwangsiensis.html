Curcuma kwangsiensis

Curcuma kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant orange to yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are rich in curcuminoids, making it valuable in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various tropical and subtropical regions.
