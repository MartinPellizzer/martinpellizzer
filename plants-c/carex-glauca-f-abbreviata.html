Carex glauca f. abbreviata

Carex glauca f. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-leaved blue sedge, is a compact, clump-forming perennial plant native to North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, with a slightly curled appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This ornamental sedge is valued for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and its attractive foliage.
