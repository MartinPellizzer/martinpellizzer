Conohoria castaneifolia

Conohoria castaneifolia, commonly known as the chestnut-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues, especially along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Conohoria castaneifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
