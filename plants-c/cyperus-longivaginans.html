Cyperus longivaginans

Cyperus longivaginans, commonly known as the long sheath sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered, with leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like structure bearing small, three-lobed flowers.

Cyperus longivaginans plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
