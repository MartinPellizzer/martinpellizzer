Correa reflexa var. scabridula

Correa reflexa var. scabridula, commonly known as the rough-leaved correa, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
