Cucubalus divaricatus

Cucubalus divaricatus, commonly known as the spreading cucumber or wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmately lobed leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. The plant bears elongated, spiny fruits that resemble cucumbers, often containing a single, large seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
