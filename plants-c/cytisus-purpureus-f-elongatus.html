Cytisus purpureus f. elongatus

Cytisus purpureus f. elongatus, commonly known as the purple broom or elongated broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense thickets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is characterized by its elongated growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
