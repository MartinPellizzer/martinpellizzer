Cassia thyrsoidea

Cassia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Cassia thyrsoidea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
