Campylospermum occidentale

Campylospermum occidentale, commonly known as the western starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped yellow fruits that resemble the fruit of the genus Averrhoa. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and flowering patterns.
