Carex aquatilis var. watsonii

Carex aquatilis var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's water sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water.

Watson's water sedge plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
