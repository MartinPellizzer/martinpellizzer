Carum komarovii

Carum komarovii, commonly known as Siberian cumin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, finely divided leaves and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves and seeds contain essential oils with compounds like limonene and carvone, contributing to its distinctive scent.

Carum komarovii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
