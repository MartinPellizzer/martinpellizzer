Cereus lemairei

Cereus lemairei, commonly known as the Lemaire's torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Cereus lemairei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
