Citrus japonica var. fructu-elliptica

Citrus japonica var. fructu-elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic-fruited Japanese citrus, is a variant of the Japanese citrus tree characterized by its elliptical-shaped fruits.

It typically produces small to medium-sized citrus fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and arranged alternately along the branches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and suitable for container gardening in suitable climates.
