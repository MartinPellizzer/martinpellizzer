Celtis timorensis

Celtis timorensis, commonly known as the Timor hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Celtis timorensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
