Calliandra pervillei

Calliandra pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's calliandra, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calliandra pervillei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
