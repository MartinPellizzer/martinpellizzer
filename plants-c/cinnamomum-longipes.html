Cinnamomum longipes

Cinnamomum longipes, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Guangxi cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by egg-shaped, red berries that contain a single seed. Its bark is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties, often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also cultivated for its essential oils and as a source of timber.
