Cereus greggii var. cismontanus

Cereus greggii var. cismontanus, commonly known as the Cismontan Cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and produces large, white flowers at night. The plant has a dense, spiny covering that provides protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
