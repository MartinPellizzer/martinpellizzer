Condalia lycioides var. microphylla

Condalia lycioides var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved condalia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
