Crossandra infundibuliformis subsp. brachystachys

Crossandra infundibuliformis subsp.

brachystachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

The plant has glossy, dark green foliage and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season.
