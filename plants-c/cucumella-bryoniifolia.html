Cucumella bryoniifolia

Cucumella bryoniifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved cucumber, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble those of ivy. Its yellow flowers are followed by green, elongated fruits that mature to a pale yellow or orange color.

Cucumella bryoniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
