Campium × lanceum

Campium × lanceum, commonly known as the hybrid boxwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Campium americanum and Campium sulphuratum. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically a deep green color. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
