Ceratochilus oculatus

Ceratochilus oculatus, commonly known as the eye-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, ovate leaves that often have a prominent central vein and a swollen base. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose spike, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
