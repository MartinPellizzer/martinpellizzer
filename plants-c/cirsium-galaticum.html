Cirsium galaticum

Cirsium galaticum, commonly known as the Galatic thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, showy purple or pink flower heads. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and full sunlight. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that disperse its seeds through the wind.

Its robust structure and distinctive appearance make it a notable species within the Cirsium genus.
