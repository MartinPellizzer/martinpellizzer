Catasetum × splendens var. punctatissimum

Catasetum × splendens var. punctatissimum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences with large, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and contrasting markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive with adequate moisture and indirect light. Its flowers are pollinated by specific bees, which are attracted by the plant's unique structure and scent.

The varietal name "punctatissimum" refers to the distinct spotted or dotted patterns on its petals and sepals, which are highly ornamental.
