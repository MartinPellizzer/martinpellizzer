Cryptochloa strictiflora

Cryptochloa strictiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptochloa, is a unique member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
