Caloplectus macrophyllus

Caloplectus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that grows slowly over time. Its leaves are typically a deep green color and are arranged in a rosette pattern around the trunk.

Caloplectus macrophyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and eye-catching foliage.
