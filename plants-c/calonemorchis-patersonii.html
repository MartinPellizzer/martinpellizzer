Calonemorchis patersonii

Calonemorchis patersonii, commonly known as the Paterson's orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing clusters of fragrant, spurred orchid flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
