Citrus nidonari

Citrus nidonari, commonly known as the Japanese bitter orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Japan, characterized by its fragrant white flowers and oval, leathery leaves. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thin, bitter peel. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional Japanese medicine.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
