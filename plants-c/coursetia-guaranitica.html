Coursetia guaranitica

Coursetia guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian thornapple, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
