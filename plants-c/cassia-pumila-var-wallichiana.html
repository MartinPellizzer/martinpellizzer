Cassia pumila var. wallichiana

Cassia pumila var. wallichiana, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in long, drooping panicles, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.
