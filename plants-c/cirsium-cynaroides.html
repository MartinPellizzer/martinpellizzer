Cirsium cynaroides

Cirsium cynaroides, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Cirsium cynaroides is often used in traditional medicine and is considered an important component of local ecosystems.
