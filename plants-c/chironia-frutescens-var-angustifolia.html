Chironia frutescens var. angustifolia

Chironia frutescens var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chironia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
