Convolvulus hermanniae var. viridis

Convolvulus hermanniae var. viridis, commonly known as the green Hermann's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly fragrant scent. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its vines can grow up to several meters in length.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
