Chilita longicoma

Chilita longicoma, commonly known as the long-stemmed chilita, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, elongated stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Chilita longicoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
