Coronilla varia f. pedemontana

Coronilla varia f. pedemontana, commonly known as the Pedemontane Coronilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or limestone areas. It is a perennial species that produces pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
