Cupressus × notabilis

Cupressus × notabilis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cypress, is a hybrid conifer native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species for various climates.
