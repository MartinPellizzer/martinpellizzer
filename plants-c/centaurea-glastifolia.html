Centaurea glastifolia

Centaurea glastifolia, commonly known as the Glastifolia thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea glastifolia is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
