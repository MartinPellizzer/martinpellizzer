Cybianthus croatii

Cybianthus croatii, commonly known as the Croatian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Cybianthus croatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
