Caltha palustris var. stagnalis

Caltha palustris var. stagnalis, commonly known as the marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist or waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
