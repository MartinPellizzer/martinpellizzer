Carapa guyanensis

Carapa guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing oily seeds. Its seeds are valued for their high content of essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for making varnishes.

The plant is also important in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
