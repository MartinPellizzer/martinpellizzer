Cyperus bowmanni

Cyperus bowmanni, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Cyperus bowmanni is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
