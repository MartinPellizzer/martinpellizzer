Campanula filicaulis var. mairei

Campanula filicaulis var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
