orophila, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada water starwort, is a rare aquatic plant native to high-elevation lakes in the Sierra Nevada mountain range. It is characterized by its small, star-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter.

The plant thrives in cold, clear, oligotrophic waters and is often found in rocky, shallow lake beds. Its flowers are tiny and inconspicuous, typically appearing in late summer.
