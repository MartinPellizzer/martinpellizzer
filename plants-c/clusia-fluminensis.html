Clusia fluminensis

Clusia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Clusia fluminensis is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in warm, humid climates.
