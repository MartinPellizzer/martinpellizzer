Cyphostemma hypargyreum

Cyphostemma hypargyreum, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and waxy, silver-green leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
