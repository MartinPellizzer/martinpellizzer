Coriandrum radians

Coriandrum radians, commonly known as Chinese coriander or cilantro, is an annual herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used fresh in culinary applications. Its flowers are small and white or pale green, arranged in umbels.

The seeds, which are used in both food and medicine, have a distinctive citrusy aroma.
