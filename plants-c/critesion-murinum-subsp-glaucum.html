Critesion murinum subsp. glaucum

Critesion murinum subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the Glaucous Critesion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery, bluish-gray coating.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
