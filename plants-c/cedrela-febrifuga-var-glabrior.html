Cedrela febrifuga var. glabrior

Cedrela febrifuga var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth cedrela, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is native to regions of Central and South America.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional uses.
