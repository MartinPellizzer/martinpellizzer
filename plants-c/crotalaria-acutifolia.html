Crotalaria acutifolia

Crotalaria acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, spiny seed pods. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria acutifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
