Coprosma ochracea var. rockiana

Coprosma ochracea var. rockiana, commonly known as the rock coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from bright yellow to orange in color, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in rocky or coastal habitats and is well adapted to coastal conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
