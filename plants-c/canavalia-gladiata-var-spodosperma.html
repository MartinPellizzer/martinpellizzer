Canavalia gladiata var. spodosperma

Canavalia gladiata var. spodosperma, commonly known as the African bean or spider bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped pods. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The seeds are typically small, round, and can be used for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
