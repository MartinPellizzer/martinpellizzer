Chenopodium amurense

Chenopodium amurense, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds.

Chenopodium amurense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
