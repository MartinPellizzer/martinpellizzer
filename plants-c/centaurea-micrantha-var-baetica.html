Centaurea micrantha var. baetica

Centaurea micrantha var. baetica, commonly known as the Baetican dwarf knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, bright yellow, and surrounded by pale blue to pink bracts, forming a composite flower head.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
