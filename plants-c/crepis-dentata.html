Crepis dentata

Crepis dentata, commonly known as the serrated hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The seeds are small, dry, and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.
