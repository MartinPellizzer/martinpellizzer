Coursetia polyphylla var. acutifolia

Coursetia polyphylla var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf coursetia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, elliptical leaves that are typically pointed at the tips. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems and a potential candidate for xeriscaping.
