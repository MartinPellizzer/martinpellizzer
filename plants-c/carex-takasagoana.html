Carex takasagoana

Carex takasagoana, commonly known as the Takasago sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex takasagoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
