Cistus italicus

Cistus italicus, commonly known as the Italian rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Cistus italicus is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
