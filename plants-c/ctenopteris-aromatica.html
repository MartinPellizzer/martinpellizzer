Ctenopteris aromatica

Ctenopteris aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near water bodies. Its leaves emit a subtle aromatic scent, which is believed to be a natural defense mechanism.

Ctenopteris aromatica is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
