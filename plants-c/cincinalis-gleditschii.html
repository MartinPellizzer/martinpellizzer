Cincinalis gleditschii

Cincinalis gleditschii, commonly known as the Gleditsch's cincinalis, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in spike-like clusters. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a waxy leaf coating to reduce water loss.

Cincinalis gleditschii is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
