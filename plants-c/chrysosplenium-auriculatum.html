Chrysosplenium auriculatum

Chrysosplenium auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate chrysosplenium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
