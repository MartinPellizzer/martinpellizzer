Citrus medica subsp. limonum

Citrus medica subsp.

limonum, commonly known as the lemon, is a citrus species native to regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small evergreen tree with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers.

The fruit is characterized by its yellow rind and juicy, acidic pulp, which contains essential oils and various bioactive compounds. The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary, medicinal, and commercial applications.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
