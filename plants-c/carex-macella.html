Carex macella

Carex macella, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it often grows in moist or wet environments. Carex macella is found in a variety of habitats, including wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
