Callistemon salignus var. sieberi

Callistemon salignus var. sieberi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
