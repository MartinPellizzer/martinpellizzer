Carex meyeriana var. scabra

Carex meyeriana var. scabra, commonly known as the rough meyer sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
