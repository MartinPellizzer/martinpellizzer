Callistemon salignus var. viridiflorus

Callistemon salignus var. viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered bottlebrush, is a variant of the bottlebrush plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive greenish-white flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight reddish tinge, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

The variety is particularly noted for its unique foliage color, which sets it apart from other callistemon species.
