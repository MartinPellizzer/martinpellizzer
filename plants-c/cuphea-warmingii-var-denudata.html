Cuphea warmingii var. denudata

Cuphea warmingii var. denudata, commonly known as the naked cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
