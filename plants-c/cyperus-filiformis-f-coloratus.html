Cyperus filiformis f. coloratus

Cyperus filiformis f. coloratus, commonly known as the red spike grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is distinguished by its vibrant red or purple bracts that contrast with its slender, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in water gardens or as a landscape accent.

Due to its compact size and colorful foliage, it is a popular choice for container gardening and bog gardens.
