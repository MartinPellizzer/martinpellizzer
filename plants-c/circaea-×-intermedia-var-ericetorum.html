Circaea × intermedia var. ericetorum

Circaea × intermedia var. ericetorum, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in woodland or heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
