Cirsium yoshinoi

Cirsium yoshinoi, commonly known as Yoshino thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage is covered in stiff, prickly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
