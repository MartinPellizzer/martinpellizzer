Clinopodium alpinum subsp. orontium

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

orontium, commonly known as alpine oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its medicinal properties have been recognized for centuries, particularly for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.

The subspecies is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to its strong, peppery scent and therapeutic potential.
