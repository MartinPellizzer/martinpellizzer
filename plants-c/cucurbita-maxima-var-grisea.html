Cucurbita maxima var. grisea

Cucurbita maxima var. grisea, commonly known as the gray squash, is a variant of the common squash species characterized by its distinctive grayish-green skin and elongated shape.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers, and is native to the Americas. The plant produces large, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they reach full maturity, often in the fall. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and culinary versatility.
