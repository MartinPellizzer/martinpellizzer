Croton jamalgota

Croton jamalgota, commonly known as the red croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse morphology and often milky sap. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or green, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

Croton jamalgota is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
