Coronilla libanotica

Coronilla libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to dry climates. It is often found in mountainous areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
