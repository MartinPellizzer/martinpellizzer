Cynanchum dichasiale

Cynanchum dichasiale, commonly known as the two-branched milkweed, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its milky sap is a notable feature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
