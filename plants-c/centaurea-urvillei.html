Centaurea urvillei

Centaurea urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles.

Centaurea urvillei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
