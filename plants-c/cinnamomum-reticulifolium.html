Cinnamomum reticulifolium

Cinnamomum reticulifolium, commonly known as the Japanese cinnamon tree, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fragrant properties.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils in perfumery.
