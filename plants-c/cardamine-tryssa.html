Cardamine tryssa

Cardamine tryssa, commonly known as slender watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other familiar plants like buttercups and larkspur.
