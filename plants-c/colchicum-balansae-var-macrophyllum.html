Colchicum balansae var. macrophyllum

Colchicum balansae var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant colchicum, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of a lily. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
