Cousinia kandewanensis

Cousinia kandewanensis, commonly known as the Kandewan cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Cousinia kandewanensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native habitat.
