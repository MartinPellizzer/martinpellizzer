Capparis odorata

Capparis odorata, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Capparis odorata is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
