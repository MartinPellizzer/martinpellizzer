Carex micropodioides

Carex micropodioides, commonly known as the small-footed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly in the eastern United States, and thrives in wet to moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets, which are typically brown or reddish in color.

Carex micropodioides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
