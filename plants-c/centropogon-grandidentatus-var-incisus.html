Centropogon grandidentatus var. incisus

Centropogon grandidentatus var. incisus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes or near the shoreline.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
