Columnea albiflora

Columnea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red or orange calyx. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Columnea albiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
