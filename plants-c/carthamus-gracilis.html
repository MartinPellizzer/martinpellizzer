Carthamus gracilis

Carthamus gracilis, commonly known as slender safflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers with radiating petals and a central disk of tubular florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
