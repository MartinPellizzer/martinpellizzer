Corymbia capricornia

Corymbia capricornia, commonly known as the red gum or red blood gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly in the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, edible fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local wildlife.
