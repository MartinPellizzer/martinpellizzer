Couepia excelsa

Couepia excelsa, commonly known as the giant couepia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its size and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in supporting local biodiversity.
