Calamintha atlantica

Calamintha atlantica, commonly known as Atlantic germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their soothing and antispasmodic properties.
