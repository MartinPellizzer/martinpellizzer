Cicer mogoltavicum

Cicer mogoltavicum, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a protein source in traditional diets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important crop for regions with limited water resources.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also contributes to soil fertility and sustainability in agricultural systems.
