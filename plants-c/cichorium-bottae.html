Cichorium bottae

Cichorium bottae, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers. The plant has a rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves and produces a tall flowering stem with numerous small blooms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible leaves and roots.

Cichorium bottae is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
