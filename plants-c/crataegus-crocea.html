Crataegus crocea

Crataegus crocea, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorny branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Crataegus crocea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
