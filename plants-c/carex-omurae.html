Carex omurae

Carex omurae, commonly known as Omura's sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Carex omurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
