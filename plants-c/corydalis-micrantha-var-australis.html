Corydalis micrantha var. australis

Corydalis micrantha var. australis, commonly known as the Australian dwarf corydalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and woodland settings.
