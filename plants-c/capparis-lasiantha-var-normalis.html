Capparis lasiantha var. normalis

Capparis lasiantha var. normalis, commonly known as the common caper, is a shrub native to parts of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, round, green buds that are harvested before they fully open. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of capers used in culinary applications.
