Cestrum herzogii

Cestrum herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum herzogii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in warm climates.
