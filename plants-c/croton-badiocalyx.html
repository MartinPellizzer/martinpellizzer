Croton badiocalyx

Croton badiocalyx, commonly known as the red-veined croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves with deep red veins contrasting against the green or yellowish leaf blades. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

However, it is important to note that some parts of the plant may be toxic to humans and animals.
