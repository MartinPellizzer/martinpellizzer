Chionanthus amblirrhinus

Chionanthus amblirrhinus, commonly known as the whiteflower fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
