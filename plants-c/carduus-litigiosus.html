Carduus litigiosus

Carduus litigiosus, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive prickly texture.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
