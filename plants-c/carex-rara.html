Carex rara

Carex rara, commonly known as the rare sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Carex rara thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
