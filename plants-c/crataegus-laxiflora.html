Crataegus laxiflora

Crataegus laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Crataegus laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn fruit.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
