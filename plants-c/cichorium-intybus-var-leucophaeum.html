Cichorium intybus var. leucophaeum

Cichorium intybus var. leucophaeum, commonly known as the white endive, is a cultivar of the chicory plant characterized by its pale, blanched leaves.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a rosette of leaves that are typically tender and crisp, with a mild, slightly bitter taste. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and is used in salads and as a vegetable.

The plant's botanical structure includes a taproot and flowering heads that produce small, blue or purple flowers.
