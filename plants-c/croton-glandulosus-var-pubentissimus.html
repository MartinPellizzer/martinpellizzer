Croton glandulosus var. pubentissimus

Croton glandulosus var. pubentissimus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions as a decorative plant.
