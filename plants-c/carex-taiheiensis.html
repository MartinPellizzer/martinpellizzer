Carex taiheiensis

Carex taiheiensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Carex taiheiensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
