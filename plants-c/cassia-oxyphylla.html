Cassia oxyphylla

Cassia oxyphylla, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Cassia oxyphylla is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
