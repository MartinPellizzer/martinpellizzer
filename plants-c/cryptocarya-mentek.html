Cryptocarya mentek

Cryptocarya mentek, commonly known as the Mentek tallowwood, is a medium to large evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subtropical rainforests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in umbels.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
