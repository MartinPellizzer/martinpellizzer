Callitriche palustris var. bolanderi

Callitriche palustris var. bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, white flowers that are usually borne in clusters near the water's surface.

This variety is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich substrates.
