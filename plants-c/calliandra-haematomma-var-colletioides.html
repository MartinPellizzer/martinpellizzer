Calliandra haematomma var. colletioides

Calliandra haematomma var. colletioides, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red inflorescences that resemble feathery plumes, which are composed of numerous small, papery flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
