Codia discolor var. rufinervis

Codia discolor var. rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined codia, is a tropical flowering plant characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a pale, almost white underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

The varietal name "rufinervis" refers to its distinctive reddish veins that run through the leaves.
