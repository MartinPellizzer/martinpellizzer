Chassalia

Chassalia, commonly known as the golden cup, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to Africa, particularly South Africa, and are characterized by their showy, yellow or orange ray flowers. Chassalia species typically grow as herbaceous perennials, often with hairy stems and leaves. They produce small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
