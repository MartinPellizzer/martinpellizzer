Clematis montana subsp. praecox

Clematis montana subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early mountain clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its twining stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and natural habitats.
