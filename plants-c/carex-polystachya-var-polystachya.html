Carex polystachya var. polystachya

Carex polystachya var. polystachya, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
