Campanula isophylla f. alba

Campanula isophylla f. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
