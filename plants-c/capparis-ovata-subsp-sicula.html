Capparis ovata subsp. sicula

Capparis ovata subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces edible, sour berries that are used in culinary applications. Its leaves are evergreen and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
