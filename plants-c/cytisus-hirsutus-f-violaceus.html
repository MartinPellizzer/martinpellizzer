Cytisus hirsutus f. violaceus

Cytisus hirsutus f. violaceus, commonly known as the purple flame acacia, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit.

Its foliage consists of small, narrow leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of striking violet to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. These flowers are typical of the Fabaceae family, featuring a bilateral symmetry with a unique "butterfly" shape.

The shrub is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
