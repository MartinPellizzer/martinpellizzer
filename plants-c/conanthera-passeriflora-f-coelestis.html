Conanthera passeriflora f. coelestis

Conanthera passeriflora f. coelestis, commonly known as the sky-blue coneflower, is a rare and striking plant characterized by its vibrant blue to sky-blue flower heads.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and features showy, composite flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in the center, surrounded by a ring of blue petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its rarity and unique coloration, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
