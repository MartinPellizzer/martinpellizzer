Centaurea fragilis

Centaurea fragilis, commonly known as the fragile coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea fragilis is known for its delicate, often ephemeral blooms and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
