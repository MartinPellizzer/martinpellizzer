Cyperus chlorocephalus

Cyperus chlorocephalus, commonly known as the yellow umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellowish-green inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
