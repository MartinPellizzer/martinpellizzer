Carex atrata var. erecta

Carex atrata var. erecta, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, stiff, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Carex atrata var. erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
