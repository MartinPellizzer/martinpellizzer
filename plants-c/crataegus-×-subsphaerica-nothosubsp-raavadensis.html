Crataegus × subsphaerica nothosubsp. raavadensis

Crataegus × subsphaerica nothosubsp.

raavadensis, commonly known as the Raavad hillbush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is typically a red or yellow berry, which contains several seeds. This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on hybridization and plant adaptation.
