Caesalpinia borneensis

Caesalpinia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean ticktree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, which contribute to its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
