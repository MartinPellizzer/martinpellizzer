Clematis peruviana var. andina

Clematis peruviana var. andina, commonly known as the Andean clematis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, slender stems that can climb to great heights. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a velvety texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
