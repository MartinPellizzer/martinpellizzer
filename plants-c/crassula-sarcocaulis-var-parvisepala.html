Crassula sarcocaulis var. parvisepala

Crassula sarcocaulis var. parvisepala, commonly known as the dwarf crassula or miniature crassula, is a compact, succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a desirable plant for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
