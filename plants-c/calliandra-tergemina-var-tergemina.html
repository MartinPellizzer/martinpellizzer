Calliandra tergemina var. tergemina

Calliandra tergemina var. tergemina, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red bract-like flowers that appear in clusters, providing ornamental value. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Additionally, it is a source of nectar for bees and other pollinators.
