Crepis tectorum var. attenuata

Crepis tectorum var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender roof thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found in urban environments such as walls and rooftops. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The variety attenuata is distinguished by its elongated, attenuated leaves and compact growth habit.
