Cyrtanthus

Cyrtanthus, commonly known as the rain lilies, are bulbous plants native to southern Africa.

They are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plants typically bloom in spring or early summer, and their flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stems. Cyrtanthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

They require well-drained soil and prefer cool, moist conditions during their growing season.
