Calathea rotundifolia var. fasciata

Calathea rotundifolia var. fasciata, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, dark green leaves with distinctive white or silver markings. The leaves are typically round or oval in shape, and they exhibit a unique pattern of color variation that changes with light exposure. This plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive indoors under proper care.
