Cerasus virginiana var. pendula

Cerasus virginiana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and turn yellow in autumn.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
