Coleus membranaceus

Coleus membranaceus, commonly known as the Java nettle, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with patterns of red, white, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its leaves are sensitive to touch and can cause a stinging sensation when handled.
