Campanula afra var. leptosiphon

Campanula afra var. leptosiphon, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
