Chrysopsis gossypina f. decumbens

Chrysopsis gossypina f. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent cottonweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, decumbent stems that lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in clusters. It typically thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in xerophytic environments.
