Cerastium pubescens

Cerastium pubescens, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cerastium pubescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
