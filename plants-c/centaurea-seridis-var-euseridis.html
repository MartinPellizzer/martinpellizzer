Centaurea seridis var. euseridis

Centaurea seridis var. euseridis, commonly known as the Egyptian crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and bright yellow to orange-red flower heads that resemble small crowns. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
