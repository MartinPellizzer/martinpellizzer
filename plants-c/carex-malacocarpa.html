Carex malacocarpa

Carex malacocarpa, commonly known as the blackberry sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil.

Carex malacocarpa is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
