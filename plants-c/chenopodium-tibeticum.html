Chenopodium tibeticum

Chenopodium tibeticum, commonly known as Tibetan goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is a hardy perennial that can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Chenopodium tibeticum is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional Tibetan cuisine.
