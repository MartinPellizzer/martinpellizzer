Cinnamomum cacharense

Cinnamomum cacharense, commonly known as the Cachar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and can reach a height of up to 10 meters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
