Calamus viridispinus var. sumatranus

Calamus viridispinus var. sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran green palm, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
