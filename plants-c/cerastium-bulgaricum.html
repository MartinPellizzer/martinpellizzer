Cerastium bulgaricum

Cerastium bulgaricum, commonly known as the Balkan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy texture.

Cerastium bulgaricum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.
