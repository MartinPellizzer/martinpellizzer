Clinogyne inaequilatera

Clinogyne inaequilatera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clinogyne, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clinogyne inaequilatera is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
