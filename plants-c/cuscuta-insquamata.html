Cuscuta insquamata

Cuscuta insquamata, commonly known as the silverleaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique life cycle that begins with seeds germinating on the surface of host plants. The plant forms a dense network of slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host, extracting water and nutrients through specialized structures called haustoria. It is native to North America and is often found growing on various plants such as grasses and shrubs.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops and natural vegetation.
