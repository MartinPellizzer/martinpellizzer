Crassula scleranthoides

Crassula scleranthoides, commonly known as the stonecrop crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
