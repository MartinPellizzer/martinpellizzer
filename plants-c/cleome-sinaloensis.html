Cleome sinaloensis

Cleome sinaloensis, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, spider-like yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cleome sinaloensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with a unique appearance.
