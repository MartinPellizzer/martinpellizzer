Cyrtandra × triens

Cyrtandra × triens, commonly known as the hybrid blue ginger, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra ciliolata and Cyrtandra hawaiiensis. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. It is native to Hawaii and is often found in moist, shaded habitats on forest floors.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
