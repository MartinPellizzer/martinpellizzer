Calanthe curtisii

Calanthe curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, dark green leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, with showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe curtisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
