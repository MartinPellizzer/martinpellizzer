Campomanesia resinosa

Campomanesia resinosa, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are covered in a waxy coating, which gives it its common name. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, acidic flavor, and is often used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
