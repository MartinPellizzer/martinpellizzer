Crinum tanganyikense

Crinum tanganyikense, commonly known as the Tanganyika Crinum, is a species of flowering plant native to the Lake Tanganyika region in East Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is adapted to the humid climate of its native habitat.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its ecosystem.
