Cyrtanthus sanguineus subsp. salmonoides

Cyrtanthus sanguineus subsp.

salmonoides, commonly known as the salmon-flowered bell-shaped lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa. It produces striking, salmon-pink to orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators.
