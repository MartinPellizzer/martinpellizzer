Cardamine hirsuta var. laxa

Cardamine hirsuta var. laxa, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the common watercress species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. It features small, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to other watercress varieties.
