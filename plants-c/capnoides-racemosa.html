Capnoides racemosa

Capnoides racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
