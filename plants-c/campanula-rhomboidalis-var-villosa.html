Campanula rhomboidalis var. villosa

Campanula rhomboidalis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
