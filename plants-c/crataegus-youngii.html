Crataegus youngii

Crataegus youngii, commonly known as the young's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Crataegus youngii is often found in woodland edges and open areas, thriving in temperate climates.
