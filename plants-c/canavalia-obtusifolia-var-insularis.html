Canavalia obtusifolia var. insularis

Canavalia obtusifolia var. insularis, commonly known as the Island Cowpea, is a tropical legume native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in disturbed habitats. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
