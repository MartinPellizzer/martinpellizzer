Centaurea pullata var. albiflora

Centaurea pullata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered cornflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright white flowers with a distinctive purple center. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This variety is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
