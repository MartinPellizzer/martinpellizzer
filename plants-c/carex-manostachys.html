Carex manostachys

Carex manostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
