Caularthron bilamellatum f. indivisum

Caularthron bilamellatum f. indivisum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually unlobed and have a distinctive two-layered structure, giving it its specific epithet.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
