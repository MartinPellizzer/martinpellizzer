Centaurea iberica subsp. holzmanniana

Centaurea iberica subsp.

holzmanniana, commonly known as the Iberian centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that typically grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture, and it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
