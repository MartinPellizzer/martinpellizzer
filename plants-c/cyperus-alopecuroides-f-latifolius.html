Cyperus alopecuroides f. latifolius

Cyperus alopecuroides f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sedgeland sedge, is a variant of the sedgeland sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its wide, flat leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, three-lobed flowers.

This botanical feature makes it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
