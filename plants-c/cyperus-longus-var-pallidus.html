Cyperus longus var. pallidus

Cyperus longus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, grass-like stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, with a pale green coloration that gives it its common name.

It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.
