Calamus penicillatus var. purpurascens

Calamus penicillatus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often exhibit a distinctive purplish hue, especially when mature. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
