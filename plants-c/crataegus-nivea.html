Crataegus nivea

Crataegus nivea, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a species of hawthorn native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its white to pink flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
