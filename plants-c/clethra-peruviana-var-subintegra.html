Clethra peruviana var. subintegra

Clethra peruviana var. subintegra, commonly known as the Peruvian sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
