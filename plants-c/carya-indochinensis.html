Carya indochinensis

Carya indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina hickory, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Cambodia.

It belongs to the walnut family, Juglandaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, rounded canopy and smooth, gray bark.

Its fruits are encased in a spiny husk and contain a single edible nut, which is often used in local cuisines.
