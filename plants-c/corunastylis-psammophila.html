Corunastylis psammophila

Corunastylis psammophila, commonly known as the sandplain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. It is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions, making it a characteristic species of coastal dunes.

Corunastylis psammophila plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
