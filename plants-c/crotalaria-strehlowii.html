Crotalaria strehlowii

Crotalaria strehlowii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a unique rattle-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its seed pods are long and cylindrical, splitting open to release its seeds.

Crotalaria strehlowii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
