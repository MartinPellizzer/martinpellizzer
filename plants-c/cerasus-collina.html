Cerasus collina

Cerasus collina, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Cerasus collina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic floral display.
