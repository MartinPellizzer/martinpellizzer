Campanula dimorphantha

Campanula dimorphantha, commonly known as the two-form bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-form growth pattern, with some stems producing flowers and others bearing leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula dimorphantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
