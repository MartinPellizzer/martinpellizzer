Canarium microcarpum f. minor

Canarium microcarpum f. minor, commonly known as the small-fruited candlenut.

It is a variant of the candlenut tree, characterized by its smaller fruit size compared to the typical species. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically yellowish-green, and the fruit is a drupe that matures to a reddish color.
