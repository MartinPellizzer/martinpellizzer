Centaurea limbata subsp. limbata

Centaurea limbata subsp.

limbata, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
