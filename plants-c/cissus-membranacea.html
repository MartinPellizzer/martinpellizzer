Cissus membranacea

Cissus membranacea, commonly known as the bonebrake or climbing cutleaf, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth makes it suitable for vertical gardening and ornamental purposes.
