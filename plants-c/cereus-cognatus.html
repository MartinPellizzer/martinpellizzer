Cereus cognatus

Cereus cognatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one evening. Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months.

Cereus cognatus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
