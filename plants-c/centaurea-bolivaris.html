Centaurea bolivaris

Centaurea bolivaris, commonly known as the Bolivian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and slender, upright stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head with ray florets.

Centaurea bolivaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
