Centaurea maculosa subsp. subalbida

Centaurea maculosa subsp.

subalbida, commonly known as the subalbida sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with reddish-brown bracts, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a noxious weed in certain regions.
