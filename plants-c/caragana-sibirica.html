Caragana sibirica

Caragana sibirica, commonly known as Siberian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, including sandy soils and poor nutrition.

Caragana sibirica is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
