Calamus karinensis

Calamus karinensis, commonly known as the Karin's rattan, is a species of palm-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and pinnate leaves. The plant is often found in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are used in traditional medicine, and its fibers are valued for their strength and flexibility.

Calamus karinensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
