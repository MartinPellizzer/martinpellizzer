Crataegus angustifolia

Crataegus angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
