Cyclophyllum longiflorum

Cyclophyllum longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered cyclophyllum, is a unique member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, elongated flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink or purple. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
