Crocus biflorus var. violaceus

Crocus biflorus var. violaceus, commonly known as the violet-flowered crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early spring flowering.

The variety is noted for its compact size and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
