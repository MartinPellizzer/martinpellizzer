Cereus costatus

Cereus costatus, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a succulent native to South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a thick, fleshy base that stores water. The plant produces striking, large, white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Cereus costatus is well adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
