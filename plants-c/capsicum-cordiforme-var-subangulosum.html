Capsicum cordiforme var. subangulosum

Capsicum cordiforme var. subangulosum, commonly known as the heart-shaped pepper, is a unique cultivar characterized by its distinctly heart-shaped fruits.

The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and the stems are hairy, typical of many Capsicum species. The fruit develops in clusters, transitioning from green to a vibrant red when mature.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for both culinary and decorative purposes.
