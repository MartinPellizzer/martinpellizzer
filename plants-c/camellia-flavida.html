Camellia flavida

Camellia flavida, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained soil.

Camellia flavida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.
