Camellia planipetala

Camellia planipetala, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Camellia planipetala is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
