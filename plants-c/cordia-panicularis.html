Cordia panicularis

Cordia panicularis, commonly known as the panicle cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble panicles, composed of numerous small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cordia panicularis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or disturbed areas.
