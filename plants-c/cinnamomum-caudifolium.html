Cinnamomum caudifolium

Cinnamomum caudifolium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Cinnamomum caudifolium thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
