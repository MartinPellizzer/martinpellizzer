Cylindrolobus perspicabile

Cylindrolobus perspicabile, commonly known as the transparent cylinder lichen, is a unique fungal-lichen composite that exhibits a distinctive cylindrical growth form.

It is characterized by its translucent, gelatinous thallus, which allows for the visibility of its internal structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on decaying wood in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, known as apothecia, are small and disc-shaped, releasing spores into the air.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
