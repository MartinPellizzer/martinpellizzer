Cytisus arboreus var. malacitanus

Cytisus arboreus var. malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in Mediterranean climates.
