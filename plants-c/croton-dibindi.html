Croton dibindi

Croton dibindi, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of red, yellow, and green hues. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton dibindi is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
