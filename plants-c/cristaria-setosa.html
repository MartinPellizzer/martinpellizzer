Cristaria setosa

Cristaria setosa, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, which help in capturing prey. The plant is a carnivorous species, using its specialized bladder-like structures to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
