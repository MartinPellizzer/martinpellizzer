Calamagrostis brevipilis

Calamagrostis brevipilis, commonly known as the short-haired reed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive short, fine-haired appearance on the lower stems. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden florets.

Calamagrostis brevipilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
