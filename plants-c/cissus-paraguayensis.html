Cissus paraguayensis

Cissus paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically bright green with a slightly waxy texture, and the plant produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus paraguayensis is also valued for its ability to climb and adapt to various growing conditions.
