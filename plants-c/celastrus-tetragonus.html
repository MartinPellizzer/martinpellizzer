Celastrus tetragonus

Celastrus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-cornered staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by yellow, four-lobed berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
