Circaea erecta

Circaea erecta, commonly known as the upright white bead vine, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round, bead-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.
