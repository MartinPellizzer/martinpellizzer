Cleisostoma amabile

Cleisostoma amabile, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Cleisostoma amabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral fragrance.
