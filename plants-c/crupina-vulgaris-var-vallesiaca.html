Crupina vulgaris var. vallesiaca

Crupina vulgaris var. vallesiaca, commonly known as the Vallesia crupina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
