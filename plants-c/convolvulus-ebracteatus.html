Convolvulus ebracteatus

Convolvulus ebracteatus, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species of morning glories.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
