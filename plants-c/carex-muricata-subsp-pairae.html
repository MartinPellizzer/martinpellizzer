Carex muricata subsp. pairae

Carex muricata subsp.

pairae, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
