Cheiranthus scoparius var. aeruginosus

Cheiranthus scoparius var. aeruginosus, commonly known as the Azure-flowered Wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often before many other plants. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky or gravelly slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
