Calamagrostis junciformis

Calamagrostis junciformis, commonly known as the tufted panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Calamagrostis junciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
