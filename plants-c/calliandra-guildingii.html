Calliandra guildingii

Calliandra guildingii, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Calliandra guildingii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
