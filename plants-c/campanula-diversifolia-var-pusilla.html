Campanula diversifolia var. pusilla

Campanula diversifolia var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
