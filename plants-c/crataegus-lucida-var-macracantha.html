Crataegus lucida var. macracantha

Crataegus lucida var. macracantha, commonly known as the shining crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest.
