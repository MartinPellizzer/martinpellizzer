Chondrosea paniculata subsp. neogaea

Chondrosea paniculata subsp.

neogaea, commonly known as the New Guinea flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display.

The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with compound leaves that are often arranged in a pinnate pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
