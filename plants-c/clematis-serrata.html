Clematis serrata

Clematis serrata, commonly known as the serrated clematis, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Clematis serrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or walls.
