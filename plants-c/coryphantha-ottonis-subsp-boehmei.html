Coryphantha ottonis subsp. boehmei

Coryphantha ottonis subsp.

boehmei, commonly known as the Bohme's morning glory, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, and it produces small, spiny fruits. Coryphantha ottonis subsp.

boehmei is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
