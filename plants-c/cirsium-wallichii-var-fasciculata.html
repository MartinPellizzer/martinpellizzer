Cirsium wallichii var. fasciculata

Cirsium wallichii var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
