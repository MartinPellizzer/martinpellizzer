Callisia glandulosa

Callisia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, glandular texture, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and prominent stamens, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or containers.

Callisia glandulosa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring regular watering during the growing season.
