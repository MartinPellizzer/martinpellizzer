Cirsium × benaci

Cirsium × benaci, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. This plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a woody base that becomes more pronounced with age.

Cirsium × benaci is often considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
