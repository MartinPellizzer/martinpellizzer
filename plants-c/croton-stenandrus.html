Croton stenandrus

Croton stenandrus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated leaves that display a range of colors including green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a compact form.

Croton stenandrus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
