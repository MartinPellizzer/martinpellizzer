Celastrus senegalensis var. coriaceus

Celastrus senegalensis var. coriaceus, commonly known as the African staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with alternate arrangement and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
