Capparis pranensis

Capparis pranensis, commonly known as the Pranese Caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

Capparis pranensis is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
