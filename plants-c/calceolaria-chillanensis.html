Calceolaria chillanensis

Calceolaria chillanensis, commonly known as the Chilean hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped petals. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a unique structure that resembles a small hood.

Calceolaria chillanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
