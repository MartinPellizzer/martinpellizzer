Cassipourea poeppigii

Cassipourea poeppigii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cassipourea poeppigii is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide dense shade in warm climates.
