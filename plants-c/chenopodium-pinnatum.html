Chenopodium pinnatum

Chenopodium pinnatum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright stem, which is often reddish in color, and its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, and is considered a weed in some regions.

Chenopodium pinnatum is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine.
