Calycanthus ferax

Calycanthus ferax, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. Calycanthus ferax thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique fragrance and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
