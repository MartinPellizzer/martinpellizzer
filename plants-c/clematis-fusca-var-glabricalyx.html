Clematis fusca var. glabricalyx

Clematis fusca var. glabricalyx, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its twining stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and typically have a smooth texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or structures.
