Cornus alternifolia f. albovariegata

Cornus alternifolia f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated dogwood, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its unique foliage.

The leaves are broadly ovate with a white or cream variegation that contrasts beautifully with the dark green background. This cultivar is known for its showy white bracts that surround small yellow flowers in late spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental berries that persist into winter, adding seasonal interest.
