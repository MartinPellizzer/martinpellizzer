Cnidoscolus subinteger

Cnidoscolus subinteger, commonly known as the Mexican bristleleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and distinctive spiny structures along the leaf margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, spiny fruits containing a starchy, tuberous pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

The plant has been used traditionally for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
