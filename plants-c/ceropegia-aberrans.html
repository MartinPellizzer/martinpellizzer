Ceropegia aberrans

Ceropegia aberrans, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble small, ghostly faces, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a succulent vine, with fleshy, pale green stems and leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, making it an important component of its local ecosystem.
