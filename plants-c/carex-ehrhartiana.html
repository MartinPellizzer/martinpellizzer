Carex ehrhartiana

Carex ehrhartiana, commonly known as the Ehrhart's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It thrives in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
