Cyperus gracilescens

Cyperus gracilescens, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are typically less than 1 meter in height, with small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and it is often mistaken for other sedges due to its similar appearance.

Cyperus gracilescens is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
