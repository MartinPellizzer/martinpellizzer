Celtis lindheimeri

Celtis lindheimeri, commonly known as the Lindheimer's hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark blue berries, which are edible for some bird species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Celtis lindheimeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to provide habitat and food for wildlife.
