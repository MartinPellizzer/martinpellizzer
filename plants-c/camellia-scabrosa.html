Camellia scabrosa

Camellia scabrosa, commonly known as the rough camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia scabrosa is also valued for its ability to bloom in late winter to early spring, adding color to the landscape during the colder months.
