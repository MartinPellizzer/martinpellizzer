Campanopsis serpillifolia

Campanopsis serpillifolia, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and moderate to high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Campanopsis serpillifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
