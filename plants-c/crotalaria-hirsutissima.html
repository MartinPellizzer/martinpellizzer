Crotalaria hirsutissima

Crotalaria hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
