Cabralea warmingiana

Cabralea warmingiana, commonly known as the redwood tree or the Brazilian redwood, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and a dense canopy of dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bark is thick and reddish-brown, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Due to habitat loss, Cabralea warmingiana is considered a threatened species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
