Convolvulus montevidensis var. ottonis

Convolvulus montevidensis var. ottonis, commonly known as the Otton's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The species produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
