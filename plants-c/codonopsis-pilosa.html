Codonopsis pilosa

Codonopsis pilosa, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or the "poisonous ginseng," is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine.

Codonopsis pilosa is valued for its purported health benefits, including enhancing energy and immune function.
