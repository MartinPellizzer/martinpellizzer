Caladium sanguinolentum

Caladium sanguinolentum, commonly known as the bloodleaf plant, is a tropical herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant red, pink, and white veining. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.

Caladium sanguinolentum is typically propagated through tuberous roots or by dividing the rhizomes.
