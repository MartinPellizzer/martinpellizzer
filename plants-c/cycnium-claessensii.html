Cycnium claessensii

Cycnium claessensii, commonly known as the African giant hyacinth, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Cycnium claessensii is also known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several weeks during the growing season.
