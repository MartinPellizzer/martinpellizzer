Chlorophytum papillosum

Chlorophytum papillosum, commonly known as the African spear or snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and grass-like leaves. The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are often adorned with white or yellow markings, adding visual interest.

Chlorophytum papillosum is also valued for its air-purifying properties and its ability to improve indoor air quality.
