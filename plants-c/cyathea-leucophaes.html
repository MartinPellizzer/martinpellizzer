Cyathea leucophaes

Cyathea leucophaes, commonly known as the silver-leaf tree fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Cyatheaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to Southeast Asia, this species thrives in humid, shaded environments with high rainfall. The plant's rhizome system supports its growth, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds for reproduction.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
