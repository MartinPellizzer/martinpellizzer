Cynanchum tunicatum

Cynanchum tunicatum, commonly known as the tunicate milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cynanchum tunicatum contains various alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
