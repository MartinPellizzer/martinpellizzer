Cryptocarya grandis

Cryptocarya grandis, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Cryptocarya grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory vegetation in tropical ecosystems.
