Calopogonium brachycarpum

Calopogonium brachycarpum, commonly known as small-seeded burr legume, is a tropical annual legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by animals or through mechanical means, contributing to its widespread distribution.
