Capsicum indicum subsp. elaeocarpon

Capsicum indicum subsp.

elaeocarpon, commonly known as the wild chili pepper, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that varies in color from yellow to red.

The plant is part of the nightshade family and has been used traditionally for its medicinal and culinary properties.
