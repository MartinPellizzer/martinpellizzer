Calamintha × degenii

Calamintha × degenii, commonly known as the hybrid catnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are fragrant and contain essential oils similar to those of its parent species, Nepeta cataria and Calamintha nepeta.

Calamintha × degenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.
