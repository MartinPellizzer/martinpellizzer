Calceolaria angustiflora

Calceolaria angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Calceolaria angustiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
