Catananche caerulea var. armerioides

Catananche caerulea var. armerioides, commonly known as blue catananche, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
