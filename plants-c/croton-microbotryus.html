Croton microbotryus

Croton microbotryus, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are often green with red or yellow veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in warm climates.
