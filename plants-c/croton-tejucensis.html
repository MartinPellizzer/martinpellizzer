Croton tejucensis

Croton tejucensis, commonly known as the white croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton tejucensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
