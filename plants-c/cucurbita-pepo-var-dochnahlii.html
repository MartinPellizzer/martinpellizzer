Cucurbita pepo var. dochnahlii

Cucurbita pepo var. dochnahlii, commonly known as the Chihuahua gourd, is a wild variety of the common gourd native to the Chihuahua region of Mexico.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and tendrils. The plant produces small, spherical, green fruits that mature to a pale yellow or white color. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct veining pattern.

This species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or traditional purposes.
