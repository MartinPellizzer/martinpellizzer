Cirsium depilatum var. glomeratum

Cirsium depilatum var. glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces spherical clusters of purple to pink flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a flowering period that occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
