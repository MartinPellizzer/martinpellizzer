Carex fischeri subsp. recedens

Carex fischeri subsp.

recedens, commonly known as the Fischer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed or transitional habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
