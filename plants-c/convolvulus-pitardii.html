Convolvulus pitardii

Convolvulus pitardii, commonly known as the Pitard's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus pitardii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
