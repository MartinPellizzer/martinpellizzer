Cyperus verrucinux

Cyperus verrucinux, commonly known as the rough flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Cyperus verrucinux is adapted to moist soils and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish habitats.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
