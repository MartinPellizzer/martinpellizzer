Cayratia gracilis

Cayratia gracilis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that give way to small, red berries. It is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.

Cayratia gracilis is also known for its ability to attract various insect pollinators.
