Cascarilla

Cascarilla, commonly known as *Bertholletia excelsa*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, grayish bark and can grow up to 50 meters tall. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain edible seeds and a valuable resin. The resin, known as copal, is used in various traditional and commercial applications.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within the rainforest ecosystem and its economic importance to local communities.
