Crocus autranii

Crocus autranii, commonly known as the Sicilian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily, and is characterized by its pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces a single, showy bloom with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crocus autranii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its historical use in perfumery.
