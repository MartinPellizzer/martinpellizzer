Calea dalyi

Calea dalyi, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique floral structure. Its leaves are velvety and dark green, often with a waxy coating.

Calea dalyi is also noted for its strong, sweet fragrance, which is most intense during the evening hours.
