Croton pseudoxalapensis var. cobanensis

Croton pseudoxalapensis var. cobanensis, commonly known as the Coban croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

The leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant may produce small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits.
