Convolvulus micranthus

Convolvulus micranthus, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its heart-shaped leaves are alternate and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Convolvulus micranthus is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
