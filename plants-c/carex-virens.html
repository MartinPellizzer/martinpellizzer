Carex virens

Carex virens, commonly known as green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and usually green, with a slightly rough texture.

Carex virens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
