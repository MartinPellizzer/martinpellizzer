Cotoneaster cuspidatus

Cotoneaster cuspidatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip, giving it its common name.

Cotoneaster cuspidatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
