Cyperus sphaeranthelus

Cyperus sphaeranthelus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus sphaeranthelus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist environments.
