Calamagrostis arundinacea var. nipponica

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, silver-white plumes in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in water garden settings.

Its delicate appearance and seasonal display make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
