Corylus kweichowensis var. sutchuenensis

Corylus kweichowensis var. sutchuenensis, commonly known as the Sichuan hazelnut, is a variant of the hazelnut genus native to China.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing oval-shaped nuts encased in a hairy husk. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the male and female flowers are separate.

This species is valued for its edible nuts and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
