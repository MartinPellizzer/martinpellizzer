Cirsium dimorphum

Cirsium dimorphum, commonly known as the two-form thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and covered with stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Cirsium dimorphum is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands at high elevations.
