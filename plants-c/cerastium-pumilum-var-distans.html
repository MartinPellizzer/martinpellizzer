Cerastium pumilum var. distans

Cerastium pumilum var. distans, commonly known as the distant prostrate mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are covered in dense, white or pale green hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
