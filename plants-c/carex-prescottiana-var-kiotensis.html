Carex prescottiana var. kiotensis

Carex prescottiana var. kiotensis, commonly known as the Kioten Carex, is a sedge species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and forest understories. The plant produces cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
