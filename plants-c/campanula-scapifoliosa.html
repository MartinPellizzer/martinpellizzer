Campanula scapifoliosa

Campanula scapifoliosa, commonly known as the scapifoliate bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features slender, leafless stems that bear bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy.

Campanula scapifoliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
