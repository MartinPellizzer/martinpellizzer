Carex acutissima

Carex acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces upright, cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Carex acutissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
