Cleome schraderi

Cleome schraderi, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Cleome schraderi is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique, spider-like appearance and easy care requirements contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and ecological gardens.
