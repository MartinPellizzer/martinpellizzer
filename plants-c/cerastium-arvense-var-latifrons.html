Cerastium arvense var. latifrons

Cerastium arvense var. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
