Cyperus exaltatus var. serpens

Cyperus exaltatus var. serpens, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense, upright clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are linear and have a slightly zigzag or twisted appearance, giving the plant its common name.

It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.
