Chamaemelum achilleifolium

Chamaemelum achilleifolium, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Chamaemelum achilleifolium has been used historically for its medicinal properties, including its ability to stop bleeding and treat wounds.
