Camara mollis

Camara mollis, commonly known as the soft camara or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in umbels. The plant is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant can become invasive in some areas.

Camara mollis is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
