Cyanthillium villosum

Cyanthillium villosum, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it typically grows in dry, rocky soils. Its small, inconspicuous fruits are surrounded by bracts, and the plant is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Cyanthillium villosum is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
