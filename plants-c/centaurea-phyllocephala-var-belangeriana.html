Centaurea phyllocephala var. belangeriana

Centaurea phyllocephala var. belangeriana, commonly known as the Belanger's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to pink flower heads. The plant produces bracts that form a spiny involucre around the flower, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-gray hue.

This variety is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
