Cristaria dissecta var. inconspicua

Cristaria dissecta var. inconspicua, commonly known as the slender swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a serrated margin with a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically pink to reddish in color.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
