Clematis dioica

Clematis dioica, commonly known as the twinflower clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Clematis dioica is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it can climb and spread along vegetation.
