Ceropegia buraoensis

Ceropegia buraoensis, commonly known as the Burao moonvine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia and Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems.

Ceropegia buraoensis is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
