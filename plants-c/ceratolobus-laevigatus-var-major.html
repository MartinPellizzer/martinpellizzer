Ceratolobus laevigatus var. major

Ceratolobus laevigatus var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-green foliage and produces cylindrical, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, hard, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
