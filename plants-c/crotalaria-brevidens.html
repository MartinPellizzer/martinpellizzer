Crotalaria brevidens

Crotalaria brevidens, commonly known as the shortleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria brevidens is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
