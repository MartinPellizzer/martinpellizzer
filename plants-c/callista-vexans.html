Callista vexans

Callista vexans, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Callista vexans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
