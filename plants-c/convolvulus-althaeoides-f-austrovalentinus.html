Convolvulus althaeoides f. austrovalentinus

Convolvulus althaeoides f. austrovalentinus, commonly known as the Southern Alpine bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in summer. It is native to the alpine regions of southern Europe and is adapted to rocky and dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native range.
