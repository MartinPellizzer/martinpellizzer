Carex pediformis

Carex pediformis, commonly known as the pediform sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex pediformis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
