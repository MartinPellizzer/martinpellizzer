Crataegus fischeri

Crataegus fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's hawthorn, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Crataegus fischeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native range.
