Cryptanthus carnosus

Cryptanthus carnosus, commonly known as the "Carnosa" or "Carnosus Zonal Geranium," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features a low-growing habit with dense, compact foliage that is often variegated. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cryptanthus carnosus is appreciated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
