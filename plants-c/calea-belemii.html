Calea belemii

Calea belemii, commonly known as the sacred candleplant, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescence composed of numerous white, waxy flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that resembles a candle. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Calea belemii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in traditional practices.
