Campomanesia hatschbachii

Campomanesia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
