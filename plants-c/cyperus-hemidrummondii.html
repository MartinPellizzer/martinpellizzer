Cyperus hemidrummondii

Cyperus hemidrummondii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often inconspicuous. It is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and can thrive in areas with seasonal flooding.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
