Carex chathamica

Carex chathamica, commonly known as the Chatham Islands sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Chatham Islands of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous, with small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex chathamica plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
