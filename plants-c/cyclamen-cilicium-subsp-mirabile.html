Cyclamen cilicium subsp. mirabile

Cyclamen cilicium subsp.

mirabile, commonly known as the Cilician cyclamen, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Syria. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and wavy margins.

The plant produces delicate, nodding flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Cyclamen cilicium subsp.

mirabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
