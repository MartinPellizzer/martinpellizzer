Carthamus caeruleus

Carthamus caeruleus, commonly known as blue poppy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique coloration.
