Cirsium lobelii

Cirsium lobelii, commonly known as Lobel's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with coarse teeth, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium lobelii is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, thriving in full sun.
