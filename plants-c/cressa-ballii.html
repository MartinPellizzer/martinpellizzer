Cressa ballii

Cressa ballii, commonly known as the narrowleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Cressa ballii is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
