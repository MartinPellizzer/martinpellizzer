Crataegus monogyna var. oliveriana

Crataegus monogyna var. oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically consumed by birds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.
