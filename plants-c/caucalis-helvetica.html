Caucalis helvetica

Caucalis helvetica, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow from a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Caucalis helvetica is often found in mountain meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
