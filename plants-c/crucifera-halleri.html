Crucifera halleri

Crucifera halleri, commonly known as Haller's cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this species thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces small, rounded fruits called siliques, which contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
