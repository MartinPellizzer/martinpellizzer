Cistus violaceus

Cistus violaceus, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of violet or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
