Cephaelis ipecacuanha

Cephaelis ipecacuanha, commonly known as Indian ipecac, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its botanical significance lies in its traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its emetic properties.

The plant's scientific classification places it in the family Rubiaceae, which includes other notable genera such as Rubia and Psychotria.
