Croton leptanthus

Croton leptanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
