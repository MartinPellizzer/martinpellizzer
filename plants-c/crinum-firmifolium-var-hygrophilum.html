Crinum firmifolium var. hygrophilum

Crinum firmifolium var. hygrophilum, commonly known as the swamp crinum, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. It produces white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
