Chrysopsis nervosa var. virgata

Chrysopsis nervosa var. virgata, commonly known as the Virginia goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
