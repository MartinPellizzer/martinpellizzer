Chilocarpus embelioides

Chilocarpus embelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exuding a resinous sap when damaged.

Chilocarpus embelioides is adapted to harsh, arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
