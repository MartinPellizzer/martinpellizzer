Cardamine graeca

Cardamine graeca, commonly known as Greek watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, and its small white flowers bloom in early spring. Due to its habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
