Crataegus notabilis

Crataegus notabilis, commonly known as the Mayflower or common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, and red berries that ripen in autumn. The plant is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open woods, thriving in a variety of soil types. Its botanical features include simple, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal medicine.
