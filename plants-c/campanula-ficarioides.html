Campanula ficarioides

Campanula ficarioides, commonly known as the Iceland bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Campanula ficarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in colder climates.
