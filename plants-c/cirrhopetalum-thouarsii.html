Cirrhopetalum thouarsii

Cirrhopetalum thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often yellow or orange with red or purple markings, making it visually distinctive.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
