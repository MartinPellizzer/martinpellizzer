Cyperus subcastaneus subsp. graomogolii

Cyperus subcastaneus subsp.

graomogolii, commonly known as the Brazilian bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family.

The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. It has tall, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
