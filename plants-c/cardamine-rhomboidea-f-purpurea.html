Cardamine rhomboidea f. purpurea

Cardamine rhomboidea f. purpurea, commonly known as purple fern sedge.

This plant is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are often tinged with purple. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate, greenish-yellow color.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.
