Centaurea kobstanica

Centaurea kobstanica, commonly known as the Kobstan's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with narrow, linear leaves.

Centaurea kobstanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
