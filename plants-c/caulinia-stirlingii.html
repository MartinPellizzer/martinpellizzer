Caulinia stirlingii

Caulinia stirlingii, commonly known as the Stirling's candelabra, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, branched structure that resembles a candelabra, with multiple stems rising from a single base. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
