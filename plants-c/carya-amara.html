Carya amara

Carya amara, commonly known as the blackjack oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Carya, which includes various species of hickory and oak trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, rough bark and elongated, elliptical leaves with a serrated edge. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single hard-shelled nut enclosed in a spiky husk.

The tree is adapted to poor, dry soils and is often found in upland areas with full sun exposure.
