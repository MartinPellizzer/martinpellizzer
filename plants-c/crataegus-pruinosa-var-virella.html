Crataegus pruinosa var. virella

Crataegus pruinosa var. virella, commonly known as the green-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green on top and paler underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red to orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
