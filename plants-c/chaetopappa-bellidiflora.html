Chaetopappa bellidiflora

Chaetopappa bellidiflora, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
