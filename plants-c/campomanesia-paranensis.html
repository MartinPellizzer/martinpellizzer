Campomanesia paranensis

Campomanesia paranensis, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its ornamental value and potential for use in horticulture make it an interesting species for botanical study.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been traditionally used in folk medicine.
