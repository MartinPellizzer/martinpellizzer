Cirsium × rodnense

Cirsium × rodnense, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the northern hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
