It is a terrestrial, epiphytic, or lithophytic plant, typically found growing on rocks or in rocky crevices. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with a dense, tufted crown of dark green, leathery leaves. Its fronds are usually pinnately compound, with a distinctive, often recurved shape.

The spores are produced in small, round sori that are located on the underside of the fronds.
