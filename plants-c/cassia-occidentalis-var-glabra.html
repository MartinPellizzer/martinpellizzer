Cassia occidentalis var. glabra

Cassia occidentalis var. glabra, commonly known as white senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and dry woodlands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its bright flowers and medicinal properties.
