Cassia geminiflora

Cassia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in pairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia geminiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
