Crotalaria thomasii var. sericea

Crotalaria thomasii var. sericea, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with elongated, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
