Cerasus duracina var. latifolia

Cerasus duracina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cherry, is a variant of the cherry species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

This variety is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
