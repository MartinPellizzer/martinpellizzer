Chrysocoma purpurea

Chrysocoma purpurea, commonly known as the purple chrysocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its striking purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chrysocoma purpurea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and adaptability to different climates.
