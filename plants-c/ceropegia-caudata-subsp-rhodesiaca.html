Ceropegia caudata subsp. rhodesiaca

Ceropegia caudata subsp.

rhodesiaca, commonly known as the Rhodesian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Asclepiadaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
