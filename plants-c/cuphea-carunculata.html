Cuphea carunculata

Cuphea carunculata, commonly known as the flamebeard plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble tiny flames, often surrounded by fuzzy, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea carunculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
