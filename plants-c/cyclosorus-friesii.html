Cyclosorus friesii

Cyclosorus friesii, commonly known as the spiral liverwort, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic liverwort that thrives in moist environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant has a simple, thalloid structure with a prostrate growth habit. It is typically found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and stream margins.

Cyclosorus friesii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
