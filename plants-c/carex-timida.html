Carex timida

Carex timida, commonly known as the shy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Carex timida produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often hidden among the foliage.
