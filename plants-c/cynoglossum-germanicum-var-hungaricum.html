Cynoglossum germanicum var. hungaricum

Cynoglossum germanicum var. hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
