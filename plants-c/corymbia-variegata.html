Corymbia variegata

Corymbia variegata, commonly known as the spotted gum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which features a mix of smooth, flaky, and fissured textures. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a pale green underside. It produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
