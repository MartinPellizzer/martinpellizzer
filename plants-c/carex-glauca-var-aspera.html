Carex glauca var. aspera

Carex glauca var. aspera, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and slender, upright stems that bear spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly rough to the touch, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
