Chloris scoparia

Chloris scoparia, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Chloris scoparia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
