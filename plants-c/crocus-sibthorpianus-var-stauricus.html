Crocus sibthorpianus var. stauricus

Crocus sibthorpianus var. stauricus, commonly known as the Stauric Crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It produces striking, deep purple to violet flowers with a distinctive, narrow, spathaceous sheath that encloses the bloom. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, and its flowers are borne on short, sturdy stems. The leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, and the plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
