Chrysopogon macleishii

Chrysopogon macleishii, commonly known as the Macleish's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are usually pale green to brown in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
