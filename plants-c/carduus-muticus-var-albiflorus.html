Carduus muticus var. albiflorus

Carduus muticus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower sickleweed, is a variant of the common thistle.

It is characterized by its pale or white flowers, which distinguish it from the typical purple or blue blooms of the standard Carduus muticus. The plant is a perennial herb with spiny leaves and a robust root system, often found in dry, disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
