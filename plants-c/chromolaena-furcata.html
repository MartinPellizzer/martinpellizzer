Chromolaena furcata

Chromolaena furcata, commonly known as the spreading chromolaena, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white to grayish seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings due to its adaptability and rapid growth.

Chromolaena furcata is also known for its invasive potential in some regions.
