Carex spreta

Carex spreta, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbon-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
