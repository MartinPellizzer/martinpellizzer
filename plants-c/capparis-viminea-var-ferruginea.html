Capparis viminea var. ferruginea

Capparis viminea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
