Chelidonium majus var. plenum

Chelidonium majus var. plenum, commonly known as the full-leaved greater celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features thick, fleshy, alternate leaves that are typically yellow-green in color and have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces bright yellow, flaring flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The plant contains various alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine, though it is toxic if ingested.
