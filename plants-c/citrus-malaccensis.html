Citrus malaccensis

Citrus malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca lemon or kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, fragrant fruits with a thick, textured rind and a high concentration of essential oils. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both culinary and aromatic uses.
