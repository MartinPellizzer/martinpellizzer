Centella asiatica var. cristata

Centella asiatica var. cristata, commonly known as the crested centella, is a rare variant of the gotu kola plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, crinkled leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a low-growing herb with a sprawling habit, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits.

This variant is valued for its traditional medicinal uses and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
