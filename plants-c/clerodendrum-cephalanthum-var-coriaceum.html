Clerodendrum cephalanthum var. coriaceum

Clerodendrum cephalanthum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are usually dark green with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and well-drained soil conditions.
