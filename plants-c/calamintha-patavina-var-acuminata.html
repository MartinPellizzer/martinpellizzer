Calamintha patavina var. acuminata

Calamintha patavina var. acuminata, commonly known as narrow-leaved calamint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a strong mint-like scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
