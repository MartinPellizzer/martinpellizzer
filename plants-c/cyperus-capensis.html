Cyperus capensis

Cyperus capensis, commonly known as the Cape flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in coastal areas and estuaries.

Cyperus capensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
