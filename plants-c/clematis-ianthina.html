Clematis ianthina

Clematis ianthina, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Clematis ianthina prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar structural and ecological traits.
