Chiococca pachyphylla

Chiococca pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved chiococca, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a striking addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
