Campanula polymorpha f. exigua

Campanula polymorpha f. exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
