Conospermum brachyphyllum var. laxifolium

Conospermum brachyphyllum var. laxifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved conospermum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
