Carex vulgaris f. ramosa

Carex vulgaris f. ramosa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikelets that produce small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
