Clethra macrophylla

Clethra macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are large, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Clethra macrophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
