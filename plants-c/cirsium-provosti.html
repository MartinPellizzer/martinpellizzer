Cirsium provosti

Cirsium provosti, commonly known as the Provost thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Cirsium provosti is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
