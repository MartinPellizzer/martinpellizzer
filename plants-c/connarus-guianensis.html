Connarus guianensis

Connarus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese connarus, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon and Orinoco basins.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow, and it bears fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds.

Connarus guianensis is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making in its native region.
