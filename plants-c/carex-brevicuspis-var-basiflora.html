Carex brevicuspis var. basiflora

Carex brevicuspis var. basiflora, commonly known as the basal-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in wetland ecosystems.
