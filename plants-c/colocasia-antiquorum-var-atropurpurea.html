Colocasia antiquorum var. atropurpurea

Colocasia antiquorum var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple giant taro, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, often with a deep purple or bronze coloration. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires moist, well-draining soil. It is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other plants like philodendrons and calla lilies.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
