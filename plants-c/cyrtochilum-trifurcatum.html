Cyrtochilum trifurcatum

Cyrtochilum trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-branched slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that bear leaves and flowers. The plant has a unique floral structure with a spurred lip and is typically found in humid, shaded environments. It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer.

Cyrtochilum trifurcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
