Crepis geracioides

Crepis geracioides, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a distinctive lobed or toothed margin.

The flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in a composite head, and are followed by achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.
