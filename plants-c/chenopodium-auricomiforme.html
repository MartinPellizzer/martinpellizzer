Chenopodium auricomiforme

Chenopodium auricomiforme, commonly known as the earleaf chenopod, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive ear-shaped leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects.
