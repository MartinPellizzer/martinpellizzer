Cerasus miyoshii

Cerasus miyoshii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Miyoshi cherry, is a species of Prunus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, tart cherries that are not typically eaten fresh but are used in jams and preserves. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.

Cerasus miyoshii is also a parent species in many cultivated cherry varieties.
