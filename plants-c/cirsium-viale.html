Cirsium viale

Cirsium viale, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to violet flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

Cirsium viale is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
