Campanula rotundifolia f. divaricata

Campanula rotundifolia f. divaricata, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial species characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are rounded and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value. This cultivar is valued for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and its suitability for garden landscapes.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
