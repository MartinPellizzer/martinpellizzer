Cochranea pearcei

Cochranea pearcei, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, puffball-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cochranea pearcei is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
