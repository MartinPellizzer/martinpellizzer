Cousinia khorramabadensis

Cousinia khorramabadensis, commonly known as the Khorramabad thistle, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia khorramabadensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
