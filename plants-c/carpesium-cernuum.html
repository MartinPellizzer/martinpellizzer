Carpesium cernuum

Carpesium cernuum, commonly known as the downy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It produces seeds in a fluffy pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.
