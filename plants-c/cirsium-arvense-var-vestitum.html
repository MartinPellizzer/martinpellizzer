Cirsium arvense var. vestitum

Cirsium arvense var. vestitum, commonly known as yellow cockle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Yellow cockle is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
