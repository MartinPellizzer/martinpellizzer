Capnoides hastata

Capnoides hastata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
