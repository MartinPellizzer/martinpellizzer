Cattleya violacea var. ashworthii

Cattleya violacea var. ashworthii, commonly known as the Ashworth's cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display a combination of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
