Calamagrostis benthamiana

Calamagrostis benthamiana, commonly known as the Bentham's reed, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Calamagrostis benthamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
