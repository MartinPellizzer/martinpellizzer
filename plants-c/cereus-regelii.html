Cereus regelii

Cereus regelii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking white flowers that open at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by moths.

Cereus regelii is popular as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
