Convolvulus duartinus

Convolvulus duartinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Convolvulus duartinus is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it spreads by seeds and rhizomes.
