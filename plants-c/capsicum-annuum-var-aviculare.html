Capsicum annuum var. aviculare

Capsicum annuum var. aviculare, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a variety of the common chili pepper native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, oblong fruits that typically measure around 1 to 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces bright red, orange, or yellow fruits that are often used in cuisines across Southeast Asia and the Caribbean. Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family and is cultivated for its pungent, spicy flavor.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both container gardening and small-scale farming.
