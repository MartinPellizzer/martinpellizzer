Cranichis pittieri

Cranichis pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
