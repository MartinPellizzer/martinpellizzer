Cucurbita clodiensis

Cucurbita clodiensis, commonly known as the Clodiensis pumpkin, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squash. The plant is characterized by its sprawling vines and large, lobed leaves. Its fruits are typically elongated and can vary in color from green to orange when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible seeds and flesh.
