Campbellia

Campbellia, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a genus of flowering plants native to the eastern United States.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive, pointed shape that resembles a bell. Campbellia plants are evergreen shrubs that can grow up to 6 feet tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves.

They thrive in well-drained soil and partial shade, making them popular in ornamental gardens.
