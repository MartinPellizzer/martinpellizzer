Cleistanthus papyraceus

Cleistanthus papyraceus, commonly known as the paper mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in various medicinal applications.
