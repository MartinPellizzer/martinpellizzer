Chrysanthemum japonicola

Chrysanthemum japonicola, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation.
