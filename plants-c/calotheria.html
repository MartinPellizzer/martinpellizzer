Calotheria

Calotheria, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a stout, upright stem with palmately lobed leaves that are often fuzzy to the touch. Calotheria thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
