Chelonopsis rosea

Chelonopsis rosea, commonly known as the rose moss, is a member of the family Hypnaceae and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form.

It features small, leaf-like structures called phyllidia that are often reddish in color, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces sporophytes with a single, elongated capsule that contains spores, typical of mosses. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Chelonopsis rosea is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
