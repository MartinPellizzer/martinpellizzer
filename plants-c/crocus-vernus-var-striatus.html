Crocus vernus var. striatus

Crocus vernus var. striatus, commonly known as the striped crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its distinctive, striped petals that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces erect, narrow leaves and blooms in early spring, typically between February and April. The flowers are often surrounded by a pale green or brownish perianth, which gives the plant a unique appearance.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
