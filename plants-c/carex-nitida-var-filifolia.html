Carex nitida var. filifolia

Carex nitida var. filifolia, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or wetlands.

Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among the foliage.
