Cyperus esculentus var. angustispicatus

Cyperus esculentus var. angustispicatus, commonly known as water chufa or narrow-pedicelled nutsedge, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tubers that are edible and often harvested for food in various parts of the world. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and can be invasive in non-native habitats.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and is closely related to other sedges in the Cyperaceae family.
