Calamagrostis breviseta var. debilis

Calamagrostis breviseta var. debilis, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
