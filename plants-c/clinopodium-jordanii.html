Clinopodium jordanii

Clinopodium jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are small, oval, and emit a strong mint-like fragrance when crushed.

Clinopodium jordanii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
