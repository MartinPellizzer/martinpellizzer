Corryocactus maximus

Corryocactus maximus, commonly known as the giant desert rose, is a striking cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its towering, columnar stems that can reach heights of over 20 feet, covered in thick, waxy spines for protection against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring and summer months. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
