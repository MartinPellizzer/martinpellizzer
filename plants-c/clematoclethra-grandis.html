Clematoclethra grandis

Clematoclethra grandis, commonly known as the largeleaf clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

Clematoclethra grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
