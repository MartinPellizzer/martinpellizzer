Cladium australe

Cladium australe, commonly known as the Australian sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with a fibrous, rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize the soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Cladium australe thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Its dense growth helps in preventing erosion and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
