Cinnagrostis hirta

Cinnagrostis hirta, commonly known as hairy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Cinnagrostis hirta plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during its flowering season.
