Calamagrostis gamblei

Calamagrostis gamblei, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silvery-blue foliage that adds a striking visual element to gardens. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it both low-maintenance and visually appealing.
