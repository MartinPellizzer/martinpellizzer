Crocosmia crocata

Crocosmia crocata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of fiery orange-red flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. Its botanical name reflects its striking appearance, with "crocata" alluding to the color of its blooms.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display and hardy nature.
