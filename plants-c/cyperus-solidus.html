Cyperus solidus

Cyperus solidus, commonly known as spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, ponds, and along waterways.

Cyperus solidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
