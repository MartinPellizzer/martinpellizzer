Chaloupkaea bonorum-hominum

Chaloupkaea bonorum-hominum, commonly known as the "flower of the gods," is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet petals and unique, spiral-shaped arrangement of leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter. Its flowers bloom only during the summer months, adding to its mystique.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected in several conservation areas.
