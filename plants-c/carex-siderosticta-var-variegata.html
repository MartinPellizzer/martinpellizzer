Carex siderosticta var. variegata

Carex siderosticta var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage with distinctive variegation. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its narrow, linear leaves are often striped with white or yellow, adding visual interest to gardens.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
