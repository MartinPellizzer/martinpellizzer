Chrysopogon distichophyllus

Chrysopogon distichophyllus, commonly known as the two-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive two-leaf structure at the base of each stem. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of the grass family.

Chrysopogon distichophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and adaptability.
