Crataegus ingens

Crataegus ingens, commonly known as the giant hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are an important food source for wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other hawthorn species.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a notable plant in ecological and horticultural contexts.
