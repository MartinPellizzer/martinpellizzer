Cirrhopetalum amplifolium

Cirrhopetalum amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cirrhopetalum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
