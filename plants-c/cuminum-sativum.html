Cuminum sativum

Cuminum sativum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has feathery, aromatic leaves. Its seeds, which are harvested when fully mature, are widely used as a spice in various cuisines.

Cumin thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
