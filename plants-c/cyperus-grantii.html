Cyperus grantii

Cyperus grantii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in shallow water or moist soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are often clustered in a panicle.

Cyperus grantii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
