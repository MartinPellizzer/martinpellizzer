Cervaria glauca

Cervaria glauca, commonly known as the blue sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its blue-green foliage is a distinguishing feature, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cervaria glauca is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
