Callista ionopus

Callista ionopus, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
