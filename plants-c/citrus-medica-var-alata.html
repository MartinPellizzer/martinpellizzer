Citrus medica var. alata

Citrus medica var. alata, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date, is a variety of the citron tree.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and have a distinctive wrinkled appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces fragrant white flowers.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.
