Camellia kwangnanica

Camellia kwangnanica, commonly known as the Guangnan camellia, is a species native to southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during late winter to early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and have a unique, slightly wavy petal structure.

Camellia kwangnanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
