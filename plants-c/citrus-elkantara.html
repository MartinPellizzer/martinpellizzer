Citrus elkantara

Citrus elkantara, commonly known as the African bitter orange, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and for its essential oils due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds.

The tree thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
