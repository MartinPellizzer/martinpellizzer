Cheiranthus × cheiri var. varius

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. varius, commonly known as the variable wallflower, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its variable flower colors, ranging from yellow to orange and red, and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in disturbed or arid environments. It produces small, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
