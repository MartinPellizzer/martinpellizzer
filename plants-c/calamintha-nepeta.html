Calamintha nepeta

Calamintha nepeta, commonly known as caraway mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant foliage. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of both mint and caraway.

Calamintha nepeta is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
