Campanula rotundifolia var. vulgaris

Campanula rotundifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, with a distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flower cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
