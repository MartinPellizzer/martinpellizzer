Carex × deinbolliana

Carex × deinbolliana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex brevicuspis and Carex comosa, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically forms compact clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Carex × deinbolliana is often found in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.
