Cucurbita aurantia

Cucurbita aurantia, commonly known as the orange pumpkin, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and Mexico, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant produces large, orange-colored fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. Its vines can grow up to 10 feet long, and the leaves are broad and lobed.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in nutrients and commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
