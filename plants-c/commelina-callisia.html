Commelina callisia

Commelina callisia, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Commelina callisia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
