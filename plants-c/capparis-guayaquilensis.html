Capparis guayaquilensis

Capparis guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody shrub structure with thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by elliptical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Capparis guayaquilensis is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
