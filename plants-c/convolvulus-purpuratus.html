Convolvulus purpuratus

Convolvulus purpuratus, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to support itself on structures or other plants.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
