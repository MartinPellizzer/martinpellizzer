Campanula scheuchzeri f. genuina

Campanula scheuchzeri f. genuina, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a rare and frost-sensitive perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
