Citrullus lanatus var. pumilus

Citrullus lanatus var. pumilus, commonly known as the African watermelon, is a compact variety of the watermelon species.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, pale green rind and juicy, red to yellow flesh. The plant is a vigorous climber with sprawling vines that produce multiple fruits per plant. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its high water content and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and ornamental purposes.
