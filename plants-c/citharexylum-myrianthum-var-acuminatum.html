Citharexylum myrianthum var. acuminatum

Citharexylum myrianthum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
