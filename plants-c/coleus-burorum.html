Coleus burorum

Coleus burorum, commonly known as the Burrow's coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coleus burorum thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
