Cerastium longipedunculatum var. sordidum

Cerastium longipedunculatum var. sordidum, commonly known as the sordid mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, pale green to reddish leaves that are oval to ovate in shape, often covered with fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, borne in clusters at the ends of slender peduncles.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
