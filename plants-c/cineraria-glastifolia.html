Cineraria glastifolia

Cineraria glastifolia, commonly known as the blue cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant features silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Cineraria glastifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
