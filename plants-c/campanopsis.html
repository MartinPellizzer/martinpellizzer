Campanopsis

Campanopsis, commonly known as the bellflower or campanula, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their bell-shaped or tubular flowers, which often display a range of colors from blue to purple. Campanopsis species are typically herbaceous perennials, with some varieties growing as shrubs. They are native to various regions in Asia, particularly China, and are valued for their ornamental appeal in gardens.

The leaves are usually opposite or whorled, and the plants often thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
