Chamaebuxus arillata

Chamaebuxus arillata, commonly known as the silver pine or white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that are typically 2 to 4 inches in length and have a silvery-blue appearance. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Chamaebuxus arillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
