Chamaecytisus falcatus var. grisebachii

Chamaecytisus falcatus var. grisebachii, commonly known as the grayleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by reddish-brown fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
