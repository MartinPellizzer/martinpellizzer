Cyathea falcata

Cyathea falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, narrow shape.

Cyathea falcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shaded, humid environments.
