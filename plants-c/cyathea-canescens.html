Cyathea canescens

Cyathea canescens, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, silver-gray rind. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea canescens thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
