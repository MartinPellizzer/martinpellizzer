Catasetum craniomorphum

Catasetum craniomorphum, commonly known as the skull-shaped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-like flowers that resemble a human skull, giving it its distinctive common name. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, yellow-orange petals and a prominent, elongated lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Catasetum craniomorphum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its striking floral morphology.
