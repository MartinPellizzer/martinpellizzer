Carica peltata

Carica peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and rich in vitamin C. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
