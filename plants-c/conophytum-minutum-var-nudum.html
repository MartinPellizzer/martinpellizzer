Conophytum minutum var. nudum

Conophytum minutum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked conophytum, is a compact, succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
