Carduus arabicus subsp. cinereus

Carduus arabicus subsp.

cinereus, commonly known as the pale thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems covered in dense, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces purple to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny bracts. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine, prickly hairs.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical uses in traditional medicine.
