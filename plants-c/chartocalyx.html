Chartocalyx

Chartocalyx, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its showy, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
