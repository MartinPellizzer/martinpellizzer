Crocus versicolor var. similis

Crocus versicolor var. similis, commonly known as the variable crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the cultivar. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants of the crocus genus. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

The flowers are composed of six petals, with three outer petals that are often more prominent than the inner ones.
