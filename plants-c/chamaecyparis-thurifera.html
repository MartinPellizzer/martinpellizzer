Chamaecyparis thurifera

Chamaecyparis thurifera, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and aromatic foliage, which is dark green on top and silvery white underneath. The tree produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs along the branches. It flowers in the spring with male and female cones, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its timber and is also cultivated in ornamental gardens for its elegant appearance.
