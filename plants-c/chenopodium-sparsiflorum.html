Chenopodium sparsiflorum

Chenopodium sparsiflorum, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples for their purported healing properties.

Chenopodium sparsiflorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
