Chrysanthemum indicum var. procumbens

Chrysanthemum indicum var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping chrysanthemum, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and ground cover.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a versatile plant in horticulture.
