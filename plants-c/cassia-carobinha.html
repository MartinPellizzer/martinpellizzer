Cassia carobinha

Cassia carobinha, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Cassia carobinha is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
