Compsoa maculata

Compsoa maculata, commonly known as the spotted leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both garden landscapes and ecological studies.
