Chrysolepis chrysophylla

Chrysolepis chrysophylla, commonly known as the goldenleaf tanoak, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which is particularly vivid during the autumn season. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate forests and thrives in areas with high humidity and moderate temperatures. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its bark is thick and deeply furrowed.

The goldenleaf tanoak plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
