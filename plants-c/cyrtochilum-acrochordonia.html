Cyrtochilum acrochordonia

Cyrtochilum acrochordonia, commonly known as the acrochordon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, including white, pink, and yellow.

Cyrtochilum acrochordonia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
