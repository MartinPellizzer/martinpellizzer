Clinopodium consentinum

Clinopodium consentinum, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces tiny, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Clinopodium consentinum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
