Cousinia nobilis

Cousinia nobilis, commonly known as the noble cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Cousinia nobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
