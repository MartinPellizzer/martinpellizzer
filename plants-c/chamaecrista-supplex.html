Chamaecrista supplex

Chamaecrista supplex, commonly known as the smallleaf burrlegume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, hard seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used for erosion control in its native habitat.
