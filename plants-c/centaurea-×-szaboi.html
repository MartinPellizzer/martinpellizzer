Centaurea × szaboi

Centaurea × szaboi, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea × szaboi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
