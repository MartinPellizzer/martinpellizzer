Cassia guatemalensis

Cassia guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cassia guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
