Cedrus libani var. deodara

Cedrus libani var. deodara, commonly known as the Deodar cedar, is a variety of the Lebanon cedar native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it a popular choice in construction and furniture-making.

The Deodar cedar is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
