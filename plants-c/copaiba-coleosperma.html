Copaiba coleosperma

Copaiba coleosperma, commonly known as the yellow cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. It produces small, edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
