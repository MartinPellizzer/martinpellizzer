Cytisus tommasinii

Cytisus tommasinii, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. Its small, trifoliate leaves and fluffy, pod-like fruits are typical of the genus Cytisus.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects.
