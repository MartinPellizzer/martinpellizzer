Cyphostemma ornatum

Cyphostemma ornatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, woody stems that can reach several meters in length, often trailing or climbing over other vegetation. The leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in terminal panicles, producing a subtle fragrance.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
