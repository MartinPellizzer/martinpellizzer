Crocus reticulatus var. variegatus

Crocus reticulatus var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and purple, often with distinctive variegation on the petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a valuable addition to gardens during the colder months. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers have faded.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
