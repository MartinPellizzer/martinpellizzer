Carex pinguipes

Carex pinguipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are broad and flat, often with a waxy surface, and the flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

Carex pinguipes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
