Clematis viticella var. coerulea

Clematis viticella var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and its ability to climb using its twining stems. The plant produces showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and provides a lush green backdrop to the blooms.

It is relatively easy to care for and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
