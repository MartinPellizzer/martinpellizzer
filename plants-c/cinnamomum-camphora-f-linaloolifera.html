Cinnamomum camphora f. linaloolifera

Cinnamomum camphora f. linaloolifera, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree or camphor laurel, is a variant of the camphor laurel species native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant, oily bark. The plant is known for producing high levels of linalool, a compound used in perfumery and pharmaceuticals. Its leaves and bark contain essential oils that have been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
