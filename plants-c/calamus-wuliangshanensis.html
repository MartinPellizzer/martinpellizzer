Calamus wuliangshanensis

It is native to China, specifically found in the Wuliangshan mountain range in Sichuan Province. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
