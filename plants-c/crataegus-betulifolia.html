Crataegus betulifolia

Crataegus betulifolia, commonly known as the birchleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its distinctive leaves that resemble those of the birch tree, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, with shallow fissures, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Crataegus betulifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
