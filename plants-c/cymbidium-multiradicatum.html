Cymbidium multiradicatum

Cymbidium multiradicatum, commonly known as the multiradicated orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid that typically grows as a terrestrial plant, often found in forested areas. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces large, showy inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, orchid-like flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red.

Cymbidium multiradicatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
