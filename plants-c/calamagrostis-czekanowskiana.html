Calamagrostis czekanowskiana

Calamagrostis czekanowskiana, commonly known as the Czekanowskian reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with silky, silver-green florets that are highly ornamental. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis czekanowskiana is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
