Cupania semiglauca var. acutifolia

Cupania semiglauca var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved cupania, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
