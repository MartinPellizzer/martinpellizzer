Cryptocarya graveolens

Cryptocarya graveolens, commonly known as the broadleaf sarsaparilla, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that have a distinctive, slightly rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow-green catkins, and it produces edible, red-brown berries.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
