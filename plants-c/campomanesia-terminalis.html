Campomanesia terminalis

Campomanesia terminalis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are a source of nutrition for various wildlife species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
