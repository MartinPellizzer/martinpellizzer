Carex morrisseyi

Carex morrisseyi, commonly known as Morrissey's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny brown flowers.

Morrissey's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
