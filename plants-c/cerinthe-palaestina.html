Cerinthe palaestina

Cerinthe palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine cerinthe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Its historical significance in ancient times adds to its botanical and cultural interest.
