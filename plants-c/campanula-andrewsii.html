Campanula andrewsii

Campanula andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Campanula andrewsii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
