Cymbopogon andongensis

Cymbopogon andongensis, commonly known as the Andong grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
