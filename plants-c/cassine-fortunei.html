Cassine fortunei

Cassine fortunei, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Cassine fortunei is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental landscaping and reforestation efforts.
