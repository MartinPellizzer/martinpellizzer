Calligonum aphyllum var. commune

Calligonum aphyllum var. commune, commonly known as the smooth sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are reduced to small scales or absent, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are adapted for pollination by wind or insects. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a useful plant for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in harsh climates.
