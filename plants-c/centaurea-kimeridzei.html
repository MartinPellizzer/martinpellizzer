Centaurea kimeridzei

Centaurea kimeridzei, commonly known as the Kimeridze's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with prominent yellow centers, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea kimeridzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
