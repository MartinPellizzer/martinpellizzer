Cenchrus echinatus var. longispinus

Cenchrus echinatus var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristly, spiny lemma tips, which give it its common name. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for erosion control.

Its fine foliage and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
