Cerastium octandrum

Cerastium octandrum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers with eight stamens, giving it the species name "octandrum," which refers to its eight stamens. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, rounded fruits called achenes. It typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Cerastium octandrum is often found in temperate regions and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
