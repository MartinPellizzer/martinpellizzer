Crocus reticulatus f. estriatus

Crocus reticulatus f. estriatus, commonly known as the Striped Crocus, is a botanical variant of the reticulatus crocus species.

It is characterized by its distinctive striped pattern on the petals, which sets it apart from other crocus varieties. This perennial plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white, often appearing in early spring.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
