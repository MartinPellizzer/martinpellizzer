Cakile aegyptica var. susica

Cakile aegyptica var. susica, commonly known as the Egyptian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

The species is also notable for its ability to tolerate salt and drought, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
