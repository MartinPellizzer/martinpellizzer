Costus mexicanus

Costus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican costus, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
