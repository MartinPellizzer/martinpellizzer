Carex globuliformis

Carex globuliformis, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit, with dense clusters of grass-like leaves. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Carex globuliformis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
