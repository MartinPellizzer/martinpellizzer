Chamaecytisus proliferus subsp. angustifolius

Chamaecytisus proliferus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in dense, yellowish-green foliage.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive waxy coating.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
