Callista lawiana

Callista lawiana, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.
