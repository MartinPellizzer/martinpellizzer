Crepis fontiana

Crepis fontiana, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Crepis fontiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
