Callista monophylla

Callista monophylla, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Callista monophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
