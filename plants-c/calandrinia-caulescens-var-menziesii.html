Calandrinia caulescens var. menziesii

Calandrinia caulescens var. menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, hairy stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
