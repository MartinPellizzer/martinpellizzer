Cineraria microrhiza

Cineraria microrhiza, commonly known as the dwarf cineraria, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, blue or violet flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a border plant.
