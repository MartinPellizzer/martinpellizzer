Corispermum dilutum var. herbecarpum

Corispermum dilutum var. herbecarpum, commonly known as the slender bladderwrack, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of the North Atlantic.

It thrives in intertidal zones, where it attaches to rocky substrates using a fibrous holdfast. The plant is characterized by its thin, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. Its reproductive structures, known as carpogonia, are borne on specialized stalks and are essential for sexual reproduction.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to saline and wave-exposed environments.
