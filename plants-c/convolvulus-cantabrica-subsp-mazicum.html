Convolvulus cantabrica subsp. mazicum

Convolvulus cantabrica subsp.

mazicum, commonly known as the Cantabrian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, funnel-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
