Charieis caerulea

Charieis caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue petals and a unique, intricate floral structure that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in humid conditions.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
