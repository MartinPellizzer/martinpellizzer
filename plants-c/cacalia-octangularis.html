Cacalia octangularis

Cacalia octangularis, commonly known as the eight-angled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive eight-angled stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cacalia octangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
