Celmisia inaccessa

Celmisia inaccessa, commonly known as the inaccessible celmisia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
