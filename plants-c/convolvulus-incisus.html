Convolvulus incisus

Convolvulus incisus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, winding stems that often climb or trail along the ground. Its heart-shaped leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom in the morning.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
