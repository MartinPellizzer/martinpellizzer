Convolvulus guadaloupensis

Convolvulus guadaloupensis, commonly known as the Guadaloupe morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has a trailing growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
