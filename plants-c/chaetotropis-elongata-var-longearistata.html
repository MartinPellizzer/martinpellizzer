Chaetotropis elongata var. longearistata

Chaetotropis elongata var. longearistata, commonly known as the long-awned chaetotropis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and distinctive, hairy bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in natural habitats.
