Crepis lactea

Crepis lactea, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-tipped leaves and purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its milky sap. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often hairy.

Crepis lactea is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
