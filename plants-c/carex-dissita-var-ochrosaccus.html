Carex dissita var. ochrosaccus

Carex dissita var. ochrosaccus, commonly known as the yellow-sac sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
