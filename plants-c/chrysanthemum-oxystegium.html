Chrysanthemum oxystegium

Chrysanthemum oxystegium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to white, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
