Croton aucubifolius

Croton aucubifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
