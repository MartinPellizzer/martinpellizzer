Cyperus flavomariscus var. peduncularis

Cyperus flavomariscus var. peduncularis, commonly known as the yellow marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features slender, grass-like stems that often grow in dense clusters, with narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically yellow or brown, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
