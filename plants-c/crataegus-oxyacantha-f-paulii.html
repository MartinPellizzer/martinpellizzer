Crataegus oxyacantha f. paulii

Crataegus oxyacantha f. paulii, commonly known as the Pauli's hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring serrated leaves and a compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
