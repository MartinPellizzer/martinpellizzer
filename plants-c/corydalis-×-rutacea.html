Corydalis × rutacea

Corydalis × rutacea, commonly known as the hybrid Chinese corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to regions of East Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
