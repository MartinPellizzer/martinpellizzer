Cuviera

Cuviera, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Cuviera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
