Clematis pseudoalpina var. tenuiloba

Clematis pseudoalpina var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the slenderleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, actinomorphic flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens, typically in shades of white or pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
