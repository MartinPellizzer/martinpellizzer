Crucifera multifida

Crucifera multifida, commonly known as the forked crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence and small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and has alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a feature common among members of the crucifer family.

Due to its unique morphology, it is often studied for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
