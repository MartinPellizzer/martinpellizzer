Cleome rutidosperma var. rutidosperma

Cleome rutidosperma var. rutidosperma, commonly known as the common cleome, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features erect, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cleome rutidosperma var. rutidosperma is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
