Cardamomum dealbatum

Cardamomum dealbatum, commonly known as the Indian cardamom or black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown pods containing aromatic seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

The plant is valued for its unique flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
