Croton jaegerianus

Croton jaegerianus, commonly known as the flame bush, is a strikingly colorful shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored leaves that can range from yellow and orange to red and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its bold foliage, it is often used as a focal point in landscape designs.
