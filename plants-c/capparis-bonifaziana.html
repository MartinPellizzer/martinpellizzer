Capparis bonifaziana

Capparis bonifaziana, commonly known as the Canary Island Caper, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid and saline conditions of its native habitat.

Capparis bonifaziana is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
