Cupressus lawsoniana var. glauca

Cupressus lawsoniana var. glauca, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress, is a variant of the Lawson cypress native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are scale-like and tightly packed, contributing to its compact form. It produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
