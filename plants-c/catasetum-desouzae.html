Catasetum desouzae

Catasetum desouzae, commonly known as the Desouza's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with red veins, and it produces a distinctive, fleshy structure called a gynostemium.

Catasetum desouzae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
