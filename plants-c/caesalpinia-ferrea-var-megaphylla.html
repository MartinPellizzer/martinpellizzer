Caesalpinia ferrea var. megaphylla

Caesalpinia ferrea var. megaphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood tree, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and long, straight pods that contain seeds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers during the dry season. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in construction and furniture-making.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
