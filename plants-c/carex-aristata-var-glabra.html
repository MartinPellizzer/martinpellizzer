Carex aristata var. glabra

Carex aristata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth arrow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spiky, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Its smooth, glabrous leaves and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
