Cyperus daphaenus

Cyperus daphaenus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or marshy environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
