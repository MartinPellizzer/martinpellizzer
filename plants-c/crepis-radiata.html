Crepis radiata

Crepis radiata, commonly known as the radiate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, showy head. The plant has a hairy stem and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Crepis radiata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
