Crataegus dayana

Crataegus dayana, commonly known as the Turkish hawthorn, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, including Turkey, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, mature in the autumn and are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The botanical features of Crataegus dayana make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
