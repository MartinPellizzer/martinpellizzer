Centaurea rutifolia subsp. jurineifolia

Centaurea rutifolia subsp.

jurineifolia, commonly known as the Jurine's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely leafy stems and produces small, pink to purple flowers in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
