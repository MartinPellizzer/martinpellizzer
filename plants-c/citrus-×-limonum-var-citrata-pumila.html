Citrus × limonum var. citrata-pumila

Citrus × limonum var. citrata-pumila, commonly known as the sour orange.

This variety is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

It is also a parent species in the hybridization of many citrus fruits.
