Capparis spinosa var. sicula

Capparis spinosa var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, greenish-brown fruits known as capers, which are harvested before they fully mature. These fruits are used in culinary applications and have a tangy, slightly spicy flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is closely related to other caper species.
