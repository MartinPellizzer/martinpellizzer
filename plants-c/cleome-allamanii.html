Cleome allamanii

Cleome allamanii, commonly known as the swamp cleome, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a hairy texture.

Cleome allamanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
