Cousinia mutehensis

Cousinia mutehensis, commonly known as the Persian goatgrass, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Cousinia mutehensis is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
