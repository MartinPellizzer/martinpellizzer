Copiapoa cinerea nothosubsp. scopa

Copiapoa cinerea nothosubsp.

scopa, commonly known as the Scopa Copiapoa, is a hybrid cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
