Convolvulus hallierianus

Convolvulus hallierianus, commonly known as the Hallier's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
