Cineraria palustris var. edentula

Cineraria palustris var. edentula, commonly known as the marsh cineraria, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wetland environments in Europe.

It features hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
