Carex firmula subsp. tenuis

Carex firmula subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in dense, compact clusters. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
