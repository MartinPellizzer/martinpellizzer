Chlorophytum filipendulum subsp. amaniense

Chlorophytum filipendulum subsp.

amaniense, commonly known as the Amani spider plant, is a succulent plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its long, arching stems that bear clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by distinctive, umbrella-shaped seed heads. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
