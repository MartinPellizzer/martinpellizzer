Calceolaria hieronymi

Calceolaria hieronymi, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped flowers that resemble small bells, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Calceolaria hieronymi is also noted for its ability to tolerate shade, making it a versatile choice for various horticultural settings.
