Cryptantha pterocarya var. genuina

Cryptantha pterocarya var. genuina, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, pink or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Cryptantha pterocarya var. genuina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
