Cymbidium × veitchii

Cymbidium × veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species originating from crosses between Cymbidium goeringii and Cymbidium lowianum.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is evergreen and thrives in cool, humid climates with bright, indirect light. It requires well-draining soil and moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Cymbidium × veitchii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
