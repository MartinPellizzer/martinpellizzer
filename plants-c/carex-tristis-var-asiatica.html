Carex tristis var. asiatica

Carex tristis var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.
