Carduncellus caeruleus var. dentatus

Carduncellus caeruleus var. dentatus, commonly known as the blue thistle or purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has spiny, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a contrast to its vibrant blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
