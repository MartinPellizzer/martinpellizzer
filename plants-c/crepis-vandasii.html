Crepis vandasii

Crepis vandasii, commonly known as the Vand's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Crepis vandasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
