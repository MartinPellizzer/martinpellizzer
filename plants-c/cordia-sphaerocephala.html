Cordia sphaerocephala

Cordia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the African star fruit or yellow star tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, star-shaped fruits that are yellow to orange in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
