Calappa acaulis

Calappa acaulis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a short-lived perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a dense cluster of small, yellow flowers surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are basal, succulent, and often covered in fine hairs.

Calappa acaulis is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
