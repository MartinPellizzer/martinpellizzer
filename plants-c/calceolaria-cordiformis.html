Calceolaria cordiformis

Calceolaria cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped calyx, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, slipper-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calceolaria cordiformis is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique floral structure.
