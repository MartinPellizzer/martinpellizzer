Cerasus jamasakura f. pubescens

Cerasus jamasakura f. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with pubescent foliage, is a cultivar of the Prunus species.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves, which distinguish it from other cherry blossom varieties. The plant produces delicate pink or white flowers in early spring, which are highly valued in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal during the blooming season.
