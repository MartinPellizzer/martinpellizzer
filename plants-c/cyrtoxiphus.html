Cyrtoxiphus

Cyrtoxiphus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, tuberous roots and their showy, velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors. Cyrtoxiphus species typically have rosettes of narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. They thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
