Citrus × aurantiifolia var. latifolia

Citrus × aurantiifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the lime or key lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, round, green fruits that are typically harvested when they are still unripe and contain a high concentration of citric acid. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a glossy dark green surface. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking, beverages, and traditional medicine.
