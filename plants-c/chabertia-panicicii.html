Chabertia panicicii

Chabertia panicicii, commonly known as the panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its flowers are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Chabertia panicicii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
