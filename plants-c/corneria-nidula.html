Corneria nidula

Corneria nidula, commonly known as the slender mistplant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery hair, which aids in reducing water loss.

Corneria nidula is often found growing in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal water.
