Cleome brevisiliqua

Cleome brevisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Cleome brevisiliqua is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
