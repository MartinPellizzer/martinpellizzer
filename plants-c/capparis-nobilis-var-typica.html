Capparis nobilis var. typica

Capparis nobilis var. typica, commonly known as the noble caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, edible seeds.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for the use of its flowers in culinary applications.
