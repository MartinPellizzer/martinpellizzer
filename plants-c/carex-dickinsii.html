Carex dickinsii

Carex dickinsii, commonly known as the Dickins sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex dickinsii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
