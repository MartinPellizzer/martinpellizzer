Connarus haemorrhaeus

Connarus haemorrhaeus, commonly known as the bloodwood tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The tree is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown bark, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
