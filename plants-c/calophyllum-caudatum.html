Calophyllum caudatum

Calophyllum caudatum, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown to black bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces yellowish-white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
