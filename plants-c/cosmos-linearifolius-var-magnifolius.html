Cosmos linearifolius var. magnifolius

Cosmos linearifolius var. magnifolius, commonly known as the Mexican aster or showy cosmos, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow center. The leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a feathery pattern along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is known for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
