Cotula coronopifolia var. integrifolia

Cotula coronopifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is a perennial herb that produces numerous small flowers throughout the growing season.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
