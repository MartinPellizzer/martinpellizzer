Crocanthemum stipulatum

Crocanthemum stipulatum, commonly known as the stipule daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Crocanthemum stipulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
