Cortia striata

Cortia striata, commonly known as the striped cortia, is a member of the Rubiaceae family, known for its distinctive appearance.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves with prominent yellow veins that create a striking striped pattern. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding to its ornamental value. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Cortia striata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
