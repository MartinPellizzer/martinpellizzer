Cyperus racemosus var. khasianus

Cyperus racemosus var. khasianus, commonly known as Khasi flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

It produces small, clustered flower spikes that are usually inconspicuous, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
