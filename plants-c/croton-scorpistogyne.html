Croton scorpistogyne

Croton scorpistogyne, commonly known as the stinging croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves and stems contain irritant chemicals, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
