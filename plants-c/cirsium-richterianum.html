Cirsium richterianum

Cirsium richterianum, commonly known as the Richter's thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
