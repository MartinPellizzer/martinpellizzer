Carex lasiolepis

Carex lasiolepis, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex lasiolepis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
