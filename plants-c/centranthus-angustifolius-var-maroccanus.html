Centranthus angustifolius var. maroccanus

Centranthus angustifolius var. maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan narrow-leaved red campion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red, pink, or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and can thrive in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
