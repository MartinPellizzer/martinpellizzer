Conyza andicola

Conyza andicola, commonly known as the Andes weed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields. Its small, white to yellow flowers are followed by fluffy seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Conyza andicola is considered an invasive species in some regions, posing challenges to local ecosystems and agriculture.
