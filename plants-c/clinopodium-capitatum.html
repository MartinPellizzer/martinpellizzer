Clinopodium capitatum

Clinopodium capitatum, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features compact, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Clinopodium capitatum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
