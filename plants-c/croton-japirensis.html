Croton japirensis

Croton japirensis, commonly known as the Japirensis croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often milky sap-containing species. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that can range from green to red and orange, making it visually striking. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
