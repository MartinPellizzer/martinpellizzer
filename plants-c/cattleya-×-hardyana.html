Cattleya × hardyana

Cattleya × hardyana, commonly known as the Hardy Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from crosses between Cattleya mossieri and Cattleya warscewiczii.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant features large, showy flowers with vibrant colors, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow, and a distinctive, fragrant scent. It has long, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This orchid is prized for its beauty and is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
