Cynoglossum dioscoridis subvar. xatarti

Cynoglossum dioscoridis subvar. xatarti, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine settings.
