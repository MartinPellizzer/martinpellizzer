Callista marmorata

Callista marmorata, commonly known as the marble plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its blooms are typically yellow or orange, often with a marbled pattern, giving it its common name.

Callista marmorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
