Calamus arfakianus var. imberbis

Calamus arfakianus var. imberbis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed arrowroot plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea and is characterized by its stout, smooth, and often ribbed rhizomes. The plant produces large, umbrella-like leaves that can reach several meters in length, and its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The rhizomes are valued for their starchy texture and are used as a food source in some local communities.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in swampy areas.
