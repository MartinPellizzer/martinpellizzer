Commiphora fulvotomentosa

Commiphora fulvotomentosa, commonly known as the yellow myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The plant typically has smooth, grayish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually followed by small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
