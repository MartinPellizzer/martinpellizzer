Coriaria luracparva

Coriaria luracparva, commonly known as the dwarf coriander, is a small evergreen shrub native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Coriariaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
