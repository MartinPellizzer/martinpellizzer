Carex brownii

Carex brownii, commonly known as Brown's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in wetland and marshy habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex brownii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
