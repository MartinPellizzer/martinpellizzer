Cirsium stenobracteatum

Cirsium stenobracteatum, commonly known as the narrow-bracted thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive narrow bract at the base of each flower head.

Cirsium stenobracteatum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
