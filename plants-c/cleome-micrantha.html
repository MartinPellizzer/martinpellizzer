Cleome micrantha

Cleome micrantha, commonly known as smallflower spiderflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems and produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are often surrounded by fine, hair-like structures. Cleome micrantha is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
