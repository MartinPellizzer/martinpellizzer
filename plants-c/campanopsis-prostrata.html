Campanopsis prostrata

Campanopsis prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
