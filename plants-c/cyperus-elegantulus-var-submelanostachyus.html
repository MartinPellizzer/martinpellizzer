Cyperus elegantulus var. submelanostachyus

Cyperus elegantulus var. submelanostachyus, commonly known as the black-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, characterized by their triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

The variety submelanostachyus is distinguished by its darker, more pigmented stems compared to the typical variety.
