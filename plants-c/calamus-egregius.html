Calamus egregius

Calamus egregius, commonly known as the noble arrowroot, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
