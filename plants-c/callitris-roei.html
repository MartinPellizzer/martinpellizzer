Callitris roei

Callitris roei, commonly known as the slender cypress, is a species of conifer native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces cones that contain both male and female reproductive structures, typical of coniferous species. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

The slender cypress plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
