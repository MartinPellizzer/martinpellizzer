Chilopogon oxysepalum

Chilopogon oxysepalum, commonly known as the narrow-sepal orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers with distinctive narrow sepals. Its leaves are typically elongated and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species within its native range.
