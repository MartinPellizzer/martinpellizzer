Chrysanthemum leptophyllum

Chrysanthemum leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates with cool summers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Chrysanthemum leptophyllum is a hardy perennial that prefers partial shade and is relatively low maintenance in garden settings.
