Carex torta var. staminata

Carex torta var. staminata, commonly known as the staminated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
