Chloris capensis var. glabrescens

Chloris capensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth chloris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
