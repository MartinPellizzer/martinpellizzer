Crassula sediflora

Crassula sediflora, commonly known as the stone rose or velvet plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
