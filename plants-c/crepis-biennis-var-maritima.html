Crepis biennis var. maritima

Crepis biennis var. maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet ray florets and yellow disk flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in saline environments.

Crepis biennis var. maritima is an important plant in coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
