Clematis florida var. hancockiana

Clematis florida var. hancockiana, commonly known as the Hancock's Virgin's Bower, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, fluffy, white fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
