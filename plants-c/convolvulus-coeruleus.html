Convolvulus coeruleus

Convolvulus coeruleus, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
