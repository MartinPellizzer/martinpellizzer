Celtis laeta

Celtis laeta, commonly known as the white ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, with shallow fissures, and it can grow up to 30 meters tall.

Celtis laeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
