Chimonocalamus cibarius

Chimonocalamus cibarius, commonly known as the edible bamboo or bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, feathery foliage. The plant is notable for its edible shoots, which are often harvested and consumed in various traditional cuisines. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions make it a resilient species.

However, it requires careful management to prevent overharvesting and ensure its sustainability in natural habitats.
