Caprifolium coerulescens

Caprifolium coerulescens, commonly known as the blue honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a red or yellow color, often containing several seeds.

Caprifolium coerulescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
