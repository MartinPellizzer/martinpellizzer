Cerasus domestica

Cerasus domestica, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its edible, sweet fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–10 meters, with simple, alternate leaves and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit develops from the flower and ripens in summer, often reaching sizes of 1–3 centimeters in diameter.

The botanical features of Cerasus domestica make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
