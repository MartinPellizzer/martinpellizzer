Crinum eleonorae

Crinum eleonorae, commonly known as the Eleanor's Crinum, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale pink blooms that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often emerge from a central bulb.

Crinum eleonorae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
