Cyperus laetus var. arechavaletae

Cyperus laetus var. arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
