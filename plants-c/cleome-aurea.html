Cleome aurea

Cleome aurea, commonly known as the golden cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom from summer to early fall. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Cleome aurea is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
