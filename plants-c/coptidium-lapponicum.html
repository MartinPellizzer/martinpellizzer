Coptidium lapponicum

Coptidium lapponicum, commonly known as the Lapland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tundra and boreal forest environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Coptidium lapponicum is adapted to cold climates and can survive under harsh environmental conditions.
