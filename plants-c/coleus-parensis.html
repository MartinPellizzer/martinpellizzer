Coleus parensis

Coleus parensis, commonly known as the mountain coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and produces small, tubular flowers in clusters.

Coleus parensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates.
