Crepis modocensis

Crepis modocensis, commonly known as Modoc mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are surrounded by bracts.

Crepis modocensis is notable for its ability to thrive in arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
