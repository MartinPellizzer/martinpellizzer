Cattleya harrisoniana

Cattleya harrisoniana, commonly known as the Harrison's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant features long, arching inflorescences and is known for its relatively large, showy petals and sepals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with good air circulation and partial shade.

Cattleya harrisoniana is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
