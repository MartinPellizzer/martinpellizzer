Cynanchum hardyi

Cynanchum hardyi, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic uses.
