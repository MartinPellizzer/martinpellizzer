Cattleya aclandiae

Cattleya aclandiae, commonly known as the Acland's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments.

Cattleya aclandiae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
