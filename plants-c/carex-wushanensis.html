Carex wushanensis

Carex wushanensis, commonly known as the Wushan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides.

Carex wushanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
