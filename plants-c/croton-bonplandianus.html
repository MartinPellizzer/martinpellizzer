Croton bonplandianus

Croton bonplandianus, commonly known as the red croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that range from deep red to yellow and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often striking members. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
