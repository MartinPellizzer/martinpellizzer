Crotalaria parsonsii

Crotalaria parsonsii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and small, palmately lobed leaves. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Crotalaria parsonsii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
