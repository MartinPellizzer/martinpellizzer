Chrysodium praestantissimum

Chrysodium praestantissimum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The botanical name reflects its striking appearance and prominent status among native flora.
