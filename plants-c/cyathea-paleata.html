Cyathea paleata

Cyathea paleata, commonly known as the pale lady fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a layer of dead fronds, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. Cyathea paleata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
