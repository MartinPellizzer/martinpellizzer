Calendula

Calendula, commonly known as the pot marigold, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange petals. The plant produces daisy-like flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. Calendula is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used for centuries in herbal medicine.

Its flowers contain compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
