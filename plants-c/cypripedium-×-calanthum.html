Cypripedium × calanthum

Cypripedium × calanthum, commonly known as the yellow lily of the valley orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive lip that often has a contrasting color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has rhizomatous growth habits. Its botanical name reflects its origin from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium pubescens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and fragrant blooms.
