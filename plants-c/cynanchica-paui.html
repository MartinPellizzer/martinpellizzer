Cynanchica paui

Cynanchica paui, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
