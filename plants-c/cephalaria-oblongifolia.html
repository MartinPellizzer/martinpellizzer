Cephalaria oblongifolia

Cephalaria oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal umbels, and typically range in color from white to pale purple.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shady woodland areas.
