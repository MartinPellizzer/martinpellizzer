Carex muriculata

Carex muriculata, commonly known as the rough-skinned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces distinctive, spiky seed heads.

Carex muriculata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
