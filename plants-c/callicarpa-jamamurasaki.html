Callicarpa jamamurasaki

Callicarpa jamamurasaki, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple to pink berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and are used in traditional medicine.
