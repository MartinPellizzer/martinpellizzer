Calanthe parvilabris

Calanthe parvilabris, commonly known as the small-lipped calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive small lip. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a yellowish throat, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Calanthe parvilabris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in shaded, moist environments.
