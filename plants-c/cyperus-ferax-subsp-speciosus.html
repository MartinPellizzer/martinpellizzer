Cyperus ferax subsp. speciosus

Cyperus ferax subsp.

speciosus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
