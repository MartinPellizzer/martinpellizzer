Convolvulus fruticosus f. tianschanica

Convolvulus fruticosus f. tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed found in the Tianshan mountain range.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale purple or white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing along slopes and in open habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
