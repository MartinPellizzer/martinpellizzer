Capsicum indicum var. sphaerium

Capsicum indicum var. sphaerium, commonly known as the round bell pepper, is a variety of Capsicum indicum native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, thick-walled fruits that are typically red, yellow, or orange when mature. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit develops from a single ovary and is used both for culinary and ornamental purposes.
