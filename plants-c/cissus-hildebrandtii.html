Cissus hildebrandtii

Cissus hildebrandtii, commonly known as the rock fig or devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Cissus hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
