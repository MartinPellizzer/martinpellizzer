Cunonia rupicola

Cunonia rupicola, commonly known as the rock cunonia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Cunonia rupicola is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species found in various dry habitats.
