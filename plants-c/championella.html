Championella

Championella, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native gardens.
