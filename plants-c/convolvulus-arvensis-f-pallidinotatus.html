Convolvulus arvensis f. pallidinotatus

Convolvulus arvensis f. pallidinotatus, commonly known as the pale-spotted field bindweed, is a variant of the field bindweed, characterized by its distinctive pale markings on the leaves and stems.

It is a flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family, known for its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a spurred structure at the base. This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of study in plant morphology and ecology.
