Capparis ecuadorica

Capparis ecuadorica, commonly known as the Ecuadorian caper, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that mature into a reddish-brown color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

Capparis ecuadorica is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.
