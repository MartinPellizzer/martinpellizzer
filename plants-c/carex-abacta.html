Carex abacta

Carex abacta, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Carex abacta is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
