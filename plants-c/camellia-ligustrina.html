Camellia ligustrina

Camellia ligustrina, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish or purple hue during the autumn season. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the winter and early spring. Its flowers are borne on short, hairy pedicels and have a five-lobed, saucer-shaped corolla.

Camellia ligustrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal floral display.
