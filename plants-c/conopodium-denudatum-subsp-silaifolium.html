Conopodium denudatum subsp. silaifolium

Conopodium denudatum subsp.

silaifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows.

The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
