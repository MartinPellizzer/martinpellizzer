Clematis acuminata var. hookeri

Clematis acuminata var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length, supported by tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and have a velvety texture.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
