Convolvulus arvensis var. macrophyllus

Convolvulus arvensis var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf field bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind and animals. Its rapid growth and aggressive spreading habit make it a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing or trailing growth pattern.
