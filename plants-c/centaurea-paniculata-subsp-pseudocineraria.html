Centaurea paniculata subsp. pseudocineraria

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

pseudocineraria, commonly known as the silver centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale pink seed heads that resemble small clouds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
