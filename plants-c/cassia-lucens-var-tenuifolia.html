Cassia lucens var. tenuifolia

Cassia lucens var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender leaf cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often tinged with silver or white on the underside. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
