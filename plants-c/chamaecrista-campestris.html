Chamaecrista campestris

Chamaecrista campestris, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow or orange pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Chamaecrista campestris is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
