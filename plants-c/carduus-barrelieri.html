Carduus barrelieri

Carduus barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive, tall stem that supports its purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Carduus barrelieri is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
