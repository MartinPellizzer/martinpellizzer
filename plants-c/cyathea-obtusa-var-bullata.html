Cyathea obtusa var. bullata

Cyathea obtusa var. bullata, commonly known as the Bullate Tree Fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive, smooth, bark-like trunk that is often covered in a layer of spore-producing structures called sori. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow to impressive lengths, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
