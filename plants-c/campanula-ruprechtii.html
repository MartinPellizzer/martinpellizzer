Campanula ruprechtii

Campanula ruprechtii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Campanula ruprechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
