Carex yodoensis

Carex yodoensis, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green color, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Carex yodoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of wet conditions.
