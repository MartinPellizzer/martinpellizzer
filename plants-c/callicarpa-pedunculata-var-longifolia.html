Callicarpa pedunculata var. longifolia

Callicarpa pedunculata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
