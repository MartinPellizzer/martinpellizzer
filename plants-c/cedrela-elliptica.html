Cedrela elliptica

Cedrela elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or yellow cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its straight, durable wood that is highly valued in the furniture and construction industries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Cedrela elliptica is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
