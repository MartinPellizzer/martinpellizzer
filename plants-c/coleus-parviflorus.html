Coleus parviflorus

Coleus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered coleus, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and can also be grown as a container plant.
