Chimonanthus grammatus

Chimonanthus grammatus, commonly known as the fragrant winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its unique flowering period and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
