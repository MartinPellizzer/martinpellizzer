Cassia insularis

Cassia insularis, commonly known as the island cassia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Cassia insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
