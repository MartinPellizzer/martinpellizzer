Carex variegata

Carex variegata, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant features striking variegated foliage with white and green patterns, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Carex variegata is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
