Cliffortia teretifolia var. alata

Cliffortia teretifolia var. alata, commonly known as the winged cliffortia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The variety alata distinguishes itself by its distinctive wing-like structures on the stems, which provide structural support and aid in water retention. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
