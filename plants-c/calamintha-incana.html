Calamintha incana

Calamintha incana, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with a fuzzy texture due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Calamintha incana is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
