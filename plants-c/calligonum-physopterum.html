Calligonum physopterum

Calligonum physopterum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and saline soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and adapted to reduce water loss.

Calligonum physopterum plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
