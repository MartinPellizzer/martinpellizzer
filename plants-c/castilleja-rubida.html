Castilleja rubida

Castilleja rubida, commonly known as the scarlet cup.

It is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the roots of other plants, drawing nutrients from them. The plant is native to the western United States and grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its striking red flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
