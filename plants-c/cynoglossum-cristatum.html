Cynoglossum cristatum

Cynoglossum cristatum, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crested leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often used in landscaping and as a garden filler.
