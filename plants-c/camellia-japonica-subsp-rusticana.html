Camellia japonica subsp. rusticana

Camellia japonica subsp.

rusticana, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the calyx. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
