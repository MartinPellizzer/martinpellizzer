Clusia rotundata

Clusia rotundata, commonly known as the waxleaf croton, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are typically oval or rounded in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Clusia rotundata is also known for its adaptability to different light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
