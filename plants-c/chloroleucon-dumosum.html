Chloroleucon dumosum

Chloroleucon dumosum, commonly known as the creeping dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered with fine, white hairs.

Chloroleucon dumosum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
