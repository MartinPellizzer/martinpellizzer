Cactus foliosus

Cactus foliosus, commonly known as the leaf cactus, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a tall, branching stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often adorned with clusters of small, fleshy leaves. These leaves are typically green and cylindrical, serving as the primary means of photosynthesis for the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ability to store water in its leaves and stems, it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
