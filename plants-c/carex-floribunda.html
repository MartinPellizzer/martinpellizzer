Carex floribunda

Carex floribunda, commonly known as the flowering sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Carex floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
