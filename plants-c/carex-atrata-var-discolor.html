Carex atrata var. discolor

Carex atrata var. discolor, commonly known as the dark-eyed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its distinctive dark-colored flowers and foliage make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
