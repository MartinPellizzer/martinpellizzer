Catasetum ollare

Catasetum ollare, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without being parasitic. Its flowers are known for their unique structure, with specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific pollinators.

Catasetum ollare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
