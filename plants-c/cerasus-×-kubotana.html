Cerasus × kubotana

Cerasus × kubotana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yaezakura, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its striking, double-flowered blossoms that appear in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant display to the landscape before the leaves emerge.
