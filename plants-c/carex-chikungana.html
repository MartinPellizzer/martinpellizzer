Carex chikungana

Carex chikungana, commonly known as the African flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Carex chikungana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
