Calamintha parvula

Calamintha parvula, commonly known as smallflower germander, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Calamintha parvula is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
