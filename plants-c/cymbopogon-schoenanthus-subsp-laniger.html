Cymbopogon schoenanthus subsp. laniger

Cymbopogon schoenanthus subsp.

laniger, commonly known as the narrow-leaved citron grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is often found in moist, tropical environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Cymbopogon genus.
