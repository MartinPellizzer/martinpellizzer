Canna denudata var. grandis

Canna denudata var. grandis, commonly known as the giant canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces showy, orange to red inflorescences that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in warm climates for its dramatic foliage and floral display.
