Cerasus conradinae

Cerasus conradinae, commonly known as the Conrad's cherry, is a rare species native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red berries. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
