Croton urticifolius var. intermedius

Croton urticifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features palmate leaves with serrated edges, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
