Cyperus pulchellus

Cyperus pulchellus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, often growing in wet or marshy habitats. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus pulchellus is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive purple-tinged foliage.
