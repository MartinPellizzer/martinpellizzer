Cerastium fischerianum

Cerastium fischerianum, commonly known as Fischer's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Cerastium fischerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
