Cestrum bracteatum var. amictum

Cestrum bracteatum var. amictum, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
