Circaeocarpus saururoides

Circaeocarpus saururoides, commonly known as the dragon's claw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence that resembles a dragon's claw, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered along the stem, followed by distinctive, woody fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
