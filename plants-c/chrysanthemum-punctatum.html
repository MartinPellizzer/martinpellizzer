Chrysanthemum punctatum

Chrysanthemum punctatum, commonly known as the dotted chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a slightly rough texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
