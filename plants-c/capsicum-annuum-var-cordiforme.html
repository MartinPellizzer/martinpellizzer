Capsicum annuum var. cordiforme

Capsicum annuum var. cordiforme, commonly known as the heart-shaped pepper, is a variant of the common bell pepper.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped fruits, which are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil. It produces clusters of white flowers that develop into the characteristic heart-shaped peppers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it popular in both culinary and decorative settings.
