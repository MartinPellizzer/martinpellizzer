Croton sclerocalyx var. lanceolatus

Croton sclerocalyx var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal flowering.
