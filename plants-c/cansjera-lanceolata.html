Cansjera lanceolata

Cansjera lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf candelabra, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Cansjera lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
