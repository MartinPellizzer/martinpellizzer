Crucifera roripa

Crucifera roripa, commonly known as the mountain mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy and adapted to high-altitude environments. Its fruits are pod-like siliques that contain several seeds.

Crucifera roripa is native to mountainous regions in Asia and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
