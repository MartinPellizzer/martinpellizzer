Campanula persicifolia var. crystallocalyx

Campanula persicifolia var. crystallocalyx, commonly known as the Crystal Flower or Persian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant a distinctive, frosty appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and compact growth habit.
