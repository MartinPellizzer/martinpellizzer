Cyathea baileyana

Cyathea baileyana, commonly known as the Bailey's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Cyathea baileyana reproduces via spores, which are released from the sporangia located on the underside of its fronds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to humid, tropical climates.
