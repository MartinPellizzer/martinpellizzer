Canthium wildii

Canthium wildii, commonly known as the African wild cucumber, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
