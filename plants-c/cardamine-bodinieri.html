Cardamine bodinieri

Cardamine bodinieri, commonly known as the Vietnamese watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Botanically, it is noted for its finely divided leaves and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
