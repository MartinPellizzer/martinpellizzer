Chenopodium trigonon subsp. leiocarpum

Chenopodium trigonon subsp.

leiocarpum, commonly known as smooth-seeded goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of North America. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its subspecies name, *leiocarpum*, refers to its smooth, hairless fruits.

This species is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional culinary practices in some indigenous communities.
