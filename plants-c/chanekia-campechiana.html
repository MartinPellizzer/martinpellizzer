Chanekia campechiana

Chanekia campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Chanekia campechiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
