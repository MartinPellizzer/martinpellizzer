Coccinia ecirrhosa

Coccinia ecirrhosa, commonly known as the smooth leaf cucumber or African horned melon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fruit resembling a melon with a spiky exterior. The fruit is typically green when unripe and turns orange-red when mature, containing a sweet, edible pulp with numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cuisines.
