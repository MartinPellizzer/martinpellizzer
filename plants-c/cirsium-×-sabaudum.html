Cirsium × sabaudum

Cirsium × sabaudum, commonly known as the Sabaudian thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the European Alps, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and high-altitude environments. Its morphology reflects a combination of traits from its parent species, Cirsium arvense and Cirsium discolor.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
