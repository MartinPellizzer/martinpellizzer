Calamus cheirophyllus

Calamus cheirophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Calamus cheirophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in tropical gardens.
