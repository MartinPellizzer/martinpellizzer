Caesalpinia solomonensis

Caesalpinia solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon's glory, is a flowering plant native to the Solomon Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
