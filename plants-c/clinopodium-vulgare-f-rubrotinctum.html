Clinopodium vulgare f. rubrotinctum

Clinopodium vulgare f. rubrotinctum, commonly known as red-stemmed betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in whorls. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological interest.
