Chenopodium foggii

Chenopodium foggii, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples, and it is also a source of food for some wildlife species.

The plant's resilience and unique morphology make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
