Cobaea stipularis

Cobaea stipularis, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has a climbing habit and produces tendrils that help it ascend support structures. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Cobaea stipularis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
