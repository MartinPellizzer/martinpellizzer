Cardamine punicea

Cardamine punicea, commonly known as the pink watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Cardamine punicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
