Chloris clementis

Chloris clementis, commonly known as the Clement's grass, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to blue-gray flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Chloris clementis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
