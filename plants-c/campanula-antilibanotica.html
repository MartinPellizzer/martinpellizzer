Campanula antilibanotica

Campanula antilibanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountains of Lebanon and Syria.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula antilibanotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
