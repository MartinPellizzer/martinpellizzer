Calliprora lutea

Calliprora lutea, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Calliprora lutea is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
