Croton trichocephalus var. brevifolius

Croton trichocephalus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are shorter and broader compared to the typical variety. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
