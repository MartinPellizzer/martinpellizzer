Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. grandiflorum

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the red-leaf chaenorhinum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct reddish hue, which becomes more pronounced in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
