Clerodendrum angustifolium

Clerodendrum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chaste tree, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
