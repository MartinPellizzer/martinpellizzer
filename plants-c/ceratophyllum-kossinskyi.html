Ceratophyllum kossinskyi

Ceratophyllum kossinskyi, commonly known as the spiked watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

Ceratophyllum kossinskyi is often used in aquariums due to its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
