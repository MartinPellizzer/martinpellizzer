Crypsis turkestanica

Crypsis turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan cryptogram, is a rare and elusive plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that blend seamlessly with its surroundings, a trait that likely aids in its survival through camouflage. The plant possesses a fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Crypsis turkestanica is considered a species of conservation concern.
