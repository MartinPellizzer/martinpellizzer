Cestrum subpulverulentum var. ovale

Cestrum subpulverulentum var. ovale, commonly known as the white-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by small, round, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual display.
