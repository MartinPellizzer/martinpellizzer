Capparis trinervia

Capparis trinervia, commonly known as the Indian snowball or mountain snowball, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a trifoliate appearance, giving it the species name *trinervia*.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
