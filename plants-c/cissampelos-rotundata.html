Cissampelos rotundata

Cissampelos rotundata, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing perennial vine native to Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

The fruit is a distinctive, round, green capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
