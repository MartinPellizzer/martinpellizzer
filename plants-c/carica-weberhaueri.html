Carica weberhaueri

Carica weberhaueri, commonly known as the Weberhauer papaya, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree belonging to the Caricaceae family, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, similar to other papaya species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
