Carex montana subsp. czetzii

Carex montana subsp.

czetzii, commonly known as the Czech sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
