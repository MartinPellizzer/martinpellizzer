Cardamine caespitosa

Cardamine caespitosa, commonly known as water chickweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and woodland edges. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent.

Cardamine caespitosa is known for its ability to thrive in wet soils and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
