Centranthus angustifolius var. lecoqii

Centranthus angustifolius var. lecoqii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved redcent, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially during the growing season. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to red, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
