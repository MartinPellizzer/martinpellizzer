Crotalaria globifera var. pubescens

Crotalaria globifera var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy globe flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, yellowish-white flowers clustered together. The plant has hairy foliage and stems, giving it a rugged appearance. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
