Cousinia acanthophysa

Cousinia acanthophysa, commonly known as the spiny cushion flower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosette structure with woolly, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows.

Cousinia acanthophysa is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
