Cassia tenuisepala

Cassia tenuisepala, commonly known as the narrow-winged cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets, and the plant often grows in woodland or savanna habitats.

Cassia tenuisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
