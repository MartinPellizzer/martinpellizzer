Carthamus dianius

Carthamus dianius, commonly known as the Persian saffron or saffron crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and is closely related to the saffron crocus, Crocus sativus. The plant produces small, showy blooms with three petals and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its scientific name reflects its historical and botanical significance in ancient cultures.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
