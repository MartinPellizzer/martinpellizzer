Cuatresia physalana

Cuatresia physalana, commonly known as the lantern vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruits, which mature to a bright red or orange color, are edible when ripe and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor.
