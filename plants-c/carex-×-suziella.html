Carex × suziella

Carex × suziella, commonly known as the Suzyella sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Carex × suziella is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity in aquatic environments.
