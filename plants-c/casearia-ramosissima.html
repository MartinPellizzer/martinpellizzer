Casearia ramosissima

Casearia ramosissima, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
