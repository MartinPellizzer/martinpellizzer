Cornalia glabra

Cornalia glabra, commonly known as the smooth cornalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Cornalia glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
