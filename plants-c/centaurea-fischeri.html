Centaurea fischeri

Centaurea fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces seed heads that are often bristly or spiny. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
