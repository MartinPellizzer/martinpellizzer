Cleome platysepala

Cleome platysepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which are surrounded by broad, fleshy sepals. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its showy floral display.

Cleome platysepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
