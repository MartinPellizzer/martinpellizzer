Chlorophytum africanum

Chlorophytum africanum, commonly known as the African snake plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellowish variegation. The plant is a monocot, belonging to the Asparagaceae family, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers on slender stalks. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to purify air and its low maintenance requirements further enhance its appeal as a houseplant.
