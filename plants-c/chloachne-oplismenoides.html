Chloachne oplismenoides

Chloachne oplismenoides, commonly known as the silvergrass or bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble those of true bluegrasses. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence.

Chloachne oplismenoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
