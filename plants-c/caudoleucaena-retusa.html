Caudoleucaena retusa

Caudoleucaena retusa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by long, slender pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Caudoleucaena retusa thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
