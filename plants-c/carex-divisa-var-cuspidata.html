Carex divisa var. cuspidata

Carex divisa var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
