Carex inagawaensis

Carex inagawaensis, commonly known as Inagawa's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex inagawaensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
