Celosia nitida

Celosia nitida, commonly known as the flame flower or sacred flame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which are the primary ornamental feature. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its vibrant color and unique appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical profile.
