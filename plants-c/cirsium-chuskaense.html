Cirsium chuskaense

Cirsium chuskaense, commonly known as the Chuska thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Cirsium chuskaense is often found in areas with low rainfall and high sun exposure.
