Cardamine pratensis var. stricta

Cardamine pratensis var. stricta, commonly known as narrow-leaved water-calcicle.

This perennial herb is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It produces delicate white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a raceme. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting insect populations.
