Coelogyne muriculata

Coelogyne muriculata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically long and strap-like, and it often grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments.

Coelogyne muriculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
