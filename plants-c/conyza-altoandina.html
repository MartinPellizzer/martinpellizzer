Conyza altoandina

Conyza altoandina, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Conyza altoandina is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
