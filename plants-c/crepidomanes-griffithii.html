Crepidomanes griffithii

Crepidomanes griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, often with a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
