Caesalpinia myabensis var. clementis

Caesalpinia myabensis var. clementis, commonly known as the Clement's flame tree, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in dense, feathery clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
