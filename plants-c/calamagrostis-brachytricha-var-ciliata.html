Calamagrostis brachytricha var. ciliata

Calamagrostis brachytricha var. ciliata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in the eastern United States.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
