Carex flagellifera

Carex flagellifera, commonly known as the flagellate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
