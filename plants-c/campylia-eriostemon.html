Campylia eriostemon

Campylia eriostemon, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
