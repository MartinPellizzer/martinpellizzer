Crepidorhopalon debilis

Crepidorhopalon debilis, commonly known as the slender crepe-mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, woolly leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
