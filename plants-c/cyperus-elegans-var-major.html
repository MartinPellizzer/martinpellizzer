Cyperus elegans var. major

Cyperus elegans var. major, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers arranged in spikes, typically found at the top of the stem. Its leaves are linear and usually have a pointed tip, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in wetland environments, where it thrives in moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
