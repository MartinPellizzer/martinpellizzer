Cirsium fallax

Cirsium fallax, commonly known as the fallax thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves and produces purple to pink flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and covered with stiff hairs.

Cirsium fallax is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
