Carex laeta subsp. gelongii

Carex laeta subsp.

gelongii, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish or brownish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
