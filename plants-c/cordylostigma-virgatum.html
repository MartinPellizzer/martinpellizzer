Cordylostigma virgatum

Cordylostigma virgatum, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, typical of many members of its family. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
