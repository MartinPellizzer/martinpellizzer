Clethra kawadana

Clethra kawadana, commonly known as the Japanese clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Clethra kawadana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
