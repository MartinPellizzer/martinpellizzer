Cedronia

Cedronia, commonly known as the white cedar or *Toona sinensis*, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Cedronia is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

It also has cultural significance in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
