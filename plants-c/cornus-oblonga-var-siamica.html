Cornus oblonga var. siamica

Cornus oblonga var. siamica, commonly known as the Siam cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but typically consumed in moderation. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
