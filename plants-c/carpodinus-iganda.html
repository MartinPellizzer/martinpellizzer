Carpodinus iganda

Carpodinus iganda, commonly known as the African water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in water ecosystems.
