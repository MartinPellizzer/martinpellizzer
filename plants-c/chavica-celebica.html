Chavica celebica

Chavica celebica, commonly known as Java cress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are edible, often used in local cuisines. Chavica celebica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
