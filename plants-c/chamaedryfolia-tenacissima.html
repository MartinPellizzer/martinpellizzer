Chamaedryfolia tenacissima

Chamaedryfolia tenacissima, commonly known as the hardy rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dense foliage that thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The leaves are typically gray-green, covered in a fine layer of hairs that help reduce water loss.
