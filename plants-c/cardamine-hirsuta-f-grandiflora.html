Cardamine hirsuta f. grandiflora

Cardamine hirsuta f. grandiflora, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the common watercress species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically alternate and trifoliate. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
