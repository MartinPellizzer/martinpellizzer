Cereus melanostele

Cereus melanostele, commonly known as the black-stemmed night bloomer, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique blooming behavior.
