Carex aquilonalis

Carex aquilonalis, commonly known as the northern sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex aquilonalis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
