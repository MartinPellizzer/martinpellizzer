Calea nana

Calea nana, commonly known as the dwarf calea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, which contribute to its unique texture and appearance.

Calea nana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shaded or semi-shaded garden environments.
