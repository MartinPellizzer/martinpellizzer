Carex austroafricana

Carex austroafricana, commonly known as the Southern African sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex austroafricana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
