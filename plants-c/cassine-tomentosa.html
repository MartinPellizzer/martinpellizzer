Cassine tomentosa

Cassine tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly cassine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has smooth, grayish-green stems and hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its small, yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by spherical, woody fruits.

Cassine tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
