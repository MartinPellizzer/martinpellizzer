Cyperus varicus var. arcuatoreflexus

Cyperus varicus var. arcuatoreflexus, commonly known as the arcuate flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
