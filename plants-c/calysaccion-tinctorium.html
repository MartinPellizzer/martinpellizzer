Calysaccion tinctorium

Calysaccion tinctorium, commonly known as the dye plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a yellow dye from its leaves, which has been historically used in traditional practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its medicinal and dye-producing properties, it holds cultural and economic significance in its native region.
