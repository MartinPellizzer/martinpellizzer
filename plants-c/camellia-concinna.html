Camellia concinna

Camellia concinna, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, double flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep red, bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia concinna prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
