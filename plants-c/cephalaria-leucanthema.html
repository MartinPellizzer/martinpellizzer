Cephalaria leucanthema

Cephalaria leucanthema, commonly known as the whiteflower cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
