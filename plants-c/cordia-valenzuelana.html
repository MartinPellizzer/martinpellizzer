Cordia valenzuelana

Cordia valenzuelana, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape. The tree has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Cordia valenzuelana is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
