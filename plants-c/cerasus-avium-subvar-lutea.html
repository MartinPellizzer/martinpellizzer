Cerasus avium subvar. lutea

Cerasus avium subvar. lutea, commonly known as the yellow cherry, is a botanical variant of the common cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas. It produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or dark purple when mature.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
