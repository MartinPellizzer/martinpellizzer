Carex echinata f. remotiuscula

Carex echinata f. remotiuscula, commonly known as the slender spikeweed, is a rare and distinctive sedge species characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has a short, rhizomatous rootstock that helps it spread vegetatively, and its flowers are usually small and clustered in spikelets. Its leaves are usually a deep green, and the inflorescence may produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences.
