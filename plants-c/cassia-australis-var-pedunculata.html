Cassia australis var. pedunculata

Cassia australis var. pedunculata, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid regions. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy blooms, which attract pollinators like bees and birds.

It is also valued for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
