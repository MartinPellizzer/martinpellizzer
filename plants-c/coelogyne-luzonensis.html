Coelogyne luzonensis

Coelogyne luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of 10 to 20, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and segmented, supporting the plant's growth and storage of nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
