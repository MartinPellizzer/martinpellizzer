Codonopsis affinis var. birmanica

Codonopsis affinis var. birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its tuberous roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
