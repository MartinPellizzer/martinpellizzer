Caprifolium ligustrinum

Caprifolium ligustrinum, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features opposite, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Caprifolium ligustrinum is often used in ornamental gardening and can also be found in natural habitats along stream banks and woodland edges.
