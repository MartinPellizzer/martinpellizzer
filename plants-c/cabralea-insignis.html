Cabralea insignis

Cabralea insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or cabralea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rubiaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
