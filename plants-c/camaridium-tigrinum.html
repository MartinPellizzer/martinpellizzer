Camaridium tigrinum

Camaridium tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that resemble tiger stripes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-draining soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture.

Camaridium tigrinum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
