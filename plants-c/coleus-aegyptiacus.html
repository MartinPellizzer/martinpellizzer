Coleus aegyptiacus

Coleus aegyptiacus, commonly known as the Egyptian coleus, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color.

Coleus aegyptiacus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
