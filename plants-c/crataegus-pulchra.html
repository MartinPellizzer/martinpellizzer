Crataegus pulchra

Crataegus pulchra, commonly known as the showy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its ornate, thorned branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Crataegus pulchra typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping due to its seasonal display of flowers and fruits.
