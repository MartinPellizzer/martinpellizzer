Convallaria bifolia var. kamtschatica

Convallaria bifolia var. kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces berries that contain toxic compounds similar to those found in other Convallaria species.
