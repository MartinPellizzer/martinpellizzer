Capparis incanescens

Capparis incanescens, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky areas, characterized by its woody stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible buds that are used in culinary applications, similar to capers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with a fine, whitish pubescence.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important plant in its native habitat.
