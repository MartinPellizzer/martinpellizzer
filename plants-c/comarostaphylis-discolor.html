Comarostaphylis discolor

Comarostaphylis discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
