Campanula adami

Campanula adami, commonly known as the Adami bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Campanula adami is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
