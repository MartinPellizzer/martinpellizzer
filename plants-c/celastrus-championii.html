Celastrus championii

Celastrus championii, commonly known as the Indian bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shady environments.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a notable species in ecological studies.
