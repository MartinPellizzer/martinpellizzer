Campanula lasiocarpa

Campanula lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to violet. The leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine, hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
