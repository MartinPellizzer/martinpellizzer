Cerastium secalli

Cerastium secalli, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Cerastium secalli is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
