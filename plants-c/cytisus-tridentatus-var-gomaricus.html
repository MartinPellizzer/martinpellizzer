Cytisus tridentatus var. gomaricus

Cytisus tridentatus var. gomaricus, commonly known as the Gomar's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. It has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
