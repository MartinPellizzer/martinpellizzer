Carex blinii subsp. shanghaiensis

Carex blinii subsp.

shanghaiensis, commonly known as the Shanghai sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern China. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
