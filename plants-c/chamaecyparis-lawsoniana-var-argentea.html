Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. argentea

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. argentea, commonly known as the Lawson cypress variegated, is a cultivar of the Lawson cypress.

It is characterized by its unique variegated foliage, featuring white to silver-green markings on its needle-like leaves. This plant is a slow-growing evergreen conifer that thrives in temperate climates. It has a conical shape and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The variegation is due to genetic mutations that affect chlorophyll production in the leaves.
