Cinchona lancifolia var. lanceolata

Cinchona lancifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf quina, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant produces red or orange berries containing elliptical seeds. It is closely related to other cinchona species, which are the source of quinine, a compound used in medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the production of medicinal compounds.
