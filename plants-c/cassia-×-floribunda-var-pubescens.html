Cassia × floribunda var. pubescens

Cassia × floribunda var. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree.
