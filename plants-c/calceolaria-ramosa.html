Calceolaria ramosa

Calceolaria ramosa, commonly known as the ramose slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, branching structure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in clusters along the stems.

Calceolaria ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
