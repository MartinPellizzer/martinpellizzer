Campanula napuligera f. intermedia

Campanula napuligera f. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine environments. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
