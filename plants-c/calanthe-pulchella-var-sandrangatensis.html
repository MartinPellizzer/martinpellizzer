Calanthe pulchella var. sandrangatensis

Calanthe pulchella var. sandrangatensis, commonly known as the Sandrangat orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
