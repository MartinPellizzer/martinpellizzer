Crataegus monogyna subsp. aegeica

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

aegeica, commonly known as the Greek hawthorn, is a subspecies of the hawthorn plant native to southeastern Europe. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and dense foliage.

The plant produces white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
