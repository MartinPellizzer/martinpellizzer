Carthamus cyaneus

Carthamus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant features silvery-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Carthamus cyaneus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
