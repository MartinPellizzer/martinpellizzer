Campanula napuligera subf. angustifrons

Campanula napuligera subf.

angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
