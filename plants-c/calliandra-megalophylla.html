Calliandra megalophylla

Calliandra megalophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its stems are often woody and thorny. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.

Calliandra megalophylla also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which can improve soil fertility.
