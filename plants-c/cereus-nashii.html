Cereus nashii

Cereus nashii, commonly known as the Nash's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, a feature common among many cactus species. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
