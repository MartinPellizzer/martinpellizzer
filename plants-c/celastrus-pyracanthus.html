Celastrus pyracanthus

Celastrus pyracanthus, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it thrives in partial to full shade in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
