Cubacroton

Cubacroton, commonly known as the Cuban croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not commonly found outside its native range.
