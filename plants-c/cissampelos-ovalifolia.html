Cissampelos ovalifolia

Cissampelos ovalifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural studies.
