Casearia bule

Casearia bule, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its striking clusters of small, violet-blue berries. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. It produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The berries, which are edible, ripen to a deep blue color and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
