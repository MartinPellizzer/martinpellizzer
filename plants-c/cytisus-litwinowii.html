Cytisus litwinowii

Cytisus litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinow's broom, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are narrow and slightly hairy.

Cytisus litwinowii is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience in challenging environments.
