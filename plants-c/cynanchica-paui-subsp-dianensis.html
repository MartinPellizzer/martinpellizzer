Cynanchica paui subsp. dianensis

Cynanchica paui subsp.

dianensis, commonly known as the Dianese Milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in temperate and alpine environments.
