Conophytum klipbokbergense

Conophytum klipbokbergense, commonly known as the Klipbokberg stone plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
