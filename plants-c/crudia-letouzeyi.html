Crudia letouzeyi

Crudia letouzeyi, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
