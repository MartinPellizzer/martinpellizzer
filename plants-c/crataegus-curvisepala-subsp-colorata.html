Crataegus curvisepala subsp. colorata

Crataegus curvisepala subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the colorata hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, often reddish or purple-colored leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural studies.
