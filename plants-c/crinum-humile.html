Crinum humile

Crinum humile, commonly known as the dwarf rain lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Crinum humile is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
