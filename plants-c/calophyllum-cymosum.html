Calophyllum cymosum

Calophyllum cymosum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy fruits containing dark blue seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
