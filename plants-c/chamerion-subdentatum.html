Chamerion subdentatum

Chamerion subdentatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats. The plant features slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and slightly toothed. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically in late summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
