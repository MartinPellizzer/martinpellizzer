Centaurea × jovinienii

Centaurea × jovinienii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are typically surrounded by a ring of pale yellow ray florets. The plant produces a tall, spindly stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the top. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea × jovinienii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
