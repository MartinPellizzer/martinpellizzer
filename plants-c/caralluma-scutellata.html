Caralluma scutellata

Caralluma scutellata, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in hot, dry climates and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces purple or red berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its ability to store water in its thick leaves makes it well-adapted to survive in harsh desert environments.

Caralluma scutellata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in low-maintenance gardening.
