Cerastium cardiopetalum

Cerastium cardiopetalum, commonly known as the heart-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cerastium cardiopetalum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
