Clusia hylaeae

Clusia hylaeae, commonly known as the Hylaean glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, which includes many species with showy flowers and often ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically evergreen and can vary in shape from ovate to elliptical. Clusia hylaeae produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, often in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom throughout the year in suitable conditions.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
