Cyathea hirta

Cyathea hirta, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its fronds are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Cyathea hirta plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to the forest canopy structure.
