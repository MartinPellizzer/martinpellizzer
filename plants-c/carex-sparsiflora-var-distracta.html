Carex sparsiflora var. distracta

Carex sparsiflora var. distracta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-stamened flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
