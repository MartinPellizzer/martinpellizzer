Celastrus gemmatus

Celastrus gemmatus, commonly known as the gemmated staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in cymes, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
