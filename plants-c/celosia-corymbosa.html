Celosia corymbosa

Celosia corymbosa, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms in shades of red, orange, and pink. Celosia corymbosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
