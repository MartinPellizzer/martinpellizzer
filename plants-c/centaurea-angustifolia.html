Centaurea angustifolia

Centaurea angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea angustifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
