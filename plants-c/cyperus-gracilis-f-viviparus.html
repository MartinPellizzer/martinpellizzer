Cyperus gracilis f. viviparus

Cyperus gracilis f. viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. This variant is notable for its viviparous reproduction, where seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
