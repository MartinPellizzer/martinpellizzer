Carex aederi

Carex aederi, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically evergreen. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark brown flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.

Carex aederi is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
