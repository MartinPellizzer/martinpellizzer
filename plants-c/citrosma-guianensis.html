Citrosma guianensis

Citrosma guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese citrosma, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, round, and have a thin, leathery skin, containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
