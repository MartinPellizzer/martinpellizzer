Cybianthus angustifolius var. alpestris

Cybianthus angustifolius var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine narrowleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold and harsh environments. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for high-altitude ecosystems.
