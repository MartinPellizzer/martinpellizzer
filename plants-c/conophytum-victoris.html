Conophytum victoris

Conophytum victoris, commonly known as the "Cape Karoo flower," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in arid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Conophytum victoris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
