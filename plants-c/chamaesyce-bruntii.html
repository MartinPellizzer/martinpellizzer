Chamaesyce bruntii

Chamaesyce bruntii, commonly known as the Brunt's sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a sprawling habit with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warm season. It is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water.

Due to its resilience and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in dry gardens and rockeries.
