Cinnagrostis viridiflavescens var. montevidensis

Cinnagrostis viridiflavescens var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
