Carduus × elatior

Carduus × elatior, commonly known as the Great Coneflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy composite flower heads that typically range from purple to pink in color. The plant produces both ray florets, which resemble petals, and disk florets that form the central cone-like structure. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it has been introduced to other regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Carduus × elatior is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
