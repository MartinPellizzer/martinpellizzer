Colletia spinosissima var. valdiviana

Colletia spinosissima var. valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian burrito cactus, is a succulent plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny bracts, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in inflorescences at the base of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
