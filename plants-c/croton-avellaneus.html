Croton avellaneus

Croton avellaneus, commonly known as the black croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
