Calytrix microphylla var. longifolia

Calytrix microphylla var. longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
