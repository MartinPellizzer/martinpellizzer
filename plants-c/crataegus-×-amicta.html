Crataegus × amicta

Crataegus × amicta, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, characterized by its ornamental value and ecological significance.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters, featuring thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins, and it is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Crataegus × amicta is valued for its hardiness, adaptability, and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
