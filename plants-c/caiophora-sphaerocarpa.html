Caiophora sphaerocarpa

Caiophora sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe fruit, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy fruits that resemble small globes, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable plant in local cuisines.
