Crossopetalum oxyphyllum

Crossopetalum oxyphyllum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and landscapes.
