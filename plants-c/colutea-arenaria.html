Colutea arenaria

Colutea arenaria, commonly known as sand senna, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its seed pods contain several small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Colutea arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
