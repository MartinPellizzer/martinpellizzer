Cyrtochilum graminifolium

Cyrtochilum graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Cyrtochilum graminifolium is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and specific ecological requirements.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
