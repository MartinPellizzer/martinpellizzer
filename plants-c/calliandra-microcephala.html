Calliandra microcephala

Calliandra microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that consist of numerous small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is well adapted to sandy or well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm climates.

Additionally, it serves as a nitrogen-fixing plant, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
