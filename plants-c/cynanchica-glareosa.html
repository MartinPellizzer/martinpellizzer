Cynanchica glareosa

Cynanchica glareosa, commonly known as the Glareous Milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators. Its milky sap, typical of the genus, contains latex and is used in traditional medicine.

Cynanchica glareosa is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
