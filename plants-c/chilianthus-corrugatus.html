Chilianthus corrugatus

Chilianthus corrugatus, commonly known as the roughleaf chilianthus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rugged, corrugated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
