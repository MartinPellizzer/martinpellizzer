Cerastium papuanum subf. columnare

Cerastium papuanum subf.

columnare, commonly known as the columnar snowbell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Guinea. It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with slender, cylindrical stems that are often mistaken for grasses. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
