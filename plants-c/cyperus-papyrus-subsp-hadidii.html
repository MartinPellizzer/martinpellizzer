Cyperus papyrus subsp. hadidii

Cyperus papyrus subsp.

hadidii, commonly known as the desert papyrus, is a reed-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies is adapted to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils, often found in wetlands and marshes. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
