Columnea consanguinea var. adpressa

Columnea consanguinea var. adpressa, commonly known as the creeping columnea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
