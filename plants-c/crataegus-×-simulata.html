Crataegus × simulata

Crataegus × simulata, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Crataegus × simulata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
