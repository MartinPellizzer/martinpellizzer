Cordia hebeclada

Cordia hebeclada, commonly known as the yellow cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and tropical regions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
