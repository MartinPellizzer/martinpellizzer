Chloris cryptostachya

Chloris cryptostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
