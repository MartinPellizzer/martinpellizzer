Cytisus sagittalis

Cytisus sagittalis, commonly known as the arrowhead broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Cytisus sagittalis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
