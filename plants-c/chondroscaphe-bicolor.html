Chondroscaphe bicolor

Chondroscaphe bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers with two distinct color zones. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained and sunny conditions. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Chondroscaphe bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
