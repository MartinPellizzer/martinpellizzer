Centropogon ferrugineus var. glabrifilaris

Centropogon ferrugineus var. glabrifilaris, commonly known as the rusty centropogon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant blooms.
