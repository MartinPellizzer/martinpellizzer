Carum filicinum

Carum filicinum, commonly known as water parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and emit a mild, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often harvested for use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Carum filicinum thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams or in marshy habitats.
