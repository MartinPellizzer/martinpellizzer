Crotalaria semperflorens var. walkeri

Crotalaria semperflorens var. walkeri, commonly known as the everblooming false cowpea, is a tropical annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often surrounded by distinctive red or orange bracts. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
