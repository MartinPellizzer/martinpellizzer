Chamaele tanakae

Chamaele tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's chamaeleon, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of wax, providing protection against intense sunlight and reducing water loss.

Chamaele tanakae is valued for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
