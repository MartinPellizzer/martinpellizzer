Crepis sinuata

Crepis sinuata, commonly known as the waved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, serrated leaves that are often wavy in appearance. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale green bracts.

Crepis sinuata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
