Centrolobium sclerophyllum

Centrolobium sclerophyllum, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage, which contributes to its ecological significance. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it plays a vital role in local biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
