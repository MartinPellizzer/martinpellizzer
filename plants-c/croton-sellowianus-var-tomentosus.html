Croton sellowianus var. tomentosus

Croton sellowianus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
