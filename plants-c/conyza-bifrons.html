Conyza bifrons

Conyza bifrons, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple composite flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to spread easily.

Conyza bifrons is often considered a weed in agricultural and garden settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
