Clematis pingbianensis

Clematis pingbianensis, commonly known as the Pingbian clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody vines and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
