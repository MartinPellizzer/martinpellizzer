Carex banksii var. abbreviata

Carex banksii var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by three-angled, spike-like fruits.
