Calamus concinnus

Calamus concinnus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions in Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters.

Calamus concinnus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
