Chamaenerion angustifolium var. pubescens

Chamaenerion angustifolium var. pubescens, commonly known as the sticky willow or fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with full sun, such as roadsides and burned sites.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
