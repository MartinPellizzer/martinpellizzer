Cytisus pallidus var. frivaldszkyanus

Cytisus pallidus var. frivaldszkyanus, commonly known as the pale broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its adaptation to arid and coastal environments.
