Chrysosplenium dickinsii

Chrysosplenium dickinsii, commonly known as the Dickins' golden spleenwort, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale yellow, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
