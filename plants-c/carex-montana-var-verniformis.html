Carex montana var. verniformis

Carex montana var. verniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex montana var. verniformis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
