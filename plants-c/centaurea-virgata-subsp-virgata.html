Centaurea virgata subsp. virgata

Centaurea virgata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments.

The plant features slender stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, bearing small, bright blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, scrublands, and along roadsides.
