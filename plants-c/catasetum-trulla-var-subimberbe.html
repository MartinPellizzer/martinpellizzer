Catasetum trulla var. subimberbe

Catasetum trulla var. subimberbe, commonly known as the woolly Catasetum, is a unique orchid species native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and striking, often fragrant, flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its inflorescence bears a cluster of showy, often yellow or orange flowers, which are adapted for specific pollination by bees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
