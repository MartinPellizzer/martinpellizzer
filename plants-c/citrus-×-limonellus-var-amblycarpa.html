Citrus × limonellus var. amblycarpa

Citrus × limonellus var. amblycarpa, commonly known as the sour orange.

This variant is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, yellowish rind. It has a high concentration of citric acid, giving it a tart flavor. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree with thorny branches and dark green, elliptical leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a rootstock for other citrus varieties.
