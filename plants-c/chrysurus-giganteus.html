Chrysurus giganteus

Chrysurus giganteus, commonly known as the giant goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Chrysurus giganteus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
