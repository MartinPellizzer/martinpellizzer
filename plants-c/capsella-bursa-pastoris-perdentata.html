Capsella bursa-pastoris perdentata

Capsella bursa-pastoris perdentata, commonly known as the common weed or shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions around the world. It has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small white or pale yellow flowers.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
