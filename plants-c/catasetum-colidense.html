Catasetum colidense

Catasetum colidense, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces vibrant, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding environment, which is typical for orchids.

Catasetum colidense is often found growing in humid, shaded forest areas where it thrives in the understory.
