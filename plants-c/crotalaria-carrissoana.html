Crotalaria carrissoana

Crotalaria carrissoana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, and it produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
