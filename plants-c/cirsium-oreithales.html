Cirsium oreithales

Cirsium oreithales, commonly known as the alpine thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its thick, hairy stems help protect it from harsh weather and herbivory.

Cirsium oreithales plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
