Cerastium fragillimum

Cerastium fragillimum, commonly known as the fragile mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its delicate appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in alpine ecosystems.

Cerastium fragillimum is often found in meadows and slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
