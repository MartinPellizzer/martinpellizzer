Cyperus diffusus f. celebicus

Cyperus diffusus f. celebicus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that typically bear three to five stamens, contributing to its distinctive floral structure. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird life.
