Centaurea rhapontica

Centaurea rhapontica, commonly known as Siberian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny leaves, which are typical of the genus Centaurea. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various environments.

Centaurea rhapontica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
