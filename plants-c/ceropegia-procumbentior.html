Ceropegia procumbentior

Ceropegia procumbentior, commonly known as the creeping ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that features fleshy, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
