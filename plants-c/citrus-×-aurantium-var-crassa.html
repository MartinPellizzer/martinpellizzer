Citrus × aurantium var. crassa

Citrus × aurantium var. crassa, commonly known as the common pummelo, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery rind, which is typically a pale yellow to greenish color. The fruit is usually oblate or round, with a smooth surface and a single chamber containing a large number of seeds. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
