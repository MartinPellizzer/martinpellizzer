Chlorophytum subpetiolatum var. subpetiolatum

Chlorophytum subpetiolatum var. subpetiolatum, commonly known as the tiger tooth plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves with a distinctive light green to white midrib, creating a striking striped appearance. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers on slender stalks during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Its adaptability and low maintenance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
