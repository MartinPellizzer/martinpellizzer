Cytisus laburnum var. quercifolium

Cytisus laburnum var. quercifolium, commonly known as the oak-leaved laburnum, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves that resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
