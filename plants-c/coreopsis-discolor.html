Coreopsis discolor

Coreopsis discolor, commonly known as the white coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture.

Coreopsis discolor is a perennial herb that is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
