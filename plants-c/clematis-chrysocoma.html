Clematis chrysocoma

Clematis chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
