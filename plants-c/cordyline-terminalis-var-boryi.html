Cordyline terminalis var. boryi

Cordyline terminalis var. boryi, commonly known as the Bory's cordyline, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features tall, stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dramatic foliage.
