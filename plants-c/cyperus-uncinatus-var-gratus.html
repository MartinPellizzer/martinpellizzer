Cyperus uncinatus var. gratus

Cyperus uncinatus var. gratus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, with the flowers being wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its adaptability to aquatic environments makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
