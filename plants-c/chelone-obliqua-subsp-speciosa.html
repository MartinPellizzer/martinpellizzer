Chelone obliqua subsp. speciosa

Chelone obliqua subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its striking, nodding, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The showy false hellebore is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
