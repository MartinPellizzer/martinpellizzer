Carex haussknechtii

Carex haussknechtii, commonly known as the Haussecknecht's sedge, is a species of sedges belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to certain regions of Europe, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
