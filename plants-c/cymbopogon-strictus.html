Cymbopogon strictus

Cymbopogon strictus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a citrus-like scent. The plant contains essential oils, particularly citral, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for use in culinary, aromatic, and pharmaceutical applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable resource in both traditional and modern uses.
