Coleus osmirrhizon

Coleus osmirrhizon, commonly known as the fragrant coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
