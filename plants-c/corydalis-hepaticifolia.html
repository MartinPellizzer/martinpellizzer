Corydalis hepaticifolia

Corydalis hepaticifolia, commonly known as the liverleaf corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Corydalis hepaticifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
