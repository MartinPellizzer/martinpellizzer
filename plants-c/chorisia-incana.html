Chorisia incana

Chorisia incana, commonly known as the white trumpet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.
