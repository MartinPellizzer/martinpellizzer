Croton rudolphianus

Croton rudolphianus, commonly known as the Rudolph's croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly colored species. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored leaves that can range from green to red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton rudolphianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
