Cachrys asperula

Cachrys asperula, commonly known as roughleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that becomes more prominent with age.

Cachrys asperula is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
