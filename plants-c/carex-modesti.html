Carex modesti

Carex modesti, commonly known as the small sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Carex modesti is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
