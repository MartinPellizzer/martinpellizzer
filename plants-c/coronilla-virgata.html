Coronilla virgata

Coronilla virgata, commonly known as the Spanish needle or goat's horn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems that often produce a distinctive, spiny fruit resembling a small horn. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It blooms with small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coronilla virgata is adapted to poor soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
