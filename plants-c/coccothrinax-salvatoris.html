Coccothrinax salvatoris

Coccothrinax salvatoris, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a species of palm native to Cuba.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and is characterized by its pinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces black, spherical fruits that are edible.

This palm is well adapted to coastal and limestone soils, making it a resilient species in tropical environments.
