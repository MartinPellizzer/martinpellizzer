Carduus creticus

Carduus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple or pinkish flower heads. The plant produces a robust root system that allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine, prickly hairs.

Carduus creticus is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
