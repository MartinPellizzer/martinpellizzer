Clematis parviloba subsp. bartlettii

Clematis parviloba subsp.

bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's clematis, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to elliptical leaflets, and it produces distinctive, elongated seed pods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
