Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. pallasii

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. pallasii, commonly known as Pallas's cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to southern India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is harvested for its essential oils and medicinal properties. The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a distinct cinnamon scent when crushed.

This variety is valued in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
