Cyanthillium cinereum var. ugandense

Cyanthillium cinereum var. ugandense, commonly known as the Ugandan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a distinctive bluish-green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
