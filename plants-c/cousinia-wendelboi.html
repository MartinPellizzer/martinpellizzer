Cousinia wendelboi

Cousinia wendelboi, commonly known as the Wendelbo's desertweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, which are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and low moisture conditions.

Cousinia wendelboi is often found in mountainous and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
