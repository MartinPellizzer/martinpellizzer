Callicarpa discolor

Callicarpa discolor, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that contrast strikingly with its green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and small mammals.
