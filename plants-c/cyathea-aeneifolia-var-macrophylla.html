Cyathea aeneifolia var. macrophylla

Cyathea aeneifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the silver tree, is a member of the fern family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, dark green fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is covered in a thick, silver-colored layer of old leaf bases. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, oval-shaped pinnae.
