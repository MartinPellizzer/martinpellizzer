Cacalia arborescens var. divaricata

Cacalia arborescens var. divaricata, commonly known as the spreading cacalia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that often spread horizontally, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.
