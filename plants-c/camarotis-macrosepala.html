Camarotis macrosepala

Camarotis macrosepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy sepals and petals that often display vibrant colors such as pink and white. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments.

Camarotis macrosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
