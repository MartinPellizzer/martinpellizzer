Campanula glomerata subsp. glomerata

Campanula glomerata subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
