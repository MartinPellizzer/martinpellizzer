Cirsium albanum

Cirsium albanum, commonly known as the Albanian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silvery-green leaves with rough textures. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its thick, waxy leaves help it retain moisture in arid environments.

Cirsium albanum is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to challenging conditions.
