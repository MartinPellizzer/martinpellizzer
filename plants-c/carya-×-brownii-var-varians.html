Carya × brownii var. varians

Carya × brownii var. varians, commonly known as the white hickory, is a hybrid species within the walnut family (Juglandaceae).

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow catkins and edible, sweet, pale yellow nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
