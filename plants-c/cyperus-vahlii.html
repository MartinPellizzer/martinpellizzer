Cyperus vahlii

Cyperus vahlii, commonly known as the Vahli's flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus vahlii is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
