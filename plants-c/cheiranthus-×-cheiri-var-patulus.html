Cheiranthus × cheiri var. patulus

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. patulus, commonly known as the broad-leaved wallflower, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. It is native to southern Europe and has been introduced to other regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
