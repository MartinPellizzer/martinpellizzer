Centaurea aetolica

Centaurea aetolica, commonly known as Aetolian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact clusters.

Centaurea aetolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
