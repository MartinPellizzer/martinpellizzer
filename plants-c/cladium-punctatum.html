Cladium punctatum

Cladium punctatum, commonly known as the punctate sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in small, raised dots. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in shallow, freshwater habitats.

Cladium punctatum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
