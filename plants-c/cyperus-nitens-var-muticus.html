Cyperus nitens var. muticus

Cyperus nitens var. muticus, commonly known as the silent flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered with fine, hair-like structures, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced or absent inflorescence, giving it the common name "silent flatsedge.".
