Croton elaeagni var. argyrocarpos

Croton elaeagni var. argyrocarpos, commonly known as the silverleaf croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
