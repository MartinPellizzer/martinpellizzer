Catalpa kaempferi

Catalpa kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain sticky, cotton-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its native habitat.

Catalpa kaempferi is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban conditions.
