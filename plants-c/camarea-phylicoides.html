Camarea phylicoides

Camarea phylicoides, commonly known as the red-tipped palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant's most distinctive feature is its bright red bracts that surround the inflorescence, giving it a dramatic appearance. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
