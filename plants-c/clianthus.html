Clianthus

Clianthus, commonly known as the New Zealand waratah, is a striking flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils. Clianthus blooms in spring, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens worldwide, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
