Cicendia campestris

Cicendia campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a slender, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
