Cosmos bipinnatus

Cosmos bipinnatus, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family, and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil. Cosmos bipinnatus is a popular choice for gardens due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple stems branching out from the base.
