Convolvulus althaeoides

Convolvulus althaeoides, commonly known as the Ethiopian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its flowers open in the morning and close in the afternoon, hence its common name.
