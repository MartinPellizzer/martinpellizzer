Cucumis melo var. santawi

Cucumis melo var. santawi, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin fruit with a smooth, waxy skin that ranges in color from pale green to yellowish-white. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit, often requiring support for its vines. Its fruit is typically harvested when immature, before it fully ripens, for a crisp, mild flavor.

This variety is valued for its culinary versatility and is commonly used in salads and fresh consumption.
