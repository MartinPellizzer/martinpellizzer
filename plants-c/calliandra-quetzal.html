Calliandra quetzal

Calliandra quetzal, commonly known as the scarlet calliandra, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange floral clusters, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting bees and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

The calliandra quetzal is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
