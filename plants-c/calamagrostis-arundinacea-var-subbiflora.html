Calamagrostis arundinacea var. subbiflora

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. subbiflora, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green foliage and its ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a light, airy texture to the landscape. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, thriving in wetland or bog environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant appearance and its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it suitable for water gardens or pond edges.
