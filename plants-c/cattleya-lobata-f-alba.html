Cattleya lobata f. alba

Cattleya lobata f. alba, commonly known as the white-lipped cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking white flowers with a distinctive pink lip.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
