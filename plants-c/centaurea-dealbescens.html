Centaurea dealbescens

Centaurea dealbescens, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with narrow, linear leaves.
