Clematis viticella subsp. walteri

Clematis viticella subsp.

walteri, commonly known as Walter's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and its ability to climb using its twining stems.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and provides good summer cover.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, ornamental vines.
