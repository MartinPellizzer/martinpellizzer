Chamaecrista psoraleopsis

Chamaecrista psoraleopsis, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
