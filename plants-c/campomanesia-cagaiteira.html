Campomanesia cagaiteira

Campomanesia cagaiteira, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when fully ripe, with a sweet and slightly acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
