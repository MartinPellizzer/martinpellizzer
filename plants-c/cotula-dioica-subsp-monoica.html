Cotula dioica subsp. monoica

Cotula dioica subsp.

monoica, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing annual herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its simple structure and rapid growth make it an important species in ecological studies of arid environments.
