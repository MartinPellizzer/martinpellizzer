Ceropegia linearis subsp. tenuis

Ceropegia linearis subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as the slender milkweed or narrow-leaved ceropegia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, linear leaves that are typically thin and narrow.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often greenish-white in color, with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
