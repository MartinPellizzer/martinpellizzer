Cyperus meyenianus var. paranaguensis

Cyperus meyenianus var. paranaguensis, commonly known as the Paraguay flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
