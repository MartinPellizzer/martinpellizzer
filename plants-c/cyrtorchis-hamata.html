Cyrtorchis hamata

Cyrtorchis hamata, commonly known as the spiked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces striking, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Cyrtorchis hamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
