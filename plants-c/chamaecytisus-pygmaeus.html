Chamaecytisus pygmaeus

Chamaecytisus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are small, leathery, and evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden planting.
