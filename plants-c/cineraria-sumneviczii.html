Cineraria sumneviczii

Cineraria sumneviczii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Spain.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, daisy-like blooms during the spring and summer months.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
