Calamus draco

Calamus draco, commonly known as dragon's tail, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves and a distinctive, creeping rhizome system. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, which can reach several meters in length.

However, it is important to note that some species within the genus Calamus are considered invasive in certain regions.
