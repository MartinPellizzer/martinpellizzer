Carex nakaii

Carex nakaii, commonly known as Nakai's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a dense rosette growth habit. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex nakaii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
