Calamintha florida

Calamintha florida, commonly known as Florida caraway, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, aromatic stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in herbal remedies. Its seeds are similar in appearance to caraway seeds and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Calamintha florida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
