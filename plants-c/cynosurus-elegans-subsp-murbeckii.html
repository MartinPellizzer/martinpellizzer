Cynosurus elegans subsp. murbeckii

Cynosurus elegans subsp.

murbeckii, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically green to bluish-green in color.

This subspecies is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting insect and bird life.
