Cystorchis salmoneus

Cystorchis salmoneus, commonly known as the salmon orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, salmon-pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in swampy areas and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its unique floral display.
