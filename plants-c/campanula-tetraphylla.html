Campanula tetraphylla

Campanula tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula tetraphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
