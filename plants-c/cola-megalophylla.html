Cola megalophylla

Cola megalophylla, commonly known as the giant cola tree, is a species native to western Africa.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody capsules containing the stimulant-rich seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it one of the tallest species in the Cola genus.
