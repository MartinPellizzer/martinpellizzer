Calamus hendersonii

Calamus hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's canescent palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered inflorescences that produce both male and female flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
