Centaurea inermis

Centaurea inermis, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Centaurea inermis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
