Caragana kansuensis

Caragana kansuensis, commonly known as the Chinese bush clover, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Caragana kansuensis is often used in reforestation and soil stabilization efforts due to its extensive root system.
