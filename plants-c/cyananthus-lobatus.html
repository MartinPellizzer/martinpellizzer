Cyananthus lobatus

Cyananthus lobatus, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyananthus lobatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
