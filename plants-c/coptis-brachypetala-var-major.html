Coptis brachypetala var. major

Coptis brachypetala var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and possesses a distinctive yellowish-brown root system. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often densely covered with fine hairs, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in terminal umbels. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its alkaloid content, which has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
