Cyperus congestus var. parishii

Cyperus congestus var. parishii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually green to reddish in color.

This species plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
