Cytisus

Cytisus, commonly known as broom, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems and bright yellow flowers, which typically bloom in the spring. Cytisus species are native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and they often thrive in well-drained soils. Many species are shrubs that can grow in a variety of climates, and some are cultivated for their ornamental value.

The genus includes over 100 species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as hedges.
