Cerastium nanhutashanense

Cerastium nanhutashanense, commonly known as the Nanhu Mountain chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
