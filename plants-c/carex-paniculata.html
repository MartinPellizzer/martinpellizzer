Carex paniculata

Carex paniculata, commonly known as the panicled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex paniculata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
