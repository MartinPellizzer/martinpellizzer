Clematis occidentalis subsp. dissecta

Clematis occidentalis subsp.

dissecta, commonly known as the western clematis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are compound, typically with deeply lobed or dissected foliage, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for climbing structures in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
