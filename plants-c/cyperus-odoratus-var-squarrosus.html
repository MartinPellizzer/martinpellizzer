Cyperus odoratus var. squarrosus

Cyperus odoratus var. squarrosus, commonly known as the woolly flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind or water.
