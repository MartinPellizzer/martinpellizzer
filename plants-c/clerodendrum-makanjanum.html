Clerodendrum makanjanum

Clerodendrum makanjanum, commonly known as the African balm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
