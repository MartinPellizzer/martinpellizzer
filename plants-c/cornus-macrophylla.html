Cornus macrophylla

Cornus macrophylla, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Cornus macrophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
