Chrysophora secundifolia

Chrysophora secundifolia, commonly known as the trailing arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize moist environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge along the veins.

It thrives in shady, waterlogged conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.
