Crassula furcata

Crassula furcata, commonly known as the elephant's foot or tree of life, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that form a dense, bushy structure resembling a tree. The plant has small, pointed leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring. Crassula furcata is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and leaves to survive drought conditions.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
