Centaurea epirotica

Centaurea epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

Centaurea epirotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
