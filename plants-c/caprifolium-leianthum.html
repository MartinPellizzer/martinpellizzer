Caprifolium leianthum

Caprifolium leianthum, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a silvery underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, sweet-scented blooms.
