Cissus rhodotricha

Cissus rhodotricha, commonly known as the red-veined cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which have a deep green background with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor spaces.
