Chamaenerion tetragonum

Chamaenerion tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angle willow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant features slender, reddish stems that are often four-angled, giving it its common name. Its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
