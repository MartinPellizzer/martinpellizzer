Clavija neglecta

Clavija neglecta, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Clavija neglecta is often found in disturbed habitats and can be considered a weed in some areas.
