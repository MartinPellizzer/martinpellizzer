Capsella bursa-pastoris hiatula

Capsella bursa-pastoris hiatula, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often prostrate or ascending. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along pathways.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces, making it both a weed and a plant of ecological significance.
