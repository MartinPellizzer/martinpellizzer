Coroya dialoides

Coroya dialoides, commonly known as the yellow coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
