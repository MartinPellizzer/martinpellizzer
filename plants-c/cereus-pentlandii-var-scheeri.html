Cereus pentlandii var. scheeri

Cereus pentlandii var. scheeri, commonly known as the Schieeri's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its flowers are typically fragrant and can remain open for several days.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
