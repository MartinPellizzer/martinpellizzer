Cylindropuntia sanfelipensis

Cylindropuntia sanfelipensis, commonly known as the San Felipe cholla, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to withstand extreme temperatures.
