Carex sharensis

Carex sharensis, commonly known as the Sharensis sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including regions of China and Japan. This perennial plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a fibrous root system characteristic of sedges.

Carex sharensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
