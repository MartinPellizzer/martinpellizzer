Choisya arizonica var. arizonica

Choisya arizonica var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona holly.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. Its flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that persists into the winter, providing food for wildlife.
