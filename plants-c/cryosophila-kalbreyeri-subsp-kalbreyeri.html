Cryosophila kalbreyeri subsp. kalbreyeri

Cryosophila kalbreyeri subsp.

kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple.

The plant is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand harsh climatic conditions. It plays an important role in pollination and serves as a food source for certain Arctic insects.

Its botanical features make it a unique example of plant adaptation to extreme environments.
