Chrysopogon aucheri

Chrysopogon aucheri, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, pale blue or white flowers in dense spikelets, which are arranged in a panicle inflorescence.

Chrysopogon aucheri is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
