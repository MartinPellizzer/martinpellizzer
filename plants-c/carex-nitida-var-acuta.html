Carex nitida var. acuta

Carex nitida var. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually brown or reddish in color, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
