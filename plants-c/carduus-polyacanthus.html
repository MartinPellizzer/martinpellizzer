Carduus polyacanthus

Carduus polyacanthus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flower heads that resemble a coneflower, with bracts that form a spiky, cylindrical shape. The plant has deeply lobed, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
