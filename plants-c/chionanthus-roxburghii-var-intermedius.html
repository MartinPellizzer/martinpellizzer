Chionanthus roxburghii var. intermedius

Chionanthus roxburghii var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate flowering dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes and ecological restoration projects.
