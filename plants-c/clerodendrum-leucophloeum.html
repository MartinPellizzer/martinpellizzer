Clerodendrum leucophloeum

Clerodendrum leucophloeum, commonly known as white-veined clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent white veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically clustered in panicles and are usually white or pale pink in color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
