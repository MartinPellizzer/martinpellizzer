Cupressus lusitanica var. communis

Cupressus lusitanica var. communis, commonly known as the Portuguese cypress, is a species of coniferous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves that are typically dark green. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often used for its aesthetic value and its ability to provide privacy and structure in cultivated settings.
