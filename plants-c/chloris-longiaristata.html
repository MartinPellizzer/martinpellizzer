Chloris longiaristata

Chloris longiaristata, commonly known as the slender grassvetch, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each branch, which produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chloris longiaristata is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
