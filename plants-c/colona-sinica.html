Colona sinica

Colona sinica, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability.
