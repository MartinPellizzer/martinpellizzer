Cordia tisserantii

Cordia tisserantii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white, spherical flower clusters that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
