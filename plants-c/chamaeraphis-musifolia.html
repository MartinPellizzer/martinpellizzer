Chamaeraphis musifolia

Chamaeraphis musifolia, commonly known as the sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Atlantic coasts of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salty and windy environments. It produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suited to maritime climates.
