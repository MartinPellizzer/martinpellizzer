Cuphea imberbis

Cuphea imberbis, commonly known as the flame beak plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble beaks, which are pollinated by hummingbirds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea imberbis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
