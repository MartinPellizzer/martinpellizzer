Cinchona obovata

Cinchona obovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its bark, which contains alkaloids such as quinine, historically used in the treatment of malaria. It thrives in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes, often found in cloud forests.

Due to its medicinal importance, it is also cultivated in various parts of the world for its pharmaceutical applications.
