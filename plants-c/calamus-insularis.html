Calamus insularis

Calamus insularis, commonly known as the island calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Aroidae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in spadices, typical of the Aroid family.

Calamus insularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
