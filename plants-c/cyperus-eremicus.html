Cyperus eremicus

Cyperus eremicus, commonly known as the desert flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.

Cyperus eremicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
