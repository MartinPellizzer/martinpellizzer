Cardamine pratensis subsp. atlantica

Cardamine pratensis subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and stream banks of the Atlantic coastal regions. It features trifoliate basal leaves with a waxy, glossy surface and slender, branching stems that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, elongated achenes enclosed in a cup-like structure called a pappus. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
