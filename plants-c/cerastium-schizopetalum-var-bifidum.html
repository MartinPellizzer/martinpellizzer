Cerastium schizopetalum var. bifidum

Cerastium schizopetalum var. bifidum, commonly known as the bifid mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and rocky slopes. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies, contributing to its ecological role in supporting insect populations.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
