Centaurea albicaulis

Centaurea albicaulis, commonly known as the white-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its bracts are often white or pale yellow. Centaurea albicaulis thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
