Cyathea ctenitoides

Cyathea ctenitoides, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive hairy appearance on its trunk and young fronds, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Cyathea ctenitoides plays an important ecological role as a habitat provider and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
