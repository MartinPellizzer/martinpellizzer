Calamagrostis epigejos var. variegata

Calamagrostis epigejos var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, bluish-green leaves that have striking white or yellow variegation along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This ornamental grass is valued for its unique foliage and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
