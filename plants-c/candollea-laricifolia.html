Candollea laricifolia

Candollea laricifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of pine trees, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Candollea laricifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
