Caltha rotundifolia var. howellii

Caltha rotundifolia var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's golden ragwort, is a variant of the golden ragwort, a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, glossy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

The leaves are usually basal, and the flowers are solitary, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
