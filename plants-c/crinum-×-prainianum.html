Crinum × prainianum

Crinum × prainianum, commonly known as the hybrid Crinum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant produces a cluster of strap-shaped leaves that are often broad and succulent. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Crinum × prainianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
