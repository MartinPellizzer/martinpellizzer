Centropogon carpinoides

Centropogon carpinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Centropogon carpinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
