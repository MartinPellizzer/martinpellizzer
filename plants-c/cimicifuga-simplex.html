Cimicifuga simplex

Cimicifuga simplex, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in herbal medicine.

Cimicifuga simplex is valued for its potential health benefits, particularly in women's wellness.
