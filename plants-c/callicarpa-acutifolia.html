Callicarpa acutifolia

Callicarpa acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-pink berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Callicarpa acutifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
