Cornus monbeigii subsp. populifolia

Cornus monbeigii subsp.

populifolia, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually smooth and dark green above, with a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
