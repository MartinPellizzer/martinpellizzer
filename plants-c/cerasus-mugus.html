Cerasus mugus

Cerasus mugus, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a species native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while the fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in summer.

The plant is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its edible fruit in some regions.
