Crescentia cujete var. puberula

Crescentia cujete var. puberula, commonly known as the African horned cucumber, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, horn-shaped fruits that resemble a hornet's nest. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, while edible when young, is typically used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
