Crateva hansemannii

Crateva hansemannii, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, velvety pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, hairy pods containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
