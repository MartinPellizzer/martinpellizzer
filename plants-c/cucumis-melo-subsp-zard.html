Cucumis melo subsp. zard

Cucumis melo subsp.

zard, commonly known as Persian melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Middle East and South Asia. It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a smooth, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh.

The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine, with palmate leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit develops from a single pistillate flower and is harvested when fully mature for its culinary and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and processing.
