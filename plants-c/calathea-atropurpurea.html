Calathea atropurpurea

Calathea atropurpurea, commonly known as the Black Velvet Plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Marantaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage with deep purple veins, which become more pronounced in low light conditions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture.

Calathea atropurpurea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
