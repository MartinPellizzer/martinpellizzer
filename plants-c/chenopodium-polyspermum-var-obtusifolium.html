Chenopodium polyspermum var. obtusifolium

Chenopodium polyspermum var. obtusifolium, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features alternate, ovate to broadly ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have rounded tips. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil conditions.
