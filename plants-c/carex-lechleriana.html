Carex lechleriana

Carex lechleriana, commonly known as the Lechler's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Carex lechleriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland conditions.
