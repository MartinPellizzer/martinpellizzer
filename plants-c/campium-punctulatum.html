Campium punctulatum

Campium punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The species is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
