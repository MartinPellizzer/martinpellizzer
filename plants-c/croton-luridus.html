Croton luridus

Croton luridus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from bright yellow to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton luridus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
