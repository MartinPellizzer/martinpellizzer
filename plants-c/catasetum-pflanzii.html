Catasetum pflanzii

Catasetum pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure, including a specialized column and pollinia. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Catasetum pflanzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
