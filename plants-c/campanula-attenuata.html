Campanula attenuata

Campanula attenuata, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula attenuata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
