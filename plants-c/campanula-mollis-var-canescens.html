Campanula mollis var. canescens

Campanula mollis var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
