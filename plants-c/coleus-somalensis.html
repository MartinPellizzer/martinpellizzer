Coleus somalensis

Coleus somalensis, commonly known as the Somali coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental uses. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a range of hues, including red, purple, and green. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Coleus somalensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
