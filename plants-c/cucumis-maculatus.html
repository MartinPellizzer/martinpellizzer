Cucumis maculatus

Cucumis maculatus, commonly known as the spotted cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-green fruits with distinctive dark spots. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped and lobed, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The fruit is edible and often used in culinary applications, though it is less common than other cucumber varieties.
