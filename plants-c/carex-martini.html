Carex martini

Carex martini, commonly known as Martini sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early to mid-summer.

Carex martini is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
