Cissus nobilis

Cissus nobilis, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a striking silver-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble silver coins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus nobilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
