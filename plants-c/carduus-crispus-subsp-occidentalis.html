Carduus crispus subsp. occidentalis

Carduus crispus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western thistle, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a purple to pink flower head that blooms in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy-like plants.
