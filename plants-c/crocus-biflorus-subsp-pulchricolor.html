Crocus biflorus subsp. pulchricolor

Crocus biflorus subsp.

pulchricolor, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive purple hue, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

The subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
