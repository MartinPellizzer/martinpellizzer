Convolvulus matutinus

Convolvulus matutinus, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry habitats. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

Convolvulus matutinus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
