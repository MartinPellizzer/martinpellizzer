Carex stricta var. macra

Carex stricta var. macra, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spiky flower cluster that emerges from the leafy stems.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
