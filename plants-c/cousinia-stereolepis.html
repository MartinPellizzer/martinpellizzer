Cousinia stereolepis

Cousinia stereolepis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are small and yellowish, arranged in dense clusters.

Cousinia stereolepis is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species in its native range.
