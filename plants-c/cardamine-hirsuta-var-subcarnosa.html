Cardamine hirsuta var. subcarnosa

Cardamine hirsuta var. subcarnosa, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often reddish in color, with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are succulent and edible, often used in salads or as a garnish.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.
