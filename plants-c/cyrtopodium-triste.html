Cyrtopodium triste

Cyrtopodium triste, commonly known as the sad orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its delicate appearance.

Cyrtopodium triste is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
