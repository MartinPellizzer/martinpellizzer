Convolvulus tridentatus

Convolvulus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

Convolvulus tridentatus is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
