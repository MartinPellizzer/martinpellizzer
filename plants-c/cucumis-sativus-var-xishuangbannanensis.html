Cucumis sativus var. xishuangbannanensis

Cucumis sativus var. xishuangbannanensis, commonly known as the Xishuangbanna cucumber, is a rare variant of the cucumber plant native to the tropical regions of China.

It is characterized by its elongated, smooth, and often pale-green fruit with a slightly bitter taste. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow and undergo both self-pollination and cross-pollination.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and adaptation to its specific ecological niche.
