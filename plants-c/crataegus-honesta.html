Crataegus honesta

Crataegus honesta, commonly known as the honest hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. Crataegus honesta typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
