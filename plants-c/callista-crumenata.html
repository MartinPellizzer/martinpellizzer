Callista crumenata

Callista crumenata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Callista crumenata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
