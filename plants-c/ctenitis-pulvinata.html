Ctenitis pulvinata

Ctenitis pulvinata, commonly known as the pulvinate shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of brown, velvety scales. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps in retaining moisture.

Ctenitis pulvinata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to humid climates.
