Capparis elliptica var. maskatensis

Capparis elliptica var. maskatensis, commonly known as the Arabian rose or desert rose, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fleshy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
