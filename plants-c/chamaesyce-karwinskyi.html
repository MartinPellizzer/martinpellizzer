Chamaesyce karwinskyi

Chamaesyce karwinskyi, commonly known as the Karwinsky's sand mat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It has a prostrate growth habit with small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Chamaesyce karwinskyi plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in disturbed areas.
