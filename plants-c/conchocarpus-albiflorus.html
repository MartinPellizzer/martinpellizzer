Conchocarpus albiflorus

Conchocarpus albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, red fruits that contain a single seed, and its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth surface. The plant thrives in brackish water environments, often forming dense stands in mangrove forests. It plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator of healthy mangrove habitats.
