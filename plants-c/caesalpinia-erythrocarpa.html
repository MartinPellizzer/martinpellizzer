Caesalpinia erythrocarpa

Caesalpinia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited legume, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain reddish, fleshy fruits. Caesalpinia erythrocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
