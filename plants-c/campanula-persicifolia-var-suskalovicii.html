Campanula persicifolia var. suskalovicii

Campanula persicifolia var. suskalovicii, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate moderate drought conditions.
