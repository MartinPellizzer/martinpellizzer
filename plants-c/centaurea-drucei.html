Centaurea drucei

Centaurea drucei, commonly known as the Druce's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Centaurea drucei is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
