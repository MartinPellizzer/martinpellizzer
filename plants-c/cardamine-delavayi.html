Cardamine delavayi

Cardamine delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's watercress, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
