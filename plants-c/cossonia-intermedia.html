Cossonia intermedia

Cossonia intermedia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Cossonia intermedia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
