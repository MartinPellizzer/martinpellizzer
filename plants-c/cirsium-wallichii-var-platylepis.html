Cirsium wallichii var. platylepis

Cirsium wallichii var. platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads composed of numerous small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
