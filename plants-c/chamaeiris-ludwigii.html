Chamaeiris ludwigii

Chamaeiris ludwigii, commonly known as the Ludwig's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Chamaeiris ludwigii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
