Crataegus basilica

Crataegus basilica, commonly known as the Basilica hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
