Catevala asperula

Catevala asperula, commonly known as roughleaf cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are rough to the touch, hence the common name, and the flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
