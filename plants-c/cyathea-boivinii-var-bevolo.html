Cyathea boivinii var. bevolo

Cyathea boivinii var. bevolo, commonly known as the Bevolo's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth habit.

The fronds are bioluminescent at night, a unique feature that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.
