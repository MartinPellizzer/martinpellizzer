Cumulopuntia boliviana subsp. dactylifera

Cumulopuntia boliviana subsp.

dactylifera, commonly known as the Bolivian cluster cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that form a compact, mound-like structure.

The plant produces clusters of small, spiny leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late summer or early autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with minimal water.
