Cacalia glabrata

Cacalia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
