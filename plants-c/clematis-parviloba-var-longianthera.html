Clematis parviloba var. longianthera

Clematis parviloba var. longianthera, commonly known as the long-anther clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 15 feet long, making it a vigorous climber.

The flowers have long, slender stamens, which give the variety its specific epithet "longianthera.".
