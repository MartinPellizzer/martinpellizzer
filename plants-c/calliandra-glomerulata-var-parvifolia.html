Calliandra glomerulata var. parvifolia

Calliandra glomerulata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is adapted to warm, sunny climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
