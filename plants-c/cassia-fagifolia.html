Cassia fagifolia

Cassia fagifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia fagifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for hedges and landscape planting.
