Conostylis teretifolia subsp. planescens

Conostylis teretifolia subsp.

planescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved conostylis, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It prefers sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
