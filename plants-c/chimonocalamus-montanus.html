Chimonocalamus montanus

Chimonocalamus montanus, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, blade-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to cool climates. Its structure resembles bamboo, though it is not a true bamboo but a member of the grass family.

Chimonocalamus montanus is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
