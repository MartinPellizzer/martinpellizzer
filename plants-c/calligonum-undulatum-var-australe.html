Calligonum undulatum var. australe

Calligonum undulatum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, seed-like fruits. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in harsh environments.

Its resilience to drought and salt makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
