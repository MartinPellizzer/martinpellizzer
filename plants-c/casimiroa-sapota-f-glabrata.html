Casimiroa sapota f. glabrata

Casimiroa sapota f. glabrata, commonly known as the rough-leafed chikoo, is a variant of the chikoo or sapota fruit.

It is a small, tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, round fruits. The fruit has a thick, inedible rind and a sweet, starchy pulp that is often consumed fresh or processed into jams and beverages. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This fruit is valued not only for its unique flavor but also for its nutritional content and potential medicinal uses.
