Calathiana bavarica

Calathiana bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are sturdy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Calathiana bavarica is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
