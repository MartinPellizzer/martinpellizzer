Clerodendrum fortunei

Clerodendrum fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Clerodendrum fortunei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in garden landscapes or as a hedge.
