Calystegia hederacea var. hederacea

Calystegia hederacea var. hederacea, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it is characterized by its milky sap and fibrous roots.
