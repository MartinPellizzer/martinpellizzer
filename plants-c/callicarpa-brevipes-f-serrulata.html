Callicarpa brevipes f. serrulata

Callicarpa brevipes f. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated beautyberry, is a variant of the beautyberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, clustered, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its specific epithet "serrulata.". The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the ornamental berries.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
