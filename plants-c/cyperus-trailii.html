Cyperus trailii

Cyperus trailii, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Cyperus trailii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both container gardening and in-ground planting in suitable climates.
