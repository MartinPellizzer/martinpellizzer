Cnidoscolus napifolius

Cnidoscolus napifolius, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, stinging hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
