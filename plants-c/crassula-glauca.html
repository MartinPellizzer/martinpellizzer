Crassula glauca

Crassula glauca, commonly known as the blue crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale blue during the spring or summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Crassula glauca is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
