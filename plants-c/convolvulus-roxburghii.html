Convolvulus roxburghii

Convolvulus roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that produces distinctive funnel-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, growing at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and traditional medicine.
