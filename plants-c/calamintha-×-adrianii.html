Calamintha × adrianii

Calamintha × adrianii, commonly known as Adrian's calamint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, producing small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.

Calamintha × adrianii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.
