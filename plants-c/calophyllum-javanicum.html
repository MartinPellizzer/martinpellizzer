Calophyllum javanicum

Calophyllum javanicum, commonly known as the Java calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
