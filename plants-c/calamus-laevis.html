Calamus laevis

Calamus laevis, commonly known as the smooth calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wet, tropical environments. The plant is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of water bodies.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and for making rattles.
