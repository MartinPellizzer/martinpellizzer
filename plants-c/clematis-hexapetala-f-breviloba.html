Clematis hexapetala f. breviloba

Clematis hexapetala f. breviloba, commonly known as the short-petioled clematis, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, recurved form. This cultivar is valued for its delicate appearance and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
