Calophyllum pentapetalum

Calophyllum pentapetalum, commonly known as the five-petaled calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
