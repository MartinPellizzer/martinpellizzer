Cnicus undulatus var. megacephalus

Cnicus undulatus var. megacephalus, commonly known as the giant sandspurry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features distinctive yellow flowers with five petals that are often larger and more rounded compared to the typical species. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to withstand arid conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies of xerophytic vegetation.
