Camaridium aureum

Camaridium aureum, commonly known as the golden broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camaridium aureum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
