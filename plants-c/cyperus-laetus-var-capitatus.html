Cyperus laetus var. capitatus

Cyperus laetus var. capitatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
