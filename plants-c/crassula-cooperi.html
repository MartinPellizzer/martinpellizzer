Crassula cooperi

Crassula cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer. It is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Crassula cooperi prefers well-draining soil and bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.
