Crataegus oxyacantha var. insegnae

Crataegus oxyacantha var. insegnae, commonly known as the Italian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that matures in late summer, attracting various wildlife.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
