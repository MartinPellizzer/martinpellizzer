Corethrodendron scoparium

Corethrodendron scoparium, commonly known as the silverleaf corethrodendron, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating that gives them their distinctive silvery appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
