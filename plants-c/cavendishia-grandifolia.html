Cavendishia grandifolia

Cavendishia grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it produces fragrant blooms during the warmer months.

Cavendishia grandifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in greenhouses and gardens worldwide.
