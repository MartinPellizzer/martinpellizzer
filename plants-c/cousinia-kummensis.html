Cousinia kummensis

Cousinia kummensis, commonly known as the Kummel's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Cousinia kummensis is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
