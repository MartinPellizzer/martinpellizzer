Centaurea incanescens

Centaurea incanescens, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
