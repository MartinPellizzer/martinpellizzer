Chrysothemis venosa

Chrysothemis venosa, commonly known as the golden tawny orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms.

Chrysothemis venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
