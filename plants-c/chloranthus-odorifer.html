Chloranthus odorifer

Chloranthus odorifer, commonly known as the fragrant chloranthus, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. It produces berries that are typically orange or red in color, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
