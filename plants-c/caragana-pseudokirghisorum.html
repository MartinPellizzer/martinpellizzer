Caragana pseudokirghisorum

Caragana pseudokirghisorum, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in arid and semi-arid regions. It also plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are edible and can be used in traditional cuisines.
