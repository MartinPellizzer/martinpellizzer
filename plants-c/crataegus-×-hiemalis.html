Crataegus × hiemalis

Crataegus × hiemalis, commonly known as the winter hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It is native to eastern North America and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
