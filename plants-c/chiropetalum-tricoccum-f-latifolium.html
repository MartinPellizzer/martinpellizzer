Chiropetalum tricoccum f. latifolium

Chiropetalum tricoccum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf sheep's ear, is a variant of the common sheep's ear plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, particularly in forested areas. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

The foliage is typically used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
