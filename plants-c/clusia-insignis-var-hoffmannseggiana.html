Clusia insignis var. hoffmannseggiana

Clusia insignis var. hoffmannseggiana, commonly known as the Hoffmannseggiana Clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, often with a yellowish or whitish margin. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.

Clusia insignis var. hoffmannseggiana is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
