Convolvulus × pseudocompactus

Convolvulus × pseudocompactus, commonly known as the hybrid bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
