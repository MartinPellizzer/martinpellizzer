Cereus chende

Cereus chende, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a nocturnal flowering plant that produces large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has a tall, cylindrical stem covered in spiny areoles, typical of many cacti. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habits, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
