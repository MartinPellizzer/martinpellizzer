Carex scoparia f. subturbinata

Carex scoparia f. subturbinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex scoparia species, which belongs to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

The subspecies subturbinata is distinguished by its more elongated and narrower leaves compared to the typical form.
