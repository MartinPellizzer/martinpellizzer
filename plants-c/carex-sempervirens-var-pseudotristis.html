Carex sempervirens var. pseudotristis

Carex sempervirens var. pseudotristis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers in the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is often used in naturalized landscapes and wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
