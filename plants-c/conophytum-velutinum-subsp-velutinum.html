Conophytum velutinum subsp. velutinum

Conophytum velutinum subsp.

velutinum, commonly known as the velvet rock rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
