Cephalanthera erecta f. pelorica

Cephalanthera erecta f. pelorica, commonly known as the purple tailed helleborine, is a striking orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant features a single, upright spike of flowers, with each bloom having a distinctive, hood-like lower lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest edges.

Botanically, it is notable for its intricate floral structure and its ability to produce nectar, attracting various pollinators.
