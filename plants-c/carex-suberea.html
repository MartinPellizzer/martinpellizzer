Carex suberea

Carex suberea, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Carex suberea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
