Cirsium shihianum

Cirsium shihianum, commonly known as Shihian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
