Cassia nilgirica

Cassia nilgirica, commonly known as the Nilgiri cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cassia nilgirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
