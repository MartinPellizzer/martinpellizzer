Cacalia flotowioides

Cacalia flotowioides, commonly known as the floating bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has a creeping rootstock and produces small, oval leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes.

Its unique growth habit and floral structure make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
