Carex paniculata var. tenuior

Carex paniculata var. tenuior, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, typically growing in dense clumps. The plant produces tall, slender spikelets that bear small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
