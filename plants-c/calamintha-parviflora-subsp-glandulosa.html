Calamintha parviflora subsp. glandulosa

Calamintha parviflora subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as narrowleaf catnip, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, aromatic stems and small, opposite leaves with a distinctive glandular texture.

The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and for its mild mint-like aroma.
