Calathea contamanensis

Calathea contamanensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage. The leaves are typically broad and have a velvety texture, with intricate patterns of green, silver, and purple. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

This ornamental plant is popular in indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
