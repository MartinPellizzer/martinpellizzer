Chrysophyllum pruniforme

Chrysophyllum pruniforme, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellow or orange edible fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
