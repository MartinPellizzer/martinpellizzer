Clematis pseudograndiflora

Clematis pseudograndiflora, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures for optimal display.
