Corydalis pterygopetala subsp. macrocarpa

Corydalis pterygopetala subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, elongated seeds.

This species is often found in woodland or shaded areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
