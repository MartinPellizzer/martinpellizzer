Crataegus oxyacantha laciniata

Crataegus oxyacantha laciniata, commonly known as the sharplobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its lobed, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
