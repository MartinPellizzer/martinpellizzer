Callicarpa candicans f. glabriuscula

Callicarpa candicans f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaf beautyberry, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by vibrant purple to red berries that attract birds. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of the species. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking berries and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
