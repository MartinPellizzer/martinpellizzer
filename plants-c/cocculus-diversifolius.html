Cocculus diversifolius

Cocculus diversifolius, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved seed pods that resemble a spine, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with alternating, palmately lobed foliage that turns red in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
