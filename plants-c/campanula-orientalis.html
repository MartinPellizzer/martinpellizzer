Campanula orientalis

Campanula orientalis, commonly known as the oriental bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula orientalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
