Coelogyne eduardii

Coelogyne eduardii, commonly known as the Edward's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Coelogyne eduardii is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
