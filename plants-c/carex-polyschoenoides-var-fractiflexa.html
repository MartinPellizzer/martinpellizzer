Carex polyschoenoides var. fractiflexa

Carex polyschoenoides var. fractiflexa, commonly known as the fragrant sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
