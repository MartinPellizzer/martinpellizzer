Campium metallicum

Campium metallicum, commonly known as the copperleaf or metallic copperleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, metallic-green leaves that shimmer in sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
