Cayratia cordifolia

Cayratia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cayratia, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening applications.
