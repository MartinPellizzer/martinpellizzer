Caragana zahlbruckneri

Caragana zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Shoshone sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, needle-like leaflets and produces woody, branching stems that are often covered in a whitish bloom. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to poor soils and harsh climates.

Caragana zahlbruckneri plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
