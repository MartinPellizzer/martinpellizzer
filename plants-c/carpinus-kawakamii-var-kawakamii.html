Carpinus kawakamii var. kawakamii

Carpinus kawakamii var. kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakami hornbeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
