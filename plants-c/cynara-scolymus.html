Cynara scolymus

Cynara scolymus, commonly known as artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and purple flower heads. The plant produces a large, edible bud that is harvested before it fully opens, making it a valuable crop in horticulture.

Its leaves are also used in cooking, and the plant has been cultivated for thousands of years for both its culinary and medicinal properties.
