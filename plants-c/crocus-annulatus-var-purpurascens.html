Crocus annulatus var. purpurascens

Crocus annulatus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its flowers have six tepals that are usually reflexed, giving the flower a distinctive appearance.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland borders.
