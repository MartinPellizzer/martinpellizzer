Camellia grijsii var. grijsii

Camellia grijsii var. grijsii, commonly known as the gray camellia, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
