Claoxylon wightii var. angustatum

Claoxylon wightii var. angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed claoxylon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glabrous. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
