Calamus inermis

Calamus inermis, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its long, narrow rhizomes and arrow-shaped leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
