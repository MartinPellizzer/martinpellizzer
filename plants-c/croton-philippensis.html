Croton philippensis

Croton philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine croton, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Croton philippensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.
