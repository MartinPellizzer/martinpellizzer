Commelina nana

Commelina nana, commonly known as dwarf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, arrow-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are often clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
