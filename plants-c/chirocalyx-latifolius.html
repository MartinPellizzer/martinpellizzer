Chirocalyx latifolius

Chirocalyx latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf chirocalyx, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
