Crassula colligata subsp. lamprosperma

Crassula colligata subsp.

lamprosperma, commonly known as the star-shaped crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that form a rosette.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
