Calligonum pulcherrimum

Calligonum pulcherrimum, commonly known as the beautiful saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are adapted for pollination by wind or insects. It thrives in saline soils and is often used for erosion control due to its hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for rehabilitation of degraded lands in dry climates.
