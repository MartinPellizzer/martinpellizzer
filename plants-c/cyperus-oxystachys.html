Cyperus oxystachys

Cyperus oxystachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, which are followed by distinctive three-angled, spike-like fruits. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Cyperus oxystachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
