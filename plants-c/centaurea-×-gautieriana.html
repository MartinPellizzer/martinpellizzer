Centaurea × gautieriana

Centaurea × gautieriana, commonly known as the hybrid centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and sturdy, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Centaurea × gautieriana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
