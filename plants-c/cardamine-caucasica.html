Cardamine caucasica

Cardamine caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many familiar edible and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a peppery flavor.

The small white or greenish flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.
