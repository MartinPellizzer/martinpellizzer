Colutea arborea

Colutea arborea, commonly known as the tree clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.

Colutea arborea is often found in woodland areas and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
