Chamaedorea microphylla

Chamaedorea microphylla, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or dwarf bamboo, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen palm native to Central America.

It features slender, upright stems and elongated, narrow leaves that give it a delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with consistent moisture. It is highly ornamental and popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.

Chamaedorea microphylla is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it suitable for a variety of indoor environments.
