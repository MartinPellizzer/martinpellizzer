Chenopodium fuegianum

Chenopodium fuegianum, commonly known as the Magellanic goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources. Its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Chenopodium fuegianum is also known for its potential medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal practices.
