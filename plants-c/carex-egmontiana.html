Carex egmontiana

Carex egmontiana, commonly known as the Egmont sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Carex egmontiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
