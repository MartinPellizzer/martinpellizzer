Crossandra spicata

Crossandra spicata, commonly known as the spiked crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Crossandra spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
