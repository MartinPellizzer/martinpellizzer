Celosia welwitschii

Celosia welwitschii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant is typically herbaceous, with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
