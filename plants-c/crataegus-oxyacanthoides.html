Crataegus oxyacanthoides

Crataegus oxyacanthoides, commonly known as the red-berried hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds and wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade in well-drained soils.

Crataegus oxyacanthoides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
