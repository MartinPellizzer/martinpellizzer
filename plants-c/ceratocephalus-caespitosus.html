Ceratocephalus caespitosus

Ceratocephalus caespitosus, commonly known as the clustered burrweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant morphology.
