Chloris argentinensis

Chloris argentinensis, commonly known as Argentine grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
