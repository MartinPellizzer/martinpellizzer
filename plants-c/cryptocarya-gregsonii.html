Cryptocarya gregsonii

Cryptocarya gregsonii, commonly known as the New Zealand treefern, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to New Zealand.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, characterized by its large, fern-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive trunk with a fibrous, spongy outer layer that is often covered in moss and lichens. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Cryptocarya gregsonii thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in native forests along the coastlines of New Zealand.
