Coleus cremnus

Coleus cremnus, commonly known as the mountain coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-draining soil.

Coleus cremnus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
