Camellia trichandra

Camellia trichandra, commonly known as the three-leaf camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers with a distinctive, waxy texture. Camellia trichandra thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
