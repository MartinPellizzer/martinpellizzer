Carex pterocarpa

Carex pterocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, typically green or brown in color, which develop into distinctive, three-lobed berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pterocarpa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
