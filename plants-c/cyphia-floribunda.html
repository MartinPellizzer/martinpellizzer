Cyphia floribunda

Cyphia floribunda, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cyphia floribunda is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
