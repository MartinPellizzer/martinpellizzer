Cephalostachyum burmanicum

Cephalostachyum burmanicum, commonly known as Burmese spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of spikelets at the top of each stem. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Cephalostachyum burmanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ecological benefits.
