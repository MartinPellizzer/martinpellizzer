Callisia amplexans

Callisia amplexans, commonly known as the creeping Jenny, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that features bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, forming dense mats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its showy inflorescences and attractive foliage.
