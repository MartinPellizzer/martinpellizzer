Cyathea callosa

Cyathea callosa, commonly known as the velvet tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem and large, pinnate fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and have a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Cyathea callosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
