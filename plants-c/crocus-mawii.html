Crocus mawii

Crocus mawii, commonly known as the Mawii crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming period, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Crocus mawii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early seasonal display.
