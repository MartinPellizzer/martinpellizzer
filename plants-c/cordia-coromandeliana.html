Cordia coromandeliana

Cordia coromandeliana, commonly known as the Indian redwood or coromandel redwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
