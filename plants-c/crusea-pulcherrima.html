Crusea pulcherrima

Crusea pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in summer. The plant has a straight, often branching trunk and broad, compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.
