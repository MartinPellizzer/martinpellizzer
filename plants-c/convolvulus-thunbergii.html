Convolvulus thunbergii

Convolvulus thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
