Crataegus incompta

Crataegus incompta, commonly known as the Canadian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval to elliptic leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that mature in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
