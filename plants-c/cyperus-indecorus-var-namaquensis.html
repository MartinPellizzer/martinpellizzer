Cyperus indecorus var. namaquensis

Cyperus indecorus var. namaquensis, commonly known as the Namakwa flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water bodies, and prefers moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flower heads, which are usually green to reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
