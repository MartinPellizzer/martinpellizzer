Cirsium breweri var. lanosissimum

Cirsium breweri var. lanosissimum, commonly known as the woolly yellow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to grayish hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in a spherical shape. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
