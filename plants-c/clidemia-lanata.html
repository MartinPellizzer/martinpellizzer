Clidemia lanata

Clidemia lanata, commonly known as velvetleaf or whiteleaf mallow, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its growth habit is often sprawling or shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

Clidemia lanata is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
