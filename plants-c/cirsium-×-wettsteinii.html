Cirsium × wettsteinii

Cirsium × wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble thistles. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a robust stem that is often covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
