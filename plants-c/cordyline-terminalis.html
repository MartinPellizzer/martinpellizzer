Cordyline terminalis

Cordyline terminalis, commonly known as the Ti plant or potted cabbage, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It features tall, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and architectural form.

Cordyline terminalis is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.
