Cassia cubensis

Cassia cubensis, commonly known as Cuban senna, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Cassia cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
