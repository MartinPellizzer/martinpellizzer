Cerasus serotina var. asplenifolia

Cerasus serotina var. asplenifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a variant of the cherry plum species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble the foliage of the fern, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal remedies.
