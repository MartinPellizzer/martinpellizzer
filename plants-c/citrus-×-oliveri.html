Citrus × oliveri

Citrus × oliveri, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between the sweet orange and the pummelo. The tree is evergreen, typically growing to a height of 5-10 meters, with dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and white, and the fruit is usually small, round, and has a thick, yellowish rind.

The fruit is known for its high acidity and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
