Cerastium rhodopeum

Cerastium rhodopeum, commonly known as the Rhodope chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
