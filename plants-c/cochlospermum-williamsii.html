Cochlospermum williamsii

Cochlospermum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' cockspur, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a distinctive, spiky corolla. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with serrated margins.

Cochlospermum williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
