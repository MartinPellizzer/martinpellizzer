Cerasus racemosa

Cerasus racemosa, commonly known as the sweet cherry or wild cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is known for its fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for both its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.
