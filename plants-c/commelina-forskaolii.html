Commelina forskaolii

Commelina forskaolii, commonly known as the purple-veined dayflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with distinctive purple veins and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of blue and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and water features.
