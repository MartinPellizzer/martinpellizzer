Combretum rochetianum

Combretum rochetianum, commonly known as the red sandalwood.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its wood is valued for its durability and has been used traditionally for crafting and construction.
