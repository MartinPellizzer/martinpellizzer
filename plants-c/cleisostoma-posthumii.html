Cleisostoma posthumii

Cleisostoma posthumii, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a rare and exquisite epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble flames, with vibrant orange and yellow hues. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil for nutrients. Its flowers bloom in summer, producing a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Cleisostoma posthumii is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
