Cuphea aperta

Cuphea aperta, commonly known as the open cup plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cuphea aperta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
