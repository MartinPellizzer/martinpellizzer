Carex tenebrosa

Carex tenebrosa, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a dark green to almost black appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered brownish flowers that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
