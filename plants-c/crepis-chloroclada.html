Crepis chloroclada

Crepis chloroclada, commonly known as the greenish mouse-ear.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, silver-green leaves that are typically basal and clustered. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which are often surrounded by pale green bracts. It is native to Europe and is often found in dry, sandy or gravelly soils.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage.
