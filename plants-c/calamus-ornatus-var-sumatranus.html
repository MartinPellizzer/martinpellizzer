Calamus ornatus var. sumatranus

Calamus ornatus var. sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by dark, round fruits. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Sumatra and Borneo.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
