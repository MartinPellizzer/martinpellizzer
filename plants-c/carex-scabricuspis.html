Carex scabricuspis

Carex scabricuspis, commonly known as the rough-serrated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex scabricuspis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
