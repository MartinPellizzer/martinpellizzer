Croton glandulosus sublusus parvifolius

Croton glandulosus sublusus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and palmate leaves.

It features small, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically clustered in panicles. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
