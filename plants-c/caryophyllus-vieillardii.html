Caryophyllus vieillardii

Caryophyllus vieillardii, commonly known as the Vieillard's carnation, is a species of flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Caryophyllus vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
