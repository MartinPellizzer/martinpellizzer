Commiphora ruquietiana

Commiphora ruquietiana, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and can reach a height of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.
