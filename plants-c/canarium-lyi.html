Canarium lyi

Canarium lyi, commonly known as the Bornean canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing oily seeds. Canarium lyi is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native forests.
