Centaurea incana subsp. ornata

Centaurea incana subsp.

ornata, commonly known as the silver centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
