Crotonogyne manniana subsp. congolensis

Crotonogyne manniana subsp.

congolensis, commonly known as the Congo croton, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or yellow.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
