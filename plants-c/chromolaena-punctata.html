Chromolaena punctata

Chromolaena punctata, commonly known as the stinging nettle or false nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small yellowish-white flowers. The plant contains irritant chemicals in its leaves and stems, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, forests, and along roadsides.

Chromolaena punctata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through vegetative propagation.
