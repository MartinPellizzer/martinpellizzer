Campanula apennina

Campanula apennina, commonly known as the Apennine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Campanula apennina is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
