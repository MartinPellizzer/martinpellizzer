Chamaecrista nictitans subsp. patellaria

Chamaecrista nictitans subsp.

patellaria, commonly known as the Florida burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The species is known for its ability to tolerate saturated soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
