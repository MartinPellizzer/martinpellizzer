Clerodendrum manettii

Clerodendrum manettii, commonly known as the purple flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by broad, waxy bracts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
