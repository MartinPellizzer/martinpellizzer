Chenopodium botrydium

Chenopodium botrydium, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, powdery hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Chenopodium botrydium is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in some traditional cuisines.
