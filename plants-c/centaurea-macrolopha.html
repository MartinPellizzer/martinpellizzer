Centaurea macrolopha

Centaurea macrolopha, commonly known as the large-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces spiny bracts that protect its seed capsules, which are typically triangular in shape. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea macrolopha is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.
