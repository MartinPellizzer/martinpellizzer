Crinum sinicum subvar. variegatum

Crinum sinicum subvar. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated Chinese lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern China.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with intricate veining. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subshrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
