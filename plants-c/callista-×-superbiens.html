Callista × superbiens

Callista × superbiens, commonly known as the hybrid callista, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Callista palustris and Callista tenuifolia, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
