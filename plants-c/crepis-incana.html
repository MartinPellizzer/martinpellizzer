Crepis incana

Crepis incana, commonly known as the hoary cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive grayish-white coating. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat cluster, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
