Chamaecyparis taiwanensis

Chamaecyparis taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique appearance.
