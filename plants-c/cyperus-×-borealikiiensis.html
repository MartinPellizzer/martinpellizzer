Cyperus × borealikiiensis

Cyperus × borealikiiensis, commonly known as the northern flatsedge, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on environmental conditions.
