Capsicum microphyllum

Capsicum microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper or miniature bell pepper, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. This plant is characterized by its small, rounded fruits that typically measure less than 2 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are usually broad and slightly lobed, with a glossy green surface.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental or for its unique, edible fruits.
