Caropodium pterocarpum subsp. pterocarpum

Caropodium pterocarpum subsp.

pterocarpum, commonly known as the African pterocarp, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with pinnate arrangements.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
