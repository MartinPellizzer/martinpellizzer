Cinnamomum multiflorum

Cinnamomum multiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces reddish-brown bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine and also in culinary applications.
