Camissonia ignota

Camissonia ignota, commonly known as the slender camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, adapting to its arid environment.

Camissonia ignota is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
