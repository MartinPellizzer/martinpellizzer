Campanula syriaca

Campanula syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
