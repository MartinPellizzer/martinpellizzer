Cousinia haesitabunda

Cousinia haesitabunda, commonly known as the hesitating cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It has hairy, blue-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Cousinia haesitabunda is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
