Cuphea simsii

Cuphea simsii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Cuphea simsii is also valued for its ability to produce vibrant, long-lasting blooms throughout the growing season.
