Cattleya × elegans var. picta

Cattleya × elegans var. picta, commonly known as the Painted Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking, colorful blooms.

It features large, showy flowers with intricate patterns of pink, white, and purple, often resembling a painter's palette. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
