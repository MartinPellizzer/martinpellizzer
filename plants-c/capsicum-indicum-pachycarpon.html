Capsicum indicum pachycarpon

Capsicum indicum pachycarpon, commonly known as the Indian chili or bird's eye chili, is a species native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and hairy leaves. Its fruit is small, round, and turns from green to red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its pungent flavor and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
