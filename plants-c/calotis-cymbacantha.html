Calotis cymbacantha

Calotis cymbacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr medic, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 50 cm in height, characterized by its slender stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for cultivation in dry regions.

The species is often used in herbal medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
