Cyperus juncelliformis

Cyperus juncelliformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in width, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus juncelliformis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
