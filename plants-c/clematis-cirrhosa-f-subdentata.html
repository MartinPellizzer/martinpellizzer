Clematis cirrhosa f. subdentata

Clematis cirrhosa f. subdentata, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, trailing stems and showy, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant features compound leaves with finely serrated margins, which distinguish it from other clematis species. Its flowers, typically blue or violet in color, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
