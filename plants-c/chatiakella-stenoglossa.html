Chatiakella stenoglossa

Chatiakella stenoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers with narrow lips. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
