Carex gonggaensis

Carex gonggaensis, commonly known as the Gongga sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of western China.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This species thrives in moist, cool environments, often found in mountain meadows and alpine regions. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex gonggaensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
