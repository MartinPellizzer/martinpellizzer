Cassia mimosoides var. auricoma

Cassia mimosoides var. auricoma, commonly known as golden cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of the mimosa plant, with slender, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia mimosoides var. auricoma thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
