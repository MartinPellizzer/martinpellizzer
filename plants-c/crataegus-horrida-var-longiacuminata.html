Crataegus horrida var. longiacuminata

Crataegus horrida var. longiacuminata, commonly known as the long-acuminate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and oval, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
