Calceolaria fruticulosa var. flavescens

Calceolaria fruticulosa var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling small cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
