Campanopsis exilis

Campanopsis exilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers. Campanopsis exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
