Cymbidium syringodorum

Cymbidium syringodorum, commonly known as the Chinese orchid or the winter orchid, is a species native to China and is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in cool, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in the late autumn to early winter.

Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with red or purple markings, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
