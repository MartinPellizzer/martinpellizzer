Cachrys pastinacea

Cachrys pastinacea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky purple flower heads that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are generally covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Cachrys pastinacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
