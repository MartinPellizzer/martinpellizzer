Comanthera nitida

Comanthera nitida, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a shiny appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, elongated capsule that contains several seeds.

Comanthera nitida is typically found in shrubland and forest margins, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
