Cyperus argyraeus

Cyperus argyraeus, commonly known as silver flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus argyraeus is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
