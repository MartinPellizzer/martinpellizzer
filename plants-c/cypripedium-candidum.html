Cypripedium candidum

Cypripedium candidum, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of white, fragrant flowers that resemble small trusses. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, elongated flower spike with numerous small, white blossoms.

Cypripedium candidum is a rare and protected species, often found in specific ecological niches.
