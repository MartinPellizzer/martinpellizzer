Centaurea salonitana

Centaurea salonitana, commonly known as the Salona centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves, which are typical of the genus Centaurea. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and dry grasslands. It is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, bract-like leaves and is known for its ornamental value.

Centaurea salonitana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
