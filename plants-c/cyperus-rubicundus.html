Cyperus rubicundus

Cyperus rubicundus, commonly known as the red spikegrass, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with slender, reddish stems and small, clustered flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Cyperus rubicundus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
