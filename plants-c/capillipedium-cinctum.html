Capillipedium cinctum

Capillipedium cinctum, commonly known as the ribbon orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Capillipedium cinctum is a perennial herb that thrives in shaded environments with rich, well-drained soil.
