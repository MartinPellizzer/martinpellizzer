Chenopodium tournefortianum

Chenopodium tournefortianum, commonly known as tumbleweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and production of small, greenish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system and drought tolerance. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant has been used traditionally for its edible leaves and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
