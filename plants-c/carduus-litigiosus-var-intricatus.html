Carduus litigiosus var. intricatus

Carduus litigiosus var. intricatus, commonly known as the intricate thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features spiny, lobed leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, purple-tinged flower head composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces a compact, spherical inflorescence that is typically surrounded by a series of overlapping bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
