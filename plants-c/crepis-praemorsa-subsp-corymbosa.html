Crepis praemorsa subsp. corymbosa

Crepis praemorsa subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish.
