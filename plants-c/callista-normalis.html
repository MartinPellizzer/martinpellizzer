Callista normalis

Callista normalis, commonly known as the common callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Callista normalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
