Chaenorhinum minus var. praetermissum

Chaenorhinum minus var. praetermissum, commonly known as the lesser common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
