Cinnamomum chengkouense

Cinnamomum chengkouense, commonly known as Chengkou cinnamon, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries containing winged seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
