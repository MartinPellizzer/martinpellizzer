Cyperus rancoanus

Cyperus rancoanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. Cyperus rancoanus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in wetlands and along water edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
