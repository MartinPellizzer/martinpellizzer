Cissus nieriensis

Cissus nieriensis, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
