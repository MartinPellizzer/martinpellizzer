Carex recurvisaccus

Carex recurvisaccus, commonly known as the narrow-sheath sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, clump-forming habit. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex recurvisaccus is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in wetland conditions.
