Coleus macranthus

Coleus macranthus, commonly known as the horse shield, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and purple, often with contrasting veins. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, typically in shades of white or pale pink.

Coleus macranthus thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate foliage.
