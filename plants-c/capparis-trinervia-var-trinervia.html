Capparis trinervia var. trinervia

Capparis trinervia var. trinervia, commonly known as the Indian date plum or wild date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits resembling dates. The fruit is edible, though often consumed raw or cooked, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils.
