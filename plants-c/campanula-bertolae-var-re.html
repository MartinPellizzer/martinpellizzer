Campanula bertolae var. re

Campanula bertolae var. re, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
