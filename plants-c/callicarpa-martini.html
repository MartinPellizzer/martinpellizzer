Callicarpa martini

Callicarpa martini, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant violet to purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by its showy fruits.

Callicarpa martini is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
