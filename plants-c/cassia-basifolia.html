Cassia basifolia

Cassia basifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds. Cassia basifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
