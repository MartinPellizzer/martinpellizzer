Caprifolium cyrenaicum

Caprifolium cyrenaicum, commonly known as the Cyprus honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a reddish underside, while its fruit is a small, red berry.

Caprifolium cyrenaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
