Carex lenta

Carex lenta, commonly known as the slow-growing sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Carex lenta has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
