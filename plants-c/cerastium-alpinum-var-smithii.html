Cerastium alpinum var. smithii

Cerastium alpinum var. smithii, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Caryophyllaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures.
