Cardiospermum loxense

Cardiospermum loxense, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lobed margin and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar morphological traits.
