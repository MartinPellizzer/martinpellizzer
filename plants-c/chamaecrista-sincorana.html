Chamaecrista sincorana

Chamaecrista sincorana, commonly known as the pink burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it an important species in its native habitat.
