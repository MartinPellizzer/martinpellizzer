Crataegus viridis var. desertorum

Crataegus viridis var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert hawthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments.
