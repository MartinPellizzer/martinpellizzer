Costus sarmentosus

Costus sarmentosus, commonly known as the creeping costus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture.

Costus sarmentosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
