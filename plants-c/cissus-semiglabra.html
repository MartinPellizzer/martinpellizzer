Cissus semiglabra

Cissus semiglabra, commonly known as the glossy goosefoot, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a shiny appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
