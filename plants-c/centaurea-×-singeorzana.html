Centaurea × singeorzana

Centaurea × singeorzana, commonly known as the hybrid perennial cornflower, is a cross between Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea stoebe.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
