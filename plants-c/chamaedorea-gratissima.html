Chamaedorea gratissima

Chamaedorea gratissima, commonly known as the silver palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It features slender, arching leaves that are dark green with a silvery underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet and has a compact, bushy form. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Chamaedorea gratissima is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
