Calanthe ombrophila

Calanthe ombrophila, commonly known as the rain forest calanthe, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, compact foliage. The plant produces strikingly beautiful, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Calanthe ombrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
