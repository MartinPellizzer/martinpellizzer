Carlina patula

Carlina patula, commonly known as the meadow cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow at the center with white rays.

Carlina patula is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
