Cytisus rigidus

Cytisus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are generally rigid and woody.

Cytisus rigidus is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.
