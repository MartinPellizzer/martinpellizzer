Celosia bicolor

Celosia bicolor, commonly known as flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Celosia bicolor is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
