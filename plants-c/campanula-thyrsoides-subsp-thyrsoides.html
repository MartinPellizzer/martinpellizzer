Campanula thyrsoides subsp. thyrsoides

Campanula thyrsoides subsp.

thyrsoides, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Campanulaceae family, sharing similar morphological traits.
