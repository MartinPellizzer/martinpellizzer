Carduus pycnocephalus subsp. sardous

Carduus pycnocephalus subsp.

sardous, commonly known as the Sardous thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces pale purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
