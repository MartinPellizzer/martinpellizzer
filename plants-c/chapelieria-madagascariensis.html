Chapelieria madagascariensis

Chapelieria madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives on nutrient-poor soils. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the surrounding substrate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
