Cacalia procumbens

Cacalia procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only a few inches. The plant produces small, delicate white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia procumbens thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
