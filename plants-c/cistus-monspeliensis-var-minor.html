Cistus monspeliensis var. minor

Cistus monspeliensis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces yellow, glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive scent. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
