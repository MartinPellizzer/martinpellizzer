Calamagrostis vicunarum var. elatior

Calamagrostis vicunarum var. elatior, commonly known as the tall reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, silvery-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscaping projects.
