Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. pendula-vera

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. pendula-vera, commonly known as the weeping Lawson cypress, is a cultivar of the Lawson cypress that exhibits a distinctive cascading growth habit.

This evergreen conifer is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that create a soft, feathery appearance. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and light green underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its unique form, it is often used as a specimen plant in gardens and parks.
