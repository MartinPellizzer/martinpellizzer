Carex parryana var. statoni

Carex parryana var. statoni, commonly known as Parry's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, three-lobed flowers. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and meadows. Its foliage is typically dark green and dense, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens with wet or semi-wet conditions.
