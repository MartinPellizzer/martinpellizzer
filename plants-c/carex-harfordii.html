Carex harfordii

Carex harfordii, commonly known as Harford's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.
