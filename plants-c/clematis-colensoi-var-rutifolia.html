Clematis colensoi var. rutifolia

Clematis colensoi var. rutifolia, commonly known as the Colenso's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale blue flowers with yellow anthers, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and serrated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
