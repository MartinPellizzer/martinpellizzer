Crataegus rubicunda

Crataegus rubicunda, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Crataegus rubicunda is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
