Clematoclethra racemosa

Clematoclethra racemosa, commonly known as the western thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when ripe, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in forested areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
