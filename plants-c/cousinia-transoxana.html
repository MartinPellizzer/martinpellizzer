Cousinia transoxana

Cousinia transoxana, commonly known as the Transoxiana cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Cousinia transoxana is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
