Calanthe anocentrum

Calanthe anocentrum, commonly known as the golden calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calanthe anocentrum is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
