Capsicum cordiforme

Capsicum cordiforme, commonly known as the heart-shaped chili, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped fruits, which can range in color from green to red when mature. The plant is an herbaceous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated edge, and the flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit contains numerous small seeds enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp.
