Carex × macilenta

Carex × macilenta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
