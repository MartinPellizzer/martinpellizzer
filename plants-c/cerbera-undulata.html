Cerbera undulata

Cerbera undulata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, green, often waxy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Cerbera undulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
