Crataegus digna

Crataegus digna, commonly known as the noble hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorny branches, which are a characteristic feature of the Crataegus genus. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

The noble hawthorn is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
