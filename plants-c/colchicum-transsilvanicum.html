Colchicum transsilvanicum

Colchicum transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem. It is adapted to grow in woodland and rocky habitats, often in shaded environments.

The species is part of the Colchicaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
