Crotalaria natalitia var. procumbens

Crotalaria natalitia var. procumbens, commonly known as Natal spurred grass, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, prostrate stems that produce small, yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
