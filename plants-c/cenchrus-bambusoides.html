Cenchrus bambusoides

Cenchrus bambusoides, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
