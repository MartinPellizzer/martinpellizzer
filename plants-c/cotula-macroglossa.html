Cotula macroglossa

Cotula macroglossa, commonly known as the large-tongue cotula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Cotula macroglossa is typically found in open, sandy habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its foliage is usually hairy, providing some protection against desiccation.
