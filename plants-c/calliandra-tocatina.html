Calliandra tocatina

Calliandra tocatina, commonly known as the Mexican orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Calliandra tocatina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
