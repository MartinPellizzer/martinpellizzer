Carex taquetii

Carex taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous or high-altitude environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Carex taquetii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
