Crataegus × porrecta

Crataegus × porrecta, commonly known as the common hawthorn or European hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and typically grows as a shrub or small tree. It has thorny branches and is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

Crataegus × porrecta is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
