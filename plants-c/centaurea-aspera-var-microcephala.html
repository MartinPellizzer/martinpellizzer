Centaurea aspera var. microcephala

Centaurea aspera var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features spiny, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color. The plant produces small, spherical flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
