Chenopodium scabricaule var. robustum

Chenopodium scabricaule var. robustum, commonly known as roughleaf goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like leaves and stout, branching stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
