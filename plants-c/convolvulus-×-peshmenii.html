Convolvulus × peshmenii

Convolvulus × peshmenii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a hybrid species native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in dry climates.
