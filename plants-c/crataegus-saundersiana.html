Crataegus saundersiana

Crataegus saundersiana, commonly known as the Saunders hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
