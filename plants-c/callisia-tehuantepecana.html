Callisia tehuantepecana

Callisia tehuantepecana, commonly known as the Mexican carpet plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with prominent white veins. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Callisia tehuantepecana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover due to its ability to spread rapidly.
