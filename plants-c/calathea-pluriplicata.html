Calathea pluriplicata

Calathea pluriplicata, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns and shades of green, often featuring a deep, almost black center. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
