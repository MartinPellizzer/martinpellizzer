Carex incerta

Carex incerta, commonly known as the uncertain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the genus Carex in the family Cyperaceae, which includes over 2,000 species of sedges. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex incerta is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
