Cousinia pulchella

Cousinia pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful cousinia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually silver-gray, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Cousinia pulchella produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
