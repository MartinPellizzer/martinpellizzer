Cathartolinum aristatum

Cathartolinum aristatum, commonly known as the spiky bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, spiky, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a desirable addition to botanical collections.
