Cirsium × hargitanum

Cirsium × hargitanum, commonly known as the Hargita thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
