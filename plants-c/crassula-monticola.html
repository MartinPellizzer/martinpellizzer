Crassula monticola

Crassula monticola, commonly known as the mountain crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Crassula monticola is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
