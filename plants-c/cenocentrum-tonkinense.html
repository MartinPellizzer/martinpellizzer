Cenocentrum tonkinense

Cenocentrum tonkinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese star anise, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit, which is the primary part used commercially, develops from a single ovary and contains multiple oil glands.
