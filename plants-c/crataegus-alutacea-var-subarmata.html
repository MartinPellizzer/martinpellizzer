Crataegus alutacea var. subarmata

Crataegus alutacea var. subarmata, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
