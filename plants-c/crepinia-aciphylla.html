Crepinia aciphylla

Crepinia aciphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, clay soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact rosette. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, ribbed seeds.

Crepinia aciphylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing food for various invertebrates.
