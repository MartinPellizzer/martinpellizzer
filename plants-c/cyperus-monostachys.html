Cyperus monostachys

Cyperus monostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Cyperus monostachys is often used in ornamental gardening and has some traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
