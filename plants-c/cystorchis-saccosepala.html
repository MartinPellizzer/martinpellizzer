Cystorchis saccosepala

Cystorchis saccosepala, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a sac-like ovary that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a greenish-yellow coloration.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
