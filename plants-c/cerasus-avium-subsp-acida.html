Cerasus avium subsp. acida

Cerasus avium subsp.

acida, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a subspecies of the sweet cherry native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, tart fruits that are widely used in cooking, baking, and preserves.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
