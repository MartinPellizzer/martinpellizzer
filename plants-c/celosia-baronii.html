Celosia baronii

Celosia baronii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flower heads that resemble flames. The plant produces showy, bract-based inflorescences in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Celosia baronii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
