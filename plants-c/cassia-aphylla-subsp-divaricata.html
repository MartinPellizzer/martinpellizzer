Cassia aphylla subsp. divaricata

Cassia aphylla subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil. Cassia aphylla subsp.

divaricata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
