Crotalaria medicaginea var. neglecta

Crotalaria medicaginea var. neglecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
