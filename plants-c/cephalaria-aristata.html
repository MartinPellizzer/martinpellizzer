Cephalaria aristata

Cephalaria aristata, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
