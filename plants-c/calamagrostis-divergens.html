Calamagrostis divergens

Calamagrostis divergens, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tinged foliage and tall, drooping inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that gives it its common name.

Calamagrostis divergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
