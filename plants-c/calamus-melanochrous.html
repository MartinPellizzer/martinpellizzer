Calamus melanochrous

Calamus melanochrous, commonly known as black-rooted arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered, fibrous fruits. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various insects and small animals.
