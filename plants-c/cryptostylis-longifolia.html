Cryptostylis longifolia

Cryptostylis longifolia, commonly known as the long-fruited star-flower, is a species of orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous minute seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as rainforests and understoreys.

Cryptostylis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
