Croton huitotorum

Croton huitotorum, commonly known as the Huitot tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala and Belize.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, rough bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fruit that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
