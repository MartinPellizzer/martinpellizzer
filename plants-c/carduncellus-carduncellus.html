Carduncellus carduncellus

Carduncellus carduncellus, commonly known as the common cardoon, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thick, ribbed stems and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a woolly coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spiky clusters during the summer months. It is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
