Cyperus fragilis

Cyperus fragilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Cyperus fragilis is an annual or perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense vegetation in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
