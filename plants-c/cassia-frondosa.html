Cassia frondosa

Cassia frondosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cassia frondosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
