Carex amgunensis

Carex amgunensis, commonly known as the Amgun sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as tundra and wet meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex amgunensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic wildlife species.
