Carex distans f. sinaica

Carex distans f. sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to semi-arid environments.
