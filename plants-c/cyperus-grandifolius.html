Cyperus grandifolius

Cyperus grandifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

Cyperus grandifolius is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various water conditions.
