Cousinia microcarpa

Cousinia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in summer, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Cousinia microcarpa is often found in steppes and mountainous areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
