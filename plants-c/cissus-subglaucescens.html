Cissus subglaucescens

Cissus subglaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its stems are succulent and can store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.
