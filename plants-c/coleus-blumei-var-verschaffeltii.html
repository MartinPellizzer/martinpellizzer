Coleus blumei var. verschaffeltii

Coleus blumei var. verschaffeltii, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that exhibit a wide range of colors, including deep red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and showy flowers. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, as the small, tubular flowers are less prominent.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its vibrant, eye-catching leaves.
