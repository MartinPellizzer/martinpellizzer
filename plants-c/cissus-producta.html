Cissus producta

Cissus producta, commonly known as the creeping dogwood or silver vine, is a member of the grape family (Vitaceae).

It is a trailing or climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, which give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Cissus producta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate moisture.
