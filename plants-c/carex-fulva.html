Carex fulva

Carex fulva, commonly known as yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to green in color. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or reddish in hue.

Carex fulva thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in shaded areas.
