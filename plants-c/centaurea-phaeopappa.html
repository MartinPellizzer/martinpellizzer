Centaurea phaeopappa

Centaurea phaeopappa, commonly known as the purple centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, violet to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Centaurea phaeopappa is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
