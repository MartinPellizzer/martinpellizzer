Cassia aristatella

Cassia aristatella, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia aristatella is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
