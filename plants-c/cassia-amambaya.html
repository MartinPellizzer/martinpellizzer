Cassia amambaya

Cassia amambaya, commonly known as the Amazonian senna, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
