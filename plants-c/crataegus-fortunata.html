Crataegus fortunata

Crataegus fortunata, commonly known as the Fortune's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters, featuring thorned branches and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, orange, or yellow berries in the fall. Its fruit is an important food source for various birds and wildlife, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
