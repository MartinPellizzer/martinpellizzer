Centaurea ghomerytha var. integrifolia

Centaurea ghomerytha var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Persian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It features striking blue to purple ray florets surrounded by a dense, spiky disk of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
