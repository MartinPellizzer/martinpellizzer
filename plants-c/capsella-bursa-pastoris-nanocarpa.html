Capsella bursa-pastoris nanocarpa

Capsella bursa-pastoris nanocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb with distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a pubescent surface, while the flowers are small and yellow with four petals.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
