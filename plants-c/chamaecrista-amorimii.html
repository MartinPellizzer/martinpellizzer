Chamaecrista amorimii

Chamaecrista amorimii, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for nitrogen-fixing abilities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodland areas.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, which aids in seed dispersal by animals.
