Cistrum asperum

Cistrum asperum, commonly known as the rough cist, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant adapted to Mediterranean climates.
