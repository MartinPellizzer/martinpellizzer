Coreopsis galeottii

Coreopsis galeottii, commonly known as the Florida coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds. The plant features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
