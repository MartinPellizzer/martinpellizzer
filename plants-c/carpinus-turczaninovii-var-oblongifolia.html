Carpinus turczaninovii var. oblongifolia

Carpinus turczaninovii var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits that mature to a dark blue color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
