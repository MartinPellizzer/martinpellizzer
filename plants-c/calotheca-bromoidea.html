Calotheca bromoidea

Calotheca bromoidea, commonly known as the bromeliad calotheca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Bromeliaceae and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calotheca bromoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting various insect and bird species.
