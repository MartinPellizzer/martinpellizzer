Cardamine rupicola

Cardamine rupicola, commonly known as rock chickweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and specialized species in many areas.
