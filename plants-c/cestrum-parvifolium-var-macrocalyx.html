Cestrum parvifolium var. macrocalyx

Cestrum parvifolium var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered nightshade, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal flowering habit.
