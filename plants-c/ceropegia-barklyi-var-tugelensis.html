Ceropegia barklyi var. tugelensis

Ceropegia barklyi var. tugelensis, commonly known as the Tugel ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is relatively rare and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized collections.
