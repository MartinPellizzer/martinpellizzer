Carex scaposa var. baviensis

Carex scaposa var. baviensis, commonly known as the Baviensian sedge, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
