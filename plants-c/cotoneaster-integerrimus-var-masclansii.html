Cotoneaster integerrimus var. masclansii

Cotoneaster integerrimus var. masclansii, commonly known as the Masclans cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
