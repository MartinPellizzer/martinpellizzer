Cissus viridescens

Cissus viridescens, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, jelly-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus viridescens is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
