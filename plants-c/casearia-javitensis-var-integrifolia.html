Casearia javitensis var. integrifolia

Casearia javitensis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved casearia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
