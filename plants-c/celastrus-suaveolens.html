Celastrus suaveolens

Celastrus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet-smelling staff-vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its opposite, simple, and often aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
