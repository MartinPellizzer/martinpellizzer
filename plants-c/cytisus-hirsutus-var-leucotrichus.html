Cytisus hirsutus var. leucotrichus

Cytisus hirsutus var. leucotrichus, commonly known as the white-haired broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
