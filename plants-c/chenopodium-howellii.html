Chenopodium howellii

Chenopodium howellii, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits called utricles.

Chenopodium howellii is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
