Copiapoa desertorum

Copiapoa desertorum, commonly known as the Copiapoa cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a short, thick stem and typically produces white or yellow flowers during the cooler months. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to the arid environment, allowing it to absorb moisture from infrequent rains. Its spines are short and cylindrical, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Copiapoa desertorum is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.
