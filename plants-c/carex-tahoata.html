Carex tahoata

Carex tahoata, commonly known as the Tahoe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or alpine environments. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex tahoata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
