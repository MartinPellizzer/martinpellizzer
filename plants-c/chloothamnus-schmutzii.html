Chloothamnus schmutzii

Chloothamnus schmutzii, commonly known as Schmutz's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it forms low, spreading mats.
