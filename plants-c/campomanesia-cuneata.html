Campomanesia cuneata

Campomanesia cuneata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, bright red berries that are edible. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
