Citrus macroptera var. southwickii

Citrus macroptera var. southwickii, commonly known as the Southwick's kumquat, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are white to pale yellow in color. The plant bears fruit that transitions from green to orange as it ripens, with a thin, edible peel and sweet, segmented pulp. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often with a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit, which is often used in both culinary and ornamental settings.
