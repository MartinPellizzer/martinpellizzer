Cinnamomum subtetrapterum

Cinnamomum subtetrapterum, commonly known as the lesser cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces oval-shaped berries that contain a single seed, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
