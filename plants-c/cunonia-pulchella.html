Cunonia pulchella

Cunonia pulchella, commonly known as the velvet bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Cunonia pulchella is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native range.
