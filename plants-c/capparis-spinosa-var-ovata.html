Capparis spinosa var. ovata

Capparis spinosa var. ovata, commonly known as the globe caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces oval-shaped, greenish-yellow fruits that mature into a red, fleshy berry. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible flowers and ornamental value in gardens.
