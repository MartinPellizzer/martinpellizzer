Carex kamtschatica

Carex kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of the Russian Far East and Alaska.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps. Its flowering spikes are usually pink to purple in color, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex kamtschatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-poor soils.
