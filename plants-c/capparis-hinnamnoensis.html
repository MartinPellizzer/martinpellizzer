Capparis hinnamnoensis

Capparis hinnamnoensis, commonly known as the Hinnamno mountain caper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Thailand.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.
