Cota tinctoria

Cota tinctoria, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and aromatic, hairy leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its flowers are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in diameter, with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Cota tinctoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
