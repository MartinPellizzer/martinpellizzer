Convolvulus uniflorus

Convolvulus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often hairy, while the stems are hairy and can twine around supports.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
