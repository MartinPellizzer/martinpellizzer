Chilocarpus suaveolens

Chilocarpus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
