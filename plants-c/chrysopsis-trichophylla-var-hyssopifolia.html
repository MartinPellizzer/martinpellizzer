Chrysopsis trichophylla var. hyssopifolia

Chrysopsis trichophylla var. hyssopifolia, commonly known as the hyssop-leaved goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showing a preference for full sun. Its botanical features include a low-growing habit and a flowering period that extends from spring to early summer.

The variety hyssopifolia is distinguished by its dense, hyssop-like foliage, which gives it its common name.
