Croton subsuavis

Croton subsuavis, commonly known as the sweet croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically thick and waxy, with a distinctive veining pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
