Cyanococcus virgatus

Cyanococcus virgatus, commonly known as the blueberry cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature common in desert-adapted cacti.

Cyanococcus virgatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
