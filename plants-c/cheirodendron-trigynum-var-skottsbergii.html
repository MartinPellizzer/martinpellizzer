Cheirodendron trigynum var. skottsbergii

Cheirodendron trigynum var. skottsbergii, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits.

This species is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
