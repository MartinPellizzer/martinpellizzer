Chrysanthemum tenuissimum

Chrysanthemum tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
