Cordiera triflora

Cordiera triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered cordiera, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited habitat and endangered status, Cordiera triflora is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
