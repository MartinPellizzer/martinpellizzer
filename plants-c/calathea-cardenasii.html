Calathea cardenasii

Calathea cardenasii, commonly known as the Cretan prayer plant, is a tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, veined, arrowhead-shaped leaves that change color in response to light exposure. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage exhibits a unique phenomenon called nyctinasty, where the leaves fold shut at night.

Calathea cardenasii requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
