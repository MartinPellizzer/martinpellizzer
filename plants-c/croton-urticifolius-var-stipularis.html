Croton urticifolius var. stipularis

Croton urticifolius var. stipularis, commonly known as the stinging croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
