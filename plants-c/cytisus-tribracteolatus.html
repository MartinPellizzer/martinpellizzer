Cytisus tribracteolatus

Cytisus tribracteolatus, commonly known as the three-bract broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters during the spring, which are borne on short peduncles. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
