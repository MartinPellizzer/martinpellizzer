Casearia souzae

Casearia souzae, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small shrub that produces clusters of bright purple berries, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Casearia souzae is valued for its ornamental berries and ecological importance in its native habitat.
