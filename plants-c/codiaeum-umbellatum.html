Codiaeum umbellatum

Codiaeum umbellatum, commonly known as the flame leaf or croton plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed capsules containing seeds.

Codiaeum umbellatum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
