Carex atrata subsp. castanea

Carex atrata subsp.

castanea, commonly known as the chestnut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges.

This subspecies is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
