Cytisus austriacus var. calcareus

Cytisus austriacus var. calcareus, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and thorned stems, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, which is reflected in its varietal name. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is often found in open woodlands and along rocky slopes, where it contributes to the local flora.
