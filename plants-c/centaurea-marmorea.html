Centaurea marmorea

Centaurea marmorea, commonly known as the marble centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea marmorea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
