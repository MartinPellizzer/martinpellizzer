Cyathea barotu

Cyathea barotu, commonly known as the Barotu tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with a single trunk that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its fronds are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and can grow to lengths of over two meters.

Cyathea barotu plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest structure and providing habitat for various organisms.
