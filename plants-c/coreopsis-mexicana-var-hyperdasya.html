Coreopsis mexicana var. hyperdasya

Coreopsis mexicana var. hyperdasya, commonly known as the Mexican coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
