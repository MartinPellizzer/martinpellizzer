Cyperus mutisii var. martinicensis

Cyperus mutisii var. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants. This variant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
