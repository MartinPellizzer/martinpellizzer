Cuphea gratioloides

Cuphea gratioloides, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant is prized for its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea gratioloides is also known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators with its nectar-rich blooms.
