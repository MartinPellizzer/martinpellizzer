Cytisus nigricans f. pubescens

Cytisus nigricans f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy black broom, is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants capable of nitrogen fixation. The plant features finely hairy foliage, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
