Carpinus distegocarpus

Carpinus distegocarpus, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by hard, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
