Cracca candida var. normalis

Cracca candida var. normalis, commonly known as the white cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. It has a low-growing habit with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
