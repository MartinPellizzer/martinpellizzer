Cuphea heydei

Cuphea heydei, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil.

Cuphea heydei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
