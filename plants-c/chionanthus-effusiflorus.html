Chionanthus effusiflorus

Chionanthus effusiflorus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that resemble lanterns, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Chionanthus effusiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
