Cleome microtatodonta

Cleome microtatodonta, commonly known as the dwarf spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spider-like flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cleome microtatodonta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
