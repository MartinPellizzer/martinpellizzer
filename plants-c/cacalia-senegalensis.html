Cacalia senegalensis

Cacalia senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Cacalia senegalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
