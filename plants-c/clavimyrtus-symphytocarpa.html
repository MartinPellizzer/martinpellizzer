Clavimyrtus symphytocarpa

Clavimyrtus symphytocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruit myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a distinctive red drupe that contains a single seed, making it visually striking.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
