Capsella bursa-pastoris jeniseiensis

Capsella bursa-pastoris jeniseiensis, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive heart-shaped seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in many parts of the world.
