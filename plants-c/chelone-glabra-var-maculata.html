Chelone glabra var. maculata

Chelone glabra var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple spot at the base. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
