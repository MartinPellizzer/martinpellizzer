Cucumis melo var. orientalis

Cucumis melo var. orientalis, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variant of the melon genus native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit with a smooth, pale green skin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for trellising or ground cover. Its fruit is typically harvested when it is still immature, resulting in a crisp, refreshing texture.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and is commonly used in salads and fresh consumption.
