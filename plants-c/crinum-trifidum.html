Crinum trifidum

Crinum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Crinum trifidum typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral displays.
