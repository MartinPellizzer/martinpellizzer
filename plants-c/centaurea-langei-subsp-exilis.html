Centaurea langei subsp. exilis

Centaurea langei subsp.

exilis, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies exilis is distinguished by its shorter stature and narrower leaves compared to other members of the Centaurea genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
