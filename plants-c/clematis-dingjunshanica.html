Clematis dingjunshanica

Clematis dingjunshanica, commonly known as the Dingjunshan clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and showy flowers. The plant produces large, bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

Clematis dingjunshanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
