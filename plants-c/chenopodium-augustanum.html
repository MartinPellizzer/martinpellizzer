Chenopodium augustanum

Chenopodium augustanum, commonly known as wild spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Chenopodium augustanum is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and have a slightly bitter taste.
