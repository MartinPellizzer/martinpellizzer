Croton nitidulus var. pubescens

Croton nitidulus var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and can vary in color from deep green to reddish tones.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
