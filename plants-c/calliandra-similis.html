Calliandra similis

Calliandra similis, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.

Calliandra similis thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
