Costus tonkinensis

Costus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin costus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
