Christia lychnucha

Christia lychnucha, commonly known as the candleweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. Its seed pods are slender and contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
