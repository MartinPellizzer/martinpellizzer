Campanula mairei f. ramosa

Campanula mairei f. ramosa, commonly known as the Maire's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that are often branched, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula mairei f. ramosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
