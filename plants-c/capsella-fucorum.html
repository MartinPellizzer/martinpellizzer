Capsella fucorum

Capsella fucorum, commonly known as the sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods containing numerous seeds. It thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and tidal zones.

Capsella fucorum plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
