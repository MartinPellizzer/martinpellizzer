Canavalia peninsularis

Canavalia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular Bean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

It is a climbing legume that produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers and bean pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Canavalia peninsularis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in local ecosystems and its potential agricultural applications.
