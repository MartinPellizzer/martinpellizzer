Calea clematidea

Calea clematidea, commonly known as the sacred plant of the Maya, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking white to violet flowers that resemble small bells. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative effects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is cultivated for both ornamental and cultural purposes.

Its unique appearance and historical significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
