Clematis villosa subsp. bakeri

Clematis villosa subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves.

The plant produces fragrant, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
