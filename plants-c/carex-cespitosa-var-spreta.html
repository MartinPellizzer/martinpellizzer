Carex cespitosa var. spreta

Carex cespitosa var. spreta, commonly known as the slender swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
