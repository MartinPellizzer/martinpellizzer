Carphopappus baccharidifolius

Carphopappus baccharidifolius, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy foliage to reduce water loss. Its leaves are alternate and often have a waxy coating, which helps in retaining moisture.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
