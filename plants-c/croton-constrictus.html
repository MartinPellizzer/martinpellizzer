Croton constrictus

Croton constrictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored leaves that exhibit a variety of shapes and patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
