Carduus acanthium

Carduus acanthium, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and large, spherical seed heads that contain numerous seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its role in pollination.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
