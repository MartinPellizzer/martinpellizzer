Carex pauciflora

Carex pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
