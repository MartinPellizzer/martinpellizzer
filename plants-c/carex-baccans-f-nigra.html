Carex baccans f. nigra

Carex baccans f. nigra, commonly known as black-fruited sedges, is a variant of the Carex baccans species, characterized by its dark, almost black berries.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is native to regions of North America. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

The dark berries are an important food source for various bird species and small mammals.
