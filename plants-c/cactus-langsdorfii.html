Cactus langsdorfii

Cactus langsdorfii, commonly known as the Langsdorf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a spineless, globular cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. Its most distinctive feature is its dense covering of white, woolly hairs, which help to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions and requires minimal water to survive.
