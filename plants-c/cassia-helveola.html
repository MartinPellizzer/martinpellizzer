Cassia helveola

Cassia helveola, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with multiple oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Cassia helveola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
