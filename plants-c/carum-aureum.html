Carum aureum

Carum aureum, commonly known as golden chervil, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are finely divided and have a delicate, anise-like aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Carum aureum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its subtle culinary uses in seasoning.
