Carex goodenowii f. silvatica

Carex goodenowii f. silvatica, commonly known as the forest sedge, is a variant of the Carex genus native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of male and female flowers arranged in separate spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
