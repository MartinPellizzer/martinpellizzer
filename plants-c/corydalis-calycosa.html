Corydalis calycosa

Corydalis calycosa, commonly known as the California larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Corydalis calycosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
