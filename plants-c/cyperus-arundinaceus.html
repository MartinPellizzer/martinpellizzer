Cyperus arundinaceus

Cyperus arundinaceus, commonly known as purple flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive purple tinge on the undersides, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
