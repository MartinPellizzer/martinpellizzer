Crataegus intricata var. straminea

Crataegus intricata var. straminea, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers and red berries, which appear in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is typically smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
