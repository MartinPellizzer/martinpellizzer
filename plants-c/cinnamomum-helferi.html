Cinnamomum helferi

Cinnamomum helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's cinnamon tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct cinnamon-like fragrance when crushed.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of spice.
