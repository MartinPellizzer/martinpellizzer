Clinopodium xalapense

Clinopodium xalapense, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Clinopodium xalapense is valued for its strong aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and cooking.
