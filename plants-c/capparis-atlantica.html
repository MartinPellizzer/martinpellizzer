Capparis atlantica

Capparis atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic Caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small greenish-white flowers, and edible, tangy fruits. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in rocky or limestone habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically borne in clusters.

Capparis atlantica is known for its ability to withstand salt spray and is often used in coastal landscaping.
