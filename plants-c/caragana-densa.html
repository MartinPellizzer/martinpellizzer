Caragana densa

Caragana densa, commonly known as Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, and it is well adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Caragana densa is often used for erosion control and as a ornamental plant in colder regions.
