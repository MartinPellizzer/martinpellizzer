Carex efflata

Carex efflata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Carex efflata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
