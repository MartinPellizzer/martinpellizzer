Cylindria

Cylindria, commonly known as the cylinder orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

These plants are characterized by their unique, elongated floral structures that resemble cylinders, hence their common name. They are native to various regions in Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. Cylindria species typically grow in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth.

Their flowers are usually small and pale, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.
