Cyathea coactilis

Cyathea coactilis, commonly known as the Coactilis tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome is thick and robust, supporting the plant's structure and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea coactilis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
