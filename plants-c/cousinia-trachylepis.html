Cousinia trachylepis

Cousinia trachylepis, commonly known as the purple sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia trachylepis produces small, purple to pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
