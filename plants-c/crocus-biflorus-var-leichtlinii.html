Crocus biflorus var. leichtlinii

Crocus biflorus var. leichtlinii, commonly known as the Alpine crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, early blooming flowers that typically appear in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in shades of yellow, orange, or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
