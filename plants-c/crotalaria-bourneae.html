Crotalaria bourneae

Crotalaria bourneae, commonly known as the African Crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria bourneae is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, some species within the genus are considered toxic, so caution is advised when handling this plant.
