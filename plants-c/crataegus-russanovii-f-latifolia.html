Crataegus russanovii f. latifolia

Crataegus russanovii f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hawthorn, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, red berry that matures in the fall, attracting various wildlife species. It is native to parts of Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
