Crowea angustifolia var. dentata

Crowea angustifolia var. dentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crowea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its varietal name "dentata.". The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
