Croton calococcus

Croton calococcus, commonly known as the red torch croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that range from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are followed by woody, spiky capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton calococcus requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
