Cupressus lusitanica var. glauca

Cupressus lusitanica var. glauca, commonly known as the blue atlas cedar, is a variant of the Atlas cedar native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and forms a conical or pyramidal shape when mature. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are densely packed, contributing to its dense, compact form.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
