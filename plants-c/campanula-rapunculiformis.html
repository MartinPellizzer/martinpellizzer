Campanula rapunculiformis

Campanula rapunculiformis, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula rapunculiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
