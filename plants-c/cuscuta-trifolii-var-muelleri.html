Cuscuta trifolii var. muelleri

Cuscuta trifolii var. muelleri, commonly known as the dwarf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around host plants. The plant is native to regions of North America and is often found parasitizing grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops and native vegetation.
