Cistus symphytifolius subsp. leucophyllus

Cistus symphytifolius subsp.

leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
