Citrus × otaitense

Citrus × otaitense, commonly known as the Tahitian lime or kaffir lime, is a hybrid citrus plant native to the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and fruit, are widely used in culinary and aromatic applications.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and consistent moisture.
