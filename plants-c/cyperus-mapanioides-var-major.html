Cyperus mapanioides var. major

Cyperus mapanioides var. major, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with triangular cross-sections, and its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
