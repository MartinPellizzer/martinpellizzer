Cryptostylis hamadryas

Cryptostylis hamadryas, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and the inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers.

Cryptostylis hamadryas is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
