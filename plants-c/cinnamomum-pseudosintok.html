Cinnamomum pseudosintok

Cinnamomum pseudosintok, commonly known as the Sunda cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial properties.

The bark, similar to true cinnamon, contains essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.
