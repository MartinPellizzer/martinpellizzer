Citrus tuberosa

Citrus tuberosa, commonly known as bitter orange, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic peel and a bitter, pungent flavor in its fruit. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that are often used in perfumery. Its roots and leaves contain compounds with medicinal properties, including antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Citrus tuberosa is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
