Clematis chrysocarpa subsp. chrysocarpa

Clematis chrysocarpa subsp.

chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia. It features slender, twining stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white to pale blue. Its most distinctive feature is its yellow, fleshy fruits, which are edible and contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
