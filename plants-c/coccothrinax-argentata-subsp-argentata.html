Coccothrinax argentata subsp. argentata

Coccothrinax argentata subsp.

argentata, commonly known as the silver palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Caribbean. It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a silvery-blue waxy coating.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, which are followed by edible, clustered fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and ornamental species.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
