Capparis micracantha

Capparis micracantha, commonly known as the dwarf caper, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, greenish-yellow berries. The plant has thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
