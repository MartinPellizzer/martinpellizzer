Cymbopogon schoenanthus var. stypticus

Cymbopogon schoenanthus var. stypticus, commonly known as the styptic grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is known for its aromatic properties, with essential oils that have been traditionally used for their antiseptic and calming effects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its medicinal value, it is often cultivated for its therapeutic benefits in herbal remedies.
