Cymbachne guineensis subvar. fastigiata

Cymbachne guineensis subvar. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright Guinea grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and sometimes have a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are often pale green or brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in landscaping and as a component in ecological restoration projects.
