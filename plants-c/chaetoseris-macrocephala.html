Chaetoseris macrocephala

Chaetoseris macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flowers are small and yellow, often grouped in clusters.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
