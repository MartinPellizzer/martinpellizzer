Carex laxiflora var. serrulata

Carex laxiflora var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, grass-like appearance.
