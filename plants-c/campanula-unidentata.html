Campanula unidentata

Campanula unidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Campanula unidentata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
