Carex scoparia var. moniliformis

Carex scoparia var. moniliformis, commonly known as the bearded sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its distinctive, bearded inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
