Chromolaena lundellii

Chromolaena lundellii, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white to grayish seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain areas.
