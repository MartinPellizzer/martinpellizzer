Crudia falcata

Crudia falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Crudia falcata is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in disturbed or shaded habitats.
