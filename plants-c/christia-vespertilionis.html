Christia vespertilionis

Christia vespertilionis, commonly known as the batflower, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent petals that glow faintly in the dark, a feature that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats. The plant's flowers open only at night, aligning with its pollination strategy. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, tropical habitat.

Christia vespertilionis is also notable for its slow growth and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
