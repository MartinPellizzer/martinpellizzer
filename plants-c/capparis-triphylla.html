Capparis triphylla

Capparis triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, usually green or yellowish leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by small, round, green fruits. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
