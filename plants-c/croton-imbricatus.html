Croton imbricatus

Croton imbricatus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a striking, colorful appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne on long, branched stems. Croton imbricatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
