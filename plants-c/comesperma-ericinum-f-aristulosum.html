Comesperma ericinum f. aristulosum

Comesperma ericinum f. aristulosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved form of the broad-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
