Callianthe striata

Callianthe striata, commonly known as the striped hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant features palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Callianthe striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
