Corchorus erodioides var. bicrenatus

Corchorus erodioides var. bicrenatus, commonly known as the two-lobed spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is notable for its potential medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
