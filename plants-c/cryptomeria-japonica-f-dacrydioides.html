Cryptomeria japonica f. dacrydioides

Cryptomeria japonica f. dacrydioides, commonly known as the Japanese cedar dwarf form, is a unique variant of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more densely packed foliage compared to the typical species. This form exhibits a slower growth rate, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. The leaves are scale-like and dark green, providing a dense, evergreen appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive, rounded shape and resilience to urban conditions.
