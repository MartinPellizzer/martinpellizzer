Convolvulus polycarpus

Convolvulus polycarpus, commonly known as the everlasting sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces numerous small, round seeds that are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate leaves with a heart-shaped base.
