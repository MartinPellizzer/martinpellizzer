Cliffortia dichotoma

Cliffortia dichotoma, commonly known as the mountain sandthorn, is a small shrub native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and is often found growing on rocky outcrops and slopes. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is well adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.
