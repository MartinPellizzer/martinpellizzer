Ceropegia edithae

Ceropegia edithae, commonly known as the "Edith's Crown," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, known for its unique floral structures.

Due to its ornamental value and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
