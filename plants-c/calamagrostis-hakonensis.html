Calamagrostis hakonensis

Calamagrostis hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakon grass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that add ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetland or bog environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are wind-pollinated.

Its foliage is often used in traditional Japanese gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
