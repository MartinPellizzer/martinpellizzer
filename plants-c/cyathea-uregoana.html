Cyathea uregoana

Cyathea uregoana, commonly known as the Uraguay tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Cyathea uregoana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
