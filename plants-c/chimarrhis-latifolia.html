Chimarrhis latifolia

Chimarrhis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved chimarrhis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
