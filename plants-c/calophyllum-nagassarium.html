Calophyllum nagassarium

Calophyllum nagassarium, commonly known as the Madagascar sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, conical fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been historically used in incense and varnishes.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts.
