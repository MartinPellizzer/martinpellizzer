Colobanthus billardieri

Colobanthus billardieri, commonly known as Antarctic hairgrass, is a small annual plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with slender stems and narrow leaves. This species thrives in harsh environments, including Antarctica and subantarctic islands, where it often grows on bare rock or soil. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, and it is adapted to survive in low-nutrient, nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its resilience and unique adaptations, it plays an important ecological role in polar ecosystems.
