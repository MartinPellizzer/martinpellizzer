Cyperus appendiculatus var. noronhae

Cyperus appendiculatus var. noronhae, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
