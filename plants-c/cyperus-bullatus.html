Cyperus bullatus

Cyperus bullatus, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Cyperus bullatus is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
