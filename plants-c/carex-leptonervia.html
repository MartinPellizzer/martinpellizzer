Carex leptonervia

Carex leptonervia, commonly known as slender-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain the seeds.

Carex leptonervia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
