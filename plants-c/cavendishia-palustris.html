Cavendishia palustris

Cavendishia palustris, commonly known as the swamp orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has strap-like leaves that help it absorb moisture from the surrounding air.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native wetland ecosystems.
