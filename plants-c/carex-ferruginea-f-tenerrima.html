Carex ferruginea f. tenerrima

Carex ferruginea f. tenerrima, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green with reddish or rusty colored veins. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet or damp habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
