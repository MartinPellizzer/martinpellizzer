Calamagrostis villosa var. pulchella

Calamagrostis villosa var. pulchella, commonly known as the silky bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet to mesic habitats, such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
