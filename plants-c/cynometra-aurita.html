Cynometra aurita

Cynometra aurita, commonly known as the golden tamarind tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
