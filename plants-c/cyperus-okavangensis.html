Cyperus okavangensis

Cyperus okavangensis, commonly known as the Okavango sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and seasonally flooded regions, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Cyperus okavangensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
