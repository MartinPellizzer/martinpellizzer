Cereus pentlandii var. violaceomutabilis

Cereus pentlandii var. violaceomutabilis, commonly known as the purple-mottled night bloomer, is a striking cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can reach up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, often with a subtle purple or pink tinge. Its spines are short and white, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
