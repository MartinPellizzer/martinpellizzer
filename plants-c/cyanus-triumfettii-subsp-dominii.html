Cyanus triumfettii subsp. dominii

Cyanus triumfettii subsp.

dominii, commonly known as the Alpine sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are typically surrounded by a ring of yellow ray florets.

The plant is herbaceous, with slender stems and narrow, hairy leaves that help it withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
