Carex capilliformis

Carex capilliformis, commonly known as the slender thread sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous.

Carex capilliformis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
