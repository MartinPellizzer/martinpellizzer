Cestrum adelbertii

Cestrum adelbertii, commonly known as the Adelbert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a smooth texture, and it often grows as a shrub or small tree.

Cestrum adelbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
