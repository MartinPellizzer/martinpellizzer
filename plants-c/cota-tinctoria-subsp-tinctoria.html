Cota tinctoria subsp. tinctoria

Cota tinctoria subsp.

tinctoria, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems.

The plant is an annual herb with alternate, ovate leaves that have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also have historical uses in traditional medicine and dye production.
