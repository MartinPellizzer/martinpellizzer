Carex acuta var. nutans

Carex acuta var. nutans, commonly known as the drooping sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
