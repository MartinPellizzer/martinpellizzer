Chamaecrista filicifolia

Chamaecrista filicifolia, commonly known as the fernleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble ferns. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Chamaecrista filicifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
