Coleus garckeanus

Coleus garckeanus, commonly known as the red-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, often featuring shades of red, purple, and green. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or blue and appear in clusters.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaves and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
