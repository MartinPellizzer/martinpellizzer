Cinchona lancifolia var. nitida

Cinchona lancifolia var. nitida, commonly known as the glossy cinchona, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to species used in the production of quinine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical regions worldwide.
