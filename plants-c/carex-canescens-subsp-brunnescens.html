Carex canescens subsp. brunnescens

Carex canescens subsp.

brunnescens, commonly known as the brownish sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its foliage is usually a mix of green and brown tones, giving it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
