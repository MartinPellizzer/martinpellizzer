Coffea odorata

Coffea odorata, commonly known as the sweet or fragrant coffee tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits called berries, each containing two seeds used for coffee production. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate.
