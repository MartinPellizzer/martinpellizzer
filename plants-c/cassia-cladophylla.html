Cassia cladophylla

Cassia cladophylla, commonly known as the clustered cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and compound leaves that are often yellow-green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
