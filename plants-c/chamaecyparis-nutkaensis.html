Chamaecyparis nutkaensis

Chamaecyparis nutkaensis, commonly known as the Nootka cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are dense and dark green. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and its tolerance to coastal conditions.
