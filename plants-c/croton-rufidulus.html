Croton rufidulus

Croton rufidulus, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Croton rufidulus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
