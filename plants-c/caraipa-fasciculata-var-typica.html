Caraipa fasciculata var. typica

Caraipa fasciculata var. typica, commonly known as the Caraipa fern, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that grow in a compact, clustered arrangement. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its leaves are usually light green and have a soft, leathery texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to purify indoor air.
