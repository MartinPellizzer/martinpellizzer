Carex samoensis

Carex samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Carex samoensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna in its native ecosystem.
