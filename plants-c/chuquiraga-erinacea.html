Chuquiraga erinacea

Chuquiraga erinacea, commonly known as the Chilean bur plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and spiny, globe-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions. Its unique structure includes hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

Chuquiraga erinacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
