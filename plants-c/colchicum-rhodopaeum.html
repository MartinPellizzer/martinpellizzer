Colchicum rhodopaeum

Colchicum rhodopaeum, commonly known as the Rhodope colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has a short, stout stem that supports its large, showy blossoms. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often with a waxy coating.

It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is considered a rare and endemic species.
