Carex oederi var. serpentini

Carex oederi var. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and ecological benefits.
