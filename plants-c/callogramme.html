Callogramme

Callogramme, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a tropical plant belonging to the Caesalpiniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. The seeds within the pods are encased in a hard, woody shell.

Callogramme is native to regions of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
