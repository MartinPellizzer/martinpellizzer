Cistus albidus f. albus

Cistus albidus f. albus, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal remedies.
