Coris monspeliensis var. denticulata

Coris monspeliensis var. denticulata, commonly known as the Corsican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically on tall, slender stems. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
