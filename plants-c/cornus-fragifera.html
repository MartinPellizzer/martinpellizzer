Cornus fragifera

Cornus fragifera, commonly known as the European cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that ripen in late summer.

Cornus fragifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
