Carpinus × grosseserrata

Carpinus × grosseserrata, commonly known as the hybrid hornbeam, is a deciduous tree that results from the cross between Carpinus betulus and Carpinus orientalis.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with doubly serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
