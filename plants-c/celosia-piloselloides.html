Celosia piloselloides

Celosia piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy spurge celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts that resemble plumes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Celosia piloselloides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
