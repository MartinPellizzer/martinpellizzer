Cucumis melo var. provincialis

Cucumis melo var. provincialis, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon family characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit.

The plant produces a vine with hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves, typical of many cucurbits. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, a common trait in Cucurbitaceae. The fruit is typically harvested when green and unripe, though it can be allowed to ripen to a yellow or orange color.

This variety is valued for its crisp texture and mild flavor, often used in salads and fresh consumption.
