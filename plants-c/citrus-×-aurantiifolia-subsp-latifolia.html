Citrus × aurantiifolia subsp. latifolia

Citrus × aurantiifolia subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the West Indian lime or sweet lime, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically round, with a thin, yellow-orange rind and a juicy, acidic pulp that is rich in vitamin C. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for both its fruit and its use in landscaping.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
