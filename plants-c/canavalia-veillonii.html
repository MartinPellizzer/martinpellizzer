Canavalia veillonii

Canavalia veillonii, commonly known as the veillonii bean or a type of cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Canavalia veillonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
