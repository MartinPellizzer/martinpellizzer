Crotalaria aculeata

Crotalaria aculeata, commonly known as the prickly sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

Crotalaria aculeata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
