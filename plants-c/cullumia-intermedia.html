Cullumia intermedia

Cullumia intermedia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Cullumia intermedia is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
