Cirsium chilaishanense

Cirsium chilaishanense, commonly known as the Chilaishan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and a single flower head at the top.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
