Chaetopappa fragilis

Chaetopappa fragilis, commonly known as the fragile composite, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is often found in open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.
