Chlorospatha caliensis

Chlorospatha caliensis, commonly known as the golden shield plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or white, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is gaining attention in botanical research and horticulture.
