Calamus kampucheaensis

Calamus kampucheaensis, commonly known as the Cambodian calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia, where it thrives in moist, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish-green flowers.

Calamus kampucheaensis is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
