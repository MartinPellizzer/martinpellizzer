Carduus acanthoides subsp. pakistanicus

Carduus acanthoides subsp.

pakistanicus, commonly known as the Pakistan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant has a robust taproot system and produces spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in a rounded head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
