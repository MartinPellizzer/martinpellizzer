Capparis jamaicensis f. neriifolia

Capparis jamaicensis f. neriifolia, commonly known as the Jamaican caper, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically narrow and glossy, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
