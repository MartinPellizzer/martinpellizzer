Clematis salsuginea

Clematis salsuginea, commonly known as the desert clematis, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and minimal water requirements.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
