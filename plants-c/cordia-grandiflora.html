Cordia grandiflora

Cordia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Cordia grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
