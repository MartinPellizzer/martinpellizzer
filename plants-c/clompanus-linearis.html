Clompanus linearis

Clompanus linearis, commonly known as the slender clompanus, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
