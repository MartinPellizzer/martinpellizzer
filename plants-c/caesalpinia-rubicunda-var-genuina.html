Caesalpinia rubicunda var. genuina

Caesalpinia rubicunda var. genuina, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, fluffy pods and bipinnate leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valued addition to gardens and landscapes.
