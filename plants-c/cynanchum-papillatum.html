Cynanchum papillatum

Cynanchum papillatum, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, milky sap-filled capsules. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
