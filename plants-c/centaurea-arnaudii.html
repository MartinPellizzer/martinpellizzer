Centaurea arnaudii

Centaurea arnaudii, commonly known as the Alboran Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea arnaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
