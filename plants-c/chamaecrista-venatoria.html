Chamaecrista venatoria

Chamaecrista venatoria, commonly known as the wandering crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and forest edges.

Its seed pods are distinctive, often curling into a spiral shape when mature, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively.
