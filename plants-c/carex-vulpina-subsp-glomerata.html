Carex vulpina subsp. glomerata

Carex vulpina subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges.

This subspecies is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.
