Campanula glomerata f. complicata

Campanula glomerata f. complicata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower or the twisted bellflower, is a compact, flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
