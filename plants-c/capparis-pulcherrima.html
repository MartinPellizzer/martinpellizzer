Capparis pulcherrima

Capparis pulcherrima, commonly known as the Christmas bell or red bell flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter season. The plant has woody stems and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Capparis pulcherrima is also known for its fragrant blooms and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
