Cattleya × carassana

Cattleya × carassana, commonly known as the Cattleya hybrid, is a popular orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it grows in segments with each new growth emerging from a basal node. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Cattleya × carassana is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
