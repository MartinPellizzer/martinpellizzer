Carpinus marlipoensis

Carpinus marlipoensis, commonly known as the Marlipo hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family, which includes birches, alders, and hazels. The tree is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its smooth, gray bark. It produces small, elongated fruits that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
