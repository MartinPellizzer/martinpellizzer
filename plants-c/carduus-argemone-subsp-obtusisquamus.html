Carduus argemone subsp. obtusisquamus

Carduus argemone subsp.

obtusisquamus, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, woolly texture.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by spiky, bristly seed heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its obtuse, rounded leaf tips and its overall compact growth habit.
