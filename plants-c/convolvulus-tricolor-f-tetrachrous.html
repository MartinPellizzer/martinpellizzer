Convolvulus tricolor f. tetrachrous

Convolvulus tricolor f. tetrachrous, commonly known as the four-color morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, yellow, white, and blue. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The varietal form 'tetrachrous' refers to its unique ability to display four distinct flower colors on a single plant.
