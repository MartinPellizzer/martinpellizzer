Crotalaria geminiflora

Crotalaria geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with two lobes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
