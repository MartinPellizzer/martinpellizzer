Chamaecrista incana

Chamaecrista incana, commonly known as the slender ticktree, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chamaecrista incana is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
