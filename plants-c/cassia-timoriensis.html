Cassia timoriensis

Cassia timoriensis, commonly known as Timor cassia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, silky coating. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Cassia timoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
