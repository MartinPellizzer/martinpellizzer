Cyathula deserti

Cyathula deserti, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cup-like structures called cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing minimal forage for wildlife.
