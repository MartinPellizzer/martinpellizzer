Canavalia sericophylla

Canavalia sericophylla, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with silky, hairy surfaces and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

Canavalia sericophylla is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
