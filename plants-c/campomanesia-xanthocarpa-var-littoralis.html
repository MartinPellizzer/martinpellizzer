Campomanesia xanthocarpa var. littoralis

Campomanesia xanthocarpa var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal apple or littoral campomanesia, is a species native to coastal regions of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and mangrove forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
