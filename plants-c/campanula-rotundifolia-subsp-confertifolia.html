Campanula rotundifolia subsp. confertifolia

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

confertifolia, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features rounded, often hairy leaves that are typically basal and arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
