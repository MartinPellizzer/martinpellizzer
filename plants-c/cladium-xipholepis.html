Cladium xipholepis

Cladium xipholepis, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves, which are typically long and narrow with a pointed tip. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and swamps. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in saturated soil, while its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cladium xipholepis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
