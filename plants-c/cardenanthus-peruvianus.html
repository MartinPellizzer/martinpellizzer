Cardenanthus peruvianus

Cardenanthus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian poppy, is a striking annual plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
