Cressa truxillensis var. minima

Cressa truxillensis var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf cressa, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow and attract pollinators like bees.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
