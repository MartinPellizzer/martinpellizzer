Carex × cetica

Carex × cetica, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Carex × cetica exhibits variability in its growth form and can sometimes be challenging to distinguish from its parent species.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
