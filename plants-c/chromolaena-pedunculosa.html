Chromolaena pedunculosa

Chromolaena pedunculosa, commonly known as flame weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, branching stems and can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Chromolaena pedunculosa is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
