Canavalia bahamensis

Canavalia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian bean, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

Canavalia bahamensis plays an important role in both ecological and cultural contexts in its native habitat.
