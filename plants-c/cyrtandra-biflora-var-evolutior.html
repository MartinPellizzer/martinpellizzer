Cyrtandra biflora var. evolutior

Cyrtandra biflora var. evolutior, commonly known as the broad-leaved Cyrtandra, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. These flowers are usually borne on short pedicels and exhibit a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
