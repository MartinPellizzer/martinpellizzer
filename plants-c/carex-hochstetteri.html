Carex hochstetteri

Carex hochstetteri, commonly known as Hochstetter's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, a common feature among sedges.

Hochstetter's sedge is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
