Chenopodium tanakae

Chenopodium tanakae, commonly known as Tanaka's goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Chenopodium tanakae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
