Cyperus marginellus

Cyperus marginellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It produces small, three-sided spikelets that are usually green or brown in color.

Cyperus marginellus thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
