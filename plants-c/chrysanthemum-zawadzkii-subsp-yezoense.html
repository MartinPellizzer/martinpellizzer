Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp. yezoense

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp.

yezoense, commonly known as the Yezo chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to Japan. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its foliage is usually broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
