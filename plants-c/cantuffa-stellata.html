Cantuffa stellata

Cantuffa stellata, commonly known as the star-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its star-shaped petals that radiate from a central tube, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy display of blue or violet hues.

Cantuffa stellata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
