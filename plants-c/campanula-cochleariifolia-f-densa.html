Campanula cochleariifolia f. densa

Campanula cochleariifolia f. densa, commonly known as the dense bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial that features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with a distinctive bell shape that opens gradually. This variety is particularly valued for its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
