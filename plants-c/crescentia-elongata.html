Crescentia elongata

Crescentia elongata, commonly known as the "Crescentia" or "Coco de Mer," is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces distinctive, woody fruits that contain a single large seed, often used in traditional practices. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a strong fragrance, and it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Crescentia elongata is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
