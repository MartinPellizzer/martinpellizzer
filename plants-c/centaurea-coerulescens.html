Centaurea coerulescens

Centaurea coerulescens, commonly known as the blueweed or blue centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, spiny seed pods that release seeds through a mechanism called explosive dehiscence.

Centaurea coerulescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
