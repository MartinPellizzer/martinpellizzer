Cocculus roxburghianus

Cocculus roxburghianus, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces distinctive, spiral-shaped fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
