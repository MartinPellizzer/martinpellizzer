Calamagrostis calderillensis

Calamagrostis calderillensis, commonly known as the Calderilla grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in riparian zones and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red.

Calamagrostis calderillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
