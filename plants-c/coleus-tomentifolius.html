Coleus tomentifolius

Coleus tomentifolius, commonly known as the woolly-leaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in color from green to shades of red and purple. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage.
