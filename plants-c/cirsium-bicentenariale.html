Cirsium bicentenariale

Cirsium bicentenariale, commonly known as the bicentennial thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flower heads and deeply lobed, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its historical significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
