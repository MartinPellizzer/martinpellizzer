Cattleya lutea

Cattleya lutea, commonly known as the yellow cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Cattleya lutea is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
