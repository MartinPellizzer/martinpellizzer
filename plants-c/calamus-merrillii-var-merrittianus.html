Calamus merrillii var. merrittianus

Calamus merrillii var. merrittianus, commonly known as the Merritt's climbing reed, is a rare and unique variant of the calamus genus.

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes that can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to climb and spread across forest floors. The plant produces striking, narrow leaves with a glossy green surface and a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikes that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the Araceae family.

This species is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
