Croton pynaertii

Croton pynaertii, commonly known as the Congo croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and Southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
