Cephalanthera × otto-hechtii

Cephalanthera × otto-hechtii, commonly known as the Otto Hecht's lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and features a dense, dark green, grass-like foliage. The plant produces a spike of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers, often with a slight purple tinge, arranged in a loose, spreading pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
