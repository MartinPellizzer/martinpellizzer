Cupania collina

Cupania collina, commonly known as the red-leaf cupania, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish in color, especially during the growing season. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Cupania collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
