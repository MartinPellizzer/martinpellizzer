Carex × kneuckeri

Carex × kneuckeri, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Carex × kneuckeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
