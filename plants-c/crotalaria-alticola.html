Crotalaria alticola

Crotalaria alticola, commonly known as the mountain crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria alticola is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
