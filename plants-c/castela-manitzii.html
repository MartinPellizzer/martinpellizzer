Castela manitzii

Castela manitzii, commonly known as the golden candelabra, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in summer. Castela manitzii is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
