Cocos nucifera var. saccharina

Cocos nucifera var. saccharina, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a variant of the coconut palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces a distinctive inflorescence that bears both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination. Its fruit, known as a sugar apple, contains a fibrous husk and a sweet, edible pulp.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and for the production of sap used in traditional sweeteners.
