Chaetanthera obtusata

Chaetanthera obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, round berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
