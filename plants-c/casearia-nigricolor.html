Casearia nigricolor

Casearia nigricolor, commonly known as the black-leaved casearia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
