Carex zallichiana

Carex zallichiana, commonly known as the Zallich's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a dense inflorescence.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Carex zallichiana is considered a rare and endangered species.
