Citrus × webberi

Citrus × webberi, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible both inedible and edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is typically grown for its fruit rather than its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, including jams, marmalades, and candied fruits.
