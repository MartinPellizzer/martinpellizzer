Cydonia communis var. oblonga

Cydonia communis var. oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by oval, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in autumn. The fruit has a thick, woody skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp when fully mature. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
