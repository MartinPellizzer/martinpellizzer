Clavimyrtus cylindrica

Clavimyrtus cylindrica, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Clavimyrtus cylindrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
