Conanthus

Conanthus, commonly known as the "flowering plant of the genus Conanthus," is a group of flowering plants belonging to the family Conanthaceae.

These plants are typically characterized by their small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. They are native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andes. Conanthus species often thrive in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates.

Their unique floral structures and growth habits make them interesting subjects for botanical study.
