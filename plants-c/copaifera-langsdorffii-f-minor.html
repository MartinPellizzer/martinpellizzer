Copaifera langsdorffii f. minor

Copaifera langsdorffii f. minor, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a member of the Burseraceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and heartwood. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and is known for its copal resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various rainforest organisms.
