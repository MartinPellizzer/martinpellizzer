Crotalaria assamica var. philippinensis

Crotalaria assamica var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure, and it produces seed pods that split open when mature.

This species is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has potential ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
