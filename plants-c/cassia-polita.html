Cassia polita

Cassia polita, commonly known as golden cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, slender pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia polita thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
