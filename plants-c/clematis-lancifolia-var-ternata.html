Clematis lancifolia var. ternata

Clematis lancifolia var. ternata, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly serrated. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem with thorn-like projections.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
