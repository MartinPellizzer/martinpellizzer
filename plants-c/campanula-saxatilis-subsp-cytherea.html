Campanula saxatilis subsp. cytherea

Campanula saxatilis subsp.

cytherea, commonly known as the Cytherean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and limestone habitats in southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to flourish in harsh, sunny environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
