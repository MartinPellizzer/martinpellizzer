Centaurea triumfettii subsp. lugdunensis

Centaurea triumfettii subsp.

lugdunensis, commonly known as the Lugdunensis Centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in grasslands, rocky slopes, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
