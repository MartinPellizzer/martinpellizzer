Convolvuloides triloba

Convolvuloides triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and trumpet-shaped, often colorful flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found growing in moist, shaded environments. Its vines can climb or trail, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
