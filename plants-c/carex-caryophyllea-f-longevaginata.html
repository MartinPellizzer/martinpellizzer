Carex caryophyllea f. longevaginata

Carex caryophyllea f. longevaginata, commonly known as the long sheath sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex caryophyllea.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, fibrous sheath, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
