Calophyllum africanum

Calophyllum africanum, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often aromatic bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and craftsmanship.
