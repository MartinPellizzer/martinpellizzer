Cyperus papyrus subsp. ugandensis

Cyperus papyrus subsp.

ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda papyrus, is a reed-like plant native to the wetlands of East Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and lakeshores. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

The plant is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
