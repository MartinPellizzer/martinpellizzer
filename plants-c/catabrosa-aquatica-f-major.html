Catabrosa aquatica f. major

Catabrosa aquatica f. major, commonly known as the aquatic catabrosa, is a variant of the Catabrosa genus native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. It thrives in moist soil and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as an ornamental plant.
