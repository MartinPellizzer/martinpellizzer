Capparis breynia f. rosea

Capparis breynia f. rosea, commonly known as the rosea form of the Cape rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often fragrant. The plant has woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
