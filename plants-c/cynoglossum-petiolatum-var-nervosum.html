Cynoglossum petiolatum var. nervosum

Cynoglossum petiolatum var. nervosum, commonly known as the veined hound's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular plant among gardeners.
