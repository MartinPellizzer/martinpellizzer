Crataegus quaesita var. egens

Crataegus quaesita var. egens, commonly known as the Egerton hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
