Carex oregonensis

Carex oregonensis, commonly known as Oregon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. Oregon sedge typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, streambanks, and forested areas. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale to dark brown flowers in late spring to early summer.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
