Cardamomum maximum

Cardamomum maximum, commonly known as large cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers that develop into dark brown, three-lobed capsules containing aromatic seeds. The seeds are highly valued for their intense flavor and are used extensively in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
