Cynoglossum castellanum

Cynoglossum castellanum, commonly known as the Castellane bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with toothed edges and produces striking violet or blue flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
