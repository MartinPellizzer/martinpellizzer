Campylotropis speciosa

Campylotropis speciosa, commonly known as the showy bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant features compound leaves with oval to lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in late spring. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique floral structure and seasonal display make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
