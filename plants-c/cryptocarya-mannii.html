Cryptocarya mannii

Cryptocarya mannii, commonly known as the Australian nut or mountain pepper, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Cryptocarya mannii is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and humid climates.
