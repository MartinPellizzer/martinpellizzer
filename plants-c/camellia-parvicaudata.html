Camellia parvicaudata

Camellia parvicaudata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Camellia parvicaudata thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its winter blooming habit.
