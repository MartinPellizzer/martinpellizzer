Cupania alba

Cupania alba, commonly known as white cupania, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, round berries that are edible for some wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
