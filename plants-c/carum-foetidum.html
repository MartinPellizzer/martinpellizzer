Carum foetidum

Carum foetidum, commonly known as devil's tongue or wild cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slender leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that are often used in culinary applications due to their pungent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Carum foetidum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to aid digestion and reduce inflammation.
