Copaifera ehie

Copaifera ehie, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored heartwood and is valued for its essential oils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its bark contains compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
