Calamagrostis ruprechtii

Calamagrostis ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and high-altitude areas.
