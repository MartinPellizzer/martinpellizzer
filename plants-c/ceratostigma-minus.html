Ceratostigma minus

Ceratostigma minus, commonly known as dwarf smoketree, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Asia.

It features small, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces delicate pink or red flowers in the summer. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance for drought. Its berries, which are dark blue to black, are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Ceratostigma minus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
