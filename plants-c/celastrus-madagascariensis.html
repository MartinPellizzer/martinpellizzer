Celastrus madagascariensis

Celastrus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Madagascar's unique flora.
