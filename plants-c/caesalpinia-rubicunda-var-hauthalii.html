Caesalpinia rubicunda var. hauthalii

Caesalpinia rubicunda var. hauthalii, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, feathery flowers that bloom in dense, clustered inflorescences, adding a vibrant display to its habitat. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique floral structure and colorful blooms make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural settings.
