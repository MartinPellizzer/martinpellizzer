Carex subconcolor

Carex subconcolor, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex subconcolor is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and ability to stabilize soil.
