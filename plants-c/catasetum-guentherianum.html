Catasetum guentherianum

Catasetum guentherianum, commonly known as the Guenther's orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers feature a unique structure with a specialized lip and a column, which are important for pollination.

Catasetum guentherianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
