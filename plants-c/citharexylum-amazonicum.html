Citharexylum amazonicum

Citharexylum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
