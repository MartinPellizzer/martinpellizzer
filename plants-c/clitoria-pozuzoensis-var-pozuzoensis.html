Clitoria pozuzoensis var. pozuzoensis

Clitoria pozuzoensis var. pozuzoensis, commonly known as the Pozuzo pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces vibrant purple or pink butterfly-shaped flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
