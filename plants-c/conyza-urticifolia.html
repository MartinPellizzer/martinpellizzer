Conyza urticifolia

Conyza urticifolia, commonly known as roughleaf weed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environments, including roadsides and fields.

Conyza urticifolia is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
