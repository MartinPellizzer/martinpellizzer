Caesalpinia sclerocarpa

Caesalpinia sclerocarpa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking bright red or orange floral clusters that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous aril. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in warmer climates.

Its rapid growth and vibrant flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
