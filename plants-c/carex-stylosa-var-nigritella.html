Carex stylosa var. nigritella

Carex stylosa var. nigritella, commonly known as blackstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green to blackish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and riparian habitats.
