Claytonia atacamensis

Claytonia atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama claytonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water in its harsh, dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well adapted to extreme desert conditions.

Claytonia atacamensis is often found in rocky areas and is a symbol of resilience in one of the world's driest deserts.
