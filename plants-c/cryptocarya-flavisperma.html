Cryptocarya flavisperma

Cryptocarya flavisperma, commonly known as the golden-fruited cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, yellow fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
