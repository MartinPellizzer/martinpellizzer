Colobanthus squarrosus

Colobanthus squarrosus, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Antarctica and southern South America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in harsh, arid environments. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in nutrient-poor soils.

Colobanthus squarrosus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its extreme habitats.
