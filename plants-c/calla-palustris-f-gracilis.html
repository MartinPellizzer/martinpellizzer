Calla palustris f. gracilis

Calla palustris f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender swamp calla, is a striking aquatic plant native to wetland environments.

It features a tall, slender stem with large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces showy, white spathes that enclose small, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater, preferring muddy or sandy substrates.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for water gardens and pond ecosystems.
