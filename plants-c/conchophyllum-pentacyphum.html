Conchophyllum pentacyphum

Conchophyllum pentacyphum, commonly known as the starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its sweet, tangy flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
