Centaurea amara var. serotina

Centaurea amara var. serotina, commonly known as the bitter centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads, composed of both ray and disk florets, which bloom in summer. Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their bitter and astringent qualities.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
