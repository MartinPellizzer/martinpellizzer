Catasetum ciliatum

Catasetum ciliatum, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, narrow petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Catasetum ciliatum is known for its unique pollination mechanism, involving specialized insects that interact with its intricate floral structure.
