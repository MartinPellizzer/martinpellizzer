Cytisus hirsutus subsp. hirsutissimus

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
