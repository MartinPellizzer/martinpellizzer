Carex muricata var. americana

Carex muricata var. americana, commonly known as the American rough sedges, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color.
