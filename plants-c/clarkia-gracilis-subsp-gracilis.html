Clarkia gracilis subsp. gracilis

Clarkia gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender clarkia, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, upright stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Its delicate appearance and early blooming period make it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
