Cyperus indicus

Cyperus indicus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color.

Cyperus indicus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
