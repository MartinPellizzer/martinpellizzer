Crepinia freynii

Crepinia freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Crepinia freynii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
