Caucanthus albidus var. fimbripetalus

Caucanthus albidus var. fimbripetalus, commonly known as the white caucanthus, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, pale white petals that resemble a fan shape, giving it the common name "fimbripetalus," which refers to its fringed petal edges. The plant thrives in cool, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
