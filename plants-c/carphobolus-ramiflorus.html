Carphobolus ramiflorus

Carphobolus ramiflorus, commonly known as the branched-flower grass, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale blue in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a common component of dry grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
