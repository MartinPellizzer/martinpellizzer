Campanula filicaulis var. pseudoradicosa

Campanula filicaulis var. pseudoradicosa, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, with a spreading growth habit.

The variety pseudoradicosa is distinguished by its more slender stem and slightly different leaf structure compared to the typical form.
