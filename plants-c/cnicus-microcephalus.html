Cnicus microcephalus

Cnicus microcephalus, commonly known as the small-headed sowthistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with a rosette-like growth habit. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
