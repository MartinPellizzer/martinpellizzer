Carex thomasii

Carex thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, arranged in a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex thomasii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
