Cephalophora anthemoides

Cephalophora anthemoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, often colorful bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually green or yellow and are clustered in dense inflorescences at the tips of the stems.

Cephalophora anthemoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
