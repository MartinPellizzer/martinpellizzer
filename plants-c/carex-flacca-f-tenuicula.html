Carex flacca f. tenuicula

Carex flacca f. tenuicula, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a variant of the flat sedge species native to North America.

It is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually slender and slightly drooping, with a distinctive blue-gray hue.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish flowers that mature to a pale brown.
