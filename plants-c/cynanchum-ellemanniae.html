Cynanchum ellemanniae

Cynanchum ellemanniae, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
