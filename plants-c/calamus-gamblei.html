Calamus gamblei

Calamus gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier reed, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of many Aroid species. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Calamus gamblei thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in lowland forests.
