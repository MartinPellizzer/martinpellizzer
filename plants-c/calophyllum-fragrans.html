Calophyllum fragrans

Calophyllum fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that emit a fragrant aroma, particularly in the evening. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
