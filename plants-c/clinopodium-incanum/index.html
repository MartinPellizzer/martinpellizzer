Clinopodium Incanum

Clinopodium incanum, also known as silverweed, is a hardy perennial plant native to arid and rocky regions of North America. It thrives in harsh environments with its silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its roots are deep and drought-resistant, making it a resilient species in challenging soils. Despite its delicate appearance, Clinopodium incanum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
