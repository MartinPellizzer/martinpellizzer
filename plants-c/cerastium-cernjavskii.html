Cerastium cernjavskii

Cerastium cernjavskii, commonly known as the Cernjavskii's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, oval leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
