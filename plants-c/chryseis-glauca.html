Chryseis glauca

Chryseis glauca, commonly known as the blue chryseis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Chryseis glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
