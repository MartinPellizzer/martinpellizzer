Crocus lageniflorus subvar. sulphureus

Crocus lageniflorus subvar. sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur yellow crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, inflated, bell-shaped corolla. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that appear after flowering.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
