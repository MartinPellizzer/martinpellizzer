Camellia japonica var. picta

Camellia japonica var. picta, commonly known as the variegated Japanese camellia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in temperate climates for its year-round visual appeal.
