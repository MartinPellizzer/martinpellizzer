Cirsium pinnatibracteatum

Cirsium pinnatibracteatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its prickly appearance. Its flowers are usually purple to pink in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium pinnatibracteatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
