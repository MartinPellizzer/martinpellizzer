Columnea sparsiflora

Columnea sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered flame vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features bright red or orange tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Columnea sparsiflora is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and ornamental value in gardens.
