Cephalostachyum pingbianense

Cephalostachyum pingbianense, commonly known as the Pingbian bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan province, where it grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms and small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on bamboo evolution and ecological adaptation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
