Centaurea kultiassovii

Centaurea kultiassovii, commonly known as the Kultiassov's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish tinge, while its stems are erect and branching.

Centaurea kultiassovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
