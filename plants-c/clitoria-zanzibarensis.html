Clitoria zanzibarensis

Clitoria zanzibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, elliptical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The flowers are also used in traditional dyeing practices and as a natural food coloring agent.
