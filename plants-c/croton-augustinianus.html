Croton augustinianus

Croton augustinianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for various purposes.
