Convolvulus erectus

Convolvulus erectus, commonly known as the upright morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant.
