Cryptolepis migiurtina

Cryptolepis migiurtina, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. Its leaves and roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cryptolepis migiurtina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
