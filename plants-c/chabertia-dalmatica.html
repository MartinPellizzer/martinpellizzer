Chabertia dalmatica

Chabertia dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian chabertia, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
