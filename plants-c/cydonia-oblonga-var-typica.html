Cydonia oblonga var. typica

Cydonia oblonga var. typica, commonly known as the European quince.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers in spring, followed by pale green, pear-shaped fruits that ripen to a golden-orange color. The fruit has a tough, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp when fully mature.

It is often used in cooking, particularly in jams, jellies, and desserts.
