Cacalia macrophylla

Cacalia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a violet or purple hue. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Cacalia macrophylla is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
