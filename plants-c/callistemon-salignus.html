Callistemon salignus

Callistemon salignus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bright red or pink cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant produces small, ovate leaves that are usually gray-green in color and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
