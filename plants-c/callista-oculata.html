Callista oculata

Callista oculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers with distinctive white spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
