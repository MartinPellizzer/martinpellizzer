Calceolaria ascendens subsp. glandulifera

Calceolaria ascendens subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-shaped petals, which resemble a small pouch or slipper.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually hairy and glandular, contributing to its common name.

It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
