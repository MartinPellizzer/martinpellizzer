Commelina umbrosa

Commelina umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Commelinaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in the shade of the forest understory. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Commelina umbrosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
