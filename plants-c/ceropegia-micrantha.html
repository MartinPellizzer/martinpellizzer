Ceropegia micrantha

Ceropegia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, aiding in water storage, while its stems are often trailing or creeping.

Ceropegia micrantha is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in limited water conditions.
