Croton grandivelum var. cremostachyus

Croton grandivelum var. cremostachyus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to reddish hues. The leaves are typically large, palmately lobed, and have a distinctive texture that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant appearance and unique foliage.
