Campanula dzyschrica

Campanula dzyschrica, commonly known as the Polish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula dzyschrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
