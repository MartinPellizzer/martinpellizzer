Carex gilliesii

Carex gilliesii, commonly known as the Gillies' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, greenish flowers in clustered spikes, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex gilliesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
