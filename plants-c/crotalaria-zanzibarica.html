Crotalaria zanzibarica

Crotalaria zanzibarica, commonly known as Zanzibar gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape, which are arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Crotalaria zanzibarica is also known for its medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
