Crepis atripappa

Crepis atripappa, commonly known as the black-haired mouse-ear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by pale purple to white ray florets. It is native to Europe and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Crepis atripappa is typically a short-lived perennial that thrives in disturbed habitats.
