Cylindropuntia Californica Var. Rosarica

Rosarica cactus, scientifically known as Cylindropuntia californica var. rosarica, is a striking member of the cactus family native to the arid regions of California and Baja California. This plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in clusters of sharp, spiny tubercles. Its vibrant pink or red flowers appear in late spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Despite its beauty, the Rosarica cactus is a hardy species that thrives in harsh desert conditions with minimal water. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
