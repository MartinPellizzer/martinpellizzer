Carex tonsa var. rugosperma

Carex tonsa var. rugosperma, commonly known as the rough-fruited sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and greenish, clustered in dense spikes, and it produces distinctive, rough-textured berries.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
