Chamaecrista fagonioides

Chamaecrista fagonioides, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
