Chondrosum polystachyum

Chondrosum polystachyum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, which provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's showy, white flowers bloom in the early morning and emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrates of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Chondrosum polystachyum plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
