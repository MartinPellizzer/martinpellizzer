Cicer maroccanum

Cicer maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan chickpea, is a leguminous plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited water availability. Its seeds are rich in protein and have been used traditionally in local cuisines.

The plant also plays a role in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
