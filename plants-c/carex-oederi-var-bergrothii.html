Carex oederi var. bergrothii

Carex oederi var. bergrothii, commonly known as Bergroth's sedges, is a variant of the Carex oederi species native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained to slightly waterlogged soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early to mid-summer.

This sedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
