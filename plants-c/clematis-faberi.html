Clematis faberi

Clematis faberi, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Clematis faberi is also valued for its ability to climb trellises and structures, adding vertical interest to landscapes.
