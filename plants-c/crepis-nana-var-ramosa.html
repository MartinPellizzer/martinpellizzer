Crepis nana var. ramosa

Crepis nana var. ramosa, commonly known as the dwarf mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with a compact, low-growing habit. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and spoon-shaped, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its dense, branched growth pattern, which gives it its specific epithet "ramosa.".
