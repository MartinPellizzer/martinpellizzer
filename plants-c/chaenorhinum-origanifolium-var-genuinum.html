Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. genuinum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often resembling those of oregano, which gives the species its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
