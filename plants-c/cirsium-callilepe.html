Cirsium callilepe

Cirsium callilepe, commonly known as the Chilean thistle, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Cirsium callilepe is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
