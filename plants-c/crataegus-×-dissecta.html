Crataegus × dissecta

Crataegus × dissecta, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, known for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and climates.
