Cakile monosperma

Cakile monosperma, commonly known as the Indian rock cress, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including India, China, and the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions.
