Coleus petrophilus

Coleus petrophilus, commonly known as the rock coleus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in whorls, and can range in color from white to pink.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
