Crataegus sanguinea var. inermis

Crataegus sanguinea var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth thornless hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it typically grows in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden and natural settings.
