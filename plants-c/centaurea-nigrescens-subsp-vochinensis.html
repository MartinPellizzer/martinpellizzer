Centaurea nigrescens subsp. vochinensis

Centaurea nigrescens subsp.

vochinensis, commonly known as the Vočin violetcentaurea, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, purple to blue, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
