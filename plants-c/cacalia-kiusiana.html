Cacalia kiusiana

Cacalia kiusiana, commonly known as the Kiusi bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a purple or pinkish hue at the base. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia kiusiana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest areas.
