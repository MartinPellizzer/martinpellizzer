Cuphea gracilis var. brasiliensis

Cuphea gracilis var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Cuphea gracilis var. brasiliensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.
