Cyperus angustifolius

Cyperus angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cyperus angustifolius is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
