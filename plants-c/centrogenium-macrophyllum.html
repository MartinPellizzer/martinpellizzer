Centrogenium macrophyllum

Centrogenium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the day and closing at night.

Centrogenium macrophyllum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter and food for numerous species.
