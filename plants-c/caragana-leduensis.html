Caragana leduensis

Caragana leduensis, commonly known as the Ledu shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia, including regions of Kazakhstan and China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for reclamation of degraded soils.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.
