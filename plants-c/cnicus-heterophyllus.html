Cnicus heterophyllus

Cnicus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from broad to narrow, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cnicus heterophyllus is often used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
