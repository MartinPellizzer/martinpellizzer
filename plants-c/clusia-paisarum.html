Clusia paisarum

Clusia paisarum, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia paisarum is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
