Clinopodium macrostemum

Clinopodium macrostemum, commonly known as large-stemmed mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong minty aroma when crushed.

The flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
