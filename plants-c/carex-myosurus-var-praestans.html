Carex myosurus var. praestans

Carex myosurus var. praestans, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and spreads through rhizomes, making it a hardy and resilient species.

The slender sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
