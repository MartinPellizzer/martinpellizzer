Crocus reinhardii

Crocus reinhardii, commonly known as the Reinhardt's crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms, which typically appear before the leaves fully emerge. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three petals and three sepals, forming a cup-like structure. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist conditions.

Crocus reinhardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
