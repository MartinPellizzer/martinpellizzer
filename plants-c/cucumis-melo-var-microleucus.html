Cucumis melo var. microleucus

Cucumis melo var. microleucus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a thin, pale green rind and a crisp, sweet flesh. The plant typically grows as an annual vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
