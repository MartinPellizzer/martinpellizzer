Carum buriaticum var. helanense

Carum buriaticum var. helanense, commonly known as Helan mountain caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Helan Mountains in China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to grow in rocky, arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
