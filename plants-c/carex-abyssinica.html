Carex abyssinica

Carex abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex abyssinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and ecological benefits.
