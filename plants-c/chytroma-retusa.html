Chytroma retusa

Chytroma retusa, commonly known as the smooth chytroma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chytromataceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Chytroma retusa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
