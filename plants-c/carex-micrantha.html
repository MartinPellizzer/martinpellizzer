Carex micrantha

Carex micrantha, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or brown, and it produces distinctive three-sided, fibrous roots.

Carex micrantha plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
