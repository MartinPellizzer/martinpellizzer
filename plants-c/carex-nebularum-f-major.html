Carex nebularum f. major

Carex nebularum f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The inflorescence consists of multiple spikes, with the upper spike being particularly large and showy, contributing to its ornamental value.
