Carex millsii

Carex millsii, commonly known as the Mill's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex millsii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
