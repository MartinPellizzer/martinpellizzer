Carduus stoechadifolius

Carduus stoechadifolius, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and maritime habitats. Its structure includes a rosette of basal leaves and a tall, branching stem with bracts that resemble leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
