Clethra repanda

Clethra repanda, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweet pepper, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen or deciduous leaves depending on the species. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Clethra repanda is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
