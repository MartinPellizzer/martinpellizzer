Carex cephalophora var. leavenworthii

Carex cephalophora var. leavenworthii, commonly known as the Leavenworth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
