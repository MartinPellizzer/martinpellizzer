Carex setigera var. ustilaginosa

Carex setigera var. ustilaginosa, commonly known as the smoky carex, is a variant of the common carex species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, pale green to silver-gray inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wet meadows. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a spike-like arrangement of small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants in riparian zones.
