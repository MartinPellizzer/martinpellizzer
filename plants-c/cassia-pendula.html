Cassia pendula

Cassia pendula, commonly known as weeping cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are produced in pendulous racemes, giving it a distinctive weeping appearance.

Cassia pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
