Cardamine palustris subvar. sessiliflora

Cardamine palustris subvar. sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-leaved water crowfoot, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces aquatic or semi-aquatic flowers that are often found in shallow water or moist soil.

It is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
