Crataegus flammata

Crataegus flammata, commonly known as the flaming crabsbush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

Crataegus flammata is often found in moist woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
