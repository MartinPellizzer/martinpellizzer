Calamagrostis purpurea f. amurensis

Calamagrostis purpurea f. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur purple grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that produce panicles of purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally green with a slight bluish tint. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and water features.
