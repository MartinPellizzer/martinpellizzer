Crotalaria schumacheri

Crotalaria schumacheri, commonly known as the Schumacher's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals, and its seed pods are typically slender and elongated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Crotalaria schumacheri is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
