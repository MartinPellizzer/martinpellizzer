Carpha borbonica

Carpha borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carpha borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
