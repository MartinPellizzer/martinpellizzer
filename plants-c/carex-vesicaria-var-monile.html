Carex vesicaria var. monile

Carex vesicaria var. monile, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
