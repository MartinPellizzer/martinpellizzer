Cryptocarya aue-ueva

Cryptocarya aue-ueva, commonly known as the Aue-ueva tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and are arranged alternately along the stems.

The tree can grow to a height of up to 20 meters, making it a significant component of the forest canopy in its native habitat.
