Conospermum flexuosum subsp. laevigatum

Conospermum flexuosum subsp.

laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved conospermum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey-green foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species in some regions.
