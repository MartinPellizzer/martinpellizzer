Crataegus sonnenbergensis

Crataegus sonnenbergensis, commonly known as the Sonnenberg hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shrublands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
