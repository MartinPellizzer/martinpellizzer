Camara aculeata f. varia

Camara aculeata f. varia, commonly known as the variable prickly nightshade, is a variant of the prickly nightshade species native to certain regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, followed by berries that change from green to bright red as they mature. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Solanaceae family.
