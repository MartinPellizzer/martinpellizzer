Clematis vitalba var. taurica

Clematis vitalba var. taurica, commonly known as the Taurian honeysuckle, is a vigorous climbing vine native to southern Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb by means of its tendrils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
