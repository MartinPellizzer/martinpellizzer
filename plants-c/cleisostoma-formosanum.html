Cleisostoma formosanum

Cleisostoma formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insects.

Cleisostoma formosanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
