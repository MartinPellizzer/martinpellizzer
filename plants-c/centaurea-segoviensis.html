Centaurea segoviensis

Centaurea segoviensis, commonly known as Segovian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Centaurea segoviensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
