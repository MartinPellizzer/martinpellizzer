Cleistanthus coriaceus

Cleistanthus coriaceus, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
