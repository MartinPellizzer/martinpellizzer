Calamintha largiflora

Calamintha largiflora, commonly known as broadleaf calamintha, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a strong mint-like scent. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Calamintha largiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
