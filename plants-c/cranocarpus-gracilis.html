Cranocarpus gracilis

Cranocarpus gracilis, commonly known as the slender cranocarpus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
