Carex karlongensis

Carex karlongensis, commonly known as the Karlong sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

Carex karlongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
