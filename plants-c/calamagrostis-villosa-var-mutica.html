Calamagrostis villosa var. mutica

Calamagrostis villosa var. mutica, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky florets and tall, slender culms that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it both a valuable ecological plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
