Convolvulus italicus

Convolvulus italicus, commonly known as sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces vibrant, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
