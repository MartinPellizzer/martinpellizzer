Capparis subbiloba

Capparis subbiloba, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments.
