Coniogramme fraxinea var. denticulatoserrata

Coniogramme fraxinea var. denticulatoserrata, commonly known as the serrated fan-leaf fern, is a variant of the fan-leaf fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, deeply divided, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The leaves are typically bright green and have a smooth, glossy texture. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
