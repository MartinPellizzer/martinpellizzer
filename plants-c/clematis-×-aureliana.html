Clematis × aureliana

Clematis × aureliana, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its vines can reach lengths of up to 15 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × aureliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
