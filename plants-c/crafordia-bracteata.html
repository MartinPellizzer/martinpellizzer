Crafordia bracteata

Crafordia bracteata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Crafordia bracteata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
