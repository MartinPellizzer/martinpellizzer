Closterandra minor

Closterandra minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved closterandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Closterandra minor is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
