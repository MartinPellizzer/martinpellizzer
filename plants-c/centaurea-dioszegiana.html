Centaurea dioszegiana

Centaurea dioszegiana, commonly known as the Hungarian centaurea, is a flowering plant native to Hungary and parts of Southeast Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, with prominent central disk florets.

Centaurea dioszegiana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
