Croton plurispicatus

Croton plurispicatus, commonly known as the many-bracted croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
