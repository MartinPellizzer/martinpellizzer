Crataegus pseudosanguinea

Crataegus pseudosanguinea, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and showy pink to red flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Crataegus pseudosanguinea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
