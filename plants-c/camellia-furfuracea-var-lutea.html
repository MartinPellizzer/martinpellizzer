Camellia furfuracea var. lutea

Camellia furfuracea var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is an evergreen shrub with dark green, leathery leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add color to gardens during the colder months.
