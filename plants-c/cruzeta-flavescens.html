Cruzeta flavescens

Cruzeta flavescens, commonly known as the yellow cruzeta, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Cruzeta flavescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
