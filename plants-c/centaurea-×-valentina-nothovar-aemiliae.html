Centaurea × valentina nothovar. aemiliae

Centaurea × valentina nothovar. aemiliae, commonly known as the Valentine's Day cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
