Convolvulus sagittifolius

Convolvulus sagittifolius, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it attach to supports. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
