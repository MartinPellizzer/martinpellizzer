Ceropegia socotricola

Ceropegia socotricola, commonly known as the Socotra ceropegia, is a unique succulent native to the Arabian island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in arid environments and is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils with minimal water requirements.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
