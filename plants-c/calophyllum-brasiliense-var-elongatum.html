Calophyllum brasiliense var. elongatum

Calophyllum brasiliense var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green, and often aromatic bark. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round berries containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
