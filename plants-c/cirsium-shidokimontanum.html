Cirsium shidokimontanum

Cirsium shidokimontanum, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces small, seed-like structures called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Cirsium shidokimontanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
