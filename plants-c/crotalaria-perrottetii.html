Crotalaria perrottetii

Crotalaria perrottetii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces bright yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant is known for its ability to self-seed and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands. Its seeds are encased in a hard, dry pod that splits open when mature.

The plant has been introduced to other regions and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
