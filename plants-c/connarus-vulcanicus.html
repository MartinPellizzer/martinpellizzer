Connarus vulcanicus

Connarus vulcanicus, commonly known as the firewood tree, is a rare and unique species native to volcanic regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous bark and dark, almost black, woody trunk. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils, often found in areas with recent volcanic activity. Its leaves are small and leathery, adapted to withstand high temperatures and harsh environmental conditions.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
