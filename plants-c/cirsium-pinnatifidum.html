Cirsium pinnatifidum

Cirsium pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant features a tall, upright stem with narrow, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, forming dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Cirsium pinnatifidum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
