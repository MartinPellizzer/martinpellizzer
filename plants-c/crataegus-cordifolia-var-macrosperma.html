Crataegus cordifolia var. macrosperma

Crataegus cordifolia var. macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to parts of eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features elliptical, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are red berries that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
