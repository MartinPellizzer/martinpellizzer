Ceropegia albisepta var. albisepta

Ceropegia albisepta var. albisepta, commonly known as the White-veined Ceropegia, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features thin, fleshy stems that can trail or climb, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are unique, with a tubular corolla and specialized structures that aid in pollination by specific insects.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.
