Centaurea scabiosa subsp. scabiosa

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

scabiosa, commonly known as the common centaury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by purple or pink bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
