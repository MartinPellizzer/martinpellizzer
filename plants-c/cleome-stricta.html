Cleome stricta

Cleome stricta, commonly known as spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow upright or prostrate, depending on the growing conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, resembling spiders, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and usually have a slightly hairy surface.

Cleome stricta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
