Crataegus cavaleriei

Crataegus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

Crataegus cavaleriei is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
