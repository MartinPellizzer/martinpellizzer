Chariomma scandens

Chariomma scandens, commonly known as the climbing corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that climb along supports, often reaching heights of several meters. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Papaveraceae, which includes other well-known genera such as Papaver and Corydalis.
