Clusia intertexta

Clusia intertexta, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Clusia intertexta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
