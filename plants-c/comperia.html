Comperia

Comperia, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plants typically grow in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing their ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. Comperia species are native to regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, where they are often found in alpine or mountainous areas.

Their unique floral structures and adaptations to harsh climates make them a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
