Carex lamprochlamys

Carex lamprochlamys, commonly known as the glossy-leaf sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the sedge family. Carex lamprochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
