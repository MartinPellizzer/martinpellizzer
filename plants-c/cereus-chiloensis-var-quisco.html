Cereus chiloensis var. quisco

Cereus chiloensis var. quisco, commonly known as the Quisco cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to coastal regions of Chile.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a cylindrical stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
