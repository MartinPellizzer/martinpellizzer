Convolvulus valentinus var. sulfureus

Convolvulus valentinus var. sulfureus, commonly known as the golden crown vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in garden ecosystems.
