Cordia intricata

Cordia intricata, commonly known as the intricate cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often pubescent beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Cordia intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
