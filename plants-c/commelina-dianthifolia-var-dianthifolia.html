Commelina dianthifolia var. dianthifolia

Commelina dianthifolia var. dianthifolia, commonly known as the white arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces striking white flowers with a prominent spadix. The plant belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. Its flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and the plant can spread through both rhizomes and seeds.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
