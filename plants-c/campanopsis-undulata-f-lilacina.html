Campanopsis undulata f. lilacina

Campanopsis undulata f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep purple, often with a distinctive wavy edge. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
