Chrysophyllum parviflorum

Chrysophyllum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered chrysophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
