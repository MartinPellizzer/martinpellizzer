Calopyxis lamiana

Calopyxis lamiana, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a cup-shaped structure, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Calopyxis lamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
