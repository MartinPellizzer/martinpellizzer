Carex ferruginea var. kerneri

Carex ferruginea var. kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that often have a reddish-brown hue at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
