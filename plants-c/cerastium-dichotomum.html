Cerastium dichotomum

Cerastium dichotomum, commonly known as the forked chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, slender leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine white pubescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its distinctive branching pattern and delicate appearance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
