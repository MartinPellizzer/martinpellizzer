Colchicum candidum var. hirtiflorum

Colchicum candidum var. hirtiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm. It is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it its varietal name.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
