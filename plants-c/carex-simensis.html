Carex simensis

Carex simensis, commonly known as the Simens sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

Carex simensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
