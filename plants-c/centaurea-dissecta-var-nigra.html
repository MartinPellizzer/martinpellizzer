Centaurea dissecta var. nigra

Centaurea dissecta var. nigra, commonly known as black centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves and striking dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, seed-like fruits called caryopses.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
