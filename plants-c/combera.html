Combera

Combera, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Combera thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
