Colutea baetica

Colutea baetica, commonly known as the Iberian shieldflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
