Cumulopuntia mistiensis

Cumulopuntia mistiensis, commonly known as the Misty Paddle Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and low rainfall.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a distinctive member of the Cactaceae family.
