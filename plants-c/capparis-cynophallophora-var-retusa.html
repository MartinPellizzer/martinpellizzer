Capparis cynophallophora var. retusa

Capparis cynophallophora var. retusa, commonly known as the rock capers, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
