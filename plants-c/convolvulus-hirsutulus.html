Convolvulus hirsutulus

Convolvulus hirsutulus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Convolvulus hirsutulus is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a decorative plant and a potential weed.
