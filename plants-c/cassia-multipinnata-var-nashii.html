Cassia multipinnata var. nashii

Cassia multipinnata var. nashii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in elongated pods and are used in traditional medicine in some regions.
