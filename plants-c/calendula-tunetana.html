Calendula tunetana

Calendula tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian marigold, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Calendula tunetana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
