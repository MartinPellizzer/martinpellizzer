Cereus crenulatus var. griseus

Cereus crenulatus var. griseus, commonly known as the gray cactus or gray-hair cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its grayish-green stems covered in short, white spines. The plant produces large, showy night-blooming flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor collections.
