Cyphomandra divaricata var. flexipes

Cyphomandra divaricata var. flexipes, commonly known as the purple leaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features oval, waxy leaves that are typically purple or bronze in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, tangy fruits resembling small tomatoes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its culinary uses.

Due to its toxic potential, it should be handled with care.
