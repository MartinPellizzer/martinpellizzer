Calceolaria pratensis

Calceolaria pratensis, commonly known as the meadow calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear clusters of showy, tubular flowers.

Calceolaria pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
