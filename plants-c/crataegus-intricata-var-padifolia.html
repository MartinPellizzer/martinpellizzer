Crataegus intricata var. padifolia

Crataegus intricata var. padifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
