Cirsium spectabile

Cirsium spectabile, commonly known as the showy thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with rough, sometimes hairy surfaces. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium spectabile is often found in meadows, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
