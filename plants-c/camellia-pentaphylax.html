Camellia pentaphylax

Camellia pentaphylax, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during late winter to early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and have a unique, five-lobed structure that gives it its scientific name.

Camellia pentaphylax is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
