Coleosanthus tiliifolius

Coleosanthus tiliifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in riparian zones. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a robust rhizome that helps it thrive in moist environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
