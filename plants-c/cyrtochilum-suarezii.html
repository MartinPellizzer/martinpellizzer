Cyrtochilum suarezii

Cyrtochilum suarezii, commonly known as the Suarez's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration that ranges from white to pale pink. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Cyrtochilum suarezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
