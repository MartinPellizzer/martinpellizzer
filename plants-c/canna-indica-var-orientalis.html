Canna indica var. orientalis

Canna indica var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a prominent midrib and a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, spathaceous flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for water gardens and tropical landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and natural ecosystems.
