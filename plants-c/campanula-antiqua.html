Campanula antiqua

Campanula antiqua, commonly known as the ancient bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
