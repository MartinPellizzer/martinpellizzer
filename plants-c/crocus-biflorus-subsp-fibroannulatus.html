Crocus biflorus subsp. fibroannulatus

Crocus biflorus subsp.

fibroannulatus, commonly known as the fibrous-crown crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features distinctive, narrow, fibrous leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, usually blooming in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
