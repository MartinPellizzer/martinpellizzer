Cucumis erivanicus

Cucumis erivanicus, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its long, slender, and often ribbed fruit. The plant typically grows as a vine, with palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is usually harvested when immature, resembling a small cucumber in appearance.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which has a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
