Cissus helenae

Cissus helenae, commonly known as the Helen's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.

Cissus helenae is also known for its ability to climb using adventitious roots that form along its stems.
