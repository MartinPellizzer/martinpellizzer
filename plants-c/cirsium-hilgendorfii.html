Cirsium hilgendorfii

Cirsium hilgendorfii, commonly known as the Hilgendorf's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with stiff, prickly edges, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium hilgendorfii is often found in open areas such as grasslands and slopes, where it thrives in full sun.
