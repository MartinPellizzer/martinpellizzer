Cassia pilidens

Cassia pilidens, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of golden-yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia pilidens thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antifungal effects.
