Colchicum jordanicola

Colchicum jordanicola, commonly known as Jordan's colchicum, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East, particularly found in Jordan and parts of Saudi Arabia.

It is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
