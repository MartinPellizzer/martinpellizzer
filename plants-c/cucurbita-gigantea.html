Cucurbita gigantea

Cucurbita gigantea, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its large, lobed leaves and sprawling growth habit.

It produces massive, round fruits that can weigh over 1,000 pounds, making it one of the heaviest cultivated plants. The plant is a trailing vine with tendrils that help it climb and spread across the ground. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit's thick, smooth rind and orange to yellow flesh make it a popular choice for both culinary and decorative purposes.
