Chamaesyce rusbyi

Chamaesyce rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and heat.
