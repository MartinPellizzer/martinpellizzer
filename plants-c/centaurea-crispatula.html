Centaurea crispatula

Centaurea crispatula, commonly known as the curlyhead centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea crispatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
