Calathea vestita

Calathea vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, dark green leaves with intricate patterns of silver and cream. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening. Its foliage is often described as having a metallic sheen, which enhances its visual appeal.

Calathea vestita thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
