Calyptranthes restingae

Calyptranthes restingae, commonly known as the Restinga calyptranthe, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy soils and saline environments. It plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
