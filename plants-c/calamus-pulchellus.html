Calamus pulchellus

Calamus pulchellus, commonly known as the elegant calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family plants.

Calamus pulchellus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
