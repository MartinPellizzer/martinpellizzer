Chaenomeles × superba f. perfecta

Chaenomeles × superba f. perfecta, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince.

It is a cultivar of the Japanese quince, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange blossoms in early spring, preceding the emergence of its attractive red or orange fruits. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a glossy green hue.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate flowers and fruit, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
