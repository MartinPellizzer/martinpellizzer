Cirsium lanceolatum

Cirsium lanceolatum, commonly known as the lance-leaved thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, while its stems are often hairy and reddish in color.

Cirsium lanceolatum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
