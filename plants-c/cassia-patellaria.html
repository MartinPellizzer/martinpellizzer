Cassia patellaria

Cassia patellaria, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia patellaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
