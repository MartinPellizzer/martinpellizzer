Citrus × bigaradia var. asperma

Citrus × bigaradia var. asperma, commonly known as the rough lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its thick, rough, and often pebbled rind.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its sour fruit, which is used in marmalades and as a flavoring agent. The plant typically grows as a small tree with dark green, glossy leaves and has a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are white and fragrant, producing small, sour fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

The rough lemon is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
