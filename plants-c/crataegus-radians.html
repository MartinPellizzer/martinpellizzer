Crataegus radians

Crataegus radians, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart and used in jams or wines. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

Its growth habit and seasonal changes make it a notable species in horticulture and natural habitats.
