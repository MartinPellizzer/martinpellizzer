Camellia pseudoelongata

Camellia pseudoelongata, commonly known as the longleaf camellia, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. Its flowers are camellia-like, with showy pink to red petals and yellow stamens, blooming in late winter to early spring.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release bright red, persistent seeds.
