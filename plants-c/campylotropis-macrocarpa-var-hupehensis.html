Campylotropis macrocarpa var. hupehensis

Campylotropis macrocarpa var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh bushclover, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters, typically ranging in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
