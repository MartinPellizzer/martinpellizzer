Clerodendrum myricoides var. involutum

Clerodendrum myricoides var. involutum, commonly known as the curled-leaved clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
