Cassia stipulata

Cassia stipulata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, stipular structures at the base of each leaflet. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Cassia stipulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
