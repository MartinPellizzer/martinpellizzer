Carpinus italica

Carpinus italica, commonly known as the Italian hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by oval, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its attractive form and seasonal interest.
