Cotoneaster ellipticus

Cotoneaster ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, providing an attractive appearance in both spring and autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its seasonal interest and structural form.
