Caesalpinia sepiaria var. japonica

Caesalpinia sepiaria var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. It produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
