Cynthia falcata

Cynthia falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cypress, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, acidic environments.

Cynthia falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
