Calythropsis aurea

Calythropsis aurea, commonly known as the golden calythropsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silver-green hue that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
