Calophyllum congestiflorum

Calophyllum congestiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flower calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
