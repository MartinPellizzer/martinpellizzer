Cyrtomium vittatum

Cyrtomium vittatum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a compact evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, leathery fronds with a distinctive golden hue along the midribs, giving it its common name. The plant has a dense, bushy appearance with a short, thick rhizome that supports its growth. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Cyrtomium vittatum is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
