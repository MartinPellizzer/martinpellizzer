Carex baccans f. recurvirostris

Carex baccans f. recurvirostris, commonly known as the recurved sedge, is a variant of the Carex baccans species, characterized by its distinctive recurved leaf tips.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
