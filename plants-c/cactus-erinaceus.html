Cactus erinaceus

Cactus erinaceus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that are often covered in a thick layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently in arid environments.

Cactus erinaceus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
