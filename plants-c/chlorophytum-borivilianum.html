Chlorophytum borivilianum

Chlorophytum borivilianum, commonly known as Indian wheatgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It is well adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species.

Its roots are known to contain bioactive compounds, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.
