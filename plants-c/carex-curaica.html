Carex curaica

Carex curaica, commonly known as the Curaica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flower heads in the summer.

Carex curaica is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
