Circaea lutetiana f. ovatifolia

Circaea lutetiana f. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved white-ivy, is a rare and attractive plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy, and dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
