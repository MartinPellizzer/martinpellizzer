Chaerophyllum hirsutum var. calabricum

Chaerophyllum hirsutum var. calabricum, commonly known as the Calabrian hogweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a stout, hairy stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive white underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of light conditions.
