Carex diamantina

Carex diamantina, commonly known as the diamond sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex diamantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
