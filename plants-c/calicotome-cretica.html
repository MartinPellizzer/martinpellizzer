Calicotome cretica

Calicotome cretica, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used for propagation. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Calicotome cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
