Cordia velutina

Cordia velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are often followed by orange or red fruits containing several seeds. Cordia velutina is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
