Chaenomeles eugenioides var. superba

Chaenomeles eugenioides var. superba, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces edible, ornamental fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It has palmately lobed leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small gardens or containers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
