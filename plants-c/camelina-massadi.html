Camelina massadi

Camelina massadi, commonly known as the Kashmiri mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive, pungent odor. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

Camelina massadi is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
