Coreopsis oniscicarpa var. simulans

Coreopsis oniscicarpa var. simulans, commonly known as the sand verbena coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in small, daisy-like heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant choice for arid landscapes.
