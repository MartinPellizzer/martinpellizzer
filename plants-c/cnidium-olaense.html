Cnidium olaense

Cnidium olaense, commonly known as the African hogweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
