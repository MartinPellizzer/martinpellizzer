Cerastium dinaricum

Cerastium dinaricum, commonly known as Dinaric mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Cerastium dinaricum is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
