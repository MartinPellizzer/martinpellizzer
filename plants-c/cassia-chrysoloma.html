Cassia chrysoloma

Cassia chrysoloma, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that appear in clusters during the flowering season. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are composed of multiple pairs of small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Cassia chrysoloma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
