Cloezia ligustrina

Cloezia ligustrina, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature. Its berries, which are typically dark purple to black, are toxic to humans and animals.

Cloezia ligustrina is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
