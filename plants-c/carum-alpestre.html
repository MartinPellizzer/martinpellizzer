Carum alpestre

Carum alpestre, commonly known as alpine caraway, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems that can grow up to 60 cm tall, often branching towards the top. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, spicy aroma.

Carum alpestre is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
