Chascanum lignosum

Chascanum lignosum, commonly known as the Andean mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Chascanum lignosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
