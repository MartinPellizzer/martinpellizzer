Chenopodium hircinum subsp. hircinum

Chenopodium hircinum subsp.

hircinum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and rough, spiny leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
