Crataegus chrysoclada

Crataegus chrysoclada, commonly known as the goldenleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. The plant has deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
