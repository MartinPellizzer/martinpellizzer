Carlina comosa

Carlina comosa, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woolly, gray-green foliage and showy blue-violet composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Carlina comosa is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.
