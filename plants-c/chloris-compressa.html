Chloris compressa

Chloris compressa, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are compressed along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
