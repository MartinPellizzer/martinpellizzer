Centaurea palfyana

Centaurea palfyana, commonly known as the Palfy's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Centaurea palfyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
