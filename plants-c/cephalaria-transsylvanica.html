Cephalaria transsylvanica

Cephalaria transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to Europe, particularly found in mountainous regions of the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its height can reach up to 1 meter, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
