Cirsium capnoideum

Cirsium capnoideum, commonly known as the smoke weed or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are usually blue or purple in color.

Cirsium capnoideum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
