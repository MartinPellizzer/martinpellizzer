Chrysanthemum bizarre

Chrysanthemum bizarre, commonly known as the "odd chrysanthemum," is a unique cultivar characterized by its unusual and often asymmetrical flower shapes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to China, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of 3 to 5 feet, with dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red, and bloom in the fall.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often used in ornamental gardening and as a cut flower.
