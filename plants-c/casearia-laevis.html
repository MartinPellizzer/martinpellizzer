Casearia laevis

Casearia laevis, commonly known as the silky casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
