Copernicia maritima

Copernicia maritima, commonly known as the sea coconut or the coconut of the sea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. Copernicia maritima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various species.
