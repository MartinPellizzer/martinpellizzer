Calligonum roseum

Calligonum roseum, commonly known as the pink saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in salty and drought-prone soils. Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, particularly in regions with dry climates.

Calligonum roseum plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its natural habitat.
