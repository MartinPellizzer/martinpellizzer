Ceropegia aridicola

Ceropegia aridicola, commonly known as the desert ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, which attract specific pollinators like flies. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
