Cereus similis

Cereus similis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spines, which help it conserve water in its natural habitat. It produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant is well adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
