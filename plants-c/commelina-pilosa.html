Commelina pilosa

Commelina pilosa, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically with three sepals and two petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina pilosa is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
