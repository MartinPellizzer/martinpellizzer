Christella crinipes

Christella crinipes, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic environments. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with whorled leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Christella crinipes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
