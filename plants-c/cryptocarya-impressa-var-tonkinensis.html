Cryptocarya impressa var. tonkinensis

Cryptocarya impressa var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin cryptocarya, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and southern China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are elliptical to ovate, with a smooth or slightly pubescent surface, and they often exhibit a dark green color on the upper surface and a lighter green or sometimes whitish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
