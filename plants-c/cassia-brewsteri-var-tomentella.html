Cassia brewsteri var. tomentella

Cassia brewsteri var. tomentella, commonly known as the silver cassia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

The silver cassia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
