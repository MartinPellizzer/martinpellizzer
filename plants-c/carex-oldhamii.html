Carex oldhamii

Carex oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in triangular clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carex oldhamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
