Cylindropsis parvifolia

Cylindropsis parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil.

Cylindropsis parvifolia is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
