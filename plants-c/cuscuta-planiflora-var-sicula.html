Cuscuta planiflora var. sicula

Cuscuta planiflora var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily, and thrives in warm, dry climates. Its life cycle involves germinating from seeds and establishing a connection with the vascular system of its host.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken or even kill its host plants if left unchecked.
