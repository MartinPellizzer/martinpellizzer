Cupressus lawsoniana f. alumnii

Cupressus lawsoniana f. alumnii, commonly known as the Lawson cypress, is a cultivar of the Lawson cypress, native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in landscaping. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, forming a conical or pyramidal shape. It is often used as a ornamental tree due to its aesthetic value and resistance to pests and diseases.

The cultivar is well-suited for smaller spaces and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate drainage.
