Cupania dimereza

Cupania dimereza, commonly known as the Mexican elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries in the fall. Its berries are edible when ripe and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
