Callicarpa longifolia var. horsfieldii

Callicarpa longifolia var. horsfieldii, commonly known as the purple honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
