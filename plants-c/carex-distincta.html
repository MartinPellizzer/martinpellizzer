Carex distincta

Carex distincta, commonly known as the distinct sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and flowering spikes that typically bear small, three-lobed flowers. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet environments such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting diverse wildlife and aiding in water filtration.
