Caralluma affinis

Caralluma affinis, commonly known as the horned nightshade, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of India and Africa.

It belongs to the family Nyctaginaceae and is characterized by its branching stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, hence its common name. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments due to its ability to store water in its thick stems.

Caralluma affinis is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.
