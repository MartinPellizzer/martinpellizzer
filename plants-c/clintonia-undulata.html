Clintonia undulata

Clintonia undulata, commonly known as the wrinkled bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features attractive, bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors.

Clintonia undulata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
