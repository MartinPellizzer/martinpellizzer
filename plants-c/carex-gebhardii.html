Carex gebhardii

Carex gebhardii, commonly known as Gebhard's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

Carex gebhardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
