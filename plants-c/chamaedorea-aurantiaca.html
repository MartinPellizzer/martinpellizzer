Chamaedorea aurantiaca

Chamaedorea aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feather-like, dark green leaves that often have a slight golden tinge. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

The plant's elegant appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both residential and commercial settings.
