Comparettia alexii

Comparettia alexii, commonly known as the Alexi's flame orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant red and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Comparettia alexii thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
