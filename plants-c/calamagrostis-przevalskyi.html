Calamagrostis przevalskyi

Calamagrostis przevalskyi, commonly known as the Przhevalsky's reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-white in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a delicate ornamental quality. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
