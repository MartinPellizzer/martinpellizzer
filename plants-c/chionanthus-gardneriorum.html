Chionanthus gardneriorum

Chionanthus gardneriorum, commonly known as Gardner's frothflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to its white blooms.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
