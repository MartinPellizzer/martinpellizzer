Chenopodium foetidum subsp. resediforme

Chenopodium foetidum subsp.

resediforme, commonly known as skunkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.
