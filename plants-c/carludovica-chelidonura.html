Carludovica chelidonura

Carludovica chelidonura, commonly known as the Mexican cactus or golden cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in yellowish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
