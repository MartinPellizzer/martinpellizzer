Coleus rehmannii

Coleus rehmannii, commonly known as the silverleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its striking silver or white variegated leaves, which contrast with its deep green veins. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

Coleus rehmannii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
