Cardamine hirsuta proles macrantha

Cardamine hirsuta proles macrantha, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves.
