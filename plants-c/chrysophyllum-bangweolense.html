Chrysophyllum bangweolense

Chrysophyllum bangweolense, commonly known as the Bangweol mango, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a smooth, yellowish-orange berry that matures in late summer. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soils.
