Carum angolense

Carum angolense, commonly known as African cumin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been utilized in various cultural and medicinal practices across its native range.
