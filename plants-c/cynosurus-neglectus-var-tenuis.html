Cynosurus neglectus var. tenuis

Cynosurus neglectus var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodland edges, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 2 millimeters wide, giving it a delicate appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain wetland and grassland ecosystems.
