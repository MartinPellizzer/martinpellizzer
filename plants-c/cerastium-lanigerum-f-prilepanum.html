Cerastium lanigerum f. prilepanum

Cerastium lanigerum f. prilepanum, commonly known as the Prilepanum mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
