Carpesium wulfenianum

Carpesium wulfenianum, commonly known as the blackweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Carpesium wulfenianum is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain environments.
