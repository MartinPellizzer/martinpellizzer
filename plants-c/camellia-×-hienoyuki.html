Camellia × hienoyuki

Camellia × hienoyuki, commonly known as the Hienoyuki camellia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Camellia sasanqua and Camellia reticulata.

It is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value, particularly its showy, double blooms that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

Camellia × hienoyuki is well-suited for cultivation in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9, thriving in partial shade and well-drained soil.
