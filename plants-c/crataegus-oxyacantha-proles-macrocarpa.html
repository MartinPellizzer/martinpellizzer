Crataegus oxyacantha proles macrocarpa

Crataegus oxyacantha proles macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces large, red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
