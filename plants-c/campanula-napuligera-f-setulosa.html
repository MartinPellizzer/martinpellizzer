Campanula napuligera f. setulosa

Campanula napuligera f. setulosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, or setulose, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
