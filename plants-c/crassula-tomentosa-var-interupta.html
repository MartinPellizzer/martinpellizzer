Crassula tomentosa var. interupta

Crassula tomentosa var. interupta, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" or "Coral Beauty," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a fuzzy, woolly texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
