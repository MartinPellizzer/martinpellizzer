Cosmea tenuifolia

Cosmea tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf cosmea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has thin, grass-like leaves. It produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Cosmea tenuifolia is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
