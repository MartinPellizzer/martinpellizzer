Carex similigena

Carex similigena, commonly known as the similar-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Carex similigena prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
