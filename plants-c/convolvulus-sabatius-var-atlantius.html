Convolvulus sabatius var. atlantius

Convolvulus sabatius var. atlantius, commonly known as the Atlantic bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along coastal areas and rocky soils. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing habits.

This variety is known for its adaptability to dry and saline conditions.
