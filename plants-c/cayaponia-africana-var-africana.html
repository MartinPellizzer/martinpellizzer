Cayaponia africana var. africana

Cayaponia africana var. africana, commonly known as African Cayaponia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, often aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives best in well-drained, loamy soils.
