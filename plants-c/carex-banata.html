Carex banata

Carex banata, commonly known as the Banat sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
