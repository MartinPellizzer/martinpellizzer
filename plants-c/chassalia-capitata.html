Chassalia capitata

Chassalia capitata, commonly known as the clustered chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in open, sunny habitats.

Chassalia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
