Carpinus omeiensis

Carpinus omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in mountainous regions and has a slow growth rate.

Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
