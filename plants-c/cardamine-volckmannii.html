Cardamine volckmannii

Cardamine volckmannii, commonly known as the Volckmann's buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a delicate, waxy texture and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are usually borne on slender stems and are surrounded by a cluster of sepals.

The plant is characterized by its short stature and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
