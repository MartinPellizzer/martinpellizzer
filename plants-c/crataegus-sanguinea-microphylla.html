Crataegus sanguinea microphylla

Crataegus sanguinea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
