Carex filiculmis

Carex filiculmis, commonly known as the fern-like sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex filiculmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
