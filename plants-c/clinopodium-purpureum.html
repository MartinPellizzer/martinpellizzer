Clinopodium purpureum

Clinopodium purpureum, commonly known as purple honeysuckle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Clinopodium purpureum is typically found in moist woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
