Conanthus parviflorus var. pumilus

Conanthus parviflorus var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf parviflorus conanthus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
