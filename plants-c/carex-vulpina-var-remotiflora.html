Carex vulpina var. remotiflora

Carex vulpina var. remotiflora, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
