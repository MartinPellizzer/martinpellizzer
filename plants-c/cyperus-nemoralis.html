Cyperus nemoralis

Cyperus nemoralis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Cyperus nemoralis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
