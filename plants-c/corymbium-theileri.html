Corymbium theileri

Corymbium theileri, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
