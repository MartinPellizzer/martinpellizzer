Cryptandra tridentata var. pubescens

Cryptandra tridentata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cryptandra, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in local biodiversity and its resilience to environmental stress.
