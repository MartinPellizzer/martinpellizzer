Carex polygama subsp. hartmaniorum

Carex polygama subsp.

hartmaniorum, commonly known as Hartman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant typically forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves, with spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Hartman's sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
