Carex mucronatiformis

Carex mucronatiformis, commonly known as the slender-spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
