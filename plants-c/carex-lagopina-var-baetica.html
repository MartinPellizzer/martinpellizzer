Carex lagopina var. baetica

Carex lagopina var. baetica, commonly known as the Baetica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
