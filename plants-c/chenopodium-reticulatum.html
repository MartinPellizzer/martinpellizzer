Chenopodium reticulatum

Chenopodium reticulatum, commonly known as netleaf goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features succulent, lobed leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium reticulatum is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
