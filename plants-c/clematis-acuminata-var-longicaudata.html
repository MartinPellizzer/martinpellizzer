Clematis acuminata var. longicaudata

Clematis acuminata var. longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its long, slender stems and hairy foliage are characteristic of the species.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
