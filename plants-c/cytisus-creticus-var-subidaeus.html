Cytisus creticus var. subidaeus

Cytisus creticus var. subidaeus, commonly known as the Cretan broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant thrives in rocky and dry soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for landscaping in arid environments.
