Cardamine dentipetala var. longifructus

Cardamine dentipetala var. longifructus, commonly known as the long-fruited watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces elongated, slender fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in shady, wet environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
