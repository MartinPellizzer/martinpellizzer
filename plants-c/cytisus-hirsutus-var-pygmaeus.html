Cytisus hirsutus var. pygmaeus

Cytisus hirsutus var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a compact shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has wiry, thorny stems and narrow, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
