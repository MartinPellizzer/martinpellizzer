Cordia guineensis

Cordia guineensis, commonly known as African snowball, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in winter. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Cordia guineensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
