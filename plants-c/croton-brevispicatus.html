Croton brevispicatus

Croton brevispicatus, commonly known as the short-spined croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with lobed margins and bright, colorful bracts that range from yellow to red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking exotic foliage plants.
