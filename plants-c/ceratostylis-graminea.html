Ceratostylis graminea

Ceratostylis graminea, commonly known as the grass orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky slopes. Its unique morphology and ability to grow in challenging conditions make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.

The orchid's rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
