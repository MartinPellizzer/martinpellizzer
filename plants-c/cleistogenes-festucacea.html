Cleistogenes festucacea

Cleistogenes festucacea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Cleistogenes festucacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
