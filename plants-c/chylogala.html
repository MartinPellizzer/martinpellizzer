Chylogala

Chylogala, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Chylogala thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
