Croton garckeanus var. major

Croton garckeanus var. major, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features large, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying vibrant green or variegated patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
