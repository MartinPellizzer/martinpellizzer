Cardaria pubescens var. elongata

Cardaria pubescens var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bladderwrack, is a variant of the bladderwrack species native to coastal regions.

It is a perennial seaweed that thrives in intertidal zones, characterized by its elongated, flexible fronds. The plant has a bluish-green color and is covered in a dense layer of small, bladder-like structures that help it float. It plays a crucial role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of rocky shore communities.
