Callicarpa baviensis

Callicarpa baviensis, commonly known as the Bavi beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that mature in the fall. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife.
