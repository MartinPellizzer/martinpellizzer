Crepis tybakiensis var. flexiscapa

Crepis tybakiensis var. flexiscapa, commonly known as the flexible bentgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, pale green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
