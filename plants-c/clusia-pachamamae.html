Clusia pachamamae

Clusia pachamamae, commonly known as the Andean glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are typically evergreen and can be used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Clusia pachamamae is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its vibrant floral display.
