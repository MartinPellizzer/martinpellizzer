Copiapoa pepiniana

Copiapoa pepiniana, commonly known as the Pachyveria, is a species of cactus native to Chile.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Copiapoa pepiniana is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
