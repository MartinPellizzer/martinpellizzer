Cryptanthus latifolius

Cryptanthus latifolius, commonly known as the Broadleaf Cryptanthus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce vibrant blooms in shades of pink, red, and white.
