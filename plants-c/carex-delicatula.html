Carex delicatula

Carex delicatula, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in shaded to partially shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in wet meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and related plants.

Carex delicatula is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
