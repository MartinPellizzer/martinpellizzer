Calliandra porphyrea

Calliandra porphyrea, commonly known as the purple calliandra, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flower clusters, which are arranged in racemes along the stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Calliandra porphyrea is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
