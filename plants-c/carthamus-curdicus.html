Carthamus curdicus

Carthamus curdicus, commonly known as the Persian saffron.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its scientific name reflects its historical and cultural significance in traditional medicine and dye production.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
