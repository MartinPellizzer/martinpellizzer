Croton parvifructus

Croton parvifructus, commonly known as the small-fruited croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
