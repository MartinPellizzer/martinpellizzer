Carex elmeri

Carex elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Carex elmeri is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
