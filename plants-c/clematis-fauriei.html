Clematis fauriei

Clematis fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a serrated margin.

Clematis fauriei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
