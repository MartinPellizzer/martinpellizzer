Cajanus crassus var. burmanicus

Cajanus crassus var. burmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese groundnut, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated for both food and agricultural purposes in its native regions.
