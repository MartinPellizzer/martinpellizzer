Campanula expansa var. welandii

Campanula expansa var. welandii, commonly known as the Weland's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly toothed, often with a silvery-white underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cooler climates.
