Cytisus ratisbonensis var. purpurascens

Cytisus ratisbonensis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple flame acacia, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and lance-shaped. This variety is notable for its striking purple-tinged flower buds and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
