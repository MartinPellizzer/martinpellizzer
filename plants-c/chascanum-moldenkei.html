Chascanum moldenkei

Chascanum moldenkei, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chascanum moldenkei is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
