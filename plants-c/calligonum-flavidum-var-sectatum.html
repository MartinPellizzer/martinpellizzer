Calligonum flavidum var. sectatum

Calligonum flavidum var. sectatum, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its yellowish flowers and succulent, fleshy stems that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
