Capsella incisura

Capsella incisura, commonly known as the narrowleaf weed, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various parts of Europe and Asia. The plant has small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, and its leaves are elongated with serrated edges. Capsella incisura produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
