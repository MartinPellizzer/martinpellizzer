Cattleya labiata var. petersii-marmorata

Cattleya labiata var. petersii-marmorata, commonly known as the marbled cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking, intricate patterns.

It features large, showy flowers with a velvety texture and a distinctive marbled pattern on its petals and lip. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air.

Its blooming period usually occurs in the spring, producing fragrant, long-lasting flowers that make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
