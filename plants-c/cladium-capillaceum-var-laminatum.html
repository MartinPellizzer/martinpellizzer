Cladium capillaceum var. laminatum

Cladium capillaceum var. laminatum, commonly known as the slender sawgrass, is a variant of the sawgrass species native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, bristle-like structures, which help reduce water loss in its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife species. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often submerged or floating, facilitating wind pollination.

The plant plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and filtering water.
