Carex pseudoconica

Carex pseudoconica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or reddish, arranged in spikelets along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pseudoconica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
