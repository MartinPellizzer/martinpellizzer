Crataegus cuneiformis

Crataegus cuneiformis, commonly known as the cuneiform hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorned branches. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges.

Crataegus cuneiformis is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along streams, thriving in well-drained soils.
