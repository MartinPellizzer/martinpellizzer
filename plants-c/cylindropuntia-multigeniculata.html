Cylindropuntia multigeniculata

Cylindropuntia multigeniculata, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Its unique morphology and adaptations make it well-suited for desert climates.
