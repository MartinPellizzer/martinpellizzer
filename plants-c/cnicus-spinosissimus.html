Cnicus spinosissimus

Cnicus spinosissimus, commonly known as the spiny sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a spiny texture, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is an annual or biennial plant that produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.
