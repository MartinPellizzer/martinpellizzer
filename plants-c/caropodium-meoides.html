Caropodium meoides

Caropodium meoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often submerged. Its flowers are tiny and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

Caropodium meoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
