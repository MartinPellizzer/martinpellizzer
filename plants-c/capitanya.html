Capitanya

Capitanya, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and a dense, spherical flower head composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. Capitanya flowers are usually purple or pink, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
