Centropogon capitatus

Centropogon capitatus, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Centropogon capitatus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in environments with full sun.
