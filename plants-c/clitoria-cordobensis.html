Clitoria cordobensis

Clitoria cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoban butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet-blue flowers, which resemble butterflies, and its trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Clitoria cordobensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
