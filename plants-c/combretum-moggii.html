Combretum moggii

Combretum moggii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The bark and leaves of Combretum moggii have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
