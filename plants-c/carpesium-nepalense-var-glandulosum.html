Carpesium nepalense var. glandulosum

Carpesium nepalense var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular cowhage, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in glandular hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often glandular-dotted. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
