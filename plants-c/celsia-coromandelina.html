Celsia coromandelina

Celsia coromandelina, commonly known as the Coromandel cress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
