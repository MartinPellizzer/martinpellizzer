Cousinia woronowii

Cousinia woronowii, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suited for xerophytic environments.

Cousinia woronowii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
