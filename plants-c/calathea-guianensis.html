Calathea guianensis

Calathea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan Calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, variegated leaf patterns that often display a combination of green, silver, and pink hues. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which are often raised and visible, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect light to flourish.

Calathea guianensis is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
