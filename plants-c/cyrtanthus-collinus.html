Cyrtanthus collinus

Cyrtanthus collinus, commonly known as the mountain crown imperial, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of vibrant orange or yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Cyrtanthus collinus is noted for its resilience and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
