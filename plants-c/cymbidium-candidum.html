Cymbidium candidum

Cymbidium candidum, commonly known as the white orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Cymbidium candidum is a popular ornamental plant valued for its elegant appearance and fragrance.
