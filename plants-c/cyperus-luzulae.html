Cyperus luzulae

Cyperus luzulae, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are often found in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus luzulae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
