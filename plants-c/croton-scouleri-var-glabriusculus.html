Croton scouleri var. glabriusculus

Croton scouleri var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the smoothleaf croton, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
