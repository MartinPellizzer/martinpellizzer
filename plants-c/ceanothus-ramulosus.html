Ceanothus ramulosus

Ceanothus ramulosus, commonly known as the bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive blue-green hue.

Ceanothus ramulosus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
