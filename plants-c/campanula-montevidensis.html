Campanula montevidensis

Campanula montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America, specifically Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Campanula montevidensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
