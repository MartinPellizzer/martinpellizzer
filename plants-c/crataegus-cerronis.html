Crataegus cerronis

Crataegus cerronis, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it typically grows in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
