Calceolaria hartwegii

Calceolaria hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Calceolaria hartwegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
