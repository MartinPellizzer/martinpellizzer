Cactus leucocentrus

Cactus leucocentrus, commonly known as the white-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The plant's structure and appearance make it a distinctive and resilient member of the cactus family.
