Crataegus recordabilis

Crataegus recordabilis, commonly known as the recordable hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
