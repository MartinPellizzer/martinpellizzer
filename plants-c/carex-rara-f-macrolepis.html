Carex rara f. macrolepis

Carex rara f. macrolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
