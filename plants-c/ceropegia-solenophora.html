Ceropegia solenophora

Ceropegia solenophora, commonly known as the sand mat or string of dolphins, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often pink or red in color. The plant has a unique ability to store water in its stems and roots, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ceropegia solenophora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
