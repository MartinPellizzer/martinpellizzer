Cuphea liebmannii

Cuphea liebmannii, commonly known as the flame wheel plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a fiery display. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a versatile addition to both landscape designs and container plantings.
