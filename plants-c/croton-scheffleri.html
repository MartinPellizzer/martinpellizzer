Croton scheffleri

Croton scheffleri, commonly known as the Schefflera croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
