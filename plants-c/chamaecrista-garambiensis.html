Chamaecrista garambiensis

Chamaecrista garambiensis, commonly known as the Garambi ticktree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, and it has a hairy, upright stem with pinnate leaves.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
