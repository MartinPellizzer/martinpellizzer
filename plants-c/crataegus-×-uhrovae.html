Crataegus × uhrovae

Crataegus × uhrovae, commonly known as the Uhrová hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and dense foliage. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is native to Central Europe and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

The Uhrová hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
