Cuphea orthodisca

Cuphea orthodisca, commonly known as the wheelplant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, wheel-shaped inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Cuphea orthodisca thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
