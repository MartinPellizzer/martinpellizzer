Clausena tetramera

Clausena tetramera, commonly known as the four-lobed tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with four lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet and sour flavor. The fruit's pericarp is fibrous and contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible produce.
