Crotalaria cordifolia

Crotalaria cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in dry, open habitats. It has a hairy stem and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Crotalaria cordifolia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.
