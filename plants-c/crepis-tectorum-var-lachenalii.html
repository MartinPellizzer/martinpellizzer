Crepis tectorum var. lachenalii

Crepis tectorum var. lachenalii, commonly known as the roof thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
