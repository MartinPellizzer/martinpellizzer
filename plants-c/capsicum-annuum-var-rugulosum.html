Capsicum annuum var. rugulosum

Capsicum annuum var. rugulosum, commonly known as the roughleaf chili, is a variant of the common chili pepper characterized by its wrinkled or rugose leaf surfaces.

This plant is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value. It typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and small, yellow or green flowers. The fruit is usually a bright red or orange chili pepper, often used in cooking for its heat and flavor.

It prefers warm climates and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical regions.
