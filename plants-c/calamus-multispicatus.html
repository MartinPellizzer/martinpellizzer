Calamus multispicatus

Calamus multispicatus, commonly known as the many-spined calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Calamus multispicatus is often found in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
