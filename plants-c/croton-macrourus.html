Croton macrourus

Croton macrourus, commonly known as the long-tongued croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Croton macrourus is valued in horticulture for its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
