Calycodendron glabrum

Calycodendron glabrum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The silver tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
