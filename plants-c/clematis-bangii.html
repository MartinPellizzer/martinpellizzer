Clematis bangii

Clematis bangii, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, arching stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Clematis bangii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
