Cylindropuntia tunicata

Cylindropuntia tunicata, commonly known as the cholla cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems covered in small, spiny tubercles. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cylindropuntia tunicata is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, storing moisture in its succulent stems and leaves.
