Cytisus syriacus

Cytisus syriacus, commonly known as Syrian broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, thorned stems and small, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus syriacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
