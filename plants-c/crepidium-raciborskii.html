Crepidium raciborskii

Crepidium raciborskii, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, and it blooms during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
