Carex × rieseana

Carex × rieseana, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescences and grass-like leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense stands.

Carex × rieseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
