Cirsium kochianum

Cirsium kochianum, commonly known as the Koch's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
