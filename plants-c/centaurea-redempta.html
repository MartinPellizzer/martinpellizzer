Centaurea redempta

Centaurea redempta, commonly known as the redempta thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and striking purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea redempta is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain areas.
