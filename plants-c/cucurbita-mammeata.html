Cucurbita mammeata

Cucurbita mammeata, commonly known as the rough gourd, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, warty surface on both the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, followed by fruits that are typically green and have a bumpy texture. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.

It is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, sunny climates.
