Campanula precatoria var. hirsuta

Campanula precatoria var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "hirsuta," meaning "hairy" in Latin. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
