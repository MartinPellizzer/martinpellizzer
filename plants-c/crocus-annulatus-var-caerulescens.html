Crocus annulatus var. caerulescens

Crocus annulatus var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers with six petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and vibrant color.
