Cousinia elephantina

Cousinia elephantina, commonly known as the elephant head plant, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous, pinkish-red flower heads that resemble an elephant's head, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid climates.

The leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
