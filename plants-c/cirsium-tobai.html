Cirsium tobai

Cirsium tobai, commonly known as the Tobai thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts.

Cirsium tobai is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Asteraceae family.
