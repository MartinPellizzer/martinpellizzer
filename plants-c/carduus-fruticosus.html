Carduus fruticosus

Carduus fruticosus, commonly known as the prickly lettuce or thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring a rough, spiny stem and deeply lobed, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by spiny, prickly seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
