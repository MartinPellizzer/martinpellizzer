Cytisus tmoleus var. jankae

Cytisus tmoleus var. jankae, commonly known as the Janka's broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, spindly growth habit and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
