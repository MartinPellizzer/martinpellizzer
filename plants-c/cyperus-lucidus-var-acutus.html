Cyperus lucidus var. acutus

Cyperus lucidus var. acutus, commonly known as the sharpleaf flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often sharply pointed, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

This plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
