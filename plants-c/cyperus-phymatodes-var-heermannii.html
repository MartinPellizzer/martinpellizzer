Cyperus phymatodes var. heermannii

Cyperus phymatodes var. heermannii, commonly known as the desert flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and has a shallow root system, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to desert ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
