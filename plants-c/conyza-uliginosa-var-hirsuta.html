Conyza uliginosa var. hirsuta

Conyza uliginosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as swamp fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It features erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with coarse hairs, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

It is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.
