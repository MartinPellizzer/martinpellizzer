Colchicum baytopiorum

Colchicum baytopiorum, commonly known as the Baytopi colchicum, is a perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a tall stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous corm as its underground storage organ. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
