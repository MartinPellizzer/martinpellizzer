Calliandra silvicola

Calliandra silvicola, commonly known as the forest calliandra, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow or orange flowers in clusters. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable in reforestation efforts.

Calliandra silvicola is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
