Carum confusum

Carum confusum, commonly known as confused caraway, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, carroty scent.

Carum confusum is often found in grasslands and open areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
