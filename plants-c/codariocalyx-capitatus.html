Codariocalyx capitatus

Codariocalyx capitatus, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
