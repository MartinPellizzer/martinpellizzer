Chaetocyperus reptans

Chaetocyperus reptans, commonly known as the creeping flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
