Carex ascotreta

Carex ascotreta, commonly known as the Ascotreta sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex ascotreta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
