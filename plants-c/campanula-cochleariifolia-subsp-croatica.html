Campanula cochleariifolia subsp. croatica

Campanula cochleariifolia subsp.

croatica, commonly known as the Croatian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
