Cyphomandra betacea

Cyphomandra betacea, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically orange to red in color when ripe. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. The fruit is edible, with a tangy, slightly acidic flavor.

It is often used in culinary applications such as jams, sauces, and desserts.
