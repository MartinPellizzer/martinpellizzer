Crataegus coccinea var. macracantha

Crataegus coccinea var. macracantha, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright red, lantern-like fruits that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant features thorny branches and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
