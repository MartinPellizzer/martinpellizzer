Ceropegia grandiflora var. conformis

Ceropegia grandiflora var. conformis, commonly known as the African violet or velvet plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, trailing stems with opposite, ovate, and velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which are often hidden within a hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
