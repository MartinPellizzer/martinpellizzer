Carex flava var. intermedia

Carex flava var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green to brown in color, and it is often found in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex flava var. intermedia is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
