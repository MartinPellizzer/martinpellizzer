Cineraria oppositifolia

Cineraria oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cineraria oppositifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
