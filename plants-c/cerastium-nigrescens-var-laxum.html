Cerastium nigrescens var. laxum

Cerastium nigrescens var. laxum, commonly known as the loose-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are white or pale pink, clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Cerastium nigrescens var. laxum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
