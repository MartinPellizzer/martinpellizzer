Cissus pergamacea

Cissus pergamacea, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
