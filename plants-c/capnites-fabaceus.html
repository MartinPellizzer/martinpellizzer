Capnites fabaceus

Capnites fabaceus, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a valuable forage crop. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of pink or purple.

The seeds are encased in hairy pods, which contribute to its common name and aid in seed dispersal.
