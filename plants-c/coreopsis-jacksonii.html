Coreopsis jacksonii

Coreopsis jacksonii, commonly known as Jackson's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
