Chamaemelum nobile

Chamaemelum nobile, commonly known as nosegay or garden chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like white flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in early summer. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas and remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Chamaemelum nobile is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
