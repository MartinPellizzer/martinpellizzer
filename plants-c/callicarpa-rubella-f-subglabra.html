Callicarpa rubella f. subglabra

Callicarpa rubella f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-hairy beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture, and the plant has a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental berries and is often used in native plant gardens.
