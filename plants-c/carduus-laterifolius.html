Carduus laterifolius

Carduus laterifolius, commonly known as the common hawk's-beard, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and basal rosette of large, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
