Carex curatorum

Carex curatorum, commonly known as the curator's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and filter water, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Carex curatorum is typically perennial and can grow up to 30 inches tall, depending on environmental conditions.
