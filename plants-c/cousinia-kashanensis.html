Cousinia kashanensis

Cousinia kashanensis, commonly known as the Kashan cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

Cousinia kashanensis is often found in mountainous and semi-desert environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
