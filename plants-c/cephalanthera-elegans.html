Cephalanthera elegans

Cephalanthera elegans, commonly known as the elegant green tongue orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive greenish-white flowers with a prominent, hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its roots are tuberous, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms from late spring to early summer.

Cephalanthera elegans is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators and its role in forest ecosystems.
