Clematopsis nigerica

Clematopsis nigerica, commonly known as the black clematopsis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
