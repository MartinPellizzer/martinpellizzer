Ceanothus celtidifolius

Ceanothus celtidifolius, commonly known as the canyon ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-green leaves and clusters of fragrant, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round structure in its native habitat.

Ceanothus celtidifolius plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its environment.
