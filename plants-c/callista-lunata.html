Callista lunata

Callista lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Callista lunata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
