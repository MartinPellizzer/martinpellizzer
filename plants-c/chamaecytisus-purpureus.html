Chamaecytisus purpureus

Chamaecytisus purpureus, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, reddish-brown seeds in follicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden conditions.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant landscapes.
