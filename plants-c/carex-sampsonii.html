Carex sampsonii

Carex sampsonii, commonly known as Sampson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive dark green color and a waxy coating.

Carex sampsonii produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by triangular, three-sided seed capsules.
