Cerastium petiolare

Cerastium petiolare, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are small, dry capsules. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Cerastium petiolare is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
