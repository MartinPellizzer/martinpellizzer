Cerastium illyricum subsp. pilosum

Cerastium illyricum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The leaves are typically hairy and rounded, while the flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
