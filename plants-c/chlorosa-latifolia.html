Chlorosa latifolia

Chlorosa latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed greenbloom, is a striking plant native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in late spring, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Chlorosa latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
