Celtis strychnoides

Celtis strychnoides, commonly known as the Indian hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to parts of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
