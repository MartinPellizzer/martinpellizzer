Clinogyne schweinfurthiana

Clinogyne schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's clinogyne, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow petals and a central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Clinogyne schweinfurthiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
