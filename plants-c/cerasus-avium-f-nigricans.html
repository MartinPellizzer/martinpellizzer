Cerasus avium f. nigricans

Cerasus avium f. nigricans, commonly known as the black cherry or black cherry tree, is a botanical variant of the sweet cherry species.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its distinctive, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
