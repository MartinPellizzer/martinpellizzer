Carex viridissima

Carex viridissima, commonly known as the green moss sedge.

It is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada. This species belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets.
