Crataegus holmesiana var. amicta

Crataegus holmesiana var. amicta, commonly known as the Holmes' hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
