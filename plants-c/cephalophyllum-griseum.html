Cephalophyllum griseum

Cephalophyllum griseum, commonly known as the silver-tipped rock cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a distinctive appearance with its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in a silvery, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle beauty to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
