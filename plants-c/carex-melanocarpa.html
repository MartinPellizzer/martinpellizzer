Carex melanocarpa

Carex melanocarpa, commonly known as blackstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. Its dark purple stems and dark green foliage make it a distinctive species within the sedge family.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a single inflorescence.
