Conoclinium integrifolium

Conoclinium integrifolium, commonly known as the whorled garden heliotrope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Conoclinium integrifolium is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
