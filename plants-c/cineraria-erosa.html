Cineraria erosa

Cineraria erosa, commonly known as the hairy cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms from spring to summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
