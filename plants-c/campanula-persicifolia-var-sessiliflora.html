Campanula persicifolia var. sessiliflora

Campanula persicifolia var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
