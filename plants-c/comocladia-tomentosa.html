Comocladia tomentosa

Comocladia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bladderwrack, is a marine algae belonging to the order Fucales.

It is characterized by its dark brown, leathery fronds covered in a dense, woolly coating of fine hairs. This species typically grows in shallow, intertidal zones along rocky coastlines. The plant's structure consists of a holdfast, stipe, and blade, with the blade being the most prominent part.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
