Cassia luetzelburgii

Cassia luetzelburgii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Cassia luetzelburgii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
