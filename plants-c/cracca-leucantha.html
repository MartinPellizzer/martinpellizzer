Cracca leucantha

Cracca leucantha, commonly known as the white cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy.

The flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems and bloom during the summer months.
