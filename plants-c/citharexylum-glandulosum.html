Citharexylum glandulosum

Citharexylum glandulosum, commonly known as the glossy cithara or honeywood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in a waxy bloom, giving it a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural displays.
