Caprifolium parviflorum

Caprifolium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in early summer. Its berries are small, dark red to black, and serve as a food source for various birds and wildlife.

Caprifolium parviflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
