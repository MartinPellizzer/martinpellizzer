Coffea rakotonasoloi

Coffea rakotonasoloi, commonly known as the Madagascar coffee.

It is a species of coffee native to the island of Madagascar, characterized by its unique botanical features. The plant typically grows as a small shrub with dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and white, followed by red fruits containing two seeds used for coffee production.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic distinctiveness, it is considered a rare and valuable species in the Coffea genus.
