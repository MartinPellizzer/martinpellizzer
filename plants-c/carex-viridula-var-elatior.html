Carex viridula var. elatior

Carex viridula var. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color, with a prominent central vein. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
