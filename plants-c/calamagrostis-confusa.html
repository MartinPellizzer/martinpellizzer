Calamagrostis confusa

Calamagrostis confusa, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to reddish in color, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Calamagrostis confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
