Chrysocoma nupera

Chrysocoma nupera, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing hard seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

The tree is valued for its strong, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.
