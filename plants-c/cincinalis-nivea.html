Cincinalis nivea

Cincinalis nivea, commonly known as the white snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
