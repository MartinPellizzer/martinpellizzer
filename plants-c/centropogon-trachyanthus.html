Centropogon trachyanthus

Centropogon trachyanthus, commonly known as the yellow pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flower heads that resemble small pincushions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in a dense, spherical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Centropogon trachyanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
