Cucumis melo var. reginae

Cucumis melo var. reginae, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a cultivar of the melon genus characterized by its elongated, thin, and crisp fruits.

The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. It typically grows on a trailing vine with lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the edible, sweet fruit. The fruit is usually harvested when it is still green, though it can be allowed to ripen to a golden or orange hue.

This variety is prized for its mild flavor and is often used in salads or as a snack.
