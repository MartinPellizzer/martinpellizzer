Corydalis orthoceras var. fumariifolia

Corydalis orthoceras var. fumariifolia, commonly known as the fumariifolia corydalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is native to regions in Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in woodland and grassland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
