Centaurea vacillans

Centaurea vacillans, commonly known as the wavering centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically arranged in loose, open clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Centaurea vacillans is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
