Crataegus rubrolutea

Crataegus rubrolutea, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorned branches. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus rubrolutea is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open fields.
