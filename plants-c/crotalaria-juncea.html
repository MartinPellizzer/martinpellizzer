Crotalaria juncea

Crotalaria juncea, commonly known as sicklepod, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped seed pods and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It is widely cultivated as a source of protein-rich meal for livestock.

Additionally, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it a potential weed in agricultural settings.
