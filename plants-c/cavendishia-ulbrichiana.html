Cavendishia ulbrichiana

Cavendishia ulbrichiana, commonly known as the Ulbrich's Cavendish, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance and resilience.
