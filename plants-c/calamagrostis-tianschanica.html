Calamagrostis tianschanica

Calamagrostis tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and riverbanks.

Calamagrostis tianschanica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
