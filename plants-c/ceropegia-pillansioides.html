Ceropegia pillansioides

Ceropegia pillansioides, commonly known as the African violet ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces specialized structures called capitate glands, which are involved in attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
