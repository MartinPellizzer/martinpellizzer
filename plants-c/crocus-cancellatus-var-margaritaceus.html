Crocus cancellatus var. margaritaceus

Crocus cancellatus var. margaritaceus, commonly known as the pearl crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale blue to white flowers with a central yellow spot, resembling a pearl. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
