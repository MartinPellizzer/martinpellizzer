Cistus libanotis var. segobricensis

Cistus libanotis var. segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobricense rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which is part of the family Ericaceae, known for its hardy and drought-resistant species. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring and summer. Its leaves are typically leathery and evergreen, adapted to conserve water in its native habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
