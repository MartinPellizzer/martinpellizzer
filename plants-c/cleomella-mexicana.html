Cleomella mexicana

Cleomella mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican cleome, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are often clustered in racemes.

Cleomella mexicana is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
