Chiranthodendron californicum

Chiranthodendron californicum, commonly known as the California flamebrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and coastal climates. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
