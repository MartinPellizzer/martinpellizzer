Cherina

Cherina, commonly known as the cherry tree, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by sweet, red fruits. Cherina is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit.
