Cydonia maulei var. tricolor

Cydonia maulei var. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolor quince, is a variant of the quince species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays three distinct colors—usually green, yellow, and red—on the same plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its unique fruit.

The tricolor quince is a hardy shrub that requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
