Centaurea shuttleworthii

Centaurea shuttleworthii, commonly known as Shuttleworth's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

It is often found in coastal and inland regions, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
