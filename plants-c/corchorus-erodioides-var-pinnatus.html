Corchorus erodioides var. pinnatus

Corchorus erodioides var. pinnatus, commonly known as the fingerleaf dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It features distinctive, deeply lobed, pinnately divided leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
