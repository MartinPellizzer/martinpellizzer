Cotoneaster mucronatus

Cotoneaster mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-tipped cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Cotoneaster mucronatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is also valued in horticulture for its use in landscaping and as a rootstock for other plants.
