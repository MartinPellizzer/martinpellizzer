Crataegus clintoniana

Crataegus clintoniana, commonly known as Clinton’s hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Clinton’s hawthorn is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
