Colutea komarovii

Colutea komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's senna, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seed pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Colutea komarovii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
