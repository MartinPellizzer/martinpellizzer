Cyperus niveoides

Cyperus niveoides, commonly known as the silver flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the base of the plant. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus niveoides is often used in ornamental gardening for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its attractive silvery foliage.
