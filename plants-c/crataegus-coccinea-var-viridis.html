Crataegus coccinea var. viridis

Crataegus coccinea var. viridis, commonly known as the green-flowered hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America. The plant is characterized by its vibrant green flowers that appear in spring, contrasting with its red berries in autumn. It has pinnately compound leaves and produces thorny branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
