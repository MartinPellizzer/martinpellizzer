Cirsium tsoongianum

Cirsium tsoongianum, commonly known as the Tsoong's thistle, is a perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium tsoongianum is valued in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
