Cryptocarya ferrea

Cryptocarya ferrea, commonly known as Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries.

Cryptocarya ferrea is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
