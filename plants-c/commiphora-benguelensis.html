Commiphora benguelensis

Commiphora benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
