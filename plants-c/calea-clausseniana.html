Calea clausseniana

Calea clausseniana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, violet-blue petals that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique nocturnal blooming habit. Its leaves are broad and often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calea clausseniana is cultivated for its beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
