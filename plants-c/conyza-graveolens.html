Conyza graveolens

Conyza graveolens, commonly known as the horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Conyza graveolens is also known for its ability to produce a strong, unpleasant odor, which can be a distinguishing feature.
