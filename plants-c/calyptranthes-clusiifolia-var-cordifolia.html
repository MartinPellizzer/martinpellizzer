Calyptranthes clusiifolia var. cordifolia

Calyptranthes clusiifolia var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf calyptranthes, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elliptical to heart-shaped leaves, which are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale yellow, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in warm, moist climates.
