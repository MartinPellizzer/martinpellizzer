Cucumis melo var. indicus

Cucumis melo var. indicus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit.

It typically measures between 15 to 30 centimeters in length and has a smooth, pale green skin with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and squash. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in salads or as a vegetable due to its crisp texture and refreshing taste.
