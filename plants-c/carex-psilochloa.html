Carex psilochloa

Carex psilochloa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
