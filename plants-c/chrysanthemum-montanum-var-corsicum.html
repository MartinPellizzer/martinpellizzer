Chrysanthemum montanum var. corsicum

Chrysanthemum montanum var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican mountain chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish to white flowers in the autumn. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is relatively hardy and can tolerate mild frost, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
