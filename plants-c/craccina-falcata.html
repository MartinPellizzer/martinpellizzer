Craccina falcata

Craccina falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf craccina, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, resembling a sickle shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Craccina falcata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
