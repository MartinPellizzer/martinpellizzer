Cucullaria elliptica

Cucullaria elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has elliptical, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cucullaria elliptica is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
