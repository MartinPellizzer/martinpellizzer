Chasseloupia microphylla

Chasseloupia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved chasseloupia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
