Crepis columnae

Crepis columnae, commonly known as the columnar hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in late summer.

Crepis columnae is known for its upright growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
