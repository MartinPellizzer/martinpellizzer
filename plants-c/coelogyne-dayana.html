Coelogyne dayana

Coelogyne dayana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple lip. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Coelogyne dayana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
