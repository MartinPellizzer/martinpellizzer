Cremosperma peruvianum

Cremosperma peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian creamroot, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a slender, branching stem with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
