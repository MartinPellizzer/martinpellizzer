Centaurea paniculata subsp. maremmana

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

maremmana, commonly known as the Maremma thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy composite flower heads that range in color from yellow to red.

The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for leaves, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for various pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
