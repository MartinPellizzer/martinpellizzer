Carex laxiculmis var. laxiculmis

Carex laxiculmis var. laxiculmis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
