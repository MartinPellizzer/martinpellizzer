Cymbopappus lasiopodus

Cymbopappus lasiopodus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 0.5 to 1.5 meters, characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Cymbopappus lasiopodus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
