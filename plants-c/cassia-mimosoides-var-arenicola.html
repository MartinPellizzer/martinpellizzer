Cassia mimosoides var. arenicola

Cassia mimosoides var. arenicola, commonly known as the coastal cassia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate growth habit and small, yellowish-green leaves that are trifoliate. The plant produces elongated, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to sandy and saline soils, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal landscaping.

Its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions make it a popular choice in maritime gardens.
