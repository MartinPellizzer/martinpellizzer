Calendula suffruticosa subsp. algarbiensis

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbi calendula, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its branched, woody stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
