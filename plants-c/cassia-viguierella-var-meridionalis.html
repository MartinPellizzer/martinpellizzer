Cassia viguierella var. meridionalis

Cassia viguierella var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm, arid climates.
