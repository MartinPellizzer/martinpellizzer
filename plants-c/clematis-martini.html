Clematis martini

Clematis martini, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep blue, depending on the specific cultivar. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis martini is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
