Cerasus clarofolia

Cerasus clarofolia, commonly known as the clearleaf cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that give it its common name, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Cerasus clarofolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
