Crataegus monogyna var. splendens

Crataegus monogyna var. splendens, commonly known as the white thornapple or splendens hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. The plant has thorny branches and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
