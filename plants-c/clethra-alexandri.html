Clethra alexandri

Clethra alexandri, commonly known as the sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in forested areas.

Clethra alexandri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
