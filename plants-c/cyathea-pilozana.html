Cyathea pilozana

Cyathea pilozana, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a dense layer of brown, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea pilozana typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
