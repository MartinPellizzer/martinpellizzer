Carex eremitica

Carex eremitica, commonly known as the desert sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear brownish flowers. The plant has short, fibrous roots and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils with minimal water availability. It thrives in full sun and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Carex eremitica is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
