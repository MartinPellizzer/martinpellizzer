Chloris simplex

Chloris simplex, commonly known as the slender grass or slender bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Chloris simplex is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
