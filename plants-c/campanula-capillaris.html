Campanula capillaris

Campanula capillaris, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, spreading stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet, and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Campanula capillaris is well adapted to moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
