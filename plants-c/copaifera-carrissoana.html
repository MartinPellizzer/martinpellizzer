Copaifera carrissoana

Copaifera carrissoana, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a member of the Leguminosae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and pinnate leaves. The plant is known for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in medicine and perfumery. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces legume pods containing seeds.

Copaifera carrissoana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
