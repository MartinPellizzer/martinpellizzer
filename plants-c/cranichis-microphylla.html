Cranichis microphylla

Cranichis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine forests and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinct lip that is often darker in color.

Cranichis microphylla is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ephemeral blooming cycle, making it a rare and elusive species.
