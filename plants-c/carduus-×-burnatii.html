Carduus × burnatii

Carduus × burnatii, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Carduus acanthoides and Carduus maximus. This plant is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to violet flowering heads. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
