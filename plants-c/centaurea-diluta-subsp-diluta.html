Centaurea diluta subsp. diluta

Centaurea diluta subsp.

diluta, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
