Cirsium × aschersonii

Cirsium × aschersonii, commonly known as the European thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the thistle family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions, including North America, where it often thrives in disturbed soils. The plant's robust growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and roadside environments.

Its botanical features include a tall, erect stem and a composite flower head composed of both ray and disk florets.
