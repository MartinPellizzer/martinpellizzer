Chrysanthemum ramosum

Chrysanthemum ramosum, commonly known as the creeping chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and profusion of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
