Chaenostoma linifolium

Chaenostoma linifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in still or slow-moving water.

Chaenostoma linifolium plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and young fish.
