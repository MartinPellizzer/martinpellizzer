Cornus alba f. sibirica

Cornus alba f. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian dogwood, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features attractive, orange-red to reddish-brown stems that remain visible throughout winter, providing winter interest in gardens. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This ornamental plant is valued for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in temperate climates.
