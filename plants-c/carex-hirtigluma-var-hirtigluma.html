Carex hirtigluma var. hirtigluma

Carex hirtigluma var. hirtigluma, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
