Canarium lian

Canarium lian, commonly known as the climbing canarium, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its long, twining stems that climb towards light. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing oily seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Canarium lian is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of natural oils.
