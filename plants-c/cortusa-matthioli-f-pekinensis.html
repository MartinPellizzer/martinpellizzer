Cortusa matthioli f. pekinensis

Cortusa matthioli f. pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking bellflower, is a hybrid form of the genus Cortusa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features rhizomatous growth and has finely divided, fern-like foliage. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this plant make it a distinctive addition to woodland or shade gardens.
