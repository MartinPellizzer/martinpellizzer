Campanula fasciculata

Campanula fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Campanula fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
