Cryphiacanthus tuberosus

Cryphiacanthus tuberosus, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients and water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular houseplant worldwide.
