Coleus giorgii

Coleus giorgii, commonly known as the Italian coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white.

Coleus giorgii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
