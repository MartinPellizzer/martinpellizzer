Cinnamomum loureiroi var. scrobiculatum

Cinnamomum loureiroi var. scrobiculatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum loureiroi var. scrobiculatum, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its essential oils and has medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its leaves are also used in the production of certain perfumes and flavorings.
