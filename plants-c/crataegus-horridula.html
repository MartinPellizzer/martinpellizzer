Crataegus horridula

Crataegus horridula, commonly known as the rough hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

The rough hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
