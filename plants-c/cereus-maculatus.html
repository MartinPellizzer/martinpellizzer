Cereus maculatus

Cereus maculatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Cereus maculatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
