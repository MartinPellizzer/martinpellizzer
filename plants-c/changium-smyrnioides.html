Changium smyrnioides

Changium smyrnioides, commonly known as the Chinese sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Changium smyrnioides is also cultivated as a drought-tolerant ornamental in gardens with well-draining soil.
