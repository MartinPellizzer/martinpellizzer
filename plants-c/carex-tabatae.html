Carex tabatae

Carex tabatae, commonly known as Tabata's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This species is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or wetlands.

Carex tabatae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
