Cordyline × gibbingsiae

Cordyline × gibbingsiae, commonly known as the red-leafed cabbage tree, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cordyline australis and Cordyline indivisa.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and is native to New Zealand. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique coloration and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and landscape settings.
