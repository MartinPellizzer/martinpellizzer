Chaenostoma × saccharatum

Chaenostoma × saccharatum, commonly known as the sugarleaf plant, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, sugary coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to parts of the southern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland areas.
