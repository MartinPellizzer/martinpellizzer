Cirsium ketzkhovelii

Cirsium ketzkhovelii, commonly known as the Ketzkhovel thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are broad, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are purple to violet in color.

Cirsium ketzkhovelii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
