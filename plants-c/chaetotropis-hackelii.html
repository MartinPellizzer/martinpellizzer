Chaetotropis hackelii

Chaetotropis hackelii, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
