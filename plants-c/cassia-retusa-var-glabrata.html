Cassia retusa var. glabrata

Cassia retusa var. glabrata, commonly known as golden shower tree or sapphire flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their various therapeutic properties.
