Ceropegia gigantea

Ceropegia gigantea, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
