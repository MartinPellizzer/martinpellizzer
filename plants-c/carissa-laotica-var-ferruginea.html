Carissa laotica var. ferruginea

Carissa laotica var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty carissa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible but have a sour taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
