Chrysothemis kuhlmannii

Chrysothemis kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to survive in drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and botanical gardens.
