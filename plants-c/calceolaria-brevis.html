Calceolaria brevis

Calceolaria brevis, commonly known as the short hooded slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Calceolaria brevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate and colorful blooms.
