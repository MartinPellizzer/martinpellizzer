Carex elongata

Carex elongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in clusters, producing dense foliage that can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Carex elongata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife in its native ecosystems.
