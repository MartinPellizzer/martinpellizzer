Chenopodium album var. heterophyllum

Chenopodium album var. heterophyllum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in compact clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
