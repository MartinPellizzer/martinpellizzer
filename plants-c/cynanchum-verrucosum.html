Cynanchum verrucosum

Cynanchum verrucosum, commonly known as the rough milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Cynanchum verrucosum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
