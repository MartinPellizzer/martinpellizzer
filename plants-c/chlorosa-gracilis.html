Chlorosa gracilis

Chlorosa gracilis, commonly known as the slender green fern, is a delicate herbaceous plant characterized by its fine, feathery fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. The plant's leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance.

Chlorosa gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
