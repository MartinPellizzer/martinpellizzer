Cynorkis gibbosa

Cynorkis gibbosa, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Cynorkis gibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
