Centropogon caligatus

Centropogon caligatus, commonly known as the clouded false heather, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle cloud-like appearance. Its leaves are linear and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Centropogon caligatus is valued in horticulture for its delicate beauty and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
