Carex lamprophysa

Carex lamprophysa, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces dark brown, three-angled berries.

Carex lamprophysa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
