Clematis uruguayensis

Clematis uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, actinomorphic flowers. The plant typically has palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clematis uruguayensis is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or fences.
