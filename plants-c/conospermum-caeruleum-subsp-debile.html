Conospermum caeruleum subsp. debile

Conospermum caeruleum subsp.

debile, commonly known as the slender blue conospermum, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
