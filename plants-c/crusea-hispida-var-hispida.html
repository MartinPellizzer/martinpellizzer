Crusea hispida var. hispida

Crusea hispida var. hispida, commonly known as the rough crusea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The leaves are hairy and rough to the touch, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
