Croton malacophyllus

Croton malacophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental leaves, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
