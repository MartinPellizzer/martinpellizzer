Cuphea obliqua

Cuphea obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cuphea obliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
