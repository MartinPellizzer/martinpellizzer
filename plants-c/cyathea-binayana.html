Cyathea binayana

Cyathea binayana, commonly known as the Philippine tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while the fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea binayana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
