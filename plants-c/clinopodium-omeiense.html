Clinopodium omeiense

Clinopodium omeiense, commonly known as the Emei mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Clinopodium omeiense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
