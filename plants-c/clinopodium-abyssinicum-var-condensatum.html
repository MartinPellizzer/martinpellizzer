Clinopodium abyssinicum var. condensatum

Clinopodium abyssinicum var. condensatum, commonly known as African mint, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued both for its medicinal uses and ornamental appeal.
