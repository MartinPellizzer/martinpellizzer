Clusia grisebachiana

Clusia grisebachiana, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Clusia grisebachiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
