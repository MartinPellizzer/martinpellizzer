Cleistanthus concinnus

Cleistanthus concinnus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
