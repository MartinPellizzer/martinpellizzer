Cedrela mexicana

Cedrela mexicana, commonly known as Mexican cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and workability. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and dense, glossy leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody capsules containing flat, winged seeds.

Cedrela mexicana is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
