Cocos campicola

Cocos campicola, commonly known as the Mexican coconut or dwarf coconut, is a small, palm-like plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, with a slender trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that contain a single kernel, similar to true coconuts but much smaller. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food.

Due to its compact size, it is well-suited for gardens and landscapes in subtropical regions.
