Cinnamomum tamala

Cinnamomum tamala, commonly known as Indian cinnamon or tamal, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and red berries, which contain the seeds used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its leaves contain essential oils rich in compounds like cinnamaldehyde, contributing to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
