Calamus merrillii var. nanga

Calamus merrillii var. nanga, commonly known as the Nanga calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

This variant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
