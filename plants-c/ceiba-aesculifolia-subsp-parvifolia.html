Ceiba aesculifolia subsp. parvifolia

Ceiba aesculifolia subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-fruited ceiba, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and parts of Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its large, buttressed trunk and pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces woody capsules as fruits.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
