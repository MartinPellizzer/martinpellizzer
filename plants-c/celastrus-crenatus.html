Celastrus crenatus

Celastrus crenatus, commonly known as the Japanese twining vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Celastrus crenatus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
