Centropogon nebularum

Centropogon nebularum, commonly known as the cloudflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble clouds, hence its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Centropogon nebularum is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and striking appearance make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
