Cucumis membranifolius

Cucumis membranifolius, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual vine characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by cucumbers that are typically thin, elongated, and covered with a fine, membranous layer. It thrives in hot, dry environments and is adapted to low-water conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on drought tolerance and plant adaptation.
