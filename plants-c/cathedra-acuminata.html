Cathedra acuminata

Cathedra acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped cathedra, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers bloom in clusters, making it an attractive addition to gardens, while its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
