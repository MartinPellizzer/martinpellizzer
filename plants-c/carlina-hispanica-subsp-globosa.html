Carlina hispanica subsp. globosa

Carlina hispanica subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its spherical, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by spiny, grass-like leaves.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its striking, showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
