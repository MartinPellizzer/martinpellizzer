Cyperus confertiflorus

Cyperus confertiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus confertiflorus often grows in wet or marshy habitats, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.
