Codonopsis retroserrata

Codonopsis retroserrata, commonly known as the mountain codonopsis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers.

Codonopsis retroserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
