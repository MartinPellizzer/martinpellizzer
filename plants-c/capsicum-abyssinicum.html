Capsicum abyssinicum

Capsicum abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian chili or Abyssinian pepper, is a species native to Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous plant with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. Its fruits are typically red or orange when mature and are used in traditional Ethiopian cuisine.

Capsicum abyssinicum is valued for its unique flavor and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
