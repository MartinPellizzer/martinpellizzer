Carex vaginata var. vaginata

Carex vaginata var. vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, thriving in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex vaginata var. vaginata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
