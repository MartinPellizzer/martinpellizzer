Clematis schillingii

Clematis schillingii, commonly known as the Schilling's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features attractive, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis schillingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens as a climbing plant.
