Corallospartium crassicaule var. racemosum

Corallospartium crassicaule var. racemosum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed coral spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
