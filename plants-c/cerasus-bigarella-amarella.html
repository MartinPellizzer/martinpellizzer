Cerasus bigarella amarella

Cerasus bigarella amarella, commonly known as the sweet cherry.

It is a species of Prunus native to the eastern United States, characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white to pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit ripens in late summer, making it a valuable crop for both commercial and home gardening.
