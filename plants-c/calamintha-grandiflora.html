Calamintha grandiflora

Calamintha grandiflora, commonly known as largeflower caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with a strong mint-like scent. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Calamintha grandiflora is often used in herbal medicine for its digestive and antiseptic properties.
