Centaurea montana var. occitanica

Centaurea montana var. occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is part of the genus Centaurea, which includes many species with similar composite flower structures. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
