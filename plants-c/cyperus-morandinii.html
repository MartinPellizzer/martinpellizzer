Cyperus morandinii

Cyperus morandinii, commonly known as the marsh cabbage, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pale green to brown in color. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.

Cyperus morandinii is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its capacity to help in water filtration.
