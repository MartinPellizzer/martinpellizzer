Colubrina articulata

Colubrina articulata, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Colubrina articulata is often found in sandy soils along coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
