Cleome scheffleri

Cleome scheffleri, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. Cleome scheffleri is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
