Commiphora viminea

Commiphora viminea, commonly known as the slender myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white. The plant is known for secreting a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
