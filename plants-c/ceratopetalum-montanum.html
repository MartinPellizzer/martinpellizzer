Ceratopetalum montanum

Ceratopetalum montanum, commonly known as mountain holly, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Ceratopetalum montanum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
