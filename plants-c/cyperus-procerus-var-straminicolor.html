Cyperus procerus var. straminicolor

Cyperus procerus var. straminicolor, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wet or marshy environments.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, grass-like appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soil.
