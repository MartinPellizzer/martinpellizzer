Corbularia nivalis

Corbularia nivalis, commonly known as the snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are small and lance-shaped, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Due to its specialized habitat, Corbularia nivalis is often found in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations.
