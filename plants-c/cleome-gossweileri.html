Cleome gossweileri

Cleome gossweileri, commonly known as the sweet scent cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spire-like clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
