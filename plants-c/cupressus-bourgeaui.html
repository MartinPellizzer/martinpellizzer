Cupressus bourgeaui

Cupressus bourgeaui, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and distinctive, rounded cones. The tree typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against wildfires and drought.

The Sierra Madre cypress plays an important ecological role in its native range, offering habitat and shelter to various wildlife species.
