Calamagrostis elatior

Calamagrostis elatior, commonly known as tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a bluish-green color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
