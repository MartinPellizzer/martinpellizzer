Chilochloa paniculata

Chilochloa paniculata, commonly known as the panicled chilochloa, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces inflorescences that are paniculate, consisting of multiple branches bearing small, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Chilochloa paniculata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forest margins and open habitats.
