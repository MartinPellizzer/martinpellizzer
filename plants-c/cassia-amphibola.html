Cassia amphibola

Cassia amphibola, commonly known as the water cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist environments, often near water bodies. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are dispersed by wind.

Cassia amphibola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
