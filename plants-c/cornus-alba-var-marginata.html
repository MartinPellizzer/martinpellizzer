Cornus alba var. marginata

Cornus alba var. marginata, commonly known as the red-twigged dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-brown stems that become more vibrant in winter, providing ornamental value throughout the year. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate and turn yellow in the autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
