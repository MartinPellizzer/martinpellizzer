Cytisus grandiflorus var. barbarus

Cytisus grandiflorus var. barbarus, commonly known as the Scottish broom, is a flowering shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
