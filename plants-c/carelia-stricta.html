Carelia stricta

Carelia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf carelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pointed tip and a serrated margin, often displaying a bluish-green hue. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, shaded woodland environments.
