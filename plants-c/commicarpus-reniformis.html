Commicarpus reniformis

Commicarpus reniformis, commonly known as the kidney bean vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several bean-like seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit is typically vine-like, with climbing stems that can reach several meters in length.

The botanical features of Commicarpus reniformis make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and pharmacology.
