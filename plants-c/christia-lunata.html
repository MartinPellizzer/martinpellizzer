Christia lunata

Christia lunata, commonly known as moonflower or nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its striking white or pale purple flowers that open at night and close during the day. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.
