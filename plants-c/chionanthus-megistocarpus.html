Chionanthus megistocarpus

Chionanthus megistocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces large, fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
