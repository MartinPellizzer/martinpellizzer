Chenopodium hircinum f. purpureum

Chenopodium hircinum f. purpureum, commonly known as purple goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to rhombic in shape and often display a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, clustered green flowers that are followed by three-angled, bead-like fruits. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
