Claoxylon purpureum

Claoxylon purpureum, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or purple hue, which adds to its ornamental appeal.

Claoxylon purpureum is valued in horticulture for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
