Cenchrus bambusiformis

Cenchrus bambusiformis, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, resembling bamboo in appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Cenchrus bambusiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful, upright growth habit.
