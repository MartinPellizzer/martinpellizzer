Cestrum benghalense

Cestrum benghalense, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic flowers.

Cestrum benghalense thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
