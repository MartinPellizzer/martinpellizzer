Crataegus immanis

Crataegus immanis, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a rough texture.

The Pacific crabapple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
