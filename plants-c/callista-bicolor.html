Callista bicolor

Callista bicolor, commonly known as the twin-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that display two distinct colors. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue and white.

Callista bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
