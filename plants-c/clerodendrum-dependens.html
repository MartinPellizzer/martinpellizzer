Clerodendrum dependens

Clerodendrum dependens, commonly known as the hanging clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

Clerodendrum dependens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
