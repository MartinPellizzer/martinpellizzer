Capparis neriifolia

Capparis neriifolia, commonly known as blackleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and is known for its distinctive, edible but somewhat bitter fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Capparis neriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
