Coreopsis rueppellii

Coreopsis rueppellii, commonly known as the Mexican coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its blooms appear from late spring to early summer. Coreopsis rueppellii is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
