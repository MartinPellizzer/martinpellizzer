Crotalaria sagittalis var. linearis

Crotalaria sagittalis var. linearis, commonly known as the linear spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, linear leaves and yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a forage crop, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
