Critoniopsis glandulata

Critoniopsis glandulata, commonly known as the sticky leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying vibrant colors like yellow or orange.

Botanically, it is of interest due to its specialized adaptations and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
