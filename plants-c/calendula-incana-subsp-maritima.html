Calendula incana subsp. maritima

Calendula incana subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea calendula, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Its flowers are used in herbal remedies and as a natural dye.
