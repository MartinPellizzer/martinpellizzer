Ceropegia cactiformis

Ceropegia cactiformis, commonly known as the Cactus-shaped Ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive, branching stem that resembles a cactus, with small, fleshy leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique, waxy texture. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
