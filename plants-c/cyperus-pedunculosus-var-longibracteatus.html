Cyperus pedunculosus var. longibracteatus

Cyperus pedunculosus var. longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, triangular stems that are often upright or slightly arching. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.
