Crotalaria amadiensis

Crotalaria amadiensis, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine in some regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
