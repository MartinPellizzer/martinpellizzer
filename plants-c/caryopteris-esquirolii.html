Caryopteris esquirolii

Caryopteris esquirolii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Caryopteris esquirolii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
