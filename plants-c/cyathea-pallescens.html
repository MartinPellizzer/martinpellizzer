Cyathea pallescens

Cyathea pallescens, commonly known as the pale tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in structure and appearance.

Cyathea pallescens plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
