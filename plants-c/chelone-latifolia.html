Chelone latifolia

Chelone latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf hedge-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and wet meadows.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species for pollinators and wildlife.
