Cedrela dugesii

Cedrela dugesii, commonly known as the Honduras mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and large, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered a threatened species.
