Cestrum vestioides

Cestrum vestioides, commonly known as the velvet leaf jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in clusters during the summer months. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Cestrum vestioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive foliage.
