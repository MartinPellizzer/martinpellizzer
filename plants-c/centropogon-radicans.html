Centropogon radicans

Centropogon radicans, commonly known as the creeping centropogon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
