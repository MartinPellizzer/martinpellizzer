Copiapoa cinerea subsp. gigantea

Copiapoa cinerea subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant white copiapoa, is a cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces white, woolly spines and clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall and high sun exposure.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a popular specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
