Convallaria biflora

Convallaria biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink blooms that typically appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, growing in a basal rosette.

Convallaria biflora is often found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.
