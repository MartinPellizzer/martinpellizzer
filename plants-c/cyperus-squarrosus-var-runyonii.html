Cyperus squarrosus var. runyonii

Cyperus squarrosus var. runyonii, commonly known as the Runyon's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and linear with a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
