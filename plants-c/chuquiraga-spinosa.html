Chuquiraga spinosa

Chuquiraga spinosa, commonly known as the spiny chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting well to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Chuquiraga spinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
