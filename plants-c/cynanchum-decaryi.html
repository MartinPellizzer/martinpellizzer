Cynanchum decaryi

Cynanchum decaryi, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, ribbed fruits. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Cynanchum decaryi thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
