Cornus davurica

Cornus davurica, commonly known as Manchurian dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces white or pinkish bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers in early summer. Its berries are red or black and provide food for birds and small mammals.

Cornus davurica is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
