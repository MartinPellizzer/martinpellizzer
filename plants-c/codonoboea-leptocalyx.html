Codonoboea leptocalyx

Codonoboea leptocalyx, commonly known as the slender-leaved codonopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
