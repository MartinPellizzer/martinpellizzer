Carex saxatilis var. inferalpina

Carex saxatilis var. inferalpina, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
