Cardamine xanthina

Cardamine xanthina, commonly known as the yellow watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a peppery taste. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

The plant is often found in calcareous soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
