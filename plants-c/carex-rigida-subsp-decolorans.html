Carex rigida subsp. decolorans

Carex rigida subsp.

decolorans, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically pale green to reddish in color, especially during the growing season.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
