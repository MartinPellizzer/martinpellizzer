Carex banksii f. pallescens

Carex banksii f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale Carex, is a perennial sedge native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
