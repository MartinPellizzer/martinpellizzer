Cynanchum truncatum

Cynanchum truncatum, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or the Chinese snake root, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

Cynanchum truncatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
