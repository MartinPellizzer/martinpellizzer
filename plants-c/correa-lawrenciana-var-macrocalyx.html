Correa lawrenciana var. macrocalyx

Correa lawrenciana var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are densely arranged along its stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with a prominent white throat. These flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
