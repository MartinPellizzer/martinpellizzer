Carex boryana var. rigidifolia

Carex boryana var. rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its stiff, rigid leaves and is often found in cold, temperate climates.
