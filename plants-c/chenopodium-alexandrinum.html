Chenopodium alexandrinum

Chenopodium alexandrinum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically appearing in late summer. It is native to temperate regions and is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium alexandrinum is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
