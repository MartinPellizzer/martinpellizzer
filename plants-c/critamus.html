Critamus

Critamus, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. The plants are characterized by their small, yellowish flowers and woody, thorny stems. They typically grow in shrub form and are often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a rough texture.
