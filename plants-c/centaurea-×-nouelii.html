Centaurea × nouelii

Centaurea × nouelii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea stoebe (knout). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
