Cinnamomum chantinii

Cinnamomum chantinii, commonly known as the Vietnamese cinnamon or Cinnamomum chantinii, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for flavoring.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
