Cornus × arnoldiana

Cornus × arnoldiana, commonly known as the white cornel.

It is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its showy white bracts that appear in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cornus × arnoldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest.
