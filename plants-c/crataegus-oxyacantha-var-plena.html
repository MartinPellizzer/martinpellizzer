Crataegus oxyacantha var. plena

Crataegus oxyacantha var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a highly ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

The variety is valued in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
