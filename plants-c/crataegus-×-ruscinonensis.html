Crataegus × ruscinonensis

Crataegus × ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters. The plant features thorny branches, elliptical to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. Its fruits are red berries that mature in the fall, providing food for various birds and small mammals.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
