Calandrinia cygnorum

Calandrinia cygnorum, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by afternoon. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Calandrinia cygnorum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
