Clematis cirrhosa var. barnadesii

Clematis cirrhosa var. barnadesii, commonly known as the red clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically red or pink, with a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
