Cirsium fani

Cirsium fani, commonly known as the Fanthorn thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant's structure includes a thick, woody stem and a composite flower head made up of many tiny florets.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
