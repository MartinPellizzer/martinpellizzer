Carex brassii

Carex brassii, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex brassii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
