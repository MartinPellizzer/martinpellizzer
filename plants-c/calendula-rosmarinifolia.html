Calendula rosmarinifolia

Calendula rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena calendula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, aromatic leaves with a distinct lemony scent, and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
