Cylindrophyllum calamiforme

Cylindrophyllum calamiforme, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant has a cylindrical stem that becomes woody with age, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in local ecosystems.
