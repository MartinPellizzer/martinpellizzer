Copaifera oblongifolia var. dawsonii

Copaifera oblongifolia var. dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's copaifera, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark contains a resinous sap, which has been traditionally used in folk medicine.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
