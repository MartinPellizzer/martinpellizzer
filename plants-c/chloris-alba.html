Chloris alba

Chloris alba, commonly known as white grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Chloris alba is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
