Chlorophytum elgonense

Chlorophytum elgonense, commonly known as the African Peace Lily, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its leaves and stems, making it drought-tolerant.

Chlorophytum elgonense thrives in bright, indirect light and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
