Calamagrostis nardifolia

Calamagrostis nardifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluejoint, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are typically arranged in dense, clumping growth patterns. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and branched, producing small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Calamagrostis nardifolia plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
