Critesion setifolium

Critesion setifolium, commonly known as the threadleaf critesion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
