Cereus pentlandii var. sanguineus

Cereus pentlandii var. sanguineus, commonly known as the blood cereus, is a species of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and the presence of spines along its surface. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming at night. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal flowering habit.
