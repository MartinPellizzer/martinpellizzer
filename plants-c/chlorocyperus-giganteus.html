Chlorocyperus giganteus

Chlorocyperus giganteus, commonly known as the giant green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features tall, slender stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Chlorocyperus giganteus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments where it thrives in shallow water or damp soil.
