Cassia quarrei

Cassia quarrei, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia quarrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in urban and rural landscapes.
