Cerasus serrulata f. shidaresakura

Cerasus serrulata f. shidaresakura, commonly known as the Shidaresakura, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blooms that appear in early spring. The flowers are white with pink hues, forming dense clusters along the branches. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
