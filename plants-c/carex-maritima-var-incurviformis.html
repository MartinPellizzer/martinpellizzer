Carex maritima var. incurviformis

Carex maritima var. incurviformis, commonly known as the bent-form sea sedge, is a variant of the sea sedge native to coastal regions.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in saline or brackish environments, such as salt marshes and tidal flats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The species is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and periodic flooding.
