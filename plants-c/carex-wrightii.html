Carex wrightii

Carex wrightii, commonly known as Wright's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has triangular stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex wrightii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
