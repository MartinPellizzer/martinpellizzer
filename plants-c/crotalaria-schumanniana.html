Crotalaria schumanniana

Crotalaria schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumacher's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain elliptical, kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.

However, some species within the genus Crotalaria can be toxic to livestock, requiring careful management when grown in agricultural settings.
