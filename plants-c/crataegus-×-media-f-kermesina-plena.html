Crataegus × media f. kermesina-plena

Crataegus × media f. kermesina-plena, commonly known as the double-flowered hybrid hawthorn, is a cultivar characterized by its ornamental value.

It is a hybrid between Crataegus media and Crataegus kermesiana, resulting in a plant with distinctive, double-layered flowers. The botanical features include thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers in spring. It produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but typically not cultivated for consumption.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
