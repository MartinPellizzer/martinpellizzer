Cerasus fruticosa f. typica

Cerasus fruticosa f. typica, commonly known as the common cherry shrub, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red, sweet-tart berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.

The species is relatively hardy and adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
