Citrosma goudotiana

Citrosma goudotiana, commonly known as the Malayan citron tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a small, round citrus-like fruit with a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves and fruit.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
