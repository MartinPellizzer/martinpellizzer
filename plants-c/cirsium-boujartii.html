Cirsium boujartii

Cirsium boujartii, commonly known as the Boujart thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium boujartii is often found in scrublands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
