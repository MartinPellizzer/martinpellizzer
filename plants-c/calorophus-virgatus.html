Calorophus virgatus

Calorophus virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's flame, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dry, sunny habitats. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand hot, arid conditions.

Calorophus virgatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
