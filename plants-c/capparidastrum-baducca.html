Capparidastrum baducca

Capparidastrum baducca, commonly known as the yellow caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.
