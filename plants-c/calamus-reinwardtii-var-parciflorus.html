Calamus reinwardtii var. parciflorus

Calamus reinwardtii var. parciflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and small, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
