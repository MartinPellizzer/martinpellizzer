Crataegus alachuaniformis

Crataegus alachuaniformis, commonly known as the Florida hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and mammals, contributing to local ecosystems.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its role in both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
