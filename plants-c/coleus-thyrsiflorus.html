Coleus thyrsiflorus

Coleus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the flame bamboo or red bamboo, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are often variegated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, appearing in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
