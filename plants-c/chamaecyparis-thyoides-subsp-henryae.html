Chamaecyparis thyoides subsp. henryae

Chamaecyparis thyoides subsp.

henryae, commonly known as the Chinese fir or Chinese white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves that are typically glaucous blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is valued for its aromatic wood and ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the cypress family and is notable for its ecological and horticultural significance.
