Cistanche phelypaea

Cistanche phelypaea, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a fibrous, branching structure. It is often found growing on the stems of other plants such as Ephedra and Amaranthus.

Due to its unique ecological role and medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.
