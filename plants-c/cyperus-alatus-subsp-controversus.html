Cyperus alatus subsp. controversus

Cyperus alatus subsp.

controversus, commonly known as the winged flatsedge, is a member of the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It is often found in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, disturbed soils.

This subspecies is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural or aquatic settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
