Casuarina lehmanniana

Casuarina lehmanniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved she-oak, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, needle-like leaves that are arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, and it is often found in coastal and sandy soils. Casuarina lehmanniana is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species in harsh conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
