Centaurea hololeuca

Centaurea hololeuca, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea hololeuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
