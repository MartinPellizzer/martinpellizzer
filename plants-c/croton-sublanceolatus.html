Croton sublanceolatus

Croton sublanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its striking leaf coloration, it is popular in horticulture for indoor and outdoor decoration.
