Ceropegia kituloana

Ceropegia kituloana, commonly known as the Kitulo moonvine, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often opening at night. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of the Kitulo Basin.
