Crataegus trianthophora

Crataegus trianthophora, commonly known as the three-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five lobes. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
