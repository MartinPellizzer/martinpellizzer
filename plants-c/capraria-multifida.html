Capraria multifida

Capraria multifida, commonly known as the mountain mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Capraria multifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
