Cirsium rhiphaeum var. tetauense

Cirsium rhiphaeum var. tetauense, commonly known as the Tetauense thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
