Celtis obliqua

Celtis obliqua, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Celtis obliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
