Cynanchum paraquense

Cynanchum paraquense, commonly known as Paraguay snake vine, is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
