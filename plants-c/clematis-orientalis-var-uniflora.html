Clematis orientalis var. uniflora

Clematis orientalis var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered oriental clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, woody stems that climb by means of twining stems. The plant produces showy, single flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth and flowering.
