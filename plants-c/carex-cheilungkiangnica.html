Carex cheilungkiangnica

Carex cheilungkiangnica, commonly known as the Korean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex cheilungkiangnica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
