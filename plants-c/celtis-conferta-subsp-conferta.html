Celtis conferta subsp. conferta

Celtis conferta subsp.

conferta, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia. It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by spherical, smooth fruits that turn red when mature. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
