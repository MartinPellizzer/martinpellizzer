Clerodendrum fuscum var. lanceolatum

Clerodendrum fuscum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
