Cogswellia elliptica

Cogswellia elliptica, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, pale green leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the cooler months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
