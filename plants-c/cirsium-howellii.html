Cirsium howellii

Cirsium howellii, commonly known as Howell's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features a robust, spiny stem and large, silver-green, lobed leaves. Its showy purple flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium howellii is characterized by its dense, prickly foliage and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
