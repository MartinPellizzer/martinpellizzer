Coptis japonica var. japonica

Coptis japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The plant contains berberine, a compound with medicinal properties used in traditional herbal medicine.
