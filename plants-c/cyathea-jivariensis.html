Cyathea jivariensis

Cyathea jivariensis, commonly known as the Jivari fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.

Cyathea jivariensis plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
