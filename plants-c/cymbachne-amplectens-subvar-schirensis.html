Cymbachne amplectens subvar. schirensis

Cymbachne amplectens subvar. schirensis, commonly known as the Schirensis grass, is a perennial herb native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
