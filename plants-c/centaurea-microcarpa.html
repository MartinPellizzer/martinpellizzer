Centaurea microcarpa

Centaurea microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with erect, branched stems and small, bright blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, globular seed heads that are characteristic of the genus Centaurea. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
