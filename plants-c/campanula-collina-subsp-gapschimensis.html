Campanula collina subsp. gapschimensis

Campanula collina subsp.

gapschimensis, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, which bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
