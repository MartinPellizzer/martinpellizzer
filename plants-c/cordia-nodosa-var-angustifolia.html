Cordia nodosa var. angustifolia

Cordia nodosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
