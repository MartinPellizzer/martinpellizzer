Clematis × pseudococcinea

Clematis × pseudococcinea, commonly known as the red-flowered clematis, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and clusters of showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × pseudococcinea is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental vine with striking floral display.
