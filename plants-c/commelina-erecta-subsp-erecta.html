Commelina erecta subsp. erecta

Commelina erecta subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the upright spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features slender, upright stems that often bear clusters of small, blue or violet flowers.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
