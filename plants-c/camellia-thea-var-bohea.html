Camellia thea var. bohea

Camellia thea var. bohea, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall. The plant produces dark green, glossy leaves and camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its most notable botanical feature is the presence of caffeine and other alkaloids in its leaves, which are harvested and processed to make various types of tea.

The plant thrives in humid, subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
