Crocus striatulus

Crocus striatulus, commonly known as the spotted crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers with distinctive purple or brown markings. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in dry conditions, allowing it to thrive in Mediterranean climates.

Crocus striatulus blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.
