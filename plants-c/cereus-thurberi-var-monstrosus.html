Cereus thurberi var. monstrosus

Cereus thurberi var. monstrosus, commonly known as the Thorny Moon Cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and blooming habit make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
