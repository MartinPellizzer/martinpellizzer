Cichorium intybus var. foliosum

Cichorium intybus var. foliosum, commonly known as chicory, is a hardy perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves with a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used to make coffee substitutes, while the leaves are consumed raw or cooked.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
