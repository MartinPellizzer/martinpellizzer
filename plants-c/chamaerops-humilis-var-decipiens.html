Chamaerops humilis var. decipiens

Chamaerops humilis var. decipiens, commonly known as the false date palm, is a variety of the dwarf date palm native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its clustering growth habit and stiff, gray-green leaves that are fan-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
