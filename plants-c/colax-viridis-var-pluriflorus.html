Colax viridis var. pluriflorus

Colax viridis var. pluriflorus, commonly known as the Green Colax with Many Flowers, is a flowering plant characterized by its vibrant green foliage and profuse blooms.

It typically features elongated leaves that are smooth and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to its clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with adventitious roots, supporting its growth in both natural and garden environments.
