Chrysanthemum tanacetum

Chrysanthemum tanacetum, commonly known as pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a natural insecticide. Its essential oils, derived from the flowers, contain compounds like pyrethrin, which are effective against pests.

Chrysanthemum tanacetum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
