Clerodendrum sansibarense

Clerodendrum sansibarense, commonly known as the Zanzibar glorybower, is a striking tropical shrub native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting red or purple markings. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, adding to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
