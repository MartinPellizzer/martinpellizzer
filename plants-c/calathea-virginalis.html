Calathea virginalis

Calathea virginalis, commonly known as the White Star Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, white-variegated leaves that resemble stars, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for indoor gardening enthusiasts.
