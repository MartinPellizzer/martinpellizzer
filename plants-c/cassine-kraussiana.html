Cassine kraussiana

Cassine kraussiana, commonly known as the kapok tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elongated leaves and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The tree is notable for its long, silky, fiber-rich seed pods that hang from the branches. Its bark is thick and cork-like, providing a natural insulating layer.

Cassine kraussiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
