Cephalostachyum mindorense

Cephalostachyum mindorense, commonly known as the Mindanao spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
