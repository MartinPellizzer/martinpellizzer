Chlorocrepis fendleri

Chlorocrepis fendleri, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used by indigenous peoples for their durable fibers.
