Chenopodium subspicatum

Chenopodium subspicatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features succulent, deltoid leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a dry, three-angled capsule containing several seeds.

Chenopodium subspicatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
