Condylocarpon laxum

Condylocarpon laxum, commonly known as the common lichen, is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium.

It thrives in a variety of environments, including tree bark, soil, and rock surfaces. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is the body of the lichen, and it lacks true roots, stems, or leaves. It plays a crucial role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in ecosystems.

Its ability to survive in extreme conditions makes it an important indicator species for environmental health.
