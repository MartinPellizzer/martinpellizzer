Carex aurea var. androgyna

Carex aurea var. androgyna, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact clumps. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
