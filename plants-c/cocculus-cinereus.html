Cocculus cinereus

Cocculus cinereus, commonly known as the gray cocculus, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spiny, pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Cocculus cinereus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.
