Chloranthus ilicifolius

Chloranthus ilicifolius, commonly known as the sweetleaf or sweetbriar, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a small shrub characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves that are often described as having a sweet, pleasant scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
