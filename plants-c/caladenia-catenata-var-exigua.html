Caladenia catenata var. exigua

Caladenia catenata var. exigua, commonly known as the slender spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape that resembles a spider, hence its common name.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
