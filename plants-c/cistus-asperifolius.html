Cistus asperifolius

Cistus asperifolius, commonly known as rough-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Cistus asperifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
