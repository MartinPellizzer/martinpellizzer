Celastrus wallichianus

Celastrus wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan bindweed, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant bears capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its woody stems and persistent foliage make it a notable species in its native habitat.
