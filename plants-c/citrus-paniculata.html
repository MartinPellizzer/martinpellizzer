Citrus paniculata

Citrus paniculata, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, acidic pulp and a high concentration of essential oils. Its citrus scent is strong, and it is often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
