Cladium ensigerum

Cladium ensigerum, commonly known as the spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spike. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist to aquatic habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cladium ensigerum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
