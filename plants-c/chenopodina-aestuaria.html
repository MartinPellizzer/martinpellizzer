Chenopodina aestuaria

Chenopodina aestuaria, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, marshes, and salt lakes, where it tolerates high salinity levels. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces edible seeds that are used in some traditional cuisines.

Chenopodina aestuaria plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in saline habitats.
