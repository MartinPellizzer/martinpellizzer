Commelina jacobii

Commelina jacobii, commonly known as Jacob's spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy inflorescences. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, blue or white flowers surrounded by three lance-shaped bracts. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Commelina jacobii typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.
