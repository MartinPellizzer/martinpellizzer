Clitoria calcarigera

Clitoria calcarigera, commonly known as the mountain blue pea, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
