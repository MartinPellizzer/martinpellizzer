Camelina hispida var. lasiocarpa

Camelina hispida var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the rough-hairy camelina, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in desert environments.

Camelina hispida var. lasiocarpa is also known for its potential as a drought-tolerant forage crop.
