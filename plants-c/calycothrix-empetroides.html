Calycothrix empetroides

Calycothrix empetroides, commonly known as the creeping goldenweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture. Calycothrix empetroides thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
