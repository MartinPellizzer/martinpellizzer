Citrus × sinensis var. plumosa

Citrus × sinensis var. plumosa, commonly known as the pomelo or sometimes referred to as the Chinese grapefruit, is a cultivar of the citrus genus.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a thick, leathery rind and a segmented, juicy interior. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with thick, waxy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, slightly tart flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
