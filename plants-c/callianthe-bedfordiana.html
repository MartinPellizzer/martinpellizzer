Callianthe bedfordiana

Callianthe bedfordiana, commonly known as the Bedford's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high altitude and cool temperatures. Its flowers have a unique structure with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
