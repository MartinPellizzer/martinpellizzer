Chionanthus spicifer

Chionanthus spicifer, commonly known as the spicate chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Chionanthus spicifer is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas.
