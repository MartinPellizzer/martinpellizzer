Carex capillaris

Carex capillaris, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

Carex capillaris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
