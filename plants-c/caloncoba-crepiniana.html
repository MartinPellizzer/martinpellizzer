Caloncoba crepiniana

Caloncoba crepiniana, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features a compact rosette of dark green, velvety leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically arranged in a spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Caloncoba crepiniana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
