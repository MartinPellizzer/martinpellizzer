Cleome pichinchensis

Cleome pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha cleome, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and branching.

Cleome pichinchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
