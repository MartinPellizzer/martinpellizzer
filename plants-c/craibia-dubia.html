Craibia dubia

Craibia dubia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise ghostly appearance. Craibia dubia is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves to survive periods of drought.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a sought-after plant among collectors and botanists alike.
