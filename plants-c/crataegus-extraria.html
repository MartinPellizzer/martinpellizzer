Crataegus extraria

Crataegus extraria, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
