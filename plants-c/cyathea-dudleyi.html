Cyathea dudleyi

Cyathea dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Cyathea dudleyi plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
