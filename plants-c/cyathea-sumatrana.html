Cyathea sumatrana

Cyathea sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its rhizomes produce roots that anchor it to the soil and absorb nutrients, while its fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea sumatrana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
