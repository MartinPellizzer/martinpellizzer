Cerasus × takinoensis

Cerasus × takinoensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a hybrid species of the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is renowned for its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn a vibrant green during the growing season.
