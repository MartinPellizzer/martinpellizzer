Chamaesciadium occidentalium

Chamaesciadium occidentalium, commonly known as western chamaesciadium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
