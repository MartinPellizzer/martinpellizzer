Croixia hispida

Croixia hispida, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy textures, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
