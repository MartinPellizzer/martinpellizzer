Crataegus × macrocarpa nothosubsp. baranecii

Crataegus × macrocarpa nothosubsp.

baranecii, commonly known as the Baranec's hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus. It is characterized by its small, bright red berries and thorny branches, typical of many hawthorn species.

The plant is native to regions of Europe and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations that can make identification challenging.
