Campanula rotundifolia var. bulgarica

Campanula rotundifolia var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
