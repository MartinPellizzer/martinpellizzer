Campuloclinium barrosoana

Campuloclinium barrosoana, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and usually have a yellow or greenish color.

It serves as a host plant for certain butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
