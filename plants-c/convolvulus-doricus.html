Convolvulus doricus

Convolvulus doricus, commonly known as the Doric bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, pale purple to white flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or spread along the ground. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
