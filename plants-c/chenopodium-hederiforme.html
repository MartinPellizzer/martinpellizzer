Chenopodium hederiforme

Chenopodium hederiforme, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the amaranth family (Amaranthaceae).

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as bloom. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that cluster in dense, terminal spikes. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

Chenopodium hederiforme is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in various traditional cuisines.
