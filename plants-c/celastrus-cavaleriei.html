Celastrus cavaleriei

Celastrus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
