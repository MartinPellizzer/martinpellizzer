Castilla fallax

Castilla fallax, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright blue berries in the fall, which are a notable feature for both ornamental and ecological purposes. Castilla fallax thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
