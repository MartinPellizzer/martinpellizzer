Chamaecyparis forsteckiensis

Chamaecyparis forsteckiensis, commonly known as the Forsteck's cypress, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is typically found in cool, moist habitats. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for studies on plant adaptation and conservation.
