Cyperus cyperoides var. microstachys

Cyperus cyperoides var. microstachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct basal rosette structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, clustered flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
