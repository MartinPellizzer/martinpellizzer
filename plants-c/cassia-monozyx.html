Cassia monozyx

Cassia monozyx, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces long, narrow, leathery leaves and has a fibrous, aromatic bark. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Cassia monozyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.
