Carex atrata var. atrosquama

Carex atrata var. atrosquama, commonly known as black-squamate sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in wetland habitats.
