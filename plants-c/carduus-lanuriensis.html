Carduus lanuriensis

Carduus lanuriensis, commonly known as the Lanuriense thistle, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, flowering stem that bears purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a distinctive, woolly appearance on its stems and leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
