Cytisus capitatus subsp. verus

Cytisus capitatus subsp.

verus, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
