Ceropegia gemmifera

Ceropegia gemmifera, commonly known as the gemmiferous ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions and requires well-draining soil.

Ceropegia gemmifera is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
