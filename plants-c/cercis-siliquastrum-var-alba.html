Cercis siliquastrum var. alba

Cercis siliquastrum var. alba, commonly known as the white Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by long, bean-like pods. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its showy blooms and distinctive foliage.
