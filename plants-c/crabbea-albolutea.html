Crabbea albolutea

Crabbea albolutea, commonly known as the white-flowered cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces slender, upright stems. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive cranesbill shape, giving the genus its name.

Crabbea albolutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
