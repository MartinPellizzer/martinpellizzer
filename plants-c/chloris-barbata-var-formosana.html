Chloris barbata var. formosana

Chloris barbata var. formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, drought-tolerant ground cover.
