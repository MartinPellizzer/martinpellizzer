Carpinus pendula

Carpinus pendula, commonly known as weeping hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive weeping habit, with long, pendulous branches that droop gracefully. The leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring followed by edible, winged fruits.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
