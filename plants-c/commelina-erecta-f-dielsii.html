Commelina erecta f. dielsii

Commelina erecta f. dielsii, commonly known as the upright commelina, is a variant of the commelina genus native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and striking blue-violet bracts that surround the small, white flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
