Clerodendrum trichotomum var. villosum

Clerodendrum trichotomum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a woody stem with a dense covering of fine, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
