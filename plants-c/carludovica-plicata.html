Carludovica plicata

Carludovica plicata, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes mountains. The plant is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to two meters in diameter. Its striking white or pink flowers are surrounded by a spathe and spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Carludovica plicata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Andean medicine for centuries.
