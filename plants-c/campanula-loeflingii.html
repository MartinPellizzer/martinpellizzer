Campanula loeflingii

Campanula loeflingii, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula loeflingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
