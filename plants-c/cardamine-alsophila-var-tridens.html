Cardamine alsophila var. tridens

Cardamine alsophila var. tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three deeply lobed leaflets, and its small white flowers are arranged in a loose cluster. It has a fibrous root system and produces aquatic-like leaves that float on the water surface.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
