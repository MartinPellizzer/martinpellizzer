Cinnamomum heterantherum

Cinnamomum heterantherum, commonly known as the Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
