Centaurea pauciflora

Centaurea pauciflora, commonly known as the sparseflower cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Centaurea pauciflora is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
