Cineraria purpurata

Cineraria purpurata, commonly known as the purple cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cineraria purpurata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its beauty and resilience.
