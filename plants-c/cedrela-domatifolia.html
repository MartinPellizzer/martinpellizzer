Cedrela domatifolia

Cedrela domatifolia, commonly known as the Cuban mahogany or yellow cedar, is a tropical tree belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, Central America, and northern South America, typically growing in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often aromatic bark and its long, narrow leaves that are usually alternate. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody fruits containing winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which has been historically used in furniture and construction.
