Cassia alcaparra

Cassia alcaparra, commonly known as Spanish cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. Cassia alcaparra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and a dense foliage structure.
