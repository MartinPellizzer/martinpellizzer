Crotalaria dissitiflora subsp. rugosa

Crotalaria dissitiflora subsp.

rugosa, commonly known as the rough hairy sida, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties. Crotalaria dissitiflora subsp.

rugosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.
