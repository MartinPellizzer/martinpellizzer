Cassia tabascoensis

Cassia tabascoensis, commonly known as the Mexican cassia, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia tabascoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
