Cenia villosa

Cenia villosa, commonly known as the hairy cenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cenia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
