Cerastium tetrandrum var. zetlandicum

Cerastium tetrandrum var. zetlandicum, commonly known as Zetland mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitat.
