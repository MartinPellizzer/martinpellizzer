Clematopsis villosa

Clematopsis villosa, commonly known as the hairy clematopsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
