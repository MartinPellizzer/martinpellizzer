Calliandra haematocephala

Calliandra haematocephala, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes, which are composed of numerous small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calliandra haematocephala is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
