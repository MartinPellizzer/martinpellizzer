Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. montanum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. montanum, commonly known as the alpine white chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
