Carex wiluica

Carex wiluica, commonly known as the Wiluic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to semi-aquatic conditions.

Carex wiluica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
