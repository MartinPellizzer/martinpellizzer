Corymbia deserticola subsp. mesogeotica

Corymbia deserticola subsp.

mesogeotica, commonly known as the Mesogeotica bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often displays a reddish or brownish hue.

The leaves are narrow, leathery, and typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces small, creamy-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, cylindrical fruit capsules.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
