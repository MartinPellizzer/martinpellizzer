Cyperus compressus var. brachiatus

Cyperus compressus var. brachiatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairless or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
