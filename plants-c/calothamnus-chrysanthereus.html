Calothamnus chrysanthereus

Calothamnus chrysanthereus, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that aids in moisture retention.

Calothamnus chrysanthereus is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
