Calla ambigua

Calla ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous calla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf known as a spathe. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, while its flowers are striking with a white spathe and a yellow spadix.

Calla ambigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
