Campanula saxifragoides f. latifolia

Campanula saxifragoides f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a grayish-green hue. The plant produces showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to alpine garden conditions.
