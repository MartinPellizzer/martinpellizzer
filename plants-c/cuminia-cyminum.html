Cuminia cyminum

Cuminia cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by the distinctive three-lobed, seed-bearing fruits. Cumin is valued for its pungent, warm aroma and is widely used as a spice in various cuisines around the world.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their digestive and antimicrobial properties.
