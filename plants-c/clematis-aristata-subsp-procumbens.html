Clematis aristata subsp. procumbens

Clematis aristata subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a flowering vine native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail along structures.
