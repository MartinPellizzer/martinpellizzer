Cheirostylis sherriffii

Cheirostylis sherriffii, commonly known as the Sherriff's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with a distinctive spurred lip.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
