Cyclamen graecum subsp. candicum

Cyclamen graecum subsp.

candicum, commonly known as the Greek cyclamen, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for winter gardens. It prefers cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil, often thriving in shaded or semi-shaded areas. Cyclamen graecum subsp.

candicum is often cultivated as a decorative plant for indoor or outdoor use.
