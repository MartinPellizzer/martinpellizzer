Cucurbita mixta var. cyanoperizona

Cucurbita mixta var. cyanoperizona, commonly known as the blue-striped gourd, is a variety of the species Cucurbita mixta.

It is characterized by its distinct blue-green stripes on the outer surface of the fruit, which contrast with the pale yellow or white background. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and pumpkins. It produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, typical of many cucurbit species.
