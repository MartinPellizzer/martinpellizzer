Commersonia salviifolia

Commersonia salviifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf commersonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
