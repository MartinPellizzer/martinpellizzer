Carex fascicularis var. minor

Carex fascicularis var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like structure bearing small, three-stamened flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
