Canscora tetraptera

Canscora tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by insects. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Canscora tetraptera is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
