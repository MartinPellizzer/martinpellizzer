Centaurea jacea subsp. transalpina

Centaurea jacea subsp.

transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer, and its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and produces achenes as its seeds.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
