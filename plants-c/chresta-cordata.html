Chresta cordata

Chresta cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arum, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
