Cestrum valerioi

Cestrum valerioi, commonly known as the sweet shrub or night bloomer, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
