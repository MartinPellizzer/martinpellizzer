Cestrum fasciculiflorum

Cestrum fasciculiflorum, commonly known as the clustered-flower cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, spicy flavor.

Cestrum fasciculiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
