Capsella bursa-pastoris helsingica

Capsella bursa-pastoris helsingica, commonly known as the common chickweed or mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright stems, small white flowers, and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions across Europe and Asia.
