Centaurea rigidifolia

Centaurea rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and blue to purple flowering heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea rigidifolia is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
