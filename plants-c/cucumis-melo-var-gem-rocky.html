Cucumis melo var. gem-rocky

Cucumis melo var. gem-rocky, commonly known as the rockmelon, is a variant of the melon species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy skin that ranges in color from pale green to yellowish-orange. The fruit typically has a firm, juicy interior with a sweet and sometimes slightly tart flavor. It is cultivated for its edible flesh, which is often used in fresh consumption or processed into juices.

This variety is well-suited for growing in well-drained soil and requires a long growing season with ample sunlight.
