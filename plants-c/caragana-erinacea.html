Caragana erinacea

Caragana erinacea, commonly known as goat's beard, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and feathery, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by distinctive, bead-like seed pods. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils and tolerating drought conditions.

Caragana erinacea is often used in erosion control and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
