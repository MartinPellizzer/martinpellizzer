Cereus tetragonus var. major

Cereus tetragonus var. major, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
