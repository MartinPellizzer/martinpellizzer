Cirsium segetum f. lactiflorum

Cirsium segetum f. lactiflorum, commonly known as the milkflower thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust stems and large, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields.
