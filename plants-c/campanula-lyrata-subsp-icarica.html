Campanula lyrata subsp. icarica

Campanula lyrata subsp.

icarica, commonly known as the Icaria bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are upright with a tendency to branch.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
