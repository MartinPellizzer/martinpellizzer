Chrysothamnus leiospermus

Chrysothamnus leiospermus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-green foliage, which help it thrive in arid environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow blooms in the spring. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
