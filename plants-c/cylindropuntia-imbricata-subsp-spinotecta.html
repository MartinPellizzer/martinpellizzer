Cylindropuntia imbricata subsp. spinotecta

Cylindropuntia imbricata subsp.

spinotecta, commonly known as the silver leaf cholla, is a spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming dense clusters that provide habitat for various desert animals.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
