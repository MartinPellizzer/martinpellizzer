Celosia pumila

Celosia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, flame-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, Celosia pumila is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in both ornamental and horticultural settings.
