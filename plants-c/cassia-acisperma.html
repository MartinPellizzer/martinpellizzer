Cassia acisperma

Cassia acisperma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that turn brown when mature.

Cassia acisperma is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
