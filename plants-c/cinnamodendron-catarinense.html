Cinnamodendron catarinense

Cinnamodendron catarinense, commonly known as the Santa Catarina cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil, particularly in the state of Santa Catarina. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its fragrant properties and is used in traditional crafts and incense.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense, evergreen forests.
