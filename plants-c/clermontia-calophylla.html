Clermontia calophylla

Clermontia calophylla, commonly known as the Haleakalā silversword, is a rare and unique plant native to the Hawaiian island of Maui.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, fleshy leaves and a tall, spire-like stem that bears a single flower. The plant thrives in the high-elevation volcanic soils of Haleakalā National Park, where it is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and threats from habitat loss, it is listed as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
