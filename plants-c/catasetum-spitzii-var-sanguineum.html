Catasetum spitzii var. sanguineum

Catasetum spitzii var. sanguineum, commonly known as the Crimson Catasetum, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features showy, pendulous flowers with a distinctive red and orange coloration, often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its compact and elegant appearance.

The species is prized in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and relatively manageable care requirements.
