Croton inaequilobus

Croton inaequilobus, commonly known as the unequal-lobed croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in shape and size. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
