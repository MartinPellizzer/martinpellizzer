Chaetospora distachya

Chaetospora distachya, commonly known as the slender spore moss, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and the presence of leafy shoots that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, stalked spore capsules that are clustered in pairs, a feature that gives it its specific epithet "distachya.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices, where it forms dense mats.

Its simple morphology and reproductive structures make it an important species for studying liverwort ecology and evolution.
