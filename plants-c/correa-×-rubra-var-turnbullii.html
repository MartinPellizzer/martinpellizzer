Correa × rubra var. turnbullii

Correa × rubra var. turnbullii, commonly known as the Turnbull's correa, is a hybrid shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
