Commelina papilionacea

Commelina papilionacea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white, tubular flowers that are often surrounded by showy bracts. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina papilionacea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed.
