Cytisus purpureus f. pendulus

Cytisus purpureus f. pendulus, commonly known as the weeping broom, is a compact shrub characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green in color and remain throughout the growing season. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
