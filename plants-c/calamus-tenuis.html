Calamus tenuis

Calamus tenuis, commonly known as slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
