Crataegus fulleriana var. gigantea

Crataegus fulleriana var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
