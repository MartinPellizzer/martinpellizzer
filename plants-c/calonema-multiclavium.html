Calonema multiclavium

Calonema multiclavium, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
