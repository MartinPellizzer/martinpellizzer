Calanthe discolor f. quinquelamellata

Calanthe discolor f. quinquelamellata, commonly known as the five-lobed calanthe, is a rare and ornamental orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white and pink floral display, with petals that have a distinctive five-lobed structure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves arranged in a compact rosette, and its flowers are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
