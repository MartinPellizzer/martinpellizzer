Clerodendrum macrosiphon

Clerodendrum macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
