Cissus formosa

Cissus formosa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by heart-shaped, edible berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a ornamental plant.

Cissus formosa is also noted for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
