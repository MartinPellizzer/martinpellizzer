Centranthus pinnatifidus

Centranthus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy pink to red flowers. The leaves are pinnately divided, giving the plant its scientific name, and are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
