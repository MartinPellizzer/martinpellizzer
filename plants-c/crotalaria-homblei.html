Crotalaria homblei

Crotalaria homblei, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the genus Crotalaria. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, sunny environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in sustainable farming practices.
