Chionanthus fragrans

Chionanthus fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are borne in clusters and emit a sweet, spicy scent, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Chionanthus fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
