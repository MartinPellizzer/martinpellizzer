Castanopsis tribuloides var. formosana

Castanopsis tribuloides var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen leaves and produces edible, chestnut-like nuts enclosed in spiny capsules. This variety is valued for its ecological role and historical use in traditional practices.
