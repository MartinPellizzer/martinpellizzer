Commelinopsis persicariifolia

Commelinopsis persicariifolia, commonly known as the purple-flowered plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features upright, branching stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. These flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
