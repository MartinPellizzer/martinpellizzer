Cassia planifolia

Cassia planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and contain numerous flat, oval seeds.

Cassia planifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
