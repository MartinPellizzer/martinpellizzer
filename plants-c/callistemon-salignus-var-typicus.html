Callistemon salignus var. typicus

Callistemon salignus var. typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and garden environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
