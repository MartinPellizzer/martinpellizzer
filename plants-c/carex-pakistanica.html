Carex pakistanica

Carex pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or alpine environments. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often found in elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.

Carex pakistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous habitats.
