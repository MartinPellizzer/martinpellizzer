Cyperus makinoi

Cyperus makinoi, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including Japan, China, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus makinoi is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
