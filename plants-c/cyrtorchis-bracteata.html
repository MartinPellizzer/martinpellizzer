Cyrtorchis bracteata

Cyrtorchis bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of bracts, which are modified leaves that often resemble petals. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
