Cinnagrostis minima

Cinnagrostis minima, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Cinnagrostis minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
