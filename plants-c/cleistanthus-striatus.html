Cleistanthus striatus

Cleistanthus striatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cleistanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered, and have a distinctive yellowish-green color with a faintly fragrant scent.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds when mature.
