Croton martii var. latifolius

Croton martii var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
