Carex firmicaulis

Carex firmicaulis, commonly known as the firm-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender stems. Carex firmicaulis prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
