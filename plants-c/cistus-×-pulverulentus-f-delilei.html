Cistus × pulverulentus f. delilei

Cistus × pulverulentus f. delilei, commonly known as the Delile's rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of gray-green, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
