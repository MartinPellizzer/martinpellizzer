Carum papillare

Carum papillare, commonly known as the hairy caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its common name.

Carum papillare is valued for its essential oils, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
