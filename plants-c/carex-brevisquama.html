Carex brevisquama

Carex brevisquama, commonly known as the short-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex brevisquama plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
