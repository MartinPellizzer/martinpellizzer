Cyperus usitatus

Cyperus usitatus, commonly known as the common flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Cyperus usitatus thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in floodplains and along waterways.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
