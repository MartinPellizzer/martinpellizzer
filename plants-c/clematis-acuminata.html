Clematis acuminata

Clematis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Clematis acuminata is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
