Ceratoschoenus inundatus

Ceratoschoenus inundatus, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Ceratoschoenus, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to the water. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the early morning.

The species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting various aquatic life forms.
