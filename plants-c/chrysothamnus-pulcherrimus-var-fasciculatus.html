Chrysothamnus pulcherrimus var. fasciculatus

Chrysothamnus pulcherrimus var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety fasciculatus is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Chrysothamnus.
