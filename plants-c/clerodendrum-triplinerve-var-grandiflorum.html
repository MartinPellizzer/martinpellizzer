Clerodendrum triplinerve var. grandiflorum

Clerodendrum triplinerve var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with three prominent veins, giving it the species name *triplinerve*. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. These flowers are typically borne on long, slender inflorescences, making the plant visually striking.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
