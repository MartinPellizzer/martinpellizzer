Chamaemelum triumfettii

Chamaemelum triumfettii, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in herbal teas due to their pleasant scent.

Chamaemelum triumfettii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.
