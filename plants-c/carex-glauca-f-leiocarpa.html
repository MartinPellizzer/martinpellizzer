Carex glauca f. leiocarpa

Carex glauca f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved blue sedge, is a variant of the blue sedge species native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow, slightly curved, and have a bluish-green hue, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
