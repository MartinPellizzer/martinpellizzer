Cousinia gilesii

Cousinia gilesii, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often at high elevations. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Cousinia gilesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
