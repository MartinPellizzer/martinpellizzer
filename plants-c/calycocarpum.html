Calycocarpum

Calycocarpum, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The fruit is a small, round, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its edible fruit in traditional Chinese horticulture.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and agricultural study.
