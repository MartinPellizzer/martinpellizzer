Crataegus horrida var. piperi

Crataegus horrida var. piperi, commonly known as the rough crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
