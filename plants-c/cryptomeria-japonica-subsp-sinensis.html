Cryptomeria japonica subsp. sinensis

Cryptomeria japonica subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China. It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown.

The leaves are scale-like and closely pressed to the branches, giving the tree a smooth, dark green appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
