Caelestina urticifolia

Caelestina urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves that release irritant chemicals when touched. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
