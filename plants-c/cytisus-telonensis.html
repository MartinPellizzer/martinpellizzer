Cytisus telonensis

Cytisus telonensis, commonly known as the Greek broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its slender, woody stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cytisus telonensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
