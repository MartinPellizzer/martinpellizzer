Cassia comosa var. lanata

Cassia comosa var. lanata, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of Cassia comosa var. lanata are encased in long, slender pods and are used in traditional medicine in some regions.
