Cirsium cernuum

Cirsium cernuum, commonly known as the nodding thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, and the plant is known for its distinctive, drooping flower heads.

Cirsium cernuum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
