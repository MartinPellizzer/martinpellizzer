Creusa revolvens var. harveyi

Creusa revolvens var. harveyi, commonly known as the Harvey's crepuscularia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in shaded gardens.
