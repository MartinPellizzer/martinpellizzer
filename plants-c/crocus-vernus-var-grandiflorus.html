Crocus vernus var. grandiflorus

Crocus vernus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant crocus, is a flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a corm as its storage organ. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically short-lived but can be quite striking in garden settings.
