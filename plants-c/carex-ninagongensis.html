Carex ninagongensis

Carex ninagongensis, commonly known as the Ninagong sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex ninagongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
