Cattleya rubescens

Cattleya rubescens, commonly known as the red lady orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a contrasting white lip, which make it visually striking. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their strong, sweet fragrance.

Cattleya rubescens is prized in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
