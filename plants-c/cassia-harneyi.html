Cassia harneyi

Cassia harneyi, commonly known as Harney's cassia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, white powder. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Cassia harneyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
