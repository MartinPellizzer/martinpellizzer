Commelina polygama

Commelina polygama, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, often swampy habitats and is often found near water bodies. It has fibrous roots and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Commelina polygama is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
