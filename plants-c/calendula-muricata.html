Calendula muricata

Calendula muricata, commonly known as the rough calendula, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disk. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
