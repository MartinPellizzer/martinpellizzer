Cyperus alatus var. serratus

Cyperus alatus var. serratus, commonly known as the saw-toothed flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features slender, triangular stems with prominent, serrated leaf margins that give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-parted flowers.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
