Crocus vernus var. albiflorus

Crocus vernus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas or garden settings. It is closely related to other crocus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by bees and other early-season insects.
