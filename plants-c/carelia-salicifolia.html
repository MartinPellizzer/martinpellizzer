Carelia salicifolia

Carelia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf carelia, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Carelia salicifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
