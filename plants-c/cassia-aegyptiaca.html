Cassia aegyptiaca

Cassia aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous flat, elliptical seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Cassia aegyptiaca is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
