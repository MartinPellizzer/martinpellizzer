Casearia costulata

Casearia costulata, commonly known as the African snowball or whitebeard tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
