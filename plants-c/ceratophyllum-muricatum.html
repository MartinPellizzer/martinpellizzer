Ceratophyllum muricatum

Ceratophyllum muricatum, commonly known as the spiked watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in South America.

It has slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves that are often mistaken for roots. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. It plays a vital role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
