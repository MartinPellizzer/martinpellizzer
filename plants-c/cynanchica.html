Cynanchica

Cynanchica, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and showy, milky sap-filled flowers. The plant is native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Many species within this genus are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

However, some parts of the plant contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
