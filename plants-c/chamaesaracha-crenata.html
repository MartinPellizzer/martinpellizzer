Chamaesaracha crenata

Chamaesaracha crenata, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with dark green, ovate leaves that have a slightly wavy edge. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
