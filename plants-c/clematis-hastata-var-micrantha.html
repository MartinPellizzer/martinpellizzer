Clematis hastata var. micrantha

Clematis hastata var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 feet, supporting its climbing habit. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three ovate to elliptic leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Clematis hastata var. micrantha thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
