Calamagrostis heterophylla var. pubescens

Calamagrostis heterophylla var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers in the summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
