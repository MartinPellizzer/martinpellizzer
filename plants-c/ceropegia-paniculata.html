Ceropegia paniculata

Ceropegia paniculata, commonly known as the string of hearts or heartleaf ceropegia, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, with a unique structure that resembles a tube or hood. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
