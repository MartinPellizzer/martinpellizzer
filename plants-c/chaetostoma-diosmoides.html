Chaetostoma diosmoides

Chaetostoma diosmoides, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to yellow spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often surrounded by a ring of bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
