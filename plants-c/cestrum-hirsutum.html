Cestrum hirsutum

Cestrum hirsutum, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough, hairy texture.
