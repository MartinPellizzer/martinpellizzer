Carpobrotus fourcadei var. alba

Carpobrotus fourcadei var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, prostrate stems and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
