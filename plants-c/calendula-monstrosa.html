Calendula monstrosa

Calendula monstrosa, commonly known as the giant marguerite, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows to heights of up to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and serrated, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula monstrosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
