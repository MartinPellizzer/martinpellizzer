Cystopteris bulbifera f. flagelliformis

Cystopteris bulbifera f. flagelliformis, commonly known as the flagelliform fern, is a variant of the bulbiferous water fern.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble flowing filaments, giving it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including the bulbiferous spore cases, are notable features that contribute to its botanical significance.

This fern is often appreciated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a specimen plant.
