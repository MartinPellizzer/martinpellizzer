Ceropegia occulta

Ceropegia occulta, commonly known as the "string of hearts" or "heartleaf," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia occulta is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
