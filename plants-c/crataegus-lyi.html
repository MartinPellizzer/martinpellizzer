Crataegus lyi

Crataegus lyi, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Crataegus lyi typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
