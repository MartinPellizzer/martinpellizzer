Cyathea matthewii

Cyathea matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea matthewii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
