Carex caryophyllata

Carex caryophyllata, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems that often display a reddish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex caryophyllata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
