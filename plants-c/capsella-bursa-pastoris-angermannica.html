Capsella bursa-pastoris angermannica

Capsella bursa-pastoris angermannica, commonly known as the common chickweed or mouse ear.

It is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pink flowers with four petals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a pioneer species and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
