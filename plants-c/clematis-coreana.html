Clematis coreana

Clematis coreana, commonly known as the Korean Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Korea.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.

Clematis coreana is also noted for its ability to climb and twine around supports, making it a versatile addition to landscaping.
