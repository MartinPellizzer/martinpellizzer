Casabitoa perfae

Casabitoa perfae, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that hang in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a woody vine structure that can climb or trail. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Casabitoa perfae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
