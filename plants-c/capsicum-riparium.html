Capsicum riparium

Capsicum riparium, commonly known as the water pepper or swamp pepper, is a species of chili pepper native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous plant with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, elongated fruits. Its botanical characteristics are adapted to thrive in moist, swampy environments.

Capsicum riparium is notable for its unique ecological niche and is often found growing along the edges of ponds and wetlands.
