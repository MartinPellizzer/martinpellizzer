Chamaecrista horizontalis

Chamaecrista horizontalis, commonly known as the creeping coralbean, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Chamaecrista horizontalis is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
