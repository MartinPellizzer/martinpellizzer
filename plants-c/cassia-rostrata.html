Cassia rostrata

Cassia rostrata, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia rostrata thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
