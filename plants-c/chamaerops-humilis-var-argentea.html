Chamaerops humilis var. argentea

Chamaerops humilis var. argentea, commonly known as the silver fan palm, is a variant of the dwarf date palm native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its short, clustered growth habit and its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery-blue sheen. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible dates, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean gardens.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
