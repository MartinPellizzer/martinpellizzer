Cinnamomum erythropus var. ovatum

Cinnamomum erythropus var. ovatum, commonly known as the red-cinnamon tree or red cinnamon, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark red to purple bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and ellipsoid berries that contain seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
