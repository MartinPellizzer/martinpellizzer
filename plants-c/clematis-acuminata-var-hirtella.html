Clematis acuminata var. hirtella

Clematis acuminata var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with a hairy texture, which gives it its botanical name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
