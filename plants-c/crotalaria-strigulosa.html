Crotalaria strigulosa

Crotalaria strigulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals.

Crotalaria strigulosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
