Charianthus purpureus var. crinitus

Charianthus purpureus var. crinitus, commonly known as the purple crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink composite flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
