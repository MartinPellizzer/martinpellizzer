Ceropegia clavigera

Ceropegia clavigera, commonly known as the string of hearts or heartleaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically green or yellow with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia clavigera is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
