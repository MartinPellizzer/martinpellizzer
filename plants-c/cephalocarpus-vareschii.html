Cephalocarpus vareschii

Cephalocarpus vareschii, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical fruits that are covered in a soft, hairy layer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
