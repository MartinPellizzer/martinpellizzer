Calamagrostis swallenii

Calamagrostis swallenii, commonly known as the Swallen's reed, is a tall perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features slender, arching blades with a bluish-green hue and produces feathery, golden-brown inflorescences in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Calamagrostis swallenii is also noted for its adaptability to moist conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
