Cyathea manniana

Cyathea manniana, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which are used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants.

Cyathea manniana plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to the forest canopy structure.
