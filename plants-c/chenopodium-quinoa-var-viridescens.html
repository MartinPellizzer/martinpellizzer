Chenopodium quinoa var. viridescens

Chenopodium quinoa var. viridescens, commonly known as green quinoa, is a cultivated variety of the quinoa plant native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its dark green, edible leaves and small, nutrient-rich seeds. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional Andean cuisine, while the seeds are a valuable source of protein and minerals.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional profile, making it an important crop in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
