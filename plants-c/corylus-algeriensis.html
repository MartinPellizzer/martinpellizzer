Corylus algeriensis

Corylus algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian hazelnut, is a species of hazel native to North Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, cup-shaped flowers followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Corylus algeriensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value.
