Cattleya lueddemanniana f. alba

Cattleya lueddemanniana f. alba, commonly known as the White Lueddemanniana Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its elegant white flowers.

This cultivar is characterized by its large, fragrant blooms that often feature a subtle pink or purple tinge at the base. It originates from the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air.

Its botanical appeal lies in its striking appearance and ease of cultivation, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
