Conophytum obscurum subsp. vitreopapillum

Conophytum obscurum subsp.

vitreopapillum, commonly known as the glassy rock pear, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

The leaves are typically green with a translucent or glassy appearance, especially when exposed to sunlight. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found growing in rocky outcrops and sandy soils.
