Chenopodium camphoratifolium

Chenopodium camphoratifolium, commonly known as camphoraceous goosefoot, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Chenopodium camphoratifolium is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
