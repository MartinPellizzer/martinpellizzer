Clinopodium haussknechtii

Clinopodium haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausknecht's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Clinopodium haussknechtii is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal value.
