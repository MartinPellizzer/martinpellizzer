Citrus × sinensis subsp. fetifera

Citrus × sinensis subsp.

fetifera, commonly known as the Pummelo, is a citrus species characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and is considered one of the parent species of the common orange. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves.

Its flowers are fragrant and white, and the fruit can vary in size and color depending on the cultivar. This subspecies is valued for its use in both culinary and horticultural applications.
