Cyclosorus × jerdonii, commonly known as the Jerdon's water fern, is a hybrid species within the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, finger-like segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of spore-producing organs located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyclosorus × jerdonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic or bog gardens.
