Carex × microstachya subsp. gaudiniana

Carex × microstachya subsp.

gaudiniana, commonly known as the Gaudin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
