Chamaepitys suffrutescens

Chamaepitys suffrutescens, commonly known as the mountain mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its woody stems and slow growth rate make it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
