Centaurea abdelkaderi

Centaurea abdelkaderi, commonly known as the Abdelkader's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with prominent yellow centers.

Centaurea abdelkaderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
