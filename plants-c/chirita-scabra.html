Chirita scabra

Chirita scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, stiff hairs, which give it its common name.

Chirita scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
