Convolvulus cordatifolius

Convolvulus cordatifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, sunny environments. It has a climbing habit, using its tendrils to support growth along structures or other plants.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, vining plants.
