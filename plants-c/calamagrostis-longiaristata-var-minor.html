Calamagrostis longiaristata var. minor

Calamagrostis longiaristata var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to purplish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
