Chytraculia tetraptera

Chytraculia tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged chytracul, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized leaf structures.

It possesses four lateral flaps on its leaves, which aid in floating and light absorption. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal via water currents.

Despite its ecological importance, it remains a relatively obscure species with limited study in botanical science.
