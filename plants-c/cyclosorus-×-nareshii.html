Cyclosorus × nareshii

Cyclosorus × nareshii, commonly known as the hybrid water fern, is a rare and fascinating hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyclosorus sibiricus and Cyclosorus macrophyllus.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble those of its parent species, with a distinctive pattern of pinnae that sets it apart. This plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and ponds, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid, offering insights into the genetic diversity and adaptability of aquatic ferns.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
