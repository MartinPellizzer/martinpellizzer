Ceanothus guineensis

Ceanothus guineensis, commonly known as the Guinean ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Ceanothus guineensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small animals.
