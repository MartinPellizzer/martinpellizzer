Cestrum turquinense

Cestrum turquinense, commonly known as the Turquin's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The berries it produces are small, yellow or orange, and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.
