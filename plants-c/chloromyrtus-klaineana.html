Chloromyrtus klaineana

Chloromyrtus klaineana, commonly known as the Australian tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant is evergreen, with lance-shaped foliage that has a slight waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its essential oils, which have various applications in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
