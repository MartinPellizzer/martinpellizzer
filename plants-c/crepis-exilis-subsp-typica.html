Crepis exilis subsp. typica

Crepis exilis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features small, basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces delicate, yellowish flowers in early summer.

The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
