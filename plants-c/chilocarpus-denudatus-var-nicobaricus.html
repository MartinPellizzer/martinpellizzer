Chilocarpus denudatus var. nicobaricus

Chilocarpus denudatus var. nicobaricus, commonly known as the Nicobar sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued for its use in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark, spherical berries.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in the tropical rainforest ecosystems of Southeast Asia.
