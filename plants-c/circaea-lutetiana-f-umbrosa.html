Circaea lutetiana f. umbrosa

Circaea lutetiana f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring rich, loamy soils. The plant's stems are typically prostrate or trailing, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.

The species is part of the Geraniaceae family, known for its diverse floral structures and ecological adaptability.
