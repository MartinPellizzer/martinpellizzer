Catodiacrum luteum

Catodiacrum luteum, commonly known as the yellow cat's whiskers, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental value. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or white, and are clustered around the spadix.

Catodiacrum luteum is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
