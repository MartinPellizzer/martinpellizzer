Chloris myriostachya

Chloris myriostachya, commonly known as the common wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a common species in arid and semi-arid regions.

Chloris myriostachya plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
