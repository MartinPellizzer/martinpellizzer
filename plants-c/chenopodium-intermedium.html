Chenopodium intermedium

Chenopodium intermedium, commonly known as wild spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in late summer. Its seeds are encased in a fleshy, bumpy capsule that splits open when mature.

Chenopodium intermedium is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
