Convolvulus elongatus

Convolvulus elongatus, commonly known as the longleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Convolvulus elongatus is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and rhizomes.
