Coriflora texensis

Coriflora texensis, commonly known as the Texas starflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its star-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Coriflora texensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
