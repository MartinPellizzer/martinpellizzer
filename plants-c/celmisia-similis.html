Celmisia similis

Celmisia similis, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Celmisia similis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is an important component of New Zealand's highland flora.
