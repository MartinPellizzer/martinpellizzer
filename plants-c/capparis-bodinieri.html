Capparis bodinieri

Capparis bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier's caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, sour fruits. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and a woody structure, making it well adapted to tropical and subtropical environments.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
