Chisocheton maxilla-pisticis

Chisocheton maxilla-pisticis, commonly known as the Pisticis tamarind.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its tropical origin and distinctive foliage. The plant typically features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
