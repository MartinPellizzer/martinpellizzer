Carapa obovata

Carapa obovata, commonly known as the obovate mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, obovate leaves that are typically leathery and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local economies.

The plant also has medicinal uses, with some parts used in traditional remedies for their purported health benefits.
