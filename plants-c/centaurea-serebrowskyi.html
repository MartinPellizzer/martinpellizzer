Centaurea serebrowskyi

Centaurea serebrowskyi, commonly known as Serebrowsky's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and bright yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Centaurea serebrowskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
