Cyperus neotropicalis

Cyperus neotropicalis, commonly known as the tropical flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus neotropicalis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
