Cerasus avium f. sphenophylla

Cerasus avium f. sphenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cherry, is a botanical variant of the common cherry.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are distinctly narrower than those of the typical species. This form exhibits a unique leaf morphology, which is a distinguishing feature in botanical classification. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage and flowering characteristics.
