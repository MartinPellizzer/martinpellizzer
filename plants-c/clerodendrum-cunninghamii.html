Clerodendrum cunninghamii

Clerodendrum cunninghamii, commonly known as the Crimson Beauty, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. These flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
