Cephaelis acanthacea

Cephaelis acanthacea, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small flames. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Cephaelis acanthacea is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
