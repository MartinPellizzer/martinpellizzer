Canarium denticulatum var. latifolia

Canarium denticulatum var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its hard, durable wood.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.
