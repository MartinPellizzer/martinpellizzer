Cirsium × urbanum

Cirsium × urbanum, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. It has a robust root system and is often perennial, returning each year.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
