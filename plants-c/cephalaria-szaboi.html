Cephalaria szaboi

Cephalaria szaboi, commonly known as the Hungarian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cephalaria szaboi is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
