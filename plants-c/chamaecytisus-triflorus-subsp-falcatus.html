Chamaecytisus triflorus subsp. falcatus

Chamaecytisus triflorus subsp.

falcatus, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, and the stems are often prostrate, forming low mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
