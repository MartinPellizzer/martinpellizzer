Campanula scheuchzeri var. inconcessa

Campanula scheuchzeri var. inconcessa, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
