Cyperus juncoides

Cyperus juncoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Cyperus juncoides produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are borne on slender, branching stems.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
