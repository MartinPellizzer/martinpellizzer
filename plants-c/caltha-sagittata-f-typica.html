Caltha sagittata f. typica

Caltha sagittata f. typica, commonly known as the arrowhead wintergreen, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
