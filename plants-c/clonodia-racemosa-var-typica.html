Clonodia racemosa var. typica

Clonodia racemosa var. typica, commonly known as the clustered raceme plant, is a flowering species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
