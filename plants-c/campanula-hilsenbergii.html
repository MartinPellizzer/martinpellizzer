Campanula hilsenbergii

Campanula hilsenbergii, commonly known as the Hilsenbeck bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in clumps, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Campanula hilsenbergii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
