Cassia applanata

Cassia applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cassia applanata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
