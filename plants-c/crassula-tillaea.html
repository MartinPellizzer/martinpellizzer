Crassula tillaea

Crassula tillaea, commonly known as the mountain ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid climates.
