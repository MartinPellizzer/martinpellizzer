Cassia praetexta

Cassia praetexta, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces small, winged seeds enclosed in a pod, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia praetexta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
