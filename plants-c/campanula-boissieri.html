Campanula boissieri

Campanula boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
