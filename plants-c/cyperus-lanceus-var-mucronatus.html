Cyperus lanceus var. mucronatus

Cyperus lanceus var. mucronatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it both a common weed and an important component of wetland ecosystems.
