Carduus paui

Carduus paui, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rough, spiny leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a distinctive purple or violet hue on the underside. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of small, purple flowers with yellow centers. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Carduus paui is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
