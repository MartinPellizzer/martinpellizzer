Cotula hispida

Cotula hispida, commonly known as the rough groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Cotula hispida is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
