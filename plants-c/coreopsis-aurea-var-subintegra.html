Coreopsis aurea var. subintegra

Coreopsis aurea var. subintegra, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
