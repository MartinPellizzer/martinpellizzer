Croton ivohibensis var. integrifolius

Croton ivohibensis var. integrifolius, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to the island of Madagascar, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are typically large, elliptical, and feature a distinctive combination of green, yellow, and red hues. This variety is prized for its ornamental value, though it requires careful management due to its susceptibility to pests and diseases.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
