Ceropegia breviflora subsp. flavida

Ceropegia breviflora subsp.

flavida, commonly known as the yellow-flowered ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in umbels, attracting specific pollinators. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
