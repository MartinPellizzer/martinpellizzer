Cassia auris-zerdae

Cassia auris-zerdae, commonly known as golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia auris-zerdae thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in horticulture and herbal remedies.
