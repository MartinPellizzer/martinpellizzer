Calophyllum jacquinii

Calophyllum jacquinii, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a white, spongy aril, which is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Calophyllum jacquinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
