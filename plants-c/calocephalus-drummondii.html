Calocephalus drummondii

Calocephalus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
