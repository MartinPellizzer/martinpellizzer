Cyperus herbicavus

Cyperus herbicavus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and unique floral structures.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often subtended by bracts. Its rhizomes are usually short and may form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.

Cyperus herbicavus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
