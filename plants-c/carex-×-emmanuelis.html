Carex × emmanuelis

Carex × emmanuelis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex lyngbyei and Carex breviculmis, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and prefers partial to full shade. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, three-ranked flowers, and it produces achene fruits.

Carex × emmanuelis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
