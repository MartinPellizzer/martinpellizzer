Calceolaria pennellii

Calceolaria pennellii, commonly known as the silverleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria pennellii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in moderate climates.
