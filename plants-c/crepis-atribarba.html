Crepis atribarba

Crepis atribarba, commonly known as the purple meadowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Crepis atribarba is often found in European and parts of Asian regions, where it plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
