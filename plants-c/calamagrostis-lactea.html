Calamagrostis lactea

Calamagrostis lactea, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet and produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Calamagrostis lactea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
