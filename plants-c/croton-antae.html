Croton antae

Croton antae, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly colored plants. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed margins, and its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the genus. Croton antae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
