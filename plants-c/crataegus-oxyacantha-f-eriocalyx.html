Crataegus oxyacantha f. eriocalyx

Crataegus oxyacantha f. eriocalyx, commonly known as the smooth thorned hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are typically edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
