Chlamydocarya capitata

Chlamydocarya capitata, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence at the top of the stem. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

The species is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
