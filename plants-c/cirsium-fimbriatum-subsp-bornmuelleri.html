Cirsium fimbriatum subsp. bornmuelleri

Cirsium fimbriatum subsp.

bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
