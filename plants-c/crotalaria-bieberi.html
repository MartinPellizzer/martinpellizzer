Crotalaria bieberi

Crotalaria bieberi, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spurred flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Crotalaria bieberi is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
