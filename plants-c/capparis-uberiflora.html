Capparis uberiflora

Capparis uberiflora, commonly known as the sweet-scented caper, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant's leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture. It is notable for its fragrant flowers, which emit a sweet aroma, and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Capparis uberiflora is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
