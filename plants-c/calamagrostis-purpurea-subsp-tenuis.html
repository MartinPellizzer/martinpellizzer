Calamagrostis purpurea subsp. tenuis

Calamagrostis purpurea subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as slender purple grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America. It features slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
