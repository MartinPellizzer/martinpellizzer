Clematis orientalis var. glauca

Clematis orientalis var. glauca, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features attractive, waxy blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to climb trellises or fences.
