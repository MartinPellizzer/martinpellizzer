Crassula hystrix

Crassula hystrix, commonly known as the hooked crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are adorned with sharp, hooked spines along the edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula hystrix is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
