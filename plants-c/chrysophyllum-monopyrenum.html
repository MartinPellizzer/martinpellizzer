Chrysophyllum monopyrenum

Chrysophyllum monopyrenum, commonly known as the small-fruited starfruit, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is typically pear-shaped and has a thin, edible pericarp. It produces small, yellow berries that are often used in local cuisines.
