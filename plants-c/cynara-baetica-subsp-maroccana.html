Cynara baetica subsp. maroccana

Cynara baetica subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves.

The plant produces a unique, edible flower head that resembles an artichoke, though it is not related to the common artichoke. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
