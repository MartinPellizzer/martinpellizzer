Cynanchum fimbricoronum

Cynanchum fimbricoronum, commonly known as the fringed milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
