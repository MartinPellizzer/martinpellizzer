Commersonia pilosa

Commersonia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy Commersonia, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. Its flowers are usually small and white or pink, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Commersonia pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
