Campanula choruhensis

Campanula choruhensis, commonly known as the Choruh Mountain bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula choruhensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
