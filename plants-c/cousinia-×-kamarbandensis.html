Cousinia × kamarbandensis

Cousinia × kamarbandensis, commonly known as the Persian hawk's-beard, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces yellow to orange ray flowers surrounded by tightly packed disk flowers, typical of the genus Cousinia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and on slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
