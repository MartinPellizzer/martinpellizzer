Chenopodium bicolor

Chenopodium bicolor, commonly known as red root, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and reddish on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dry, three-angled fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium bicolor is known for its high nutritional value and has been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.
