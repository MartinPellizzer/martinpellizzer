Corylus iberica

Corylus iberica, commonly known as the Iberian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in catkins. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are typically harvested in the autumn. Corylus iberica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
