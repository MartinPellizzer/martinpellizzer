Campanula baumgartenii subsp. bohemica

Campanula baumgartenii subsp.

bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in naturalized gardens.
