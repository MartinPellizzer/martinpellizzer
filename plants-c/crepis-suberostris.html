Crepis suberostris

Crepis suberostris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in late summer.

Crepis suberostris is generally short-lived, with a lifespan of one to two years.
