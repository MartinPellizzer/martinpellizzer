Commiphora reflexa

Commiphora reflexa, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
