Codonopsis canescens

Codonopsis canescens, commonly known as the hairy ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Codonopsis canescens is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
