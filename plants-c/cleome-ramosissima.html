Cleome ramosissima

Cleome ramosissima, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its branching stems and showy, spider-like flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cleome ramosissima is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
