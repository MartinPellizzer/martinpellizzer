Chaetochloa sulcata

Chaetochloa sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag or sinuous growth pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and often exhibits a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity.

Chaetochloa sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
