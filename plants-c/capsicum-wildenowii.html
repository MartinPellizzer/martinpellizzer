Capsicum wildenowii

Capsicum wildenowii, commonly known as the wild chili pepper, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its branched stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow or green flowers that are followed by bright red, elongated fruits. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in culinary applications compared to other Capsicum species.
