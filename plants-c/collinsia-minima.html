Collinsia minima

Collinsia minima, commonly known as the dwarf fairybell, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of only 2 to 4 inches, featuring delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Collinsia minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming, early-blooming flowers.
