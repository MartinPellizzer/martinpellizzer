Coaxana malvifolia

Coaxana malvifolia, commonly known as the whitemallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

Coaxana malvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
