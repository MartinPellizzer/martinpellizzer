Capsella stellata

Capsella stellata, commonly known as the starpea or stellate knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, giving it a star-like appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capsella stellata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and gardens.
