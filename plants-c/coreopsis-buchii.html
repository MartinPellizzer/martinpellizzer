Coreopsis buchii

Coreopsis buchii, commonly known as the Buch's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, sandy soils. It is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis buchii is also known for its resilience and adaptability to various growing conditions.
