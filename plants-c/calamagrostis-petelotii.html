Calamagrostis petelotii

Calamagrostis petelotii, commonly known as the Mexican switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are often white or pale green in color.

Calamagrostis petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscaping.
