Carlina acaulis subsp. caulescens

Carlina acaulis subsp.

caulescens, commonly known as the slenderstem cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is characterized by its upright, unbranched stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering heads are composed of blue to violet ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
