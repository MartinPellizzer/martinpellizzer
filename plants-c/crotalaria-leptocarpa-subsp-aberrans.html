Crotalaria leptocarpa subsp. aberrans

Crotalaria leptocarpa subsp.

aberrans, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds, which are typically used as a food source in some parts of the world.
