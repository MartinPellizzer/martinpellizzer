Cerasus laurocerasus var. otini

Cerasus laurocerasus var. otini, commonly known as the common cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, cherry-like fruits. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and fruits are toxic, containing cyanogenic glycosides that can release hydrogen cyanide when ingested.
