Chamaecrista chamaecristoides var. cruziana

Chamaecrista chamaecristoides var. cruziana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
