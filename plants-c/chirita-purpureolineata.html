Chirita purpureolineata

Chirita purpureolineata, commonly known as the purple-line chervil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden environments. Its ornamental value is enhanced by the contrasting purple veins on its foliage, which add visual interest to any garden setting.

Chirita purpureolineata is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
