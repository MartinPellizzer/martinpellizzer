Crataegus oxyacantha f. macrocarpa

Crataegus oxyacantha f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its notably larger, more robust fruits compared to the typical species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring thorny branches and dense foliage. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
