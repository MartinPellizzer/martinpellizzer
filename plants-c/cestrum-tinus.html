Cestrum tinus

Cestrum tinus, commonly known as night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, sour taste. Cestrum tinus thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
