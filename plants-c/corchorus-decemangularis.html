Corchorus decemangularis

Corchorus decemangularis, commonly known as the Indian mallow or ten-angled mallows, is a flowering plant native to the warmer regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

Corchorus decemangularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
