Clematis javana

Clematis javana, commonly known as the Java clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, often with five sepals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately veined, with a slightly hairy texture.

Clematis javana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
