Corispermum nitidum

Corispermum nitidum, commonly known as the smooth bladderwrack, is a halophytic plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in coastal environments, particularly in salt marshes and sandy soils.

Corispermum nitidum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
