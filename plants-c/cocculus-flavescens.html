Cocculus flavescens

Cocculus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often growing in clusters.

Cocculus flavescens is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
