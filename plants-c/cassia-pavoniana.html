Cassia pavoniana

Cassia pavoniana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in warm, humid climates.
