Clematis maritima

Clematis maritima, commonly known as the sea clematis, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and is often found growing in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Clematis maritima is adapted to salt spray and strong coastal winds, making it a resilient and hardy species.
