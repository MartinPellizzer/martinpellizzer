Cheilanthes argentea

Cheilanthes argentea, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the arid and rocky regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-veined fronds that are typically erect and somewhat pinnately divided. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found growing on rock faces or in crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and survive in dry conditions.

The silver maidenhair fern is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
