Carthamus carduncellus

Carthamus carduncellus, commonly known as the artichoke thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, spiny stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with prominent central disks, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
