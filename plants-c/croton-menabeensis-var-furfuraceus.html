Croton menabeensis var. furfuraceus

Croton menabeensis var. furfuraceus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on its leaves and stems, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and may have a waxy coating.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
