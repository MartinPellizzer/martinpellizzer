Cerastium luridum

Cerastium luridum, commonly known as the yellow mouse-ear, is a low-growing flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Cerastium luridum thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
