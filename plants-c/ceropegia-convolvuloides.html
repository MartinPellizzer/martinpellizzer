Ceropegia convolvuloides

Ceropegia convolvuloides, commonly known as the creeping milkweed or silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, somewhat fleshy, often silver- or blue-green colored leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Ceropegia convolvuloides is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
