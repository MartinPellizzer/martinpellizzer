Cirsium macranthum

Cirsium macranthum, commonly known as the large-headed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.
