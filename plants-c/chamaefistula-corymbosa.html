Chamaefistula corymbosa

Chamaefistula corymbosa, commonly known as the Kashmiri weeping willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in corymbs, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its graceful appearance.
