Carex paradoxa var. pseudoparadoxa

Carex paradoxa var. pseudoparadoxa, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
