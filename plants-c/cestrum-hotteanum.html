Cestrum hotteanum

Cestrum hotteanum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blossoms, which are often followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The leaves are lance-shaped and evergreen, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
