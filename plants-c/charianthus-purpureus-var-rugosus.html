Charianthus purpureus var. rugosus

Charianthus purpureus var. rugosus, commonly known as the purple mountain daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, rugose (rough) stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
