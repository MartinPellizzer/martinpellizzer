Carica sphaerocarpa

Carica sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the golden apple or sweet apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Caricaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, crisp flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
