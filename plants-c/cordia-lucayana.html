Cordia lucayana

Cordia lucayana, commonly known as the Lucayan cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in local traditional medicine. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
