Cupressus glauca var. tristis

Cupressus glauca var. tristis, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a variant of the blue cypress native to the southwestern United States.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a bluish-gray appearance. The plant has compact, pyramidal growth habits and produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
