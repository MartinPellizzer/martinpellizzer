Chaenostoma cinereum

Chaenostoma cinereum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Chaenostoma cinereum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
