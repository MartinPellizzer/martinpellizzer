Clinopodium julianum

Clinopodium julianum, commonly known as Julian's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

Clinopodium julianum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
