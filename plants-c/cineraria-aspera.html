Cineraria aspera

Cineraria aspera, commonly known as the rough cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Cineraria aspera is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
