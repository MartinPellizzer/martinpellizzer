Cypripedium × sinapoides

Cypripedium × sinapoides, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow pouch-shaped flower, which is a modified leaf known as a labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments and has a tall, erect stem with a single spike of flowers. Its leaves are broad and ovate, and it produces a unique scent to attract pollinators.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
