Carduncellus monspelliensium var. almeriensis

Carduncellus monspelliensium var. almeriensis, commonly known as the Almerian thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
