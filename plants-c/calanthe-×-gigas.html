Calanthe × gigas

Calanthe × gigas, commonly known as the giant calanthe, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Calanthe × gigas thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Calanthe genus.
