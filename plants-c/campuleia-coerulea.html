Campuleia coerulea

Campuleia coerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Campuleia coerulea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
