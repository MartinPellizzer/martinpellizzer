Convolvulus arenarius

Convolvulus arenarius, commonly known as the sand everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with trailing stems that produce small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow or orange and bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its fibrous roots and hairy leaves help it survive in arid environments.
