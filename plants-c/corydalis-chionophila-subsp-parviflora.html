Corydalis chionophila subsp. parviflora

Corydalis chionophila subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the snow-flower corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
