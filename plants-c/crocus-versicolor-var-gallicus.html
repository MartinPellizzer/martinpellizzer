Crocus versicolor var. gallicus

Crocus versicolor var. gallicus, commonly known as the French crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The variety is distinguished by its vibrant coloration and slightly different morphology compared to the typical Crocus versicolor.
