Cymbachne ciliata

Cymbachne ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or silky couchgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cymbachne ciliata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
