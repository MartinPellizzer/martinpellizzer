Carduus occidentalis var. candidissimus

Carduus occidentalis var. candidissimus, commonly known as the white cocklebur, is a variant of the cocklebur species native to North America.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bracts, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. Its growth habit is typically upright, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The white cocklebur is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.
