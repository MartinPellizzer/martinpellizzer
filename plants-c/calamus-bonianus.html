Calamus bonianus

Calamus bonianus, commonly known as the giant bamboo or Chinese arrowroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with spadices and umbels, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Calamus bonianus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
