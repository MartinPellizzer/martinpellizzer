Cirsium lucidum

Cirsium lucidum, commonly known as the shining thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium lucidum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
