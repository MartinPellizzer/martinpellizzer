Calophyllum inophylloide var. singapurense

Calophyllum inophylloide var. singapurense, commonly known as Singapore calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces elliptical, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
