Cicerbita acuminata

Cicerbita acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf bittercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive sharp tip, while its small white or pink flowers appear in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.
