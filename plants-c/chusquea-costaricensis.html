Chusquea costaricensis

Chusquea costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a clump-forming plant characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in understory layers of forests in Central America.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a structural element in landscaping.
