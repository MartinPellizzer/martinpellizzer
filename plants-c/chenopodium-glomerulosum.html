Chenopodium glomerulosum

Chenopodium glomerulosum, commonly known as clustered goosefoot, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and features small, green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in compact inflorescences. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Chenopodium glomerulosum is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
