Citrus × changshan-huyou

Citrus × changshan-huyou, commonly known as the Guangxi mandarin orange, is a hybrid citrus species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, yellow-orange peel and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is often used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for cultivation in subtropical climates.
