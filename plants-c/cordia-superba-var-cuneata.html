Cordia superba var. cuneata

Cordia superba var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
