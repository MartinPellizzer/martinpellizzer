Centella abbreviata

Centella abbreviata, commonly known as Asiatic pennywort, is a small herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a creeping stem with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported skin-healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves and adaptability to moist environments.
