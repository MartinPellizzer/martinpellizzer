Cucumis zambianus

Cucumis zambianus, commonly known as the Zambian cucumber, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its vines grow horizontally along the ground.

The Zambian cucumber is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines across its native regions.
