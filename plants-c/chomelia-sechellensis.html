Chomelia sechellensis

Chomelia sechellensis, commonly known as the Seychelles flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Seychelles.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is often fissured and rough to the touch. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or on limestone soils.

The Seychelles flame tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
