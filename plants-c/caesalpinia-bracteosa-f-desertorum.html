Caesalpinia bracteosa f. desertorum

Caesalpinia bracteosa f. desertorum, commonly known as the desert crown flower, is a variant of the crown flower species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bright yellow to orange-red floral spikes. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in desert environments.
