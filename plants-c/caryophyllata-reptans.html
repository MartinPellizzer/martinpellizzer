Caryophyllata reptans

Caryophyllata reptans, commonly known as the creeping carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Caryophyllata reptans is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
