Commelina rupestris

Commelina rupestris, commonly known as the rock commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Commelina rupestris is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed habitats.
