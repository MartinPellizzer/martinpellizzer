Cotoneaster multiflorus var. atropurpureus

Cotoneaster multiflorus var. atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple-leaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features dark purple foliage that contrasts beautifully with its clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or ornamental plant in gardens.
