Commelina vermoesenii

Commelina vermoesenii, commonly known as the yellow arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow spatulate bracts that surround the inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a single pistil, making it an attractive species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
