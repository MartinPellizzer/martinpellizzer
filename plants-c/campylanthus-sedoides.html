Campylanthus sedoides

Campylanthus sedoides, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated by birds and insects.
