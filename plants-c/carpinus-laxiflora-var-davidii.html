Carpinus laxiflora var. davidii

Carpinus laxiflora var. davidii, commonly known as the David's hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins that are typically male, while female flowers are clustered in short racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
