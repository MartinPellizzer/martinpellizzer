Coussarea insignis

Coussarea insignis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
