Cirsium consanguineum

Cirsium consanguineum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray florets and tubular disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium consanguineum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
