Carex litorhyncha

Carex litorhyncha, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to high-salinity environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Carex litorhyncha plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
