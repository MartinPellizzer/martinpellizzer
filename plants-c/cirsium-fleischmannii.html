Cirsium fleischmannii

Cirsium fleischmannii, commonly known as Fleischmann's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are generally gray-green with rough, prickly surfaces, and the flowers bloom from late summer to early fall.

Cirsium fleischmannii is a hardy species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
