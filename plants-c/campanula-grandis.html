Campanula grandis

Campanula grandis, commonly known as the great bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
