Calathea affinis

Calathea affinis, commonly known as the "prayer plant," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that often display a mix of green, silver, and pink hues. The plant is a member of the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to close its leaves at night, a trait that gives it its common name. Calathea affinis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
