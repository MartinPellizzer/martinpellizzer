Catapodium loliaceum subsp. syrticum

Catapodium loliaceum subsp.

syrticum, commonly known as the desert catapodium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and North Africa. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage. Its flowers are typically small and greenish, with a subtle aromatic quality.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils in degraded landscapes and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
