Carex livida

Carex livida, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

Carex livida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
