Coccothrinax munizii

Coccothrinax munizii, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a species of palm native to the island of Cuba.

It is a small to medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces feather-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces dark blue, spherical berries that are edible.

This palm is adapted to coastal and dry habitats, making it an important component of Cuba's native flora.
