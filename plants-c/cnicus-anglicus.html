Cnicus anglicus

Cnicus anglicus, commonly known as English sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
