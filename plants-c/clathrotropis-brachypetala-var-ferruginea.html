Clathrotropis brachypetala var. ferruginea

Clathrotropis brachypetala var. ferruginea, commonly known as the red-leafed clathrotropis, is a variant of the clathrotropis genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
