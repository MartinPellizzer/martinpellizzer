Clusia criuva subsp. parviflora

Clusia criuva subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered clusia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. Clusia criuva subsp.

parviflora is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate salt spray.
