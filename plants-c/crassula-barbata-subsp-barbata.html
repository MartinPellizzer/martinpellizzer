Crassula barbata subsp. barbata

Crassula barbata subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the woolly crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
