Ceiba mandonii

Ceiba mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dispersal.

The bark and leaves of Ceiba mandonii have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
