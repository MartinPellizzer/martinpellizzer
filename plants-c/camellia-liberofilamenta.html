Camellia liberofilamenta

Camellia liberofilamenta, commonly known as the filamentous camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial evergreen shrub characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. Camellia liberofilamenta thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in late winter to early spring.
