Citrus gracilis

Citrus gracilis, commonly known as the slender lemon.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, with a thin, juicy rind and a tart, acidic flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful attention to soil drainage and climate conditions to thrive.
