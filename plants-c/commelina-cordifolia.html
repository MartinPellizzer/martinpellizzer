Commelina cordifolia

Commelina cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Commelina cordifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
