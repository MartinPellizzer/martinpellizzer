Conyza nivalis

Conyza nivalis, commonly known as snowflake weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Conyza nivalis is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions.
