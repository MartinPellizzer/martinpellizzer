Clematis chiupehensis

Clematis chiupehensis, commonly known as the Chiupehensis Clematis, is a flowering vine native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its twining stems and clusters of small, usually white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and provides a lush backdrop to its delicate blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and greenhouses.
