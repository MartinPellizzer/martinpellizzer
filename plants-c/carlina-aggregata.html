Carlina aggregata

Carlina aggregata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a distinctive conical inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts. Carlina aggregata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
