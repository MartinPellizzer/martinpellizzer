Cyathula cylindrica var. mannii

Cyathula cylindrica var. mannii, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems that are often used as a vegetable in local cuisines. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in cup-like structures called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued not only for its edible tubers but also for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
