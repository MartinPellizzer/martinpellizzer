Crocus pendulus

Crocus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features delicate, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important plants.
