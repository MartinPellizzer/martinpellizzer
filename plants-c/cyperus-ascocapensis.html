Cyperus ascocapensis

Cyperus ascocapensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in wet or seasonally flooded habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered inflorescences consist of pale green to brown spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Cyperus ascocapensis is often found in swampy areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
