Callicarpa pentandra var. paloensis

Callicarpa pentandra var. paloensis, commonly known as the Palo Alto beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, which are edible for humans and wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and small mammals.
