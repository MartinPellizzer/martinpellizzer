Cerasus erzincanica

Cerasus erzincanica, commonly known as the Erzincan cherry, is a species of Prunus native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its small, red berries that ripen in the summer. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
