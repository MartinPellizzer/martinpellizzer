Cleistogenes hackelii var. nakaii

Cleistogenes hackelii var. nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, spikelets.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
