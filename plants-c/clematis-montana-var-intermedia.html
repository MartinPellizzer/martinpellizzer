Clematis montana var. intermedia

Clematis montana var. intermedia, commonly known as the Dutch white clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
