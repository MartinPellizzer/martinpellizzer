Camellia liberistamina

Camellia liberistamina, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia liberistamina is also valued for its ability to bloom in late winter or early spring, adding color to the landscape during the colder months.
