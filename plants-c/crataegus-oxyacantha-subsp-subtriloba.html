Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. subtriloba

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

subtriloba, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
