Cynanchum albonervum

Cynanchum albonervum, commonly known as white-veined milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap, which can be toxic. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent white veins. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pale green to yellowish in color, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
