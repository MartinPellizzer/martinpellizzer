Cissus adnata

Cissus adnata, commonly known as the creeping fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus adnata is also valued for its ability to climb and cover structures, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
