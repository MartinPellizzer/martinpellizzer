Croton campestris var. dupariaei

Croton campestris var. dupariaei, commonly known as the two-leaved croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are usually divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, which can vary in color from green to red or purple.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
