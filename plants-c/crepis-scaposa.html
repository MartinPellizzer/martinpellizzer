Crepis scaposa

Crepis scaposa, commonly known as the scapose mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are hairy and basal, forming a rosette that helps it survive in dry conditions.
