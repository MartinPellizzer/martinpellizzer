Crataegus macracantha var. rhombifolia

Crataegus macracantha var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptic to rhombic leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive shape. The plant produces white to pink flowers in clusters during spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
