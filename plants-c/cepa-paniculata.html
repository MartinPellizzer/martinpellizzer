Cepa paniculata

Cepa paniculata, commonly known as the yellow wood orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the inflorescence consists of a panicle of clustered flowers.

Cepa paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
