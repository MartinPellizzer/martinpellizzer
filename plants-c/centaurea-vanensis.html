Centaurea vanensis

Centaurea vanensis, commonly known as Van's coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea vanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
