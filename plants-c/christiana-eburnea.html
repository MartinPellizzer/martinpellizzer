Christiana eburnea

Christiana eburnea, commonly known as the white wood or white ironwood, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, pale wood that is highly valued for its durability and workability. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with smooth, grayish bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various local wildlife.
