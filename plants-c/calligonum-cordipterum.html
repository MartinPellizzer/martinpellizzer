Calligonum cordipterum

Calligonum cordipterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goosefoot, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Calligonum cordipterum is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
