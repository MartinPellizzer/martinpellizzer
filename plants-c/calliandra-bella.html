Calliandra bella

Calliandra bella, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, feathery inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets and produces clusters of pink to red flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm climates.

Calliandra bella also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
