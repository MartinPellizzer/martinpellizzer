Caesalpinia oyamae

Caesalpinia oyamae, commonly known as the Oyama candle bush, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like yellow flowers. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets, and its growth habit is typically shrub-like. Caesalpinia oyamae is known for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens where its vibrant blooms add visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for coastal and garden landscapes.
