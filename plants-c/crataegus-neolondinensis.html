Crataegus neolondinensis

Crataegus neolondinensis, commonly known as the London hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern England and parts of western Europe. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the autumn.

The London hawthorn is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
