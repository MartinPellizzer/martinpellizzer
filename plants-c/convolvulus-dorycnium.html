Convolvulus dorycnium

Convolvulus dorycnium, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround the flower cluster. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a darker central spot. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
