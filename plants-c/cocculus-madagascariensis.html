Cocculus madagascariensis

Cocculus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces smooth, green, egg-shaped fruits that turn yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
