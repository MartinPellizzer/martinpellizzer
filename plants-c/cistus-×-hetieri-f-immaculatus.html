Cistus × hetieri f. immaculatus

Cistus × hetieri f. immaculatus, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
