Crotalaria squarrosa

Crotalaria squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are palmately compound, and the pods contain several seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria squarrosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
