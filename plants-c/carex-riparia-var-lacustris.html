Carex riparia var. lacustris

Carex riparia var. lacustris, commonly known as the lake sedge, is a wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found along stream banks, lake edges, and marshy areas.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
