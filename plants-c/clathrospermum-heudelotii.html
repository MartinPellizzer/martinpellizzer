Clathrospermum heudelotii

Clathrospermum heudelotii, commonly known as the African blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across the region.
