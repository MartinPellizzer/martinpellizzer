Crotalaria raffillii

Crotalaria raffillii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers and is often found in grassland habitats. The plant has a distinctive taproot system and produces a capsule containing several seeds. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Crotalaria raffillii is also known for its role in promoting seed germination in certain grass species.
