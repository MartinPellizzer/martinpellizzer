Cymbidium hyacinthinum

Cymbidium hyacinthinum, commonly known as the Hyacinth Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that bears a cluster of fragrant, hyacinth-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for winter flowering. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Cymbidium hyacinthinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
