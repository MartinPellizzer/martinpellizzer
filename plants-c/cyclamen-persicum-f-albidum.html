Cyclamen persicum f. albidum

Cyclamen persicum f. albidum, commonly known as the white cyclamen, is a hybrid form of the Persian cyclamen.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white flowers that contrast with its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. This cultivar typically blooms in early spring, producing delicate, showy flowers that are popular in horticulture. The plant prefers cool, moist conditions and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation or shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
