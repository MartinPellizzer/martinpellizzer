Carex anceps var. patulifolia

Carex anceps var. patulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Carex anceps.
