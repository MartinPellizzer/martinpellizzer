Cuphea linarioides var. glabrescens

Cuphea linarioides var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
