Cayaponia bonariensis var. dissecta

Cayaponia bonariensis var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected cayaponia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
