Cerris crinita

Cerris crinita, commonly known as the silky walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by spherical, green fruits that mature to a dark blue color. Its wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it suitable for carving and furniture.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
