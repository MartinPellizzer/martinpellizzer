Ceuthocarpus involucratus

Ceuthocarpus involucratus, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
