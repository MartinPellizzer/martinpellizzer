Cynanchum hancockianum

Cynanchum hancockianum, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing silky seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its latex contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested.
