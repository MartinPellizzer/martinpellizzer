Cissus quinquefolia

Cissus quinquefolia, commonly known as devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for humans and animals. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its rapid growth rate.

Cissus quinquefolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
