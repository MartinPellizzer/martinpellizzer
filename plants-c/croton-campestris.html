Croton campestris

Croton campestris, commonly known as the field croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Croton campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
