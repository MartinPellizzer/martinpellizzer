Capparis parviflora

Capparis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cape rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
