Crataegus azarolus typica

Crataegus azarolus typica, commonly known as the rock rose or Mediterranean hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
