Crotalaria sphaerocarpa subsp. sphaerocarpa

Crotalaria sphaerocarpa subsp.

sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive globular seed pods, which contain small, round seeds.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a source of forage for livestock.
