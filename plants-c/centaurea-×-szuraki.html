Centaurea × szuraki

Centaurea × szuraki, commonly known as the Hungarian knapweed, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiky, cone-like flower heads that range in color from reddish-brown to purple, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea × szuraki is native to Europe and has been introduced to various regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
