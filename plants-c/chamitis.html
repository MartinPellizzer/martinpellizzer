Chamitis

Chamitis, commonly known as tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and aromatic, fern-like leaves. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, chamitis thrives in moist, sunny environments. The plant contains compounds such as thujone, which have been used in traditional medicine.

However, it is toxic in large quantities and should be handled with care.
