Chamaecrista auris-zerdae

Chamaecrista auris-zerdae, commonly known as the ear-shaped burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that produces distinctive, spiny pods that resemble small ears, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
