Carex rivularis

Carex rivularis, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found along streams, lakes, and marshes.

Carex rivularis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
