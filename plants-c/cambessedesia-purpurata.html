Cambessedesia purpurata

Cambessedesia purpurata, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
