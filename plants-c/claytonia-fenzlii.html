Claytonia fenzlii

Claytonia fenzlii, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, violet-colored, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in early spring.

Claytonia fenzlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
