Chenopodium probstii var. glaucescens

Chenopodium probstii var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its glaucous, waxy coating on the leaves, which gives it a bluish-green appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped.

This variety is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ecological significance in arid regions.
