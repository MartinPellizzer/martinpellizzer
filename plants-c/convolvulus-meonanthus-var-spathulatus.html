Convolvulus meonanthus var. spathulatus

Convolvulus meonanthus var. spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to climb or trail along structures.
