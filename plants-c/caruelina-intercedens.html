Caruelina intercedens

Caruelina intercedens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Caruelina intercedens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
