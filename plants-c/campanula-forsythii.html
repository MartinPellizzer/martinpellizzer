Campanula forsythii

Campanula forsythii, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula forsythii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
