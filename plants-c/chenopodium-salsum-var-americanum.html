Chenopodium salsum var. americanum

Chenopodium salsum var. americanum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Lambsquarters is a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens, and it is also used in some traditional herbal practices.

Its nutritional value makes it a potential food source for both humans and livestock.
