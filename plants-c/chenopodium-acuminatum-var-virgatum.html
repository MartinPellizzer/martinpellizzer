Chenopodium acuminatum var. virgatum

Chenopodium acuminatum var. virgatum, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, and its erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to North America and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides.

Silverleaf goosefoot is sometimes considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain wildlife species.
