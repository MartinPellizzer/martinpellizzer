Crepis virens var. linifolia

Crepis virens var. linifolia, commonly known as the slender crested hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces small, cylindrical seed heads, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
