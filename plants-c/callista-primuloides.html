Callista primuloides

Callista primuloides, commonly known as the New Zealand broom, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forest margins and coastal areas, and is often found in the wild.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
