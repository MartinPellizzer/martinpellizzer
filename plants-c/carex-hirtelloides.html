Carex hirtelloides

Carex hirtelloides, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive hairy appearance.

Its reproductive structures are typically arranged in a single spike, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.
