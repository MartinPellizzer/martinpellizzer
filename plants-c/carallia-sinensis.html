Carallia sinensis

Carallia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple to blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds, contributing to ecological diversity.
