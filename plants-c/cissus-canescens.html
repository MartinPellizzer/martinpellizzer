Cissus canescens

Cissus canescens, commonly known as the sand dollar plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive waxy, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by heart-shaped, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardens.

Cissus canescens is also valued for its ability to climb and its ornamental appeal.
