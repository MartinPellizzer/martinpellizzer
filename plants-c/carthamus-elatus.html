Carthamus elatus

Carthamus elatus, commonly known as the Persian crown flower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and vibrant yellow to orange flower heads that resemble small crowns. The plant produces numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Carthamus elatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
