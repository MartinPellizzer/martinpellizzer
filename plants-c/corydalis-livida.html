Corydalis livida

Corydalis livida, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has slender, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
