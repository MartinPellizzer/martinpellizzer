Crataegus × grandiflora

Crataegus × grandiflora, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are typically harvested in the fall.

Crataegus × grandiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
