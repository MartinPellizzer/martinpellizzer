Cynanchum brevidens var. zambesiaca

Cynanchum brevidens var. zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi milk-vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which can be toxic. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves and produces yellowish flowers with a distinctive corona. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of African flora.
