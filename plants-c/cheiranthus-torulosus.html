Cheiranthus torulosus

Cheiranthus torulosus, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Cheiranthus torulosus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
