Chrysosplenium wrightii var. beringianum

Chrysosplenium wrightii var. beringianum, commonly known as the Beringian golden moss, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in cymes. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered with a fine, waxy coating.

This species thrives in moist, rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.
