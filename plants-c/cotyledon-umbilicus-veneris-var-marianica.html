Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris var. marianica

Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris var. marianica, commonly known as the Moonstone Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often pale green with a bluish tint, and it produces striking pink or red flowers during the growing season. The plant is characterized by its unique, rounded leaves that resemble small stones, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant color and compact form.
