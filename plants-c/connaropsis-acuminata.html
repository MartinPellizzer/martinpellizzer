Connaropsis acuminata

Connaropsis acuminata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and striking appearance make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
