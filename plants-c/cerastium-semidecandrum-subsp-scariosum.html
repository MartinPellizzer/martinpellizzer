Cerastium semidecandrum subsp. scariosum

Cerastium semidecandrum subsp.

scariosum, commonly known as the scaly mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a scaly texture on the underside, giving it its subspecies name. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
