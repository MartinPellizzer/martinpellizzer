Centaurea timbalii

Centaurea timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalii knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Centaurea timbalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
