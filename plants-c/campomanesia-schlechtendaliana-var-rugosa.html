Campomanesia schlechtendaliana var. rugosa

Campomanesia schlechtendaliana var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved satinash, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery, rough-textured leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
