Chondroscaphe plicata

Chondroscaphe plicata, commonly known as the pleated rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are typical of the Prunus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
