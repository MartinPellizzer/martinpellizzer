Cleome cardinalis

Cleome cardinalis, commonly known as the Mexican winecup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often resembling wine cups. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 2 to 3 feet. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and branching.

Cleome cardinalis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
