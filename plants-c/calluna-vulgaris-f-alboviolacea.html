Calluna vulgaris f. alboviolacea

Calluna vulgaris f. alboviolacea, commonly known as the white-flowered heather, is a cultivar of the common heather.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. This plant is evergreen, with compact, shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
