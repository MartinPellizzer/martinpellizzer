Campanula exilis

Campanula exilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula exilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
