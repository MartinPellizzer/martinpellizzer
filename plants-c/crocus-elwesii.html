Crocus elwesii

Crocus elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. This perennial herb produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming period.
