Centaurea aegialophila

Centaurea aegialophila, commonly known as the coastal centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, bright blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.

Centaurea aegialophila is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
