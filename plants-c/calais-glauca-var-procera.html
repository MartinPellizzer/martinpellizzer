Calais glauca var. procera

Calais glauca var. procera, commonly known as the tall bluebelle, is a striking member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue, often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its showy appearance and hardy nature.
