Crinum capense var. princeps

Crinum capense var. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's Crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is characterized by its robust, underground bulb and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
