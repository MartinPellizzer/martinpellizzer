Cenchrus

Cenchrus, commonly known as beardgrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the family Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root systems. Many species of Cenchrus are found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they thrive in sandy or rocky soils. They often have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

Some species are used in forage or as ornamental plants due to their hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
