Carex praecox var. pallida

Carex praecox var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically pale green to bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, leafy stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
