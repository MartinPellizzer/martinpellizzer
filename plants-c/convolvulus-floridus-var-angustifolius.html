Convolvulus floridus var. angustifolius

Convolvulus floridus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habits.
