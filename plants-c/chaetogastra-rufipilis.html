Chaetogastra rufipilis

Chaetogastra rufipilis, commonly known as the red-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and savanna habitats, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence along the edges.

Chaetogastra rufipilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
