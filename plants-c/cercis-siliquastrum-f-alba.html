Cercis siliquastrum f. alba

Cercis siliquastrum f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered Judas tree, is a cultivar of the Judas tree species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking white blossoms that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This ornamental plant is valued for its beauty and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
