Carpinus shimenensis

Carpinus shimenensis, commonly known as the Shimen hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates.

Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with distinctive ridges that become more pronounced with age.
