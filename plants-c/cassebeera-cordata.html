Cassebeera cordata

Cassebeera cordata, commonly known as the red-stemmed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cassebeera cordata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
