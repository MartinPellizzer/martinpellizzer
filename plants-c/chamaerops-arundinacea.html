Chamaerops arundinacea

Chamaerops arundinacea, commonly known as the fan palm, is a species of palm native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fan-shaped leaves and slender, multi-branched trunk. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits.

Chamaerops arundinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
