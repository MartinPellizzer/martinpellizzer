Carponema filiformis

Carponema filiformis, commonly known as the thread liverwort, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the order Marchantiales.

It is a small, leafy liverwort that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having structures called rhizoids for anchorage and absorption.

Carponema filiformis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its ecosystem.
