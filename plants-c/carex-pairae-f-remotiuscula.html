Carex pairae f. remotiuscula

Carex pairae f. remotiuscula, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the Carex pairae species, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant native to certain regions of North America, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands in suitable environments.

The slender sedge plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
