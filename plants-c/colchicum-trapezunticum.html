Colchicum trapezunticum

Colchicum trapezunticum, commonly known as the Trapezountian colchicum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces a bulbous corm, which serves as its primary storage organ. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in a waxy coating.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
