Cerastium decalvans f. isophyllum

Cerastium decalvans f. isophyllum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are generally hairless. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its reduced leaf hairs compared to the typical form, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
