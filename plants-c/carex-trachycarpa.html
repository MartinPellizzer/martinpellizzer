Carex trachycarpa

Carex trachycarpa, commonly known as rough-sheathed sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex trachycarpa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
