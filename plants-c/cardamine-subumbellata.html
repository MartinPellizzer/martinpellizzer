Cardamine subumbellata

Cardamine subumbellata, commonly known as the small-umbel buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers called umbels.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
