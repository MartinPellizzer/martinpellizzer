Cordia inornata

Cordia inornata, commonly known as the white cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, woody, spherical fruits. Cordia inornata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
