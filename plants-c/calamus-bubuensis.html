Calamus bubuensis

Calamus bubuensis, commonly known as the Bubu calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Calamus bubuensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
