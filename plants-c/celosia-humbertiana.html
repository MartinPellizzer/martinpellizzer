Celosia humbertiana

Celosia humbertiana, commonly known as the flameflower or red dwarf celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, feather-like bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, and it thrives in full sunlight with well-drained soil.

Celosia humbertiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.
