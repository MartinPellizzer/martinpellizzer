Chaetocyperus setaceus

Chaetocyperus setaceus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its slender, grass-like stems are often mistaken for grasses, but it has distinct sedge characteristics, including triangular stems and small, clustered flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
