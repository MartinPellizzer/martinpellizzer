Cytisus biflorus var. glaber

Cytisus biflorus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-flowered broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that are typically covered in short, smooth hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
