Convolvulus pseudocantabrica subsp. dianthoides

Convolvulus pseudocantabrica subsp.

dianthoides, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
