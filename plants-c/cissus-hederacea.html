Cissus hederacea

Cissus hederacea, commonly known as the ivy creeper, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. Its rapid growth makes it suitable for vertical gardening and erosion control.

Cissus hederacea thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
