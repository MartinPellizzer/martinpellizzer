Coleus macropus

Coleus macropus, commonly known as the large-leaved coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that can be red, purple, or green, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Coleus macropus is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in container planting and landscape design.
