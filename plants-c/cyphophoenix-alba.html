Cyphophoenix alba

Cyphophoenix alba, commonly known as the white date palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the southern United States, particularly Florida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling dates, though they are not typically consumed by humans. Cyphophoenix alba is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils near the ocean.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
