Campanula scheuchzeri var. calycina

Campanula scheuchzeri var. calycina, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

Campanula scheuchzeri var. calycina is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to flourish in high-altitude environments.
