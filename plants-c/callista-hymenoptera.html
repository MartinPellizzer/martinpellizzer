Callista hymenoptera

Callista hymenoptera, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also important in ecological restoration projects.

Callista hymenoptera plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
