Crinitina pontica

Crinitina pontica, commonly known as the Pontic sea anemone, is a marine cnidarian found in the Black Sea.

It belongs to the class Anthozoa and is characterized by its delicate, translucent polyps and long, flowing tentacles. The organism typically inhabits sandy or muddy substrates at depths ranging from 10 to 50 meters. Its reproductive system includes both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its population dynamics in the region.

The plant plays a role in the marine ecosystem by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as prey for larger organisms.
