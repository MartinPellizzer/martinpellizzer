Chlorogalum leichtlinii

Chlorogalum leichtlinii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces a tall, slender stem that bears a cluster of white or pale pink flowers during the spring. Its flowers are tubular and adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
