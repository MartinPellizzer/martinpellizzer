Coccothrinax argentea var. guantanamensis

Coccothrinax argentea var. guantanamensis, commonly known as the silver palm, is a species of palm native to the island of Cuba.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, round fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and wind exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in tropical environments.
