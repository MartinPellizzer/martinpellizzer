Cochlospermum tetraporum

Cochlospermum tetraporum, commonly known as the four-lobed glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with four to six leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
