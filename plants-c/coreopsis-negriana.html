Coreopsis negriana

Coreopsis negriana, commonly known as the black coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant produces bright yellow to orange ray flowers that are surrounded by numerous disk flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Coreopsis negriana is valued for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
