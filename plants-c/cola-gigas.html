Cola gigas

Cola gigas, commonly known as the giant cola tree, is a species native to western Africa.

It is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish flowers that develop into hard, woody pods containing the seeds. Cola gigas is notable for its ability to thrive in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and have economic significance in certain regions.
