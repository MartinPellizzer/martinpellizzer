Casearia calciphila

Casearia calciphila, commonly known as the limestone casearia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in calcareous soils, making it a calciphile. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical berries.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental potential.
