Camellia montana

Camellia montana, commonly known as the mountain camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates with partial shade. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Camellia montana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
