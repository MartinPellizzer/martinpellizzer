Cardiospermum triphyllum

Cardiospermum triphyllum, commonly known as the heartleaf or morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often fold at night. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, opening in the morning. Its fruits are explosive capsules that disperse seeds forcefully when mature.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
