Campanula macrorhiza var. sabatina

Campanula macrorhiza var. sabatina, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
