Cladopus nymanii

Cladopus nymanii, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss.

Cladopus nymanii is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
