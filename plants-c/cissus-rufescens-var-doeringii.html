Cissus rufescens var. doeringii

Cissus rufescens var. doeringii, commonly known as the Doering's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
