Cerasus juliana var. cafra

Cerasus juliana var. cafra, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cafra cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bright red fruits that are edible. The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in early spring.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit-bearing capacity.
