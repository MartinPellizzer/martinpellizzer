Crepis rostrata

Crepis rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the fruit is a small, hairy achene.

Crepis rostrata is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
