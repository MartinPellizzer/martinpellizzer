Carduus schimperi subsp. nanus

Carduus schimperi subsp.

nanus, commonly known as the dwarf cardoon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of East Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it distinct from its larger relatives. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in arid conditions with minimal water requirements.
