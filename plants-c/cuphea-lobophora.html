Cuphea lobophora

Cuphea lobophora, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and unique appearance.

Cuphea lobophora is also known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
