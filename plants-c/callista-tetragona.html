Callista tetragona

Callista tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled callista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Callista tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
