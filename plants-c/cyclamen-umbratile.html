Cyclamen umbratile

Cyclamen umbratile, commonly known as the shadow cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, nodding flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it well-suited for woodland gardens. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding an early touch of color to the landscape.

The flowers are borne on short stalks and are often accompanied by a strong, spicy scent.
