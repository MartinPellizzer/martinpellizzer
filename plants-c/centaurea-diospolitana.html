Centaurea diospolitana

Centaurea diospolitana, commonly known as the Diospolitana thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Centaurea diospolitana is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
