Crataegus monogyna var. punicea-plena

Crataegus monogyna var. punicea-plena, commonly known as the double-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features thorny branches and small, oval leaves with serrated edges. It produces red berries in the autumn, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
