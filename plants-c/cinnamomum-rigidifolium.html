Cinnamomum rigidifolium

Cinnamomum rigidifolium, commonly known as the rigid-leaved cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its stiff, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant's essential oils, derived from its leaves and bark, are valued for their use in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
