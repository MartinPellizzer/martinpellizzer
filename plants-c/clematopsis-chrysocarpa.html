Clematopsis chrysocarpa

Clematopsis chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited clematopsis, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically alternate and have a serrated edge. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive yellow berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
