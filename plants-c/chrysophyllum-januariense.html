Chrysophyllum januariense

Chrysophyllum januariense, commonly known as the January cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical regions.
