Chrysopsis compacta

Chrysopsis compacta, commonly known as the compact goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chrysopsis compacta is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
