Centaurea codruensis

Centaurea codruensis, commonly known as the Codru's coneflower, is a perennial herb native to Romania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and it produces a cylindrical flower head composed of both ray and disc florets.

Centaurea codruensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
