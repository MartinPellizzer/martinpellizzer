Cassia rojasiana

Cassia rojasiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

Cassia rojasiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
