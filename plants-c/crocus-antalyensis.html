Crocus antalyensis

Crocus antalyensis, commonly known as the Antalya crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iris family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Turkey and parts of southern Europe. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in mild climates.

The bulbs of Crocus antalyensis are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental beauty.
