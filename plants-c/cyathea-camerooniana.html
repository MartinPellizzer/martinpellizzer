Cyathea camerooniana

Cyathea camerooniana, commonly known as the Cameroon tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the rainforests of Cameroon and Nigeria.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments and thrives in tropical climates. Its rhizome system supports its growth, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds for reproduction.

Cyathea camerooniana plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
