Clematis flava

Clematis flava, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
