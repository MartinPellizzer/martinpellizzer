Ceanothus fontanesianus var. cyaneus

Ceanothus fontanesianus var. cyaneus, commonly known as the blue ceanothus, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flower clusters.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
