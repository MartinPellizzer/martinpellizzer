Cistus populifolius subsp. populifolius

Cistus populifolius subsp.

populifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a leathery texture, and the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
