Centaurea cuneifolia var. angustiloba

Centaurea cuneifolia var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and yellow, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
