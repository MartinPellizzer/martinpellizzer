Cayratia schumanniana

Cayratia schumanniana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely pubescent, heart-shaped leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor plant displays.
