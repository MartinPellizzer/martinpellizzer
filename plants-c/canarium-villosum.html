Canarium villosum

Canarium villosum, commonly known as the hairy canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lanceolate leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
