Cytisus affinis

Cytisus affinis, commonly known as the creeping firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for hedging and landscaping.
