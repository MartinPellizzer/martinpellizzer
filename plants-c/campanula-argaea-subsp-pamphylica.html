Campanula argaea subsp. pamphylica

Campanula argaea subsp.

pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
