Cyperus forskalianus

Cyperus forskalianus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, blade-like leaves and spikelets that bear small, pale purple flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native habitats.
