Croton planaltoanus

Croton planaltoanus, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple tinge. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton planaltoanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in warm, humid climates.
