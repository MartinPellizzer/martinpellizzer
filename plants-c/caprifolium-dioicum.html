Caprifolium dioicum

Caprifolium dioicum, commonly known as common honeysuckle.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its opposite, simple leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces both male and female flowers on separate plants, a trait known as dioecy. Its berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
