Cuphea axilliflora

Cuphea axilliflora, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Cuphea axilliflora is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
