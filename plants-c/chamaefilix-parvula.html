Chamaefilix parvula

Chamaefilix parvula, commonly known as the dwarf rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are typically grayish-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often used in xeriscaping.
