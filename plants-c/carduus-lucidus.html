Carduus lucidus

Carduus lucidus, commonly known as the shining thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance, and the flowers are typically arranged in a single, upright head.

Carduus lucidus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
