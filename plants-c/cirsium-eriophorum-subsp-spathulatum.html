Cirsium eriophorum subsp. spathulatum

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad, spiny, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are purple or pink, arranged in a typical daisy-like pattern.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
