Carduus spinosissimus

Carduus spinosissimus, commonly known as the spiked thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Carduus spinosissimus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
