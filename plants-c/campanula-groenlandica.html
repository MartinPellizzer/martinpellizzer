Campanula groenlandica

Campanula groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features slender stems that often grow close to the ground, and its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Campanula groenlandica is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
