Crotalaria mitchellii var. tomentosa

Crotalaria mitchellii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the flowers are arranged in racemes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
