Calceolaria × polyantha

Calceolaria × polyantha, commonly known as the hybrid slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Calceolaria microphylla and Calceolaria tetragona.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and purple. It produces small, hairy leaves and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting.

Calceolaria × polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
