Chrysocalyx gracilis

Chrysocalyx gracilis, commonly known as the slender chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Chrysocalyx gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its refined appearance.
