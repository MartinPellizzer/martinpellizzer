Carex cesanensis

Carex cesanensis, commonly known as the Cesan's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex cesanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
