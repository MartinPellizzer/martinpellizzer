Cracca concinna

Cracca concinna, commonly known as the red sandalwood.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and distinctive reddish color.

However, due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species in many regions.
