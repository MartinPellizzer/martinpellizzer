Cyanus adamovicii

Cyanus adamovicii, commonly known as the Adamovici's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Cyanus adamovicii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its visual interest.
