Cerastium poelianum

Cerastium poelianum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing, perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are white or pink, with five petals, and appear in early spring. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, gardens, and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and stolons.
