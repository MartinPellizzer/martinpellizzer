Centranthus lecoqii var. longicalcaratus

Centranthus lecoqii var. longicalcaratus, commonly known as the long-spurred centranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, dry fruits called capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders in horticulture.
