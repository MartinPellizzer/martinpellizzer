Cinnamomum obovatifolium

Cinnamomum obovatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are also utilized in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

This species thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
