Cerastium hyperboreum

Cerastium hyperboreum, commonly known as the Arctic chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It is often found in tundra and alpine habitats where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in these extreme environments.
