Cinnamomum pallidum

Cinnamomum pallidum, commonly known as the pale cinnamon tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, round berries that contain a single seed, which is used for propagation. Its bark and leaves are valued for their aromatic compounds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
