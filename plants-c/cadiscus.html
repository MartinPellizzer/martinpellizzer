Cadiscus

Cadiscus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers with brightly colored petals and a central disc of smaller florets. They typically grow in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Cadiscus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
