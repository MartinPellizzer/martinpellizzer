Carduus jacquinii

Carduus jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and along roadsides. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
