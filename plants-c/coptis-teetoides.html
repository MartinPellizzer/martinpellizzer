Coptis teetoides

Coptis teetoides, commonly known as the yellow monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and deeply lobed leaves. The plant contains alkaloids, such as coptisine, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential pharmacological applications, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
