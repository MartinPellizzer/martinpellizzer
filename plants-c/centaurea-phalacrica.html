Centaurea phalacrica

Centaurea phalacrica, commonly known as the purplehead centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinctive fuzzy texture. Its striking purple to pink flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea phalacrica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
