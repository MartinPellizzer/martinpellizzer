Carex leptostachys

Carex leptostachys, commonly known as slender-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil, typical of the sedge family.
