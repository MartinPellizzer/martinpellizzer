Chamomilla hookeri

Chamomilla hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Chamomilla hookeri is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
