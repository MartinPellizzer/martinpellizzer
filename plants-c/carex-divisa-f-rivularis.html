Carex divisa f. rivularis

Carex divisa f. rivularis, commonly known as the divided sedge.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered growth habit. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically green, with a distinct division in the leaf blade, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
