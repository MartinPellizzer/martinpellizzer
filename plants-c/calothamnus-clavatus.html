Calothamnus clavatus

Calothamnus clavatus, commonly known as the clustered bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in spring. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in coastal and inland regions.

Calothamnus clavatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
