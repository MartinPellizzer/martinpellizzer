Corydalis racemosa var. ecalcarata

Corydalis racemosa var. ecalcarata, commonly known as the scarlet lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
