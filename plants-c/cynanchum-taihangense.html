Cynanchum taihangense

Cynanchum taihangense, commonly known as the Taihang milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruit is a follicle containing numerous seeds. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
