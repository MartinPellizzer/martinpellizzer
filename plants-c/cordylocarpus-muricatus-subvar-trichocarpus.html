Cordylocarpus muricatus subvar. trichocarpus

Cordylocarpus muricatus subvar. trichocarpus, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spiky flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

This subvariety is often found in dune habitats and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
