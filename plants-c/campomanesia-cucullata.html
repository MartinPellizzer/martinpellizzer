Campomanesia cucullata

Campomanesia cucullata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or orange when mature. The fruit's skin is thick and waxy, giving it a distinctive appearance, while the pulp is juicy and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native region.
