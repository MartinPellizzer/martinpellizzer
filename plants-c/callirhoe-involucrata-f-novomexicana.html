Callirhoe involucrata f. novomexicana

Callirhoe involucrata f. novomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits called achenes, which are typically hairy and adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suited to arid and semi-arid environments.
