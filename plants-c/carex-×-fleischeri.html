Carex × fleischeri

Carex × fleischeri, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which includes a wide variety of sedges characterized by their grass-like appearance and spike-like flower clusters. This particular species is known for its ability to thrive in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Carex × fleischeri is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to various water conditions.
