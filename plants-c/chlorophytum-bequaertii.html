Chlorophytum bequaertii

Chlorophytum bequaertii, commonly known as the African spear lily, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in summer. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Chlorophytum bequaertii is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
