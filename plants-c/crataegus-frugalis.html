Crataegus frugalis

Crataegus frugalis, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus frugalis is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
