Carex parciflora

Carex parciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flower sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces three-sided, triangular stems and has a rhizomatous growth habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
