Coreopsis trilobata

Coreopsis trilobata, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis trilobata is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
