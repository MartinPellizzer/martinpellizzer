Campylotheca menziesii

Campylotheca menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' campylotheca, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along streams.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
