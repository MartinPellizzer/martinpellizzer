Cleomella arborea var. angustata

Cleomella arborea var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cleomella, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a distinctive papery bract. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes grown in gardens for its delicate floral display.
