Chenopodium albescens

Chenopodium albescens, commonly known as white goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is typically found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is an annual herb that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Chenopodium albescens is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
