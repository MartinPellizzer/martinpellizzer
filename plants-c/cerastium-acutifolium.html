Cerastium acutifolium

Cerastium acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves that are typically pointed at the tips, giving it its common name. Its flowers are white or pink, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.
