Cybianthus fendleri

Cybianthus fendleri, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and extreme temperatures. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water, while its stems are often hairy to reduce transpiration.

Cybianthus fendleri plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar and habitat for various desert wildlife.
