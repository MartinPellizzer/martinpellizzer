Cassia pilifera var. subglabra

Cassia pilifera var. subglabra, commonly known as silky cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, silky-haired leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, narrow pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
