Cissus pisicarpa

Cissus pisicarpa, commonly known as the sour fig or African sour orange, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, citrus-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are used for ornamental purposes, and the plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Cissus pisicarpa is a versatile species with applications in horticulture, medicine, and local cuisine.
