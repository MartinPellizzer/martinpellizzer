Cornus pumila

Cornus pumila, commonly known as smooth dogwood, is a low-growing shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Cornus pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
