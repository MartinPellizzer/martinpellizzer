Coptis daisetsuensis

Coptis daisetsuensis, commonly known as the Japanese goldthread, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five sepals and is typically found in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Coptis daisetsuensis is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of alkaloids in its roots.
