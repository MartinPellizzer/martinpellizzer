Cyperus cataphyllatus

Cyperus cataphyllatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can dominate aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Cyperus cataphyllatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
