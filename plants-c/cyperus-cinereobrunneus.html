Cyperus cinereobrunneus

Cyperus cinereobrunneus, commonly known as the gray-brown flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces light, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus cinereobrunneus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
