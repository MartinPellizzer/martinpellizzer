Calea axillaris

Calea axillaris, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calea axillaris is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine.
