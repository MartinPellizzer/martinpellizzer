Camellia obtusifolia

Camellia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically leathery and dark green. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

Camellia obtusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
