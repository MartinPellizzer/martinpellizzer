Coleus latifolius var. madiensis

Coleus latifolius var. madiensis, commonly known as the African shield leaf plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including green, red, and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in partial shade. Its foliage is often used in decorative gardening for its vibrant and unique appearance.

It is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ease of care in indoor environments.
