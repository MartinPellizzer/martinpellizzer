Carduus pycnocephalus subsp. pycnocephalus

Carduus pycnocephalus subsp.

pycnocephalus, commonly known as the thick-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, pale green to blue-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is known for its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
