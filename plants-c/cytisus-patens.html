Cytisus patens

Cytisus patens, commonly known as the creeping yellow crouch, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with slender, woody stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus patens is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
