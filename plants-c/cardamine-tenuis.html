Cardamine tenuis

Cardamine tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf water-cress, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.
