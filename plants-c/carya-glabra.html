Carya glabra

Carya glabra, commonly known as the bur oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thick, deeply furrowed bark and large, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces hard, spiny acorns that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Carya glabra is valued for its durability and is commonly used in landscaping and as a source of timber.
