Campanula rapunculoides f. crenata

Campanula rapunculoides f. crenata, commonly known as the Creased Bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically broad and crenate, giving the plant its specific epithet. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in garden settings.
