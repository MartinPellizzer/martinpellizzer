Carex pulla f. pedunculata

Carex pulla f. pedunculata, commonly known as the purple sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex pulla.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish stems and leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to yellowish flowers in the summer.

This sedgy plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique coloration and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
