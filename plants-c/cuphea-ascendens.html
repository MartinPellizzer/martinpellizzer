Cuphea ascendens

Cuphea ascendens, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange tubular flowers that resemble candles, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Cuphea ascendens is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 feet tall, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
