Castanopsis armata

Castanopsis armata, commonly known as the armed chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its hard, thick-shelled fruits. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a dense, rounded canopy and dark, glossy leaves. Its bark is typically thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.
