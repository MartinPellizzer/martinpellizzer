Camellia tachangensis var. tachangensis

Camellia tachangensis var. tachangensis, commonly known as the Tachang camellia, is a species native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its deep green, leathery leaves. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom in winter, making it a notable ornamental plant. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and have a distinctive, showy appearance.

Camellia tachangensis var. tachangensis is also valued for its potential in horticulture and as a source of tea.
