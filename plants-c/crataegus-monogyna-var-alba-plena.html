Crataegus monogyna var. alba-plena

Crataegus monogyna var. alba-plena, commonly known as the white-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its showy, double white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. It produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used for ornamental purposes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its spring blossoms.
