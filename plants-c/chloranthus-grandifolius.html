Chloranthus grandifolius

Chloranthus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is one of the smaller flowering plant families. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually large and ovate in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
