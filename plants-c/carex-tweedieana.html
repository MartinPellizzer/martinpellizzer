Carex tweedieana

Carex tweedieana, commonly known as the Tweedie's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to New Zealand, it typically grows in wetland or moist environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
