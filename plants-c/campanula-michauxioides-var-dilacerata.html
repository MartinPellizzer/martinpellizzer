Campanula michauxioides var. dilacerata

Campanula michauxioides var. dilacerata, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the stems are erect and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
