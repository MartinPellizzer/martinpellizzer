Ceropegia praetermissa

Ceropegia praetermissa, commonly known as the silver leaf or white milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky areas and along riverbanks. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in a silvery waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, white, and tubular, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators like flies and beetles.

Ceropegia praetermissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to survive in low-water conditions.
