Careya arborea

Careya arborea, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces black, hardy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its density and resistance to decay, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.
