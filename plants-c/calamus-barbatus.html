Calamus barbatus

Calamus barbatus, commonly known as the feathered canes or the barbate calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and used in traditional medicine.

Calamus barbatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
