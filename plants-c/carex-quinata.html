Carex quinata

Carex quinata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream edges. Its leaves are broad and flat, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

Carex quinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
