Campanula radchensis

Campanula radchensis, commonly known as the Radchenskya bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Campanula radchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
