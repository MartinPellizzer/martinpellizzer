Crocus reticulatus var. albicans

Crocus reticulatus var. albicans, commonly known as the white Persian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a prominent, showy flower that blooms in early spring. The plant produces a single, cup-shaped flower with a central stigma that is often more prominent than the petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
