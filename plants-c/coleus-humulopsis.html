Coleus humulopsis

Coleus humulopsis, commonly known as the purple leaf coleus, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from deep purples to bright reds and oranges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

While it does not produce showy flowers, its ornamental value lies primarily in its striking leaf patterns and colors.
