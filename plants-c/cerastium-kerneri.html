Cerastium kerneri

Cerastium kerneri, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover in cold climates.
