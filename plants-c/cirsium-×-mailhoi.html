Cirsium × mailhoi

Cirsium × mailhoi, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium palustre, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for various pollinators.
