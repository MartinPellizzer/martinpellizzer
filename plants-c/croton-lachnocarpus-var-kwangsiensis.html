Croton lachnocarpus var. kwangsiensis

Croton lachnocarpus var. kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
