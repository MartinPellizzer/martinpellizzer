Coffea herbacea

Coffea herbacea, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
