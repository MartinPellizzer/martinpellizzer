Claytonia calycotricha

Claytonia calycotricha, commonly known as the "hare's foot," is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
