Cerinthe glabra subsp. pirinica

Cerinthe glabra subsp.

pirinica, commonly known as the Pirin cerinthe, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
