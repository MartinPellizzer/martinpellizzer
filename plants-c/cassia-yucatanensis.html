Cassia yucatanensis

Cassia yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan cassia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Cassia yucatanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
