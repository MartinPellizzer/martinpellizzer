Celtis leveillei var. cuspidatophylla

Celtis leveillei var. cuspidatophylla, commonly known as the African boxthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

The species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its hardy nature and distinctive foliage.
