Calea quadrifolia

Calea quadrifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms. Calea quadrifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study and a popular ornamental plant.
