Caryophyllus carthusianorum

Caryophyllus carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian pink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
