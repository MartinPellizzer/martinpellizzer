Clerodendrum lasiocephalum

Clerodendrum lasiocephalum, commonly known as the woolly-headed clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
