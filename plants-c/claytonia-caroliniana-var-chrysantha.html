Claytonia caroliniana var. chrysantha

Claytonia caroliniana var. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a culinary plant in some regions.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
