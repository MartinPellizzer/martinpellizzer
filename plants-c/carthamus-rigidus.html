Carthamus rigidus

Carthamus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
