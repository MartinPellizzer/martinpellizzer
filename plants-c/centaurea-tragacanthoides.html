Centaurea tragacanthoides

Centaurea tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Persian globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped heads and bright blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Centaurea tragacanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
