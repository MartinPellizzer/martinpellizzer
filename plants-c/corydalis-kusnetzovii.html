Corydalis kusnetzovii

Corydalis kusnetzovii, commonly known as the Siberian fumitory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Corydalis kusnetzovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
