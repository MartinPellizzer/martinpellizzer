Conomorpha peruviana var. brasiliensis

Conomorpha peruviana var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in regions with low rainfall. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
