Cirsium × lojkae

Cirsium × lojkae, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. This hybrid plant is typically found in disturbed soils and open areas, such as roadsides and meadows. Its growth habit is upright, with a woody base that becomes more pronounced with age.

Cirsium × lojkae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
