Chaetochloa brevispica

Chaetochloa brevispica, commonly known as the short-bristled hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marsh environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and short, stiff bristles along the leaf sheaths. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Chaetochloa brevispica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
