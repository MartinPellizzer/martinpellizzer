Cremaspora microcarpa

Cremaspora microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf milkbush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its leaves contain a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
