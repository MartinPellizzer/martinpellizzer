Chamaemelum tchihatchewii

Chamaemelum tchihatchewii, commonly known as the Tchihatchew's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and steppe areas.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal practices.
