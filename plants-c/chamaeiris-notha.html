Chamaeiris notha

Chamaeiris notha, commonly known as the small-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Chamaeiris notha is often found growing on rocky slopes and in well-drained soils.
