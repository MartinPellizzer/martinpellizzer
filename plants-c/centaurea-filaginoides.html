Centaurea filaginoides

Centaurea filaginoides, commonly known as the filagrate bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features slender, branching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its flowers are small and blue to purple, often arranged in dense clusters.
