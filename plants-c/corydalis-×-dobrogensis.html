Corydalis × dobrogensis

Corydalis × dobrogensis, commonly known as the Dobrogian corydalis, is a hybrid species within the genus Corydalis, which belongs to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, often blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Corydalis × dobrogensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in cool climates.
