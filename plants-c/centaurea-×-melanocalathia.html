Centaurea × melanocalathia

Centaurea × melanocalathia, commonly known as the purplehead cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking deep purple to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by pale blue to white ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
