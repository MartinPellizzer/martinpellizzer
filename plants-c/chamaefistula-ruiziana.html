Chamaefistula ruiziana

Chamaefistula ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's thorny fiddlewood, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
