Campanula rigidipila

Campanula rigidipila, commonly known as the rigid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant features lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula rigidipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
