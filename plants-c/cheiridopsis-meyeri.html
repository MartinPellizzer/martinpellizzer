Cheiridopsis meyeri

Cheiridopsis meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the winter months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
