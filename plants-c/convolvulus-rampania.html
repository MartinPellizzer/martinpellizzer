Convolvulus rampania

Convolvulus rampania, commonly known as the spreading bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in a variety of soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
