Carthamus laevis

Carthamus laevis, commonly known as the smooth safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered with soft, hairy foliage and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers with prominent central disks. The plant is characterized by its large, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Carthamus laevis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its historical use in dyeing and medicinal applications.
