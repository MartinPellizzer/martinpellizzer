Cunninghamia sarmentosa

Cunninghamia sarmentosa, commonly known as the Chinese stone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its slow growth and distinctive appearance, featuring long, drooping branches and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
