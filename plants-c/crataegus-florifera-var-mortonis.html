Crataegus florifera var. mortonis

Crataegus florifera var. mortonis, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
