Ceropegia denticulata var. brownii

Ceropegia denticulata var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
