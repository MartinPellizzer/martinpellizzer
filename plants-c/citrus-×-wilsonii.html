Citrus × wilsonii

Citrus × wilsonii, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, orange-colored fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed whole, including the peel. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly grown in subtropical climates.

It is also used in traditional Chinese medicine and for its essential oils.
