Chaetogastra hispida

Chaetogastra hispida, commonly known as the rough leaved chaetogastra, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are clustered in dense heads, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
