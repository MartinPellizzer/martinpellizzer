Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. denudatum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. denudatum, commonly known as the slender leucanthemum, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
