Carex verrucosa

Carex verrucosa, commonly known as the warty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Carex verrucosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
