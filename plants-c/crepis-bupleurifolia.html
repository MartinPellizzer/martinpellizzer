Crepis bupleurifolia

Crepis bupleurifolia, commonly known as the bupleurifoliate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant has hairy stems and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold and dry climates.
