Crataegus macracantha

Crataegus macracantha, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptical leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red to orange berries that mature in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
