Carex phacota var. shichiseitensis

Carex phacota var. shichiseitensis, commonly known as the Shikishima sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
