Crossopteryx febrifuga

Crossopteryx febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
