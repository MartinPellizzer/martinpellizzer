Celastrus vaniotii

Celastrus vaniotii, commonly known as the Vaniot's staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.

However, it is important to note that it may have toxic properties, requiring caution in handling.
