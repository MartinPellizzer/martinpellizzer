Calappa sancona

Calappa sancona, commonly known as the broadleaf calappa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along the edges of dunes.

Calappa sancona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
