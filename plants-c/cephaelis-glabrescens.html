Cephaelis glabrescens

Cephaelis glabrescens, commonly known as the golden flame or red flame vine, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a climbing shrub that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically grown in gardens or as a houseplant.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
