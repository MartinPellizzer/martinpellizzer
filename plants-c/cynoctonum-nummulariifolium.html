Cynoctonum nummulariifolium

Cynoctonum nummulariifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces spiny, bract-covered flower clusters that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
