Cynoctonum nemorosum

Cynoctonum nemorosum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and often bioluminescent flowers that emit a faint glow in the dark. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitats. Cynoctonum nemorosum is primarily pollinated by nocturnal insects, such as moths and beetles, due to its night-blooming nature.

Its botanical features make it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists alike.
