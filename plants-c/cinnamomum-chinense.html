Cinnamomum chinense

Cinnamomum chinense, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is closely related to other cinnamon species. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed.
