Colubrina retusa var. latifolia

Colubrina retusa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 15 feet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its persistent fruit capsules and ability to attract pollinators.
