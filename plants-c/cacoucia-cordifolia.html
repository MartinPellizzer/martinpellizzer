Cacoucia cordifolia

Cacoucia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
