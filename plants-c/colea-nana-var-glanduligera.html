Colea nana var. glanduligera

Colea nana var. glanduligera, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are densely covered with glandular hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in alpine gardens.
