Cucumis melo convar. chandalak

Cucumis melo convar. chandalak, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a botanically distinct variety within the melon species.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a herbaceous vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, arid climates. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing genetic traits with other melons and cucumbers.

Its cultivation is often associated with traditional agricultural practices in regions like Iran and Central Asia.
