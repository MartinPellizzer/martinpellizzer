Coelogyne maleolens

Coelogyne maleolens, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, and its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients. It produces multiple flower spikes from the base of the plant, and the flowers bloom in clusters.

Coelogyne maleolens is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
