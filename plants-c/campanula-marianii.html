Campanula marianii

Campanula marianii, commonly known as the Marian's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Campanula marianii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
