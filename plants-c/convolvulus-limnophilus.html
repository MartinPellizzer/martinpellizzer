Convolvulus limnophilus

Convolvulus limnophilus, commonly known as the water bindweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found along riverbanks and lakeshores.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain areas if not managed properly.
