Conium maculatum subsp. croaticum

Conium maculatum subsp.

croaticum, commonly known as Croatian hemlock, is a toxic plant belonging to the Apiaceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, particularly coniine, which can be lethal to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded areas and is native to parts of Europe, including Croatia.

Due to its high toxicity, it is considered a dangerous weed and should be handled with caution.
