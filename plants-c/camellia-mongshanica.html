Camellia mongshanica

Camellia mongshanica, commonly known as the Mong Shan camellia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink, often with a contrasting yellow or white base. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Camellia mongshanica is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
