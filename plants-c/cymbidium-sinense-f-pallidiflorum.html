Cymbidium sinense f. pallidiflorum

Cymbidium sinense f. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered orchid, is a rare and elegant orchid species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a distinctive yellow lip, and bloom during the winter or early spring. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

It requires well-draining soil and moderate light conditions to thrive.
