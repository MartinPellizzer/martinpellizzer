Crocus aerius var. pulchricolor

Crocus aerius var. pulchricolor, commonly known as the violet crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, often darker center. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
