Connarus oblongus

Connarus oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf connarus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
