Cymbachne alata

Cymbachne alata, commonly known as the arrowhead grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cymbachne alata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
