Calytrix formosa

Calytrix formosa, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.

Calytrix formosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
