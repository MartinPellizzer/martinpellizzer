Carex triceps var. pedunculata

Carex triceps var. pedunculata, commonly known as the three-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to wetland vegetation structure.
