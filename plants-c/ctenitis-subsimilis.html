Ctenitis subsimilis

Ctenitis subsimilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

The fronds are typically bright green and can be used as a decorative element in gardens or indoor spaces.
