Calamagrostis griffithii

Calamagrostis griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's reed, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering spikes are usually pinkish or purplish, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Calamagrostis griffithii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or arid environments.
