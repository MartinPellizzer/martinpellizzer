Croton sphaerogynus

Croton sphaerogynus, commonly known as the globe-headed croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, globe-shaped flower heads that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Croton sphaerogynus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
