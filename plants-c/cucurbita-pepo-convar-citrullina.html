Cucurbita pepo convar. citrullina

Cucurbita pepo convar. citrullina, commonly known as the watermelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces large, fleshy fruits with a high water content. The plant has lobed leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing and supporting its heavy fruit. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.

The fruit's outer rind is typically green and turns yellow or striped as it matures.
