Carex orsiniana

Carex orsiniana, commonly known as the Sierra marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex orsiniana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
