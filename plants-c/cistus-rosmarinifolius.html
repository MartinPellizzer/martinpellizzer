Cistus rosmarinifolius

Cistus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, evergreen leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, resembling rosemary. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky slopes.

Cistus rosmarinifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
