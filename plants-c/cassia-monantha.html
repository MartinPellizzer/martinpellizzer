Cassia monantha

Cassia monantha, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. Cassia monantha thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
