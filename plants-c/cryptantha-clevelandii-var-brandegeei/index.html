Cryptantha Clevelandii Var. Brandegeei

Cleveland's cryptantha, scientifically known as Cryptantha clevelandii var. brandegeei, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the San Francisco Bay Area. This endemic species thrives in the unique microclimates of coastal bluffs and rocky outcrops along the northern California coast. Its small, violet-blue flowers bloom in late spring, adding a striking touch of color to the local landscape. Due to habitat loss and urban development, the plant faces increasing threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are underway to protect and preserve this rare botanical gem.
