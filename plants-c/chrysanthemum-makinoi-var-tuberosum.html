Chrysanthemum makinoi var. tuberosum

Chrysanthemum makinoi var. tuberosum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum or Makino's chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features robust, upright stems and produces daisy-like flowers in a range of colors, including yellow, white, and red. The plant has lobed, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its tuberous roots are notable for their medicinal and ornamental uses in traditional practices.
