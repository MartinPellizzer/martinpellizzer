Chrysocoma dracunculoides

Chrysocoma dracunculoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
