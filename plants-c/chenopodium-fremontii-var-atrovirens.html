Chenopodium fremontii var. atrovirens

Chenopodium fremontii var. atrovirens, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss and enhancing drought tolerance.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it a common pioneer plant in desert ecosystems.
