Cassia catingae

Cassia catingae, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cassia catingae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
