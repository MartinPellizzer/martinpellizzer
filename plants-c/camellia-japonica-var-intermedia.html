Camellia japonica var. intermedia

Camellia japonica var. intermedia, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white, often with a contrasting central band. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the landscape.
