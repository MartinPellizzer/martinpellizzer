Caladium virginicum

Caladium virginicum, commonly known as the purpleheart caladium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display a range of colors including purple, red, and green. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant foliage.

The leaves are edible when cooked, though they are more commonly appreciated for their aesthetic value.
