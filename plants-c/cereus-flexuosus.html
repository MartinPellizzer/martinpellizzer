Cereus flexuosus

Cereus flexuosus, commonly known as the Christmas Cactus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often brightly colored. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom during the holiday season, hence its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cereus flexuosus is a popular ornamental plant due to its long blooming period and attractive appearance.
