Cyperus carnosus

Cyperus carnosus, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Cyperus carnosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
