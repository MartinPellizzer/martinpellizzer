Crataegus × corniculata

Crataegus × corniculata, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and red berries.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces thorned branches, which are typical features of the Crataegus genus. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.

The fruit, known as a haw, is a important food source for birds and wildlife.
