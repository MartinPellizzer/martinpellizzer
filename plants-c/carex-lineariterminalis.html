Carex lineariterminalis

Carex lineariterminalis, commonly known as the narrow-tipped sedge, is a perennial plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes or along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Carex lineariterminalis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
