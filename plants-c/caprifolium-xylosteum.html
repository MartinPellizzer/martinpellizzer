Caprifolium xylosteum

Caprifolium xylosteum, commonly known as the woodbine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, fragrant, tubular flower that is typically pink or purple. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Caprifolium xylosteum thrives in woodland areas and is often found along hedgerows and in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
