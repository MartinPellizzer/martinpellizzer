Cryptocoryne pallidinervia

Cryptocoryne pallidinervia, commonly known as the pale-veined cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green with distinct, pale veins. This plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it forms dense underwater colonies. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, anchoring it to the substrate and facilitating vegetative reproduction.

Cryptocoryne pallidinervia is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
