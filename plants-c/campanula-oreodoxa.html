Campanula oreodoxa

Campanula oreodoxa, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often glandular. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Campanula oreodoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
