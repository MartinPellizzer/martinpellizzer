Cupressus madeirensis

Cupressus madeirensis, commonly known as the Madeira cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Azores.

It has a tall, slender trunk with a conical shape and is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are dense and dark green. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance.

Cupressus madeirensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its use in landscaping.
