Caryota alberti

Caryota alberti, commonly known as the Albert's fishtail palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that resemble a fishtail, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that eventually develop into clustered, spiny fruits. Caryota alberti thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
