Centaurea × delicata

Centaurea × delicata, commonly known as the pink cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
