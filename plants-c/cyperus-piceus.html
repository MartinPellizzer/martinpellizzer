Cyperus piceus

Cyperus piceus, commonly known as the purple rush, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or purple in color. Cyperus piceus thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and riverbanks.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soil.
