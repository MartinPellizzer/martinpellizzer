Chassalia psychotrioides

Chassalia psychotrioides, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
