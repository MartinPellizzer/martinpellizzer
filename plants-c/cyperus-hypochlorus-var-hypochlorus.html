Cyperus hypochlorus var. hypochlorus

Cyperus hypochlorus var. hypochlorus, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the base of the plant. It thrives in wet or disturbed soils, often found in marshy areas, along waterways, and in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an invasive species in many regions.
