Carex pensylvanica var. vespertina

Carex pensylvanica var. vespertina, commonly known as the evening sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas, along stream banks, and in shaded woodlands. Its leaves are grass-like, slender, and usually have a bluish-green hue, while its flowering spikes are usually clustered and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
