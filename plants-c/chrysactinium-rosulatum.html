Chrysactinium rosulatum

Chrysactinium rosulatum, commonly known as the roseleaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

Chrysactinium rosulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
