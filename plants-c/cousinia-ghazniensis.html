Cousinia ghazniensis

Cousinia ghazniensis, commonly known as the Ghazni cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typically blooming in the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture.

Cousinia ghazniensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying drought-resistant flora.
