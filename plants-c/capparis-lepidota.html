Capparis lepidota

Capparis lepidota, commonly known as the rough caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces round, green, edible fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Capparis lepidota thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in shrubland and coastal dunes.
