Camellia thanxaensa

Camellia thanxaensa, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant is part of the Theaceae family and is closely related to camellias and tea plants. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia thanxaensa is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
