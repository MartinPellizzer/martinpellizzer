Camissoniopsis guadalupensis subsp. clementina

Camissoniopsis guadalupensis subsp.

clementina, commonly known as the Guadalupe sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
