Cassia arayatensis

Cassia arayatensis, commonly known as the Arayat cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The bark and leaves of Cassia arayatensis have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
