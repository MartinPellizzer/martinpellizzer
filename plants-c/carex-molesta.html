Carex molesta

Carex molesta, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex molesta is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
