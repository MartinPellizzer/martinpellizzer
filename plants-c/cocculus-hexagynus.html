Cocculus hexagynus

Cocculus hexagynus, commonly known as the devil's-bit cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, spherical fruit that resembles a small, rough ball. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a waxy coating.

Cocculus hexagynus is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.
