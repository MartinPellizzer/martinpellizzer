Carya tonkinensis

Carya tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin hickory, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and southern China.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same tree, and it produces fruit in the fall.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
