Calacinum diclinum

Calacinum diclinum, commonly known as the twin-flower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a deep green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, with separate male and female flowers on different plants, a characteristic known as dioecy. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
