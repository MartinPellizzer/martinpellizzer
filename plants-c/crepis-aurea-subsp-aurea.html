Crepis aurea subsp. aurea

Crepis aurea subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its stems are upright and branched, supporting the inflorescence of yellow disk flowers.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and along roadsides.
