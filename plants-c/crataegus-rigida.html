Crataegus rigida

Crataegus rigida, commonly known as the thorny hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its stiff, thorny branches and small, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Crataegus rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
