Cleomella cornuta

Cleomella cornuta, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like calyces that enclose the flowers. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Cleomella cornuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
