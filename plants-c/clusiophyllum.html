Clusiophyllum

Clusiophyllum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow-orange flowers that appear in clusters, often giving the plant a fiery appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a smooth texture with a glossy surface. Clusiophyllum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
