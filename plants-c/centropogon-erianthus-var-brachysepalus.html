Centropogon erianthus var. brachysepalus

Centropogon erianthus var. brachysepalus, commonly known as the short-sepal centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers or in shaded forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections in warm regions.
