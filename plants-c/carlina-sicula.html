Carlina sicula

Carlina sicula, commonly known as Sicilian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Carlina sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
