Coleus dewildemanianus

Coleus dewildemanianus, commonly known as the dewildemanian coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple, often contrasting with its broad, green foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
