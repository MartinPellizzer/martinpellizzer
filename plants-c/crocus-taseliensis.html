Crocus taseliensis

Crocus taseliensis, commonly known as the Taseli crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow, and pointed shape. Its corms are small and rounded, typical of many crocus species, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
