Cobaea lutea f. viorna

Cobaea lutea f. viorna, commonly known as the golden cohosh, is a striking flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
