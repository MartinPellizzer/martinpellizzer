Coreopsis sambucifolia

Coreopsis sambucifolia, commonly known as the sambucus-leaved coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which resemble those of the European elderberry (Sambucus), hence its common name. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in clustered heads, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is usually palmately lobed, adding a unique visual appeal to gardens.

Coreopsis sambucifolia is often grown for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
