Cyrtorchis subcylindrifolia

Cyrtorchis subcylindrifolia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It bears small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its delicate appearance.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
