Cephalostachyum chapelieri

Cephalostachyum chapelieri, commonly known as the golden bamboo or chapelier's bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking color and elegant appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Cephalostachyum chapelieri is a perennial plant that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a notable feature in tropical gardens.
