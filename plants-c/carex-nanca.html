Carex nanca

Carex nanca, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. Carex nanca is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
