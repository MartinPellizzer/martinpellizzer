Corybas vespertilionis

Corybas vespertilionis, commonly known as the bat orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flower that resembles a bat, hence its common name. The plant is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests or undergrowth. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, including flies and beetles, which are attracted to its scent and color.

Corybas vespertilionis is a terrestrial orchid with a short, tuberous rhizome and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.
