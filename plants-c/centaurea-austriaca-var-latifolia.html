Centaurea austriaca var. latifolia

Centaurea austriaca var. latifolia, commonly known as the Austrian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, often found in alpine regions.
