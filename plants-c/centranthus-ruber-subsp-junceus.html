Centranthus ruber subsp. junceus

Centranthus ruber subsp.

junceus, commonly known as red valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular red or pink flowers.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
