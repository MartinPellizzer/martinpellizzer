Campylotropis latifolia

Campylotropis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Campylotropis latifolia is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in traditional medicine.
