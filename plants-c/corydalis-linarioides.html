Corydalis linarioides

Corydalis linarioides, commonly known as the creeping yellow corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with two upper petals and two lower ones, typical of the genus Corydalis. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often used in garden settings for its vibrant color and foliage.
