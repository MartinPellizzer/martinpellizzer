Campanula hybrida

Campanula hybrida, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Campanula hybrida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
