Carduus × groedigensis

Carduus × groedigensis, commonly known as the hybrid common thistle.

This plant is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carduus benedictus and Carduus nutans, resulting in a species with characteristics of both parents. It is a perennial herb with spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers, typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species in the Asteraceae family, showcasing genetic diversity in thistle populations.
