Carduus acuatus

Carduus acuatus, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and wastelands. Its leaves are spiny and covered in fine hairs, while its purple flowers bloom in late summer.

The plant is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
