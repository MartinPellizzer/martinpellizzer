Cirsium nebraskense

Cirsium nebraskense, commonly known as the Nebraska thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and are often covered in sharp prickles.

Cirsium nebraskense plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a valuable component of native plant communities.
