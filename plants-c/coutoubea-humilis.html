Coutoubea humilis

Coutoubea humilis, commonly known as the dwarf trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Coutoubea humilis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
