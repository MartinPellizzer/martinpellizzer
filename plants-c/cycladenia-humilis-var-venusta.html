Cycladenia humilis var. venusta

Cycladenia humilis var. venusta, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has slender, creeping stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and sought-after species in native plant gardens.
