Corytholoma gracile

Corytholoma gracile, commonly known as the slender cap mushroom, is a basidiomycete fungus belonging to the family Tricholomataceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate cap that is typically pale yellow to ochraceous in color. The gills are closely spaced and initially pale, becoming darker with age. The stem is slender and often slightly curved, with a fibrous surface.

This species is found in temperate regions, often growing in association with decaying wood or leaf litter.
