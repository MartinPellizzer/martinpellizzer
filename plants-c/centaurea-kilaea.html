Centaurea kilaea

Centaurea kilaea, commonly known as the Cyprus star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its purple to blue flower heads that resemble stars. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a sprawling growth habit with stiff, spiny leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea kilaea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
