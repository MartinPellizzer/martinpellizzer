Clitoria glycinoides var. aurantiaca

Clitoria glycinoides var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-like purple or yellow flowers. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye due to their vibrant color and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
