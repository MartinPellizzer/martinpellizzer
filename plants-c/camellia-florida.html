Camellia florida

Camellia florida, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Camellia florida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
