Conophytum sulcatum

Conophytum sulcatum, commonly known as the "Corky Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal watering.

Conophytum sulcatum is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
