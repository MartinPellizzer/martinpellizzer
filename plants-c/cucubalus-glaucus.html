Cucubalus glaucus

Cucubalus glaucus, commonly known as the devil's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant's fruit is a large, spiny capsule that contains several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and potential toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
