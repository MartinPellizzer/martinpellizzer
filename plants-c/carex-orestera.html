Carex orestera

Carex orestera, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

Carex orestera is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
