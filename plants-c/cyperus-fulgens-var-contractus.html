Cyperus fulgens var. contractus

Cyperus fulgens var. contractus, commonly known as the compact flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender, triangular stems and small, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, pale brown to reddish seeds.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
