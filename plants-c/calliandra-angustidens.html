Calliandra angustidens

Calliandra angustidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf calliandra, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, papery flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, arid climates.

Calliandra angustidens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
