Campanula intybus

Campanula intybus, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Campanula intybus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
