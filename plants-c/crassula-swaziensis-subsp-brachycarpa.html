Crassula swaziensis subsp. brachycarpa

Crassula swaziensis subsp.

brachycarpa, commonly known as the Swazi crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, dense clusters of leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
