Cirrhopetalum stramineum

Cirrhopetalum stramineum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that resemble bells, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are elongated and have a smooth texture, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
