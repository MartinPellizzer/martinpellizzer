Cystium griseolum

Cystium griseolum, commonly known as gray thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features small, grayish-green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale green, and clustered in umbels at the tips of the branches.

Cystium griseolum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in areas with high humidity.
