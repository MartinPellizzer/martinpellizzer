Chrysopogon fulvus var. tremulus

Chrysopogon fulvus var. tremulus, commonly known as the trembling golden grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often exhibit a slight tremulous movement in the wind. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
