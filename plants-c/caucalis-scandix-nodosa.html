Caucalis scandix-nodosa

Caucalis scandix-nodosa, commonly known as the nodose climbing plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems that climb by means of tendrils, often found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Caucalis scandix-nodosa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
