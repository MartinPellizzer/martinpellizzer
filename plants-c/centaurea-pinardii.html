Centaurea pinardii

Centaurea pinardii, commonly known as the Pinard's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
