Coleus matopensis

Coleus matopensis, commonly known as the Philippine coleus, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. It typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive coloration.

Coleus matopensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
