Catasetum integerrimum var. purpurascens

Catasetum integerrimum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple-tinged Catasetum, is a flowering orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange with a distinctive purple or reddish tinge on the lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is a raceme of fragrant, bilateral flowers, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and unique morphology.
