Capparis velloziana

Capparis velloziana, commonly known as the Brazilian mallow or velvet bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate or lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis velloziana is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
