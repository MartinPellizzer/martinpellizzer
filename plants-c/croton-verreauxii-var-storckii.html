Croton verreauxii var. storckii

Croton verreauxii var. storckii, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a combination of yellow, red, and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
