Crassula hemisphaerica

Crassula hemisphaerica, commonly known as the African snowball, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, blue-green leaves that form dense clusters. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
