Crepis pungens

Crepis pungens, commonly known as the spiked cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Crepis pungens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
