Calycanthus fertilis

Calycanthus fertilis, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, fragrant flowers that are typically reddish-purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Calycanthus fertilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
