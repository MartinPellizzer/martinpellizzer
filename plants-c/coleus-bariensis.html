Coleus bariensis

Coleus bariensis, commonly known as the red curl coleus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often features shades of red, purple, and green. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink.

Coleus bariensis is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions.
