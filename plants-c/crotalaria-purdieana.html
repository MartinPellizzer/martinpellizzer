Crotalaria purdieana

Crotalaria purdieana, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring palmate leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often surrounded by distinctive leaf-like bracts. Its seeds are encased in hard, spiny pods, which help in seed dispersal.

The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
