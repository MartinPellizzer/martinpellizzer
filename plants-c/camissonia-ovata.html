Camissonia ovata

Camissonia ovata, commonly known as the slender camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Camissoniaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Camissonia ovata is an important species for pollinators and is often found in coastal and inland regions with suitable soil conditions.
