Caesalpinia pinnata

Caesalpinia pinnata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flame-like inflorescences that bloom in the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in elongated, cylindrical spikes. It produces long, narrow pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia pinnata is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
