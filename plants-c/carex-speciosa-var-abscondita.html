Carex speciosa var. abscondita

Carex speciosa var. abscondita, commonly known as the hidden beauty sedge, is a striking sedge species native to the wetlands of North America.

It features tall, slender stems that produce dense clusters of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often coated with a fine, silvery bloom, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
