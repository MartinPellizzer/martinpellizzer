Canavalia pilosa

Canavalia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in disturbed habitats.

Canavalia pilosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
