Centaurea attica subsp. asperula

Centaurea attica subsp.

asperula, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, conical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
