Cereus melanotrichus

Cereus melanotrichus, commonly known as the black-haired cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dark green stems and spiny, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a dense covering of fine, black bristles, which give it its common name. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, making it a notable plant in desert ecosystems.
