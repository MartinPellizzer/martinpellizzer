Cypripedium × wenqingiae

Cypripedium × wenqingiae, commonly known as the Wenqingiae lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that serves as a pollination chamber. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
