Campanula filicaulis subsp. filicaulis

Campanula filicaulis subsp.

filicaulis, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
