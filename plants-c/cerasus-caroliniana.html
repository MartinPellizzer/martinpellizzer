Cerasus caroliniana

Cerasus caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina cherry or the wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red drupes that are often used in jams and preserves.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
