Cerasus shikokuensis

Cerasus shikokuensis, commonly known as the Shikoku cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has oval, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The fruit, a small cherry, is edible but less commonly cultivated for commercial use.
