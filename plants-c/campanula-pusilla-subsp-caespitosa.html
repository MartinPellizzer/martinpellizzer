Campanula pusilla subsp. caespitosa

Campanula pusilla subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August.

The plant has compact, mat-forming growth habit with short, creeping stems and hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wet meadow habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
