Cirsium × grenieri

Cirsium × grenieri, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the United States, particularly in the western regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its morphology includes a robust stem with rough, serrated leaves, and it produces a cluster of small, showy flowers at the top.

Cirsium × grenieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
