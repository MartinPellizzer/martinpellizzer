Cereus hoffmannseggii

Cereus hoffmannseggii, commonly known as the Hoffmannsegg's cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, spindly stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features arecent, spiny stems and produces large, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
