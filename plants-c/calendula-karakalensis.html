Calendula karakalensis

Calendula karakalensis, commonly known as the Karakalinsky marigold, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
