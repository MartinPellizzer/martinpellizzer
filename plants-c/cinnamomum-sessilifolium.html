Cinnamomum sessilifolium

Cinnamomum sessilifolium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a flowering plant native to southern China, Vietnam, and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and have a distinct cinnamon scent. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, contain compounds like cinnamaldehyde and eugenol.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
