Campanula balcanica subf. normalis

Campanula balcanica subf.

normalis, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
