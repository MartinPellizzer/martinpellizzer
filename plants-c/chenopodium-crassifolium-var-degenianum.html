Chenopodium crassifolium var. degenianum

Chenopodium crassifolium var. degenianum, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its resilience, it is often considered a pioneer species in desert ecosystems.
