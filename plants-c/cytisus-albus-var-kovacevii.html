Cytisus albus var. kovacevii

Cytisus albus var. kovacevii, commonly known as the white broom, is a variant of the common broom, characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
