Calliandra pedicellata

Calliandra pedicellata, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.
