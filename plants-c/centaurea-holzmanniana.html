Centaurea holzmanniana

Centaurea holzmanniana, commonly known as the Holzmann's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea holzmanniana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
