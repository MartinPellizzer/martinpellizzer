Crepis bermejana

Crepis bermejana, commonly known as the Bermejana mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has basal leaves that are rough and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Crepis bermejana is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
