Chlorocardium

Chlorocardium, commonly known as the bluebell or false bluebell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its beauty and ease of cultivation, it is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
