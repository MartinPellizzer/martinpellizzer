Callitris acuminata

Callitris acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cypress, is a species of coniferous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

The tree plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
