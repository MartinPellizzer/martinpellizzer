Ceradenia arthrothrix

Ceradenia arthrothrix, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive violet to purple coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Ceradenia arthrothrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.
