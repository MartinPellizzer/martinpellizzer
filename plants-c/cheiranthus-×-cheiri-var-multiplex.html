Cheiranthus × cheiri var. multiplex

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. multiplex, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, four-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It produces small, rounded seeds in a capsule and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are basal and linear, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its adaptability in harsh environments.
