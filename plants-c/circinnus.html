Circinnus

Circinnus, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling arrangement of leaflets, which give it a crown-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bright green with a slightly waxy texture, and they can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Circinnus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
