Cirsium conspicuum

Cirsium conspicuum, commonly known as the showy thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

Cirsium conspicuum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
