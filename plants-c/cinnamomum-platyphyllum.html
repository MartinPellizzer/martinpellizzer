Cinnamomum platyphyllum

Cinnamomum platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, flat leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark purple berries containing winged seeds. Its bark is aromatic and has been used traditionally in medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
