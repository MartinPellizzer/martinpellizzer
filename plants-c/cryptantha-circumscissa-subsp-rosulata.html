Cryptantha circumscissa subsp. rosulata

Cryptantha circumscissa subsp.

rosulata, commonly known as the Rosulate Cryptantha, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
