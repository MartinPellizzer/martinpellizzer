Carex preissii

Carex preissii, commonly known as the Preiss' sedge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage is often bright green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Carex preissii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
