Cestichis sympodialis

Cestichis sympodialis, commonly known as the slender sympodial cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its branching, stem-like structure that grows from a central caudex. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Cestichis sympodialis is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can store water in its thickened stems.
