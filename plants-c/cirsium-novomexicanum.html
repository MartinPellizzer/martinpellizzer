Cirsium novomexicanum

Cirsium novomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cirsium novomexicanum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
