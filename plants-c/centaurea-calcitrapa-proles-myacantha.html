Centaurea calcitrapa proles myacantha

Centaurea calcitrapa proles myacantha, commonly known as the Myacanthus Centaury, is a flowering plant species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive central disk.
