Cucubalus spicatus

Cucubalus spicatus, commonly known as the spiny cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds. The plant is characterized by its trailing vines, hairy stems, and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces elongated, spiny fruits that are edible when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
