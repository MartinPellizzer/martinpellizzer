Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. lapeyrouseanum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. lapeyrouseanum, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's heartleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as sagebrush steppe.
