Crataegus vitellina

Crataegus vitellina, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers and red berries, which appear in the fall. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorned branches. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The yellow hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
