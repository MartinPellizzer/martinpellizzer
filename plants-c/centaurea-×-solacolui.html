Centaurea × solacolui

Centaurea × solacolui, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea solstitialis (yellow starthistle). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–100 cm, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
