Crataegus rhombifolia

Crataegus rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sunlight.

Crataegus rhombifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
