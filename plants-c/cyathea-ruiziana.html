Cyathea ruiziana

Cyathea ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in lowland forests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant is known for its slow growth rate.

Cyathea ruiziana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
