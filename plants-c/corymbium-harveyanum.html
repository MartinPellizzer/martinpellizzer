Corymbium harveyanum

Corymbium harveyanum, commonly known as the Harvey's corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are clustered in corymbs, which are flat-topped or rounded inflorescences, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Corymbium harveyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
