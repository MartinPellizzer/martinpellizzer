Carlina cynara

Carlina cynara, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its showy purple-blue flower heads are surrounded by spiny bracts, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Carlina cynara is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
