Celtis tala var. weddelliana

Celtis tala var. weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The small, white flowers are followed by spherical, blue-black berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
