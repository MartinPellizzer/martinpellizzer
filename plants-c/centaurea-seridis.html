Centaurea seridis

Centaurea seridis, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Centaurea seridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
