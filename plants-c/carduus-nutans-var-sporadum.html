Carduus nutans var. sporadum

Carduus nutans var. sporadum, commonly known as the nodding thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of purple to pink flowers, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are deeply lobed, with a rough, hairy texture, and often have a spiny margin.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
