Cousinia harazensis

Cousinia harazensis, commonly known as the Haraz mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and narrow, aiding in water conservation.

Cousinia harazensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
