Carex fauriei

Carex fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
