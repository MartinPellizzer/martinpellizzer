Carex hypsipedos

Carex hypsipedos, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, upright leaves and clustered spikelets that form its inflorescence. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex hypsipedos is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
