Crotalaria filipes

Crotalaria filipes, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Crotalaria filipes is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its hairy foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
