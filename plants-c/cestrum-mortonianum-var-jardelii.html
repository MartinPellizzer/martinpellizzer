Cestrum mortonianum var. jardelii

Cestrum mortonianum var. jardelii, commonly known as the Jardel's white angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, its berries are toxic and should be handled with care.
