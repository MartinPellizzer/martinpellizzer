Cymbopogon hookeri

Cymbopogon hookeri, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

Cymbopogon hookeri is often used in ornamental gardening for its decorative appearance and drought tolerance.
