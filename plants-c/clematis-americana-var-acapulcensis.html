Clematis americana var. acapulcensis

Clematis americana var. acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Central America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements contribute to its popularity in horticultural settings.
