Cyperus umbellatus

Cyperus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, umbrella-shaped clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. Cyperus umbellatus typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
