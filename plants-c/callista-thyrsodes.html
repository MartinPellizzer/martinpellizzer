Callista thyrsodes

Callista thyrsodes, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slight pubescence.

Callista thyrsodes thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
