Crypsinopsis albidopaleata

Crypsinopsis albidopaleata, commonly known as the white-pale sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
