Cactus triangularis var. foliaceus

Cactus triangularis var. foliaceus, commonly known as the leafy cactus or the foliaceus cactus, is a unique hybrid species that combines features of both cacti and leafy succulents.

It originates from the Canary Islands and is characterized by its flattened, leaf-like stems that perform photosynthesis. The plant has spiny edges and produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
