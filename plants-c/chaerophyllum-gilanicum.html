Chaerophyllum gilanicum

Chaerophyllum gilanicum, commonly known as Gilanian cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
