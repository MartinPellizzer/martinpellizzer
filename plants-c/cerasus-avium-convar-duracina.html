Cerasus avium convar. duracina

Cerasus avium convar. duracina, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a variant of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark purple berries. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces fragrant white flowers in spring.

This convarietal is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in hedging or as a specimen plant.
