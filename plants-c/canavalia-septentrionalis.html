Canavalia septentrionalis

Canavalia septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern bean or horse bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
