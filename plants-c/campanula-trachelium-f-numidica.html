Campanula trachelium f. numidica

Campanula trachelium f. numidica, commonly known as the Numidian bellflower, is a compact, perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its charming floral display.
