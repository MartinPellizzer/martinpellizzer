Crocus vernus var. stylosus

Crocus vernus var. stylosus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape that distinguishes it from other crocus species. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate appearance.
