Cereus euphorbioides

Cereus euphorbioides, commonly known as the yellow star cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss.

Cereus euphorbioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
