Clematis speciosa

Clematis speciosa, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into blue or black berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
