Campanula lobata

Campanula lobata, commonly known as the bellflower or lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows in clumps, often reaching heights of up to 30 inches. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
