Centaurea phaeopappa subsp. jiroftensis

Centaurea phaeopappa subsp.

jiroftensis, commonly known as the Jiroft centaury, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple to reddish flower heads.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bracteate flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, semi-desert environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
