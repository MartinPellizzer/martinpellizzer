Coutarea latiflora

Coutarea latiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved coutarea, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Coutarea latiflora is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
