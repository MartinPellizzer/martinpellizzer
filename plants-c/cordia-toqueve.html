Cordia toqueve

Cordia toqueve, commonly known as the red cedar or red gum, is a tropical tree native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.
