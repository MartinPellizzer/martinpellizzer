Cynanchum jumellei

Cynanchum jumellei, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic to many animals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pink or purple, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cynanchum jumellei is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
