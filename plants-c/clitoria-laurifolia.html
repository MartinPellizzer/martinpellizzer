Clitoria laurifolia

Clitoria laurifolia, commonly known as the sacred bean or blue pea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in racemes, often with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are rich in anthocyanins, giving them their vibrant color and making them popular in natural dyeing.

Clitoria laurifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
