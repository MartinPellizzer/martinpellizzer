Carex japoniciformis

Carex japoniciformis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Carex japoniciformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
