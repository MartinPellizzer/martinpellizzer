Cissampelos pareira subvar. crassifolia

Cissampelos pareira subvar. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed moon vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces yellow, tubular flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
