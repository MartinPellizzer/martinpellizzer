Cyperus lanceolatus

Cyperus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in two rows. Cyperus lanceolatus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, upright appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
