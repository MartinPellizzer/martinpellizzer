Carex pensylvanica f. gracilifolia

Carex pensylvanica f. gracilifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
