Carduus laxiflorus

Carduus laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, fluffy flower heads that bloom in the summer. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
