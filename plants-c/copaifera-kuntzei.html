Copaifera kuntzei

Copaifera kuntzei, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces a resin that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its bark and resin contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
