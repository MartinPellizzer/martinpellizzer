Cordia caffra

Cordia caffra, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy when young and turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, pinkish-red fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Cordia caffra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
