Chenopodium lycioides

Chenopodium lycioides, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Chenopodium lycioides is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other economically important plants.
