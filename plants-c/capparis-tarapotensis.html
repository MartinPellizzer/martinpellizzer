Capparis tarapotensis

Capparis tarapotensis, commonly known as the Andean sourberry, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various shrubs and herbs characterized by their often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sour-tasting berries. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to the dry, high-altitude environments where it thrives.

Capparis tarapotensis is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional Andean cuisine and medicine.
