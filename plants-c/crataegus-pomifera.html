Crataegus pomifera

Crataegus pomifera, commonly known as the European hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, apple-like fruits called pomes, which are typically red or orange when ripe.

Crataegus pomifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
