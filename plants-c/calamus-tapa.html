Calamus tapa

Calamus tapa, commonly known as the white bamboo or sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in clusters, with long, narrow leaves that are typically aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, followed by cylindrical, segmented fruits. Calamus tapa is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
