Cephalaria amplexicaulis

Cephalaria amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are often clasping around the stem, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
