Calycolpus australis

Calycolpus australis, commonly known as the southern calycolpus, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

Calycolpus australis is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is often found in maritime climates.
