Cuphea virgata

Cuphea virgata, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, drought-tolerant plants.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
