Cubeba sumatrana

Cubeba sumatrana, commonly known as cubeb or Java pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green flowers that develop into the aromatic fruits. Its roots and berries contain essential oils and are used for their medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Cubeba sumatrana thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic and therapeutic uses.
