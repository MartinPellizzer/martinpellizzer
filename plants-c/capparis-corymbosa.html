Capparis corymbosa

Capparis corymbosa, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring woody stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when ripe.

The plant is characterized by its thorny branches and is often found in dry, rocky areas.
