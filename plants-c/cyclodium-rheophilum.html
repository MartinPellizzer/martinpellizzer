Cyclodium rheophilum

Cyclodium rheophilum, commonly known as the river cyclodium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Cyclodium rheophilum plays a role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
