Carex laxiflora var. ormostachya

Carex laxiflora var. ormostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with a distinct texture that distinguishes it from other sedges.
