Coccinia helenae

Coccinia helenae, commonly known as Helen's morning glory, is a tropical vine native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
