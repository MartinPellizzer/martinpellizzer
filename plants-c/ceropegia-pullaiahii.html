Ceropegia pullaiahii

Ceropegia pullaiahii, commonly known as the Malabar milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ceropegia pullaiahii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
