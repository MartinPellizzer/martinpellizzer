Ceanothus leschenaultii

Ceanothus leschenaultii, commonly known as the bluecurls, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its clusters of sky-blue, curling flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Ceanothus leschenaultii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
