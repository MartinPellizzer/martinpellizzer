Crotalaria boehmii

Crotalaria boehmii, commonly known as the Boehm's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
