Carex multipes

Carex multipes, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typically in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex multipes plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
