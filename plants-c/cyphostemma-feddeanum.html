Cyphostemma feddeanum

Cyphostemma feddeanum, commonly known as the desert milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Cyphostemma feddeanum is also known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
