Cissampelos cocculus

Cissampelos cocculus, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
