Catasetum multifidum

Catasetum multifidum, commonly known as the multifidum orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is notable for its unique pollination mechanism, involving specialized structures that facilitate insect pollination.
