Coleus pallidiflorus

Coleus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered coleus, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from white to pink and purple. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Coleus pallidiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
