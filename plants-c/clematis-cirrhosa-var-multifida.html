Clematis cirrhosa var. multifida

Clematis cirrhosa var. multifida, commonly known as the fringed clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The leaves are compound, with finely divided, feathery lobes that give the plant a delicate appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or fences with its cascading blooms.
