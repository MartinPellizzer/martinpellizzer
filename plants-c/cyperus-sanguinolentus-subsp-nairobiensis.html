Cyperus sanguinolentus subsp. nairobiensis

Cyperus sanguinolentus subsp.

nairobiensis, commonly known as the Nairobi flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Africa. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish in color, giving it its species name "sanguinolentus," meaning "blood-red.".

The plant is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
