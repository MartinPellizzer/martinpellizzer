Carex bajacalifornica

Carex bajacalifornica, commonly known as the California sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are usually green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-sided, triangular seed heads.
