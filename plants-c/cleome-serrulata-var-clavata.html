Cleome serrulata var. clavata

Cleome serrulata var. clavata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and alternate, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome serrulata var. clavata is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
