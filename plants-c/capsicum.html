Capsicum

Capsicum, commonly known as bell pepper, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, swollen fruits that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and green. The plant is an annual herb that produces flowers followed by the development of the fruit. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

Capsicum is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
