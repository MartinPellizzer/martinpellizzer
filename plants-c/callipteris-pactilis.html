Callipteris pactilis

Callipteris pactilis, commonly known as the coastal maidenhair fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Callipteris pactilis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
