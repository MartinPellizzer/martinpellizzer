Citrus gracilis var. dulcis

Citrus gracilis var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet lemon or dwarf lemon, is a small citrus tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, green, and glossy leaves, which are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, round, and yellow-orange fruits that are known for their sweet and tangy flavor. The flowers are white and fragrant, contributing to the plant's ornamental value.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
