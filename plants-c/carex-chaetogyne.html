Carex chaetogyne

Carex chaetogyne, commonly known as the long-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage, with spikelets bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate climates.

Carex chaetogyne is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
