Callicarpa rubrocarpa

Callicarpa rubrocarpa, commonly known as the red-berried beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking, bright red berries in the fall, which are a key feature for attracting wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

Callicarpa rubrocarpa thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
