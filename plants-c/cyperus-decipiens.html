Cyperus decipiens

Cyperus decipiens, commonly known as the false papyrus, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Cyperus decipiens plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
