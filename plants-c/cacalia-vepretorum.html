Cacalia vepretorum

Cacalia vepretorum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cacalia vepretorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
