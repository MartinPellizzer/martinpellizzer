Carex remota var. remotiformis

Carex remota var. remotiformis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones and boggy habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
