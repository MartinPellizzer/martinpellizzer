Clematis tashiroi

Clematis tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Clematis tashiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
