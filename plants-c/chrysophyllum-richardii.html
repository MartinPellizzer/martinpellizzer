Chrysophyllum richardii

Chrysophyllum richardii, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are encased in a thick, waxy coating. The fruit's pulp is rich in oils and has a sweet, buttery flavor, making it a valuable food source.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
