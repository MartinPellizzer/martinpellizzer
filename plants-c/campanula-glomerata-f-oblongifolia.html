Campanula glomerata f. oblongifolia

Campanula glomerata f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval to oblong in shape, hence the subspecies name, and are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and easy maintenance in garden settings.
