Conyza scabra

Conyza scabra, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is characterized by its branched, erect stems and is often mistaken for other members of the Asteraceae family.

Conyza scabra is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
