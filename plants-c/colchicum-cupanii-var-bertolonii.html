Colchicum cupanii var. bertolonii

Colchicum cupanii var. bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm, which stores nutrients for the flowering cycle. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
