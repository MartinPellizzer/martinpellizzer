Cedrus libanotica subsp. atlantica

Cedrus libanotica subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlas cedar, is a subspecies of the cedar tree native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color.

The cones of this plant are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in local cultures.
