Cyathea toroi

Cyathea toroi, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species in its natural habitat.

Its rhizome system supports the development of its massive fronds, and it plays an important role in the ecological structure of tropical forests.
