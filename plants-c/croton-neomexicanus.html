Croton neomexicanus

Croton neomexicanus, commonly known as New Mexico croton, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, yellow, and green throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
