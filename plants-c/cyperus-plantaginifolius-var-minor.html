Cyperus plantaginifolius var. minor

Cyperus plantaginifolius var. minor, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 meter in height. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-sided flowering structures.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to fluctuating water levels.
