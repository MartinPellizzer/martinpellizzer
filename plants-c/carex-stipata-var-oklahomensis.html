Carex stipata var. oklahomensis

Carex stipata var. oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This variant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
