Chaetogastra lasiophylla

Chaetogastra lasiophylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures, giving it its scientific name "lasiophylla," meaning "rough leaves.". The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive as a partial parasite.
