Carex flacca subsp. praetutiana

Carex flacca subsp.

praetutiana, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

This subspecies is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
