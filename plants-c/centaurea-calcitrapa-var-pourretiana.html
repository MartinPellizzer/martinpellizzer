Centaurea calcitrapa var. pourretiana

Centaurea calcitrapa var. pourretiana, commonly known as the Mediterranean knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces, and it thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
