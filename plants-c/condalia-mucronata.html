Condalia mucronata

Condalia mucronata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. Its bark and leaves contain milky sap, which is used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
