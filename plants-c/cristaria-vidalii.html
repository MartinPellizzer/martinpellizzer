Cristaria vidalii

Cristaria vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's crista, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-arranged leaves that form a rosette pattern, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the short growing season in its alpine habitat. Its botanical features suggest adaptations to cold and arid conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that help conserve moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Cristaria vidalii is considered a species of conservation concern.
