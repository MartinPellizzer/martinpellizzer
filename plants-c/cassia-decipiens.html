Cassia decipiens

Cassia decipiens, commonly known as the false toon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia decipiens is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its rapid growth makes it a useful species for reforestation and soil stabilization.
