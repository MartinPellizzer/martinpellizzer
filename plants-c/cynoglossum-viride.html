Cynoglossum viride

Cynoglossum viride, commonly known as green hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Cynoglossum viride is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
