Chrysanthemum kaschgaricum

Chrysanthemum kaschgaricum, commonly known as the Karakorum chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Chrysanthemum kaschgaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
