Caltha palustris

Caltha palustris, commonly known as marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and damp meadows in temperate regions.

It features bright yellow, buttercup-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found near streams or in marshy areas.

Caltha palustris is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
