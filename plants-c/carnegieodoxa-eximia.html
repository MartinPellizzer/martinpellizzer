Carnegieodoxa eximia

Carnegieodoxa eximia, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its enormous, heart-shaped leaves that can span over three meters in diameter, supported by long, sturdy petioles. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its botanical features include a complex root system that helps stabilize the soil and filter water.

Carnegieodoxa eximia is valued for its ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
