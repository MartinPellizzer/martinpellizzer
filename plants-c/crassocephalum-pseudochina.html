Crassocephalum pseudochina

Crassocephalum pseudochina, commonly known as the Chinese headplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and prominent capitula. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
