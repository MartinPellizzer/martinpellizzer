Calylophus hartwegii subsp. lavanulifolia

Calylophus hartwegii subsp.

lavanulifolia, commonly known as the lavenderleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, daisy-like white flowers with yellow centers.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive, grass-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
