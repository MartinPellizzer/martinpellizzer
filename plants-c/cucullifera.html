Cucullifera

Cucullifera, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with a prominent hood-like structure formed by the sepals and petals. The plants are typically epiphytic or lithophytic, growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. They produce showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus includes several species, each with distinct morphological variations in leaf shape and flower color.
