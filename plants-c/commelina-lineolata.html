Commelina lineolata

Commelina lineolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes a variety of grass-like and flowering plants. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have three blue or violet petals with yellow markings.

Commelina lineolata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
