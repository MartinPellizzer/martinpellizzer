Cerastium arvense subsp. molle

Cerastium arvense subsp.

molle, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it often forms dense mats on disturbed soils. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent appearance and ability to thrive in moist, temperate environments. Cerastium arvense subsp.

molle is often found in lawns, fields, and along roadsides.
