Creusa tetragona

Creusa tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled creusa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Creusa tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
