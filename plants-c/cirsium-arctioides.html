Cirsium arctioides

Cirsium arctioides, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to cold climates. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
