Cassia organensis

Cassia organensis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia organensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
