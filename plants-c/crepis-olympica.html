Crepis olympica

Crepis olympica, commonly known as the Olympic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Crepis olympica is notable for its resilience in harsh environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
