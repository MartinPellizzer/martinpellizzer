Chaptalia pumila

Chaptalia pumila, commonly known as the roughleaf chaptalia, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and rough to the touch. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Chaptalia pumila is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
