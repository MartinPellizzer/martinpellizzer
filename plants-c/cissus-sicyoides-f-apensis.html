Cissus sicyoides f. apensis

Cissus sicyoides f. apensis, commonly known as the Apennine grape ivy, is a varietal form of the genus Cissus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
