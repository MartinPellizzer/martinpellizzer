Cleome insolata

Cleome insolata, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

Cleome insolata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.
