Carex wilkesii

Carex wilkesii, commonly known as Wilkes' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or reddish in color.

Carex wilkesii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
