Catapodium nepalense

Catapodium nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal catapodium, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It typically grows in moist, temperate habitats such as meadows and forest margins. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets. It is characterized by its robust rhizomes, which help it spread and establish in various soil types.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in soil stabilization.
