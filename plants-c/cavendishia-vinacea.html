Cavendishia vinacea

Cavendishia vinacea, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking red stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
