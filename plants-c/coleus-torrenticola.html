Coleus torrenticola

Coleus torrenticola, commonly known as the torrent coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish hues.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
