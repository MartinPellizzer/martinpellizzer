Calamus leiospathus

Calamus leiospathus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in wet, swampy areas and is well adapted to humid tropical climates.
