Caralluma baradii

Caralluma baradii, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments by storing water in its thick stems.

Caralluma baradii is also known for its traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
