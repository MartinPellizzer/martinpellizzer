Conostegia acutidentata

Conostegia acutidentata, commonly known as the sharp-toothed cypress, is a species of evergreen coniferous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The plant produces small, scale-like cones that mature to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
