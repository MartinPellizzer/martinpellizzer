Cyperus pseudocallistus

Cyperus pseudocallistus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus pseudocallistus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
