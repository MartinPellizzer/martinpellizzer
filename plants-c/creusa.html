Creusa

Creusa, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Berberidaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Creusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
