Carex pengii

Carex pengii, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Carex pengii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
