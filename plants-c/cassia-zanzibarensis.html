Cassia zanzibarensis

Cassia zanzibarensis, commonly known as Zanzibar cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Cassia zanzibarensis thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
