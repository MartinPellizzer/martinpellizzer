Cyclamen immaculatum

Cyclamen immaculatum, commonly known as the white cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its blooming period occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Cyclamen immaculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
