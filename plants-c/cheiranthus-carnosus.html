Cheiranthus carnosus

Cheiranthus carnosus, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
