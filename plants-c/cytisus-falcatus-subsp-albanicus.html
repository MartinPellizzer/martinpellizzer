Cytisus falcatus subsp. albanicus

Cytisus falcatus subsp.

albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are pinnate, with several pairs of oval, hairy leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
