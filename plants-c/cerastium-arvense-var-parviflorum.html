Cerastium arvense var. parviflorum

Cerastium arvense var. parviflorum, commonly known as lesser mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and horticultural settings.
