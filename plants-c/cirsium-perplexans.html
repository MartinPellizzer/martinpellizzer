Cirsium perplexans

Cirsium perplexans, commonly known as the perplexing thistle, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields. Its leaves are usually gray-green with rough, bristly textures, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium perplexans is often found in the western and central regions of the United States.
