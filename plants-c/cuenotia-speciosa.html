Cuenotia speciosa

Cuenotia speciosa, commonly known as the showy cuenotia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue petals and serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
