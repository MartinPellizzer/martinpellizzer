Campomanesia sellowiana

Campomanesia sellowiana, commonly known as pitanga-preta or black pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible fruits with a tart, acidic flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
