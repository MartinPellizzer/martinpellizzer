Capparis tweedieana

Capparis tweedieana, commonly known as the Christmas caper, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and leaves that are typically thick and waxy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, greenish fruits resembling capers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
