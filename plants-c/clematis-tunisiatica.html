Clematis tunisiatica

Clematis tunisiatica, commonly known as the Tunisian Clematis, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, often with a distinct fragrance. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

Clematis tunisiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
