Cissus variifolia

Cissus variifolia, commonly known as the variable cutleaf iceplant, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped or arrowhead-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by green, edible fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its stems can climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.
