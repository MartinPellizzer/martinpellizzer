Chamaecrista serpens var. mensarum

Chamaecrista serpens var. mensarum, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage.

The variety var. mensarum is distinguished by its slender growth habit and smaller leaves compared to other varieties of Chamaecrista serpens.
