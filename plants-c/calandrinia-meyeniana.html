Calandrinia meyeniana

Calandrinia meyeniana, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Calandrinia meyeniana is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides in its native habitat.
