Centaurea eriocaulon

Centaurea eriocaulon, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Centaurea eriocaulon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
