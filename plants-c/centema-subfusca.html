Centema subfusca

Centema subfusca, commonly known as the white centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Centema subfusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
