Carex poiformis

Carex poiformis, commonly known as the rounded sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a distinct sheathing base. Its flowering spikes are usually borne in clusters, producing small, three-lobed flowers.

Carex poiformis prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
