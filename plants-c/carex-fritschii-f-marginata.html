Carex fritschii f. marginata

Carex fritschii f. marginata, commonly known as the margined sedge, is a variant of the Carex fritschii species, characterized by its distinctive marginal variegation.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green with white or pale margins, giving it a striking visual appearance. It produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers during the summer months.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in water garden settings.
