Cicerbita Filicina

Cicerbita filicina is a lesser-known member of the Cucurbitaceae family, often mistaken for a weed due to its sprawling growth habit. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and sunny areas. Its delicate, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers make it a unique addition to botanical collections. Despite its beauty, it is often overlooked in favor of more common garden plants. This article explores the characteristics, habitat, and ecological role of Cicerbita filicina.
