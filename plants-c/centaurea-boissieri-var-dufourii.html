Centaurea boissieri var. dufourii

Centaurea boissieri var. dufourii, commonly known as Dufour's starthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright purple to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
