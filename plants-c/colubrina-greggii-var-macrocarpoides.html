Colubrina greggii var. macrocarpoides

Colubrina greggii var. macrocarpoides, commonly known as Gregg's colubrina, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, oval, dark green leaves and produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. The plant bears small, round, orange to red fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The botanical features of this variety include its compact growth habit and distinctive floral and fruit characteristics.
