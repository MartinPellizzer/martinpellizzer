Capsicum frutescens var. longum

Capsicum frutescens var. longum, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant produces small, fiery red fruits that are widely used as a spice and condiment. It is valued for its high capsaicin content, which gives it its characteristic heat.

The plant is often grown for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
