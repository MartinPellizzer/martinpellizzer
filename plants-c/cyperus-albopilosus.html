Cyperus albopilosus

Cyperus albopilosus, commonly known as the white-hairy flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in wet or marshy environments.

Cyperus albopilosus is often found in disturbed soils and can be an invasive species in some areas.
