Coburgia incarnata

Coburgia incarnata, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense, attractive appearance.

Coburgia incarnata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny, dry conditions.
