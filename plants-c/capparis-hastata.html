Capparis hastata

Capparis hastata, commonly known as the rock caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Capparis hastata is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant for coastal and dry garden settings.
