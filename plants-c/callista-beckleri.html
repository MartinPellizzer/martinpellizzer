Callista beckleri

Callista beckleri, commonly known as the New Zealand bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Callista beckleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
