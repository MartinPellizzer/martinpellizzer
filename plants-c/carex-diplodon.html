Carex diplodon

Carex diplodon, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in shaded, damp areas such as forest floors or wetlands.
