Calliandra minutifolia

Calliandra minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf calliandra, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that produce small, pink to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, elliptical leaflets, giving it a finely textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is often cultivated in warm climates.
