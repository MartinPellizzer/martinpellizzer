Cyperus wallichianus

Cyperus wallichianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in wetlands and along water bodies.

Cyperus wallichianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
