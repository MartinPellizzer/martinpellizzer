Callista leucolophota

Callista leucolophota, commonly known as the white-veined callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with white veins that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.
