Coleus monostachyus subsp. monostachyus

Coleus monostachyus subsp.

monostachyus, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae). It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct scent. Its flowers are small and tubular, arranged in spikes, and are usually pale blue or white.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
