Capparis breynia

Capparis breynia, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly aromatic scent. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, aiding in water retention.

Capparis breynia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
