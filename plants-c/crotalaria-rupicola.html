Crotalaria rupicola

Crotalaria rupicola, commonly known as the rock crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in rocky and sandy soils, often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Crotalaria rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
