Correa decumbens

Correa decumbens, commonly known as the low correa or prostrate correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
