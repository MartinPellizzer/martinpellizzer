Chirita crassifolia

Chirita crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed chirita, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Chirita crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
