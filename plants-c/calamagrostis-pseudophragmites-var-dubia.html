Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. dubia

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. dubia, commonly known as the silver reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It is a grass species native to wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. It produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in moist conditions.
