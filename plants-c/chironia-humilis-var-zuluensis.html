Chironia humilis var. zuluensis

Chironia humilis var. zuluensis, commonly known as the Zuluensis chironia, is a low-growing, perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or sandy soils.
