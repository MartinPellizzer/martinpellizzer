Clitandra parvifolia

Clitandra parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. The tree is valued for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
