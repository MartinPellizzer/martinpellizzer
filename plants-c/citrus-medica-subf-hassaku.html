Citrus medica subf. hassaku

Citrus medica subf.

hassaku, commonly known as the Japanese kumquat, is a citrus plant characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel. It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in subtropical climates.

The plant has small, glossy leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. Its fruit develops from a single flower and is typically harvested when it turns a vibrant orange color. The botanical features of this subsp.

include a relatively short stature and a tendency to form compact shrubs or small trees.
