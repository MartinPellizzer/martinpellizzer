Cylindropuntia tephrocactoides

Cylindropuntia tephrocactoides, commonly known as the desert nightshade cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless cactus with cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with reduced leaves and thick stems that store water.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
