Claoxylon kirkii

Claoxylon kirkii, commonly known as the African ebony, is a tropical tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, often with a glossy surface.

Claoxylon kirkii is also known for its small, fragrant flowers and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
