Chenopodium triangulare var. angustifolium

Chenopodium triangulare var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in compact inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
