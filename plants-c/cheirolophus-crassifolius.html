Cheirolophus crassifolius

Cheirolophus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments, showcasing succulent features. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
