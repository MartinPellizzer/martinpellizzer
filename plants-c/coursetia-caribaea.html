Coursetia caribaea

Coursetia caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean coursetia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the branches. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in dry, coastal habitats.

Coursetia caribaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
