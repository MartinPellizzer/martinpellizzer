Cassia riedelii

Cassia riedelii, commonly known as golden cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a fluffy, white aril. Cassia riedelii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.
