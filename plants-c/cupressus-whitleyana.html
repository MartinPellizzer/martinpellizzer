Cupressus whitleyana

Cupressus whitleyana, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit. The tree has scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, arranged in crossed pairs. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in landscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
