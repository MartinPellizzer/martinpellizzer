Calea stenophylla

Calea stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calea, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Calea stenophylla is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
