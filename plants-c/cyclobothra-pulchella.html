Cyclobothra pulchella

Cyclobothra pulchella, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the fern family and is characterized by its distinctive, silver-green fronds that form a rosette shape. The plant's leaves are composed of multiple pinnae, which are arranged in a spiral pattern around the central axis. Cyclobothra pulchella thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
