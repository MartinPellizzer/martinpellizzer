Cyphomandra aculeata

Cyphomandra aculeata, commonly known as the tree tomato or carambola, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, acidic fruits. The fruit's shape resembles a star, and it is often used in culinary applications due to its tart flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
