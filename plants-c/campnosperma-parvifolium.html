Campnosperma parvifolium

Campnosperma parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved sandblow, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. It is often found in coastal and inland regions with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
