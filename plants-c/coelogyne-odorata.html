Coelogyne odorata

Coelogyne odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The leaves are typically strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, and the plant is often grown as a decorative orchid due to its ornamental value.

Coelogyne odorata is known for its ability to bloom repeatedly during the growing season, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
