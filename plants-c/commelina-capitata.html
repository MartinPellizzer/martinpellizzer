Commelina capitata

Commelina capitata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense clusters at the top of the plant. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable garden plant and a potential weed.
