Campanula kadargavanica

Campanula kadargavanica, commonly known as the Kadargavanica bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula kadargavanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
