Chionodoxa siehei

Chionodoxa siehei, commonly known as the Persian glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It produces clusters of delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Chionodoxa siehei is known for its early blooming habit and low maintenance requirements.
