Cyperus viridis

Cyperus viridis, commonly known as green flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Cyperus viridis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
