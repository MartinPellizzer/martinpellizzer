Cyperus tataricus

Cyperus tataricus, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Cyperus tataricus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
