Cyclopia montana

Cyclopia montana, commonly known as the mountain cyclopia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds.

Cyclopia montana is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
