Chenopodium ambrosioides var. genuinum

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. genuinum, commonly known as wild buckwheat, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in small, dry fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

This species is also known for its potential medicinal uses and is sometimes used as a forage plant for livestock.
