Campanula rapunculoides var. grandiflora

Campanula rapunculoides var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its attractive blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
