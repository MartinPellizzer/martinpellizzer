Copiapoa echinoides

Copiapoa echinoides, commonly known as the globular cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, which is covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water due to its adaptation to arid environments.

Copiapoa echinoides is a slow-growing plant that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
