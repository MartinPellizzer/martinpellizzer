Ceropegia distincta var. rostrata

Ceropegia distincta var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive hooded structure. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
