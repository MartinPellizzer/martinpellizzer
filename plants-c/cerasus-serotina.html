Cerasus serotina

Cerasus serotina, commonly known as the late cherry or wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Cerasus serotina is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
