Copaifera camibar

Copaifera camibar, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing numerous seeds. Its bark is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

Copaifera camibar plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native environment.
