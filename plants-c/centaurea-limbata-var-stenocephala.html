Centaurea limbata var. stenocephala

Centaurea limbata var. stenocephala, commonly known as narrow-headed cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow, blue to purple flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are branching and often hairy. This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower structure.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
