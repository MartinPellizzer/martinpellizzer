Clusia radicans

Clusia radicans, commonly known as the rubber plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

Its leaves are typically broad, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, which helps in photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

Clusia radicans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing environments.
