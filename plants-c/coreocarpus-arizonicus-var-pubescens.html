Coreocarpus arizonicus var. pubescens

Coreocarpus arizonicus var. pubescens, commonly known as the Arizona coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.

The flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
