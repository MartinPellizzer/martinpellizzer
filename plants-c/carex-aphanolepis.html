Carex aphanolepis

Carex aphanolepis, commonly known as the ghost sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in consistently damp conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex aphanolepis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
