Croton micans

Croton micans, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with unique and sometimes toxic sap. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a velvety texture that gives it its common name.

Croton micans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf colors and adaptability to various growing conditions.
