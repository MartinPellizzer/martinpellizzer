Carex balfourii

Carex balfourii, commonly known as Balfour's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the genus Carex. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
