Carlina biebersteinii subsp. intermedia

Carlina biebersteinii subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate cottonwood, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange composite flower heads.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
