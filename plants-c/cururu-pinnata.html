Cururu pinnata

Cururu pinnata, commonly known as the Brazilian white wood, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

Cururu pinnata is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional construction and crafts.
