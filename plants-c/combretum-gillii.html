Combretum gillii

Combretum gillii, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its foliage. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Combretum gillii is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
