Centropogon dubius

Centropogon dubius, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Centropogon dubius thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
