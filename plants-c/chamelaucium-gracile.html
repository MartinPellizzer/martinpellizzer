Chamelaucium gracile

Chamelaucium gracile, commonly known as the slender tanglehead, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal conditions. Chamelaucium gracile is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
