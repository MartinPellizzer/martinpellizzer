Chloris radiata var. beyrichiana

Chloris radiata var. beyrichiana, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that helps reflect sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
