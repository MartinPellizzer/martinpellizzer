Cnicus nudiflorus

Cnicus nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflowered goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairless, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
