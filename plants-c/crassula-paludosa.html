Crassula paludosa

Crassula paludosa, commonly known as the marsh crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or near salt marshes. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value.

Crassula paludosa is a hardy species that is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
