Coleus adolfi-friderici

Coleus adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the Adolf-Friedrich coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Its foliage displays a wide range of colors, including red, orange, and purple, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
