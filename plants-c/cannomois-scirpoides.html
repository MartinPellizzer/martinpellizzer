Cannomois scirpoides

Cannomois scirpoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist to aquatic habitats, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Cannomois scirpoides is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and water filtration.
