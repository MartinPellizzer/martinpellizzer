Crataegus intricata var. fortunata

Crataegus intricata var. fortunata, commonly known as the Fortunata hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
