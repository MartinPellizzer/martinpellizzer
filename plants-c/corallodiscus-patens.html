Corallodiscus patens

Corallodiscus patens, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Corallodiscus patens prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
