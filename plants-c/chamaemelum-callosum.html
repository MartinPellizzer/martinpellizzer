Chamaemelum callosum

Chamaemelum callosum, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with grayish-green, woolly foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain compounds used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
