Clematis hastata

Clematis hastata, commonly known as the Pacific clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a distinctive white or grayish underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.

Clematis hastata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
