Citrus grandis f. buntan

Citrus grandis f. buntan, commonly known as the Buntan citrus, is a cultivar of the grapefruit species.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits with a thick, yellow-orange rind. The fruit typically contains a high concentration of sugars and a mild, sweet flavor compared to other citrus varieties. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often grown for its fruit rather than its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
