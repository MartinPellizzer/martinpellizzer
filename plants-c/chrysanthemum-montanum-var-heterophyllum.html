Chrysanthemum montanum var. heterophyllum

Chrysanthemum montanum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the mountain chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its hardy nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
