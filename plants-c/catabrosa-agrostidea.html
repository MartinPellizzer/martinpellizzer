Catabrosa agrostidea

Catabrosa agrostidea, commonly known as the grassy catabrosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its floral structure consists of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Catabrosa agrostidea is often found in regions with temperate climates and well-drained soils.
