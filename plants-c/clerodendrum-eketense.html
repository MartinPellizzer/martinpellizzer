Clerodendrum eketense

Clerodendrum eketense, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or red throat. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
