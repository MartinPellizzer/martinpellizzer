Chamaecrista pygmaea var. roigii

Chamaecrista pygmaea var. roigii, commonly known as the dwarf ticktree, is a low-growing shrub native to the Caribbean.

It features small, bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, feathery clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
