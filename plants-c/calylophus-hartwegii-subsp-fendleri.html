Calylophus hartwegii subsp. fendleri

Calylophus hartwegii subsp.

fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its ecological importance to native pollinators.
