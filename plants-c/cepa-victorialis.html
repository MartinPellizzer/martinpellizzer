Cepa victorialis

Cepa victorialis, commonly known as the Victor's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and strap-like, grassy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
