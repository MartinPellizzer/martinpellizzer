Commelina undulata var. setosa

Commelina undulata var. setosa, commonly known as the curly commelina, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with broad, heart-shaped leaves that have wavy margins. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers with three petals and a distinctive white spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
