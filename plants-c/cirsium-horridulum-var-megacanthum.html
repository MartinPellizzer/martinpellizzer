Cirsium horridulum var. megacanthum

Cirsium horridulum var. megacanthum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with sharp teeth and a dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various insects and wildlife.
