Chaetochloa perennis

Chaetochloa perennis, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Chaetochloa perennis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small wildlife.
