Cattleya elongata var. maculata

Cattleya elongata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, often with distinctive spots or streaks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and inflorescence. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
