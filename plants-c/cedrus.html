Cedrus

Cedrus, commonly known as cedar, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees belonging to the family Pinaceae.

These trees are characterized by their pyramid-shaped crowns, needle-like leaves, and thick, aromatic wood. Cedrus species are native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, Middle East, and Central Asia. They are valued for their durable timber, which is resistant to decay and pests.

Additionally, cedar trees play an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
