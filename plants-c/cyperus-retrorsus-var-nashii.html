Cyperus retrorsus var. nashii

Cyperus retrorsus var. nashii, commonly known as the Nash's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
