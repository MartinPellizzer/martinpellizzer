Carex × moriyoshiensis

Carex × moriyoshiensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves and dense clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. This species is native to Japan and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces cylindrical spikelets that mature into brown, seed-bearing structures.

Carex × moriyoshiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
