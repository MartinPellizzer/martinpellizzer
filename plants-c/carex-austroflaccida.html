Carex austroflaccida

Carex austroflaccida, commonly known as the southern flaccid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and riparian zones. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated.
