Centaurea urvillei subsp. stepposa

Centaurea urvillei subsp.

stepposa, commonly known as the steppe centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of steppe ecosystems.
