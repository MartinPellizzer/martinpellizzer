Carex atrata var. subgracilenta

Carex atrata var. subgracilenta, commonly known as the slender black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically brown or black in color, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
