Cirsium stojanovii

Cirsium stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanov's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
