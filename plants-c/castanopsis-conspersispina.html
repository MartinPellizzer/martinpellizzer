Castanopsis conspersispina

Castanopsis conspersispina, commonly known as the rough-hairy tanyip.

It is a deciduous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with short, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by hard, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Castanopsis conspersispina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
