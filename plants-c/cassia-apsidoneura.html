Cassia apsidoneura

Cassia apsidoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, leathery pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
