Calycanthus floridus var. inodorus

Calycanthus floridus var. inodorus, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, maroon-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
