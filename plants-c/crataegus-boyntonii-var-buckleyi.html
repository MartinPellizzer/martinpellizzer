Crataegus boyntonii var. buckleyi

Crataegus boyntonii var. buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley’s hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in partial to full sunlight.

Buckley’s hawthorn is often found in woodland edges and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
