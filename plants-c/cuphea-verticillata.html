Cuphea verticillata

Cuphea verticillata, commonly known as the whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features whorled leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea verticillata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage.
