Chaetochloa scabrifolia

Chaetochloa scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf hairgrass, is a species of grass native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers.

Chaetochloa scabrifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
