Cryptocarya woodii

Cryptocarya woodii, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

The plant produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Cryptocarya woodii typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests, and is adapted to cool, humid climates.
