Chromatotriccum nubigenum

Chromatotriccum nubigenum, commonly known as the cloud-dwelling moss, is a species of moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and its ability to retain water, which helps it survive in humid conditions. The plant has a simple structure with leaf-like structures that are typically arranged in two rows. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized capsules found on the stem.

Chromatotriccum nubigenum plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
