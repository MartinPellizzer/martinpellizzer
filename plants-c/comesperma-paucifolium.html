Comesperma paucifolium

Comesperma paucifolium, commonly known as the few-leaved comesperma, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a common sight in coastal environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
