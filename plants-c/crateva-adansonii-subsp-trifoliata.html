Crateva adansonii subsp. trifoliata

Crateva adansonii subsp.

trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf morning glory, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, often with a reddish throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

The plant is also known for its edible fruits, which are fibrous and have a mild, sweet flavor.
