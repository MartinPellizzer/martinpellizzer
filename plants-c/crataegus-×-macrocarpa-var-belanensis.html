Crataegus × macrocarpa var. belanensis

Crataegus × macrocarpa var. belanensis, commonly known as the Belanensis hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe.

It features thorny branches and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant's leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a glossy green surface. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, attracting various bird species.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
