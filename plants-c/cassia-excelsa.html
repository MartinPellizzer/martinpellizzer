Cassia excelsa

Cassia excelsa, commonly known as the giant senna, is a towering shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia excelsa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
