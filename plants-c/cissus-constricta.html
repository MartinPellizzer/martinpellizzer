Cissus constricta

Cissus constricta, commonly known as the climbing fig or rock fig, is a perennial vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a versatile and attractive plant.
