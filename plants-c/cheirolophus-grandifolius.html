Cheirolophus grandifolius

Cheirolophus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
