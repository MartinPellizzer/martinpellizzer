Crataegus × collicola

Crataegus × collicola, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that appear in the fall. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in hedgerow management.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife, providing food for birds and insects.
