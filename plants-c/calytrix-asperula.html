Calytrix asperula

Calytrix asperula, commonly known as the rough calytrix, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Calytrix asperula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
