Campanopsis pumilio

Campanopsis pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Campanopsis pumilio is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
