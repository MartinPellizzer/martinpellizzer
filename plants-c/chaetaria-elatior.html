Chaetaria elatior

Chaetaria elatior, commonly known as the greater hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence is a loose panicle composed of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Chaetaria elatior plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
