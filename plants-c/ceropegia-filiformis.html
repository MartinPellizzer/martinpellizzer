Ceropegia filiformis

Ceropegia filiformis, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating, and its stems are long and flexible, allowing it to trail or climb. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
