Copiapoa hypogaea var. barquitensis

Copiapoa hypogaea var. barquitensis, commonly known as the Barquitense cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a dense, spiny coat that helps it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for cultivation in dry climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and hardy nature.
