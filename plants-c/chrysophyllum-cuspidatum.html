Chrysophyllum cuspidatum

Chrysophyllum cuspidatum, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow-wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor.

Its fruit is often used in jams, beverages, and traditional medicine, while the wood is valued for its durability and workability.
