Convolvulus arvensis f. pentastictus

Convolvulus arvensis f. pentastictus, commonly known as the five-streaked field bindweed, is a variant of the common field bindweed.

It is a flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five prominent stamens that give it its name. This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other convolvulus species, particularly in its leaf and floral morphology.
