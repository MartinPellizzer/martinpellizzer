Carex scita var. tenuiseta

Carex scita var. tenuiseta, commonly known as the slender-haired sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
