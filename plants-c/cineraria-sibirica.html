Cineraria sibirica

Cineraria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Cineraria sibirica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
