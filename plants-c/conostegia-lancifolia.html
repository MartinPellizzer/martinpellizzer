Conostegia lancifolia

Conostegia lancifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved balsa, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
