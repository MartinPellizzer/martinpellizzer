Costus letestui

Costus letestui, commonly known as the African costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often with a strong, spicy aroma. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Costus letestui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
