Carex rotundata f. laeta

Carex rotundata f. laeta, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
