Cereus pentalophus var. subarticulatus

Cereus pentalophus var. subarticulatus, commonly known as the Christmas Cactus, is a hybrid species native to Brazil.

It is a flowering cactus characterized by its thick, segmented stems and showy, brightly colored flowers that bloom around the holiday season. The plant typically grows in a compact, branching form with waxy, pointed leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique blooming cycle makes it a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
