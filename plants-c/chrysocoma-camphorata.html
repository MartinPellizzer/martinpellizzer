Chrysocoma camphorata

Chrysocoma camphorata, commonly known as the African camphor tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
