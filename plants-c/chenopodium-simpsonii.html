Chenopodium simpsonii

Chenopodium simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Chenopodium simpsonii is also known for its edible leaves, which have been used by indigenous peoples for culinary and medicinal purposes.
