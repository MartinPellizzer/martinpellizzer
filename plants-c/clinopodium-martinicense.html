Clinopodium martinicense

Clinopodium martinicense, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or white, and it produces small, oval berries.

Clinopodium martinicense is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and cooking.
