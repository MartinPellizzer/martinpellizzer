Capparis guenezii

Capparis guenezii, commonly known as the Canary Islands Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often followed by spherical, green fruits. Its leaves are typically small, oval, and covered in a fine pubescence.

Capparis guenezii is adapted to the arid and rocky environments of its native habitat.
