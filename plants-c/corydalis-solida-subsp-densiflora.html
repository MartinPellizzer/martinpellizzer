Corydalis solida subsp. densiflora

Corydalis solida subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, often pink or purple flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Corydalis. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
