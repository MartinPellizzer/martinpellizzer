Chamaedorea minima

Chamaedorea minima, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo palm, is a compact, evergreen plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features slender, upright stems with small, elongated leaves that give it a delicate, bamboo-like appearance. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and air-purifying qualities.

Its compact size makes it an ideal choice for small spaces or as a tabletop plant.
