Chamaesyce crepitata var. longa

Chamaesyce crepitata var. longa, commonly known as the longleaf sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
