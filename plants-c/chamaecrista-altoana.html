Chamaecrista altoana

Chamaecrista altoana, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands. Its seed pods are typically woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Chamaecrista altoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
