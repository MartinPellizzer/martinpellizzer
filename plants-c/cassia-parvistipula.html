Cassia parvistipula

Cassia parvistipula, commonly known as the small-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically small and opposite, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, followed by small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Cassia parvistipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
