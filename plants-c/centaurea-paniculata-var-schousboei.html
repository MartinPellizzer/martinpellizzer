Centaurea paniculata var. schousboei

Centaurea paniculata var. schousboei, commonly known as Schousboe's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
