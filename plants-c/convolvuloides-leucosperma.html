Convolvuloides leucosperma

Convolvuloides leucosperma, commonly known as the white-fruited morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its helical stems that twine around supports, and its heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, white seeds.

It is native to parts of South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
