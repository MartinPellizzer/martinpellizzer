Centaurea fraatii

Centaurea fraatii, commonly known as the Armenian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Centaurea fraatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
