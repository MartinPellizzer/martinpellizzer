Cardamine arisanensis

Cardamine arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly found in Japan and Taiwan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water bodies. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The species is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
