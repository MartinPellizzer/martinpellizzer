Crotalaria leucoclada

Crotalaria leucoclada, commonly known as the white crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and palmate leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which are often accompanied by inflated, pod-like fruits. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Crotalaria leucoclada is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
