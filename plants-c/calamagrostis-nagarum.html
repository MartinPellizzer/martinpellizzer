Calamagrostis nagarum

Calamagrostis nagarum, commonly known as the Japanese feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Calamagrostis nagarum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
