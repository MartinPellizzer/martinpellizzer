Commelina involucrosa

Commelina involucrosa, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, with three blue or violet petals and three white sepals, forming a distinctive whorled arrangement.

Commelina involucrosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
