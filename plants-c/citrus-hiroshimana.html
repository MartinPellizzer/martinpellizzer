Citrus hiroshimana

Citrus hiroshimana, commonly known as the Hiroshima lemon, is a rare and endangered citrus species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thin, smooth rind. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

Due to habitat loss and limited cultivation, it is critically endangered in the wild.
