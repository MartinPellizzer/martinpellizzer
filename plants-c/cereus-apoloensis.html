Cereus apoloensis

Cereus apoloensis, commonly known as the APOLONSIS CACTUS, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
