Carpinus hupeana var. henryana

Carpinus hupeana var. henryana, commonly known as Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy ornamental tree.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
