Calycogonium glabratum var. microphyllum

Calycogonium glabratum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved calycogonium, is a unique variant of the calycogonium species.

It is characterized by its reduced leaf size compared to the typical form, with leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variation and potential ecological role in its native habitats.
