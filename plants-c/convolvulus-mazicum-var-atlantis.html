Convolvulus mazicum var. atlantis

Convolvulus mazicum var. atlantis, commonly known as the Atlantis bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in agricultural and natural settings.
