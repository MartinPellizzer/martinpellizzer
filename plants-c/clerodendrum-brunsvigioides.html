Clerodendrum brunsvigioides

Clerodendrum brunsvigioides, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The bark is smooth and can be light to dark brown, with a distinctive aromatic scent when cut.
