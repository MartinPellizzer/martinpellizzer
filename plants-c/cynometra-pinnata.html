Cynometra pinnata

Cynometra pinnata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves that are pinnately arranged. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, brown seeds. It is often found in moist forested areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
