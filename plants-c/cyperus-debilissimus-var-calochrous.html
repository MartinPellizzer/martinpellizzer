Cyperus debilissimus var. calochrous

Cyperus debilissimus var. calochrous, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that grow from a rhizomatous root system, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
