Clematis leschenaultiana var. rubifolia

Clematis leschenaultiana var. rubifolia, commonly known as the red-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, clustered blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its climbing habit requires a sturdy support structure, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and floral display.
