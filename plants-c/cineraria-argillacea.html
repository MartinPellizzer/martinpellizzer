Cineraria argillacea

Cineraria argillacea, commonly known as the clay cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cineraria argillacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in Mediterranean climates.
