Carex chihuahuensis

Carex chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that appear in late spring or early summer.

Carex chihuahuensis is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
