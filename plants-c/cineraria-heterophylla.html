Cineraria heterophylla

Cineraria heterophylla, commonly known as the variable cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes and vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves vary in size and shape, ranging from lobed to strap-like, which adds to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
