Cyperus nudicaulis

Cyperus nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus nudicaulis thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable wetland indicator species and a potential invasive plant in non-native environments.
