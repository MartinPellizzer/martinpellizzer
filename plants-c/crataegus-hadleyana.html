Crataegus hadleyana

Crataegus hadleyana, commonly known as Hadley's hawthorn, is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
