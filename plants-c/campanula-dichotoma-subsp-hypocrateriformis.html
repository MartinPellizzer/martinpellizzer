Campanula dichotoma subsp. hypocrateriformis

Campanula dichotoma subsp.

hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a rough texture. This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
