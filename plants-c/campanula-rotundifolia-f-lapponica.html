Campanula rotundifolia f. lapponica

Campanula rotundifolia f. lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features rounded, broad leaves and produces bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas across northern Europe and Asia.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
