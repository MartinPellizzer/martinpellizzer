Carex ayako-maedae

Carex ayako-maedae, commonly known as the Ayako-maedae sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland and woodland ecosystems.
