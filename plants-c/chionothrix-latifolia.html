Chionothrix latifolia

Chionothrix latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
