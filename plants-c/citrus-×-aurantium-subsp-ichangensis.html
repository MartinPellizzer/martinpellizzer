Citrus × aurantium subsp. ichangensis

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang citrus or Chinese pomelo, is a subspecies native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers and thick, waxy leaves.

The fruit is typically large, with a thick rind and a sweet, juicy pulp. It is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its high vitamin C content.

This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
