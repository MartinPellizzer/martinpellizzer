Cirsium reglense

Cirsium reglense, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium reglense is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
