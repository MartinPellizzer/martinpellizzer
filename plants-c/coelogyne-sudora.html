Coelogyne sudora

Coelogyne sudora, commonly known as the sweat orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellow or greenish hue. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne sudora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
