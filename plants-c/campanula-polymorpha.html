Campanula polymorpha

Campanula polymorpha, commonly known as the blue bell or harebell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Campanula polymorpha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
