Carex torfacea

Carex torfacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in wetland environments.

Carex torfacea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
