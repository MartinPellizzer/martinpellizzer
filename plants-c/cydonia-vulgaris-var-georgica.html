Cydonia vulgaris var. georgica

Cydonia vulgaris var. georgica, commonly known as the Georgian quince, is a variant of the common quince native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring. The fruit is typically oval or pear-shaped, with a rough, pale-yellow skin that turns deep orange when mature. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
