Coreopsis major var. oemleri

Coreopsis major var. oemleri, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow to orange flower heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant collections.
