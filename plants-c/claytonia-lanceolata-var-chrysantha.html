Claytonia lanceolata var. chrysantha

Claytonia lanceolata var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender stems. Its leaves and flowers are edible and often used in salads or as a garnish.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and thrives in cool climates.
