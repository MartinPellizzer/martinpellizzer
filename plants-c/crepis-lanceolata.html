Crepis lanceolata

Crepis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, hairy involucre that resembles a hawk's beak. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Crepis lanceolata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
