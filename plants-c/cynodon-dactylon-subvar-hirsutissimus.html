Cynodon dactylon subvar. hirsutissimus

Cynodon dactylon subvar. hirsutissimus, commonly known as hairy Bermuda grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the leaf margins and stems. This variant is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions. It is often used for erosion control and as a ground cover due to its rapid growth and durability.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.
