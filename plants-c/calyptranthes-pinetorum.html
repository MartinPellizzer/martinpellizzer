Calyptranthes pinetorum

Calyptranthes pinetorum, commonly known as the pineleaf calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of western Mexico.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
