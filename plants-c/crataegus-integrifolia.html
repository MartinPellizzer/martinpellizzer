Crataegus integrifolia

Crataegus integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth along the edges. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Crataegus integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
