Catasetum mojuense

Catasetum mojuense, commonly known as the Mojuense orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure, with specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific insect pollinators.

Catasetum mojuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
