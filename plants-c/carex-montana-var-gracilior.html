Carex montana var. gracilior

Carex montana var. gracilior, commonly known as slender mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish-brown flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
