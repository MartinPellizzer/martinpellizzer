Celtis georgiana

Celtis georgiana, commonly known as the southern dogwood, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by spherical, greenish fruit that turns red when mature. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.

Celtis georgiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
