Crataegus brachyacantha var. maxima

Crataegus brachyacantha var. maxima, commonly known as the giant hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its dense growth habit makes it useful for erosion control and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
