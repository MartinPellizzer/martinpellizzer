Celosia plumosa

Celosia plumosa, commonly known as the plume cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes or cockscombs, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Celosia plumosa is known for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
