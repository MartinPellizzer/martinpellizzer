Chaetogastra cisplatensis

Chaetogastra cisplatensis, commonly known as the Paraguay arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starchy food source. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and features broad, arrow-shaped leaves. Its flowering structure consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
