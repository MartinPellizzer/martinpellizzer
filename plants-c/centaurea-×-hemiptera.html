Centaurea × hemiptera

Centaurea × hemiptera, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
