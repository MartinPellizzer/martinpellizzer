Calliandra brevicaulis f. grandiflora

Calliandra brevicaulis f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences composed of numerous small, papery flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
