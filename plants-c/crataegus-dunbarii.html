Crataegus dunbarii

Crataegus dunbarii, commonly known as Dunbar hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
