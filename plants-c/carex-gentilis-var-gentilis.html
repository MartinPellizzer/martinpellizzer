Carex gentilis var. gentilis

Carex gentilis var. gentilis, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.

Carex gentilis var. gentilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
