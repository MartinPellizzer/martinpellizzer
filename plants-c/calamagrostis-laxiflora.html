Calamagrostis laxiflora

Calamagrostis laxiflora, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to golden in color.

Calamagrostis laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
