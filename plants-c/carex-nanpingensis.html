Carex nanpingensis

Carex nanpingensis, commonly known as the Nanping sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to China and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its limited distribution, Carex nanpingensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
