Carex austroafghanica

Carex austroafghanica, commonly known as the Afghan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Carex austroafghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.

Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, add a distinctive visual element to its habitat.
