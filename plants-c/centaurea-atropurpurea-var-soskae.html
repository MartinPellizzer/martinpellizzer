Centaurea atropurpurea var. soskae

Centaurea atropurpurea var. soskae, commonly known as the Soska's purple centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking purple to crimson flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
