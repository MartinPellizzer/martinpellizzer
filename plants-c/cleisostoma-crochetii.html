Cleisostoma crochetii

Cleisostoma crochetii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has fleshy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cleisostoma crochetii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
