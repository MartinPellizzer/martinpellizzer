Chenopodium fremontii

Chenopodium fremontii, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a short-lived perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or arid environments. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. Its small, green flowers are clustered in cup-like structures called inflorescences.

Chenopodium fremontii is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
