Cojoba beckii

Cojoba beckii, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often at the ends of the branches. Its fruits are berries that mature to a bright red color, containing several seeds.

Cojoba beckii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
