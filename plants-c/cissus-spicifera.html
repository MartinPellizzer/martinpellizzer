Cissus spicifera

Cissus spicifera, commonly known as the spiky cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, giving it its species name "spicifera.". It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Cissus spicifera is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
