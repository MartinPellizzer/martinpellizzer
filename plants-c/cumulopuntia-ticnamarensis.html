Cumulopuntia ticnamarensis

Cumulopuntia ticnamarensis, commonly known as the Ticnamarensis Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring spiny structures that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

This cactus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
