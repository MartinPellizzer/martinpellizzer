Clompanus rugosa

Clompanus rugosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed clompanus, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
