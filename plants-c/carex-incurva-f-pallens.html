Carex incurva f. pallens

Carex incurva f. pallens, commonly known as the pale form of the drooping sedge.

This plant is a perennial herbaceous species belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to greenish flowers. The foliage tends to have a bluish-gray hue, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in temperate regions.
