Corymbia jacobsiana

Corymbia jacobsiana, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a species of flowering tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The tree produces small, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both coastal and inland environments.

Corymbia jacobsiana is also valued for its timber and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal.
