Colysis pothifolia

Colysis pothifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Colysis pothifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
