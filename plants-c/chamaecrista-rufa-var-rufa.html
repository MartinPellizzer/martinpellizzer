Chamaecrista rufa var. rufa

Chamaecrista rufa var. rufa, commonly known as the red burr legume, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and distinctive red or orange seed pods that resemble burrs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
