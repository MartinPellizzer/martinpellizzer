Cuphea epilobiifolia f. hoffmannii

Cuphea epilobiifolia f. hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's cuphea, is a compact shrub native to Mexico.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. Its flowering period typically occurs from spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
