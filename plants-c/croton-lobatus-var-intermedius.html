Croton lobatus var. intermedius

Croton lobatus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky seed pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
