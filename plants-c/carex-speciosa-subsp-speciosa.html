Carex speciosa subsp. speciosa

Carex speciosa subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses.

This subspecies is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, triangular leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces spikelets of small, white to pale green flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
