Chrysanthellum indicum subsp. afroamericanum

Chrysanthellum indicum subsp.

afroamericanum, commonly known as the African chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
