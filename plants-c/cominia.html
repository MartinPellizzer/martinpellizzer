Cominia

Cominia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cominia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
