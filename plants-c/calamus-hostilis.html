Calamus hostilis

Calamus hostilis, commonly known as the hostile calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are typically pollinated by wind.

The plant is often found growing in wetlands and along the edges of freshwater bodies.
