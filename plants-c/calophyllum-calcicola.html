Calophyllum calcicola

Calophyllum calcicola, commonly known as the limestone calophyllum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal or limestone-rich habitats. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
