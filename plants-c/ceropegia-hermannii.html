Ceropegia hermannii

Ceropegia hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves and has a preference for well-drained soil and bright, indirect light. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Ceropegia hermannii is also noted for its ability to produce offsets, making it relatively easy to propagate.
