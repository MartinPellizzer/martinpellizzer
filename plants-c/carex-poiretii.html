Carex poiretii

Carex poiretii, commonly known as the Poiret's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Carex in the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Carex poiretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
