Cytisus ratisbonensis subsp. ruthenicus

Cytisus ratisbonensis subsp.

ruthenicus, commonly known as the Ruthenian broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers.

The leaves are small, trifoliate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological habitats.
