Cyperus distans f. pachyanthos

Cyperus distans f. pachyanthos, commonly known as the broadflower sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Cyperus.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like foliage and distinctive, broad, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions. Its flowers are often pale green to brown, and it is known for its ornamental value in water gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local wildlife.
