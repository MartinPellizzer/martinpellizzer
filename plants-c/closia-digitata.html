Closia digitata

Closia digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finger-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique foliage and floral display.
