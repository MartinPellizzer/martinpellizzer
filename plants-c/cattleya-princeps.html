Cattleya princeps

Cattleya princeps, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is a tall, sympodial orchid with elongated pseudobulbs and broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cattleya princeps is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
