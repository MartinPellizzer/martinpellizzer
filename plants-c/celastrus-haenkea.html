Celastrus haenkea

Celastrus haenkea, commonly known as the creeping staff-vine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
