Cyperus laxus subsp. macrostachyus

Cyperus laxus subsp.

macrostachyus, commonly known as the broad-stalked flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in two rows.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are often subtended by leaf-like bracts. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common species in marshy areas and along waterways.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
