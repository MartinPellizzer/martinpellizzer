Carex oaxacana

Carex oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxacan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex oaxacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
