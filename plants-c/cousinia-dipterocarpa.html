Cousinia dipterocarpa

Cousinia dipterocarpa, commonly known as the two-winged flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and yellowish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, contributing to the ecological stability of its habitat.

Cousinia dipterocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
