Cinnagrostis boliviensis

Cinnagrostis boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and has small, narrow leaves adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
