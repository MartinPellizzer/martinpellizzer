Cassia sennoides

Cassia sennoides, commonly known as sicklepod cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Cassia sennoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
