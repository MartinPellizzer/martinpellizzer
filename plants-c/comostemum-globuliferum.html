Comostemum globuliferum

Comostemum globuliferum, commonly known as the globe-headed comostemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Comostemum globuliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in succulent gardens.
