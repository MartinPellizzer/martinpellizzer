Clematis gracilifolia var. pentaphylla

Clematis gracilifolia var. pentaphylla, commonly known as the slenderleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically 3 to 5 inches long and have a finely divided structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
