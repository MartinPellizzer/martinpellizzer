Cirsium abkhasicum

Cirsium abkhasicum, commonly known as the Abkhazian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
