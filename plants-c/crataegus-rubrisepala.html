Crataegus rubrisepala

Crataegus rubrisepala, commonly known as the red-sepal hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its small, red sepals that persist on the fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a red, fleshy pome.

Crataegus rubrisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
