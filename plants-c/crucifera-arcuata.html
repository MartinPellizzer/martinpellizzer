Crucifera arcuata

Crucifera arcuata, commonly known as the curved crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has a slender, branching stem. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
