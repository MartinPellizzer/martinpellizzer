Curcas hernandiifolia

Curcas hernandiifolia, commonly known as the wild hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to red, and its alternate, simple leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

Curcas hernandiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures for its medicinal properties.
