Carex montis-eeka var. viridens

Carex montis-eeka var. viridens, commonly known as the green mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

The leaves are usually a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
