Cerbera fruticosa

Cerbera fruticosa, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a red throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cerbera fruticosa is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
