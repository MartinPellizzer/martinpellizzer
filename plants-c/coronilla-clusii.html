Coronilla clusii

Coronilla clusii, commonly known as the Clusius' vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Coronilla clusii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
