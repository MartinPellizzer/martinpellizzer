Campanula macrorhiza subvar. re

Campanula macrorhiza subvar. re, commonly known as the large-rooted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has large, fleshy roots that help it store water and nutrients, adapting it to moist or wet environments. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in humid conditions.
