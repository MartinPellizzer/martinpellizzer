Cephalaria flava

Cephalaria flava, commonly known as yellow cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
