Calamus georgei

Calamus georgei, commonly known as George's arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its rhizomes, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
