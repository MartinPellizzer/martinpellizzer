Calea multiplinervia

Calea multiplinervia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, nocturnal blooming habit. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. Its leaves are typically large, palmately lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

Calea multiplinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique blooming behavior.
