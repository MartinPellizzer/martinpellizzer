Chelonopsis moschata var. longipes

Chelonopsis moschata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features aromatic, linear leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
