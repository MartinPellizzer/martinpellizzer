Calceolaria fruticosa subsp. reticulata

Calceolaria fruticosa subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its erect, branching growth habit and produces showy, hooded flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern that gives the species its subspecific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
