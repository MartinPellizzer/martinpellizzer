Cinnamomum bureaui

Cinnamomum bureaui, commonly known as Bureaui cinnamon, is a species of tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval-shaped berries. Its bark is used to make a spice similar to cinnamon, though it is less commonly traded than Ceylon or Cassia cinnamon.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.
