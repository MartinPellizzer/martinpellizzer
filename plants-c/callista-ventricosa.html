Callista ventricosa

Callista ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

Callista ventricosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures or trellises.
