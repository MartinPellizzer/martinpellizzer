Caricina stellulata

Caricina stellulata, commonly known as the starry caricina, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Caricina stellulata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
