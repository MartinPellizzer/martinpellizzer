Caryopteris grata

Caryopteris grata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring slender, upright stems covered in silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
