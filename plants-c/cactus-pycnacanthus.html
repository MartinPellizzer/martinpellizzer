Cactus pycnacanthus

Cactus pycnacanthus, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and milky sap. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in summer. Its leaves are typically small and alternate, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Cactus pycnacanthus is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in well-drained soils.
