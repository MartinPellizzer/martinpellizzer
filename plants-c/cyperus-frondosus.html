Cyperus frondosus

Cyperus frondosus, commonly known as the fronded flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus frondosus is adapted to moist environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
