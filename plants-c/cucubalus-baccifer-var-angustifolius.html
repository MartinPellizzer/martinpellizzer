Cucubalus baccifer var. angustifolius

Cucubalus baccifer var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed raw or processed into beverages. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
