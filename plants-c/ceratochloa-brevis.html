Ceratochloa brevis

Ceratochloa brevis, commonly known as the shortleaf grass, is a small, perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Ceratochloa brevis is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in semi-desert environments.
