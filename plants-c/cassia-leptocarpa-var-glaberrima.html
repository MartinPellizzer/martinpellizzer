Cassia leptocarpa var. glaberrima

Cassia leptocarpa var. glaberrima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Its seeds are encased in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
