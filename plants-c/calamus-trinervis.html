Calamus trinervis

Calamus trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a robust rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a distinct three-veined pattern that gives it its scientific name.

Calamus trinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
