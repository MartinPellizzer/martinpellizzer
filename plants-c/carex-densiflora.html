Carex densiflora

Carex densiflora, commonly known as dense-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in shaded areas.

Carex densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
