Commiphora pyracanthoides subsp. glandulosa

Commiphora pyracanthoides subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular myrrh tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family. It is native to parts of East Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudates, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a distinctive glandular surface.
