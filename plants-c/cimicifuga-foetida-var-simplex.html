Cimicifuga foetida var. simplex

Cimicifuga foetida var. simplex, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is the primary source of its medicinal compounds, which have been used traditionally for women's health.

It thrives in rich, moist soils and partial to full shade in woodland environments.
