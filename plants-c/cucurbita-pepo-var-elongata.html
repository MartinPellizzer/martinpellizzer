Cucurbita pepo var. elongata

Cucurbita pepo var. elongata, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, smooth, and often ribbed fruit, which typically ranges in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant is an annual vine that produces both male and female flowers, with the fruit developing from the female flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and tend to sprawl along the ground.

This variety is valued for its nutty flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications.
