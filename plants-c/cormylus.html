Cormylus

Cormylus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from violet to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant's most distinctive feature is its spiky, cone-shaped center, which is composed of numerous tubular flowers. Cormylus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

It also attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
