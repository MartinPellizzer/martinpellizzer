Chlorocyperus cordobensis

Chlorocyperus cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoban flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, linear leaves. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to water-saturated soils.

Its flowering structures, known as spikelets, are usually green or yellowish and are arranged in dense clusters.
