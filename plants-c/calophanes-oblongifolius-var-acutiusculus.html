Calophanes oblongifolius var. acutiusculus

Calophanes oblongifolius var. acutiusculus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved calophanes, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed at the tips. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
