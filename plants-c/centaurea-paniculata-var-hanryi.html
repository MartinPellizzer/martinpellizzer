Centaurea paniculata var. hanryi

Centaurea paniculata var. hanryi, commonly known as the Hanryi knotted thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking, cone-shaped flower heads composed of numerous purple to reddish bracts. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers that are surrounded by involucral leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
