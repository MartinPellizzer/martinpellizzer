Clematis orientalis var. roschanica

Clematis orientalis var. roschanica, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with hooked prickles. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
