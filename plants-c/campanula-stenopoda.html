Campanula stenopoda

Campanula stenopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula stenopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
