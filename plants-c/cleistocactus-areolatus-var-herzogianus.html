Cleistocactus areolatus var. herzogianus

Cleistocactus areolatus var. herzogianus, commonly known as the Herzog's areolated cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in prominent areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a compact growth habit. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of cacti.
