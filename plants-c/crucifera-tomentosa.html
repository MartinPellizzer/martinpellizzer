Crucifera tomentosa

Crucifera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a wildflower, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
