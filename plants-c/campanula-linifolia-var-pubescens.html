Campanula linifolia var. pubescens

Campanula linifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a pubescent texture, contributing to its overall hairy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and ecological benefits.
