Cyperus stramineus

Cyperus stramineus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, and its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female structures. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or marshy habitats.

Cyperus stramineus is often found in disturbed soils and can be an invasive species in certain environments.
