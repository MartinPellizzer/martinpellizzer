Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. glauca

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. glauca, commonly known as the Lawson cypress glauca form, is a cultivar of the Lawson cypress that exhibits distinctive blue-gray foliage.

It is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The foliage is dense and scale-like, giving the plant a compact, pyramidal shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is valued for its attractive color and hardy nature, though it may be susceptible to certain pests and diseases.
