Cocos nucifera var. alba

Cocos nucifera var. alba, commonly known as the white coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm characterized by its white flesh and milk.

It thrives in tropical climates with high temperatures and ample sunlight. The plant produces large, fibrous husks that encase the white, fleshy interior. It is often cultivated for its edible flesh and coconut milk, which are used in various culinary and commercial applications.

The white coconut is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in coastal regions.
