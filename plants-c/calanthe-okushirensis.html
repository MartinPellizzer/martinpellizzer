Calanthe okushirensis

Calanthe okushirensis, commonly known as the Okushiren orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to violet flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky substrates. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Calanthe okushirensis is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
