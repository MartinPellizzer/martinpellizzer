Campium boivinii

Campium boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Campium boivinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
