Cassia trichosepala

Cassia trichosepala, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species.

Cassia trichosepala is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
