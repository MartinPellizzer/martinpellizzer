Cacabus hondurensis

Cacabus hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras acacia, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, though its botanical study remains limited.
