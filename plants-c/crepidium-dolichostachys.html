Crepidium dolichostachys

Crepidium dolichostachys, commonly known as the long-stalked orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a cluster of elongated, pale green to yellowish leaves. The plant produces a dense inflorescence of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
