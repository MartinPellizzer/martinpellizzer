Calliandra concinna

Calliandra concinna, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Calliandra concinna is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
