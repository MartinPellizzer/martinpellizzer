Chamaecyparis pisifera f. aurea-nana

Chamaecyparis pisifera f. aurea-nana, commonly known as the dwarf golden cedar, is a compact, evergreen conifer with a broad, rounded form.

It features scale-like leaves that are bright golden yellow, providing a striking contrast to its dark bark. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that are typically inconspicuous and not showy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

This variety is a slow-growing, hardy plant that offers year-round interest with its vibrant foliage.
