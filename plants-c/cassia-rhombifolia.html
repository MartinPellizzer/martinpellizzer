Cassia rhombifolia

Cassia rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often rhombic in shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cassia rhombifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
