Cucumis pseudocolocynthis

Cucumis pseudocolocynthis, commonly known as the prickly cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing or climbing vine that produces small, spiny fruits. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are unisexual, typical of the family.

The fruit is typically harvested when green and is used in local cuisines for its mild, cucumber-like flavor.
