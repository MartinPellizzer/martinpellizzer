Crotalaria zimmermannii

Crotalaria zimmermannii, commonly known as Zimmermann's cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria zimmermannii is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
