Combretum violaceum

Combretum violaceum, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
