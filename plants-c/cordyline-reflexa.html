Cordyline reflexa

Cordyline reflexa, commonly known as the New Zealand cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, unbranched stems with large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, which are followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a unique, architectural appearance.

Cordyline reflexa is well-suited to coastal areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
