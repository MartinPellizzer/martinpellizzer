Commelina donii

Commelina donii, commonly known as the blue water spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water, adapting to its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat. It is a member of the Commelinaceae family, which includes other water plants.

Commelina donii is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines.
