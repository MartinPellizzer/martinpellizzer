Cucumis myriocarpus subsp. leptodermis

Cucumis myriocarpus subsp.

leptodermis, commonly known as the slender-skinned cucumber, is a wild cucumber species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its slender, elongated fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in warm, arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability.
