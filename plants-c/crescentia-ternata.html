Crescentia ternata

Crescentia ternata, commonly known as the sweet sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by green, inedible fruits that turn yellow or red when ripe.

Its fruit is notable for its creamy, aromatic pulp, which is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
