Cedronella pallida

Cedronella pallida, commonly known as white cedron, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Cedronella pallida is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
