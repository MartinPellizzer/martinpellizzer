Crataegus rotundifolia f. rubescens

Crataegus rotundifolia f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest throughout the year.
