Clerodendrum serratum

Clerodendrum serratum, commonly known as the serrated clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiny, orange-red fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
