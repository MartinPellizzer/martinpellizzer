Calliandra expansa

Calliandra expansa, commonly known as the showy calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink or red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some regions for soil stabilization and as a source of fodder.
