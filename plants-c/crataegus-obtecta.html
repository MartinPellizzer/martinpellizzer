Crataegus obtecta

Crataegus obtecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Crataegus obtecta is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
