Citharexylum dentatum

Citharexylum dentatum, commonly known as the roughleaf cithara, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have serrated margins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in racemes, often appearing in summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
