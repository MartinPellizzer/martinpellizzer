Cyphostemma helenae

Cyphostemma helenae, commonly known as the Helen's moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and cascading clusters of small, pale greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing as a vine or shrub.

Cyphostemma helenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
