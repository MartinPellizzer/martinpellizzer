Canarium longistipulatum

Canarium longistipulatum, commonly known as the long-stipule canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, elliptical leaves with prominent stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
