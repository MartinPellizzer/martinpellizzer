Carduus nidulans

Carduus nidulans, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow composite flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles.
