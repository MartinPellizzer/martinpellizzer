Codonopsis pilosula var. modesta

Codonopsis pilosula var. modesta, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or "dang shen," is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots, which are thick and fleshy, are the primary part used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both medicinal and ornamental purposes.
