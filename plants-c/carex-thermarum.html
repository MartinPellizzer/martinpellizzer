Carex thermarum

Carex thermarum, commonly known as the hot spring sedge, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils near springs or in wet meadows.

Carex thermarum is often found in thermophilic environments, thriving in areas with higher temperatures and consistent moisture.
