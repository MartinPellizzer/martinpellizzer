Crataegus praeclara

Crataegus praeclara, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring and vibrant red or orange fruits in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Crataegus praeclara is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
