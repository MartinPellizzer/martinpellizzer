Corylus imeretica

Corylus imeretica, commonly known as the Caucasus hazelnut, is a species of hazel native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in forested areas and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, similar to other hazel species. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are catkins.

Corylus imeretica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
