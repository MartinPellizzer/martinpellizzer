Calathea villosa

Calathea villosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, waxy leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting colors. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is not only aesthetically pleasing but also features a unique texture that gives it its common name.

Calathea villosa requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
