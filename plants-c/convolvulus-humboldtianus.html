Convolvulus humboldtianus

Convolvulus humboldtianus, commonly known as the Humboldt's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, pale yellow to white flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Convolvulus humboldtianus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
