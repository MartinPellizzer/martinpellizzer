Clerodendrum coriaceum

Clerodendrum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, woody stems and heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warm season. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Clerodendrum coriaceum thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
