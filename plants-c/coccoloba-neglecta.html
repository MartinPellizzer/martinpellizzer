Coccoloba neglecta

Coccoloba neglecta, commonly known as the coastal beauty or seaside spinach, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils along the shoreline. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

Coccoloba neglecta is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a hardy species for coastal environments.
