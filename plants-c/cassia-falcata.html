Cassia falcata

Cassia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. Cassia falcata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
