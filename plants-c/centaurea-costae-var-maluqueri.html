Centaurea costae var. maluqueri

Centaurea costae var. maluqueri, commonly known as the Maluqueri's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
