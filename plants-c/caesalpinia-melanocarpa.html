Caesalpinia melanocarpa

Caesalpinia melanocarpa, commonly known as black thorn or black bean tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with thorn-like tips and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, bean-like pods that contain dark, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry tropical and subtropical regions.

Caesalpinia melanocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
