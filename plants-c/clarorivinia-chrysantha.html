Clarorivinia chrysantha

Clarorivinia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange petals that resemble small bells, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Clarorivinia chrysantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
