Chenopodium cornutum

Chenopodium cornutum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, three-angled capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is characterized by its rough, hairy texture and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
