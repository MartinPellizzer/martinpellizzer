Cucurbita pepo var. brayana

Cucurbita pepo var. brayana, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that can vary in color from green to orange or red. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers typical of the genus Cucurbita. It is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
