Chamaefistula papillosa

Chamaefistula papillosa, commonly known as the silky ash or silky buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Chamaefistula papillosa is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.
