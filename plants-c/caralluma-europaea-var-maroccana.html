Caralluma europaea var. maroccana

Caralluma europaea var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan torch cactus, is a succulent plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
