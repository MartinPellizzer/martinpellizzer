Clematis orientalis subsp. brachiata

Clematis orientalis subsp.

brachiata, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its climbing habit requires support structures, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
