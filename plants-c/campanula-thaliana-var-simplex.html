Campanula thaliana var. simplex

Campanula thaliana var. simplex, commonly known as the simple bellflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
