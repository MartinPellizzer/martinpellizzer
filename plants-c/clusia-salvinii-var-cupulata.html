Clusia salvinii var. cupulata

Clusia salvinii var. cupulata, commonly known as the cupulata clusia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The varietal form cupulata is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and growth habit.
