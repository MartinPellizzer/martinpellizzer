Carex vulgaris f. angustifolia

Carex vulgaris f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
