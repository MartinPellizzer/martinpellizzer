Colpodium kochii

Colpodium kochii, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in brackish and freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant has thin, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It typically grows in shallow water, often near the edges of ponds or tidal areas.

Colpodium kochii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by serving as both a predator and a food source for other organisms.
