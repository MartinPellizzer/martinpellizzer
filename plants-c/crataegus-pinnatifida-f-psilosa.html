Crataegus pinnatifida f. psilosa

Crataegus pinnatifida f. psilosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, narrow leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
