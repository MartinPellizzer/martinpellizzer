Caesalpinia pluviosa var. sanfranciscana

Caesalpinia pluviosa var. sanfranciscana, commonly known as the San Francisco glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like, bipinnate leaves and vibrant red and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens. Its ornamental value comes from its showy inflorescences and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
