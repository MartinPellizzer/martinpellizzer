Convolvulus muticus

Convolvulus muticus, commonly known as the silent convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family, Convolvulaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus muticus is known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, which helps it reach sunlight.
