Canavalia loureiroi

Canavalia loureiroi, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein. Canavalia loureiroi is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various traditional cuisines.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
