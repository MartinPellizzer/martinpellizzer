Ctenopteris rigescens

Ctenopteris rigescens, commonly known as the rigid sea holly, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, stiff, and often waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a leathery texture and a blue-green hue. This plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
