Campylotropis glauca

Campylotropis glauca, commonly known as the bluebells or bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Campylotropis glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
