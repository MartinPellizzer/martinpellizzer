Chloris verticillata var. intermedia

Chloris verticillata var. intermedia, commonly known as the variable blue grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
