Cistus monspeliensis proles affinis

Cistus monspeliensis proles affinis, commonly known as the Moroccan rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in areas of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to grow in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
