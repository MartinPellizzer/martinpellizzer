Cyananthus cordifolius

Cyananthus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.
