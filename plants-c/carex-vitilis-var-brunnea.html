Carex vitilis var. brunnea

Carex vitilis var. brunnea, commonly known as the brown bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or brownish hue, especially along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
