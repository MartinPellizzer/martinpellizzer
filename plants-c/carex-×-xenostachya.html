Carex × xenostachya

Carex × xenostachya, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex × xenostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
