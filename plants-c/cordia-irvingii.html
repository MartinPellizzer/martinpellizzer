Cordia irvingii

Cordia irvingii, commonly known as the red cedar or Irving's cordia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitats.
