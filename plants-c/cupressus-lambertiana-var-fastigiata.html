Cupressus lambertiana var. fastigiata

Cupressus lambertiana var. fastigiata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lacebark cedar, is a variety of the western redcedar.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and dense foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking visual contrast. It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Nevada.

This evergreen is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in mild climates.
