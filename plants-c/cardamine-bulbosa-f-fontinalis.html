Cardamine bulbosa f. fontinalis

Cardamine bulbosa f. fontinalis, commonly known as the spring celandine.

This plant is a low-growing perennial herb with delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant is native to moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of early-succession plant communities.
