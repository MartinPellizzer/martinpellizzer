Carex macrostylos

Carex macrostylos, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and ribbed, with a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in a terminal inflorescence. Carex macrostylos is native to various regions of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
