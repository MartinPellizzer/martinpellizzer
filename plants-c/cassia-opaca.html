Cassia opaca

Cassia opaca, commonly known as the white cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Cassia opaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
