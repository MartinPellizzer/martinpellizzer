Crataegus popovii

Crataegus popovii, commonly known as the Popov's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Crataegus popovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
