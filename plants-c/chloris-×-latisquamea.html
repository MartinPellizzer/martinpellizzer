Chloris × latisquamea

Chloris × latisquamea, commonly known as the broad-scaled grass, is a hybrid perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, scaly structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used for soil stabilization in certain regions.
