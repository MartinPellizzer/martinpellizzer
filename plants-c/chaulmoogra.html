Chaulmoogra

Chaulmoogra, commonly known as *Hydnocarpus wightiana*, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and distinctive, woody fruits containing a single, hard seed. Its most notable botanical feature is the presence of a unique, oily substance in its seeds, which has historically been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
