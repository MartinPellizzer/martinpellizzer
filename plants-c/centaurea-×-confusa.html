Centaurea × confusa

Centaurea × confusa, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular florets, typical of the daisy family.

Centaurea × confusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower habitats.
