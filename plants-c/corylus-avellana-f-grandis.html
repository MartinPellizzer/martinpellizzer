Corylus avellana f. grandis

Corylus avellana f. grandis, commonly known as the giant hazel, is a cultivar of the common hazel.

It is characterized by its large, elongated nuts and robust growth habit. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible nuts.
