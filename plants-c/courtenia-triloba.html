Courtenia triloba

Courtenia triloba, commonly known as the African thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thorny branches and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of stipules.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
