Cynanchum obliquum

Cynanchum obliquum, commonly known as Chinese snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is found in regions such as China, Korea, and Japan.

Cynanchum obliquum is known for its chemical compounds, including alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
