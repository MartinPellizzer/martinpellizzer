Cistus × aguilari f. maculatus

Cistus × aguilari f. maculatus, commonly known as the spotted rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
