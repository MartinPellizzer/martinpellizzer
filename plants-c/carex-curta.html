Carex curta

Carex curta, commonly known as dwarf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section typical of sedges.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually clustered at the tips of the stems.
