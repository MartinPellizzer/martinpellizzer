Columnea ascendens

Columnea ascendens, commonly known as the ascending flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Columnea ascendens thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
