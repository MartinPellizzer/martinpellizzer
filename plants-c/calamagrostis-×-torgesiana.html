Calamagrostis × torgesiana

Calamagrostis × torgesiana, commonly known as the silvergrass or blue grama, is a hybrid grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue foliage and tall, slender culms that give it a graceful appearance. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions. Calamagrostis × torgesiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, add a touch of elegance to gardens and natural landscapes.
