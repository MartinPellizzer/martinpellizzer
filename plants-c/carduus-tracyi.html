Carduus tracyi

Carduus tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are generally spiny and lobed, while the flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers.

Tracy's thistle plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
