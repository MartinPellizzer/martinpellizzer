Calliandra sessilis

Calliandra sessilis, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that produce small, pink to red flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Calliandra sessilis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
