Copaifera langsdorffii var. glabra

Copaifera langsdorffii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Leguminosae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is known for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in medicine and perfumery.

The varietal name "glabra" refers to its smooth, hairless bark.
