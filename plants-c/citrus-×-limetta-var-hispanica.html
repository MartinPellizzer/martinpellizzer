Citrus × limetta var. hispanica

Citrus × limetta var. hispanica, commonly known as the Lisbon lemon.

This variety is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, round, and yellow-green fruit with a thin, smooth peel. The fruit is typically seedless and has a high concentration of citric acid, making it popular for culinary and commercial uses. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and productivity.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
