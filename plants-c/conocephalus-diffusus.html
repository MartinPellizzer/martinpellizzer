Conocephalus diffusus

Conocephalus diffusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved water clover, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive, overlapping leaves that create a water-repellent surface. Conocephalus diffusus is valued for its ecological importance in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
