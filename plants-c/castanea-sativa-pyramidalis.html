Castanea sativa pyramidalis

Castanea sativa pyramidalis, commonly known as the European chestnut tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, pyramidal shape and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which ripen in the autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in forestry and agriculture.
