Chamaecrista glandulosa var. rapidarum

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. rapidarum, commonly known as the rapid-growing crown vetch, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, which helps it thrive in poor soil conditions and makes it resilient to drought. It is often used for erosion control due to its ability to stabilize soil with its extensive root network.

Additionally, it attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies with its nectar-rich flowers.
