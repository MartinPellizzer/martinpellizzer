Cassia capensis

Cassia capensis, commonly known as the sicklepod or flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a serrated edge. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia capensis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
