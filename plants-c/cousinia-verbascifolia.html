Cousinia verbascifolia

Cousinia verbascifolia, commonly known as the silky cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall, branched stems. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Cousinia verbascifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
