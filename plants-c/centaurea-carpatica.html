Centaurea carpatica

Centaurea carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender stems and produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Centaurea carpatica is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of mountain ecosystems.
