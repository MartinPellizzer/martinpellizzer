Cereus cinerascens

Cereus cinerascens, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, a feature typical of many nocturnal flowering plants. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

The plant is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
