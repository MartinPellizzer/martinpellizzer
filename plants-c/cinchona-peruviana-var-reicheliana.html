Cinchona peruviana var. reicheliana

Cinchona peruviana var. reicheliana, commonly known as the Peruvian quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and striking red flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of quinine, a compound historically used in the treatment of malaria. This variant is often distinguished from other cinchona species by its unique floral structure and leaf morphology.
