Calochortus flavus

Calochortus flavus, commonly known as the golden cupcup, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Calochortus.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Calochortus flavus is a short-lived perennial, blooming in early spring and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
