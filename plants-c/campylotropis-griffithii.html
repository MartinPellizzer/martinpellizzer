Campylotropis griffithii

Campylotropis griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir broom.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
