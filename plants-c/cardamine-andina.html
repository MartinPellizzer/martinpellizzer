Cardamine andina

Cardamine andina, commonly known as Andean watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in mountain streams and shaded forest floors. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress, making it a valued ingredient in local cuisines.

Cardamine andina is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to purify water in its natural habitat.
