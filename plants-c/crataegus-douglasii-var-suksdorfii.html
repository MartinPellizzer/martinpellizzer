Crataegus douglasii var. suksdorfii

Crataegus douglasii var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

The species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its hardiness and ecological benefits.
