Cassia oligoclada var. subsinguliflora

Cassia oligoclada var. subsinguliflora, commonly known as the single-flowered cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
