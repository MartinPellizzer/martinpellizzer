Calamagrostis epigejos subsp. acrathera

Calamagrostis epigejos subsp.

acrathera, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, silver-tinged leaves and feathery panicles that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It has a clumping growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
