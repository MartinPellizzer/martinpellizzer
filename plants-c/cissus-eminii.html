Cissus eminii

Cissus eminii, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often white or pale green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus eminii is also valued for its ability to climb and its adaptability to various soil types.
