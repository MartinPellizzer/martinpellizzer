Combretum praecox

Combretum praecox, commonly known as the early flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the late summer and early autumn. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow before falling off in response to seasonal changes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of Combretum praecox make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
