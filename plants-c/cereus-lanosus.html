Cereus lanosus

Cereus lanosus, commonly known as the woolly candleplant, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, a feature common among many cacti. Its stems are succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently in arid environments.

Cereus lanosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering habits.
