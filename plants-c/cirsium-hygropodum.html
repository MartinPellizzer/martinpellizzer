Cirsium hygropodum

Cirsium hygropodum, commonly known as the water thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in calcareous soils. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
