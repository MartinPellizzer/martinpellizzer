Calamagrostis × andrejewii

Calamagrostis × andrejewii, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a hybrid grass species native to the wetlands of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple to pinkish plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture that adds visual interest to gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
