Carex mcvaughii

Carex mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Carex mcvaughii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
