Carex glauca var. melanocarpa

Carex glauca var. melanocarpa, commonly known as blackstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its dark-colored stems and unique morphology make it a distinctive component of wetland ecosystems.
