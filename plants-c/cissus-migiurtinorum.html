Cissus migiurtinorum

Cissus migiurtinorum, commonly known as the African stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its dry environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its stems are succulent and often have a woody texture, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions.

Cissus migiurtinorum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
