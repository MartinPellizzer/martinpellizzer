Clematis montana var. sterilis

Clematis montana var. sterilis, commonly known as the Virgin's bower, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences quickly. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Despite its ornamental value, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
