Carex dioica var. redowskiana

Carex dioica var. redowskiana, commonly known as the Redowskiana sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
