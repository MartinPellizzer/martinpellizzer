Carnegiea fulviceps

Carnegiea fulviceps, commonly known as the Mexican barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 10 meters in height, often with a reddish or yellowish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. Its spines are relatively short and yellowish, providing protection from herbivores.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stem and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.
