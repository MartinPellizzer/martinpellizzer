Celastrus pauciflorus

Celastrus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is found, contributing to both ornamental and ecological value.
