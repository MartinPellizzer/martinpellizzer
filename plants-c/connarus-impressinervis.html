Connarus impressinervis

Connarus impressinervis, commonly known as the impressed-veined connarus, is a member of the family Connaraceae.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a distinctive impressed venation pattern that gives the species its scientific name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
