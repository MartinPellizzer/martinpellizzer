Cattleya aurantiaca

Cattleya aurantiaca, commonly known as the Orange Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers with a yellow lip, which are highly prized in horticulture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cattleya aurantiaca is known for its fragrant blooms and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
