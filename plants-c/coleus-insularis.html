Coleus insularis

Coleus insularis, commonly known as the island coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are often variegated with shades of green, red, and purple, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
