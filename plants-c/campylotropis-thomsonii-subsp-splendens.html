Campylotropis thomsonii subsp. splendens

Campylotropis thomsonii subsp.

splendens, commonly known as the splendour beggar's-tick, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
