Cornus alba var. sibirica

Cornus alba var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features distinctive red stems that become more vibrant in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small white flowers in clusters during early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This hardy species is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
