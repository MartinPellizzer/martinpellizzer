Caulophyllum thalictroides var. giganteum

Caulophyllum thalictroides var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant blue poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant landscapes.
