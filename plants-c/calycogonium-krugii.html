Calycogonium krugii

Calycogonium krugii, commonly known as the Krug's spider plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky seed pods. It is native to South Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
