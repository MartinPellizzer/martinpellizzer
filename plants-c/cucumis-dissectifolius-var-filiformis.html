Cucumis dissectifolius var. filiformis

Cucumis dissectifolius var. filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf gherkin, is a unique variant of the cucurbit family.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, thread-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, cucurbit-like fruits that are typically green and have a mild, slightly tart flavor. It is native to arid regions of Central Asia and thrives in warm, dry climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
