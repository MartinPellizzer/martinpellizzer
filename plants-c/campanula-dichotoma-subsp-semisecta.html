Campanula dichotoma subsp. semisecta

Campanula dichotoma subsp.

semisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has opposite, narrow, and often toothed leaves that are typically hairy. It grows in rocky or dry habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
