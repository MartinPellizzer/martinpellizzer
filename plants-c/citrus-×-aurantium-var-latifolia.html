Citrus × aurantium var. latifolia

Citrus × aurantium var. latifolia, commonly known as the pomelo, is a large citrus tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically large, with a thick rind and segmented pulp that can be sweet or sour depending on the variety. It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in subtropical climates.
