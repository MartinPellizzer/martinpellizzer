Calanthe johorensis

Calanthe johorensis, commonly known as the Johore orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Calanthe johorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
