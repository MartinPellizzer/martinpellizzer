Callirhoe triangulata

Callirhoe triangulata, commonly known as the triangular mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Callirhoe triangulata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
