Coelodiscus muricatus

Coelodiscus muricatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of velvety, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a distinctive spotted or striped pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Coelodiscus muricatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
