Chusquea septentrionalis

Chusquea septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters, giving it a bamboo-like appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its structural similarities to true bamboo, it is sometimes used in traditional construction and basket-making in its native regions.
