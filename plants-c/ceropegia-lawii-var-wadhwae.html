Ceropegia lawii var. wadhwae

Ceropegia lawii var. wadhwae, commonly known as the Wadhwai ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
