Carex acuta var. ruffa

Carex acuta var. ruffa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is native to regions of North America and thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with a tendency to curl at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
