Crataegus monogyna var. serromonogyna

Crataegus monogyna var. serromonogyna, commonly known as the Serre's hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
