Crataegus barbara

Crataegus barbara, commonly known as the Barbara hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, featuring thorny branches and clusters of white flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and persistent, spiny twigs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
