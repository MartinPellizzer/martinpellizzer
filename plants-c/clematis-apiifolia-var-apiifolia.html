Clematis apiifolia var. apiifolia

Clematis apiifolia var. apiifolia, commonly known as the common clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, often resembling the shape of a parsley leaf, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis apiifolia var. apiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
