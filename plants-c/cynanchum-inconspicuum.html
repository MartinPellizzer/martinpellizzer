Cynanchum inconspicuum

Cynanchum inconspicuum, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed areas. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cynanchum inconspicuum is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical feature.
