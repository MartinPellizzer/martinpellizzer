Carum orientalum

Carum orientalum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is a herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its seeds, which are the primary part used in cooking, are harvested when mature and dried.

Carum orientalum is valued for its distinct spicy and earthy flavor, making it a key ingredient in various traditional cuisines.
