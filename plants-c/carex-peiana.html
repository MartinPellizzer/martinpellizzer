Carex peiana

Carex peiana, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex peiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
