Chionanthus polycephalus

Chionanthus polycephalus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, cascading inflorescences that resemble flowing curtains.
