Clematitis integrifolia

Clematitis integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad and entire, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
