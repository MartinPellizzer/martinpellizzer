Crataegus × spicata

Crataegus × spicata, commonly known as the spiny hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus × spicata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local biodiversity.
