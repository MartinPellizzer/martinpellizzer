Cyathoselinum tomentosum

Cyathoselinum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly cyathoselinum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
