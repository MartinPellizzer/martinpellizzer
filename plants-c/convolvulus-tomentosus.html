Convolvulus tomentosus

Convolvulus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus tomentosus is a perennial herb that can spread through its trailing stems and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
