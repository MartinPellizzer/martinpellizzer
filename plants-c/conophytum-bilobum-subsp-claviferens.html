Conophytum bilobum subsp. claviferens

Conophytum bilobum subsp.

claviferens, commonly known as the two-lobed conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, lobed leaves that store water.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, typically in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
