Cortaderia bifida var. grandiflora

Cortaderia bifida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant feather reed, is a striking ornamental grass native to South America.

It features tall, slender, silver-green foliage that resembles plumes, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces panicles of tiny, pale yellow to golden flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its elegant form and is often used in landscaping for its visual impact and drought tolerance.
