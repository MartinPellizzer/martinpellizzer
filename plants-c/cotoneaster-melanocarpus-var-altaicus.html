Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. altaicus

Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. altaicus, commonly known as the Altai cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, orange-red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
