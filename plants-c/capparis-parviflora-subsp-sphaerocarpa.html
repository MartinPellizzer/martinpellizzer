Capparis parviflora subsp. sphaerocarpa

Capparis parviflora subsp.

sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globular caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The plant has thorned stems and produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and have a slightly succulent texture, adapted to arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
