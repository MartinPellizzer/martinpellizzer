Cynanchum caudatum

Cynanchum caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed milkweed, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is known for its milkweed species, and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

Cynanchum caudatum is noted for its milky sap, which is a common feature among milkweed plants, and it often grows in grassy or open areas.
