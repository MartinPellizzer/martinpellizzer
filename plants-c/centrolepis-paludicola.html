Centrolepis paludicola

Centrolepis paludicola, commonly known as the swamp centrolepis, is a unique fern-like plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unusual morphology, featuring a creeping rhizome and small, scale-like leaves. This plant thrives in wet, swampy environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its reproductive structures are distinctive, with spore-bearing cones that are borne on the stems.

Centrolepis paludicola is of interest to botanists due to its primitive features and its role in understanding the evolution of ferns.
