Conostomium natalense var. hirsuta

Conostomium natalense var. hirsuta, commonly known as Natal hairy conostomium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
