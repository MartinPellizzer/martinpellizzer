Crepidium sumatrense

Crepidium sumatrense, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, often hairy stem and produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a waxy coating to retain moisture.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
