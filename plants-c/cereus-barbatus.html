Cereus barbatus

Cereus barbatus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a short, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pink and have a pleasant, sweet fragrance.

Cereus barbatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
