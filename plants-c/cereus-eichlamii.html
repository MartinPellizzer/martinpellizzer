Cereus eichlamii

Cereus eichlamii, commonly known as the Eichler's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and is often cultivated for its striking white flowers that open at night. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers with a strong, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cereus eichlamii is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
