Cirsium rupinarum

Cirsium rupinarum, commonly known as the Alpine thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flower heads and silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cirsium rupinarum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold climates.
