Cnemidaria glandulosa

Cnemidaria glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cnemidaria glandulosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
