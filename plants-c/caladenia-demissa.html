Caladenia demissa

Caladenia demissa, commonly known as the small spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a single, slender stem with a single or two flowers that are usually pale pink or white. Its flowers have a distinctive, narrow spur and are often mistaken for spiders due to their appearance.

Caladenia demissa is known for its delicate beauty and is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
