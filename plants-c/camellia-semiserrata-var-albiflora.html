Camellia semiserrata var. albiflora

Camellia semiserrata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces large, showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge. The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.

The plant is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
