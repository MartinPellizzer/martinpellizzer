Coleus laxus

Coleus laxus, commonly known as the looseleaf coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and prefers well-draining soil.

Coleus laxus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ability to add color to gardens and indoor spaces.
