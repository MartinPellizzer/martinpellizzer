Carex remotiuscula

Carex remotiuscula, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
