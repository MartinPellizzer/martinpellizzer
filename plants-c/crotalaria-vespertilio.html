Crotalaria vespertilio

Crotalaria vespertilio, commonly known as the evening crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Crotalaria vespertilio is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
