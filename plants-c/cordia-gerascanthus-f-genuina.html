Cordia gerascanthus f. genuina

Cordia gerascanthus f. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
