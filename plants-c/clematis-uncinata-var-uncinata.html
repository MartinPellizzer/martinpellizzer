Clematis uncinata var. uncinata

Clematis uncinata var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, usually white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has distinctive hooked tendrils that help it climb and cling to supports. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
