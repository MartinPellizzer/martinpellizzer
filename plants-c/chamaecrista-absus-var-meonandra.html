Chamaecrista absus var. meonandra

Chamaecrista absus var. meonandra, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits and are used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
