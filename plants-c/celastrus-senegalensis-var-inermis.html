Celastrus senegalensis var. inermis

Celastrus senegalensis var. inermis, commonly known as the Senegal moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
