Crossandra fascicularis

Crossandra fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Crossandra fascicularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
