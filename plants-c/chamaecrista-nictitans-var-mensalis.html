Chamaecrista nictitans var. mensalis

Chamaecrista nictitans var. mensalis, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and roadside areas. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. Its seeds are encased in a hard, leathery pod that splits open when mature.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
