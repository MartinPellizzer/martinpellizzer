Cerasus itosakura f. ascendens

Cerasus itosakura f. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry species.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

This variety is well-suited for gardens and landscapes where a compact, flowering shrub is desired.
