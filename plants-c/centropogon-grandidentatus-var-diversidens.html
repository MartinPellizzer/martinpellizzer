Centropogon grandidentatus var. diversidens

Centropogon grandidentatus var. diversidens, commonly known as the broad-toothed centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically range in color from red to orange. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
