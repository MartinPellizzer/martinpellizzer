Coriandrum seselifolium

Coriandrum seselifolium, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, known as coriander, are widely used as a spice.

The entire plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.
