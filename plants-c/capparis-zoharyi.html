Capparis zoharyi

Capparis zoharyi, commonly known as the Palestine caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form, with thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis zoharyi thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
