Calochortus umpquaensis subsp. umpquaensis

Calochortus umpquaensis subsp.

umpquaensis, commonly known as the Umpqua mariposa lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the genus Calochortus, which includes a variety of bulbous plants commonly referred to as mariposa lilies.

This subspecies is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and sometimes yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
