Crataegus indicens

Crataegus indicens, commonly known as the Indian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it has a woody stem with thorn-like projections.

Crataegus indicens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
