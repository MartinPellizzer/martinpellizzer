Chenopodium lineatum

Chenopodium lineatum, commonly known as lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in dry, three-lobed capsules. Lamb's quarters thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of nutrients such as beta-carotene and protein.
