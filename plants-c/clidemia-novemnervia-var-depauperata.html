Clidemia novemnervia var. depauperata

Clidemia novemnervia var. depauperata, commonly known as the scarlet waxplant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine, white hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
