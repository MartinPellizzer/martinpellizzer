Carex warmingii

Carex warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex warmingii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
