Catasetum trulla var. maculatissimum

Catasetum trulla var. maculatissimum, commonly known as the spotted Catasetum, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often yellow or orange flowers with distinctive dark markings, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is a raceme of showy, spurred flowers that attract specific pollinators, such as bees and wasps.

Botanically, it belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is noted for its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitat.
