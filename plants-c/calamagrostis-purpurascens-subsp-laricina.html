Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp. laricina

Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp.

laricina, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of purple-tinged flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more pronounced in the fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland ecosystems.
