Carumbium populneum

Carumbium populneum, commonly known as the common poplar.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family, characterized by its tall, straight trunk and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its bark is smooth and gray, often peeling in thin layers as the tree matures.

Carumbium populneum is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found near water sources.
