Cornus florida f. pluribracteata

Cornus florida f. pluribracteata, commonly known as the flowering dogwood with multiple bracts, is a cultivar of the flowering dogwood tree.

It is characterized by its showy, white bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, creating an attractive display in spring. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, turning vibrant red in the fall.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
