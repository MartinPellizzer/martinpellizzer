Calceolaria palustris subsp. micrantha

Calceolaria palustris subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the marsh calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
