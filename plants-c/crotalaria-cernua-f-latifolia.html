Crotalaria cernua f. latifolia

Crotalaria cernua f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, pendulous racemes of pale yellow to orange flowers.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and features trifoliate leaves with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are followed by narrow, cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
