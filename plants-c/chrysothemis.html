Chrysothemis

Chrysothemis, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to various regions of the Americas, particularly in arid and semi-arid environments. They are characterized by their bright yellow flowers, which are typically daisy-like in appearance. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish or grayish hue.

Chrysothemis species are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their ornamental appeal in gardens.
