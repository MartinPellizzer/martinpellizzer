Callicarpa acutidens

Callicarpa acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces vibrant purple to pink berries in the fall, which are a key feature for both ornamental and ecological purposes. These berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Callicarpa acutidens is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
