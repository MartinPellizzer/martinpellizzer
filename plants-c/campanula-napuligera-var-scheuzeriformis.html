Campanula napuligera var. scheuzeriformis

Campanula napuligera var. scheuzeriformis, commonly known as the Scheuzer's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
