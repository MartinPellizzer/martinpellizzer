Camellia indochinensis

Camellia indochinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves and produces striking red or pink flowers during the winter months. The plant is closely related to the tea plant, Camellia sinensis, and shares similar botanical features. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Camellia indochinensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential in horticulture and botanical research.
