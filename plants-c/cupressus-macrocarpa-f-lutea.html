Cupressus macrocarpa f. lutea

Cupressus macrocarpa f. lutea, commonly known as the golden cupressus, is a variant of the Australian timber tree characterized by its distinctive golden foliage.

This cultivar exhibits a dense, compact growth habit with scale-like leaves that turn a vibrant yellow in the summer months. It is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for landscaping purposes. The plant is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

While it is not commonly used for timber, its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for garden design.
