Ceropegia mucronata

Ceropegia mucronata, commonly known as the African violet or the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which are adapted to attract specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
