Cytisus attleanus

Cytisus attleanus, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Cytisus attleanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
