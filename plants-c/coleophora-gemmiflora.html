Coleophora gemmiflora

Coleophora gemmiflora, commonly known as the gemmiferous casebearer, is a small moth species belonging to the family Coleophoridae.

Its larvae are known for feeding on the buds and shoots of various plants, particularly those in the rose family. The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for this species, contributing to ecological interactions in its native habitat. It is typically found in temperate regions, often in woodland or shrubland environments.

The plant's morphology includes woody stems and clustered buds, which provide both structural support and nourishment for the larvae.
