Cestrum osnoense

Cestrum osnoense, commonly known as the Osorno nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum osnoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
