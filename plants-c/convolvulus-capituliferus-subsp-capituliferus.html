Convolvulus capituliferus subsp. capituliferus

Convolvulus capituliferus subsp.

capituliferus, commonly known as the clustered bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats, and it blooms throughout the warmer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
