Conophytum uviforme f. framesii

Conophytum uviforme f. framesii, commonly known as the "Cape fynbos" or "lily-shaped stone plant," is a succulent belonging to the Aizoaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features a compact, rosette-shaped body with thick, fleshy leaves that are usually green or bluish-green in color. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months.

This species is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
