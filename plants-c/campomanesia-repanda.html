Campomanesia repanda

Campomanesia repanda, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic fruit that is used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its fast growth and ability to colonize open spaces, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
