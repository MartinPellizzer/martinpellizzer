Carex bonariensis var. bonariensis

Carex bonariensis var. bonariensis, commonly known as the Buenos Aires sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
