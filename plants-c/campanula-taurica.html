Campanula taurica

Campanula taurica, commonly known as Tauric bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Campanula taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
