Caragana grandiflora var. pedunculosa

Caragana grandiflora var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the peacock brier, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and its showy, yellow to orange flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control in arid and semi-arid environments.
