Calamus griffithianus

Calamus griffithianus, commonly known as the Griffith's arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by dark blue or black berries.

Calamus griffithianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of fiber.
