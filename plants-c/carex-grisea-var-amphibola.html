Carex grisea var. amphibola

Carex grisea var. amphibola, commonly known as the amphibolic sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with standing water or high humidity.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
