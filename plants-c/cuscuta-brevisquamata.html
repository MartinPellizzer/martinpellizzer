Cuscuta brevisquamata

Cuscuta brevisquamata, commonly known as dwarf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to various regions of North America and is often found growing on grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or human activity, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host plants, making it a potential agricultural concern.
