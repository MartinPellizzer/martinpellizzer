Catasetum callosum var. typum

Catasetum callosum var. typum, commonly known as the orchid with the distinctive yellow and green flowers, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are unique for their intricate structure, including specialized pollinia that are transferred to pollinators.

The varietal form typum is characterized by its typical floral morphology and coloration, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
