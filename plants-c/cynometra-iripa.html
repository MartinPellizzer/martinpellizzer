Cynometra iripa

Cynometra iripa, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is notable for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for construction and tool-making. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cynometra iripa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
