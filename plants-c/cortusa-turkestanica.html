Cortusa turkestanica

Cortusa turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
