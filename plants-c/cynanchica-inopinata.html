Cynanchica inopinata

Cynanchica inopinata, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Cynanchica inopinata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
