Calcitrapa rigida

Calcitrapa rigida, commonly known as rigid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Calcitrapa rigida is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
