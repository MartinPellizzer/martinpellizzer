Cyperus diaphanus

Cyperus diaphanus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically develop in dense clusters. Cyperus diaphanus often grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
