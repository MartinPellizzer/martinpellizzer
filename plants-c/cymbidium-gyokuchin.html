Cymbidium gyokuchin

Cymbidium gyokuchin, commonly known as the Japanese orchid or golden orchid, is a species native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, sword-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of large, showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium gyokuchin is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a potted plant.
