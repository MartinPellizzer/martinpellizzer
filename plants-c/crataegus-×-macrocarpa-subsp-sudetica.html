Crataegus × macrocarpa subsp. sudetica

Crataegus × macrocarpa subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Bohemian hawthorn, is a hybrid species native to Central Europe. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
