Calchas copiosiflorus

Calchas copiosiflorus, commonly known as the abundant-flowered calchas, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in dense clusters. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Calchas copiosiflorus is relatively rare and is found primarily in specific regions of Southeast Asia.
