Carex procera var. brasiliensis

Carex procera var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variant is distinguished by its tall, slender inflorescence that bears small, three-lobed spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
