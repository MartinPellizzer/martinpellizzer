Cuphea pringlei

Cuphea pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its botanical name honors the botanist William P. Pringle, and it belongs to the family Lythraceae.

Cuphea pringlei thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
