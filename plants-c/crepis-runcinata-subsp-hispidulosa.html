Crepis runcinata subsp. hispidulosa

Crepis runcinata subsp.

hispidulosa, commonly known as the hairy rough hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single, branched head, surrounded by a series of leaf-like bracts.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
