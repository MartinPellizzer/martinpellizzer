Cryptocarya resinosa

Cryptocarya resinosa, commonly known as the resinous cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves contain essential oils and resins, which contribute to its ecological and medicinal significance.

Cryptocarya resinosa thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
