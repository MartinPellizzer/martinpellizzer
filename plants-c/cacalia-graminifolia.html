Cacalia graminifolia

Cacalia graminifolia, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in a loose cluster.

Cacalia graminifolia has lance-shaped leaves and produces achenes as its seed type.
