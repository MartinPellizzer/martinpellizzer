Calliandra fernandesii

Calliandra fernandesii, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra fernandesii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
