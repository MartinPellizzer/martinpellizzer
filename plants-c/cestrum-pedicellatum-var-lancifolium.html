Cestrum pedicellatum var. lancifolium

Cestrum pedicellatum var. lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
