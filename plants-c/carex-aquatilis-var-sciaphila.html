Carex aquatilis var. sciaphila

Carex aquatilis var. sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-loving sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This variety thrives in poorly drained soils and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its leaves are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
