Capparis singularis

Capparis singularis, commonly known as the Indian Caper, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, greenish-black fruits. Its botanical features include a pungent odor and a unique ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
