Cardamine caldeirarum var. parviflora

Cardamine caldeirarum var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in woodland areas. Its leaves are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.
