Cinnagrostis cuzcoensis

Cinnagrostis cuzcoensis, commonly known as the Cuzco goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Cinnagrostis cuzcoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
