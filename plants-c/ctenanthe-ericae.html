Ctenanthe ericae

Ctenanthe ericae, commonly known as the African nettle fern, is a tropical evergreen plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery foliage with a velvety texture. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued as a ornamental plant for their aesthetic appeal.

Ctenanthe ericae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
