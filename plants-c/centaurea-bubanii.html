Centaurea bubanii

Centaurea bubanii, commonly known as the Buban's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its striking blue or purple flowers. Centaurea bubanii has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in open woodlands and is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
