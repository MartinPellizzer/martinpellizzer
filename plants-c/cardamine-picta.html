Cardamine picta

Cardamine picta, commonly known as the painted watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in tiny, white spots, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, producing a subtle, peppery scent.

The plant is known for its edible leaves, which are used in some traditional cuisines.
