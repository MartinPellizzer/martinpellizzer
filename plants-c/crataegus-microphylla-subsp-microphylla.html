Crataegus microphylla subsp. microphylla

Crataegus microphylla subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length.

The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

Its compact size and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for landscaping.
