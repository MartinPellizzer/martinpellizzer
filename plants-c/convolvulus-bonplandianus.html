Convolvulus bonplandianus

Convolvulus bonplandianus, commonly known as the Bonpland's morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and twining.

Convolvulus bonplandianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
