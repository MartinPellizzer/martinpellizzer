Carex petricosa

Carex petricosa, commonly known as mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.

Carex petricosa is a hardy species that prefers full sun to partial shade.
