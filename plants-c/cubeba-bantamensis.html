Cubeba bantamensis

Cubeba bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam cubeb, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, round berries with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, typical of the Piperaceae family.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its essential oils and bioactive compounds.
