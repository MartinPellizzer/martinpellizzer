Cissus cabui

Cissus cabui, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa and Madagascar. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

Cissus cabui is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
