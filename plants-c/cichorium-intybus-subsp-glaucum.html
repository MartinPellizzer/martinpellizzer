Cichorium intybus subsp. glaucum

Cichorium intybus subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as blue chicory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bluish-green leaves and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are used in salads, and the roots can be roasted and used as a coffee substitute.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact growth and vivid flower color compared to other chicory varieties.
