Cistus polymorphus subvar. corsicus

Cistus polymorphus subvar. corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
