Carex sachalinensis var. musashiensis

Carex sachalinensis var. musashiensis, commonly known as the Musashi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets as its flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
