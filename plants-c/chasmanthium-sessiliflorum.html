Chasmanthium sessiliflorum

Chasmanthium sessiliflorum, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of purple to pinkish flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it forms dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
