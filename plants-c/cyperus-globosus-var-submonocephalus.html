Cyperus globosus var. submonocephalus

Cyperus globosus var. submonocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces dense, spherical clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological settings.
