Centropogon grandidentatus f. denticulatus

Centropogon grandidentatus f. denticulatus, commonly known as the denticulate centropogon, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins and vibrant, tubular flowers that often display a striking color contrast. Native to certain regions of South America, this plant thrives in moist, tropical environments. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped, and the flowers are often arranged in clusters, enhancing its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscaped areas.
