Cyphomandra coriacea

Cyphomandra coriacea, commonly known as the rough cape gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It produces small, yellow, edible berries that are often used in cooking. The plant has thick, leathery leaves with a rough texture, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has a tart flavor, making it a valued crop in some regions.
