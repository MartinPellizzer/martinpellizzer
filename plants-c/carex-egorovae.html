Carex egorovae

Carex egorovae, commonly known as the Egorov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes.

Carex egorovae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
