Coreosma viscosissima

Coreosma viscosissima, commonly known as sticky coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with orange centers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, aromatic leaves and stems, which contribute to its sticky texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
