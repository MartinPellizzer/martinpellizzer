Cascarilla citrifolia

Cascarilla citrifolia, commonly known as lemon tree or citrus tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are similar to lemons. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to produce fruit in a relatively short growing season.
