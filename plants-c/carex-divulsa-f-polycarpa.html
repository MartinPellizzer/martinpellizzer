Carex divulsa f. polycarpa

Carex divulsa f. polycarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a sedge species characterized by its robust, grass-like appearance.

It typically features narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly broader than those of typical sedges, giving it a distinctive look. The plant produces spikes of small, greenish flowers, which are usually clustered at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
