Cyperus ornatissimus

Cyperus ornatissimus, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, yellowish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in marshy areas or along waterways. Its ornamental value comes from its striking, spiky appearance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cyperus ornatissimus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland ecosystems.
