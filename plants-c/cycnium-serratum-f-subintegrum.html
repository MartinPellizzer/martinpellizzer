Cycnium serratum f. subintegrum

Cycnium serratum f. subintegrum, commonly known as the smoothleaf horsebean, is a variant of the horsebean plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, followed by pods that contain several seeds. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is characterized by its smooth, less hairy foliage compared to the typical form.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable within the genus Cycnium.
