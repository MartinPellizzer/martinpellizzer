Crataegus ribesius

Crataegus ribesius, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Crataegus ribesius is often found in woodland edges and open areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
