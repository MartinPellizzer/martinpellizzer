Croton lundianus var. subfloccosus

Croton lundianus var. subfloccosus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a striking plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The leaves are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
