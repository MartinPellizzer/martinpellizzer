Carex lepidocarpa subsp. jemtlandica

Carex lepidocarpa subsp.

jemtlandica, commonly known as Jemtland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in wet to moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often concealed within the foliage. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in acidic soil conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
