Closaschima

Closaschima, commonly known as the glassworts, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically found in saline or coastal environments, thriving in areas with high salt content. They are characterized by their succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which help them retain moisture in harsh conditions. Closaschima species often produce small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color.

The genus includes several species that are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
