Calea longipedicellata

Calea longipedicellata, commonly known as the "Candle Plant," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, candle-like inflorescence. The plant features showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, surrounded by bracts that resemble candle wicks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Calea longipedicellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
