Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. delphinense

Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. delphinense, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and hairy leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive color.
