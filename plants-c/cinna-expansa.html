Cinna expansa

Cinna expansa, commonly known as the broad-leafed cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Cinna expansa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.
