Crepis kochii

Crepis kochii, commonly known as the Koch's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Crepis kochii is often found in high-altitude environments where it adapts to cold and harsh conditions.
