Cymbidium tsukengensis

Cymbidium tsukengensis, commonly known as the Tsukeng orchid, is a rare and elegant orchid native to southern China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its long, pendulous flower spikes that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This species is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in traditional Chinese gardens.
