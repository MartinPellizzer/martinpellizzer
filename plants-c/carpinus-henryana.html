Carpinus henryana

Carpinus henryana, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, round fruits that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal beauty.
