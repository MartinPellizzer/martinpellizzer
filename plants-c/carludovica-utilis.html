Carludovica utilis

Carludovica utilis, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small and oval, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Carludovica utilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
