Cyclopogon comosus

Cyclopogon comosus, commonly known as the hairy grass or the hairy panic grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and its long, narrow leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

Cyclopogon comosus is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its unique appearance.
