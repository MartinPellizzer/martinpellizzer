Cleome obtusa

Cleome obtusa, commonly known as the bluntleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. Cleome obtusa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of growth.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.
