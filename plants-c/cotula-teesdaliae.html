Cotula teesdaliae

Cotula teesdaliae, commonly known as the silver cotula, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Cotula teesdaliae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
