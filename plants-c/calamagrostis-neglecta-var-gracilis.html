Calamagrostis neglecta var. gracilis

Calamagrostis neglecta var. gracilis, commonly known as slender reedgrass, is a grass species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
