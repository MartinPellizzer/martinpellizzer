Capparis loranthifolia var. bancroftii

Capparis loranthifolia var. bancroftii, commonly known as the African dateberry, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
