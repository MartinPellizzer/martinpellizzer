Citrus medica var. riversii

Citrus medica var. riversii, commonly known as the Persian lime or kaffir lime, is a citrus variant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, green, and highly aromatic fruit with a distinctive bumpy, textured rind. The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a glossy surface.

The fruit is widely used in culinary applications for its fragrant zest and juice, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
