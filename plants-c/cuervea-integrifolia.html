Cuervea integrifolia

Cuervea integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf copperleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Cuervea integrifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant in horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
