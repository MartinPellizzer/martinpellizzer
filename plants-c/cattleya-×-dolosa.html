Cattleya × dolosa

Cattleya × dolosa, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossiae.

It is a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs that produce multiple leaves and flower spikes. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. It is prized for its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white.

Cattleya × dolosa is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
