Cyperus confertus f. debilior

Cyperus confertus f. debilior, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, often contributing to soil stabilization.

The inflorescence consists of several spikelets arranged in a loose, cylindrical cluster, which is a defining feature of the Cyperus genus.
