Chuquiraga

Chuquiraga, commonly known as the Andean bur plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and yellow or orange composite flowers. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures.

Chuquiraga species are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their ornamental appeal.
