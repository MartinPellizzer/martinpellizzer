Croton agrestis

Croton agrestis, commonly known as the coastal croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by three-lobed, reddish-orange capsules. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils.

Croton agrestis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
