Cousinia hamadanensis

Cousinia hamadanensis, commonly known as the Hamadan cushion plant, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It has adaptations to survive in cold, arid environments, including a compact growth form and hairy foliage.

Cousinia hamadanensis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 2,000 meters.
