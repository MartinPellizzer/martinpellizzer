Campanula lusitanica var. matritensis

Campanula lusitanica var. matritensis, commonly known as the Madrid bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and hardiness.
