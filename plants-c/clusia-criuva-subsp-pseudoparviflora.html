Clusia criuva subsp. pseudoparviflora

Clusia criuva subsp.

pseudoparviflora, commonly known as the Caribbean beauty leaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean region. It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. Clusia criuva subsp.

pseudoparviflora is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.
