Cirsium walkerianum

Cirsium walkerianum, commonly known as the Walker's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium walkerianum is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
