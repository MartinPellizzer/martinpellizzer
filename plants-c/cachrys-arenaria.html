Cachrys arenaria

Cachrys arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cachrys arenaria is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
