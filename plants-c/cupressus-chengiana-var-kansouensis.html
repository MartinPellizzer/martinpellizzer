Cupressus chengiana var. kansouensis

Cupressus chengiana var. kansouensis, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Japanese cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
