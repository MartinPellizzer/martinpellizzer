Cytisophyllum

Cytisophyllum, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Cytisophyllum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
