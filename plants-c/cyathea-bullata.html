Cyathea bullata

Cyathea bullata, commonly known as the bullate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive, smooth, grayish-brown stem that is often covered in a waxy coating. Its fronds are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea bullata is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid conditions.
