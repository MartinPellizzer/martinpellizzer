Cucumis melo convar. cantalupensis

Cucumis melo convar. cantalupensis, commonly known as the cantaloupe melon, is a variant of the melon species characterized by its netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers and develops oval to round fruits with a distinctive ribbed exterior. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its juicy, flavorful fruit used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
