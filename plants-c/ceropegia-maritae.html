Ceropegia maritae

Ceropegia maritae, commonly known as the coastal ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent perennial that thrives in arid and coastal environments. The plant has fleshy, branching stems and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy soils, making it a hardy species.

Its unique floral structure attracts specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in coastal ecosystems.
