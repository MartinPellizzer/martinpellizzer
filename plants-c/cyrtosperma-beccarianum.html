Cyrtosperma beccarianum

Cyrtosperma beccarianum, commonly known as the Borneo arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are enclosed within a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Cyrtosperma beccarianum is often found in wet, lowland areas and is adapted to thrive in humid tropical climates.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
