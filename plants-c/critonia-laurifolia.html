Critonia laurifolia

Critonia laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Critonia laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
