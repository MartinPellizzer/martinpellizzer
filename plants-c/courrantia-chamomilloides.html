Courrantia chamomilloides

Courrantia chamomilloides, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
