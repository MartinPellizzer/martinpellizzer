Cymbidium × armainvilliense

Cymbidium × armainvilliense, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cymbidium goeringii and Cymbidium lowianum.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear numerous racemes of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It is native to China and thrives in cool, humid environments with well-draining soil.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
