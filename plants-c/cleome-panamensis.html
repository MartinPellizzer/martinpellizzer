Cleome panamensis

Cleome panamensis, commonly known as Panama cleome, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange flowers with five petals, often accompanied by leaf-like bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Cleome panamensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
