Coreopsis rosea

Coreopsis rosea, commonly known as the pink coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces bright pink to red blooms in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Coreopsis rosea is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
