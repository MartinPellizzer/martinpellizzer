Cardanthera uliginosa

Cardanthera uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It produces showy, pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cardanthera uliginosa is an important nectar source for pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
