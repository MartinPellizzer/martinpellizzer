Coelogyne prodigiosa

Coelogyne prodigiosa, commonly known as the orchid of the gods, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, showy flower that can be white, pink, or red, depending on the variety. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces a fragrant bloom that can last for several weeks. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Coelogyne prodigiosa is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
