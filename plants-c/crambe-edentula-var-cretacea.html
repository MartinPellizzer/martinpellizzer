Crambe edentula var. cretacea

Crambe edentula var. cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous crambe, is a rare and ancient plant species.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dune environments.

Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical and ecological research.
