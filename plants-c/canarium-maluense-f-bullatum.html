Canarium maluense f. bullatum

Canarium maluense f. bullatum, commonly known as the Bulatum candlenut, is a variant of the candlenut tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a hard, woody capsule containing oily seeds.

This plant is valued for its oil-rich seeds and is often cultivated for both ornamental and economic purposes.
