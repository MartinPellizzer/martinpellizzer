Croton argyratus var. gracilis

Croton argyratus var. gracilis, commonly known as silverleaf croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-green foliage with a waxy texture. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight curl. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
