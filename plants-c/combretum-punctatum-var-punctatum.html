Combretum punctatum var. punctatum

Combretum punctatum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a distinctive red or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by bright red, papery fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant foliage.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
