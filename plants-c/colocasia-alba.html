Colocasia alba

Colocasia alba, commonly known as the white elephant arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking white spadices surrounded by a purple spathe, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Colocasia alba is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
