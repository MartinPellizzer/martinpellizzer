Centropogon fulvus

Centropogon fulvus, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink to red in color, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Centropogon fulvus is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in various ecological settings.
