Carmichaelia vexillata

Carmichaelia vexillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kowhai, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native birds like the tui. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.
