Capparis volkensii

Capparis volkensii, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive white or purple center. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with thorned stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Capparis volkensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
