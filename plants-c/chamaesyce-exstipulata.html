Chamaesyce exstipulata

Chamaesyce exstipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically linear to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
