Cyperus jucundus

Cyperus jucundus, commonly known as the happy sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water. Its slender, triangular stems are often green or reddish, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus jucundus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
