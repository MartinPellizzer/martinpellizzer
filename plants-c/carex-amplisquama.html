Carex amplisquama

Carex amplisquama, commonly known as the broad-sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and flat, with a smooth texture and a distinct midrib.

Carex amplisquama plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
