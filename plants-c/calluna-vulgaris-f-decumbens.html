Calluna vulgaris f. decumbens

Calluna vulgaris f. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate heather, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This form is particularly adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
