Cotoneaster chadwellii

Cotoneaster chadwellii, commonly known as Chadwell's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster chadwellii is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats and erosion control.
