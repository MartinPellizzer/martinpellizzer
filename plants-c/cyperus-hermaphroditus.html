Cyperus hermaphroditus

Cyperus hermaphroditus, commonly known as the flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus hermaphroditus is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
