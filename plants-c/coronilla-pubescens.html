Coronilla pubescens

Coronilla pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable species in both ornamental and agricultural settings.
