Cyathea × smithiana

Cyathea × smithiana, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyathea cooperi and Cyathea medullifera.

It is a member of the family Cyatheaceae and is native to tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia and New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Cyathea × smithiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
