Cyperus subtenuis

Cyperus subtenuis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Cyperus subtenuis is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
