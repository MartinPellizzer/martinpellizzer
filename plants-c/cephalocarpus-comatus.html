Cephalocarpus comatus

Cephalocarpus comatus, commonly known as the hairy-headed corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of northern Australia and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, spherical fruiting bodies. The flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.
