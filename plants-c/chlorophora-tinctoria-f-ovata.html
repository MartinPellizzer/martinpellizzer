Chlorophora tinctoria f. ovata

Chlorophora tinctoria f. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf tallow tree, is a variant of the tallow tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody fruits containing edible seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in landscaping and reforestation projects in suitable climates.
