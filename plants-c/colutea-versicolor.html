Colutea versicolor

Colutea versicolor, commonly known as the variable cowpea or variable broomrape, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow or pink flowers. The plant produces small, dry pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Colutea versicolor is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and medicinal research.
