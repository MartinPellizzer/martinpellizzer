Carex darlingtonii

Carex darlingtonii, commonly known as Darlington's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Carex darlingtonii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
