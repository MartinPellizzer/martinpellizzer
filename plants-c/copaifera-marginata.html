Copaifera marginata

Copaifera marginata, commonly known as the Brazilian yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish resin that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in panicles, and it is typically found in humid lowland forests.

Copaifera marginata is valued for its ecological role and its resin, which contains compounds with potential therapeutic properties.
