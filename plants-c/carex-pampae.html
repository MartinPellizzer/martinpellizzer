Carex pampae

Carex pampae, commonly known as the Pampas sedge, is a species of sedges native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Carex pampae is valued for its ecological contributions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
