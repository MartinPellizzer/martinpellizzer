Catasetum callosum var. eucallosum

Catasetum callosum var. eucallosum, commonly known as the large-flowered catasetum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, featuring a distinctive yellow or orange lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include thick, fleshy roots and a pseudobulb structure that helps store water and nutrients.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract specific pollinators, making it an important species in orchid conservation efforts.
