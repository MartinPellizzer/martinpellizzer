Coreopsis cosmoides

Coreopsis cosmoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Coreopsis cosmoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
