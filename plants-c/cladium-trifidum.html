Cladium trifidum

Cladium trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its triangular, sword-like leaves that are often ribbed and have a distinctive three-lobed shape at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically found at the base of the leaf blades. Cladium trifidum thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Its structure and growth pattern make it an essential component of aquatic ecosystems.
