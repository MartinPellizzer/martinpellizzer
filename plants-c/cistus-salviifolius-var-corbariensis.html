Cistus salviifolius var. corbariensis

Cistus salviifolius var. corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbariensis rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
