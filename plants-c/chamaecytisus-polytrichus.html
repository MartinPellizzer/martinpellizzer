Chamaecytisus polytrichus

Chamaecytisus polytrichus, commonly known as the creeping rosemary or evergreen shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth habit and fragrant, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
