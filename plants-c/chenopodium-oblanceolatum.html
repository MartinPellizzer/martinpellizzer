Chenopodium oblanceolatum

Chenopodium oblanceolatum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the base. The leaves are alternate, ovate to oblanceolate in shape, and have a rough, textured surface. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called heads.

This plant is commonly found in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
