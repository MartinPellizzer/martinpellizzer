Cerastium minutulum

Cerastium minutulum, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Cerastium minutulum is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
