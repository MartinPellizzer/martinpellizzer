Centaurea inexpectata

Centaurea inexpectata, commonly known as the unexpected plumevetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces bracts that resemble plumes, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Centaurea inexpectata is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
