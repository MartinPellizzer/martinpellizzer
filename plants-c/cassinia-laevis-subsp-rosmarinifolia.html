Cassinia laevis subsp. rosmarinifolia

Cassinia laevis subsp.

rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silverleaf cassinia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flower heads arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
