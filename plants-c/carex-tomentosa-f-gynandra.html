Carex tomentosa f. gynandra

Carex tomentosa f. gynandra, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
