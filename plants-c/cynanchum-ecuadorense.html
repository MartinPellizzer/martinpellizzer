Cynanchum ecuadorense

Cynanchum ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
