Calamintha rotundifolia var. villosa

Calamintha rotundifolia var. villosa, commonly known as woolly roundleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden use and ecological landscaping.
