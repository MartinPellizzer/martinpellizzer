Chrysodracon halapepe

Chrysodracon halapepe, commonly known as the Pepe's golden dragon, is a rare and striking plant native to the island of Pepe in the southern Pacific.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a woody stem with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a conservation concern.
