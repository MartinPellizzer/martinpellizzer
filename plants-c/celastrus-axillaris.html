Celastrus axillaris

Celastrus axillaris, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with four petals, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its stems are woody and can twine around supports, making it a common ornamental and invasive species in some areas.
