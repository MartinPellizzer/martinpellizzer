Cleomella lutea

Cleomella lutea, commonly known as the yellow cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cleomella lutea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
