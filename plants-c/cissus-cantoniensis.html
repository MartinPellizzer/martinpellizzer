Cissus cantoniensis

Cissus cantoniensis, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to cover surfaces make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and trellis coverage.
