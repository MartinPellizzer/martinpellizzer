Carex purpurifera

Carex purpurifera, commonly known as the purple-fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive purple edge along the leaf margins.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that are often hidden among the foliage.
