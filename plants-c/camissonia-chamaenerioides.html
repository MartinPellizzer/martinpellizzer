Camissonia chamaenerioides

Camissonia chamaenerioides, commonly known as the mountain sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky soils and sunny environments, often found in alpine and coastal regions.

Camissonia chamaenerioides is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
