Centaurea halophila

Centaurea halophila, commonly known as sea centaury, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb that thrives in saline and sandy environments, showcasing its halophytic nature. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking blue or purple flowers with prominent central disks. It is well adapted to withstand salt spray and arid conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological significance includes stabilizing dunes and providing nectar for pollinators.
