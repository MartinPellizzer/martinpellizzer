Coccoloba grandifolia

Coccoloba grandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf coccoloba, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically bright green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red or orange berries, which are a food source for various birds and animals. Coccoloba grandifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
