Cissus microdiptera

Cissus microdiptera, commonly known as the small-leaved grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in tropical gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Vitaceae, which includes other climbing plants like grapes and Virginia creeper.
