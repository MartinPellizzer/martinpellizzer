Centropogon minimus

Centropogon minimus, commonly known as the dwarf piper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Centropogon minimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
