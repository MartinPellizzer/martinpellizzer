Ciminalis riparia

Ciminalis riparia, commonly known as the riverbank cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in moist soils along streams, rivers, and lake shores. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Ciminalis riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
