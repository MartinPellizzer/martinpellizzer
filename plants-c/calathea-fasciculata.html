Calathea fasciculata

Calathea fasciculata, commonly known as the pinky plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a combination of green, white, and pink hues. The plant has a compact growth habit and typically reaches a height of about 12 to 18 inches. Its foliage is often marked with intricate patterns and contrasting colors, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Calathea fasciculata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
