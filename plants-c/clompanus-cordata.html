Clompanus cordata

Clompanus cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf clompanus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Clompanus cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
