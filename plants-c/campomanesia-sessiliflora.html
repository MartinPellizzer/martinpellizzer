Campomanesia sessiliflora

Campomanesia sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, orange-yellow berries. Its fruits are rich in nutrients and are often consumed raw or used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
