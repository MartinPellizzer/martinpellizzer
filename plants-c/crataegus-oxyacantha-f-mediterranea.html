Crataegus oxyacantha f. mediterranea

Crataegus oxyacantha f. mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
