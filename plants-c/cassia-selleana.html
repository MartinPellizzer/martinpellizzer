Cassia selleana

Cassia selleana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fern-like leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia selleana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
