Carex sasakii

Carex sasakii, commonly known as the Sasakii sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex sasakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
