Canavalia mauiensis

Canavalia mauiensis, commonly known as Maui morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a vine, with compound leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the morning. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
