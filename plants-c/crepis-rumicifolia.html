Crepis rumicifolia

Crepis rumicifolia, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
