Cirrhopetalum micholitzii

Cirrhopetalum micholitzii, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, attracting various pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
