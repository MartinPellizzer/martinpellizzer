Cestrum amplum

Cestrum amplum, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet scent. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
