Circaea lutetiana var. longipes

Circaea lutetiana var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stalked wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of delicate white or pink flowers. Its flowers are usually followed by small, lemon-yellow berries that contain the seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
