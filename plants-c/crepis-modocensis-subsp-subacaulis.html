Crepis modocensis subsp. subacaulis

Crepis modocensis subsp.

subacaulis, commonly known as the Modoc dwarf hawkweed, is a small herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and the plant is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils.

It is an important component of the flora in its native habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
