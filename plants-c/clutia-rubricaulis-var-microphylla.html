Clutia rubricaulis var. microphylla

Clutia rubricaulis var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
