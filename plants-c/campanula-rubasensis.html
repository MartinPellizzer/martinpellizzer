Campanula rubasensis

Campanula rubasensis, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula rubasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
