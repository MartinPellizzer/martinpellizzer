Centropogon albolimbatus var. villosus

Centropogon albolimbatus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy centropogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy foliage and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with prominent yellow stamens that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The varietal form 'villosus' is distinguished by its dense, velvety pubescence on the leaves and stems.
