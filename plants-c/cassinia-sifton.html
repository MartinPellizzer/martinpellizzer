Cassinia sifton

Cassinia sifton, commonly known as Sifton's cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features yellow or golden flower heads surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, often in regions with well-drained soils.

Cassinia sifton is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, eye-catching blooms.
