Carya ovalis var. nuttallii

Carya ovalis var. nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its oval-shaped acorns and compound leaves with 11 to 15 leaflets. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk, which are a food source for various wildlife species.

Its bark is deeply furrowed and becomes more rugged with age, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
