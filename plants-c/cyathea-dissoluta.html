Cyathea dissoluta

Cyathea dissoluta, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a slow growth rate. Its fronds are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea dissoluta plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
