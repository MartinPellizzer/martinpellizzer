Comarostaphylis glabrata

Comarostaphylis glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually smooth and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Comarostaphylis glabrata is often found in open habitats and can thrive in full sun.
