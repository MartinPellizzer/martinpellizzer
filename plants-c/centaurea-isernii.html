Centaurea isernii

Centaurea isernii, commonly known as the Isernii knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features bright purple to red flowers arranged in a composite head, surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

Centaurea isernii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
