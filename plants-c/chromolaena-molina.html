Chromolaena molina

Chromolaena molina, commonly known as horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators. Chromolaena molina is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields due to its adaptability.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
