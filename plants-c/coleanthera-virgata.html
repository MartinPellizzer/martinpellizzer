Coleanthera virgata

Coleanthera virgata, commonly known as the slender sand orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its unique floral structure.
