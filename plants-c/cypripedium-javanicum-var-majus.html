Cypripedium javanicum var. majus

Cypripedium javanicum var. majus, commonly known as the giant lesser panda orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive greenish-yellow lip and a prominent, spurred column. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitats.
