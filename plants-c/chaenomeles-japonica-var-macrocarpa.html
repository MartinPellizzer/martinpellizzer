Chaenomeles japonica var. macrocarpa

Chaenomeles japonica var. macrocarpa, commonly known as Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, appearing in late spring. The plant produces large, yellow or orange fruits in autumn, which are edible but often used in jams and jellies. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and persistent fruit, making it a popular garden plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
