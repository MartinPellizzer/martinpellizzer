Cynanchum kiyohikoanum

Cynanchum kiyohikoanum, commonly known as the Kiyohiko's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellowish-green with a distinctive hooded structure, and it produces small, woolly fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
