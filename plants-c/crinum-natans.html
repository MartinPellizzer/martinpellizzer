Crinum natans

Crinum natans, commonly known as the floating lily, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and ponds. The plant is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and the plant is often found floating on the water's surface.

Crinum natans is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
