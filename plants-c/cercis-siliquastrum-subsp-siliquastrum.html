Cercis siliquastrum subsp. siliquastrum

Cercis siliquastrum subsp.

siliquastrum, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
