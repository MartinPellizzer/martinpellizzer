Crepis heterotricha subsp. heterotricha

Crepis heterotricha subsp.

heterotricha, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
