Chirocalyx divaricatus

Chirocalyx divaricatus, commonly known as the variable calyx plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx that becomes twisted or curling as the flower matures. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual with slender stems and small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they emit a faint fragrance, attracting various pollinators.
