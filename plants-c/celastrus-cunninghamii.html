Celastrus cunninghamii

Celastrus cunninghamii, commonly known as the Sydney creeper, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows along forest floors and rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
