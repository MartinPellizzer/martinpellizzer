Cuminum odorum

Cuminum odorum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the carrot family. Its seeds, which are the primary part used in culinary and medicinal applications, are harvested when fully mature.

Cuminum odorum thrives in warm, dry climates and is widely cultivated for its distinctive flavor and essential oils.
