Convolvulus leucotrichus

Convolvulus leucotrichus, commonly known as the white-haired bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Native to Europe and western Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
