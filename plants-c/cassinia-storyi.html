Cassinia storyi

Cassinia storyi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassinia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cassinia storyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
