Chrysogonum album

Chrysogonum album, commonly known as white chrysogonum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
