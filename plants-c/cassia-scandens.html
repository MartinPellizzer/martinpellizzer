Cassia scandens

Cassia scandens, commonly known as the climbing cassia, is a tropical shrub or vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia scandens is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
