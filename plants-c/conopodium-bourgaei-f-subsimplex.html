Conopodium bourgaei f. subsimplex

Conopodium bourgaei f. subsimplex, commonly known as the smooth bladderwort, is a variant of the bladderwort genus characterized by its simplified morphology.

It is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands. The plant's most distinctive feature is its small, bladder-like structures used for capturing microscopic organisms. These bladders are typically smooth and less complex compared to those of the typical species.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microfauna.
