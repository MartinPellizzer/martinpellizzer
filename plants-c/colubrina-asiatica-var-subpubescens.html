Colubrina asiatica var. subpubescens

Colubrina asiatica var. subpubescens, commonly known as the hairy colubrina, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with hairy undersides, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and usually have a yellowish or greenish hue.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.
