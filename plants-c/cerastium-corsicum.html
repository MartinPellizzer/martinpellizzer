Cerastium corsicum

Cerastium corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are typically arranged in umbel-like inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
