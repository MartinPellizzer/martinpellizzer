Chenopodium paganum

Chenopodium paganum, commonly known as wild spinach or lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by dry, three-lobed fruits that contain minute seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Chenopodium paganum is a nutritious plant, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals, and has been used traditionally in various cuisines.
