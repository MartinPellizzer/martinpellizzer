Corytholoma bulbosum var. splendens

Corytholoma bulbosum var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendour Bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant has a short, tuberous root system and produces narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with adequate sunlight and drainage.
