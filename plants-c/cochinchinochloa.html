Cochinchinochloa

Cochinchinochloa, commonly known as the Vietnamese grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and thrives in moist, tropical environments. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading herb with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers.

Cochinchinochloa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
