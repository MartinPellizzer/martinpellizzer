Cerastium mucronatum

Cerastium mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowering stems are often topped with a short, sharp spur, giving it its common name.
