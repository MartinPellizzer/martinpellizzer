Cinnamomum maximoviczii

Cinnamomum maximoviczii, commonly known as the Siberian cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Far East.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that resembles true cinnamon. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by egg-shaped berries containing glossy black seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions of China, Korea, and Russia.

Due to its unique fragrance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable and somewhat rare botanical species.
