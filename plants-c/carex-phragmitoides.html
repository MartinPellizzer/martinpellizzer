Carex phragmitoides

Carex phragmitoides, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires consistently moist to wet soil conditions.

Carex phragmitoides plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
