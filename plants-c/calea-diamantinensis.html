Calea diamantinensis

Calea diamantinensis, commonly known as the diamond leaf or diamond plant, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, diamond-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Calea diamantinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
