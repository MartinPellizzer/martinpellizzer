Cymbidium sanguinolentum

Cymbidium sanguinolentum, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, dark red to orange-red flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its striking, long-lasting blooms that appear during the winter and early spring. Its leaves are strap-like, narrow, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cymbidium sanguinolentum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower.
