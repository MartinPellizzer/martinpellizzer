Cyathea amphicosmioides

Cyathea amphicosmioides, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach impressive lengths. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.

Cyathea amphicosmioides plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
