Chaenomeles maulei f. superba

Chaenomeles maulei f. superba, commonly known as the Maule cherry or Maule rose.

This plant is a hybrid form of Chaenomeles maulei, characterized by its ornamental value and compact growth habit. It is native to Chile and is known for its showy, pink to red flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are typically not cultivated for consumption.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
