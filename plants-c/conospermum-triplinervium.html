Conospermum triplinervium

Conospermum triplinervium, commonly known as the Three-veined Honey Myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, honey-scented flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent venation, and its flowers are typically pink to pale red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

Conospermum triplinervium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
