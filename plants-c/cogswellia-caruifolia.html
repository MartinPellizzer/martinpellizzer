Cogswellia caruifolia

Cogswellia caruifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
