Campomanesia diversifolia

Campomanesia diversifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-yellow berries. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
