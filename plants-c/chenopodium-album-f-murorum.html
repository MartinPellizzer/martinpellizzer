Chenopodium album f. murorum

Chenopodium album f. murorum, commonly known as mouse barley, is a variant of the common lambsquarters.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is widely distributed in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This form is notable for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
