Cactus ensiformis

Cactus ensiformis, commonly known as the sword cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that resemble swords, which are covered in spines for protection. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Cactus ensiformis plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
