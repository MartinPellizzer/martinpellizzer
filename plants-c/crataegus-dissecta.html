Crataegus dissecta

Crataegus dissecta, commonly known as the dissected hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Crataegus dissecta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
