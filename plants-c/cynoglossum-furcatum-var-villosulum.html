Cynoglossum furcatum var. villosulum

Cynoglossum furcatum var. villosulum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, with five lobes and a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
