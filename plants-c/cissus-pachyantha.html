Cissus pachyantha

Cissus pachyantha, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive waxy, translucent leaves that resemble small, rounded shields. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its stems are succulent and can be used for propagation, making it relatively easy to grow.

Cissus pachyantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
