Convolvulus flavus

Convolvulus flavus, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas.

Convolvulus flavus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
