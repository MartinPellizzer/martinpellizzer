Carex rara subsp. patanicola

Carex rara subsp.

patanicola, commonly known as the rare sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and preference for wetland habitats. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
