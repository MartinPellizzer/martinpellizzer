Crotalaria dinteri

Crotalaria dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soils. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Crotalaria dinteri is also noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
