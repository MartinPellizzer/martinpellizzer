Carpobrotus aequilaterus

Carpobrotus aequilaterus, commonly known as pigface, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is extensive, helping to prevent soil erosion in coastal and sandy areas.

Carpobrotus aequilaterus is also known for its ability to colonize disturbed soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
