Cleome nationae

Cleome nationae, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Cleome nationae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
