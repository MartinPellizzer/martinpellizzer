Capsicum solanaceum

Capsicum solanaceum, commonly known as the chili pepper or capsicum, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes.

It is characterized by its fleshy, berry-like fruits that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and green. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and simple, lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white and are followed by the development of the fruit.

The fruit contains numerous seeds and is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
