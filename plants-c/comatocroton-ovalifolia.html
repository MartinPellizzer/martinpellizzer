Comatocroton ovalifolia

Comatocroton ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage.
