Carex textorii

Carex textorii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in early to mid-summer, typically in green or brown hues.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
