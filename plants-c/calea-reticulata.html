Calea reticulata

Calea reticulata, commonly known as the netleaf calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers. The plant features a hairy stem and leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Calea reticulata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
