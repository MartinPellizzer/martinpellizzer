Cyperus pluricephalus

Cyperus pluricephalus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of grass-like leaves and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense mats that can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyperus pluricephalus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of moist conditions.
