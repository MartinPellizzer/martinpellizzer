Croton payerianus

Croton payerianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
