Chamissoa nodiflora

Chamissoa nodiflora, commonly known as the nodding chamissoa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chamissoa nodiflora is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
