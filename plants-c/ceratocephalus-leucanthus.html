Ceratocephalus leucanthus

Ceratocephalus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-headed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive white center, and it produces a capsule-like fruit that releases seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its resilience and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
