Calanthe millotae

Calanthe millotae, commonly known as the Millot's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its inflorescence consists of several clustered blooms. Calanthe millotae thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
