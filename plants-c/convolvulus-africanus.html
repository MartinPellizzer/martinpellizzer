Convolvulus africanus

Convolvulus africanus, commonly known as the African bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
