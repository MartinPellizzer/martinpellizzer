Chrysanthemum capusii

Chrysanthemum capusii, commonly known as the Persian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to the landscape during the cooler months.
