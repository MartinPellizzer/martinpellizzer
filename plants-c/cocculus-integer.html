Cocculus integer

Cocculus integer, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, inflated fruits that resemble a horse's hoof. Its seeds are enclosed in these unique, three-lobed pods, which split open when mature.

Cocculus integer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
