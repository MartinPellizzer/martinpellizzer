Cryptocoryne edithiae

Cryptocoryne edithiae, commonly known as the Edith's cryptogram, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and is often found in slow-moving freshwater environments. It is a popular choice for aquariums due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Cryptocoryne edithiae is also noted for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water parameters, making it a versatile addition to planted tanks.
