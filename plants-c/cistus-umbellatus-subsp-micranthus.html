Cistus umbellatus subsp. micranthus

Cistus umbellatus subsp.

micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
