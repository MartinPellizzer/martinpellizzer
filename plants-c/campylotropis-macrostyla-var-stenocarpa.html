Campylotropis macrostyla var. stenocarpa

Campylotropis macrostyla var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited bottlebrush clover, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, typically blooming in shades of pink or red. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
