Chamaepeuce mutica

Chamaepeuce mutica, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of tundra and alpine ecosystems.
