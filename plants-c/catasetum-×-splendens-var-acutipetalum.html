Catasetum × splendens var. acutipetalum

Catasetum × splendens var. acutipetalum, commonly known as the acutipetalum orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often orange or red flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a sympodial growth habit.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
