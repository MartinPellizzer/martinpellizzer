Crepis darvazica

Crepis darvazica, commonly known as the Darvaz thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and compact, spherical heads of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as steppes and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Crepis darvazica is often found in dry habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
