Cerasus sacra

Cerasus sacra, commonly known as the sacred cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.
