Chamelaucium drummondii

Chamelaucium drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's chamelaucium, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and dunes. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Chamelaucium drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
