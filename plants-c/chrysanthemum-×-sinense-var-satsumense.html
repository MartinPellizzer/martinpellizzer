Chrysanthemum × sinense var. satsumense

Chrysanthemum × sinense var. satsumense, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features finely divided, feathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a symbol of longevity and good fortune in some cultures.
