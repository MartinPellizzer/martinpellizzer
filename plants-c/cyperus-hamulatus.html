Cyperus hamulatus

Cyperus hamulatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus hamulatus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
