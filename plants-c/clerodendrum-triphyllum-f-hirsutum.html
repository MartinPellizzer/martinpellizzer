Clerodendrum triphyllum f. hirsutum

Clerodendrum triphyllum f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy tripleleaf clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its specific epithet "hirsutum," which means hairy in Latin. It produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and striking plant.
