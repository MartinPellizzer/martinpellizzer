Caladenia ampla

Caladenia ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that are typically pale yellow with reddish-brown markings. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. Caladenia ampla thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It is a protected species due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
