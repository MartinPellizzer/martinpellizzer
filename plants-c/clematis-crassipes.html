Clematis crassipes

Clematis crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with five petals. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Clematis crassipes is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.
