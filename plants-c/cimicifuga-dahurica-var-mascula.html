Cimicifuga dahurica var. mascula

Cimicifuga dahurica var. mascula, commonly known as the male Chinese angelica, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its root is often used to treat conditions such as menstrual disorders and digestive issues.
