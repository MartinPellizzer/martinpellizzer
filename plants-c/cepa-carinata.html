Cepa carinata

Cepa carinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or grassy areas. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Cepa carinata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
