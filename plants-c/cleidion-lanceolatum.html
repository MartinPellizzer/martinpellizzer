Cleidion lanceolatum

Cleidion lanceolatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Cleidion lanceolatum is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
