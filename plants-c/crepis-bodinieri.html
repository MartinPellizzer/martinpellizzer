Crepis bodinieri

Crepis bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier's mouse-ear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It is a perennial herb that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
