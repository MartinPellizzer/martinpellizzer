Crocus vallicola var. intermedia

Crocus vallicola var. intermedia, commonly known as the Alpine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
