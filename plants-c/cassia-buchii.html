Cassia buchii

Cassia buchii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits containing seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Cassia buchii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
