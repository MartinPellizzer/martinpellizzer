Cotula turbinata

Cotula turbinata, commonly known as the turban daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Cotula turbinata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact growth habit and delicate appearance.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
