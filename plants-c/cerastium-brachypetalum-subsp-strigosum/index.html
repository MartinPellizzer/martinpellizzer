Cerastium Brachypetalum Subsp. Strigosum

Cerastium brachypetalum subsp. strigosum is a small, flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, known for its delicate and often hairy foliage. This subspecies is distinguished by its short petals and dense, woolly covering that helps it survive in harsh environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Despite its modest size, it plays an important role in its ecosystem and is a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts.
