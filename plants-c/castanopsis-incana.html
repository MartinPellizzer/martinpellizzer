Castanopsis incana

Castanopsis incana, commonly known as the silverleaf tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its fruit is a spiny acorn enclosed in a cupule, typical of the Fagaceae family.

Castanopsis incana is valued for its hard, dense wood and its role in forest ecosystems.
