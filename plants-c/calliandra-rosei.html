Calliandra rosei

Calliandra rosei, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy appearance and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

Calliandra rosei also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
