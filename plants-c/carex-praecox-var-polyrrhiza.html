Carex praecox var. polyrrhiza

Carex praecox var. polyrrhiza, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-ranked flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has fibrous roots and is often found in temperate regions of North America.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
