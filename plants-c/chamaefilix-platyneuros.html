Chamaefilix platyneuros

Chamaefilix platyneuros, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water in arid conditions.

Chamaefilix platyneuros is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
