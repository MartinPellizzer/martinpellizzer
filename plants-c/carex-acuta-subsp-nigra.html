Carex acuta subsp. nigra

Carex acuta subsp.

nigra, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America. It is characterized by its dark, almost black, leaf blades that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Carex acuta subsp.

nigra plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
