Centaurea decipiens subsp. ruscinonensis

Centaurea decipiens subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonese bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.
