Cavendishia pilobracteata

Cavendishia pilobracteata, commonly known as the featherbract orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, feather-like bracts that give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and beauty, it is a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.
