Chrysobalanus racemosus

Chrysobalanus racemosus, commonly known as the wax apple or yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits with a single seed inside. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its high vitamin C content.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
