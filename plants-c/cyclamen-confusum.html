Cyclamen confusum

Cyclamen confusum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, Iran, and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Cyclamen confusum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
