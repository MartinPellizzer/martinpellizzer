Cakile maritima subsp. baltica

Cakile maritima subsp.

baltica, commonly known as the Baltic sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Baltic Sea. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines.

The plant features small, pinnately divided leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is well adapted to saline and wind-exposed environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
