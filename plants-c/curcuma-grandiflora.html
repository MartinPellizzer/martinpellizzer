Curcuma grandiflora

Curcuma grandiflora, commonly known as the giant turmeric or golden ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to turmeric.

The botanical features of Curcuma grandiflora make it both ornamental and valuable for its medicinal properties.
