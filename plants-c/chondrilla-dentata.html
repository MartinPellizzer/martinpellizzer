Chondrilla dentata

Chondrilla dentata, commonly known as the toothed chondrilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Chondrilla dentata is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
