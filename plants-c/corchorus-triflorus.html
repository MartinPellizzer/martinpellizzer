Corchorus triflorus

Corchorus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered dock, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers. It has a short growing season and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
