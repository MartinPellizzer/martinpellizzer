Cyathea humblotii

Cyathea humblotii, commonly known as the Humboldt's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in rainforests or along riverbanks. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's vertical growth.

Cyathea humblotii plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
