Comarum palustre var. stipulatum

Comarum palustre var. stipulatum, commonly known as the stipulate marsh cinquefoil, is a herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with stipules, which are small leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense colonies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
