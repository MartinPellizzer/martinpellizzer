Commelina indehiscens

Commelina indehiscens, commonly known as the blue spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded habitats.

Commelina indehiscens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
