Campanula cervicaria var. longifolia

Campanula cervicaria var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
