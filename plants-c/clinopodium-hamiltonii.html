Clinopodium hamiltonii

Clinopodium hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium hamiltonii is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
