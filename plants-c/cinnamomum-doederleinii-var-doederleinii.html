Cinnamomum doederleinii var. doederleinii

Cinnamomum doederleinii var. doederleinii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Guangxi cinnamon, is a species of tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a strong cinnamon scent. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a glossy upper surface.

The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries, making it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
