Colchicum borisii

Colchicum borisii, commonly known as the Bor's meadow saffron, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a single, spurred flower that emerges from a short stem, often above a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in meadows and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
