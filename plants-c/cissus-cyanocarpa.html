Cissus cyanocarpa

Cissus cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue fruit cissus, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple fruits, which are borne in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
