Christensonella pacholskii

Christensonella pacholskii, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and short, wiry stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted to the harsh, cold climate of its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss in the dry, windy conditions.

Christensonella pacholskii plays a vital role in stabilizing the soil and supporting local ecosystems in one of the most extreme environments on Earth.
