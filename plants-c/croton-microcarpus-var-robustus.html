Croton microcarpus var. robustus

Croton microcarpus var. robustus, commonly known as the roughleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its vibrant color and unique leaf structure.
