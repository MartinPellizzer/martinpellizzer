Carex caryophyllea f. refracta

Carex caryophyllea f. refracta, commonly known as the refracta sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds or streams.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
