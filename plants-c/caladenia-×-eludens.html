Caladenia × eludens

Caladenia × eludens, commonly known as the hybrid spider orchid, is a striking plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like flower structure. The plant typically features a single, erect stem with a single or occasionally two flowers, each with a distinctive, elongated labellum. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to red, and are adapted for pollination by specific insect species.

Caladenia × eludens is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Caladenia flava and Caladenia tuberosa, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus.
