Clethra papuana

Clethra papuana, commonly known as the Papuan clethra, is a flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, elliptical leaves. Its botanical features include stipules at the base of the leaves and a distinctive, elongated fruit that splits open to release seeds.

Clethra papuana is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
