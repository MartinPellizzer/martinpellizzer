Centaurea riaeana

Centaurea riaeana, commonly known as the Riaean cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disc.

Centaurea riaeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
