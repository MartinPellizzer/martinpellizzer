Cyphostemma unguiformifolium

Cyphostemma unguiformifolium, commonly known as the claw-leaved cyphostemma, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, claw-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cyphostemma unguiformifolium is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and resilience to drought.
