Carex atrorufa

Carex atrorufa, commonly known as black-tinged sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark brown to blackish seeds.

Carex atrorufa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
