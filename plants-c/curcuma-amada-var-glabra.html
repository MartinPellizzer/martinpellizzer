Curcuma amada var. glabra

Curcuma amada var. glabra, commonly known as white turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow to white rhizomes. The plant produces tall, leafy stems with small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

The varietal name "glabra" refers to its smooth, hairless surface.
