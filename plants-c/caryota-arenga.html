Caryota arenga

Caryota arenga, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into spiny, orange-colored fruits. Caryota arenga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are sometimes used in the production of thatch and handicrafts.
