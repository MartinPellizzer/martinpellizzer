Cirsium eremogenes

Cirsium eremogenes, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and purple to pinkish purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adapted to reduce water loss.

Cirsium eremogenes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
