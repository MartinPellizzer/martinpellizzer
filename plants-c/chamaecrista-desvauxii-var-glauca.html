Chamaecrista desvauxii var. glauca

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. glauca, commonly known as the blue crown senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in elongated clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its small, bean-like pods contain several seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.
