Coptis japonica var. major

Coptis japonica var. major, commonly known as Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its underground stems contain alkaloids, making it notable in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
