Corylus mongolica

Corylus mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The plant typically grows in forest edges, rocky slopes, and mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it relies on wind pollination.

The nuts are enclosed in a spiny husk and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
