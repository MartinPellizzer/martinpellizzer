Chlorophytum perlongibracteatum

Chlorophytum perlongibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract spider plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that form a rosette, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces inflorescences of small white flowers, which are followed by tubular berries containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
